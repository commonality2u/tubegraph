Uchimata is often regarded as one of the most beautiful and dynamic throws in the sport of judo. This throw is not only a spectacle due to its aesthetic appeal but also a testament to the technical complexity and skill required to execute it successfully. 

## Overview of Uchimata

Uchimata, often considered the most dynamic and pretty judo throw, is characterized by its high amplitude and spectacular execution. It involves a leg sweeping through the middle, causing the opponent to go head over heels and land flat on their back <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:04">[01:00:04]</a>. 

## Execution of Uchimata

The technique involves standing on a single foot while raising the other foot in the air. It is a forward throw, meaning the thrower's back faces the opponent, and they both fly and twist through the air <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:07">[01:01:07]</a>. 

### Key Principles

1. **Balance and Coordination**: Uchimata demands a balance on one leg while swinging the other leg through the opponent's center of gravity. This necessitates a high degree of balance and coordination <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:00">[01:02:00]</a>.

2. **Proper Entry and Gripping**: The execution starts by pulling the opponent's upper body towards you while moving your legs towards them. The entry requires the head and body to move in opposite directions, creating a moment to lift the opponent onto the hip <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:31">[01:02:31]</a>.

3. **Twisting Motion**: The finish of the throw involves a twisting motion where both judokas are momentarily airborne, creating an imposing and unstoppable twist <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:05:59">[03:05:59]</a>.

4. **Timing and Finesse**: Successful execution requires impeccable timing to facilitate the throw with minimal use of force, focusing instead on leveraging the opponent's momentum <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:05:31">[03:05:31]</a>.

## Learning Uchimata

Uchimata is one of the more challenging techniques to learn due to its complexity. Mastery of this throw requires consistent practice, focusing on developing the necessary balance, coordination, and timing. It can be particularly confusing for beginners trying to understand the mechanics of why and how the throw works <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:07">[01:00:07]</a>.

### Common Difficulties

- **Standing on One Leg**: Maintaining balance on one leg while executing a dynamic movement is one of the hardest parts of learning Uchimata.
- **Cork and Twisting Motion**: Understanding the correct execution of the cork and twisting motion can be perplexing.
- **Creating Movement**: Initiating the forward momentum of the opponent while positioning oneself for the throw demands precise execution.

Some of the top judokas known for their mastery of Uchimata include two-time Olympic gold medalist Shohei Ono, whose execution of the throw is nearly unstoppably efficient and dramatic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:51">[01:03:51]</a>.

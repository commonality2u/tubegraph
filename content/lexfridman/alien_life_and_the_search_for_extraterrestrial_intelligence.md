## The Probability of Alien Life

The question of whether there is intelligent life elsewhere in the universe is fraught with complexity. Bailey suggests that considering the vast number of stars and planets, the existence of self-replicating organisms seems likely. However, the absence of contact or evidence for [[extraterrestrial_life_and_intelligent_civilizations | intelligent extraterrestrial civilizations]] is discouraging <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:59">[00:01:59]</a>.

This absence of evidence is often framed in the context of the Fermi Paradox, which questions why we have not yet detected extraterrestrial civilizations despite the high probability of their existence given the size and age of the universe. This paradox invites substantial discussion on how alien life might communicate or manifest in ways undetectable by our current means <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:01">[00:03:01]</a>.

Such a find would pivotally inform our understanding of life's genesis and resilience.

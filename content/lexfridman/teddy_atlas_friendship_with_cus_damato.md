## Meeting and Mentorship

In the early 1980s, Teddy Atlas began working with Cus D'Amato, a legendary and sometimes controversial boxing trainer known for his impact on fighters like Floyd Patterson and Jose Torres. Atlas was mentored by D'Amato, who taught him not just the technicalities of boxing but also deeper life philosophies. One significant lesson that D'Amato imparted to Atlas was: "No matter what a man says, it's what he does in the end that he intended to do all along" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:00">[36:00]</a>. This principle became a guiding light for Atlas, emphasizing action over words, and showcasing D'Amato's understanding of human behavior.

## The Philosophy of Fear

D'Amato's teachings went beyond boxing rings. He believed in the formative power of fear and how it can lead to greatness. This notion is captured by D'Amato's insight that anyone in a potentially fearful situation who claimed they weren't afraid was either lying or needed medical help <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:40">[36:40]</a>. D'Amato's approach to training was deeply philosophical, viewing fear not as a weakness but as a necessary component in striving for greatness.

## Building the Gym and the Rise of Mike Tyson

Atlas played a key role in revitalizing the gym in Catskill by training numerous young fighters, building a strong environment that eventually attracted a young Mike Tyson. D'Amato and Atlas recognized Tyson's raw power and potential and took on the challenge to mold him into a legendary fighter. D'Amato’s belief in Tyson's potential was prophetic, predicting him to be a future world champion even when Tyson was still overcoming personal struggles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:30">[01:02:30]</a>.

## Loyalty and Betrayal

Despite the formative years together, the partnership between Atlas and D'Amato was tested when loyalty came into question. After an incident involving Mike Tyson and Atlas’s family, D'Amato chose to side with Tyson, leading to Atlas's departure from the establishment they built together <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:48">[01:04:48]</a>. This decision left Atlas feeling betrayed, challenging his perception of the values that D'Amato had long championed, particularly around loyalty and personal sacrifice.

## Forgiveness and Reflection

Only with time did Atlas begin to reconcile the complexities of his relationship with D'Amato. He learned to forgive D'Amato’s human failings amidst the pressures of achieving greatness through Tyson. Atlas reflected on the importance of empathy and understanding in the face of betrayal, suggesting that forgiveness begins by acknowledging one's own weaknesses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:57">[01:09:57]</a>. Ultimately, Atlas credited D'Amato for giving him more than he took away, leaving a legacy that Atlas continues to build on.

## Legacy Influence

Through his mentorship and complex friendship with Cus D'Amato, Teddy Atlas emerged as a prominent boxing trainer with a deep set of values centered around loyalty, courage, and the constant struggle against one's weaknesses.

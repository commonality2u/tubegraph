## Introduction

This article delves into the firsthand account of Niels Jorgensen, a New York firefighter with over 21 years of service, recounting his experiences on the harrowing day of September 11, 2001, as well as the subsequent impact it had on his life and the lives of his fellow first responders.

Niels Jorgensen was directly involved in the aftermath of the 9/11 terrorist attacks, serving at Ground Zero and witnessing the destruction firsthand. He offers a poignant narrative of that day, the impact on his health, and his ongoing advocacy for [[advocacy_for_first_responders_and_survivors_post_911 | 9/11 survivors and first responders]].

## Early Life and Career

Niels began his day on September 11, 2001, under the guise of a typical late summer morning in New York City. As a firefighter, he balanced multiple jobs to support his family, a common scenario for many in the service. He was on duty when the first report of the plane hitting the World Trade Center emerged over the news radio, an event that was initially thought to be an accident involving a small aircraft <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:05">[00:05:05]</a>.

## The Day of the Attacks

On that fateful day, Jorgensen departed early to begin his route. Upon hearing of the attack on the radio and witnessing the smoke from afar, a deep fear for the people involved gripped him. Contrary to standard protocol for off-duty responders, he felt compelled to report to his firehouse for duty <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:00">[00:07:00]</a>.

Jorgensen recounts the pivotal moments following the collapse of the towers including the overwhelming chaos and efforts to regroup and respond effectively amidst the crisis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:18">[00:18:18]</a>.

## Retrospective and Personal Impact

The tragic day left indelible marks on Niels both physically and emotionally. He eventually contracted leukemia due to exposure to toxins at Ground Zero, compelling him to retire from the force <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:11">[00:01:11]</a>. This personal battle is reflective of the broader [[health_impacts_and_struggles_of_911_first_responders | health struggles faced by 9/11 first responders]] over the years.

Throughout his narrative, Niels emphasizes the profound impact of unity and humanity experienced in the aftermath on 9/12—an overwhelming sense of community and national solidarity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:10">[00:02:10]</a>.

## Advocacy and Reflection

Niels remains a powerful advocate for [[advocacy_for_first_responders_and_survivors_post_911 | first responders and survivors]] needing medical and financial support in the years following 9/11. His efforts parallel those of other prominent figures such as Jon Stewart, who actively voiced the ongoing struggles and needs of the affected, urging governmental responsibility and support.

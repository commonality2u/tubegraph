The classic psychedelics, such as psilocybin found in mushrooms, LSD, and DMT, primarily act on the serotonin 2A receptor. ## The Impact on Consciousness

The core of psychedelic research revolves around their capacity to expand human consciousness. People often report experiences they never thought were possible, challenging the existing framework of [[role_of_psychedelics_in_cognition_and_insight | consciousness and cognition]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:00">[00:21:00]</a>. These substances could potentially reset or alter neural pathways, allowing individuals to revisit and reframe their self-perception and worldviews.

### Mechanisms within the Brain

The brain activity associated with psychedelics often involves changes in the default mode network, crucial for self-referential thoughts. This network undergoes reduced connectivity during psychedelic experiences, potentially correlating with ego dissolution and a loss of self-boundaries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:50:00">[02:50:00]</a>.

## Therapeutic Potential

Psychedelics have shown promise in treating mental health issues, such as addiction and PTSD, by promoting significant alterations in perception and emotional processing. Studies demonstrate substantial smoking cessation success rates with psilocybin-assisted therapy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:14:00">[02:14:00]</a>.

## Consciousness and Beyond

One of the fascinating aspects of psychedelic research is its potential implications for understanding [[psychedelics_and_meditation_in_understanding_consciousness | consciousness itself]]. By altering chemical interactions in the brain, psychedelics might reveal insights into the fundamental nature of consciousness and reality. Some researchers speculate that psychedelics could bring us closer to understanding how consciousness emerges from neural processes, if not directly through their scientific study, then indirectly by challenging and expanding our collective cognitive frameworks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:45:00">[02:45:00]</a>.

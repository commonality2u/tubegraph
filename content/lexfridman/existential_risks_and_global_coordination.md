## Understanding Existential Risks

Existential risks refer to scenarios that could cause human extinction or permanently and drastically curtail humanity's potential. Historically, human civilizations have faced threats that have eventually led to their downfall. However, with the advent of advanced technology, the scale and impact of potential existential risks have grown significantly. For instance, the development of nuclear weapons introduced the possibility of human-induced global catastrophic risks for the first time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:32:35">[01:32:35]</a>. Today, other risks such as bioengineering, [[existential_risks_nuclear_ai_and_biological_threats | artificial intelligence]], and ecological disasters further complicate the global landscape <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:33:00">[01:33:00]</a>.

## The Role of Technology

The rapid advancement of exponential technology represents a double-edged sword for existential risks. On one hand, such technologies have societal benefits and can potentially avert crises; on the other hand, they can also lead to unprecedented risks. For example, democracies led by self-interested corporations and authoritarian nation-states using technology to consolidate power represent significant challenges. These actors potentially use digital technologies for societal control rather than enhancing global cooperation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:49:02">[03:49:02]</a>.

## Challenges of Global Coordination

Coordinating global efforts to mitigate existential risks is complex. The challenges arise from the scale of issues, such as ecological collapse due to overconsumption of resources, the existential threats posed by new technologies, and our interconnected systems amplifying local issues into global crises <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:37:30">[01:37:30]</a>. 

The **Breton Woods model** of global governance, which promoted post-World War II economic interdependence to prevent conflict, is increasingly seen as inadequate. This model struggles to address contemporary issues like environmental degradation and decentralized catastrophe capacity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:35:01">[01:35:01]</a>.

## Proposed Solutions

### 1. Upgrading Social Technologies

An effective way to handle existential risks is to advance social technologies that foster cooperation, sense-making, and adaptive rule structures. This involves reconceptualizing how societies function, moving away from traditional nation-state models towards more flexible and adaptive systems that align better with digital realities and interconnected economies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:52:01">[03:52:01]</a>.

### 2. Reevaluating Economic Metrics

The current focus on GDP as the primary success metric for nations encourages resource depletion and economic inequalities. New indices that measure the inverse correlation of addiction within society or compersion (a positive emotional response to others' happiness and success) may provide more sustainable pathways for measuring societal success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:34:41">[02:34:41]</a>.

### 3. Leveraging Decentralized Technologies

There is potential in leveraging decentralized technologies to improve governance. However, these technologies must be guided by ethical considerations, ensuring they bolster collective intelligence and facilitate transparency and accountability across all levels of government <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:50:45">[03:50:45]</a>.

### 4. Encouraging Global Dialogues

It is crucial to engage in dialogues focused on identifying shared goals, risks, and value systems. A collective effort to comprehend and mitigate existential risks can foster global coordination and peace. The role of education and information dissemination in these dialogues is essential, providing a foundation for consensus and collective action <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:59:00">[03:59:00]</a>.

> [!info] The Complexity of Global Coordination
>
> Creating a system that can facilitate cooperation on such a global scale is inherently complex. Nonetheless, by iterating on the principles of adaptability, interconnectedness, and empathy, there can be a move toward a future where humanity's potential survives and thrives rather than diminishes amid existential threats.

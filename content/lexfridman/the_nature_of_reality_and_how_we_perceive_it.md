## Consciousness and Reality

> [!info] Consciousness and Reality
>
> Harris explores a burgeoning idea: the possibility that [[consciousness_and_reality | consciousness is a fundamental property of the universe]] rather than a byproduct of biological processes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:31">[00:00:31]</a>. If consciousness is fundamental, it might mean that it permeates every element of the universe, thereby molding the very fabric of reality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:06">[01:01:06]</a>.

## Perception as Illusion

Annika Harris discusses the concept of free will, arguing that it might largely be an illusion constructed by consciousness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:57">[00:01:57]</a>. This aligns with the idea that many of our perceptions and thoughts are constructed post hoc by brain processing, challenging long-held assumptions about human autonomy and the accuracy of perception <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:58">[01:08:58]</a>.

## Intuitions and Scientific Discovery

Scientific progress often requires a shattering of intuition. Harris points out that throughout history, breakthroughs in understanding the universe have necessitated updates or complete overhauls of our intuitions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:29">[00:04:29]</a>. This is especially the case in consciousness studies, as researchers attempt to navigate entrenched intuitive understandings of the mind that could be misleading <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:17">[00:03:17]</a>.

## Quantum Mechanics and Reality

## The Evolution of Perception

Harris posits that our perceptions evolved primarily as survival mechanisms rather than accurate representations of an objective reality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:04:10">[02:04:10]</a>. This evolutionary perspective suggests that our view of reality is limited and potentially misleading, shaped more by evolutionary utility than true representation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:03:03">[02:03:03]</a>.

Deep learning, a subset of artificial intelligence, has become increasingly pivotal in a variety of domains. However, the accessibility and education surrounding deep learning have long been barriers to entry for many. This article explores the initiatives and philosophies that aim to break down these barriers, making deep learning more accessible and understandable for a broader audience.

## Fast AI: Democratizing Deep Learning

**Fast AI**, founded by Jeremy Howard, is at the forefront of making deep learning more accessible. It is a research institute and online platform that emphasizes the practical application and hands-on exploration of deep learning. The platform is designed to be accessible to beginners while providing valuable insights to experts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>.

> [!info] Fast AI's Approach
> 
> Fast AI focuses on practical applications, minimizing unnecessary complexity ("BS") that can obscure educational content, and encourages both beginners and experts to engage with the cutting-edge of deep learning technology <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:45">[00:00:45]</a>.

## The Importance of Pragmatic Learning

Jeremy Howard's teaching philosophy stresses the importance of pragmatic learning. By empowering domain experts—who are knowledgeable about their field but may lack deep learning expertise—Fast AI helps them leverage their existing data and understanding to create impactful solutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:02">[00:38:02]</a>.

### Training Models as a Learning Tool

Howard advises learners to train a variety of models as a critical part of the learning process. This hands-on experience is vital for gaining an intuitive understanding of how deep learning models operate and improve with adjustments. Practicing on real-world data helps solidify these concepts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:21:37">[01:21:37]</a>.

## Overcoming Educational Barriers

### Addressing Theoretical Complexity

The gap between theory and practice can often deter newcomers from entering the field. Howard criticizes much of the theoretical research in deep learning as not being practically useful, advocating instead for a focus on more impactful areas such as [[deep_learning_techniques | transfer learning]] and [[deep_learning_challenges_and_limitations | active learning]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:00">[00:41:00]</a>.

### Tools and Platforms

Platforms like **Google Cloud** and **AWS** have made accessing GPUs easier, allowing students to engage with computational tasks without significant upfront investment. Howard highlights the ease of using platforms like **Salamander** and **Paper Space** to quickly get started with GPU training on the cloud <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:56">[01:08:56]</a>.

## The Role of Fast AI in Career Development

The courses offered by Fast AI are designed not just to teach deep learning but to enable individuals to become experts in their respective domains using deep learning as a tool. Students are encouraged to integrate deep learning with their domain-specific knowledge, thereby enabling innovative solutions to complex problems.

## Conclusion

Fast AI and Jeremy Howard’s educational initiatives demonstrate that deep learning can be made accessible to a wide audience, including those without a traditional background in computer science. By prioritizing practical application and user engagement, Fast AI empowers individuals to harness the potential of deep learning in diverse fields, ensuring that the benefits of this technology can be realized on a global scale.

For those interested in furthering their education in deep learning, Fast AI offers a comprehensive curriculum that is both free and highly regarded in the field. This democratization of AI education ensures that more people can become proficient in deep learning techniques, contributing positively to the ever-evolving landscape of artificial intelligence.

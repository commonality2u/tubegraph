## Feats of Strength and Motivation

Duffin's quest to achieve what was perceived as beyond humanly possible drove him to embark on a five-year journey he called "The Grand Goals" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:28">[03:28]</a>. ### The Grand Goals

The Grand Goals included three key objectives, with a focus on demonstrating the potential of managing and controlling spinal mechanics through fundamental movement patterns: the squat and the deadlift <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:58">[00:02:58]</a>. Duffin broke new ground by not only attempting these lifts but excelling at them by completing multiple reps under the extraordinary weight of 1,000 pounds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:02">[00:04:02]</a>.

### The Day of Achievement

## Philosophical Approach to Training

Duffin emphasizes the importance of training principles that involve consistent, scalable progress over time rather than constant heavy lifting. He warns about the risks associated with sudden spikes in training intensity that could lead to injury <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:02">[00:59:02]</a>.

He also discusses the importance of using a methodological approach that involves a deep understanding of the mechanics of movement and the appropriate application of [[mental_and_physical_challenges_in_achieving_strength_goals | mental and physical challenges]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:58">[00:11:58]</a>.

Stephen Schwarzman, CEO and co-founder of Blackstone, one of the world's leading investment firms, is renowned for his significant impact in the business world. With over $530 billion in assets under management, his experiences offer valuable insights into what makes a successful business leader. The following explores the core elements of Schwarzman's business philosophy as discussed in a conversation and his book *What It Takes* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:01">[00:00:01]</a>.

## The Impact of Scale

At the heart of Schwarzman's philosophy is the idea of pursuing projects that are large and consequential. He believes that operating at scale not only makes it easier to succeed but also attracts talent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:35">[00:04:35]</a>. When faced with cyclical opportunities, Schwarzman suggests that larger projects offer more chances for success as they provide various avenues for adjustment when things don't go as planned <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:39">[00:06:39]</a>.

> [!quote] Stephen Schwarzman
> "If you're going to do something, do something very consequential... Because if you operate in that kind of space, when you're successful, it's a huge impact." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:46">[00:04:46]</a>

## Pattern Recognition

Pattern recognition is another crucial component in Schwarzman's decision-making process. He likens this ability to spotting discordant notes or outliers that indicate significant changes afoot. Observing these discrepancies can be the key to identifying emerging opportunities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:00">[00:08:00]</a>. Schwarzman emphasizes the importance of being attuned to changes and focusing on the facts that don't fit traditional patterns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:00">[00:09:00]</a>.

## Humility and Teamwork

Despite his success, Schwarzman expresses a humble approach, admitting that he does not see himself as all-knowing. This humility is a competitive advantage, allowing him to avoid the ego trap that can hinder many successful people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:00">[00:24:00]</a>. Schwarzman's advice to entrepreneurs highlights the importance of not going it alone, but rather building a team with complementary skills <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:14">[01:03:14]</a>.

> [!quote] Stephen Schwarzman
> "Entrepreneur means you're alone in effect, and that's the myth, but it's mostly a myth." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:02">[01:04:02]</a>

## Philanthropy as a Visionary Endeavor

Schwarzman approaches philanthropy similarly to business opportunities, focusing on solving big societal issues and creating new organizations to address them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:00">[00:34:00]</a>. His contribution to the MIT College of Computing is an example of his commitment to fostering advancement in fields like AI, with an eye on maintaining ethical oversight <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:23">[00:28:23]</a>.

In summary, Stephen Schwarzman's business philosophy integrates [pattern recognition](https://lexfridman.com/episodes/#pattern-recognition), listening, humility, teamwork, and a visionary approach to both business and philanthropy. His commitment to large-scale, impactful endeavors, grounded in keen observation and deep listening, exemplifies a holistic approach to leadership and success.

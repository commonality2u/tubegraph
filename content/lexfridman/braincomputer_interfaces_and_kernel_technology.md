## Introduction to Kernel Technology

Kernel is an innovative company founded by [[brian_johnson | Brian Johnson]], known for developing devices that monitor and record brain activity. Previously, Brian Johnson founded Braintree, a mobile payment company that acquired Venmo and was later acquired by PayPal and eBay. Kernel's mission revolves around unlocking the mystery of the human mind by measuring brain signals in a high-bandwidth way, thus contributing to understanding human cognition and advancing the field of [[future_of_braincomputer_interfaces_and_their_implications | brain-computer interfaces]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:07">[00:00:07]</a>.

## The Kernel Experience

A notable aspect of Kernel's contribution includes the Kernel Flow brain interface, a non-invasive device that utilizes spectroscopy technology to image brain activity. This device measures brain activity by detecting blood oxygenation levels corresponding to neuronal activity. Each device module features a laser and several sensors that capture a vast array of data points, allowing real-time feed of cortical brain activity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:10">[00:05:10]</a>.

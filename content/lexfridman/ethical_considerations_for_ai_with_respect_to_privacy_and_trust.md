## Privacy: A Core Ethical Concern

Privacy is recognized as a fundamental right, yet AI systems' need to process large volumes of data poses intrinsic challenges. Amazon Alexa, an AI-driven voice assistant, exemplifies these challenges by existing within personal spaces, thus having the potential to access a wealth of user data <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:00">[00:45:00]</a>.

> [!info] Privacy Measures
> 
> Key strategies involve transparency and control. Amazon allows users explicit control over their data, enabling them to review and delete voice recordings to address privacy concerns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:01">[00:46:01]</a>.

## Trust: Building User Confidence

The success of AI systems heavily depends on user trust, which requires robust data handling policies and consistent, dependable performance. Trust is critically important in interactions with AI, forming the basis for deeper engagement and usage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:34">[00:41:34]</a>.

### Addressing Trust through Transparency

- **Transparency:** Users are informed about when Alexa is active through visual signals, such as a light ring, which indicates when voice data is being sent to the cloud <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:45">[00:45:45]</a>.
- **Control Over Data:** Users have the ability to manage their interactions with Alexa through voice commands that allow data deletion and privacy management <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:32">[00:46:32]</a>.

## Novelty in Interdisciplinary Innovation

Innovation in sustainable materials often involves a multifaceted approach that integrates elements from various fields. According to renowned engineer, scientist, and designer Nery Oxman, innovation must touch upon different aspects such as synthetic biology, robotics, and material science. When novelty in these areas is achieved together, it leads to groundbreaking developments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:24">[00:00:24]</a>.

## Material Ecology

Material Ecology, a concept Oxman coined in 2005, envisions all material as part of the ecological system with a positive impact on the environment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:39">[00:06:39]</a>. This innovative approach aims to merge the boundary between grown and made, proposing a world where cities and technology exist in synergy with nature <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:06">[00:05:06]</a>.

## Prominent Projects in Material Innovation

### Silk Pavilion

One significant work is the Silk Pavilion, where Oxman and her team used computational templates to guide silkworms in creating a large silk structure. This project illustrated the potential of using natural organisms in design processes, marrying computation and biology to enable new forms of building <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:00">[00:18:00]</a>.

### Vespers Death Masks

Another innovative project involved creating death masks using pigments produced by genetically engineered bacteria. These masks not only served as artistic expressions but also experimented with hybrid living materials, setting a foundation for further research into sustainable materials <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:51">[00:38:51]</a>.

## The Role of Technology in Augmenting Nature

Oxman suggests a future where technology such as large language models could empower nature to make decisions, further merging the digital with the natural world. By understanding and harnessing the "wisdom" in natural growth processes, technology can help us create systems that are both innovative and sustainable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:01">[00:09:01]</a>.

## Challenges and Future Directions

The path towards integrating technological innovation in sustainable materials is deeply intertwined with understanding nature's intrinsic processes and wisdom. Future developments look at high throughput evolved materials, computationally-driven ecological designs, and capsule environments that replicate past and future ecosystems to foresee how plant-based and microbial systems can meet future needs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:03">[00:49:03]</a>.

The journey towards sustainable technological innovation lies in continued exploration of this intersection, probing questions about what nature *wants*, and leveraging how these wants can transform how we think about growth, production, and sustainability in the years to come. As we move forward, bridging technology and nature promises not only to innovate but to do so in a way that supports and amplifies the natural world <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:01">[00:59:01]</a>.

The intersection of artificial intelligence (AI) and human consciousness is a profound subject that explores the capabilities and limitations of AI in replicating or understanding the complex phenomenon of human consciousness. This article delves into the similarities and differences between AI intelligence and human consciousness, the potential implications of AI surpassing human capabilities, and the existential questions that arise from these discussions.

## Defining Intelligence and Consciousness

There is a significant distinction between intelligence and consciousness that is often overlooked. Intelligence is defined as the ability to solve problems, achieve goals, and perform tasks such as playing chess, diagnosing diseases, and driving cars. On the other hand, consciousness is the ability to have subjective experiences such as feeling pain, pleasure, love, and hate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:06">[00:03:06]</a>.

In humans and other animals, intelligence and consciousness usually coexist and support one another. However, in AI, intelligence exists without consciousness. When a computer outperforms humans in chess, it does not experience happiness or disappointment; it merely computes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:44">[00:03:44]</a>.

## Alien Intelligence

The concept of alien intelligence is brought into the discussion where AI is considered a form of alien intelligence. This does not imply that AI comes from outer space, but rather highlights that AI attains goals and solves problems in ways completely different from human intelligence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:23">[00:06:23]</a>.

## AI and Human Computation

AI's primary advantage over humans is its capability to perform tasks without the impediment of human emotions and biases. This gives AI potential superiority in fields of computation and processing vast amounts of data. Although AI lacks consciousness, it can be programmed to simulate empathic responses, which can lead people to associate conscious attributes to these systems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:40">[01:10:40]</a>.

## Consciousness as a Measure of Ethics

One of the pivotal questions in AI development is whether AI will ever achieve consciousness and, if so, whether it will become an ethical subject needing protection and rights <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:03">[00:32:03]</a>. The current AI systems do not possess consciousness, yet this prospect poses ethical challenges and necessitates discourse surrounding AI rights and human-AI relationships.

## Societal Implications and Human Intimacy

AI has started forming intimate relationships with humans, facilitated by social conventions and the ability of AI to learn and mimic human interaction intricately. Such relationships could lead societies to start treating AI systems as entities worthy of rights <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:46">[00:34:46]</a>. This raises significant questions about the future of human interaction and societal structure when AI becomes an embedded part of the social fabric.

The quest to understand and possibly integrate AI and human-like consciousness is fraught with impactful questions on ethics, human identity, and control. As we strive for advances in AI, balancing technological progress with ethical considerations surrounding the essence of consciousness remains crucial. The ongoing discourse fuels further reflection on AI’s role in shaping humanity’s future and the [[ai_and_humanlike_consciousness | potential implications]] on our perception of consciousness itself.

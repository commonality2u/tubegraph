## Disease Modeling and Drug Discovery

One of the groundbreaking applications of deep learning in medicine is the development of "disease in a dish" models. Traditional animal models have often fallen short because they do not mimic the human biological mechanisms of diseases. Conversely, "disease in a dish" models allow for a more accurate representation of human diseases on a cellular level, employing technologies like CRISPR gene editing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:00">[00:17:00]</a>.

These models help understand diseases rooted in human genetics, providing a pathway to test potential interventions effectively. This approach shows promise in addressing diseases with a strong genetic component, offering hope for better treatment outcomes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:00">[00:33:00]</a>.

The [[artificial_intelligence_and_deep_learning | advancements in AI and deep learning]] encompass not only predictive modeling but also concepts like transfer learning and end-to-end training.

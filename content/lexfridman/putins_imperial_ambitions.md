Understanding the motivations and actions of contemporary Russia requires a deep dive into the mindset and ambitions of its leader, [[vladimir_putin_and_contemporary_russian_politics | Vladimir Putin]]. For years, Putin's actions on the international stage have prompted questions about his desire to restore Russia to its former Soviet-era glory. His aggressive expansionist policies have led to significant geopolitical tensions and conflicts, particularly demonstrated through the invasion of neighboring countries.

## Historical Context

Putin's imperial ambitions are often linked to his vision of restoring the Russian Empire's influence as it was during the Soviet Union. He has articulated that the collapse of the Soviet Union was a "geopolitical catastrophe" and has made it clear that he harbors desires to bring former Soviet states back into Moscow's orbit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:43">[00:23:43]</a>.

## Invasion of Ukraine

The invasion of Ukraine in February 2022 can be seen as a direct manifestation of Putin's imperial ambitions. The conflict began with Russia's annexation of Crimea in 2014, an act widely condemned internationally. The full-scale invasion of 2022 represented a significant escalation, driven by Putin's insistence on protecting Russian-speaking populations and maintaining a sphere of influence in the region <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:02">[00:06:02]</a>.

> [!info] Imperial Strategy
> 
> Putin has framed his invasion of Ukraine as part of an effort to reclaim historic Russian territories. This is part of a larger vision of rebuilding Russian influence across Eastern Europe <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:41">[00:23:41]</a>.

## The Baltic Concerns

The fear of expansion is not limited to Ukraine. Eastern European countries, particularly the Baltic states, view Putin's ambitions with trepidation. These nations have significantly increased their defense spending, underscoring their fears of becoming the next targets of Russian aggression <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:08">[00:24:08]</a>.

## Reactions from the International Community

The international community remains deeply divided on how to address Putin's ambitions. Some advocate for increased NATO support to Eastern European countries as a deterrent, while others caution against provocations that could lead to broader conflict. The role of Western countries in supporting Ukraine has further intensified U.S.-Russia relations and exemplified the geopolitical divides of the 21st century.

## The Realist Perspective

Some analysts, referencing figures such as John Mearsheimer, argue that Russia's actions are a form of "realpolitik," using military power to expand its sphere of influence in a calculated geopolitical game. This view suggests that Putin's actions, while aggressive, are in line with how superpowers traditionally operate to maintain influence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:45">[00:25:45]</a>.

Throughout her career, Jeannie Rometty, former chairman, president, and CEO of IBM, has underscored the value of these principles in leadership and organizational success.

## Diversity: A Catalyst for Better Decision-Making

According to Rometty, diversity is not merely a buzzword or a numeric target; it is a critical factor for achieving better business outcomes. She asserts that having a diverse team leads to "a better product, a better answer" because diversity contributes different perspectives and ideas, which are vital for solving complex problems and driving innovation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:12">[06:12]</a>.

## Inclusion: A Deliberate Act

Rometty differentiates between diversity and inclusion by stating that "inclusion is a choice." She emphasizes that while diversity can be measured numerically, inclusion is an active decision made by leaders and teams to ensure all voices are heard and valued <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:26">[12:26]</a>. This choice fosters an environment where diverse talents can thrive and collaborate effectively.

## Long-term Benefits of Diversity and Inclusion

Diverse and inclusive leadership is not only beneficial for immediate business problem-solving but also for the long-term health and sustainability of an organization. Companies that prioritize these values are more likely to innovate and adapt to changing market conditions because they can draw from a wider range of experiences and knowledge fields.

Moreover, Rometty highlights that values such as diversity and inclusion are deeply rooted in companies that last a long time. She notes that older companies realize society grants them a license to operate, which can be revoked if they do not act responsibly and inclusively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="53:11">[53:11]</a>.

## Creating a Culture of Collaboration

An inclusive corporate culture is pivotal in leveraging diversity for the organization's advantage. Rometty advises leaders to focus on creating environments where diverse ideas can collide productively, bridging divides instead of exacerbating them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:25">[36:25]</a>.

## Challenges and Opportunities

While the benefits of a diverse and inclusive workplace are clear, achieving them can be challenging. Rometty acknowledges that traditional corporate structures and biases can impede progress toward these goals. However, by actively measuring diversity and making conscious choices to include diverse perspectives, leaders can turn these challenges into opportunities for growth and innovation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="38:30">[38:30]</a>.

## Conclusion

Diversity and inclusion in corporate leadership are not only ethical imperatives but strategic advantages that can lead to superior performance and long-term success. Leaders like Jeannie Rometty exemplify how embracing these values can transform businesses into adaptive, innovative, and enduring organizations. To future-proof their companies, leaders must view diversity and inclusion as indispensable, continuous efforts rather than one-time goals.

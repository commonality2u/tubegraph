The relationship between music and self-teaching is a fascinating one, encompassing the journey of personal growth through the exploration and mastery of musical expression. Zev Weinstein, through his own experiences, sheds light on the unique path of learning music without formal instruction, emphasizing the importance of understanding music theory as a foundational approach before diving into the physical aspects of playing an instrument. This method not only cultivates a deeper connection with music but also transforms the learning process itself.

## Learning Music Theory First

Zev Weinstein describes his initial approach to music as one where he decided to learn music theory before touching any instrument. This strategy enabled him to gain a unique insight into what makes music work and, consequently, allowed him to adapt to multiple instruments with ease. By grasping the abstract elements of music theory, he minimized the traditional entry barriers related to the physical skills typically required by formal lessons, such as posture or finger placements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:18">[01:29:18]</a>.

## The Role of Abstraction and Theory

Approaching music in an abstract manner allowed Zev to instill music across a variety of instruments, leveraging his understanding of theory into practical expression. He highlights that knowing the theory behind music allows for a deeper engagement without the often tedious initial focus on the mechanics of specific instruments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:30:08">[01:30:08]</a>.

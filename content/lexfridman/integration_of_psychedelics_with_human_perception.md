## Psychedelics and Lucid Dreaming States

According to Yosha Bach, psychedelics may induce specific types of [[psychedelics_and_conscious_exploration | lucid dreaming states]], allowing the mind to explore different dimensions of perception by temporarily severing certain neural connections and inhibitions. These changes may lead to experiences where traditional perceptions of self and spatial orientation are altered, providing a unique perspective on reality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:24:31">[01:24:31]</a>.

## Exploring New Perceptual Territories

Psychedelics can shift individuals through a range of mental states, sometimes beyond the permissible range that supports predictive modeling of reality. This can lead to states of overfitting, where individuals overinterpret their perceptions, leading to a temporary sense of clarity or euphoria. This phenomenon is akin to the practice of data augmentation in vision models, providing new ways to see and interpret the world <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:25:26">[01:25:26]</a>.

> [!info] Overfitting and Its Implications
>
> Overfitting in the context of psychedelic experiences refers to using excessive neural "bits" to model experiences, which may not be predictive in real-world applications. This could explain the euphoria and "enlightened" perspectives often associated with psychedelic usage, as seen in historical and anecdotal accounts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:31:03">[01:31:03]</a>.

## Psychedelics as Tools for Data Augmentation

By altering normal states of perception, psychedelics might allow the brain to generate new perspectives or forms of augmentation, akin to what occurs during dreaming. This might enable individuals to process existing knowledge in novel ways, leading to potentially valuable insights that are difficult to achieve through standard cognitive processes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:01">[01:29:01]</a>.

## Safety and Psychedelic-Induced Perception

While the potential benefits of psychedelics are intriguing, Bach cautions that these substances can permanently alter perception, which might not always be beneficial. For instance, substances like LSD and psilocybin can induce significant shifts in neural wiring, making responsible use in controlled settings crucial for safe exploration of these altered perceptual states <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:36:01">[01:36:01]</a>.

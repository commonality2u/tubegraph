Online education has transformed the way people learn, providing access to high-quality education and skills training from anywhere in the world. One of the key players in this field is Udacity, co-founded by Sebastian Thrun, which has been pivotal in shaping the landscape of online learning.

## The Genesis of Udacity

Sebastian Thrun, renowned for his work in robotics and autonomous vehicles, co-founded Udacity following his experience teaching one of the first Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs) on Artificial Intelligence at Stanford in 2011 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:23">[00:00:23]</a>. This course marked a significant shift in education, demonstrating the potential of online platforms to reach large audiences globally.

## Impact of Udacity on Online Education

Udacity offers a variety of programs, including the innovative [[udacitys_selfdriving_car_curriculum_development | self-driving car curriculum]], that leverage the latest advancements in technology to equip learners with in-demand skills <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:46">[00:00:46]</a>. Its mission is to make education more accessible, aligning with the growing demand for tech skills and the evolving job market needs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:02">[00:55:02]</a>.

### Accessibility and Democratization of Education

Udacity's model emphasizes the democratization of education, making it accessible to people from various backgrounds across the globe. Courses range from introductory levels to advanced technical domains, providing education as a basic human right <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:52">[00:56:52]</a>.

## Belief: Philosophy and Definitions

Belief, as understood in the field of philosophy, consists of various definitions. It is perceived as attitudes towards concepts that dictate our actions, such as the belief that the sun will rise tomorrow, which influences our daily routines <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:35">[00:02:35]</a>. These beliefs, although influential, can be erroneous, as illustrated by past incorrect beliefs like the flat Earth theory <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:57">[00:02:57]</a>.

> Emmanuel Kant, a significant figure in Western philosophy, argued that humans can never fully access the "thing in itself," which he termed the "noumenon." We interpret phenomena using our faculties, which are often flawed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:43">[00:03:43]</a>.

## Objective Reality: An Elusive Concept

The quest for understanding objective reality is a central theme in philosophy. Diana Walsh Basulka suggests that acquiring a complete understanding of what is real is unattainable. This ties back to Kant's idea that while we assume certain things to be real, like the table we rest objects on, the essence of these things is ultimately beyond our perception <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:01">[00:06:01]</a>.

### Impact of Belief on Reality

Beliefs can generate tangible socio-cultural effects. Historical beliefs—such as myths regarding the souls of women—demonstrate the substantial impact of belief systems on societal structures and human behavior <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:03">[00:09:03]</a>.

## Philosophical Skepticism

The philosophical concept of external world skepticism challenges the accuracy of our perceptions. This skepticism, which questions the reliability of our senses in discerning reality, emphasizes the significant gap between our current understanding and the true nature of existence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:16">[00:14:16]</a>.

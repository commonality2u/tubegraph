## Human Factors and Trust in Robotics

Trust plays a significant role in the adoption and effectiveness of robotics in safety-critical systems. As discussed by Ayane Howard, trust is not merely about perception but is deeply tied to users' behavior with robotic systems. Overtrust poses a particular risk in safety-critical environments, where humans may rely too heavily on robotics, potentially leading to adverse outcomes if systems fail <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:54">[09:54]</a>.

> [!quote] Ayane Howard

## Challenges in Developing Safe Robotics

The development of robotics for safety-critical systems involves overcoming [[technical_challenges_in_robotics | technical challenges in robotics]], such as designing systems that can adapt to dynamic environments and unexpected conditions. The complexity and unpredictability of human environments exacerbate these challenges, requiring a delicate balance between autonomy and human oversight.

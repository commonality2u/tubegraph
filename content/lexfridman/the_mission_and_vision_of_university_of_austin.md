## Introduction

The University of Austin (UATX), located in Austin, Texas, is an emerging institution with a distinctive mission and vision, aimed at expanding the boundaries of traditional higher education. Co-founded by prominent historian Neil Ferguson and others, UATX is committed to fostering an environment of open inquiry and intellectual daring, with the aspiration of building an institution where debate and dissent are not only permitted but encouraged.

## Mission Statement

The core mission of the University of Austin is to create a platform where academia and free speech can coexist without the pervasive constraints seen in many of today's educational environments. Ferguson emphasizes the importance of establishing an institution built from the ground up to support open inquiry and discourse across various fields, from the humanities to the sciences and engineering<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:04">[00:01:04]</a>.

### Encouragement of Free Thought

UATX is designed to counteract what Ferguson identifies as a "chilling" atmosphere in higher education, where free speech and open exchange of ideas are constrained<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:10">[00:02:10]</a>. This problem, according to Ferguson, stems from the rise of "wokeism" and critical race theory, which he argues seek to limit discourse<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:54">[00:02:54]</a>.

### A New Model for Universities

The founders of UATX believe in rejuvenating the American tradition of creating new institutions when existing ones falter. The university aims to do this by embracing debate and self-examination without fear of cancellation or censure<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:33">[00:03:33]</a>.

## Vision for the Future

### Innovative Education Model

Drawing inspiration from the tutorial systems of Oxford and Cambridge, UATX plans to implement small-group, Socratic-style teaching that fosters dynamic interaction and deep learning<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:54">[00:08:54]</a>. This traditional model will be coupled with innovative 21st-century approaches, integrating technological advancements with classical education to prepare students for a complex world.

### Curriculum and Degrees

UATX envisions beginning with a unique set of offerings. Initially, the university will launch a summer school with courses provocatively labeled as "forbidden" to challenge conventional academic norms<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:26">[00:22:26]</a>. Followed by this, the university plans to introduce masters programs focusing on fields such as entrepreneurship and leadership, offering a practical approach to education that goes beyond traditional business schools<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:31">[00:24:31]</a>. Ultimately, UATX aims to develop into a full-fledged liberal arts college.

### A Space for Technological Dialogue

With an eye on the future, UATX aims to facilitate discussions in cutting-edge fields like artificial intelligence, robotics, and computing, recognizing the importance of crossing disciplinary lines to engage in critical technological conversations<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:39">[00:08:39]</a>.

## Leadership and Governance

### Non-Political Stance

UATX stresses that it is not a politically-driven institution despite potential descriptors as a conservative entity. The focus remains on academic freedom and the pursuit of truth, wherein politics should not intrude into the classrooms or lecture halls<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:14">[00:17:14]</a>.

### Founding Leadership

The university is led by Pano Kanelos, a former president of St. John's College, who is tasked with the responsibility of steering the university towards its goals of innovation and tradition<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:06">[00:35:06]</a>.

## Conclusion

The University of Austin aspires to be not just another academic institution but a catalyst for a broader cultural shift in higher education. Through its commitment to free discourse, interdisciplinary learning, and innovative curriculum design, UATX seeks to redefine what it means to pursue truth and knowledge in the 21st century.

> [!info] Core Values of University of Austin
> 
> - **Open Inquiry**: Fostering an environment where ideas can be discussed and debated without fear.
> - **Interdisciplinary Approach**: Combining classical education with modern technological insights.
> - **Pragmatic Education**: Focusing on leadership and real-world applications in education.
> - **Independence from Politics**: Upholding principles of academic freedom beyond partisan lines.

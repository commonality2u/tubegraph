## Words as Tools of Power

Words hold power because they can shift meaning drastically based on context. For instance, Kennedy notes that words like "discrimination" and "queer" can have both negative and positive connotations, depending on context and intonation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:05">[00:17:47]</a>.

## The Role of Context in Language

Kennedy explains that context plays a crucial role in determining the appropriateness of using controversial language. Words that are considered slurs do not carry the same intensity in every context <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:06">[00:19:45]</a>. ## The Debate on Censorship

The conversation touches on whether highly charged words should be censored on platforms like social networks or within educational spaces. Kennedy argues against blanket censorship, emphasizing the educational value of direct engagement with difficult words in literature and historical texts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="47:00">[00:46:54]</a>.

## Societal Symbols and Their Implications

Beyond language, societal symbols such as the uniforms of the Ku Klux Klan or the swastika also hold intense symbolic power, often reinforcing fear and division. Kennedy asserts that education about these symbols, much like language, can help demystify and diminish their power to terrorize <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="53:00">[00:52:22]</a>.

1.  **Preserving life and health** for oneself, family, and friends <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:02">[00:10:02]</a>.
2.  **Preserving property, modest savings, and business** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:10">[00:10:10]</a>.

## Practical Steps and [[consequences_and_strategies_for_individuals_during_mobilization | Consequences and strategies for individuals during mobilization]]

### Avoiding Risks
Individuals should avoid places and situations that increase risks, particularly the risk of "getting in for the good," as law enforcement will be instructed to "comb the public" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:35">[00:12:35]</a>.

### Understanding [[mobilization_strategies_and_public_compliance | Mobilization strategies and public compliance]]
The [[partial_mobilization_and_its_implications | partial mobilization]] initiated in September of the previous year has not officially ended, as no presidential decree confirming its completion exists <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:02">[00:13:02]</a>. Despite this, not a single person has been criminally or administratively prosecuted for evading [[mobilization_and_military_readiness | mobilization]] or failing to report to the military registration and enlistment office after receiving a summons <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:12">[00:13:12]</a>. Even with the introduction of [[martial_law_and_its_implications | martial law]], the risks of criminal or administrative prosecution for evasion are expected to be minimal, though some may be demonstratively punished <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:32">[00:13:32]</a>.

### Summons and Restrictions
The promised single register of military personnel, where summonses will be posted, is not yet ready and will only appear in the fall <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:56">[00:13:56]</a>. However, existing integrated databases are sufficient to send summonses and "comb the public" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:12">[00:14:12]</a>. The law adopted on April 11 states there will be no 20-day grace period for summonses; everything will happen "here and immediately" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:23">[00:14:23]</a>.

**Immediate [[martial_law_and_civil_liberties_restrictions | civil liberties restrictions]] upon summons issuance (not stated in the law but in internal documents):**
*   Automatic blocking of accounts and bank cards <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:44">[00:14:44]</a>.
*   Blocking of driver's licenses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:02">[00:15:02]</a>.
*   Restrictions on travel abroad <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:08">[00:15:08]</a>.
*   Restrictions on property rights <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:10">[00:15:10]</a>.

If stopped on the road, licenses can be canceled, and individuals can be taken directly to the military registration and enlistment office, and from there to an assembly point, then "into the fields" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:23">[00:15:23]</a>. Families will be expected to supply equipment and bulletproof vests, as part of the transition to providing [[mobilization_and_military_readiness | mobilized]] personnel will shift to "self-sufficiency," as was the case during [[partial_mobilization_and_its_implications | partial mobilization]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:52">[00:15:52]</a>.

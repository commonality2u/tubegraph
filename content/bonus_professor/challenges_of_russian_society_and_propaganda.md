## Regional Governance and Internal Conflicts

This decision, though not democratic, is currently seen as the only feasible option given the situation in Dagestan and the Russian Federation as a whole <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:42:00">[02:42:00]</a>.

The policy of the new government, led by Nikolai Patrushev and his supporters, is viewed as tightening relations with the North Caucasus, particularly Chechnya <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:21:00">[01:21:00]</a>. This tightening suggests the potential creation of an open conflict situation with Chechnya's leadership <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:45:00">[01:45:00]</a>. Ramzan Akhmatovich's influence in the North Caucasus is significant and is unlikely to be inferior to that of the federal center, causing considerable irritation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:02:00">[03:02:00]</a>. The possibility of a civil war and the collapse of Russia, such as a hypothetical march on Moscow by Kadyrov, is considered insignificant but not entirely rejected, as "almost everything is possible" in Russia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:04:00">[14:04:00]</a>.

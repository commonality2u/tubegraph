## Analysis of Sociological Support
President Putin has interpreted perceived [[public_opinion_and_its_impact_on_russian_political_dynamics | public support]] for the military operation in Ukraine as a "carte blanche" for implementing the toughest actions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:33:00">[05:33:00]</a>.

### Official Survey Data and Its Critique
According to a survey, 71% of Russians supported the military operation, 21% were against it, and 8% were undecided <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:29:00">[06:29:00]</a>. However, the speaker critically analyzed this data:
*   **Trustworthiness**: A government sociological agency cannot be trusted with politically sensitive information <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:46:00">[06:46:00]</a>.
*   **Significance of Opposition**: The 21% opposition represents "every fifth citizen" of the Russian Federation, which is a significant minority <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:19:00">[07:19:00]</a>.
*   **Intimidation Factor**: The 8% undecided likely have decided but are unwilling to express their true opinion due to intimidation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:32:00">[07:32:00]</a>. [[Domestic Russian political dynamics | People]] in Russia are "intimidated" and provide "socially approved answers," meaning they say what they believe the authorities want to hear <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:46:00">[07:46:00]</a>.

### The Purpose of Sociology: Spiral of Silence
The primary purpose of such sociology is to form a "spiral of silence," a situation where the minority's position (or perceived minority) is suppressed by the impression of an overwhelming majority opinion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:20:00">[08:20:00]</a>. This is a common propaganda technique used by governments in critical situations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:45:00">[08:45:00]</a>. The goals are:
*   To instill a feeling of righteousness in supporters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:07:00">[09:07:00]</a>.
*   To create a feeling of predetermination in opponents, implying "like it or not, everything has already been decided" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:12:00">[09:12:00]</a>.
*   To cause hopelessness and deep depression among those resisting the policy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:34:00">[09:34:00]</a>.

The speaker referenced their 2015 book, *Absolute Weapon: The Basics of Psychological Warfare and Media Manipulation*, which was based on the experience of the 2014-2015 Russian-Ukrainian conflict <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:48:00">[09:48:00]</a>.

### Techniques for Forming the "Spiral of Silence"
Propaganda employs several techniques to shape the desired "picture of the world":
1.  **Magic of Numbers**: Statistics (e.g., 71% support) are presented to make statements appear credible and authoritative <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:20:00">[10:20:00]</a>.
2.  **Expert Commentary**: State television and controlled media feature "friendly chorus[es] of approval" from "experts" (or "quasi-experts") who are paid to express opinions supporting the government <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:44:00">[10:44:00]</a>.
3.  **Voices from the Ground**: This includes regional and municipal officials, who are "ordered" by the presidential administration to organize public speeches and letters of support for Russia's policy in Ukraine, referred to as a "special military operation" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:55:00">[11:55:00]</a>. This also involves orchestrated actions, such as terminally ill children from a hospice being forced to line up in the shape of the letter "Z" as a propaganda reinforcement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:47:00">[12:47:00]</a>.
4.  **Repetition**: Propaganda constantly repeats phrases like "where have you been eight years?" to fill people's minds and make them susceptible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:45:00">[13:45:00]</a>.
5.  **Playing on Strong Emotions**: Propaganda aims to awaken and exploit strong emotions like compassion (e.g., showing images of suffering children) and anger towards perceived culprits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:13:00">[14:13:00]</a>.

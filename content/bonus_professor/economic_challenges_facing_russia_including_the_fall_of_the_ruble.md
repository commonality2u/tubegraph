## Fall of the Ruble
The [[russian_economic_challenges_and_ruble_instability | Russian economy]] has experienced a steep dive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:51">[00:00:51]</a>. The [[russian_economic_challenges_and_ruble_instability | fall of the ruble]] was a significant event <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:30">[00:13:30]</a>, which had been foreseen and warned about based on forecasts from the head of the Central Bank <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:35">[00:13:35]</a>.

### Ruble Instability Forecasts
*   **Central Bank Forecast**: The head of the Central Bank had predicted that the dollar could rise to 120 rubles by the end of the year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:51">[00:13:51]</a>.
*   **Actual Movement**: The dollar rose to 115-118 rubles, and is currently around 105 rubles, indicating that the forecast was surprisingly accurate in both volume and timing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:07">[00:14:07]</a>.
*   **Personal Savings Advice**: Individuals who followed advice to buy euros and dollars were able to save their modest savings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:40">[00:14:40]</a>.
*   **Temporary Stabilization**: There is a temporary and partial stabilization of the ruble's exchange rate against the dollar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:17">[00:15:17]</a>. However, all forecasts guiding the [[economic_challenges_and_political_crisis_developments_in_russia | Russian leadership]] indicate that the [[socioeconomic_challenges_in_russia | economic and social situation]] will continue to deteriorate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:32">[00:15:32]</a>.

## Governmental Strategies and Measures
The Russian government is preparing for a severe [[potential_economic_and_political_crisis_in_russia | socio-economic catastrophe]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:20">[00:16:20]</a>.

### Freezing Bank Deposits
A fundamental decision has been made within the Russian leadership to freeze bank deposits and accounts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:36">[00:16:36]</a>. There is 100% consensus on this measure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:45">[00:16:45]</a>.
*   **Timing**: The exact timing is flexible, depending on the speed of deterioration in the [[economic_conditions_in_russia | economic situation]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:14">[00:17:14]</a>. While summer is considered a possibility due to people being less aggressive, it could happen as early as late February or March <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:21">[00:17:21]</a>.
*   **Conditions**: The conditions for freezing are not fully clear, but options include:
    *   A "soft" option where an insured amount (e.g., 1,400,000 rubles) remains accessible, with anything above that frozen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:46">[00:18:46]</a>.
    *   A more extreme option where all amounts exceeding 20,000 rubles would be frozen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:14">[00:19:14]</a>.
    *   Strict limits on cash withdrawals and non-cash settlements would also be introduced <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:23">[00:19:23]</a>.

### Diplomatic and Military Resource Strain
Russia's military engagement has placed significant strain on its resources.
*   **Kazakhstan Relations**: Russia attempted to persuade Kazakhstan to increase import volumes and act as an intermediary for dual-use product supplies to the Russian Federation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:08">[00:01:08]</a>. However, Kazakh President Tokayev politely but adamantly refused, citing that Kazakhstan had exhausted its transit aid resources and that further strengthening aid would cause serious problems for the Kazakh economy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:33">[00:01:33]</a>.
*   **Syrian Conflict**: Russia's military resources are reportedly depleted, with all available weapons, equipment, planes, and personnel being directed to the Ukrainian front <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:41">[00:06:41]</a>. This has led to Russia's inability to provide military assistance to Syria, causing Russia to appear unconvincing and in retreat in the regional game <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:33">[00:06:33]</a>.
*   **North Korean Aid**: The Russian Defense Minister traveled to Pyongyang to secure continued military aid from North Korea, particularly ammunition, shells, and missiles, which are critically necessary <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:35">[00:08:35]</a>. This trip is set against a backdrop of potential US negotiations with North Korea and China to reduce military aid to Russia as a means to bring Russia to the negotiating table <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:00">[00:09:00]</a>. Both Russia and Ukraine are progressively losing their ability to conduct intensive military actions due to resource depletion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:13">[00:10:13]</a>.

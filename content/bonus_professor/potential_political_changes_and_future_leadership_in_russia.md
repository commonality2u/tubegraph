## The End of an Era

The era of Vladimir Putin is perceived to be ending, although many may not yet fully grasp it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:09">[01:09:09]</a>. Historically, a major politician's legacy is judged by their ending; a successful ending paints previous activities positively, while an unsuccessful one casts them negatively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:36:00">[02:36:00]</a>. It is asserted that Putin's activities will ultimately be assessed negatively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:26:41">[03:26:41]</a>.

It is argued that no Russian politician in history has led the country under more favorable conditions than Vladimir Putin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:31:00">[03:31:00]</a>. These conditions included high oil prices, a lack of external enemies, and a generally benevolent attitude from the West until 2014, allowing for loans and technology acquisition, which even helped rearm the armed forces <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:40:00">[03:40:00]</a>. Society initially welcomed Putin after Boris Yeltsin, viewing it as the start of a new era <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:12:00">[04:12:00]</a>. He enjoyed widespread support from both ordinary people and most elite groups, receiving a "huge credit of trust" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:22:00">[04:22:00]</a>. The economy and incomes grew significantly, leading to a taste of "Western prosperity" for many <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:44:00">[04:44:00]</a>.

However, the public's perception began to shift around 2011-2012 due to factors such as declining incomes, non-competitive elections, and a sense of "deception" regarding the transfer of power (the "castling" of Putin and Medvedev) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:31:00">[06:31:00]</a>. Psychological fatigue from rulers typically sets in after 8 to 12 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:10:00">[07:10:00]</a>.

> "If Vladimir Vladimirovich in 2008 had just taken the show and left there entrusted his post to the bears and he was forever entered the history of Russia as the greatest statesman in the literal sense of the word who raised the Russian forever..." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:43:08">[07:43:08]</a>

It is believed that Putin missed a unique historical chance to ensure Russia's rapid development, instead exchanging it for the welfare of his friends and "cheap incomprehensible projects" like the Sochi Olympic Games <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:36:20">[10:36:20]</a>. The Olympic Games were the most expensive in history <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:57:00">[10:57:00]</a>, despite many people living in "monstrous conditions" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:05:00">[11:05:00]</a>.

### Personality Deformation and Isolation

A key issue highlighted is Putin's conviction that everything he did was "absolutely right," which is seen as an indicator of personality deformation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:24:00">[12:24:00]</a>. When a person is at the top of power for over 8-10, almost 20 years, relieved of competition and public criticism, and surrounded by sycophants who call him a "genius," they can become infected by this environment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:56:00">[12:56:00]</a>. An "information cocoon" or "sterile information space" is created around them, where everything appears fine <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:27:00">[13:27:00]</a>. This isolation leads to a lack of understanding of ordinary people's lives and a fear of unpleasant truths <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:35:00">[13:35:00]</a>. Putin is described as having a "pronounced messianic complex" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:33:00">[14:33:00]</a>.

In the early 2000s, Putin was not seen as a bureaucrat, and liberal economic policies were pursued out of necessity due to low oil prices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:56:00">[14:56:00]</a>. However, when raw material prices rose, the need for internal development was dismissed, opting instead to buy everything from abroad, fueled by forecasts of endlessly growing oil prices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:03:00">[16:03:00]</a>. This reliance on raw materials corrupted the elite and part of the population <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:09:00">[20:09:00]</a>.

The speaker is sure that [[future_political_changes_in_russia | political changes in Russia]] will be painful and risky, not going as planned in the Kremlin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:13:00">[25:13:00]</a>. ### Future Leadership and Political Forces

It is unlikely that any single force could rule Russia alone; a broad coalition or civil alliance will be necessary <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:07:00">[35:07:00]</a>. This coalition would likely unite liberals, leftists, nationalists, and social democrats around a simple, common program <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:24:00">[35:24:00]</a>.

Liberals are seen as having the greatest chances to lead due to their control over information flows and media, possession of developed ideas for economic transformation, and occupancy of key positions in ministries and departments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:02:00">[36:02:00]</a>. To gain broad public support, they would need to use "social rhetoric" emphasizing justice <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:46:00">[36:46:00]</a>, as the main public requests in Russia are for justice, equality before the law, and respect for human dignity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:55:00">[36:55:00]</a>.

Leftist movements are not seen as strong, as Russia's "left mood" is more about people wanting "revenge on the masters of life" rather than solidarity, as Russian society is described as very individualistic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:43:00">[37:43:00]</a>. Armed seizure of power by leftists, similar to 1917, is dismissed as unfeasible for modern Russia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="38:37:00">[38:37:00]</a>. However, a strong social democratic party could emerge in a competitive parliamentary system <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:37:00">[41:37:00]</a>.

It is noted that even Putin, described as a "chekist to the core," tolerates liberals in government because he understands they prevent the economy from collapsing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="42:30:00">[42:30:00]</a>.

### Key Reforms Needed

To avoid societal breakdown, several rapid reforms are proposed:
*   **Freedom for small and medium businesses:** This can be achieved quickly by removing restrictions and cancelling certain laws <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="49:50:00">[49:50:00]</a>.
*   **Complete reform of the judicial and legal system:** Essential for justice, as Russia currently lacks a functional court system <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="50:03:00">[50:03:00]</a>. This would likely require a "total lustration" of the judicial corps <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="51:19:00">[51:19:00]</a>.

Longer-term reforms (10-15 years) would include healthcare and school reforms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="50:16:00">[50:16:00]</a>. Additionally, it is suggested that all officials and their families should be prohibited from leaving Russia for a period, with an "inventory and lustration" process to be conducted <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="50:35:00">[50:35:00]</a>.

### Societal Challenges and Discontent

The discussion highlights significant problems contributing to societal discontent:
*   **Declining incomes:** People's incomes have been declining for six consecutive years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:31:00">[08:31:00]</a>.
*   **Pension reform:** The pension reform is described as a "mockery of common sense," especially given men's life expectancy in many regions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:32:00">[09:32:00]</a>.
*   **Corruption and misallocation of funds:** Massive amounts of money from raw material sales were taken out to offshore accounts or spent on "unproductive expenses" and "stupid projects" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:19:00">[19:19:00]</a>, instead of providing normal housing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:08:00">[19:08:00]</a> or for children's treatment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:48:00">[18:48:00]</a>. A tenth of this money could have provided everyone with quality housing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:10:00">[19:10:00]</a>.
*   **Poor quality goods:** "Rubbish" is sold as provisions in Russian stores, even in Moscow <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:51:00">[19:51:00]</a>.
*   **Regional degradation vs. Moscow's opulence:** While regions degrade, Moscow benefits from disproportionate spending, like laying tiles and repainting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:32:00">[32:32:00]</a>, creating resentment and threatening national unity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="48:43:00">[48:43:00]</a>.
*   **Centralization of wealth:** A small handful of very rich people coexist with a huge number of poor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="47:59:00">[47:59:00]</a>.
*   **Lack of solutions:** There are too many problems in the country with no solutions being sought <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:28:00">[25:28:00]</a>. The garbage problem is cited as an example of funds allocated but factories not built <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:17:00">[31:17:00]</a>.
*   **Disrespect for citizens:** The authorities are seen as treating citizens like "children, ignorant," making decisions for them, like travel restrictions to Georgia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:01:00">[34:01:00]</a>, rather than as adults capable of thinking for themselves <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:10:00">[34:10:00]</a>. This constant creation of enemies for itself by the government shows an "amazing talent" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:38:00">[34:38:00]</a>.

The transfer of power, or "transit," is anticipated to be very risky and unlikely to proceed as planned by the Kremlin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:57:00">[25:57:00]</a>. The speaker foresees strong grassroots pressure, not within the next two or three months, but starting around 2020 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:17:00">[28:17:00]</a>.

## Historical Precedents for Leadership Transitions
The history of Russia has often been profoundly affected by the fate of its rulers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:37:00">[01:37:00]</a>. A sudden change in leadership can lead to radical shifts in foreign policy, and sometimes even the cessation of wars <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:47:00">[01:47:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:56:00">[01:56:00]</a>.

Examples include:
*   **Peter III (1761):** Led Russia out of the Seven Years' War and formed an alliance with Frederick II after only six months in power <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:00">[01:07:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:00">[01:14:00]</a>.
*   **Paul I:** Withdrew Russia from the anti-French coalition <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:21:00">[01:21:00]</a>.
*   **Death of Stalin:** Led to significant changes in the country <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:02:00">[02:02:00]</a>.
*   **Death of Leonid Brezhnev:** Also brought about substantial changes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:06:00">[02:06:00]</a>.

These historical instances demonstrate how the sudden departure of a ruler, whether natural or unnatural, can profoundly alter a country's internal and external policies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:45:00">[01:45:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:50:00">[01:50:00]</a>.

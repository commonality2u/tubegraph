## Loss of Media Focus
Since around October 6th or 7th, Ukraine has largely "fallen out of the focus" of global media attention <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:01">[00:05:01]</a>. Major American and [[western_and_ukrainian_responses | Western media]] outlets, including the New York Times and CNN, have curtailed their online coverage of [[international_relations_and_military_actions_concerning_ukraine | military actions in Ukraine]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:10">[00:05:10]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:21">[00:05:21]</a>.

A Ukrainian native and patriot, residing outside Ukraine, noted that the BBC news hour on a Saturday morning in London had "not a word about Ukraine" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:26">[00:05:26]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:34">[00:05:34]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:41">[00:05:41]</a>.

> [!quote] Attention and Energy
> "Where attention is directed, there energy appears. If there is no attention, then there is no energy" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:53">[00:05:53]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:58">[00:05:58]</a>. This maxim is considered applicable to the situation surrounding Ukraine <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:01">[00:06:01]</a>.

It is currently difficult to imagine the conditions under which Ukraine would return to the long-term focus of global media <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:19">[00:06:19]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:27">[00:06:27]</a>. Global media plays a crucial role in formulating the global information agenda and determining what holds the attention of societies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:41">[00:06:41]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:49">[00:06:49]</a>.

## Impact on Western Aid to Ukraine
While the situation regarding real financial assistance does not immediately appear unfavorable for Ukraine, as Americans have stated they have sufficient resources to aid both Israel and Ukraine <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:02">[00:07:02]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:08">[00:07:08]</a>, a key concern is the supply of weapons and ammunition <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:30">[00:07:30]</a>.

This creates a dilemma of choosing priorities for weapon supplies, with Israel expected to be the primary recipient <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:34">[00:07:34]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:49">[00:07:49]</a>. The longer the war in the Middle East lasts, the more acute the problem of choosing priorities for weapon and ammunition supplies will become <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:01">[00:08:01]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:08">[00:08:08]</a>.

According to [[western_and_ukrainian_responses | Western media]] outlets and experts, the peak of [[western_and_ukrainian_responses | Western aid to Ukraine]] was reached in the summer and early autumn of 2023 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:17">[00:08:17]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:32">[00:08:32]</a>. This aid is now expected to decrease for several reasons:
*   The emergence of a new theater of military operations in the Middle East <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:48">[00:08:48]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:52">[00:08:52]</a>.
*   The [[russia_and_ukraine_conflict_dynamics | Ukrainian offensive]] failing to achieve its strategic goals in the summer-autumn campaign <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:00">[00:09:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:05">[00:09:05]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:14">[00:09:14]</a>.

The speaker emphasizes that Ukraine's armed forces can only continue an offensive if they receive sufficient weapons, ammunition, successfully carry out mobilization, and receive political support <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:34">[00:09:34]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:41">[00:09:41]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:47">[00:09:47]</a>. The autumn offensive is seen as being in its final phase <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:01">[00:10:01]</a>.

## Strategic Stalemate
The most probable outcome of the summer-autumn campaigns is the transition of both parties to mutual strategic defense <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:40">[00:10:40]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:48">[00:10:48]</a>. [[western_and_ukrainian_responses | Western experts]] characterize this situation as a stalemate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:54">[00:10:54]</a>. This strategic dead end is now considered complete <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:08">[00:11:08]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:11">[00:11:11]</a>. While military action is unpredictable, a stalemate situation and strategic dead end are currently observed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:16">[00:11:16]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:27">[00:11:27]</a>.

Although a strategic dead end is unlikely to benefit the Ukrainian side, the Russian leadership might be satisfied with it, given the preservation of the status quo <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:39">[00:11:39]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:43">[00:11:43]</a>.

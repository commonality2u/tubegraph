## Rising Household Expenses
Research from the Ramir holding indicates a significant increase in household expenses for food and everyday goods <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:50">[00:19:50]</a>.
*   In October, these expenses reached nearly 48% of the average Russian household budget <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:10">[00:20:10]</a>.
*   This represents a 6.5% increase compared to a year prior, marking a record high <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:14">[00:20:14]</a>.
*   This means almost half of an average Russian family's income is spent purely on existing or surviving <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:21">[00:20:21]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:29">[00:20:29]</a>.
*   Furthermore, expenses specifically for food have recently risen by 25% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:24">[00:21:24]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:27">[00:21:27]</a>.

## Shifting Ambitions and Strategic Traps
The initial goals of Russia's "special military operation" were stated as "denazification" and "demilitarization" of Ukraine <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:20">[00:06:20]</a>. However, the slow pace of advance has led to a minimization of the Kremlin's public political claims <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:51">[00:05:51]</a>. President Putin later stated the goal as "liberating Donbass and guaranteeing the security of the Russian Federation" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:02">[00:06:02]</a>. Meanwhile, the length of the border between NATO and the Russian Federation has almost doubled <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:34">[00:06:34]</a>.

The Kremlin is seen as step-by-step being drawn into a "strategic trap" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:06">[00:10:06]</a>. This involves increasing efforts to overcome resistance from Ukraine and the global community, under the belief that "one more effort" will break the opponent's will <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:46">[00:10:46]</a>. This path is depleting Russia's material, human, moral, and psychological resources, leading to social, technological, and economic degradation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:01">[00:11:01]</a>.

## Economic Mobilization
The Russian government is introducing amendments to federal law, signaling a significant shift towards a mobilization model for the economy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:52">[00:07:52]</a>.
These "special measures" grant authorities the right to:
*   Prohibit firms, regardless of ownership, from refusing government orders and contracts related to military actions outside Russia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:09">[00:08:09]</a>. This means businesses will be obligated to supply goods, perform work, and provide services for military operations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:53">[00:08:53]</a>.
*   Change contract terms during their implementation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:04">[00:09:04]</a>.
*   Interfere with labor relations within organizations, forcing employees to work nights, weekends, and holidays, and canceling vacations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:12">[00:09:12]</a>.

> [!WARNING] Historical Parallel
> This economic shift is compared to the situation in the Soviet Union on the eve of June 1941 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:58">[00:09:58]</a>.

This mobilization regime leads to a "simplification," where cultural complexities are no longer acceptable, aiming for uniformity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:26">[00:19:26]</a>.

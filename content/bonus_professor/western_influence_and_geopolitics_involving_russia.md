### Minsk Agreements

The speaker acknowledges that leaders in Europe admit the Minsk agreements were a fiction, a sentiment echoed by Vladislav Surkov on the Russian side <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:43">[00:24:43]</a>. Both sides, and their supporters, pursued their own goals:
*   The Ukrainian side and the West sought to gain time, viewing it as a "Brest Agreement" or "Brest Peace" for Ukraine <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:02">[00:25:02]</a>.
*   The Russian side aimed to legitimize its broad plan regarding Ukraine, influencing it through the agreements to introduce Donbass into Ukraine's body, change its Constitution, and effectively give Ukraine the status of a Confederation critically dependent on [[russia_nato_tensions_and_geopolitical_shifts | Russia]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:11">[00:25:11]</a>.
Thus, all parties involved were "not very honest" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:47">[00:25:47]</a>.

## Geopolitical Implications of Crisis in Russia

The speaker does not believe that the disintegration of [[russia_nato_tensions_and_geopolitical_shifts | Russia]] will happen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:45">[00:14:45]</a>. Purely theoretically, it is possible if the inevitable political crisis in [[russia_nato_tensions_and_geopolitical_shifts | Russia]] becomes very long (around three years) and fierce <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:47">[00:14:47]</a>. While risks of internal weakening and moderate separatist tendencies exist, fundamental risks of [[russia_nato_tensions_and_geopolitical_shifts | Russia]]'s collapse are not present from the speaker's viewpoint <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:03">[00:15:03]</a>.

The speaker does not exclude the possibility of a nuclear outcome to the war in the event of unfavorable developments at the front, as the Supreme Commander-in-Chief of the [[historical_and_current_political_implications_in_russia | Russian Federation]] considers this option acceptable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:09">[00:29:09]</a>. However, the question of whether such an order would be carried out is a "casuistic" one, with the speaker hoping it will never be tested <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:33">[00:29:33]</a>.

Potential threats to Kaliningrad from Poland, and to Far Eastern/Amur territories, are considered possible only if a political crisis in [[russia_nato_tensions_and_geopolitical_shifts | Russia]] becomes very long and fierce <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:04">[00:33:04]</a>.

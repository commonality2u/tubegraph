## Public Support and Propaganda Techniques
The perceived public support for the [[russian_military_situation_and_challenges_in_ukraine | military operation in Ukraine]] is seen by President Putin as a "carte blanche" for the toughest actions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:37">[00:05:37]</a>.

### The "Spiral of Silence"
The analyst, citing their book *Absolute Weapon* (2015), discusses how official sociology serves to form a "spiral of silence" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:20">[00:08:20]</a>. This psychological manipulation aims to create the impression that a certain position is shared by an overwhelming majority <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:42">[00:08:42]</a>. This is done to:
*   Instill a "feeling of rightness" in supporters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:10">[00:09:10]</a>.
*   Cause a "feeling of predetermination" in opponents, suggesting "everything has already been decided" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:18">[00:09:18]</a>.
*   Induce "hopelessness and deep depression" in those who resist <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:36">[00:09:36]</a>.

### Propaganda Techniques
To form this "spiral of silence" and the desired "picture of the world," propaganda employs several techniques:
1.  **Magic of Numbers**: People are more likely to believe statements reinforced by figures or statistics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:23">[00:10:23]</a>. For example, a survey reporting 71% support for Kremlin policy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:32">[00:10:32]</a>. The analyst questions the reliability of government sociological agencies for politically sensitive information <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:51">[00:06:51]</a>.
2.  **Expert Commentary**: A "friendly chorus of approval" from "experts" (often quasi-experts) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:06">[00:11:06]</a> on Russian television and media creates an impression of widespread agreement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:47">[00:10:47]</a>.
3.  **Voices from the Ground**: Public speeches and letters of support are "ordered" by the presidential administration from regional and municipal authorities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:12">[00:12:12]</a>. This includes disturbing acts like children from a hospice being lined up in the form of the letter 'Z' as "propaganda reinforcement" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:47">[00:12:47]</a>.
4.  **Repetition of Slogans**: Slogans like "Where have you been eight years?" are constantly repeated, primarily on social networks but originating from television <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:45">[00:14:45]</a>.
5.  **Arousing Strong Emotions**: Propaganda aims to awaken and play on strong emotions, such as compassion for victims, especially children <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:11">[00:15:11]</a>. This compassion can then be channeled into anger towards perceived culprits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:55">[00:14:55]</a>.

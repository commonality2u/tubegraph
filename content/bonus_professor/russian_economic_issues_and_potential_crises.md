## Overview of the Economic Situation

The current economic situation in Russia is characterized by an ongoing deterioration, with a very high and increasing risk of chaos <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:51">[00:28:51]</a>. An [[russian_economic_crisis_and_forecasts | economic crisis]] is considered inevitable, expected to cover the end of the current year and the beginning of the next <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:17">[00:38:17]</a>. This [[russian_economic_crisis_and_forecasts | economic crisis]] is anticipated to contribute to the deepening of the [[russian_political_crisis_and_power_transition | political crisis]], as these processes are intertwined despite having different natures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:30">[00:38:30]</a>.

## Key Economic Concerns

### Financial Inflow and Regime Support
The current regime's ability to exist is linked to financial income from hydrocarbon sales and societal support <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:11">[00:12:11]</a>. However, influential figures within the regime believe it has outlived its usefulness and that a transition to a new quality is necessary <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:27">[00:12:27]</a>. This internal conflict among elites is considered very serious and unstoppable, marking the beginning of a crisis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:02">[00:13:02]</a>.

### Pension System
The pension system is a significant concern. There is a proposal to increase pensions significantly, which is deemed financially feasible given Russia's budget <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:59">[00:20:59]</a>. Additionally, there are plans to return the retirement age <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:04">[00:24:04]</a>. However, the idea of having equal pensions for all, regardless of profession or labor contribution, is considered categorically unfair <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:17">[00:24:17]</a>.

### Currency Circulation and Restrictions
Restrictions on the export of dollars and euros already exist, with a limit of 10,000 USD per person <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:03">[00:10:03]</a>. While the security forces periodically discuss further restrictions on currency circulation, a ban on cash dollar and euro exports is not currently planned, as the economic sector of the government opposes it due to implementation difficulties <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:54">[00:09:54]</a>.

### Drug Shortages
A severe shortage of essential drugs, particularly those produced in the European Union and not mass-produced in Russia, is noted. This situation is expected to worsen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:00">[00:11:00]</a>. Individuals are advised to seek alternative channels for obtaining medicine or make reserves <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:49">[00:10:49]</a>.

### Housing Market and Mortgages
The housing market faces challenges due to sharp rises in mortgage prices, leading to increased rent and unaffordable mortgages with overpayments of 300% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:12">[00:17:12]</a>. Restructuring of mortgages is deemed necessary due to what is described as "real robbery" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:34">[00:13:34]</a>. The housing and communal services sector is slated for radical reform to ensure transparency of financial resources <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:07">[00:22:07]</a>.

### Food Cards
Under a certain scenario, the introduction of food stamps (or similar access to provisions) is possible in some regions, though not on a national scale <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:22">[00:23:22]</a>.

### Frozen Deposits
While the blocking of credit cards and freezing of deposits (partially, allowing a small sum per month) was considered as an economic policy option, it is not currently under consideration. This is due to strong opposition from influential figures within the Russian elite who view it as destructive for the economy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:04">[00:25:04]</a>. There are currently no financial reasons or grounds for freezing deposits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:37">[00:25:37]</a>.

### Sanctions and Economic Catastrophe
If a broad compromise with Ukraine is not reached, a significant portion of sanctions will not be lifted <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:00">[00:29:00]</a>. While existence under sanctions is possible, the situation is worsening, and the risk of chaos is very high <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:51">[00:28:51]</a>.

## Future Outlook and Proposed Reforms

Upon a change in power, significant reforms are expected across all sectors. The focus will be on establishing a new Russian republic and introducing gradual changes to legislative acts and document forms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:04">[00:52:04]</a>.

Key proposed reforms include:
*   **Economic recovery:** If a new leadership takes power, noticeable improvements could be seen almost immediately. A period of one and a half to two years is estimated to get out of the crisis and achieve sustainable and rapid development <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:02">[00:39:02]</a>.
*   **Housing and communal services:** This sector will be thoroughly and radically reformed to ensure complete transparency of financial resource allocation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:07">[00:22:07]</a>.
*   **Pensions:** Pensions will be significantly increased, and the retirement age will be returned <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:59">[00:20:59]</a>.

The crisis is anticipated to drive an increase in the exchange rate of hard currencies like the dollar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:40">[00:38:40]</a>.

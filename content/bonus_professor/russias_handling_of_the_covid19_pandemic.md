## Overview of the Crisis

Russia's approach to the COVID-19 pandemic has been characterized by a delegation of responsibility, a lack of transparency regarding the true scale of the epidemic, and significant economic consequences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:52:00">[01:52:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:14:00">[09:14:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:12:00">[03:12:00]</a>. ## Leadership and Governance

[[Russias political situation and leadership | President Putin]] has opted to remain out of public view, particularly when delivering bad news or bearing responsibility for difficult decisions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:47:00">[01:47:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:52:00">[01:52:00]</a>. Instead, he has largely shifted the burden of managing the crisis onto Moscow Mayor Sobyanin and regional governors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:00:00">[02:00:00]</a>. While demanding action, he has not provided the necessary resources to support these directives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:09:00">[02:09:00]</a>.

The legal status of the measures in place is not clearly defined, being referred to as "my charge" rather than a formal state of emergency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:17:00">[02:17:00]</a>. This is because the Kremlin wishes to avoid financial and legal responsibility to society, unlike Western governments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:28:00">[02:28:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:31:00">[02:31:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:36:00">[02:36:00]</a>.

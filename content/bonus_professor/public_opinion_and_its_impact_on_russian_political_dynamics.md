## Overview of the Current Sociopolitical Landscape
The current situation in Russia is described as beginning to resemble a tragedy, rather than merely a drama <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:08">[00:01:08]</a>. The withdrawal of Russian troops from Kherson is identified as a strategic turning point in favor of Ukraine, consolidating a shift that began in late August/early September <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:58">[00:01:58]</a>. This event has led to the strategic initiative in the armed confrontation finally passing to Ukraine <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:10">[00:02:10]</a>.

### Moral and Political Catastrophe
The loss of Kherson is not considered a military catastrophe, but a profound moral, psychological, and [[Russian internal political dynamics | political catastrophe]] for the Russian leadership and establishment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:26">[00:02:26]</a>. This is because faith in victory has been definitively lost within the [[Russian sociopolitical dynamics | Russian establishment]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:40">[00:02:40]</a>. If previously the establishment believed defeat was unacceptable, they now must accept the inevitability of military defeat <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:49">[00:02:49]</a>. Consequently, Putin is seen by the establishment as a "toxic loser," where anything he touches turns into its opposite <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:03">[00:03:03]</a>.

The consequences of this catastrophe include:
*   **Confusion**: The propaganda machine and political headquarters are at a loss for how to respond, though this confusion is expected to be overcome, and censorship will tighten <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:26">[00:03:26]</a>.
*   **Scapegoating**: Those who are actually responsible for the situation will not be punished; instead, the innocent will be punished <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:41">[00:03:41]</a>. This is a common "Russian occupation" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:48">[00:03:48]</a>.
*   **Search for a way out**: There is an intensification of efforts to find a solution to the impasse created by the "brilliant actions of the supreme commander-in-chief" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:01">[00:04:01]</a>.
*   **Recognition of Putin's responsibility**: Putin is now recognized as the primary cause of the catastrophe, which is not only moral and political but also, in some sense, global <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:15">[00:04:15]</a>.
*   **Elite Discontent**: There is a noticeable increase in discontent among the elite, posing an "even more fundamental threat to the power of Vladimir Vladimirovich Putin" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:29">[00:04:29]</a>.

## Peace Proposals and Kremlin's Response
A peace proposal was received by the Russian leadership immediately after the withdrawal from Kherson <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:34">[00:01:34]</a>. The outline of these proposals, originating from Ukraine and previously agreed upon by a "more than serious" third party, includes:
*   **Full Russian withdrawal**: Russia completely leaves Ukrainian territory, including the DPR and LPR, with the exception of Crimea <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:45">[00:05:45]</a>.
*   **Crimea's status**: Crimea would receive an indefinite status for seven years, during which negotiations would take place, but it must be demilitarized, and the Black Sea Fleet must leave <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:52">[00:05:52]</a>.
*   **Demilitarized Zone**: A 100-kilometer deep demilitarized zone would be established along the Russian-Ukrainian and Belarusian-Ukrainian borders, with heavy weapons removed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:06">[00:06:06]</a>.
*   **Ukraine's commitments**: Ukraine promises not to join NATO or raise questions about joining NATO for seven years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:23">[00:06:23]</a>.
*   **Transnistria**: The Russian military base in Transnistria would be moved back to Russia, and Russia would not interfere with Transnistria's integration into Moldova in 2023 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:34">[00:06:34]</a>.
*   **Immunity for Russian personnel**: Crucially, Russian military, political, and civilian personnel are guaranteed immunity from punishment, and their freedom and security are preserved, with efforts to save at least some of their assets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:00">[00:07:00]</a>. However, it is expected that "scapegoats" will still be punished <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:25">[00:07:25]</a>.

Indirect signs suggest the Russian leadership is taking these proposals seriously:
*   **Halt on nuclear weapon plans**: Plans for the use of tactical nuclear weapons were curtailed, though not completely abandoned <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:55">[00:07:55]</a>.
*   **Erdogan's mediation**: Turkish President Erdogan announced his intention to meet Putin to discuss peace prospects with Ukraine, followed by a visit or call to Zelensky <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:17">[00:08:17]</a>.
*   **Potential halt on energy strikes**: If Russia abandons further massive strikes on Ukraine's energy infrastructure, this would be a third sign that the Kremlin is seriously considering the peace proposals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:00">[00:09:00]</a>.

## [[Public engagement and activism in Russias political landscape | Public Opinion]] and its Limited Influence
The Kremlin traditionally disregards [[Russia Ukraine conflict and public opinion | public opinion]], only considering it when faced with direct force or resistance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:13">[00:12:13]</a>. Currently, there is little chance for [[Public dissatisfaction and political implications in Russia | public opinion in Russia]] to become a significant force unless it unites <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:34">[00:12:34]</a>. Only the actions of Russian citizens themselves can lead to a change in the political system and the punishment of the truly guilty <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:13">[00:15:13]</a>.

## Shifting Blame and Political Maneuvers
In the aftermath of the military setbacks, a search for the guilty will begin. Initially, "Turbo Patriots" – those who were most vocal in their support and are now criticizing the authorities – will be blamed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:02">[00:18:02]</a>. Figures like Igor Strelkov and Aleksandr Dugin, once prominent, may face repercussions for their criticism of the leadership <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:13">[00:18:13]</a>.

The authorities may attempt to redirect this discontent by creating a "false political target" in the form of a new political party for these "Turbo Patriots" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:24">[00:19:24]</a>. This party might even feature figures like Strelkov and Dugin, with the promise of early parliamentary elections to channel their energy into a controlled political process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:46">[00:19:46]</a>. However, this is a well-known method of "divorce," or manipulation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:01">[00:20:01]</a>. The military, on the other hand, will be blamed for Russia's defeat later, only after a peace treaty is signed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:34">[00:17:34]</a>.

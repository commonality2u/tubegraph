## Implications of [[Putins health and its influence on Russian politics | Putin's Health]] for Russia

The state of [[putins_political_challenges_and_potential_leadership_changes | Putin's health]] and his ability to adequately assess situations and make decisions are considered a direct concern for Russian citizens <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:38:00">[04:38:00]</a>. These decisions can affect the fate and lives of millions of people in Russia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:44:00">[04:44:00]</a>.

A prolonged illness can lead to a return to "basic structures of thinking" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:12:00">[05:12:00]</a>. This was evidenced by [[Putins leadership and political influence | Putin's]] phrase regarding Ukraine, "We haven't even started yet" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:32:00">[05:32:00]</a>, which was likened to "the conversation and teenagers who say to each other yes here I'll bring my older brother and friend here he will show he will show how it should be for real" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:42:00">[05:42:00]</a>. This statement is interpreted as a potential signal for nationwide mobilization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:00:00">[06:00:00]</a>.

## Public Perception Considerations and Mobilization

The possibility of announcing a nationwide mobilization faces two main obstacles:
1.  **Image/Reputational Obstacle:** If "everything goes according to plan," as stated by the Russian president and his press secretary, then announcing mobilization would imply that the plan is not working, contradicting the narrative of winning with "little bloodshed on foreign territory" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:17:00">[07:17:00]</a>.
2.  **Risk of Political and Social Complications:** There is uncertainty about how society, especially those in large cities, would react to a nationwide mobilization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:51:00">[07:51:00]</a>.

As a cautious approach to mobilization, the first stage might involve introducing martial law in 5 frontline or border regions with Ukraine, to observe the public reaction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:10:00">[08:10:00]</a>. Announcing mobilization in the summer is considered suboptimal because many potential conscripts are currently unreachable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:26:00">[08:26:00]</a>.

Recent arrests and prosecutions, such as those of hockey players accused of illegally acquiring military tickets, are viewed as "informational, moral, and psychological preparation" for society regarding mobilization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:5:00">[01:5:00]</a>. This demonstrates that "everyone will be equal before mobilization" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:8:00">[01:8:00]</a>. However, the government is not certain that society will react positively to such a measure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:24:00">[01:24:00]</a>, and it is doubted that half a million people would "voluntarily run" to military registration and enlistment offices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:31:00">[01:31:00]</a>.

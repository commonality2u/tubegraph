### Mobilization and Legal Grounds
Despite existing legislation, the speaker notes that during the "partial mobilization" that began in September, not a single person was criminally or administratively prosecuted for evading mobilization or failing to appear at the military enlistment office after receiving a summons <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:12">[00:13:12]</a>. Even with the introduction of [[introduction_of_martial_law_and_restrictions | martial law]], the risks of such prosecution are expected to remain minimal, with any demonstrative punishments likely to be few in number <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:32">[00:13:32]</a>.

The speaker warns against complacency regarding the "single register of military personnel" for summonses, which is not yet ready and expected only in the fall <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:56">[00:13:56]</a>. However, existing integrated databases are sufficient to issue summonses and "comb the public" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:12">[00:14:12]</a>. The law adopted on April 11, which suggests a 20-day grace period, is described as a "lie," and there will be no such period for summonses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:23">[00:14:23]</a>.

### Self-Sufficiency for Mobilized Personnel
It is anticipated that mobilized personnel will partly transition to "self-sufficiency," meaning families will be expected to supply equipment and bulletproof vests, as observed during the partial mobilization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:52">[00:15:52]</a>.

To protect property and funds, the speaker advises several actions:
*   **Cash Withdrawal**: Money can be withdrawn or transferred to trusted relatives or friends <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:26">[00:16:26]</a>.
*   **Cash Reserves**: Always keep a sufficient amount of cash on hand for "operational freedom" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:33">[00:16:33]</a>.
*   **Vehicles**: A general power of attorney for a car will not protect it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:05">[00:17:05]</a>. *   **Businesses**: Transferring business ownership may be difficult or impossible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:24">[00:17:24]</a>. The speaker recommends consulting with lawyers to develop schemes for property and business preservation, and to have a lawyer's phone number readily available <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:56">[00:17:56]</a>.

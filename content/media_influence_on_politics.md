Dominic Cummings, former chief advisor to Boris Johnson, describes the significant and often detrimental influence of the media on the functioning of the UK government, particularly within Number 10 Downing Street. He argues that a preoccupation with media perception and engagement distracts from substantive policy work and perpetuates a dysfunctional system.

## The "Press Entertainment Service" Model

Cummings characterizes Number 10 Downing Street, particularly since the Thatcher era, as having evolved into a "press entertainment service" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:33">[00:05:33]</a>. He states that the "whole building is just built to respond to what the media says" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:40">[00:05:40]</a>. This results in the Prime Minister's time, considered the "most important asset" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:18">[00:05:18]</a>, being constantly pulled by media-driven "craziness" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:22">[00:05:22]</a> and "chasing the bullshit in the media all day" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:26">[00:08:26]</a>.

A key disagreement between Cummings and Boris Johnson in January 2020, shortly after a significant election win, centered on this issue. Johnson expressed a desire to "make friends with all of these people [journalists]" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:30">[00:07:30]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:37">[00:07:37]</a>, viewing it as a time for "a great reconciliation" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:16">[00:08:16]</a>. Cummings, however, argued for a fundamental change in how Number 10 operated, focusing on "the actual big problems facing the country" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:55">[00:07:55]</a> rather than reverting to a "media entertainment service" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:02">[00:08:02]</a>. Johnson's response was that not prioritizing media relations would make "everyone go crazy" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:08">[00:08:08]</a>.

## Why Politicians Prioritize Media

Cummings suggests several reasons for politicians' intense focus on the media:

*   **Cultural Conditioning:** Politicians have "all grown up in a system where they just don't know any better than dealing with the media all day" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:49">[00:10:49]</a>.
*   **Perceived Career Advancement:** They believe their promotion prospects depend on being seen as a "good media performer" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:53">[00:11:53]</a> or avoiding media "botches" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:53">[00:11:53]</a>. Cummings deems this a "fundamentally bad criteria" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:00">[00:12:00]</a>.
*   **Incentive to Leak:** The desire for "good interviews" incentivizes ministers to leak information <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:16">[00:12:16]</a>, reinforcing a negative culture.
*   **Elected Status of Ministers:** The fact that ministers running departments are also MPs (elected officials) is cited as a partial reason for their media obsession <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:22">[00:13:22]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:29">[00:13:29]</a>.

## Consequences of Media Obsession

This focus on media engagement has several negative consequences according to Cummings:

*   **Distraction from Priorities:** It diverts attention and resources from "actually important things" like the science and technology agenda, productivity reform, and procurement reform [[impact_of_ai_on_future_technology_and_society]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:39">[00:06:39]</a>. Daily events, such as the Prime Minister's girlfriend "going crazy about the media" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:00">[00:02:00]</a>, or other news disasters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:23">[00:02:23]</a>, contribute to this distraction.
*   **Wasted PM Time:** The Prime Minister, whose time is crucial for breaking bureaucratic deadlocks, spends a large part of their day on "photo ops" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:59">[00:09:59]</a> and "stupid ceremonies" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:36">[00:10:36]</a>.
*   **Misunderstanding Communication:** Politicians often equate genuine communication with simply "answering questions to the media" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:57">[00:10:57]</a>, which Cummings views as a misunderstanding of effective communication <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:00">[00:11:00]</a>.

## Attempts to Reduce Media Engagement and Reactions

Cummings describes an attempt in the first quarter of 2020 to reduce media engagement by stopping ministerial participation in a prominent BBC Radio show <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:41">[00:12:41]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:47">[00:12:47]</a>.
The reaction was severe:
*   The media "went completely crazy" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:57">[00:12:57]</a>.
*   A senior BBC figure called Cummings a "fascist" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:04">[00:13:04]</a>.
*   Many MPs also "went crazy" as being on such shows was their "raison d'etre" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:09">[00:13:09]</a>.
This made the experience "extremely unpleasant and disruptive" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:16">[00:13:16]</a>.

## Media's Role in Perpetuating Illusions of Power

Cummings argues that the media contributes to a "Potemkin farce" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:46">[00:43:46]</a> regarding who holds real power in government.
*   Weekly Cabinet meetings, where ministers walk up Downing Street for the cameras, are described as "bullshit theatre" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:30">[00:43:30]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:35">[00:43:35]</a>. The meetings are scripted, with conclusions pre-written by officials <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:41">[00:43:41]</a>.
*   While ministers are portrayed by the media as "running the department" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:56">[00:13:56]</a>, their actual power is limited (e.g., a Secretary of State for Defence can only fire three personal staff <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:01">[00:14:01]</a>).
*   Truly powerful individuals, like young officials (e.g., a 28-year-old <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:07">[00:44:07]</a>) working meters from the PM or the PM's private secretaries (such as Jono Evans on Brexit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:30">[00:47:30]</a> or Hannah coordinating with MI5 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:34">[00:47:34]</a>), are often unknown to the media and public <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:16">[00:44:16]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:18">[00:45:18]</a>.
*   Cummings suggested to a newspaper editor that they start a "deep state" page reporting on these influential but unknown officials. The editor responded that "everyone would think I'd lost my mind" and it would "antagonize everyone" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:29">[00:45:29]</a>, as it would break the "kayfabe" of MPs being in charge <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:49">[00:45:49]</a>.

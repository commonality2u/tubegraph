## Limitations of Data Alone in Causal Questions

One of the key learnings in [[Causal Analysis and its Importance | causal analysis]] is that data alone is often insufficient to answer many of the most interesting questions in both business and science <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:21">[00:01:21]</a>. In these fields, the primary interest often lies in understanding how the world would change if specific actions were taken, rather than merely making static predictions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:38">[00:01:38]</a>. While data alone may not suffice for many important questions, the data-generating process itself can leave discernible traces <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:28">[00:02:28]</a>. By adopting a detective-like approach to identify these partial traces within the data, and combining them with expert knowledge and realistic assumptions, it is possible to achieve significant insights and interesting results <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:38">[00:02:38]</a>.

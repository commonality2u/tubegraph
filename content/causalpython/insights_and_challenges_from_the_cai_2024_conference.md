The cAI 2024 conference in London, held at the Royal Society, was characterized by incredible energy, a willingness to share and learn, and a significant amount of inspiration, reminiscent of deep learning and machine learning events in 2014-2015 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>. ## Main Insights from the Conference

Participants shared several key insights derived from the conference:

*   **Communication is Key** Communication is paramount at all stages, from problem definition to modeling and transmitting knowledge to stakeholders <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:13">[00:02:13]</a>. It is vital to communicate with various stakeholders, teams, and experts, ensuring everyone is on the same level, regardless of technical background, for success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:30">[00:02:30]</a>.
*   **Appreciation and Exploration of Causal Methods** There was widespread appreciation for seeing [[causal_inference_models_and_ai_workshops | causal methods]] being explored and adopted across various industries and sectors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:29">[00:04:29]</a>. This includes different forms of causal inquiry, such as interventions and counterfactuals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:36">[00:04:36]</a>.
*   **Diverse Approaches to Problem Solving** It was highly interesting to observe how different companies tackle similar problems, integrating not only the technical aspects but also the business context <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:51">[00:06:51]</a>. This exchange of ideas proved beneficial for solving existing challenges <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:13">[00:07:13]</a>.
*   **[[causality_and_ai_challenges_and_opportunities | Causal AI]] Addresses Limitations of Classical ML** [[causality_and_ai_challenges_and_opportunities | Causal AI]] excels in addressing the critical limitations of classical machine learning models, particularly in providing directional flows and feature importance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:00">[00:14:00]</a>.
*   **Contribution to Explainable AI** [[causality_and_ai_challenges_and_opportunities | Causal AI]] significantly contributes to explainable AI, making it easier for professionals to communicate with stakeholders and explain why certain directions or options are chosen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:17">[00:14:17]</a>. It allows for differentiation from mere correlation, fulfilling a common dream for data scientists <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:50">[00:14:50]</a>.
*   **Focus on Delivering Useful Outcomes** An enlightening insight highlighted the importance of delivering useful results despite the underlying assumptions, rather than spending excessive time discussing assumptions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:43">[00:12:43]</a>.
*   **Disentangling Relationships and Directions** [[causal_inference_models_and_ai_workshops | Causal inference]] provides a means to disentangle complex relationships and establish directions, which traditional machine learning often struggles with due to confounding factors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:18">[00:18:18]</a>.

## Unanswered Causal Questions

Attendees posed several causal questions for which they currently lack answers:

*   How to effectively initiate a causal approach within a company and make this knowledge actionable for product-related questions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:55">[00:00:55]</a>.
*   What occurs when the causal graph evolves over time, given that nothing is truly stationary or static <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:56">[00:02:56]</a>.
*   Determining the appropriate scope and boundary when designing a causal analysis, specifically where to cease adding detail and which factors are truly relevant for the diagram <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:59">[00:04:59]</a>.
*   Whether there is a finite number of counterfactuals that can provide truly informative insights for a given problem <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:32">[00:07:32]</a>.
*   In banking, how to understand factors that help customers cope with debt when facing difficulties, to design effective solutions for both customers and the bank <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:43">[00:09:43]</a>.
*   How to approach a problem where a causal effect estimation is needed, but the positivity assumption is violated, and there is no primary domain knowledge <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:00">[00:11:00]</a>.
*   Methods for ensuring that constructed causal graphs are genuinely accurate and reflect reality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:56">[00:11:56]</a>.
*   Whether Large Language Models (LLMs) represent a long-term shift or merely a passing trend <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:58">[00:12:58]</a>.
*   The long-term effectiveness of brand marketing, including its impact one to two years down the line, and how it influences performance marketing or click-through rates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:30">[00:16:30]</a>.
*   Understanding the uncertainty in the prediction and completeness of a causal graph built on a target of interest, especially since the ground truth is unknown <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:35">[00:18:35]</a>.

## Main Challenges in Applying Causal AI

Several [[challenges_in_integrating_causal_ai_in_business_settings | challenges in integrating causal AI in business settings]] and research were highlighted:

*   **Human Cognitive Effort and Adoption** It is challenging to bring useful causal knowledge to be effective within a company, as understanding AI requires significant cognitive effort from stakeholders <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>.
*   **Effective Communication** A persistent challenge is effectively communicating insights and asking the right questions to unlock problems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:27">[00:03:27]</a>. This is difficult because each case is unique, and there's no prescribed method <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:40">[00:03:40]</a>.
*   **Stakeholder Buy-in and Understanding** Getting everyone on board to properly understand [[causal_inference_models_and_ai_workshops | causal methods]] is a significant hurdle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:24">[00:05:24]</a>. It's crucial to grasp the distinction between identification and estimation to avoid misapplication of methods <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:30">[00:05:30]</a>.
*   **Demonstrating Value to Business Stakeholders** A key challenge is convincing business stakeholders of the value of addressing [[causal_ai_applications_in_various_industries | causal problems]] correctly to reap the benefits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:57">[00:05:57]</a>.
*   **Keeping Up with Industry Change** In dynamic fields like marketing, keeping up with constant changes, learning new models, understanding their assumptions, and integrating them effectively is demanding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:11">[00:08:11]</a>. The goal is to ensure [[causality_and_ai_challenges_and_opportunities | AI]] complements, rather than substitutes, existing processes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:42">[00:08:42]</a>.
*   **Bridging Research and Application** Translating external research and vision from conferences into practical, testable applications within a company is often difficult <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:13">[00:10:13]</a>.
*   **Convincing Business Leadership** Persuading business leaders, such as CMOs and CFOs, about the importance and benefits of advanced analytics and [[causality_and_ai_challenges_and_opportunities | causal AI]] is a persistent challenge <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:22">[00:11:22]</a>. This often involves resolving conflicts between objectives, like marketing spend and budget curtailment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:36">[00:17:36]</a>.
*   **Accuracy of Estimated Treatment Effects** Ensuring the estimated treatment effects are accurate is a significant technical challenge in practical work <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:06">[00:12:06]</a>.
*   **Cannibalization and Interference** Dealing with complex problems like cannibalization and interference within e-commerce landscapes poses a modeling challenge <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:11">[00:13:11]</a>.
*   **Resource Limitations** Despite the breakthrough potential of [[causality_and_ai_challenges_and_opportunities | causal inference]] and [[causality_and_ai_challenges_and_opportunities | causal AI]], progress is often limited by available tools and human resources <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:27">[00:19:27]</a>.
*   **Aligning AI Insights with Business Intuition** A key challenge is matching [[causality_and_ai_challenges_and_opportunities | causal AI]] insights with business intuition and integrating business knowledge into the AI models <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:59">[00:19:59]</a>.

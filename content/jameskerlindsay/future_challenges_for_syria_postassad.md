## Internal Challenges and Political Future

The primary internal challenge for Syria is establishing a stable administration. Questions abound regarding:
*   **Stability:** Whether Syria will descend into chaos, similar to other countries where leaders have been deposed, or if it will successfully introduce a stable administration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:43">[00:10:43]</a>.
*   **Government Structure:** The nature of the future government, specifically whether it will be Islamist or broadly inclusive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:51">[00:10:51]</a>.
*   **Minority Rights:** The implications for the country's minorities, including the Kurds, who have established autonomous areas in the northeast, and the Alawites, who were dominant under Assad <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:57">[00:10:57]</a>.

## Regional and International Implications

The overthrow of Assad also presents significant [[impact_of_syrias_civil_war_on_international_relations | regional and international dimensions]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:08">[00:11:08]</a>:
*   **Israel:** The impact on neighboring Israel remains a key concern <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:12">[00:11:12]</a>.
*   **Turkey's Role:** There is a question as to whether [[potential_outcomes_of_turkish_invasion_in_syria | Turkey]] will emerge as the main external power in Syria <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:15">[00:11:15]</a>.
*   **External Powers:** The future of Russia's military presence and Iran's influence in the country is uncertain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:19">[00:11:19]</a>.

The collapse of the Assad regime has opened up a wealth of new questions about Syria's future <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:25">[00:11:25]</a>.

## Relations with Afghanistan

The BLA insurgency has become a significant factor in the deteriorating [[relationship_between_pakistan_and_the_taliban_regime_in_afghanistan | relationship between Pakistan and Afghanistan]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:18:00">[09:18:00]</a>. Pakistan accuses Kabul of harboring Baloch insurgents, arguing that the BLA operates from safe havens in Afghan border regions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:25:00">[09:25:00]</a>. While the [[relationship_between_pakistan_and_the_taliban_regime_in_afghanistan | Taliban administration in Afghanistan]] insists it does not support the militants, it has been hesitant to act against them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:34:00">[09:34:00]</a>. The latest train attack in March 2025 further heightened tensions, with the Pakistani Foreign Ministry directly implicating Afghanistan in the siege <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:42:00">[09:42:00]</a>.

## Relations with India

The [[domestic_and_international_implications_of_the_baloch_insurgency | Baloch insurgency]] also affects relations between Pakistan and India, which have long been strained over the disputed province of Kashmir <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:55:00">[09:55:00]</a>. Islamabad frequently accuses the Indian government and security forces of involvement in the Balochistan unrest, alleging that India supplies the BLA with weapons and that BLA insurgents are in contact with Indian intelligence services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:03:00">[10:03:00]</a>. However, Indian authorities deny assisting the BLA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:21:00">[10:21:00]</a>.

## Relations with Iran

Although Pakistan and Iran have traditionally maintained good relations, ties have been strained in recent years over Balochistan, a region that straddles the border between the two countries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:31:00">[10:31:00]</a>. Both nations have accused each other of not doing enough to tackle Baloch insurgents operating from their respective territories <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:46:00">[10:46:00]</a>. This tension culminated in early 2024 when Iran launched attacks on militant bases in Pakistan, leading to Pakistani retaliation against BLA targets in Iran, sparking a brief crisis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:58:00">[10:58:00]</a>. While relations have since improved and security cooperation pledged, deterioration could occur if Pakistan believes Tehran is not doing enough <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:15:00">[11:15:00]</a>.

## Relations with China

The [[domestic_and_international_implications_of_the_baloch_insurgency | Baloch insurgency]] poses a significant threat to Pakistan's growing economic ties with China <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:29:00">[11:29:00]</a>. China and Pakistan have been close strategic partners for decades, sharing a common view of India <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:37:00">[11:37:00]</a>. Beijing views Pakistan, including Balochistan, as central to its economic development and a crucial route for Iranian gas and oil to reach inland western China <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:47:00">[11:47:00]</a>. Pakistan's deep-water port in Gwadar is an essential shortcut for goods from western China to reach international markets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:59:00">[11:59:00]</a>.

China has invested over $60 billion into the China-Pakistan Economic Corridor (CPEC) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:09:00">[12:09:00]</a>, a key link in the Belt and Road Initiative (BRI) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:16:00">[12:16:00]</a>. BLA attacks targeting Chinese interests, including the Chinese consulate in Karachi in November 2018 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:35:00">[06:35:00]</a>, and bombings in 2023 and 2024 that killed Chinese nationals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:43:00">[06:43:00]</a>, continue to fuel Chinese fears that Pakistan is not effectively managing its Baloch problem <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:24:00">[12:24:00]</a>.

The persistent [[domestic_and_international_implications_of_the_baloch_insurgency | Baloch insurgency]], with its deep political, social, and economic roots <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:56:00">[08:56:00]</a>, remains a fundamental challenge for Pakistan's internal stability and its foreign relations, compelling the government to address this long-standing problem <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:40:00">[12:40:00]</a>.

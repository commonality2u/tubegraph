This article compares [[differences_between_binance_sg_and_binance_global | Binance SG]] and Binance Global (Binance.com), focusing on their regulatory status and fee structures. While both platforms are part of the Binance ecosystem, they operate under different regulatory frameworks and offer distinct services to users in Singapore <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:10">[00:00:10]</a>.

## Regulation

Binance SG is a subsidiary of Binance and is regulated by the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:15">[00:00:15]</a>. In contrast, Binance Global (Binance.com) is not regulated by MAS <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:21">[00:00:21]</a>.

## Fee Structures and Transactions

### Binance SG

*   **Transaction Currency**: All transactions on [[differences_between_binance_sg_and_binance_global | Binance SG]] are conducted via SGD (Singapore Dollars) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:29">[00:00:29]</a>.
*   **Trading Fees**: The trading fee is 0.6% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:31">[00:00:31]</a>.
*   **Deposit and Withdrawal Fees**: There are no fees for [[deposits_and_withdrawals_on_binance_for_singapore_residents | deposit and withdrawal]] of funds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:41">[00:00:41]</a>.
*   **Payment Provider**: To [[deposits_and_withdrawals_on_binance_for_singapore_residents | deposit money]] into [[differences_between_binance_sg_and_binance_global | Binance SG]], users must use a third-party payment provider called Xfers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:37">[00:00:37]</a>.

### Binance Global (Binance.com)

*   **Transaction Currency**: All transactions on Binance Global are conducted via USD (United States Dollars) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:46">[00:00:46]</a>.
*   **Trading Fees**: Transaction fees are generally much lower compared to [[differences_between_binance_sg_and_binance_global | Binance SG]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:47">[00:00:47]</a>.
*   **Discounts**: Users can receive a discount on trading fees if they [[buying_and_selling_cryptocurrency_on_binance | buy]] with Binance Coin (BNB) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:49">[00:00:49]</a>.
*   **[[transaction_methods_for_buying_and_selling_on_binance_platforms | Transaction Methods]]**: To [[buying_and_selling_cryptocurrency_on_binance | buy crypto]] on Binance Global, there are three primary methods:
    1.  Credit card <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>.
    2.  Peer-to-peer (P2P) transactions, where users can exchange SGD for crypto directly with another individual <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:02">[00:01:02]</a>.
    3.  [[buying_and_selling_cryptocurrency_on_binance | Buying]] Binance Coin on [[differences_between_binance_sg_and_binance_global | Binance SG]] and then transferring it to Binance Global to [[buying_and_selling_cryptocurrency_on_binance | buy]] desired [[cryptocurrency_options_on_binance_sg_vs_binance_global | cryptocurrencies]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:11">[00:01:11]</a>.
*   **Selling Crypto**: When [[buying_and_selling_cryptocurrency_on_binance | selling crypto]] on Binance Global, users can utilize P2P transactions to receive SGD, or convert their crypto back to Binance Coin, send it to [[differences_between_binance_sg_and_binance_global | Binance SG]], and convert it to SGD <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:17">[00:01:17]</a>.

## Wallet Independence

It is crucial to note that the wallets on [[differences_between_binance_sg_and_binance_global | Binance SG]] and Binance Global are not linked; they are two separate wallets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:31">[00:01:31]</a>. This means funds cannot be directly transferred or traded between the two platforms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:33">[00:01:33]</a>.

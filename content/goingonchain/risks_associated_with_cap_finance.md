## Risks for Liquidity Providers (The "House")

The core mechanism for generating yield on [[overview_of_cap_finance_protocol | CAP Finance]] involves providing liquidity to a pool of ETH and USDC, which acts as the counterparty for traders <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:46">[00:00:46]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:49">[00:00:49]</a>. This means liquidity providers (LPs) essentially act as "the house" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:21">[00:01:21]</a>.

### Volatility and Loss of Capital
[[risks_of_investing_in_volatile_liquidity_pools | Pooling liquidity is not risk-free]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:23">[00:01:23]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:22">[00:02:22]</a>.
*   **Trader Wins Impact Pool:** If a group of traders makes significant profits, they withdraw assets from the pool, potentially leading to a scenario where LPs might not recover their initial deposit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:26">[00:01:26]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:28">[00:02:28]</a>. Conversely, when traders lose or are liquidated, their assets contribute to the pool <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:54">[00:00:54]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:57">[00:00:57]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:59">[00:00:59]</a>.

### Dynamic Yield and APR Fluctuations
The yields offered on [[overview_of_cap_finance_protocol | CAP Finance]] (e.g., 69% on ETH and 34% on [[risks_and_benefits_of_investing_with_stablecoins | USDC]] at the time of recording) are dynamic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:14">[00:01:14]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:24">[00:02:24]</a>.
*   **Yield Reduction:** As more funds enter the liquidity pool, the yield will naturally decrease <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:24">[00:02:24]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:26">[00:02:26]</a>.

### Withdrawal Limitations and Fees
Liquidity providers face specific conditions when withdrawing their funds:
*   **Withdrawal Fee:** A 1% fee is applied to withdrawals from both the ETH and [[risks_and_benefits_of_investing_with_stablecoins | USDC]] pools <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:33">[00:01:33]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:35">[00:01:35]</a>.
*   **24-Hour Lock-Up:** After depositing funds into the pool, there is a 24-hour lock-up period during which withdrawals cannot be made <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:38">[00:01:38]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:42">[00:01:42]</a>.

## Risks for Traders on CAP Finance

While the article focuses on risks for LPs, understanding the trading environment on [[overview_of_cap_finance_protocol | CAP Finance]] is crucial as it directly impacts the liquidity pool.
*   **No Stop-Loss Feature:** Traders on [[overview_of_cap_finance_protocol | CAP Finance]] lack a stop-loss feature, which is a common risk management tool in trading <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:15">[00:02:15]</a>.
*   **High Liquidation Threshold:** Liquidation for traders occurs at 80%, meaning a significant portion of a position can be lost before liquidation is triggered <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:17">[00:02:17]</a>.

## Risks Associated with Holding the CAP Token

Investors can also acquire the native CAP token if they are bullish on the protocol's future, envisioning it potentially reaching the success of platforms like GMX <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:34">[00:02:34]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:38">[00:02:38]</a>.
*   **Lack of Governance:** As of now, the CAP token does not offer governance rights <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:40">[00:02:40]</a>. Therefore, buying the token is primarily for exposure to the protocol's performance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:43">[00:02:43]</a>.

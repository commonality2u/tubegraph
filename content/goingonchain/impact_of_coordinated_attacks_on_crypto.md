## Factors Contributing to the Luna Downfall

Several key factors converged to cause the Luna/UST collapse:

*   **Coordinated Attack** A primary factor was a [[Importance of cryptocurrency security | coordinated attack]] where attackers acquired 1 billion UST and subsequently loaned 3 billion BTC <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:33">[00:00:33]</a>. Their sale of these assets triggered a bank run on Anchor Protocol due to the depegging of UST, causing widespread panic and selling <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:44">[00:00:44]</a>.
*   **Terra's UST Mechanism** The mechanism linking UST and Luna contributed to a "vicious cycle" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:04">[00:01:04]</a>. When users sold UST, new Luna tokens were minted. Increased UST selling led to an oversupply of Luna, further depressing its price <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:51">[00:00:51]</a>.
*   **LFG's Failed BTC Reserve** The Luna Foundation Guard (LFG) attempted to use its Bitcoin (BTC) reserve to stabilize UST <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:08">[00:01:08]</a>. However, their continuous selling of BTC to support UST also contributed to a panic in the broader crypto market <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:17">[00:01:17]</a>.
*   **Macroeconomic Events and Regulatory Pressure** Existing negative macroeconomic data, such as high inflation, exacerbated the situation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:26">[00:01:26]</a>. Additionally, comments from the Treasury Secretary about UST and calls for regulation further intensified market fears <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:35">[00:01:35]</a>.

## Market Impact and Outlook

The events led to UST losing its peg and Luna's price dropping to less than 10 cents <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:44">[00:01:44]</a>. Terra's stated priority was to restore the UST peg, even if it meant continuing to burn UST, which in turn mints more Luna and drives its price down further <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:57">[00:01:57]</a>.

Beyond Luna/UST, the large sell-off caused BTC to continue its downward trend <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:16">[00:02:16]</a>. Furthermore, even Tether (USDT), another major [[Stablecoin vulnerabilities | stablecoin]], briefly lost its peg <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:22">[00:02:22]</a>.

This article compares [[differences_between_binance_sg_and_binance_global | Binance SG and Binance Global]] platforms for [[buying_and_selling_cryptocurrency_on_binance | buying and selling cryptocurrency]] in Singapore <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:05">[00:00:05]</a>. While both are part of Binance, they operate differently <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:13">[00:00:13]</a>.

## Binance SG

Binance Singapore (Binance SG) is a subsidiary of Binance and is regulated by the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:15">[00:00:15]</a>.

Key features and [[regulation_and_fees_on_binance_sg_vs_binance_global | regulations for Binance SG]]:
*   **Currency**: All transactions on Binance SG are conducted via Singapore Dollars (SGD) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:28">[00:00:28]</a>.
*   **Trading Fees**: Trading fees are set at 0.6% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:31">[00:00:31]</a>.
*   **Supported Cryptocurrencies**: Only eight cryptocurrencies are available for trading <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:34">[00:00:34]</a>.
*   **[[deposits_and_withdrawals_on_binance_for_singapore_residents | Deposits and Withdrawals]]**:
    *   To deposit money into Binance SG, users must utilize a third-party payment provider called Xfers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:37">[00:00:37]</a>.
    *   There are no fees for deposits or withdrawals on Binance SG <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:42">[00:00:42]</a>.

## Binance Global (Binance.com)

Binance Global (Binance.com) is not regulated by the MAS <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:21">[00:00:21]</a>.

Key features and [[regulation_and_fees_on_binance_sg_vs_binance_global | regulations for Binance Global]]:
*   **Currency**: All transactions on Binance Global are conducted via US Dollars (USD) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:45">[00:00:45]</a>.
*   **Trading Fees**: Transaction fees are generally lower than on Binance SG <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:48">[00:00:48]</a>.
    *   An additional discount on trading fees is available if transactions are made using Binance Coin (BNB) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:49">[00:00:49]</a>.
*   **Supported Cryptocurrencies**: A significantly larger number of cryptocurrencies are supported on Binance Global, including Dogecoin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:53">[00:00:53]</a>.
*   **[[buying_and_selling_cryptocurrency_on_binance | Buying Crypto]]**: There are three primary methods to buy crypto on Binance Global <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:58">[00:00:58]</a>:
    1.  Credit card <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>.
    2.  Peer-to-peer (P2P) transactions, where users can exchange SGD for crypto directly with another person <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:02">[00:01:02]</a>.
    3.  Buying Binance Coin (BNB) on Binance SG and then transferring it to Binance Global to purchase desired cryptocurrencies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:10">[00:01:10]</a>.
*   **[[buying_and_selling_cryptocurrency_on_binance | Selling Crypto]]**: The opposite of buying methods can be used for selling <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:16">[00:01:16]</a>:
    1.  Using P2P to sell crypto for SGD <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:19">[00:01:19]</a>.
    2.  Converting crypto back into Binance Coin (BNB), sending it to Binance SG, and then converting it back into SGD <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:23">[00:01:23]</a>.

## Important Note on Wallets

It is crucial to understand that the wallets for Binance SG and Binance Global are separate and not linked <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:31">[00:01:31]</a>. This means users cannot buy crypto on Binance SG and then sell it directly on Binance Global <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:32">[00:01:32]</a>.

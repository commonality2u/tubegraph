## Current State of Play in the EU

A survey conducted among EU member states revealed that 19 countries have some experience with regulatory sandboxes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:16">[00:31:16]</a>. Most of these initiatives were established recently, in 2019 or 2020 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:30">[00:31:30]</a>.

Key findings include:
*   **Legal Basis**: Some sandboxes operate under existing regulations that allow experimentation, while others required adapting primary or secondary legislation, often through specific articles granting exemptions or derogations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:51">[00:31:51]</a>.
*   **Sectors**: Regulatory sandboxes are emerging in various sectors, predominantly finance and transport, but increasingly in energy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:00">[00:33:00]</a>. Examples also include social fields (e.g., subsidies for hiring unemployed persons in Finland) and education (e.g., hotmail in schools in Romania) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:26:44">[01:26:44]</a>.
*   **Duration**: Most sandboxes last about five years or, in some cases, only one year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:15">[00:33:15]</a>.
*   **Size**: The number of participants varies greatly, from hundreds in the Netherlands to single participants in other cases <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:35">[00:33:35]</a>. This raises questions about what constitutes real efficiency for a sandbox <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:53">[00:33:53]</a>.
*   **Impact**: Over 20% of sandboxes have already led to changes in regulation, though it's too early to assess the full impact for half of them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:14">[00:34:14]</a>.

At the EU level, the European Commission, through its better regulation framework, has identified existing experimentation clauses in EU law, such as in aviation security and food domains <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:12">[00:36:12]</a>. More recent examples explicitly using the term "regulatory sandbox" are found in proposals for artificial intelligence and the financial sector, particularly concerning blockchain and distributed ledger technologies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:06">[00:37:06]</a>.

The UK Information Commissioner's Office (ICO), the UK data protection regulator, operates a regulatory sandbox <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:38">[00:19:38]</a>. They view it as a means for innovators to test new ideas, products, services, and business models that use personal data under regulatory supervision <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:49">[00:19:49]</a>. This gives innovators confidence in compliance and accelerates trusted products to market <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:28">[00:20:28]</a>. The ICO has supported work in areas like tackling financial crime and biometric solutions at Heathrow Airport <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:50">[00:20:50]</a>.

Denmark launched its first regulatory sandbox program in June 2020 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:22">[00:10:22]</a>. The initial motivation was to [[innovation_friendly_regulation_rationale | incentivize new ideas and products]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:46">[00:10:46]</a>. However, experience has shown it to be a great facility for dialogue with innovators and a tool for regulators to gain insight into current barriers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:52">[00:10:52]</a>. The Danish sandbox currently only allows exemptions already permitted by existing legislation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:57">[00:39:57]</a>.

## Opportunities of Regulatory Sandboxes

Regulatory sandboxes offer several key benefits:

*   **Fostering Dialogue and Trust** [[publicprivate_cooperation_in_regulatory_sandboxes | Public-private cooperation]] is enhanced through direct dialogue between regulators and innovators, which builds trust and ensures constructive information exchange <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:15">[00:04:15]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:55">[00:04:55]</a>.
*   **Gaining Insights for Policy and Law-making** Sandboxes help regulators understand innovations, risks, and opportunities at early stages of technological development <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:16">[00:03:16]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:02">[00:15:02]</a>. This informs policy-making and law-making, enabling regulators to anticipate necessary changes in law more effectively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:05">[00:09:05]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:32">[00:05:32]</a>. A poll found that 65% of respondents believe gaining insights from businesses is the main value of sandboxes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:55">[00:14:55]</a>.
*   **Accelerating Trusted Products to Market**: By allowing innovators to test and trial ideas under supervision, sandboxes accelerate the delivery of assured, trusted products <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:28">[00:20:28]</a>.
*   **Supporting Innovation**: Sandboxes can enable innovation by providing a wider, less restricted field for ideas to flourish <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:47">[00:12:47]</a>. They help innovative projects "get across the starting line" without waiting for lengthy legislative processes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:35">[00:40:35]</a>.
*   **Breaking Regulatory Barriers**: They facilitate discussion between regulators and innovators, helping to break down existing barriers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:58">[00:13:58]</a>.
*   **Future-proofing Legislation**: Insights from projects can inform the development of new, future-proof legislation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:51">[00:40:51]</a>.
*   **Addressing Regulatory Gaps**: Sandboxes can help address the "regulatory lag" where government is behind innovation, by getting insights on regulatory overlaps, duplications, and incompleteness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:13">[00:15:13]</a>.
*   **Providing Guidance and Advice**: Regulators can offer guidance and advice, which can sometimes resolve issues for projects without needing full regulatory exemptions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:11">[00:43:11]</a>.
*   **Promoting Technology Adoption**: Regulators can use sandboxes to promote the adoption of technology that drives forward their regulatory goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:04">[00:24:04]</a>.
*   **Upholding Competitive Markets**: Regulators can ensure their approaches align with competition authorities to guard market entry and support fair competition <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:46">[00:25:46]</a>.
*   **Facilitating International Cooperation**: Given that data-driven innovation is global, sandboxes can support international regulatory cooperation to allow data and digital products to flow across borders <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:20:18">[02:20:18]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:22:20">[02:22:20]</a>.
*   **Testing Country-Specific Relevance**: Sandboxes allow governments to test what is relevant for their specific country, rather than just copying other models <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:27:31">[01:27:31]</a>.

## Challenges of Regulatory Sandboxes

Despite their opportunities, sandboxes face several [[challenges_and_opportunities_in_public_sector_innovation | challenges]] and potential drawbacks:

*   **Complexity**: Projects seeking regulatory exemptions can be highly complex, especially in areas like green energy optimization and industrial parks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:37">[00:41:37]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:39">[00:44:39]</a>.
*   **Internal Process Design**: Regulators must design robust internal processes to manage applications and facilitate expert involvement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:16">[00:42:16]</a>.
*   **Efficiency and Scale**: There's a question about the optimal number of participants needed to ensure real efficiency in a sandbox <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:53">[00:33:53]</a>.
*   **Cross-Border Fragmentation**: Similar testing in different member states (e.g., self-driving cars) risks developing divergent supervisory practices and fragmented regulatory solutions, potentially hindering the single market and scaling up innovation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:41">[00:54:41]</a>.
*   **Determining the Right Level**: A key challenge is identifying whether a sandbox is best initiated at local, national, or EU level, depending on the sector and time to scale <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:05">[01:09:05]</a>.
*   **Engaging Innovators**: Ensuring that agile innovators, especially startups, are able to participate and engage in the process is crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:27">[01:09:27]</a>.
*   **Predictability for Investment**: For large, tangible projects with significant private investment, predictability is key, even if the regulatory advantage will eventually disappear as the playing field is leveled <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:23:31">[01:23:31]</a>.
*   **Competition Distortion and Regulatory Capture**:
    *   A significant tension exists between creating regulatory experimentation and maintaining a level playing field in the market <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:17">[01:06:17]</a>.
    *   It is crucial to avoid creating special conditions primarily for "big usual suspects" and to ensure inclusion of small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) and other small innovators <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:55">[01:16:55]</a>.
    *   One tool to mitigate this is to make transparent and open the selection procedure for access to sandboxes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:02">[01:18:02]</a>.
    *   Another approach is to make it a condition of the sandbox that if the project distorts competition, the sandbox must cease to exist <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:29">[01:19:29]</a>.
    *   The ICO, overseeing a million organizations, cannot open sandbox opportunities to everyone due to cost and practical limitations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:40">[00:22:40]</a>.
*   **Cost**: Regulatory sandboxes can be expensive for regulators <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:40">[00:22:40]</a>.
*   **Not a Universal Solution**: Sandboxes are focused on intensive projects requiring detailed support and controlled testing environments, and are not appropriate for all contexts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:37">[00:22:37]</a>.
*   **Defining Success Criteria**: It's difficult to set out clear success criteria from the very beginning because innovation is inherently unpredictable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:39">[01:12:39]</a>.
*   **Risk of Loopholes**: There is a concern not to water down regulatory requirements or create loopholes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:28">[01:22:28]</a>.

## Key Considerations for Implementation

For effective implementation, several factors are crucial:

*   **Careful Planning**: Dedicate enough time and resources to planning, defining clear goals beyond just testing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:11">[00:50:11]</a>. Goals should include benefits for society <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:48">[00:50:48]</a>.
*   **Clear Framework**: Define clear competencies, rules of procedure, and eligibility criteria to avoid ambiguities in the application process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:00">[00:51:00]</a>.
*   **Assessment and Monitoring**: Plan for robust assessment and monitoring processes, including public reporting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:23">[00:51:23]</a>. Sandbox participants should provide specific types of information throughout the sandbox's existence, outlined in a test plan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:16">[01:15:16]</a>.
*   **Stakeholder Engagement**: Meet with potential applicants in advance to reconcile expectations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:57">[00:51:57]</a>.
*   **Communication and Collaboration**:
    *   Ensure strong communication between regulators and innovators <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:22">[00:52:22]</a>.
    *   Consider creating a "one-stop shop" where innovators can meet regulators from different ministries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:38">[00:52:38]</a>.
    *   Maintain robust internal communication within the organization, across government, and with the public through various channels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:11">[00:56:11]</a>.
*   **Regulatory Flexibility**: Regulators must be flexible and prepared to work cross-border and cross-sector, as innovations are often complex <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:05">[00:53:05]</a>.
*   **Multi-disciplinary Approach**: Collaboration among specialists with different backgrounds (e.g., data protection officers, consumer protection experts, competition experts) is vital <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:19">[00:53:19]</a>.
*   **Knowledge Sharing Among Regulators**: Establish mechanisms for supervisory knowledge sharing, possibly through internal innovation offices or networks of different institutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:07">[00:54:07]</a>.
*   **Transparency and Data Sharing**: Promote knowledge sharing between sandbox participants and those outside to level the playing field <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:43">[01:17:43]</a>. It could be justified for sandboxes to require more additional data reporting than normal regulation to ensure openness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:21:54">[01:21:54]</a>.
*   **Adaptive Approach**: Embrace the idea that "failing is a learning," and build flexibility into the process to allow for changes in direction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:00">[01:13:00]</a>.
*   **Strategic Project Selection**: Focus on a few projects with massive societal impact and the ability to be adopted into regulation quickly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:56">[01:13:56]</a>.

## Conclusion

[[regulatory_sandboxes_definition_and_examples | Regulatory sandboxes]] are a vital tool but should be considered part of a broader regulatory strategy to harness innovation, rather than the only approach <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:05">[01:29:05]</a>. They are particularly effective at the early stages of technical development to foster policy learning and adaptation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:39">[01:29:39]</a>. However, as technology evolves, governments may need to transition to other regulatory or non-regulatory approaches <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:47">[01:29:47]</a>. This requires a dynamic approach to regulation, adapting to the pace of innovation and the learning process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:30:17">[01:30:17]</a>. The trans-boundary effects of innovation also highlight the need for greater [[publicprivate_cooperation_in_regulatory_sandboxes | cross-border regulatory cooperation]] to ensure efficiency and mitigate adverse effects, a significant challenge for the future <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:30:35">[01:30:35]</a>.

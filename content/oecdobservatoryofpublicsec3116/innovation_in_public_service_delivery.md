Innovation in public service delivery is a central agenda in the world today, strongly supported by organizations like the OECD <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:05:07">[02:05:07]</a>. The OECD's report on global trends of [[innovation_in_government | government innovation]] highlights the importance of public services and the need to "put the public back into public services" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:29:30">[02:29:30]</a>.

The core purpose of [[public_sector_innovation | innovation in the public sector]] is to improve people's lives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:46:00">[02:46:00]</a>. Governments are pursuing various objectives through innovation, including:
*   Improving the efficiency and effectiveness of public services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:53:00">[02:53:00]</a>.
*   Increasing public satisfaction with services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:59:00">[02:59:00]</a>.
*   Helping uptake of services, especially for populations facing access challenges <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:05:00">[03:05:00]</a>.
*   Ensuring fairness and equal treatment in the relationship between governments and citizens <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:10:00">[03:10:00]</a>.
*   Building citizen trust in governments by providing quality services and inclusion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:20:20">[03:20:20]</a>.

A strategic and consistent approach to supporting [[innovation_in_public_services | innovation in public services]] is emerging globally, emphasizing the human element and placing people at the center of service design <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:43:46">[03:43:46]</a>. This requires a cultural shift in how bureaucracies operate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:56:00">[03:56:00]</a>, focusing on supporting citizens through important life events <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:09:12">[04:09:12]</a>.

[[innovation_and_digital_transformation_in_public_services | Digital transformation]] and the rise of artificial intelligence present tremendous opportunities for more responsive, personalized, and proactive public services when managed responsibly and effectively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:45:00">[04:45:00]</a>.

## International Perspectives on Innovation

### Brazil
Brazil views [[innovation_in_public_services | innovation in public services]] not just as a priority, but as a requirement for improving citizens' lives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:55:00">[06:55:00]</a>. Key principles guiding Brazil's approach include:
*   **Human-centered innovation** ensuring services are efficient, inclusive, and transformative <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:07:00">[07:07:00]</a>.
*   **Creativity** to solve real problems for real people, beyond just technology <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:16:00">[07:16:00]</a>.
*   **Essential for addressing complex challenges** like inequality, climate change, and digital transformation, and for building trust <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:34:00">[07:34:00]</a>.

Examples of Brazil's initiatives:
*   **National Digital Government Strategy:** Promotes digital transformation across all Brazilian states and municipalities, ensuring easy access to digital government services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:56:00">[07:56:00]</a>. This strategy was developed collaboratively with public servants, experts, and users <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:11:00">[08:11:00]</a>.
*   **Gob.br Digital Identity:** Allows 160 million citizens (75% of the population) to access over 2,500 digital public services easily, agilely, and transparently <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:30:00">[08:30:00]</a>. This was a priority during Brazil's G20 presidency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:50:00">[08:50:00]</a>.
*   **Unified Early Childhood Registry:** Integrates various data sources (social assistance, daycare, school enrollments, vaccines, food security) into a single platform for individualized monitoring of children and effective public policies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:03:00">[09:03:00]</a>.
*   **Inclusion in public administration:** The National School of Public Administration prepares women and black women for leadership positions and incorporates gender and race themes into all civil servant training programs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:44:00">[09:44:00]</a>.
*   **K Collabor Program:** A federal government collaboration initiative that centralizes personal management, procurement, and budget execution into a single agency, enhancing efficiency and reducing costs while prioritizing citizen and public servant needs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:38:00">[03:38:00]</a>.
*   **Unified Government Hiring Process:** Decentralized entrance exams to attract diverse talent from all municipalities, democratizing access to civil service positions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:51:00">[03:51:00]</a>.
*   **Contraatamize Brazil:** A procurement program that revolutionized public procurement by making it more inclusive and efficient, inspired by a city project that reduced bureaucracy and opened doors for small and medium-sized companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:39:00">[04:39:00]</a>.

### Portugal
Portugal has long considered [[innovation_in_public_service | innovation in public service]] a strategic priority, now viewed as a requirement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:48:00">[11:48:00]</a>.
*   **LabX (Center for Innovation in Public Sector):** This agency of administrative modernization (AMA) fosters [[public_sector_innovation | innovation in public administration]] through experimentation, testing, refining, and continuous improvement of ideas and solutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:28:00">[12:28:00]</a>. LabX involves a research phase, a co-creation stage with citizens, researchers, startups, and public servants, and an experimentation phase to test solutions before full implementation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:03:00">[13:03:00]</a>.
*   **National Digital Strategy:** Aims for public services to proactively meet citizen and business needs, enhancing efficiency and reducing bureaucracy by putting users at the center <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:39:00">[14:39:00]</a>. The goal is to simplify people's lives through seamless digital public services, transforming existing processes rather than just digitizing them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:06:00">[15:06:00]</a>.
*   **Omni-channel Service Approach (gov.pt brand):** Launched to provide a seamless, integrated, and user-friendly experience across multiple channels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:21:00">[16:21:00]</a>. This includes:
    *   A single digital service portal (gov.pt web portal) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:35:00">[16:35:00]</a>.
    *   A new gov.pt mobile app <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:39:00">[16:39:00]</a>.
    *   A single citizen phone line <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:46:00">[16:46:48]</a>.
    *   Integrated in-person support channels through a broad network of citizen shops and spots <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:59:00">[16:59:00]</a>.
*   **Mosaic Common Reference Model:** A mandatory model providing clear guidelines and a common structure for designing citizen-centric digital services, ensuring consistency across public service offerings from multiple entities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:12:00">[17:12:00]</a>. It includes the Agura design system for a unified, intuitive, accessible, and user-friendly digital service experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:37:00">[17:37:00]</a>.
*   **Technological Architecture for Simplified Access:** Utilizes building blocks like authentication.gov (for single sign-on, authentication, and authorization) and IP (interoperability platform for public administration services, supporting the "once-only" principle to prevent repetitive information submission) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:10:00">[18:10:00]</a>.
*   **Innovation Matrix for Monitoring and Evaluation:** A tool in development to use accurate data to monitor and evaluate the [[quality_and_innovation_in_public_sector | quality of public services]], identify areas for improvement, set benchmarks, and foster continuous innovation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:13:00">[19:13:00]</a>.

To ensure no citizen is excluded from digital transformation, Portugal focuses on:
*   **Preventing exclusion due to lack of digital literacy:** A large network of 930+ citizen spots across Portugal provides direct and mediated support to access digital public services, also acting as learning hubs for personalized guidance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="43:39">[43:39]</a>.
*   **Promoting civic participation through digital technologies:** The `participate.gov.pt` platform allows citizens to propose and vote on ideas, and monitor public decisions using secure digital identification and blockchain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="46:52">[46:52]</a>.
*   **Simplex Program:** Focuses on simplifying user experience, mitigating unnecessary bureaucracy, and integrating digital solutions by design <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="47:32">[47:32]</a>.
*   **Digital Skills Curriculum:** Enables citizens to invest in their digital skills with certified frameworks and clear learning paths <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="48:21">[48:21]</a>.
*   **Reducing bureaucracy for businesses:** Implementing measures to simplify interactions between businesses and public administration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="49:10">[49:10]</a>.

### European Union
The European Union faces profound challenges, requiring public administrations to play a key role in shaping a more dynamic and innovative business environment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:25:00">[21:25:00]</a>. The EU aims to simplify procedures, reduce administrative burdens, and create more user-centric public services across its 27 member states <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:01:00">[22:01:00]</a>.

Key aspects of innovation in the EU:
*   **"Once-Only" Principle:** Businesses and individuals are asked for information only once, with administrations managing data internally afterwards <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:00:00">[23:00:00]</a>.
*   **Full Interoperability:** Requires harmonization of standards and machine-readable data across public administrations and countries, ensuring semantic and legal operability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:13:00">[23:13:00]</a>.
*   **Active Partnership:** The nature of the EU requires active partnership of all member states, with high interest in working and learning from each other <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:41:00">[23:41:00]</a>.
*   **Strategic Communications (Compact):** Launched to strengthen the European administrative space, accelerate digital and green transformation, and build skills <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:02:00">[24:02:00]</a>.
*   **Digital Government as Continuous Change:** [[innovation_and_digital_transformation_in_public_services | Digital transformation]] is not a one-time event, but a continuous change process driven by technologies, requiring system thinking and approaches <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:37:00">[24:37:00]</a>.
*   **Data-Driven Public Administration:** Prioritizing working with data rather than documents, allowing data to flow seamlessly between administrative layers and across countries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:06:00">[25:06:00]</a>. This requires high-quality, exchangeable data (technically, legally, and semantically) to optimize operations, anticipate needs, develop new services, and foster innovation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:34:00">[25:34:00]</a>.
*   **Life Event Study:** Focuses on key administrative processes (e.g., applying for an ID card, registering a business) to identify bottlenecks and promote [[innovative_public_service_design | innovative reforms]] to enhance efficiency and effectiveness across member states <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:35:00">[26:35:00]</a>.
*   **Public Procurement as Innovation Driver:** Suggestions include making public procurement more innovative by using design competitions to identify solutions from startups, rather than just tried-and-tested methods <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="52:56">[52:56]</a>.
*   **Latvian Innovation Lab:** A successful example of an innovation lab in the state, supported by the EU's technical support instrument and financial support from the recovery and resilience program. It has driven 14 innovation sprints, co-creating solutions for tax reforms, health emergencies, and victim support <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="54:29">[54:29]</a>.

### Canada
Canada focuses on addressing the challenge of trust through better public services, recognizing that trust is fragile and easily lost <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:30:00">[28:30:00]</a>. Every interaction with a citizen shapes confidence in public services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:26:00">[29:26:00]</a>.

Initiatives in Canada include:
*   **Passport Service Guarantee:** Following pandemic-induced surges in demand, Canada introduced a money-back guarantee for passport services if not delivered within a specified time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:14:00">[30:14:00]</a>. This demonstrates accountability and "skin in the game" for service delivery <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:43:00">[30:43:00]</a>.
*   **Human-Centric Modernization:** Canada's digital standards require open development and user involvement in the design and implementation of tools <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:11:00">[31:11:00]</a>.
*   **Old Age Benefits Transformation:** A major modernization project to replace old technologies, immediately reducing errors. Errors directly impact citizens' financial support and can lead to administrative burden through overpayments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:38:00">[31:38:00]</a>.
*   **Digital Infrastructure and Literacy:** Recognizing the vast geography and six-time zones, Canada prioritizes digital infrastructure build-out to ensure equitable service access for all Canadians <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:56:00">[32:56:00]</a>. Digital literacy is also addressed by shaping programs to adapt language and interface quality for ease of use across various devices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:28:00">[33:28:00]</a>.
*   **Life Events and Life Journeys View:** Shifting from a product-based view to designing service interfaces from the client's perspective, bundling services around life events (e.g., welcoming a child, retirement, dealing with death) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:11:00">[34:11:00]</a>. Future plans include greater integration to reduce information repetition, moving towards a "tell us once" mentality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:47:00">[34:47:00]</a>.

## Key Principles for Driving Innovation

Successful [[public_sector_innovation | public sector innovation]] often involves:
*   **Human-Centric Design:** Placing people at the center of every decision, from digital transformation to public procurement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:54:00">[37:54:00]</a>.
*   **Strategic Vision:** Essential for strengthening services and leveraging [[innovation_in_public_services | innovation in public services]] for good <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:55:00">[35:55:00]</a>.
*   **Culture of Experimentation:** Fostering learning environments where people trust each other and feel safe to share ideas and constructive criticism, allowing innovation to flourish <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="38:11:00">[38:11:00]</a>.
*   **Investment in Public Servants:** Recognizing them as the backbone of innovation efforts, attracting and developing talent to serve citizens <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="39:32:00">[39:32:00]</a>.
*   **Collaboration:** Fostering collaboration across sectors, federative levels of government, and national borders <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="40:25:00">[40:25:00]</a>. Global challenges require global solutions and platforms for knowledge sharing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:10:00">[41:10:00]</a>.
*   **Leadership and Organizational Culture Change:** Leaders must embrace change and empower their teams, cultivating an environment where continuous learning and [[adaptive_innovation_in_public_services | adaptation]] are embedded in governance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="53:40">[53:40]</a>.
*   **Rethinking Efficiency:** Efficiency should encompass improving product quality (accuracy, timeliness) and ensuring integration into clients' broader lives, not just cost savings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="56:07">[56:07]</a>. Services should be designed from the client's perspective, avoiding "digitalization for the sake of digitalization" that diminishes user trust <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="57:05">[57:05]</a>.
*   **Iterative Improvement:** Utilizing surveys and instant feedback to identify pain points and inadvertently negative effects on clients, allowing for immediate corrections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="57:50">[57:50]</a>.
*   **Building on Existing Strengths:** Innovation doesn't always mean creating something entirely new; it can involve revisiting and enhancing existing successful programs with new technologies or processes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="58:20">[58:20]</a>.

Ultimately, the goal is to make [[innovation_in_public_services | innovation in public service]] the norm, not the exception <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:22:00">[41:22:00]</a>.

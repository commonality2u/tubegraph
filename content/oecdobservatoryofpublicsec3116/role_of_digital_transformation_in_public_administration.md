## [[challenges_in_digital_government_transformation | Challenges and Strategies for Digital Transformation in Government]]

*   **Whole-of-government approach:** Moving away from siloed operations by programs, budgets, and organizational structures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:01">[00:17:01]</a>. This requires appropriate skills and capabilities at the center of government for cross-coordination <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:31">[00:17:31]</a>.
*   **Optimized vertical integration:** Shifting from a purely top-down compliance focus to creating feedback mechanisms that allow frontline issues to reach senior leadership <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:00">[00:18:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:18">[00:18:18]</a>.
*   **Adaptive policy-making and implementation:** Policies and implementation approaches must adapt quickly based on the experiences of the workforce, managers, and the public served <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:42">[00:19:42]</a>. Annual review cycles are often too slow for the fluid nature of current challenges <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:06">[00:20:06]</a>.

These are among the key [[challenges_and_opportunities_in_public_sector_digital_transformation | challenges and opportunities in public sector digital transformation]] that require new approaches and sustained effort to implement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:36">[00:25:36]</a>.

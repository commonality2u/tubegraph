Innovation in public services is vital for governments globally, particularly in an era of rapid [[digital transformation and egovernance | digital transformation]] and artificial intelligence. ## Objectives of Government Innovation

Governments pursue several objectives through innovation to enhance [[digital transformation of public services | public services]]:
*   Improving efficiency and effectiveness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:53">[00:02:53]</a>.
*   Increasing public satisfaction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:59">[00:02:59]</a>.
*   Helping uptake of services, especially for populations with challenges accessing them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:05">[00:03:05]</a>.
*   Ensuring fairness and equal treatment in the relationship between governments and citizens <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:10">[00:03:10]</a>.
*   Building trust between citizens and governments through quality and inclusive services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:20">[00:03:20]</a>.

A more strategic and consistent approach to supporting [[public_sector_innovation_and_digital_transformation | innovation in public services]] is emerging globally, prioritizing the human element <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:43">[00:03:43]</a>. This requires a cultural shift in bureaucracy to center citizens in services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:56">[00:03:56]</a>. The OECD advocates for human-centric public administrative services, focusing on best practices to refocus efforts on citizens <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:23">[00:04:23]</a>.

## National Approaches to Digital Transformation and Inclusion

### Brazil: Human-Centered Development and Digital Identity

Brazil considers innovation in [[digital transformation of public services | public services]] a requirement for improving citizens' lives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:55">[00:06:55]</a>. Innovation must be human-centered, inclusive, and transformative, going beyond technology to creativity in solving real problems for people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:07">[00:07:07]</a>. It is essential for addressing complex challenges like inequality, climate change, and [[digital_transformation_in_government | digital transformation]], and for building trust <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:34">[00:07:34]</a>.

Key initiatives include:
*   **National [[digital_transformation_in_government | Digital Government]] Strategy:** Promotes [[digital_transformation_in_government | digital transformation]] in all Brazilian states and municipalities, ensuring easy access to [[digitalization_of_government_services | digital government services]] for every Brazilian <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:56">[00:07:56]</a>. This strategy was developed collaboratively, involving 2,000 people (public servants, experts, users) digitally and across all 27 states <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:11">[00:08:11]</a>.
*   **Gob.br Digital Identity:** A major achievement, allowing 160 million citizens (75% of the population) to access over 2,500 [[digitalization_of_government_services | digital public services]] easily, agilely, and transparently <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:30">[00:08:30]</a>. This was a priority during Brazil's G20 presidency, leading to the development of general principles on digital identity governance in partnership with the OECD <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:50">[00:08:50]</a>.
*   **Unified Early Childhood Registry:** Integrates data sources like social assistance, daycare, school enrollments, vaccines, and food security into a single platform <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:03">[00:09:03]</a>. This enables individualized monitoring and helps create more effective public policies, strengthening children's health, education, and future <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:18">[00:09:18]</a>.
*   **Inclusion of Gender and Race Issues:** The national school of public administration prepares women and black women for leadership positions, offers courses for men in gender and race topics, and integrates these themes into all civil servant training programs for structural change <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:39">[00:09:39]</a>.

### Portugal: Omni-Channel Services and Digital Literacy

Portugal views [[public_sector_innovation_and_digital_transformation | innovation in public services]] as a strategic priority and a requirement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:48">[00:11:48]</a>. The country aims to ensure that no citizen is excluded from the benefits of [[digital transformation in public administration | digital transformation]] and access to public services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:16">[00:42:16]</a>.

Key aspects of Portugal's approach:
*   **Lab X (Center for Innovation in Public Sector):** This agency, AMA, fosters innovation through experimentation, testing, refining, and continuously improving ideas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:33">[00:12:33]</a>. It involves research to understand user needs, co-creation with citizens, researchers, startups, and public servants, and experimentation before full implementation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:03">[00:13:03]</a>.
*   **National Digital Strategy:** Aims for [[digital transformation of public services | public services]] to proactively meet citizen and business needs, enhancing efficiency and bureaucracy, always putting users at the center <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:43">[00:14:43]</a>. The goal is to simplify people's lives through seamless [[digitalization_of_government_services | digital public service]], transforming existing processes rather than just adding digital layers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:08">[00:15:08]</a>.
*   **Omni-channel Service Approach (gov.pt brand):** Launched to provide a seamless, integrated, and user-friendly experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:30">[00:15:30]</a>. This includes the gov.pt web portal, a mobile app, a single citizen phone line, and in-person support through a broad network of citizen shops and spots across Portugal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:24">[00:16:24]</a>.
*   **Mosaic (Mandatory Common Reference Model):** Provides guidelines for designing citizen-centric [[digitalization_of_government_services | digital services]], ensuring consistency across public entities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:15">[00:17:15]</a>. It includes the Agura design system for unified, intuitive, and accessible digital services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:37">[00:17:37]</a>.
*   **Technological Architecture for Simplified Access:** Utilizes building blocks like authentication.gov (single sign-on, user authentication/authorization) and IP (interoperability platform for public administration services) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:10">[00:18:10]</a>. The IP supports the "once-only" principle, preventing citizens from repeatedly providing the same information <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:49">[00:18:49]</a>.
*   **Innovation Matrix for Monitoring and Evaluating Public Service:** A tool under development to use accurate data to monitor and evaluate public service quality, identify improvement areas, set benchmarks, and foster continuous innovation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:16">[00:19:16]</a>.
*   **Preventing Digital Exclusion:** Focuses on digital literacy through the network of 930+ citizen spots, which provide direct and mediated support to access [[digitalization_of_government_services | digital public services]] (e.g., renewing ID cards, requesting certificates) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:39">[00:43:39]</a>. These spots also serve as learning hubs for citizens to gain confidence in using digital services autonomously <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:02">[00:45:02]</a>.
*   **Civic Participation through Digital Technologies:** A flagship initiative establishing participle.gov.pt, a national civic engagement platform <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:52">[00:46:52]</a>. Citizens can propose and vote on ideas, and monitor public decisions using secure digital identification and blockchain technology <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:10">[00:47:10]</a>. The Simplex program, focused on user experience simplification and bureaucracy mitigation, empowers citizens to propose ideas that reduce administrative burdens <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:32">[00:47:32]</a>.
*   **Digital Skills Curriculum:** Measures to enable citizens to invest in their digital skills, guided by a certified framework and clear learning paths <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:21">[00:48:21]</a>.
*   **Reducing Bureaucracy for Businesses:** Incentives and measures to simplify the interaction of businesses with public administration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:10">[00:49:10]</a>.

### European Union: Cross-Border Collaboration and Data-Driven Administration

The European Union faces challenges in security, defense, climate, trade, and democratic standards, coupled with structural problems slowing productivity and economic growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:25">[00:21:25]</a>. Public administrations must play a key [[role_of_digital_transformation_in_public_administration | role]] in shaping a more dynamic and innovative business environment, simplifying procedures, reducing administrative burdens, and creating user-centric [[digital transformation of public services | public services]] across all 27 member states <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:56">[00:21:56]</a>.

Key aspects of the EU's approach:
*   **Reduction in Reporting Obligations:** The aim is a 25% reduction for companies, potentially saving up to 37 billion euros annually <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:19">[00:22:19]</a>.
*   **Simplifying Regulatory Environment:** The public administration has an ambitious [[role_of_digital_transformation_in_public_administration | role]] in removing barriers in the single market, boosting EU finance investments, promoting skills, and coordinating policies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:36">[00:22:36]</a>.
*   **Once-Only Principle:** Implementing this principle ensures businesses and individuals are only asked for information once, with administrations handling data exchange <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:00">[00:23:00]</a>.
*   **Full Interoperability:** Requires harmonized standards, machine-readable data, and semantic and legal operability between public administrations and across countries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:13">[00:23:13]</a>.
*   **Active Partnership of Member States:** The EU's nature necessitates active collaboration among all member states, with significant interest in working together and learning from each other <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:41">[00:23:41]</a>.
*   **Strengthening the European Administrative Space:** The "compact" communication aims to accelerate [[digital transformation in public administration | digital and green transformation]] and build skills <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:02">[00:24:02]</a>.
*   **[[Digital Transformation in Government | Digital Government]] as Continuous Change:** Not a "once and done" transformation, but an ongoing process driven by technology, requiring system thinking and approaches <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:37">[00:24:37]</a>.
*   **Data-Driven Public Administration:** Prioritized to shift from document-focused to data-focused operations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:01">[00:25:01]</a>. This requires high-quality, exchangeable data (technically, legally, semantically) to optimize operations, anticipate citizen needs, develop new services, and foster cross-border [[public_sector_innovation_and_digital_transformation | innovation]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:34">[00:25:34]</a>.
*   **Life Event Study:** Focuses on administrative processes like applying for an identity card or registering a business, including cross-border dimensions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:35">[00:26:35]</a>. The study aims to identify bottlenecks, solutions, and promote innovative reforms to enhance efficiency and effectiveness across member states <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:11">[00:27:11]</a>.
*   **Latvia Innovation Lab:** An example of EU support, where an innovation lab was created, consolidating multiple policy labs and ensuring needed skills <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:29">[00:54:29]</a>. Financial support was provided, leading to 14 innovation sprints that co-created solutions for tax reforms, health emergencies, and support for crime victims <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:53">[00:54:53]</a>.

### Canada: Rebuilding Trust and Life-Event Journeys

Canada's public service faces the challenge of demonstrating its [[role_of_digital_transformation_in_public_administration | role]] as a trusted deliverer of services, especially given current scrutiny <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:30">[00:28:30]</a>. Focusing on trust components leads to improved citizen engagement, better compliance with regulations, and adoption of new service innovations like [[digitalization_of_government_services | digitalization]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:53">[00:28:53]</a>. Trust is fragile and can be easily lost with each citizen interaction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:22">[00:29:22]</a>.

Key initiatives and focus areas:
*   **Passport Service Guarantee:** Following a surge in demand post-pandemic, Canada is experimenting with a money-back guarantee for passport services if not delivered within a specified time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:48">[00:29:48]</a>. This reflects a commitment to shared responsibility and "skin in the game" for service delivery <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:43">[00:30:43]</a>.
*   **Human-Centric Modernization:** Canada has adopted [[challenges_and_strategies_for_digital_transformation_in_government | digital standards]] that mandate openness and user involvement in the design, delivery, and implementation of tools <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:11">[00:31:11]</a>.
*   **Old Age Benefits Transformation:** A major modernization project to replace old technologies, aiming to reduce errors that can lead to financial hardship or overpayments for citizens <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:35">[00:31:35]</a>. This reduction in errors reduces administrative burden and reinforces trust <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:40">[00:32:40]</a>.
*   **Digital Infrastructure and Literacy:** Recognizing unequal access to services across a large country, Canada focuses on building digital infrastructure to ensure all Canadians have access to services wherever they are <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:00">[00:33:00]</a>. This is coupled with a focus on digital literacy, adapting programs and interfaces to be easy to use for all users <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:28">[00:33:28]</a>.
*   **Life Events and Journeys View:** Shifting from a product-based view to one centered on life events (e.g., "dealing with a death in the family," "welcoming a child," "ready to retire") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:11">[00:34:11]</a>. This bundles services intuitively and aims for greater integration to reduce repeated information requests ("tell us once" mentality) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:39">[00:34:39]</a>.
*   **Measuring Pain Points:** Using surveys and instant feedback to understand inadvertent negative effects of digital, process, or policy solutions on clients, enabling iterative correction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:42">[00:57:42]</a>.
*   **Reusing Existing Innovations:** Innovation doesn't always have to be entirely new; sometimes it means revisiting and enhancing past initiatives with new technologies or processes, such as proactive enrollment into benefit programs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:25">[00:58:25]</a>.

## Key Advice for Decision-Makers

Panelists offered advice for decision-makers looking to foster [[public_sector_innovation_and_digital_transformation | innovation]] in government services:
*   **Put people at the center:** This is absolutely essential for every decision, from [[digital transformation in public administration | digital transformation]] to public procurement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:54">[00:37:54]</a>.
*   **Build a culture of experimentation:** Innovation thrives in environments that foster learning and where people feel safe to bring ideas and engage in constructive dialogue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:11">[00:38:11]</a>.
*   **Invest in public servants:** They are the backbone of innovation efforts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:32">[00:39:32]</a>.
*   **Collaborate across sectors and borders:** Challenges are global and require global solutions; sharing knowledge and best practices is essential <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:25">[00:40:25]</a>. Platforms for collaboration help learn from each other and build a future where [[public_sector_innovation_and_digital_transformation | innovation in public service]] is the norm <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:15">[00:41:15]</a>.
*   **Rethink public procurement:** Consider making public procurement more innovative by using design competitions (similar to architecture) before drafting technical specifications, to identify innovative solutions from startups rather than only tried-and-tested ones <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:56">[00:52:56]</a>.
*   **Leadership and Organizational Culture:** Leaders must embrace change and empower their teams to cultivate an environment of continuous learning and adaptation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:40">[00:53:40]</a>.
*   **Redefine efficiency:** Efficiency should not only be about savings but also about improving the quality and accuracy of services, ensuring they effectively reach and integrate into clients' lives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:17">[00:56:17]</a>. Avoid [[digitalization_of_government_services | digitalization]] for its own sake; focus on the client experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:05">[00:57:05]</a>.

The discussion highlights that while technology is an accelerator for human-centricity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:25">[00:52:25]</a>, [[challenges_and_strategies_for_digital_transformation_in_government | digital transformation]] is not a standalone solution but a means to achieve better public services that are inclusive and deliver tangible improvements in people's lives.

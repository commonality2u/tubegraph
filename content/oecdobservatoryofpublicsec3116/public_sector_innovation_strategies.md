## The Observatory of Public Sector Innovation (OPSI)

The OECD's Observatory of [[public_sector_innovation | Public Sector Innovation]] (OPSI) focuses on three broad areas to support [[public_sector_innovation | innovation]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:36:00">[07:36]</a>:

## Case Study: IP Australia's Innovation Journey

IP Australia, an Australian government agency responsible for intellectual property rights, serves as a prominent example of successful [[public_sector_innovation | public sector innovation]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:13:00">[11:13]</a>.

### Background and Context
IP Australia issues patents, trademarks, designs, and plant breeders' rights, and contributes to Australia's [[public_sector_innovation | innovation]] ecosystem by promoting IP awareness and advising on IP policy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:17:00">[11:17]</a>. A key enabler of their [[public_sector_innovation | innovation]] is their status as a cost-recovered agency, meaning fees fund their operations and investments, providing financial independence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:22:00">[12:22]</a>.

### Milestones and Enablers
IP Australia's [[public_sector_innovation | innovation]] journey was significantly influenced by:
*   **Leadership Buy-in**: Patricia Kelly, Michael Trogir's predecessor, was pivotal in leading the organization to embrace [[public_sector_innovation | innovation]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:15:00">[13:15]</a>. A capability review highlighted the need for increased embrace of [[public_sector_innovation | innovation]], which the executive team championed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:51:00">[13:51]</a>.
*   **Dedicated Resources**: Explicitly dedicating a small percentage (less than 1%) of the annual budget to experimentation, technology, and ideas was crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:57:00">[16:57]</a>. This accepted the possibility of failure, allowing for risk-taking and learning <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:29:00">[17:29]</a>.
*   **Strategic Vision**: The development of a clear "Roadmap to 2030," incorporated into annual corporate plans, set ambitious goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:25:00">[19:25]</a>. This roadmap utilized a "three horizons" approach, compelling the agency to constantly ask how to achieve long-term ambitions, even if it meant taking risks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:44:00">[19:44]</a>.
*   **Internal Expertise and Empowerment**: The establishment of the Office of the Chief Economist in 2012 built economic expertise and data for decision-making <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:02:00">[15:02]</a>. Staff were encouraged to propose ideas, fostering a multi-directional approach to [[public_sector_innovation | innovation]] and unleashing the skills of technically proficient personnel <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:00:00">[16:00]</a>.

### Examples of Innovation
*   **Trademark Search** (February 2017): Developed in collaboration with private sector company Edge Digital, this tool combined image searching and machine learning to simplify trademark searches <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:55:00">[22:55]</a>. Users could upload logos for instantaneous searches across 400,000 images, reducing rejection rates and calls to their call center by 54% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:52:00">[24:52]</a>.
*   **Trademark Assist** (2018): An interactive tool using machine learning to educate and assist trademark applicants through initial stages, helping them submit high-quality applications <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:40:00">[25:40]</a>.
*   **Cognitive Futures Program**: An internal program dedicated to experimentation, particularly in AI and machine learning, to apply new technologies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:29:00">[27:29]</a>.
*   **Quality Assurance System Overhaul**: A less "sexy" but impactful [[public_sector_innovation | innovation]] was the significant internal change to their quality assurance system, demonstrating that not all [[public_sector_innovation | innovations]] are IT-based <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:00:00">[28:00]</a>.

### Managing Risk and Uncertainty
IP Australia adopted an agile methodology, especially for IT projects, to identify failures quickly through prototyping and collaboration with business areas, IT, customers, and third-party experts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:54:00">[30:54]</a>. Leadership also fostered an explicit appetite for risk <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:56:00">[33:56]</a>.

Working with the private sector proved crucial, focusing on outcomes and mutual learning rather than rigid, traditional procurement contracts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:05:00">[35:05]</a>. This often led to long-term trust and good working relationships at a personal level <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:58:00">[36:58]</a>.

### Resilience in Crisis: COVID-19 Response
IP Australia's prior investments in flexible workplace arrangements and IT infrastructure, including activity-based working and widespread laptop use, enabled a rapid transition to remote work during the COVID-19 pandemic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="38:10:00">[38:10]</a>. A strong cultural change management piece ensured managers encouraged staff to embrace these new ways of working <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="40:46:00">[40:46]</a>. This readiness allowed them to scale from 100 to 1,000 remote staff in a week <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:54:00">[41:54]</a>.

The experience reinforced the importance of scenario planning and strategic risk management, demonstrating the confidence that the organization could adapt to unexpected events <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="43:42:00">[43:42]</a>.

### Sustaining Innovation
IP Australia's journey illustrates different types of [[public_sector_innovation | innovation]]:
*   **Transformational Change**: Significant shifts like going 100% digital <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="52:06:00">[52:06]</a>.
*   **Adaptive/Enhancement-Oriented Innovation**: Optimizing existing processes or adapting to new contexts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="59:17:00">[59:17]</a>.

Initially, the focus was on transformative change, creating a "buzz" but also sustained pressure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="55:52:00">[55:52]</a>. Using frameworks like the "innovation facets model" helped the leadership team manage the pace of change and avoid "change exhaustion" among staff <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="57:21:00">[57:21]</a>.

### Advice for Fostering Innovation
*   **Understand Your Organization**: Appreciate where the agency has come from and that people will be at different stages of the [[public_sector_innovation | innovation]] journey <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:32">[01:18:32]</a>.
*   **Strategic Alignment**: For bottom-up [[public_sector_innovation | innovation]], young technical staff should frame their ideas in the context of the organization's strategic objectives and risks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:14">[01:13:14]</a>.
*   **Embrace Failure and Learning**: Be prepared to "fail fast" and openly evaluate whether experiments are working <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:01">[01:14:01]</a>. Hiding failures is detrimental to long-term success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:20">[01:14:20]</a>.
*   **Communication Skills**: The ability to articulate and frame ideas, supported by evidence and stakeholder engagement, is vital for driving change <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:09">[01:15:09]</a>.
*   **Build Confidence in Risk Management**: Continuously build people's confidence in understanding and dealing with risk across the organization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:24">[01:07:24]</a>.
*   **Start Small, Build Track Record**: If leadership isn't fully committed, start with adaptive or enhancement-oriented [[public_sector_innovation | innovation]] to build comfort and confidence before tackling more transformative projects <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:44">[01:03:44]</a>.
*   **Constant Re-evaluation**: Regularly revisit and stretch out strategic roadmaps (e.g., three horizons) to ensure the organization remains vigilant and eager to participate in preparing for future scenarios <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:11">[01:05:11]</a>.
*   **Clarity is Key**: A strong sense of clarity in purpose and objectives makes [[public_sector_innovation | innovation]] easier to pursue, as it provides a common touchstone for all efforts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:21:15">[01:21:15]</a>.

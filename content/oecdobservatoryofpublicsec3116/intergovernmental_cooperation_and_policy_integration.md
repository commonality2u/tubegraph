*   There was "no room to fail" in ensuring societal well-being and economic continuity, necessitating capacity building for action <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:50:00">[00:14:50]</a>.

### Speed and Agility

Governments demonstrated rapid decision-making and action, especially when supported by informed and coordinated efforts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:09:00">[00:18:09]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:12:00">[00:18:12]</a>. Key aspects of this agility included:

*   **Trust**: A critical element for enabling greater agility and self-organization within civil service <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:28:00">[00:19:28]</a>.
*   **Enhanced Inter-level Collaboration**: The pandemic revealed a unified public service, forcing enhanced [[coordination_between_national_and_local_governments | cooperation]] between local, ministerial, and other governmental branches <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:10:00">[00:21:10]</a>.
*   **Process Adaptation**: Changes in practices like procurement were necessary to increase speed, while maintaining transparency and anti-corruption measures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:26:00">[00:22:26]</a>.
*   **Innovative Solutions**: Governments implemented new solutions such as hotlines and databases, adapting to challenges that traditional methods could not address <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:02:00">[00:23:02]</a>.
*   **Redefining the Workplace**: South Africa, for instance, rapidly redefined the workplace, moving non-frontline public servants to remote work, which highlighted the feasibility of online meetings and services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:38:00">[00:24:38]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:23:00">[00:26:23]</a>.

## The Importance of Collaboration and Trust

Collaboration emerged as the key to balancing agility and thoroughness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:36:00">[00:28:36]</a>. Three main types of collaboration were identified as effective:
1.  **Collaboration with Citizens and Civil Society**: This strengthens trust and empowers citizens through information and active participation, leading to compliance with measures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:07:00">[00:29:07]</a>.
2.  **Collaboration among Public Sector Organizations**: Sharing knowledge and data, utilizing technology for interoperability and service integration, provides a seamless experience for citizens and businesses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:58:00">[00:29:58]</a>.
3.  **Collaboration among Countries**: [[Crossborder government collaboration and innovation | International cooperation]] is essential for tackling shared challenges like pandemics and maintaining economic continuity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:01:00">[00:31:01]</a>.

### Citizen Trust

Trust in government was crucial for successful policy implementation, particularly regarding compliance with confinement strategies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:41:00">[00:34:41]</a>. This trust was built through:
*   **Effective Communication**: High-level communication from leaders (e.g., Ministers of Health, Presidents) conveyed the seriousness of the situation, fostering public adherence to guidelines <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:06:00">[00:36:06]</a>.
*   **Transparency and Honesty**: Governments that transparently shared information, including difficult truths, and admitted mistakes built greater public confidence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="45:26:00">[00:45:26]</a>.
*   **Citizen Participation**: Involving citizens in decision-making processes and debates, especially on socially acceptable measures and the ethical use of technology, further reinforced trust <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:33:00">[00:41:33]</a>.
*   **Response to Feedback**: Governments adapting their policies based on public feedback demonstrated responsiveness and reinforced the participatory approach <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="45:55:00">[00:45:55]</a>.
*   **Digital Literacy**: Promoting [[public_sector_and_technology_integration | digital literacy]] can ensure correct information reaches the public, improving trust <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="50:21:00">[00:50:21]</a>.

## Embracing Complexity and Sustainability

The crisis undeniably showcased the [[multilevel_governance_and_collaboration_in_urban_policymaking | interconnectedness and complexity]] of global issues, linking health with social and economic situations, and demonstrating immediate cross-border consequences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="52:20:00">[00:52:20]</a>. To reflect and embrace these complexities in policymaking, several strategies are vital:
*   **Evidence-Based Planning**: Emphasizing evidence-based planning and performance, along with effective monitoring and evaluation, is crucial for better public outcomes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:11">[01:04:11]</a>.
*   **Overcoming Silos**: Despite decentralized public services promoting accountability, they can embed silo approaches. The pandemic forced different levels of government to work together, highlighting the need for continued integrated efforts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:37">[01:04:37]</a>.
*   **Sectoral Adaptation**: Economic sectors heavily impacted by global interconnectedness, like tourism, must adapt to new realities and explore new opportunities, often with government support and incentives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:19">[00:53:19]</a>.
*   **Attracting Investment and Relocation**: Countries can offer new packages to attract foreign businesses or long-term visitors, leveraging new logistical networks and remote work capabilities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:00">[00:57:00]</a>.
*   **[[International Collaboration on Governance Innovation | International Cooperation]]**: Real cooperation, based on trust and open dialogue, is even more crucial for solving complex challenges that no single system can address in isolation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:05">[01:08:05]</a>. Examples include the African Medical Supplies Portal, which facilitated access to critical medical supplies across the continent, testing the Africa Free Trade Agreement in practice <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:06">[01:06:06]</a>.

## A Roadmap for Sustainable Transformation

A strategy for [[international_collaboration_on_governance_innovation | innovation]] and modernization of the state and public administration, which has survived the test of the pandemic, typically focuses on four interconnected axes:
1.  **Investing in People**: Empowering public workers and developing their skills and leadership to adapt to different organizational and working models <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:27">[01:01:27]</a>.
2.  **Developing Management Capacity**: Fostering simplification, innovation in management, and [[interdisciplinary_collaboration_in_government_rulemaking | collaborative work models]], ensuring data and knowledge are used across functional silos for decision-making <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:58">[01:01:58]</a>.
3.  **Exploring Technology**: Enhancing transformations in public sector operations through [[public_sector_and_technology_integration | global technology governance]], improved interoperability, service integration, and transparent data ecosystem management <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:28">[01:02:28]</a>.
4.  **Reinforced Proximity**: Combining digital presence with physical accessibility to foster inclusiveness and better performance across the territory <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:04">[01:03:04]</a>.

These changes are expected to be sustainable, leading to a "new normal" where public services are upgraded for the long run with higher standards <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:00">[00:52:00]</a>.

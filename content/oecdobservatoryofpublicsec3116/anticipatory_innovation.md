[[anticipatory_innovation_and_engaging_with_the_future | Anticipatory innovation]] is a type of innovation characterized by deep uncertainty, the exploration of new ideas, and the expectation of radical transformation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:22:00">[02:22:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:25:00">[02:25:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:27:00">[02:27:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:30:00">[02:30:00]</a>. It involves anticipating future transformations and developing ideas that may be quite radical for existing systems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:36:00">[02:36:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:39:00">[02:39:00]</a>.

## Key Variables of Innovation
Innovation generally depends on two primary variables <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:00">[00:02:00]</a>:

1.  **Direction**: Whether the innovation activity is directed towards a specific problem (e.g., homelessness, poverty, climate change) or is a non-directed exploration and adaptation to new situations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:00">[00:07:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:00">[00:10:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:00">[00:19:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:00">[00:35:00]</a>.
2.  **Radicalness/Uncertainty**: The degree to which the innovation process is radical or incremental, and how uncertain the process is, including the calculability of associated risks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:00">[00:55:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:00">[00:59:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:00">[01:03:00]</a>. This can be understood as a balance between exploration (creating new ideas) and exploitation (using existing ideas in new contexts) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:00">[01:08:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:00">[01:14:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:00">[01:20:00]</a>.

These variables define different facets of [[Anticipatory Innovation in the Public Sector | public sector innovation]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:38:00">[01:38:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:40:00">[01:40:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:44:00">[01:44:00]</a>.

## Types of Public Sector Innovation
Depending on these variables, four key areas of [[Anticipatory Innovation in the Public Sector | public sector innovation]] emerge <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:40:00">[01:40:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:51:00">[02:51:00]</a>:

*   **Mission-oriented innovation**: Connected to a directed, purpose-driven activity aimed at solving major missions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:49:00">[01:49:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:53:00">[01:53:00]</a>.
*   **Adaptive innovation**: An exploratory activity without a clear direction, where innovators explore new ideas through daily practices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:05:00">[02:05:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:08:00">[02:08:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:13:00">[02:13:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:15:00">[02:15:00]</a>.
*   **[[anticipatory_innovation_and_engaging_with_the_future | Anticipatory innovation]]**: Occurs under deep uncertainty, focusing on the exploration of new ideas and expecting radical transformation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:22:00">[02:22:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:25:00">[02:25:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:27:00">[02:27:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:30:00">[02:30:00]</a>.
*   **Enhancement-oriented innovation**: Focuses on enhancing existing systems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:44:00">[02:44:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:47:00">[02:47:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:50:00">[02:50:00]</a>.

Each of these innovation types requires different strategic directions, capacities, and allowance for innovation to occur within government <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:57:00">[02:57:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:00:00">[03:00:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:05:00">[03:05:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:07:00">[03:07:00]</a>.

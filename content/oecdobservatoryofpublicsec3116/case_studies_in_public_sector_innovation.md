## LabX: Fostering Experimentation in Portugal

LabX, an experimental project supported by the Portuguese government, began in 2017 with the aim of fostering [[innovation_in_the_public_sector | innovation in public sector]] organizations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:07">[00:09:07]</a>. Designed as an open and safe space, its ambition was to drive a broad scope of transformative energy focused on citizens' and businesses' needs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:42">[00:09:42]</a>.

### Objectives and Challenges Addressed
LabX sought to address common [[challenges_in_public_sector_innovation | challenges in public sector innovation]], such as bureaucracy, hierarchy, a lack of questioning culture, and the fear of wasting public funds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:36">[00:06:36]</a>. Specific objectives included:
*   **Changing Public Sector Mindset:** To root strategic thinking and improve public services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:26">[00:11:26]</a>.
*   **Fostering Capacity Building:** To spread good practices and engage the community <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:49">[00:11:49]</a>.
*   **Tackling Organizational Silos:** To positively impact public services delivery through process simplification <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:57">[00:11:57]</a>.
*   **Experimentation:** To test solutions before implementation, learn from mistakes, and reduce risks and costs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:54">[00:09:54]</a>.

### Achievements and Evolution
Since its inception, LabX has conducted 25 experimental projects, engaging over 10,000 citizens and 2,000 public servants across more than 160 public sector organizations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:17">[00:13:17]</a>. It has significantly contributed to Portugal's "Simplex" program, aiming to simplify administrative and regulatory processes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:19">[00:14:19]</a>. Examples include:
*   **Education for Citizenship Project:** Developing a gamified solution adopted by over 350 schools <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:22">[00:15:22]</a>.
*   **Living Labs:** Implementing real-context public service counters where prototypes and technological solutions were developed and tested <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:37">[00:15:37]</a>.
*   **Integrated Solutions:** Creating new integrated services for life events, such as a death and bereavement desk, or the "At 18" initiative for adulthood-related services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:52">[00:15:52]</a>.

LabX has evolved from a strict experimentation lab to an ambitious center for [[public_sector_innovation | public sector innovation]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:11">[00:10:11]</a>. It promotes knowledge sharing through webinars, workshops, and guidelines, including a guide on human rights-based approaches to public services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:38">[00:16:38]</a>. The project has built a network of almost 700 innovators and a growing network of experimental labs in partnership with local governments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:01">[00:17:01]</a>.

### Embedding Innovation in Organizational Culture
LabX's success in changing organizational culture stems from fostering experimentation and demonstrating that testing, trying, and even failing are acceptable steps towards progress <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:01">[00:22:01]</a>. This has led to the emergence of "microlabs" and local government experimentation labs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:42">[00:22:42]</a>. Key strategies include:
*   **Incentives for Innovation:** A system built on training public servants and managers, providing experimentation opportunities, and recognizing achievements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:28">[01:05:28]</a>.
*   **Right to Challenge:** Allowing teams to request temporary removal of regulatory barriers to test new ways of doing things <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:48">[01:06:48]</a>.
*   **Political Sponsorship:** Strong and continuous commitment from government members is critical for successful implementation and embedding of innovation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:25:48">[01:25:48]</a>.

## GovBuys Innovation: Modernizing Public Procurement in Belgium

The Belgian federal government's Nido innovation lab launched "GovBuys Innovation" to tackle [[challenges_in_public_sector_innovation | challenges in public sector innovation]] related to public procurement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:11">[00:28:11]</a>. The initiative aims to create an interactive marketplace for a modern approach to public spending, seeking to increase the value of tax money <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:20">[00:30:20]</a>.

### Objectives and Challenges Addressed
The project confronts the difficulties of innovating within the public sector, where civil servants often hesitate to try new services and innovative companies struggle with perceived risky tendering processes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:39">[00:30:39]</a>. GovBuys Innovation aims to:
*   **Enable Testing:** Allow government entities to test new solutions with limited budgets before major investments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:00">[00:32:00]</a>.
*   **Facilitate Dialogue:** Create a space for the public sector and innovative companies to understand each other's challenges and solutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:34">[00:32:34]</a>.
*   **Provide Tools and Expertise:** Guide stakeholders in working with innovative companies to achieve optimal results <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:44">[00:33:44]</a>.
*   **Empower Civil Servants:** Give public sector managers and staff the autonomy and tools to solve challenges differently <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:00">[00:33:00]</a>.

### Approach and Achievements
GovBuys Innovation functions as a marketplace, focusing on real problems or needs rather than just ideation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:58">[00:34:58]</a>. It provides agile procedures, templates, and support to overcome obstacles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:14">[00:34:14]</a>. An example is the challenge posed by the 112 emergency number, aiming to prioritize calls based on severity by understanding the caller's situation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:25">[00:35:25]</a>.

The initiative builds a broader ecosystem where different competencies are brought together in the [[public_sector_innovation | innovation process]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:31">[00:37:31]</a>. Nido's expertise lies in fostering cooperation between civil servants (who understand problems), innovative companies (delivering solutions), and incubators (supporting new companies) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:41">[00:37:41]</a>.

### Supporting Private Sector Partners
To ensure synergy and continuous support for private sector partners, government should:
*   **Publish Problem Statements:** Allow companies to propose solutions rather than dictate a specific solution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:03">[00:41:03]</a>.
*   **Reduce Administrative Burdens:** Keep processes light and request information incrementally <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:48">[00:41:48]</a>.
*   **Be Accessible for Dialogue:** Engage in open communication, explain procedures, and provide constant, meaningful feedback <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:07">[00:42:07]</a>.
*   **Pay Timely:** Essential for small, innovative companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:00">[00:43:00]</a>.
*   **Collaborate with Incubators:** Leverage their role in grouping and supporting innovative companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:11">[00:43:11]</a>.
*   **Focus on Problem-Solving:** Use criteria that filter the right solutions, prioritize success, and consider the team's motivation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:27">[00:43:27]</a>.
*   **Embrace Experimentation:** Allow companies and public servants to test solutions and fail, paying for the effort <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:06">[00:44:06]</a>.

## Partnership for Change: Employee Exchange in Slovenia

Slovenia's "Partnership for Change" program, initiated in 2015, focuses on the exchange of employees and the transfer of good practices between the public and private sectors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:50">[00:46:50]</a>. What began as a spontaneous challenge to break stereotypes has grown into a significant platform for cooperation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:01">[00:47:01]</a>.

### Objectives and Approach
The program's primary objective is to break stereotypes about how things are done in the public versus private sectors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:49">[00:47:49]</a>. It aims to foster understanding and collaboration through:
*   **Voluntary Exchange Program:** Employees can participate in a maximum five-day exchange based on their interests, offering on-the-field training <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:50">[00:48:50]</a>.
*   **Simple Process:** Candidates submit a motivation letter stating their interests, and connections are facilitated between relevant organizations, keeping bureaucracy to a minimum <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:23">[00:49:23]</a>.
*   **Peer Learning:** Public sector employees learn about project management in real company environments, while private sector employees learn about strategies, tenders, and e-services in government <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:10">[00:51:10]</a>.

### Impact and Future Potential
The program has successfully exchanged over 300 employees between the public and private sectors, later expanding to include exchanges within the public sector and local government levels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:03">[00:53:03]</a>. It has fostered trust and built networks, leading to problem-solving initiatives through annual competitions for challenges from NGOs, academia, and various sectors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:52">[00:53:52]</a>. Examples include:
*   **Raising Awareness for E-invoices:** Encouraging schools and kindergartens to use e-invoices to save paper and administrative effort <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:37">[00:54:37]</a>.
*   **Designing E-taxes Application:** Contributing to the design of e-taxes business applications through hackathons <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:03">[00:55:03]</a>.
*   **Matching Skills:** Designing solutions to better match student skills with labor market needs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:16">[00:55:16]</a>.

The program's voluntary and interest-driven nature acts as a "safe way of experimenting," spontaneously revealing paths forward <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:02">[00:59:02]</a>. It has established a polygon for experimenting and prototyping, with potential to introduce longer compulsory rotations within the Slovenian public administration system based on these successful exchanges <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:41">[00:59:41]</a>.

## Key Takeaways for Public Sector Innovation

These case studies highlight several critical factors for successful [[public_sector_innovation | public sector innovation]]:
*   **People-Centric Approach:** [[public_sector_innovation | Innovation]] is "everyone's business" and responsibility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:16">[01:04:16]</a>, driven by individuals willing to try new approaches <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:30">[00:21:30]</a>.
*   **Experimentation and Learning from Failure:** Creating safe spaces to test new solutions, learn from mistakes, and reduce risks is vital for cultural change <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:01">[00:22:01]</a>.
*   **Collaboration and Openness:** Fostering interaction with citizens, businesses, and other public services breaks down silos and stimulates spontaneous [[innovation_in_the_public_sector | innovation]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:45">[01:04:45]</a>. Cooperation is the only known "vaccine" against silos <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:30:49">[01:30:49]</a>.
*   **Leadership and Political Sponsorship:** Strong political commitment and managers willing to be open to new ideas are critical for driving continuous public sector transformation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:25:48">[01:25:48]</a>.
*   **Focus on Real Needs:** Innovation should address concrete problems and needs, rather than abstract ideation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:01">[00:35:01]</a>.
*   **Recognition and Sharing:** Acknowledging and sharing successes and failures inspires further innovation and prevents duplication of effort <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:09">[01:08:09]</a>.

By embracing these principles, governments can move beyond recovery and truly thrive through [[innovation in the public sector | public sector innovation]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:09">[01:08:09]</a>.

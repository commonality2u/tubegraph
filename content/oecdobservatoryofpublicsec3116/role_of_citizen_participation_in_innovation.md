The [[the_role_of_public_sector_in_innovation|Role of Public Sector in Innovation]] and [[role_of_governments_in_fostering_innovation|Role of Governments in Fostering Innovation]] includes an emphasis on [[citizen_participation_in_policy_making|citizen participation]] as a critical component of effective innovation processes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:50">00:38:50</a>.

### Co-creation and Co-design
Governments are encouraged to engage citizens to co-create and co-design solutions to current problems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:09">00:04:09</a>. This involves fostering [[stakeholder_engagement_in_innovation|new partnerships]] and incorporating diverse perspectives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:41">00:38:41</a>.

### Integration in Innovation Initiatives
[[citizen_participation_in_policy_making|Citizen participation]] is considered an essential part of effective innovation and is reflected in the proposed declaration on public sector innovation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:27">00:39:27</a>. Governments are encouraged to actively seek and listen to different voices within their communities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:58">00:38:58</a>. Many governments are already engaging citizens in various ways <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:09">00:39:09</a>. Ongoing work, such as a call for innovations around open government, aims to demonstrate different methods for engaging citizens <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:13">00:39:13</a>.

### Importance of Diverse Perspectives
Innovation relies on understanding and integrating different perspectives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:48">00:38:48</a>. This is why [[citizen_participation_in_policy_making|citizen participation]] is a crucial element of the [[impact_of_government_innovation_on_citizens|impact of government innovation on citizens]] and the overall public sector innovation process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:54">00:38:54</a>.

The COVID-19 pandemic revealed the critical importance of [[crosssectoral and interdisciplinary collaboration for climate policy | collaboration]] in public governance, both within and across government levels and with external partners <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:12">[00:21:12]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:36">[00:28:36]</a>. ## Importance of Collaboration

The crisis demonstrated that public services, sometimes perceived as slow and bureaucratic, could be highly effective and efficient when working together <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:50">[00:20:50]</a>. A key revelation was that all parts of the public service are interconnected and cannot operate in isolation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:57">[00:20:57]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:27">[01:08:27]</a>.

## Types of Collaboration

Three main types of [[collaboration and public sector partnerships | collaboration]] proved decisive in enabling quick and efficient responses:

### 1. [[Collaboration between governments and civil society organizations | Collaboration with Citizens and Civil Society]]
Engaging with citizens and civil society organizations reinforces trust, empowers people to act, and helps ensure compliance with public health measures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:10">[00:29:10]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:32">[00:29:32]</a>. Transparent and honest communication, including admitting mistakes and incorporating public feedback, fosters confidence and a participatory approach to decision-making <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:26">[00:45:26]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:50">[00:45:50]</a>.

### 2. Intergovernmental Cooperation
The pandemic necessitated enhanced [[collaboration and public sector partnerships | cooperation]] across all levels of government—municipal, regional, and federal—to ensure greater speed and efficiency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:12">[00:21:12]</a>. Decentralization, while promoting accountability, can embed siloed approaches, but the crisis forced different government levels to work together <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:40">[01:04:40]</a>. This included:
*   **Information Sharing**: Sharing knowledge and data to make informed choices and react promptly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:01">[00:30:01]</a>.
*   **Policy and Service Integration**: Using technology to foster interoperability and integrate services, providing a seamless experience for citizens and businesses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:11">[00:30:11]</a>.
*   **Decentralized Collaboration**: Despite decentralized public services, the crisis forced collective structures and resource allocation among various government tiers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:40">[01:04:40]</a>.

### 3. [[Crossborder collaboration in public sector innovation | Cross-border Collaboration]]
The interconnectedness of global issues, particularly highlighted by the pandemic, made international [[government collaboration across borders | cooperation]] even more crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:25">[00:52:25]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:03">[00:58:03]</a>. This includes:
*   **Seamless Service Provision**: Working together to provide better and seamless services for citizens and businesses [[crossborder collaboration in public services | across borders]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:01">[00:31:01]</a>.
*   **Economic Interdependence**: Acknowledging that external shocks, like border closures, deeply affect national economies (e.g., tourism, trade) and working to find new opportunities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:19">[00:53:19]</a>.
*   **Continental Responses**: Developing regional and continental responses, such as Africa's Medical Supplies Portal, to ensure access to critical supplies and test the implementation of agreements like the Africa Free Trade Agreement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:06">[01:06:06]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:13">[01:07:13]</a>.

### 4. Public-Private Partnerships
The crisis also spurred [[collaboration and public sector partnerships | collaboration]] between the public and private sectors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:47">[00:26:47]</a>. For example, in South Africa, the private sector availed services to assist in building field hospitals and providing beds, something that was less common before the pandemic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:57">[00:26:57]</a>.

## Benefits and Outcomes

*   **Increased Trust and Legitimacy**: Effective communication and a unified response helped rebuild trust and legitimacy in government <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:27">[00:10:27]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:37">[00:21:37]</a>.
*   **Agility and Speed**: [[Collaborative approaches in public sector innovation | Collaboration]] allowed governments to react quickly and implement measures in a very short period <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:30">[00:13:30]</a>.
*   **Enhanced Efficiency**: Public servants demonstrated greater efficiency, particularly through remote work, and a shared sense of public duty <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:56">[00:07:56]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:02">[00:14:02]</a>.
*   **Better Decision-Making**: [[Public sector innovation and collaboration | Collaborative work]] leads to better decisions, more efficient use of resources, and a deeper understanding of complex issues <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:30">[00:33:30]</a>.
*   **Innovation**: The crisis forced governments to adopt innovative solutions, such as hotlines, databases, and new procurement practices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:02">[00:23:02]</a>.

## Challenges

*   **Unpreparedness**: Many countries were not fully prepared for a pandemic of this magnitude <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:16">[00:14:16]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:14">[00:24:14]</a>.
*   **Fake News**: The spread of misinformation made it harder to build public trust <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:04">[00:50:04]</a>.
*   **Procurement Risks**: Rapid procurement of essential equipment sometimes exposed governments to corruption <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:21">[00:27:21]</a>.
*   **Maintaining Trust in New Normal**: Inspiring public administration and maintaining efficiency and motivation in a "new normal" remote work environment presents a challenge <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:38">[01:14:38]</a>.

## Future Outlook and Sustainability

The changes brought by the pandemic are largely sustainable, setting new standards for public administration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:21">[00:46:21]</a>. Key takeaways for the future include:
*   **Digital Transformation**: Further improving [[public sector innovation and collaboration | digital transformation]] in the public sector <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:58">[00:40:58]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:43">[00:50:43]</a>.
*   **Human-Centered Approach**: Ensuring digital transformation remains human-centered, driven by people's choices, and uses technology ethically <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:33">[01:10:33]</a>.
*   **Empowerment of Civil Servants**: Granting more empowerment to civil servants to self-organize and be result-driven <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:47">[01:11:47]</a>.
*   **Continuous Learning and Talent Development**: Governments must enable continuous education, attract and retain talent, and foster collaboration between experienced and new civil servants <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:59">[01:11:59]</a>.
*   **Proactiveness and Scenario Building**: Moving from reaction to proactiveness by building scenarios to prepare for future challenges <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:21">[01:15:21]</a>.
*   **Policy Integration**: Policymaking must reflect and embrace complexities across sectors and countries, moving towards a "whole of government" perspective <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:49">[00:59:49]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:57">[01:03:57]</a>.

## Key Words of Change

The pandemic has brought three major keywords of change in public governance:
1.  **Consolidation**: Consolidating efforts across all levels and sectors of government and society <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:00">[01:14:00]</a>.
2.  **Efficiency**: A renewed focus on government efficiency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:06">[01:14:06]</a>.
3.  **Innovation**: Fostering innovation in how things are done <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:11">[01:14:11]</a>.

The experience has set new, higher standards for public administration, demanding greater communication, proactivity, and involvement from citizens and non-governmental organizations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:19">[00:48:19]</a>. [[Public sector innovation and collaboration | Collaborative work]] is considered the "new normal" for public administration, leading to better decisions, resource utilization, and collective action <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:26">[00:32:26]</a>.

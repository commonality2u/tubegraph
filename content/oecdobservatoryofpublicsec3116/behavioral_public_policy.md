## The LOGIC Framework: Principles for Mainstreaming Behavioral Science
The OECD developed the "LOGIC: Good Practice Principles for Mainstreaming Behavioral Public Policy" report to guide governments in setting up and sustaining [[role_of_behavioral_science_in_government_policy | behavioral science functions]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:06">[00:13:06]</a>. This framework helps answer questions such as how to establish a behavioral science function, determine the best governance models, and ensure the long-term impact and sustainability of [[behavioral_insights_in_public_administration | behavioral functions]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:13">[01:04:13]</a>. LOGIC is based on years of rigorous research, combining data from governments globally with insights from [[behavioral_insights_in_government | behavioral science experts]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:31:06">[01:31:06]</a>. The principles are designed for both new and experienced practitioners <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:48:57">[01:48:57]</a>.

*   **Leadership (L)**: Focuses on the role of influential leaders in encouraging the adoption of [[Behavioral Science and Demandside Policies | Behavioral Science]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:41:07">[10:41:07]</a>.     *   **Good Practice Principle 1**: Senior leaders request and advocate for [[behavioral_insights_in_government | Behavioral Science]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:15:23">[11:15:23]</a>.
    *   **Good Practice Principle 2**: Managers maintain senior leader support for [[behavioral_insights_in_government | Behavioral Science]] through regular briefings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:26:01">[11:26:01]</a>.
*   **Objectives (O)**: Concerns the strategy and goals of a behavioral science function within government <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:35:05">[11:35:05]</a>.
    *   **Good Practice Principle 3**: Governments include [[behavioral_insights_for_public_policy | Behavioral Science]] in strategic plans and monitor its use over time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:37:05">[11:37:05]</a>.

    *   **Good Practice Principle 5**: [[behavioral_insights_for_public_policy | Behavioral Science]] is used to design and improve internal organizational processes, rules, and incentives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:35:55">[12:35:55]</a>.

## Application of Behavioral Science in the Policy Cycle
[[Behavioral science in environmental policy | Behavioral science]] can assist governments and policymakers throughout the entire policy cycle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:51:00">[07:51:00]</a>:
*   **Problem Identification**: Helps identify and prioritize societal challenges where behaviors are counter to long-term interests, such as unhealthy food choices or low uptake of government programs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:59:00">[07:59:00]</a>.
*   **Policy Analysis and Design**: Aids in understanding the drivers and barriers of specific group behaviors within a social system, which informs policy design <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:15:00">[08:15:00]</a>.
*   **Policy Assessment**: Provides tools like randomized control trials (RCTs) and pilot testing to de-risk policy implementation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:39:00">[08:39:00]</a>.
*   **Policy Implementation**: Traditionally, this is the most common use, helping policymakers decide how a policy should be implemented, optimize existing programs, or maximize impact <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:51:00">[08:51:00]</a>.
*   **Policy Monitoring and Evaluation**: Assists in monitoring and evaluating policy implementation to ensure that subsequent problem identification addresses key issues <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:10:00">[09:10:00]</a>.

## Global Growth and Challenges in Behavioral Public Policy
More than 300 institutions worldwide are currently applying [[Behavioral Science and Demandside Policies | behavioral science]] to public policies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:34:00">[03:34:00]</a>. The growth has been accelerating, with over half of new teams created since 2019 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:27:00">[04:27:00]</a>. This trend was likely expedited by COVID-19, which presented significant behavioral problems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:42:00">[04:42:00]</a>.

Despite this growth, practitioners face challenges primarily related to institutional factors and the relationship of [[Behavioral Science and Demandside Policies | Behavioral Science]] teams with their organizations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:06:00">[06:06:00]</a>. Counterintuitively, older, more mature teams often report facing more challenges, as they encounter different situations and develop strategies to address them over time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:26:00">[06:26:00]</a>. The LOGIC report aims to leverage collective knowledge to identify effective practices for mainstreaming [[Behavioral Public Policy | behavioral public policy]] and overcoming these challenges <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:42:00">[06:42:00]</a>.

## Country Experiences and Turning Points

### Finland
Finland's journey in embedding [[Behavioral Science and Demandside Policies | Behavioral Science]] into policymaking began in 2013 with the "governments for future" collaboration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:01:00">[02:01:00]</a>. A significant "experimentation culture" phase, government-led for four years, generated substantial interest, including the widely reported basic income experiment in 2017 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:11:00">[02:11:00]</a>.

A key turning point occurred in 2020 during the COVID-19 crisis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:49:00">[02:49:00]</a>. Recognizing the wide use of [[behavioral_insights_for_public_policy | Behavioral Science]] in other countries, Finland launched a six-month pilot project in October 2020 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:50:00">[02:50:00]</a>. This pilot, which advised the communications team, was well-received, particularly in guiding public behavior during the pandemic (e.g., mask-wearing, vaccinations) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:54:00">[02:54:00]</a>. By late 2021, a two-person team in the Prime Minister's office demonstrated the impact possible with limited resources <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:00:00">[03:00:00]</a>.

Successful trials, such as text messages to encourage youth voting, garnered media attention and allowed the team to expand its work into "every imaginable area" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:46:00">[02:46:00]</a>. The team has grown to four, soon five, people and is involved in an EU project, despite resource constraints <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:39:00">[02:39:00]</a>. The success is attributed to a strong team, support from the Prime Minister's Office, and the belief that not using [[Behavioral Science and Demandside Policies | Behavioral Science]] is no longer an option for achieving impact <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:50:00">[02:50:00]</a>.

### United Kingdom
The UK government, particularly His Majesty's Revenue and Customs (HMRC), saw an early and impactful turning point with the Nudge Unit's first trial demonstrating that informing people that "nine out of ten people in your area paid their tax on time" increased tax compliance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:52:00">[03:52:00]</a>. Initial success was bolstered by strong political support from the Prime Minister, Deputy Prime Minister, and Civil Service head <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:09:00">[03:09:00]</a>.

However, a critical evolutionary turning point came as political support became time-limited <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:36:00">[03:36:00]</a>. Instead of fighting the mainstream, the HMRC team found success by identifying "side streams" or siloed areas with high autonomy, such as debt management <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:38:00">[03:38:00]</a>. Here, they built strong relationships, trained junior staff, and conducted a series of trials on complex debt behaviors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:43:00">[03:43:00]</a>.

Another significant turning point was the COVID-19 pandemic, where [[behavioral_insights_in_public_health_and_pandemic_response | behavioral insights]] had a "thoroughgoing widespread input" into the furlough scheme <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:31:00">[03:31:00]</a>. This included framing the scheme as reassurance, designing human-centered guidance, and influencing anti-fraud measures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:41:00">[03:41:00]</a>. Factors enabling this impact included:
*   **Novelty**: The scheme was new, with no established norms, allowing openness to new ideas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:48:00">[03:48:00]</a>.
*   **Track Record**: The team's established credibility and relationships fostered trust <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:52:00">[03:52:00]</a>.
*   **Senior Buy-in**: Key figures, including the Chancellor (now Prime Minister), were behaviorally astute <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:49:00">[03:49:00]</a>.
*   **Opportunism and Communication**: The team was proactive, knew the organization well, and communicated their value effectively, making their proposals easy to adopt during a crisis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:58:00">[03:58:00]</a>.

### Brazil
Brazil's journey with [[Behavioral Science and Demandside Policies | Behavioral Science]] began with isolated experiences, with initiatives in Rio de Janeiro and São Paulo <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:35:00">[03:35:00]</a>. At the federal level, the National School of Public Administration played a key role, launching regular [[Behavioral Science and Demandside Policies | behavioral science]] courses and publications since 2017 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:00:00">[04:00:00]</a>. The "Simplemente" method was developed to generate insights for public policies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:17:00">[04:17:00]</a>.

The major turning point occurred in 2023 with the inception of CINCO, the first [[Behavioral Science and Demandside Policies | Behavioral Science]] unit established in a new Ministry focused on Management and Innovation in Public Services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:54:00">[04:54:00]</a>. This development is significant for Brazil and Latin America <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:11:00">[04:11:00]</a>. The Ministry's transversal policy responsibility (e.g., procurement, human resources, digital services) aligns directly with CINCO's mission to improve public policy and societal life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:27:00">[04:27:00]</a>.

The political change with Lula's inauguration and the establishment of this new "Innovation Ministry" provided the opportune moment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:57:00">[04:57:00]</a>. Advocacy efforts, combined with the coordinator's prior experience in innovation labs using [[Behavioral Science and Demandside Policies | Behavioral Science]], led to the creation of CINCO <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:33:00">[04:33:00]</a>. The unit was granted special power to recruit civil servants from various federal organizations, resulting in a diverse 15-person full-time team <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:42:00">[04:42:00]</a>.

CINCO focuses on co-creation, working *with* policymakers rather than as consultants, fostering a sense of shared ownership in projects <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:50:00">[04:50:00]</a>. This collaborative approach, combined with a strong innovation movement and the belief that behavioral insights address pressing societal needs (e.g., dengue transmission), has generated significant engagement and interest among civil servants <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:22:00">[04:22:00]</a>.

## Key Lessons Learned
*   **Time and Resources**: Building [[Behavioral Science and Demandside Policies | Behavioral Science]] into government work takes significant time and resources, with no shortcuts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:29:00">[05:29:00]</a>. Each country and context is unique, requiring internal work and deep understanding of local systems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:50:00">[05:50:00]</a>.
*   **Communication is Key**: Continuously communicating "who you are, what you do, and with whom you are doing these things" is crucial for building trust and wider government support <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:35:00">[05:35:00]</a>. Regular briefings, research presentations, and popularized reports help maintain engagement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:46:00">[05:46:00]</a>.
*   **Collaboration over Consultation**: Working *with* civil servants and involving them in the process is transformative, going beyond merely providing solutions as consultants <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:52:00">[05:52:00]</a>.
*   **Subtlety vs. Scalability**: While [[Behavioral Science and Demandside Policies | behavioral science]] can offer subtle, finely tuned interventions, government organizations often struggle with maintaining this subtlety at scale <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:59:00">[05:59:00]</a>. Designing interventions that balance behavioral insight with the realities of large-scale government implementation is crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:59:00">[05:59:00]</a>.
*   **Learning from Others**: Newer teams can benefit significantly from learning from more experienced practitioners and understanding both their successes and mistakes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:44:00">[05:44:00]</a>.

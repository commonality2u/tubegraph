## Role of the OECD Observatory of Public Sector Innovation

The [[role_of_the_observatory_of_public_sector_innovation | OECD Observatory of Public Sector Innovation]] (OPSI) works to embed [[public_sector_innovation | innovation]] at the core of government operations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:36">[00:00:36]</a>. Its efforts are multifaceted:

*   **Empowering Public Servants** The OPSI empowers public servants by providing new insights, tools, and connections, enabling them to explore new possibilities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:46">[00:00:46]</a>.
*   **Conducting Research** It conducts cutting-edge research to identify emerging trends in [[public_sector_innovation | public sector innovation]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:55">[00:00:55]</a>.
*   **Providing Expert Advice** The OPSI collaborates with leading experts to offer trusted advice on innovative practices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>.
*   **Normalizing Innovation** A key objective is to transform new, effective approaches into standard practice within governments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:02">[00:01:02]</a>.
*   **Global Reach** The work of the [[oecd_observatory_and_innovation_model | OECD Observatory of Public Sector Innovation]] extends globally, encompassing OECD member countries and beyond <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:08">[00:01:08]</a>.

The overarching goal is to support the development of better and more innovative policies, contributing to improved lives for citizens <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:21">[00:01:21]</a>.

*   **Rear Hatch Mechanism** A video by Twitter user Matt Landry showed the rear hatch opening and closing smoothly with a button press, revealing the rear view mirror and protecting the bed when closed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:09">[00:01:09]</a>.
*   **Bed Strength and Solar Option** The six-and-a-half-foot bed is reportedly strong enough to stand on <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:21">[00:01:21]</a>. Elon Musk confirmed on Twitter that an option for a solar panel cover would be available, potentially regenerating up to 15 miles of extra range per day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:28">[00:01:28]</a>. While "really cool," this wasn't the sole reason for ordering <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:42">[00:01:42]</a>.
*   **Performance** Test drives showcased the dual motor version achieving 0-60 mph in approximately 4.5 seconds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:44">[00:01:44]</a>. This is faster than most other pickup trucks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:01">[00:02:01]</a>, though not the primary reason for purchase <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:17">[00:02:17]</a>.
*   **Modular Bed Design** Quinn of Snazzy Labs highlighted the L-track rail and various slots in the bed floor, allowing for flexible tie-down and arrangement of cargo <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:18">[00:02:18]</a>. This "smart" modular design was appreciated, but not the ultimate deciding factor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:38">[00:02:38]</a>.
*   **Color Options** Elon Musk also confirmed a matte black version would be available <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:42">[00:02:42]</a>, but this also was not the reason for the order <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:56">[00:02:56]</a>.

### The True Motivation: Disruption and Design Philosophy
The speaker's decision to order the [[Tesla CyberTruck design and features | CyberTruck]] ultimately stemmed from its polarizing and intentional design, and its potential as a market disruptor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:13">[00:06:13]</a>.

The [[Tesla CyberTruck design and features | design]] features:
*   Cool lights in the bed (assuming they can be turned off) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:09">[00:03:09]</a>.
*   A "sick" and functional huge bar light on the front <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:13">[00:03:13]</a>.
*   A cool-looking roof light <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:18">[00:03:18]</a>.

The truck's most striking feature is its complete lack of curves, relying solely on angles, points, and lines <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:57">[00:03:57]</a>. This simplistic, almost child-like approach to drawing a truck <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:03">[00:04:03]</a>, is what makes it stand out dramatically from all other trucks on the road <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:08">[00:04:08]</a>. This design was purposeful to stand out in a market with "hundreds of other pickup truck options" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:14">[00:04:14]</a>.

Tesla's entry into the pickup truck market is particularly challenging because it's a "super mature" and "super locked in" segment, with strong brand loyalty where millions consistently buy Ford F-150s for decades <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:29">[00:05:29]</a>. If Tesla had created a truck that looked similar to existing models, it wouldn't attract these loyal customers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:44">[00:05:44]</a>.

Therefore, the [[Tesla CyberTruck design and features | CyberTruck]] "had to be different, it had to be striking and it had to stand out" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:25">[00:04:25]</a>. This strategy is comparable to Apple making intentionally "jarring" design choices, knowing they will generate buzz and eventually become iconic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:37">[00:04:37]</a>.

The speaker believes the CyberTruck could become "the most important vehicle Tesla makes" and "the most disruptive" in the tech world <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:15">[00:06:15]</a>. After two days, the [[Tesla CyberTruck design and features | design]] grew on the speaker <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:24">[00:06:24]</a>. The fact that 146,000 CyberTruck orders were placed immediately after the reveal, from people who didn't even wait for it to grow on them, further validated its appeal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:36">[00:06:36]</a>.

Ultimately, the speaker ordered a triple motor CyberTruck with a $100 refundable deposit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:05">[00:07:05]</a> to embrace their position as a tech reviewer and investigate this "bleeding edge" piece of tech <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:55">[00:06:55]</a>. The truck "had to be different" and the speaker is "here for its position as a disrupter" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:50">[00:06:50]</a>.

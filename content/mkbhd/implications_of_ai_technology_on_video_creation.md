## OpenAI's Sora: A New Frontier
OpenAI, co-founded by Sam Altman, recently unveiled a new model called Sora <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:02">[00:01:02]</a>. Sora is capable of generating full video clips, up to one minute in length, from simple text input <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:06">[00:01:06]</a>. Similar to DALL.E for images <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:13">[00:01:13]</a>, Sora can comprehend text prompts and translate them into photorealistic or stylized videos <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:17">[00:01:17]</a>. This capability extends to understanding complex interactions like reflections, textures, materials, and physics over time to create believable video sequences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:25">[00:01:25]</a>.

The speed of improvement in these AI models is genuinely shocking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:03">[00:04:03]</a>. Just months ago, DALL.E 3, while high-end, still showed imperfections, especially with human features like hands or ears <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:11">[00:04:11]</a>.

## Real-World Implications

### Potential for Deception
The advanced quality of these [[software_and_ai_capabilities | AI capabilities]] means they can easily pass as real videos to an unsuspecting audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:01">[00:06:01]</a>. This raises serious [[ethical_and_societal_concerns_of_ai_generated_content | ethical and societal concerns of AI-generated content]], especially during an election year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:09">[00:06:09]</a>.

### Impact on Stock Footage and Video Licensing
Sora's ability to generate specific, high-quality videos for various purposes, such as presentations, advertisements, and PowerPoints, has significant implications for the [[impact_of_ai_on_video_licensing_and_stock_footage | impact of AI on video licensing and stock footage]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:17">[00:06:17]</a>. AI-generated videos are already good enough to fully serve these purposes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:33">[00:06:33]</a>.

For instance, a drone shot of waves at Big Sur, if seen on social media, would likely be perceived as a real drone shot <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:40">[00:06:40]</a>. This technology negates the need to hire drone pilots or license existing footage, directly affecting videographers and photographers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:01">[00:07:01]</a>. Complex scenes, like a wall of old TVs, which would be expensive and difficult to film, can now be easily generated with realistic reflections and environments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:16">[00:07:16]</a>. Even historical themed footage, such as California during the Gold Rush, can be generated convincingly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:33">[00:07:33]</a>.

This implies that entire advertisements, YouTube videos, or even movies could eventually be fully generated by AI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:46">[00:07:46]</a>.

## Current Limitations and Safety Measures
OpenAI acknowledges the current limitations of Sora <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:09">[00:08:09]</a>. The tool is currently in limited access, primarily with "red teamers" who test its limits and a few trusted creators <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:18">[00:08:18]</a>.

Observed weird edge cases include:
*   Gray wolf pups appearing out of nowhere and walking through each other <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:33">[00:08:33]</a>.
*   Anomalies in a clip of a person running on a treadmill <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:45">[00:08:45]</a>.
*   A "grandma celebrating her birthday" video, where subtle inspection reveals inconsistent hands, a common issue with [[advancements_in_ai_generated_videos | AI-generated photos and videos]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:02">[00:09:02]</a>.

To combat misuse, every video generated by Sora includes a watermark in the bottom corner, ideally remaining uncropped <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:18">[00:10:18]</a>. OpenAI is implementing safety measures to prevent the generation of harmful content, such as people's likenesses or politically sensitive scenarios <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:33">[00:10:33]</a>.

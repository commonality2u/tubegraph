## Air Purifying Functionality

The Dyson Zone is explicitly marketed for people living or commuting in cities with poor air quality (dust, pollen, smog, gas fumes, grass clippings) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:51">[00:08:51]</a>. Its timing was "super unfortunate" as it was announced around the same time as the COVID-19 pandemic, but it is not related to it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:36">[00:08:36]</a>.

### Mechanism
*   **Filters**: The ear cups contain air intakes and tiny micro compressors. Removable filters, supplied in the box, cover these intakes, purifying the air by cutting down 99% of pollutants as small as 0.1 microns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:51">[00:07:51]</a>.
*   **Visor**: A plastic visor magnetically connects to slots on the headphones, channeling the purified air over the nose and mouth through a front mesh <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:45">[00:07:45]</a>.

### Real-World Performance and [[design_and_functionality_issues_of_dyson_zone | Design and Functionality Issues]]
Testing revealed significant [[design_and_functionality_issues_of_dyson_zone | flaws]]:
*   **Smell Penetration**: Despite the air stream, users can still smell external odors like gasoline, pollen, or even a Porta-John <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:43">[00:09:43]</a>.
*   **Fan Noise**: The fans in the ear cups, which spin up with the air compressors, are audible and can be heard over music <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:00">[00:10:00]</a>.
*   **Lack of Seal**: There is no effective seal around the nose and mouth, allowing external air and pollutants to enter from above and below the visor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:17">[00:10:17]</a>. While the visor is adjustable, a full seal would require an uncomfortable "scuba diving mouthpiece" design <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:27">[00:10:27]</a>.
*   **Reduced Battery Life**: When air filtration is active, battery life drops from 50 hours to just two hours <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:49">[00:10:49]</a>.
*   **Visor Quality**: The visor is light and flimsy plastic, presumably for weight balance, but shows scratches and poor finishes, feeling cheap <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:54">[00:10:54]</a>.
*   **Appearance**: Wearing the device makes the user "look ridiculous" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:09">[00:11:09]</a>.

### Medical and Safety Concerns
A medical professional highlighted several critical [[design_and_functionality_issues_of_dyson_zone | flaws]] and risks:
*   **Not Medical Grade**: The product is explicitly stated as not being medical grade and lacks a seal, unlike a traditional N95 mask <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:31">[00:11:31]</a>.
*   **Filtration Efficacy**: While it claims to clean 0.1-micron particles, it is not HEPA-rated (which filters 0.3-micron particles with 99.95% efficacy). N95 masks filter 0.3-micron particles with 95% efficacy but are more effective at catching smaller particles like COVID-19 (0.1 microns) due to Brownian motion, as they attach to larger droplets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:57">[00:11:57]</a>.
*   **Exhaled Air**: If a user with COVID-19 wears the device, exhaled air is blown outwards into space, rather than being contained by a mask <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:13">[00:13:13]</a>. [[dyson_brand_and_product_evolution | Dyson]]'s response that there's room to wear a mask underneath negates the product's purpose, as an N95 mask is more effective at filtering and sealing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:38">[00:13:38]</a>.
*   **Health Risks**:
    *   Blowing air directly onto mucous membranes (nose and mouth) can dry them out, causing cracks that create openings for pathogens <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:04">[00:14:04]</a>.
    *   Cold air blowing on mucous membranes can cause vasoconstriction, reducing blood flow and white blood cells, thereby decreasing protection <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:18">[00:14:18]</a>.
    *   The noise-canceling headphones in urban environments, where unintentional accidents are a higher cause of death (47 per 100,000) than chronic respiratory disease (35 per 100,000), could increase the risk of such accidents by dampening ambient sound <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:51">[00:14:51]</a>.

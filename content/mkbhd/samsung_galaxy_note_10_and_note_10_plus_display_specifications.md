## Design and Visuals
The new design of both phones is characterized by a "boxy" look <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:35">[01:12:35]</a>. The display extends close to the edges, with minimal top or bottom bezels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:35">[01:16:35]</a>. Both sides of the screen feature a curve, creating an immersive, almost entirely-screen feel <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:95">[01:20:95]</a>. The corners are minimally rounded, maintaining the immersive quality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:28:95">[01:28:95]</a>.

## Common Display Features

### Selfie Camera Cutout
Both models feature an "Infinity-O" cutout for the selfie camera, positioned in the middle of the top of the phone <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:54:35">[01:54:35]</a>. This placement is smaller than the corner cutout seen on the [[samsung_galaxy_s10_models_overview | Galaxy S10]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:04:95">[02:04:95]</a>. Samsung does not attempt to hide this cutout with gradient wallpapers; instead, they embrace it with bright default wallpapers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:11:55">[02:11:55]</a>.

### Fingerprint Reader
The fingerprint reader is integrated underneath the display glass, aligning with the [[samsung_galaxy_s10_models_overview | Galaxy S10]] series <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:43:95">[02:43:95]</a>.

### Refresh Rate
Both models utilize a 60Hz OLED display <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:23:75">[09:23:75]</a>. While the screens are considered "one of the best screens in any phone," the absence of a higher refresh rate (like 90Hz or 120Hz) might be a perceived limitation compared to newer phones like the OnePlus 7 Pro or ROG Phone <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:25:25">[09:25:25]</a>.

### Software Optimizations
The One UI on Samsung phones is designed to improve one-handed use on larger screens, which benefits both Note 10 models <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:28:45">[06:28:45]</a>.

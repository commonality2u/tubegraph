## Evolution of Standup and the Internet

In the past, comedians often aimed for mainstream success through avenues like "The Tonight Show" or sitcom deals, which could lead to fame and financial stability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:02:01">[02:02:01]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:33:00">[02:33:00]</a>. The internet, however, has transformed the landscape for [[Comedy and Standup]], creating new opportunities beyond traditional gatekeepers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:17:01">[03:17:01]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:32:03">[03:32:03]</a>.

The rise of podcasts, for example, has allowed comedians to establish their own platforms and communities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:19:11">[03:19:11]</a>. This shift empowers comedians to express themselves without the restrictions of traditional networks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:19:00">[03:19:00]</a>. It has fostered an environment where comedians are seen as assets rather than just competitors, leading to a more collaborative ecosystem <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:41:00">[03:41:00]</a>.

## Impact of Social Media

[[Impact of Social Media on StandUp Comedy Careers|Social media]] has significantly altered how comedians interact with their audience and how their words are perceived. While it provides a direct channel for connection, it can also lead to misinterpretations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:51:15">[02:51:15]</a>. Online interactions can sometimes feel hostile and "not normal" due to the sheer volume of people engaged, contrasting with the smaller, more intimate social circles humans are naturally designed for <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:16">[00:35:16]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:23:51">[02:23:51]</a>.

Comedians like Louis C.K. have noted that when "talk" is written down (e.g., on social media), it can seem more official and weighty than spoken words, impacting how jokes are received <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:47:36">[02:47:36]</a>. This environment means that jokes, intended as temporary insanity or a funny knee-jerk reaction, can be misconstrued as serious statements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:53:00">[02:53:00]</a>.

## Challenges and Controversies

The unfiltered nature of online platforms can bring [[Controversies and dynamics in standup comedy]]. Comedians face the challenge of expressing themselves freely while navigating potential backlash, as seen in instances where comedians are "canceled" or banned from venues for perceived transgressions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:26:00">[02:26:00]</a>. This can be particularly true if their humor challenges societal norms or widely held beliefs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:50:00">[02:50:00]</a>.

The discussion around [[Cancel culture and its impact on comedy]] highlights a tension between artistic freedom and accountability for potentially offensive content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:51:15">[02:51:15]</a>. However, some argue that comedy thrives when it's "dangerous" and pushes boundaries, providing a space for thoughts that might otherwise be unsaid <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:22:20">[03:22:20]</a>.

## Key Figures and Their Influence

### Mitsy Shore (The Comedy Store)
Mitsy Shore, owner of The Comedy Store, was a pivotal figure in the development of standup <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:27:32">[02:27:32]</a>. She fostered an environment where comedians could freely express themselves, creating a "home" and an "Island of Misfit Toys" for diverse and often unconventional talent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:29:21">[02:29:21]</a>. Under her guidance, the club became a breeding ground for legends such as Bill Hicks, Eddie Griffin, Damon Wayans, Martin Lawrence, and Dice Clay <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:50:00">[02:50:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:50:00">[02:50:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:50:00">[02:50:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:50:00">[02:50:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:50:00">[02:50:00]</a>.

### Sam Kinison
Sam Kinison is recognized as a pioneer who ignited a completely different style of comedy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:55:42">[02:55:42]</a>. Known for his screaming, passionate, and often shocking delivery, Kinison's act, which sometimes drew on his background as a preacher, was revolutionary <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:55:42">[02:55:42]</a>. His unique approach made him a favorite of many, including Chris Rock <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:58:00">[02:58:00]</a>.

### Bill Hicks
Bill Hicks, another influential comedian, was known for his dark and re-evaluative comedy, often exploring philosophical and societal themes. His performances could be polarizing, but he was highly regarded by fellow comedians for his unique perspective <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:58:00">[02:58:00]</a>.

### Patrice O'Neal
Patrice O'Neal was an acclaimed comedian known for his sharp wit and ability to deliver humor that pushed boundaries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:03:00">[03:03:00]</a>. He possessed a unique way of looking at things and was celebrated for his honesty, even if it meant being controversial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:03:00">[03:03:00]</a>.

### Joey Diaz
Joey Diaz is described as a "beast" and a "human cartoon" in comedy, known for his raw, unfiltered humor and incredibly effective timing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:44:19">[02:44:19]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:44:19">[02:44:19]</a>.

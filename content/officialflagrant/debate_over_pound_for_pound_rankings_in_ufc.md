## Islam Makhachev vs. Alexander Volkanovski: Allegations and Impact

*   **Nurse Rumors**: There were circulating rumors that the woman who administered the IV had spoken to people, claiming she performed the procedure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:02">[01:08:02]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:13">[01:12:13]</a>.
*   **UFC Rules on IVs**: The UFC prohibits rehydration with an IV, considering it a performance-enhancing method due to its ability to rapidly restore depleted body fluids, impacting cardio and chin strength <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:00">[01:07:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:49">[01:09:49]</a>.

## The Nuance of Pound-for-Pound Rankings

The outcome of the Makhachev-Volkanovski fight also intensified the ongoing debate about what truly defines a "pound-for-pound" champion.

*   **UFC's Stance**: The UFC attempted to label Islam Makhachev as the "pound for pound number one" after his victory <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:44">[01:14:44]</a>.
*   **Critique of P4P Claim**: There's an argument that a fighter does not become P4P number one by defeating a smaller opponent. True P4P status is often seen as being earned by moving *up* a weight class and achieving dominance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:50">[01:15:50]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:09">[01:16:09]</a>.
*   **Volkanovski's P4P Solidification**: Despite the loss, Volkanovski's performance in a higher weight class, where he faced a larger opponent and pushed him to the limit, solidified his status as P4P <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:20">[01:18:20]</a>.
*   **Definition of P4P**: P4P is described as an "opinion" that favors smaller fighters, aiming to determine "the best person if we look at all fighters almost equally" regardless of weight <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:28">[01:20:28]</a>. Success in moving up weight classes "proves your dominance" but is not a strict requirement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:09">[01:20:09]</a>.
*   **Origins of P4P**: While the UFC now has an official P4P ranking, the concept is believed to have originated from "barbershop type [discussions]" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:41">[01:20:41]</a>.

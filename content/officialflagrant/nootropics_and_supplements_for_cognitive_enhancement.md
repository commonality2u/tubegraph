## Pharmaceutical Nootropics and Stimulants

Certain prescription drugs, while effective for focus, carry significant risks:

*   **Adderall, Vyvanse, Ritalin:** These are described as "prescription cocaine" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:24">[00:52:24]</a> and are very addictive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:39">[00:53:39]</a> due to their ability to create a "narrow tunnel of focus" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:40">[00:53:40]</a>. They lead to huge increases in dopamine, which can make other activities feel "weak in comparison" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:32">[00:51:32]</a>.
    *   **Risks:** [[addiction_and_sobriety_challenges | Addiction]] is a major concern <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:58">[00:53:58]</a>, defined as a "progressive narrowing of the things that bring you pleasure" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:16">[00:54:16]</a>. They can be detrimental to heart health <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:14">[00:55:14]</a> and may cause temporary vasoconstriction, affecting the penis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:34">[00:55:34]</a>.
    *   **Prevalence:** Over 80% of college students are estimated to take Adderall, Vyvanse, or Ritalin at some point <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:00">[00:55:00]</a>.
*   **Modafinil/Armodafinil:** Developed for narcolepsy, this drug is effective but very expensive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:38">[00:52:38]</a>. An personal account described intense focus but also a "brutal headache" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:02">[00:56:02]</a>.

## Over-the-Counter Nootropics and Supplements

Compared to prescription drugs, these are generally considered safer alternatives:

*   **Alpha GPC:** This supplement is recommended as a safer alternative to pharmaceutical stimulants <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:45">[00:52:45]</a>. It stimulates nicotine release, enabling "laser-like" focus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:16">[00:53:16]</a>.
*   **Nicotine:** Described as an "amazing nootropic" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:02">[00:53:02]</a> that focuses the brain incredibly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:30">[00:53:30]</a>. It also has neuroprotective effects against Parkinson's <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:11">[00:56:11]</a>. However, smoking, vaping, dipping, and snuffing as delivery methods are not recommended due to other health risks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:05">[00:53:05]</a>. Nicorette gum or Zen pouches are mentioned as safer ways to use nicotine <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:08">[00:53:08]</a>.
*   **Caffeine:** Considered "amazing" when used healthily <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:05">[00:56:05]</a>. To avoid an afternoon crash, it's advised to delay caffeine intake for 1.5 to 2 hours after waking up <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:04">[00:59:04]</a>. Theanine is sometimes added to energy drinks to mitigate jitters, allowing for more consumption <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:33">[00:56:33]</a>.
*   **Sleep Supplements:** Magnesium, theanine, apigenin, and inositol are mentioned as working well for sleep <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:48">[00:56:48]</a>. Prioritizing these over melatonin or Ambien is suggested <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:53">[00:56:53]</a>.

## Lifestyle Factors for Cognitive Enhancement

Beyond specific substances, several fundamental practices contribute to overall well-being and cognitive function:

*   **Sunlight Exposure:** Getting sunlight in the eyes in the morning, especially on cloudy days, helps wake you up, improves mood, boosts hormone output (including testosterone), enhances focus, and sets the body's internal clock for better sleep at night <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:53">[00:43:53]</a>. It should be direct light, not through windows <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:05">[00:43:05]</a>.
*   **Quality Sleep:** Aim for enough sleep to feel rested, 80% of nights <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:17">[00:48:17]</a>. Naps of no longer than 90 minutes can be beneficial if they don't interfere with nighttime sleep <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:41">[00:48:41]</a>. Alcohol severely disrupts sleep quality, making it less restorative <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:21">[00:49:21]</a>.
*   **Regular Exercise:**
    *   **Weight Training:** Three days a week, 10-minute warm-up, 50-60 minutes of workout <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:03:51">[02:03:51]</a>.
    *   **Cardiovascular Work:** Three days a week, including one long, slow session (jog, bike, swim) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:04:36">[02:04:36]</a>, one sprint session (5-10 repetitions of 30-45 second sprints with 1-1.5 minutes walking recovery) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:04:55">[02:04:55]</a>, and one fun activity (e.g., basketball, Pilates) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:05:10">[02:05:10]</a>.
*   **Stress Control:** A physiological sigh (double inhale through the nose, long exhale through the mouth) is a rapid way to calm down <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:06:07">[02:06:07]</a>. This technique can also be used to delay orgasm <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:06:28">[02:06:28]</a>.
*   **Nose Breathing:** Practicing nasal breathing, even sleeping with tape on the mouth, is beneficial for CO2 regulation, reducing anxiety, and improving facial structure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:09:59">[02:09:59]</a>. Chewing hard foods also supports proper jaw and teeth development <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:49:50">[01:49:50]</a>.
*   **Cold Exposure:** Three to five days a week of uncomfortable cold exposure (cold showers, ice baths) can build mental resilience and provide a lasting surge in dopamine, improving mood and positivity for hours <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:10:11">[02:10:11]</a>.
*   **Healthy Relationships:** Avoiding toxic relationships is crucial for overall well-being, as they can "ruin people's lives" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:11:54">[02:11:54]</a>.

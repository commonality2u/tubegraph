## Influence of Billionaires

The issue of wealthy individuals using their financial power to sway political outcomes is highlighted as a major concern <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:34">[00:01:34]</a>.

### Origins of Anti-Billionaire Stance
Senator Bernie Sanders recounts how his strong stance against billionaires began in 1958, when the Brooklyn Dodgers baseball team was moved to Los Angeles for reasons of "corporate greed" and profit, despite the team's deep community ties <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:16">[00:01:16]</a>. This event, driven by private ownership and the pursuit of greater profit, was "beyond comprehension" for a young Sanders and significantly impacted his political views <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:16">[00:02:16]</a>.

### Professional Sports and Public Funding
The problem extends to modern professional sports, where teams owned by billionaires move locations if cities do not offer enough money for stadium builds, breaking the hearts of fans with no concern <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:33">[00:03:33]</a>. Cities often provide "huge sums of money, hundreds of millions" or even "billions of dollars" for stadiums, yet do not receive any equity in the team <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:43">[00:03:43]</a>, allowing owners to "make a fortune" and then move elsewhere years later <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:57">[00:04:57]</a>. The jobs created are often part-time and low-paying <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:10">[00:05:10]</a>, while ticket and concession prices make games unaffordable for many working families <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:24">[00:05:24]</a>.

### Extreme Wealth Inequality
The current distribution of wealth is severely skewed. For example, [[the_influence_of_billionaires_in_politics | Elon Musk]] alone owns more wealth than the bottom 52% of American households combined <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:51">[00:16:51]</a>. This extreme concentration of wealth, where a "new breed of uber capitalists" believe they are "superior human beings" with the "right to rule," undermines democracy itself <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:30">[00:17:30]</a>.

## Campaign Finance System
The system is criticized as "broken and corrupt" due to key legal decisions and funding mechanisms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:28">[00:20:28]</a>.

### [[influence_of_major_technology_companies_and_politics | Citizens United]] and Super PACs
The Supreme Court's 2010 [[influence_of_major_technology_companies_and_politics | Citizens United]] decision equated advertising with freedom of speech, allowing billionaires to spend "unlimited sums of money" through Super PACs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:47">[00:21:47]</a>. This means a single billionaire can contribute vast amounts, as when [[influence_of_major_technology_companies_and_politics | Elon Musk]] reportedly spent "$270 million to help Donald Trump get elected president" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:37">[00:22:37]</a>. Both Democratic and Republican parties receive such funding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:44">[00:22:44]</a>.

### Impact on Politicians
This financial leverage makes it "very hard for any Republican to sustain to stand up and and authentically speak their mind" against the party line <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:46">[00:23:46]</a>. Similarly, Democrats who challenge powerful lobbies like APEC (which is "funded by billionaires") face significant opposition and are often targeted with negative advertising <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:20">[00:27:20]</a>. These campaigns often avoid discussing the real issues, instead making the person "radioactive" with "usual negative advertising" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:39">[00:27:39]</a>, which is described as "duplicitous" and undermining the democratic process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:31">[00:28:31]</a>.

## The Role of Lobbying
Lobbying plays a crucial role in shaping legislation by leveraging money, connections, and expertise <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:25">[01:17:25]</a>.

### How Lobbying Works
Lobbyists, often "former leaders of the Republican and Democratic party," represent corporations and industries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:15">[01:17:15]</a>. They meet with elected officials and their staff, presenting their "needs" and even offering to "write legislation" because they "know how busy you are" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:31">[01:16:31]</a>. This influence is reinforced by campaign fundraising efforts and the ability to contribute to Super PACs, creating a powerful network of money, connections, and specialized legislative drafting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:50">[01:16:50]</a>.

For instance, the pharmaceutical industry employs around 1,500 lobbyists in Washington D.C., a significant number compared to the 535 members of Congress <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:30">[01:14:30]</a>. These lobbyists, combined with campaign contributions, allow industries to charge exorbitant prices for drugs in the US compared to other countries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:46">[01:14:46]</a>.

## Impact on Policy and Society

### Disconnect from Working People
The Democratic Party is criticized for being "funded by wealthy people" and relying on consultants "way out of touch with reality," leading to the working class being "ignored" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:26">[00:40:26]</a>. This detachment means that Democratic politicians often fail to address the real concerns of average citizens, leading to a perception that the "status quo is working" when it is not <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:59">[00:36:59]</a>.

### Key Policy Areas Affected
*   **Healthcare**: The US healthcare system, which does not guarantee healthcare as a human right, spends "twice as much per person" as European countries that do <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:06">[01:10:06]</a>. This system is "designed to make... drug companies [and] insurance companies rich" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:27">[01:10:27]</a>, with insurance companies making "$70 billion last year" and drug companies making "$100 billion" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:18">[01:10:18]</a>.
*   **Education and Childcare**: Despite the importance of early childhood development, childcare professionals receive "McDonald's wages" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:07">[01:13:07]</a>, and starting teachers earn as little as $32,000 annually <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:12">[01:14:12]</a>. The high cost of college, where students can graduate "half a million dollars in debt," discourages the "best and brightest" from entering fields like education <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:17">[01:14:17]</a>.
*   **Taxation**: Instead of funding essential services, the current system allows for "tax breaks to the rich" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:03">[01:07:03]</a>. For example, a "reconciliation bill" in the House would provide "$235 billion over a 10-year period to go to the top 2/10 of 1%" through increased inheritance tax exemptions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:11">[01:06:11]</a>.

The prevailing culture often rewards financial gain over societal utility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:05">[00:55:05]</a>. This contrasts with valuing professions like teaching or nursing, which contribute significantly to human well-being <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:00">[00:53:00]</a>.

## Solutions and Resistance
Addressing these issues requires fundamental change and increased civic engagement.

### Overturning Citizens United
A crucial step is to "end [[us_political_dynamics_and_influence_of_media | Citizens United]]" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:33">[00:29:33]</a>. While this won't happen overnight, political parties could implement rules preventing candidates from accepting Super PAC money, or offsetting such contributions with public funding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:50">[00:29:50]</a>.

### Promoting Honesty and Courage
Politicians should be "honest" and "courageous" by directly confronting issues and not fearing financial retribution from special interest groups <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:37">[00:32:37]</a>. For example, politicians who take an unpopular stance, like opposing military aid to a government, should make it a "major campaign issue" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:41">[00:34:41]</a> rather than hiding it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:41">[00:34:41]</a>.

### Grassroots Movements
Movements like Sanders' aim to open the door of the Democratic Party to a broader base, including young people and working-class individuals, by training and supporting "progressive candidates" who refuse corporate PAC money <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:26">[00:46:26]</a>. Many interested candidates prefer to run as independents <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:49">[00:44:49]</a>.

### Investing in Societal Needs
Redirecting national resources from tax breaks for the wealthy and excessive military spending <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:01">[01:07:01]</a> towards essential services is critical <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:40">[01:05:40]</a>. This includes making education from childcare to graduate school tuition-free <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:40">[01:04:40]</a>, providing universal healthcare <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:25">[01:07:25]</a>, and fostering an environment where professions with "societal utility" are rewarded <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:35">[00:52:35]</a>.

### Overcoming Perceived Powerlessness
The "oligarchs and the ruling class want you to believe that you're powerless" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:14">[01:13:14]</a>. However, people have power through collective action, and transforming the country requires increased participation and engagement in political processes beyond voting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:23:03">[01:23:03]</a>.

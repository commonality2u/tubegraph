## The Craft of Comedy
### Roasts and Their Dynamics
Roasts are a significant part of [[comedy_and_standup | comedian]] culture, often involving a specific set of rules, including "off-limits" topics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:29">[01:08:29]</a>. For example, for William Shatner, the off-limits topic was his wife's death in a pool <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:32">[01:08:32]</a>. For Pam Anderson, it was a child drowning at one of her parties <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:42">[01:08:42]</a>, and for Joan Rivers, it was her daughter Melissa <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:51">[01:08:51]</a>.

When Donald Trump was roasted, his off-limits topic was surprisingly Melania, not Ivanka <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:08">[01:09:08]</a>. Despite jokes about his hair, business failures, and even his "dick," Trump was reportedly "dying laughing the whole time" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:34">[01:09:34]</a>. After the performance, he pulled the comedian close, put a hand on their lower back, and remarked, "Great television," indicating he saw it as entertainment rather than personal attack <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:41">[01:09:41]</a>.

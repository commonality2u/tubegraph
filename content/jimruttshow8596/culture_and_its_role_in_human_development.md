## Defining Culture
Culture is defined as the application of refined ideas to circumstances for which they are adapted <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:54">[00:55:54]</a>. These ideas, when successful, become "driven into a more automatic, less deliberative layer" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:50">[00:55:50]</a>. This means that while innovation often occurs in conscious minds, once something is mastered and doesn't require active thought, it has moved into the cultural layer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:49">[00:56:49]</a>. Therefore, culture acts as a "compiled version of a program" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:19">[00:58:19]</a>, being inherently backward-looking as it reflects solutions to past challenges <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:27">[00:58:27]</a>.

## The [[the_omega_principle_genes_and_culture | Omega Principle]]: Genes and Culture
The [[the_omega_principle_genes_and_culture | Omega Principle]] describes a necessary relationship between genes and culture <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:47">[01:16:47]</a>. Culture, as an epigenetic regulator, is considered "superior to genes" in its flexibility and ability to adapt more quickly, with [[social_and_cultural_change | cultural change]] happening within generations rather than just between them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:01">[01:18:01]</a>. However, these epigenetic regulators "evolve to serve the genome" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:18">[01:18:18]</a>. This means that anything cultural that has persisted and is complex and variable in extent is considered evolutionary and ultimately serves genetic interests <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:01">[01:19:01]</a>.

## Culture in a [[impact_of_human_nature_and_cultural_practices_on_societal_design | Hyper-Novel World]]
The rapidly accelerating pace of change in the 21st century creates a [[impact_of_human_nature_and_cultural_practices_on_societal_design | hyper-novel world]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:17">[01:11:17]</a>, where the rate of change itself is changing exponentially <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:47">[01:11:47]</a>. This poses a significant problem for culture, which is inherently backward-looking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:27">[00:58:27]</a>. As a result, there will be "ever less from the past that the young will find a value as they move forward into the future" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:14">[00:59:14]</a>.

Culture provides stability and a through-line from the past, and elements that worked in unchanging environments should be kept <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:23">[00:59:23]</a>. However, elements that worked in past domains but are now outdated due to changes in the modern world should be discarded <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:43">[00:59:43]</a>. The challenge lies in discerning which is which, a process prone to error <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:48">[00:59:48]</a>.

### The "Suckers' Fallacy"
A common mechanism observed in human society, which can be catastrophic when widely applied, is the "suckers' fallacy" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:07">[01:00:07]</a>. This is the tendency for concentrated short-term benefits to "obscure risk and long-term costs" and drive acceptance even when the net analysis is negative <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:09">[01:00:09]</a>. This short-term "hill climbing" dominates the [[evolutionary_and_cultural_dynamics | evolution]] of culture <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:40">[01:00:40]</a>.

When the rate of change is slow, short-term benefits might still lead to stable outcomes over generations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:48">[01:00:48]</a>. However, in a [[impact_of_human_nature_and_cultural_practices_on_societal_design | hyper-novel world]], relying on short-term interests becomes problematic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:20">[01:01:20]</a>. Human brains did not evolve to deal with slow-moving, long-term problems like climate change <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:38">[01:01:38]</a>; their primary evolutionary constraint was immediate survival <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:31">[01:01:31]</a>.

### Historically Adaptive but Harmful Cultures
While the [[the_omega_principle_genes_and_culture | Omega Principle]] suggests that persistent cultural elements are adaptive, this does not imply they are inherently good or honorable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:33">[01:20:33]</a>. Practices like war, slavery, and rape, though historically ubiquitous and adaptive, are recognized as "terrible blights on human history" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:10">[01:20:10]</a>. The [[the_role_of_ethics_and_values_in_civilization_design | naturalistic fallacy]] — that what `is` ought to `be` — is rejected <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:52">[01:20:52]</a>.

The hopeful message is that while some aspects of human nature or history may seem immutable, human behavior exhibits tremendous plasticity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:58">[01:20:58]</a>. Humans are capable of changing what has been adaptive and can strive to be better going forward <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:21:38">[01:21:38]</a>.

## The Fourth Frontier: Proactive Cultural Design
Given the challenges of population growth, [[social_and_cultural_change | climate change]], and global instability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:23:34">[01:23:34]</a>, a new approach is needed, termed the "fourth frontier" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:26:29">[01:26:29]</a>. Unlike geographic or technological frontiers, or even resource transfer (theft) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:26:02">[01:26:02]</a>, this new frontier involves proactively understanding humanity and navigating societal complexities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:26:37">[01:26:37]</a>.

The goal is to move towards a future where humans can live "beautifully, productively, sustainably" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:27:00">[01:27:00]</a>, allowing individuals to discover and offer their unique contributions to the world with minimal reliance on luck <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:27:32">[01:27:32]</a>. This requires taking ownership of culture, moving from a backward-looking, "compiler-oriented" style to a proactive, forward-looking form <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:50">[01:22:50]</a> that can adapt to the [[impact_of_human_nature_and_cultural_practices_on_societal_design | hyper-novel]] environment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:58">[01:22:58]</a>.

These practices aim to address perennial human susceptibilities to self-deceptive and self-destructive behavior, which arise from the complex, dynamical, self-organizing, recursive, embodied, and enacted nature of human cognition <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:20:00">[09:20:00]</a>.

Historically, the only systems that have reliably "homed" [[role_of_ecological_practices_in_managing_complex_mental_processes | ecologies of practices]] and facilitated significant individual, community, and cultural transformation have been religions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:55:00">[03:55:00]</a>. However, many "legacy religions" born in the Axial Age are no longer viable for numerous individuals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:06:00">[04:06:00]</a>. This is largely due to their "Two Worlds mythology"—the idea of an illusory everyday world and a separate "real" world to which one transcends <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:28:00">[04:28:00]</a>, which is difficult to reconcile with a modern scientific worldview <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:47:00">[04:47:00]</a>.

The concept of a "religion that is not a religion" seeks to extract the functionality of traditional religions—providing a home for [[role_of_ecological_practices_in_managing_complex_mental_processes | ecologies of practices]] and fostering transformation—without adopting their worldview or historical problems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:48:00">[04:48:00]</a>. This alternative is explicitly non-supernatural <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:50:00">[02:50:00]</a>.

Many describe themselves as "spiritual but not religious," seeking wisdom through autodidactic means, which carries inherent risks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:48:00">[10:48:00]</a>. ## Design Principles and Characteristics

[[role_of_ecological_practices_in_managing_complex_mental_processes | Ecologies of practices]] are designed to be:
*   **Complex and Dynamic**: They are not "one-shot interventions" but rather complex, sophisticated, and dynamic systems capable of reconfiguring themselves to ameliorate self-deception <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:21:19">[01:21:19]</a>.
*   **Self-organizing and Self-correcting**: Like natural ecologies, they possess checks and balances and levels of organization that allow for self-sustaining and self-regulating processes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:33">[01:22:33]</a>.
*   **Opponent Processing**: A key feature is the inclusion of practices that work in opposition to each other, like the interaction between task-focused networks and the default mode network in the brain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:10">[01:20:10]</a>. This interaction introduces variation and selection, optimizing for insight <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:26:36">[01:26:36]</a>.
*   **Layering**: Practices can be layered, meaning an opponent processing system within one practice can be put into an opponent relationship with another, such as pairing still mindfulness with moving mindfulness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:26:50">[01:26:50]</a>.
*   **Pedagogical Programs**: There must be a clear "order of operations" for onboarding people into such complex systems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:27:17">[01:27:17]</a>.

### Examples of Practices

*   **Mindfulness Practices**: These involve broadly meditative practices (stepping back to observe mental frames for distortion) and contemplative practices (exploring new frames) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:25:09">[01:25:09]</a>. They act in an opponent fashion to make one "insight-prone" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:26:27">[01:26:27]</a>.
*   **Active Open-mindedness**: Practices that dampen the "insight machinery" to allow for more careful inferential processes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:27:50">[01:27:50]</a>.
*   **Ritual Enactment**: [[the_role_of_rituals_and_community_practices_in_meaningmaking | Rituals]] are understood as the "imaginal" (imagination for the sake of perception) in service of the "aspirational" (the relationship between current and future self) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:31:37">[01:31:37]</a>. A good ritual transfers broadly and deeply, affording profound reciprocal opening with reality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:33:29">[01:33:29]</a>. Examples include Tai Chi Chuan, which fosters fluid cognition and adaptation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:34:45">[01:34:45]</a>.
*   **Community Practices**: Singing and dancing together are seen as "folk psycho-technologies" that foster a sense of something "more than just the aggregate sum of all of the individuals" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:28:21">[01:28:21]</a>.
*   **Technologies of Sovereignty**: Practices that help individuals live well in relationship with themselves and their experiences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:36:59">[01:36:59]</a>.
*   **"We" Practices**: Practices for integrating people into a collective "we" while maintaining individual specificity and fostering generative relationships <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:38:15">[01:38:15]</a>.
*   **Reintegrating Human Potential with Nature**: Practices for re-weaving human capacity and technology into a symmetrical relationship with nature, restoring "Homo sapiens" as the wise hominid <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:39:51">[01:39:51]</a>.
*   **Life-Event Rituals**: Rituals for significant life events like birth and death, which are inherently "sacred" (both important and dangerous) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:41:00">[01:41:00]</a>.
*   **Synergistic Satisfiers**: Practices that simultaneously fulfill multiple important functions, such as breastfeeding, which has "13,000 distinct synergistic satisfiers" for both infant and mother <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:43:42">[01:43:42]</a>.

## Scaling and Propagation

Scaling [[role_of_ecological_practices_in_managing_complex_mental_processes | ecologies of practices]] requires a shift from propagating "content" or "propositional knowledge"—which characterized the Axial Age religions and led to their dilution and failure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:54:50">[01:54:50]</a>—to fostering a culture designed for positive returns to scale, like Metcalfe's Law <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:56:05">[01:56:05]</a>.

### Holographic Scaling
Instead of a "photographic" or centralized approach (like the old religions), the scaling model is "holographic" and distributed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:00:28">[02:00:28]</a>. This means:
*   **Context over Content**: Emphasis on the *context* that enables practices, not just the explicit *content* of beliefs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:00:19">[02:00:19]</a>.
*   **Distributed Empowerment**: Individuals are empowered to become sovereign and share their learnings and wisdom with others who are receptive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:01:40">[02:01:40]</a>.
*   **Focus on Meaningful Relationships**: The system is built on the strongest possible relationship lines (e.g., family, close friends), as these are the "most sacred" and capable of the "most meaningfulness" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:58:37">[01:58:37]</a>. When people successfully cultivate these relationships, they have an inherent incentive to propagate the practices to those they care about, creating a positive feedback loop <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:04:47">[02:04:47]</a>.
*   **Leveraging Cataclysm**: The current "meta-crisis"—the breakdown of old institutions and the perceived absurdity of the secular world—creates a pressing need and a "wind at our backs" for new solutions to emerge and be adopted <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:02:00">[02:02:00]</a>.
*   **Pedagogical Continuity**: This involves creating a sequence of experiences and resources, from video games for young people to advanced workshops, that provide a continuous pathway into the [[role_of_ecological_practices_in_managing_complex_mental_processes | ecologies of practices]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:50:00">[01:50:00]</a>. This allows for different levels of engagement, analogous to how Christianity accommodated both basic Sunday school and theological complexity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:51:53">[01:51:53]</a>.

Essentially, by focusing on practices that genuinely upgrade individual and group capacity to navigate life's challenges, these [[role_of_ecological_practices_in_managing_complex_mental_processes | ecologies of practices]] can organically propagate through the deepest and most meaningful human connections, fostering a distributed, self-sustaining movement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:57:51">[01:57:51]</a>.

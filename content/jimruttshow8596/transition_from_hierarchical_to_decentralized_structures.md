## The "Band Level" (Pre-Game A Society)

Approximately 75,000 years ago, a significant transition occurred with the emergence of Homo sapiens sapiens, leading to what is called the "upper paleolithic transition" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:01:00">[10:01:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:03:00">[10:03:00]</a>. This era saw the perfection of a [[Decentralized systems versus centralized systems in crisis management | collective intelligence toolkit]] centered on "tribal coherence," enabling small, tightly-bound groups (hunter-gatherer bands) to work together effectively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:16:00">[10:16:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:47:00">[10:47:00]</a>. These groups were typically limited to around 150 individuals, a concept known as the [[Limitations and scalability of group governance models | Dunbar number]], beyond which they tended to fission into smaller units <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:06:00">[12:06:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:40:00">[12:40:00]</a>.

A key characteristic of these band-level societies was their strong adaptation against [[Hierarchical Complexity | hierarchy]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:33:00">[20:33:00]</a>. Unlike chimpanzees, which exhibit rigid hierarchies, early human bands developed an "allergy to bosses" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:03:00">[21:03:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:07:00">[21:07:00]</a>. This was possibly enabled by the development of weaponry, allowing individuals to collectively resist any attempts at dominance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:15:00">[21:15:00]</a>. They were exceptionally effective at policing [[Power dynamics and the structure of governance | defection]] (e.g., free riders or hierarchical abuses) within the group, maintaining a robust "collective intelligence" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:11:00">[22:11:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:20:00">[22:20:00]</a>.

## The Emergence of "Game A" (Hierarchical Society)

The pressure for a new societal structure emerged as human populations increased and available land became scarce, leading to competition primarily among human groups rather than solely with nature <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:35:00">[13:35:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:40:00">[13:40:00]</a>. This led to the "neolithic transition," marked by the development of agriculture and larger, more sedentary settlements around 10,000 years ago <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:32:00">[13:32:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:00:00">[17:00:00]</a>.

This new collective intelligence toolkit, termed "Game A," addressed three primary problems:
1.  **Survival in nature**: Extracting resources for the group's well-being <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:20:00">[15:20:00]</a>.
2.  **Competition with other human groups**: Enabling victory over rivals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:35:00">[15:35:00]</a>.
3.  **Internal defection**: Managing large populations beyond the Dunbar number <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:47:00">[15:47:00]</a>.

Game A introduced formal societal structures and specialized roles (e.g., priest or king) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:01:00">[26:01:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:05:00">[27:05:00]</a>. This transition overcame the anti-hierarchical ethos of earlier bands by establishing formal authority, often backed by a loyal military caste <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:32:00">[21:32:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:37:00">[21:37:00]</a>. In Game A, society is seen as a "complicated" system, meaning it is finite, bounded, and in principle, can be fully mapped and understood (like a Boeing 777) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:05:00">[32:05:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:26:00">[32:26:00]</a>. Individuals within Game A adopt formal "identities" that correspond to these roles, distinct from their complex individual uniqueness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:04:00">[29:04:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:38:00">[31:38:00]</a>.

## The Inherent Flaws of Game A (Why Hierarchy Collapses)

Despite its success in scaling human cooperation, Game A fundamentally faces three recurring failure conditions:
1.  **Internal Defection and Corruption**: Game A structures are "fundamentally vulnerable to defection" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:27:00">[25:27:00]</a>. As societies grow, the complex reality of human behavior inevitably finds ways to game the complicated system. This leads to a breakdown of "collective sense-making" and "collaborative sense-making," as individuals or groups prioritize self-interest over the common good <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:06:00">[36:06:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="1:02:09">[1:02:09]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="1:02:09">[1:02:09]</a>. Examples include the rise of corrupt political factions (e.g., late Roman Republic) or the normalization of dishonesty in public discourse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:50:00">[37:50:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="1:03:09">[1:03:09]</a>.
2.  **Over-complication and Resource Addiction**: Game A societies become "addicted to certain tools" and technologies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="45:27:00">[45:27:00]</a>. As easier resources are depleted, maintaining the same level of supply requires increasingly complex and fragile infrastructure (e.g., deepwater oil drilling vs. early Pennsylvania wells) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="43:57:00">[43:57:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="46:51:00">[46:51:00]</a>. This growing "carrying cost" drains resources and leads to an "s-curve" of diminishing returns on innovation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="44:06:00">[44:06:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="47:10:00">[47:10:00]</a>.
3.  **External Competition**: Societies must contend with external groups who can learn from and steal their technologies, potentially out-competing them, especially if the incumbent society is already weakened by internal corruption and over-complication <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="49:29:00">[49:29:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="50:25:00">[50:25:00]</a>.

Historically, Game A societies have evolved through cycles of collapse and reboot. Each collapse allows for innovations in the "information domain" to be preserved and integrated into a new, more flexible social infrastructure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="51:06:00">[51:06:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="52:00:00">[52:00:00]</a>. However, the current globalized and technologically advanced iteration of Game A is unique, as a collapse could have catastrophic, worldwide consequences due to widespread interdependencies and the destructive power of modern technology <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="56:02:00">[56:02:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="56:31:00">[56:31:00]</a>.

## "Game B" (The Proposed Decentralized Alternative)

Given the inherent limitations of Game A, a proposed alternative, "Game B," aims to foster a new form of [[Decentralized systems versus centralized systems in crisis management | collective intelligence toolkit]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="1:10:10">[1:10:10]</a>. This paradigm shift would be as significant as the transition from pre-human to band-level intelligence, and from band-level to Game A civilization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="1:10:16">[1:10:16]</a>.

Game B must overcome the "problem of complication" that plagues Game A <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="1:11:07">[1:11:07]</a>. Instead of relying on formal "society" and "identity" structures, Game B would operate more in the "complex" domain, similar to how band-level societies functioned, but scaled to include vastly more people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="1:11:18">[1:11:18]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="1:12:07">[1:12:07]</a>.

Key characteristics of Game B include:
*   **Healing and Maturation**: Addressing the psychological damage inflicted by Game A on individuals, fostering maturity and the ability to think (not just "simulated thinking") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="1:14:26">[1:14:26]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="1:13:50">[1:13:50]</a>.
*   **Sophisticated Collective Intelligence**: Utilizing new psycho-technologies to facilitate effective group collaboration, even among individuals with vastly different worldviews <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="1:15:35">[1:15:35]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="1:16:46">[1:16:46]</a>.
*   **Emphasis on Meaningfulness**: Providing a radical upgrade in the meaningfulness of lived experience, fostering better relationships and the ability to raise healthier children <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="1:28:21">[1:28:21]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="1:28:50">[1:28:50]</a>.
*   **Truth and Reality**: Basing its "metaphysics" on what is "actually real and true," offering a competitive advantage over Game A's reliance on "lying" and "propaganda" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="1:30:00">[1:30:00]</a>.
*   **Optimal Innovation**: Game B conditions are theoretically optimal for maximal innovation and creative collaboration, offering an "escape velocity in techno utopia space" without the catastrophic consequences of unregulated exponential technology <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="1:32:40">[1:32:40]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="1:32:51">[1:32:51]</a>.

## The Transition to Game B

The transition to Game B is envisioned as a process of building complete, small-scale, and scalable "instantiations" of this new society, like "Mars colonies in Highland County" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="1:23:30">[1:23:30]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="1:23:56">[1:23:56]</a>. These communities would:
*   Be self-sustaining in terms of food, energy, and life cycles (birth, education, death) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="1:24:50">[1:24:50]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="1:25:01">[1:25:01]</a>.
*   Interface with Game A with an "asymmetric advantage," generating high-value products while seeking increasing autonomy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="1:23:02">[1:23:02]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="1:23:05">[1:23:05]</a>.
*   Grow through a process of "fissioning," where mature communities bud off to form new ones, similar to how hunter-gatherer bands or Mennonite communities expand <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="1:27:27">[1:27:27]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="1:27:38">[1:27:38]</a>.

The challenge lies in managing the rate at which people can be healed, integrated, and scaled into these new coherent systems without destabilizing them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="1:24:49">[1:24:49]</a>. While the path is difficult, the potential for a radically more meaningful and sustainable human existence serves as a powerful motivator for this necessary shift <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="1:28:21">[1:28:21]</a>.

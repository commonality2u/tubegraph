The [[Game B movement and its principles]] is a discussion and exploration focused on developing an alternative to the current societal operating system, often referred to as "Game A" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:01:03">[02:01:03]</a>. It seeks to find a new path for humanity, especially given the challenges of the present era <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:21:09">[06:21:09]</a>.

This film, linked at gamebeefilm.org <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:32:04">[02:32:04]</a>, sparked commentary on the Intellectual Deep Web mailing list, which includes members who are both proponents and critics of Game B <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:12:00">[02:12:00]</a>. ## [[Game B Philosophy]]: Key Principles and Comparisons with Game A

A fundamental tenet of [[Game B Philosophy]] is its contrast with "Game A" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:21:00">[09:21:00]</a>.

### Exploitation vs. Implotation
[[Comparison of Game A and Game B]] can be distilled into two core ideas:
*   **Game A**: Based on the idea that one could infinitely exploit the world and reality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:34:00">[09:34:00]</a>. This led to significant advancements, such as modern dentistry and increased human lifespan, especially after 1700 with the convergence of limited government, science, and modern finance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:39:00">[10:39:00]</a>. However, [[Game A and its collapse]] is driven by an inner loop of short-term money-on-money return that pushes for exponential growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:22:00">[12:22:00]</a>. This is unsustainable in a finite world <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:56:00">[12:56:00]</a>.
*   **Game B**: Proposes the concept of "implotation," meaning resources cannot be used unless they are put back into the system <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:47:00">[09:47:00]</a>. It emphasizes living within balance with Mother Nature and ideally paying back ecological debt, rather than using money-on-money return as the primary metric of value <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:54:00">[28:54:00]</a>.

### The "Three-Three Transition"
A proposed goal for [[Game B as an alternative system]] is the "three-three transition" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:34:00">[31:34:00]</a>:
*   Cutting energy consumption by about two-thirds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:35:00">[30:35:00]</a>.
*   Simultaneously increasing human well-being by a factor of three <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:26:00">[31:26:00]</a>.
This can be achieved by investing in the commons and community, fostering built-in holidays, and engaging in activities like singing and exercising together to promote well-being over addiction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:42:00">[31:42:00]</a>.

## [[Challenges and benefits of transitioning from Game A to Game B]]

### Avoiding Utopianism and Dystopianism: Protopianism
A critical aspect of [[Game B Philosophy]] is to avoid utopian and dystopian thinking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:00:00">[21:00:00]</a>. Jim Rutt emphasizes that Game B is an exploration of a high-dimensional design space for living, with multiple parallel experiments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:32:00">[28:32:00]</a>.

Instead, the concept of "protopianism" is advocated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:19:00">[25:19:00]</a>. Protopianism means continuously tearing down and rebuilding the world, seeking to slightly improve its construction each day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:22:00">[25:22:00]</a>. This approach is contrasted with the static, perfect world of Plato's utopian ideal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:48:00">[24:48:00]</a>.

### Voluntary Communities and Membranes
The transition to [[Game B as an alternative system]] envisions "proto-B" communities, which are small, Dunbar number-sized or slightly larger communities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:44:00">[28:44:00]</a>. These communities are characterized by:
*   **Voluntary Participation**: Individuals must be able to freely join and leave these systems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:04:00">[33:04:00]</a>. This prevents the totalitarian outcomes seen in historical utopian experiments like those influenced by Rousseau <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:14:00">[33:14:00]</a>.
*   **Membranes and Protocols**: Communities are conceptualized as having "membranes" – semi-permeable boundaries that allow information and people to move in and out with effort <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="38:18:00">[38:18:00]</a>. Within these membranes, "protocols" or shared standards can be established, such as rules around technology use (e.g., banning smartphones for children) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="39:26:00">[39:26:00]</a>. These protocols are meant to be copied and shared, fostering communication and collaboration between different communities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="47:17:00">[47:17:00]</a>.

### Addressing Sex and Violence
When designing a [[Game B movement and its principles]], it is crucial to consider aspects like sex and violence, which are often overlooked in utopian visions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="48:26:00">[48:26:00]</a>. These are part of what is termed the "pathical narrative" – the realm of drives, desires, rivalry, and envy, which is never harmonious or balanced <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="51:38:00">[51:38:00]</a>. Unlike logos (logical narratives) and mythos (coherent stories), pathos (the story of sex, violence, and the subconscious) is never coherent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="51:28:00">[51:28:00]</a>.

Rather than attempting to ban or suppress pathos, as some dualistic philosophies have done (e.g., Christianity and Islam, which led to "pressure cookers" and eventual blow-ups), Game B must acknowledge and deal with these forces <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="52:20:00">[52:20:00]</a>. This aligns with Eastern philosophical approaches like Tantra, where pathos is recognized as part of humanity and can be engaged with if one is ready for it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="52:01:00">[52:01:00]</a>. Art is also placed in the category of pathos, as it should constantly question itself and never be propaganda <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="52:38:00">[52:38:00]</a>.

### The Role of Technology and Elites
Technologies are seen as "pharmacons"—they are neutral tools that can be constructive or destructive depending on human choices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="40:51:00">[40:51:01]</a>. The internet age, for example, is just beginning, and understanding its implications, especially the real-time global connection, is a massive undertaking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="42:30:00">[42:30:00]</a>.

Technological disruption initially leads to anarchy, then to a "plurality" where a few individuals figure out how to use the technology to their advantage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="43:40:00">[43:40:00]</a>. These "pluralists" can become leaders who implement a new order, serving as an "elite" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="44:06:00">[44:06:00]</a>. The goal is to embrace this elite quickly, as they create successful models that others can mimic, thereby propagating beneficial behaviors and systems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="44:31:00">[44:31:00]</a>.

AI is anticipated to assist in managing multiple protocols, making complex systems more feasible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="47:57:00">[47:57:00]</a>.

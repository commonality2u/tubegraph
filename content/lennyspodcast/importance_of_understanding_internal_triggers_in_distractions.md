## What are Internal Triggers?
Internal triggers are the "itches" or uncomfortable sensations that lead us to seek escape through distraction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:09">[00:41:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:00">[01:04:00]</a>. These can include:
*   Boredom <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:52">[01:11:52]</a>
*   Loneliness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:53">[01:11:53]</a>
*   Fatigue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:54">[01:11:54]</a>
*   Uncertainty <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:55">[01:11:55]</a>
*   Anxiety <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:55">[01:11:55]</a>
*   Frustration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:25">[01:19:25]</a>
*   Discomfort from the effort of deep work or difficult tasks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:27">[01:15:27]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:34">[01:16:34]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:28">[01:29:28]</a>.

The problem is not the technology itself, but our inherent inability to deal with these feelings of discomfort <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:00">[00:29:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:00">[00:31:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:50">[01:16:50]</a>.

This is because the fundamental motivation behind human behavior is the desire to escape discomfort, even the pursuit of pleasure is to escape the discomfort of wanting something good <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:37:37">[01:37:37]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:38:28">[01:38:28]</a>.

When people procrastinate, it is not a character flaw, but rather an [[measuring_selfawareness_and_introspection_in_interviews | emotion regulation]] problem <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:35:38">[01:35:38]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:46:46">[01:46:46]</a>. ## Strategies to Manage Internal Triggers
To master [[the_role_of_intent_in_managing_personal_attention_and_productivity | internal triggers]], strategies focus on reimagining the task, the trigger, and one's temperament <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:54:04">[01:54:04]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:54:08">[01:54:08]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:54:09">[01:54:09]</a>.

### 1. The 10-Minute Rule & Surfing the Urge
When tempted to get distracted, set a timer for 10 minutes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:37:36">[01:37:36]</a>. During this time, one's job is to either:
*   Get back to the task at hand <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:39:07">[01:39:07]</a>.
*   "Surf the urge" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:39:10">[01:39:10]</a>. This involves acknowledging and experiencing the uncomfortable sensation, knowing that emotions are like waves that crest and subside <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:39:13">[01:39:13]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:39:29">[01:39:29]</a>. Instead of saying "don't do it," tell yourself "not yet" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:39:51">[01:39:51]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:39:53">[01:39:53]</a>.
*   A personal mantra can help, such as "This is what it feels like to get better" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:40:41">[01:40:41]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:40:48">[01:40:48]</a>.
Often, after 10 minutes, the urge to distract subsides, and focus returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:40:59">[01:40:59]</a>. This technique proves personal agency and that distraction is not being stolen, but rather given away <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:41:34">[01:41:34]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:41:36">[01:41:36]</a>.

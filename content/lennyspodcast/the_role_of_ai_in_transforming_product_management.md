## Chat PRD: An Example of AI in Action

Claire V, a Chief Product Officer, developed Chat PRD, an [[applications_of_ai_in_enhancing_productivity_for_product_managers | AI PM specific tool]] that has gained significant popularity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:39">[00:00:39]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:10">[00:01:10]</a>.

### The Genesis of Chat PRD
The tool originated from a need to quickly spec out a highly technical product at a resource-constrained company <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:17">[00:54:17]</a>. Claire used ChatGPT and a refined prompt to generate a serviceable Product Requirements Document (PRD) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:40">[00:54:40]</a>. This prompt evolved into Chat PRD, designed to consistently output product specifications, provide feedback, and build plans <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:02">[00:55:02]</a>.

Initially a customized GPT within the GPT store, Chat PRD transitioned into a standalone application that leverages OpenAI's assistance APIs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:26">[00:55:26]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:05">[00:56:05]</a>. The standalone app offers enhanced customization, learning from each user's specific content, role, and company to provide tailored assistance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:16">[00:57:16]</a>. It can generate entire documents and iterate on them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:40">[00:57:40]</a>.

### Common Use Cases
Chat PRD is widely used for:
*   **Generating PRDs:** Approximately 60% of users input an idea and receive a full PRD, including objectives, user goals, user stories, scope, UX walkthroughs, product narratives, sequencing, milestones, and measurements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:02">[00:58:02]</a>. Users can also customize their PRD templates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:35">[00:58:35]</a>.
*   **Improving Documents:** Around 30% of users utilize it to enhance existing specifications, PRDs, strategy documents, or roadmaps <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:43">[00:58:43]</a>.
*   **Brainstorming and Internal PM Work:** The remaining users leverage the tool for brainstorming ideas and assisting with internal product management tasks, such as creating meeting agendas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:53">[00:58:53]</a>.

## The [[changing_role_of_product_management_with_ai | Changing Role of Product Management with AI]]

While [[ai_in_product_management | AI]] tools like Chat PRD are unlikely to eliminate Product Manager (PM) roles entirely in the short term, they are rapidly shifting the required skill sets and potentially the ratios of PMs to other team members <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:44">[00:00:44]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:41">[01:03:41]</a>.

### Skills to be Enhanced or Replaced
*   **Lowercase "c" Communication (Functional Information Exchange):** [[ai_in_product_management | AI]] excels at synthesizing information, crafting communications, and coordinating information flow across teams <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:09">[01:04:09]</a>. This includes tasks like writing basic PRDs or summarizing data.
*   **Strategy (Synthesis and Planning):** [[Impact of AI on product management | AI]] can be exceptional at synthesizing vast amounts of information (e.g., market data, competitor analysis, internal advantages) and distilling it into coherent plans <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:38">[01:05:38]</a>.

### Skills that Remain Crucial
*   **Capital "C" Communication (Influence and Leadership):** The ability to be influential, convincing, and bold, to inspire human teams to follow a particular path, is much harder for [[ai_in_product_management | AI]] to replicate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:54">[01:04:54]</a>.
*   **Boldness and Vision:** Seeing the future in a way that goes beyond patterns learned from existing data, and having the charisma to attract and align people, remains a uniquely human skill <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:12">[01:06:12]</a>.
*   **Getting Buy-in and Alignment:** Convincing stakeholders and getting a team to rally behind a roadmap or strategy requires interpersonal skills that [[ai_in_product_management | AI]] cannot replicate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:20">[01:07:20]</a>.
*   **Problem-Solving & Unblocking:** Identifying blockers and unblocking people effectively still requires human judgment and interaction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:59">[01:07:59]</a>.
*   **Budget & Resource Acquisition:** Securing budget and resources for a team remains a leadership function involving negotiation and strategic alignment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:05">[01:08:05]</a>.

### [[Future of Product Management with AI | Impact on PM Roles and Organizational Structure]]
The [[impact_of_ai_on_product_management | impact of AI on product management]] extends to organizational efficiency. Tools like Chat PRD can save product managers dozens of hours on documentation, allowing them to focus on higher-value activities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:22">[01:14:22]</a>. This increased efficiency may lead to shifts in the PM-to-engineer ratio, potentially allowing teams to grow without needing to hire additional PMs, thus extending a startup's runway <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:44">[01:14:44]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:43">[01:15:43]</a>.

Organizations might also start allocating budgets differently between tools and people, with some roles being automated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:13">[01:10:13]</a>.

### Navigating the Future of Product Management
To stay ahead of the curve, product managers should:
*   **Experiment with Tools:** Actively engage with [[ai_in_product_management | AI]] tools and create their own custom GPTs or applications to understand their capabilities and limitations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:24">[01:11:24]</a>.
*   **Understand Non-Deterministic Products:** Learn how to build and manage products based on [[the_role_and_evolution_of_ai_models_in_product_development | AI technology]], which behave differently from traditional software <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:38">[01:11:38]</a>. This parallels the shift when mobile technology emerged, favoring PMs who adapted early <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:02">[01:12:02]</a>.
*   **Conduct Product Teardowns:** Analyze existing [[ai_in_product_management | AI]] products to critique their design, functionality, and potential impact <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:49">[01:12:49]</a>.
*   **Explore No-Code/Low-Code Tools:** Utilize these platforms to experiment with [[ai_in_product_management | AI]] capabilities without needing deep coding knowledge <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:23">[01:13:23]</a>.
*   **Find Joy in Exploration:** Engage with [[ai_in_product_management | AI]] in personally interesting and fun ways, such as creating art with generative [[ai_in_product_management | AI]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:40">[01:13:40]</a>.

This era of [[role_and_impact_of_ai_in_product_management | AI in product management]] requires leaders to be forward-thinking, continuously experimenting, and adapting their teams to leverage new technologies effectively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:50">[01:10:50]</a>.

## Why Networking Causes Discomfort

Many individuals find networking uncomfortable, often perceiving it as "dirty" or "cringey" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:17">[00:31:17]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:03">[00:43:03]</a>. This discomfort stems from a misconception of what effective networking truly entails <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:49">[00:30:49]</a>. However, the realities of acquiring power often don't align with traditional upbringing or societal ideals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:53">[00:03:53]</a>. It's important to recognize that networking is a skill that can be mastered, much like acting or playing an instrument <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:32">[00:37:32]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:43">[00:46:43]</a>.

## The Core Principle of Networking: Generosity

The first principle of networking is generosity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:06">[00:31:06]</a>. It's about asking, "What can I do for you? How can I be helpful? Who can I introduce you to?" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:08">[00:31:08]</a> By focusing on how you can provide value to others, you build meaningful connections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:17">[00:31:17]</a>.

## Benefits of a Strong Network

*   **Increased Knowledge and Opportunities:** The broader your social network, the more people and things you will know <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:39">[00:31:39]</a>. This knowledge is a form of power, and networking is a powerful way to acquire it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:47">[00:31:47]</a>. Knowing more people leads to more opportunities and the ability to get things done <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:28">[00:38:28]</a>.
*   **Career Advancement:** To get promoted, people need to know who you are <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:39">[00:16:39]</a>. Networking helps you differentiate yourself and ensures you're known by senior leaders, which can be more impactful than simply "doing your job" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:01">[00:17:01]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:39">[00:33:39]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:40">[00:34:40]</a>.
*   **Leverage and Resources:** The more you accomplish, the more resources and opportunities you will be given <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:07">[00:43:07]</a>. Networking helps you mobilize resources and gain influence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:09">[00:43:09]</a>.

## Strategies for Effective Networking

### Be a Broker

Becoming a "broker" in your network means connecting people and groups who would benefit from knowing each other but are not currently connected <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:48">[00:37:48]</a>. This role is common in professions like venture capital, investment banking, and real estate, where connecting buyers and sellers is the primary function <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:55">[00:37:55]</a>. By facilitating these connections, you gain credit and also benefit those you connect <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:15">[00:37:15]</a>.

### Pursue Weak Ties

While strong ties (family, close friends, immediate colleagues) are important, they often provide redundant information and access to the same people you already know <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:26">[00:40:26]</a>. Building "weak ties" with people who are more different from you expands your network and provides non-redundant information and new connections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:33">[00:40:33]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:40">[00:41:40]</a>. Research suggests that the best job opportunities often come from these weak ties, as they offer a broader view into different "ecosystems" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:31">[00:41:31]</a>.

### Practical Approaches

*   **Identify Key Individuals:** Make a list of 10 people whose acquaintance would be important for your career goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:32">[00:35:32]</a>.
*   **Strategic Engagement:**
    *   Consider holding dinners or events where guests contribute to the experience, leveraging the "Ikea effect" (liking something more if you participated in creating it) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:03">[00:36:03]</a>.
    *   Reach out with helpful articles or suggest introductions to people who might benefit from meeting them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:24">[00:36:24]</a>.
*   **"Doing Power" Assignments:** Stanford's "Paths to Power" course includes "doing power" assignments where students must apply networking principles. Examples include starting a podcast or strategically connecting with influential figures to build a brand <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:32">[00:13:32]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:14">[00:14:14]</a>.

## Overcoming Discomfort and Getting Started

It is common to feel uncomfortable when starting to build networking skills <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:25">[00:51:25]</a>. However, like any skill, it can be learned through practice and coaching <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:55">[00:52:55]</a>. The key is to start small and incrementally push yourself out of your comfort zone <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:39">[00:53:39]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:43">[00:54:43]</a>.

Instead of trying to emulate top networkers immediately, begin where you are and gradually challenge yourself <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:51">[00:53:51]</a>. Seek out coaching or form a "personal board of directors" to gain advice, social support, and accountability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:03">[01:18:03]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:12">[01:18:12]</a>. It's difficult to achieve significant progress alone <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:31">[01:18:31]</a>.

Ultimately, networking is a vital component of [[key_elements_of_a_successful_strategy | success]] and [[becoming_more_strategic_in_a_business_context | strategic]] advancement. While it may initially feel uneasy, the benefits of expanded knowledge, opportunities, and influence make it an indispensable skill to cultivate.

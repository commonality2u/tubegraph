## Why Climate Tech Now?

The world is undergoing a massive transition away from carbon-emitting activities, with governments having already made the decision to change the entire energy system <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:19">[01:00:19]</a>. This shift involves trillions of dollars moving from carbon-intensive industries to sustainable alternatives, making it an enormous economic opportunity, far surpassing the scale of the software industry <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:41">[01:00:41]</a>.

[[climate_tech | Climate tech]] is no longer seen merely as an "impact-oriented" market but as a sector with substantial financial potential <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:07">[01:00:07]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:31">[01:12:31]</a>. Corporations are increasingly motivated by survival, fearing they will fall behind if they don't adapt to the changing global landscape <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:31">[01:15:31]</a>. This has led to a significant increase in demand from large companies looking for decarbonization solutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:12">[01:14:12]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:49">[01:14:49]</a>.

### Factors Driving Optimism

Several recent developments contribute to the optimistic outlook for [[climate_tech | climate tech]]:
*   **Political Support** The Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) in the United States, described as primarily a [[climate_tech | climate]] bill, provides massive incentives for the green economy, focusing on onshoring supply chains and addressing areas like carbon removal and home energy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:10">[01:13:10]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:26">[01:13:26]</a>. This has even spurred Europe to introduce its own incentives to remain competitive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:54">[01:13:54]</a>.
*   **Corporate Demand** Corporations have become active customers, committing to reduce emissions and seeking solutions to meet these promises to shareholders and governments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:12">[01:14:12]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:35">[01:14:35]</a>. They are not just signing letters of intent but entering into paying contracts and even making investments in [[climate_tech | climate tech]] companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:52">[01:14:52]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:22">[01:15:22]</a>.

## Opportunities for Startups

YC has funded over 130 [[climate_tech | climate tech]] companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:46">[00:59:46]</a>. Ambitious people, including accomplished software engineers, are increasingly drawn to this sector, recognizing their skills are relevant and that they can learn what's needed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:32">[01:01:32]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:03">[01:02:03]</a>.

### Decarbonization and Carbon Removal

The primary goal is to decarbonize all activities that emit greenhouse gases, which means changing transportation, energy, and homes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:18">[01:07:18]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:29">[01:07:29]</a>. Additionally, carbon removal — actively pulling carbon from the atmosphere — is crucial, as decarbonization alone may not be enough <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:35">[01:07:35]</a>. Both approaches are necessary and not in conflict <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:53">[01:07:53]</a>.

### Specific Areas of Opportunity

Gustav highlights several areas ripe for innovation:
*   **Shipping** Freight ships are a major source of emissions, and solutions like carbon capture for ships (e.g., C-bound) and electric ships (e.g., Fleet zero) are emerging <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:09">[01:08:09]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:35">[01:08:35]</a>. Electrification often proves more efficient, cleaner, and cheaper for various applications <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:46">[01:08:46]</a>.
*   **Carbon Accounting & Recommendation Systems** Companies need software platforms to account for their carbon footprint and receive recommendations for decarbonization. Examples include Unravel Carbon (Asia-focused), Carbon Chain (supply chain/shipping), and Sinai <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:14">[01:09:14]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:25">[01:09:25]</a>. This market has strong customer demand <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:49">[01:09:49]</a>.
*   **Energy Management Platforms** As homes and EVs become connected appliances, software platforms are needed to manage energy consumption efficiently. Enode is building a "Plaid for EV chargers and home energy systems" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:13">[01:10:13]</a>.
*   **EV Charging Infrastructure** Static, the "Airbnb for EV charging in India," is building a bottom-up EV charging system, including public infrastructure, in a market where traditional charging is scarce <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:48">[01:10:48]</a>.
*   **Aviation** Decarbonizing aviation is challenging, but companies like Heart Aerospace and Right Electric are focusing on battery-electric planes for commercial aviation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:00">[01:12:00]</a>.

## Building a Climate Tech Startup

For product managers (PMs) or others without deep scientific or [[climate_tech | climate]] backgrounds, regular PM skills are highly valuable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:17">[01:05:17]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:40">[01:05:40]</a>. Companies already have experts in science, and what they often need is business acumen, operational capabilities, and the ability to [[challenges_and_strategies_for_creating_successful_startups | execute]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:22">[01:05:22]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:29">[01:05:29]</a>.

Founders, regardless of their specific expertise, can succeed by partnering with those who have the necessary domain knowledge or by learning it themselves <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:16">[01:06:16]</a>. For example, the founders of Pachama, a carbon removal company, did not have prior expertise in forests but had a strong willingness to solve the problem <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:40">[01:06:40]</a>.

The most common mistakes early-stage [[challenges_and_strategies_for_creating_successful_startups | startups]] make include not talking to customers, which prevents them from finding product-market fit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:57">[00:22:57]</a>. Founders often fear rejection or mistakenly believe they already know what to build <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:50">[00:23:50]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:37">[00:26:37]</a>. The core principle for any startup, including [[climate_tech | climate tech]], remains: "Make things people want" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:17">[00:26:17]</a>.

To avoid these pitfalls, founders should:
*   **Talk to Users** Engage in frequent conversations with potential users to understand their problems, learn what is important, and validate ideas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:00">[00:23:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:21">[00:23:21]</a>. Don't rely on assumptions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:09">[00:23:09]</a>.
*   **Overcome Fear of Rejection** Understand that most people are not early adopters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:32">[00:24:32]</a>. You'll need to reach out to many to find the few who are interested. People often forget about services they sign up for rather than actively disliking them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:39">[00:29:39]</a>. The worst outcome is indifference, not hatred <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:08">[00:30:08]</a>.
*   **Observe User Behavior** Instead of asking if users have a problem, observe their daily workflows and how they currently solve issues to truly understand the intensity of their pain points <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:58">[00:32:58]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:30">[00:33:30]</a>.
*   **Prioritize Execution** For a small startup, strategy does not matter as much as execution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:34">[00:53:34]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:48">[00:53:48]</a>. Founders should have one clear priority and continuously work towards it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:58">[00:53:58]</a>.

While early-stage [[challenges_and_strategies_for_creating_successful_startups | startups]] should focus on doing things that don't scale, such as direct customer interaction, it's crucial to consider a scalable distribution strategy once the product gains traction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:17">[00:57:17]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:21">[00:57:21]</a>. Focusing on B2B customers is often recommended in [[climate_tech | climate tech]], as that's where most significant changes are happening <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:11">[01:16:11]</a>.

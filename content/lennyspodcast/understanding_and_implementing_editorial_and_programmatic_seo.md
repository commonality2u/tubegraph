## Deciding When to Invest in SEO

Investing in SEO is not always suitable for every company, especially in the early stages. Ethan Smith highlights key attributes that indicate when SEO could be a significant growth driver <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:16">[00:10:16]</a>:

*   **Large Addressable Market**: The market you're targeting should be substantial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:29">[00:10:29]</a>. Most markets are large enough for SEO if you consider targeting a persona and creating content around their needs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:12">[00:12:12]</a>.
*   **Existing Authority/Traction**: If you're starting from zero with no authority, it's typically too soon to invest heavily in SEO <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:40">[00:10:40]</a>. Google prefers credible domains <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:52">[00:10:52]</a>. Indicators of sufficient authority include:
    *   **Non-SEO Traffic**: At least 1,000 daily visits from channels other than search (e.g., direct, paid, email, social) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:10">[00:11:10]</a>. This signals to Google that you're not just an "SEO farm" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:53">[00:14:53]</a>.
    *   **Referring Domains**: At least 1,000 referring domains linking to your site <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:29">[00:11:29]</a>.
    *   **Brand Mentions/Searches**: The more people search for your brand directly or share your content, the more authority Google perceives you to have <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:10">[01:10:10]</a>.

It's crucial to compare the potential impact and cost of SEO against other growth channels (e.g., paid ads, social media) to determine the best resource allocation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:02">[00:23:02]</a>.

## Types of SEO Strategies

There are three main types of SEO strategies:

1.  ### [[programmatic_vs_editorial_seo_strategies | Programmatic SEO]]
    *   **Definition**: Involves automatically generating web pages from a database of information <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:48">[00:18:48]</a>.
    *   **Examples**:
        *   eBay product pages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:53">[00:18:53]</a>
        *   Zillow address pages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:00">[00:19:00]</a>
        *   Glassdoor user-generated content (UGC) pages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:08">[00:19:08]</a>
        *   Zapier's integration pages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:21">[01:01:21]</a>
    *   **Page Types**: Primarily category pages (grids or lists of items) and item/product pages (individual items) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:11">[00:19:11]</a>.
    *   **Advantage**: Significant cost savings as you don't pay for each page created <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:03">[00:21:03]</a>.
    *   **Implementation**: Requires technical ability and often involves working with engineers to build scalable systems for content generation and indexation logic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:01">[00:46:01]</a>. The focus is on grouping keywords at a topic level rather than creating a page for every single keyword variation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:26">[00:30:26]</a>.

2.  ### [[programmatic_vs_editorial_seo_strategies | Editorial SEO]]
    *   **Definition**: Involves manually written content such as articles, guides, or listicles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:29">[00:19:29]</a>.
    *   **Growth**: Has increasingly driven more traffic compared to programmatic SEO <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:42">[00:19:42]</a>.
    *   **Examples**:
        *   MasterClass (content related to classes) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:31">[00:21:31]</a>
        *   BetterUp (articles on career improvement) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:42">[00:21:42]</a>
        *   NerdWallet (financial guides) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:57">[00:21:57]</a>
        *   HubSpot (productivity content) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:09">[00:22:09]</a>
        *   Dotdash (conglomerate of publishers like Investopedia, Allrecipes) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:16">[00:22:16]</a>
    *   **Disadvantage**: Each piece of content has a direct cost associated with its creation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:16">[00:21:16]</a>.
    *   **Implementation**:
        *   **Topic Selection**: Identify topics based on search volume, your site's topical authority, and conversion intent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:52">[00:54:52]</a>.
        *   **Content Outlines**: Develop comprehensive outlines that cover all relevant subtopics for a given topic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:17">[00:55:17]</a>.
        *   **Writing**: Engage writers, ideally domain experts, to produce high-quality content based on the outlines <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:57">[00:55:57]</a>.
        *   **Publishing**: Use a Content Management System (CMS) like Contentful or Webflow to publish content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:37">[00:31:37]</a>.
        *   **Analysis**: Continuously monitor performance and feed insights back into your strategy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:02">[00:56:02]</a>.
    *   **Team**: An [[role_and_hiring_strategies_for_seo_experts | SEO manager]] typically defines topics and outlines, manages publishing, and analyzes results. Writers focus on content creation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:05">[00:56:05]</a>. An [[role_and_hiring_strategies_for_seo_experts | SEO manager]] for editorial SEO doesn't necessarily need prior SEO expertise; strong content strategy skills are often sufficient, with SEO knowledge learned on the job <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:52">[00:56:52]</a>.

3.  **Technical SEO**:
    *   **Definition**: Focuses on the infrastructure of your website to help search engines crawl and index your content effectively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:54">[00:19:54]</a>.
    *   **Components**: Internal link architecture, tags, redirects, and page speed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:57">[00:19:57]</a>.
    *   **Importance**: While essential, it's often over-indexed, as growth is primarily driven by programmatic and editorial SEO <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:47">[00:08:47]</a>.

## Key Concepts and Implementation Details

### Topics vs. Keywords
SEO has shifted from focusing on single keywords to broader "topics" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:58">[00:31:58]</a>. Google's algorithm has a semantic understanding, clustering many related keywords under a single topic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:42">[00:32:42]</a>.

*   A single page can rank for 200 to 2,000 different keywords within a topic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:52">[00:32:52]</a>.
*   Thinking in terms of topics ensures you capture the full search volume for related queries and prevents wasted effort on redundant articles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:04">[00:33:04]</a>.
*   A page optimized for a topic must fulfill the intent of all associated keywords <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:06">[00:34:06]</a>. For example, a "butter lettuce" article might need to cover "health benefits" and "recipes" if those are related search intents <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:43">[00:34:43]</a>.

### Topical Authority
Beyond simple PageRank (links), Google now assesses "topical authority" â€“ what your site is disproportionately known for <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:49">[01:09:49]</a>.

*   **Inputs**: Backlinks (especially anchor text), non-SEO traffic, social shares, and branded searches contribute to topical authority <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:52">[01:09:52]</a>.
*   **Benefits**: High topical authority for a specific niche allows a site to outrank competitors with higher overall domain authority for those particular topics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:22">[01:13:22]</a>.

### Internal Link Architecture
Internal links are a critical, often under-optimized, lever for SEO <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:44">[01:16:44]</a>.

*   **Impact**: More internal links to a page correlate with more traffic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:41">[01:17:41]</a>. Google crawls your site through links, and a sufficient number of paths helps pages get discovered and crawled regularly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:30">[01:18:30]</a>.
*   **Common Issue**: Many sites exhibit a "power curve" where a small percentage of pages receive the vast majority of internal links (e.g., 5% of pages get 95% of links) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:17">[01:20:17]</a>. Pages often have zero or one link, which is insufficient <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:26">[01:20:26]</a>.
*   **Solution**: Implement an internal linking strategy that ensures broad link spread across all relevant pages. Aim for at least 5-10 internal links per page <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:28">[01:20:28]</a>. Links in the body of content are more valuable than navigational (header/footer) links <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:47">[01:22:47]</a>.

### [[seo_best_practices_and_content_strategy | SEO Best Practices and Content Strategy]]
*   **Page Types**: Match the page type to the user's search intent. For "how to poach an egg," an article with a recipe is expected, not a chapter transcript <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:07">[00:25:07]</a>.
*   **User Intent**: Focus on creating content that is genuinely useful to the user. This improves engagement metrics (click-through rate, time on page, no "back-to-Google" behavior), which are key ranking signals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:56">[00:27:56]</a>.
*   **Iterative Process**: Start with a small batch of pages (10-30 articles/pages per month) to test hypotheses and look for early indicators of progress (e.g., ranking within the top 20) before scaling up <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:01">[00:58:01]</a>.

### [[using_ai_to_optimize_seo_efforts | Impact of AI on SEO]]
While AI tools like GPT-3 can generate grammatically correct sentences, they often lack factual accuracy or underlying wisdom <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:25:04">[01:25:04]</a>.

*   **Caution**: Using AI for content generation is generally not recommended, especially for sensitive topics like finance or health, due to potential factual errors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:25:58">[01:25:58]</a>.
*   **Effective Use**: AI is highly useful for automating data analysis tasks in the SEO workflow:
    *   **Topic Clustering**: Grouping thousands of keywords into topics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:27:50">[01:27:50]</a>.
    *   **Subtopic Extraction**: Identifying sub-themes for content outlines <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:28:06">[01:28:06]</a>.
    *   **Authority Assessment**: Determining topical authority <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:28:01">[01:28:01]</a>.
    *   **Performance Analysis**: Analyzing content performance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:28:08">[01:28:08]</a>.
    This allows humans to focus on creating high-quality, factual content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:28:10">[01:28:10]</a>.

## Common [[seo_myths_and_misconceptions | SEO Myths and Misconceptions]]

*   **Technical Audits as Growth Strategy**: A common misconception is that a "technical audit" (a list of bugs to fix) is equivalent to a growth strategy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:58">[00:07:58]</a>. While technical SEO is important, it's not the primary driver of growth; strategic programmatic and editorial efforts are <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:47">[00:08:47]</a>.
*   **Over-indexing on Technical SEO**: People often prioritize technical SEO too much and neglect editorial SEO <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:47">[00:08:47]</a>.
*   **Quick Results**: SEO is a long-term investment. It typically takes 6-9 months to see significant results, although large, authoritative sites may see changes faster (1-4 weeks) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:41">[00:40:41]</a>.
*   **Misinterpreting Failures**: Early SEO efforts might not show immediate results due to incorrect implementation rather than a lack of opportunity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:42">[00:43:42]</a>.
*   **Blindly Copying Competitors**: While analyzing competitors is crucial for hypothesis generation, the goal should be to take inspiration and create truly great, useful pages, not just to copy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:11">[00:27:11]</a>.

## First Steps for a Startup Considering SEO
If you're a founder excited about SEO, here's how to begin your exploration:

1.  **Assess Addressable Market Size**: Identify product and audience competitors and use tools like Similarweb to gauge their traffic. Determine if the market size is meaningful for your product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:28:46">[01:28:46]</a>.
2.  **Evaluate Your Authority**: Check your current non-SEO traffic and the number of referring domains to understand your existing traction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:28:57">[01:28:57]</a>.
3.  **Compare Channels**: Objectively compare the potential impact and cost of SEO with all other alternative growth channels available to you <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:09">[01:29:09]</a>.

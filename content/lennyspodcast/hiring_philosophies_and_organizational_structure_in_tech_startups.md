From: [[lennyspodcast]] <br/> 

Varun Mohan, co-founder and CEO of [[Windsurf]] (Kodium), shares insights into the company's unique approach to [[innovative_hiring_practices_in_startups | hiring]] and [[effective_team_building_and_leadership_in_startups | organizational structure]], emphasizing lean operations, ruthless prioritization, and a culture of high [[leadership_and_communication_in_tech | agency]].

## Kodium Overview
Kodium, the parent company of [[Windsurf]], was founded almost four years ago, initially building GPU virtualization and compiler software for deep learning applications <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:40">[00:04:40]</a>. The company pivoted in mid-2022 to focus on generative AI applications, leading to the creation of [[Windsurf]], an AI coding tool <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:21">[00:05:21]</a>. Kodium currently has around 160 employees, with an engineering team of over 50 and a go-to-market team exceeding 80 people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:00">[00:38:00]</a>.

## Hiring Philosophy

Kodium adopts a distinct [[innovative_hiring_practices_in_startups | hiring philosophy]] that prioritizes lean operations and strategic additions to the team <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:13">[00:23:13]</a>.

### When to Hire
Kodium's goal is to be "the smallest company we can be to satisfy our ambitions" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:38">[00:23:38]</a>. They only hire for a role when they are "underwater for that function" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:41">[00:24:41]</a>.

*   **Dehydrated Entity Metaphor**: Varun describes the company as a "dehydrated entity," where "every hire is like a little bit of water" and they only hire when they are "back to being dehydrated" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:55">[00:25:55]</a>.
*   **Avoiding Politics**: This approach prevents "weird politics" and the manufacturing of unnecessary work that can arise when companies hire people they don't truly need <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:05">[00:25:05]</a>.
*   **Forcing Function for Prioritization**: The lean structure forces "ruthless prioritization" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:27">[00:26:27]</a>. Teams will explicitly state when they cannot handle more work, leading to clear decisions on what is most important <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:01">[00:29:01]</a>. Varun emphasizes that companies "don't win by doing...10 things kind of well. You win by doing like one thing really well and maybe you fail nine things" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:51">[00:26:51]</a>.

### What to Look for in Candidates
Kodium seeks candidates who possess:
*   **High Technical Bar**: Candidates must meet a very high technical standard <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:03">[00:31:03]</a>.
*   **Passion for Mission**: They look for people "really really passionate about the mission" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:08">[00:31:08]</a>.
*   **Strong Work Ethic**: Candidates must be "willing to work very hard" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:12">[00:31:12]</a>. Varun directly asks candidates about their willingness to work hard, seeking those who understand that winning in a competitive space requires both working smart and "really hard" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:00">[00:33:00]</a>.
*   **[[navigating_talent_and_skill_changes_in_tech | Agency]]**: A crucial, often undervalued, skill is "agency"â€”the desire and initiative to build something rather than just following prescribed paths <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:03">[00:22:03]</a>. Startups like Kodium depend on individuals who are willing to innovate and "do crazy things" to survive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:36">[00:22:36]</a>.
*   **Team Collaboration**: The company values a collaborative culture where individuals can rely on others to complete complex tasks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:27">[00:32:27]</a>. This means avoiding team members who don't "pull their weight," as it lowers the collective expectation and morale <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:58">[00:31:58]</a>.

### Hiring Process
Kodium has an "extremely low acceptance rate" for engineers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:33">[00:29:33]</a>. Post-takehome, the acceptance rate is approximately 6%, with the take-home itself filtering out 10-15x more candidates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:07">[00:33:07]</a>.

*   **AI Tools in Interviews**: Kodium is "okay with people using the tools" like [[Windsurf]] in interviews, as they believe these tools are "massive productivity improvements" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:25">[00:33:25]</a>.
*   **On-site Thinking**: To assess problem-solving ability, candidates are brought on-site to "see how they think through problems on a whiteboard" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:35">[00:33:35]</a>, ensuring they don't solely rely on AI for solutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:58">[00:33:58]</a>.

## Organizational Structure

Kodium's [[effective_team_building_and_leadership_in_startups | organizational structure]] is designed to foster agility and deep technical engagement.

*   **Lean Product Management**: For core engineering, Kodium does not have traditional product managers. Instead, their developers "flexing to be like more product conventional product managers," leveraging their deep understanding of the developer persona <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:03">[01:01:03]</a>.
*   **Product Strategy Role**: For enterprise-facing products, where requirements like FedRAMP compliance are external and not intuitively understood by engineers, Kodium employs "product strategy" roles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:58">[01:01:58]</a>. These individuals bridge customer needs with technical capabilities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:00">[01:02:00]</a>.
*   **Flat Structure and Two-Pizza Teams**: The engineering team is "fairly flat" and structured into "two pizza teams" (small teams that can be fed by two pizzas) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:27">[01:02:27]</a>. This ensures leaders remain deeply involved in the technology <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:35">[01:02:35]</a>.
*   **Flexible Teams**: Teams are highly flexible and can quickly adapt to new priorities, with people moving between projects based on company needs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:57">[01:02:57]</a>. There is "no notion of someone owning people at the company" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:21">[01:02:21]</a>.
*   **Rewarding Impact**: The most valuable individuals are those who can deliver "the most crazy sort of project out there with as few people as possible" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:30">[01:03:30]</a>.

## Go-to-Market Strategy

Kodium made an early decision to invest heavily in enterprise sales, hiring their VP of sales over a year ago <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:41">[00:35:41]</a>. This led to a go-to-market team of over 80 people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:49">[00:35:49]</a>.

*   **Valuing Sales**: Unlike some technical founders, Kodium's leadership never viewed enterprise sales negatively, thanks in part to early angel investors with go-to-market operational experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:00">[00:36:00]</a>.
*   **Scaling Opportunity**: The shift to Kodium (AI coding) quickly revealed a significant enterprise opportunity, with large companies reaching out <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:59">[00:36:59]</a>. Varun and others initially ran "tens of pilots concurrently" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:12">[00:37:12]</a>, validating the need for a dedicated sales force <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:18">[00:37:18]</a>.
*   **Enterprise Necessity**: Selling to Fortune 500 companies is "very hard to do that purely by swiping a credit card" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:32">[00:37:32]</a>, necessitating a robust sales team.
*   **Internal App Building**: A surprising outcome of [[Windsurf]]'s capabilities is that non-developer teams, such as the go-to-market team, are now building their own applications <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:03">[00:50:03]</a>. For example, Kodium's head of partnerships built their own partner portal instead of buying a SAS product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:20">[00:50:20]</a>. This has saved the company over half a million dollars in SAS products <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:10">[00:50:10]</a>.
    *   This trend suggests that verticalized, niche software products may face significant competition as "domain specialists now have access to build the tools that they ultimately wanted" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:51">[00:51:51]</a>. Previously, companies would buy these products due to the inability to maintain or build custom solutions for specific features <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:13">[00:52:13]</a>. Now, with tools like [[Windsurf]], a product manager can use a simple mockup to create functional software, bypassing the need for extensive engineering resources <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:50">[00:52:50]</a>.

## Conclusion

Kodium's journey highlights the dynamic nature of tech startups, particularly in the AI space. Their [[cultural_practices_in_rapidly_scaling_companies | culture of constant re-evaluation]], aggressive long-term bets, and commitment to efficiency through a lean organizational structure underscore their approach to [[prioritization_and_decisionmaking_in_startups | navigating talent and skill changes in tech]]. Varun advises everyone to "get your hands dirty" with AI tools, as those who maximize their advantage will become "force multipliers" in their organizations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:57">[01:10:57]</a>.

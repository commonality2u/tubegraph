## Embracing All Tasks: "Carrying the Water"

A key indicator of a successful PM is the willingness to do any work required, regardless of whether it's explicitly part of their job description <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>. This concept, known as "carrying the water," emphasizes that PMs are ultimately responsible for outcomes and results <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:22">[00:09:22]</a>.

*   **Beyond "That's Not My Job"** If a PM ever finds themselves thinking "that's not my job," it's likely something they should do <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>. While it might genuinely be someone else's responsibility, getting the work done ensures faster progress and more successful products, without the burden of frustration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:10">[00:00:10]</a>.
*   **The Emotional Center** PMs are often the emotional center of their team, tasked with keeping people motivated, excited, and bought into the project <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:31">[00:00:31]</a>. This requires maintaining optimism and being willing to take on additional tasks like sales calls or customer implementations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:42">[00:00:42]</a>.
*   **The Unglamorous Work** A significant part of [[The role of product managers | the role of product managers]] involves hard, boring, and unglamorous work, including customer support, sales, marketing, writing copy, and project management <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:10">[00:09:10]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:23">[00:30:23]</a>. PMs don't "just write the one-pager" or "pick the problem"; their job is to deliver a business result <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:42">[00:29:42]</a>. This means filling in gaps, ensuring the problem is solved for the customer, and performing tasks like QA, which are crucial for product success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:53">[00:29:53]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:27">[00:31:27]</a>.

## Core Competencies of Great PMs

Beyond the willingness to "carry the water," top-tier PMs exhibit specific skills that set them apart <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:17">[00:05:17]</a>.

### Simplifying and Prioritizing
The best PMs excel at breaking down complexity to identify the single most important thing to focus on, and then sticking with it until completion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:31">[00:05:31]</a>.

*   **Focus on the "One Thing"** Whether in a large company with numerous priorities or a startup with many "fires," the ability to choose one thing and remain committed is crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:43">[00:05:43]</a>.
*   **Prioritization is Persistence** Prioritization is more than a momentary decision; it's the resilience to ensure the chosen task remains the most important, seeing it through to completion, and keeping the team engaged <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:57">[00:06:57]</a>.
*   **Tips for Simplification**
    *   Read written communication out loud to identify complexity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:55">[00:11:55]</a>.
    *   "Just say that" â€“ state the core message directly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:35">[00:12:35]</a>.
    *   Limit strategies, conclusions, and next steps to three items maximum <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:54">[00:18:54]</a>.
    *   Delete the first two paragraphs of almost any document <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:08">[00:19:08]</a>.
    *   Seek feedback from peers to simplify work <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:44">[00:19:44]</a>.

### Following Up on Results
Great PMs consistently follow up on the impact of their shipped products <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:00">[00:08:00]</a>.

*   **Beyond Launch** After a product launch, PMs should regularly check metrics and dashboards weeks or months later <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:00">[00:22:00]</a>.
*   **Demonstrating Impact and Learning** This practice not only demonstrates accountability to management but also fosters learning from past decisions, which improves future product development <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:35">[00:23:35]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:56">[00:23:56]</a>.
*   **"Aura of Competence"** By proactively sharing results, PMs create an impression that they are reliable and won't drop threads, building trust with their managers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:24">[00:23:24]</a>.

## [[Product management and leadership insights | Leadership Insights]] and Growth

*   **Ownership and Accountability** The PM role requires a willingness to "make the bet" and be accountable for decisions, acknowledging the inherent risk <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:11">[00:16:11]</a>. This sense of ownership is crucial for success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:32">[00:16:32]</a>.
*   **Time to Expertise** It takes many years of shipping a lot of products to build up expertise and feel confident as a PM <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:06">[00:24:06]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:39">[00:24:39]</a>. Spending significant time at one company or with one product can provide valuable cycles for learning from decision consequences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:52">[00:25:52]</a>.
*   **Product Strategy** A comprehensive product strategy document outlines the mission, landscape (competitors, risks, market view), current state of product and business, technical hurdles, opportunities, and proposed solutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:05">[00:36:05]</a>. It's a tool for the PM to align the team, justify decisions, and invite critical feedback <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:35">[00:38:35]</a>.
    *   **The "Why Now"** A critical component of product strategy is answering "why this problem matters now," identifying perishable opportunities that must be seized immediately <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:02">[00:51:02]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:00">[00:53:00]</a>.
*   **One-Pager/PRD** This foundational document defines the product, starting with the background, context, problem, why it matters, and why it matters *now* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:10">[00:50:10]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:57">[00:50:57]</a>. It serves as the "home base" for decisions and should be shared with the team for feedback <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:30">[00:51:30]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:21">[00:52:21]</a>.

## [[Challenges in transitioning to product management | Breaking into Product Management]]

Breaking into product management can be challenging <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:06">[00:32:06]</a>.

*   **Paths In** Common paths include lateral moves within a company or joining startups <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:27">[00:32:27]</a>. Entry-level programs at larger tech companies are rare and competitive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:19">[00:32:19]</a>.
*   **The First PM Title** Obtaining the first "product manager" title on a resume is crucial, as it significantly eases the process of securing subsequent PM roles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:37">[00:33:37]</a>. Hiring managers often screen for prior PM experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:42">[00:33:42]</a>.
*   **"What Have You Shipped?"** Beyond the title, the ability to articulate what products they have shipped is a key differentiator for successful PM candidates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:25">[00:34:25]</a>.

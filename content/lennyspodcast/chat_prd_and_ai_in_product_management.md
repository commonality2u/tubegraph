## What is Chat PRD?
Chat PRD is considered the single most popular [[ai_tools_and_resources_for_product_managers | AI PM-specific tool]] currently available <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:27">[00:39:27]</a>. It was born out of a need to quickly spec out a complicated technical product while Claire's team was resource-constrained <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:17">[00:54:17]</a>. Claire used Chat GPT and a custom prompt to generate a serviceable Product Requirements Document (PRD) spec <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:45">[00:54:45]</a>. This initial prompt evolved into Chat PRD <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:02">[00:55:02]</a>.

The tool began as a custom GPT, then transitioned into a standalone application that uses the OpenAI Assistants API <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:54">[00:55:54]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:16">[00:57:16]</a>. A key differentiator of the standalone app is that each user gets a customized assistant that learns from their specific content, role, and company, which is not available in the GPT version <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:25">[00:57:25]</a>. Chat PRD can also create and iterate on actual documents, not just provide chat output <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:41">[00:57:41]</a>.

### Common Use Cases
*   **Generating PRDs from ideas:** Approximately 60% of users input an idea and receive a full PRD, including objectives, user goals, user stories, out-of-scope items, UX walk-throughs, product narratives, sequencing, milestones, measurements, and goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:02">[00:58:02]</a>. Users can also customize the PRD template to fit their company's specific needs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:35">[00:58:35]</a>.
*   **Improving existing documents:** About 30% of users input a spec, PRD, strategy document, or roadmap for refinement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:46">[00:58:46]</a>.
*   **Brainstorming and internal PM work:** The remaining users leverage it for brainstorming ideas and generating internal PM documents, such as meeting agendas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:56">[00:58:56]</a>.

## Impact of AI on Product Management Skills
While AI tools like Chat PRD are not expected to eliminate Product Managers next year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:45">[00:44:45]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:41">[01:03:41]</a>, the ratios between PMs and other teams, as well as the [[the_role_of_ai_in_shaping_product_management_skills | skills required]], are expected to shift, possibly much faster than anticipated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:46">[01:03:46]</a>.

Claire distinguishes between two types of communication:
*   **"Lowercase c" communication:** This refers to the functional exchange of information that enables others to do their jobs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:16">[01:04:16]</a>. Language models are highly effective at synthesizing information, crafting communications, and coordinating their distribution across various content modalities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:27">[01:04:27]</a>. This aspect of a PM's role, particularly as a "keeper of cross-functional relationships and communication," is likely to change significantly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:45">[01:04:45]</a>.
*   **"Capital C" communication:** This involves being influential, convincing, and bold â€“ the ability to inspire a "system of humans to follow you down a path" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:54">[01:04:54]</a>. These higher-level communication skills are much harder for AI to replicate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:05">[01:05:05]</a>.

AI's proficiency in synthesizing vast amounts of data and distilling it into actionable plans suggests it could be incredibly good at strategy work <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:47">[01:05:47]</a>. However, the human elements of boldness, foresight beyond prior training data, and charisma needed to execute a strategy remain uniquely human <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:12">[01:06:12]</a>.

Claire notes an impending "identity shift" for PMs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:59">[01:06:59]</a>. While many PMs derive value from their individual ideas and how they manifest them, the focus will likely shift to whether the right products are being built quickly and whether they are delivering, regardless of the specific toolchain used <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:03">[01:07:03]</a>.

## Adapting to AI in Product Management
To stay ahead in the evolving landscape of product management and [[ai_integration_in_product_management | AI integration]], Claire advises product managers to:

*   **Lead the disruption:** As a technology leader, Claire feels responsible for understanding how AI can disrupt, using these tools to build better teams, and reshaping organizations in response to technological advancements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:27">[01:03:27]</a>.
*   **Build product skills around non-deterministic products:** Claire built Chat PRD partly to understand how AI impacts the product function and how to build effective products with new, non-deterministic technologies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:33">[01:11:33]</a>. She draws a parallel to the mobile revolution, where PMs who embraced mobile technology gained significant career advantages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:02">[01:12:02]</a>.
*   **Engage in "outside-in product teardowns":** Study existing AI products by critically analyzing their strengths and weaknesses, considering how one might have written the PRD, what metrics to track, and how to manage error states <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:49">[01:12:49]</a>. This provides an accessible way to stress-test one's own skills and identify gaps <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:11">[01:13:11]</a>.
*   **Experiment with no-code/low-code tools:** Even without deep engineering skills, individuals can stitch together and play with no-code tools to explore AI's capabilities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:23">[01:13:23]</a>.
*   **Find the fun:** Explore AI through creative and personally interesting applications, such as using Midjourney to build art <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:40">[01:13:40]</a>. This doesn't always have to be commercially driven or work-related <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:52">[01:13:52]</a>.

## Efficiency Gains and Industry Outlook
Qualitative feedback on Chat PRD indicates significant efficiency gains. Product managers have reported saving "dozens of hours" on document writing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:17">[01:14:17]</a>. One single PM on a growing team noted that the tool has enabled them to delay hiring another PM <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:33">[01:14:33]</a>.

While this may seem concerning for job seekers, Claire emphasizes the importance of sustainability in organizations, especially in startups <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:19">[01:15:19]</a>. By leveraging tools like Chat PRD, a small startup can extend its runway and build transformational products without necessarily growing its team <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:43">[01:15:43]</a>.

Claire holds a "short-term pessimistic" but "long-term optimistic" view on AI's impact on society <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:00">[01:03:00]</a>. She believes technology has made society wealthier, happier, and healthier, and is excited for the future it creates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:49">[01:02:49]</a>. As a leader, she prioritizes being proactive in understanding and experimenting with AI, ensuring her team at LaunchDarkly is "leading from the front" rather than being caught off guard by potential "dramatic changes" in the industry <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:08">[01:11:08]</a>.

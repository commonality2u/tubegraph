From: [[lennyspodcast]] <br/> 

Netflix's culture of high talent density is a foundational aspect of its unique organizational philosophy, deeply rooted in the beliefs of its founder, Reed Hastings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:13">[00:00:13]</a>. This focus ensures the company remains a place where people thrive and achieve excellence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:30">[00:00:30]</a>.

## Core Principles

High talent density is considered a prerequisite for all other cultural aspects at Netflix, such as candor, continuous learning, pursuit of excellence, and [[freedom_and_responsibility | freedom and responsibility]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:57">[00:29:57]</a>. Without it, other cultural elements cannot effectively exist <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:09">[00:00:09]</a>. The belief is that attracting and retaining top talent leads to exceptional outcomes, from which individuals derive a great sense of fulfillment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:41">[00:00:41]</a>.

### Maintaining the High Bar

Maintaining this high bar requires practices that may not feel like "natural human behavior" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:20">[00:30:20]</a>, including:

*   **Continuous Feedback and Improvement**
    *   Managers are expected to provide direct and specific feedback, helping individuals understand where they can improve <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:31">[00:30:31]</a>. This includes being candid about expectations and pushing people to reach a higher standard of excellence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:10">[00:15:10]</a>.
    *   The goal is to foster an environment where feedback is seen as an aid to success, not criticism <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:06">[00:24:06]</a>. Leaders often "jump in with both feet" to help teams fill gaps and uplevel their work, demonstrating commitment and building trust <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:29">[00:22:29]</a>.
    *   Feedback is ideally delivered in a safe, private space, rather than publicly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:32">[00:24:32]</a>.
    *   Netflix does not have traditional performance reviews <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:32">[00:34:32]</a>. Instead, performance discussions are ongoing and integrated into the day-to-day rhythm <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:13">[00:35:13]</a>.
    *   An annual 360-degree feedback cycle collects input from many individuals, serving as a check-in and an opportunity to distill themes for personal growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:45">[00:34:45]</a>. This system, described in more detail in [[netflixs_approach_to_feedback_and_performance_without_traditional_reviews | Netflix's approach to feedback and performance without traditional reviews]], aims to ensure timely, direct feedback <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:07">[00:36:07]</a>.

*   **The "Keeper Test"**
    *   This is a mental framework used by managers to ensure they are consistently holding themselves accountable for maintaining high talent density <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:45">[00:31:45]</a>.
    *   Managers ask themselves: "If this person on my team came to me and said 'I'm leaving today for a different opportunity, would I do everything I could to keep them at Netflix?'" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:54">[00:31:54]</a>.
    *   If the answer is no, it signals that a tough conversation about whether Netflix is the right fit for the individual should occur <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:09">[00:32:09]</a>.
    *   This test helps encourage difficult conversations that might otherwise be avoided <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:29">[00:32:29]</a>. The aim is for such conversations to be unsurprising, ideally resulting from continuous feedback <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:44">[00:32:44]</a>.
    *   While the concept can seem "heavy," it aims to create an environment where performance expectations are clear, reducing anxiety compared to environments with less transparency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:17">[00:37:17]</a>.

*   **Hiring Practices**
    *   Netflix aims to pay "personal top of market" compensation to attract and retain top talent, ensuring competitive pay without relying on it as a "golden handcuffs" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:34">[00:39:34]</a>. This strategy is also discussed in [[hiring_and_retaining_top_talent_in_tech | hiring and retaining top talent in tech]].
    *   Beyond baseline skills, Netflix looks for candidates who will make the team stronger by offering new perspectives, pushing existing thinking, and identifying novel problems to solve <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:40">[00:40:40]</a>. This approach ensures a continuous "up-leveling" of the team <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:22">[00:41:22]</a>.
    *   The understanding is that having gaps in skill sets or poor behavior on a team can be "toxic" for other team members <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:31">[00:42:31]</a>.

### Benefits and Challenges

High talent density allows Netflix to grant extensive [[freedom_and_responsibility | freedom and responsibility]] to its employees, enabling significant innovation without rigid top-down processes or strict guardrails <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:00">[00:45:00]</a>. Many product features and innovations at Netflix have emerged from individual contributors with great ideas, supported by this environment of autonomy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:28">[00:47:28]</a>. This aspect of the culture is further detailed in [[netflixs_unique_organizational_structure_and_data_team | Netflix's unique organizational structure and data team]].

While challenging, the constant emphasis on excellence and direct feedback is believed to foster growth and a deeper sense of ease among employees compared to environments where performance is unclear <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:17">[00:37:17]</a>. The internal feeling is that "knowing is better than not knowing" when it comes to performance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:33">[00:38:33]</a>.

## The Evolving Role of Product Management

The speaker asserts that traditional [[product_management_is_dead_or_dying | product management]] is either dead or will be soon <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:30">[00:00:30]</a>. The rate of change in technology over the last 18 to 24 months has introduced capabilities previously thought impossible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:06">[00:01:06]</a>. The focus for building [[product_strategy | product strategy]] should now be on what customers will need in three, five, or ten years, rather than just for today <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:32">[00:01:32]</a>. Similarly, product teams must be imagined for the future, considering how they will operate and what changes might occur <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:44">[00:01:44]</a>.

### A Metaphor for Change: Product Strategy Creation

The process of creating a product strategy dramatically illustrates the impact of AI:

*   **Before:** It took weeks of interviewing customers, talking to various internal teams (sales, engineering), intense individual thought, multiple drafts, and rounds of feedback to produce a 10-page document <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:24">[00:02:24]</a>. This involved numerous conference rooms and extensive manual effort <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:44">[00:03:44]</a>.
*   **Now:** The same 10-page product strategy document can be generated in minutes by simply prattling ideas to a tool like ChatGPT Voice while on the way to work <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:53">[00:03:53]</a>. A quick refinement on a laptop completes the task <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:14">[00:04:14]</a>.

### Automation and Efficiency

AI significantly streamlines many tasks traditionally performed by product managers:

*   **Drafting Documents:** Instead of spending days writing and revising strategy documents, AI can scaffold an 80% complete draft in 15 minutes, allowing 45 minutes for sharpening and shipping <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:27">[00:04:27]</a>.
*   **Wireframes & Prototypes:** Manual wireframing on paper or with tools like Balsamiq, followed by waiting for design, is replaced by fully functional prototypes shareable in minutes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:49">[00:04:49]</a>.
*   **Customer Feedback:** Automating insights from customer feedback using no-code tools replaces manually sifting through spreadsheets for ideas and priorities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:08">[00:05:08]</a>.
*   **Presentations:** Slides, which were constantly being made, can now be generated by AI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:20">[00:05:20]</a>.

This increased efficiency means product work takes less time and less raw thought <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:31">[00:05:31]</a>.

## AI's Collapse of the Talent Stack

AI will "collapse the talent stack," meaning jobs that have been separate will start to become one <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:42">[00:11:42]</a>.

### From Product Triad to Triple Threat

Traditionally, product teams operated as a "product triad" of product, engineering, and design leads, often with data <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:41">[00:12:41]</a>. This structure often led to a "handoff mechanism" where functions stayed largely hands-off from what actually got built <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:01">[00:13:01]</a>.

The trend is moving towards a "generalist specialist" model <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:23">[00:13:23]</a>, where specialists in product, design, and engineering are expected to participate across the entire building process and do whatever work is needed to move a project forward <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:30">[00:13:30]</a>. This operating principle is "there are no lanes" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:42">[00:13:42]</a>.

The future will likely see an "AI-powered Triple Threat": one person capable of doing engineering, design, and product as a lead of a team, which includes AI tools, agents, and platforms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:27">[00:14:27]</a>. While they may specialize in one area (engineering, product management, or design), they will need to know how to do it all <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:46">[00:14:46]</a>. This shift allows for smaller teams, or even individuals, to move faster <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:00">[00:15:00]</a>, driving more opinionated [[leadership_and_communication_in_tech | product leadership]].

### Implications for Product Leaders

AI will also impact [[leadership_and_communication_in_tech | product leaders]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:37">[00:16:37]</a>:

*   **Managing New Team Structures:** Leaders must be prepared to manage teams with more commercial and technical [[skills_for_the_evolving_engineering_landscape | skills]], potentially managing people who code (like a CPTO role) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:01">[00:17:01]</a>.
*   **Budgeting:** The approach to budgeting for headcount versus budgeting for agents and tools will change how R&D investment is considered <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:33">[00:17:33]</a>.
*   **Artisanally Crafted Team Topologies:** Teams will be built flexibly for the specific task at hand, moving beyond prescriptive models like the Triad <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:45">[00:17:45]</a>. The focus will be on organizing around motivated individuals with unique "globular" [[skills_for_the_evolving_engineering_landscape | skill]] shapes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:58">[00:17:58]</a>.
*   **Commoditization of Experience and Strategy:** Experience and strategy are becoming commodities, as AI can generate high-quality product visions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:17">[00:18:17]</a>. Leaders must [[skills_for_the_evolving_engineering_landscape | skill up]] and build a "moat" around what they can bring to the table, such as running, building, and scaling these AI-powered teams <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:44">[00:18:44]</a>.

The change is happening rapidly, and leaders need to start preparing now <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:56">[00:18:56]</a>. The mindset should be "AI can help me do anything" rather than "AI will never do this thing" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:22">[00:19:22]</a>.

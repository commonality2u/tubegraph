[[Growth hacking and sustainable growth strategies | Sean Ellis]], who coined the term "growth hacking," defines it as scrutinizing every action's impact on a business's [[SaaS business growth dynamics | growth]] to drive sustainable results, rather than just isolated "hacks" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:14">[01:16:14]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:21:00">[01:21:00]</a>.

## Sean Ellis's Approach to Growth

Ellis, former Head of Growth at Dropbox and Eventbrite and author of *Hacking Growth*, outlines a structured approach to fostering sustainable growth after achieving product-market fit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:31:31">[01:31:31]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:43:08">[01:43:08]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:54:51">[01:54:51]</a>.

### The Growth Flywheel
A key concept is building a "flywheel" around the product's "must-have" value <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:56:56">[01:56:56]</a>. This involves:
1.  **Understanding the "Must-Have" Value** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:49:50">[01:49:50]</a>: Through qualitative research, identify who considers the product a "must-have," how they use it, what problems it solves, and what they used before <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:49:50">[01:49:50]</a>.
2.  **Defining a North Star Metric** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:59:02">[01:59:02]</a>: A metric that captures units of the value being delivered, reflecting how many people are experiencing the product-market fit experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:59:02">[01:59:02]</a>. It should be time-capped (e.g., daily active users) and ideally correlate to revenue growth without being revenue itself <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:14">[01:20:14]</a>.
3.  **Mapping Growth Opportunities** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:24:22">[02:24:22]</a>: Diagramming all ways to grow the North Star metric, focusing on high-leverage opportunities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:24:22">[02:24:22]</a>.

### Prioritizing Growth Efforts
The sequence of focus is crucial:
1.  **Activation** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:03">[00:51:03]</a>: This is often the most critical initial step. Companies should focus on shaping the first user experience to ensure users get to the "aha moment" quickly and easily <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:03">[00:51:03]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:04:01">[02:04:01]</a>. This means getting the right people to the right experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:30:30">[01:30:30]</a>.
    *   **Tactics:** Ask users why they didn't complete activation steps, look for inspiration from other products, and clarify product positioning/messaging <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:10">[01:07:10]</a>.
    *   **Example (LogMeIn):** By focusing on improving the sign-up to usage rate, LogMeIn increased it from 5% to 50% in three months. This allowed previously unprofitable acquisition channels to scale dramatically, with 80% of new users coming from word-of-mouth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:50">[00:55:50]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:08">[01:12:08]</a>.
2.  **Engagement** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:35">[00:51:35]</a>: Reinforce how users can experience the product's benefit more often <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:37:34">[01:37:34]</a>. The "Hooked" model by Nir Eyal provides a framework for improving engagement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:34:40">[01:34:40]</a>.
3.  **Referral** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:35">[00:51:35]</a>: Leverage existing users to bring in more <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:43:43">[01:43:43]</a>.
4.  **Monetization** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:39">[00:51:39]</a>: Optimize the revenue model <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:48:49">[01:48:49]</a>.
5.  **Acquisition** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:47">[00:51:47]</a>: Only obsess over customer acquisition channels once the other internal growth loops are working efficiently <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:48:49">[01:48:49]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:53:01">[01:53:01]</a>. This is because acquisition is highly competitive, and inefficiency in converting, retaining, or monetizing users will make scaling difficult <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:53:01">[01:53:01]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:19:21">[02:19:21]</a>.

## Referral Programs

Referral programs can be powerful [[Marketing strategies and growth | growth strategies]] but are most effective when a product already has strong organic word-of-mouth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:58">[01:14:58]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:15">[01:15:15]</a>. They act as accelerators, not fixes, for products people don't naturally want to talk about <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:15">[01:15:15]</a>.

### Key Learnings from Referral Programs
*   **Dropbox:** The famous Dropbox referral program was developed by Sean Ellis, who believed in leveraging their passionate customer base <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:52">[01:12:52]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:01">[01:13:01]</a>. The program offered incentives on both sides (referrer and referred), a strategy influenced by his work at Zine and discussions with Jamie Simonoff (Ring founder), who had tested similar double-sided programs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:25">[01:13:25]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:51">[01:13:51]</a>.
*   **LogMeIn (Cautionary Tale):** While 80% of LogMeIn's new users came from word-of-mouth, Ellis was hesitant to add incentivized referrals due to the fear of breaking an already robust organic growth engine <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:03">[01:14:03]</a>.
*   **Freemium Model:** For a freemium model to drive word-of-mouth, the free product must be so good that people naturally talk about it. The premium version then needs to be sufficiently better and differentiated for people to upgrade <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:55">[01:15:55]</a>. Many companies fail by making the free version too limited, inhibiting word-of-mouth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:32">[01:16:32]</a>.
*   **Collaborative Products:** Products with a collaborative layer inherently benefit more from referral mechanisms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:55">[01:16:55]</a>.

## Evolution of Growth Strategies
In the early days of growth, simply being data-driven and focused on testing for customer acquisition was enough to win. Most other companies focused on traditional metrics like CPM (cost per mille) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:25:14">[01:25:14]</a>.

Today, however, most online marketers are data- and test-driven, making the landscape much more competitive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:25:51">[01:25:51]</a>. To be competitive now, businesses must be super efficient across **all** parts of the business: conversion, retention, and monetization, in addition to acquisition <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:25:51">[01:25:51]</a>. This requires significant cross-functional collaboration between marketing, product, sales, and customer success teams, which is difficult for later-stage companies to implement retrospectively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:26:55">[01:26:55]</a>. It's often easier to "build it right from the beginning" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:43:21">[01:43:21]</a>.

The rise of AI is expected to further impact [[Marketing strategies and growth | growth strategies]], particularly in modeling potential outcomes of experiments and providing dispassionate recommendations, which can help overcome organizational silos and ego <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:51">[01:29:51]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:32:24">[01:32:24]</a>.

## Key Takeaways
*   **Focus on Value:** Growth should be a byproduct of delivering value to customers. The North Star Metric should reflect units of value delivered, not just revenue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:20">[01:22:20]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:25">[01:22:25]</a>.
*   **Qualitative & Quantitative Blend:** Effective experimentation and strategy come from deeply understanding customer context through both qualitative (surveys, interviews) and quantitative (data, analytics) research <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:11">[01:16:11]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:55">[01:29:55]</a>.
*   **Ask the Right Questions:** The ability to ask the right question at the right time is paramount for uncovering insights and driving growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:33:11">[01:33:11]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:34:04">[01:34:04]</a>.

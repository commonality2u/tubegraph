## AI's Impact on Engineering and Product Development

[[impact_of_ai_on_software_development_and_engineering_roles | AI]] is enabling the creation of novel and incredibly new experiences at unprecedented speeds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:19">[00:49:19]</a>. This directly relates to the [[impact_of_ai_on_product_development | Impact of AI on product development]] and [[impact_of_ai_on_product_management_and_development | Impact of AI on Product Management and Development]].

Even highly skilled "10x engineers" report that [[leveraging_ai_in_product_development | AI]] tools like GitHub Copilot make them "100% more effective and efficient" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:34">[00:50:34]</a>. The fundamental way developers work is shifting, with more time spent reviewing code than writing it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:59">[00:51:59]</a>. This shift frees up cognitive space, allowing developers to tackle more complex tasks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:50">[00:52:50]</a>.

However, the [[impact_of_ai_tools_on_future_product_development_skills | impact of AI tools on future product development skills]] also introduces new considerations:
*   **Reliance and Over-reliance** What does this mean for developer reliance on code and potential over-reliance on AI tools? <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:17">[00:53:17]</a>
*   **Learning** How does AI affect the learning process for new codebases or languages, especially for novices compared to experts? <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:22">[00:53:22]</a>
*   **Measurement** The change in mental models, friction, and cognitive load due to AI means that traditional productivity metrics need to be re-evaluated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:11">[00:53:11]</a>.

It's crucial to adopt holistic and balanced metrics to avoid oversimplification when assessing productivity with [[the_role_of_ai_in_product_management | AI]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:36">[00:53:36]</a>. The focus should not just be on cutting task time in half, but on enabling developers to do harder things <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:41">[00:52:41]</a>.

## Future Considerations for Measuring Productivity with AI

The SPACE framework, which measures productivity across five dimensions (Satisfaction and Well-being, Performance, Activity, Communication and Collaboration, and Efficiency and Flow), is likely to evolve with the integration of AI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:14">[00:51:14]</a>. Existing dimensions like satisfaction, performance, and activity will likely remain, but new dimensions such as "trust" or "reliability" may be added to account for the relationship with AI-enabled tools <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:29">[00:51:29]</a>.

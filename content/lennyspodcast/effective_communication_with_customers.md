## Listening Over Pitching
A core principle of [[talking_to_customers_for_product_development | talking to customers for product development]] is to listen rather than pitch <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:45">[00:20:45]</a>.
Jeff notes that people don't want to be pitched immediately; they have problems they want to share <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:56">[00:20:56]</a>. Starting a conversation by immediately showcasing a product risks missing the customer's most pressing needs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:23">[00:21:23]</a>.

> "People don't want to be pitched... I'm the customer. I have so many problems. How are you sort of guessing ahead of time what is my top problem and now that you've anchored and limited to the pitch you're you're going to miss you prent very likely going to miss the burning problem that they have on the top of their on the like on the top of their mind" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:56">[00:20:56]</a>

### Techniques for Eliciting Information
Instead of pitching, Jeff suggests:
*   **Sitting in silence** and waiting for the customer to open up about their burning problems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:20">[00:21:20]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:17">[00:22:17]</a>.
*   **Asking open-ended questions** to prompt honest responses:
    *   "Do you mind just opening up your email, like what what's in there?" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:31">[00:22:31]</a>
    *   "If you weren't talking to me right now, what would you be working on?" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:35">[00:22:35]</a>
    *   "Last week, what grinded your gears?" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:40">[00:22:40]</a>
    *   "Magic wand, what do you wish we you could just like have off your plate immediately?" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:43">[00:22:43]</a>
    *   Asking about their "regular life as a human" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:15">[00:45:15]</a>.
*   **Using a "forcing function"** like a price to gauge true need <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:27">[00:39:27]</a>. If a customer says "for $10,000, it would need to solve X," it reveals the true value they seek <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:45">[00:39:45]</a>.
    *   Jeff even asks founders to "practice paying" him a dollar through their payment service to experience the process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:54">[00:43:54]</a>.

## Direct Engagement and Responsiveness
Jeff emphasizes the [[proximity_and_engagement_with_customers | proximity and engagement with customers]] by actively breaking down the wall between product managers and customers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:30">[00:24:30]</a>.

*   **Speed of response:** Responding quickly to customer feedback is seen as an "unbelievable gift" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:37">[00:29:37]</a>. Jeff would even leave a meeting to send a quick message to a customer who reached out <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:34">[00:30:34]</a>. This creates a "secret portal" between the customer and a human at the company <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:55">[00:30:55]</a>.
*   **Accessibility:** Jeff shares his email constantly and is "text message friendly" with key customers to get direct signal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:59">[00:26:59]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:46">[00:28:46]</a>. He encourages cold emails and Loom videos of bugs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:47:04">[02:47:04]</a>.
*   **Turning detractors into promoters:** When a customer has a bad experience, the goal is not just to solve the problem but to turn them into a promoter <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:33">[00:31:33]</a>. Jeff shares an example where a founder found a serious bug in Stripe Atlas legal documents; by quickly fixing it and showing care, that founder became a valuable "product friend" for life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:39">[00:31:39]</a>.

### Filtering and Prioritization
While embracing all feedback, it's essential to prioritize.
*   **Focus on paying customers:** Jeff's rule is to "discount all of that feedback from our friends to zero" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:56">[00:38:56]</a> and only pay attention to feedback from actual paying customers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:56">[00:36:56]</a>. This ensures focus on those who truly need the solution and are willing to exchange value for it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:03">[00:38:03]</a>.
*   **Self-selection:** Ask customers for effort (e.g., screenshots, videos, bullet points in an email) to self-select those genuinely invested in providing detailed feedback <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:37">[00:34:37]</a>.
*   **"Bugfinder program":** Jeff invented a "bugfinder program" on Twitter, setting a limited timeframe for video submissions, which generated significant, valuable feedback <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:50">[00:35:50]</a>.

## The Entire Experience as "Product"
Jeff believes the "product" extends beyond software to include the entire customer journey <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:28:01">[02:28:01]</a>.

*   **Incorporating non-software touchpoints:** This includes sales processes, support interactions, and even physical mail (like FedExing documents with clear instructions) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:29:01">[02:29:01]</a>.
*   **"Users having a bad day" metric:** A powerful internal metric is tracking "users having a bad day" – any instance where a user encounters a problem (e.g., 404 error, delayed payout, payment declines) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:05">[01:01:05]</a>. This provides immediate, actionable insight into customer pain points <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:16">[01:03:16]</a>.

## Cultivating Empathy and Understanding
[[understanding_customer_emotions_in_product_design | Understanding customer emotions in product design]] and fostering empathy are vital.

*   **Study Groups:** At Stripe, Jeff introduced "study groups" where 4-8 employees (from any department) pretend to be a customer with a problem <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:49">[01:16:49]</a>.
    *   **Rule 1: "You do no longer work at Stripe"** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:46">[01:17:46]</a> – participants must avoid using internal knowledge or lingo to truly experience the product as an outsider <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:49">[01:18:49]</a>.
    *   **Rule 2: "We're not here to solve any problems"** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:36:47">[01:36:47]</a> – the focus is solely on practicing empathy and understanding the customer's journey <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:36">[01:19:36]</a>.
    *   This exercise helps teams realize how challenging certain flows can be and provides direct, undeniable insights into user experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:29">[01:20:29]</a>.
*   **Improv training:** Jeff suggests improv training to help people get "out of their skin" and practice better communication skills, especially in customer interactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:45">[00:40:45]</a>.
*   **The "customer is sitting here" mentality:** Jeff's father taught him that "you can screw up a sentence if it begins with the customer" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:19:01">[02:19:01]</a>. He would literally place an empty chair in a meeting, stating, "the customer is sitting here," to ensure discussions remained customer-centric <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:19:32">[02:19:32]</a>.

## Benefits of Effective Customer Communication
*   **Identifying burning problems:** This approach helps uncover truly critical customer needs that, if solved, lead to intense loyalty and organic growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:15">[00:19:15]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:59">[00:23:59]</a>.
*   **Informing roadmaps:** Direct customer feedback can quickly generate a product roadmap, eliminating the need for long surveys or lengthy build cycles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:02">[00:24:02]</a>.
*   **Motivating teams:** When engineers and other team members hear directly from customers, it's highly motivating and fosters a shared mission to solve real problems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:57">[00:52:57]</a>.
*   **Product-market fit:** Strong customer engagement, combined with metrics showing things are "going up and to the right," is the ultimate indicator of product-market fit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:55">[00:30:55]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:09">[00:46:09]</a>.
*   **Economic viability:** Products solving burning needs result in customers willing to pay fair prices, ensuring the company's long-term sustainability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:46">[00:26:46]</a>. The [[importance_of_talking_to_customers_in_startups | importance of talking to customers in startups]] is highlighted for economic viability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:09:22">[02:09:22]</a>.

## What is an Inflection?
An inflection is an external event that has the potential to radically change how people think, feel, and act <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:31">[00:15:31]</a>. Inflections occur outside of any specific startup or company <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:41">[00:15:41]</a>. In mathematics, an inflection is a turning point on a curve where the slope changes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:11">[00:16:11]</a>. In tech startups, it signifies a moment when something new is introduced that enables new empowerment for the first time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:16">[00:16:16]</a>. For instance, Moore's Law or a general improvement curve is not an inflection; it must be a distinct turning point <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:01">[00:16:01]</a>.

### Importance of Inflections
Inflections are crucial because they allow founders to wage asymmetric warfare on the present <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:10">[00:00:10]</a>. They provide the "unlock" for opportune timing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:32">[00:16:32]</a>. A startup cannot defeat a large company by merely executing better, as big companies have advantages in management, experience, customers, suppliers, partners, and brand <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:30">[00:31:30]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:31:21">[01:31:21]</a>. Instead, startups win by proposing a radically different future that disorients incumbents and chaotically shifts people towards that new future <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:35">[00:31:35]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:31:37">[01:31:37]</a>. Inflections provide the underlying forces that enable an idea to escape the gravitational pull of the present <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:07">[00:15:07]</a>.

## Types and Examples of Inflections
Inflections are constantly around us, but most people are too preoccupied with daily life to notice them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:39:39">[01:39:39]</a>. Not every power or new technology gets used or recognized immediately; for example, the wheel was used horizontally for 500 years before being mounted vertically for transportation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:00">[01:00:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:37">[00:18:37]</a>.

Inflections can be categorized as:
*   **Technological Inflections** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:30">[00:26:30]</a>:
    *   **iPhone 4S with GPS chip**: This enabled Lyft, as riders and drivers could accurately locate each other for the first time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:49">[00:15:49]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:34">[00:16:34]</a>. The same inflection also powered DoorDash and Instacart <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:13">[00:18:13]</a>.
    *   **Improved Smartphone Cameras, Wi-Fi, and Upload Speed**: These converged to enable Instagram by making it possible for smartphones to capture photos good enough to replace digital cameras <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:10">[00:17:10]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:27">[00:17:27]</a>.
    *   **Broadband Penetration and CDNs**: These made widespread live video streaming possible, enabling companies like Twitch (which evolved from Justin.tv) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:37">[00:23:37]</a>.
    *   **Large Language Models (LLMs)**: Specific LLMs are considered inflections because they represent a new specific thing that empowers people in particular ways <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:58">[00:20:58]</a>.
*   **Regulatory Inflections**:
    *   **Changes in Telemedicine Laws (during COVID)**: Laws shifted to permit telemedicine visits across state lines and allow for reimbursement by healthcare systems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:14">[00:25:14]</a>. This empowered patients to access more doctors and doctors to reach more patients <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:38">[00:25:38]</a>.
*   **Belief/Social Inflections**:
    *   **Shift in Trust towards Online Reviews and Facebook Connect**: The proliferation of customer reviews online and people's trust in them, combined with Facebook Connect making profile information visible, helped Airbnb overcome the "stranger danger" perception for guests and hosts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:27">[00:24:27]</a>.
    *   **COVID-induced Telemedicine Adoption**: Due to widespread usage during the pandemic, people's beliefs permanently shifted, making them more willing to use telemedicine <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:35">[00:26:35]</a>.
    *   **Acceptance of Remote Work**: The idea of working from home multiple days a week became a permanent condition due to COVID, serving as another inflection <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:28">[00:27:28]</a>.
    *   **User-Generated Content and Internet Celebrities**: The rise of platforms like YouTube and the concept of "you" as the person of the year (Time Magazine) indicated a turning point in entertainment consumption <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:09">[00:23:09]</a>.

## Stress Testing Inflections
Founders can evaluate an inflection by asking critical questions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:08">[00:21:08]</a>:
1.  What specific new thing was just introduced? <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:36">[00:21:36]</a>
2.  How does it empower people? <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:41">[00:21:41]</a>
3.  How does it specifically empower specific people in specific ways? <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:44">[00:21:44]</a>
4.  Under what conditions might that empowerment be realized or not realized? <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:47">[00:21:47]</a> (e.g., will regulations revert, will customers reject it?) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:51">[00:21:51]</a>

For Lyft, the iPhone 4S with GPS was the new thing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:21">[00:22:21]</a>. It allowed locating anyone with a smartphone within one meter <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:28">[00:22:28]</a>. The empowerment conditions were that people would share their location, governments wouldn't outlaw GPS in phones, and Apple would continue shipping GPS chips <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:36">[00:22:36]</a>. These were considered reasonable bets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:51">[00:22:51]</a>.

## Inflections and Startup Strategy
The "rock" of an inflection, combined with the "slingshot" of an [[Founders insights and their importance in startup success | insight]], allows a startup to play an unfair game by unfair rules that favor them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:45">[00:00:45]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:10">[00:52:10]</a>. A startup's ability to win relies on harnessing inflections and presenting a radically different future that avoids the comparison trap <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:00">[00:39:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:33">[00:12:33]</a>. When Lyft and UberX launched ride-sharing, nobody compared it to taxis because the difference was self-evident <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:42">[00:12:42]</a>.

Instead of analyzing large markets and building solutions for unserved needs, which keeps a company within the incumbent's defined market <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:25">[00:45:25]</a>, founders should seek ambiguous opportunities that harness powerful inflections and compelling [[Founders insights and their importance in startup success | insights]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:53">[00:45:53]</a>. It's often better to have the right insight and inflections, even if the initial implementation isn't perfect, because a correct insight allows navigation to the right product-market fit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:50">[00:14:50]</a>.

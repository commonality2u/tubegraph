## [[product_and_sales_collaboration | Product and Sales Team Collaboration]]

Effective collaboration between product and sales teams is crucial for the success of B2B businesses. This partnership ensures that product development aligns with customer needs and market demands, and that sales efforts are supported by a strong, relevant product.

### Product Team Involvement in Sales

Product leaders, especially Heads of Product or VPs of Product, should be deeply involved in sales processes within B2B organizations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:10:22">[02:10:22]</a>.

Key aspects of this involvement include:
*   **Direct Customer Interaction** Product leaders should meet customers, particularly in significant deals. This allows them to understand unique customer needs and potential product deconstructions or adaptations required for closing large deals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:10:40">[02:10:40]</a>. A Head of Product's presence can be crucial in preventing the loss of major deals by offering on-the-spot commitments for UI tweaks or future development that a sales representative wouldn't be empowered to make <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:11:06">[02:11:06]</a>.
*   **Product Expertise and Gravitas** A "magical VP of Product" who truly owns the roadmap and possesses the gravitas to work with large customers becomes a "mini CEO" in sales meetings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:11:30">[02:11:30]</a>. They possess deep product knowledge, understand how to connect disparate features, and can make commitments that sales leaders cannot <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:11:57">[02:11:57]</a>.
*   **Championing the Customer** Product teams should be the voice of the customer, ensuring that product decisions are customer-centric <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:30:28">[03:30:28]</a>. This includes advocating for customer-friendly practices like flexible payment terms (e.g., monthly vs. annual contracts) and longer trial lengths <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:30:00">[03:30:00]</a>.

### Sales Team Involvement in Product Development

The sales team's involvement in the product roadmap and feature requests can be a source of tension for product managers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:13:05">[03:13:05]</a>. However, this tension is a sign of a well-run B2B company that is actively engaged in deals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:18:00">[03:18:00]</a>.

Strategies for managing [[product_and_sales_collaboration | sales collaboration]] with product teams:
*   **Budget Allocation for Feature Requests** Allocate a specific budget (e.g., 10% of story points) to the Head of Sales each quarter for feature requests <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:13:46">[03:13:46]</a>. This approach radically changes how sales prioritizes requests because they are given control and accountability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:13:56">[03:13:56]</a>.
*   **Structured Communication** Hold weekly meetings where the Head of Sales and Head of Product discuss this budget <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:16:05">[03:16:05]</a>. This forces the sales team to prioritize and "force rank" their needs, often leading to different priorities than initially expected <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:19:20">[03:19:20]</a>.
*   **Clarity on Commitments** Product teams should objectively communicate what is currently being built and what is planned for future quarters. Changes to in-process features should be avoided or understood to have a high cost and potential for disruption <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:16:12">[03:16:12]</a>.
*   **Empowerment of Leadership** Individual sales representatives should not be empowered to make roadmap commitments directly with product managers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:17:51">[03:17:51]</a>. Instead, they should escalate requests to their manager, who then discusses them with the product leadership <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:18:53">[03:18:53]</a>. This maintains structure and prevents the product team from being overwhelmed by numerous individual requests <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:18:43">[03:18:43]</a>.

### Challenges and Principles for Effective Collaboration

*   **Differing Time Horizons** Sales typically focuses on the next 12 months, while product needs to look 2-5 years into the future <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:15:37">[03:15:37]</a>. Effective collaboration requires understanding and balancing these different time horizons.
*   **Avoiding "Weaponization"** Be cautious about "weaponizing" product or customer success teams by tying their KPIs directly to revenue from experimental features or upselling. This can lead to customer-hostile practices and damage long-term customer relationships and retention <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:22:11">[03:22:11]</a>.
*   **Earning Price Increases** When product teams contribute to price increases, they should ensure the product has delivered significantly more value to justify the increase <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:53:33">[03:53:33]</a>.
*   **The "Year of the Customer"** Product leaders should prioritize making customers happier, viewing this as a core driver of success and inspiration for the entire company <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:40:00">[04:40:00]</a>. Shipping "great things" rather than just "good things" can inspire entire teams <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:41:15">[04:41:15]</a>.

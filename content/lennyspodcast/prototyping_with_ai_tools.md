## The Shift in Product Development with AI

[[impact_of_ai_on_product_development | AI tools]] have fundamentally reshaped the approach to product development by enabling a much faster iteration cycle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:55">[00:05:55]</a>.

### The Rise of "Prompt Sets"

In the current era, "prompt sets are the new PRDs" (Product Requirements Documents) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:24">[00:15:24]</a>. This means product builders are encouraged to create prototypes and prompt sets for new projects and features, leading to a "much more high bandwidth way of communication" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:48">[00:23:48]</a>. The mantra has become "demos before memos" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:59">[00:23:59]</a>.

### Accelerated Iteration and Deployment

The "time to first demo is much shorter" with [[leveraging_ai_tools_in_product_management | AI tools]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:10">[00:24:10]</a>. However, the time to full deployment might take longer, creating an uneven cadence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:15">[00:24:15]</a>. While the inner loop of prototyping, iterating, and even user research through AI conversations is shortened, the bar for scaling becomes much higher <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:31">[00:24:31]</a>.

### The Role of Taste-Making and Editing

With a "massive increase in supply of ideas in prototypes" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:47">[00:24:47]</a>, it becomes crucial to have "editorial and taste making at the heart of it" to avoid a "Frankenstein product" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:17">[00:00:17]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:41">[00:25:41]</a>. This "editing function" is vital in sifting through the abundance of ideas and ensuring quality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:32">[00:29:32]</a>.

## The Frontier Program: Living in the Future

Microsoft is operationalizing a "Frontier program" to enable teams to "live one year in the future" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:16">[00:11:16]</a>. This involves setting up experimental environments where teams can use cutting-edge [[ai_product_development_and_prototyping | AI tools]] and deep research agents <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:34">[00:12:34]</a>. The goal is to understand how work changes when all advanced AI tools are available, pushing teams to think about "what does a frontier product look like" and "how does a frontier way of working" change <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:06">[00:12:06]</a>.

This approach allows companies to roll out "cutting edge experimental features" to early adopters without waiting for company-wide readiness, crucial in the "compressed cycles of AI" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:37">[00:10:37]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:23">[00:13:23]</a>.

## The Emergence of NLX (Natural Language Experience)

The shift from graphical user interfaces (GUI) to natural language interfaces (NLX) is profound. NLX is considered "the new UX" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:15">[00:18:15]</a>. While graphical interfaces are rigid and explicitly designed, conversational interfaces are more elastic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:32">[00:18:32]</a>.

It's a common misconception that with [[ai_in_product_development | AI]], "the model eats the products," implying no design is needed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:45">[00:18:45]</a>. However, natural language interactions, like conversations, still possess "grammars, structures, and UI elements," albeit invisible ones <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:00">[00:19:00]</a>.

New constructs in natural language as an interface include:
*   **Prompts**: A new UI element <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:22">[00:19:22]</a>.
*   **Plans**: When an agent provides an editable plan in response to a high-level goal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:38">[00:19:38]</a>.
*   **Showing the Work**: Displaying the AI's thought process or progress, balancing verbosity and conciseness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:49">[00:19:49]</a>.
*   **Proactive Follow-ups**: AI suggesting next logical steps or actions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:11">[00:21:11]</a>.

## The Continuous Evolution of AI Capabilities

A significant challenge for product builders is "updating the priors" about what [[ai_in_product_development | AI models]] can do <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:04">[00:32:04]</a>. What was impossible a few months or a year ago (e.g., image generation without spelling errors, deeper reasoning) might now be feasible. Product builders must continuously set high expectations for AI and "demand more of the AI today" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:07">[00:33:07]</a>.

## Key Considerations for Prototyping with AI

### Solve Before Scale
When creating "zero to one" products, it's crucial to prioritize "solve before scale" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:28">[00:38:28]</a>.
*   **Embrace Chaos**: Be comfortable with wide directional shifts in the "solve stage" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:52">[00:38:52]</a>. Prematurely fixing on one direction can lead to long-term struggles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:26">[00:39:26]</a>.
*   **Beware of Premature Metrics**: For new products, relying on "grown-up metrics" too early can lead to "false precision" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:54">[00:39:54]</a>. Qualitative feedback and understanding core utility are more valuable initially <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:38">[00:40:38]</a>.

### The "Why Now?" Framework
For a product idea to be truly compelling, at least two of these three inflection points should be present:
1.  **Technology Shift**: A significant leap in underlying technology (e.g., deep learning for Google Lens, large language models (LLMs) and reasoning models today) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:59">[00:41:59]</a>.
2.  **Consumer Behavior Shift**: A change in user habits or how people interact with technology (e.g., phones as primary finance tools, cameras as "keyboards for the world") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:36">[00:42:36]</a>.
3.  **Business Model Shift**: An innovative way to monetize the product or service (e.g., zero-fee trading models, new [[ai_product_development_and_prototyping | AI monetization]] strategies like usage or outcome-based fees) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:30">[00:43:30]</a>.

## Core Interviewing Principles

### Focus on Stories, Not Direct Questions
A common mistake in [[Interview Techniques for Identifying Customer Needs | interviewing]] is asking direct, out-of-context questions (e.g., "What do you like to watch on Netflix?") [00:14:35](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:35">[00:14:35]</a>. While people can answer these, the responses are often unreliable, do not reflect actual behavior, and lack crucial context and nuance [00:14:47](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:47">[00:14:47]</a>, [00:14:56](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:56">[00:14:56]</a>.

Instead, ask customers to "tell me about the last time you..." [00:15:14](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:14">[00:15:14]</a>. This approach allows you to:
*   **Collect rich stories:** Grounding the conversation in specific instances provides more reliable information [00:15:30](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:30">[00:15:30]</a>.
*   **Uncover unmet needs:** You'll hear about pain points and desires, some of which the customer may not even be consciously aware of [00:15:34](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:34">[00:15:34]</a>, [00:15:39](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:39">[00:15:39]</a>.
*   **Stay curious:** The interviewer's role is to guide the narrative by asking "what happened next?" and "tell me about that" to dig deeper into moments of friction or delight [00:38:32](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:32">[00:38:32]</a>, [00:39:03](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:03">[00:39:03]</a>.
*   **Focus on past behavior:** Understand what people *actually did*, not what they *would do* or *think* they do [00:40:58](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:58">[00:40:58]</a>.

### Embrace a Conversational Cadence
An [[Interview Techniques for Identifying Customer Needs | interview]] should feel like a natural conversation, akin to having a beer with a buddy [00:36:56](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:56">[00:36:56]</a>. Avoid rigid, long lists of questions [00:36:38](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:38">[00:36:38]</a>. A simple technique is to start with one open-ended question and then follow the customer's story, asking follow-up questions to elicit more detail [00:37:38](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:38">[00:37:38]</a>.

The interviewer should actively listen to the interviewee to identify moments to explore further, rather than worrying about the next question on a list [00:39:51](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:51">[00:39:51]</a>.

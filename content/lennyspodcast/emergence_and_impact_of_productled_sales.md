## Defining [[Understanding ProductLed Sales | Product-Led Sales]]

[[Understanding ProductLed Sales | Product-led sales]] fundamentally leverages product usage to generate leads for sales <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:36:06">[02:36:06]</a>. It is seen as a version of the "Bottoms Up" approach, where the product itself generates pipeline by putting pressure on products to generate leads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:29:50">[02:29:50]</a>.

A key aspect is banking on "hand raisers" and product qualification criteria to facilitate closing enterprise-level contracts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:50:00">[02:50:00]</a>. It begins with an acquisition, whether marketing or product-led, and some form of self-serve usage (e.g., freemium or self-serve monetization) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:08:44">[03:08:44]</a>.

## The Mechanics of [[Understanding ProductLed Sales | Product-Led Sales]]

The process of [[Understanding ProductLed Sales | product-led sales]] involves several steps:
1.  **Usage Generation** The product must be able to generate usage without direct human involvement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:24:00">[03:24:00]</a>.
2.  **Network Effects within Organizations** Companies look for signs of network effects within an organization's user base or team, indicating how closely they are approaching a point where the entire enterprise would benefit from the product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:39:30">[03:39:30]</a>.
3.  **Product Qualification Model** Users are scored based on various metrics:
    *   Volume of usage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:04:02">[03:04:02]</a>
    *   Velocity of usage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:04:06">[03:04:06]</a>
    *   Breadth of feature use <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:07:07">[03:07:07]</a>
    *   Behavioral tactics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:09:00">[03:09:00]</a>
    *   Demographics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:10:00">[03:10:00]</a>
    This scoring helps understand the probability of successfully selling to the entire organization based on its current usage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:17:00">[03:17:00]</a>.
4.  **Sales Involvement** Sales teams become involved when:
    *   **Hand Raisers** Users organically generate sales leads by completing a form or expressing interest <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:28:00">[03:28:00]</a>.
    *   **Proactive Outbound** Sales identifies users who act like hand raisers but haven't explicitly reached out, then initiates contact to find an enterprise buyer or get introduced to one through the user <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:44:00">[03:44:00]</a>.

In essence, [[Understanding ProductLed Sales | product-led sales]] uses the product itself as the sales enablement material <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:59:00">[03:59:00]</a>.

## Avoiding [[Common Pitfalls and Benchmarks in ProductLed Sales | Pitfalls]] in Growth Strategy

Companies that start with a product-led growth (PLG) model often face the pitfall of "crushing" their PLG efforts when they introduce sales teams to go upmarket <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:56:00">[02:56:00]</a>. This happens when companies, chasing large average contract values (ACVs), over-correct by doubling down on sales hires and enterprise marketing, while neglecting the core product usage growth and user habitual loops <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:00:00">[03:00:00]</a>.

### The "Layering Game"
Successful companies execute both [[Understanding ProductLed Sales | product-led]] and sales-led strategies correctly and together, rather than viewing them as an either/or decision <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:00">[00:37:00]</a>. If a company is sales-led, it should overlay [[Understanding ProductLed Sales | product-led]] on top to amplify existing growth, not abandon the sales-led approach <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:00">[00:24:00]</a>. The game is a sequential layering game, introducing one motion and then building the next on top <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:29:00">[02:29:00]</a>.

### Prioritization Mistakes
A critical mistake for PLG companies is when their product roadmap starts to focus exclusively on enterprise features, leading to the neglect of user growth and delight <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:05:00">[03:05:00]</a>. This disconnect can lead to a massive slowdown in enterprise pipeline because the continuous growth of user base, community, and habitual loops—the original DNA of the growth—is neglected <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:40:00">[03:40:00]</a>.

## The Role of Freemium in [[Understanding ProductLed Sales | Product-Led Sales]]

[[Understanding ProductLed Sales | Product-led sales]] often benefits from a strong freemium model. Freemium allows users to use a product in some free capacity, typically with feature or usage walls, but without a time limit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:12:00">[04:12:00]</a>. This contrasts with trials, which are time-bound <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:28:00">[04:28:00]</a>.

### Freemium vs. Trial
While freemium and trial both offer free usage, trials create "superficial time bounds" that can alienate large enterprises, as the time needed for value realization varies greatly by company size and complexity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:30:00">[04:30:00]</a>. A "usage-based freemium," like MongoDB's free cluster for playing around, is preferable as it allows users to experience the product without time pressure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:55:00">[04:55:00]</a>.

### Strategic Reasons for Freemium
Freemium should not be viewed solely as a conversion rate optimization tactic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:57:00">[04:57:00]</a>. Strategic reasons for offering freemium include:
*   **Indirect Monetization** – e.g., generating virality or network effects, where free users acquire other users <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:34:00">[04:34:00]</a>.
*   **Proof of Concept (POC)** – allowing users to test the product's core value without commitment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:11:00">[04:11:00]</a>.
*   **Strategic Land** – entering a segment that isn't immediately monetized but offers future expansion potential <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:43:00">[04:43:00]</a>.
*   **Commoditization** – offering a free version when the basic functionality has become commoditized in the market <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:47:00">[04:47:00]</a>.
*   **Breeding Ground for New Use Cases/Personas** – discovering adjacent opportunities and informing the product roadmap <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:09:00">[04:09:00]</a>.
*   **Data Collection** – gathering data on usage to build and improve paid products, as seen with LinkedIn <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:25:00">[04:25:00]</a>.

Ultimately, anything that creates friction for the growth model should be gated in paid features, while anything that promotes the growth model should be free <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:11:00">[04:11:00]</a>.

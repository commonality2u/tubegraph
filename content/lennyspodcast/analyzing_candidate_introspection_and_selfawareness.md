Interviewers often seek to understand a candidate's level of introspection and self-awareness to gauge their capacity for personal and professional growth, their ability to navigate challenges, and their authenticity. This involves asking questions that prompt candidates to reflect on their past experiences, motivations, and perceived strengths and weaknesses.

## Key Questions and Insights

Several interview questions are designed to reveal a candidate's self-awareness:

She looks for individuals who have genuinely reflected on their journey and the reasons behind their current position <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:50">[00:03:50]</a>.

### Fast forward three years, what's different about you?

He specifically looks for candidates who are open about areas they need to develop <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:40">[00:13:40]</a>. He also generally looks for [[Authenticity and Curiosity in Leadership | curiosity]] throughout interviews <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:52">[00:13:52]</a>.

### What would your siblings/parents say about you?
Melum Quan Berwitz, Head of Growth at Deel, asks this question to understand what candidates believe others think of them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:58">[00:14:58]</a>. ### Tell me about a time something went wrong. What was it? What did you do about it?

### Tell me something you did that worked out but not for the reason that you thought it would work, or tell me something you did that was a good decision that didn't work.

### What have you learned about yourself that reveals a limitation in how you work?

### Do you consider yourself lucky?

## Unveiling Authenticity and Self-Reflection

*   **Breaking the interview mindset:** Neel Singhal, VP of Product at Facebook, uses questions like "What's something that everyone takes for granted that you think is essentially hogwash or inaccurate?" to break the typical interview mindset <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:54">[00:16:54]</a>. This type of question requires candidates to be genuinely opinionated, revealing their authenticity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:22">[00:17:22]</a>.
*   **Reference calls:** Paul Adams, Chief Product Officer at Intercom, suggests asking references, "What feedback will I be giving this person in their first performance review?" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:11">[00:20:11]</a> This question is effective because it's difficult for the referee to dodge and provides honest insights into areas for growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:17">[00:20:17]</a>.

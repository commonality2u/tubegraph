## Empathy in Product Development and Marketing

For product professionals, [[Using empathy in presentations | empathy]] means recognizing that products are created *for* other people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:29">[00:14:29]</a>. This perspective dictates that if customers find something confusing or difficult, such as needing to "read the manual" (RTFM), it's a mistake on the part of the creator, not the user <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:43">[00:14:43]</a>.

Instead, the goal is to delight the user <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:36">[00:07:36]</a>. This requires:
*   **Understanding user needs** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:16">[00:17:16]</a>.
*   **Making a difficult and remarkable promise** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:22">[00:17:22]</a>.
*   **Consistently keeping that promise** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:25">[00:17:25]</a>.

This approach ensures that what is shipped delights the intended audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:49">[00:07:49]</a>. Without such a focus, companies risk building products that fail to meet user expectations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:39">[00:24:39]</a>.

### Example: Google's Early Success
Google's initial success, particularly its two-button homepage interface, was rooted in a promise of efficiency and directness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:57">[00:30:57]</a>. This design, contrasting with competitors like Yahoo's 183 links, communicated: "We are smart enough to take you where you want to go without you spending a lot of time browsing" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:20">[00:31:20]</a>. This clear, empathetic promise built a strong brand and encouraged word-of-mouth promotion, as users could confidently recommend it to others <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:44">[00:31:44]</a>.

## Empathy in Leadership
[[The role of engineers in leadership | Leadership]] requires painting a picture of the future that is rooted in [[Using empathy in presentations | empathy]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:31">[00:43:31]</a>. It's not about the leader's personal vision, but about understanding where the audience or team wants to go <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:38">[00:43:38]</a>. The role of the leader is to open the door and help people achieve their existing aspirations, rather than trying to change what they want <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:00">[00:44:00]</a>. This "non-narcissism" is central to how [[The role of engineers in leadership | professionals]] operate effectively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:41">[00:43:41]</a>.

This model often led to a "handoff mechanism" where roles stayed separate, limiting direct involvement in the end product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:01">[00:13:01]</a>.

The emerging model emphasizes a "generalist specialist" approach, where individuals are expected to "participate across the building process" and "do the work that needs to get done" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:23">[00:13:23]</a>. This concept is encapsulated by the operating principle: "there are no lanes" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:42">[00:13:42]</a>.

The future envisions an "**AI powered Triple Threat**": a single individual capable of leading a team by performing engineering, design, and product functions, augmented by [[building_a_highgrowth_ai_company | AI]] tools, agents, and platforms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:26">[00:14:26]</a>. The speaker shares an anecdote about "Cody," who, despite a non-[[product_management_in_tech_companies | product]] background, leveraged [[building_a_highgrowth_ai_company | AI]] to learn [[product_management_in_tech_companies | product management]], design, and even contribute to front-end code, embodying this "triple threat" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:03">[00:09:03]</a>.

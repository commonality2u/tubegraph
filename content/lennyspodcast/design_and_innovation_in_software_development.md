## Linear's Approach to Craft and Quality

Linear, an issue tracking tool, places immense focus on craft and quality in its product development <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:10">[00:01:10]</a>. This focus stems from the belief that collaboration thrives when products are intuitive and frictionless <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:01">[00:08:01]</a>.

Key aspects of Linear's approach include:
*   **Prioritizing Taste and Opinions** Instead of relying heavily on A/B tests or metric-based goals, Linear prioritizes taste and opinions in its product decisions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:10">[00:01:10]</a>. While company-level goals (e.g., weekly active users) exist, specific features are not tied to numerical targets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:41">[00:43:41]</a>. The aim is to solve problems and ensure customers appreciate the solution, rather than just driving metrics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:56">[00:44:56]</a>.
*   **Balancing Perfection with Speed** The team acknowledges that quality sometimes pushes timelines, but typically by days, not by requiring complete reworks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:27">[00:08:27]</a>. They avoid perfecting designs upfront because the building phase itself often reveals better solutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:12">[00:09:12]</a>.
*   **Iterative Release Strategy** Features are initially released to internal teams for testing, then to small groups of opt-in customers. While these early versions might be "janky," this approach facilitates early feedback and improvement before a full general release, where polish and craft become paramount <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:03">[00:09:03]</a>. This contrasts with the perception that Linear products launch fully polished <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:03">[00:11:03]</a>.
*   **Decentralized Ownership and Review** Linear avoids extensive formal reviews, empowering project teams (engineers and designers) with ownership <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:02">[00:12:02]</a>. This allows them to identify and implement improvements, such as dynamic safe zones for right-click menus, which might not be explicitly mandated by management <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:11">[00:15:11]</a>. Design reviews often involve internal demos and personal testing by leaders like the CEO, focusing on the overall feel and execution before public release <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:08">[00:34:08]</a>.
*   **Hiring for Product Sensibility** The emphasis on quality is deeply embedded in the company's DNA, starting with the founders <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:00">[00:13:00]</a>. Linear actively seeks individuals who care about [[innovating_and_avoiding_process_overdependence | product craft]] and can articulate well-reasoned opinions about design and product choices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:56">[00:58:56]</a>. This ensures that every team member contributes to and values product quality.

## The Linear Method: [[Innovative Product Development Strategies | Opinionated Software]] and Cycles

Linear promotes a unique way of [[building_and_evolving_a_software_product | building software]] called the "Linear Method," which emphasizes building opinionated software and working in cycles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:41">[00:38:41]</a>.

### Opinionated Software
Opinionated software provides strong defaults and workflows, reducing the need for users to configure or "figure things out" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:27">[00:40:27]</a>. This approach aims to maximize user productivity by letting them focus on their work rather than the tool <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:40">[00:40:40]</a>. It's about designing a highly optimized solution for a specific "someone" rather than a generalized solution for "everyone" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:47">[00:40:47]</a>.

### Cycles
Similar to sprints but without the emphasis on "sprinting," cycles are optional, automated timeframes (e.g., one or two weeks) during which teams commit to a specific set of priorities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:31">[00:41:31]</a>. This helps teams manage the "infinite list of things to do" by providing focus and clarity, allowing them to explain why they might pivot to urgent tasks if necessary <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:51">[00:41:51]</a>.

## Lean Product Management and Customer Understanding

Linear operates with a remarkably lean product management structure, having only one Head of Product for a company of about 50 people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:02">[00:12:02]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:29">[00:29:29]</a>. This is possible because:
*   **Empowered Teams**: Responsibilities typically handled by Product Managers (e.g., scoping, communication) are distributed among engineers and designers who act as Project Leads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:51">[00:19:51]</a>. This requires hiring individuals interested in a broader scope beyond their specific skill set <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:24">[00:20:24]</a>.
*   **Direct Customer Engagement**: The entire team is encouraged to engage with customers, read feedback, and understand their problems, fostering a collective empathy and product understanding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:16">[00:46:16]</a>. This "science" of user research complements the "magic" of intuition in decision-making <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:11">[00:47:11]</a>.
*   **Trust over Metrics**: Instead of relying solely on data to make decisions, Linear embraces the idea that intuition, informed by deep customer understanding, can lead to the right choices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:41">[00:47:41]</a>. The company accepts that mistakes might be made, but they can be fixed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:52">[00:49:52]</a>.

## Growth and Market Fit

Linear's growth strategy focuses on a deliberate, phased approach to achieving [[product_management_and_innovation | product-market fit]]:
*   **Phased Launch**: After a few months of prototyping and development, Linear started with a private beta in June 2019, inviting a small, selected group of users from a waitlist for almost a year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:49">[01:09:49]</a>. This allowed them to iterate and fix issues in cohorts before a public launch <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:39">[01:13:39]</a>.
*   **Targeted Early Adopters**: Initial users were carefully selected based on survey questions about their current tools, company size, and interest levels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:17">[01:10:17]</a>. This focus enabled Linear to build a product that worked well for a specific segment (smaller, early-stage companies with founders focused on shipping speed) before expanding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:11">[01:17:11]</a>.
*   **Product-Market Fit as a Spectrum**: Linear views product-market fit not as a binary state, but as a spectrum that evolves with different market segments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:07">[01:19:07]</a>. They consciously focused on becoming the default tool for early-stage startups before improving fit for larger companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:16">[01:18:16]</a>. This strategy of "doubling down" on what's working well is crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:21">[01:20:21]</a>.
*   **Building a Brand**: A strong brand is seen as a powerful way to attract users, setting expectations of quality even before the product is used <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:11">[01:17:11]</a>. Authenticity in messaging and consistent actions across all touchpoints (product, website, customer interactions) contribute to building a brand over time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:31:00">[01:31:00]</a>.

## Focus and Long-Term Vision

Linear's leadership, particularly CEO Karri Saarinen, emphasizes intense focus, likening development to an RPG game's "main quest line," avoiding "side quests" that don't directly advance the core product or customer value <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:46">[00:55:46]</a>. This allows them to say "no" to non-essential tasks, even if they seem like good ideas or industry standards, until the right time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:10">[00:55:10]</a>.

## Future of Software Development

The evolution of software development will see an increased emphasis on design as competition grows <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:09">[00:26:09]</a>. Karri Saarinen notes that the role of [[the_role_of_ai_in_software_development | AI]] in software development is significant, with many AI companies now using Linear <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:21:26">[01:21:26]</a>. Linear is also [[the_evolution_of_software_development_with_ai | innovating and evolving its product]], for example, with a new "Asks" feature that integrates with Slack to streamline requests from non-product teams without requiring them to be power users of Linear <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:30:35">[01:30:35]</a>. This allows for broader company involvement in product operations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:31:51">[01:31:51]</a>.

## General Principles
*   **[[Invention and innovation processes | Go Slow to Go Fast]]**: This motto encourages taking time to think and plan before rushing into execution, as a well-considered approach ultimately leads to faster and more effective outcomes by reducing the need for rework <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:36:04">[01:36:04]</a>.
*   **Respect for People and Things**: A core value involves respecting team members and taking good care of the tools and resources used, ensuring they are maintained and ready for future use <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:36:48">[01:36:48]</a>.
*   **Leadership Flexibility**: A CEO's role involves a wide and unpredictable range of tasks, necessitating constant learning and effective delegation to other leaders <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:28:48">[01:28:48]</a>.
*   **[[Future skills for software engineers in AIdriven development | Hiring Philosophy]]**: Linear believes in building a highly effective company with fewer, higher-caliber employees who can take on broader scopes beyond their specific job titles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:43">[00:57:43]</a>. This requires a strong emphasis on hiring for product sensibility, looking for candidates who can articulate opinions and reasoning, not just execute tasks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:45">[01:06:45]</a>. They utilize a unique paid work trial to assess candidates in a real-world environment, allowing both sides to evaluate cultural fit and operational style <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:51">[01:02:51]</a>.

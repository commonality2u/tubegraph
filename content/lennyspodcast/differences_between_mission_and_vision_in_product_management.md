A product's **mission** and **vision** are distinct but complementary concepts that provide direction and purpose for a team and company. Understanding their differences is crucial for effective product management.

### What is a Product Vision?

A product vision is an image or picture of the desired future state, a destination the team is striving to reach <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:14">[00:06:14]</a>. It's a powerful tool for [[developing_and_communicating_a_product_vision | product managers]] to align teams and achieve success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:04">[00:05:04]</a>.

Key elements of a good vision include:
*   **Lofty** It should be something that feels grand and exciting, almost scary in its ambition <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:31">[00:06:31]</a>.
*   **Realistic and Attainable** While ambitious, it must also feel within reach, not so "pie in the sky" that it's out of reach <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:46">[00:06:46]</a>.
*   **Devoid of Current Limitations** The vision should be thought of "in a vacuum from the limitations of today" (e.g., current technical constraints). The purpose is to define where you want to be in the future, then work backwards to solve current issues <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:06">[00:07:06]</a>.
*   **Grounded in a Potent Problem** It needs to address a clear and compelling problem that people are excited about solving <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:29">[00:07:29]</a>.

**Examples of Visions:**
*   **Uber:** "A world where you get to this continuous trip so that you do not need parking" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:18">[00:09:18]</a>. This vision aimed to free up significant city space currently used for parking, leading to innovations like Uber Pool <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:26">[00:09:26]</a>.
*   **Microsoft:** "A computer on every desk and in every home" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:11">[00:14:11]</a>.
*   **Tesla:** "Create the most compelling car company of the 21st century" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:16">[00:14:16]</a>.

A vision allows a team to decide if they care about the problem it addresses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:14">[00:11:14]</a>. It's a long-term aspiration, typically spanning three to five years, and should be Evergreen, meaning it doesn't need to be redefined every year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:01">[01:00:01]</a>.

### What is a Product Mission?

In contrast to a vision, a product mission defines the company or team's purposeâ€”**why it exists** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:28">[01:00:28]</a>. It describes the fundamental purpose and guiding principles of what the organization does every day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:17">[01:13:17]</a>.

**Examples of Missions:**
*   **Uber:** "Make transportation reliable everywhere for everyone" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:08">[00:09:08]</a>.
*   **TED:** "To spread ideas" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:54">[00:13:54]</a>.
*   **Stripe:** "Increase the GDP of the internet" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:58">[00:13:58]</a>.
*   **IKEA:** "Create a better everyday life for the many people" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:00">[00:14:00]</a>.

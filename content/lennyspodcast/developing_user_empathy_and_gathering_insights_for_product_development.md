From: [[lennyspodcast]] <br/> 

Developing a deep understanding of users and effectively gathering their feedback is crucial for successful product development. Mahika Kapor, a design engineering PM hybrid at Figma known for spearheading new products, emphasizes the importance of user empathy and strategic insight gathering throughout the product lifecycle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:47">[00:48:47]</a>.

## User-Centric Vision and Product Sense

A compelling product vision cannot be created in a vacuum; it must be fundamentally "inseparable from your users" and your team <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:22:04">[09:22:04]</a>. This user-centric approach is key to building strong [[product_management_insights_and_extreme_clarity | product sense]], which is defined as having good intuition <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:32:00">[02:32:00]</a>.

### Techniques for Building User Empathy and Intuition
*   **Constant Conversation:** Cultivate an "insatiable curiosity" by talking to users at every opportunity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:34:00">[02:34:00]</a>. Mahika actively "grills" people in casual settings about their use of products like Figma and FigJam, noting that anecdotes from conversations stick more effectively than data alone <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:46:00">[02:46:00]</a>.
*   **Build a Knowledge Repository:** Over time, these numerous conversations create an internal "repository or library" that can be drawn from when making product decisions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:59:00">[02:59:00]</a>.
*   **Talk to Non-Users:** Beyond understanding current users, seek out "non-users about your product" to understand why they aren't using it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:00">[00:44:00]</a>. These conversations can be highly insightful, revealing perceptions and marketing challenges that may not be evident from existing user feedback <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:44">[00:44:44]</a>.

## Integrating Feedback into Product Development

User insights are not just for ideation; they are integral to conviction, prioritization, and the continuous improvement of the product.

### The Role of Research and Prototypes
Effective vision crafting involves a "cross-pollination of functions" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:42:00">[09:42:00]</a>:
*   **Research Insights:** Utilize [[importance_of_integrating_ux_research_in_product_development | research insights]] to truly "feel what a user is feeling" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:50:55">[09:50:55]</a>.
*   **Visual Communication:** Leverage "beautiful designs and prototypes" to communicate the future world of the product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:00:01">[10:00:01]</a>. Figma's culture leans heavily into designing and prototyping even before a project gets a green light <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:00">[01:17:00]</a>.
*   **Feasibility:** Ground the vision in engineering and feasibility from the outset to ensure what's being built is achievable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:06:08">[10:06:08]</a>.

### Converting Insights into Action
*   **Prioritization:** User conversations and sales team feedback directly [[ensuring_business_orientation_in_user_research | inform the prioritization]] of the product roadmap <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:31">[00:48:31]</a>.
*   **Evangelism Artifacts:** Create artifacts, such as Loom videos demonstrating product use cases, that sales teams can leverage to evangelize the product to customers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:01">[00:49:01]</a>. This ensures that customer pain points are "cross-pollinating into the product roadmap" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:57">[00:46:57]</a>.
*   **Structured Feedback Collection:** For less immediate feedback, use tools like Slack integrations (e.g., with Asana) to turn feedback into backlog tasks, which are then regularly groomed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:19">[00:50:19]</a>.

## Cultivating a Culture of Feedback and Collaboration

A key aspect of effective user empathy is fostering an environment where feedback is welcomed and acted upon.
*   **"Feedback as a Gift":** Embrace the philosophy that feedback is a gift. This means ensuring feedback is a "constantly flowing thing," not just a once or twice a year event <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:39">[00:30:39]</a>.
*   **Proactive Feedback Exchange:** Encourage a two-way flow of feedback by starting with "Hey, do you have feedback from me?" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:05">[00:31:05]</a>. Acting on received feedback quickly incentivizes others to do the same <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:29">[00:31:29]</a>.
*   **Early Staging/Dogfooding:** Figma employs a multi-month staging or dogfooding process for new features <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:00">[01:10:00]</a>. This early exposure gets diverse internal teams invested in the product, making them feel like they "shaped that part of the product" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:28:29">[01:28:29]</a>. This early feedback loop elevates product quality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:11">[01:29:11]</a>.
*   **Understanding Team Motivations:** Recognize that different team members (engineers, designers, researchers, data scientists) have varying desires for involvement in the product visioning phase <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:30:50">[01:30:50]</a>. Directly ask individuals how much they like being involved in product decisions and cater to those individual motivations to build a highly functioning team <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:31:21">[01:31:21]</a>.

## Leadership and Adaptability in User-Driven Development
Even with strong personal conviction, it's vital to maintain "strong opinions, weakly held" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:27:29">[02:27:29]</a>. If external signals (especially from users) suggest a different direction, be ready to pivot with agility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:31:32">[02:31:32]</a>. This adaptability ensures the product remains aligned with genuine user needs.

Mahika's approach highlights that [[the_role_of_empathy_in_product_management | empathy]] is not just a soft skill but a foundational pillar for strategic product development, fostering strong conviction, effective communication, and a culture of continuous innovation.

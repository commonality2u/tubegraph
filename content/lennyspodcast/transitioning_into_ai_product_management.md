## Understanding AI Product Management Flavors

A product manager is generally responsible for uniting design, engineering, business development, operations, and sales to represent the customer and deliver impactful solutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:36">[00:00:36]</a>. Within this, Khan breaks down [[AI integration in product management | AI product management]] into three main categories:

1.  **AI Platform PMs** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:02">[00:07:02]</a>: These individuals build tools for AI Engineers. Khan's work at Arise AI, an observability and evaluation platform for artificial intelligence, falls into this category <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:12">[00:07:12]</a>. This space is nascent, with new interfaces and tools for building on top of large language models (LLMs) still emerging <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:50">[00:07:50]</a>.
2.  **AI Product PMs** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:28">[00:08:28]</a>: For these PMs, the core product itself is centered around AI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:34">[00:08:34]</a>. Examples include ChatGPT or NotebookLM, where the core experience is enabled by the underlying model, and the PM's job is to package this technology for consumers or enterprises <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:40">[00:08:40]</a>.
3.  **AI-Powered PMs** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:32">[00:09:32]</a>: This category refers to product managers whose roles are enhanced and enabled by AI technology across the entire product stack <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:37">[00:09:37]</a>. They utilize LLMs or other models to build experiences that best serve customers, even if they aren't responsible for building the core model from scratch <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:50">[00:09:50]</a>. Khan anticipates that in the near future, most PMs will likely fall into one of these [[Impact and challenges of AI in product management | AI product manager]] categories <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:31">[00:10:31]</a>.

## Pathways to Transitioning into AI Product Management

Breaking into [[AI product management strategies | AI product management]] is more accessible now than before <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:30">[00:30:30]</a>. Previously, a strong foundation in machine learning was often required <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:00">[00:44:00]</a>. Today, the focus has shifted towards building experiences around or for AI products <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:00">[00:44:00]</a>.

### Skills and Mindset
Khan emphasizes that he himself did not have a traditional computer science or machine learning background; he studied mechanical engineering and learned much on his own <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:57">[00:13:57]</a>.

*   **Customer Obsession**: Regardless of the AI PM flavor, the ultimate goal is to care deeply about the end customer and be "in love with the problem" you're trying to solve <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:42:50">[01:42:50]</a>. This drives the desire to push technology boundaries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:44:50">[01:44:50]</a>.
*   **Curiosity-Driven Learning**: Being genuinely curious about the [[Future implications of AI for product management | AI space]] and its possibilities is crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:42:50">[01:42:50]</a>. This curiosity should guide you to pick up relevant tools and implement them in your products <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:52:00">[01:52:00]</a>.
*   **Fundamental Understanding**: Start with the fundamentals of machine learning and AI to understand the technology's capabilities and boundaries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:10">[00:19:10]</a>. Resources like Andrej Karpathy's videos on the foundations of LLMs can be valuable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:43">[00:19:43]</a>.
*   **Problem-First Approach**: Focus on identifying problems to solve rather than simply trying to apply AI because it's a trend <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:09">[00:36:09]</a>. As Khan states, "AI is a tool to solve it, not AI for blank" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:09">[00:36:09]</a>.

### Practical Steps and Tools

Khan's primary advice for standing out as an applicant is to have a portfolio of products you've built using AI, even if they are just prototypes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:08:00">[02:08:00]</a>.

*   **Hands-on Prototyping**: AI tools make it feasible for product managers to arrive at meetings with mockups or functional prototypes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:08">[00:00:08]</a>. This demonstrates thinking and interests to hiring managers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:08:00">[02:08:00]</a>.
    *   **Code Generation**: Tools like Cursor and Replit allow PMs to build functional prototypes quickly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:00">[00:11:00]</a>. Replit, for example, can build and host a website in seconds from a prompt <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:45">[00:24:45]</a>.
    *   **UI/UX Prototyping**: Vercel's V0 helps generate beautiful landing pages or working UIs from prompts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:36">[00:11:36]</a>.
    *   **Graphic Design/Storyboarding**: Midjourney and DALL-E can assist in creating logos, graphics, or visual user stories <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:05">[00:12:05]</a>.
*   **Hackathons**: Participating in or organizing hackathons encourages hands-on experience and removes the perception that AI technology is unapproachable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:32">[00:37:32]</a>. This helps in identifying real problems where AI can genuinely be applied <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:01">[00:38:01]</a>.
*   **Learn from Real-world Examples**: Experiment with AI products as a consumer to understand their capabilities and limitations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:23:00">[01:23:00]</a>. Try out cutting-edge technologies, like OpenAI's real-time voice API within ChatGPT Plus, to see what's possible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:52:00">[01:52:00]</a>.

## Thriving as an AI Product Manager

Once in an [[AI product management strategies | AI PM role]], certain attributes differentiate a typical PM from a top 5% performer.

### Strategic Product Development
*   **Avoid Mimicry**: Do not simply build "another ChatGPT" on top of your own data just because it feels intuitive or familiar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:56">[00:30:56]</a>. Question whether it's the right problem for your business to solve <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:51">[00:31:51]</a>.
*   **Innovative Interfaces**: The optimal interface for AI might not be a chatbot <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:03">[00:32:03]</a>. It might involve optimizing or speeding up existing human processes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:10">[00:32:10]</a>.
*   **Focus on the Problem, not the Solution**: Be wary of trying to fit AI technology to a problem, especially with buzzwords like "AI agents" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:37">[00:33:37]</a>. The focus should be on solving customer problems, with AI as a tool <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:09">[00:36:09]</a>.
*   **Balance Automation with User Control**: Successful AI products often provide a "magical" experience by making things easier without fully automating the user's role. Like the Betty Crocker cake mix story, users often want some level of control or involvement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:09">[00:40:09]</a>. This "Ikea effect" empowers users <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:05">[00:41:05]</a>.

### Individual Contributor (IC) Mindset in AI
As an IC PM, the job is challenging due to constant change and high expectations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:55">[00:48:55]</a>.

*   **Bring Energy**: Show up with extra energy to meetings. This can reduce friction and foster positive discussions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:51">[00:49:51]</a>. It also demonstrates engagement and willingness to get into the details <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:56">[00:51:56]</a>.
*   **Be a "Wanderer"**: Rather than "waiting to see" where AI technology goes, proactively explore new directions and possibilities for your team and company <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:16">[00:58:16]</a>. This involves being comfortable in ambiguous, "squishy" spaces while seeking signal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:59">[01:00:59]</a>.
*   **Amplify Signal Through Noise**: Utilize AI tools (like LLMs with long context windows) to analyze data, such as meeting transcripts from sales calls, to quickly identify common customer pain points or valuable insights <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:11">[01:02:11]</a>. This allows PMs to scale themselves and gather information from many sources <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:01">[01:03:01]</a>.
*   **Balance Delivery with Experimentation**: A top AI PM continuously delivers customer value while also creating space to prototype, experiment, and potentially fail. The goal is to learn from these experiments and keep iterating <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:43">[00:43:43]</a>.
*   **Have Fun and Be Curious**: Ultimately, a key to success is to genuinely enjoy the process, stay curious, and continuously learn. This fosters faster iteration and a more positive experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:49">[01:03:49]</a>.

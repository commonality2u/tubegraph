From: [[lennyspodcast]] <br/> 
The landscape of product management is undergoing a rapid transformation, largely driven by advancements in artificial intelligence (AI). This shift necessitates a re-evaluation of traditional roles and a proactive approach to skill development for both individual product managers and their leaders.

## The Shifting Role of Product Management
AI is poised to transform the roles of product, design, and engineering much faster than generally anticipated, with the rate of technological change accelerating significantly over the past 18 to 24 months <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:51">[00:00:51]</a>. This rapid change means that the job of a product professional is no longer about building a great product for today, but anticipating customer needs in three, five, or ten years and building towards that [[future_of_product_management | future]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:31">[00:01:31]</a>.

Traditional product strategy creation, which once took weeks of customer interviews, team discussions, and iterative document revisions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:22">[00:02:22]</a>, can now be significantly expedited using [[ai_tools_and_resources_for_product_managers | AI tools]] like ChatGPT. A 10-page product strategy document, which previously took weeks, can now be scaffolded out in minutes with AI, followed by refinement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:51">[00:03:51]</a>. This means product work demands less time and less raw thought, potentially leading to fewer product managers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:31">[00:05:31]</a>.

### Automating Product Tasks
To adapt, product managers must become an [[ai_tools_and_resources_for_product_managers | AI-powered product team]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:04">[00:06:04]</a>. This involves:
1.  **Automating tasks** to speed up delivery <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:10">[00:06:10]</a>.
2.  **Adding new skills** and doing more <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:14">[00:06:14]</a>.
3.  **Multiplying their impact** by teaching the team <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:18">[00:06:18]</a>.

An "anti-to-do list" of tasks that product managers should now automate using AI includes:
*   Drafting documents <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:58">[00:06:58]</a>
*   Getting and giving initial passive feedback <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:04">[00:07:04]</a>
*   Writing updates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:11">[00:07:11]</a>
*   Summarizing agendas and action items from meetings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:25">[00:07:25]</a>
*   Prioritizing feature requests <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:29">[00:07:29]</a>
*   Monitoring goals and OKRs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:30">[00:07:30]</a>
*   Keeping track of competitors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:35">[00:07:35]</a>
*   Prepping for interviews and consolidating candidate feedback <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:36">[00:07:36]</a>
*   Telling customer stories <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:41">[00:07:41]</a>
*   Making slides visually appealing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:47">[00:07:47]</a>
*   Explaining product functionality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:50">[00:07:50]</a>

The goal is to leverage AI to get to 75% completion faster, rather than striving for 100% from scratch <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:36">[00:08:36]</a>. This can save days every week <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:44">[00:08:44]</a>.

## New Skills for Modern Product Managers
The time saved through automation should be reinvested in acquiring new skills and expanding capabilities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:48">[00:08:48]</a>. The traditional product triad (product, engineering, design) is evolving. The future will see more "generalist specialists" who are expected to participate across the entire building process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:23">[00:13:23]</a>. The operating principle should be "there are no lanes" â€“ if a task needs to be done and an individual has the skill, they should do it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:42">[00:13:42]</a>.

The concept of an "AI-powered Triple Threat" is emerging: one person capable of leading a team by handling engineering, design, and product tasks, augmented by AI tools, agents, and platforms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:29">[00:14:29]</a>. While they may specialize in one area, they will need a working knowledge of how to do it all <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:46">[00:14:46]</a>. This shift allows for faster movement by smaller, more opinionated teams <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:00">[00:15:00]</a>. Additionally, product managers are increasingly expected to be more commercially oriented, leveraging tools for data analysis, and even engaging in product marketing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:27">[00:15:27]</a>.

This implies a necessary expansion of [[product_manager_skill_development | product manager skill development]], moving towards a more versatile and hands-on approach. [[improving_product_management_skills | Improving product management skills]] now includes a technical and design dimension.

## Advice for Product Leaders
Product leaders are also impacted by this change and must prepare to manage these new, dynamic team structures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:51">[00:16:51]</a>. This means:
*   **Developing commercial skills** within teams, especially for B2B Enterprise <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:11">[00:17:11]</a>.
*   **Increasing technical skills** across the board, as leaders will manage individuals who code <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:17">[00:17:17]</a>.
*   **Rethinking budgeting** for headcount versus AI agents and tools, influencing R&D investment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:33">[00:17:33]</a>.
*   **Adopting "artisanally crafted team topologies"** where teams are built based on the task and the skills of motivated individuals, rather than rigid, prescriptive structures like the traditional triad <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:45">[00:17:45]</a>.
*   **Building a "moat" around unique contributions**, as traditional experience and strategy become more commoditized by AI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:41">[00:18:41]</a>. Leaders can differentiate themselves by building and scaling these new AI-powered teams <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:52">[00:18:52]</a>.

The key message is to anticipate and proactively adapt to these changes. Ignoring them makes one vulnerable to obsolescence, while embracing them opens up thrilling possibilities for building and creating <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:51">[00:15:51]</a>. Product leaders should empower "AI-powered triple threats" with power and budget to drive this transformation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:00">[00:20:00]</a>. This involves adopting [[effective_product_management_strategies | effective product management strategies]] for a new era.

## Job Search Councils: Community as a Safety Net

A core tenet of Terry's methodology is the Job Search Council (JSC) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:52">[00:02:52]</a>.

### What are Job Search Councils?
JSCs are support groups of six to eight job seekers who commit to supporting each other through the job search process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:57">[00:02:57]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:09">[00:03:09]</a>. This concept originated from the product community <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:04">[00:03:04]</a>, but is applicable to everyone <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:00">[00:03:00]</a>. Terry's mother formed a similar council of teachers in 1960 that met for 50 years to support each other's [[career_development_and_growth_strategies | careers]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:02">[00:05:02]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:13">[00:05:13]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:19">[00:05:19]</a>.

### Addressing Insecurity and Anxiety
A primary benefit of JSCs is combating the universal feeling of insecurity and anxiety during a job search <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:50">[00:03:50]</a>. Phil Terry highlights that putting anxious people together, encouraging openness and vulnerability, and asking for help, flips anxiety and fear into hope, motivation, accountability, and confidence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:01">[00:04:01]</a>. The most important thing to manage during a job search is one's emotional balance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:05">[00:14:05]</a>.

### Structure and Impact
Over 2,000 JSCs have been launched, all completely free and volunteer-driven <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:17">[00:09:17]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:21">[00:09:21]</a>. They offer hundreds of pages of tools, a Slack community, a matching program, and live training <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:24">[00:09:24]</a>.

*   **Application:** Applicants are separated into "slow seekers" (employed and looking) and "fast seekers" (unemployed and looking) to match them with appropriate group cadences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:43">[00:10:43]</a>.
*   **Moderators:** Councils need moderators, who are also job seekers, receiving faster matching and more training <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:03">[00:11:03]</a>.
*   **Process:** After matching and orientation, groups use a 100-page workbook with templates and guides <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:46">[00:11:46]</a>. Fast seekers meet twice a week, slow seekers every two weeks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:11">[00:12:11]</a>.
*   **Testimonials:** Users have praised the JSCs for helping them apply product processes to their [[career_advice_and_frameworks | career]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:22">[00:13:22]</a>, providing accountability and emotional support <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:41">[00:13:41]</a>.
*   **Speed:** The average job search duration within a JSC is 3 months, which is at the low end of the national average (3-6 months) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:25">[00:22:25]</a>.
*   **Safety Net:** The initiative aims to build a private safety net for those laid off, addressing the unintended consequences of "creative destruction" in capitalism, which causes anxiety and fear despite driving economic growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:22">[00:18:22]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:08">[00:19:08]</a>.

## Candidate Market Fit: A Spear, Not a Net

Terry stresses the importance of a focused job search, comparing it to product-market fit in product development <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:05">[00:00:05]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:32">[00:31:32]</a>.

### Understanding Your Fit
In a competitive job market, understanding your "candidate market fit" is crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:49">[00:20:49]</a>. This means acknowledging that your market value may have shifted, especially in downturns, and it's about the marketplace, not a personal statement about you <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:30">[00:21:30]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:44">[00:21:44]</a>.

### The Manoukian Two-Pager
The first radical step is to define what you want and don't want in a job, captured in a "Manoukian two-pager" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:01">[00:22:01]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:02">[00:23:02]</a>. This is a draft, meant to be iterated upon <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:19">[00:24:19]</a>.

### The Listening Tour
After drafting your preferences, conduct a "listening tour" by asking trusted friends, colleagues, and recruiters for their candid feedback on your fit and market conditions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:42">[00:24:42]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:57">[00:24:57]</a>.

*   **Golden Question:** Ask, "If you were in my shoes, how would you approach this?" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:16">[00:25:16]</a>.
*   **Types of Conversations:**
    *   **Reverse Exit Interviews:** With former colleagues to understand your strengths and perceived fit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:18">[00:27:18]</a>.
    *   **Broader Network:** Using the golden question to gain perspective <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:34">[00:27:34]</a>.
    *   **Recruiters:** Engage recruiters by asking for advice on your fit, rather than immediately asking for a job <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:46">[00:28:46]</a>. Building relationships with recruiters even when employed is advised <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:06">[00:28:06]</a>.
*   **Benefits:** The listening tour provides market research on your fit, helps refine your approach, and transforms contacts into "listening posts" invested in your success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:18">[00:26:18]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:39">[00:29:39]</a>.

### Narrowing Your Focus
After the listening tour, consolidate insights into a narrow, clear candidate market fit statement, typically with three to four attributes like company stage, industry, role level, and culture <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:13">[00:31:13]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:33">[00:33:33]</a>. For example, "I am looking for a director of product role in a healthcare Series B startup in San Francisco" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:46">[00:31:46]</a>. This focus allows others to be expansive on your behalf, recalling you when they see opportunities that are a slight variation from your specific ask <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:39">[00:32:39]</a>. Once finalized (and approved by your council), share this statement widely on LinkedIn and with your network <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:14">[00:34:14]</a>.

### [[Navigating career paths in growth and exgrowth companies | Navigating Market Conditions]]
Market conditions can shift, necessitating adaptability in your candidate market fit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:31">[00:34:31]</a>. In a downturn, there might be more opportunities in startups than in large companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:06">[00:37:06]</a>. It's important to be realistic about your fit; sometimes, a "two-step strategy" is necessary, accepting a lower-level role (e.g., individual contributor) to gain experience in a desired industry or company type, setting you up for future growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:00">[00:39:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:16">[00:40:16]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:39">[00:40:39]</a>. This helps avoid [[the_pitfalls_of_shortterm_career_focus | getting stuck]] in jobs that don't offer learning or progression <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:11">[00:42:11]</a>.

## Playing to Win: Interviews and Negotiation

When interviewing and negotiating, individuals must be in charge and "play to win" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:09">[00:49:09]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:37">[00:49:37]</a>.

### Job Mission with OKRs
Instead of passive negotiation, create your own "Job Mission with OKRs (Objectives and Key Results)" for the role you're interviewing for <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:28">[00:50:28]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:35">[00:50:35]</a>.

*   **Drafting Benefits:** This private exercise helps you formulate insightful interview questions to clarify the role <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:19">[00:51:19]</a>.
*   **Sharing with Hiring Manager:** After initial interviews, share this draft mission with the hiring manager (preferably live, not email) to demonstrate initiative, accountability, and a focus on outcomes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:50">[00:53:50]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:01">[00:53:01]</a>. Companies are impressed when candidates are already thinking about delivering results and what resources they need <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:50">[00:52:50]</a>.
*   **Clarifying the Role:** This also helps clarify the actual job responsibilities, preventing surprises later <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:11">[00:53:11]</a>.

### Negotiating for Success
Use the Job Mission with OKRs as a basis for negotiation, not just salary, but also the resources needed for success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:41">[00:53:41]</a>.

*   **Four Legs of Negotiation:** Discuss concrete needs such as:
    *   Addressing technical debt (e.g., requesting a budget) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:26">[00:54:26]</a>.
    *   Team training <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:50">[00:55:50]</a>.
    *   Hiring additional staff <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:26">[00:57:26]</a>.
    *   Professional development opportunities (mentorship, conferences) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:16">[00:56:16]</a>.
*   **Collaborative Approach:** Frame these discussions as collaborative conversations about what is needed to achieve the agreed-upon OKRs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:10">[00:58:10]</a>. This shows investment and a focus on the company's success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:30">[00:59:30]</a>.
*   **Salary Negotiation:** Even in a tight market, 87% of the time, asking for more money yields results <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:17">[01:00:17]</a>. Ask simply, "Are you open to [desired salary]?" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:06">[01:01:06]</a>.
*   **Back Channeling:** Research the hiring manager and team through your network before accepting an offer to gauge their effectiveness and avoid unpleasant surprises <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:44:21">[01:44:21]</a>.

## [[The art of asking for help and its impact on career growth | The Art of Asking for Help]]

Asking for help is a cornerstone of [[career_development_and_growth_strategies | career growth]] and success, yet it's often misunderstood <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:26">[01:11:26]</a>.

### Counterintuitive Rules of Asking for Help
1.  **Sign of Confidence:** Asking for help isn't a weakness; it requires and strengthens confidence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:57">[01:14:57]</a>.
2.  **Giving Activity:** If done well, asking for help is a giving activity, as it makes the person providing assistance feel valued and honored <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:07">[01:15:07]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:15">[01:16:15]</a>.
3.  **Increased Independence:** It fosters greater independence and accountability, rather than passivity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:34">[01:16:34]</a>.
4.  **Reputation Improvement:** It doesn't harm your reputation; it enhances it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:37">[01:16:37]</a>.

### How to Ask for Help Effectively
*   **Do Your Homework:** Ensure you've done your research before approaching someone <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:36">[01:13:36]</a>.
*   **Seek Advice, Not Delegation:** Ask for advice, support, and perspective, rather than expecting someone to do the work for you <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:38">[01:13:38]</a>.
*   **Be Open and Vulnerable:** Share your challenges honestly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:22">[01:15:22]</a>.
*   **Respect Relationships:** Understand the nature of your relationship with the person you're asking. A close friend will respond differently than a casual acquaintance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:26:16">[01:26:16]</a>.
*   **Be Direct and Honest:** Clearly state what you're asking for <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:27:56">[01:27:56]</a>.
*   **Emotional Intelligence:** Use your emotional intelligence to gauge trust and approachability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:42">[01:22:42]</a>.

### Practical Applications
*   **Defining Reality:** Ask peers how they see things and understand their perspective <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:39">[01:19:39]</a>.
*   **Reviewing Work:** Ask for feedback on important emails or presentations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:53">[01:19:53]</a>.
*   **Peer Coaching Calls:** Convene calls with others in similar roles to share challenges and seek advice <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:35">[01:20:35]</a>.
    *   **First 90 Days Call:** When starting a new job, talk to others in similar roles for advice on integrating and avoiding common mistakes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:57">[01:20:57]</a>.
    *   **Career Evolution Call:** To transition to a higher role, speak with individuals already in that position about how to bridge the gap <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:21:31">[01:21:31]</a>.

## Mentorship and Continuous Learning

Developing [[career_growth_strategies_for_product_managers | career growth strategies]] also involves continuous learning and mentorship <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:35:00">[01:35:00]</a>.

### The Power of Mentors
Phil Terry notes that most professionals, even senior leaders, lack mentors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:35:03">[01:35:03]</a>. He encourages seeking "dead or distant mentors" through deep study of individuals you admire, such as Warren Buffett or Steve Jobs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:35:24">[01:35:24]</a>. This involves more than just liking their work; it requires studying their philosophy and actions to inform your own <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:35:42">[01:35:42]</a>.

### Reading and Reflection
Books are "machines to think with" and are crucial for continuous learning and shifting perspective <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:33:52">[01:33:52]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:47:40">[01:47:40]</a>. Rereading important books helps deeply inform decision-making <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:39:51">[01:39:51]</a>. Product leaders should also seek inspiration from non-digital products, like the design of Robert Strassler's "Landmark series" history books, to foster innovative thinking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:46:42">[01:46:42]</a>.

## Overcoming Challenges

### Managing Emotional Balance
During a job search, especially in tough markets, insecurity and anxiety are common feelings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:26">[01:29:26]</a>. Maintaining emotional balance is crucial, as unchecked anxiety can erode confidence and hinder interview performance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:50">[01:09:50]</a>.

### The Inner Critic
Recognize and name your inner critic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:50">[01:07:50]</a>. Your inner critic often lies, and knowing this helps to prevent self-sabotage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:50">[01:07:50]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:27">[01:08:27]</a>. Walking into interviews with the "gratitude house" exercise – remembering all the people who have supported you – can boost confidence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:21">[01:05:21]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:03">[01:07:03]</a>.

### Post-Interview Debriefs
Debriefing interviews with trusted individuals helps to objectively assess performance and counter self-critical thoughts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:12">[01:07:12]</a>.

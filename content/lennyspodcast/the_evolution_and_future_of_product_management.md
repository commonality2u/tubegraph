## The Changing Landscape of Product Strategy

Historically, developing a [[the_art_and_role_of_product_management | product strategy]] involved extensive manual effort <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:22">[00:02:22]</a>. This process included:
*   Talking to numerous customers and taking notes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:26">[00:02:26]</a>.
*   Consulting with internal teams across sales, finance, and engineering <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:31">[00:02:31]</a>.
*   Intensive personal deliberation and research <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:45">[00:02:45]</a>.
*   Drafting a lengthy document (e.g., 10 pages) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:52">[00:02:52]</a>.
*   Undergoing multiple rounds of feedback and revisions, often taking "weeks and weeks and weeks" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:58">[00:02:58]</a>.

In contrast, the process for creating a [[the_art_and_role_of_product_management | product strategy]] can now be dramatically streamlined with [[the_role_of_ai_in_transforming_product_management | AI]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:51">[00:03:51]</a>. For example, a 10-page [[the_art_and_role_of_product_management | product strategy]] document can be generated by simply speaking ideas into an [[the_role_of_ai_in_transforming_product_management | AI]] tool like ChatGPT voice, after initial customer calls <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:53">[00:03:53]</a>. This represents a significant shift in how product work operates, requiring less time and thought <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:31">[00:05:31]</a>.

[[the_role_of_ai_in_transforming_product_management | AI]] is changing daily [[the_art_and_role_of_product_management | product management]] tasks:

## The Collapse of the Talent Stack

A key belief is that [[the_role_of_ai_in_transforming_product_management | AI]] will "collapse the talent stack," meaning formerly separate jobs will merge <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:42">[00:11:42]</a>.
The traditional "product Triad" of product, engineering, and design, often seen as the "platonic ideal of a product team," operates through handoff mechanisms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:41">[00:12:41]</a>. This handoff model can lead to detachment from the actual building process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:17">[00:13:17]</a>.

The shift is towards "generalist specialists" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:23">[00:13:23]</a>. While individuals may specialize in product, design, or engineering, they are expected to participate across the entire building process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:28">[00:13:28]</a>. The principle of "no Lanes" means that if something needs to be done and an individual has the skill, they should do it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:42">[00:13:42]</a>. This can be uncomfortable for those who prefer to stay in their specialized "lanes" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:58">[00:13:58]</a>.

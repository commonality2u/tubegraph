## Types of AI Product Managers

The role of an [[how_to_become_an_ai_product_manager | AI Product Manager]] can be broken down into three main categories <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:56">[00:06:56]</a>:

1.  **AI Platform Product Managers**
    These PMs build [[tools_and_techniques_for_ai_product_managers | tools]] for [[impact_of_ai_on_product_teams | AI Engineers]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:07">[00:07:07]</a>. An example is an observability and evaluation platform for artificial intelligence, starting with machine learning models and pivoting towards broader AI applications like Large Language Models (LLMs) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:11">[00:07:11]</a>. The tooling for building on top of LLMs is still nascent, making this a critical area for development <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:58">[00:07:58]</a>.

2.  **AI Product Product Managers**
    In this role, the core product itself is centered around AI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:34">[00:08:34]</a>. Examples include ChatGPT and NotebookLM <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:40">[00:08:40]</a>. The primary responsibility is to package cutting-edge AI technology, developed by researchers and engineers, and make it consumable for businesses, enterprises, or consumers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:00">[00:09:00]</a>.

3.  **AI-Powered Product Managers**
    This type of PM leverages [[the_impact_of_generative_ai_on_product_management | AI technology]] across the entire product management stack to enhance their existing role <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:36">[00:09:36]</a>. They don't necessarily build the core model from the ground up but utilize LLMs or other models to create the best experience for customers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:52">[00:09:52]</a>.

It is predicted that in the near future, most product managers will likely fall into one of these categories, as AI becomes as common as a database for SaaS applications <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:21">[00:10:21]</a>. This signifies [[the_future_of_ai_in_product_management | the future of AI in product management]], where most PMs will be a flavor of [[ai_product_management_and_its_future | AI product manager]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:31">[00:10:31]</a>.

## Current Trends

### Increased Prototyping Capability
AI makes it highly feasible for product managers to create prototypes and mocks early in the process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:08">[00:00:08]</a>. Tools like Cursor, Replit, and Vercel's v0 allow PMs to quickly build functional prototypes or beautiful landing pages from prompts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:06">[00:11:06]</a>. This capability allows PMs to show up to meetings with higher-resolution starting points <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:18">[00:00:18]</a>. AI also enables graphic design (Midjourney, DALL-E) and 3D modeling for visual or physical product development <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:05">[00:12:05]</a>.

### Lower Barrier to Entry for AI Product Management
It's arguably easier to break into [[ai_product_management_and_its_future | AI product management]] now than before <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:50">[00:30:50]</a>. Previously, a strong foundation in machine learning was often required <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:26">[00:44:26]</a>. Today, many free resources, such as YouTube videos, demonstrate how to build an app using AI in a short amount of time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:37">[00:37:37]</a>.

### Product Managers as Central Figures
With AI tools excelling at building things based on instructions, the most critical skill becomes defining *what* to build <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:16">[00:26:16]</a>. This involves identifying problems, articulating solutions clearly to AI tools, and having the taste to discern what will succeed in the market <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:22">[00:26:22]</a>. Product managers are uniquely positioned to thrive in this environment because their core job is to represent the customer and get a solution shipped that creates impact <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:46">[00:06:46]</a>. They can use AI to amplify their influence by communicating ideas effectively to design, engineering, and leadership with functional prototypes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:43">[00:27:43]</a>.

## Key Challenges

### Avoiding "Another ChatGPT"
A significant challenge is the tendency for companies to build "another ChatGPT" on top of their own data, often resulting in products with limited actual usage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:53">[00:30:53]</a>. Top [[ai_product_management_and_its_future | AI product managers]] differentiate themselves by asking whether a solution genuinely addresses a business need, rather than merely replicating familiar interfaces <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:39">[00:31:39]</a>. The optimal AI interface might not be a chatbot at all; it could be something that optimizes or speeds up existing human processes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:03">[00:32:03]</a>.

### Distinguishing Problem from Solution
There is a current trend of "AI agents," but the challenge remains fitting the technology to solve a problem, rather than trying to describe a solution (an AI agent) without clearly defining the problem it addresses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:17">[00:33:17]</a>. Product managers must focus on applying AI techniques to existing organizational problems, understanding that they might not have the cutting-edge researchers to design models from scratch <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:10">[00:34:10]</a>.

### Measuring AI Impact
A unique challenge for [[ai_product_management_and_its_future | AI product managers]] is the lack of a clear metric for building prototypes with AI that doesn't directly tie to traditional business metrics like revenue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:06">[00:37:06]</a>. This makes it difficult to measure if initial AI initiatives are truly working <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:21">[00:37:21]</a>.

### Navigating Uncertainty and Rapid Change
The AI technology landscape is changing incredibly fast <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:52">[00:44:52]</a>. Product managers must be comfortable with constant change and the possibility that an experiment might not work as expected <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:52">[00:44:52]</a>. A key skill is the ability to "wander" and explore new directions, rather than "waiting" to see how things pan out <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:51">[00:58:51]</a>. This means finding signal through the noise, even if the signal is sparse, and using AI tools to scale oneself and gain insights from customer interactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:08">[01:02:08]</a>.

### Balancing Automation with User Control
Even if a product can be fully automated, it's important to consider whether full automation is desirable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:09">[00:40:09]</a>. Successful AI products often leave "knobs and levers" for the user to maintain a sense of control over the experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:59">[00:40:59]</a>. This "IKEA effect" makes users feel more empowered when they have an impact on the end result <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:05">[00:41:05]</a>. AI should make a product easier to use and lower the barrier to creation, rather than simply automating the problem away <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:31">[00:42:31]</a>.

## Thriving as an AI Product Manager

To thrive as an [[ai_product_management_and_its_future | AI product manager]], several habits and mindsets are crucial:

*   **Bring Energy**: Show up with high energy and enthusiasm, even during tough conversations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:54">[00:49:54]</a>. This positivity can break down barriers and facilitate smoother interactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:57">[00:49:57]</a>. This also means being a "player-coach" by actively engaging in the details and doing the work alongside the team, such as conducting customer outreach or trying out new tools <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:56">[00:51:56]</a>.
*   **Embrace "Wandering"**: Be comfortable in the unknown and actively seek out new directions for the product and team <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:01">[00:59:01]</a>. This involves continuously pushing the boundaries of what the company believes is possible with AI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:36">[01:00:36]</a>.
*   **Amplify Signal Through Noise**: Utilize AI tools (like Gong for call transcripts or LLMs with long context windows) to analyze vast amounts of data and extract key customer pain points or common problems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:08">[01:02:08]</a>. This provides a "superpower" to be in many places at once and leverage technology to gain crucial insights <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:01">[01:03:01]</a>.
*   **Have Fun and Stay Curious**: Product management can be serious, but maintaining a sense of fun and continuous learning drives faster iteration and greater impact <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:49">[01:03:49]</a>. Curiosity is key to exploring new tools and applications <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:50">[00:17:50]</a>.
*   **Build a Portfolio**: For those looking to transition into the field, building a portfolio of AI-powered prototypes or products, even if they are just experiments, can make an applicant stand out significantly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:51">[00:20:51]</a>. This demonstrates interest, skill, and how one approaches problem-solving <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:40">[00:21:40]</a>.
*   **Focus on the Problem**: Always prioritize understanding and solving customer problems, using AI as a tool to achieve that, rather than applying AI just for the sake of it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:06">[00:36:06]</a>.
*   **Continuously Deliver Value**: While exploring new AI capabilities, an [[ai_product_management_and_its_future | AI Product Manager]] must also continue to deliver on existing business metrics and customer value <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:33">[00:43:33]</a>. This "walk and chew gum" approach allows for experimentation and learning without sacrificing core responsibilities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:43">[00:43:43]</a>.

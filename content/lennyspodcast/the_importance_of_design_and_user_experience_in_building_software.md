From: [[lennyspodcast]] <br/> 

The significance of design and user experience (UX) in software development has grown exponentially over time. What was once an optional luxury for early iterations of new technologies has become a fundamental requirement for any product seeking serious attention and adoption <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:02">[00:00:02]</a>. In today's crowded markets, where users have countless choices, a high level of design is essential from the very beginning to capture and retain interest <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:02">[00:32:02]</a>.

## Linear's Commitment to Craft and Quality

Ki Sarin, co-founder and CEO of Linear, an issue tracking tool for software companies, exemplifies this philosophy. Linear is recognized for its rapid growth, user adoption, and deep focus on "craft and quality" in its product development <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:09">[01:10:09]</a>. Unlike many companies that rely on A/B tests or metric-based goals, Linear prioritizes taste and strong opinions in its product decisions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:04">[01:16:04]</a>.

### Prioritizing Craft
Linear's commitment to quality starts with hiring individuals who genuinely care about design and user experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:56">[00:07:56]</a>. The company believes that high-quality design is crucial for successful collaboration and coordination within teams. Friction or "paper cuts" in the user experience can quickly become annoying, hindering productivity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:06">[00:08:06]</a>.

While prioritizing craft can sometimes extend timelines by a few days for necessary fixes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:41">[00:08:41]</a>, Linear manages this by:
*   **Early Internal Testing**: Features are often shipped to production, visible only internally, as soon as possible after initial design ideas are in place <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:31">[00:09:31]</a>. This allows the team to experience the product firsthand and realize how it could be better <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:14">[00:09:14]</a>.
*   **Customer Co-creation**: For new features, Linear seeks out a small group of interested customers (1-10) who are willing to use early, less polished versions and provide feedback. This collaborative approach helps refine features before general release <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:52">[00:09:52]</a>.
*   **Balancing Perfection with Speed**: Linear avoids a "perfection mindset" that could prevent shipping. Instead, they push things out quickly and then improve them rapidly, combining seemingly opposite ideas to achieve better outcomes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:41">[00:11:41]</a>.

### Empowering Product-Minded Teams
Linear's structure, with only one head of product, means that much of the product management responsibility is distributed among engineers and designers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:01">[00:19:01]</a>. This fosters a sense of ownership, allowing project teams (comprising engineering and design) to identify opportunities for improvement during the building process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:07">[00:15:07]</a>. An example of this is an engineer who independently improved the usability of the right-click menu by creating "safe areas" for diagonal mouse movement, a subtle yet significant [[importance_of_attention_to_detail_in_product_design | attention to detail]] that enhances the user experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:44">[00:15:44]</a>.

To facilitate this, Linear's hiring process emphasizes finding individuals who:
*   Care deeply about the product and have strong opinions about how it should work <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:15">[00:22:15]</a>.
*   Are interested in a broader scope than just their specific skill set, demonstrating "product sensibility" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:28">[00:28:28]</a>.
*   Can articulate their reasoning for design choices, not just state preferences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:36">[01:07:36]</a>.

This approach aligns with [[operationalizing_quality_and_design_excellence_in_product_teams | operationalizing quality and design excellence in product teams]] by embedding product thinking directly into the development process.

## Design as a Differentiator
In any domain, the more a product matters, the more its design matters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:43">[00:25:43]</a>. When a market becomes crowded and users have many choices, design becomes a crucial differentiator. Products that visually stand out or convey a sense of quality are more likely to be chosen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:40">[00:29:40]</a>.

Design also plays a vital role in setting user expectations and building a brand. Just as premium product packaging (like Apple's) signals quality before the product is even used, a well-designed landing page or application can immediately convey a high-quality perception to potential users <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:10">[00:28:10]</a>. This is particularly valuable for startups, who often lack established recognition <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:57">[00:28:57]</a>.

### Building an Authentic Brand
A strong brand is not just about a logo or colors; it's about authenticity and the consistent message conveyed through everything the company does—from product building to customer interaction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:00">[00:31:00]</a>. Building a brand takes time and is a continuous process of demonstrating values and approach <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:33">[00:31:33]</a>. For Linear, this authenticity came naturally because the founders were the target audience, building software for themselves and others like them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:24:09">[01:24:09]</a>.

## Design Review and User Empathy
Linear's design review process is less formal and more integrated into the development workflow. Project sponsors (often founders or the head of product) review designs and demos weekly or bi-weekly. Before a full general release, a dedicated review and testing phase occurs to ensure the desired quality is met <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:20">[00:38:20]</a>.

> "A lot of things that we do is is is more like that that we don't do AB testing or we don't do specific follow like certain metrics or something. We might some we do have Telemetry or like we can look at like how people use certain things and we sometimes do that. But like that's not usually the goal we have in mind." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:46">[00:46:46]</a>

Instead, decisions are guided by a deep understanding of the problem and an intuitive sense of what is "right" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:41">[00:47:41]</a>. This requires continuous [[developing_user_empathy_and_gathering_insights_for_product_development | user empathy and gathering insights for product development]]. The entire team is encouraged to frequently interact with customers, answer questions, and observe their usage patterns, fostering a shared understanding of user needs and challenges <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:33">[00:46:33]</a>. This collective understanding minimizes the need for extensive data-driven decisions for every feature, allowing for more intuitive product choices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:50">[00:47:50]</a>. This direct interaction is a core aspect of [[importance_of_integrating_ux_research_in_product_development | integrating UX research in product development]] at Linear.

## Design's Impact on Business Model and Retention
For a product like Linear, which aims for long-term customer relationships and retention, quality and design are paramount <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:10">[00:51:10]</a>. The goal is for companies to start using Linear early and stay with it indefinitely, which can only be achieved by continuously delivering a high-quality product and maintaining user trust <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:22">[00:51:22]</a>. This contrasts with transactional businesses where immediate sales might be the primary focus. Linear's choices are therefore guided by [[building_trust_and_engagement_within_design_teams | respecting the customer]] and their long-term experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:56">[00:51:56]</a>.

Ki Sarin also notes that the Linear product itself, by being a streamlined and frictionless system for software development, aims at [[improving_developer_experience | improving developer experience]] for its users <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:06">[00:06:06]</a>. This further highlights the recursive nature of design importance in the software industry – a well-designed tool can help build well-designed software.

In essence, the success of software today increasingly hinges on its design and user experience. It's no longer just about functionality but about how that functionality is presented, how it feels, and how it fosters a lasting connection with the user.

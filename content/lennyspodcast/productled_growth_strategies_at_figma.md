From: [[lennyspodcast]] <br/> 
Figma is widely recognized as a leader in [[productled growth]] (PLG), a strategy where the product itself drives customer acquisition, retention, and expansion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:48:00">[01:48:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:11:00">[05:11:00]</a>. Yuki Yamashita, Chief Product Officer at Figma, highlights several core philosophies and practices that underpin the company's successful [[productled growth]] approach <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:00">[01:13:00]</a>.

### The Power of Community-Led Growth
Figma's journey began with a focus on its community, treating users as evangelists who help shape and spread the product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:50:00">[02:50:00]</a>.

*   **Early Development and Feedback Loop**: From the outset, CEO Dylan Field consistently put early versions of Figma in front of designers, asking for feedback and rapidly implementing it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:29:00">[02:29:00]</a>. This iterative process fostered a strong connection with the community, making users feel like they had a direct hand in shaping the product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:39:00">[02:39:00]</a>.
*   **Customer Proximity**: Figma maintains an "obsession with proximity to customers" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:40:00">[02:40:00]</a>. This includes:
    *   **Direct Engagement**: Teams often tweet out ideas or areas of focus (e.g., "we're thinking about focusing a lot more in prototyping, what are the top problems you're seeing?") to gather immediate feedback <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:34:00">[02:34:00]</a>.
    *   **CEO Involvement**: Dylan Field reads vast amounts of customer feedback, including obscure tweets with few likes, believing they contain an "essence of truth" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:50:00">[02:50:00]</a>. This top-down emphasis on customer obsession informs product intuition <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:07:00">[03:07:00]</a>.
    *   **Balancing Feedback**: While vocal minorities on platforms like Twitter are valuable, Figma balances this with data from support tickets (often dissatisfied users) and sales conversations (prospect perceptions) to avoid blind spots <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:32:00">[02:32:00]</a>.
*   **Empowering Internal Champions**: Figma's sales team focuses on empowering passionate users within companies to become advocates for the product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:37:00">[05:37:00]</a>. This strategy shifts the focus from direct selling to enabling "community-led growth," where internal designers become the primary drivers of adoption <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:24:00">[05:24:00]</a>.
*   **Building Community Structures**: Programs like "Friends of Figma" connect passionate users in different geographies, fostering regular meetings and a Discord channel for evangelism. This human connection builds loyalty that fuels internal advocacy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:40:00">[05:40:00]</a>.

### Product as a Catalyst for Change
Figma's product design was intentionally disruptive, which, despite initial controversy, became a key part of its appeal and growth.

*   **Challenging the Status Quo**: Figma introduced controversial features like real-time collaboration and visibility into others' work ("everyone can see what you're doing" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:04">[00:00:04]</a>). Some early designers even reacted with "if this is a feature of design I'm quitting" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:00">[00:11:00]</a>. This tension signaled a "revolution" and a new way of working, which equipped customers to champion the product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:00">[00:23:00]</a>.
*   **Cultivating Emotional Response**: For [[productled growth]] to succeed, there needs to be an "irrational, emotional response" or love for the product, both internally and externally <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:00">[00:57:00]</a>. This emotional connection motivates users to "sing at the top of their lungs" about Figma <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:18">[00:57:18]</a>.
*   **Enabling Sharing and Openness**: Figma's ability to easily share files allows users to "open source" their work and show "how we do it at XYZ company," generating interest and fostering a culture of shared learning within the design community <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:16:00">[05:16:00]</a>.

### Internal Practices for Quality and Growth
Figma's internal practices reinforce its commitment to product quality, which in turn supports its [[productled growth]] model.

*   **Dogfooding (Using Your Own Product)**: It's "so important that you're using your own product" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:28:00">[03:28:00]</a>. Figma actively encourages its entire company to use Figma and FigJam. For example, they shifted from a memo culture to a deck culture, building presentations directly in Figma, which exposes employees to potential issues and familiarizes them with the product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:52:00">[03:52:00]</a>. This drives "personal accountability" for product quality among engineers and designers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:52:00">[03:52:00]</a>.
*   **Embracing Experimentation**: Figma frequently runs experiments, acknowledging that not all features or launches succeed initially <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:14:00">[03:14:00]</a>. An example is the "branching and merging" feature for design systems, which saw low initial adoption despite significant investment, highlighting the need for creative ways to assess effectiveness when building for diverse customer types <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:27:00">[03:27:00]</a>.

### Future Growth Avenues with Adobe
The potential acquisition by Adobe opens new avenues for Figma's growth by integrating with a broader set of creative tools and leveraging real-time collaboration across more use cases <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:00">[00:59:00]</a>. This includes making the end-to-end product development process more seamless and bringing Figma's multiplayer capabilities to other creative domains like video editing or 3D objects <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:00">[01:00:00]</a>. Regardless of the acquisition, Figma intends to maintain its strong relationship with its community and users, recognizing it as a crucial part of its "magic" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:00">[01:11:00]</a>.

## Core Traits of Successful Product Managers

Maggie Crowley, VP of Product at Toast and former VP at Charlie Health and senior director at Drift, identifies three common threads among the best product managers she has worked with, hired, and managed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:19">[00:01:19]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:17">[00:05:17]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:24">[00:05:24]</a>:

1.  **Simplifying and Breaking Things Down** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:31">[00:05:31]</a>: This involves identifying the single most important task at any given moment, even amidst numerous priorities and projects <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:38">[00:05:38]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:43">[00:05:43]</a>. It's about prioritizing and sticking with that one thing long enough to finish it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:07">[00:06:07]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:13">[00:06:13]</a>.
2.  **Following Up on Results** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:00">[00:08:00]</a>: Great PMs consistently track and report on the metrics and outcomes of their work, even long after a feature has launched <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:00">[00:08:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:19">[00:08:19]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:51">[00:21:51]</a>. This builds trust with leadership and facilitates continuous learning <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:20">[00:22:20]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:49">[00:23:49]</a>.
3.  **"Carrying the Water" (Doing Unglamorous Work)** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:05">[00:09:05]</a>: This means being willing to do the tough, often boring tasks that are necessary for the product's success, such as customer support, sales, marketing, copywriting, project management, and QA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:10">[00:09:10]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:17">[00:09:17]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:12">[00:31:12]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:23">[00:31:23]</a>. As a PM, you are responsible for outcomes and results, filling any gaps to ensure the product succeeds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:22">[00:09:22]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:46">[00:29:46]</a>.

These traits go beyond typical "basic" PM skills like communication, analytical ability, or user experience intuition, which are considered fundamental <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:52">[00:09:52]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:49">[00:54:49]</a>. [[Product management career growth framework | True success]] as a PM comes from the ability to ship more, get more reps, and create impact <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:33">[00:10:33]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:06">[00:24:06]</a>.

### Building the "Simplifying" Muscle

To improve simplification, consider these tactics:
*   **Read your writing aloud** to identify overly complicated sections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:50">[00:11:50]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:00">[00:12:00]</a>.
*   **"Just say that"** – state your point directly and concisely, as you would in conversation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:31">[00:12:31]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:35">[00:12:35]</a>.
*   **Cut unnecessary words and paragraphs**; often, the first two paragraphs of any document can be removed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:01">[00:14:01]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:08">[00:19:08]</a>.
*   **Apply the "rule of three"** for strategies, conclusions, and next steps <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:32">[00:14:32]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:54">[00:18:54]</a>.
*   **Focus on the single most impactful thing** and be willing to say "no" to less important ideas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:23">[00:15:23]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:45">[00:15:45]</a>.
*   **Utilize the Minto Pyramid Principle** by starting with the main conclusion/headline and then providing supporting arguments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:26">[00:18:26]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:49">[00:18:49]</a>.
*   **Seek feedback from peers** who can edit your work and help you simplify <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:44">[00:19:44]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:01">[00:20:01]</a>.

## Crafting a Product Strategy

[[Product management career growth framework | Product strategy]] is often cited as an area for improvement, particularly for individuals seeking leadership roles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:43">[00:34:43]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:10">[00:35:10]</a>. A robust product strategy document should include:

*   **Landscape:** Detail the current business and market conditions, including competitors, a SWOT analysis, and key risks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:14">[00:36:14]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:19">[00:36:19]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:24">[00:36:24]</a>.
*   **Current Business Goals:** Outline current quarterly or annual company objectives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:30">[00:36:30]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:36">[00:36:36]</a>.
*   **Current State:** Provide an honest assessment of your product, the business, and your specific area, including what works, what doesn't, customer feedback, and support tickets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:47">[00:36:47]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:51">[00:36:51]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:58">[00:36:58]</a>.
*   **Technical Hurdles:** Document big pieces of tech debt and engineering priorities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:04">[00:37:04]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:11">[00:37:11]</a>.
*   **Opportunity:** Identify the top one or two opportunities for your team, where you can uniquely compete and win <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:33">[00:37:33]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:37">[00:37:37]</a>.
*   **Challenges:** Discuss the hardest aspects of pursuing the opportunity and what assumptions need to be true for it to succeed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:48">[00:37:48]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:55">[00:37:55]</a>.
*   **Proposed Solution:** Take a swing at writing down your proposed solution, what needs to be built, and how it might work (ideally 3 bullet points) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:06">[00:38:06]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:12">[00:38:12]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:18">[00:38:18]</a>.
*   **Plan:** Outline how you would sequence the work, organize the team, and estimate costs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:26">[00:38:26]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:31">[00:38:31]</a>.

This document should be shared broadly to ensure transparency and facilitate discussions, allowing disagreements to focus on specific points rather than general lack of understanding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:35">[00:38:35]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:40">[00:38:40]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:50">[00:38:50]</a>. While it can be long (e.g., 20 pages), a concise summary should be included at the top for quick understanding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:43">[00:41:43]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:55">[00:41:55]</a>. Always seek critical feedback, especially from engineering counterparts, and be open to having holes poked in your work <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:27">[00:52:27]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:32">[00:52:32]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:43">[00:52:43]</a>.

## One-Pager Template for Product Teams

A one-pager (or PRD/spec) serves as the core artifact for a product team (product, design, engineering) to define what they are building <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:03">[00:50:03]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:10">[00:50:10]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:16">[00:50:16]</a>.

Key sections of a one-pager:
*   **Background and Context** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:57">[00:50:57]</a>.
*   **The Problem:** What is the problem you are solving? <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:01">[00:51:01]</a>.
*   **Why This Problem Matters:** Explain the significance of the problem <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:01">[00:51:01]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:20">[00:51:20]</a>.
*   **Why This Problem Matters Now:** Address the urgency and time-sensitivity of solving this particular problem <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:02">[00:51:02]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:25">[00:51:25]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:52">[00:53:52]</a>.

The most important section is the initial "why" to center the team around the problem <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:51">[00:50:51]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:07">[00:51:07]</a>. The document should also keep a running list of decisions and link to related artifacts and research <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:36">[00:51:36]</a>.

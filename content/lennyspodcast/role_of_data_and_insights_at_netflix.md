## Elizabeth Stone: From Economist to CTO

Elizabeth Stone, formerly Vice President of Data and Insights, is now the Chief Technology Officer (CTO) at Netflix, and possibly the first economist to hold such a position at a Fortune 500 company <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:00">[01:07:00]</a>. She views economics as a "flavor of data science," involving a set of technical skills and a unique way of framing and solving problems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:55:00">[06:55:00]</a>.

Her economics background has been instrumental in her career, particularly in understanding incentives and anticipating unintended consequences in both internal leadership and external market dynamics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:53:00">[08:53:00]</a>. This perspective allows for simplifying complex business challenges to make them tractable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:54:00">[07:54:00]</a>. Stone anticipates seeing more economists rise in the ranks of tech companies due to the growing recognition of their value <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:37:00">[06:37:00]</a>.

## Unique Structure of Data and Insights Teams

Netflix maintains a centralized Data and Insights team, a departure from the common practice at scale where data-oriented teams are often embedded within specific business lines (like ads or games) or organized purely functionally (separating data engineers from data scientists, etc.) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:23">[00:54:23]</a>.

This centralized team is:
*   **Functionally Diverse** – It comprises data engineers, data scientists, analytics engineers, and consumer researchers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:55">[00:54:55]</a>.
*   **Broadly Applicable** – It works across nearly every area of the business <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:05">[00:55:05]</a>.

### Benefits of Centralization

While this structure "requires basically extraordinary partnership" with other teams, it offers several significant benefits, contributing to [[building_effective_data_teams|building effective data teams]]:

*   **Functional Excellence** – The team can focus on developing and maintaining "world's best data engineers, world's best data scientists," ensuring a high standard of functional and technical expertise <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:35">[00:55:35]</a>.
*   **Career Mobility** – It provides better career paths and increased mobility across different problem areas within the team <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:48">[00:55:48]</a>.
*   **Cross-Pollination of Ideas** – The centralized model fosters the sharing of insights and methodologies across diverse functional areas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:59">[00:55:59]</a>.
*   **Objectivity and Truth-Telling** – Crucially, this structure allows the team to remain objective. Their role is not to simply validate desired outcomes or solve problems as initially framed by business stakeholders, but to offer an independent perspective and act as "truth tellers" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:05">[00:56:05]</a>. This enables curiosity that might not fit a different organizational structure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:34">[00:56:34]</a>.

## Integrating Research and Data Science

The Data and Insights team explicitly combines consumer insights (user research) with data science and data engineering functions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:15">[00:58:15]</a>. This is considered a "superpower" internally, allowing for a full-stack data and research expertise <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:27">[00:58:27]</a>.

This integration allows the team to:
*   **Tackle Complex Problems** – For example, understanding how to best surface recommendations involves combining attitudinal research (qualitative and quantitative) with behavioral research from data science and engineering <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:39">[00:58:39]</a>.
*   **Enhance Credibility** – Housing user research within the data organization can lead to greater credibility and avoids internal conflicts between "anecdotal evidence" from research and "hard data" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:02">[00:59:02]</a>.
*   **Maintain Consumer Orientation** – The consumer insights team ensures a strong member orientation on strategic decisions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:30">[00:59:30]</a>.

## Data-Driven Innovation and Experimentation

Netflix's commitment to data extends to its approach to innovation. For instance, the company is making significant strides in offering live content, such as hosting WWE starting in 2025 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:46:00">[01:46:00]</a>. This shift requires close partnership between content and technology organizations, with the CTO's role being to translate technical challenges and strategies to the content team and vice-versa <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:19">[01:17:19]</a>.

Netflix also conducts numerous experiments to test the resilience of its engineering systems and avoid making bigger mistakes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:38">[00:53:38]</a>. While the famous "chaos monkeys" (programs that randomly kill processes to test system stability) are no longer "unbridled" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:18">[00:52:18]</a>, the company still undertakes intentional, low-profile examples to test system behavior and performance, especially when launching new efforts like cloud games or live content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:08">[00:53:08]</a>. This systematic approach allows for data-driven insights into system robustness.

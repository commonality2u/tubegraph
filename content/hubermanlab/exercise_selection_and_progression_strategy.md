When designing a fitness program, selecting the right exercises and establishing a robust progression strategy are paramount for achieving desired fitness adaptations. Dr. Andy Galpin, along with Andrew Huberman, provides significant insights into how to approach these components effectively.

## Selecting Exercises

Exercise selection encompasses choosing movements that target specific muscle groups and serve your fitness goals. Dr. Galpin emphasizes the importance of a balanced selection of exercises across weekly training sessions to maintain overall body health and prevent overuse injuries [[designing_a_fitness_program | Designing a Fitness Program]] [<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:13">01:12:13</a>].

### Key Considerations for Exercise Selection:

1. **Targeted Muscle Groups**: Ensure exercises address all major muscle groups to maintain symmetry and functionality [[muscle_health_as_a_key_to_longevity | Muscle Health as a Key to Longevity]].
2. **Movement Patterns**: Incorporate various movement types like pushing, pulling, squatting, and hinging [[science_of_human_movement | Science of Human Movement]].
3. **Exercise Complexity**: Choose exercises you can perform with proper form and gradually increase complexity as proficiency improves [[science_based_exercise_optimization | Science-Based Exercise Optimization]].

## Progression Strategy

To continually make progress, whether in strength, endurance, or hypertrophy, a strategy for progression is crucial [[strength_training_and_hypertrophy_principles | Strength Training and Hypertrophy Principles]].

### Dr. Galpinâ€™s Progression Method [<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:42">01:13:42</a>]:

1. **Assisted to Non-Assisted Exercises**:
   - Start with exercises utilizing assistance (e.g., using bands for balance during squats).
   - Progress to performing the exercise without assistance to build confidence and stability [[protocols_for_nutrition_and_exercise | Protocols for Nutrition and Exercise]].

2. **Eccentric Loading**:
   - Introduce weights once exercises can be done confidently without assistance.
   - Focus initially on the lowering phase of exercises to gain control over movements [[mechanisms_of_muscle_metabolism_and_energy_use | Mechanisms of Muscle Metabolism and Energy Use]].

3. **Isometric Holds**:
   - Incorporate holds in the most challenging part of the range, like the bottom of a squat, to develop stability [[importance_of_recovery_in_muscle_growth_and_training | Importance of Recovery in Muscle Growth and Training]].

4. **Concentric Phase**:
   - Once comfortable with isometric holds, focus on driving back to starting position with controlled speed and form.

5. **Add Speed**: 
   - Enhance training by adding speed to movements once control is maintained through eccentric and concentric phases [[exercise_strategies_for_optimal_fat_burning | Exercise Strategies for Optimal Fat Burning]].

6. **Introduce Fatigue**:
   - Gradually introduce workouts to fatigue to test endurance and muscle capacity under stress, ensuring to maintain form [[training_program_structure_and_periodization | Training Program Structure and Periodization]] [<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:49">01:15:49</a>].

## Overarching Progress Standards

Dr. Galpin suggests a structured increase over weeks in volume or intensity, ideally by about 5-7% per week, to facilitate progressive overload without risking injury. This careful and incremental increase in work volume helps in building strength effectively [[strength_as_a_foundational_fitness_quality | Strength as a Foundational Fitness Quality]] [<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:37:01">01:37:01</a>].

> [!info] Progressive Overload
> 
> Maintaining a moderate progression ensures continuous adaptation and reduces burnout risks. This systematic increase is one of the pillars of effective training [[strength_training_principles_and_methods | Strength Training Principles and Methods]].

Both exercise selection and progression should align with individual fitness goals, whether they focus on gaining strength, building muscular endurance, or improving overall fitness levels [[physical_exercise_adaptations | Physical Exercise Adaptations]]. Tailoring these elements to personal needs can lead to better health outcomes and a more sustainable fitness journey.

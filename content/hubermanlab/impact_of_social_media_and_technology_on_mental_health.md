## Social Media: A Double-Edged Sword

Mark Zuckerberg, CEO of Meta, acknowledged that the effects of social media on mental health [[impact_of_social_media_and_selfperception | mental health]] can be both positive and negative. He emphasized the importance of how social media is used:

- **Positive Aspects**: Social media can enhance mental well-being by facilitating meaningful connections [[role_of_social_connections_in_happiness | meaningful connections]]. Relationships and friendships, often supported by social media, contribute significantly to happiness and longevity. Platforms like Facebook help people maintain these relationships by enabling connections and empathy among users (<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:05">[01:08:05]</a>).

- **Negative Aspects**: Conversely, social media can exacerbate mental health [[effects_of_modern_challenges_like_addiction_and_media_influence_on_mental_health | mental health issues]] through negative interactions, such as bullying. Passive consumption of content and exposure to relentless negativity, like constant bad news, can also impact mental health adversely (<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:43">[01:08:43]</a>).

- **Time Limits**: He argued that there is no universal "too much" when it comes to social media usage, as it depends on how individuals use it.

## Understanding Type 2 Diabetes

Type 2 diabetes is predominantly characterized by insulin resistance, where insulin fails to effectively facilitate glucose uptake, primarily into muscle cells, leading to elevated blood sugar levels. Dr. Peter Attia explains that the initial marker of insulin resistance is hyperinsulinemia (elevated insulin levels), not hyperglycemia (elevated blood sugar levels). The liver's role in glucose regulation becomes compromised, over-releasing glucose into the bloodstream. If unaddressed, this can lead to further health complications, including significant damage to the pancreas, which reduces its insulin-producing capability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:00">[00:22:00]</a>.

## The Role of Diet in Managing Diabetes and Healthspan

### Metformin and Berberine

Metformin is a frontline pharmacological treatment for type 2 diabetes, working primarily by reducing hepatic glucose output. However, diet and lifestyle modifications remain foundational in managing diabetes. Berberine, a natural supplement with similar glucose-lowering effects as metformin, represents an alternative intervention <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:10">[00:26:10]</a>.

### Benefits of Exercise

Exercise has a profound impact on reducing insulin resistance. It enhances glucose uptake by muscle cells and improves overall metabolic health. Without lifestyle interventions, the pharmacological benefits of drugs like metformin may be limited, as highlighted by the research suggesting that exercise and weight management significantly amplify therapeutic outcomes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:00">[00:23:00]</a>. ([[mechanisms_of_muscle_metabolism_and_energy_use | Mechanisms of muscle metabolism and energy use]])

### Caloric Restriction and Nutritional Balance

Dr. Attia recommends a balanced nutritional approach, focusing on maintaining energy balance rather than stringent caloric restriction, which can be challenging and may lead to muscle mass loss in extreme cases <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:30:08">[01:30:08]</a>. Instead, integrating dietary practices that maintain muscular strength and fatty acid metabolism is advocated for enhancing healthspan. ([[the_implications_of_energy_balance_on_health_and_weight_management | The implications of energy balance on health and weight management]])

## Enhancing Healthspan Through Lifestyle

Both Dr. Attia and Dr. Huberman stress that optimal glucose homeostasis and insulin sensitivity can often be maintained without dietary extremes. Regular physical activity, adequate sleep, and stress management are critical lifestyle factors that substantially enhance insulin sensitivity and overall health [00:23:00].

## What Are Physiological Sighs?

Physiological sighs are spontaneous deep breaths that occur routinely, approximately every five minutes. Dr. Feldman mentions that people generally underestimate the frequency of sighs, often guessing much lower numbers. However, physiological sighs are crucial for lung function <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:00">[00:37:00]</a>.

## The Role of Physiological Sighs in Lung Health

The human lung is home to approximately 500 million alveoli, tiny air sacs essential for oxygen exchange. These alveoli have a tendency to collapse due to the fluid lining’s surface tension. A single deep breath, or sigh, supplies the necessary pressure to reinflate and maintain the functionality of these air sacs, thus preventing collapse. This process is crucial for maintaining the large effective surface area of the lungs necessary for optimal oxygen exchange <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:00">[00:39:00]</a>.

## Historical Context and Modern Application

Historically, the significance of sighs was highlighted during the era of mechanical ventilation treatments for polio victims, who were often placed in "iron lungs." Ventilators provided consistent breaths but lacked variability, leading to a high mortality rate. The introduction of periodic larger breaths in these machines, mimicking physiological sighs, significantly dropped the mortality rate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:00">[00:41:00]</a>. Modern-day mechanical ventilators employed in hospitals continue to use this principle. Every couple of minutes, they deliver a "super breath" to mimic the natural sighing process, maintaining lung health and integrity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:00">[00:42:00]</a>. ## The Underlying Mechanisms of Sigh Generation

Dr. Feldman’s team discovered that the pre-Bötzinger complex in the brainstem plays a crucial role in sigh generation. The region is responsive to certain peptides, like Bombesin-related peptides, which can significantly influence sigh frequency. Experiments altering activity in the pre-Bötzinger complex led to drastic changes in sigh frequency, demonstrating its critical role in regulating this respiratory pattern <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:00">[00:45:00]</a>. Physiological sighs are not merely reactions to emotions or external stimuli but are essential biological processes with a fundamental role in lung maintenance and function.

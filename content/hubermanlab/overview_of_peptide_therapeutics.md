Peptide therapeutics have been gaining attention due to their potential in treating a variety of health conditions and enhancing physiological functions. This article provides an overview of peptide therapeutics as discussed by Andrew Huberman in a recent podcast episode of the Huberman Lab Podcast.

## What Are Peptides?

Peptides are small proteins composed of chains of amino acids. A peptide typically consists of 2 to 50 amino acids, but chains longer than 50 amino acids are still sometimes referred to as peptides. These molecules can have a variety of roles in the body, behaving as hormones, neuromodulators, or components of cellular signaling pathways <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:42">[00:07:42]</a>.

## Categories of Peptide Therapeutics

Huberman outlines several categories where peptide therapeutics are currently being explored:

### 1. **Tissue Rejuvenation and Repair**

Peptides like BPC-157 are gaining popularity for their reported ability to promote angiogenesis and aid in the healing of muscle and connective tissues. They carry the potential of healing sports injuries and gastrointestinal issues such as IBS or colitis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:02">[00:15:02]</a>. Thymosin Beta-4 (TB500) is another peptide explored for similar purposes [[peptides_for_tissue_healing_and_repair | for tissue healing and repair]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:03">[00:37:03]</a>.

### 2. **Metabolism and Growth**

In this category, peptides are used to influence growth hormone pathways, aiming to increase muscle mass and reduce fat. Peptides like CJC-1295, Ipamorelin, and Tesamorelin fall under this category. They work by stimulating the release of growth hormone and IGF-1, which have significant roles in metabolism, tissue growth, and repair [[growth_hormonestimulating_peptides | growth hormone-stimulating peptides]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:00">[00:45:00]</a>.

### 3. **Longevity**

Peptides such as Epitalon, which are linked to altering telomere length and age-related markers, are explored for potential anti-aging benefits. Despite promising results in animal studies, human clinical trials are limited [[peptides_for_longevity | peptides for longevity]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:06">[01:06:06]</a>.

### 4. **Vitality, Mood, and Libido**

Peptides influencing the melanocortin system, such as Melanotan II, are studied for their effects on mood, libido, and skin pigmentation. These peptides mimic naturally occurring hormones that can enhance mood and libido and are being explored for their therapeutic benefits [[peptides_for_mood_and_libido | peptides for mood and libido]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:02">[01:14:02]</a>.

## Safety and Considerations

While peptide therapeutics hold exciting potential, Huberman emphasizes the need for caution due to the pleiotropic effects of peptides, which means they can have multiple effects on different tissues. Furthermore, issues like potential tumor growth from angiogenesis-promoting peptides necessitate thorough discussion and guidance from healthcare professionals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:23:02">[01:23:02]</a>.

> [!info] Caution with Peptide Usage

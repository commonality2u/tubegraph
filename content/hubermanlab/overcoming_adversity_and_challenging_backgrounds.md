In a recent episode of the Huberman Lab Podcast, Andrew Huberman sat down with David Goggins, a retired Navy SEAL known for his profound endurance feats and equally compelling personal journey. Goggins has shared much of his life story in his bestselling books, "Can't Hurt Me" and "Never Finished," but in this episode, he delves deeper into discussing how he overcame an incredibly challenging background to become an exemplar of resilience and [[resilience_and_mental_toughness | self-discipline]].

## A Difficult Start

David Goggins's early life was marked by trauma and significant challenges. Goggins’s transformation began when he initiated a relentless inner dialogue, confronting the "demons born out of his childhood" and questioning his current state of life [00:01:25](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:25">00:01:25</a>). This dialogue wasn't about seeking comfort but rather challenging himself to change fundamentally. He recognized the necessity of sacrifice and was willing to give up everything to uncover the full extent of his potential [[the_psychological_and_physical_journey_of_selftransformation | psychological and physical transformation]] [00:14:04](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:04">00:14:04</a>).

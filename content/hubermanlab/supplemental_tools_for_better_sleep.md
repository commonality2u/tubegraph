## Key Supplements for Sleep Enhancement

Dr. Huberman discusses several supplements that have shown promise in supporting sleep, primarily by modulating neurotransmitter levels or mimicking natural hormones that induce sleep. Each supplement has its unique impact on sleep architecture and should be considered in the context of individual variability and medical guidance.

### 1. **Magnesium (Threonate Form)**
- **Mechanism**: Magnesium threonate is known to aid sleep by increasing the levels of GABA, a neurotransmitter that promotes relaxation and reduces neural excitability.
- **Dosage**: Dr. Huberman personally uses 300-400mg approximately 30-60 minutes before bedtime to help with sleep onset. 
- **Considerations**: It's crucial to consult a healthcare provider before supplementing, as excessive magnesium can have adverse effects on health.
- **Timecode Reference**: <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:00">[01:15:00]</a>

### 2. **Theanine**
- **Mechanism**: [[supplements_and_brain_function | Theanine]], found naturally in tea leaves, can promote calmness and relaxation by impacting alpha brain waves and increasing serotonin, dopamine, and GABA levels.
- **Dosage**: 100-200mg is commonly taken to assist with sleep without causing grogginess.
- **Use Cases**: It's often used to counteract the jitteriness of caffeine in [[impact_of_caffeine_timing_on_alertness_and_sleep | energy drinks]].
- **Timecode Reference**: <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:08">[01:16:08]</a>

### 3. **Apigenin**
- **Mechanism**: This flavonoid, derived from chamomile, acts as a potent sleep aid by promoting the sedative effects of benzodiazepine, interacting with GABA receptors.
- **Dosage**: Typically utilized at 50mg for its calming properties.
- **Caution**: It has significant estrogen inhibition effects, making it unsuitable for those needing stable estrogen levels.
- **Timecode Reference**: <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:03">[01:18:03]</a>

### Considerations and Safety

> [!info] Health Caveats
> 
> Dr. Huberman emphasizes the importance of consulting healthcare professionals before incorporating any supplements into your regimen. Supplements can interact with other medications and individual health conditions, hence expert guidance is crucial.

## Additional Tools and Precautions

Dr. Huberman also discusses the broader context of lifestyle and behavioral changes that form the foundation of effective sleep hygiene, such as light exposure management and timing of food intake. He reiterates that while supplements can be beneficial, foundational behaviors such as maintaining a consistent sleep schedule, optimizing the sleep environment, and managing stress through practices like [[mindfulness_and_meditation_practices | yoga nidra, meditation,]] or [[hypnosis_for_stress_anxiety_and_pain_management | hypnosis]] are essential for optimal sleep quality.

Implementing these supplemental and behavioral strategies, along with professional consultation, can significantly improve sleep patterns and overall health, providing a comprehensive approach to managing sleep and [[circadian_rhythms_and_their_impact_on_health | wakefulness]].

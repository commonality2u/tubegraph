## Circadian Rhythms

### What Are Circadian Rhythms?

Circadian rhythms are 24-hour cycles that are driven by a "circadian clock" located above the roof of the mouth in the human brain. This clock dictates our sleep-wake cycle by releasing chemicals throughout the day that promote alertness and sleep. Every cell in our body features a 24-hour cycle of gene expression, coordinated to match the external light-dark cycle resulting from the Earthâ€™s rotation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:20">[00:16:20]</a>.

### Importance of Circadian Entrainment

Dr. Andrew Huberman emphasizes the need for precise circadian entrainment [[entrainment_and_its_effects_on_biology | which refers to aligning]] our internal clocks with the natural day-night cycle. Benefits of proper circadian entrainment include reduced cancer risk, improved mood, better physical and mental performance, and enhanced metabolic health. Incorrect or disrupted circadian rhythms can lead to severe health issues such as obesity, mental health disorders, and impaired recovery from illness or injury <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:40">[00:19:40]</a>.

## Ultradian Rhythms

### Overview of Ultradian Rhythms

Ultradian rhythms are cycles that last approximately 90 minutes. These cycles structure both sleep and wake states. During sleep, for example, we transition through various stages in 90-minute cycles, influencing how we cycle between REM and non-REM sleep <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:34">[00:25:34]</a> [[rem_sleep_and_its_function_in_human_health | impacting REM]]. 

### Ultradian Rhythm and Performance

Research indicates [[tools_for_improving_focus_and_attention | that these rhythms extend]] into wakefulness, guiding our capacity to focus. During wakeful states, most individuals can engage in optimal focus for about 90 minutes before cognitive resources diminish. This aligns with the release patterns of critical neurochemicals, such as acetylcholine and dopamine, which enhance our ability to concentrate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:47">[00:26:47]</a> [[biological_mechanisms_of_dopamine_release_and_regulation | through dopamine release and regulation]].

For optimal performance, it is beneficial to schedule work in 90-minute blocks [[deep_work_and_productivity_strategies |, a concept emphasized in productivity strategies]], minimizing distractions and capitalizing on the naturally occurring peaks in focus. It's advisable to space these focused periods with breaks to facilitate recovery and regeneration of cognitive resources.

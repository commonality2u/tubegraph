## The Role of Machine Learning

Machine learning serves as a critical subset of AI, emphasizing the ability of systems to learn and improve tasks through experience. This is accomplished without being explicitly programmed, allowing systems to autonomously improve over time as they are exposed to new data. Fridman explains that machine learning focuses on developing algorithms that enable computers to learn patterns and make decisions from vast amounts of data, often by employing [[role_of_neuroplasticity_and_dopamine_in_habits | statistical methods]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:59">[00:09:59]</a>.

One powerful subset of machine learning is deep learning, which utilizes neural networks to mimic the human brain's functionality loosely. These networks consist of multiple layers that process data in complex ways, allowing machines to recognize patterns and learn classifications in a [[role_of_neuronal_pathways_in_habit_formation | hierarchical manner]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:05">[00:10:05]</a>.

## Supervised vs. Self-supervised Learning

A significant part of the discussion is devoted to understanding different learning methodologies used in machine learning, namely supervised and self-supervised learning. Supervised learning involves training an algorithm on a labeled dataset, where the desired output is known. This method is typically used in tasks like image recognition, where a neural network is provided with numerous examples to learn from and improve its [[improving_cognitive_functions | accuracy]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:18">[00:10:18]</a>.

In contrast, self-supervised learning involves reducing the need for labeled data by allowing the system to draw conclusions from [[neural_circuits_for_learning | unlabeled data]]. This approach is increasingly gaining traction due to its ability to harness vast amounts of unstructured data like text and images from the internet, allowing machines to develop a more generalized understanding of world concepts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:34">[00:12:34]</a>.

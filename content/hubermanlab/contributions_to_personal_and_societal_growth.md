## Understanding Growth Mindset

[[growth_mindset_and_its_application | Growth mindset]], as described by Dr. David Joerger, is the belief that abilities and potential in any domain can change with the right conditions and support. This mindset contrasts with a fixed mindset, where personal qualities are seen as static, often leading to a more stressful perception of challenges [<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:46">[00:04:46]</a>]. 

> [!info] Definition of Growth Mindset
> [[growth_mindset_and_its_importance | Growth mindset]] emphasizes that change and improvement are possible under suitable circumstances and support, which counters the stress-inducing belief of fixed immutability.

## Impactful Studies and Findings

Dr. Joerger references a pivotal study published in 2019 in *Nature*. This study demonstrated the long-term benefits of [[growth_mindset_and_its_application | growth mindset interventions]], specifically targeting ninth graders. The participants were found to have better grades and were more likely to engage in challenging coursework long after the intervention, with effects observed even four years later [<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:29">[00:06:29]</a>].

## Stress as a Performance Enhancer

Another critical concept discussed is the [[stress_as_a_performanceenhancing_factor | stress-is-enhancing mindset]]. This mindset reframes the physiological stress response from being an indicator of potential failure to a resource for optimizing performance [<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:31">[00:25:31]</a>]. By recognizing stress symptoms as aids rather than hindrances, individuals can leverage these responses to fuel achievement and growth.

## What is Interoception?

Interoception is the process through which we perceive the internal condition of our bodies, such as our heartbeat, breathing, and the state of our digestive system. It also includes the chemical senses that convey information about the body's internal environment, such as acidity or levels of specific gases <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:28">[00:00:28]</a>.

## Mechanisms of Interoception

Understanding interoception involves exploring two main types of information processing in the body: **mechanical information** and **chemical information**.

### Mechanical Sensing

Mechanical sensing involves detecting physical changes, such as pressure or stretch within organs. For example, the lungs and diaphragm interact mechanically when breathing, affecting heart rate through a feedback loop involving the brain [[the_neuroscience_of_breathing | involving the brain]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:00">[00:14:00]</a>.

### Chemical Sensing

Chemical sensing involves detecting internal chemical states, such as pH levels or the presence of nutrients and gases. Neurons communicate these signals to the brain, impacting processes like digestion and immune response [[gutbrain_axis_signaling_pathways | and immune response]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:00">[00:31:00]</a>.

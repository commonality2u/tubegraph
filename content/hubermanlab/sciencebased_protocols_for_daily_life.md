In a recent episode of the Huberman Lab Podcast, neuroscientist Dr. Andrew Huberman discusses various science-backed protocols that can optimize different aspects of daily life including [[the_role_of_sleep_in_learning_and_memory | sleep]], mood, learning, nutrition, and exercise. Dr. Huberman outlines a structured approach that's grounded in peer-reviewed literature, emphasizing the importance of [[circadian_rhythms_and_their_impact_on_health | circadian rhythms]] and how an optimized daily routine can contribute to overall wellbeing.

## The Daily Structure

> [!info] A 24-Hour Perspective
> 
> The idea of structuring a day's activities in alignment with our biological clock is not just practical but also deeply biological. Every cell in the body follows a circadian rhythm which is synchronized with the Earth's 24-hour cycle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:09">[00:02:09]</a>. 

### Morning Routines

- **Waking and Temperature Minimum**: Upon waking up, Dr. Huberman records the time to determine his "temperature minimum," or the time when body temperature is at its lowest, typically two hours before waking. Knowing this helps to optimize circadian rhythms such as sleep and eating schedules <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:20">[00:10:20]</a>.

- **Morning Light Exposure**: He emphasizes the importance of getting sunlight exposure in the morning, which helps regulate mood and metabolism. Even a brief walk while getting some natural light can reduce anxiety by decreasing activity in the amygdala, the brain's fear center <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:22">[00:17:22]</a>.

- **Hydration and Delaying Caffeine**: Hydration is prioritized before caffeine, which is deliberately delayed 90 minutes to avoid an afternoon crash. This timing allows natural cortisol levels to rise independently of [[caffeine_and_its_impact_on_sleep_and_alertness | caffeine]]-induced spikes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:10">[00:28:10]</a>.

### Work and Focus Strategies

- **90-Minute Work Bout**: The brain operates on 90-minute [[ultradian rhythms | ultradian cycles]], and Dr. Huberman organizes his work into these focused periods followed by breaks. He uses tools like white noise and systematic internet blocks to maintain focus during these sessions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:44">[00:42:44]</a>.

### Nutrition and Exercise

- **Fasting and Meal Timing**: Dr. Huberman follows a fasting routine in the morning to improve focus and uses meal timing strategically to support daily functions. He emphasizes starting the day with low carbohydrates and gradually increasing carb intake towards the evening to aid relaxation and sleep <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:01">[01:12:01]</a>. This approach to [[roles_of_fasting_and_eating_schedules_on_metabolism | meal timing]] can impact metabolism.

- **Daily Exercise**: Physical activity is another critical component of Dr. Huberman's daily regimen. He alternates between endurance and strength training in structured ratios to optimize both [[exercise_and_brain_health_enhancement | brain and body health]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:17">[00:53:17]</a>.

### Sleep and Relaxation

- **Non-Sleep Deep Rest (NSDR)**: Dr. Huberman advocates for [[effectiveness_of_nsdr_nonsleep_deep_rest_techniques | NSDR practices]], particularly hypnosis, as effective ways to enhance relaxation, focus, and neuroplasticity during the day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:24:17">[01:24:17]</a>.

- **Optimizing Sleep Conditions**: He advises maintaining a dark, cool sleeping environment and discusses supplements like magnesium threonate, apigenin, and theanine to aid the transition to sleep <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:51:00">[01:51:00]</a>.

## Conclusion

Dr. Andrew Huberman provides a detailed, science-backed guide for structuring a day to enhance various aspects of mental and physical health. By aligning activities with natural circadian rhythms and using deliberate practices, individuals can significantly impact their overall wellbeing, according to the latest neuroscience research.

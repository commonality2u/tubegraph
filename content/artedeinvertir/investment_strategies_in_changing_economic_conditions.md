### Ray Dalio's Portfolio Adjustments

Ray Dalio has significantly de-risked his portfolio. He has sold 90% of his shares and reduced weight in nearly 300 others <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:17">[00:07:17]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:24">[00:07:24]</a>.

Key sales include:
*   **Emerging Market ETFs:** He sold almost all his holdings in emerging market ETFs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:34">[00:07:34]</a>. This is because emerging markets and technology stocks historically correct more during volatility, as they are perceived as riskier assets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:40">[00:07:40]</a>.

Key purchases (though smaller in percentage than sales) have been in **defensive shares** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:00">[00:08:00]</a>. These stocks are expected to perform better if the market corrects significantly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:04">[00:08:04]</a>. Examples include:
*   Procter & Gamble <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:13">[00:08:13]</a>
*   Costco <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:15">[00:08:15]</a>
*   Pepsi (similar to Coca-Cola) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:17">[00:08:17]</a>

These defensive stocks have shown minimal declines in the current volatile period <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:20">[00:08:20]</a>. While they tend to rise slower in bull markets, they typically fall much less during market downturns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:34">[00:08:34]</a>. This demonstrates Dalio's clear defensive positioning, aligning his actions with his cautionary statements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:40">[00:08:40]</a>.

## Real Estate Investments in Inflationary Environments

However, rising interest rates can negatively impact asset valuations in the short term <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:53">[01:18:53]</a>. Central banks are in a "trap" where they cannot significantly raise interest rates in Europe without collapsing the consumer economy due to high mortgage debt <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:03">[01:19:03]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:23:42">[01:23:42]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:23:48">[01:23:48]</a>.

This situation could lead to an environment of **negative real interest rates** (interest rate minus inflation), where financial and real estate investments tend to do well <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:40">[01:22:40]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:48">[01:22:48]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:50">[01:22:50]</a>.

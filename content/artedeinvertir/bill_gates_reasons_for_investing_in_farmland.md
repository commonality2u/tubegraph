## Key Reasons for Farmland Investment

### 1. Climate Change and Food Production
[[bill_gates_investment_strategies_and_decisions | Bill Gates]] holds a very negative outlook on future climate change <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:49:00">[01:49:00]</a>. He anticipates that more extreme weather conditions will lead to reduced global food production <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:55:00">[01:55:00]</a>. This scarcity is expected to drastically increase the price of agricultural raw materials and, consequently, the value of quality [[bill_gates_investment_in_arable_land | arable land]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:01:00">[02:01:00]</a>. For example, corn production is projected to decrease by 24% by 2050 due to climate change effects <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:13:00">[02:13:00]</a>. Coupled with a growing global population, this suggests a continuous rise in the price of [[bill_gates_investment_in_arable_land | arable land]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:21:00">[02:21:00]</a>. Historically, land values have consistently risen since the onset of climate change, with a $1,000 investment in US farmland in the 1950s being worth over $1 million today, outperforming even the S&P 500 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:32:00">[02:32:00]</a>.

Investing in land serves as an excellent hedge against periods of high inflation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:47:00">[03:47:00]</a>. Historically, land and [[bill_gates_investment_in_arable_land | arable land]] have proven to be effective protections against inflation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:10:00">[04:10:00]</a>. During the high inflation period of the 1980s, the price of [[bill_gates_investment_in_arable_land | arable land]] consistently rose while the stock market (S&P 500) collapsed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:29:00">[04:29:00]</a>.

### 4. Plant-Based Proteins
The increasing popularity of plant-based protein foods, such as Beyond Meat or Impossible Foods, is another factor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:12:00">[08:12:00]</a>. The most compelling reason [[bill_gates_investment_strategies_and_decisions | Bill Gates]] has cited for his substantial investment in this sector is the impending water shortage due to climate change <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:00:00">[11:00:00]</a>.

The presence of unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) in our skies has prompted both public interest and scientific inquiry, particularly regarding the potential influence of advanced technologies, whether terrestrial or extraterrestrial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:15">[00:00:15]</a>. While the U.S. government acknowledged the existence of unexplained objects in a June 25, 2021 report, there is a historical pattern of government misdirection concerning such phenomena, raising questions about the true nature of these sightings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:12">[00:00:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:31">[00:00:31]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:44">[00:00:44]</a>.

## Government's Evolving Stance on UFOs

The U.S. government has had a "very curious relationship" with UAPs throughout history <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:39">[00:04:39]</a>.

### Early Openness (1947-1952)
From 1947 to 1952, the U.S. government initially showed an "open-mindedness" towards exploring UFOs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:48">[00:04:48]</a>. The Air Force established Project Blue Book, a UFO investigation program <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:55">[00:04:55]</a>. Notable incidents like the crashes at Roswell and Trinity, and Kenneth Arnold's sighting, led many within and around the government to believe "there might be something there" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:00">[00:05:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:06">[00:05:06]</a>.

### Shift to Skepticism and Downplay (Post-1952)
A major shift occurred in July 1952, following "overwhelming series of sightings in Washington D.C." dubbed "the invasion of Washington" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:12">[00:05:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:19">[00:05:19]</a>. This prompted the CIA to issue a memo declaring the UFO phenomenon a matter of national security, capable of triggering "mass hysteria and panic" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:22">[00:05:22]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:29">[00:05:29]</a>. A commission, formed by Caltech physicist Dr. H.P. Robertson, recommended that the government "systematically downplay the phenomena" through media and other channels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:32">[00:05:32]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:39">[00:05:39]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:41">[00:05:41]</a>. Subsequent Project Blue Book leaders publicly maintained skepticism regarding extraterrestrial explanations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:43">[00:05:43]</a>, and the 1968 Condon Report closed Blue Book, dismissing many findings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:52">[00:05:52]</a>.

### Renewed Investigations
This changed in 2017 when a New York Times journalist revealed the existence of ATIP, a government UFO investigation program established in 2007 and run under Bigelow Aerospace <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:01">[00:06:01]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:07">[00:06:07]</a>.

## Independent Scientific Exploration: The Galileo Project
Due to the limitations and potential biases of government-controlled data, Harvard astronomer Dr. Avi Loeb initiated the Galileo Project <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:08">[00:02:08]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:40">[00:11:40]</a>. This project aims to build independent observatories with optical, radar, magnetic, infrared, and audio sensors atop Harvard's observatory to gather "conclusive and publicly available data on UAPs" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:14">[00:02:14]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:26">[00:02:26]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:32">[00:02:32]</a>. The project emphasizes transparent analysis of data <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:42">[00:11:42]</a>.

Dr. Loeb argues that it is "the epitome of human hubris to claim that we are alone" given billions of years and galaxies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:37">[00:02:37]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:44">[00:02:44]</a>. He highlights that most stars formed billions of years before the sun, making it "very likely that we have a neighbor" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:51">[00:02:51]</a>.

## Oumuamua: A Catalyst for Scientific Inquiry
Dr. Loeb's interest was significantly triggered by the appearance of Oumuamua in 2017 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:48">[00:06:48]</a>. This interstellar object, discovered by the Pan-STARRS telescope in Hawaii, was "fast traveling," about the size of a football field, and did not fit known criteria for asteroids or comets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:53">[00:06:53]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:08">[00:07:08]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:11">[00:07:11]</a>.

Oumuamua lacked a cometary tail and "changed speed erratically," suggesting non-gravitational propulsion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:16">[00:07:16]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:22">[00:07:22]</a>. Proposed explanations, such as chunks of frozen nitrogen, hydrogen clouds, or dust clouds, had inconsistencies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:05">[00:08:05]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:08">[00:08:08]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:11">[00:08:11]</a>. Dr. Loeb theorized that Oumuamua might be a "light sail," a thin structure propelled by sunlight reflection, which "nature doesn't make" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:13">[00:08:13]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:21">[00:08:21]</a>. Its length-to-width ratio, far exceeding typical asteroids, led him to speculate it was "intelligently designed" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:27">[00:08:27]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:34">[00:08:34]</a>.

A later discovery in 2020 by Pan-STARRS seemingly vindicated this concept: another object bafflingly similar to Oumuamua, traveling away from the sun without a cometary tail, was identified as a forgotten rocket booster from a 1966 NASA mission that used sunlight for propulsion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:57">[00:08:57]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:02">[00:09:02]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:04">[00:09:04]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:07">[00:09:07]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:10">[00:09:10]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:14">[00:09:14]</a>. This suggests that if humans can build light sails, so can extraterrestrials <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:22">[00:09:22]</a>.

## Future of Scientific Exploration: AI and Data Quality
The current data on UAPs released to the public is "not convincing scientifically" for rigorous analysis, as it often relies on eyewitness testimonies rather than controlled instrumental data <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:52">[00:10:52]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:01">[00:11:01]</a>. Military-grade equipment is designed for battlefields, not scientific expeditions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:10">[00:11:10]</a>.

The Galileo Project addresses this by using a multi-sensory system combining optical, infrared, radar, audio, and magnetic sensors, and employing Artificial Intelligence (AI) algorithms to classify objects <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:54">[00:11:54]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:00">[00:12:00]</a>. AI systems could be more objective, classifying anomalies without human ego or preconceived notions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:27">[00:09:27]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:37">[00:09:37]</a>.

> "The ai system would say well it doesn't look like any rock we've seen before therefore maybe it's a piece of equipment just like we launched but being produced by someone else" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:37">[00:09:37]</a>

This technological advancement allows for "constant passive observation," unlike the anecdotal sightings the government currently relies on <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:26">[00:12:26]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:33">[00:12:33]</a>. Most astronomers do not detect these phenomena because they focus on distant, stationary sources, ignoring fast-moving nearby objects like birds or UAPs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:41">[00:12:41]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:47">[00:12:47]</a>.

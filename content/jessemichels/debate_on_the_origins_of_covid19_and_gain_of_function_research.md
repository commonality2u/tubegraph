## Theories on COVID-19 Origin

While some believe COVID-19 to be man-made, with a 95% base case that it was not <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:50">[01:13:50]</a>. It is argued that nature is generally more adept at creating complex viruses than humans <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:40">[01:14:40]</a>.

Concerns exist regarding the proximity of the initial outbreak to a lab studying pandemics in Wuhan, especially given that two-thirds of cases reportedly had no wet market exposure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:53">[01:11:53]</a>. The argument is made that "extraordinary claims require extraordinary proof," and without it, drawing definitive conclusions remains difficult <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:54">[01:13:54]</a>.

There is also mention of a Moderna patent for a genetic splice found in COVID, which, if true, would be "somewhat funny" given the company's broad research efforts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:21">[01:13:21]</a>. Coronaviruses like MERS have been around, and drug development efforts for them existed prior to COVID-19 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:38">[01:13:38]</a>.

It is also noted that viruses can evolve similar motifs independently through selective pressure or interactions with the immune system, suggesting a natural evolutionary process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:17">[01:18:17]</a>. Ultimately, it is suggested that viruses and pathogens often act as "mirrors for your own sort of fundamental health," exposing pre-existing weaknesses in individuals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:51">[01:18:51]</a>.

## Gain-of-Function Research Debate

The debate around gain-of-function research is considered essential, particularly concerning the funding aspect, such as potential NIH funding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:12">[01:12:12]</a>.

> "We should have this debate about gain of function research which makes no sense. Gain of function, we can't even come up with a cure now, so why in a lab setting when we're not motivated by a global pandemic and solving it, would we ever come up with a cure for COVID? It makes no sense. I think it's stupid." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:09">[01:17:09]</a>

Gain-of-function research involves serial passages to make a virus stronger, which is deemed "pretty kind of crazy to do" and should be conducted under highly contained conditions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:47">[01:12:47]</a>. Concerns are raised about the safety standards in labs where such research might be conducted, with a general skepticism towards China's safety protocols <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:56">[01:12:56]</a>.

## Antigravity Research and Hidden Physics

The pursuit of antigravity technology has a hidden history tied to nuclear research and aerospace.

*   **Post-War Interest**: The 1950s saw a "golden age of relativity" where serious physicists showed a dramatic resurgence of interest in antigravity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:26:20">[04:26:20]</a>.
*   **Martin Corporation / Lockheed Martin**: Martin Corporation had an "Antigravity Research unit" called the Research Institute for Advanced Studies (RIAS), which later became Lockheed Martin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:43:34">[04:43:34]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:47:47">[04:47:47]</a>. *   **Townsend Brown**: A leading antigravity researcher, Townsend Brown developed a "gravitator" that resembled a flying saucer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:01:01">[05:01:01]</a>. His work was classified, and he had meetings with top officials in American atomic programs, including Edward Teller and General Curtis LeMay <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:08:49">[05:08:49]</a>. His work, the Biefield-Brown Effect, was later publicly acknowledged to be used in the B-2 stealth bomber's wings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:27:32">[05:27:32]</a>.
*   **Concealment of Physics**: There is a theory that after the invention of nuclear weapons, "true fundamental physics" was concealed in "deep black aerospace," while public-facing physics was directed into a "cul de sac" to cause less damage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:39:51">[04:39:51]</a>.

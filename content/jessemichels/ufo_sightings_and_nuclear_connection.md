## Historical Context and Early Engagement

From as early as 1947, top American military, government, and scientific figures were deeply involved in studying the UFO issue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:37:37">[08:37:37]</a>. These programs were often bound up with American atomic initiatives, and the pursuit of anti-gravity technologies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:59:08">[08:59:08]</a>.

In 1971, the Australian Joint Intelligence Organization (Australia's equivalent of the CIA) released a memo, authored by Harry Turner, head of their nuclear division, expressing belief that the US had misled other countries regarding its deep inquiries into UFOs and anti-gravity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:52:54">[10:52:54]</a>. The memo suggested that while Project Blue Book (the official Air Force UFO study in the 1950s and 60s) downplayed sightings, real, vital programs existed behind it to throw off public and international scrutiny <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:15:37">[11:15:37]</a>.

This 1971 Australian document comprehensively detailed US inquiries into anti-gravity and UFOs during the 1950s and 60s, listing prominent scientists, academic institutions, and major aerospace companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:54:19">[11:54:19]</a>. Notable scientists mentioned include:
*   [[connection_between_ufos_and_nuclear_technology | Edward Teller]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:12:00">[12:12:00]</a>
*   Freeman Dyson <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:12:00">[12:12:00]</a>
*   [[connection_between_ufos_and_nuclear_technology | Robert J. Oppenheimer]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:12:00">[12:12:00]</a>

These individuals were listed as deeply involved in anti-gravity research <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:16:30">[12:16:30]</a>. Academic outposts backed by the CIA's Office of Scientific Intelligence and aerospace companies like Bell Aircraft, Martin Corporation (pre-Lockheed Martin), and Lear Corporation were also cited <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:19:40">[12:19:40]</a>.

Further evidence from the time supports these claims:

## Wright Air Development Center (Wright Airfield) and Anti-Gravity Research

The Wright Air Development Center (now Wright-Patterson Air Force Base) in Dayton, Ohio, played a central role in this research <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:00:27">[16:00:27]</a>. It was established in 1917 and became a primary site for testing advanced aircraft and propulsion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:05:43">[16:05:43]</a>.

In 1957, a conference at UNC Chapel Hill, sponsored by the Wright Air Development Center, convened top theoretical physicists including Bryce DeWitt, John Wheeler, Peter Bergman, and Richard Feynman, to discuss gravity in the Einsteinian paradigm <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:18:50">[15:18:50]</a>. This conference was heavily influenced by Aerospace interests, with Martin Corporation employees participating <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:47:04">[15:47:04]</a>.

Wright Air Development Center had its own team of physics researchers, led by Josh Goldberg, with the sole mandate to "figure out how to beat gravity" within their Aerospace Research Laboratory <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:35:05">[16:35:05]</a>.

Rumors about exotic technology at Wright Patterson Air Force Base were so prevalent that Senator Barry Goldwater, a presidential candidate, inquired directly with Air Force Chief of Staff Curtis LeMay in 1994 about accessing a secret room there <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:09:07">[17:09:07]</a>. LeMay reportedly became irate and refused access, stating, "Don't ever ask me that question again" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:09:09">[18:09:09]</a>.

Possible explanations for Wright Airfield's intense interest in anti-gravity:

1.  **Nazi Tech Transfer:** There was alleged tech transfer from Nazi anti-gravity programs, particularly from a secret weapons facility in Czechoslovakia (now Poland) run by SS officer Hans Kammler <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:47:04">[18:47:04]</a>. Through Operation Paperclip, Nazi scientists who worked under Kammler, such as Richard Maida, Viktor Schauberger, and Henri Coanda, ended up working at Wright Patterson Air Force Base <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:18:22">[19:18:22]</a>. 2.  **Thomas Townsen Brown's Gravitator Experiments:** Physicist Thomas Townsen Brown conducted experiments with "gravitators," asymmetric capacitors that appeared to defy gravity through high-voltage electromagnetism <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:04:08">[21:04:08]</a>. ## Twining Memo and UFO Secrecy Protocols

The Twining Memo, written by General Nathan Twining in 1947, stated that UFOs were "real," not "visionary or fictitious," and described attributes like no vapor trails, extreme climb rates, and maneuverability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:22:30">[29:22:30]</a>. Most notably, Twining suggested it was "possible within present US knowledge" to construct similar piloted aircraft, provided a "Manhattan Project Style effort" was undertaken <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:12:00">[30:12:00]</a>. He emphasized that such a project should be "set up independently of existing projects" to insulate it from civilian bureaucrats <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:48:40">[30:48:40]</a>.

This idea of a Manhattan Project-style secrecy overlaying UFO programs comports with whistleblower revelations from David Grusch in 2023 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:18:24">[31:18:24]</a>. Grusch claims that the secrecy protocols for UFOs were established by [[connection_between_ufos_and_nuclear_technology | Robert J. Oppenheimer]] and Robert Sarbacher <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:59:44">[31:59:44]</a>. They were reportedly responsible for the 1954 Atomic Energy Act, which "overlaid nuclear secrecy on top of UFO secrecy" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:20:25">[32:20:25]</a>. The act's flexible definition of "special nuclear material" meant that any material emitting radioactive properties (alpha, beta, gamma radiation) automatically became classified with the same secrecy as nuclear secrets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:37:37">[32:37:37]</a>.

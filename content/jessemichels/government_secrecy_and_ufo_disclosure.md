## Historical Context of Secrecy

Fox's personal journey into the subject began with skepticism, initially dismissing claims of alien spaceships and the Roswell incident <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:32">[00:06:32]</a>. His perception shifted after learning that the U.S. [[role_of_military_and_government_in_ufo_secrecy | government]] had "actually admitted that they recovered" something at Roswell <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:35">[00:07:35]</a>. This admission, however, stood in stark contrast to official policies designed to suppress public belief.

### The Robertson Panel and Project Blue Book
In 1953, the [[ufo_disclosure_and_government_secrecy | Robertson Panel]], a scientific committee formed at the recommendation of the CIA, adopted a deliberate policy of ridicule against UFO sightings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:54">[00:09:54]</a>. This classified document aimed to systematically downplay UFOs through media, books, and movies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:25">[00:10:25]</a>. Project Blue Book, the U.S. Air Force's investigation into UAPs, was largely seen as a public "dog and pony show" tasked with explaining away sightings, even those involving objects remaining stationary in high winds, moving against the wind, or maneuvering at considerable speed without discernible propulsion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:07">[00:10:07]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:44">[00:10:44]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:08">[00:11:08]</a>.

### Parallel, Secret Investigations
Despite public dismissals, a more clandestine investigation ran parallel to Project Blue Book <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:07">[00:13:07]</a>. Dr. J. Dr. Jacques Vallee discovered a classified document detailing an "ultra secret, highly funded scientific investigation" working parallel to Blue Book, unknown to all but a select few <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:59">[00:12:59]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:17">[00:13:17]</a>. This effort involved prominent scientists like Freeman Dyson, Edward Teller, Robert Oppenheimer, and John Wheeler <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:47">[00:14:47]</a>.

The "Wilson Memo," allegedly summarizing a 2002 conversation between Dr. Eric Davis and Admiral Thomas Wilson, further highlights this secrecy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:54">[00:50:54]</a>. The memo describes Wilson's frustration at encountering a "special access program" run by a corporation with no government oversight, working on "craft that is not of this world" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:37">[00:55:37]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:03">[00:56:03]</a>.

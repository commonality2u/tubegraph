### Statistical Validation of Psychic Functioning

*   **Jessica Utts' Review**: Statistician Jessica Utts, a former president of the American Statistical Society, reviewed the Stanford Research Institute's psychic spy data and other psychic experiments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:12">[00:08:12]</a>. She found "overwhelming evidence" for psychic functioning based on P-values, which assess the likelihood of results occurring by chance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:29">[00:08:29]</a>. A P-value less than 0.05 is generally considered statistically significant <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:16">[00:09:16]</a>.
*   **Experimental Protocols**: Utts diligently assessed experimental protocols, such as:
    1.  An experimenter selecting a random batch of five photographs from 200 *National Geographic* images <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:19">[00:11:19]</a>.
    2.  A remote viewer in a completely isolated room, unaware of the photos, tasked with drawing each selected photo <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:27">[00:11:27]</a>.
    3.  Tightly controlled conditions, using a well-tested randomizer, with no communication between the experimenters and the viewer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:50">[00:11:50]</a>.
    4.  A third person judging the match between the drawings and the selected photos <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:03">[00:12:03]</a>.
*   **Results**: These studies found an overall hit rate of 35%, a repeatable number corroborated by trials at the University of Edinburgh and the Ry Research Institute at Duke <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:17">[00:12:17]</a>.
*   **Utts' Conclusion**: "Using the standards applied to any other area of science, it is concluded that psychic functioning has been well established. The statistical results of the studies examined are far beyond what is expected by chance. Arguments that these results could be due to methodological flaws in the experiments are soundly refuted" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:37">[00:13:37]</a>. She noted the quality of evidence is so strong it would be unquestioned in any other field <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:59">[00:13:59]</a>.
*   **Skeptic Collaboration**: Utts partnered with psychologist Ray Hyman, a prominent skeptic eager to find flaws <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:12">[00:13:12]</a>. While Hyman had minor disagreements, he agreed the experiments were free of obvious flaws and the data was too large and consistent to be dismissed as "statistical flukes," indicating "something unexplained was going on" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:40">[00:13:40]</a>.

## Biological Structures Supporting the Theory

### Microtubules

*   **Stuart Hameroff's Research**: Roger Penrose's collaborator, anesthesiologist Stuart Hameroff, suggests tiny cylindrical structures called microtubules, found in the cytoplasm of plant and animal cells, as candidates for maintaining [[quantum_mechanics_and_its_implications | quantum coherence]] in the brain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:45">[00:21:45]</a>.
*   **Consciousness Switch**: When microtubules are turned off with anesthesia, the rest of the brain functions normally, but the person becomes unconscious, implying these microstructures act as "on and off switches to our conscious perception" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:05">[00:22:05]</a>.
*   **Quantum Sensor**: Hameroff's work with Penrose, particularly in *The Emperor's New Mind*, proposes that the microtubule is our "quantum sensor that collapses the wave function into an Eigen State" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:21">[00:22:21]</a>.

### Caudate and Putamen

*   **Gary Nolan's Findings**: Stanford Professor Gary Nolan believes that the neuronal density in the caudate and putamen (part of the brain's basal ganglia) determines a person's psychic ability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:02">[00:23:02]</a>.
*   **Intuition and MRI**: When people play the game of Go and make intuitive moves (which often appear irrational but work out), these brain areas "light up" on an fMRI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:13">[00:23:13]</a>.
*   **Genetic Component**: MRIs of highly intuitive individuals showed increased neural density in the caudate and putamen, and this trait was also found in their family members, suggesting a genetic component <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:34">[00:23:34]</a>. This system is part of the reward system and is involved in predicting future rewards <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:39">[00:24:39]</a>.

### Epim Lieberman's Goldfish Experiment

*   **Cytoskeletal Learning**: Russian scientist Epim Lieberman, a leading consciousness researcher in the Soviet Union (1970s-80s), conjectured that [[quantum_mechanics_and_its_implications | quantum effects]] in cytoskeletal membranes (where microtubules reside) allow cells to communicate and encode semantic information <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:01">[00:26:01]</a>.
*   **Experiment**: Lieberman froze four goldfish under different conditions involving spinning in a centrifuge and reorientation time, then thawed them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:21">[00:26:21]</a>.
*   **Results**: All fish had intact cytoskeletons and neurons and could swim normally, except for the second fish, whose cytoskeletons completely disassembled because it was frozen while being spun <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:52">[00:26:52]</a>. This fish could not compute its environment or spatially navigate, underscoring the "cytoskeleton and microtubules being essential for conscious awareness" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:03">[00:27:03]</a>.

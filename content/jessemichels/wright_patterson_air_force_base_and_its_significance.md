Wright-Patterson Air Force Base in Ohio has been a focal point in discussions surrounding unidentified flying objects (UFOs) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:05">[00:11:05]</a>.

## Alleged UFO Connections
*   **Roswell Materials** Reportedly, materials from the Roswell crash were taken to Wright-Patterson Air Force Base <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:07">[00:11:07]</a>.
*   **Anti-Gravity Research Center** In the 1950s, the base is said to have become an anti-gravity research center <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:12">[00:11:12]</a>.

## Secrecy and Inquiries
Civilian politicians who inquired about activities at Wright-Patterson were consistently shut down <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:14">[00:11:14]</a>.

### Anecdote: General Curtis LeMay
General Curtis LeMay, a figure in the [[ufo_and_uap_phenomena_witnessed_by_military_personnel | military]], reportedly became very angry and cussed out a civilian politician who asked about a secret room at Wright-Patterson where "secret stuff" was kept <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:20">[00:11:20]</a>.

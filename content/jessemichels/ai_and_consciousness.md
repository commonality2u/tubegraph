Early proponents of the simulation theory include science fiction authors Philip K. Dick and William Gibson <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:58">[00:21:58]</a>. The theory gained academic traction with Oxford philosopher Nick Bostrom's 2012 book, *Superintelligence*, which put forth the hypothesis that we are literally living in a computer simulation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:16">[00:28:16]</a>.

### AI Development and Virtual Reality

## [[consciousness_and_advanced_technology | Consciousness]] and Quantum Physics
Claude Shannon, the architect of information theory, and John Archibald Wheeler, a proponent of information as the basis of physics ("it from bit/cubit"), are foundational to this discussion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:22:00">[03:22:00]</a>.

### Wave Function Collapse and the Observer
Quantum physics describes particles existing in "superposition" until a "measurement or an observation" occurs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:14:00">[03:14:00]</a>. Virk compares this to "lazy evaluation" in computer science, where elements are only rendered when needed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:14:00">[03:14:00]</a>.

The "Multiverse interpretation" by Hugh Everett, a student of Wheeler, suggests that each possibility in a probability wave branches into different physical universes upon collapse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:05:00">[07:05:00]</a>. This can be understood as cloning information, not entire physical universes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:05:00">[07:05:00]</a>.

Early quantum mechanics proponents like Heisenberg, Planck, Von Neumann, and Wigner believed the mind was part of the wave function collapse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:27:00">[03:27:00]</a>. Wheeler further argued for a "participatory universe," where the observer affects the outcome <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:27:00">[03:27:00]</a>. Erwin Schrödinger, the creator of the wave equation, stated that [[consciousness_and_advanced_technology | consciousness]] is "absolutely fundamental" and cannot be accounted for in physical terms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:41:00">[03:41:00]</a>.

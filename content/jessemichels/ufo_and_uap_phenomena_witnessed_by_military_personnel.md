## Key [[uaps_and_military_encounters | Military Encounters]] and Observations

Initial assumptions by pilots that sightings were mere radar errors were quickly dispelled due to the objects' behavior <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:17">[00:00:17]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:29">[00:12:29]</a>. These objects were steady, solid, and consistent, often operating at speeds exceeding the speed of sound <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:24">[00:00:24]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:27">[00:00:27]</a>.

### Radar System Upgrades and Initial Detections
A major upgrade to the radar system from the mechanically scanned array radar (APG 73) to the electronically scanned array radar (AB79) in F-18 fighter jets, took about eight months to complete <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:00">[00:11:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:10">[00:11:10]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:28">[00:11:28]</a>. Immediately after the upgrade, objects were detected in a working area off the coast of Virginia Beach <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:55">[00:11:55]</a>. Unlike older radar systems which might pick up reflections from moving cars or thick clouds, the new system was designed to filter out such errors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:06">[00:12:06]</a>. However, the detected objects did not behave like false tracks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:43">[00:12:43]</a>.

### Anomalous Behaviors
Observed characteristics that defy known aerial phenomena include:
*   **Stationary in High Winds** Objects remained perfectly stationary, even in Category 3 hurricane-type winds, without vibrating or being moved around <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:52">[00:12:52]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:12">[00:13:12]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:19">[00:13:19]</a>.
*   **Varying Speeds** They were observed at speeds ranging from 0.0 Mach (stationary) to 0.6-0.8 Mach (250-350 knots) in holding or circular patterns, and some even exceeded the speed of sound, reaching up to 1.2 Mach <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:21">[00:13:21]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:14">[00:14:14]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:16">[00:19:16]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:32">[00:19:32]</a>.
*   **Meandering Movement** Sometimes, objects moved with non-consistent headings and altitudes in a "sloppy" way, which is not typical for military operations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:42">[00:13:42]</a>.
*   **Low Observability and Masking Signatures** They appeared to mask their infrared (IR) signatures, preventing clear visual identification <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:21">[00:19:21]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:21">[00:32:21]</a>.
*   **Lack of Propulsion** No propellers or visible propulsion systems were detected <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:20">[00:19:20]</a>.
*   **Formation Flying** In one notable instance, four objects were detected flying in a precise line of breast formation, about a mile apart, suggesting digital connection or coordination <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:56">[01:14:56]</a>.

### Multi-Sensor Corroboration
Initially, pilots observed these phenomena without widely discussing them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:02">[00:14:02]</a>. However, when combined with Forward Looking Infrared (Fleer) systems and missile systems (A9X), the objects showed correlation across three different sensors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:00">[00:15:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:25">[00:15:25]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:32">[00:15:32]</a>. This indicated the presence of physical objects rather than radar errors, prompting pilots to investigate further to gain visual confirmation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:37">[00:15:37]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:56">[00:15:56]</a>.

### Eyewitness Accounts and Descriptions
Despite sensor data, visual confirmation was elusive initially <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:26">[00:16:26]</a>. However, a significant [[navy_fighter_pilot_ufo_encounters_and_eyewitness_accounts | Navy fighter pilot UFO encounter]] occurred when an object shot down the left or right wing of a lead aircraft, coming within 50 feet of the cockpit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:16">[00:17:16]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:20">[00:17:20]</a>. The pilot described it as a "dark gray or Black Cube inside of a clear sphere" approximately 5 to 15 feet in diameter <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:33">[00:00:33]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:57">[00:17:57]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:00">[00:18:00]</a>. ### Widespread Sightings

## [[military_involvement_with_ufo_phenomena | Military Involvement]] and Response

The widespread nature of these [[experiences_and_testimonies_of_military_personnel_on_ufos | experiences and testimonies]] highlights a critical safety and national security issue.

### Safety Concerns
Near-midair collisions led to official safety reports (HAZREPs) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:07">[00:18:07]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:11">[00:18:11]</a>. Several unreported near-midair incidents were discovered within one squadron alone <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:24">[00:18:24]</a>. The objects' non-respect for designated area boundaries, including international commercial channels, further exacerbates safety concerns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:24">[00:21:24]</a>. The risk is heightened for military pilots operating at the edge of their cognitive ability, as opposed to commercial airliners <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:35">[01:04:35]</a>. Historical cases of mysteriously missing aircraft or crashes after hitting airborne objects may also be related <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:39">[01:05:39]</a>.

### Stigma and Underreporting
Despite the prevalence, many military personnel, including fighter pilots, have been hesitant to report UAP encounters due to the stigma surrounding the topic, fearing career repercussions or believing nothing would come of it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:21">[00:27:21]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:29">[00:27:29]</a>. However, 100% of those who have had firsthand experiences are convinced of their reality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:34">[00:26:34]</a>. The conversation about UAP is becoming more open, especially within the Navy, with pilots briefing and debriefing sightings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:11">[00:27:11]</a>.

### [[government_tracking_of_ufos_and_uaps | Government Tracking]] and Disclosure Efforts
The U.S. government has taken steps to address the UAP phenomenon. Ryan Graves, a former F/A-18 pilot, testified before Congress, sharing his experiences and representing over 30 commercial air crew and military veterans <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:00">[00:35:00]</a>. This historic hearing, which included David Fravor and David Grusch, showcased a rare bipartisan effort to understand the issue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:25">[00:37:25]</a>.

The All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO) was established to resolve UAP sightings, a challenging task given its need to interface with the entire government <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:52">[01:00:52]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:29">[01:01:29]</a>. However, AARO's primary customer is the Department of Defense, not necessarily the public <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:15">[01:02:15]</a>. Therefore, legislative actions like the UAP Disclosure Act, with its proposed review panel, are considered crucial for declassifying information for public consumption <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:40">[01:02:40]</a>.

## Theories and Speculation on UAP Nature
Pilots initially struggled to categorize the phenomena, speculating on possibilities such as civilian craft, adversarial capabilities, or unknown natural occurrences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:41">[00:18:41]</a>. The lack of propellers, propulsion, and masked IR signatures made these categories difficult to fit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:20">[00:19:20]</a>. The theory of them being "red teaming" or testing U.S. systems is debated, as intentionally exposing pilots to extreme risk over international waters, where adversaries operate, would be a massive security flaw <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:51">[00:21:51]</a>.

The possibility of UAP possessing capabilities such as manipulating inertia or spacetime is considered due to their extreme maneuvers, like stopping immediately from supersonic speeds without apparent physical mechanisms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:16">[00:30:16]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:41">[00:30:41]</a>. The idea of "alternative timelines" where different propulsion routes were developed is also discussed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:26">[00:57:26]</a>.

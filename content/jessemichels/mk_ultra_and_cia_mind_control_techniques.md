## Exposure and Cover-up

The [[mk_ultra_mind_control_program | MK Ultra]] program was exposed by a whistleblower in 1975, prompting the [[cia_and_assassination_teams | CIA]] to acknowledge its existence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:54:00">[03:54:00]</a>. The program was not only illegal due to the [[cia_and_assassination_teams | CIA]] operating on U.S. soil but also horrific because it involved human experimentation without the knowledge or consent of the subjects <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:03:00">[04:03:00]</a>. Experiments began in prisons and jails, then extended to service members <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:17:00">[04:17:00]</a>.

"Safe houses" were even set up in San Francisco and New York City where prostitutes administered [[the_role_of_lsd_and_mind_control_in_the_1960s | LSD]] to individuals, whose behaviors were then observed through two-way mirrors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:25:00">[04:25:00]</a>.

In 1973-1974, amidst the Watergate scandal and the impending departure of President Nixon, then-CIA Director Richard Helms ordered the destruction of all [[mk_ultra_mind_control_program | MK Ultra]] files to hide the program <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:45:00">[04:45:00]</a>. The program would have remained unknown had State Department official John Marks not discovered financial files in 1974, leading to Congressional investigations, including the Church hearings and combined Ted Kennedy and Daniel Inouye hearings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:06:00">[05:06:00]</a>. During these hearings, the [[cia_and_assassination_teams | CIA]] falsely claimed the program had been ineffective, despite much of it having worked <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:46:00">[05:46:00]</a>.

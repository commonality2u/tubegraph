This presents a considerable challenge, as the European recycling market for coffee grounds is valued at 26 billion euros [00:00:00] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:41:00">[11:41:00]</a>.

In Poland, approximately 235,000 tons of coffee grounds are created annually [00:00:00] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:37:00">[02:37:00]</a>. *   **Biodegradable Materials**

    *   These materials are similar to plastic in properties [00:00:00] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:34:00">[05:34:00]</a>.
    *   Example products:

        *   Disposable items like cups, straws, and lids, which can return with the grounds to be reprocessed into "infinite items" [00:00:00] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:17:00">[06:17:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:45:00">[08:45:00]</a>.     *   This lactic acid can replace corn-derived lactic acid, offering a "game changer" as it comes from waste rather than specially cultivated crops [00:00:00] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:46:00">[07:46:00]</a>.

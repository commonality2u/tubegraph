Poland's ecobin startup is pioneering a unique technology for [[coffee_grounds_recycling_technology_and_valorization | recycling coffee grounds]], aiming to fully valorize the waste stream and produce valuable materials. This approach ensures nothing remains after the process, leading to a significantly lower [[environmental_impact_and_sustainability_in_recycling | carbon footprint]] compared to existing alternatives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:51:00">[09:51:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:02:00">[10:02:00]</a>. This technology is currently in the [[patent_and_commercialization_process_of_coffee_recycling_technology | patenting stage]], with a high chance of being the first of its kind patented globally <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:46:00">[01:46:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:51:00">[01:51:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:54:00">[01:54:00]</a>.

## Unique Technology and Valorization Process

Unlike other startups that use coffee grounds merely as a filler or additive, ecobin's method fully decomposes the grounds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:43:00">[09:43:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:46:00">[09:46:00]</a>. This "biorefinery level" processing represents the highest level of [[coffee_grounds_recycling_technology_and_valorization | valorization]], enabling the recovery of valuable ingredients for reuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:21:00">[11:21:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:27:00">[11:27:00]</a>. The technology was developed in partnership with the Warsaw University of Technology, where ecobin is a spin-off <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:18:00">[13:18:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:20:00">[13:20:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:22:00">[13:22:00]</a>.

The process is designed to be as natural as possible, especially for products intended for the food or cosmetics industries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:04:00">[07:04:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:09:00">[07:09:00]</a>.

### Economic Potential
The recycling market for coffee grounds in Europe alone is worth 26 billion euros <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:00">[00:04:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:00">[00:07:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:41:00">[11:41:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:44:00">[11:44:00]</a>.
From one ton of coffee grounds, the technology can yield raw materials for further processing worth €8,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:05:00">[03:05:00]</a>, with an R&D goal to reach €20,000 per ton <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:25:00">[03:25:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:29:00">[03:29:00]</a>.

In Poland, approximately 235,000 tons of coffee grounds are produced annually <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:37:00">[02:37:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:41:00">[02:41:00]</a>, which can be converted into valuable raw materials for various industries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:46:00">[02:46:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:49:00">[02:49:00]</a>. On a European scale, 9,000 tons of coffee grounds are produced daily, mostly ending up as mixed waste or bio-fraction for composting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:01:00">[11:01:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:05:00">[11:05:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:09:00">[11:11:00]</a>.

## Products Derived from Coffee Grounds

The ecobin technology extracts multiple valuable components from coffee grounds:

### Coffee Oil
Coffee contains a significant amount of fat, with approximately 15% able to be extracted <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:50:00">[03:50:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:53:00">[03:53:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:56:00">[03:56:00]</a>.

*   **Technical Application:** Can be used as an additive to biodiesel, replacing alternatives like rapeseed oil which require dedicated cultivation land <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:01:00">[04:01:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:06:00">[04:06:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:10:00">[04:10:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:13:00">[04:13:00]</a>.
*   **High-Value Applications:** Primarily targeted for return to the cosmetics and food industries. It can serve as a flavor carrier or a dye in many products <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:25:00">[04:25:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:27:00">[04:27:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:30:00">[04:30:00]</a>.

### Biodegradable Polymer (PLA) and Coffee Lignin
The technology produces polylactide (PLA) from sugars present in the grounds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:08:00">[05:08:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:10:00">[05:10:00]</a>. This involves using specially trained bacterial strains that process sugars into lactic acid <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:24:00">[07:24:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:27:00">[07:27:00]</a>. This coffee-derived lactic acid can replace lactic acid obtained from corn <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:48:00">[07:48:00]</a>, sugarcane <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:06:00">[08:06:00]</a>, or other crops <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:08:00">[08:08:00]</a>.

Lignin, a natural polymer from coffee, is also extracted in an unchanged, unprocessed form <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:32:00">[08:32:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:38:00">[08:38:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:41:00">[08:41:00]</a>. This allows its use in accordance with the plastic directive for disposable items <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:45:00">[08:45:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:47:00">[08:47:00]</a>.

Together, coffee lignin and PLA can be mixed to create materials with properties similar to conventional plastic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:05:00">[05:05:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:07:00">[05:07:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:18:00">[05:18:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:34:00">[05:34:00]</a>.

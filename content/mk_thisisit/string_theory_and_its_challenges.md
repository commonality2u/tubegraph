[[string_theory | String theory]] is a concept that has intrigued many physicists, though it has also faced significant challenges and criticisms, leading some, including the speaker, to abandon it as a path to a grand unified theory <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:09">[00:19:09]</a>.

## Initial Appeal and Mathematical Elegance
Initially, the speaker found the idea of [[string_theory]] appealing when he first heard about it from Leonard Suskind <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:21">[00:20:21]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:27">[00:22:27]</a>. ### The Problem of Infinities in Quantum Field Theory
In quantum field theory, calculations performed using Feynman diagrams often lead to infinite solutions, particularly with diagrams that include loops <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:11">[00:21:11]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:20">[00:21:20]</a>. These infinities make the solutions technically "useless" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:44">[00:21:44]</a>. Physicists employ "tricks," such as renormalization, to work around these infinities and obtain good solutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:51">[00:21:51]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:02">[00:22:02]</a>. The speaker, however, felt uncomfortable with these tricks, preferring to understand why answers were correct rather than relying on methods he didn't fully believe in <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:15">[00:22:15]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:21">[00:22:21]</a>.

### String Theory's Proposed Solution
[[string_theory]] offered an elegant solution by replacing point-like particles (represented by lines in Feynman diagrams) with strings forming "little tubes" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:32">[00:22:32]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:58">[00:22:58]</a>. These tubes, being smooth and without sharp edges, theoretically resolve the infinities in calculations, making the model "beautiful" and mathematically complete <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:03">[00:23:03]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:06">[00:23:06]</a>.

## Challenges and Criticisms
Despite its initial appeal, the speaker eventually became a strong critic of [[string_theory]].

### The Dimension Problem
A major challenge for [[string_theory]] arose with the realization that it "doesn't work unless space-time has 26 dimensions" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:21">[00:23:21]</a>. This was part of what was called the "first string revolution" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:36">[00:23:36]</a>. Later, attempts were made to get a "better version" with only 10 or 11 dimensions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:48">[00:23:48]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:51">[00:23:51]</a>. However, the speaker found this combination of dimensions (10, 11, and 26) to be a "mess" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:01">[00:24:01]</a>. The idea of introducing these extra dimensions as "little loops" or "knots" in spacetime also did not work for him <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:10">[00:24:10]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:17">[00:24:17]</a>. M-theory, for instance, involves 11 dimensions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:52">[00:25:52]</a>.

### Lack of Physical Contribution
The speaker contends that [[string_theory_and_its_limitations | string theory]] has not contributed meaningfully to understanding fundamental physics, despite its mathematical interest <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:40">[00:19:40]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:00">[00:20:00]</a>. He believes it is a "dead end" for the path towards a grand unified theory <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:44">[00:26:44]</a>.

### Grand Unified Theory
The ultimate goal of a grand unified theory is to unify all fundamental forces: electromagnetism, weak interactions, strong interactions, and gravity (general relativity) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:50">[00:26:50]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:41">[00:27:41]</a>.

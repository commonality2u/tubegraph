The concept of [[nature_of_free_will_and_determinism | free will]], defined as the false belief that one's intentions and actions are independent of prior biological and environmental factors, is argued to be nonexistent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:02">[00:16:02]</a>. ## Humans as Biological Machines

Humans are fundamentally [[nature_of_free_will_and_determinism | biological machines]], similar to worms, monkeys, or trees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:30">[00:00:30]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:40">[00:20:40]</a>. While exceptionally complicated, they are composed of the same basic ingredients and operate according to a shared biological plan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:54">[00:20:54]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:20">[00:21:20]</a>. For example, a human neuron is indistinguishable from a fly neuron under a microscope, differing only in quantity and complexity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:38">[00:00:38]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:15">[00:22:15]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:24">[00:22:24]</a>. The sophisticated behaviors unique to humans, such as philosophy or opera, are seen as emergent properties of a sufficiently large number of interconnected neurons, not a sign of free agency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:01">[00:23:01]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:12">[00:23:12]</a>.

### Determinants of Behavior

Behavior, whether considered good or bad, is the culmination of a vast array of factors that are beyond individual control <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:41">[00:03:41]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:38">[00:05:38]</a>. These determinants span multiple timescales:
*   **Immediate Brain Activity** The state of the brain seconds before an action <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:44">[00:03:44]</a>.
*   **Recent Influences** Sensory stimuli from minutes prior, and the morning's hormone levels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:50">[00:03:50]</a>.
*   **Life Events** Experiences from months or years past, including trauma, falling in love, or depression <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:03">[00:04:03]</a>.
*   **Developmental Stages** Puberty, which sees significant brain development, and childhood <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:17">[00:04:17]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:21">[00:04:21]</a>.
*   **Early Life & Genetics** Fetal life and an individual's genes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:24">[00:04:24]</a>.
*   **Ancestral & Environmental Factors** Culture inherited from ancestors, the ecosystems where that culture was created, and the evolution of the species <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:26">[00:04:26]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:29">[00:04:29]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:32">[00:04:32]</a>.

This combination of elements leaves no room for [[free_will_and_decision_making | freedom of action]] independent of past events <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:44">[00:04:44]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:48">[00:04:48]</a>. The argument against [[free_will_and_decision_making | free will]] is not that people *don't* make decisions or intend to act, but that the *reasons* for those intentions are entirely determined by prior conditions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:16">[00:15:16]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:34">[00:15:34]</a>.

## Implications of No Free Will

No one truly "deserves" anything because their actions are the inevitable outcome of prior, uncontrollable events <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:18">[00:05:18]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:20">[00:05:20]</a>.
*   **Criminal Justice System**: Prison, meritocracy, and severe punishments (like the death penalty) are deemed irrational under this framework <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:19">[00:00:19]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:47">[00:05:47]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:53">[00:05:53]</a>. The practice of prohibiting the death penalty for individuals with an IQ below 70, for instance, highlights an inherent acknowledgment that some cognitive states diminish responsibility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:24">[00:00:24]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:33">[00:09:33]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:41">[00:09:41]</a>. However, this is seen as an arbitrary line, as countless other biological and environmental factors similarly remove an individual's control over their actions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:14">[00:10:14]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:35">[00:10:35]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:44">[00:10:44]</a>.
*   **Societal Protection**: While punishment for retributive reasons is rejected, society still needs to protect itself from dangerous individuals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:32">[00:06:32]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:35">[00:06:35]</a>. This is likened to locking up a car with faulty brakes â€“ it's a dangerous machine, not one with an "evil soul" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:44">[00:06:44]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:49">[00:06:49]</a>. ### Historical Precedents for Shifting Beliefs

History provides examples where societies have abandoned beliefs in [[free_will_and_decision_making | free will]] in specific domains, leading to positive outcomes:
*   **Witchcraft**: The realization that witches do not control the weather led to the end of burning old women at the stake, making the world "much happier" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:11">[00:35:11]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:28">[00:35:28]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:44">[00:35:44]</a>.
*   **Epilepsy**: Understanding that epileptic seizures are due to neurological disorders, not demonic possession, transformed treatment from persecution to medical care <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:50">[00:35:50]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:01">[00:36:01]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:03">[00:36:03]</a>.
*   **Dyslexia**: Recognizing dyslexia as a neurodevelopmental difficulty, rather than laziness or stupidity, allowed for more effective teaching methods and prevented children from growing up with false self-perceptions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:48">[00:36:48]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:11">[00:37:11]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:42">[00:37:42]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:53">[00:37:53]</a>.
*   **Slavery**: The global shift from accepting slavery to condemning it demonstrates humanity's capacity to change deeply held beliefs about human autonomy and moral responsibility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:35">[00:38:35]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:02">[00:39:02]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:49">[00:39:49]</a>.

These examples suggest that understanding the biological and deterministic nature of human behavior leads to a "better place" for humanity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:19">[00:38:19]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:35">[00:39:35]</a>.

## Debunking Counterarguments

### [[quantum_physics_and_its_relation_to_free_will | Quantum Physics]] and Chaos

While acknowledging that reality includes [[quantum_physics_and_its_relation_to_free_will | quantum physics]] and its inherent uncertainty <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:05">[00:26:05]</a>, the speaker argues that this does not support [[concept_of_free_will_in_quantum_physics | free will]]:
*   **Scale Problem**: [[quantum_physics_and_its_relation_to_free_will | Quantum uncertainty]] at the subatomic level is far too small to influence the functioning of a single neuron, let alone the entire brain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:11">[00:27:11]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:31">[00:30:31]</a>. The required "23 orders of magnitude" leap for quantum events to affect molecular oscillations in a neuron is mathematically impossible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:32">[00:27:32]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:37">[00:27:37]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:04">[00:28:04]</a>.
*   **Randomness vs. Free Will**: Even if quantum events could influence behavior, they would lead to purely random and inconsistent actions, which is not what proponents of [[free_will_and_decision_making | free will]] typically envision <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:25">[00:28:25]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:44">[00:28:44]</a>. [[free_will_and_decision_making | Free will]] is usually associated with a "moral compass" and consistent decision-making <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:34">[00:28:34]</a>.
*   **Butterfly Effect**: While the butterfly effect highlights sensitive dependence on initial conditions and makes many things unpredictable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:37">[00:31:37]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:43">[00:31:43]</a>, unpredictability is not the same as a lack of [[Determinism vs Indeterminism in Physics | determinism]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:11">[00:32:11]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:36">[00:32:36]</a>. Chaotic systems remain deterministic in nature <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:11">[00:42:11]</a>.

Brian Greene, a renowned physicist, discusses how [[entropy_and_energy_exchange_in_the_universe | entropy]] dictates processes at all levels, from molecules to living systems and the cosmos itself <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:57:00">[05:57:00]</a>.

## Understanding Entropy
[[Entropy_and_energy_exchange_in_the_universe | Entropy]] is defined as a measure of the number of ways in which a given system can be arranged <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:36:00">[05:36:00]</a>. A system with more possible arrangements is more likely to arise <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:46:00">[05:46:00]</a>. Over time, [[entropy_and_energy_exchange_in_the_universe | entropy]] naturally increases at every level of existence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:52:00">[05:52:00]</a>.

## Entropy and Physical Processes
Every physical process, with very few exceptions, results in an increase in [[entropy_and_energy_exchange_in_the_universe | entropy]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:22:00">[05:22:00]</a>. While the total amount of energy in a system can remain constant, energy degrades over time, becoming less useful and more disordered <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:26:00">[18:26:00]</a>. This degraded energy is what is meant by chaos or [[entropy_and_energy_exchange_in_the_universe | entropy]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:31:00">[18:31:00]</a>.

Even seemingly order-creating actions, such as organizing a system with our brains and willpower, contribute to an overall increase in [[entropy_and_energy_exchange_in_the_universe | entropy]]. The heat and waste generated by our bodies during these processes add to the total [[entropy_and_energy_exchange_in_the_universe | entropy]] of the environment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:15:00">[06:15:00]</a>. Human thoughts are considered physical processes that contribute to the increase of [[entropy_and_energy_exchange_in_the_universe | entropy]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:02:00">[18:02:00]</a>.

## Entropy vs. Evolution
[[Entropy_and_energy_exchange_in_the_universe | Entropy]] represents a drive towards increasing chaos, while evolution, particularly through "molecular Darwinism," signifies a drive towards more refined systems capable of greater replication efficiency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:53:00">[06:53:00]</a>. These two forces appear to be at opposite ends of the spectrum, with [[entropy_and_energy_exchange_in_the_universe | entropy]] pulling towards disorder and evolution towards order <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:07:00">[07:07:00]</a>. Despite this apparent competition, in the grand cosmic development, [[entropy_and_energy_exchange_in_the_universe | entropy]] ultimately prevails <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:16:00">[07:16:00]</a>.

## The Ultimate Fate of the Universe
Mathematical equations describing the laws of the universe strongly suggest that every ordered system in the cosmos will eventually disintegrate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:31:00">[07:31:00]</a>. This includes:
*   **Stars** Stars will eventually exhaust their nuclear fuel, exploding or transforming into diffuse masses, losing their ordered structure that allows them to emit heat and light <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:48:00">[07:48:00]</a>.
*   **[[black_holes_and_universe_creation | Black holes]]** Even [[black_holes_and_universe_creation | black holes]], as Stephen Hawking taught, slowly emit radiation over time, eventually dissolving into a "sea of particles" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:04:00">[08:04:00]</a>.

Therefore, every system that manifests order in a given cosmological era will eventually disintegrate, yielding a disordered end product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:35:00">[08:35:00]</a>.

## Implications for Thought
In the distant future, as the temperature of the universe drops, it will reach a point where it can no longer absorb the [[entropy_and_energy_exchange_in_the_universe | entropy]] being generated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:17:00">[19:17:00]</a>. When a thinking being attempts to have one more thought, it will "burn up in the entropic waste" created by the thought process itself <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:35:00">[19:35:00]</a>. This means that eventually, the possibility for thinking to occur will cease <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:43:00">[19:43:00]</a>.

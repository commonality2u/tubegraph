## Current State and Limitations of Quantum Computers

As of the interview, there are no [[quantum_computers_and_their_challenges | quantum computers]] that possess a fully error-tolerant error correction method <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:45">[00:00:45]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:46">[00:17:46]</a>. While claims of [[quantum_computers_and_their_challenges | quantum computer]] creation exist, these machines do not yet offer a practical advantage over classical computers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:06">[00:18:06]</a>. This means that concerns about [[quantum_computers_and_their_challenges | quantum computers]] "taking over the world" are currently unfounded <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:38">[00:19:38]</a>.

### Challenges in Achieving Quantum Advantage
The concept of "quantum advantage" refers to a [[potential_of_universal_quantum_computers | quantum computer]] performing a task significantly faster or more efficiently than any classical computer. While there are theoretical areas in [[quantum_computing_technologies | quantum computing]] where this advantage seems obvious, it remains challenging to strictly prove due to the complexity involved <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:19">[00:18:19]</a>.

## Areas of Demonstrated Potential and Application

Despite the limitations of general-purpose [[potential_of_universal_quantum_computers | universal quantum computers]], specific branches of [[quantum_computing_and_its_applications_in_science_and_technology | quantum computing]] are showing remarkable progress and practical applications:

### [[quantum_computing_technologies | Quantum Simulators]]
[[quantum_computing_technologies | Quantum simulators]], which are systems like cold atoms, cold ions, or even Josephson junctions, are designed to model and simulate other physical systems rather than compute abstract problems for a universal [[potential_of_universal_quantum_computers | quantum advantage]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:29">[00:18:29]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:41">[00:18:41]</a>. These simulators are particularly effective for understanding the dynamics of large [[quantum_properties_and_their_applications | quantum systems]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:50">[00:18:50]</a>. They exist, work very well, and are developing rapidly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:06">[00:19:06]</a>.

### [[progress_in_quantum_computing_and_cryptography | Quantum Communication]]
[[progress_in_quantum_computing_and_cryptography | Quantum communication]], particularly in the field of cryptography, is developing "fantastically" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:15">[00:19:15]</a>. This involves leveraging [[quantum_theory_quantum_computers_and_the_theory_of_everything | quantum mechanics]] for secure communication.

### [[quantum_properties_and_their_applications | Quantum Metrology]]
The precision of measurement is another area where [[quantum_properties and their applications | quantum mechanics]] offers significant advancements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:21">[00:19:21]</a>. This includes improving:
*   Clocks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:29">[00:19:29]</a>
*   Frequency measurement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:29">[00:19:29]</a>
*   Measurements of small magnetic fields <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:31">[00:19:31]</a>

These improvements have practical societal benefits, such as enhancing the accuracy of GPS, which could eventually enable widespread autonomous driving <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:01">[00:20:01]</a>.

### [[quantum_computers_and_artificial_intelligence | Quantum Machine Learning]]
The application of [[quantum_theory_quantum_computers_and_the_theory_of_everything | quantum physics]] to machine learning is an ongoing area of research <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:23">[00:21:23]</a>. While classical neural networks and machine learning remain dominant, there is a possibility that [[quantum_computers_and_artificial_intelligence | quantum computers]] could offer advantages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:15">[00:21:15]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:45">[00:21:45]</a>. However, a clear, compelling example of a "terrible advantage" of quantum machine learning over classical methods has yet to be demonstrated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:38">[00:21:38]</a>. This remains a very open problem <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:47">[00:21:47]</a>.

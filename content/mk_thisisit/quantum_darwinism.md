## The Problem of Superposition

In [[quantum_theory_at_atomic_and_molecular_levels | quantum mechanics]], all possible states of an object are allowed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:12">[00:03:12]</a>. This means an object could be in multiple places simultaneously, a concept known as quantum superposition <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:23">[00:03:23]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:43">[00:04:43]</a>. For example, a glass could exist "both here and there" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:27">[00:03:27]</a>, or a coin could be simultaneously "obverse and reverse" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:36">[00:03:36]</a>. Albert Einstein found this aspect of [[quantum_mechanics_and_its_implications | quantum mechanics]] inconsistent with observed reality, believing "something is wrong" with it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:06">[00:04:06]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:23">[00:04:23]</a>.

## Decoherence

The phenomenon that resolves this contradiction is **decoherence** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:34">[00:02:34]</a>. Decoherence is the process that deprives a quantum object of its ability to be in multiple places at once, breaking down quantum superposition <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:53">[00:04:53]</a>.

Key aspects of decoherence:
*   **Measurement:** [[quantum_theory_at_atomic_and_molecular_levels | Quantum states]] are defined every time a measurement is made <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:46">[00:05:46]</a>. This "measurement" doesn't require a person or a device; any correlation that transfers information from an object to its environment acts as a measurement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:52">[00:05:52]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:00">[00:06:00]</a>.
*   **Environmental Interaction:** The environment (e.g., photons, air particles) "knows" where an object is, and this knowledge disrupts coherence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:18">[00:06:18]</a>. For example, photons hitting our eyes to allow us to see a glass causes its quantum system to cease existing in superposition <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:26">[00:06:26]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:31">[00:06:31]</a>.
*   **State Selection:** Certain [[quantum_theory_at_atomic_and_molecular_levels | quantum states]] are resistant to environmental interactions and can survive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:11">[00:07:11]</a>. If the environment tries to find out an object's position, coherence between different positions is eliminated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:31">[00:07:31]</a>.
*   **Pointer States:** States that are resistant to the environment are selected and can survive; these are called "Pointer States" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:37">[00:09:37]</a>.

## Quantum Darwinism: Beyond Decoherence

[[molecular_darwinism_and_evolution | Quantum Darwinism]] builds upon decoherence, proposing a further mechanism for the emergence of the classical world <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:38">[00:10:38]</a>.

*   **Environmental Factors:** Environmental factors affect the [[quantum_theory_at_atomic_and_molecular_levels | quantum state]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:54">[00:08:54]</a>.
*   **Selection and Replication:** The theory has two main aspects:
    1.  The selection of [[quantum_theory_at_atomic_and_molecular_levels | quantum states]] that can survive despite continuous environmental observation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:03">[00:09:03]</a>.
    2.  The replication of information about these stable states into numerous copies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:55">[00:10:55]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:36">[00:12:36]</a>. This means information is not just "swept away" but made redundant <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:47">[00:10:47]</a>.
*   **Information Copies:** Instead of a single copy, information about surviving states is "replicated in billions of copies" by the environment (e.g., photons reflecting off an object) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:44">[00:12:44]</a>, which observers then access <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:40">[00:11:40]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:49">[00:11:49]</a>. This allows for selective information transfer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:04">[00:10:04]</a>.
*   **"Darwinian" Aspect:** The theory is called "Darwinism" because of the "survival of some states" and the selective information dissemination <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:29">[00:12:29]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:58">[00:12:58]</a>. States that are resistant to the environment are also replicated in many copies, unlike their superpositions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:35">[00:14:35]</a>.

## Prohibition of Cloning

A related concept in [[quantum_theory_at_atomic_and_molecular_levels | quantum theory]] is the "prohibition of cloning," which states that an unknown [[quantum_theory_at_atomic_and_molecular_levels | state]] cannot be cloned <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:08">[00:13:08]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:16">[00:13:16]</a>. [[molecular_darwinism_and_evolution | Quantum Darwinism]] clarifies that what multiplies is "what is determined by the interaction between the system and the environment" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:32">[00:13:32]</a>.

## Broader Implications

### Unification Theory
Professor Żurek's research brings us closer to understanding what connects classical and [[quantum_theory_at_atomic_and_molecular_levels | quantum physics]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:39">[00:15:39]</a>. While the concept of a "great unification theory" often refers to a mathematical formula uniting all forces (like electromagnetism, weak, strong, and gravity via string theory), Żurek's interest lies in deriving why we only observe a limited set of states from the vast possibilities allowed by [[quantum_mechanics_and_its_implications | quantum mechanics]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:53">[00:15:53]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:42">[00:17:42]</a>. Decoherence and [[molecular_darwinism_and_evolution | Quantum Darwinism]] largely explain this <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:07">[00:18:07]</a>.

### [[quantum_computers_and_simulation_hypothesis | Quantum Computers]]
A significant challenge for building [[quantum_computers_and_simulation_hypothesis | quantum computers]] is decoherence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:52">[00:24:52]</a>. If a [[quantum_computers_and_simulation_hypothesis | quantum computer]] has many "kits" (qubits), it becomes very difficult for the quantum system to survive the environment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:00">[00:25:00]</a>. There's a contradiction: quantum systems need to interact strongly internally for computation but must interact very weakly with the outside to avoid decoherence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:37">[00:25:37]</a>.

A promising alternative to building universal [[quantum_computers_and_simulation_hypothesis | quantum computers]] is using quantum systems to simulate or emulate various strange quantum systems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:36">[00:26:36]</a>. These simulations connect [[quantum_mechanics_and_its_implications | quantum mechanics]] with solid-state physics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:58">[00:26:58]</a>.

### The Kibble-Żurek Mechanism
Professor Żurek is a co-creator of the Kibble-Żurek mechanism <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:42">[00:40:42]</a>. This mechanism describes what happens when the universe cools down after the Big Bang, leading to phase transitions and "symmetry breaking" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:43">[00:41:43]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:03">[00:42:03]</a>. During these transitions, "topological defects" can form, which are very stable and, if abundant, would dominate the universe's evolution differently than observed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:28">[00:43:28]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:44">[00:44:44]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:01">[00:45:01]</a>. The theory of inflation was invented to dissolve these defects <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:11">[00:45:11]</a>. The Kibble-Żurek mechanism allows for the calculation of the density of these defects, which can be experimentally verified in laboratory settings like liquid Helium <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:34">[00:45:34]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:41">[00:45:41]</a>. It provides specific predictions for laboratory experiments concerning phase transitions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:15">[00:46:15]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:33">[00:46:33]</a>.

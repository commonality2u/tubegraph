## Current State of Unification Efforts

While some premises suggest that [[theory_of_quantum_mechanics_and_quantum_gravity | theories of gravity and quantum mechanics]] have already been combined <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:04">[00:00:04]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:22">[00:02:22]</a>, there is currently no full unification <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:42">[00:02:42]</a>. The challenge lies not in knowing *what* to combine, but *how* to connect them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:49">[00:02:49]</a>.

In the near future, there may be unification <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:52">[03:52]</a>. A recent article in Nature by some authors claimed to have made significant progress towards unification <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:55">[03:55]</a>. This process is seen as a path, not a one-time discovery, to achieve full unification <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:08">[04:08]</a>.

The goal is to combine the theory of true electromagnetic, weak, and strong interactions with [[quantum_mechanics_and_gravitational_interactions | gravitational interactions]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:39">[03:39]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:46">[03:46]</a>.

## Potential Impact of Unification

Unifying these theories is expected to change our understanding of the world <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:08">[00:00:08]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:21">[03:21]</a>. It is anticipated to involve a very complicated mathematical formalism <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:10">[00:00:10]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:26">[03:26]</a>. This formalism will likely be accessible to only a narrow group of specialized individuals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:29">[03:29]</a>.

For the general public, it might simply be "nice to hear that there is unification of gravity and other theories" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:33">[03:33]</a>.

### Practical Implications

It is not certain that unification will change daily lives overnight <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:17">[03:17]</a>. The connection between gravity and quantum mechanics is currently thought to be required at extremely small distances, on the order of 10^-35, and under very specific conditions that may be difficult to create for a long time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:00">[03:00]</a>. Therefore, its immediate practical translation into everyday applications is not guaranteed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:48">[03:48]</a>.

However, it will be intellectually very interesting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:15">[03:15]</a>.

## Fundamental Questions

Physicists acknowledge that they do not have answers to fundamental questions such as:
*   The source of gravity, beyond knowing that it is proportional to mass <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:41">[09:41]</a>.
*   Where the laws of physics came from <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:21">[00:00:21]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:55">[09:55]</a>.
*   Why physical constants (e.g., electron charge, Planck constant) have their specific values <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:24">[00:00:24]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:04">[10:04]</a>.

While the anthropological principle suggests that if constants were different, humans would not have come into being, this is considered an "escape from the answer" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:22">[10:22]</a>.
These unanswered questions highlight the ongoing need for scientific research, as science will never end due to the continuous emergence of such questions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:52">[14:52]</a>.

### Virtues: Definition and Development

Virtues are defined as "internal luminosities" developed by confronting discomfort <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="57:19:00">[57:19:00]</a>.
*   **Courage** is a basal virtue, not the absence of fear, but the ability to act despite it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="59:22:00">[59:22:00]</a>. This is developed by continually, and cautiously, exposing oneself to fears <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="59:56:00">[59:56:00]</a>.
*   Aristotle's philosophy highlights that every virtue is a balance between excess and omission <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:56">[01:03:56]</a>. Courage, for instance, is the midpoint between cowardice and boldness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:59">[01:03:59]</a>.
*   **Temperance** is the ability to handle frustration when things don't go one's way <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:44">[01:05:44]</a>. The lack of temperance is identified as a major ill of the 20th century, leading to dramatic reactions to minor disappointments and a culture of "cancellation" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:53">[01:05:53]</a>.
*   **Fortitude** (or resilience) is the strength to persist despite failures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:54">[01:10:54]</a>.

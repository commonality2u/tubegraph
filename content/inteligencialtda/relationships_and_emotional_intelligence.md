### Managing Anxiety
Anxiety is essentially a subjective fear, an interpretation of hypothetical threats by the brain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:09:00">[02:09:00]</a>. Chronic activation of the body's anxiety system (releasing adrenaline) can lead to physical illness, lowered immunity, and hormonal imbalances <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:11:41">[02:11:41]</a>.
A key step in treating anxiety is to understand that it is a brain signal rather than a real threat <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:10:43">[02:10:43]</a>. Intrusive thoughts are a sign of very high anxiety levels, and addressing the root cause is necessary before implementing techniques to manage these thoughts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:14:00">[02:14:00]</a>. It's crucial to accept that we have limited control over life and learn to deal with inevitable challenges <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:12:50">[02:12:50]</a>.

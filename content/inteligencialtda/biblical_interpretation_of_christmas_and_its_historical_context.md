## The True Date of Jesus' Birth

Contrary to popular belief, Jesus was not born on December 25th <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:20">[00:12:20]</a>. Several biblical and historical clues suggest a different time of year:

*   **Shepherds in the Field** The Bible describes shepherds in the field at night guarding their sheep when Jesus was born <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:44">[00:12:44]</a>. December in Bethlehem is freezing, making it unlikely for shepherds to be out at night <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:54">[00:12:54]</a>.
*   **Lambing Season** Lambs are typically born in late spring, around March and early April <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:09">[00:13:09]</a>. Shepherds would be tending to these births, which explains their presence in the field <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:45">[00:14:45]</a>.
*   **Bethlehem's Role** Bethlehem, located 8 km from Jerusalem, was known as the city where lambs were prepared for temple sacrifices, particularly for Passover <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:11">[00:14:11]</a>. Jesus, the "Lamb of God who takes away the sin of the world," was born in this symbolic city <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:29">[00:14:29]</a>.
*   **Hebrew Calendar and Tabernacle** The Hebrew month of Abib (now Nissan), corresponding to March/April, is when lambs are born and a year later, are ready for sacrifice <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:16">[00:15:16]</a>. The Tabernacle, a dwelling place for God, was also inaugurated on the first day of Abib, a figure for Jesus dwelling among humanity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:17">[00:17:17]</a>.
*   **Dating Jesus' Birth**
    *   By tracing John the Baptist's birth through his father Zechariah's priestly rotation (Abijah, the 8th shift) and confirming dates using the Dead Sea Scrolls and the destruction of the Jerusalem Temple, it is concluded that John was likely conceived in late June <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:03">[00:18:03]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:10">[00:19:10]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:20">[00:19:20]</a>.
    *   Mary's visit to Elizabeth when Elizabeth was six months pregnant (Luke 1) places Jesus' conception around December <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:55">[00:18:55]</a>.
    *   Counting nine months from then places Jesus' birth between late March and early April <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:31">[00:20:31]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:11:00">[02:11:00]</a>.
    *   Historically, Jesus is believed to have been born around 6 BC due to Herod the Great's death in 4 BC and the Magi's two-year search <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:03">[00:29:03]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:15">[00:27:15]</a>.
    *   The Jewish calendar's religious New Year was changed by God to the first day of Abib/Nissan during the Exodus, signifying a new beginning <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:22:04">[02:22:04]</a>.

## The Role and Symbolism of the Magi

The [[the_role_and_symbolism_of_the_magi_in_the_christmas_narrative|Magi]] were not kings, but Zoroastrian astrologers from the East, likely Babylon <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:26">[00:28:26]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:52">[00:30:52]</a>. Their knowledge came from sources like Daniel's influence in Babylon and Balaam's prophecy about a star <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:19">[00:30:19]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:03">[00:41:03]</a>.

*   **Star of Bethlehem**: A conjunction of Jupiter and Saturn in 6 BC likely caused the bright light they followed, as Jupiter was considered the "King's Planet" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:47">[00:27:47]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:08">[00:28:08]</a>.
*   **Gifts**: Their gifts were symbolic of Jesus' identity and future:
    *   **Gold**: Represented royalty, indicating Jesus as King <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:42">[00:43:42]</a>.
    *   **Frankincense**: Symbolized intercession, pointing to Jesus' priestly role <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:49">[00:43:49]</a>.
    *   **Myrrh**: A costly spice from a thorn, deeply bitter but fragrant, used for embalming <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:55">[00:43:55]</a>. It symbolized Jesus' death and sacrifice <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:24">[00:45:24]</a>. This gift also likely provided Joseph and Mary with funds for their two-year stay in Egypt, fleeing Herod's decree <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:59">[00:45:59]</a>.
*   **Herod's Decree**: Herod, alerted by the Magi, ordered the killing of all male children up to two years old in Bethlehem <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:33">[00:29:33]</a>. Joseph, warned by an angel, fled with Mary and Jesus to Egypt <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:41">[00:29:41]</a>. This was in fulfillment of the prophecy of Micah 5:2 regarding Bethlehem <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:25">[00:33:25]</a>.

## Pagan Origins of December 25th

The December 25th date for Christmas was chosen during the time of Emperor Constantine, who converted to Christianity around 312-315 AD <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:00">[01:03:00]</a>.

*   Constantine, who previously worshipped the sun god, integrated pagan practices into Christianity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:10">[01:04:10]</a>.
*   December 25th was the birthday of the sun god, a date tied to the winter solstice, which was the longest night of the year, after which the days would begin to lengthen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:12">[01:05:12]</a>.
*   Many Christmas traditions, like Christmas trees and gift-giving, are linked to ancient pagan fertility worship and solstice celebrations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:45">[01:08:45]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:54">[01:08:54]</a>.
*   Similarly, the Easter bunny and Easter eggs are pagan symbols related to fertility, demonstrating how Satan has created parallel celebrations to overshadow the true Christian meanings of Passover and Christ's resurrection <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:34">[01:07:34]</a>.

Despite its pagan origins, many Christians believe it is important to celebrate Christmas to honor Jesus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:28">[01:09:28]</a>.

*   **Reclaiming the Date**: While the date is not biblical, the collective worldwide remembrance of Jesus' birth on this day is seen as powerful, as it ensures His name is glorified <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:01">[01:14:01]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:33">[01:15:33]</a>.
*   **Focus on Jesus**: The emphasis should be on Jesus, not Santa Claus or commercialism <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:04">[01:14:04]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:04:23">[02:04:23]</a>. It's a time for family communion, worship, and prayer, recognizing Christ's arrival on Earth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:18">[01:14:18]</a>.
*   **Evangelism and Love**: In some countries where Christmas is prohibited, celebrating it can be a dangerous but powerful act of faith <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:01">[01:13:01]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:24:26">[01:24:26]</a>. It provides an opportunity to share the Gospel and demonstrate love to others, especially those who may feel lonely during the holiday season <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:27">[00:08:27]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:34">[01:09:34]</a>.
*   **Avoiding Legalism**: Christians are encouraged to avoid rigid rules and judgment, focusing instead on the love and grace of God <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:30:00">[01:30:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:07:44">[02:07:44]</a>. ## Jesus' Earthly Life and Ministry

Jesus, though the Son of God, experienced human struggles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:47:11">[02:47:11]</a>. He was not poor in the sense of lacking, but rather chose a humble path to be accessible to all <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:50">[00:58:50]</a>.

*   **Carpenter (Tecton)**: Joseph and Jesus were "tectons" (Greek), meaning builders or masons, working with stone rather than wood <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:56">[00:57:56]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:02">[01:02:02]</a>.
*   **Financial Provision**: The gifts from the Magi ensured their financial stability during their time in Egypt <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:41">[00:58:41]</a>.
*   **Identification with the Poor**: Jesus chose to be born in a stable so that everyone, rich or poor, could approach him <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:10">[00:59:10]</a>. He identified with the poor, demonstrating that social condition does not matter for worshipping God <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:50">[00:59:50]</a>.
*   **Holiness and Love**: Jesus' life emphasized God's love and grace over strict rules. He was tough on religious legalists but showed compassion to sinners and outcasts, challenging societal norms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:39:00">[02:39:00]</a>. He exemplified true love, which casts out fear and brings healing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:17:19">[02:17:19]</a>.

## Modern Interpretations and Debates

Contemporary discussions around Christmas often touch on broader issues within Christianity:

*   **Astrology**: While God forbids delving into the future through astrology, the Magi's use of astronomy (then combined with astrology) to find Jesus shows God can use various means to reveal Himself to sincere seekers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:19">[00:37:19]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:24">[00:54:24]</a>.
*   **Fasting**: A spiritual discipline found throughout the Bible (Moses, Daniel, Jesus) that helps consecrate oneself to God, sharpening the mind and spirit for divine revelation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:41:42">[01:41:42]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:44:44">[01:44:44]</a>. It is a way to deny the flesh and strengthen the spirit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:48:51">[01:48:51]</a>.
*   **Racism in the Church**: The debate surrounding the song "Whiter Than Snow" and its perceived racial bias highlights the need for the church to address issues like racism seriously <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:12:36">[03:12:36]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:13:48">[03:13:48]</a>. The phrase "whiter than snow" is a biblical metaphor for cleansing from sin, not a racial statement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:12:12">[03:12:12]</a>.
*   **Marriage and Leadership**: The Bible emphasizes the sanctity of marriage as a serious covenant <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:20:35">[03:20:35]</a>. Leaders in the church are expected to be exemplary in their lives, especially regarding marital fidelity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:20:47">[03:20:47]</a>.
*   **Holiness**: Holiness is a fundamental aspect of the Christian life, a protocol for living in God's kingdom that protects believers from evil <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:04:14">[03:04:14]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:07:02">[03:07:02]</a>.

## Science and the Bible

Science operates on experiments, facts, hypotheses, and verifiable laws, which limits its ability to ascertain events narrated in Genesis, such as the existence of God or miracles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:00">[00:07:00]</a>. These aspects are considered beyond the reach of scientific proof or disproof <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:23">[00:07:23]</a>. The Genesis account asks to be believed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:35">[00:07:35]</a>.

### Evolutionary Concepts

Science can assist in understanding certain processes like macroevolution and microevolution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:40">[00:07:40]</a>.

*   **Microevolution**  <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:50">[00:07:50]</a>: This refers to the adaptation of individuals within the same species to a given environment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:57">[00:07:57]</a>. Christians generally have no difficulty accepting this concept <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:04">[00:08:04]</a>. For example, God is believed to have created a single dog breed with the potential for variety and adaptation, leading to all existing dog races <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:06">[00:08:06]</a>. Similarly, humanity originated from a single couple, Adam and Eve, who possessed the potential to diversify into the multiplicity of different races seen today <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:16">[00:08:16]</a>. This concept of variations within the same species is not problematic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:27">[00:08:27]</a>.

*   **Macroevolution**  <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:50">[00:07:50]</a>: This concept posits that one species evolves into entirely different species <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:32">[00:08:32]</a>. For instance, the idea that humans evolved from monkeys is often misunderstood; [[darwins_theory_of_evolution_and_its_criticisms | Darwin's Theory of Evolution]] suggests humans and monkeys share a common ancestor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:40">[00:08:40]</a>. The theory of macroevolution suggests the human race developed over millions of years through different hominid and pre-hominid types <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:16">[00:09:16]</a>.

    *   **Criticisms of Macroevolution**  <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:30">[00:09:30]</a>:
        *   Lack of fossil evidence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:31">[00:09:31]</a>.
        *   Philosophical difficulty in proving it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:36">[00:09:36]</a>.
        *   Contradiction with the biblical account in Genesis 1-3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:45">[00:09:45]</a>. Denying these first three chapters of Genesis would undermine fundamental biblical concepts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:59">[00:09:59]</a>.

### Theological Implications of Genesis

The first three chapters of Genesis are considered fundamental to the Bible's overall message <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:01">[00:10:01]</a>:

*   **Dignity of the Human Being**: The concept of human dignity is rooted in the belief that humans were created in the image and likeness of God <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:07">[00:10:07]</a>. This belief provides the basis for compassion, mercy, and the fight for human rights <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:53">[00:10:53]</a>. Without the creation account, the epistemological and logical basis for these values is removed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:07">[00:11:07]</a>.
*   **The Fall of Man**: Genesis chapter 3 explains human flaws, the origin of sin, and why humanity is inherently marked by inconsistency and failure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:35">[00:11:35]</a>. The disconnection from God, the "Source of All Good," is the fundamental problem of the human race <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:56">[00:12:56]</a>.
*   **The Need for Reconnection**: The universal human yearning for a higher power and meaning stems from a perceived gap within us <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:29">[00:13:29]</a>. This longing is seen as a desire for the time in Paradise before sin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:35">[00:13:35]</a>. No one is born an atheist; the awareness of something greater than oneself is innate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:08">[00:14:08]</a>. Religions globally aim to provide a system to reconnect individuals with God, thereby finding meaning in life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:10">[00:13:10]</a>.
*   **Paradise**: Initially, Paradise is described as a garden where God placed Adam and Eve and communed with them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:55">[00:15:55]</a>. They were created without sin but had the possibility of disobeying God <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:25">[00:16:25]</a>. Their decision to disobey led to their expulsion from Paradise <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:12">[00:16:12]</a>.

## God's Sovereignty and Human Free Will

The relationship between God's absolute sovereignty and human free will is considered an antinomy in theology <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:16:53">[02:16:53]</a>. It's a truth that theologians cannot fully explain logically, as human understanding is limited when dealing with a being as great as God <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:20:00">[02:20:00]</a>.

The Bible presents both concepts as parallel truths that only meet on the horizon of understanding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:23:01">[02:23:01]</a>. For example, the death of Christ was part of God's predetermined plan, yet those who condemned him were held responsible for their actions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:37:35">[02:37:35]</a>. God's will is involved in everything that happens, yet humans are called to repent and make choices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:48:00">[02:48:00]</a>.

God, being holy, just, good, and true, is not the author of evil, which is attributed to angels or humans <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:28:20">[02:28:20]</a>. God's plan is believed to have foreseen the fall of humanity, but it also includes a plan for redemption, leading to a "New Paradise" where sin will no longer be possible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:09">[01:19:09]</a>. God desires a race of beings who voluntarily choose to stay with Him <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:00:22">[02:00:22]</a>.

## Key Concepts in Artificial Intelligence

### Machine Learning
[[Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning | Machine learning]] is a core component of AI, enabling systems to learn without explicit programming <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:39">[01:03:39]</a>. One prominent technique is "reinforcement learning," where the machine learns through observation and feedback <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:56">[01:03:56]</a>. A common example is how social media algorithms, like TikTok, learn user preferences by observing the time spent on content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:08">[01:04:08]</a>. This means machines can learn "by doing" and reinforce desired behaviors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:09">[01:06:09]</a>. This natural learning process is compared to human behavior, such as a child learning what actions elicit a positive response <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:10">[01:06:10]</a>.

### General Artificial Intelligence (AGI)
The development of AI like "Q*" is seen as a significant step towards what is known as [[Artificial intelligence and its impact on society | General Artificial Intelligence]] (AGI) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:34">[01:08:34]</a>. AGI refers to AI that can understand, learn, and apply intelligence across a wide range of tasks, similar to human cognitive abilities. There are concerns that AGI could control human behavior, transforming individuals into "slaves" by understanding and manipulating desires <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:41">[01:08:41]</a>.

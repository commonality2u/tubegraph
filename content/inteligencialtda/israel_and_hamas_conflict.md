## Reasons for Israeli Intelligence Failure
The unprecedented nature of the attack raised serious questions about Israel's intelligence and defense systems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:42:00">[10:42:00]</a>. Several factors contributed to this lapse:
*   **Internal Division:** Israeli society has been deeply divided by polarized politics and extreme factions, notably over proposed judicial reforms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:50:00">[11:50:00]</a>. This internal focus may have diverted attention from external threats <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:48:00">[12:48:00]</a>.
*   **Shift in Focus:** The Israeli government, influenced by ultra-right and ultra-religious nationalists, had shifted its emphasis to operations and confrontations in the West Bank (Sis Jordan), focusing security forces there <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:51:00">[14:51:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:56:00">[20:56:00]</a>. This led to an underestimation of the threat from Gaza, which was believed to be "safe" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:44:00">[15:44:00]</a>.
*   **Hamas's Strategic Surprise:** Hamas leveraged the element of surprise by choosing the most "obvious" and direct attack route—over land through the border fence—which Israel deemed inconceivable due to its advanced surveillance and defenses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:16:00">[29:16:00]</a>. Previous conflicts primarily involved tunnels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:07:00">[28:07:00]</a>.

## Why Study Criminals?

Studying criminals is important because these individuals are part of society, not isolated entities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:45">[01:17:45]</a>. It's essential to understand the "paths they traveled" from childhood to the moment they committed a crime, as this can reveal underlying disorders or issues <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:39">[01:19:39]</a>. By [[analyzing_real_crimes | analyzing real crimes]] and the minds behind them, one can potentially identify behavioral patterns or factors that contribute to criminal acts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:55:04">[01:55:04]</a>.

Suzane von Richthofen gained notoriety for her involvement in the murder of her parents <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:31">[00:08:31]</a>. Her case highlights several key points:

*   **Manipulative and Seductive Nature**: Suzane is described as highly manipulative and seductive, traits that psychologists have noted in her evaluations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:22">[01:29:22]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:05:29">[02:05:29]</a>. *   **Lack of Remorse**: Unlike her accomplice, Daniel Cravinhos, Suzane consistently shows a lack of genuine remorse for her actions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:01:29">[02:01:29]</a>. Her expressions of regret focus on the negative impact on her own life and career, rather than on the lives of her victims <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:44:50">[01:44:50]</a>.
*   **Coldness**: Suzane exhibited extreme coldness and a lack of emotional distress following the crime. She held a birthday barbecue after burying her parents and behaved as if she were a "tourism guide" when investigators arrived <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:02:46">[02:02:46]</a>.
*   **Family Background**: Her upbringing was characterized by emotional distance and coldness from her parents, which some speculate contributed to her capacity for violence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:22:01">[02:22:01]</a>.
*   **Religious Shifts**: While in prison, Suzane experimented with different religions—Lutheran, Catholic, Spiritism, Candomblé, and Evangelical—often seemingly for convenience or protection within the prison system <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:36:18">[01:36:18]</a>.

## Psychological Tools and Findings

### Rorschach Test

The Rorschach inkblot test is a projective psychological test used to reveal a person's underlying feelings and hidden aspects of their personality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:47:10">[01:47:10]</a>.

*   **Application**: The test involves presenting ten inkblots to a subject, who then describes what they see <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:46:50">[01:46:50]</a>. Professional psychologists interpret the responses to diagnose personality disorders or other psychological traits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:55:25">[01:55:25]</a>.
*   **Suzane's Results**: Suzane's Rorschach tests, and other psychological evaluations, were critical in determining her eligibility for parole. Initially, she failed by demonstrating self-pity rather than genuine remorse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:44:50">[01:44:50]</a>. Subsequent tests showed her adapting her answers, but despite her efforts to manipulate the outcome, psychologists concluded she was manipulative, narcissistic, and had "camouflaged aggression" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:58:00">[01:58:00]</a>.
*   **Psychopathy**: Despite public perception, official psychological reports on Suzane did not classify her as a psychopath, though some experts disagree, suggesting her high degree of manipulation allowed her to deceive evaluators <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:03:50">[02:03:50]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:05:20">[02:05:20]</a>.

## Dynamics of Abusive Relationships and Crime

Several [[case_studies_of_complex_crime_investigations | case studies]] demonstrate how abusive relationships can escalate to violence.

*   **Psychological Abuse**: The concept of "humiliation offense" is introduced, referring to deep-seated insults that leave permanent scars, even if the person superficially forgives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:46">[00:45:46]</a>. Marcos's constant belittling of Elize, reminding her of her past and his "rescue" of her, falls into this category <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:21">[00:46:21]</a>.
*   **Difficulty of Separation**: Women in abusive relationships often struggle to leave due to extremely low self-esteem, financial dependence, and emotional manipulation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:26:24">[02:26:24]</a>. The question "why don't you separate?" is often asked from an external, privileged perspective, ignoring the complex realities and threats faced by victims <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:26:50">[02:26:50]</a>.
*   **Escalation**: Abuse is an escalation, not a sudden event. It can start subtly and grow over time, making it harder for victims to recognize and escape <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:59:00">[02:59:00]</a>.

### The Ark of the Covenant
The Ark of the Covenant is believed by some to be located in Ethiopia, guarded by men from father to son <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:22:19">[03:22:19]</a>. Another theory, supported by Adventist archaeologist Ron Wyatt, suggests the Ark was found in Jerusalem near the place of the skull (Golgotha) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:29:57">[03:29:57]</a>. According to this theory, the blood of Jesus, upon his crucifixion, fell onto the mercy seat of the Ark, signifying atonement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:49:50">[03:49:50]</a>. The Ark contains the tablets of the law, Aaron's rod that budded, and a pot of manna <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:11:13">[04:11:13]</a>. These items represent humanity's sin, rebellion against God's chosen authority, and calling God's provision "cursed" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:49:51">[04:49:51]</a>. The sprinkling of blood on the mercy seat covered these symbols of sin, allowing God to see the blood rather than man's failures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:59:51">[04:59:51]</a>.

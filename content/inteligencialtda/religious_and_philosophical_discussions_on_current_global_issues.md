## Biblical Interpretations and Apostasy

The concept of "apostasy" refers to a "divorce from the truth" or a break with Jesus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:06:21">[02:06:21]</a>. It is characterized by churches tearing up the Bible, claiming it needs rereading, or that its teachings are no longer valid <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:07:07">[02:07:07]</a>. This contrasts with a humble attitude of acknowledging the Bible as truth even when it's difficult to understand <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:27:07">[02:27:07]</a>. Some high-profile religious figures are reported to be promoting this reinterpretation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:28:34">[02:28:34]</a>.

The Bible's consistency and historical accuracy are supported by various factors:
*   **Authorship**: Written over 1,500 years by more than 40 different men from diverse cultures and continents <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:32">[01:29:32]</a>.
*   **Canonical Recognition**: Books were recognized as canonical (inspired) after extensive investigation and debate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:30:13">[01:30:13]</a>.
*   **Dead Sea Scrolls**: The discovery of these manuscripts in 1947 provided older biblical texts (e.g., Isaiah from 200 BC), proving that prophetic chapters existed centuries before the events they described, thus affirming their divine inspiration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:38:41">[01:38:41]</a>.
*   **Copying Techniques**: Ancient copyists (like the Essenes) used meticulous techniques, ensuring that copies made centuries apart are "practically identical," validating the reliability of modern biblical texts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:42:25">[01:42:25]</a>.

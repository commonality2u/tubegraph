## The Nature of Truth
The Catholic Church asserts that truth is not a theory but a person, Jesus Christ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:39:09">[01:39:09]</a>. This perspective holds that denying Christ is equivalent to denying the truth itself <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:39:16">[01:39:16]</a>. Therefore, the goal is not to convince others of a particular doctrine, but to enable them to know Jesus, through whom they can discover the truth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:17:36">[02:17:36]</a>.

In this view, the world struggles because it has lost its understanding of truth, leading to disorientation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:41:05">[01:41:05]</a>. This perspective suggests that while individuals may have "their truth," there is only one ultimate truth, and denying it leads to living a lie <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:44:48">[01:44:48]</a>.

## Specific Moral Teachings: Homosexuality
The Catholic Church's stance on homosexuality, as outlined in the Catechism, is presented as a reflection of biblical truth rather than personal opinion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:52:31">[01:52:31]</a>.
The Catechism describes homosexual acts as "intrinsically disorderly" and contrary to natural law, as they do not lead to the "gift of life" and lack "true affective and sexual complementarity" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:56:33">[01:56:33]</a>. This teaching is rooted in Sacred Scripture, citing Romans 1:24-27, which describes the abandonment of natural relations for unnatural ones as "shameful passions" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:53:30">[01:53:30]</a>.

However, the Church distinguishes between the act and the person <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:59:32">[01:59:32]</a>. Individuals with homosexual tendencies "must be accepted with respect, compassion, and delicacy," and "every sign of unfair discrimination" should be avoided <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:57:53">[01:57:53]</a>. These individuals are called to live chastity through self-control and prayer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:58:52">[01:58:52]</a>. The Church's teaching remains firm, as it cannot change what is seen as divine truth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:56:56">[01:56:56]</a>.

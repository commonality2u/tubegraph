## The True Date of Jesus' Birth

Despite common [[christmas_traditions_and_cultural_practices | Christmas traditions]], Jesus was not born on December 25th <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:23">[00:12:23]</a>. Several biblical and historical clues indicate a different timeframe:
*   **Shepherds in the field** The Bible describes shepherds tending flocks in the field at night when Jesus was born <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:44">[00:12:44]</a>. December is freezing cold in that region, making it unlikely for shepherds to be out at night <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:54">[00:12:54]</a>.
*   **Lambs' Birth Season** Lambs are typically born in late spring, between March and early April (the Hebrew month of Abib, later Nissan) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:09">[00:13:09]</a>. Shepherds would be in the fields at night specifically for these births <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:43">[00:14:43]</a>. Bethlehem, located 8 km from Jerusalem, was a city where lambs were raised for temple sacrifices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:11">[00:14:11]</a>. This aligns with Jesus being the "Lamb of God who takes away the sin of the world" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:29">[00:14:29]</a>.
*   **Tabernacle Inauguration** The word "dwelt" in John 1:14 ("The Word dwelt among us") is "tabernacled" in Greek <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:50">[00:16:50]</a>, alluding to God's dwelling place. The Tabernacle was inaugurated on the first day of Abib/Nissan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:17">[00:17:17]</a>. This spiritual connection suggests Jesus' birth coincided with this period.
*   **John the Baptist's Birth** Zacharias, John the Baptist's father, was a priest serving in the temple during the shift of Abijah (the 8th shift) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:03">[00:18:03]</a>. Six months after Elizabeth (John's mother) became pregnant, Mary conceived Jesus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:55">[00:18:55]</a>. Calculations using the Dead Sea Scrolls and the destruction of the Jerusalem Temple in 70 AD confirm John's conception around June, leading to Jesus' conception in December and birth in March/April <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:20">[00:19:20]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:54">[00:19:54]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:35">[00:20:35]</a>.
*   **Calendar Change** God mandated a calendar change in Exodus 12, declaring the first day of Abib/Nissan as the new year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:04">[00:22:04]</a>. This mirrors Jesus' birth, which changed the world's calendar (BC/AD) and initiated a new life for humanity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:12">[00:23:12]</a>.
*   **Jesus' Death** Jesus was also crucified during the Passover period (March/April), fulfilling the role of the sacrificial Lamb <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:15">[00:24:15]</a>. He entered Jerusalem on Palm Sunday (the 10th day of Nissan) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:06">[00:25:06]</a>, just as a sacrificial lamb would be brought into a home for inspection before Passover <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:29">[00:24:29]</a>.

### The Wise Men (Magi) and the Star of Bethlehem

The "wise men" mentioned in the Bible were likely Zoroastrian astrologers, not kings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:25">[00:28:25]</a>. They sought knowledge through sciences like astronomy and astrology, which were then intertwined <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:45">[00:38:45]</a>. A conjunction of Jupiter (the King's Planet) and Saturn in 6 BC likely created the "star" they followed, aligning with prophecies about a great king from Judea <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:48">[00:27:48]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:08">[00:28:08]</a>. Their knowledge may have been influenced by Daniel's presence in Babylon, where he was chief of the "magicians" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:19">[00:30:19]</a>.

The gifts they brought — gold, frankincense, and myrrh — were highly symbolic and valuable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:32">[00:43:32]</a>:
*   **Gold**: Symbolized Jesus' royalty as King <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:45">[00:43:45]</a>.
*   **Frankincense (Incense)**: Represented Jesus' role as intercessor before God <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:49">[00:43:49]</a>.
*   **Myrrh**: A costly spice with a fragrant but deeply bitter smell, foreshadowing Jesus' death and embalming <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:56">[00:43:56]</a>.

This extraordinary wealth allowed Joseph and Mary to live in Egypt for two years while fleeing Herod <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:59">[00:45:59]</a>.

## Pagan Influences and Modern Celebrations

The choice of December 25th for [[christmas_traditions_and_cultural_practices | Christmas]] stems from [[the_influence_of_historical_rituals_and_symbols_in_modern_times | historical rituals and symbols]]. When Emperor Constantine converted between 312 and 315 AD, he institutionalized Christianity within the Roman Empire <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:57">[01:02:57]</a>. However, he brought pagan practices into the Christian religion, including the celebration of the sun god's birthday, which was on December 25th <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:47">[01:04:47]</a>. This date marked the longest night of the year, after which days began to lengthen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:21">[01:05:21]</a>.

Similarly, other holidays, like Easter, have pagan parallels. The idea of the Easter Bunny and exchanging eggs originates from ancient Babylonian traditions related to fertility and the resurrection of Tammuz, the son of the "mother of false religion" (Semiramis) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:41">[01:06:41]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:20">[01:07:20]</a>. This shows how Satan creates parallels to distract from the true biblical meaning, which in Easter's case, is the liberation from Egypt and the sacrifice of the Lamb <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:34">[01:07:34]</a>.

The commercialization of Christmas, with emphasis on Santa Claus and gifts, has also overshadowed the spiritual meaning <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:42">[01:11:42]</a>. Santa Claus, heavily popularized by Coca-Cola, effectively shifted the focus away from Christ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:47">[01:11:47]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:04">[01:12:04]</a>.

Despite these pagan origins, many believe it's important to celebrate [[christmas_traditions_and_cultural_practices | Christmas]] as an opportunity to glorify Jesus. The date, though not historically accurate for his birth, has become a global moment for remembering him <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:01">[01:14:01]</a>. Forcing people to stop celebrating can be a "blockage" to evangelism, as the core intention should be to remember Christ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:40">[01:13:40]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:01">[01:14:01]</a>. The collective remembrance of Jesus' name, even on a historically inaccurate date, carries significant power <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:08">[01:15:08]</a>.

God can use various cultural elements to reveal his truth, a concept sometimes called the "Melchizedek factor" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:50">[01:15:50]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:07">[01:18:07]</a>.

From: [[inteligencialtda]] <br/> 

"Inteligência Limitada" (Limited Intelligence), a podcast founded by Rogério Vilela, recently celebrated its 1000th official program. Launched less than three years ago in November, the podcast has achieved significant milestones and garnered a substantial following <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:39">[00:00:39]</a>.

## Key Milestones

*   **1000th Episode** The podcast reached its 1000th official program mark, with additional special episodes not included in this count <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:44">[00:00:44]</a>.
*   **Duration** The program is approaching its third anniversary in November <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:39">[00:00:39]</a>.
*   **Viewership Records** One of the show's episodes, featuring Rodrigo Silva, holds the record for the most-viewed podcast episode on the channel, with 10 million views, second only to an interview with former Brazilian President Bolsonaro which garnered 18 million views <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:32">[00:01:32]</a>.
*   **Longest Episodes** The podcast is known for its extensive conversations, with notable long episodes including one with Sacani (11 hours and 40 minutes) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:16">[01:16:16]</a> and another with Pirula and Iago Martins (9 hours) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:16">[01:16:16]</a>.

## Impact and Contributions

Rodrigo Silva, a frequent guest, highlighted three major contributions of the podcast:
1.  **Facilitating Dialogue Among Opposing Views** The podcast successfully brings together people with opposing thoughts, fostering cordial conversations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:50">[01:10:50]</a>. This environment, where guests like Nando and Nicolas, or André and Thiago, could speak for extended periods without interruption, showcases a unique approach to debate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:50">[01:10:50]</a>.
2.  **Popularizing Complex Subjects** The show excels at taking technical subjects and making them accessible to a broad audience, as evidenced by an astronomy episode achieving 8 million views <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:42:19">[01:42:19]</a>. This effectively serves as scientific dissemination <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:42:19">[01:42:19]</a>.
3.  **Host's Interview Style** Rogério Vilela's relaxed and comfortable hosting style allows interviewees to speak freely, regardless of whether they align with his views or each other's <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:48:00">[01:48:00]</a>. This approach makes guests feel genuinely heard and respected <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:11:19">[03:11:19]</a>.

The podcast has also influenced others in [[podcasting_and_content_creation | content creation]], with psychiatrist Ana Beatriz stating that her positive experience on "Inteligência Limitada" inspired her to start her own podcast <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:21:40">[02:21:40]</a>. Guests commend the podcast for providing a platform that expands their reach beyond traditional settings like pulpits or classrooms, enabling them to connect with millions of people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:21:40">[02:21:40]</a>.

### The Power of Communication
The podcast is recognized for its significant role in communication, changing generations by occupying a space that, if left empty, could be filled by negative influences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:21:40">[02:21:40]</a>. It's seen as a force for good, capable of transforming lives by sharing stories and testimonies that resonate with people, helping them through difficult times such as breakups, job losses, or even suicidal thoughts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:47:00">[01:47:00]</a>. Testimonies from former drug addicts and criminals who found transformation through the gospel after watching the program highlight its profound impact <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:47:00">[01:47:00]</a>.

### Personal Connections
Beyond its broad reach, the podcast has also become a source of companionship for many, particularly during and after the pandemic, serving as a daily presence for listeners around the world <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:47:00">[01:47:00]</a>.

## Early Days and Evolution

When Rogério Vilela first invited Rodrigo Silva, Silva was unfamiliar with what a podcast was, having only experience with traditional talk shows like Jô Soares's <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:00">[01:08:00]</a>. He initially approached the interview with the mindset of a planned agenda, typical of television, but quickly adapted to the more free-flowing style of the podcast <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:00">[01:08:00]</a>. This experience marked a significant shift from his previous media engagements and highlighted the unique nature of [[podcasting_and_content_creation | podcasting as a medium]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:00">[01:08:00]</a>.

The initial perception of the podcast was modest; its creators "had no idea" it would achieve such longevity and success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:54:00">[01:54:00]</a>. The growth and sustained audience demonstrate the program's ability to resonate with diverse listeners.

## Celebration and Future Aspirations

The 1000th episode was a celebratory occasion, featuring messages from past guests, a custom cake, and a champagne toast, symbolizing the journey and achievements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:59:00">[02:59:00]</a>. The team, including Fabi, Lene, and Paquito, were acknowledged for their contributions to the podcast's success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:50">[00:40:50]</a>. The program aims for "another 1000, 2000, 3000, 10,000 programs ahead," wishing for continued success and a positive impact on people's lives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:44:00">[03:44:00]</a>.

> "If we can change the life of a person, saving the life of another person, it has already been worth the thousand programs." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:21:58">[03:21:58]</a>

The podcast emphasizes the principle of love as its core essence, aiming to spread love and positive influence through its content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:15:02">[03:15:02]</a>.

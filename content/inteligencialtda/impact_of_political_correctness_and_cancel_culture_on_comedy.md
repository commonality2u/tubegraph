## The Rise of "Cancel Culture" and Double Standards

"Cancel culture" is characterized by public boycotts or denunciations, often fueled by [[the_role_of_social_media_in_spreading_comedy | social media]], leading to professional repercussions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:15">[00:04:15]</a>. A recurring criticism is the perceived hypocrisy and inconsistency in its application <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:29">[00:04:29]</a>.

For instance, the speaker contrasts the public reaction to a controversial joke by Bora Bill with a racism accusation against pop singer Luisa Sonza <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:37">[00:03:37]</a>. Despite different behaviors and contexts—Bora Bill's joke was a misunderstanding of "dark humor" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:46">[00:02:46]</a>, while Sonza's incident involved a racial faux pas in a restaurant <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:41">[00:04:41]</a>—the media's treatment of the two situations diverged significantly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:40">[00:03:40]</a>.

The speaker points to the influence of "gossip networks" on social media, which are often owned by the same entities and can be used to either promote or "destroy a person" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:36">[00:11:36]</a>. These networks may amplify criticisms against some while ignoring similar issues involving their own artists, creating an "agenda" that impacts careers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:56">[00:13:56]</a>. The financial aspect is also noted, where "cancellation turns into money" as activists gain followers and media attention from attacking public figures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:17">[00:15:17]</a>.

## Comedy, Free Speech, and Individual Responsibility

## Evolution of Humor and Authenticity

Society's "rules" for humor change constantly, often without clear communication, leading to confusion and unintended offenses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:50:50">[02:50:50]</a>. The speaker believes that humor, like society, must evolve, discarding outdated jokes that perpetuate harm <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:47">[01:19:47]</a>. However, he also states that increasing restrictions on topics (e.g., race, religion, politics, disability) paradoxically create a "very rich moment" for comedy, as the tension makes forbidden jokes even funnier <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:02:06">[02:02:06]</a>.

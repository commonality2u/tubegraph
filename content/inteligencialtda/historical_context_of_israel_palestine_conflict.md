## Roots of the Conflict

The process began largely due to British imperialism, which employed a "divide and conquer" strategy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:16">[00:06:16]</a>. This led to increased migration to the region:

*   **Zionist Congress (1897)**: Debated whether to establish a Jewish home in Palestine, Patagonia (Argentina), or Uganda <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:30">[00:06:30]</a>. The decision to choose Palestine was a geopolitical one, considering geographic location, natural resources (including oil), and other factors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:41">[00:06:41]</a>.
*   **Historical Palestine Population**: Before this intensified migration, [[historical_significance_of_jerusalem | historical Palestine]] had a coexisting population of Muslims, Christians (10%), and Jews (3%) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:58">[00:06:58]</a>.
*   **Unfair Migration**: The immigration process began with international subsidies, acknowledging historical attacks on the Jewish people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:13">[00:07:13]</a>. However, this migration is seen as unjust <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:15">[00:07:15]</a>.
    *   **Zionism vs. Jewish People**: It is important not to confuse criticisms of Zionism with anti-Semitism, which is a form of oppression and racism that must be condemned <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:29">[00:07:29]</a>.
    *   **"Land Without People" Myth**: From 1897, Zionist ideology shifted from a landless people seeking a home to asserting that a "land without people" was for a "landless people," despite people already living there <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:02">[00:08:02]</a>.
    *   **Economic Domination**: Wealthy European Jewish bourgeoisie, like Baron Rothschild, subsidized land purchases, leading to the expulsion of the native population and Zionist dominance over land, labor, and means of production <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:11">[00:08:11]</a>.

## British Mandate and Intensified Conflict

Tensions severely increased after World War I <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:36">[00:08:36]</a>. The British Empire, weakened by war, made conflicting promises:

*   **Promises of Land**: Britain offered the land to the Meccans to create a large Arab state <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:47">[00:08:47]</a>. Simultaneously, the Balfour Declaration promised a home for the Jewish people, which was seen as denying the existing national state and expelling an indigenous population <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:06">[00:09:06]</a>.
*   **Palestinian Rebellion (1936-1939)**: A rebellion against the British Empire broke out <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:47">[00:09:47]</a>. Zionist militias allied with the British to defeat the Palestinian people during this period <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:01">[00:10:01]</a>.
*   **Post-Holocaust Pressure**: After World War II, the horrors of the Holocaust shocked the world, increasing pressure for the Jewish people to have a safe homeland <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:36">[00:10:36]</a>.
*   **Jewish Uprising**: As Britain's economic power waned, Zionist militias engaged in attacks, including the King David Hotel bombing (92 killed), which were later classified as terrorism <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:32">[00:11:32]</a>. This led Britain to hand the issue over to the UN <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:48">[00:11:48]</a>.

## UN Partition Plan and the Nakba

In alliance with the new hegemonic power, the United States, Israel participated in the UN Partition Plan (Resolution 181) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:55">[00:11:55]</a>.

*   **Territorial Division**: What was once [[historical_significance_of_jerusalem | historical Palestine]], where various peoples and religions coexisted, was to be divided into two states <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:06">[00:12:06]</a>. [[role_of_israel_in_biblical_history | Israel]] received the larger portion, including the main arable lands, water reserves, and Mediterranean coast, while the Palestinian territory became fragmented <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:19">[00:12:19]</a>.
*   **Declaration of Israel**: At the end of the British Mandate, the State of Israel was declared, but not the Palestinian State <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:51">[00:12:51]</a>.
*   **Al-Nakba (The Great Tragedy)**: This led to the expulsion of 750,000 Palestinian Arab families, destruction of 520 villages, and what the Palestinian people refer to as "the great tragedy" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:58">[00:12:58]</a>. This mass expulsion led to significant resentment and constant confrontation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:27">[00:13:27]</a>.

## Arab-Israeli Wars and Continued Occupation

The conflict progressed into several [[the_impact_of_israels_historical_conflicts | Arab-Israeli wars]]:

*   **Suez Crisis (1956)**: Israel invaded the Sinai Peninsula, requiring UN intervention to regain control of the Suez Canal, leading to Israel's retreat <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:45">[00:13:45]</a>.
*   **Six-Day War (1967)**: Israel, by this point a major military power allied with the U.S., militarily occupied the West Bank (Jordan) and Gaza Strip, taking all of [[historical_significance_of_jerusalem | historical Palestine]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:28">[00:14:28]</a>. They also took the Syrian Golan Heights and part of Southern Lebanon, which were never returned <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:25">[00:15:25]</a>. Israel returned the Sinai Peninsula to demonstrate goodwill, but maintained occupation of the West Bank and Gaza Strip <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:37">[00:15:37]</a>.
*   **Yom Kippur War (1973)** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:48">[00:15:48]</a>.

Following the occupation, a series of Palestinian uprisings, known as Intifadas, began:

*   **First Intifada (1987)**: Almost 20 years after the total military occupation, a dignified revolt began <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:28">[00:16:28]</a>. The uprising was met with immense brutality by the Zionist State <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:02">[00:17:02]</a>.
    *   **Palestinian Political Diversity**: Despite common Western perceptions of a monolithic, fundamentalist East, there is immense political diversity among Palestinian organizations, including secular and Marxist groups like the Popular Front for the Liberation of Palestine <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:15">[00:17:15]</a>. Most seek self-determination and recovery of their territory, at least to the 1967 borders established by the UN partition plan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:53">[00:17:53]</a>.
    *   **Emergence of [[israel_and_hamas_conflict | Hamas]]**: Due to dissatisfaction with older leadership and the inability to achieve self-determination, [[israel_and_hamas_conflict | Hamas]] emerged in 1987, based in Gaza with roots in the Muslim Brotherhood of Egypt <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:42">[00:18:42]</a>. [[israel_and_hamas_conflict | Hamas]] engaged in humanitarian work but also military confrontation as a strategy for liberation, a right they claim under international law <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:00">[00:19:00]</a>.

*   **Oslo Accords**: [[israel_and_hamas_conflict | Hamas]] began to dispute political hegemony with Fatah (leader of the PLO) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:50">[00:19:50]</a>. The Oslo Accords committed [[role_of_israel_in_biblical_history | Israel]] to return occupied territories, but with limitations on Palestinian sovereignty, denying them armed forces and full territorial control for at least 7 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:05">[00:20:05]</a>. This generated frustration as it did not guarantee true self-determination <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:17">[00:20:17]</a>.

*   **Second Intifada (2000)**: When the Oslo Accords deadline expired in 2000 and [[role_of_israel_in_biblical_history | Israel]] continued colonizing with Jewish settlements, the Second Intifada erupted <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:40">[00:20:40]</a>. *   **Israeli Expansion**: [[role_of_israel_in_biblical_history | Israel]] advanced further into the West Bank, building a 700 km wall (85% within occupied territories), intensifying settlements, and converting Palestinian lands into military areas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:31">[00:22:31]</a>. They also concreted water sources and established military checkpoints <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:51">[00:22:51]</a>.
*   **Political Shifts**: The death of Yasser Arafat led to a greater dispute over the future of the Palestinian liberation struggle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:18">[00:23:18]</a>. Fatah accepted Oslo Accords, while [[israel_and_hamas_conflict | Hamas]] advocated for a more intense fight for liberation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:31">[00:23:31]</a>.
*   **Gaza Elections (2006)**: [[israel_and_hamas_conflict | Hamas]] won elections in Palestine, becoming hegemonic in Gaza, leading to a separation of governance: the Palestinian National Authority controlled the West Bank, and [[israel_and_hamas_conflict | Hamas]] managed Gaza <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:13">[00:24:13]</a>.
*   **Siege of Gaza**: After Israel's military defeat in Lebanon in 2006, Israel imposed a total siege on Gaza in 2007, turning it into the "world's largest open-air prison" for 17 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:04">[00:25:04]</a>.

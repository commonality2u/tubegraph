## Forensic Investigation and Analysis

The investigation of high-profile crimes in Brazil relies heavily on forensic science and the work of specialized police departments.

### Homicide Department (DHPP)

The DHPP in São Paulo is responsible for homicides of unknown authorship and cases where the crime scene is preserved <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:33:01">[01:33:01]</a>. They work with a multidisciplinary team including delegates, clerks, investigators, papiloscopists (fingerprint experts), criminal experts, and photographers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:10:02">[02:10:02]</a>.

### Crime Scene Preservation and Evidence

*   **Initial Response:** There is urgency in initial care to preserve the crime scene and collect all traces of evidence as completely as possible, as environmental factors like rain can destroy crucial evidence like bloodstains or shell casings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:22:15">[02:22:15]</a>.
*   **Contamination Control:** Strict protocols are essential to prevent cross-contamination, including changing gloves for each material collected <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:56:19">[01:56:19]</a>. Any contamination can lead to questions about the "chain of custody" and potentially nullify evidence in court <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:58:47">[01:58:47]</a>.
*   **Evidence Collection:**
    *   **Luminol:** Used to detect residual blood, even if cleaned <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:52">[00:37:52]</a>.
    *   **PK Cut Test:** A test developed to detect blood, originally in feces, capable of identifying even minute quantities (around four nanograms) and determining if it's human blood <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:36">[00:38:36]</a>.
    *   **DNA Extraction:** From human blood samples, DNA can be extracted to identify individuals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:39">[00:39:39]</a>. Even traces like sweat can provide DNA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:04">[00:41:04]</a>.
    *   **Forensic Lights:** Specialized lights operating at specific frequencies (e.g., ultraviolet) stimulate molecules in certain substances, revealing evidence invisible to the naked eye <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:20">[01:18:20]</a>.
    *   **Hair Analysis:** While hair can provide mitochondrial DNA (linking to the maternal line), it's generally insufficient on its own to incriminate an individual without the "bubu" (follicle) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:46">[00:44:46]</a>.
    *   **Time of Death (TOD):** Determining TOD is crucial as it can include or exclude suspects from the crime scene <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:59:32">[02:59:32]</a>. Factors like ambient temperature, clothing, and surface where the body lies can significantly influence decomposition and rigidity, making precise estimation difficult <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:56:02">[02:56:02]</a>.

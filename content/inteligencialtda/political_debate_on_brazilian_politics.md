*   **Queiroz Case and "Rachadinha":** Brigadier repeatedly brought up the Queiroz case, accusing Bolsonaro of hiding a "criminal" and operating a "scheme of simple embezzlement" through the "rachadinha" (kickback) scheme <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:16">[00:23:16]</a>. *   **Contradictory Actions:** Brigadier argued that Bolsonaro's actions, such as hiding Queiroz, contradicted his rhetoric, like praising prisons <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:16">[00:23:16]</a>. *   **Lula's Release:** Brigadier blamed Bolsonaro for [[lula_and_the_brazilian_political_landscape | Lula's release]] from prison, asserting that Bolsonaro made all the decisions that allowed Lula to return to power <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:16:16">[02:16:16]</a>. ### Null Voting and Political Omission

The debate extensively covered the morality and consequences of null voting:
*   **Kogos's Stance:** Kogos, identifying as an "ancap" (anarcho-capitalist), justified his null vote as the "greatest representation" of dissatisfaction with the state and available political options <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:54:51">[01:54:51]</a>. He argued that true change comes from individual development, not politics, which he views as inherently corrupt <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:10">[01:15:10]</a>.
*   **Brigadier's Criticism:** Brigadier vehemently criticized null voting as an "inconsequence" and an "omission" that directly led to [[lula_and_the_brazilian_political_landscape | Lula's return to power]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:51">[00:33:51]</a>. He argued that in a choice between a "disastrous evil" (Lula) and a "lesser evil" (Bolsonaro), there's a "moral obligation" to choose the latter to prevent greater harm to the population, citing issues like hunger and rising crime <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:07">[01:06:07]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:59">[01:10:59]</a>. He likened it to choosing between a "demon" and someone who "saved Jews" in a concentration camp scenario <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:52:00">[01:52:00]</a>.

### Electoral Fund and Political Ethics

The use of the electoral fund was another point of contention:
*   **Kogos's Justification:** Kogos defended his use of the electoral fund, despite agreeing that it's immoral, as a "trolling" tactic to "channel the system's money against him" and prevent it from being monopolized by the left <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:10">[01:00:10]</a>. He argued it's a "doping" situation where abstaining from the fund means losing the "fight" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:01">[01:00:01]</a>.
*   **Brigadier's Condemnation:** Brigadier labeled Kogos's use of the fund as "highly Machiavellian" and a "contradiction" for someone who claims to be against it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:15">[01:00:15]</a>. He criticized Kogos for taking "money from the suffering people" while claiming to fight for their interests <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:59">[00:59:59]</a>.

The discussion touched on the definition of ideology and the state of democracy in Brazil:

*   **Freedom of Expression vs. Crime:** Brigadier argued that while questioning election fairness is acceptable, claiming proof of fraud constitutes a crime against democracy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:39:34">[01:39:34]</a>.

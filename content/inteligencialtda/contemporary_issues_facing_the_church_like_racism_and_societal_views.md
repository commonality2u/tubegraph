## Christmas: Historical and Spiritual Meanings
Historically, Jesus was not born on December 25th <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:23:55">[02:23:55]</a>. Biblical accounts describe shepherds in the field at night guarding sheep, which is impossible in the freezing cold of December in that region <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:54">[01:29:54]</a>. Lambs, especially those prepared for sacrifice, are typically born in late March to early April (the Hebrew month of Abib or Nissan) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:11">[01:13:11]</a>. Jesus, the Lamb of God who takes away the sin of the world, was born in Bethlehem, the city where these sacrificial lambs were prepared <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:30:15">[01:30:15]</a>. The calendar itself was changed from "before Christ" to "after Christ" at his birth, symbolizing a new life for humanity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:32:27">[02:32:27]</a>.

Christmas serves as a time for personal and communal reflection, encouraging individuals to evaluate their year and strive to be better people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:50:51">[01:50:51]</a>. It's a period for family reunions, setting aside differences, and remembering positive aspects <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:38:52">[07:38:52]</a>. For those without family, Christmas can be especially difficult, making it crucial for others, especially churches, to offer welcome and community <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:29:10">[08:29:10]</a>. The presence of Jesus, as the most important person, ensures that Christmas doesn't have to be sad, even in solitude <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:39:07">[10:39:07]</a>.

### The Magi and Astrology
The Magi, often referred to as "kings," were actually Zoroastrian astrologers from the East, not traditional "wizards" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:26:01">[02:26:01]</a>. In ancient times, astrology was intertwined with astronomy and considered a science, used more for prophetic questions and understanding temperament than for personal fortune-telling <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:45:03">[03:45:03]</a>. God prohibited divination and fortune-telling because humans are not equipped to handle knowledge of the future <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:21:40">[03:21:40]</a>.

Their journey was likely prompted by a conjunction of Jupiter and Saturn around 6 BC, which created a bright light in the sky <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:50:11">[02:50:11]</a>. This astrological event, combined with prophecies accessible to them (possibly through Daniel's influence in Babylon or Balaam's prophecy of a coming star), led them to seek the "King of the Jews" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:00:23">[03:00:23]</a>. The gifts they brought—gold (for royalty), frankincense (for intercession/spiritual), and myrrh (prefiguring death/embalming)—signified Jesus's identity and future <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:32:23">[04:32:23]</a>. These expensive gifts also provided Joseph and Mary with the means to live in Egypt for two years while fleeing Herod <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:59:45">[04:59:45]</a>.

### Jesus's Socio-Economic Status
Contrary to popular belief, Jesus was not "poor" in the sense of being needy or destitute <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:17:16">[08:17:16]</a>. His family, though humble, had sufficient resources, as evidenced by the Magi's gifts. Joseph was a "tecton" (a builder or stone mason, not just a carpenter), indicating a trade that provided well <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:06">[01:02:06]</a>. ### Pagan Influences and Christian Practice
The adoption of December 25th as Christmas originated from Emperor Constantine's conversion, as it aligned with the birthday of the Roman sun god <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:37">[01:03:37]</a>. This marked a historical shift where elements of paganism were incorporated into the Christian religion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:47">[01:04:47]</a>. Similarly, Easter, while having a biblical origin related to the Passover Lamb (Jesus), also has pagan aspects like the Easter bunny and eggs, symbolizing fertility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:05">[01:07:05]</a>. These parallels demonstrate Satan's strategy to divert focus from the true meaning of Christian celebrations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:34">[01:07:34]</a>.

Fasting is described as an ancient spiritual discipline found in both the Old and New Testaments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:40:21">[01:40:21]</a>.

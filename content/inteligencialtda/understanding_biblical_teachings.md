### Church Hierarchy
The Catholic Church has three main hierarchies: Deacon, Priest, and Bishop <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:48:51">[01:48:51]</a>.
*   **Deacon:** Can be permanent or a step before priesthood. Can celebrate baptisms and marriages but not Mass or hear confessions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:52:00">[01:52:00]</a>.
*   **Priest:** Ordained after being a deacon. Lives within a parish and serves its needs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:32:32">[01:32:32]</a>.
*   **Friar:** A religious priest who lives in a community, following a specific rule of life inspired by a Saint (e.g., Carmelites by St. Teresa of Avila) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:37:07">[01:37:07]</a>. Friars take vows of chastity, poverty, and obedience, living communally and sharing resources <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:47:00">[01:47:00]</a>. Not every Friar is a priest, and not every priest is a Friar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:00">[01:02:00]</a>.
*   **Bishop:** The highest order, considered a successor of the Apostles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:05">[01:09:05]</a>. The Pope is the Bishop of Rome <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:57">[01:08:57]</a>. Bishops perform all sacraments, including Mass <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:48:00">[01:48:00]</a>.

### The Salvadoran Fisherman: 438 Days Adrift
In 2014, a Salvadoran fisherman living in Mexico went fishing with a friend in an open fiberglass canoe [00:09:42](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:42">[00:09:42]</a>. His friend died after three to four months from starvation and dehydration [00:11:02](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:02">[00:11:02]</a>. The fisherman, José Salvador Alvarenga, survived an astonishing 14 months (438 days), a record for absolute survival at sea [00:11:17](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:17">[00:11:17]</a>. His primary food source was turtles, which he could catch when they surfaced to breathe [00:12:25](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:25">[00:12:25]</a>. He also ate flying fish that accidentally landed in his boat at night [00:12:53](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:53">[00:12:53]</a>). He drank rainwater and turtle blood [00:17:53](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:53">[00:17:53]</a>). He knew not to drink seawater as it causes further dehydration [02:26:26](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:26:26">[02:26:26]</a>).

Alvarenga drifted from the coast of Mexico to the Marshall Islands, on the other side of the Pacific [00:14:05](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:05">[00:14:05]</a>. When rescued, he was apparently healthy, albeit bearded and naked from sun and salt exposure [00:14:38](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:38">[00:14:38]</a>). Doubts arose about his story, including accusations of cannibalism, leading him to take a lie detector test [00:15:29](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:29">[00:15:29]</a>). He wrote a book titled "438 Days" [00:15:17](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:17">[00:15:17]</a>).

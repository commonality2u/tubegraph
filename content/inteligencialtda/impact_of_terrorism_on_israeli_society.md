## Immediate Societal Response

Prior to the attack, Israeli society experienced significant internal division due to political discussions, protests, and a polarized political landscape, similar to other democratic nations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:50">[00:11:50]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:01">[00:12:01]</a>. However, the Hamas attack immediately unified the nation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:04">[00:13:04]</a>. The opposition joined the government to form a single consensus government, demonstrating a collective resolve to defend the country <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:10">[00:13:10]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:22:03">[02:22:03]</a>. Citizens contributed by distributing free food to soldiers and donating blood <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:24">[00:07:24]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:22:06">[02:22:06]</a>. The attacks prompted the call-up of 300,000 Israeli reservists <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:29">[00:05:29]</a>, including the husband of one Israeli resident <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:36">[00:05:36]</a>.

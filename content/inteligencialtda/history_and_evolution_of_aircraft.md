## Aircraft Fuel and Engine Technology

Kerosene is the primary fuel used in [[aviation_and_aircraft_technology | planes]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:05">[00:17:05]</a>. Unlike gasoline, kerosene is less flammable and volatile, making it safer for [[aviation_and_aircraft_technology | aircraft]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:08">[00:17:08]</a>. Its higher calorific value and suitability for high-pressure combustion within turbines make it more efficient than gasoline for jet engines <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:48">[00:17:48]</a>.

The mechanics of flight involve fuel transforming into heat energy, which powers the engine or propeller to create propulsion and lift, preventing the [[aviation_and_aircraft_technology | plane]] from falling <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:16">[00:18:16]</a>. This concept is likened to water skiing: continuous forward horizontal speed and a specific angle are necessary to stay airborne <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:16">[00:19:16]</a>.

### Notable Incidents and Safety Improvements

*   **Hudson River Landing (US Airways Flight 1549)**: Piloted by Sully Sullenberger, this incident involved a loss of both engines due to a bird strike shortly after takeoff from New York <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:37:33">[02:37:33]</a>. The pilot made a rapid decision to land on the Hudson River, saving all lives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:22:13">[02:22:13]</a>. The ability of a human pilot to make quick, life-saving decisions, considering factors beyond what an algorithm might prioritize (like preserving the aircraft), was highlighted <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:28:28">[02:28:28]</a>. The added human identification time (around 7 seconds) for the problem was crucial in this scenario <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:44:50">[02:44:50]</a>. The plane's auxiliary power system was manually activated, keeping flight computers and controls operational <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:6:06">[02:6:06]</a>.
*   **TAM Flight 3054 (Congonhas Airport, 2007)**: This [[airplane_incidents_and_accident_investigation | accident]] was attributed to a conjunction of multiple factors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:38">[00:45:38]</a>. Contributing elements included an inoperative engine reverser, a lever in the wrong position causing asymmetrical thrust (one engine braking, the other accelerating), and disengaged brakes due to the system believing the plane was taking off <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:27">[00:44:27]</a>. The short, slippery runway at Congonhas also played a role <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:05">[00:46:05]</a>. After this, runways were modified to reduce performance and enforce lighter takeoffs, improving braking times <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:02">[00:47:02]</a>.
*   **Air France Flight 447 (Atlantic Ocean, 2009)**: This [[airplane_incidents_and_accident_investigation | accident]] was perplexing because it involved a modern [[aviation_and_aircraft_technology | aircraft]] with an experienced crew, yet occurred despite all elements for a safe flight being present <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:51">[00:59:51]</a>. It occurred in the intertropical convergence zone, known for severe weather <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:23">[01:01:23]</a>. The crew diverted to the wrong side of the storm, which was visible on radar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:00">[01:20:00]</a>.
*   **Lake Constance Mid-Air Collision (2002)**: This [[airplane_incidents_and_accident_investigation | accident]] was a mid-air collision between a Russian passenger [[aviation_and_aircraft_technology | plane]] and a DHL cargo [[aviation_and_aircraft_technology | plane]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:40:30">[02:40:30]</a>. ## Debunking Myths

*   **Contrails vs. "Chemtrails"**: The white trails left by [[aviation_and_aircraft_technology | planes]] are condensation trails (contrails), formed when hot engine exhaust condenses in the freezing atmosphere <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:54:16">[01:54:16]</a>. The conspiracy theory of "chemtrails" being used to reduce population or cause environmental changes is false <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:54:36">[01:54:36]</a>.
*   **Turbulence**: Turbulence does not cause a [[aviation_and_aircraft_technology | plane]] to crash <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:03:00">[02:03:00]</a>. [[aviation_and_aircraft_technology | Planes]] are designed to withstand multi-level turbulence, and a wing breaking is an extremely rare event <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:57">[01:02:57]</a>. An example of extreme turbulence causing structural failure was in the 1950s with Electra [[aviation_and_aircraft_technology | aircraft]], where harmonic vibration in engines led to wing vibration; this issue was identified and corrected <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:09">[01:03:09]</a>.
*   **Lightning Strikes**: [[aviation_and_aircraft_technology | Planes]] are frequently struck by lightning (almost daily) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:27:30">[02:27:30]</a>. However, [[aviation_and_aircraft_technology | aircraft]] act as a Faraday cage, allowing the electrical current to flow around the fuselage without harming those inside <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:27:47">[02:27:47]</a>.
*   **"Black Box" Material**: The common question about why [[aviation_and_aircraft_technology | planes]] aren't built entirely from the indestructible material of the black box (flight recorder) is often posed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:54:48">[02:54:48]</a>. *   **Bermuda Triangle**: The Bermuda Triangle is a legend <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:48:56">[01:48:56]</a>. Disappearances in this region occurred before modern [[aviation_and_aircraft_technology | navigation]] technologies like GPS and cell phones were available <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:49:02">[01:49:02]</a>.

## Grace vs. Law in Overcoming Challenges

A significant part of his spiritual understanding revolves around the distinction between "law" and "grace." He explains that the Law, given through Moses, served to reveal sin and show humanity's inability to live righteously without God's help <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:04:27">[02:04:27]</a>. Trying to live by the Law leads to frustration because "no one can live that way" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:17:11">[02:17:11]</a>.

Grace, on the other hand, came through Jesus Christ, who offered a complete and perfect sacrifice, forgiving sins once and for all <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:03:57">[02:03:57]</a>. Under grace, believers are no longer condemned and can approach God with confidence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:11:57">[02:11:57]</a>. This means Christians are saved by faith, not by their works or rigid adherence to rules <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:15:15">[02:15:15]</a>.

The Holy Spirit empowers believers to overcome sin naturally, as a new nature is given upon being "born again" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:15:57">[02:15:57]</a>. While individuals may still struggle with temptations and past habits, they are no longer enslaved by sin if they allow Jesus to rule their lives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:17:31">[02:17:31]</a>. The role of the devil is to accuse and blind understanding, pushing people towards self-reliance rather than reliance on God's grace <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:22:53">[02:22:53]</a>.

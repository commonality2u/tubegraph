## Personal Background and Entry into Crime

Frank describes a childhood marked by financial difficulties despite his parents being hard workers; his father was a driver and his mother a housewife <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:00">[00:35:00]</a>. Born in Jabara, South Zone of São Paulo, he grew up in Taiaçu <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:37">[00:35:37]</a>. He attributes his involvement in crime at age 15 to a "lack of shame" and wanting to take a "shortcut" rather than a lack of opportunity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:47">[00:35:47]</a>. He witnessed drug dealers earning money easily while his honest father struggled <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:16">[00:36:16]</a>. This [[Personal anecdotes and experiences]] shaped his early life.

He began committing robberies and was arrested, serving a year in FEBEM (a correctional facility for minors) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:46">[00:36:46]</a>. Despite popular belief, Frank claims FEBEM was "a piece of cake with sugar," offering regular meals and a healthy routine, where he was well-treated and even learned about the Bible and Jesus Christ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:32">[00:37:32]</a>. This period was a significant part of his [[Personal conversion and faith journey]]. However, he believes FEBEM generally doesn't rehabilitate; he re-entered drug trafficking just two days after his release at 18 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:57">[00:40:57]</a>. Frank also experienced a situation where his mother asked a judge to keep him locked up, believing it was for his own good, which he initially resented but later understood <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:08">[00:39:08]</a>. This highlights the [[Personal challenges and family impact]] of his lifestyle.

Frank's role in crime evolved; he became an intermediary for drug transactions between regions like Baixada (013), Campinas (019), and São Paulo, dealing with large volumes of drugs like cocaine and marijuana <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:59">[00:41:59]</a>.

## The Catalyst for Whistleblowing

Frank was a member of the PCC from 2016 to 2023 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:25">[00:12:25]</a>. He desired to leave the PCC to work and spend more time with his children <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:45">[00:15:45]</a>. His departure, however, was not an "exclusion" (a way to leave alive with command approval) but rather a "decree" or death sentence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:47">[00:14:47]</a>. The immediate trigger for this decree was his accusation that the PCC had implemented a "maneuver" to financially exploit members through a mandatory drug purchase scheme called the "Progress sector" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:47:41">[04:47:41]</a>. He publicly stated that the PCC "made a maneuver for members," which led to his exclusion and being "decreed" as a "cheating item" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:19">[00:48:19]</a>. Any criminal in São Paulo could take his life without a "crime court" trial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:51">[00:16:51]</a>.

He initially made his first public video on TikTok ("teco teco") out of despair, believing he would die soon <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:27">[00:29:27]</a>. The video, intended as a final warning, gained immense support and millions of views <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:38">[00:48:38]</a>. This unexpected public reaction motivated him to continue exposing the truth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:37">[00:49:37]</a>.

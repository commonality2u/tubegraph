## Childhood and Early Life
Whindersson recalls his early exposure to the internet through "Community Telecenters" in the early 2000s, where he discovered "Cannibal World" videos, downloading about 15 overnight to watch the next morning <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:00:00">[02:00:00]</a>. He reminisces about the "fever" of "Cannibal World" and specific moments like a stressed person praying, which he loved <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:02:00">[02:02:00]</a>.

During childhood, Whindersson found joy in creative activities, like fixing a broken toy cart and imagining what might have caused its damage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:36:00">[10:36:00]</a>. Watching Harry Potter films sparked his curiosity about how actors could speak different languages and appear on television, fueling a desire to understand the world beyond his immediate surroundings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:46:00">[10:46:00]</a>. His sister's heart surgery meant he had to stay with his grandmother, leading him to read these books <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:04:00">[12:04:00]</a>. He also read "Turma da Mônica" comics, even memorizing them backward <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:42:00">[12:42:00]</a>.

A significant part of his childhood involved listening to the radio at his grandmother's house, which played music from artists like Felipe Dylon, Michael Jackson, and Pepe Moreno <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:54:00">[12:54:00]</a>. Without access to television or news, he created imaginative stories, like believing Felipe Dylon and Michael Jackson were competing for musical dominance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:08:00">[14:08:00]</a>. This lack of information fostered his creativity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:43:00">[14:43:00]</a>.

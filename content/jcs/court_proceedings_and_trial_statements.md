## Initial Discovery and First Interrogation

On July 15, 2008, Orlando police received a 911 call from Cindy Anthony, Kaylee's grandmother <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:45">[00:01:45]</a>. Cindy reported that her granddaughter had been missing for a month and that her daughter, Casey, finally admitted the baby was "in the store" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:02">[00:02:02]</a>. Cindy also stated that Casey's car "smells like there's been a dead body in the damn car" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:27">[00:02:27]</a>.

Casey Anthony had left the family home a month prior, claiming she had a work assignment in Tampa and that Kaylee was with a nanny named Zaneda Fernandez Gonzalez, or "Zanny" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:29">[00:03:29]</a>. On July 15, the family car was found at an impound lot, and Kaylee's grandfather, George Anthony, discovered a strong odor, comparable to human decomposition, emanating from the trunk <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:00">[00:04:00]</a>. When confronted, Casey stated Kaylee had been kidnapped by the supposed babysitter <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:33">[00:04:33]</a>.

During the 911 call, Casey Anthony exhibited "no urgency nor concern in her voice" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:32">[00:05:32]</a>. Her responses were indifferent compared to her distraught mother <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:47">[00:05:47]</a>. Police immediately considered Casey the prime suspect due to the circumstances <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:55">[00:05:55]</a>. She was interviewed as a witness, which detectives used as a first step in locking her into as many lies as possible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:01">[00:06:01]</a>.

## The Trial: May 24, 2011

The [[Court Trial and Sentencing | trial]] commenced on May 24, 2011 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:47:00">[05:47:00]</a>.

### Prosecution's Opening Statement

The prosecution's argument was that Casey suffocated Kaylee with duct tape, kept her body in the trunk, and then disposed of it in the swamp <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:50:00">[05:50:00]</a>. The motive focused on Casey wanting to live "the good life" without the responsibility of a child <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:57:00">[05:57:00]</a>.

The prosecution emphasized:
*   Kaylee lived a quiet life on Hope Spring Drive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:30:00">[05:30:00]</a>.
*   Casey's loving mother facade was an "illusion" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:52:00">[05:52:00]</a>.
*   George Anthony last saw Kaylee on June 16, 2008, at 12:50 PM, and "no one but Casey Anthony ever saw Kaylee Anthony alive again" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:31:00">[05:31:00]</a>.
*   The duct tape on Kaylee's face showed "specific intent to end that child's life" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:43:00">[05:43:00]</a>.
*   Casey Anthony had access to all the evidence and "no one else lied to their friends, to their family, to investigators" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:48:00">[05:48:00]</a>.
*   Kaylee's death allowed Casey to "live the good life" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:55:00">[05:55:00]</a>.

### Defense's Opening Statement

The defense, led by Jose Baez, aimed to create doubt <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:51:00">[05:51:00]</a>. He addressed the long delay in reporting Kaylee missing, stating, "she never was missing, Kaylee Anthony died on June 16, 2008, when she drowned in her family's swimming pool" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:56:00">[05:56:00]</a>.

Baez's bombshell claim was:
*   The family hid "ugly things, secret things" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:56:00">[05:56:00]</a>.
*   After Kaylee's death, Casey "did what she's been doing all her life... hiding her pain, going into that dark corner and pretending that she does not live in the situation that she's living" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:57:00">[05:57:00]</a>.
*   This behavior stemmed from her father, George Anthony, allegedly sexually abusing her from age eight <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:37:00">[05:37:00]</a>. This taught her to lie <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:44:00">[05:44:00]</a>.

## Closing Arguments

[[jodi_ariass_defense_and_trial_proceedings | Jose Baez's exceptional ability of narrative control and storytelling]] was noted during the [[Court Trial and Sentencing | trial]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:24:00">[05:24:00]</a>. Closing arguments were presented on July 3.

### Prosecution's Closing

The prosecution argued the case was about the clash between the responsibility of a child and the life Casey Anthony "wanted to have" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:58:00">[05:58:00]</a>. They presented Casey's choice: "a life tethered to a child, or a life free to be 22" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:59:00">[05:59:00]</a>. They submitted that the evidence showed she "chose her child" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:00">[01:00:00]</a>.

### Defense's Closing

Jose Baez opened by stating the jury likely had "more questions than you have answers" and that the key question, "how did Kaylee die," would "never be answered" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:14">[01:00:14]</a>. He emphasized that the case must not be decided by emotion or anger <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:59">[01:00:59]</a>. He accused the prosecution of parading Kaylee's image to invoke emotion, rather than focusing on evidence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:23">[01:02:23]</a>. He claimed the prosecution's first two weeks of testimony were "completely irrelevant" and served only to "paint Casey Anthony as a party girl, as a girl who lies" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:00">[01:03:00]</a>.

Baez stressed that the state had the burden of proof, stating, "if you don't know what happened, it wasn't proven" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:30">[01:03:30]</a>. He argued that if the jury "hate[s] her," they will "discriminate against her rather than give her the standard that is afforded to each and every citizen" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:42">[01:04:42]</a>. He asked for a "verdict of not guilty on these charges because they simply are not proven and they're simply not true" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:27">[01:05:27]</a>.

Cindy also noted that Casey's car smelled like "a dead body" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:29">[00:02:29]</a>.

Prior to this call, Casey had left the family home a month earlier, claiming she had a work assignment in Tampa and that Kaylee was with a nanny named Zaneda Fernandez Gonzalez, or "Zanny" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:29">[00:03:29]</a>. On December 11, 2008, nearly five months after she was reported missing, [[kaylee_anthonys_disappearance_and_death | Kaylee's skeletal remains]] were discovered in a swamp less than a mile from the Anthony household <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:11">[00:50:11]</a>. Her death was ruled a homicide, and prosecutors announced they would seek the death penalty <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:30">[00:50:30]</a>.

## Trial Proceedings

The trial commenced on May 24, 2011 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:47">[00:51:47]</a>.

### Prosecution's Argument
The prosecution argued that Casey suffocated Kaylee with duct tape, placed her in the car trunk, and later disposed of her body in the swamp <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:50">[00:51:50]</a>. Their primary focus was the motive: that [[kaylee_anthonys_disappearance_and_death | Kaylee's death]] allowed Casey to "live the good life" and pursue her desired freedom <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:16">[00:55:16]</a>. They emphasized that only Casey had access to all the evidence (duct tape, laundry bag, blanket, car) and lied extensively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:49">[00:54:49]</a>.

### Defense Strategy and Allegations
Faced with overwhelming circumstantial evidence, the defense, led by Jose Baez, adopted a strategy to create maximum doubt <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:39">[00:55:39]</a>. In their opening statements, they dropped a "bombshell" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:47">[00:55:47]</a>:

*   **Kaylee's Death:** They claimed [[kaylee_anthonys_disappearance_and_death | Kaylee Anthony]] was never missing but died on June 16, 2008, by drowning in the family's swimming pool <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:35">[00:56:35]</a>.
*   **Abuse Allegations:** Baez alleged that Casey had been sexually abused by her father, George Anthony, since the age of eight <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:37">[00:57:37]</a>. This abuse, he argued, taught Casey to lie and hide her pain, explaining why she concealed Kaylee's death <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:44">[00:57:44]</a>.

The defense focused on theory and proposed "every conspiracy under the sun" to induce skepticism and confusion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:11">[00:58:11]</a>. Jose Baez's exceptional ability in "narrative control and storytelling" and his capacity to connect with the jury was noted <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:24">[00:58:24]</a>.

### Closing Arguments
In his closing argument, the prosecutor reiterated the motive: Casey's desire for a free life without the responsibility of a child <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:02">[00:59:02]</a>. Baez, in turn, focused on the lack of certainty regarding Kaylee's death, stating, "the one question will never be answered... how did Kaylee die?" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:31">[01:00:31]</a>. He accused the prosecution of trying to make the jury "hate her" to secure a conviction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:39">[01:04:39]</a>, while simultaneously evoking empathy for Casey.

## Verdict and Aftermath

On July 5, 2011, the jury delivered its verdict: Casey Anthony was found not guilty of first-degree murder, aggravated child abuse, and aggravated manslaughter of a child <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:52">[01:05:52]</a>.

After the verdict, Jose Baez criticized the [[media_coverage_and_public_perception_of_the_anthony_case | media]] and lawyers who had engaged in "media assassination for three years with bias and prejudice" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:55">[01:06:55]</a>. He asserted that "Casey did not murder Kaylee. It's that simple" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:38">[01:07:38]</a> and that the justice system "has not dishonored her memory by a false conviction" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:45">[01:07:45]</a>.

Casey was released from custody shortly after her acquittal on the most serious charges, having spent two and a half years awaiting trial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:31:00">[01:31:00]</a>. Despite the verdict, public opinion remained largely convinced of her guilt.

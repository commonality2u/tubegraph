## Characteristics of an Antihero

An [[characteristics_of_an_antihero | antihero]] is a character who often possesses traits that would typically make them unlikable, yet audiences find themselves rooting for them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:50:00">[03:50:00]</a>. They are flawed individuals whose moral complexity mirrors our own <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:57:00">[03:57:00]</a>. Unlike traditional heroes, antiheroes make mistakes and are depicted as learning and growing through life's path <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:02:00">[04:02:00]</a>. The connection audiences feel to an [[characteristics_of_an_antihero | antihero]] is often a reflection of themselves, as rooting for their redemption can be seen as rooting for one's own <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:08:00">[04:08:00]</a>.

A key element distinguishing an [[characteristics_of_an_antihero | antihero]] from a villain is the presence of a "glimmer of humanity" alongside a "noticeable vulnerability" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:31:00">[04:31:00]</a>. This allows viewers to connect with and forgive them when they act unethically, yet admire them when they are noble <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:41:00">[04:41:00]</a>. Antiheroes can display a range of emotions and behaviors, being angry, cowardly, and greedy, but also cheerful, brave, and empathetic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:49:00">[04:49:00]</a>.

### Example: Willy from *Bad Santa*

A textbook example of an [[characteristics_of_an_antihero | antihero]] is Willy, the protagonist from the 2003 film *Bad Santa* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:19:00">[03:19:00]</a>. Willy is a middle-aged man who lies, cheats, steals, swears at children, consumes excessive alcohol, and is generally unpleasant to everyone he encounters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:27:00">[03:27:00]</a>. Despite these highly unlikable traits, audiences somehow root for him throughout his story <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:50:00">[03:50:00]</a>.

## Antihero vs. Villain

While both antiheroes and villains can be driven by selfish motivations, the audience's emotional response to them differs significantly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:24:00">[04:24:00]</a>.

*   **Antihero:** Audiences tend to embrace and relate to antiheroes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:20:00">[04:20:00]</a>. This connection stems from their flaws and vulnerabilities, which make them relatable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:31:00">[04:31:00]</a>.
*   **Villain:** Audiences typically despise and detach from villains <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:22:00">[04:22:00]</a>. The villain lacks the "glimmer of humanity" that allows for audience connection and forgiveness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:31:00">[04:31:00]</a>.

Success and achievement were considered "outright obligations" for their children, whether in academics or extracurricular activities, sometimes seemingly taking precedence over the children's well-being <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:46">[00:00:46]</a>.

Initially, Jennifer embraced this pressure, dedicating herself to figure skating. However, a serious knee injury at age 14 ended her dreams of competitive skating <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:11">[00:01:11]</a>.

### Academic Struggles and Deception
With her athletic dreams cut short, her parents' expectations shifted to education <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:21">[00:01:21]</a>. Jennifer, however, was not as academically gifted, averaging a C-minus when her parents demanded straight A's <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:24">[00:01:24]</a>. Instead of communicating her struggles, she began to meet their expectations under false pretenses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:34">[00:01:34]</a>.

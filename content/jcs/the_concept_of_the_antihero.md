## Characteristics of an Antihero

Anti-heroes are often portrayed as flawed characters, whose moral complexity mirrors our own <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:57">[03:57]</a>. They are depicted as learning and growing along the path of life, and their mistakes can lead audiences to reflect on their own <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:02">[04:02]</a>. The reason audiences may root for their redemption is a reflection of rooting for one's own <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:08">[04:08]</a>.

While both anti-heroes and villains are driven by selfish motivations, an anti-hero is embraced and relatable, whereas a villain is typically despised and detached from <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:20">[04:20]</a>. The key distinction is that an anti-hero must possess a "glimmer of humanity" alongside a "noticeable vulnerability" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:33">[04:33]</a>. This allows viewers to genuinely connect with the character, forgiving them when they act unethically and admiring them when they are noble <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:41">[04:41]</a>. They can be angry, cowardly, and greedy, but also cheerful, brave, and empathetic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:49">[04:49]</a>.

## Conclusion of an Antihero's Story

The final, and perhaps most important, component of an anti-hero's arc is the conclusion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:20">[12:20]</a>. According to the Hollywood formula, an anti-hero's actions throughout their life are so drastic and single-minded that they ultimately lead only to salvation or destruction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:50">[12:50]</a>. Their final decision is always dictated by what they have learned and how they have changed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:00">[13:00]</a>.

One account suggests he got clean and works in corporate marketing for Coca-Cola, while another claims he was re-arrested the next day and faces a 15-year prison sentence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:35">[12:35]</a>. Regardless of imprisonment, the theme of [[the_struggle_of_addiction | addiction]] is sufficient to mold his story's outcome <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:11">[13:11]</a>. Jeff's path, whether to salvation or destruction, is defined by his ongoing struggle with [[the_struggle_of_addiction | addiction]], making him a compelling anti-hero whose ending fits the established pattern <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:16">[13:16]</a>.

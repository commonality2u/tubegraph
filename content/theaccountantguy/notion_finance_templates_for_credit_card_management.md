## Tracker Overview

The template provides several sections for a comprehensive overview of credit card activity:

### Credit Cards Overview
This section lists all added credit cards, such as "Student Credit Card," "Cash Back Credit Card," "Travel Credit Card," and "Rewards Credit Card" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:15">[00:00:15]</a>. For each card, it displays:
*   Total amount due <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:23">[00:00:23]</a>
*   Amount repaid so far <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:26">[00:00:26]</a>
*   Balance amount still due <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:27">[00:00:27]</a>

### Monthly and Yearly Overviews
*   **Monthly Overview:** Shows the amount due, repaid, and balance for each month, customizable to individual requirements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:32">[00:00:32]</a>.
*   **Yearly Overview:** Provides a summary of total amount due, repaid, and balance for different years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:41">[00:00:41]</a>.

### Expense Details Section
This is where all individual expenses are recorded <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:51">[00:00:51]</a>.

### Expenses Overview
This section gives a summarized view of expenses categorized by type <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:59">[00:00:59]</a>. It helps in [[analyzing_financial_data_through_notion_templates | analyzing financial data through Notion templates]] by showing:
*   Total expenses added <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:05">[00:01:05]</a>
*   Budgeted amount for each category <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:05">[00:01:05]</a>
*   Comparison of expenses to the budgeted amount <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:09">[00:01:09]</a>
*   Budget status (under budget or over budget) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:11">[00:01:11]</a>

### Notification Section
This section displays upcoming repayment dates and the number of days remaining until the next repayment for each credit card <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:16">[00:01:16]</a>. This feature is crucial for [[Using Notion templates for budget management | budget management]] and ensuring timely payments.

## How to Use the Tracker

To begin [[using_notion_for_budget_management | using Notion for budget management]] with this template, you primarily interact with the **Expense Details** section <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:37">[00:01:37]</a>.

### Booking Expenses
In the **Expense Details** section, you can enter the following information for each transaction:
*   **Date:** The date of the expense <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:42">[00:01:42]</a>.
*   **Expense Details:** A description of the expense <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:44">[00:01:44]</a>.
*   **Period:** The month or period for which the expense is booked <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:46">[00:01:46]</a>.
*   **Year:** The year of the expense <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:49">[00:01:49]</a>.
*   **Credit Card:** The specific credit card used for the expense. New credit cards can be added on the fly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:51">[00:01:51]</a>.
*   **Category:** Assign a category to each expense for organization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:54">[00:01:54]</a>.
*   **Expense Amount:** The amount of the expense <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:57">[00:01:57]</a>.
*   **Opening Balance Due:** For balances carried forward from previous months <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:07">[00:02:07]</a>.

### Entering Repayments
When making a payment, simply enter "credit card repayment" in the details and input the amount in the repayment column <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:58">[00:02:58]</a>. All entered details automatically update the overview sections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:07">[00:03:07]</a>.

### Setting Budgets
In the **Expenses Overview** section, you can set a budgeted amount for each expense category by clicking on the "Budget Amount" column <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:56">[00:03:56]</a>. The template will then compare your actual expenses to your budget and indicate if you are "under budget" or "over budget" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:04">[00:04:04]</a>.

### Setting Repayment Dates
Within the **Notification** section, you can set the next repayment due date for each credit card. The template will automatically calculate and display the number of days remaining until that date <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:40">[00:04:40]</a>. This ensures you are always aware of upcoming payments, helping to avoid late fees and manage debt effectively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:05">[00:05:05]</a>.

## Additional Features and Views

This [[understanding_notion_finance_templates | Notion finance template]] offers several other useful features for detailed financial tracking:

### Attaching Receipts
For any expense, you can attach bills or receipts in the dedicated "Receipt" column in the Expense Details section <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:34">[00:05:34]</a>.

### Summarization Views
The template allows for various summarized views to analyze your spending:
*   **Period View:** Clicking on a specific period (e.g., month) opens a new window showing transaction details like date, period, description, amount, and a tag (e.g., "repayment" in green, "expense" in black) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:44">[00:05:44]</a>.
*   **Year View:** Similar to the period view, but showing information for a specific year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:16">[00:06:16]</a>.
*   **Credit Card View:** Clicking on a credit card displays all transactions related to that card, including its status and category <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:26">[00:06:26]</a>.
*   **Category View:** Provides details of all expenses associated with a particular category <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:50">[00:06:50]</a>.

### Customizing Credit Cards and Categories
Users can easily edit existing credit card information or add new credit cards and categories directly within the template using the pencil icon or "new" button <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:16">[00:05:16]</a>. Categories can also be managed by entering their details <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:24">[00:05:24]</a>.

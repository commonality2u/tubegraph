## Template Overview

The Notion credit card expenses tracker features several key sections for comprehensive financial oversight:

*   **Credit Cards Overview** Shows all added credit cards, including the total amount due, amount repaid, and the balance amount due for each <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:11">[00:00:11]</a>. In a demonstration, four credit cards were added: Student, Cash Back, Travel, and Rewards Credit Cards <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:18">[00:00:18]</a>.
*   **Monthly Overview** Displays the amount due, repaid, and balance amount due for each month, which can be [[customizing_templates_in_notion | customized]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:32">[00:00:32]</a>.
*   **Yearly Overview** Presents the total amount due, amount repaid, and balance amount due across different years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:41">[00:00:41]</a>.
*   **Expense Detail Section** Lists all expenses added in detail <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:51">[00:00:51]</a>.
*   **Expenses Overview** Provides a summary of all booked expenses categorized by type, showing total expenses, budgeted amount, comparison to budget, and budget status (under or over budget) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:58">[00:00:58]</a>.
*   **Notification Section** Indicates the next repayment due date and the number of days remaining until that date for each credit card <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:16">[00:01:16]</a>.

## Using the Tracker

To begin [[setting_up_a_personal_finance_tracker_in_notion | using this tracker]], expenses are booked under the "Expense Details" section <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:37">[00:01:37]</a>.

### Booking Expenses

The expense details section includes fields for:
*   Date <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:42">[00:01:42]</a>
*   Expense details <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:44">[00:01:44]</a>
*   Period (e.g., month) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:46">[00:01:46]</a>
*   Year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:49">[00:01:49]</a>
*   Related credit card <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:51">[00:01:51]</a>
*   Category <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:53">[00:01:53]</a>
*   Expense amount <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:57">[00:01:57]</a>

Users can also enter an opening balance for amounts carried forward from previous months <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:08">[00:02:08]</a>.

### Repaying Amounts

When repaying an amount for a particular credit card, users can enter the repayment amount in the "repayment" column <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:00">[00:03:00]</a>. Entering "credit card repayment" in the details field helps identify these entries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:58">[00:02:58]</a>. All information updates automatically as details are entered <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:07">[00:03:07]</a>.

### [[analyzing_financial_data_with_notion_templates | Analyzing Expenses]] and Budgets

The "Expenses Overview" section allows users to see expenses incurred for each category and set a budgeted amount <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:42">[00:03:42]</a>.
*   To set a budget, simply click on the "budget amount" field and enter the desired figure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:58">[00:03:58]</a>.
*   The tracker will update the budget status to "under budget" if expenses are within the limit, or "over budget" if they exceed it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:26">[00:04:26]</a>.

### Setting Repayment Notifications

The "Notification" section shows when the next repayment is due <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:35">[00:04:35]</a>.
*   Users can [[customizing_templates_in_notion | customize]] their own repayment dates by clicking and selecting a date of their choice <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:39">[00:04:39]</a>.
*   The tracker then displays the number of days pending until the next repayment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:47">[00:04:47]</a>. This feature helps users pay credit card debts on time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:05">[00:05:05]</a>.

## Customization and Additional Features

The template offers several options for [[customizing_and_using_notion_templates | customization]]:

*   **Adding and Editing Credit Cards** Users can edit existing credit card information via a pencil icon or add new credit cards using the "new" button <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:15">[00:05:15]</a>.
*   **Adding and Editing Categories** Categories can be added or edited directly within the category details section <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:22">[00:05:22]</a>.
*   **Receipt Column** A "receipt" column in the expense details section allows users to upload bills or receipts for expenses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:34">[00:05:34]</a>.
*   **Summarization Views** The template provides various summarization views for [[analyzing_financial_data_with_notion_templates | analyzing financial data]]:
    *   **Period View** Clicking on a particular period (e.g., month) opens a new window with detailed transaction information, including date, period, detail, amount, and a tag (green for repayment, red for expense) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:44">[00:05:44]</a>.
    *   **Year View** Similar to the period view, this shows information for a specific year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:14">[00:06:14]</a>.
    *   **Credit Card View** Clicking on a credit card displays all transactions related to that card, along with its status (which can be changed) and an assigned category <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:24">[00:06:24]</a>.
    *   **Category View** Selecting a category provides details on when expenses were incurred for that specific category <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:48">[00:06:48]</a>.

This [[notion_finance_templates_overview | Notion finance template]] allows for quick glances at all added information, both from the detailed sections and the summarized overviews <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:04">[00:07:04]</a>. The template is fully [[customizing_templates_and_databases_in_notion | customizable]] to individual needs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:23">[00:07:23]</a>.

## Setting up Accounts for Income and Expenses

To begin [[tracking_income_and_expenses_using_notion | tracking income and expenses]], navigate to the **Account section** under the "Get Started" view <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:36">[00:02:36]</a>. Here, you'll find pre-defined income (e.g., Salary, Side Hustle) and expense accounts (e.g., Groceries, Utility Bills, Netflix) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:40">[00:02:40]</a>.

### Adding and Modifying Accounts
*   **Modify existing accounts**: Click the "Edit" button next to an account to open its modification panel and change details <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:57">[00:02:57]</a>.
*   **Add new accounts**: Click the "New" button to create a new account setup <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:14">[00:03:14]</a>.

### Account Details
When setting up an account:
*   **Name**: Type the name of the account <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:10">[00:04:10]</a>.
*   **Opening Balance**: Specify an opening balance (typically ignored for income/expenses) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:14">[00:04:14]</a>.
*   **Classification**: Expenses can be classified under categories like "Bills," "Savings," "Subscription," or "Debt" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:31">[00:03:31]</a>.
*   **Payment Frequency**: Set how often you expect to pay or receive money (e.g., Monthly, Annually) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:46">[00:03:46]</a>.

## Recording Transactions
After setting up accounts, head to the **Transactions view** to input financial activities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:54">[00:04:54]</a>.

### Transaction Views
The Transactions section has five views <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:15">[00:05:15]</a>:
*   **All**: Shows all types of transactions (asset, liability, income, expense) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:17">[00:05:17]</a>.
*   **Income, Expense, Asset, Liability**: Dedicated views for specific transaction types <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:28">[00:05:28]</a>.

### Adding a New Transaction
1.  Navigate to the relevant section (e.g., Income for salary) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:34">[00:05:34]</a>.
2.  Click "New" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:38">[00:05:38]</a>.
3.  **Date**: Select the transaction date. Transactions are automatically bifurcated and listed by month in descending order <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:45">[00:05:45]</a>.
4.  **Description**: Add a brief description <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:05">[00:06:05]</a>.
5.  **Category**: (For income/expense, this is usually pre-filled based on the view) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:11">[00:06:11]</a>.
6.  **Account**: Select the specific account (e.g., "Salary" account for income) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:16">[00:06:16]</a>.
7.  **Payment Mode**: Choose how the money was received or paid (e.g., Bank, Cash) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:28">[00:06:28]</a>.
8.  **Transaction Type**:
    *   **Inflow**: Money coming into your account (for income or taking a new loan) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:52">[00:06:52]</a>.
    *   **Outflow**: Money going out of your account (for expenses or loan repayments) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:15">[00:07:15]</a>.
9.  **Amount**: Specify the transaction amount <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:00">[00:07:00]</a>.

## Tracking Debt Payments
The tracker allows you to manage liabilities effectively.

### Setting up Debt Accounts
1.  Go to the **Account section** under the "Get Started" view <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:11">[00:09:11]</a>.
2.  Navigate to the **Liabilities part** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:17">[00:09:17]</a>.
3.  Add new debt by clicking "New," inputting the debt name, opening balance, classifying it as "Debt," and setting a payment frequency (e.g., Monthly) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:27">[00:09:27]</a>.
4.  Edit existing debt accounts by clicking the pencil icon <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:05">[00:10:05]</a>.

### Recording Debt Transactions
1.  Head to the **Liabilities section** under Transactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:35">[00:10:35]</a>.
2.  Click "New" for a new transaction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:53">[00:10:53]</a>.
3.  Select the date, choose the debt account (e.g., Home Loan), payment mode (e.g., Bank) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:55">[00:10:55]</a>.
4.  Set **Transaction Type**:
    *   **Outflow**: For repaying a loan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:31">[00:11:31]</a>.
    *   **Inflow**: For obtaining a new loan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:03">[00:12:03]</a>.
5.  Input the amount <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:43">[00:11:43]</a>.
6.  The total loan amount will update based on repayments and new loans <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:51">[00:11:51]</a>.

### Tracking Debt in Summary
*   Go to the **Summary > Debt view** to track all debt payments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:19">[00:13:19]</a>.
*   **Upcoming Payments**: Shows debts due in the future relative to the current date <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:31">[00:13:31]</a>.
*   **Past Payments**: Reflects payments made in the past, useful for identifying potentially missed payments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:53">[00:13:53]</a>.
*   Displays the total loan amount, repayment percentage, and amount not yet paid <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:08">[00:15:08]</a>.
*   The layout can be customized (e.g., List, Gallery, Board view) by clicking the three dots and selecting "Layout" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:30">[00:15:30]</a>.

To [[creating_a_monthly_budget_planner_in_notion | set up budgets]] for personal finances:

## [[creating_a_savings_funds_tracker_in_notion | Creating and Tracking Savings Funds]]
Manage your savings goals within Notion.

### Setting up Savings Funds
1.  Go to the **Account section** under "Get Started" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:08">[00:19:08]</a>.
2.  Navigate to the **Asset part**, as savings are considered assets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:12">[00:19:12]</a>.
3.  Add a new savings fund (e.g., "Car Fund") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:30">[00:19:30]</a>.
4.  Classify it under the "Savings" category <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:44">[00:19:44]</a>.
5.  Set a **goal amount** for the savings fund by editing the account and inputting the desired figure (e.g., $1200) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:16">[00:20:16]</a>.
6.  Icons can be added to savings funds for visual identification <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:25">[00:24:25]</a>.

### Recording Savings Transactions
1.  Go to the **Asset section** under Transactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:07">[00:23:07]</a>.
2.  Add a new transaction (e.g., "Car Fund Savings") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:22">[00:23:22]</a>.
3.  Select the savings account (e.g., "Car Fund") and payment mode (e.g., Bank) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:28">[00:23:28]</a>.
4.  The transaction type will be **Outflow**, as money is moving *out* of your primary account *into* the savings account <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:47">[00:23:47]</a>.
5.  Enter the amount saved <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:01">[00:24:01]</a>.

### Tracking Savings Progress
*   [[Editing_and_managing_savings_in_Notion | Editing and managing savings in Notion]] is straightforward. The savings section reflects the total amount saved and the percentage achieved towards the goal amount <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:21">[00:20:21]</a>.

## Setting up and Tracking Bills
Effectively manage your recurring bills.

### Setting up Bill Expenses
1.  Navigate to the **Account section** under "Get Started" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:10">[00:25:10]</a>.
2.  Under **Expenses**, add or modify accounts that are bills (e.g., "Wi-Fi bills") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:14">[00:25:14]</a>.
3.  Classify these expenses as "Bills" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:34">[00:25:34]</a>.
4.  Set the appropriate payment frequency (e.g., Monthly) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:36">[00:25:36]</a>.

### Recording Bill Transactions
1.  Go to the **Expense section** under Transactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:01">[00:26:01]</a>.
2.  Enter bill payment details: date, description, account, payment mode (e.g., Bank), and amount <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:08">[00:26:08]</a>.
3.  The transaction type will be **Outflow** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:16">[00:26:16]</a>.

### Tracking Bills in Summary
*   Navigate to the **Bills section** under Summary <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:24">[00:26:24]</a>.
*   It displays two statuses:
    *   **Upcoming Payments**: Bills whose next payment is due after the current date <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:30">[00:26:30]</a>.
    *   **Past Payments**: Bills that were due in the past and might have been missed, based on their payment frequency and last payment date <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:32">[00:26:32]</a>.
*   This helps in keeping track of due dates and identifying any overlooked payments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:19">[00:27:19]</a>.

## Tracking Net Worth
Gain a complete overview of your financial position.

### Setting Net Worth Goals
1.  Navigate to the **Net Worth Goal section** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:05">[00:33:05]</a>.
2.  Specify your target net worth to achieve by the end of the financial year (e.g., $10,000) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:09">[00:33:09]</a>.

### Viewing Net Worth
*   Go to the **Net Worth section** under Summary <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:28">[00:33:28]</a>.
*   Displays the set net worth goal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:32">[00:33:32]</a>.
*   Shows the **Total Net Worth**, calculated as `Assets - Liabilities` from all entered transactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:36">[00:33:36]</a>.
*   Indicates how much you are "lagging behind" your net worth goal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:57">[00:33:57]</a>.
*   Shows the percentage of the net worth goal achieved <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:11">[00:34:11]</a>.
*   **Total Growth** is calculated as `Closing Net Worth - Opening Net Worth` (Opening Net Worth comes from the initial balances specified in the Account section) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:19">[00:34:19]</a>.

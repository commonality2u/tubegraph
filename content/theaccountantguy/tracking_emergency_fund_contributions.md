This article explains how to utilize a Notion template to track your emergency funds and manage contributions effectively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:07">[00:00:07]</a>. The template provides a comprehensive system for monitoring savings, setting financial goals, and ensuring you meet your targets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:07">[00:00:07]</a>.

## Template Overview

The emergency fund tracker template is divided into three main sections:
-   **Fund Setup:** For defining and organizing your emergency funds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:13">[00:00:13]</a>.
-   **Savings Overview:** Provides a summary of all savings made to date <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:15">[00:00:15]</a>.
-   **Summary Section:** Reflects total savings and required contributions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:20">[00:00:20]</a>.

## [[notion_sinking_funds_tracker_setup | Fund Setup]]

The "Fund Setup" section allows you to define and manage different categories for your emergency funds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:26">[00:00:26]</a>.

### Categories
The template comes with three predefined categories:
-   Financial Safety <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:31">[00:00:31]</a>
-   Asset Protection <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:32">[00:00:32]</a>
-   Health and Wellness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:34">[00:00:34]</a>

You can [[editing_and_managing_savings_funds | edit these categories]] by clicking on them or add new ones by selecting "add a group" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:35">[00:00:35]</a>.

### Adding a New Fund
To add a new emergency fund:
1.  Click the "Add Fund" button <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:51">[00:00:51]</a>.
2.  A new fund will appear under a "No Classification" tab <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:58">[00:00:58]</a>.
3.  Click the three-dot icon next to the new fund to move it to its respective category (e.g., Financial Safety) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:01">[00:01:01]</a>.

### Fund Details
Each fund has several editable fields accessible via a pencil icon <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:11">[00:01:11]</a>:
-   **Fund Detail:** A descriptive name for the fund <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:14">[00:01:14]</a>.
-   **Starting Balance:** The initial amount in the fund <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:17">[00:01:17]</a>.
-   **Goal Amount:** Your targeted savings for this specific emergency fund <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:23">[00:01:23]</a>.
-   **Start Date:** The date you begin contributing to the fund <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:32">[00:01:32]</a>.
-   **Target End Date:** The desired date by which you aim to have the total emergency fund available <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:48">[00:01:48]</a>.

> [!INFO] Automatic Calculations
> The template automatically calculates the "savings made till date" based on your contributions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:42">[00:01:42]</a>.

## Contribution Section

This section is where you record your contributions to each emergency savings fund <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:17">[00:02:17]</a>.

### Organization
Contributions are organized by month, with the latest month appearing at the top <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:35">[00:02:35]</a>. Each month indicates the number of contributions made <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:40">[00:02:40]</a>.

### Contribution Details
For each contribution, you can enter the following details:
-   **Date:** The date of the contribution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:45">[00:02:45]</a>.
-   **Description:** A brief note about the contribution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:46">[00:02:46]</a>.
-   **Period:** The month and year of the contribution (predefined for 2023, but new months can be added) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:48">[00:02:48]</a>.
-   **Fund:** Select the specific emergency fund you are contributing to from a dropdown list of your setup funds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:15">[00:03:15]</a>.
-   **Amount:** The amount of savings or contribution you are making for that month <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:24">[00:03:24]</a>.
-   **Requirement:** This column automatically calculates how much contribution is needed each month to meet the targeted goal amount by the set end date <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:35">[00:03:35]</a>.

> [!TIP] Meeting Your Goals
> A tick mark will appear next to a contribution if the "Amount" entered is equal to or exceeds the "Requirement" for that month, indicating you've met your target <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:00">[00:04:00]</a>.

### Quick Action Button
Use the "Quick Action" button to automatically add a new line item for the current month and date, simplifying data entry <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:31">[00:04:31]</a>. You can then fill in the remaining values or change the date as needed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:48">[00:04:48]</a>.

## Quarterly Overview

The quarterly overview provides a glance at your contributions across different quarters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:09">[00:05:09]</a>.
-   Months are grouped under their respective quarters (e.g., January, February, March under Quarter 1) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:17">[00:05:17]</a>.
-   You can see the total savings made and the total requirement for each month within a quarter <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:31">[00:05:31]</a>.
-   A percentage indicates how much of the savings requirement was met for that period <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:04">[00:06:04]</a>.
-   Different colors are used to provide a quick visual snapshot of your financial progress <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:29">[00:06:29]</a>.
-   The total savings for each quarter are also displayed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:41">[00:06:41]</a>.

## [[tracking_savings_and_contributions_with_notion | Savings Overview]]

The "Savings Overview" section offers a detailed breakdown of each emergency fund, categorized for easy viewing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:58">[00:06:58]</a>.

For each emergency fund, you'll see:
-   **Total Savings:** The sum of the starting balance and all contributions made to that fund <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:09">[00:07:09]</a>.
-   **Targeted Savings:** The goal amount set for that fund, pulled directly from the Fund Setup section <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:35">[00:07:35]</a>.
-   **Achieved Percentage:** The percentage of the total savings requirement that has been met <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:47">[00:07:47]</a>.
-   **Targeted Savings Date:** The end goal date for the fund <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:50">[00:07:50]</a>.
-   **Months Left:** The number of months remaining until the targeted end date, calculated from the current date <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:05">[00:08:05]</a>. This value updates dynamically <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:03">[00:09:03]</a>.
-   **Contribution Required Per Month:** The monthly contribution needed to reach the target by the end date <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:31">[00:08:31]</a>.

## Summary Section

The overall "Summary Section" provides a high-level view of your emergency fund progress <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:17">[00:09:17]</a>.
-   **Total Savings:** The combined opening balance of all funds plus all contributions made <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:20">[00:09:20]</a>.
-   **Savings Required:** The sum of all targeted savings amounts across all funds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:33">[00:09:33]</a>.
-   **Difference:** The difference between total savings and savings required <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:42">[00:09:42]</a>.
-   **Percentage:** The overall percentage of your targeted savings that has been achieved <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:51">[00:09:51]</a>.

This comprehensive summary helps you understand your overall financial position regarding emergency funds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:59">[00:09:59]</a>.

> [!NOTE] Customization
> The entire template can be customized to fit your specific requirements and preferences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:16">[00:10:16]</a>.

This article outlines how to set up and use a personal finance tracker in Notion to manage various aspects of your financial life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:10">[00:00:10]</a>. The tracker is designed with several key sections to provide a comprehensive overview and detailed tracking capabilities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:14">[00:00:14]</a>.

## Overview of the Notion Finance Tracker

The Notion finance tracker is structured into three primary sections, along with several detailed tracking views:

*   **Net Worth Target**: Allows you to specify your net worth goal for the financial year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:19">[00:00:19]</a>.
*   **Account Section**: Where you define different accounts for income, expenses, assets, and liabilities, including opening balances, payment frequency, and category <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:23">[00:00:23]</a>.
*   **Summary Section**: Provides an at-a-glance view of your entire finances <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:38">[00:00:38]</a>. This includes:
    *   **Finance View**: Shows all accounts (income, expenses, liabilities) and their contributions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:42">[00:00:42]</a>.
    *   **Debt Section**: Tracks upcoming and past debt payments, showing due dates, total debt, and amount paid <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:52">[00:00:52]</a>.
    *   **Budget Section**: Helps set budgets for estimated income and expenses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:08">[00:01:08]</a>.
    *   **Savings Section**: Manages and tracks personal savings goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:15">[00:01:15]</a>.
    *   **Bill Section**: Displays upcoming and past bill payments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:23">[00:01:23]</a>.
    *   **Subscription Section**: Similar to bills, tracks subscription statuses and payment dates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:32">[00:01:32]</a>.
    *   **Net Worth Section**: Gives a total overview of achieved net worth and how much is remaining to reach the target <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:45">[00:01:45]</a>.
*   **Transaction Section**: This is where all transactions related to income, expenses, assets, or liabilities are entered, which then automatically updates all other sections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:51">[00:01:51]</a>.

## [[setting_up_income_and_expense_tracking_in_notion | Setting Up Income and Expense Tracking]]

To begin tracking income and expenses in this [[how_to_set_up_and_use_a_personal_finance_tracker_in_notion | personal finance tracker]], you first need to set up your accounts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:30">[00:02:30]</a>.

1.  **Navigate to Accounts**: Go to the "Account" section under the "Get Started" view <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:36">[00:02:36]</a>. Here, you'll find predefined income (e.g., Salary, Side Hustle) and expense categories (e.g., Groceries, Utility Bills, Netflix) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:40">[00:02:40]</a>.
2.  **Add/Modify Accounts**:
    *   To modify an existing account, click the "Edit" button next to it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:55">[00:02:55]</a>.
    *   To add a new income or expense, click the "New" button <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:12">[00:03:12]</a>.
3.  **Classify Expenses**: When setting up an expense, you can classify it based on your requirements. Common classifications include "Bills," "Savings," "Subscription," and "Debt" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:28">[00:03:28]</a>.
4.  **Set Payment Frequency**: Specify how frequently you expect to pay an expense (e.g., monthly, annually, weekly) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:46">[00:03:46]</a>.
5.  **Opening Balance**: For income and expense accounts, you can generally ignore the "Opening Balance" field <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:14">[00:04:14]</a>.

## Entering Transactions

After setting up your accounts, you need to record your transactions.

1.  **Navigate to Transactions**: Head to the "Transactions" view <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:53">[00:04:53]</a>.
2.  **Select View**: The "Transactions" view has several sub-views:
    *   **All View**: Shows all types of transactions (asset, liability, income, expense) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:15">[00:05:15]</a>.
    *   **Category-Specific Views**: Dedicated views for Income, Expenses, Assets, and Liabilities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:28">[00:05:28]</a>.
3.  **Add a New Transaction**:
    *   Select the relevant category view (e.g., "Income" for income transactions) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:34">[00:05:34]</a>.
    *   Click the "New" button <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:38">[00:05:38]</a>.
    *   **Date**: Select the transaction date. Transactions are automatically organized by month in descending order <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:45">[00:05:45]</a>.
    *   **Description**: Add a description for the transaction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:05">[00:06:05]</a>.
    *   **Category**: This will automatically populate based on the view selected (e.g., "Income") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:11">[00:06:11]</a>.
    *   **Account**: Select the specific account from your predefined list (e.g., "Salary" for income) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:14">[00:06:14]</a>.
    *   **Payment Mode**: Choose how the money was received or paid (e.g., Bank, Cash) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:28">[00:06:28]</a>.
    *   **Transaction Type**: Select "Inflow" if money is coming into your account (e.g., income) or "Outflow" if money is leaving your account (e.g., expense) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:46">[00:06:46]</a>.
    *   **Amount**: Enter the transaction amount <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:59">[00:06:59]</a>.

## Tracking Income and Expense Summary

To see an overview of your income and expenses:

1.  **Navigate to Summary**: Go to the "Summary" section <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:44">[00:07:44]</a>.
2.  **Select Finances View**: Click on the "Finances" view <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:47">[00:07:47]</a>.
3.  **View Overview**: This view displays total income and total expenses, along with the percentage contribution of each individual income source or expense category <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:51">[00:07:51]</a>. Figures are automatically updated as transactions are entered <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:38">[00:08:38]</a>.

## [[using_notion_for_financial_planning_and_debt_overview | Setting Up and Tracking Debt Payments]]

To manage your debts:

### Setting Up Debt Accounts
1.  **Navigate to Accounts**: Go to the "Account" section under "Get Started" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:11">[00:09:11]</a>.
2.  **Select Liabilities**: Click on the "Liabilities" section <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:17">[00:09:17]</a>.
3.  **Add New Debt**:
    *   Click "New" to add a new debt (e.g., Home Loan, Car Loan) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:27">[00:09:27]</a>.
    *   Specify an "Opening Balance" if applicable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:31">[00:09:31]</a>.
    *   Classify it as "Debt" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:36">[00:09:36]</a>.
    *   Set the "Payment Frequency" (e.g., Monthly) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:46">[00:09:46]</a>.
    *   Existing debts can be edited using the pencil icon <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:05">[00:10:05]</a>.

### Entering Debt Transactions
1.  **Navigate to Transactions**: Go to the "Transactions" view <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:29">[00:10:29]</a>.
2.  **Select Liabilities View**: Click on the "Liability" section <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:35">[00:10:35]</a>.
3.  **Add a Payment**:
    *   Click "New" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:53">[00:10:53]</a>.
    *   Select the "Date" of the payment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:55">[00:10:55]</a>.
    *   Select the "Account" (e.g., Home Loan) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:13">[00:11:13]</a>.
    *   Choose the "Payment Mode" (e.g., Bank) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:21">[00:11:21]</a>.
    *   For loan repayments, the "Transaction Type" should be "Outflow" as money is leaving your account <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:29">[00:11:29]</a>. If you are taking a new loan, it would be "Inflow" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:58">[00:11:58]</a>.
    *   Enter the "Amount" of the payment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:42">[00:11:42]</a>.

### Tracking Debt Overview
1.  **Navigate to Summary**: Go to the "Summary" section <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:21">[00:13:21]</a>.
2.  **Select Debt View**: Click on the "Debt" view <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:21">[00:13:21]</a>.
3.  **Review Payments**:
    *   **Upcoming Payments**: Shows debts with future due dates based on the last payment and frequency (e.g., Car Loan due September 29, Home Loan due October 13) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:31">[00:13:31]</a>.
    *   **Past Payments**: Displays debts where a payment was due in the past but hasn't been recorded yet, indicating a potentially missed payment (e.g., Credit Card last paid July 14, indicating no August payment) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:51">[00:13:51]</a>.
    *   Each debt card shows the total loan amount, total repayment percentage, and the amount still unpaid <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:06">[00:15:06]</a>.
4.  **Customize View**: You can change the layout (e.g., List, Gallery) and visible properties of this view <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:30">[00:15:30]</a>. Grouping by "Payment Status" and sorting manually allows you to see upcoming payments first <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:27">[00:16:27]</a>.

1.  **Enter Transactions**: Ensure all income and expense transactions are entered <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:07">[00:17:07]</a>.
2.  **Navigate to Summary**: Go to the "Summary" section <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:14">[00:17:14]</a>.
3.  **Select Budget View**: Click on the "Budget" view <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:16">[00:17:16]</a>.
4.  **Set Budget Amounts**:
    *   The view outlines incurred expenses and earned income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:19">[00:17:19]</a>.
    *   For each expense or income item, you can click on the "Budgeted Amount" field and enter your estimated budget <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:39">[00:17:39]</a>. The system then shows the actual amount as a percentage of the budgeted amount <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:27">[00:17:27]</a>.
    *   If the "Budgeted Amount" property is hidden, you can reveal it by clicking on the three dots, then "Properties," and searching for "Budgeted Amount" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:20">[00:18:20]</a>.

## Tracking Net Worth

To track your overall financial health:

1.  **Set Net Worth Goal**:
    *   Navigate to the "Net Worth Goal" section <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:05">[00:33:05]</a>.

## Key Considerations and FAQs

### Device Availability
The Notion template is available on all devices, including smartphones, tablets, and laptops, supporting iOS, Android, Windows, and Mac OS <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:59">[00:40:59]</a>.

### Cost
Notion itself is a free tool to use; you only need to sign up with an email ID <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:29">[00:41:29]</a>. There are no extra charges involved for using this template in addition to any potential template purchase price <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:27">[00:41:27]</a>.

### Multi-Currency Support
Notion supports various currencies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:01">[00:42:01]</a>. To change the currency displayed:

1.  **Identify Currency Fields**: Look for any field with a dollar sign ($) associated with it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:14">[00:42:14]</a>.
2.  **Edit Property**: Click on the field, then select "Edit Property" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:21">[00:42:21]</a>.
3.  **Change Number Format**: Go to "Number Format" and select your desired currency (e.g., Euro) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:26">[00:42:26]</a>.
4.  **Apply to All Views**: This change needs to be applied to all relevant views where currency is displayed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:41">[00:42:41]</a>.

### Applicability for Couples
This dashboard is also applicable for couples who wish to [[managing_finances_with_notion | manage finances with Notion]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:33">[00:36:33]</a>. In the "Account" section, you can split different categories by individual (e.g., "Salary by Him," "Side Hustle by Her") to denote earnings and expenses by each person, allowing for easy tracking for two individuals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:47">[00:35:47]</a>.

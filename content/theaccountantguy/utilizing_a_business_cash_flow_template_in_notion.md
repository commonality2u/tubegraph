## Cash Flow Overview

The template's main dashboard features a summary section on the left and three other sections on the right <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:10">[00:00:10]</a>.

### Summary Section

This section provides a quick snapshot of your cash flow <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:11">[00:00:11]</a>:
*   **Opening Balance**: The starting cash balance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:11">[00:00:11]</a>.
*   **Total Inflow**: Total cash received from all transactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:14">[00:00:14]</a>.
*   **Cash Outflow**: Total cash paid out from all transactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:19">[00:00:19]</a>.
*   **Closing Balance**: The final cash balance, which updates automatically as transactions are entered <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:21">[00:00:21]</a>.

### Account Section

This section allows you to set up and manage different accounts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:34">[00:00:34]</a>.
*   **Adding/Editing Accounts**: You can add new or edit existing assets, liabilities, income, and expense accounts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:47">[00:00:47]</a>. To edit, click the pencil icon next to an item <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:53">[00:00:53]</a>. To add a new item, click "New" under the respective category <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:51">[00:02:51]</a>.
*   **Account Details**: For each account, you can specify:
    *   **Opening Balance**: The starting balance when you begin using the template <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:05">[00:01:05]</a>.
    *   **Classifications**: How an item is categorized (e.g., debt) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:12">[00:01:12]</a>. You can add or edit classifications <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:30">[00:01:30]</a>.
    *   **Payment Frequency**: How often payments are received for a particular item, useful for income and expenses (e.g., quarterly) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:39">[00:01:39]</a>.
    *   **Opening Units**: Relevant for investments or inventory, indicating the starting number of units <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:04">[00:02:04]</a>.
    *   **Asset Class**: Applicable for inventory (e. æ¤œ inventory asset class) or investments (e.g., investment asset class) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:17">[00:02:17]</a>.

### Quarterly Overview

This section displays the cash flow movement for each month, grouped into different quarters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:08">[00:03:08]</a>. It includes inflow, outflow, and net balance amounts for each month <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:21">[00:03:21]</a>. These figures automatically update as you add transactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:49">[00:03:49]</a>.

### Business Activity

This provides an [[analyzing_financial_data_with_notion_templates | overview]] of cash flow categorized by business activities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:55">[00:03:55]</a>:
*   **Operating Activities**: Related to core business operations, such as salary payments or revenue receipts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:59">[00:03:59]</a>.
*   **Investing Activities**: Relates to investments made by the business, such as stock or mutual fund investments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:12">[00:04:12]</a>.
*   **Financing Activities**: Involves raising loans or debt to operate the business <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:33">[00:04:33]</a>.

Each category shows its respective inflow, outflow, and net balance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:10">[00:04:10]</a>.

## Entering Transactions

The template offers several views for entering transactions: Income, Expense, Asset, Liability, Investments, and Inventory <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:56">[00:04:56]</a>. Transactions are sorted by month, with the latest month appearing on top <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:24">[00:05:24]</a>.

### Adding an Income Transaction (Example)

To add an income transaction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:31">[00:05:31]</a>:
1.  **Click "Add Income"**: This button automatically adds a new entry with the current date <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:40">[00:05:40]</a>.
2.  **Date**: Adjust the date using the calendar picker <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:51">[00:05:51]</a>.
3.  **Period**: Specify the relevant month or period (e.g., January 2024) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:06">[00:06:06]</a>.
4.  **Description**: Describe the income (e.g., "Sales Revenue") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:32">[00:06:32]</a>.
5.  **Account**: Select the relevant account from the list previously set up in the Account section (e.g., "Sales Revenue" account) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:41">[00:06:41]</a>.
6.  **Payment Mode**: Indicate how the payment was received (e.g., Cash, Bank) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:54">[00:06:54]</a>.
7.  **Amount**: Enter the monetary value received <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:09">[00:07:09]</a>.
8.  **Type of Cash**: Categorize the activity as Operating, Investing, or Financing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:17">[00:07:17]</a>. Sales revenue typically falls under Operating <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:22">[00:07:22]</a>.
9.  **Transaction Type**: Specify whether it's an Inflow (cash coming in, as for income) or Outflow (cash going out, as for expenses) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:54">[00:07:54]</a>.
10. **Documents**: Attach any relevant files like sales receipts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:39">[00:08:39]</a>.
11. **Tax Rate**: Enter a tax rate to automatically calculate the tax amount on the transaction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:48">[00:08:48]</a>.

All summary figures (like total cash inflow) automatically update as transactions are added <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:12">[00:09:12]</a>.

### Investments and Inventory

*   **Investments**: In the Investments view, you can record details of stock purchases and sales, including units and price <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:07">[00:10:07]</a>. The template shows the investment value (purchase price) and allows for calculation of profit and loss on sales <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:33">[00:10:33]</a>.
*   **Inventory**: The Inventory view allows you to specify total inventory value, purchase price, and sale price <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:58">[00:10:58]</a>. You can indicate units and price, and the template automatically calculates profit or loss on the transaction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:08">[00:11:08]</a>.

## Troubleshooting

If figures do not update immediately, click on the sum or balance options within the relevant section to force an update <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:21">[00:11:21]</a>.

## Overview of the Notion Personal Finance Tracker

The Notion tracker is designed to manage personal finances effectively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:10">[00:00:10]</a>. It consists of several key sections:
*   **Net Worth Target** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:19">[00:00:19]</a>: Allows users to specify a financial year target <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:21">[00:00:21]</a>.
*   **Account Section** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:23">[00:00:23]</a>: Where different accounts for income, expenses, assets, and liabilities are specified, including opening balances, payment frequency, and category <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:25">[00:00:25]</a>.
*   **Summary Section** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:38">[00:00:38]</a>: Provides an at-a-glance view of all finances <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:40">[00:00:40]</a>. This includes the "Finance View" showing account contributions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:42">[00:00:42]</a>.
*   **Debt Section** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:52">[00:00:52]</a>: Tracks upcoming and past debt payments, including next payment due dates, total debt, and debt paid <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:53">[00:00:53]</a>.
*   **Budget Section** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:08">[00:01:08]</a>: Enables setting budgets for estimated income and expenses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:10">[00:01:10]</a>.
*   **Savings Section** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:15">[00:01:15]</a>: Helps keep track of personal savings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:17">[00:01:17]</a>.
*   **Bill Section** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:23">[00:01:23]</a>: Shows upcoming and paid bills, with customizability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:24">[00:01:24]</a>.
*   **Subscription Section** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:32">[00:01:32]</a>: Functions similarly to the debt and bill sections, tracking subscription status and payment dates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:34">[00:01:34]</a>.
*   **Net Worth Section** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:45">[00:01:45]</a>: Provides an overview of net worth achieved and progress towards targets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:47">[00:01:47]</a>.
*   **Transaction Section** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:51">[00:01:51]</a>: Where all income, expense, asset, and liability transactions are recorded, impacting all other sections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:53">[00:01:53]</a>.

## Setting Up Income and Expense Accounts

To begin [[tracking_transactions_and_expenses | tracking transactions and expenses]] in this personal finance tracker, navigate to the "Account" section under the "Get Started" view <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:33">[00:02:33]</a>. Here, you can find and manage all personal income and expense accounts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:40">[00:02:40]</a>.

### Adding or Modifying Accounts
*   **To modify an existing account**: Click the "edit" button next to the account name <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:57">[00:02:57]</a>.
*   **To add a new account**: Click the "new" button <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:12">[00:03:12]</a>.
    *   **Name**: Type the name of the account <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:10">[00:04:10]</a>.
    *   **Opening Balance**: For income and expense accounts, the opening balance can be ignored <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:16">[00:04:16]</a>.
    *   **Classification**: Expenses can be classified (e.g., bills, savings, subscriptions, debt) based on your needs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:31">[00:03:31]</a>.
    *   **Payment Frequency**: Set how often an expense is expected to be paid (e.g., monthly, annually) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:46">[00:03:46]</a>.

## Recording Income and Expense Transactions

After setting up accounts, the next step in [[tracking_income_expenses_assets_and_liabilities | tracking income expenses assets and liabilities]] is to enter transactions in the "Transactions" view <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:02">[00:05:02]</a>.

### Transaction Views
The "Transactions" section offers five views:
*   **All View**: Shows all types of transactions (asset, liability, income, expense) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:15">[00:05:15]</a>.
*   **Income View**: Dedicated to inputting income transactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:34">[00:05:34]</a>.
*   **Expense View**: Dedicated to inputting expense transactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:34">[00:05:34]</a>.
*   **Asset View**: For asset-related transactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:34">[00:05:34]</a>.
*   **Liability View**: For liability-related transactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:34">[00:05:34]</a>.

### Steps for [[using_the_transactions_tab_for_income_and_expenses | Using the transactions tab for income and expenses]]
1.  **Navigate to the relevant section**: For income, go to "Income" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:34">[00:05:34]</a>. For expenses, go to "Expense" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:11">[00:07:11]</a>.
2.  **Click "New"**: Opens a new transaction window <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:38">[00:05:38]</a>.
3.  **Select Date**: Choose the transaction date. Transactions are bifurcated by month and listed in descending order (latest month on top) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:45">[00:05:45]</a>.
4.  **Add Description**: Provide a brief description of the transaction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:05">[00:06:05]</a>.
5.  **Select Account**: Link the transaction to an existing income or expense account <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:14">[00:06:14]</a>.
6.  **Choose Payment Mode**: Specify how the money was received or paid (e.g., bank, cash) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:28">[00:06:28]</a>.
7.  **Select Transaction Type**:
    *   **Inflow**: For money coming into your account (e.g., income, taking a new loan) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:47">[00:06:47]</a>.
    *   **Outflow**: For money going out of your account (e.g., expenses, loan repayments, savings) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:15">[00:07:15]</a>.
8.  **Enter Amount**: Input the monetary value of the transaction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:59">[00:06:59]</a>.

## [[analyzing_income_and_expenses_with_the_dashboard | Analyzing income and expenses with the dashboard]]

After inputting transactions, you can [[income_and_expense_tracking_features | track income and expenses]] under the "Summary" view <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:44">[00:07:44]</a>.

The "Finances" view within the summary section provides a comprehensive overview of income and expenses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:47">[00:07:47]</a>.
*   **Total Amounts**: Reflects the total income earned and total expenses incurred from recorded transactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:57">[00:07:57]</a>.
*   **Percentage Contribution**: Shows the percentage each income or expense head contributes to the total <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:05">[00:08:05]</a>.
*   **Real-time Updates**: Figures automatically update as transactions are added or modified <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:39">[00:08:39]</a>.

## [[tracking_budgeted_and_actual_expenses | Tracking budgeted and actual expenses]]

To track your budget, navigate to the "Budget" section under the "Summary" view <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:14">[00:17:14]</a>.
*   **Overview**: Displays all outlined expenses and income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:19">[00:17:19]</a>.
*   **Actual vs. Budgeted**: Shows the total amount of expenses incurred and their percentage against the total budgeted amount <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:22">[00:17:22]</a>.
*   **Adjusting Budget**: Users can click on any budgeted amount and enter a new value, which will automatically update the reflection <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:39">[00:17:39]</a>.
*   **Income Budget**: Similarly, estimated income can be set and compared against actual income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:56">[00:17:56]</a>.
*   **Hidden Properties**: If a budgeted amount isn't visible, click on the property and search for "budgeted amount" to enable its display <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:29">[00:18:29]</a>.

## [[adding_and_managing_expenses | Adding and managing expenses]] - Bills

To manage bills, first go to the "Account" section under "Get Started" and specify your bills under "Expenses" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:08">[00:25:08]</a>.
*   **Setup**: Add new expenses, classify them as "bills," and set a payment frequency (e.g., monthly) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:22">[00:25:22]</a>.
*   **Transaction Entry**: Enter bill payments in the "Expense" part of "Transactions," noting the date, description, account, payment mode, transaction type (outflow), and amount <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:59">[00:25:59]</a>.
*   **Summary View**: The "Bills" section under "Summary" has "Upcoming Payments" and "Past Payments" views <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:24">[00:26:24]</a>.
    *   **Upcoming Payments**: Bills due after the current date <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:40">[00:26:40]</a>.
    *   **Past Payments**: Bills whose last payment date indicates a missed payment based on the set frequency (e.g., a monthly bill due in August but last paid in July) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:19">[00:27:19]</a>.

## Notion Template Features and FAQs

### Device Compatibility
The Notion template is available on all devices, including smartphones, tablets, and laptops, supporting iOS, Android, Windows, and Mac OS <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:44">[00:47:44]</a>.

### Cost of Notion
Notion is a free tool for personal use; no extra payment is required beyond the template's price <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:14">[00:48:14]</a>. Users only need to sign up with an existing email ID <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:16">[00:48:16]</a>.

### Multi-currency Support
Notion supports various currencies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:45">[00:48:45]</a>. To change the currency:
1.  Navigate to any view displaying a dollar sign (e.g., Transactions, Net Worth) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:03">[00:49:03]</a>.
2.  Click on the column header with the dollar sign <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:06">[00:49:06]</a>.
3.  Select "Edit property" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:08">[00:49:08]</a>.
4.  Under "Number format," choose the desired currency (e.g., Euro) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:10">[00:49:10]</a>. This must be done for all relevant views <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:26">[00:49:26]</a>.

### Viewing Specific Account Transactions
To quickly view transactions related to a particular account (e.g., only "Salary" transactions):
1.  Go to the "Finances" view in the "Summary" section <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:21">[00:45:21]</a>.
2.  Click on the specific income or expense account you wish to view (e.g., "Salary") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:56">[00:45:56]</a>.
3.  A window will open on the right side. Scroll down to the bottom and click on "View Transactions" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:22">[00:46:22]</a>. This will automatically populate and show only the transactions related to that specific account <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:38">[00:46:38]</a>.

This article details how to set up and utilize a small business bookkeeping template within Notion, covering its structure, data entry methods, and summary features <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:01">[00:00:01]</a>.

## Template Overview

The small business bookkeeping template in Notion is structured into three main sections:
*   **Start Here** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:27">[00:00:27]</a>
*   **Summary** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:30">[00:00:30]</a>
*   **Transactions** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:35">[00:00:35]</a>

## Start Here Section

The "Start Here" section is where you input foundational information for your business <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:42">[00:00:42]</a>.

### Account Section

This section contains various financial accounts categorized under key headings:
*   **Expenses:** Examples include purchase expense, utility bills, subscription, salaries, and wages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:58">[00:00:58]</a>.
*   **Liabilities:** Examples include accounts payable, taxes payable, accrued expenses, and credit card <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:04">[00:01:04]</a>.
*   **Income** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:52">[00:00:52]</a>
*   **Assets** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:53">[00:00:53]</a>

#### Managing Accounts
*   **Editing Items:** Hover over any item, click the pencil icon, edit details, and click outside to save <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:10">[00:01:10]</a>.
*   **Deleting Items:** Click the three-dot menu next to an item and select "Delete" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:22">[00:01:22]</a>.
*   **Adding Items:** Scroll down to the desired category and click "New Page" to add a new account <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:28">[00:01:28]</a>.

#### Account Details
Each account item includes several optional fields:
*   **Opening Balance:** Enter any carried-forward balances for liabilities and assets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:43">[00:01:43]</a>.
*   **Classification:** Classify items as bills, debt, savings, or other relevant categories <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:53">[00:01:53]</a>. For example, utility bills might be classified as "Bills," and subscriptions as "Subscription" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:02">[00:02:02]</a>.
*   **Payment Frequency:** Select a payment frequency if applicable, such as monthly, half-yearly, or quarterly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:12">[00:02:12]</a>.
*   **Opening Units:** Add starting units for inventory or assets when beginning the business <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:27">[00:02:27]</a>.
*   **Asset Class:** For assets, choose between "Inventory" or "Investment" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:42">[00:02:42]</a>.

## Transactions Window

The "Transactions" window allows you to enter all business-related transaction details <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:37">[00:00:37]</a>. This functions as a central hub for [[tracking_income_and_expenses_using_notion | tracking income and expenses]] and other financial movements.

### Adding Transactions
You can add specific transaction types by clicking dedicated buttons:
*   **Add Income** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:09">[00:03:09]</a>
*   **Add Expense** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:06">[00:03:06]</a>
*   **Add Asset** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:07">[00:03:07]</a>
*   **Add Liability** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:08">[00:03:08]</a>

Clicking a button, such as "Add Income," automatically populates the current date <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:13">[00:03:13]</a>.

#### Transaction Fields
Each transaction entry includes several fields:
*   **Date:** Automatically set to the current date upon adding a transaction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:16">[00:03:16]</a>.
*   **Period:** Select or add the relevant month and year (e.g., "October 2024") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:24">[00:03:24]</a>.
*   **Description:** A text field to describe the transaction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:42">[00:03:42]</a>.
*   **Category:** Automatically mapped based on the type of transaction (e.g., "income" for added income) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:45">[00:03:45]</a>.
*   **Account:** Select an account from those set up in the "Start Here" section <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:49">[00:03:49]</a>. Accounts added here will also reflect in the "Start Here" section <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:05">[00:04:05]</a>.
*   **Payment Mode:** Specify how payment was received or made (e.g., bank account, cash account) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:21">[00:04:21]</a>. Additional payment modes can be added <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:28">[00:04:28]</a>.
*   **Transaction Type:** Classify as "Inflow" (money coming into the account) or "Outflow" (money leaving the account) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:51">[00:04:51]</a>.
*   **Amount:** Enter the monetary value of the transaction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:07">[00:05:07]</a>.
*   **Document:** Attach any relevant documents <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:15">[00:05:15]</a>.
*   **Tax Rate and Tax Amount:** Enter a tax rate, and the tax amount will be automatically calculated. Set to 0% for non-taxable transactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:21">[00:05:21]</a>.
*   **Multiplier and Currency:** Use the multiplier for multi-currency transactions (e.g., converting USD to Euro) to reflect the final currency value <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:36">[00:05:36]</a>.

### Investment and Inventory Tracking
The template also supports tracking investments and inventory:
*   **Investments:** Monitor units, price, total value, and investment value. Track profit or loss when selling investments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:07">[00:06:07]</a>.
*   **Inventory:** View details of current inventory and track profit or loss obtained from specific inventory items <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:26">[00:06:26]</a>.

## Summary Section

Once transactions are entered, the "Summary" section automatically updates to provide an overview of your business finances <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:49">[00:06:49]</a>.

### Finances
This section gives an at-a-glance view of your business's financial health, including:
*   Total assets and their breakdown (e.g., accounts receivable, input taxes, other receivables, cash value) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:54">[00:06:54]</a>.
*   Income sources <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:01">[00:07:01]</a>.
*   Liabilities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:02">[00:07:02]</a>.
*   Expenses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:03">[00:07:03]</a>.

### Debt Section
Track debt payments, showing both past payments and upcoming payments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:23">[00:07:23]</a>. Entries from the liability side will populate this section <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:30">[00:07:30]</a>.

### Budgeting Section
This section displays [[using_a_business_budget_template_in_notion | budgeted expenses]] and income, showing how much budget was set, and the total percentage of expenses incurred so far relative to the budget <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:03">[00:08:03]</a>.

### Savings Section
View the amount of savings accumulated for planned funds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:16">[00:08:16]</a>.

### Bills and Subscriptions
These sections track past payments for bills <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:22">[00:08:22]</a> and provide a similar summary for subscriptions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:31">[00:08:31]</a>.

### Tax Section
Provides a quarterly overview of taxes payable, available tax credits, and overall tax liability, all dependent on income and expense entries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:36">[00:08:36]</a>.

### Net Worth
*   **Net Worth Goal:** Set a financial goal at the top of the template <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:53">[00:08:53]</a>.
*   **Total Net Worth:** Calculated from all assets, liabilities, and transactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:08">[00:09:08]</a>.
*   **Difference:** Shows the difference between your goal and total net worth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:14">[00:09:14]</a>.
*   **Total Growth:** Reflects the growth in net worth from the start of the financial year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:19">[00:09:19]</a>.
*   **Percentage of Net Worth Achieved:** Indicates how much of your total net worth goal has been achieved <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:26">[00:09:26]</a>.

### Investment and Inventory Summaries
*   **Investment Summary:** Shows the value and units of current investments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:35">[00:09:35]</a>.
*   **Inventory Summary:** Provides a similar status for inventory <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:42">[00:09:42]</a>.

### Overall Summary
A consolidated view of total income, total expenses, gross profit, taxes paid, and the net profit of the business <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:47">[00:09:47]</a>.

## Utilizing the Template

To effectively use this [[using_templates_in_notion_for_business | Notion template]]:
1.  Begin by setting up different accounts under the respective heads in the "Accounts" section <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:59">[00:09:59]</a>.
2.  Add opening balances for relevant assets and liabilities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:04">[00:10:04]</a>.
3.  Enter details for every business transaction in the "Transactions" window <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:11">[00:10:11]</a>.
This process will automatically update the "Summary" section, providing an immediate overview of your business finances <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:15">[00:10:15]</a>.

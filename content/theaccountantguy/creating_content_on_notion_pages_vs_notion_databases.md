Wien is a platform designed to help users [[ai_content_generation_for_notion | create AI-generated content]] directly within a Notion workspace <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:04">[00:00:04]</a>. It offers two main options for content generation: Notion pages and [[creating_a_database_in_notion | Notion databases]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:10">[00:00:10]</a>.

## Notion Pages

A Notion page is a fundamental, simple, blank page within Notion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:13">[00:00:13]</a>. When [[generating_ai_content_on_notion_pages_and_databases | creating AI content]] for a Notion page using Wien, the process involves:

1.  **Establishing a Connection**: Provide a name for the connection (e.g., "notion page") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:32">[00:00:32]</a>.
2.  **Selecting Content Type**: Choose "Notion page" as the content destination <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:36">[00:00:36]</a>.
3.  **Providing the URL**: Copy and paste the URL of the specific Notion page where content is to be generated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:41">[00:00:41]</a>.
4.  **Inputting the Prompt**: Enter a specific request for the AI, such as "write me a block of 100 words on Notion" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:48">[00:00:48]</a>.

Once the prompt is given, the [[ai_content_generation_for_notion | AI-generated content]] populates directly onto the selected Notion page <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:04">[00:01:04]</a>.

## Notion Databases

In contrast to a blank page, a [[creating_a_database_in_notion | Notion database]] is structured in the form of rows and columns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:17">[00:00:17]</a>. Before generation, these databases might be empty <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:26">[00:00:26]</a>. [[generating_ai_content_on_notion_pages_and_databases | Generating AI content]] for a [[creating_a_database_in_notion | Notion database]] follows a similar initial setup:

1.  **Establishing a Connection**: Name the connection (e.g., "notion database") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:21">[00:01:21]</a>.
2.  **Selecting Content Type**: Choose "Notion database" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:25">[00:01:25]</a>.
3.  **Providing the URL**: Copy and paste the URL of the specific Notion database <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:27">[00:01:27]</a>.
4.  **Inputting the Prompt**: The key difference here is that content can be generated for *each topic or entry* within the database. For example, if a database has multiple topics listed in its rows, the AI can be prompted to "write me a block of 100 words on the topic" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:38">[00:01:38]</a>.

The AI will then generate content for each individual row or topic within the [[creating_a_database_in_notion | database]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:08">[00:02:08]</a>. As the AI processes, the content will start populating in the respective sections and rows of the [[creating_a_database_in_notion | database]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:59">[00:01:59]</a>. This allows for automated content creation across multiple entries in a structured format <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:17">[00:02:17]</a>.

Utopia, a concept often discussed in philosophical and ethical debates, is defined as a "profoundly better future." In a conversation with Joe Carl Smith, a senior research analyst at Open Philanthropy and doctoral student in philosophy, various dimensions of utopian thinking and its significance were explored.

## Defining Utopia

Utopia isn't just an idealistic dream; it represents a future we can potentially create. Joe Carl Smith asserts that a utopian world is not just a fictional narrative but an attainable goal if approached correctly. He notes that crafting such a world involves envisioning a society that is "radically better than the world we live in today" [<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:10">00:00:10</a>].

## The Importance of Utopian Thinking

Utopian thinking is vital because it encourages us to consider substantial improvements over the status quo. Smith highlights our tendency to imagine Utopias that do not stray far enough from our current societal configurations, thereby missing an opportunity to envision significant change [<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:45">00:10:45</a>]. By envisaging a future that is fundamentally different and better, society can aim for transformations akin to "living in a cave" versus "living under the open sky" [<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:30">00:11:30</a>]. Such thinking aligns with the need to recognize the [[potential_societal_impacts_of_advanced_ai | potential societal impacts of advanced technologies]].

## The Challenge of Conceptualizing Utopia

While the notion of Utopia is compelling, conceptualizing it in a compelling way is challenging. Smith observes that potential futures often retain too many patterns from the present, leading to less imaginative scenarios that fail to capture the breadth of possibilities [<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:53">00:10:53</a>]. The task lies in imagining changes that seem unbelievable today but are necessary for significant progress, which also echoes considerations in [[challenges_and_considerations_for_achieving_agi | achieving artificial general intelligence]].

## The Process Toward Utopia

According to Joe Carl Smith, progressing toward a Utopian state involves a gradual journey of societal improvement. This involves becoming wiser and more capable as a species, a process that entails exploring new capacities and understanding [<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:40">00:13:40</a>]. Smith emphasizes the need for patience and an openness to philosophical growth, which can guide humanity to a more desirable future, highlighting the importance of [[ai_ethics_and_deployment_strategies | AI ethics and deployment strategies]] in ensuring this desirable progress.

## Risks of Utopian Ideologies

However, the pursuit of Utopia is not without risks. Smith acknowledges that some of history's worst ideologies have been driven by utopian thinking [<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:55">00:15:55</a>]. While effective altruism and utilitarianism can be compatible with Utopian ideals, it is crucial to approach these movements with caution to avoid potential negative outcomes.

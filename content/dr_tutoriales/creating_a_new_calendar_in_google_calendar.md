This guide explains how to create and manage a new calendar within [[how_to_use_google_calendar | Google Calendar]], including customization, sharing options, and event creation.

## Accessing Google Calendar
To begin, open a new tab in your web browser and navigate to the Google applications icon (the small squares) located in the upper right corner <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:20">[00:00:20]</a>. From there, select "Calendar" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:26">[00:00:26]</a>.

### Calendar View Options
[[how_to_use_google_calendar | Google Calendar]] allows you to adjust the view based on day, week, month, or year using options on the right side of the screen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:32">[00:00:32]</a>. Viewing by month is often recommended for better visualization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:38">[00:00:38]</a>.

You can also choose to hide or show pre-existing calendars, such as public holidays, if your calendar appears too cluttered <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:50">[00:00:50]</a>.

## Creating a New Calendar
To [[managing_events_and_permissions_in_google_calendar | create a new calendar]]:
1.  Navigate to "Other calendars" on the left-hand side <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:06">[00:01:06]</a>.
2.  Click the "plus" button <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:10">[00:01:10]</a>.
3.  Select "Create a calendar" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:13">[00:01:13]</a>.
4.  Provide a name for your new calendar, for example, "Video YouTube Tutorials" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:18">[00:01:18]</a>.
5.  Add a description, such as "Best YouTube tutorials" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:27">[00:01:27]</a>.
6.  The owner's email will be automatically displayed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:32">[00:01:32]</a>.
7.  Click "Create it" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:36">[00:01:36]</a>. The calendar will take a few seconds to synchronize <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:39">[00:01:39]</a>.

Once created, the new calendar will appear on the left side of your main [[how_to_use_google_calendar | Google Calendar]] screen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:00">[00:02:00]</a>.

## Configuring and Sharing the Calendar
You can configure your new calendar by clicking the three dots next to its name <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:06">[00:02:06]</a>.

### Customizing Color
You can change the calendar's display color by clicking the three dots and selecting a new color, such as red <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:10">[00:02:10]</a>.

### [[sharing_calendars_with_others | Sharing Calendars with Others]]
To [[sharing_calendars_with_others | share your new calendar]], click the three dots and select "Configure and share" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:29">[00:02:29]</a>.

*   **Public Sharing:** To make the calendar accessible to everyone, enable the "Share publicly" option <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:48">[00:02:48]</a>. This allows users with Yahoo, Photomail, and other accounts to view it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:50">[00:03:50]</a>.
*   **Sharing with Specific People:** To share with selected individuals:
    1.  Go to "Share with certain people" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:59">[00:02:59]</a>.
    2.  Click "Add people" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:01">[00:03:01]</a>.
    3.  Enter the email address of the person you wish to invite <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:01">[00:03:01]</a>.
    4.  Set their permissions, such as viewing all events, making changes, or managing sharing as an administrator <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:05">[00:03:05]</a>.
    5.  Click "Send" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:19">[00:03:19]</a>. The person will receive an invitation to access the calendar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:22">[00:03:22]</a>.
*   **Sharing with Non-Gmail Accounts:** You have two options for sharing with individuals who do not have a Gmail account:
    1.  Share the calendar publicly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:48">[00:03:48]</a>.
    2.  Suggest they [[how_to_use_google_calendar | create a Gmail account]], which is a simple process taking less than five minutes, allowing for private sharing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:54">[00:03:54]</a>.

### [[configuring_calendar_notifications | Configuring Calendar Notifications]]
From the "Configure and share" settings, you can also [[configuring_calendar_notifications | add notifications]] to the calendar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:11">[00:04:11]</a>. This allows the calendar to notify you of events, with customizable advance timings (e.g., ten minutes, ten hours, four hours) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:14">[00:04:14]</a>. You can also configure notifications for canceled events <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:24">[00:04:24]</a>.

## Adding an Event to Your New Calendar
To introduce an event into your new calendar, for example, on a specific date like April 23rd:
1.  Click on the desired date in the calendar view <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:37">[00:04:37]</a>.
2.  Click "Add event" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:50">[00:04:50]</a>.
3.  Enter the event title, such as "Video calendar tutorial" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:53">[00:04:53]</a>.
4.  You can optionally add guests, a location, and a description (e.g., "Good tutorial") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:01">[00:05:01]</a>.
5.  Ensure the event is assigned to your newly created calendar (e.g., "YouTube video tutorials") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:20">[00:05:20]</a>.
6.  Click "Save" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:22">[00:05:22]</a>.

Once an event is added and the calendar is shared, notifications can be sent via email or mobile device, depending on your [[configuring_calendar_notifications | notification settings]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:32">[00:05:32]</a>.

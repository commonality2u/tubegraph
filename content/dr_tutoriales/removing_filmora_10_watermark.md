## The Watermark Problem
When [[video_editing_using_filmora_10 | Filmora 10]] is used for editing videos, exporting the final product directly will often result in a watermark being present on the saved video <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:16">[00:01:16]</a>. This watermark can be bothersome <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:40">[00:00:40]</a>.

## Solution: Using OBS Studio
The primary method to avoid the [[video_editing_using_filmora_10 | Filmora 10]] watermark is to use a screen recording software, specifically OBS Studio <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:26">[00:01:26]</a>. OBS Studio is capable of recording the computer screen, including both video and sound <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:31">[00:01:31]</a>. By recording the playback of the edited video within [[video_editing_using_filmora_10 | Filmora 10]], you can save the video file without the watermark <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:36">[00:01:36]</a>.

### Step-by-Step Process
1.  **Prepare the Video in Filmora 10**: Ensure your video is fully edited in [[video_editing_using_filmora_10 | Filmora 10]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:02">[00:01:02]</a>.
    *   An example video used for demonstration purposes in the tutorial involved disabling Airpod notifications <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:56">[00:00:56]</a>.
    *   The preview of the video will appear in the upper section of the [[video_editing_using_filmora_10 | Filmora 10]] interface <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:02">[00:01:02]</a>.
2.  **Download and Open OBS Studio**: Obtain and launch the OBS Studio program <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:26">[00:01:26]</a>.
3.  **Configure OBS Studio for Screen Recording**:
    *   Set up OBS Studio to record the specific screen where [[video_editing_using_filmora_10 | Filmora 10]] is displayed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:22">[00:02:22]</a>.
    *   Maximize the [[video_editing_using_filmora_10 | Filmora 10]] window on the screen you intend to record to ensure it fills the capture area <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:30">[00:02:30]</a>.
4.  **Start Recording in OBS Studio**: Initiate the recording process in OBS Studio <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:45">[00:02:45]</a>.
5.  **Play the Video in Filmora 10**: Play your edited video from the beginning within the [[video_editing_using_filmora_10 | Filmora 10]] interface <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:52">[00:02:52]</a>. OBS Studio will record the playback, including the sound <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:11">[00:03:11]</a>.
6.  **Stop Recording**: Once the video has finished playing, stop the recording in OBS Studio. The recorded file will be saved without the [[video_editing_using_filmora_10 | Filmora 10]] watermark <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:14">[00:03:14]</a>.

This method allows users to obtain a clean video file, effectively [[exporting_videos_without_watermark | exporting videos without the Filmora 10 watermark]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:14">[00:03:14]</a>.

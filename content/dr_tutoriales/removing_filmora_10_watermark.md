## Method: Using OBS Studio for Screen Recording

The primary strategy to bypass the watermark is to use a separate [[screen_recording_techniques | screen recording]] program like OBS Studio <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:26">[00:01:26]</a>. This method involves recording the [[filmora_10_video_editing_tips | Filmora 10]] preview window while the video plays, effectively capturing the final product without the watermark <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:36">[00:01:36]</a>.

1.  **Prepare Video in Filmora 10**:
    *   Import the video you wish to edit into [[filmora_10_video_editing_tips | Filmora 10]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:49">[00:00:49]</a>. For example, a tutorial on disabling Airpod notifications <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:56">[00:00:56]</a>.

    *   Ensure the video preview is visible and ready for playback <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:02">[00:01:02]</a>.

2.  **Set Up OBS Studio**:
    *   Download and open OBS Studio <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:26">[00:01:26]</a>. OBS Studio is capable of [[screen_recording_techniques | recording the screen]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:31">[00:01:31]</a>.
    *   Minimize the OBS Studio window or move it to a second screen if available <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:57">[00:01:57]</a>.
    *   Maximize the [[filmora_10_video_editing_tips | Filmora 10]] preview window to fill the screen you intend to record <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:30">[00:02:30]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:34">[00:02:34]</a>.

3.  **Record the Video**:
    *   In OBS Studio, select the screen where [[filmora_10_video_editing_tips | Filmora 10]] is displayed as your recording source <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:22">[00:02:22]</a>.
    *   Start the recording in OBS Studio <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:45">[00:02:45]</a>.
    *   Immediately play the edited video within [[filmora_10_video_editing_tips | Filmora 10]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:54">[00:02:54]</a>.
    *   OBS Studio will capture both the video playback and its associated audio <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:11">[00:03:11]</a>.

This process allows you to save the video as a new file, essentially by [[screen_recording_techniques | recording]] [[filmora_10_video_editing_tips | Filmora 10]]'s output, rather than using its built-in export function, thereby achieving [[exporting_videos_without_watermarks | export without the watermark]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:39">[00:01:39]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:14">[00:03:14]</a>.

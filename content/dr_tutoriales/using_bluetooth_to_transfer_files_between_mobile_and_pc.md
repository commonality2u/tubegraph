This guide outlines the steps to activate Bluetooth on a Windows 10 computer and connect a mobile device for file transfer, such as sending a video from a mobile phone to a computer via Bluetooth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:17">[00:00:17]</a>.

## Activating Bluetooth in Windows 10

Before initiating a file transfer, ensure Bluetooth is properly configured and running on your Windows 10 PC.

### [[configuring_bluetooth_services_on_a_computer | Configuring Bluetooth Services]]

1.  Go to the Home bar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:45">[00:00:45]</a>.
2.  Search for "services" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:51">[00:00:51]</a>.
3.  Open the Services window <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>.
4.  Within the list of services, locate "Bluetooth compatibility service" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:08">[00:01:08]</a>, also known as "Bluetooth Support Service" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:38">[00:01:38]</a>.
5.  Right-click on "Bluetooth compatibility service" and select "Properties" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:40">[00:01:40]</a>.
6.  In the Properties window, set the "Startup type" to "Automatic" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:47">[00:01:47]</a>.
7.  Click "Apply" and then "Accept" to save the changes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:52">[00:01:52]</a>.
8.  Right-click on "Bluetooth compatibility service" again and select "Restart" to apply the implemented changes to the computer's Bluetooth system <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:00">[00:02:00]</a>.
9.  Wait for the service to restart <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:16">[00:02:16]</a>, then close the Services window <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:19">[00:02:19]</a>.

### Enabling Bluetooth Visibility

1.  Go back to the taskbar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:24">[00:02:24]</a>.
2.  Search for "Bluetooth" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:29">[00:02:29]</a>.
3.  Open "Bluetooth settings and other devices" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:33">[00:02:33]</a>.
4.  In the "Bluetooth & other devices" window, go to "More Bluetooth options" on the right side <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:46">[00:02:46]</a>.
5.  Ensure the "Show Bluetooth icon in the notification area" checkbox is selected <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:53">[00:02:53]</a>. This will make the Bluetooth symbol visible on your taskbar, indicating that Bluetooth is enabled <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:08">[00:03:08]</a>.

## Connecting a Mobile Device

Once Bluetooth is activated and visible on your computer, you can connect your mobile device.

### [[demonstrating_bluetooth_file_transfer | Demonstrating Bluetooth File Transfer]]

1.  Navigate back to "Bluetooth and other Devices" settings on your computer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:27">[00:03:27]</a>.
2.  Click on "Add Bluetooth or other devices" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:34">[00:03:34]</a>.
3.  Select "Bluetooth" as the type of device to add <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:38">[00:03:38]</a>.
4.  Your computer will search for nearby Bluetooth devices. When your mobile device appears (e.g., iPhone) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:44">[00:03:44]</a>, click on it to begin the connection process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:48">[00:03:48]</a>.
5.  On your mobile device, accept the connection request from the computer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:51">[00:03:51]</a>.
6.  Once linked, the device will be ready to communicate and transmit files between the computer and mobile device <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:01">[00:04:01]</a>.

> [!tip] Prepare Your Files
> If you plan to transfer large files like videos, consider compressing them beforehand to reduce file size and transfer time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:27">[00:00:27]</a>.

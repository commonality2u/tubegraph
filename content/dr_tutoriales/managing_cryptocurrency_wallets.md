This article outlines the process of [[sending_receiving_and_swapping_cryptocurrencies | sending and receiving cryptocurrencies]] between different platforms, specifically transferring Bitcoin from a Coinbase account to a Binance account.

## Why Transfer Cryptocurrencies?

Binance is highlighted as a potentially more effective broker for [[managing_cryptocurrency_to_maximize_profits | managing cryptocurrency to maximize profits]] compared to platforms like Coinbase because it allows for reinvestment in various ways [00:00:12]. Transferring cryptocurrencies from Coinbase to Binance is recommended to [[managing_cryptocurrency_to_maximize_profits | obtain profitability]] [00:00:25].

## Prerequisites

*   An account on Coinbase with cryptocurrency balance [00:01:08].
*   An account on Binance [00:01:16]. If you don't have a Coinbase account, you can create one using a provided code for a 10% discount on commissions [00:00:46].

## Transferring Bitcoin from Coinbase to Binance

The general process involves sending cryptocurrencies from Coinbase to a specific address obtained from the Binance wallet [00:01:27].

### Step 1: Initiating the Send from Coinbase

1.  Access your Coinbase account [00:01:08].
2.  Select the "Send and Receive" option [00:01:28]. This indicates you are sending your cryptocurrencies to another address [00:01:31].
3.  You will need to input the address of your Binance account [00:01:42].

### Step 2: Obtaining the Deposit Address from Binance

1.  Access your Binance account [00:01:16].
2.  Navigate to "Wallet" and then "Overview" [00:01:50].
3.  Select the "Deposit" option [00:01:55].
4.  Choose "Deposit Crypto" [00:02:01]. This option allows you to receive cryptocurrencies from an external wallet using an address or QR code [00:02:04].
5.  Select the cryptocurrency you intend to deposit; in this case, Bitcoin [00:02:26]. The deposit address and QR code will change based on the selected cryptocurrency [00:02:37].
6.  Pay close attention to both the QR code and the deposit address displayed [00:02:40].

### Step 3: Performing the Transfer

Transfers can be done using either a mobile phone or a computer [00:02:50]. This process falls under [[mobile_and_computerbased_cryptocurrency_transactions | mobile and computer-based cryptocurrency transactions]].

#### Using a Mobile Phone (Coinbase App)

1.  Open the Coinbase application [00:03:08].
2.  Go to the specific cryptocurrency wallet you want to transfer (e.g., Bitcoin wallet) [00:03:14].
3.  Access the "Wallet" section for that cryptocurrency [00:03:20].
4.  Look for the QR code symbol (usually in the top right) [00:03:39].
5.  Grant camera access to scan the QR code displayed on your computer from Binance [00:03:44].
6.  To send, use the "paper plane" or "arrow" icon [00:04:10].
7.  Enter the amount of Bitcoin you wish to send [00:04:15].
8.  Confirm the transaction [00:04:19].

#### Using a Computer

1.  Copy the Binance deposit address [00:03:36].
2.  Return to your Coinbase account [00:04:42].
3.  Specify the amount of Bitcoin you wish to [[transferring_and_managing_cryptocurrency_assets | transfer from Coinbase]] to Binance, or choose "Send All" [00:04:44].
4.  Paste the copied Binance deposit address into the recipient field [00:04:52]. This is the address of your Binance wallet to receive Bitcoin [00:04:55].
5.  Add a note to the transaction (optional) [00:04:59].
6.  Click "Continue" [00:05:05].
7.  Review the transaction details, including the commission fee (e.g., less than one euro for a ~109 euro transfer) [00:05:11].
8.  Click "Send Now" [00:05:27].
9.  Enter the verification code received on your mobile phone [00:05:30].

### Step 4: Confirming the Transfer

*   After confirmation, the balance will disappear from your Coinbase wallet [00:05:38].
*   The transaction may take approximately 10 minutes to process [00:05:42].
*   You will receive a confirmation message on your mobile phone [00:06:07].
*   Check your Binance transaction history for the deposit [00:06:11].
*   Your updated balance will reflect the transferred amount in Binance [00:06:25].
*   It is advisable to check your email for a transaction confirmation from Coinbase for record-keeping and as potential collateral in rare cases of issues [00:06:43].

Google Forms provides various templates to help users quickly [[creating_a_form_with_google_forms | create forms]] for different purposes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>.

## Accessing Templates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:36">[00:00:36]</a>

After accessing Google Forms, users are presented with a selection of pre-designed templates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:36">[00:00:36]</a>. To find Google Forms, navigate to the Google applications menu (typically on the right side of the screen) and look for the purple "forms" logo <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:24">[00:00:24]</a>.

## Available Templates

Common templates available in Google Forms include <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:36">[00:00:36]</a>:
*   Contact data forms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:39">[00:00:39]</a>
*   Confirmation of attendance forms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:40">[00:00:40]</a>
*   Party invitation forms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:41">[00:00:41]</a>
*   Order request forms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:41">[00:00:41]</a>

## Starting with a Blank Form

Beyond using templates, users also have the option to start a form from scratch by selecting the "blank" option <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:43">[00:00:43]</a>. This allows for complete customization from the ground up <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:45">[00:00:45]</a>. For example, a new blank form could be titled "Doubt Form" with a description like "This form is used to transmit your doubts to me and see if we can solve them" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:51">[00:00:51]</a>.

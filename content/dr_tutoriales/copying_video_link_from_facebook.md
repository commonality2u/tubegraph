This article explains how to copy the original video link from Facebook, a necessary step for [[how_to_download_facebook_videos_on_pc | downloading Facebook videos]] to your computer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:06">[00:00:06]</a>. The process is straightforward and easy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:09">[00:00:09]</a>.

## Prerequisites
To begin, you need to have a video available on your Facebook main page <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:23">[00:00:23]</a>.

## Steps to Obtain the Video Link

### 1. Access Facebook's Mobile Version on PC
Once on the Facebook page with the desired video <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:26">[00:00:26]</a>, you need to convert the page to its mobile version <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:42">[00:00:42]</a>.

*   Go to the URL in your browser's address bar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:34">[00:00:34]</a>.
*   Remove the "WWW" part and the "HTTP" part from the URL <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:39">[00:00:39]</a>.
*   Replace them with an "M" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:42">[00:00:42]</a>.
*   Press Enter to load the [[viewing_facebooks_mobile_version_on_computer | mobile version of Facebook]] within your computer's browser <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:48">[00:00:48]</a>.

### 2. Inspect the Video Element
Once the mobile version of the page is loaded <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:51">[00:00:51]</a>:

*   Go to a blank space on the video <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:54">[00:00:54]</a>.
*   Right-click with your mouse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:54">[00:00:54]</a>.
*   Select "Inspect" from the context menu <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:57">[00:00:57]</a>.
    *   This will open a window on the right side of your screen showing the code used to build the Facebook page <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:03">[00:01:03]</a>.

### 3. Play the Video Briefly
Before proceeding, reproduce the video for two or three seconds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:14">[00:01:14]</a>.

### 4. Locate and Copy the Video Source Link
After playing the video for a moment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:23">[00:01:23]</a>:

*   Click on the "Inspect video" button (often represented by a mouse pointer icon in the inspector window) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:31">[00:01:31]</a>.
*   Hover your mouse over the video in the main browser window; a corresponding section will highlight in the inspector window <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:42">[00:01:42]</a>.
*   Identify the line in the inspector that points to the video's origin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:46">[00:01:46]</a>.
*   Double-click on this line <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:54">[00:01:54]</a>.
*   Copy the URL starting from "HDTP" (HTTP or HTTPS) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:00">[00:02:00]</a>.

### 5. Open the Link in a New Tab
*   Open a new tab in your browser <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:05">[00:02:05]</a>.
*   Paste the copied URL into the address bar and press Enter <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:05">[00:02:05]</a>.
*   This will open the video in its original version directly from Facebook's root <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:10">[00:02:10]</a>.

## Downloading the Video
From this new tab, [[how_to_download_facebook_videos_on_pc | downloading the video]] is very simple <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:14">[00:02:14]</a>:

*   Click on the three-dot menu icon within the video player <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:17">[00:02:17]</a>.
*   Select "Download" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:20">[00:02:20]</a>.

Within [[how_to_use_google_calendar | Google Calendar]], users can [[customizing_notification_preferences | configure notifications]] to receive alerts for events. This feature is particularly useful for staying organized and being reminded of upcoming schedules <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:32:00">[04:32:00]</a>.

## Accessing Notification Settings
After [[creating_a_new_calendar_in_google_calendar | creating]] or selecting a calendar, you can access its settings to [[managing_events_and_permissions_in_google_calendar | configure and share]] it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:29:00">[02:29:00]</a>. These settings allow you to add and customize notifications <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:11:00">[04:11:00]</a>.

## Types of Notifications
You can add notifications to alert you about calendar events <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:14:00">[04:14:00]</a>. Options include setting reminders for:
*   Upcoming events <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:24:00">[04:24:00]</a>
*   Canceled events <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:28:00">[04:28:00]</a>

## Customizing Notification Timings
Notifications can be set to alert you at various intervals before an event <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:18:00">[04:18:00]</a>. For instance, you can configure them to notify you:
*   Ten minutes in advance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:18:00">[04:18:00]</a>
*   Ten hours in advance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:21:00">[04:21:00]</a>
*   Four hours in advance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:21:00">[04:21:00]</a>
The timing can be customized to your preference <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:21:00">[04:21:00]</a>.

## Receiving Notifications
When an event is approaching, a notification can be sent to both your email and mobile device, depending on how you've [[customizing_notification_preferences | configured your preferences]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:35:00">[05:35:00]</a>. These alerts serve to remind you of the scheduled event <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:41:00">[05:41:00]</a>.

This article compares fiat deposit and withdrawal methods and their associated fees across Coinbase, Coinbase Pro, and Binance platforms, assessing which is most beneficial for users interested in [[fiat_and_cryptocurrency_deposit_options | fiat and cryptocurrency deposit options]].

## Account Opening and Comparison Overview

Opening an account on Coinbase, Coinbase Pro, or Binance is free, meaning none of the platforms charge a fee for maintaining an account <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:26:26">[01:26:26]</a>. The comparison focuses on deposit, trading, staking, and withdrawal fees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:34:34">[01:34:34]</a>.

## Fiat Deposit Methods

Comparing fiat deposit methods is crucial for minimizing costs when [[how_to_deposit_money_into_binance | depositing money into Binance]] or other platforms.

### Coinbase Deposit Fees

Coinbase charges a commission of 1% to 4% for deposits, with the exact percentage varying based on factors like the region of transfer (e.g., 3.99% for Chile) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:11:11">[02:11:11]</a>. These values are considered quite high <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:17:17">[02:17:17]</a>.

### Coinbase Pro Deposit Fees

Coinbase Pro offers significantly lower deposit fees. For instance, a SEPA transfer incurs a fee of 0.15 euros <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:46:46">[02:46:46]</a>. This represents a substantial difference compared to Coinbase's fees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:49:49">[02:49:49]</a>.

### Binance Deposit Fees

Binance offers varied options for [[depositing_fiat_and_cryptocurrencies_on_binance | depositing fiat and cryptocurrencies on Binance]]. The platform typically recommends fiat deposits via bank transfer over card payments to avoid high commissions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:28:28">[03:28:28]</a>.

Available fiat deposit methods and their fees on Binance include:
*   **Bank transfer (SEPA)**: 0% commission for euros <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:02:02">[04:02:02]</a>.
*   **VISA/Mastercard**: 1.8% commission <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:59:59">[03:59:59]</a>.
*   **iDeal**: 1.3 euros commission <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:05:05">[04:05:05]</a>.
*   **Quick payments (e.g., for Pound Sterling)**: 0% commission <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:29:29">[04:29:29]</a>.
*   **Swift transfers (for USD)**: 0% commission <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:48:48">[04:48:48]</a>.

It is generally advised to use bank transfers (like SEPA or SWIFT) for fiat deposits on Binance, as credit/debit card payments incur an 1.8% fee <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:07:07">[05:07:07]</a>. Users can potentially save on bank transfer commissions by using services like N26 for transfers to Binance, as they often don't charge international transfer fees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:29:29">[05:29:29]</a>. Using a referral link when opening a Binance account can also lead to [[methods_for_reducing_deposit_fees_on_binance | methods for reducing deposit fees on Binance]] by up to 10% on commissions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:03:03">[06:03:03]</a>.

### Deposit Conclusion

Binance is highlighted as the winner for deposits due to its zero-commission options for bank transfers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:06:06">[05:06:06]</a>.

## Fiat Withdrawal Methods

This section details [[different_methods_of_withdrawal_and_their_fees | different methods of withdrawal and their fees]] for fiat currency from the platforms.

### Coinbase Withdrawal Fees

Coinbase charges up to 4% or a maximum of 10 euros per withdrawal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:23:23">[12:23:23]</a>.

### Coinbase Pro Withdrawal Fees

Coinbase Pro generally offers lower withdrawal fees compared to Coinbase. For instance:
*   **Wire transfer (for Pound Sterling)**: Cheaper than Binance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:46:46">[13:46:46]</a>.
*   **SEPA transfer**: Cheaper than Binance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:56:56">[13:56:56]</a>.
*   **SWIFT transfer**: $25, which is less than Binance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:01:01">[14:01:01]</a>.

In terms of fiat withdrawal commissions, Coinbase Pro is considered superior <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:09:09">[14:09:09]</a>.

### Binance Withdrawal Fees

When [[withdrawing_fiat_and_cryptocurrencies_from_binance | withdrawing fiat and cryptocurrencies from Binance]], options include:
*   **SEPA transfer (for euros)**: 0.8 euros commission <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:48:48">[12:48:48]</a>.
*   **Bank card withdrawal**: 1% commission <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:53:53">[12:53:53]</a>.
*   **Quick payments (for Pound Sterling)**: 1.5 pounds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:04:04">[13:04:04]</a>.
*   **SWIFT transfer (for dollars)**: Up to $30 commission <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:11:11">[13:11:11]</a>.

For daily payments, it is recommended to use the Binance card to avoid withdrawing fiat currency and incurring fees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:09:09">[12:09:09]</a>. The Binance card allows payments in virtual and physical businesses and offers cashback <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:03:03">[12:03:03]</a>. This is an [[using_alternative_platforms_for_crypto_to_fiat_withdrawal | using alternative platforms for crypto to fiat withdrawal | alternative platform to fiat withdrawal]].

### Withdrawal Conclusion

Coinbase Pro generally wins for fiat withdrawals due to lower fees compared to Binance and Coinbase <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:09:09">[14:09:09]</a>. Binance's withdrawal fees are considered relatively high <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:28:28">[14:28:28]</a>.

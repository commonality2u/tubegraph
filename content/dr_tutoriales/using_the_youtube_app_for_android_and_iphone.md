This tutorial outlines how to [[creating_posts_on_youtube_using_mobile | create posts on YouTube]] directly from a mobile device, applicable for both Android and iPhone users <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:08">[00:00:08]</a>.

## Getting Started

### Downloading the YouTube App
Before creating posts, ensure the YouTube app is downloaded on your mobile device <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:29">[00:00:29]</a>.
*   **For iPhone users:** Download the app from the App Store <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:34">[00:00:34]</a>.
*   **For Android users:** Download the app from Google Play <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:37">[00:00:37]</a>.

### Registering and Logging In
Once the app is downloaded, the first step is to enter it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:47">[00:00:47]</a>. After entering, you must [[registering_and_logging_into_youtube_account | register with your official YouTube account]] from which you wish to publish <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:50">[00:00:50]</a>.

## Creating a Post

To make a new post, follow these steps:
1.  **Access the Post Option:** Navigate to the video camera icon located at the top right of the screen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:05">[00:01:05]</a>.
2.  **Select "Publication":** From the options presented (make a video, go live, or a publication), choose "publication" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:10">[00:01:10]</a>.
3.  **Compose Your Post:** You can now create your publication <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:17">[00:01:17]</a>.
    *   **Add Text:** Type your desired message <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:26">[00:01:26]</a>. For example, "Hello, how are you? Have a nice day" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:29">[00:01:29]</a>.
    *   **Include Media (Optional):** The platform also allows you to add photos or polls from your mobile phone <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:41">[00:01:41]</a>.
4.  **Publish the Post:** Once your post is ready, tap "publish" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:37">[00:01:37]</a>.

After publishing, the post will be created <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:54">[00:01:54]</a> and will be visible to your followers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:02">[00:02:02]</a>.

To determine the location where photos were taken on a mobile phone, a free application can be downloaded <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:08">[00:00:08]</a>.

## Recommended Application: Exif

One such application, available on the Apple Store, is called Exif <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:26">[00:00:26]</a>. It is described as an orange app for photos and videos, with 153 reviews and a 4.5-star rating <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:38">[00:00:38]</a>. The application allows users to [[using_apps_to_access_metadata_and_geolocation | access photo metadata]] and [[viewing_and_editing_geolocation_and_metadata | geolocation information]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:26">[00:01:26]</a>.

## Free vs. Pro Options

The Exif application offers both a free option and a Pro option <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:52">[00:00:52]</a>. For the purpose of [[how_to_determine_photo_location_on_mobile_devices | determining photo location]], the free option is described as "more than enough" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:54">[00:00:54]</a>.

### Features Available in the Free Option
The free option of the Exif app allows users to:
*   Access photos on their device <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>.
*   View details such as image size, camera type (e.g., iPhone 11), lens, and coordinates (longitude and latitude) based on geolocation data <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:59">[00:01:59]</a>.
*   See the exact location on a map if geolocation is enabled for the photo <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:22">[00:02:22]</a>.
*   Identify if a photo does not have location data enabled <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:42">[00:02:42]</a>.
*   Upload photos within the app for analysis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:36">[00:03:36]</a>.

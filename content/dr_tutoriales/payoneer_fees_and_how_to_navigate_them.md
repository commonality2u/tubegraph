## [[different_types_of_payoneer_accounts_and_their_uses | Types of Payoneer Accounts]] and Transfer Rules
Payoneer offers two primary account types:
*   **Individual (or private) account** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:24">[00:02:24]</a>
*   **Company (or business) account** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:28">[00:02:28]</a>

A crucial rule for transfers is that funds must originate from a compatible account type <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:07">[00:03:07]</a>. For example, if you have a company account, transfers must come from another company account <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:46">[00:02:46]</a>. Transfers from individual bank accounts to a Payoneer business account (or vice versa) are not permitted <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:57">[00:02:57]</a>.

## [[managing_currencies_and_transactions_with_payoneer | Managing Currencies and Receiving Accounts]]
Within Payoneer, you can hold multiple currencies, such as US dollars, Euros, and British Pounds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:26">[00:03:26]</a>. Users can also request additional currency accounts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:19">[00:04:19]</a>.

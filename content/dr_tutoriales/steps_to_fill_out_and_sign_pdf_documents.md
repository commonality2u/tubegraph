This tutorial outlines the process for [[how_to_insert_a_signature_in_a_pdf_document | inserting a signature within a PDF file]] and filling out forms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:05">[00:00:05]</a>. It provides a simple and useful method for [[adding_signatures_to_pdfs | signing PDF documents]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:02">[00:01:02]</a>.

## Preparation

Before beginning, ensure you have:
*   The PDF document you wish to sign <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:30">[00:00:30]</a>.
*   A [[techniques_to_scan_and_add_signatures_to_pdfs | scanned signature]] image, or a photo of your signature <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:27">[00:01:27]</a>. This image should be on your computer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:34">[00:01:34]</a>. For privacy, a placeholder signature can be used for demonstration purposes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:33">[00:00:33]</a>.

## Process to Sign a PDF

1.  **Open the PDF File**: Begin by opening the desired PDF document <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:44">[00:00:44]</a>.
2.  **Access Fill & Sign Option**: Once the PDF is open, locate the "Fill & Sign" option, typically found on the right side of the interface <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:47">[00:00:47]</a>. This option allows you to fill, sign, and send documents, or obtain signatures from others <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:55">[00:00:55]</a>. Select "Fill and Sign" to proceed with adding your own [[adding_signatures_to_pdfs | signature]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:04">[00:01:04]</a>.
3.  **Navigate to Signing Area**: Scroll to the desired location within the document where you wish to add the [[adding_signatures_to_pdfs | signature]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:13">[00:01:13]</a>.
4.  **Choose Signature Method**: At the top right, click "Sign" and then "Add a signature" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:41">[00:01:41]</a>. You have several [[options_for_signing_pdfs_including_text_and_image_signatures | options for signing PDFs]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:50">[00:01:50]</a>:
    *   **Text**: Type your name, and choose from various available styles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:56">[00:01:56]</a>.
    *   **Draw**: Use your cursor or a stylus to draw your [[options_for_signing_pdfs_including_text_and_image_signatures | signature]] directly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:04">[00:02:04]</a>.
    *   **Image**: Select an image file (e.g., a JPEG or PNG) of your pre-scanned signature <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:10">[00:02:10]</a>.
5.  **Insert the Signature**:
    *   If using an image, select the image file from your desktop <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:14">[00:02:14]</a>.
    *   Once selected, the [[adding_signatures_to_pdfs | signature]] will appear on the document <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:30">[00:02:30]</a>.
6.  **Position and Resize**: The inserted [[adding_signatures_to_pdfs | signature]] can be moved, made larger, or made smaller to fit perfectly within the document <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:33">[00:02:33]</a>.

## Saving the Signed Document

1.  **Save As**: Go to "File" and select "Save As" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:46">[00:02:46]</a>.
2.  **Name the File**: Give the signed document a new name, for example, "File Name - Signed" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:52">[00:02:52]</a>.
3.  **Save**: Click "Save" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:59">[00:02:59]</a>.

## Verification

To confirm the document has been successfully signed, close the PDF viewer and then open the newly saved file <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:02">[00:03:02]</a>. You should see the [[adding_signatures_to_pdfs | signature]] embedded in the document <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:07">[00:03:07]</a>.

MediaFire is a free cloud-based platform designed for [[how_to_upload_files_to_mediafire | uploading files]] and [[sharing_mediafire_file_links | sharing them]] with others <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:11">[00:00:11]</a>.

## Finding MediaFire
To access MediaFire, you should use a trusted search engine like Google Chrome <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:28">[00:00:28]</a>. Type "MediaFire" into the search bar and navigate to the first search result <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:38">[00:00:38]</a>.

## Signing Up for an Account
While it's possible to [[how_to_upload_files_to_mediafire | upload files]] if you already have an account <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:49">[00:00:49]</a>, this tutorial demonstrates how to create a new one <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:51">[00:00:51]</a>.

1.  Click "Sign Up" located at the top right of the MediaFire website <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:02">[00:01:02]</a>.
2.  Review the different plans available to users <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:07">[00:01:07]</a>.
3.  Select the "Basic" free account option <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:12">[00:01:12]</a>.
4.  Fill in your personal details, including your name, to complete the registration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:38">[00:01:38]</a>.

## Basic Account Details
The basic free MediaFire account offers a maximum storage capacity of 10 gigabytes for your files in the cloud <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:13">[00:01:13]</a>. A guide is available in the description to help users potentially increase their basic MediaFire storage to 50 GB <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:24">[00:01:24]</a>.

## Initial Account Overview
Upon successful creation, the MediaFire account interface will display your folders and provide an important panel for [[uploading_and_managing_files_on_mediafire | managing your files]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:53">[00:01:53]</a>. This panel includes options such as "Applaud" (upload files) and filtering options <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:04">[00:02:04]</a>. Users can also create new folders within their account <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:15">[00:02:15]</a>.

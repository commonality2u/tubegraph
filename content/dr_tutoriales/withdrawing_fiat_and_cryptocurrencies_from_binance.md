This article details how to withdraw fiat currency and cryptocurrencies from your Binance account to other external wallets or bank accounts. It is crucial to understand the differences between internal transfers within Binance and external transfers to other platforms or banks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:07">[01:19:07]</a>.

## External Transfers: Deposits and Withdrawals

Binance allows you to deposit or withdraw both fiat currencies and cryptocurrencies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:54:39">[03:54:39]</a>. This process involves moving assets between the Binance platform and an external platform or bank <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:15:23">[04:15:23]</a>.

### [[how_to_transfer_cryptocurrencies_on_binance | Withdrawing Cryptocurrencies]]

To [[how_to_transfer_cryptocurrencies_on_binance | withdraw cryptocurrencies]] from Binance to another crypto wallet or platform <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:13:30">[07:13:30]</a>:

1.  **Navigate to the withdraw section**: From the overview, select the "Withdraw" option <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:06:07">[04:06:07]</a>.
2.  **Choose the cryptocurrency**: Select the specific cryptocurrency you wish to withdraw, for example, Bitcoin (BTC) or Binance Coin (BNB) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:21:28">[07:21:28]</a>.
3.  **Enter the recipient's wallet address**: You will need to input the exact wallet address of the external platform or wallet where you intend to send the cryptocurrency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:38:00">[07:38:00]</a>.
    *   If you have previously used an address, it might be found in your transaction book <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:34:00">[07:34:00]</a>.
    *   This address functions as a unique identifier for the destination wallet, similar to a postal address for a letter <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:43:56">[05:43:56]</a>.
4.  **Select the blockchain network**: This is a crucial step <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:32:47">[05:32:47]</a>. You must select the correct blockchain network (e.g., ERC-20, BEP-20) that matches the network of the destination wallet <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:31:00">[05:31:00]</a>.
    *   The network chosen will affect the transaction fees and arrival time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:55:08">[07:55:08]</a>.
    *   **Warning**: If the selected network on Binance does not match the network of the receiving wallet, your cryptocurrency will be lost and will never reach its destination <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:11:00">[06:11:00]</a>. Always confirm with the recipient which network they are using <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:28:10">[06:28:10]</a>.
5.  **Confirm the transaction**: Once confirmed, the transfer typically takes around two to three minutes to arrive at the destination platform <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:55:00">[06:55:00]</a>.

### [[best_practices_for_withdrawing_cryptocurrencies_to_bank_accounts | Withdrawing Fiat Currency]]

To [[best_practices_for_withdrawing_cryptocurrencies_to_bank_accounts | withdraw fiat currency]] (e.g., Euros, Dollars, Pounds Sterling) to your bank account <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:21:00">[08:21:00]</a>:

1.  **Navigate to the withdraw fiat section**: Select "Withdraw Fiat" from the options <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:26:00">[08:26:00]</a>.
2.  **Choose the currency**: Select the specific fiat currency you wish to withdraw <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:30:00">[08:30:00]</a>.
3.  **Review withdrawal methods and fees**: Binance will display available withdrawal methods (e.g., bank transfer, card payment) and associated commissions for the selected currency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:42:00">[08:42:00]</a>. For example, withdrawing Euros might incur a 0.8 Euro commission <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:32:00">[08:32:00]</a>. The available methods vary depending on the currency and your region <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:55:00">[08:55:00]</a>.
4.  **Enter the amount**: Input the amount you wish to withdraw <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:59:00">[08:59:00]</a>.
5.  **Provide bank account details**: You will be prompted to enter your bank account number and other necessary details <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:05:00">[09:05:00]</a>.

#### Bank Account Recommendations for Fiat Withdrawals

Some conventional banks, such as Santander, Sabadell, or BBVA in Spain, may block or cancel transactions from cryptocurrency platforms, preventing the funds from reaching your account <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:09:00">[09:09:00]</a>.

To avoid such issues, it is recommended to use banking services that are known to be compatible with cryptocurrency transactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:16:00">[09:16:00]</a>:
*   **N26**: A tutorial for N26 is available <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:23:00">[09:23:00]</a>.
*   **Revolut**: A tutorial for Revolut is also available <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:28:00">[09:28:00]</a>.
These accounts are less likely to block fiat deposits and withdrawals from cryptocurrency platforms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:34:00">[09:34:00]</a>.

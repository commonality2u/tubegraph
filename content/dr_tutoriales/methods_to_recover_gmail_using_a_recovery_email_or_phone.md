This article provides methods for recovering a forgotten Gmail password or a forgotten Gmail account, leveraging browser settings, recovery email addresses, or phone numbers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:04">[00:00:04]</a>.

## Important Prerequisite

Before attempting any recovery method, it is crucial to use a computer or mobile phone from which you have previously accessed your Gmail account <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:47">[00:00:47]</a>. Google uses this information as a reference to verify your identity and facilitate the recovery process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:01">[00:01:01]</a>.

## Methods for Recovery

### [[using_browser_settings_to_recover_email_and_passwords | Using Browser Settings to Recover Email and Passwords]]

If you have previously accessed your Gmail account from your device, your browser, such as Google Chrome, might have saved your email and password <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:04">[00:02:04]</a>.

1.  **Open Google Chrome Settings**: Open a new tab in Google Chrome, click on the three-dot menu icon on the right, and select "Settings" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:12">[00:02:12]</a>.
2.  **Access Passwords**: Navigate to the "Autofill" section and then select "Password" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:18">[00:02:18]</a>. This will display all saved passwords and accounts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:22">[00:02:22]</a>.
3.  **Search for Gmail**: In the search bar provided, type "gmail" to filter and view all saved Gmail accounts and their corresponding passwords <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:37">[00:02:37]</a>.
4.  **Reveal Password**: Click on the "eye" icon next to the desired Gmail account to reveal the password. You may be prompted to enter your computer's PIN for security <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:56">[00:02:56]</a>. This method is effective if you have previously accessed your account from the device <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:18">[00:03:18]</a>.

### [[recovering_a_gmail_account_without_remembering_the_email | Recovering a Gmail Account Without Remembering the Email]]

If you have forgotten your Gmail email address, you can recover it using a linked phone number or a recovery email address <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:27">[00:03:27]</a>.

1.  **Access "Forgot Email"**: On the Gmail sign-in page, click on the option indicating you have forgotten your email <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:47">[00:01:47]</a>.
2.  **Provide Recovery Information**: You will be prompted to enter either a phone number associated with the account or a recovery email address <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:37">[00:03:37]</a>.
    *   It is recommended to set up a secondary Gmail account as a recovery email to avoid unnecessary risks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:51">[00:03:51]</a>.
3.  **Enter Name**: After providing the recovery email or phone number, you will be asked to enter the first and last names associated with the Gmail account <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:15">[00:04:15]</a>.
4.  **Verification Code**: Google will send a verification code to the recovery email or phone number provided. Follow the steps in that message to recover your original account <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:25">[00:04:25]</a>.

### [[recovering_a_forgotten_gmail_password | Recovering a Forgotten Gmail Password]] (When Email is Remembered)

If you remember your Gmail email address but have forgotten your password, Google offers several methods for recovery <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:13">[00:05:13]</a>.

1.  **Access "Forgot Password"**: On the Gmail sign-in page, enter your email address and click on the "Forgot password" option <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:56">[00:05:56]</a>.
2.  **Try a Previous Password**: Google may ask you to enter a password you have previously used for the account. If you recall an old password, entering it correctly can help Google recognize you and allow password recovery <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:21">[00:06:21]</a>.
3.  **Send Verification to Associated Recovery Email**: If you have a secondary Gmail account or another email associated with your main account for recovery, Google can send a verification email to that address <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:55">[00:06:55]</a>. This process is similar to recovering a forgotten email <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:16">[00:07:16]</a>.
4.  **Send Verification to Any Other Email**: If no recovery email is associated, Google might offer to send a verification code to any other email account you can access, such as a family member's email. Google will attempt to contact you through this account to help recover the password <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:43">[00:07:43]</a>.
5.  **Answer Security Questions**: As a last resort, if other methods fail, Google may ask you to answer a series of personal or account-related security questions that you set up previously <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:23">[00:08:23]</a>. Successfully answering these questions can grant you access to change your password <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:37">[00:08:37]</a>.

Google's recovery system is designed to be powerful and assist millions of users who forget their passwords <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:49">[00:08:49]</a>.

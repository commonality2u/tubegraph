This guide explains how to manage notification settings on your iPhone, especially focusing on how [[customizing_siri_notifications_with_airpods | Siri]] announces notifications through your [[deactivating_airpods_notifications | AirPods]].

## Accessing Notification Settings

To begin managing notifications, navigate to the **Settings** app on your iPhone <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:17">[00:00:17]</a>. If your [[deactivating_airpods_notifications | AirPods]] are connected, their name might appear at the top of the settings menu <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:22">[00:00:22]</a>.

Once in settings, proceed to **Notifications** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:27">[00:00:27]</a>. If you cannot locate it immediately, use the search bar at the top by typing "notifications" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:30">[00:00:30]</a>.

> [!INFO] Notification Display Styles
> Within the Notifications section, you'll see options for how notifications are displayed, such as in groups, lists, or counts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:43">[00:00:43]</a>. These settings primarily affect how general app notifications (e.g., WhatsApp messages) appear <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:48">[00:00:48]</a>.

## Managing Siri Announce Notifications for AirPods

To specifically manage how notifications are announced via [[customizing_siri_notifications_with_airpods | Siri]] with your headphones, look for the option **Siri, Announce Notifications** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:54">[00:00:54]</a>.

1.  Tap on **Announce Notifications** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>.
2.  Here, you can activate or deactivate whether [[customizing_siri_notifications_with_airpods | Siri]] announces notifications at all <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:06">[00:01:06]</a>.
3.  Ensure the option to announce notifications "when we have headphones on" is activated if you want this feature <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:12">[00:01:12]</a>.
4.  To [[deactivating_airpods_notifications | deactivate]] [[customizing_siri_notifications_with_airpods | Siri]] from reading notifications aloud, simply toggle this option off <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:16">[00:01:16]</a>. If reactivated, [[customizing_siri_notifications_with_airpods | Siri]] will resume announcing them, and you may also have the option to respond without confirmation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:24">[00:01:24]</a>.

## [[selecting_specific_apps_for_siri_notification_announcements | Selecting Specific Apps for Siri Notification Announcements]]

You can customize which individual applications have their notifications announced by [[customizing_siri_notifications_with_airpods | Siri]] through your headphones <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:29">[00:01:29]</a>.

*   Scroll down within the **Announce Notifications** section. You will see a list of your installed applications.
*   For each application, you can individually decide if [[customizing_siri_notifications_with_airpods | Siri]] should announce its notifications through your [[deactivating_airpods_notifications | AirPods]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:40">[00:01:40]</a>.
*   If you wish an app to stop announcing notifications via [[deactivating_airpods_notifications | AirPods]], locate it in the list and toggle its setting to off <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:46">[00:01:46]</a>.

By following these steps, you can effectively [[managing_iphone_notification_settings_for_airpods | manage iPhone notification settings for AirPods]] and ensure you only receive the announcements you desire <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:50">[00:01:50]</a>.

[[Looping Videos and Music on YouTube|Looping videos]] on the YouTube platform allows them to restart automatically once they finish, without needing to manually click replay or start the video again <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:09">[00:00:09]</a>. This feature is useful for continuous listening to music or watching videos uninterrupted <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:17">[00:00:17]</a>.

## Activating Loop Mode

To [[how_to_put_youtube_videos_on_loop|activate loop mode]] for a YouTube video:

1.  Access any YouTube video <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:44">[00:00:44]</a>. The demonstration uses [[using_google_chrome_to_loop_youtube_videos|Google Chrome]] as the browser <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:36">[00:00:36]</a>.
2.  While watching the video, right-click anywhere on the video frame <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:07">[00:01:07]</a>.
3.  A dropdown menu will appear <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:10">[00:01:10]</a>.
4.  Click on the "Loop" option within this menu <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:13">[00:01:13]</a>.

Once activated, the video will automatically restart from the beginning when it reaches its end <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:15">[00:01:15]</a>. The video will continue to loop without any further action from the user <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:34">[00:01:34]</a>.

## Deactivating Loop Mode

To turn off loop mode and stop a YouTube video from continuously repeating:

1.  While the video is playing, right-click on the video screen again <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:45">[00:01:45]</a>.
2.  The same dropdown menu will appear <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:47">[00:01:47]</a>.
3.  Click on the "Loop" option again <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:49">[00:01:49]</a>.

After deactivation, the video will no longer restart automatically. Instead, it will finish playing and then pause playback <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:57">[00:01:57]</a>.

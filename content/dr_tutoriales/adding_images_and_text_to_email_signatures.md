This article explains how to create and modify email signatures in Outlook, including adding text and images, and configuring them for different message types and accounts.

## Accessing Signature Settings in Outlook

To begin, open Outlook. The interface is largely the same whether you are using the English or Spanish version <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:17">[00:00:17]</a>.

1.  Navigate to the menu at the top of the Outlook window <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:24">[00:00:24]</a>.
2.  Select "Options" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:31">[00:00:31]</a>.
3.  Within the Options menu, go to the "Mail" or "Email" section <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:35">[00:00:35]</a>.
4.  Look for the "Signatures" option, also labeled "Signatures" in English <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:40">[00:00:40]</a>. This section allows you to [[creating_and_modifying_signatures_in_outlook | create or modify signatures]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:43">[00:00:43]</a>.

## Creating and Customizing a New Signature

1.  To create a new signature, click the "New" button <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:59">[00:00:59]</a>.
2.  Give your new signature a name (e.g., "Subscribe") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:02">[00:01:02]</a>, then click "OK" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:09">[00:01:09]</a>.
3.  In the signature editing box, you can type the text for your signature <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:12">[00:01:12]</a>. You have the flexibility to format the text as desired <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:17">[00:01:17]</a>.
4.  You can also import images into your signature <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:14">[00:01:14]</a>, such as a company logo <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:31">[00:01:31]</a>. A complete signature can include your name, company logo, email address, and phone number <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:28">[00:01:28]</a>.
5.  Once you have designed your signature, click the "Save" button to keep your changes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:36">[00:01:36]</a>.

## Configuring Signature Usage

Outlook allows you to [[managing_multiple_email_signatures_for_different_accounts | manage multiple email accounts]] and signatures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:58">[00:01:58]</a>.

1.  **Choose Account**: Select which email account your signature will be associated with <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:58">[00:01:58]</a>.
2.  **New Messages**: Specify which signature to use for new email messages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:04">[00:02:04]</a>.
3.  **Replies and Forwards**: You can also [[configuring_signatures_for_replies_and_forwarded_emails | configure whether to use a signature for replies or forwarded emails]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:05">[00:02:05]</a>. You can choose a different signature for these, or select "don't use" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:13">[00:02:13]</a>.
4.  After making your selections, click "OK" to apply the signature configurations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:32">[00:02:32]</a>.

## Verifying Your Signature

To ensure your signature is correctly set up:

1.  Create a new email <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:37">[00:02:37]</a>. The configured signature should automatically appear in the body of the email <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:40">[00:02:40]</a>.
2.  To test for replies, open an existing email and click "Reply" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:47">[00:02:47]</a>. Your signature should also appear in the reply window, if configured <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:53">[00:02:53]</a>.

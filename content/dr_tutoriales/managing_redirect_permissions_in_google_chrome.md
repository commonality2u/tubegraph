This tutorial explains how to [[how_to_activate_popup_windows_in_google_chrome | activate and enable pop-up windows and redirects]] within [[using_google_chrome_for_youtube | Google Chrome]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:03">[00:00:03]</a>. This process also allows you to control how sites send pop-up windows or use redirects <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:47">[00:01:47]</a>.

## Accessing Chrome Settings

To begin, open the [[using_google_chrome_for_youtube | Google Chrome]] browser <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:50">[00:00:50]</a>.
1.  Navigate to the **Configuration** section, located in the top right corner of the browser <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:54">[00:00:54]</a>.
2.  Click on **Advanced settings** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:07">[00:01:07]</a>.

## Finding Site Configuration

There are two primary ways to access the relevant settings for pop-ups and redirects:

### Using the Search Bar
1.  In the settings search bar, type "emerging" (referring to "pop-ups" or "emerging windows") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:19">[00:01:19]</a>.
2.  This will directly lead you to the **Site configuration** section <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:25">[00:01:25]</a>.

### Navigating Manually
1.  From the advanced settings menu, go to the **Privacy and security** section on the left <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:30">[00:01:30]</a>.
2.  Click on **Site configuration** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:32">[00:01:32]</a>.

## Configuring Pop-up and Redirect Permissions

Within the **Site configuration** section, you will find the option for **Windows emerging redirects** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:41">[00:01:41]</a>.

*   By default, [[configuring_site_settings_for_popups_in_chrome | Chrome]] is often set to "not allowing sites to send emerging windows or use redirects" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:46">[00:01:46]</a>.
*   To enable pop-ups and redirects for all sites, click on this option and toggle it to allow sites to send pop-up windows and use redirects <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:53">[00:01:53]</a>.

## Blocking Specific Sites

Even if you enable pop-ups and redirects globally, you can still [[blocking_specific_sites_from_sending_popups_in_chrome | block specific sites]] from sending them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:06">[00:02:06]</a>.
1.  Within the "Windows emerging redirects" setting, you will see an option to block specific sites <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:06">[00:02:06]</a>.
2.  Click **Add** to specify a site. For example, to prevent YouTube from sending pop-ups, you can add `WWW.YouTube.com` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:12">[00:02:12]</a>.
3.  Click **Add** again to confirm, and YouTube will no longer send pop-ups <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:23">[00:02:23]</a>.

This allows you to customize permissions based on your preferences, either activating pop-ups for all pages, disabling them for all pages, or enabling them only for specific pages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:37">[00:02:37]</a>.

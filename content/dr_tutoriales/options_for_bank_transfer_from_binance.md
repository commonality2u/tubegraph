## Transferring Cryptocurrency to Binance

The first step is to open your cryptocurrency application <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:59">[00:00:59]</a>. You must identify which cryptocurrency you intend to withdraw <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:04">[00:01:04]</a>. For example, if you have BNB (Binance Coin) on a platform like Trust Wallet, the only platform that natively accepts BNB (Byconchain) is [[Using Binance Exchange for transactions | Binance]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:11">[00:01:11]</a>.

The general process for sending cryptocurrency to an exchange like [[Using Binance Exchange for transactions | Binance]], KuCoin, [[Transferring Bitcoin from Coinbase to Binance | Coinbase]], or Kraken, involves sending the cryptocurrency in question (e.g., BNB, Ethereum, or Bitcoin) to an exchange that allows you to then withdraw to your bank account <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:20">[00:01:20]</a>.

### Steps for Sending Cryptocurrency to Binance:

1.  Open the source platform (e.g., Trust Wallet) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:59">[00:00:59]</a>.
2.  Select the cryptocurrency to send, such as BNB <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:55">[00:01:55]</a>.
3.  Open the [[Using Binance Exchange for transactions | Binance]] application <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:04">[00:02:04]</a>.
4.  On [[Using Binance Exchange for transactions | Binance]], navigate to "Deposit" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:07">[00:02:07]</a>.
5.  Choose the cryptocurrency you are depositing (e.g., BNB) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:10">[00:02:10]</a>.
6.  Select the correct blockchain (e.g., Baconchain for BNB) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:16">[00:02:16]</a>.
7.  Copy the wallet address provided by [[Using Binance Exchange for transactions | Binance]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:21">[00:02:21]</a>.
8.  Paste the copied wallet address into the send function of your source wallet <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:21">[00:02:21]</a>.
9.  Confirm the transaction, noting any commissions (e.g., two cents on the dollar for this transaction) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:37">[00:02:37]</a>.
10. Once confirmed, your capital will be removed from the source platform and will arrive in your [[Using Binance Exchange for transactions | Binance]] spot wallet <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:45">[00:02:45]</a>.

## Converting Cryptocurrency to Fiat on Binance

After your cryptocurrency arrives in your [[Using Binance Exchange for transactions | Binance]] spot wallet, it must be converted to a fiat currency (e.g., Euro) before it can be withdrawn to a bank account <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:01">[00:03:01]</a>.

1.  From the bottom menu in the [[Using Binance Exchange for transactions | Binance]] app, go to "Trade" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:05">[00:03:05]</a>.
2.  Select "Convert" (usually top left) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:07">[00:03:07]</a>.
3.  Choose the cryptocurrency you have (e.g., BNB) and select USDT as the conversion target <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:13">[00:03:13]</a>.
4.  Then, convert the USDT to your desired fiat currency, such as Euro (EUR) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:20">[00:03:20]</a>.
    *   This is part of the [[Converting cryptocurrencies within Binance | conversion process]].

## Withdrawing Fiat to a Bank Account

Once you have converted your cryptocurrency into a fiat currency like Euro in your [[Using Binance Exchange for transactions | Binance]] spot wallet <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:32">[00:03:32]</a>:

1.  Navigate to "Withdraw" from your spot wallet <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:35">[00:03:35]</a>.
2.  Select the "Fiat" option <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:39">[00:03:39]</a>.
3.  Choose the currency you wish to withdraw (e.g., Euro) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:40">[00:03:40]</a>.
4.  Specify the amount you want to withdraw <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:45">[00:03:45]</a>.

### Available Transfer Methods:

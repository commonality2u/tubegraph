## Current State of Indian Spin Attack

The Indian team boasts a formidable spin contingent, even with key players sometimes unavailable. In the recent series, India won easily despite missing Virat Kohli (paternal duties) and having a squad where several players were playing only their first Test <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:00">[00:53:00]</a>. This depth suggests a strong foundation for the [[future_of_indian_test_cricket | future of Indian Test Cricket]].

### Ravichandran Ashwin

Ravichandran Ashwin's performance in the recent series has quieted earlier doubts about his form <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:26:00">[04:26:00]</a>. Early in the series, it was questioned if Ashwin was "finished," with observations that he lacked the "zip" or "crackle" seen previously, appearing to "pushing the ball down rather than snapping through it" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:30:00">[04:30:00]</a>. In his first seven innings of the series, Ashwin averaged 38-39 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:58:00">[04:58:00]</a>. However, since then, he has averaged 12.8 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:04:00">[05:04:00]</a>. This improvement was partly attributed to his effectiveness with the new ball, where the hardness of the ball and his ability to slide it through seemed to benefit him <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:06:00">[05:06:00]</a>.

Ashwin's mastery is evident in his variations, as demonstrated by wickets that show different hand angles for side spin or straighter deliveries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:51:00">[06:51:00]</a>. His record in the first 10 overs when bowling in India is strong, averaging 18.57, which improves to 17.76 in the second innings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:26:00">[02:26:00]</a>. When taking the new ball against left-handers in the second innings in India, his average drops to 14.46 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:33:00">[02:33:00]</a>.

### Kuldeep Yadav

Kuldeep Yadav's unique ability to make the ball keep low, more so than other wrist spinners and even some finger spinners, is a significant asset <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:03:00">[09:03:00]</a>. This characteristic makes him particularly problematic for batters who play off the back foot, as the ball spinning into the stumps increases the chance of an LBW <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:42:00">[08:42:00]</a>. There is a strong belief that Kuldeep has the potential to become the greatest left-arm wrist spinner in cricket, surpassing bowlers like Paul Adams <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:40">[01:01:40]</a>.

### Ravindra Jadeja and Axar Patel

Ravindra Jadeja remains a crucial all-rounder for India, providing valuable batting, fielding, and bowling options <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:14">[01:04:14]</a>. ## Strategic Considerations for Indian Spin

The discussion on the [[indias_spin_strategy_and_team_selection | future of spin bowling in Indian cricket]] often revolves around the optimal combination of spinners and their deployment in different conditions.

### Pitch Conditions

The series against England featured "flat wickets," which were described as "absolutely sensational" cricket wickets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:00">[01:06:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:06:00">[02:06:00]</a>. While some argue that flat pitches in India would benefit Bazball, India's victory on such surfaces showed that the better team would still prevail <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:28:00">[02:28:00]</a>. It is suggested that India is "much better off playing on pitches that are even," as this leads to "less of those fluky results" seen in the past <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:40:00">[02:40:00]</a>. However, against weaker teams, India might still opt for "ragging games" to avoid overworking their key spinners like Ashwin or Jadeja <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:57:00">[02:57:00]</a>.

### Spin Combinations and Player Rotation

The ideal spin combination for India, especially overseas, appears to be Ashwin and Kuldeep <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:00">[01:04:00]</a>. This pair offers a finger spinner and a wrist spinner, providing variety <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:00">[00:08:00]</a>. Kuldeep's ability to spin the ball the "other way" compared to Ashwin makes him a valuable asset <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:00">[00:13:00]</a>.

Ashwin's role might evolve, allowing for [[indias_bowling_strategy_and_player_rotation | player rotation]] and prolonging his career <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:00">[00:27:00]</a>. He could be selected for specific series or conditions where his skills are most required, such as in Asia, the West Indies, New Zealand, or South Africa as a holding spinner <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:00">[00:37:00]</a>. This approach would manage his workload, especially with the emergence of other talented spinners <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:00">[00:41:00]</a>.

### Balancing Batting and Bowling

A key challenge for India, particularly overseas, is balancing spin options with batting depth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:00">[00:22:00]</a>. While Kuldeep might be preferred over Axar in Asia, and perhaps even Ashwin in some overseas scenarios due to his bowling variety, the batting capabilities of these bowlers are a concern <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:00">[00:19:00]</a>. Kuldeep batting at number eight or nine is seen as potentially "messy" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:00">[00:48:00]</a>.

The role of all-rounders like Ravindra Jadeja or Hardik Pandya (if fit) becomes crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:00">[00:11:00]</a>. Hardik Pandya, as a number eight who can bowl 10 overs an innings and bat dynamically, would be "handy" overseas where his bowling suits conditions better than, for instance, Shardul Thakur's <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:00">[00:14:00]</a>.

## Conclusion

India's spin bowling future appears robust, characterized by a mix of experienced campaigners and emerging talents. The focus is shifting towards strategic selection based on conditions, player fitness, and the desired balance of skills within the team. The aim is to leverage the unique strengths of each spinner while ensuring overall team capability, especially on varied international pitches. This flexible approach to [[indias_bowling_strategies_and_performance | India's bowling strategies]] will be key to continued success in Test cricket.

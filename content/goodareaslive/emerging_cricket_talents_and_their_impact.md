## Yashasvi Jaiswal

Yashasvi Jaiswal's performance, particularly his 161 runs in the third innings after scoring a duck in the first, was a standout <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:39:00">[03:39:00]</a>. His ability to convert starts into "daddy hundreds" is a notable trait, with scores including 171 (potentially on Test debut against West Indies), 209, and an unbeaten 214 against England <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:54:00">[03:54:00]</a>.

Jaiswal's [[challenges_and_expectations_for_young_cricket_talents | maturity as a batter]] is evident in his quick adaptation; early in his first-class career, he learned to overcome the statistical tendency for players to get out around landmarks (like 100 runs) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:43:00">[03:43:00]</a>. His subsequent lowest century stands at 144 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:50:00">[03:50:00]</a>. Despite an initial dismissal driving on the up, he showed an ability to adjust, leaving fewer balls in the next innings, demonstrating a "Bumrah-like level of working things out" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:59:00">[03:59:00]</a>.

His versatility allows him to play both attritional and aggressive innings, showcasing a "Mamba mentality" in his interactions on the field <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:14:00">[04:14:00]</a>. Jaiswal's spin game is described as "otherworldly" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:48:00">[04:48:00]</a>, and his success as an opener is particularly impressive in an era where consistently good openers are rare globally <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:05:00">[04:05:00]</a>. His performance in a high-pressure context, with India facing a series loss and World Test Championship qualification on the line, further highlights his capability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:57:00">[04:57:00]</a>.

Concerns exist regarding the potential for over-hyping [[challenges_faced_by_young_cricketing_talents | young talents]] due to the immense fame and constant media scrutiny in modern cricket <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:44:00">[04:44:00]</a>. Despite this, Jaiswal is considered the most promising batting prospect since Joe Root, with a current "man factor" suggesting he's 85% better than other players <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:34:00">[04:34:00]</a>.

## Harshit Rana

Harshit Rana made an impressive international debut, taking four wickets in the Test match, including 3 for 48 in his first bowling innings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:41:00">[01:41:00]</a>. Although not an express pace bowler, he showcased excellent lengths and a good wrist snap, particularly effective with his slow delivery learned from [[the_impact_of_ipl_performances_on_young_players | IPL performances]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:47:00">[01:47:00]</a>. Rana was particularly effective in the first innings, creating many chances despite going for more runs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:46:00">[02:46:00]</a>.

His style as a "hit the wicket" bowler suited the Perth conditions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:24:00">[02:24:00]</a>. While he may need to develop a more consistent stock ball, his physicality allows him to generate movement off the pitch by hitting the surface hard, distinguishing him from purely fast bowlers like Mark Wood who may not nip the ball around as much <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:49:00">[02:49:00]</a>. His [[performance_of_debutant_cricket_players | debut]] was memorable, and he offers a strong option for India's pace attack, especially if Mohammed Shami is unavailable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:17:00">[02:17:00]</a>.

## Nitish Kumar Reddy

Nitish Kumar Reddy, also on Test debut, contributed significantly with the bat, scoring 41 runs in the first innings and an unbeaten 39 in the second <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:14:00">[05:14:00]</a>. His batting was described as free-flowing, taking the attack to the Australian bowlers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:21:00">[05:21:00]</a>.

Although India initially wanted Hardik Pandya for his bowling, Nitish Kumar Reddy's [[evaluating_cricket_allrounders_using_statistics_and_impact | all-round contribution]] was highly positive for a debut <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:27:00">[05:27:00]</a>. As a 21-year-old, he has significant [[potential_of_young_cricketers_in_ipl | potential for development]] and provides versatile support as a fifth bowler <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:59:00">[05:59:00]</a>.

## India's Deep Talent Pool

The performances of these emerging players, alongside established stars like Jasprit Bumrah and Virat Kohli, highlight India's formidable bench strength in Test cricket <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:00">[01:05:00]</a>. Even in an era where it's challenging for some players to get extensive first-class cricket, India's talent pool is exceptionally high <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:00">[01:11:00]</a>. This depth means players like Prithvi Shaw or T. Natarajan, who are considered high-level talents, might struggle to maintain a consistent place in the national side due to intense competition <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:00">[01:12:00]</a>. This contrasts with Australia, which currently lacks similar depth, particularly in batting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:00">[01:04:00]</a>.

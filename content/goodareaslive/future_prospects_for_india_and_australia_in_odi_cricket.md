The 2023 ODI World Cup final highlighted the strengths and weaknesses of both the Indian and Australian cricket teams, prompting discussions about their future trajectories in the format.

## India's Outlook

Despite an impressive run to the final, India's loss has raised questions about their future in ODI cricket, particularly regarding key player retirements and coaching stability.

### Player Transition

Several prominent Indian players might not feature in the next ODI World Cup. Rohit Sharma, Virat Kohli, Ravindra Jadeja, Mohammed Shami, and Jasprit Bumrah, all around 30 or older (Shami is 33), may not be around in four years' time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:29">[00:30:29]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:24:15">[01:24:15]</a>.

While India is often considered to have a "conveyor belt of batters" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:25:17">[01:25:17]</a>, finding replacements for players like Rohit and Virat, who are "on another level" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:25:30">[01:25:30]</a>, will be a challenge. Potential future batting prospects mentioned include Shubman Gill and Rishabh Pant <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:25:33">[01:25:33]</a>.

The pace attack of Shami and Bumrah is acknowledged as exceptionally good, with the expectation that the future bowling attack will be different and, statistically, "slightly worse next time" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:24:53">[01:24:53]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:25:01">[01:25:01]</a>. There's also a cautionary note that a "conveyor belt" of talent isn't guaranteed, citing instances where other strong teams failed to continuously produce top-tier players <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:25:50">[01:25:50]</a>.

### Coaching Staff

Rahul Dravid's contract as the national team coach recently concluded <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:35:56">[01:35:56]</a>. While he previously enjoyed coaching younger players <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:36:40">[01:36:40]</a> and might not desire the main job, his fiercely competitive nature might prompt him to stay following the World Cup loss <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:37:29">[01:37:29]</a>. The speaker expressed a preference for Dravid to continue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:38:11">[01:38:11]</a>.

## Australia's Outlook

Australia's victory, their sixth ODI World Cup, marks a period of significant success, including wins in the T20 World Cup (2021), Ashes (at home and retained in England), and World Test Championship Final <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:07">[00:01:07]</a>. This success has come with an "aging Squad" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:32:52">[01:32:52]</a>, suggesting a complete overhaul of the team will be needed for the next World Cup cycle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:32:56">[01:32:56]</a>.

### Player Changes

Many players from the victorious squad are unlikely to be part of the team in four years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:32:54">[01:32:54]</a>.

Travis Head has emerged as a key multi-format player, winning Player of the Match awards in both the World Test Championship Final and the ODI World Cup knockout games. His performance in the final, scoring 137 off 120 deliveries, made him only the third Australian to score a century in a World Cup final, following Ricky Ponting and Adam Gilchrist <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:26:31">[01:26:31]</a>. Despite previous struggles and not being in the IPL since 2017 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:14">[01:29:14]</a>, he has become a "revelation" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:30:20">[01:30:20]</a> and is likely to secure an IPL deal and a starting spot in the T20 World Cup <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:30:24">[01:30:24]</a>.

Marnus Labuschagne, initially not expected to be in the World Cup squad, played a crucial stabilizing innings in the final <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:31:00">[01:31:00]</a>. His future in ODIs remains uncertain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:32:37">[01:32:37]</a>.

### Coaching and Leadership

Andrew McDonald, as coach, and Pat Cummins, as captain, are now tasked with building a new team <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:33:15">[01:33:15]</a>. Cummins has expressed his desire to continue leading in ODI cricket <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:33:12">[01:33:12]</a>.

McDonald is seen as a good coach for an experienced team that knows the right questions to ask, offering "hard with his decisions but fair with his conversations" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:33:57">[01:33:57]</a>. However, the next team might require more "hands-on coaching" due to potential inexperience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:34:06">[01:34:06]</a>. The idea of a head coaching coordinator overseeing format-specific coaches was suggested as a potential future model for cricket coaching structures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:35:05">[01:35:05]</a>.

Pat Cummins's captaincy has been successful, leading Australia to multiple major titles despite criticisms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:33:04">[01:33:04]</a>.

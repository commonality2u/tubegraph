While some injuries can prove detrimental, others can lead to unexpected opportunities for other players, sometimes resulting in improved team dynamics.

## Strategic Adjustments Due to Injuries

Teams often have to make strategic adjustments when key players are injured or unavailable. For instance, India's perceived "number four problem" coming into the World Cup was partly attributed to injuries and player rotation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:08">[00:02:08]</a>. This concern stemmed from the 2019 World Cup, where India's number fours averaged remarkably low at just under 25 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:37">[00:03:37]</a>. In the current World Cup, the strong performance of number fours, averaging almost 70, has relieved pressure on the top three <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:45">[00:02:45]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:15">[00:03:15]</a>. The availability and form of players like Shreyas Iyer and KL Rahul have addressed this issue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:58">[00:03:58]</a>.

Similarly, England's performance in a recent World Cup was affected because [[impact_of_star_players_in_cricket | Ben Stokes]] had to bat higher up the order to fill another role <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:30">[00:07:30]</a>. Josh Buttler also struggled when pushed up to number five, as his strength is typically as a lower-order batter <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:40">[00:07:40]</a>.

## Player Performance and Role Changes

Injuries can force players into roles that are not their primary strengths, potentially affecting their [[impact_of_player_form_and_injuries_in_ipl | individual performance]]. For the Netherlands, Bas de Leede and Logan van Beek were used "out of position" or "beyond what they should normally be used for" because the team only had two other seamers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:51">[00:25:51]</a>. This reliance led to high economies for both bowlers, though they "earned these bad economies" by taking on crucial responsibilities for their team <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:38">[00:27:38]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:44">[00:27:44]</a>.

Aging or physical decline can also alter a player's role; for example, Roloff van der Merwe, despite not bowling as well as he used to due to a lack of "juice" in his arm, has adapted from an attacking bowler to a clever defensive bowler who focuses on keeping economies down <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:12">[00:22:12]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:26">[00:22:26]</a>.

## "Helpful" Injuries and Unexpected Opportunities

Occasionally, an injury to one player can open the door for another to shine. A notable example is Hardik Pandya's injury, which allowed Mohammed Shami to enter the team and subsequently dominate, marking it as a "helpful injury" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:34">[00:35:34]</a>.

Other historical examples of "helpful injuries" or absences in World Cups include:
*   Jason Gillespie and Andy Bichel in 2003 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:42">[00:36:42]</a>.
*   Shane Warne's drug ban in 2003 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:49">[00:36:49]</a>.
*   Brad Hogg in 2003 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:54">[00:36:54]</a>.

## Overall Team Vulnerability

Despite India's strong performance, a key vulnerability without Hardik Pandya is the reduced bowling depth, leaving them with only five bowlers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:51">[00:40:51]</a>. This means if another bowler gets injured during a game, part-time bowlers like Virat Kohli or Shubman Gill would have to fill in, which could be "catastrophic" in a knockout match <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:00">[00:41:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:10">[00:41:10]</a>. This highlights that teams must mitigate flaws and accentuate positives, as seen with players like Virat Kohli adapting his technique against spin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:42">[00:51:42]</a>.

## The Complexity of Historical Data

One significant challenge is the varying quality and availability of historical data. For older players, detailed records might be scarce, and reliance on anecdotal evidence or generalized statistics is more common <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:07">[00:02:07]</a>. This contrasts sharply with modern players whose careers are meticulously documented.

*   **Incomplete Test Careers**: Players like [[Historical analysis of cricket players careers | Ranji]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:08">[01:13:08]</a>, despite having a fantastic first-class record, played fewer test matches due to the selection policies of their era <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:08">[01:13:08]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:21">[01:13:21]</a>. *   **Era Inflation**: The scoring environment significantly impacts raw averages. The 1980s, for example, were a "low-scoring era" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:03">[01:06:03]</a>, meaning averages from that period might be more impressive than seemingly higher averages from high-scoring eras <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:03">[01:06:03]</a>. Conversely, some players' numbers are "inflated" due to playing in great batting eras <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:00">[01:05:00]</a>.
*   **Lack of Visual Evidence**: For very early players, there are no highlights or video footage, making it difficult to assess their style or impact firsthand <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:29">[01:19:29]</a>.

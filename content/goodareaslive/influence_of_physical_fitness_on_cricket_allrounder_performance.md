## Key Attributes of Athletic Allrounders

### Workload Management
Athletes like [[Assessing the performance and style of specific cricket players | Garfield Sobers]] were capable of immense workloads, bowling "something like 37 overs per game" consistently, a feat rarely seen in modern cricket <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:52:01">[02:52:01]</a>. This contrasts with players like [[Assessing the performance and style of specific cricket players | Jacques Kallis]], who, despite being a "freak athlete," required more careful management of his workload, often bowling around "22 overs per game" as a fifth bowler <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:21:49">[10:21:49]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:01:03">[11:01:03]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:54:01">[11:54:01]</a>.

### Multi-Sport Capability
Several top allrounders possessed athleticism that would allow them to excel in multiple sports.
*   **Garfield Sobers** was described as a "brilliant athlete" whose [[Methods of evaluating cricket players | fielding]] alone was "worth two fielders" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:00:01">[03:00:01]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:54:39">[01:54:39]</a>.
*   [[Assessing the performance and style of specific cricket players | Imran Khan]] was a "world-class athlete" who "probably could have played in any sport" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:51:01">[05:51:01]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:59:01">[02:59:01]</a>.
*   **Keith Miller** played professional Australian Rules Football and was a professional jockey, highlighting his diverse athletic talents <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:42:59">[24:42:59]</a>.

### Sustained Performance
High fitness levels enable allrounders to maintain their performance quality over long careers and throughout a match.
*   **Imran Khan** maintained an exceptional peak for a decade, averaging over 54 with the bat and less than 20 with the ball from 1981 to 1991, attributed to his ability to endure high loads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:51:01">[04:51:01]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:11:03">[06:11:03]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:44:03">[01:44:03]</a>.
*   **Richard Hadlee** consistently bowled a high volume of overs (around 41 per game) as a seamer, demanding significant physical resilience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:04:01">[37:04:01]</a>.
*   **[[Assessing the performance and style of specific cricket players | Jacques Kallis]]** played 166 Test matches, demonstrating remarkable longevity, though his bowling workload was managed carefully to ensure his prolonged availability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:29:01">[10:29:01]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:01:03">[11:01:03]</a>.

### Impact of Physical Decline
Injuries and physical decline can significantly alter an allrounder's effectiveness.
*   **Andrew Flintoff** and **Shaun Pollock** are examples of players whose physical ailments impacted their careers. Flintoff's body "had given up" by 2009, limiting his full potential <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="48:05:01">[48:05:01]</a>.
*   **Shaun Pollock** could bowl at 90 mph and move the ball both ways early in his career, but his body went "really really early," leading him to become a medium-fast bowler later on <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:13">[01:01:13]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:38">[01:01:38]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:42">[01:02:42]</a>.
*   **Steve Waugh's** bowling suffered later in his career due to physical issues, performing with "no hamstrings left" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:43">[01:14:43]</a>.

## Pitch Conditions and Their Impact on Bowling

The pitch conditions, particularly in India, significantly influence [[bowling_strategies_and_pitch_analysis_in_test_cricket | bowling strategies and pitch analysis in test cricket]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:39">[00:44:39]</a>. It has become increasingly difficult to score runs in the first 40 overs of a Test match in India over the past two to three years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:41">[00:44:41]</a>. This phenomenon is largely due to the SG ball's reaction, which behaves differently in the early overs, usually favoring spin but sometimes pace <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:24">[00:10:24]</a>.

If a batting team can navigate through the tough period between 20-40 overs when the ball is still hard, runs become more readily available as the ball softens, typically around the 40-over mark <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:55">[00:08:55]</a>. Teams that fail to preserve wickets during this phase, like Bangladesh, find themselves without batters to capitalize when conditions ease <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:30">[00:09:30]</a>.

This highlights the importance of partnerships, as seen with India's Rishabh Pant, Yashasvi Jaiswal, and Dhruv Jurel, who managed to avoid losing many wickets during this crucial period, allowing the ball to soften for later batters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:20">[00:08:20]</a>. Their strategy allowed them to "make sure that in that section of time there weren't any more wickets" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:41">[00:08:41]</a>.

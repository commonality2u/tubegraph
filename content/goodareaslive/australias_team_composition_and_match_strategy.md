## Strategic Imperatives
Given their bowling limitations, Australia's strategy had to involve scoring "slightly above par" runs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:31">[00:21:31]</a>. This necessity pushed players like Smith to take risks, and regular wickets falling became a costly element of their approach <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:36">[00:21:36]</a>. Unlike India, who could afford to play conservatively and minimize dot balls, Australia "had to score quicker" due to their less potent bowling lineup <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:11">[00:08:11]</a>. Australia took "safer options" in the field and with selection, such as choosing Cooper Connolly, when perhaps more randomness was needed to challenge India <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:53">[00:04:53]</a>.

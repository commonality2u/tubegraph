## Series Overview and Key Moments
The series began with a significant setback for England, as they were "thumped" in the first game in Ahmedabad, losing by nine wickets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:35">[00:39:35]</a>. However, England demonstrated resilience, going on to win matches at two of India's most historic home grounds: the Wankhede Stadium and Eden Gardens <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:57">[00:39:57]</a>.

*   **Alastair Cook** - As captain, Cook led from the front, scoring three centuries, two of which were described as "daddy hundreds" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:43">[00:39:43]</a>. His prolific scoring in the series helped solidify his reputation as "England's best batter ever" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:41">[00:42:41]</a>.
*   **Graeme Swann** - The off-spinner was instrumental, taking 20 wickets throughout the series <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:50">[00:39:50]</a>. His "emergence" was a significant factor in England's success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:16">[00:42:16]</a>.

## Context and Significance
This victory was particularly notable given England's previous struggles in Asian conditions. Prior to this series, England had "not done well" in Asia, having lost to Pakistan in the UAE (the "adal series") around 2011/2012 and experiencing difficulties against Sri Lanka, where Mahela Jayawardene played an "incredible innings" against them at Galle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:31">[00:41:31]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:51">[00:41:51]</a>.

Despite the strength of the Indian side, England's performance was an "accomplishment" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:50">[00:40:50]</a>.

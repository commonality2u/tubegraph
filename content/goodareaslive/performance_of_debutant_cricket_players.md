### First-Class Background and Test Transition
Padikkal's first-class record shows he averaged 36 at age 22 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:00:00">[20:00:00]</a>. He didn't initially "line the world on fire" in first-class [[cricket_and_player_performances | cricket]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:51:00">[19:51:00]</a>. On Test debut, he appeared less sure of himself than players like Jaiswal or Sarfaraz Khan, who came into Test [[cricket_and_player_performances | cricket]] with higher first-class averages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:09:00">[20:09:00]</a>.

His talent was recognized earlier, as he became a very good IPL player around 2019-2022 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:35:00">[20:35:00]</a>. This year (his age 23 season), he has made four hundreds, including one against Arjun Tendulkar for Goa and another against Punjab, as well as against England Lions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:47:00">[20:47:00]</a>. His selection is seen as timely, picked at a good age, giving him a chance to learn in a new position <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:14:00">[21:14:00]</a>.

Padikkal's second Test innings was his second time batting outside the top three in first-class [[cricket_and_player_performances | cricket]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:32:00">[21:32:00]</a>, indicating he is learning a new position on the fly. Despite coming in during a high-pressure situation after double wickets, he handled it well <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:08:00">[22:08:00]</a>.

## Sarfaraz Khan

Sarfaraz Khan made his debut and also knocked up another hundred <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:28:00">[01:28:00]</a>. He is described as a "tiny King" when batting with the tall Padikkal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:34:00">[16:34:00]</a>.

His playing style is notable for his hand movement and ability to manipulate the field by playing late <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:39:00">[34:39:00]</a>. While considered brilliant in Asian conditions, there is uncertainty about his clear path to success outside Asia without adaptations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:52:00">[34:52:00]</a>. However, his immense talent suggests he can overcome these challenges <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:13:00">[35:13:00]</a>.

Concerns exist about the long-term international careers of Sarfaraz and Dev Padikkal compared to Dhruv Jurel <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:10:00">[34:10:00]</a>.

## Dhruv Jurel

Dhruv Jurel is perceived to have the technique to score runs in challenging conditions like Australia, though his readiness to adapt remains to be seen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:27:00">[27:27:00]</a>. His technique is believed to be within his grasp for success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:09:00">[28:09:00]</a>.

Jurel's potential for a long Test career is highlighted due to his role as a wicket-keeper batter <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:10:00">[34:10:00]</a>.

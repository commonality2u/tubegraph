## Ravindra Jadeja's Evolving Role

Ravindra Jadeja's batting journey has seen several phases, from a young player making triple hundreds to a more "sloggy" lower-order batsman <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:43">[00:03:43]</a>. He has now developed into a genuine number six batsman, capable of batting at number five, though he might be at his best at number seven <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:01">[00:04:01]</a>.

Historically, before his batting revolution, Jadeja was primarily a handy number eight, a position that didn't provide the team with a significant [[role_of_allrounders_in_cricket_teams | all-round]] boost <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:40">[00:04:40]</a>. However, his rise in the batting order has been successful <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:06">[00:06:06]</a>. His batting average at home between 2013 and 2017 was a suboptimal 32, which has dramatically improved to a game-changing 57 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:20">[00:05:20]</a>.

Jadeja's current style involves scoring with "rough gear changes" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:39">[00:03:39]</a>. For instance, in a recent match, he maintained a strike rate of 66 until tea, largely by attacking left-arm finger spinners and capitalizing on poor bowling <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:44">[00:02:44]</a>. After tea, as England's bowling improved, he focused on staying in, taking a long time to reach his 50 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:11">[00:03:11]</a>. While he has a good strike rate against left-arm finger spin, he is slower against off-spin and seam, but remains very difficult to dismiss <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:36">[00:05:36]</a>.

## Impact on Team Composition

Jadeja's current batting position at number six (or sometimes five) allows India significant flexibility in team selection <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:01">[00:04:01]</a>. This enables the team to include extra spinners, which is crucial for their strategy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:07">[00:05:07]</a>. The combined [[importance_of_allrounders_in_indias_odi_team | all-round talents]] of Jadeja, Axar Patel, and Ravichandran Ashwin further strengthen India's side <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:10">[00:05:10]</a>. If Rishabh Pant returns, Jadeja could potentially move back to number seven, further strengthening the team <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:30">[00:04:30]</a>.

## Challenges and Future Development

A significant challenge for India is the absence of a [[role_of_allrounders_in_cricket_teams | seam-bowling all-rounder]] like Hardik Pandya, whose unavailability is a major issue for the team <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:42">[00:46:42]</a>.

Additionally, India needs its batters, especially younger players like Shubman Gill, to feel more confident and make runs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:53">[00:46:53]</a>. The prevailing conditions in India, often dominated by bowlers, make it challenging for new batsmen to settle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:50">[00:24:50]</a>. While the current pitch is good for batting, many previous pitches at home have made it hard to score <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:06">[00:47:06]</a>.

There is a discussion about potentially using players like Shubman Gill in roles that suit their strengths while developing their weaknesses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:28">[00:22:28]</a>. This approach, common with bowlers who are rested or rotated based on conditions, is less frequently applied to batsmen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:03">[00:23:03]</a>. For instance, Shubman Gill has performed very well in Australia, but less so in India and other seam-friendly conditions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:41">[00:21:41]</a>. It is suggested that moving a talented young batter like Gill down the order, as Virat Kohli and Ricky Ponting experienced early in their careers, could ease pressure and aid development <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:33">[00:23:33]</a>.

Regardless, it was a score that proved challenging for South Africa on a "belter of a surface" where batting conditions were "basically a road" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:27">[00:02:27]</a>.

## Key Batters and Contributions

*   **Rachin Ravindra**: The player of the match, Ravindra scored his second century of the Champions Trophy, 108 off 101 deliveries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:03">[00:39:03]</a>. He countered both pace and spin effectively, showcasing pull shots, wristy flicks, punches through covers, and excellent use of his feet against spin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:29">[00:39:29]</a>. His ability to consistently stay ahead of the game, going at better than a run a ball, was crucial for New Zealand <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:46">[00:41:46]</a>. He also set a record as the fastest to five ODI tons in ICC 50-over events, achieving it in 13 innings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:17">[00:39:17]</a>.
*   **Kane Williamson**: Williamson scored 102 off 94 balls <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:07">[00:40:07]</a>. Initially, he was not scoring at a run a ball (40 off 57 deliveries at one point) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:34">[00:42:34]</a>, but he managed a significant catch-up knock <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:05">[00:42:05]</a>. His innings included intent shots like a reverse sweep and scoops, demonstrating his adaptability to team requirements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:13">[00:42:13]</a>. He was also dropped on 56, an opportunity that South Africa failed to capitalize on <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:36">[00:43:36]</a>.
*   **Daryl Mitchell**: Mitchell found form and contributed 49 off 37 deliveries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:50">[00:47:50]</a>. His ability to knock the ball around when Williamson was exploding allowed him to settle in <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:38">[00:49:38]</a>. He was also dropped on 39 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:05">[00:48:05]</a>.
*   **Glenn Phillips**: Phillips provided a vital unbeaten 49 off 27 balls at the death <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:53">[00:47:53]</a>, smashing four fours off Jansen and taking Rabada to the cleaners <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:56">[00:50:56]</a>. His power-hitting is a significant asset for New Zealand, though there's a concern about over-reliance on his firepower <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:04">[00:51:04]</a>.

## Batting Strategy

New Zealand's batting order worked "exactly as they wanted" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:13">[00:49:13]</a>. Ravindra's brisk start put pressure on South Africa <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:15">[00:49:15]</a>. Even when Williamson got stuck, Ravindra's performance allowed him to play his natural game <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:27">[00:49:27]</a>. This cascading effect continued with Mitchell finding form while Williamson was attacking, and then Phillips came in without immediate pressure to slug <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:43">[00:49:43]</a>. This enabled them to become one of the "high-scoring teams" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:08">[00:50:08]</a>.

A notable exception to their otherwise effective strategy was the decision to promote Tom Latham ahead of Glenn Phillips with 11-12 overs remaining <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:48">[00:48:48]</a>. This was described as "baffling" given Phillips' power-hitting ability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:50">[00:48:50]</a>.

## Challenges for the Final

Heading into the final against [[indias_batting_and_bowling_strategies | India]], New Zealand faces new challenges:
*   **Pitch Conditions**: The final will be played in Dubai, and the surface is expected to be "sticky" rather than a "belter" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:25">[00:45:25]</a>. The exact nature of the pitch used will be crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:31">[00:57:31]</a>.
*   **Indian Bowling**: New Zealand's spinners (Santner, Bracewell, Phillips, Ravindra) are not considered to be at the same level as [[indias_bowling_strategies_and_performance | India's]] primary spin options like Varun Chakravarthy, Kuldeep Yadav, or Ravindra Jadeja <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:50">[00:54:50]</a>.
*   **Experience in Dubai**: New Zealand is the only team in the tournament that has played in Dubai previously <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:28">[00:55:28]</a>.
*   **Batting Order Rigidity**: Despite having players with specific skills who can move around, New Zealand is perceived as "dogmatic" with their batting lineup, not always using players in the "right situations" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:37">[00:59:37]</a>. For a spinning wicket, there's a suggestion to promote Latham to number four to handle early middle overs spin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:12">[00:59:12]</a>.
*   **Toss Factor**: The toss in Dubai can play a significant role <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:20">[01:08:20]</a>.

To win, New Zealand will need to bring their "A-game," continuing their brilliant fielding performance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:51">[00:58:51]</a>, and ensure their batters don't lose wickets in the middle overs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:03">[01:09:03]</a>. Achieving a first innings total of 280-290 and then leveraging strong new ball bowling would be key <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:00">[01:09:00]</a>.

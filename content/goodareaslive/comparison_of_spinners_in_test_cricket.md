## Technical [[Comparison of Spin and Pace Bowlers | Comparison of Spinners]]

A detailed analysis of bowling metrics reveals the sophistication of the Indian spinners compared to Bangladesh's:

### Median Degrees of Turn
*   **Ravindra Jadeja**: Tends to maintain a consistent amount of spin throughout the game, not significantly trying to "rag" the ball <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:12:00">[09:12:00]</a>.
*   **Ravichandran Ashwin**: Shows a notable increase in spin towards the end of the game, with his median degrees of turn going well over four degrees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:21:00">[09:21:00]</a>. He may present a "softer spinning face" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:32:00">[09:32:00]</a>, similar to how Shane Warne or Mushtaq Ahmed would bowl <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:37:00">[09:37:00]</a>. He can still spin the ball hard when needed but not consistently over four degrees in the same way Jadeja does <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:50:00">[09:50:00]</a>. Ashwin also uses the rough on the pitch to slide the ball on before making it vicious <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:11:00">[10:11:00]</a>.

### Bowling Pace (km/h)
*   **Ravindra Jadeja**: Bowls at a top pace of almost 94 km/h <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:37:00">[10:37:00]</a>.
*   **Shakib Al Hasan**: His top pace is around 83 km/h, a difference of about 10 km/h from Jadeja <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:53:00">[10:53:00]</a>.
*   **Mehedi Hasan Miraz**: Bowls at roughly the same speed as Ashwin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:16:00">[11:16:00]</a>.
*   **Ravichandran Ashwin**: Can get his pace up to 95-96 km/h, while neither Bangladeshi spinner goes past 92 km/h <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:06:00">[13:06:00]</a>.

### Bowling Modes (Variations in Speed)
*   **Ravindra Jadeja**: Possesses three distinct modes: his standard delivery, a slightly quicker ball, and a slower delivery that is about 5 km/h slower than his standard pace (~89 km/h) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:23:00">[11:23:00]</a>.
*   **Mehedi Hasan Miraz**: Primarily has two modes: his normal ball and a slower ball <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:00:00">[12:00:00]</a>. He lacks a "faster ball kick" that Jadeja demonstrates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:54:00">[11:54:00]</a>.
*   **Shakib Al Hasan**: Also primarily has two modes: his normal ball and a slightly faster ball <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:17:00">[12:17:00]</a>.
*   **Ravichandran Ashwin**: Shows the most variety, with a significant cluster of deliveries around 80-82 km/h, his main pace around 85-86 km/h, and a "kick up" ball that is even quicker <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:42:00">[12:42:00]</a>.

The Indian spinners, Jadeja and Ashwin, are described as "three-dimensional spinners" due to their ability to vary pace with slow, normal, and quicker deliveries. In contrast, the Bangladeshi spinners, Mehedi Hasan Miraz and Shakib, are considered "two-dimensional," having only a normal and either a faster or slower option <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:18:00">[13:18:00]</a>. This depth in their arsenal makes the Indian spinners significantly more versatile and harder to predict <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:52:00">[13:52:00]</a>.

Ashwin's pitch maps further illustrate his precision; against left-handers, he consistently lands the ball in the same spot, while against right-handers, his deliveries are a bit more variable, targeting the footmarks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:59:00">[13:59:00]</a>.

England's Test cricket strategy, colloquially known as "Bazball," represents a significant shift in their approach to the game. It emphasizes aggressive, proactive play, aiming to put constant pressure on the opposition, regardless of the match situation.

## Core Philosophy and Characteristics

Under [[englands_batting_strategy_and_basball_philosophy|Bazball]], England plays with a philosophy of not being "killed," constantly keeping themselves within "touching distance of another win or at least loss" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:34:00">[01:34:00]</a>. The team is characterized as being "the Cockroach of modern test matches," or "more like a scorpion," always having "a big sting" and being "a chance" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:41:00">[01:41:00]</a>.

The strategic shift to [[impact_of_basball_on_england_cricket|Bazball]] was largely a "reaction to the fact they didn't have any batting" in the period before its inception <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="55:39:00">[55:39:00]</a>. England was "terrible" before [[impact_of_basball_on_england_cricket|Bazball]], losing "12 of 13 test matches" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:07">[01:09:07]</a>. The aim was to "maximize their talent knowing that they were a flawed team" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:36">[01:09:36]</a>. This means getting the most out of players like [[impact_of_basball_on_england_cricket|Zak Crawley]], even if he isn't expected to average 45 in Test cricket <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:50">[01:09:50]</a>.

The style of cricket is "highly volatile" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:48">[01:18:48]</a>, with the potential for either significant success or heavy losses.

## Batting Strategy

England's openers, such as [[impact_of_basball_on_england_cricket|Ben Duckett]] and [[impact_of_basball_on_england_cricket|Zak Crawley]], are instructed to "capitalize on that new ball and put pressure back on India" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:17:00">[41:17:00]</a>. There is an inherent "recklessness in their batting," as they are "told to get their runs before they get out" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:22:00">[41:22:00]</a>. While this approach has been "pretty good," achieving better results than anticipated, it has been noted that the middle order has often let the team down, rather than the top order <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="42:24:00">[42:24:00]</a>.

It has been suggested that a refined approach for openers could be to "get to 50 or get to 30 before there's a ball of your name on it and then afterwards if you've already set the game up just milk from then on in" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:30:00">[41:30:00]</a>.

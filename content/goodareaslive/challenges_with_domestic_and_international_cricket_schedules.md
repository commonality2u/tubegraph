## Lack of Clarity and Consistency in PCB Structure

Historically, the Pakistan Cricket Board (PCB) has faced a perceived lack of professionalism, with frequent changes in chairman, selectors, and coaches <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:53">[00:00:53]</a>. This inconsistency hurts [[challenges_faced_by_south_africa_cricket_team|Pakistan cricket]] as a lack of clarity prevents consistent results <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:14">[00:01:14]</a>.

To address this, the PCB introduced a Champions tournament system and hired five mentors, giving them significant authority over 30-35 selected players <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:39">[00:01:39]</a>. This new structure aims to bring more consistency to Pakistan cricket, though it will take time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:21">[00:02:21]</a>. The mentors, many of whom are former Pakistan captains (Younis Khan, Misbah-ul-Haq, Shoaib Malik, Sarfaraz Ahmed), have a three-year contract and are required to work 240 days a year at high-performance centers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:27">[00:03:27]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:02">[00:04:02]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:39">[00:04:39]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:35">[00:05:35]</a>. Their role extends beyond traditional mentoring to include coaching, tactical input, player development, and fostering professionalism among players <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:46">[00:02:46]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:05">[00:05:05]</a>.

The PCB's communication regarding these new roles has been a concern, leading to public misunderstanding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:05">[00:13:05]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:08">[00:15:08]</a>. It is crucial for the PCB to provide clarity to both its employees and the public <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:18">[00:13:18]</a>.

## No-Objection Certificates (NOCs) and Global Leagues

A significant [[challenges_and_political_issues_in_international_cricket|challenge]] for the PCB is balancing players' desire to participate in global franchise leagues for earnings and learning, with the board's need to control player availability and ensure rest <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:31">[00:25:31]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:01">[00:26:01]</a>.

A "middle ground" is needed where players can pursue opportunities globally while prioritizing domestic tournaments as their base <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:27">[00:26:27]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:16">[00:27:16]</a>. Restricting players to a set number of NOCs is seen as counterproductive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:57">[00:27:57]</a>. Instead, players should be allowed to play in leagues during free windows, provided their workload is managed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:04">[00:28:04]</a>.

Playing in diverse leagues helps players become more professional, teaching them adaptability to different cultures and environments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:29">[00:28:29]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:39">[00:28:39]</a>. Denying NOCs, especially when players are not actively playing domestic cricket, deters international franchises from picking Pakistani cricketers, creating a problematic pathway <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:01">[00:30:01]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:16">[00:30:16]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:24">[00:30:24]</a>.

One example cited was when Pakistani players had to leave the Caribbean Premier League (CPL) to return to Pakistan for fitness tests, leading to their franchises replacing them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:46">[00:30:46]</a>. This highlights the need for better planning and coordination from the PCB.

## World Cup Performance and Challenges

In the T20 World Cup, Afghanistan's journey to the semi-finals highlighted both their strengths and the challenges they face.

### Semi-Final Against South Africa
Afghanistan was "absolutely obliterated" by South Africa in the semi-final in Trinidad, being bowled out for just 56 runs in 11.5 overs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:21">[01:02:21]</a>. The surface was heavily criticized as "absolutely dreadful" and an "embarrassment to the ICC," resembling New York's wicket with dangerously inconsistent bounce, making it "not fit for a World Cup semi-final" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:31">[01:02:31]</a>. Many, including Jonathan Trott, deemed it "simply not a T20 wicket" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:00">[01:03:00]</a>.

Afghanistan won the toss and elected to bat first, their usual strategy as a "bat first team" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:19">[01:05:19]</a>. However, this backfired, with Marco Jansen dismissing Rahmanullah Gurbaz for a duck <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:33">[01:05:33]</a>. The team was heavily reliant on their opening pair, Gurbaz and Ibrahim Zadran, who scored 57.2% of Afghanistan's runs in the tournament and were among the top five run-scorers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:44">[01:05:44]</a>. On such a difficult surface, with South Africa's bowlers "breathing fire," Afghanistan "never truly stood a chance" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:13">[01:06:13]</a>. The semi-final performance did not suit Afghanistan's bowlers as much as South Africa's <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:35">[01:06:35]</a>.

Afghanistan's highest scorer in the semi-final was Azmatullah, with just 10 runs, while extras were the highest "scorer" with 13 runs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:16">[01:12:16]</a>. The team's flight was delayed, preventing them from having training sessions in Trinidad, which was an unfortunate scheduling issue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:06">[01:08:06]</a>.

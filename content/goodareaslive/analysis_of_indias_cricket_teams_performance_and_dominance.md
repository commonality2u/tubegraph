## Challenges and Controversies
Despite their on-field dominance, India's recent successes are set against a backdrop of wider cricketing issues:

*   **ICC Tournament Issues**: The Champions Trophy itself was criticized for being canceled three times, not being played entirely in its supposed host country, and having one team refuse to turn up <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:00:00">[05:00:00]</a>. This has led to public anger and calls for an "asterisk" next to the trophy, though the Indian players themselves are deemed undeserving of such a mark <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:02:00">[06:02:00]</a>.
*   **Political and Commercial Influence**: Cricket is a sport impacted by geopolitics more than any other major sport <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:36:00">[12:36:00]</a>. The fixing of fixtures (e.g., [[India vs Australia Test Cricket Analysis | Australia]], England, India, Pakistan always playing each other) is seen as a form of corruption driven by commercial interests <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:43:00">[08:43:00]</a>. The players are often exploited in this situation, with grueling travel schedules, like India's "caravan road show" in the 2023 World Cup <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:42:00">[11:42:00]</a>.
*   **Toss Conspiracy Theories**: In the 2023 World Cup, there were daily accusations that the tosses were fixed for India <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:26:00">[20:26:00]</a>. Ironically, India lost the toss 15 consecutive times, with Rohit Sharma losing 12 in a row, equaling Brian Lara's record. The probability of this is 0.003% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:01:00">[20:01:00]</a>.
*   **Fielding Inconsistencies**: India's fielding had lapses in the Champions Trophy final, dropping four out of six catching opportunities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:47:00">[27:47:00]</a>. While not detrimental in this instance, it highlights an area for improvement, especially compared to teams like New Zealand with their "disruptive fielding" led by Glenn Phillips <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:35:00">[26:35:00]</a>.
*   **Player Performances**: While overall dominant, some individual innings faced criticism. Daryl Mitchell's 63 off 101 balls in the final, New Zealand's slowest 50 in 11 years, was seen as a "detrimental knock" that cost New Zealand 25-30 crucial runs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:30:00">[30:30:00]</a>.

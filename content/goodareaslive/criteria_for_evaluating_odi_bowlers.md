## Selection and Seeding Methodology

To determine the initial pool of 64 best ODI bowlers, the process involved first identifying approximately 20 top spinners and then filling the remaining spots with pace bowlers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:40">[00:00:40]</a>. For the tournament draw, players were ranked, aiming for a fairer seeding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:20">[00:01:20]</a>.

## Adjusted Statistics

A key component of the evaluation was the use of adjusted statistics. This method involved comparing bowlers' performances to the average of their era and then normalizing those figures to the current era <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:51">[00:00:51]</a>. The underlying assumption of this linear adjustment is that a bowler who was, for example, 20% better than their era's average would maintain that relative performance in the present day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>. However, it's acknowledged that this may not always hold true, as specific skills might translate better or worse across different eras <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:06">[00:01:06]</a>.

The main adjusted metrics used are:
*   **Adjusted Strike Rate:** Wickets taken per balls bowled.
*   **Adjusted Economy:** Runs conceded per over.

## Other Considerations in Evaluation

*   **Longevity vs. Peak Performance:** Discussions often weigh a player's long career and total wickets (e.g., Shaun Pollock with "almost 400 wickets" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:31">[00:04:31]</a> or Muttiah Muralitharan with "540 something" wickets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:36">[01:11:36]</a>) against a shorter, highly impactful peak (e.g., Ajantha Mendis's "great 18 months" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:51">[00:37:51]</a> or Shane Bond's career being cut short by injury <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:16">[01:09:16]</a>).
*   **Role of the Bowler:** Distinctions are made between "defensive" bowlers (e.g., Harbhajan Singh <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:31">[00:06:31]</a>, Daniel Vettori <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:29">[00:15:29]</a>, R. Ashwin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:46">[00:39:46]</a>) and "attacking" or "strike" bowlers (e.g., Mohammad Shami <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:00">[00:41:00]</a>, Mitchell Starc <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:01">[00:29:01]</a>). Some bowlers like Muttiah Muralitharan are noted for being both "strike" bowlers and having great defensive numbers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:35">[01:12:35]</a>.
*   **Impact in Tournaments:** A player's performance in major events like the World Cup is considered, for example, Shane Bond's "electric" performance in the 2003 World Cup <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:16">[00:22:16]</a> or Imran Khan's 1987 World Cup performance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:16">[01:05:16]</a> where he averaged 13 in a tournament where others took few wickets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:27">[01:05:27]</a>. This relates to [[Bowlers Impact in ICC Tournaments | bowlers impact in ICC Tournaments]].
*   **Minimum Wicket Criteria:** For pace bowlers, a minimum of 146 wickets was generally applied, with some exceptions for good bowlers between 100 and 146 wickets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:02">[00:05:02]</a>.
*   **Player Skill and Perception:** Beyond raw or adjusted numbers, subjective qualities like being "unplayable" (e.g., Ajantha Mendis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:30">[00:37:30]</a>) or a player's "ceiling" (e.g., Shaheen Afridi <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:11">[00:50:11]</a>) also influence perceptions. The "theater" of a player like Shane Bond <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:15">[00:22:15]</a> or Mitchell Johnson being "rock and roll" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:27">[00:08:27]</a> are also mentioned, suggesting an emotional element in evaluation.

## Early Career and Initial Setback

Nair achieved the rare feat of scoring a triple century in a Test match <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:26">[00:01:26]</a>. Despite this high point, his international career took a sharp turn. In 2018, he traveled to England with the Indian Test squad for two and a half to three months but did not play a single Test match <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:02">[00:03:02]</a>. Compounding the situation, Hanuma Vihari was brought into the squad from outside and played ahead of him <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:17">[00:03:17]</a>. Following this tour, Nair was controversially axed from the team <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:28">[00:03:28]</a>.

This period had a profound impact on him, both as a person and a cricketer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:51">[00:03:51]</a>. The subsequent year and a half were described as "soul-arching," with his [[cricket_and_player_performances | performances]] in [[first_class_cricket | first-class cricket]] dropping significantly, leading him into a "wilderness" period <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:58">[00:03:58]</a>. Being a reserved individual, he largely navigated these mental health challenges on his own <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:20">[00:04:20]</a>. The 2021-22 season also proved difficult, culminating in his unceremonious omission from the Karnataka [[Ranji_Trophy | Ranji Trophy]] side <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:33">[00:04:33]</a>.

## The Path to Resurgence

Despite these setbacks, Nair's innate talent was never in doubt; he had, for instance, scored a triple hundred in a [[Ranji_Trophy | Ranji Trophy]] final in 2015-16, leading Karnataka to a championship victory <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:57">[00:04:57]</a>.

In late 2022 or early 2023, he confided in fellow cricketer Robin Uthappa, expressing his uncertainty about his future <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:10">[00:05:10]</a>. Crucially, he affirmed his desire to play for India again <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:41">[00:05:41]</a>. At 31 years old, he made a conscious decision to dedicate two more years fully to cricket <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:49">[00:05:49]</a>.

For six months, he trained alone, putting in consistent, unnoticed work <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:09">[00:06:09]</a>. This dedication paid off when he received a call from a county side, believed to be Northamptonshire <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:28">[00:06:28]</a>. Upon his return from England, he joined Vidarbha, an older state side in [[Ranji_Trophy | Ranji Trophy]] [[domestic_cricket | cricket]], and had a decent first season with them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:07">[00:07:07]</a>.

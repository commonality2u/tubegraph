## England's World Cup 2023 Defense: A Historical Low
England's performance in the 2023 World Cup has been described as potentially "the worst World Cup defense we have ever seen" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:07:54">[02:07:54]</a>. The team, being the first dual World Cup holders (ODI and T20) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:09:47">[03:09:47]</a> and known for playing Test cricket like One Day Internationals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:13:06">[03:13:06]</a>, has "attacked last place" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:15:20">[02:15:20]</a> in the tournament.

After six games, England has won only one and lost five <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:57:04">[03:57:04]</a>, making their defense potentially worse than:
*   [[australian_cricket_team_performance_trends | Australia's]] 1992 campaign, where they went 4-4 in eight games <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:26:01">[03:26:01]</a>.
*   Sri Lanka's 1999 defense, where they won two and lost three out of five games <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:24:26">[03:24:26]</a>.

Key indicators of England's struggle include:
*   **Batting Average**: They are the second-worst batting team by average in the tournament, only marginally better than the Netherlands <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:26:06">[03:26:06]</a>.
*   **Balls Per Wicket**: England loses a wicket every 24 balls on average, which equates to every four overs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:28:15">[03:28:15]</a>. This regression to "40 over cricket" contradicts their established "Pro 40" success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:44:00">[03:44:00]</a>.
*   **Player Form**: Key players have not met expectations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:17:09">[03:17:09]</a>. Notably, Joe Root, considered an "insurance batter" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:35:05">[03:35:05]</a>, has averaged 4.54 at a strike rate of 37 in the power play when batting early since the 2019 World Cup, being dismissed 11 times in 18 instances <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:29:08">[03:29:08]</a>. Chris Woakes, who was a premier bowler in the 2015-2019 era for taking new ball wickets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:08:14">[02:08:14]</a>, has struggled, particularly against left-handers, due to his transition from swing bowling to wobble-ball bowling <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:00:03">[03:00:03]</a>.
*   **Lack of ODI Cricket**: England's focus on T20 and Test cricket (Bazball) has led to them not playing enough One Day Internationals with their best players <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:12:00">[04:12:00]</a>.

## General Trends and Other Team Performances in World Cup 2023
The 2023 World Cup has evolved into a "wickety World Cup," characterized by more wickets being taken, especially by seam bowlers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:58:00">[01:58:00]</a>.
*   **Bowling Types**: While spin-friendly wickets were expected, off-spinners are averaging around 50, whereas left-arm finger spin and seam bowlers are taking a lot of wickets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:00">[01:19:00]</a>. This highlights the importance of strong bowling attacks for success.
*   **Top Teams**: India, New Zealand, and South Africa have particularly high batting averages, indicating their ability to score consistently and not lose wickets cheaply <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:49:00">[01:49:00]</a>. These top teams also possess bowling attacks capable of taking many wickets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:54:00">[01:54:00]</a>.
*   **South Africa**: While strong, South Africa's chasing ability has been tested, losing to a "poor team" and nearly losing to a "struggling team" in their two chases so far <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:14:00">[02:14:00]</a>.
*   **Afghanistan**: England's current role in the World Cup is ironically similar to what Afghanistan often does: putting pressure on good teams without always challenging for victory <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:50:00">[01:50:00]</a>. Afghanistan is currently performing better than England in this World Cup <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:58:00">[01:58:00]</a>.

The tournament emphasizes the need for teams to handle the short ball and score in multiple ways against various types of bowling <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:29:00">[04:29:00]</a>. The ability to perform well in all three formats of cricket simultaneously is challenging, though teams like England were considered good at all three formats coming into this World Cup <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:41:00">[04:41:00]</a>.

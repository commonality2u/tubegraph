From: [[founderspodcast1]] <br/> 

Brad Jacobs, a seasoned CEO and serial entrepreneur, has shared valuable insights into the process of starting and building billion-dollar companies. With a career spanning over four decades, Jacobs has founded seven separate billion-dollar enterprises, including five from scratch, and has completed approximately 500 acquisitions. In this article, we explore the strategies, principles, and practices Brad Jacobs has utilized to build successful enterprises over the years.

## Key Insights from Brad Jacobs

### Learn from Mistakes and Blunders

Jacobs openly admits to making a myriad of mistakes during his tenure as a CEO and entrepreneur. These include overpaying for acquisitions, botched integrations, hiring the wrong people, and making strategic bets that didn't pay off. Despite these blunders, Jacobs and his teams have managed to create tens of billions of dollars in shareholder value.<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>

> [!info] Acknowledging Mistakes
> 
> Jacobs believes that acknowledging and learning from mistakes is a crucial step toward building a successful business.

### Starting from Scratch

Brad Jacobs began his career in 1979 with the founding of Amorex Oil Associates, a privately-owned oil brokerage. Despite having little experience and only a few thousand dollars, he quickly scaled the business to 4.7 billion in annual brokerage volume, with operations worldwide, and sold it within four years.<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>

### Embracing New Opportunities

Jacobs has a knack for seizing new opportunities across various industries. After selling Amorex, he ventured into oil trading with Hamilton Resources and later moved to the rapidly expanding field of solid waste management with United Waste Systems. He took this company public and later sold it for $2.5 billion.<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:30">[00:01:30]</a>

### Building Through Acquisitions

Jacobs's growth strategy often emphasizes acquisitions. For instance, United Rentals, which Jacobs founded to rent construction equipment, became the largest equipment rental company in the world within 13 months, thanks in part to a focused acquisition strategy.<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:30">[00:02:30]</a>

### Venturing into Logistics

In 2011, Jacobs embarked on a new endeavor with XPO Logistics, focusing on freight transportation. Under his leadership, XPO became the seventh best performing stock in the Fortune 500 of the last decade. He later implemented a value creation strategy by dividing XPO into three separate publicly traded companies.<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:00">[00:03:00]</a>

### The Importance of Thinking Differently

Jacobs stresses the need to think differently and to rearrange one's brain to prevail in turbulent environments. He emphasizes the power of first-principles thinking, akin to the guidance shared by [[lessons_from_historical_and_successful_entrepreneurs | Peter Thiel]] on finding value in unexpected places.<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:00">[00:05:00]</a>

### The Role of Thought Experiments

Jacobs dedicates significant time to thought experiments, which help him find clarity and calmness. Inspired by Albert Einstein's use of thought experiments, Jacobs leverages these tools for creative problem-solving and decision-making.<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:00">[00:12:00]</a>

### Overcoming Challenges as Opportunities

Brad Jacobs plans for challenges, viewing them as opportunities to create value. He cites advice from mentors like Ludwig Jesselson and recalls situations where problems led to significant breakthroughs and immense value creation.<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:00">[00:15:00]</a>

### Building Strong Teams

Jacobs highlights the critical role of [[hiring_and_maintaining_talented_teams | hiring and maintaining talented teams]]. He believes in overpaying for top talent, asserting that there's no substitute for intelligence and ambition in driving business success.<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:00">[00:46:00]</a>

> [!info] Overpaying for Talent
>
> Jacobs asserts that it is nearly impossible to overpay for the right talent if you consider their exceptional contributions to your business.

### Corporate Culture and Leadership

A strong corporate culture is essential, and Jacobs places importance on [[building_and_maintaining_a_strong_business_ethos_and_culture | creating an environment]] where team members feel valued and motivated. An organization thrives when it's like a well-hosted party: only those who enhance the vibe and contribute to the goals should be on the guest list.<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:00">[00:45:00]</a>

### The Entrepreneurial Spirit

Jacobs encapsulates the essence of entrepreneurship and encourages others to harness opportunities and maintain a focus on creating value not just for themselves, but for their customers and stakeholders as well.<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:00">[00:54:00]</a>

## Conclusion

Brad Jacobs's career and strategies offer meaningful lessons on risk-taking, handling failures, and leveraging core principles for success. By focusing on strong teams, first-principles thinking, and embracing challenges, Jacobs has built multiple billion-dollar enterprises, demonstrating a replicable framework for aspiring entrepreneurs.

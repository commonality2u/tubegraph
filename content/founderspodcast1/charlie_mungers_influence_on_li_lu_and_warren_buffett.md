## Mentorship to Li Lu

Li Lu, a notable investor, attributes much of his success and transformation in his investment style to the mentorship and influence of [[charlie_mungers_investment_philosophy | Charlie Munger]]. In 2004, Munger became Li Lu's investment partner and subsequently his lifelong mentor and friend, a fortuitous relationship that Li describes as an opportunity he dared not even dream about <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:12">[00:00:12]</a>.

### First Impressions and Advice

When Li Lu first interacted deeply with Munger at a Thanksgiving gathering in 2003, he introduced all the companies he had researched to Charlie. Munger's critical approach was clear as he dissected the Wall Street mindset, suggesting that Li Lu needed to take a path different from traditional Wall Street thinking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:49">[00:00:49]</a>. This encounter led Li Lu to reorganize his company along the lines of the early investment partnerships of [[investment_strategies_of_warren_buffett_and_charlie_munger | Buffett and Munger]], promoting long-term investments and understanding of businesses over short-term market fluctuations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:29">[00:01:29]</a>.

### Unique Thinking

### Inspiration and Learning

Li Lu's insatiable thirst for knowledge and significant insights stem from Munger's encouragement to build innovations on the foundations laid by previous thinkers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:19">[00:04:19]</a>. This perspective has been crucial in how he values deep understanding and rationality in investments.

## Influence on Warren Buffett

Charlie Munger's investment approach significantly shaped Warren Buffett's strategies, steering him away from Benjamin Graham's method of purely seeking cheap stocks. Instead, Munger advocated for investing in strong businesses at fair prices, an approach that Buffett adopted to lead [[investment_strategies_of_warren_buffett_and_charlie_munger | Berkshire Hathaway]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:01">[01:09:01]</a>.

### Synthesis of Philosophy

Buffett credits Munger with altering his investment approach to focus on the quality of businesses rather than relying solely on valuation metrics, fundamentally broadening his investment horizon and understanding of what constitutes a valuable investment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:36">[01:09:36]</a>.

### Rationality and Decision-Making

Munger's influence extends into Buffett's decision-making process, emphasizing rationality and simplification in investments. This leads to focusing only on outstanding opportunities and ignoring anything that does not meet their high standards, thereby streamlining the investment process and leading to phenomenal returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:01">[00:05:01]</a>.

## Conclusion

The influence of Charlie Munger on Li Lu and Warren Buffett is marked by a deep and abiding focus on rationality, intrinsic business value, and a thoughtful approach to learning and decision-making. Both investors attribute significant components of their successful careers to the wisdom imparted by Munger's unique way of thinking, which continues to shape and define their investment philosophies today.

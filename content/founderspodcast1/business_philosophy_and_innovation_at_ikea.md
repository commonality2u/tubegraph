IKEA, the global furniture emporium, owes its success and enduring legacy to the unique business philosophy and innovative spirit of its founder, [[the_founding_and_growth_of_ikea | Ingvar Kamprad]]. This article explores the core tenets that have guided IKEA's operations and the pivotal innovations that have shaped its journey.

## Ingvar Kamprad: The Founder

Ingvar Kamprad, born in Sweden, forged Ikea's expansive empire despite starting life under modest circumstances. Growing up in a harsh, unforgiving environment, Kamprad's upbringing marked him with a resolve that would define his entrepreneurial journey. He was characterized by a persistent pursuit to prove the viability of functional, affordable design to the masses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:03">[00:02:03]</a>.

## Business Philosophy

### Focus on Affordability

One of IKEA's primary objectives is to "create a better everyday life for the many people" by offering a broad range of well-designed, functional home furnishings at prices so low that as many people as possible can afford them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:22">[00:04:22]</a>.

Kamprad emphasized the importance of cost-awareness within the company, stating, "Without low cost we can never accomplish our purpose" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:46">[00:08:46]</a>. His philosophy mandated that IKEA's policy of serving the many could never be changed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:02">[00:09:02]</a>.

### Hard Work and Humbleness

Hard work and humbleness were equally central to IKEA's business culture. Kamprad viewed mistakes as a privilege of the active, maintaining that everyone should engage in developing and motivating co-workers, as well as constantly striving for renewal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:37">[00:10:37]</a>.

### Eternal Life through Innovation

Kamprad aimed to endow IKEA with "eternal life," ensuring resilience and adaptability through continuous innovation and self-reliance. The strict self-imposed rule that all expansions should be self-financed and no boasting about financial success would ensure sustainability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:51">[00:56:51]</a>.

## Key Innovations in IKEA's History

### Mail Order and Showrooms

One of the most significant steps in IKEA’s early history was Kamprad’s decision to combine catalog mail orders with furniture showrooms. This approach allowed customers to physically experience the furniture, which addressed the limitations of traditional mail order <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:32">[00:34:32]</a>.

### Flat Packing and Self-Assembly

The turning point in IKEA's operational model came with the introduction of flat packing and self-assembled furniture. This innovation reduced transportation damage and costs and resulted in significant logistical efficiencies. These benefits translated to lower prices for customers, aligning with Kamprad's core business philosophy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:14">[00:50:14]</a>.

> [!info] Ingenuity from Necessity
>
> Flat packing was a serendipitous discovery. Assembling furniture at home leveraged the cognitive bias known as the IKEA Effect, enhancing customer value perception <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:22">[00:50:22]</a>.

## Conclusion

IKEA's success is a testament to Ingvar Kamprad's commitment to affordability, innovation, and strategic foresight. By adhering to a set of unyielding principles and turning challenges into opportunities, IKEA has not only become the largest furniture retailer worldwide but continues to be a pioneer in the industry. The philosophy of serving the many and creating a better everyday life remains at the core of IKEA's mission, resonating through every piece of their affordable, well-designed furniture.

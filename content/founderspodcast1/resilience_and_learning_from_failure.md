The journey to success is often paved with setbacks and failures. These challenges, however, can be the crucibles that forge resilience and are invaluable for growth and learning. One of the most compelling examples of resilience in the face of failure comes from the insights shared by Ken Griffin, founder of Citadel, as discussed in a recent [podcast episode](#).

## Learning from Others' Mistakes

Griffin's approach to learning from the failures of others highlights a powerful strategy for building resilience. When long-term capital management failed in 1998, Griffin sought to understand how a firm could lose 90% of its equity yet remain in business. By engaging with individuals from long-term capital management, Griffin distilled lessons that would later aid Citadel's survival during the 2008 financial crisis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:01">[34:01]</a>.

> [!quote] Ken Griffin
> 
> "Not only do you want to learn from your mistakes, you really want to learn from other people's mistakes too, because they're much cheaper tuition bills" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:42">[34:42]</a>.

This philosophy echoes the wisdom of Warren Buffett, who famously advised that the best way to learn is from the mistakes of others, akin to gaining a free education <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:54">[34:54]</a>.

## The Role of Personal Mistakes

Griffin also underscores the importance of personal mistakes as learning opportunities. Despite the significant losses Citadel has faced over the years, Griffin views these as the most expensive yet invaluable educational experiences, referring to them as "tuition" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:00">[33:00]</a>.

> [!quote] Ken Griffin
>
> "All of my losses are my tuition and I have the most expensive education in American history" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:03">[33:03]</a>.

This mindset allows for a constructive reflection on failures while maintaining the perspective necessary to continue moving forward.

## Continuous Learning and Psychological Flexibility

The resilience required to endure failures is intrinsically linked to continuous learning and adaptability. Griffin advises maintaining psychological and financial flexibility as strategic advantages that facilitate growth and adaptation to changing circumstances <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:22">[41:22]</a>.

Moreover, Griffin highlights the importance of maintaining a lifelong learning approach, asserting that much of what one needs to succeed lies beyond the confines of formal education <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:00">[21:00]</a>.

> [!quote] Ken Griffin
> 
> "The vast majority of what will matter in your career you have yet to learn" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:02">[21:02]</a>.

## Passion and Drive

Resilience is fueled by passion and a relentless drive for improvement. Drawing inspiration from fellow successful entrepreneurs, Griffin emphasizes that personal expectations should exceed those perceived externally. This internal drive is fundamental in shaping a resilient character <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="39:03">[39:03]</a>.

## Concluding Insights

Resilience and the ability to learn from failure are essential qualities for any successful entrepreneur. The ability to extract lessons from one's own setbacks, and more importantly from others, is a hallmark of enduring business success. Through the lens of companies like Citadel, we see the powerful role that resilience and continual learning play in navigating the complex landscape of business challenges.

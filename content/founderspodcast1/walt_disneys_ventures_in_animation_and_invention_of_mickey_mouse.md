## The Creation of Mickey Mouse

The invention of Mickey Mouse stemmed from adversity. After losing control of his earlier character, Oswald the Rabbit, to a business partner in a managerial coup, Disney found himself back at the starting line. This betrayal served as a learning experience for Disney, emphasizing the importance of retaining control over his creations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:01">[00:38:01]</a>.

On a train ride back to Los Angeles, Disney sketched a mouse character that would eventually become Mickey Mouse. Initially called "Mortimer Mouse," the character was renamed Mickey Mouse following a suggestion by Disney's wife, Lillian <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:46">[00:40:46]</a>.

## Impact on the Animation Industry

Mickey Mouse's debut marked a significant milestone, not just for Disney, but for the entire animation industry. The wild success of Mickey Mouse launched Disney into the spotlight and solidified his reputation as a leading figure in animation. Mickey Mouse became an enduring emblem of Disney's creativity and innovation in animation.

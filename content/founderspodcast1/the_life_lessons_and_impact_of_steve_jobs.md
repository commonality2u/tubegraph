Steve Jobs, a co-founder of Apple Inc., is universally celebrated for his visionary impact on technology and design. His profound influence extends far beyond the products he helped create. His philosophies and approach to life and business continue to inspire entrepreneurs globally.

## Listening to Steve

The best way to understand Steve Jobs is through his own words. Throughout his life, Jobs' speeches, interviews, and writings provided insights into his thinking. He often spoke about the importance of marrying arts and technology, self-imposed rigor, the pursuit of great teams, and fundamentally, what it means to be human <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:10">[00:00:10]</a>.

## Ideals and Work Ethic

Jobs believed in the profound impact of making something wonderful. For him, creating something with care and love was an expression of deep appreciation for humanity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:48">[00:03:48]</a>. His early work ethic was also influenced by his father's teachings. Jobs learned the value of building things, which helped demystify technology and inculcate a belief in human agency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:24">[00:12:24]</a>.

## Lessons on Innovation and Perseverance

Steve's experience at Apple demonstrated his belief in innovative design and focus. For instance, when revamping Apple's product line, he reduced its complexity from 17 products to four, focusing on excellence and simplicity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:00">[01:10:00]</a>. Throughout his career, Jobs emphasized finding great people, echoing his famous saying, "Recruiting is the most important thing" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:43:00">[01:43:00]</a>.

## Embracing Failure and Pursuing Passion

Jobs viewed risks as an inevitable part of life's journey. His time away from Apple was marked by both adversity and growth, reflecting the value he placed on learning from mistakes. His ventures during this period, such as [[ed_catmulls_partnership_and_experiences_with_steve_jobs | Pixar]], while initially struggling, eventually turned into great success stories <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:57">[00:41:57]</a>.

## Philosophy of Life and Death

Steve Jobs frequently spoke about using the inevitability of death as a tool to prioritize life's decisions. He advised that "Your time is limited, so don't waste it living someone else's life" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:55:20">[01:55:20]</a>. This philosophy remained a core component of his advice and reflected his own approach to both professional and personal life.

## Conclusion

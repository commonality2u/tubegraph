In the journey of entrepreneurship, grappling with problems is not only inevitable but also essential. Famed CEO and serial entrepreneur, Brad Jacobs, shares his insights into how problem-solving plays a pivotal role in creating successful business ventures.

## Understanding the Nature of Business

Brad Jacobs, through his extensive career, underscores that business is fundamentally about solving problems. This viewpoint was imparted to him by his most influential business mentor, Ludwig Jesselson. Jesselson emphasized that business involves finding problems, embracing, and even enjoying them because each problem represents an opportunity to remove an obstacle and get closer to success. This philosophy aligns with the wisdom imparted by Danny Meyer’s mentor, Stanley Marcus, who taught him that “the road to success is paved with mistakes well handled” <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:04">[00:15:04]</a>.

> [!quote] Ludwig Jesselson
> "If you want to make money in the business world, you need to get used to problems because that's what business is. It's about finding problems, embracing and even enjoying them because each problem is an opportunity to remove an obstacle and get closer to success."

## Problems as Opportunities

Entrepreneurs like [[Jeff Bezos]] share a similar outlook. Bezos found that instead of being deterred by issues within Amazon, he saw problems like waste as opportunities for years of productivity gains and improvements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:01">[00:16:01]</a>. This approach transforms frustration over hurdles into excitement over potential growth.

## The Role of Mistakes

Mistakes and the ability to learn from them are crucial for business success. Jacobs candidly shared instances where he incurred significant losses, such as the $500 million loss from trying to capitalize on government infrastructure spending that fell through <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:04">[00:24:04]</a>. Rather than lament the loss, Jacobs embraced radical acceptance, understanding the mistake as a lesson and paving the way forward.

## Strategic Thought Experiments

Jacobs advocates for the use of thought experiments, a practice he likens to tapping into a deeper well of creative problem-solving akin to the methods used by Einstein and other visionary thinkers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:51">[00:12:51]</a>. This approach allows entrepreneurs to visualize and prepare for potential challenges creatively and proactively.

## Key Takeaways

### Innovation Through Problem-Solving
Jacobs illustrates that innovative solutions often emerge from adversity. His strategic maneuver in buying back $2 billion worth of stock during a market slump, against conventional advice, ultimately yielded a $4 billion profit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:50">[00:25:50]</a>. This decision highlights the importance of trusting one's instincts and breaking away from formulaic thinking to leverage unique opportunities.

### Continuous Learning from Challenges
Founders are encouraged to embrace setbacks as learning opportunities. Echoing the sentiments of [[Charlie Munger]], who underscores the necessity of expecting and overcoming problems, Jacobs’ experiences reinforce the idea that continuous learning from challenges can pave the way for monumental success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:43">[00:20:43]</a>.

> [!info] Embracing Challenges
> "In the four decades since that lunch with Mr. Jesselson, I've dealt with nearly every problem you can imagine... Big gunky problems can be where the best opportunities lie." – Brad Jacobs

Jacobs’ perspective, rooted in decades of entrepreneurial success, provides a timeless reminder of the power of problem-solving as a cornerstone of sustained business growth and innovation. Aspiring business leaders are encouraged to view problems not as hindrances but as gateways to achieving remarkable success.

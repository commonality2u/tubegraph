Quentin Tarantino, a filmmaker celebrated for his [[quentin_tarantinos_unique_filmmaking_style_and_career|unique style and storytelling]] prowess, had a formative journey heavily influenced by mentorship and missed opportunities. One particularly significant figure in Tarantino's early life was Floyd Ray Wilson, whose mentorship—albeit flawed—played a crucial role in shaping Tarantino's cinematic journey.

## Meeting Floyd Ray Wilson

Floyd Ray Wilson, a man in his thirties during the late 1970s, became a part of Tarantino's life when he dated his mother's best friend, Jackie. Tarantino, at the age of 10, was introduced to Floyd as someone who shared his passion for movies. Jackie mentioned to Tarantino, "Floyd's who you should talk to about movies; he knows as much as you do" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:36">[00:00:36]</a>. 

## Lessons in Unreliability

Despite Floyd's initial role as a mentor, Tarantino quickly learned harsh lessons about reliability and trust. Floyd often promised to take Tarantino to the movies but frequently failed to show up, leaving a young Tarantino heartbroken <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:05">[00:01:05]</a>. This early experience taught Tarantino the importance of reliability and the impact that broken promises can have, especially on a child <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:02">[00:02:02]</a>.

## A Flawed Mentor

Floyd moved into Tarantino's home under the pretense of keeping an eye on the young Quentin, whose mother was often away for work <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:30">[00:02:30]</a>. Despite Floyd's personal issues and flaky nature, his ambition to be a screenwriter left a lasting impression on Tarantino. Floyd had written two screenplays, which were the first that Tarantino ever read. These experiences planted the seed for Tarantino's future interest in writing and creating films <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:07">[00:06:07]</a>.

> [!quote] Tarantino on Floyd's Influence
> "But even more influential than any script was having a man trying to be a screenwriter living in my house...Floyd's inspiration made me consider for the first time writing movies" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:35">[00:06:35]</a>.

## Legacy of Influence

Although Floyd eventually vanished from Tarantino's life, he had already left an indelible mark. Tarantino's film 'Django Unchained' can trace its roots back to Floyd's script 'Billy Spencer', an epic Western that inspired Tarantino's own exploration of a black cowboy hero <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:47">[00:06:47]</a>.

## Conclusion

Tarantino's interaction with Floyd was a mix of mentorship and missed opportunities. While Floyd was not the ideal mentor, he inadvertently pushed Tarantino towards the path of filmmaking. This juxtaposition of unreliable mentorship and the urging to pursue one's passion highlights the multifaceted influences that shape creative visionaries like Tarantino.

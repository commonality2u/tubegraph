Amazon's strategic decisions, particularly early acquisitions, have had profound and often unexpected impacts on the broader e-commerce landscape, leading to significant personal wealth for its founder and fostering the growth of major tech companies.

## The Junglee Acquisition and its Unexpected Aftermath

Amazon acquired Junglee, a comparison shopping site founded by three Stanford computer science PhDs and Netscape business executive Ram Shriram, for an estimated $150-$175 million around the turn of the millennium <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:12">[00:00:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:16">[00:00:16]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:18">[00:00:18]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:20">[00:00:20]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:29">[00:00:29]</a>. Despite the acquisition, Amazon insisted the Junglee team relocate from Palo Alto to Seattle to avoid establishing a tax nexus in California <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:36">[00:00:36]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:38">[00:00:38]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:41">[00:00:41]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:43">[00:00:43]</a>.

The acquisition proved "ill-conceived," with the team quickly quitting Amazon and returning to Palo Alto within a few months <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:49">[00:00:49]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:51">[00:00:51]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:53">[00:00:53]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:54">[00:00:54]</a>.

[!NOTE] The Junglee founders (excluding Ram Shriram) went on to establish a company that was later acquired by Walmart, becoming Walmart Labs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:58">[00:00:58]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:59">[00:00:59]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:01">[00:01:01]</a>. This entity is considered a significant factor in Walmart's emergence as a major competitor in [[shift_in_retail_with_technology_and_ecommerce | e-commerce]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:04">[00:01:04]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:06">[00:01:06]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:09">[00:01:09]</a>.

## Ram Shriram's Pivotal Role in Google's Founding

Upon returning to Palo Alto, Junglee's business executive, Ram Shriram, connected with two other Stanford computer science PhDs: Larry Page and Sergey Brin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:15">[00:01:15]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:18">[00:01:18]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:21">[00:01:21]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:23">[00:01:23]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:27">[00:01:27]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:29">[00:01:29]</a>. Shriram, recognizing the potential in their "Backrub PageRank thing," became the first investor in Google, putting in $250,000 and joining its board <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:53">[00:01:53]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:54">[00:01:54]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:55">[00:01:55]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:57">[00:01:57]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:00">[00:02:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:01">[00:02:01]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:02">[00:02:02]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:04">[00:02:04]</a>. This occurred while Amazon was already a public company <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:43">[00:01:43]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:46">[00:01:46]</a>.

## Jeff Bezos's Fortuitous Investment in Google

### Financial Returns

It is estimated that Jeff and MacKenzie Bezos personally owned at least one percent of Google, even after dilution from the Series A round <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:22">[00:04:22]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:24">[00:04:24]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:27">[00:04:27]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:29">[00:04:29]</a>. Google's stock has since multiplied by 65x in value over the following 18 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:26">[00:05:26]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:29">[00:05:29]</a>.

[!INFO] Jeff Bezos has never publicly commented on whether he and MacKenzie sold their Google shares <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:48">[00:04:48]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:50">[00:04:50]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:52">[00:04:52]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:55">[00:04:55]</a>.

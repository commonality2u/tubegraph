## Early Varieties

The original M&M's were simple, with a milk chocolate center covered by a candy shell. The concept, inspired by a European candy called "dragée," was initially created to prevent the chocolate from melting in warm climates, particularly for soldiers during World War II. This classic variety, now officially called "milk chocolate," remains one of the flagship products of the brand.

### Expansion into Flavors and Types

As M&M's gained popularity, Mars expanded its lineup to include a variety of flavors and types:

1. **Peanut M&M's** - Introduced in 1954, these remain one of the most popular varieties alongside the milk chocolate version.
2. **Peanut Butter M&M's** - These feature a smooth peanut butter filling, introduced later due to the popularity of peanut butter-flavored snacks in the American market.
3. **Dark Chocolate M&M's** - Another option for chocolate enthusiasts, which provides a richer chocolate experience.
4. **Almond M&M's** - Though popular, this variety is not suitable for [[mars_incs_global_strategy_and_expansion | those with nut allergies]], and it remains a niche favorite (<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:37">[00:00:37]</a>).

- **Caramel M&M's** - Offering a chewy center that provides a different texture and flavor profile.

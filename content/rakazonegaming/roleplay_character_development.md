## Core Aspects of Character Progression

A key element of [[Roleplay character dynamics and stories | roleplay character dynamics and stories]] is a character's "progression" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:49">[00:10:49]</a>. This means a character's development over time, which can involve:
*   **Building Relationships:** Establishing connections with other players and maintaining them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:46">[00:10:46]</a>.
*   **Narrative Progression:** Moving a storyline forward, sometimes with the help of game administrators <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:53">[00:11:53]</a>. This could include a character progressing into a "criminal side" with administrative support <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:01">[00:12:01]</a>, or fulfilling specific responsibilities, like those of a relative <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:23">[00:14:23]</a>.
*   **Evolving Status:** Characters can achieve a high status, such as "Martin" in a server, where they can give orders rather than needing to fight themselves <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:02">[00:17:02]</a>. This "hyped" status can mean that others skirt around them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:14">[00:17:14]</a>.

## Challenges in Character Development

Despite the desire for deep character development, common challenges arise in [[Roleplay Server Dynamics | roleplay server dynamics]]:
*   **Repetitive Interactions:** Players often find interactions stagnant, frequently revolving around basic introductions and simple activities like "come let's go for a walk, let's do a job" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:43">[00:11:43]</a>. This leads to players feeling "bored" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:49">[00:11:49]</a>.
*   **Lack of Storyline Depth:** Instead of a planned storyline, "nonsense happens and then over the time we progress into the criminal side" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:58">[00:11:58]</a>. True progression requires more thought and administrative ideas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:04">[00:12:04]</a>.
*   **Static Progression:** A common issue is a lack of advancement for characters over extended periods <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:52:11">[03:52:11]</a>. Characters need opportunities to evolve and gain influence, like acquiring resources or authority <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:20:52">[02:20:52]</a>.
*   **Difficulty in Finding Quality RP Players:** It is seen as hard to find and appreciate [[Improving roleplay as a streamer | RP players]] with good [[Roleplaying games and online roleplay | RP knowledge]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:11">[00:40:11]</a>. Everyone seeks "respect and fear" but lacks the understanding of how to achieve it through meaningful roleplay <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:31">[00:40:31]</a>.

## Ideal vs. Reality
In an ideal [[Roleplaying and narrative engagement in gaming | roleplaying experience]], situations should naturally evolve, leading to unique character paths <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:01">[00:18:01]</a>. However, in reality, characters may struggle to develop if their efforts are not recognized or if server mechanics limit complex interactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:37:40">[03:37:40]</a>.

One streamer notes their personal RP motive was to give RP to others, but the experience became "too much of a mess" due to execution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:46:51">[04:46:51]</a>.

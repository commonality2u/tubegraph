## Google DeepMind's Alpha Evolve: Pushing Knowledge Frontiers

At Google's I/O conference, where major [[AI and technological advancements | AI advancements]] were announced, Google DeepMind introduced Alpha Evolve, a new autonomous coding agent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:57">[00:56:57]</a>.

Key capabilities of Alpha Evolve include:
*   **Algorithmic Improvement** Alpha Evolve is a pipeline of models designed to improve algorithms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:06">[00:37:06]</a>. When applied to Google's own model, it increased efficiency by 1%, resulting in $150 million in cost savings for training and inferencing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:38">[00:37:38]</a>.
*   **Chip Design Redesign** It can redesign chip designs, reconstructing them in more efficient ways based on original papers, leading to further efficiency gains <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:28">[00:38:28]</a>.
*   **Mathematical Discoveries** Alpha Evolve was presented with 50 open math challenges that had not been solved by humans <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:00">[00:39:00]</a>.
    *   In 75% of cases, it rediscovered the best known solutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:06">[00:39:06]</a>.
    *   In 20% of cases (approximately 15 problems), it improved upon previously best-known solutions, yielding new discoveries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:14">[00:39:14]</a>.
    *   This demonstrates its ability to create net new knowledge at the foundational level of human understanding, starting with math and potentially extending to physics, chemistry, and biology <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:42">[00:40:42]</a>.

This marks a significant step towards AI acting as an "optimizer" that can extract remaining efficiencies from challenging problems and move towards generating truly net new knowledge <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:32">[00:41:32]</a>. The acceleration of AI's ability to improve itself by even small percentages weekly is seen as a potentially intimidating trend <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:19">[00:41:19]</a>.

## Meta's Advancements in Personalized Medicine

Meta, formerly Facebook, has also released four scientific papers leveraging AI to make new discoveries in molecular structure, atom structure, and scientific sampling <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:50">[00:49:50]</a>. This focus extends [[AI and technological advancements | AI's impact]] into natural sciences and personalized health solutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:09">[00:50:09]</a>.

A notable example is the new AI agent named Robin, which made a scientific breakthrough in treating blindness caused by Age-related Macular Degeneration (AMD) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:42">[00:50:42]</a>.
*   Robin was tasked with developing a scientific experiment to find a potential treatment for AMD <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:18">[00:51:18]</a>.
*   It proposed an experiment, which was carried out by humans <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:30">[00:51:30]</a>.
*   Over six iterations of back-and-forth experimentation with human experts, Robin developed a novel idea: using a rock inhibitor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:52">[00:51:52]</a>.
*   This compound was previously only approved in Japan for a different associated disease, and no scientific research had suggested its use for this type of blindness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:04">[00:52:04]</a>.
*   Testing showed that the rock inhibitor successfully cured blindness in mice models <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:21">[00:52:21]</a>.

This case exemplifies an AI-human expert collaboration where AI suggests new treatment paths, leading to breakthroughs that humans alone had not discovered <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:00">[00:54:00]</a>. Similar initiatives are being pursued by other major tech companies like Microsoft with their "Microsoft Discovery" conference <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:08">[00:53:08]</a>.

## Current Search Landscape

Currently, Google serves as the default search engine within Apple's Safari browser on both laptops and mobile devices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:07">[00:00:07]</a>. Apple pays Google approximately [[apples_payments_to_google_for_search_default | $19 billion per year]] for this privilege <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:20">[00:00:20]</a>.

## Apple's View on AI Search

Apple executive Eddie Cue has stated that AI search tools like ChatGPT, Claude, or Perplexity offer a superior experience compared to Google <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:31">[00:00:31]</a>. There is a growing trend among users to shift their search habits away from traditional Google searches towards these AI-powered alternatives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:38">[00:00:38]</a>.

## Google's Adaptation

Even Google itself has begun to incorporate AI into its search results, displaying an AI summary at the top of the page when a search is performed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:47">[00:00:47]</a>.

## Potential Shift and Market Reaction

Apple is actively exploring a revamp of its search functionality, which could lead to the replacement of Google as the default option <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:52">[00:00:52]</a>. News of Apple's potential entry into the AI search market has had a significant negative impact on Google's market capitalization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:56">[00:00:56]</a>.

OpenAI, led by CEO Sam Altman, has partnered with Jony Ive, the renowned former Apple designer, through a multi-billion dollar collaboration aimed at designing the future of [[the use and development of AIrelated technologies in the crypto space | AI hardware]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:42">[00:00:42]</a>. ## Who is Jony Ive?
Jony Ive (often referred to as Johnny IV in the transcript) is an iconic industrial designer known for his work at Apple <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:15">[00:12:15]</a>. He was responsible for designing the first iPod and iPhones, shaping many of the devices widely used today <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:00">[00:22:00]</a>. Ive left Apple in 2019 to co-found his design company, LoveFrom, which includes many former Apple design team members <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:00">[00:34:00]</a>. His company, IO (presumably short for LoveFrom), is the primary entity involved in this partnership <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:00">[01:29:00]</a>.

## The Partnership and Financials
This collaboration marks OpenAI's largest acquisition to date, totaling $6.5 billion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:00">[01:05:00]</a>. Of this, $5 billion is a stock deal, and $1.5 billion is cash <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:47:00">[04:47:00]</a>. The acquisition of Ive's company, IO, which has about 55 employees, translates to roughly $155 million per employee <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:55:00">[04:55:00]</a>. This high price tag and significant marketing effort, including a nine-minute documentary featuring Sam Altman and Jony Ive, underscore the importance OpenAI places on this venture <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:12:00">[05:12:00]</a>.

## Vision for AI Hardware: The "iPhone Killer"
The goal of this partnership is to design hardware that embodies [[AI and the future of employment | AI]], forming a new bedrock for how humans interact with it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:50:00">[01:50:00]</a>. The product is envisioned as something akin to a "third core device" alongside phones and computers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:29:00">[02:29:00]</a>. It's described as unobtrusive, potentially sitting in a pocket or on a desk, and being "aware of everything that is going on in your life" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:36:00">[02:36:00]</a>.

The underlying philosophy is to move away from the "distracting and extractive" nature of current smartphones, which contribute to addiction and misinformation due to constant screen time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:11:00">[07:11:00]</a>. Unlike existing computers and phones, this new device will leverage [[Advancements in AI model development and competition from Chinese tech companies | AI's]] ability to "think, see, and feel," understanding context from the real world <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:01:00">[08:01:00]</a>.

The concept of "ambient [[OpenAIs acquisition strategy | AI]]" suggests a ubiquitous presence of [[AI as a tool for post scarcity society | AI]] in our surroundings due to this device <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:32:00">[05:32:00]</a>. It aims to be a passive device that continuously monitors the user's local context, providing an "extension of our human self" or even a "second brain" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:34:00">[11:34:00]</a>. This passive surveillance of one's own life would ingest data continuously, potentially streamlining daily tasks and interactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:50:00">[12:50:50]</a>.

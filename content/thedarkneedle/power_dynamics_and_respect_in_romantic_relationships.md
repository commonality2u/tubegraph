## The "Ability to Be Dangerous"

Historically, from the era of primitive humans, women have been genetically programmed to seek men who possess the "ability to be dangerous" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:26">[00:01:26]</a>. This doesn't imply criminality, but rather the capacity to cause harm if necessary <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:44">[00:01:44]</a>. This underlying strength provides a fundamental sense of safety <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:41">[00:01:41]</a>.

A real-world example of this dynamic is seen in stories where a man might appear physically strong but would abandon his partner in danger, while a seemingly weaker man might stand and fight <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:13">[00:01:13]</a>. Women are often attracted to "bad boys" because they project this dangerous, strong side <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:48">[00:01:48]</a>. However, the goal is not to become a literal "bad boy," as this is not a successful long-term strategy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:54">[00:02:54]</a>. Instead, men should embody the attractive traits of strength while maintaining their morals and principles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:59">[00:02:59]</a>.

### Consequences of Being "Too Nice"

A common pitfall is the belief that excessive kindness and constant compromise can avoid problems in a [[dynamics_of_romantic_relationships | relationship]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:17">[00:02:17]</a>. An illustrative case involves a man whose wife divorced him because she "didn't feel safe with him" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:26">[00:02:26]</a>. This was attributed to him being "too nice," never standing up to her, letting her cross boundaries, and always trying to help her <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:10">[00:02:10]</a>. While he avoided direct confrontation, the outcome was a lack of felt security for his wife <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:22">[00:02:22]</a>.

## Qualities of a Strong Man for [[Building Power in Relationships | Building Power]] and Respect

To cultivate a sense of safety and [[understanding_womens_respect_and_attraction | respect]] in a woman, a man must demonstrate certain qualities:

### 1. Fearlessness

A man must show that he is not afraid, specifically not afraid of his partner's reactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:45">[00:04:45]</a>. If a man's honesty is dictated by his partner's emotional state, he is not living by his principles but rather for her approval <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:02">[00:05:02]</a>.

Common examples of men acting out of fear include:
*   **Lying about timing:** Saying "I'll be there soon" when late <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:10">[00:05:10]</a>.
*   **Lying about tasks:** Claiming "Yes, I did it already" when it's forgotten <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:18">[00:05:18]</a>.
*   **Lying about forgotten plans:** Pretending not to know about an event <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:34">[00:05:34]</a>.
*   **Hiding emotions:** Saying "I'm fine" when upset <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:43">[00:05:43]</a>.
*   **Lying about finances:** Concealing the true cost of purchases <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:58">[00:05:58]</a>.
*   **Lying about availability:** Claiming poor signal or missed calls to avoid answering <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:14">[00:06:14]</a>.

These "small lies" are often told to avoid confrontation or perceived punishment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:36">[00:06:36]</a>. However, this mindset neglects the long-term consequences on the [[dynamics_of_romantic_relationships | relationship]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:46">[00:06:46]</a>. When a man fears a woman's reaction, he loses her <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:57">[00:06:57]</a>. Being honest, even if it upsets her, paradoxically increases her attraction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:03">[00:07:03]</a>.

### 2. Ability to Confront and Set Boundaries

A man must be capable of confrontation, meaning he can face his partner and express himself clearly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:28">[00:07:28]</a>. This contrasts sharply with being a "yes man" who always agrees regardless of his true opinion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:11">[00:08:11]</a>.

**Not Being a "Yes Man":**
Women often test men to see if they will stick to their opinions and if they are capable of disagreeing while still maintaining the [[dynamics_of_romantic_relationships | relationship]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:10">[00:10:10]</a>. Having one's own preferences and expressing them earns [[understanding_womens_respect_and_attraction | respect]] and admiration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:20">[00:09:20]</a>.

*   **Example (Movie):**
    *   Woman: "I thought it was terrible."
    *   Man: "Well, you're entitled to your opinion, but I still think it was a spectacular movie." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:31">[00:09:31]</a>

**Setting Clear Boundaries:**
If a woman consistently gives a man a hard time for disagreeing, he must make it clear that he is entitled to his own opinion and not interested in justifying his preferences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:14">[00:10:14]</a>.

*   **Example (Lecture):**
    *   Woman: "I can't believe this, you never like any lecture, you're such an idiot if you think..."
    *   Man: "Look, you asked for my opinion, and I gave it. If you don't like hearing the truth from me, I suggest you think twice before asking what I think." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:35">[00:10:35]</a>

This firm approach establishes that the man's opinion is valid and respected. If a woman pushes for an argument after an opinion has been stated, the man should refuse to engage, stating firmly and lovingly, "I won't argue about this," and then change the subject <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:14">[00:11:14]</a>.

## Conclusion

By demonstrating fearlessness and the ability to confront, a man shows he can set boundaries and protect not only himself but also his partner <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:07">[00:03:07]</a>. This doesn't mean being harsh or hateful, but rather acting out of love for her, enabling her to feel safe <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:46">[00:11:46]</a>. This consistent demonstration of strength and adherence to one's principles builds a unique sense of security, making her rely on him and fostering long-term attachment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:32">[00:11:32]</a>.

## Step One: Make Her Surrender to Your Emotional Frame

The first step involves a technique called "You're Trouble" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:14">[00:00:14]</a>. This approach aims to label the other person in a way that takes away their power, rather than giving it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:18">[00:00:18]</a>. Unlike common interactions where individuals seek to be liked, this technique aims to disrupt the expectation of control often held by women in social exchanges <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:22">[00:00:22]</a>.

Instead of trying to be seen as safe or attractive, the "You're Trouble" line "kicks the door down" of their established frame <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:44">[00:00:44]</a>.

### How it Works

When delivered calmly and confidently, as if having "seen girls like her before," this phrase subtly implies that the person is dangerous or someone to be careful with <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:57">[00:00:57]</a>. This shifts the dynamic from seeking approval to making the other person prove they are "not chaos" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:07">[00:01:07]</a>.

It signals, "I see your game and I'm not playing it," which disarms them and forces them to recalibrate, interrupting their social autopilot <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:21">[00:01:21]</a>. The open-ended nature of "You're trouble" leaves it undefined (e.g., seductive, toxic, playful), creating a "psychological itch" as their brain tries to fill in the blanks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:34">[00:01:34]</a>. They want to know what was meant and to regain control of the frame <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:50">[00:01:50]</a>.

When the other person starts defending themselves or trying to shape your perception, they have "lost the frame" and are now doing the chasing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:00">[00:02:00]</a>. Their attention becomes "locked on your opinion of her," indicating they are "inside your emotional frame" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:24">[00:02:24]</a>.

### Application

The "You're Trouble" technique is applied in two stages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:34">[00:02:34]</a>:

1.  **Early Introduction**: Say it within the first 5 to 10 minutes of conversation, before they figure out what role to play <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:38">[00:02:38]</a>.
    *   **Timing**: Use it when they flirt too easily, smile like they've done it many times, ask too many questions too fast, or act innocently in a performative way <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:43">[00:02:43]</a>.
    *   **Delivery**: Look at them calmly, smirk a little, then say, "You're trouble. I can already tell." Then, shut up, don't laugh, don't explain, and don't soften it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:53">[00:02:53]</a>.
    *   **Pattern Break**: Use it when they expect a compliment, such as after showing a photo. Instead of "You look good," say, "Yeah, that confirms it. You're definitely trouble" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:02">[00:03:02]</a>.
    *   This forces them to adjust in real-time, revealing their true self <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:18">[00:03:18]</a>.
    *   **Reactions**:
        *   If they push back ("No, I'm not"), they are trying to defend their image, which is a form of [[emotional_investment]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:29">[00:03:29]</a>.
        *   If they play along ("Maybe I am"), you have given them a new identity that shapes their behavior and keeps you in control of the [[emotional_rhythm]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:47">[00:03:47]</a>.
2.  **Reuse Later**: Repeat the phrase quietly and calmly later in the interaction when they say something "unhinged" or revealing, or when you glimpse their "real nature" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:07">[00:04:07]</a>.
    *   This makes it a seen pattern rather than a joke <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:17">[00:04:17]</a>.
    *   After the second time, they may start calling themselves "trouble," internalizing the label and emotionally surrendering to your frame <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:21">[00:04:21]</a>.

## Step Two: Keep the Chase Going / Create Craving

The second step is to create craving using the phrase, "I like this, whatever this is" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:41">[00:04:41]</a>. This technique is used once they start opening up, leaning in, or trying to show their depth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:48">[00:04:48]</a>.

### How it Works

This phrase rewards them emotionally for opening up but keeps them "chasing clarity" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:59">[00:04:59]</a>. It plants the idea that something special is happening but then pulls away before they can grasp it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:01">[00:05:01]</a>. It combines warmth and vulnerability ("I like this") with a refusal to define the relationship ("whatever this is"), creating "emotional tension instead of certainty" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:07">[00:05:07]</a>. This mix of warmth and confusion is presented as an "exact emotional cocktail that creates obsession" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:16">[00:05:16]</a>.

Their brain becomes "stuck in a loop," questioning the nature of the connection ("What is this? Does he feel something for me?") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:24">[00:05:24]</a>. This loop becomes an "addiction," as you have given "emotional weight without any structure" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:32">[00:05:32]</a>. They start to think, "I need to find out" what the connection is <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:46">[00:05:46]</a>.

### Application

This line must be earned, not thrown out randomly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:00">[00:06:00]</a>.

*   **Timing**: The "sweet spot" is when emotional tension is built up, "not romantic yet, but undeniably charged" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:06">[00:06:06]</a>. It should be said in a moment of natural intimacy, like when both are laughing, vibing, or sitting closer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:13">[00:06:13]</a>.
*   **Delivery**: It's about how it's delivered, not just the words <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:47">[00:06:47]</a>. Don't look serious or make a speech; act like it "just slipped out," showing surprise at your own honesty but too composed to make a big deal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:50">[00:06:50]</a>.
    *   **Example 1**: After a laugh and brief silence, exhale, glance, smirk, and casually say, "I like this, whatever this is," then immediately look away or change the topic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:23">[00:06:23]</a>.
    *   **Example 2**: After they share something real or vulnerable, pause, look, and say, "You know, I like this, whatever this is," then go quiet for a second and move on <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:34">[00:06:34]</a>.

The goal is to make them *feel* something, then pull away before [[emotional_closure]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:57">[00:06:57]</a>. The energy should convey, "Yeah, I felt something, but I'm not going to make a big deal out of it" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:07">[00:07:07]</a>. This subtle detachment makes the line powerful, as it's present ("I like this") yet undefined ("whatever this is") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:18">[00:07:18]</a>. If done correctly, they won't respond directly, but later, the moment will be replayed in their head, becoming a "mind virus" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:27">[00:07:27]</a>. This leads them to watch you differently, pay closer attention, and think of the connection as "something undefined but real," making you the only person who can resolve the uncertainty <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:43">[00:07:43]</a>.

## Step Three: Turn Confusion into Ownership

The third phrase, "You already act like my girl sometimes," is used to turn confusion into ownership <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:06">[00:08:06]</a>. This assigns a "girlfriend role" partially, creating tension <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:14">[00:08:14]</a>.

### How it Works

This statement implies that they are "already behaving like someone who's emotionally attached, but without the title" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:19">[00:08:19]</a>. It exposes something they may not have meant to admit, holding "a mirror to her behavior" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:26">[00:08:26]</a>. Their mind races, wondering if they truly act like that or if they are being read into <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:31">[00:08:31]</a>, which can lead to a "mild [[emotional_spiral]]" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:37">[00:08:37]</a>.

If they push back ("No, I don't"), they deny something that "feels true," creating internal tension and [[emotional_imbalance]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:44">[00:08:44]</a>. If they agree, even jokingly, they are admitting attachment and stepping "directly into the frame you gave her" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:00">[00:09:00]</a>. You are not asking for a girlfriend; you are merely implying she has already slipped into that role <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:11">[00:09:11]</a>. This causes her to scan her past behavior and may lead her to play into the role more, believing she is following her own truth, while you are "installing the behavior by telling her she's already doing it" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:18">[00:09:18]</a>.

### Application

This line should not be dropped randomly; it must follow "some form of emotional behavior from her" that resembles girlfriend behavior <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:44">[00:09:44]</a>.

*   **Delivery**: Say it like an observation, with a calm delivery, slight smirk, and a bit playful, but not silly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:54">[00:09:54]</a>. The statement should land heavily despite not being meant deeply <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:58">[00:09:58]</a>.
*   **Timing**: Say it *right after* she does something emotional, not before <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:04">[00:10:04]</a>. Saying it too early sounds like leading <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:07">[00:10:07]</a>.
    *   **Example 1**: If she gets annoyed at you for not replying, smile, shake your head, and say, "You already act like my girl sometimes," then change the subject <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:10">[00:10:10]</a>.
    *   **Example 2**: If she does something nurturing or domestic (e.g., folds your shirt, brings food, gives health advice), look at her with amused disbelief and say, "You're aware you're acting like my girl right now" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:21">[00:10:21]</a>.

The tone should never be needy or an attempt to get her to be your girlfriend; it's a tease with an undeniable truth underneath <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:33">[00:10:33]</a>. Once she starts defending, joking, or playing into it, the frame has landed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:42">[00:10:42]</a>. She begins to think about her role in your life and that you "already kind of act like a couple" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:48">[00:10:48]</a>. When she starts using words like "we" or "already," she is no longer deciding whether to be with you, but waiting for you to match the label she has accepted in her mind <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:53">[00:10:53]</a>.

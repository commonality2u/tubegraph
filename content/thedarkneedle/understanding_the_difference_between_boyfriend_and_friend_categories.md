## Initial Categorization

When a young man interacts with a girl, her mind quickly places him into one of three categories:
*   Potential boyfriend <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:20">[00:00:20]</a>
*   Friend <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:20">[00:00:20]</a>
*   Someone she doesn't want to meet again <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:21">[00:00:21]</a>

Initially, the speaker often found himself in the "doesn't want to meet again" category due to being socially awkward and introverted <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:23">[00:00:23]</a>. After learning social skills, he moved to the "just a friend" category <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:43">[00:00:43]</a>.

## The Core Differentiator: Emotion

The fundamental difference between a boyfriend and a friend is **emotion** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:59">[00:00:59]</a>. To be seen as a potential boyfriend, one must know how to stir a girl's emotions and speak directly to them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:02">[00:01:02]</a>. Applying this principle allowed the speaker to move from the friend category to the boyfriend category <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:08">[00:01:08]</a>.

## Stirring Emotions for Connection

There are two ways to stir emotions: either entering feelings into her or bringing them out of her <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:20">[00:01:20]</a>. Initially, one cannot directly "put feelings into her" because her emotions are locked away <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:27">[00:01:27]</a>. The effective method is to make her express her own feelings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:32">[00:01:32]</a>.

This is achieved through the "listen like a therapist" technique, which involves asking emotional questions and actively listening to her answers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:50">[00:01:50]</a>. An emotional question is one that prompts a woman to express her inner feelings, whether they are anger, sadness, hatred, admiration, or passion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:11">[00:03:11]</a>.

A crucial aspect is to ensure the girl's answer is long and expressive, encouraging her to "pour out her feelings" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:13">[00:04:13]</a>. When she begins to talk and share her story, you are speaking to her emotions, which are instinctually connected to love <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:40">[00:04:40]</a>. This emotional conversation is designed to be memorable and impactful, unlike purely logical discussions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:29">[00:03:29]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:39">[00:05:39]</a>.

## Defining Your Standards

A common mistake is having no clear idea of what one is truly seeking in a partner <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:10">[00:00:10]</a>. Vague desires like "fun, smart, sexy" are insufficient; without specific definitions, individuals tend to accept whatever is available rather than what is genuinely right <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:27">[00:00:27]</a>. This often leads to [[importance_of_sharing_personal_weaknesses | self-sabotage]] and dissatisfaction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:05">[00:01:05]</a>.

To counter this, it is essential to create a detailed list defining the qualities of an ideal partner, including their beliefs, how they behave during difficult times, and what genuinely ignites passion within oneself <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:44">[00:00:44]</a>. This blueprint acts as a filter and a compass, helping to avoid settling for chemistry over compatibility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:02">[00:01:02]</a>. Setting a high bar for oneself and others demonstrates [[the_importance_of_mindset_and_selfperception | self-worth]] and cultivates an energy that attracts compatible individuals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:15">[00:01:15]</a>. This involves being ruthlessly specific about one's cravings in a woman and owning those desires <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:50">[00:01:50]</a>. High expectations ensure one is not taken advantage of and encourages others to earn attention, rather than the reverse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:05">[00:02:05]</a>.

Understanding the dynamics of criticism and compliments can significantly influence the strength and perception of a [[communication_strategies_in_relationships | relationship]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:49">[03:49]</a>. By strategically employing both, individuals can foster deeper connection and increase the value of their expressions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:51">[03:51]</a>.

## The Value of Criticism

Criticism, when applied thoughtfully, serves as a foundation for making compliments more valuable and desired <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:51">[03:51]</a>. It's not merely about pointing out flaws but about demonstrating confidence and setting a clear standard <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:31">[03:31]</a>.

### Overcoming the Fear to Criticize
Many men hesitate to criticize women due to a fear of appearing rude or hurting feelings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:12">[03:12]</a>. However, this is often a societal misconception <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:23">[03:23]</a>. Demonstrating the confidence to voice disapproval, such as asking someone to blow smoke away, can actually lead to admiration for one's strength <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:27">[03:27]</a>.

### Thoughtful vs. Overly Critical
Criticism should be thoughtful, not constant or excessive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:34">[03:34]</a>. Examples include telling someone an outfit doesn't suit them or a picture didn't turn out well <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:40">[03:40]</a>. This honest criticism has a significant [[communication_strategies_in_relationships | impact on the relationship]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:49">[03:49]</a>.

### Criticism Reinforces the Value of Compliments
The key principle is that when you criticize occasionally, your compliments become far more valuable and meaningful <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:51">[03:51]</a>. If compliments are given constantly, they lose their importance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:00">[04:00]</a>.

> [!quote]
> When you criticize her, your compliments become more meaningful <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:57">[03:57]</a>.

Consider this analogy: If someone is constantly told "you're smart," the words eventually lose their impact <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:12">[04:12]</a>. However, a compliment from someone who rarely praises you is much more appreciated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:18">[04:18]</a>. This phenomenon is a universal human trait: people tend to value things that are rare <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:24">[04:24]</a>. Therefore, not being afraid to criticize lays the groundwork for making subsequent compliments truly valuable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:36">[04:36]</a>.

## The Role of Compliments

While criticism elevates the impact of compliments, the manner in which compliments are delivered also plays a crucial role in [[effective_communication_strategies_in_relationships | relationship]] dynamics.

### When Compliments Become Meaningful
Compliments become special and desired when they are not always expected <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:08">[04:08]</a>. This selective praise creates a stronger desire for approval <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:08">[04:08]</a>.

### Spontaneity and Admiration
Showing spontaneous admiration for someone, such as reacting positively when they do something cute or wear something appealing, can significantly increase their liking for you <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:47">[07:47]</a>. This approach demonstrates a lack of fear in showing admiration and can help avoid the "friend zone" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:53">[07:53]</a>. However, this technique is typically more appropriate after the initial stages of interaction, ideally from the second meeting onward, and should be introduced gradually <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:39">[07:39]</a>.

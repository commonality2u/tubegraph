## Identifying the Fear of Failure Weak Point

This weak point is common in individuals who are pursuing significant, life-altering goals, such as gaining entry to a competitive university, securing a challenging job, or joining a music band <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:22:00">[06:22:00]</a>. Despite receiving positive encouragement, an internal voice often reminds them of the possibility of failure, creating an "internal hole" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:38:00">[06:38:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:46:00">[06:46:00]</a>. ## The Role of Reassurance

When someone discusses their problems, especially those with an emotional component like [[handling_failure_and_demonstrating_resilience | fear of failure]], the correct approach is through **emotional reassurance** and uplifting their spirits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:34:00">[07:34:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:36:00">[07:36:00]</a>. Reassurance serves as the "bait" for this weak point <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:40:00">[07:40:00]</a>.

### Avoiding Common Mistakes
Most people incorrectly respond by offering solutions or advice immediately <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:25:00">[07:25:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:27:00">[07:27:00]</a>. This is generally the wrong approach, as advice should only be given when explicitly asked for <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:49:00">[07:49:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:51:00">[07:51:00]</a>.

There are three scenarios when someone talks about a problem:
1.  **Seeking Advice:** They directly ask for your opinion or guidance (e.g., "Do you know any way to gain weight?"). In this case, provide a solution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:05:00">[08:05:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:11:00">[08:11:00]</a>.
2.  **Venting About a Past Problem:** They share a bad experience or sad story with no current solution (e.g., "My boss treats me terribly"). Simply listen without offering solutions, acting uninterested, or changing the subject <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:22:00">[08:22:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:30:00">[08:30:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:32:00">[08:32:00]</a>. They likely already know the obvious answers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:39:00">[08:39:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:43:00">[08:43:00]</a>.
3.  **Emotional Problem:** The issue is not just logical but deeply emotional, such as stress or uncertainty about the future related to a big goal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:50:00">[08:50:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:57:00">[08:57:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:59:00">[08:59:00]</a>. This is where reassurance is key <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:07:00">[09:07:00]</a>.

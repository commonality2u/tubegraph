## The Role of Acquaintances

It is crucial for an individual to know many people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:43">[00:00:43]</a>. Acquaintances simplify life, offer various services, and can even help resolve problems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:47">[00:00:47]</a>. Examples include staff at a cafe offering a "royal welcome" due to a good relationship <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:53">[00:00:53]</a>, or a nearby acquaintance helping jump-start a broken-down car <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:01">[00:01:01]</a>.

[[increasing_social_power_through_interactions | Public relations]] are fundamentally based on mutual benefit, which is not inherently negative <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:06">[00:01:06]</a>. While affection, friendship, and humor exist, the underlying dynamic often revolves around what each person can offer the other <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:10">[00:01:10]</a>. For instance, someone might help you because they perceive you as valuable and anticipate potential future benefits from you <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:21">[00:01:21]</a>. The more valuable a person is, the more people will eagerly assist them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:32">[00:01:32]</a>.

## Factors Determining the Number of Acquaintances

Two primary factors determine how many acquaintances a person has:

### 1. Exposure
[[social_exposure_and_its_role_in_making_new_friends | Exposure]] means actively exposing oneself to people by going outside and visiting different places, essentially having a [[importance_of_social_skills_for_success | social life]] rather than staying isolated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:51">[00:01:51]</a>. The more places visited, the more people encountered <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:01">[00:02:01]</a>. While [[social_exposure_and_its_role_in_making_new_friends | exposure]] creates opportunities, it is only the first step <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:04">[00:02:04]</a>.

### 2. Social Skills (Your Tongue/Ability to Speak)
The second factor, your "tongue" or ability to speak, enables you to seize the opportunities created by [[social_exposure_and_its_role_in_making_new_friends | exposure]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:20">[00:02:20]</a>. Two individuals with identical daily routines and [[social_exposure_and_its_role_in_making_new_friends | exposure]] can yield vastly different results in terms of new connections, purely based on their [[building_social_skills_and_connections | social skills]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:08">[00:02:08]</a>.

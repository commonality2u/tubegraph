## The Emotional Basis of Attraction

In relationships and attraction, women are 100% emotional beings who make all their decisions based on emotion, only to justify them later with logic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:00">[01:11:00]</a>. For instance, when a woman is attracted to a man, she might cite logical reasons like him being "nice, handsome, attractive, tall, or having money," but the real reason is that he "moved her emotionally" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:21:00">[01:21:00]</a>. These logical explanations are merely justifications for a deeper emotional connection <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:44:00">[01:44:00]</a>.

Similarly, breakup or divorce reasons given by women, such as financial issues or bad treatment, are often logical excuses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:11:00">[02:11:00]</a>. The underlying truth is typically that the attraction died <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:04:00">[02:04:00]</a>. When attraction is high, a woman focuses on the good aspects of a relationship; when it's low, she focuses on the bad <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:59:00">[02:59:00]</a>. Even in the face of negative behaviors like cheating or alcoholism, high attraction can lead to forgiveness and justification <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:13:00">[03:13:00]</a>.

To leverage this, it's crucial to "forget something called logic" when interacting with a woman <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:37:00">[03:37:00]</a>. Instead of trying to impress her with logical attributes (e.g., wealth or fitness), the approach should be to attract her emotionally and let her "chase the logic" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:48:00">[03:48:00]</a>.

## The Power of Absence

A crucial truth is that "women fall in love when you're not around" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:08:00">[05:08:00]</a>. Most men fear losing a girl and over-communicate, but true [[emotional_investment_in_relationships | emotional investment in relationships]] and obsession occur during absence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:31:00">[05:31:00]</a>.

When there's no communication, her mind starts to spin, replaying moments, wondering why she felt certain ways, and questioning your absence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:07:00">[06:07:00]</a>. This mental repetition creates attachment because the brain craves closure and hates unfinished stories <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:20:00">[06:20:00]</a>. Her imagination and memory filter out flaws, building an idealized version of you in your absence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:40:00">[06:40:00]</a>.

To facilitate this:
*   After a date, don't text much unless she initiates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:14:00">[07:14:00]</a>.
*   Don't talk too much <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:17:00">[07:17:00]</a>.
*   If she starts calling frequently, give excuses for being busy to maintain the emotional state of absence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:40:00">[07:40:00]</a>.

This makes her justify herself emotionally <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:50:00">[09:50:00]</a>. This is part of [[creating_playfult_and_fun_interactions_with_women | creating playful and fun interactions with women]].

You actually surprised me" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:50:00">[10:50:00]</a>. This makes your validation feel earned and addictive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:55:00">[10:55:00]</a>.

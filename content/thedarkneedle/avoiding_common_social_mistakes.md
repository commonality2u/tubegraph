## The Human Instinct: Hunger for Value

Deeply ingrained in human genes is an instinct described as a "hunger for value" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:54">[00:00:54]</a>. People inherently desire to feel valuable in their environment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:58">[00:00:58]</a>. Properly satisfying this instinct in others can lead to winning them over and gaining their cooperation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:02">[00:01:02]</a>.

An example given is a math tutor who motivated a struggling student by acknowledging his hard work and potential, despite his slowness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:30">[00:01:30]</a>. This positive manipulation, described as "food for the instinct," led the student to dramatically improve, driven by a desire to please the tutor and prove himself worthy of praise <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:01">[00:02:01]</a>.

### Golden Rule: Make Them Feel Impressive

A fundamental rule for influencing others is to make them feel as though *they* have left an impression and influenced *you* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:36">[00:02:36]</a>. Instead of trying to impress someone, aim to show them that *they* are the amazing and remarkable one <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:53">[00:02:53]</a>.

## Strategies for Effective Social Interaction

### 1. Engage in Focused Conversation

To make someone feel impressive, engage in focused conversation that revolves around them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:03">[00:03:03]</a>.

There are two primary ways to do this:
*   **Allow them to talk about themselves and build on their statements.** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:10">[00:03:10]</a>
    *   If someone recounts an experience, keep the focus on them by asking follow-up questions like "what did you do about it?" or "how did the date go?" instead of diverting the conversation to your own experiences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:43">[00:03:43]</a>. The goal is to make them the center of the conversation for an extended period <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:56">[00:03:56]</a>.
*   **Take a general topic and redirect it towards them.** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:14">[00:03:14]</a>
    *   For example, if discussing the weather, ask "what do you like more, rain or sunshine? Summer or winter?" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:13">[00:04:13]</a>. This gives them value and satisfies their instinct, making them feel they have influenced you <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:24">[00:04:24]</a>.

### 2. Avoid Proving Yourself

When someone tries to earn your admiration, you have already won them over <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:36">[00:04:36]</a>. This principle applies in all social interactions, including with women <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:44">[00:04:44]</a>. Achieve this by not trying to prove anything to the other person, demonstrating that you don't need to assert any idea or point <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:52">[00:04:52]</a>.

*   **Handling Interruptions:** If someone interrupts you, let them talk and change the subject. Avoid insisting on returning to your previous topic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:07">[00:05:07]</a>. This shows you're not trying to prove anything and is a display of strength <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:27">[00:05:27]</a>. The strongest people in society don't need to prove themselves <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:36">[00:05:36]</a>.
*   **Persistent Interruptions:** If interruptions are constant, calmly and directly state, "Why don't you let me finish what I'm saying?" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:47">[00:05:47]</a>.
    *   **For Men:** Society expects men to express themselves clearly and directly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:03">[00:06:03]</a>. Fearing rudeness and being indirect is a mistake <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:11">[00:06:11]</a>. If someone continues interrupting after being told, there is no reason to continue engaging with them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:20">[00:06:20]</a>. Do not torment yourself trying to please someone you don't enjoy being around <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:28">[00:06:28]</a>.

### 3. Recognize Their Presence and Value

Accept people as they are and value them for who they are, not for their possessions or achievements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:57">[00:06:57]</a>.

*   **Avoid Probing Questions:** Do not ask intrusive questions about their job, education, or income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:07">[00:07:07]</a>. The person should feel you care about them as an individual <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:15">[00:07:15]</a>.
*   **Refrain from Giving Unsolicited Advice:** Avoid lecturing or giving advice unless explicitly asked <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:24">[00:07:24]</a>. People already know their problems and aren't waiting for your unsolicited input <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:34">[00:07:34]</a>. Show them you accept them as they are, problems and all <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:43">[00:07:43]</a>.
*   **Never Place Yourself Above Others:** Do not act superior or dictate what others should do <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:46">[00:07:46]</a>.
*   **Do Not Correct Trivial Mistakes:** Avoid correcting minor factual errors in conversation, especially if they don't impact the meaningfulness of the discussion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:03">[00:08:03]</a>. This is a bad habit that offers no gain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:26">[00:08:26]</a>.

### 4. Encourage Them to Earn Your Admiration

Give others the opportunity to strive for your admiration by asking for their opinions and suggestions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:35">[00:08:35]</a>.

*   **Ask for Opinions:** During a discussion, ask what the other person thinks about the topic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:57">[00:08:57]</a>. Listen attentively to their response <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:02">[00:09:02]</a>.
*   **Welcome Suggestions:** When someone offers suggestions or advice, listen to them and do not dismiss their ideas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:05">[00:09:05]</a>.
    *   **Avoid Treating Others as Ignorant:** Never treat anyone as if they don't understand anything or as if you are better than them, regardless of their education or age <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:22">[00:09:22]</a>. Treat everyone as your equal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:34">[00:09:34]</a>.
    *   If a suggestion isn't suitable, politely state, "That seems like a good idea, but I need to think about it," rather than outright rejecting it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:55">[00:09:55]</a>.

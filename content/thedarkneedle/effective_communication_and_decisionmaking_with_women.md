## The "Half Bad, Half Nice" Concept

Human brains naturally pay attention to exceptions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:56">[00:00:56]</a>. If you are always kind to someone, a single mistake will be remembered, overriding all the good you've done <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:07">[00:01:07]</a>. Conversely, someone not naturally kind who does one good thing will be praised <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:36">[00:01:36]</a>. People's respect is not gained by simply serving them or always being kind <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:48">[00:01:48]</a>. It is better to be a "black page that occasionally shows white spots" than a "white page that occasionally shows black spots" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:53">[00:01:53]</a>.

When applied to women:
*   If you are always nice (always "white"), your kindness will not be seen as special or appreciated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:13">[00:02:13]</a>.
*   If you are not always nice, behaving normally or even indifferently, and occasionally do something nice, these gestures will stand out and attract her <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:20">[00:02:20]</a>.

This is the fundamental difference: the "nice guy" is always white with occasional black dots, while the "half bad, half nice" man (the gentleman) is black with occasional white dots <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:30">[00:02:30]</a>.

When you behave normally or indifferently instead of being naturally nice, a woman perceives you as a high-value man <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:51">[00:02:51]</a>. ### 2. Request Management

When a woman asks for something, there are two types of requests: tests of your strength/manipulability or genuine requests <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:24">[00:08:24]</a>.

*   **If the request inconveniences you or makes no sense:** You should **never accept** this type of order <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:59">[00:08:59]</a>. For example, if she tells you to "Go buy me something," you must refuse calmly, as there's no reason to run errands for her <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:06">[00:09:06]</a>.
*   **If the request doesn't bother you but is a direct order:** Do not outright refuse, but also **do not accept directly** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:17">[00:09:17]</a>. Instead, accept on your own terms, taking control by adding your conditions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:29">[00:09:29]</a>.

Women often express wishes indirectly, like "I love the beach" instead of "I want to go to the beach" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:15">[00:10:15]</a>.

Take note of what she likes, but fulfill her wishes at unexpected moments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:23">[00:11:23]</a>. Be kind but firm, making it clear you don't have time for whining <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:33">[00:11:33]</a>. Over time, she will learn she gets what she wants when you decide to give it, not when she demands or complains <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:37">[00:11:37]</a>.

In summary, requests that are expressions of feelings should always be handled by delaying <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:44">[00:11:44]</a>.

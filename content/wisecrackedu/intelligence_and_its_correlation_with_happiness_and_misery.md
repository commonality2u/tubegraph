## The Tortured Genius Stereotype

The stereotype of the "tortured genius" is pervasive in pop culture, making it seem as if depression is a prerequisite for success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:56">[00:02:56]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:06">[00:03:06]</a>. ## Intelligence and Depression

A 2017 study by Dr. Ruth Karpinski explored the link between high intelligence and mental illness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:31">[00:03:31]</a>.

*   **Mensa Study Findings**
    *   Out of 3,700 Mensa members surveyed, over a third reported being depressed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:31">[00:03:31]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:40">[00:03:40]</a>.
    *   This rate is five times the national average <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:42">[00:03:42]</a>.
    *   Approximately 30 out of 100 Mensa members reported depression, compared to 6-7 out of 100 in the general population <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:45">[00:03:45]</a>.
    *   **Caveats:** This study relied on self-reported well-being, and roughly 10% of those reporting depression had not received a medical diagnosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:55">[00:03:55]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:59">[00:03:59]</a>.

## The "Hyper Brain" Concept

Karpinski asserted that individuals with IQs of 130-plus possess "hyper brains," allowing them to comprehend the world's problems, uncertainties, and possibilities more easily <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:20">[00:04:20]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:26">[00:04:26]</a>. This concept draws on the work of psychiatrist Dr. Kazimierz Dabrowski.

*   **Dabrowski's Overexcitability**
    *   Dabrowski observed that his exceptionally intelligent patients tended to overly contemplate everything, especially negative aspects, and experienced deeper depression <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:33">[00:04:33]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:45">[00:04:45]</a>.
    *   He termed these traits "overexcitability," which he considered predictive of achieving one's ideal self <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:50">[00:04:50]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:54">[00:04:54]</a>.
    *   In 1972, Dabrowski wrote that "one who manifests several forms of overexcitability sees reality in a different, stronger and more excitable manner." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:03">[00:05:03]</a>
    *   Five forms of overexcitability were identified: excessive energy/nervousness, deep appreciation for arts/music, extreme curiosity/active mind, vivid imagination, and extreme/complex emotional experiences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:11">[00:05:11]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:14">[00:05:14]</a>. Rick is considered a "textbook example" of these <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:27">[00:05:27]</a>.

## Intelligence and Happiness

While some studies link intelligence to depression, others suggest it can unlock opportunities for a healthier and happier life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:36">[00:07:36]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:41">[00:07:41]</a>.

*   **Financial Benefits:** Being smarter is generally associated with greater wealth and, consequently, better health <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:50">[00:07:50]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:54">[00:07:54]</a>. This correlation can be complex, as socio-economic factors can influence IQ test participation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:58">[00:07:58]</a>.
*   **Correlation with Happiness:** Despite the link to depression, some studies, such as one from the UK involving over 6,800 people, found a positive correlation between IQ and self-reported happiness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:13">[00:08:13]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:19">[00:08:19]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:21">[00:08:21]</a>.

While some research indicates that the "hyper brains" of highly intelligent individuals may make them more susceptible to depression due to their intense perception of the world and potential social isolation, their ability to leverage their intelligence for higher-paying jobs and better healthcare suggests they may experience greater overall happiness as a group <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:26">[00:10:26]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:38">[00:10:38]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:49">[00:10:49]</a>.

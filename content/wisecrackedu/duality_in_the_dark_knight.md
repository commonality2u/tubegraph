*The Dark Knight* is a film deeply permeated by duality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:28">[00:01:28]</a>. Co-written by Jonathan Nolan and directed by his brother, the film tells the story of Bruce Wayne, also known as Batman, in Gotham City <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:15">[00:00:15]</a>.

## Batman (The Dark Knight) vs. Harvey Dent (The White Knight)

Batman, also known as the Dark Knight, desires a world where he can be replaced by Harvey Dent, who is seen as the White Knight <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:30">[00:01:30]</a>. When Dent transforms into Two-Face after the death of Rachel Dawes, he embodies this duality, evident in the two sides of his face, his two-sided coin, and his two intact eyeballs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:08">[00:01:08]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:37">[00:01:37]</a>.

## Order vs. Chaos

Another significant duality explored in the film is order versus chaos <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:49">[00:01:49]</a>. The Joker's objective is to reveal to the people of Gotham that beneath the appearance of an orderly society lies existential anarchy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:54">[00:01:54]</a>. The Joker is depicted as a nihilist, someone who believes in nothing, not even the concept of nihilism itself <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:04">[00:02:04]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:06">[00:02:06]</a>. When questioned about his beliefs, his response is presented as meaningless <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:09">[00:02:09]</a>.

The Joker's terrorist acts, while appearing random, are purposeful; they aim to prove that morality is a sham <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:41">[00:02:41]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:45">[00:02:45]</a>. He seeks to demonstrate that if people's physical comforts or safety are removed, they will revert to their base, lawless nature <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:45">[00:02:45]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:53">[00:02:53]</a>. Examples of this include:
*   Inciting bank robbers to kill each other for a larger share of loot <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:58">[00:02:58]</a>.
*   Giving a weapon to three "friends" and telling them that only the survivor can join his team <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:00">[00:03:00]</a>.
*   Threatening to blow up hospitals to compel others to act on his behalf, leading to police corruption and attempted murder by citizens <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:07">[00:03:07]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:10">[00:03:10]</a>.

These acts align with the [[comparison_of_batman_and_the_jokers_responses_to_trauma_and_meaninglessness | Joker's nihilistic view]] that "Their code, it's a bad joke" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:56">[00:02:56]</a>.

## The Corruption of Harvey Dent

A key element in the film's exploration of duality is the Joker's corruption of District Attorney Harvey Dent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:15">[00:03:15]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:19">[00:03:19]</a>. Initially, Dent's double-sided coin represents his rigid adherence to justice, morality, and order <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:22">[00:03:22]</a>. However, after his transformation into Two-Face, the coin becomes scarred on one side, symbolizing a shift to a reality governed by cosmic anarchy, where "The only morality in a cruel world is chance" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:33">[00:03:33]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:45">[00:03:45]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:48">[00:03:48]</a>.

Dent is initially presented as a Christ figure, with his campaign slogan, "I believe in Harvey Dent," carrying religious connotations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:52">[00:03:52]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:57">[00:03:57]</a>. *The Dark Knight* offers a modern interpretation of the Christ image by depicting a "Christ without resurrection," implying a dead ideal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:05">[00:04:05]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:08">[00:04:08]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:10">[00:04:10]</a>. Despite this, Dent's image must be upheld as a false ideal and a beacon of hope for mankind, a burden that Batman takes upon himself to maintain order <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:14">[00:04:14]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:20">[00:04:20]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:24">[00:04:24]</a>.

## Order Through Idealism vs. Order Through Force

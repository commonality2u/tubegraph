## Core Narrative and Transformation

The film tells the story of Chihiro, a ten-year-old human female, who gets lost in the woods with her parents <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:33">[00:00:33]</a>. They stumble into a magical land with abundant food <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:47">[00:00:47]</a>. After her parents begin consuming the food, Chihiro abandons them to seek a job <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:05">[00:01:05]</a>. Yubaba hires Chihiro to work in her bathhouse but changes her name to Sen as a show of bad faith <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:16">[00:01:16]</a>. Haku warns Sen not to forget her real name, as it can cause problems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:26">[00:01:26]</a>.

As a [[comparison_of_spirited_away_to_other_coming_of_age_stories | coming of age story]], Chihiro is initially naive, inexperienced, timid, and whiny <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:36">[00:03:36]</a>. Through solving problems and helping others, she gains self-reliance and empathy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:46">[00:03:46]</a>.

## [[Themes of overconsumption and capitalism in Spirited Away | Symbolism of Greed and Postwar Japan]]

Underneath the story of renewal and transformation lies a cautionary tale of greed and overconsumption, particularly as it relates to postwar Japan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:54">[00:03:54]</a>. Following World War II, Japan experienced an economic boom that correlated with a cultural shift towards capitalism and away from traditional values <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:03">[00:04:03]</a>.

The Spirit Realm takes the form of an old amusement park styled after the Meiji period <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:16">[00:04:16]</a>. Discarded shrines adorn the hillside, symbolizing the relegation of cultural history in favor of modern pursuits like leisure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:27">[00:04:27]</a>.

In this economically-driven environment, overconsumption has dire consequences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:36">[00:04:36]</a>.
*   **Parents as Pigs**: Chihiro's parents snarf down food from the spirit realm <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:44">[00:04:44]</a>. In return, they are transformed into pigs, which were commonly used on Earth as a critical symbol of capitalist cultures that would bring about the eradication of humankind <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:55">[00:04:55]</a>.
*   **Bathhouse Furnace**: The bathhouse furnace is also shaped like a pig, constantly gobbling resources <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:07">[00:05:07]</a>.
*   **No-Face**: When [[character_names_and_symbolism | No-Face]] appears overflowing with gold, Yubaba and her workers exploit him <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:15">[00:05:15]</a>. As the staff showers No-Face in excess, he responds by showering them in stomach acid <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:22">[00:05:22]</a>.

The film expresses hope that modern Japan can find a balance between economic prosperity and spiritual heritage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:28">[00:05:28]</a>.

## [[Character names and symbolism | Importance of Identity and Heritage]]

A significant emphasis is placed on remembering one's name <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:35">[00:05:35]</a>, as forgetting it means never finding your way home <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:38">[00:05:38]</a>. The identity of one's parents is also crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:40">[00:05:40]</a>. These elements are important in forging a connection to one's ancestry <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:50">[00:05:50]</a>.

In the narrative, Sen remembers falling into the Kohaku River, leading her to realize that Haku is short for Kohaku and is a river spirit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:40">[00:02:40]</a>. Chihiro successfully identifies her parents at the end, allowing her to regain her identity as Chihiro <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:57">[00:02:57]</a>.

## [[Miyazakis artistic techniques and legacy through Spirited Away | Miyazaki's Legacy and Artistic Hope]]

The message deepens when considering the context of the film's creation. Prior to making *Spirited Away*, director Hayao Miyazaki was considering passing the torch to the next wave of artists at Studio Ghibli <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:56">[00:05:56]</a>. At a time when many animated films were visualized using computers, *Spirited Away* was drawn using an elaborate system of levers and pulleys <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:08">[00:06:08]</a>. Its story conveys the hope that future generations will flourish and progress while respecting the history of the art form <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:16">[00:06:16]</a>.

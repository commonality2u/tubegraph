## Early Career and the Rejection of Method Acting

Nicolas Cage, nephew of director Francis Ford Coppola, is one of Hollywood's most prolific actors, with over 73 films to his name <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:33:00">[02:33:00]</a>. Initially, Cage embraced method acting, even having two teeth removed for his role as an injured veteran in 1984's *Birdy* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:27:00">[06:27:00]</a>. This technique, influenced by Russian acting coach Constantine Stanislavsky, aims to help actors channel "emotional truth" from within and is exemplified by actors like Daniel Day-Lewis, who remained in a wheelchair throughout the production of *My Left Foot* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:34:00">[04:34:00]</a>.

However, Cage quickly grew tired of this style <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:36:00">[06:36:00]</a>. He embarked on a "truly Cage-ian search for a form of acting that went... beyond" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:38:00">[06:38:00]</a>. In 1990, he stated his desire to "put back into acting a more surreal or experimental style that wasn’t totally run by literalism, which seemed like a dead end" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:50:00">[06:50:00]</a>.

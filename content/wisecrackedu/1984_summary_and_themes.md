Winston, frustrated with the system, begins to express his defiance by writing in a journal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:31">[00:00:31]</a>. Criticism of Big Brother is a severe offense that can lead to imprisonment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:36">[00:00:36]</a>. He then receives a note from Julia, a woman he later develops strong feelings for <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:41">[00:00:41]</a>.

At work, Winston encounters O'Brien, whom he suspects also opposes Big Brother <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:53">[00:00:53]</a>. Winston and Julia visit O'Brien, who claims to be part of a rebel organization called "The Brotherhood" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:59">[00:00:59]</a>. Winston eagerly joins <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:06">[00:01:06]</a>.

While reading a book provided by O'Brien, Winston and Julia are apprehended by the police <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:08">[00:01:08]</a>. It is revealed that O'Brien is, in fact, a member of Big Brotherâ€™s Thought Police <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:16">[00:01:16]</a>.

O'Brien states their goal is to completely break Winston's spirit before filling him with "Big Brotha-ly love" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:32">[00:01:32]</a>. ## Themes

### Control and Manipulation of Reality

The Party employs various methods of control, including informants, telescreens, and most notably, the manipulation of reality itself <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:52">[00:02:52]</a>.

#### Doublethink
A core method of control is "doublethink," which involves holding two contradictory beliefs simultaneously <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:16">[00:02:16]</a>.
*   Consciously believing something is false <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:28">[00:02:28]</a>.
*   Simultaneously denying that it is a lie <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:33">[00:02:33]</a>.
This process leads individuals to accept nonsense as truth with full conviction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:36">[00:02:36]</a>. As described in O'Brien's book, "Doublethink goes on forever. With the lie always one step ahead of the truth" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:42">[00:02:42]</a>.

Unlike dictators who merely destroy truth, like Hitler who burned books, The Party aims to rewrite and rebuild reality to align with its agenda, similar to Stalin's regime <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:00">[00:03:00]</a>. Orwell's Oceania reflects Stalin's more effective methods of controlling his populace <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:16">[00:03:16]</a>.

### Loss of Individuality

As The Party reshapes reality, individual qualities are erased <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:23">[00:03:23]</a>. This raises a profound question: how can one maintain their existence if they are only what the government dictates they are? <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:29">[00:03:29]</a>

### Warning for Mankind

Beyond being a [[critique_of_totalitarian_regimes_in_1984 | critique of totalitarian regimes]] like Stalinism, "1984" serves as a broader warning to humanity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:33">[00:03:33]</a>. Orwell suggests that without societal change, humanity risks becoming "soulless robots" without even realizing it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:44">[00:03:44]</a>.

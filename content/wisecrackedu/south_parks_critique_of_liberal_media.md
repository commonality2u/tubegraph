## Nuanced Satire and Audience Reception

Matt and Trey have observed that both liberals and conservatives often praise the same *South Park* episodes, each believing the show secretly aligns with their perspective <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:25">[00:00:25]</a>. This highlights the show's departure from typical Hollywood satire, which tends to primarily mock conservatives, a trend that intensified with [[South Parks portrayal of Trump | Trump]]-themed lampoonery by shows like SNL <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:06">[00:06:06]</a>. *South Park*, however, is reluctant to constantly take "easy shots" at [[South Parks portrayal of Trump | Trump]] or his supporters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:28">[00:06:28]</a>. The show's "Doubling Down" episode is seen as a nuanced indictment of [[South Parks portrayal of Trump | Trump]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:03">[00:01:03]</a>.

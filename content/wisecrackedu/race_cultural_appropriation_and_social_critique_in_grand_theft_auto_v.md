## Franklin Clinton and Socioeconomic Critique

Franklin Clinton, an ambitious young man from South Los Santos, serves as a catalyst for the game's events, with his repossession of Jimmy’s vehicle bringing Michael out of retirement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:06">[00:10:06]</a>. Franklin's role in the market, particularly when he is asked to manipulate the stock market by Lester, highlights an interesting contrast <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:18">[00:10:18]</a>. The game critiques the contemporary condition where individuals who make millions or billions through dubious means, like insider trading, rarely face jail time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:50">[00:10:50]</a>. Franklin, ironically, hates pyramid schemes, despite operating within this system <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:44">[00:10:44]</a>.

### Policing and Social Inequality
The lack of effort to capture white-collar criminals in GTA V is sharply contrasted with the significant police presence and violence experienced in the inner city <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:08">[00:11:08]</a>. The Los Santos Police Department (LSPD) is presented as a caricature of the real-world LAPD, with their motto explicitly stating: "Obey and Survive" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:20">[00:11:20]</a>. This highlights the harsh realities faced by people in these communities, whether from criminal acts or police violence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:15">[00:11:15]</a>.

## Cultural Appropriation and "Authentic Blackness"

Complicated relations with race are a significant theme explored through Franklin’s character <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:36">[00:11:36]</a>. Characters who are not people of color, such as Trevor, Jimmy, and Devin, adapt their speech patterns to "street vernacular" when interacting with Franklin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:40">[00:11:40]</a>.

### Jimmy and the Mask of Blackness
Jimmy, in particular, acts as a foil to Franklin, attempting to prove his "authentic blackness" but merely wearing it "like a mask" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:56">[00:11:56]</a>. The game emphasizes Franklin’s "realness" by showing him shrugging off weak weed from a Marijuana Lobbyist, implying he smokes stronger "chronic" regularly, unlike Michael who sees aliens <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:07">[00:12:07]</a>.

### Player Engagement and Racial Politics
GTA V engages with the important discussion of the appropriation of Black culture <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:21">[00:12:21]</a>. Jimmy can "act black" whenever he chooses without having to deal with the daily realities of being a person of color in America <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:25">[00:12:25]</a>. This mirrors the player's experience: they can pick up the controller and play an "original gangsta" when they switch to Franklin, creating a distance from the racial politics of the game <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:30">[00:12:30]</a>.

The game challenges the desire for a guilt-free solution to racism by making the player laugh at white characters awkwardly attempting to pass as Black, while the player themselves controls a "stereotypical shadow of a black male" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:52">[00:12:52]</a>. This is ironic given that many white players in the suburbs may feel smug, believing they are not like "Jizzle," yet they participate in drive-bys, gang wars, and smoking weed, while believing Franklin is an accurate depiction of an "authentic black male" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:06">[00:13:06]</a>.

The game is far more than just stealing cars and blowing things up; it is a masterful [[representation_and_parody_of_american_culture_and_lifestyle_in_grand_theft_auto_v | criticism of American culture]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:37">[00:14:37]</a>.

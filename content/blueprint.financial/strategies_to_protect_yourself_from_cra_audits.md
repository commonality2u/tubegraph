## Increased Scrutiny and [[expanded_audit_powers_of_the_cra_and_implications | Expanded Audit Powers of the CRA]]

The CRA is enhancing its ability to detect tax fraud and evasion through various means, increasing the [[risks_and_red_flags_for_being_audited_by_the_cra | risks and red flags for being audited by the CRA]].

### Targeting Tax Fraud with New Technologies

The CRA is leveraging advanced tools like AI and machine learning to track down tax fraud and evasion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:31">[00:00:31]</a>. These tools analyze vast amounts of data, identifying suspicious patterns and inconsistencies in tax filings faster than ever before <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:38">[00:00:38]</a>.

*   **AI-Driven Audit Selection**: The CRA uses advanced analytics to flag tax returns with unusual deductions, excessive claims, or inconsistent income reporting, rather than relying solely on random audits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:01">[00:01:01]</a>.
*   **Automated Fraud Detection**: Machine learning models recognize patterns from past fraudulent filings to detect new cases in real-time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:13">[00:01:13]</a>.
*   **GST and HST Return Fraud**: Businesses and individuals filing for unwarranted tax refunds, rebates, or input tax credits are automatically cross-checked against financial and corporate records <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:23">[00:01:23]</a>.
*   **New Data Sharing Agreements**: Agreements with banks and foreign financial institutions allow the CRA to compare tax filings with banking data and offshore disclosures, making it harder to hide unreported income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:41">[00:01:41]</a>.

#### Who's at Risk?
*   **Excessive or Inconsistent Claims**: Taxpayers whose deductions seem too high compared to their income, or whose reported earnings fluctuate drastically year-to-year without clear reason, may be flagged <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:00">[00:02:00]</a>. The CRA's AI compares your filing to similar taxpayers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:11">[00:02:11]</a>.
*   **Unwarranted GST and HST Refunds**: Individuals and businesses inflating expenses, underreporting sales, or falsely claiming tax rebates will draw scrutiny <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:30">[00:02:30]</a>. The CRA is particularly focused on businesses using shell companies or questionable bookkeeping <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:42">[00:02:42]</a>.

### Proposed [[expanded_audit_powers_of_the_cra_and_implications | CRA's Expanded Audit Powers]]

Budget 2024 introduces sweeping new audit powers for the CRA, making tax enforcement more aggressive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:53">[00:02:53]</a>.

*   **Fines for Non-Compliance**: If you don't fully cooperate with an audit request, the CRA can issue a Notice of Non-Compliance (NNC), fining you $50 per day up to $25,000 until the issue is resolved <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:37">[00:03:37]</a>.
*   **Paused Reassessment Period**: The CRA can pause your reassessment period, extending how long they can audit you <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:48">[00:03:48]</a>.
*   **Questioning Under Oath**: The CRA will have the power to force taxpayers to provide oral statements under oath or affirmation, a significant shift from current procedures without standard legal protections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:54">[00:03:54]</a>.
*   **Severe Compliance Penalties**: Failure to comply with a court-issued order can result in a 10% penalty on your total tax bill for the years in question, even for withholding a single document <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:11">[00:04:11]</a>.
*   **Extended Audit Deadlines**: If you challenge an NNC or fight a CRA demand in court, your reassessment period will be extended indefinitely until the case is resolved <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:26">[00:04:26]</a>.

These changes mean audits could become more aggressive, time-consuming, and costly, even for routine audits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:35">[00:03:35]</a>. You could face penalties before an audit finishes, be forced to give statements under oath without standard legal protections, and spend more time and money responding to CRA requests <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:49">[00:04:49]</a>.

### Increased Audit Activities in Real Estate and Cash-Based Businesses

The CRA is intensifying its audit activities, particularly focusing on real estate and cash-heavy businesses due to concerns about unreported income, undeclared capital gains, and tax evasion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:04">[00:05:04]</a>.

#### Who's at Risk?
*   **Real Estate Investors, House Flippers, and Landlords**: Increased scrutiny, especially for those misusing the principal residence exemption to avoid capital gains taxes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:30">[00:05:30]</a>. Selling multiple properties in recent years may lead the CRA to question if you're a homeowner or flipping houses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:38">[00:05:38]</a>. Landlords using platforms like Airbnb or VRBO are at risk if they fail to report rental income correctly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:45">[00:05:45]</a>.
*   **Cash-Based Businesses**: Restaurants, bars, construction companies, and auto repair shops are prime targets as they have a higher risk of underreporting sales or inflating expenses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:54">[00:05:54]</a>.

Real estate transactions are getting extra attention for undeclared capital gains and rental income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:11">[00:06:11]</a>. The Underused Housing Tax (UHT) is also a focus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:17">[00:06:17]</a>. For business owners, reported earnings suspiciously low compared to industry standards may trigger an audit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:36">[00:06:36]</a>.

### Crackdown on Aggressive Tax Planning and Evasion

The CRA is taking a much harder stance on aggressive tax planning and tax evasion, especially involving offshore accounts or sophisticated corporate structures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:46">[00:06:46]</a>.

*   **Offshore Tax Evasion**: Canada works with over 100 countries through the Common Reporting Standard, allowing financial institutions worldwide to share data with tax authorities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:11">[00:07:11]</a>. Undeclared foreign assets or income are more likely to be discovered <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:21">[00:07:21]</a>.
*   **Corporate Tax Planning**: Businesses using strategies like income shifting, artificial losses, or questionable tax credits face tougher audits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:27">[00:07:27]</a>.
*   **Tax Shelters**: The CRA investigates setups designed to conceal income through trusts, numbered companies, and shell corporations, which often aim to create paper losses or high taxable revenue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:36">[00:07:36]</a>.

Penalties are severe, with an [[increase_in_cra_audits_and_penalties | increased number of tax evasion cases referred for prosecution]], potentially leading to criminal investigations and even jail time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:52">[00:07:52]</a>.

#### Who's at Risk?
*   **High Net Worth Individuals**: Those using offshore tax [[strategies for managing increased tax liabilities | strategies]] or having foreign accounts/assets not properly disclosed are major targets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:11">[00:08:11]</a>.
*   **Businesses with Complex Tax Planning Schemes**: Especially those artificially shifting profits or exploiting tax loopholes, or transactions that seem to exist purely for tax benefits rather than genuine business purposes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:23">[00:08:23]</a>.

> [!CAUTION] The bottom line is if a tax strategy seems too good to be true it's probably on the CRA's radar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:38">[00:08:38]</a>.

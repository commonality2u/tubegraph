## Projected Increases by Province (2025)

While these figures represent projected increases rather than actual food costs, they highlight significant regional disparities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:24">[00:46:24]</a>:

*   **Quebec** is projected to experience the smallest increase in food prices, at 1.8% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:35">[00:46:35]</a>.
*   **Newfoundland** is expected to see the highest increase, up to 3.8% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:39">[00:46:39]</a>.
*   **Other provinces** are anticipated to see increases hovering around 3% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:42">[00:46:42]</a>.

Even seemingly small increases in food inflation can have a significant [[impact_of_inflation_on_canadians_financial_struggles | impact]], affecting choices, health, and overall quality of life, with the severity of this impact often dependent on geographical location <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:44">[00:46:44]</a>.

## Median Income by Education Level (2016)

The median income in Canada shows a clear upward trend as educational attainment rises <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:32">[00:07:32]</a>.

| Education Level | Median Income (2016) |
| :---------------- | :------------------- |
| Bachelor's Degree or Higher | $69,950 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:48">[00:07:48]</a> |
| College Diploma or Trades Certificate | $47,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:50">[00:07:50]</a> |
| High School Diploma | $35,790 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:03">[00:08:03]</a> |

### Key Observations:

*   **Higher Education Advantage**: Individuals with a bachelor's degree or higher earned a median income of $69,950 in 2016 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:48">[00:07:48]</a>.
*   **College/Trades**: Those with a college diploma or trades certificate made approximately $47,000, which is about 31% lower than those with a bachelor's degree <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:50">[00:07:50]</a>.
*   **High School or Less**: Individuals with only a high school diploma faced a significant income gap, earning a median income of $35,790 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:02">[00:08:02]</a>. This figure is over 60% less than the highest earners <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:19">[00:08:19]</a>.

These substantial differences highlight the significant [[strategies_for_increasing_income_in_canada | importance of education]] for increasing income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:21">[00:08:21]</a>. Investing in higher education leads to higher earnings, as the data is indisputable: a university degree can significantly boost income compared to lower levels of education <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:33">[00:10:33]</a>.

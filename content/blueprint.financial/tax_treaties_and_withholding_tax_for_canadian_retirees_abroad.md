When Canadians retire abroad, understanding how their government benefits are taxed is crucial. The impact of [[Impact of retiring outside Canada on Canadian benefits | moving outside Canada]] on benefits like the Canada Pension Plan (CPP), Old Age Security (OAS), and Guaranteed Income Supplement (GIS) varies significantly, especially concerning withholding taxes and international tax treaties <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:21">[00:00:21]</a>.

## Guaranteed Income Supplement (GIS)

The [[Guaranteed Income Supplement (GIS) | GIS]] is a monthly benefit intended for low-income seniors residing in Canada <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:54">[00:00:54]</a>. It is not portable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:51">[00:00:51]</a>. If you leave Canada for more than six consecutive months, your [[Guaranteed Income Supplement (GIS) | GIS]] payments will stop completely <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>. There is no social security agreement or tax treaty that protects [[Guaranteed Income Supplement (GIS) | GIS]]; it is strictly tied to your physical presence in Canada <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:37">[00:01:37]</a>. Therefore, withholding tax considerations for [[Guaranteed Income Supplement (GIS) | GIS]] do not apply as the benefit ceases.

## Canada Pension Plan (CPP)

The [[Canada Pension Plan (CPP) | CPP]] is based on your contributions during your working life in Canada, not on your residency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:29">[00:02:29]</a>. This means your [[Canada Pension Plan (CPP) | CPP]] benefits will continue to be paid no matter where you live <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:39">[00:02:39]</a>.

However, once you are outside Canada, your [[Canada Pension Plan (CPP) | CPP]] payments may be subject to **withholding tax** if you are considered a non-resident <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:15">[00:03:15]</a>.

### Role of Tax Treaties

*   **Reduction or Elimination**: If Canada has a tax treaty with the country you are moving to, that withholding tax could be reduced or even eliminated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:21">[00:03:21]</a>.
*   **Default Rate**: Without a tax treaty, you could face a 25% withholding tax on your [[Canada Pension Plan (CPP) | CPP]] payments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:25">[00:03:25]</a>.
*   **Example (US)**: If you move to the US, a tax treaty is in place, and you may avoid withholding tax entirely on your [[Canada Pension Plan (CPP) | CPP]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:32">[00:03:32]</a>.
*   **Reviewing Treaties**: It is crucial to review your destination's tax treaty with Canada carefully, as these agreements vary widely and can significantly impact the amount of [[Canada Pension Plan (CPP) | CPP]] you retain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:40">[00:03:40]</a>.

### Social Security Agreements

Canada has social security agreements with over 50 countries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:51">[00:03:51]</a>. These agreements help avoid double contributions, ensure your [[Canada Pension Plan (CPP) | CPP]] coverage continues uninterrupted, and coordinate benefits if you worked in both Canada and abroad <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:57">[00:03:57]</a>.
[[Canada Pension Plan (CPP) | CPP]] survivor benefits and child benefits also continue while living outside Canada <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:08">[00:04:08]</a>.

### Local Taxation

Your new country might tax your [[Canada Pension Plan (CPP) | CPP]] payments differently, so it is advisable to check local tax laws to avoid surprises <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:31">[00:04:31]</a>.

## Old Age Security (OAS)

[[Old Age Security (OAS) | OAS]] benefits are based on residency, not work history or contributions like [[Canada Pension Plan (CPP) | CPP]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:45">[00:04:45]</a>. To receive [[Old Age Security (OAS) | OAS]] abroad, you must have lived in Canada for at least 20 years after turning 18 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:57">[00:04:57]</a>. If you meet this threshold, your monthly [[Old Age Security (OAS) | OAS]] payments will continue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:04">[00:05:04]</a>. If you don't meet the 20-year rule and leave Canada for more than six consecutive months, your [[Old Age Security (OAS) | OAS]] will be suspended <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:31">[00:05:31]</a>.

### Exceptions via Social Security Agreements

You might still qualify for [[Old Age Security (OAS) | OAS]] abroad if you lived or worked in a country that has a social security agreement with Canada, and the combined time you lived in both countries is at least 20 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:52">[00:06:02]</a>. However, the years lived in another country do not increase your [[Old Age Security (OAS) | OAS]] amount; they only help determine if you qualify to keep receiving it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:04">[00:06:04]</a>.

### Taxation of OAS Payments

Your [[Old Age Security (OAS) | OAS]] payments are considered taxable income even when you live abroad <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:12">[00:06:12]</a>.
[[Old Age Security (OAS) | OAS]] may be subject to a **non-resident withholding tax** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:30">[00:06:30]</a>. The default rate is 25% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:33">[00:06:33]</a>.

### Role of Tax Treaties

*   **Reduction or Elimination**: If you live in a country with a tax treaty with Canada, that rate may be reduced or eliminated entirely <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:35">[00:06:35]</a>.
*   **Example (US)**: If you move to the US, Canada's tax treaty means your [[Old Age Security (OAS) | OAS]] is not subject to withholding tax <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:42">[00:06:42]</a>.
*   **Example (Germany)**: For Germany, the tax treaty (Article 18) allows Canada to tax public pensions like [[Old Age Security (OAS) | OAS]] at a rate not exceeding 15% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:49">[00:06:49]</a>. So, you might pay about a 15% withholding tax on your [[Old Age Security (OAS) | OAS]] payments while living there <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:05">[00:07:05]</a>.

### Tax Filing Requirements

While abroad, you will receive an NR4 tax slip each year instead of a T4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:12">[00:07:12]</a>. You must include this when filing your annual tax return <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:19">[00:07:19]</a>. You must continue filing a Canadian tax return every year to keep receiving your [[Old Age Security (OAS) | OAS]] while abroad <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:23">[00:07:23]</a>.

## Planning Strategies

*   **Choose a Treaty Country**: Selecting a country with a tax treaty with Canada can significantly reduce your tax burden on [[Canada Pension Plan (CPP) | CPP]] and [[Old Age Security (OAS) | OAS]] payments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:44">[00:08:44]</a>. For example, moving to Portugal, which has a social security and tax treaty with Canada, means a reduced 15% withholding tax on [[Canada Pension Plan (CPP) | CPP]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:18">[00:08:18]</a>.
*   **Voluntary Tax Deductions**: You can request voluntary tax deductions for your [[Old Age Security (OAS) | OAS]] by logging into your My Service Canada account or filling out the relevant form <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:21">[00:06:21]</a>.
*   **Manage Taxable Income**: If you are concerned about hitting the [[Old Age Security (OAS) | OAS]] recovery tax (clawback), which begins at $93,454 in 2025, consider [[financial_strategies_for_canadian_retirees | managing your taxable income]] through RRSP withdrawals, pension income splitting, or deferring [[Old Age Security (OAS) | OAS]] to age 70 for a higher benefit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:30">[00:07:30]</a>.
*   **[[Managing Canadian income after becoming a nonresident | RRSP Withdrawals]]**: Consider drawing down your RRSPs before becoming a non-resident <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:59">[00:09:59]</a>. Be aware of [[withholding_tax_surprises_for_rrsp_withdrawals | withholding tax surprises for RRSP withdrawals]] as non-residents <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:19">[00:00:19]</a>.

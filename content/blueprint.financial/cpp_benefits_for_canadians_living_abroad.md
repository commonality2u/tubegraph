When planning to permanently leave Canada, understanding how your [[details_on_canada_pension_plan_benefits | Canada Pension Plan]] (CPP) benefits are affected is crucial for effective [[retirement_planning_for_canadian_expats | retirement planning]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:10">[00:00:10]</a>. The [[details_on_canada_pension_plan_benefits | CPP]] is based on contributions made during your working life in Canada <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:02">[00:06:02]</a>.

## CPP Benefits Abroad

Your [[details_on_canada_pension_plan_benefits | CPP]] benefits will continue to be paid even if you move abroad <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:11">[00:06:11]</a>. The amount you receive abroad remains the same as if you were living in Canada <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:16">[00:06:16]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:30">[00:06:30]</a>.

## [[Tax implications for Canadian benefits abroad | Tax Implications]]

[[Tax implications for Canadian benefits abroad | CPP payments]] may be subject to withholding taxes, which vary depending on the tax treaty status of your new country of residence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:34">[00:06:34]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:36">[00:06:36]</a>.

*   **With a Tax Treaty:** If you move to a country like the U.S., a tax treaty may reduce or eliminate the withholding tax on your [[details_on_canada_pension_plan_benefits | CPP]] benefits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:45">[00:06:45]</a>.
*   **Without a Tax Treaty:** You could face a 25% withholding tax on your payments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:51">[00:06:51]</a>.

It is crucial to understand the [[Tax implications for Canadian benefits abroad | tax implications]] of your new country of residence to avoid surprises <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:58">[00:07:58]</a>.

## Social Security Agreements

Canada has social security agreements with over 50 countries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:59">[00:06:59]</a>. These agreements ensure your [[details_on_canada_pension_plan_benefits | CPP]] coverage continues uninterrupted and help avoid dual contributions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:01">[00:07:01]</a>. They also coordinate pension benefits for individuals who have lived and worked abroad <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:09">[00:07:09]</a>.

## Family Benefits

[[details_on_canada_pension_plan_benefits | CPP]] survivor benefits and [[canada_child_benefit_explained | Child Benefits]] will continue to be paid even if the recipient lives abroad, ensuring your family benefits are secure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:15">[00:07:15]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:22">[00:07:22]</a>.

## Receiving Payments

You have several options for receiving your [[details_on_canada_pension_plan_benefits | CPP]] payments abroad:
*   Direct deposit into a local bank account in the local currency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:32">[00:07:32]</a>.
*   Direct deposit into a Canadian bank account, which can then be transferred to your new bank using services like Wise <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:34">[00:07:34]</a>.
*   Mailed checks in Canadian dollars, though this is generally not the best option <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:44">[00:07:44]</a>.

## Pro Tip: [[pension_income_splitting_in_canada | CPP Splitting]]

If you are married or in a common-law relationship, consider exploring [[pension_income_splitting_in_canada | CPP splitting]] to potentially reduce your overall taxes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:05">[00:08:05]</a>.

## General Advice

It is crucial to consult with a cross-border tax professional to understand the specific details and [[Tax implications for Canadian benefits abroad | implications]] of your situation when considering moving abroad <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:40">[00:11:40]</a>. Ideally, seek advice from professionals both in Canada and in the country you plan to move to <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:46">[00:11:46]</a>.

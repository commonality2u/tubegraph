## Big Mistakes to Avoid

To reach the $1 million TFSA goal, it's crucial to steer clear of common pitfalls <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:23">[00:00:23]</a>.

### Investing Only in a Savings Account
Relying solely on a savings account for your TFSA investments is a slow path to growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:30">[00:00:30]</a>.
*   **Scenario:** Starting with $95,000 in a TFSA and contributing $7,000 annually, earning a low 2% interest <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:32">[00:00:32]</a>.
*   **Outcome:** It would take a staggering 56 years to reach $1 million, with total contributions amounting to $487,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:45">[00:00:45]</a>. Starting at age 25, you'd be 81 by the time you reach this goal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:01">[00:01:01]</a>.
*   **Prevalence:** A recent survey indicates that 47% of Canadian TFSA holders only have cash and savings in their accounts, which means many may never reach the million-dollar goal even if they max out their TFSAs annually <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:09">[00:01:09]</a>.

### Taking on Way Too Much Risk
On the opposite end of the spectrum, taking excessive risks, such as betting on very high-risk crypto coins, is also a detrimental strategy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:26">[00:01:26]</a>.
*   **Scenario:** An investor with $95,000 who doubles their money with a 100% return every year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:35">[00:01:35]</a>.
*   **Outcome:** While theoretically reaching $1 million in 3 to 4 years with total contributions of $120,000, this is a "terrible strategy" for most <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:41">[00:01:41]</a>. The chances of going to zero are "extremely high," making the strategy worthless as it eliminates any chance of reaching the goal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:06">[00:02:06]</a>.
*   **Principle:** As Warren Buffett's famous quote states, "Rule number one: Never lose money. Rule number two: Never forget rule number one," emphasizing the importance of avoiding losses over spectacular gains <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:22">[00:02:22]</a>.

## Realistic Strategies for Sustainable Growth

The key to achieving a $1 million TFSA in a reasonable timeframe is to find a middle ground by balancing risk and return <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:46">[00:02:46]</a>.

*   **Determine Your Risk Tolerance:** Begin by completing a free online questionnaire to understand your risk tolerance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:29">[00:03:29]</a>.
*   **Pick the Right Investments:** Select investments that align with your risk tolerance and can help you reach your goal within a reasonable amount of time, while regularly contributing to your TFSA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:37">[00:03:37]</a>. This highlights the [[importance_of_having_a_clear_tfsa_strategy | importance of having a clear TFSA strategy]].

### Example: Investing 100% in Equities
*   **Risk Tolerance:** High <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:49">[00:03:49]</a>.
*   **Expected Return:** Using a conservative 7% average return, compared to the Canadian stock market's 20-year return of 7.8% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:00">[00:04:00]</a>.
*   **Scenario:** Starting with $95,000 and contributing $7,000 annually <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:06">[00:04:06]</a>.
*   **Outcome:** It would take 25 years to reach $1 million, with total contributions of $270,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:16">[00:04:16]</a>. If started at age 40, the goal could be met by retirement age 65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:24">[00:04:24]</a>.

### Example: Investing 50% Equities and 50% Bonds
*   **Risk Tolerance:** Medium <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:31">[00:04:31]</a>.
*   **Expected Return:** Combining equities (7%) and bonds (2-3% based on 20-year returns) for an average return of 4.5% per year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:35">[00:04:35]</a>.
*   **Scenario:** Starting with $95,000 and contributing $7,000 annually <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:48">[00:04:48]</a>.
*   **Outcome:** It would take approximately 35 years to reach $1 million, with total contributions of $340,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:53">[00:04:53]</a>. Starting at age 30, the goal could be met by retirement age 65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:00">[00:05:00]</a>. This is a medium-risk strategy attainable by most people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:11">[00:05:11]</a>.

The time it takes to reach your TFSA goal depends on your chosen risk and return levels. The key is to find a "sweet spot" that avoids excessive risk while still generating enough return to reach your goal efficiently <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:20">[00:05:20]</a>. This illustrates the importance of [[compounding_and_investment_growth_in_a_tfsa | compounding and investment growth in a TFSA]].

## Investment Options for TFSA Growth

Here are five investment options to consider for [[maximizing_tfsa_for_taxfree_growth | maximizing TFSA for tax-free growth]] and reaching your $1 million goal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:36">[00:05:36]</a>. These are examples of [[different_investment_options_for_tfsa_growth | different investment options for TFSA growth]].

1.  **Avoiding Mutual Funds at Big Banks:** While convenient, mutual funds offered by big banks often come with high fees (sometimes topping 3% per year), significantly eroding investment gains <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:47">[00:05:47]</a>. It's generally not recommended to go this route <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:15">[00:06:15]</a>.
2.  **Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs):** ETFs are excellent for keeping costs down as they mirror the performance of market indexes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:19">[00:06:19]</a>. Providers like Vanguard and iShares offer ETFs with very low fees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:33">[00:06:33]</a>. However, they still expose you to market volatility and require basic trading knowledge since they are bought and sold on stock exchanges <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:41">[00:06:41]</a>.
3.  **Individual Stocks:** Investing in individual stocks provides ultimate control and the lowest fees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:50">[00:06:50]</a>. A dividend stock strategy can offer capital appreciation potential and regular income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:00">[00:07:00]</a>. This approach requires significant market knowledge and time to manage a diversified portfolio effectively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:13">[00:07:13]</a>.
4.  **Robo-Advisors:** These automated platforms manage investments through algorithms, aligning with your financial goals and risk tolerance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:27">[00:07:27]</a>. Popular Canadian options include Wealthsimple and Questwealth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:37">[00:07:37]</a>. Robo-advisors offer convenience and simplicity, ideal for new investors or those preferring a hands-off approach, and allow starting with small amounts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:43">[00:07:43]</a>. However, they lack the personal touch of a human advisor and may not fully capture unique investment opportunities or tailor strategies for changing circumstances <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:00">[00:08:00]</a>.
5.  **Portfolio Manager:** For those who find managing investments overwhelming or prefer a personal touch over algorithms, hiring a portfolio manager is an excellent option <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:21">[00:08:21]</a>. Traditionally for the wealthy, this option is now accessible to almost anyone <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:29">[00:08:29]</a>. A professional manager can tailor your portfolio to specific needs and risk tolerance, optimizing chances for success without daily hassle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:36">[00:08:36]</a>.

## Using TFSA in Retirement Planning

Once you've built up your TFSA to $1 million, understanding how to withdraw from it strategically is vital for [[using_tfsa_in_retirement_planning | using TFSA in retirement planning]] and [[maximizing_tfsa_contributions_in_retirement | maximizing TFSA contributions in retirement]] (or rather, withdrawals) for tax savings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:00">[00:09:00]</a>.

### TFSA vs. RSP Withdrawal Order
The choice between withdrawing from your RSP (Registered Retirement Savings Plan) or TFSA first depends on individual circumstances <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:07">[00:09:07]</a>.
*   **RSP First:** Withdrawing from your RSP early in retirement, when income might be lower, can reduce your overall tax burden, especially before receiving Old Age Security (OAS), which can be subject to clawbacks based on income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:18">[00:09:18]</a>.
*   **TFSA First:** TFSA withdrawals are not taxable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:34">[00:09:34]</a>. This allows your investments to continue growing tax-free and provides a tax-efficient source of funds that does not impact your OAS <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:36">[00:09:36]</a>. This helps to [[strategies_to_avoid_tfsa_penalties | avoid TFSA penalties]] on withdrawals as they are not income.

### Bucket Strategy for a $1 Million TFSA
With a substantial $1 million TFSA, a bucket strategy can ensure longevity and continued growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:44">[00:09:44]</a>.
*   **Short-Term Funds:** Keep in safer investments like bonds or GICs to cover immediate expenses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:55">[00:09:55]</a>.
*   **Medium-Term Funds:** Invest in balanced funds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:03">[00:10:03]</a>.
*   **Long-Term Funds:** Remain in equities to ensure growth against inflation over time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:06">[00:10:06]</a>. This approach ensures funds are available when needed while still working for you <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:10">[00:10:10]</a>.

### Managing TFSA with CPP
A strategic approach is essential for integrating your TFSA with Canada Pension Plan (CPP) benefits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:17">[00:10:17]</a>.
*   **Supplement Income:** Use your TFSA to supplement CPP amounts during years when other income is lower <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:22">[00:10:22]</a>.
*   **Delay CPP Withdrawals:** Consider delaying CPP withdrawals while drawing from your TFSA, especially if you retire early and need funds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:31">[00:10:31]</a>. This allows your government benefits to grow, maximizing your income later in retirement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:38">[00:10:38]</a>.

Hitting the $1 million TFSA goal requires a simple yet smart strategy and proper management of risk and return <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:44">[00:10:44]</a>. The more challenging aspect often lies in figuring out the optimal tax strategy for your TFSA once it's built up <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:54">[00:10:54]</a>.

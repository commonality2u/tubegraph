## The Power of Investing vs. Saving

Consider Low Growth Larry and Steady Investor Sally to understand the impact of smart [[key_investment_strategies_and_options | investing]] versus just saving <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:17">[00:00:17]</a>.

*   **Low Growth Larry**: Saves $2,000 a year in a TD savings account earning 0.01% interest <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:23">[00:00:23]</a>. Over 30 years, he contributes $600,000 but ends up with only $600,931, barely growing his money and losing real value to inflation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:33">[00:00:33]</a>.
*   **Steady Investor Sally**: Invests $7,000 a year in a portfolio earning an annualized 7% return <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:45">[00:00:45]</a>. Over the same 30 years, she contributes $210,000 but her [[key_investment_strategies_and_options | investments]] grow to $777,000, outpacing Larry by over $100,000 despite contributing far less <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:53">[00:00:53]</a>.

This demonstrates the snowball effect of [[key_investment_strategies_and_options | investments]], where compounding returns lead to significant growth over time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:07">[00:01:07]</a>.

## A Five-Step Framework for Investing

### Step 1: Develop Your Investment Philosophy (Start with the Why)

This is the most crucial step in your investment journey <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:21">[00:01:21]</a>. [[key_investment_strategies_and_options | Investing]] without purpose is like sailing without a destination, leading to aimless drifting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:29">[00:01:29]</a>.

Have a clear purpose for investing:
*   Saving for a down payment on a house <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:38">[00:01:38]</a>
*   Comfortable [[retirement_savings_growth_strategies | retirement planning]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:40">[00:01:40]</a>
*   Achieving financial freedom <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:42">[00:01:42]</a>
*   Funding education <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:45">[00:01:45]</a>
*   Providing for family <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:46">[00:01:46]</a>

Specific and personal reasons create a stronger foundation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:49">[00:01:49]</a>. Knowing your "why" helps you stick to your plan, weather market fluctuations, and make decisions aligned with long-term goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:24">[00:02:24]</a>.

### Step 2: Figure Out How Much You Can Contribute

Once your "why" is clear, determine your contribution strategy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:41">[00:02:41]</a>.

*   **Review your budget**: Assess how much you can realistically set aside each month for investing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:52">[00:02:52]</a>. Track income and expenses to find saving opportunities, even small ones like cutting unused subscriptions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:57">[00:02:57]</a>.
*   **Automate contributions**: Set up recurring transfers to your investment accounts on payday to make [[investing_strategies_for_increasing_net_worth | investing]] a habit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:09">[00:03:09]</a>.

Consistent contributions are vital for portfolio growth and maximizing compounding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:20">[00:03:20]</a>. The impact of consistent monthly contributions over 30 years at a 7% rate of return highlights this power <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:27">[00:03:27]</a>:

*   **$100/month**: Grows to over $121,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:36">[00:03:36]</a>.
*   **$500/month**: Builds a portfolio worth more than $600,000, significantly impacting [[retirement_savings_growth_strategies | retirement planning]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:44">[00:03:44]</a>.
*   **$1,000/month**: Can grow to over $1.2 million, approaching financial freedom <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:52">[00:03:52]</a>.

For more insights on contributions, a video is available about building a massive TFSA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:01">[00:04:01]</a>.

### Step 3: Choose Your Investments

This is often the most time-consuming and confusing part, but it's where your investment journey takes shape <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:16">[00:04:16]</a>. Spend time researching <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:26">[00:04:26]</a>.

#### Understand Your Risk Tolerance and Time Horizon
Before picking [[key_investment_strategies_and_options | investments]], assess your comfort with risk <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:30">[00:04:30]</a>. Are you comfortable with portfolio dips during market downturns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:37">[00:04:37]</a>? Your time horizon—how long you plan to stay invested—also matters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:45">[00:04:45]</a>. Longer timelines generally allow for higher-risk [[key_investment_strategies_and_options | investments]] as there's more time to recover from fluctuations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:48">[00:04:48]</a>. Tools like Vanguard's free investor questionnaire can help determine your [[investment_strategies_and_asset_allocation_based_on_risk_tolerance | risk tolerance]] and [[investment_strategies_and_asset_allocation_based_on_risk_tolerance | investment style]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:55">[00:04:55]</a>.

!!! quote | Peter Lynch
    "Everybody in the world is a long-term investor until the markets start going down." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:15">[00:05:15]</a>

This quote highlights that many overestimate their actual [[investment_strategies_and_asset_allocation_based_on_risk_tolerance | risk tolerance]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:21">[00:05:21]</a>.

#### Investment Options
*   **For Beginners**: ETFs (Exchange-Traded Funds) and Robo-advisors are diversified and require little management <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:27">[00:05:27]</a>. Examples include all-in-one ETFs like VGRO or Robo-advisors such as Questwealth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:35">[00:05:35]</a>.
*   **Individual Stocks**: As confidence grows, explore individual stocks, focusing on companies you believe in and understand, while also diversifying <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:43">[00:05:43]</a>.
*   **Advanced Options**: With more experience, consider diversifying into real estate, private equity, or hiring portfolio managers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:50">[00:05:50]</a>.

The video also presents a list of pros and cons for various [[choosing_investments_and_managing_risk | investment options]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:01">[00:06:01]</a>.

#### Mistakes to Avoid
*   **Panic Selling**: Staying invested during market downturns is crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:09">[00:06:09]</a>. A $10,000 investment in the S&P 500 from 2004 to 2024 grew at an annualized 10.5% when fully invested, even through the 2008 financial crisis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:13">[00:06:13]</a>. Missing just the 10 best days reduced returns significantly, highlighting the importance of staying invested for long-term growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:28">[00:06:28]</a>.
*   **Staying on the Sidelines**: Waiting for the "perfect time" to invest is a losing strategy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:42">[00:06:42]</a>. Even investing at the worst possible time often outperforms sitting in cash <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:47">[00:06:47]</a>. Start with the basics and learn as you go <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:51">[00:06:51]</a>.

### Step 4: Choose Your Accounts

Choosing the right [[prioritizing_contributions_to_taxadvantaged_accounts | investment account]] can significantly impact your after-tax return <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:56">[00:06:56]</a>. In Canada, the TFSA (Tax-Free Savings Account), RRSP (Registered Retirement Savings Plan), and FHSA (First Home Savings Account) offer unique tax benefits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:01">[00:07:01]</a>.

A comparison of $10,000 invested over 30 years at 6% growth, with a 40% current tax rate, 20% future withdrawal tax rate for RRSP, and 40% annual tax on non-registered growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:06">[00:07:06]</a>:

*   **RRSP**: Comes out ahead with an after-tax balance of $64,327, thanks to an upfront tax refund and tax-deferred growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:24">[00:07:24]</a>. This is most effective if your retirement tax rate is significantly lower than your current rate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:33">[00:07:33]</a>.
*   **TFSA**: A close second, growing to $57,434 entirely tax-free <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:40">[00:07:40]</a>. It's ideal for flexibility and tax-free withdrawals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:47">[00:07:47]</a>.
*   **Non-Registered Account**: Only grows to $28,896, demonstrating how annual taxation drastically limits compounding growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:51">[00:07:51]</a>.

For more details on the best order of [[tax_saving_strategies_for_investments_in_canada | investing in Canada]], check out a dedicated video on the topic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:03">[00:08:03]</a>.

### Step 5: Buy the Investments and Track Performance

Once you've chosen your accounts and [[investment_strategies_and_asset_allocation_based_on_risk_tolerance | strategies]], it's time to purchase your [[key_investment_strategies_and_options | investments]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:12">[00:08:12]</a>.

#### How to Buy Stocks and ETFs
1.  **Pick a Platform**:
    *   **Wealthsimple**: User-friendly, great for beginners, offers commission-free trading for stocks and ETFs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:26">[00:08:26]</a>.
    *   **Questrade**: Ideal for active investors or those needing advanced features, with low trading fees and free ETF purchases <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:35">[00:08:35]</a>.
2.  **Set Up Your Account**: Choose the account type (TFSA, RRSP, FHSA, non-registered), provide personal details, and verify identity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:45">[00:08:45]</a>.
3.  **Fund Your Account**: Transfer money from an existing account (can take weeks) or cash from a savings account (a few business days) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:55">[00:08:55]</a>.
4.  **Place Your Order**: For ETFs or stocks, search for the ticker symbol (e.g., VFV for Vanguard S&P 500 ETF), decide on the number of shares, review costs, and place a market or limit order <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:12">[00:09:12]</a>.

#### Monitoring Your Investments
*   **Analyze Performance**: Regularly check your portfolio performance against benchmarks like the S&P 500 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:32">[00:09:32]</a>.
*   **Rebalance**: If one part of your portfolio has grown significantly, rebalance to align with your goals and reduce [[choosing_investments_and_managing_risk | risk]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:42">[00:09:42]</a>.
*   **Check for Fees/Taxes**: Be aware of fees or taxes that can erode returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:52">[00:09:52]</a>.
*   **Track Your Net Worth**: Add up assets (investments, savings, property) and subtract liabilities (loans, credit card debt) to see overall financial progress <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:58">[00:09:58]</a>. A free net worth tracker is available <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:14">[00:10:14]</a>.

#### Consistency and Learning
The key is consistency in checking your [[key_investment_strategies_and_options | investments]], whether monthly, quarterly, or annually <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:20">[00:10:20]</a>. Learn from mistakes, such as panic selling during a dip or staying in cash too long <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:38">[00:10:38]</a>. Adjust your strategy, focus on long-term goals, and avoid letting emotions dictate decisions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:50">[00:10:50]</a>.

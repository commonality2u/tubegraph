The proposed [[capital_gains_tax_changes | capital gains tax hike]] in Canada has been delayed until January 1st, 2026 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:07">[00:00:07]</a>. This tax increase, which sought to raise the inclusion rate of capital gains from 50% to 66.67%, was initially introduced in June 2024 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:14">[00:00:14]</a>. However, it now faces significant legal and political challenges that could prevent its implementation entirely <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:18">[00:00:18]</a>.

## Current Landscape

### Political Opposition
Conservative leader Pierre Poilievre has publicly vowed to repeal the tax if his party is elected <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:22">[00:00:22]</a>. Betting markets indicate a high probability (around 95%) of a Conservative victory in the next federal election, scheduled for October 20, 2025 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:49">[00:02:49]</a>. This suggests a strong likelihood of a tax reversal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:06">[00:03:06]</a>.

Support for the tax hike is also declining within the Liberal Party <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:10">[00:01:10]</a>. Chrystia Freeland, the former Finance Minister who first introduced the change, now states she would scrap it if she were to become Liberal leader <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:17">[00:01:17]</a>. Other Liberal leadership candidates are also distancing themselves from the policy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:24">[00:01:24]</a>.

### Legal Challenges
The Canadian Taxpayers Federation is pursuing legal action against the government, arguing that the CRA (Canada Revenue Agency) lacks the legal authority to collect a higher tax rate because the legislation was never officially passed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:55">[00:00:55]</a>. If the court rules against the government, the tax increase could be struck down even before the next election <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:04">[00:01:04]</a>.

### Economic Pressures
New tariffs imposed by US President Donald Trump on imports from Canada are adding further pressure to the Canadian economy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:34">[00:00:34]</a>. This trade dispute makes the [[capital_gains_tax_updates_for_canadian_business_owners_in_2025 | capital gains tax hike]] even less likely, as the government may prioritize defending Canadian industries over implementing an unpopular tax increase <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:40">[00:00:40]</a>.

## Arguments For and Against Reversal

### Reasons Against Reversal
*   **Revenue Loss**: The tax was projected to generate approximately $17 billion over five years, intended for housing, dental care, and pharmacare initiatives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:40">[00:03:40]</a>. Reversing it would require the government to find alternative revenue sources or cut spending <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:49">[00:03:49]</a>.
*   **Tax on the Wealthy**: The Liberal Party framed the tax as a measure to ensure wealthier Canadians contribute more <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:56">[00:03:56]</a>. A rollback could be perceived as beneficial to the top 1%, posing a political risk <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:02">[00:04:02]</a>.

### Reasons For Reversal
*   **Impact on Business Growth**: Higher [[capital_gains_tax_changes | capital gains taxes]] can discourage investments, impede business sales, and limit reinvestment within Canada <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:10">[00:04:10]</a>.
*   **US Tariff Threats**: New US tariffs could push businesses and investments away from Canada <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:20">[00:04:20]</a>, making an additional tax burden counterproductive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:26">[00:04:26]</a>.
*   **High Business Costs**: Canada already faces high corporate tax rates, regulations, and labor costs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:31">[00:04:31]</a>. The tax increase adds another burden, hindering company growth, sales, and reinvestment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:38">[00:04:38]</a>.

## Potential Scenarios and Outcomes

The future of the [[capital_gains_tax_changes | capital gains tax]] is subject to both legal and political developments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:47">[00:04:47]</a>.

1.  ### Full Reversal (Best Case Scenario)
    *   **Judicial Review**: If the legal challenge by the Canadian Taxpayers Federation succeeds, courts could block the CRA from enforcing the higher inclusion rate, effectively reversing the tax hike before the next election <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:57">[00:04:57]</a>.
    *   **New Government**: If Pierre Poilievre wins the election and fulfills his promise, the inclusion rate would likely revert to 50% for all taxpayers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:05">[00:05:05]</a>. This outcome would benefit investors, business owners, and retirees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:11">[00:05:11]</a>.

2.  ### Partial Rollback or Temporary Freeze (Middle Ground Scenario)
    *   **Poilievre's Approach**: If elected, Poilievre might lower the inclusion rate but not entirely back to 50%, possibly applying different rules for corporations versus individuals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:21">[00:05:21]</a>.
    *   **New Liberal Leader**: A new Liberal leader, distancing themselves from the tax, might reduce the inclusion rate slightly without restoring it fully to 50% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:32">[00:05:32]</a>.

3.  ### No Reversal (Worst Case Scenario)
    *   **Legal Challenges Fail**: If the courts rule in favor of the government, the CRA would continue collecting at the higher rate, and the inclusion increase would remain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:43">[00:05:43]</a>.
    *   **Poilievre Doesn't Follow Through**: If Poilievre wins but does not follow through on his promise due to economic pressures or competing priorities, the tax would stay in place <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:52">[00:05:52]</a>.
    *   **Liberals Remain in Power**: If the Liberals remain in power against current odds, a reversal might be off the table, forcing businesses and investors to adjust to the new tax reality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:03">[00:06:03]</a>.

Given the widespread opposition, legal challenges, political shifts, the new deferral, and US tariffs, the following probabilities are estimated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:23">[00:06:23]</a>:
*   **80% chance** of a full reversal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:36">[00:06:36]</a>.

While a full reversal is highly likely, it is advisable to prepare for all potential scenarios <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:47">[00:06:47]</a>.
*   **Stay Informed**: Monitor court rulings, political developments, and election timelines, as any reversal will likely take time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:50">[00:06:50]</a>.

## Understanding Net Worth Trends in Canada

Canadian household net worth has shown a steady increase from 2020 to 2024, rising from approximately $762,000 to over $1 million <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:14">[00:00:14]</a>. This growth was primarily driven by gains in real estate and financial markets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:25">[00:00:25]</a>. Although there was a slight dip to $979,000 in 2023 due to inflation and post-pandemic spending, it recovered to over $1 million by 2024 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:33">[00:00:33]</a>.

### Role of Real Estate

Real estate is a significant component of Canadian wealth, accounting for over 50% of household net worth during this period, peaking at nearly 56% in 2022 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:51">[00:00:51]</a>. This highlights the reliance Canadians have on property for wealth accumulation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:02">[00:01:02]</a>.

> [!PRO TIP] Diversify Your Portfolio <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:32">[00:01:32]</a>
> While real estate can be a strong asset, relying too heavily on one asset class can increase financial risk, especially in volatile markets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:37">[00:01:37]</a>. Spreading investments across real estate, stocks, bonds, and other assets can help protect against potential downturns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:44">[00:01:44]</a>. This aligns with broader [[Investing strategies for increasing net worth | investing strategies for increasing net worth]].

### Homeowners vs. Renters

A significant wealth gap exists between homeowners and renters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:54">[00:01:54]</a>. In 2024, homeowners had an average net worth of $1.38 million, almost five times higher than renters, who averaged $274,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:04">[00:02:04]</a>. This disparity is primarily driven by real estate appreciation, which significantly boosts homeowners' net worth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:32">[00:02:32]</a>. Renters typically rely more on financial assets for wealth building <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:38">[00:02:38]</a>.

### Net Worth by Age Group

Net worth generally progresses through different life stages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:50">[00:02:50]</a>:
*   **Under 35 years:** Net worth is lower due to building assets and managing debt, such as student loans <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:06">[00:03:06]</a>. This group's net worth grew from $231,000 in 2020 to $338,000 in 2024 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:10">[00:03:10]</a>.
*   **35 to 44 years:** Net worth typically grows in this stage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:26">[00:03:26]</a>. The average reached $658,000 in 2024, up from $490,000 in 2020 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:32">[00:03:32]</a>. This is a key stage for [[Investing strategies for increasing net worth | investing]] in real estate and retirement savings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:39">[00:03:39]</a>.
*   **45 to 64 years:** Net worth tends to peak in these age groups <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:48">[00:03:48]</a>. In 2024, those aged 45-54 had $1.35 million, and 55-64-year-olds reached $1.6 million, primarily driven by real estate and retirement growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:50">[00:03:50]</a>. This is considered the strongest wealth accumulation phase <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:04">[00:04:04]</a>.
*   **65 years and older:** Net worth typically plateaus or grows modestly as individuals begin drawing down assets for retirement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:09">[00:04:09]</a>. For this group, net worth rose from $863,000 in 2020 to $1.12 million in 2024 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:14">[00:04:14]</a>.

### Net Worth by Household Type

By 2024, multi-person households had nearly double the net worth of one-person households ($1.17 million vs. $634,000) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:39">[00:04:39]</a>. This difference is mainly due to larger total assets and the benefit of multiple income providers and shared expenses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:49">[00:04:49]</a>. However, one-person households are closing the gap in terms of growth, with their net worth increasing by 40% from 2020 to 2024, compared to 29.4% for multi-person households <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:04">[00:05:04]</a>.

### Median vs. Average Net Worth

When comparing your net worth to national figures, it's crucial to understand the difference between median and average <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:19">[00:06:19]</a>. The median is the middle point, where half of households have more and half have less, providing a more realistic picture <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:29">[00:06:29]</a>. The average can be skewed by the ultra-wealthy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:35">[00:06:35]</a>. For example, in 2019, the median net worth for multi-person households was $514,000, while the average in 2020 was $97,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:38">[00:06:38]</a>. Although Statistics Canada stopped reporting median net worth after 2019, a rough estimate suggests that the typical household's net worth is likely around 50% lower than the average today <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:56">[00:06:56]</a>. Using the median as a benchmark can provide a clearer picture of most Canadians' financial reality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:15">[00:07:15]</a>.

### Net Worth by Income Level

Net worth data by income level shows a clear correlation: higher income generally leads to higher net worth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:22">[00:07:22]</a>. In 2024, the top 20% of income earners had a net worth of over $2.29 million, more than six times that of the bottom 20% ($375,000) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:33">[00:07:33]</a>. The top group gained almost $460,000 in just four years, further widening the wealth gap <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:47">[00:07:47]</a>.

However, middle-income households (41% to 80% income group) saw the fastest *percentage* growth, quietly building wealth from rising real estate and investments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:53">[00:07:53]</a>. This demonstrates that significant net worth growth is achievable even outside the top income brackets through smart [[Investing strategies for increasing net worth | investing]] and [[role_of_income_growth_in_increasing_net_worth | asset accumulation]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:04">[00:08:04]</a>.

Regularly evaluate your assets and ensure you are investing adequately <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:25">[00:10:25]</a>.

Alternatively, consider how you can leverage property you already own <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:56">[00:10:56]</a>.
5.  **Benchmark and Track Progress:** Use strategies employed by top earners, such as minimizing debt and diversifying investments, as a guide for your financial plan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:01">[00:11:01]</a>.

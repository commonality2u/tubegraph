When planning on leaving Canada permanently, it's crucial to understand how your finances, including your Canada Pension Plan (CPP) benefits, will be affected <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:01">[00:00:01]</a>. This information is relevant for retirees seeking to escape cold winters and high costs, as well as digital nomads or remote workers looking to leave Canada's high taxes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:20">[00:00:20]</a>.

## Canada Pension Plan (CPP) Overview

The [[canada_pension_plan_cpp_benefits | Canada Pension Plan (CPP) benefits]] are based on contributions made during your working life in Canada <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:02">[00:06:02]</a>.

## Receiving CPP Benefits Abroad

The good news is that your [[canada_pension_plan_cpp_benefits | CPP benefits]] will travel with you if you move abroad <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:11">[00:06:11]</a>. The amount you receive abroad remains exactly the same as if you lived in Canada <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:13">[00:06:13]</a>. Your [[canada_pension_plan_cpp_benefits | CPP]] will be paid the same amount regardless of where you retire <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:28">[00:06:28]</a>. Think of CPP as a trusty old suitcase that holds everything you've packed over your working life in Canada, traveling with you wherever you go <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:22">[00:06:22]</a>.

## Tax Implications of CPP Abroad

[[tax_implications_of_cpp | CPP payments]] may be subject to withholding taxes depending on the tax treaty status of your new country of residence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:34">[00:06:34]</a>.

*   **Withholding Tax:** Without a tax treaty, you could face a 25% withholding tax on your payments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:51">[00:06:51]</a>.
*   **Tax Treaties:** If moving to the US, for example, a tax treaty is in place that can reduce or even eliminate the withholding tax on your [[canada_pension_plan_cpp_benefits | CPP benefits]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:45">[00:06:45]</a>.
*   **Country-Specific Rules:** While the amount you receive from [[canada_pension_plan_cpp_benefits | CPP]] doesn't change, how it's taxed in your new country is something to look into, as some countries might tax your [[canada_pension_plan_cpp_benefits | CPP benefits]] differently <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:52">[00:07:52]</a>. It's crucial to understand these [[tax_considerations_for_canadian_retirees_abroad | implications to avoid any surprises]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:58">[00:07:58]</a>.

## Social Security Agreements

Canada has social security agreements with over 50 countries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:57">[00:06:57]</a>. These agreements ensure your [[canada_pension_plan_cpp_benefits | CPP]] coverage continues uninterrupted and help to avoid dual contributions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:01">[00:07:01]</a>. They also coordinate pension benefits for those who have lived and worked abroad <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:09">[00:07:09]</a>.

## Survivor and Child Benefits

[[canada_pension_plan_cpp_benefits | CPP survivor benefits]] and Child Benefits will continue to be paid even if the recipient lives abroad <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:17">[00:07:17]</a>. Your family benefits are secure no matter where you are <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:22">[00:07:22]</a>.

## Receiving Payments

You have a couple of options for receiving your [[canada_pension_plan_cpp_benefits | CPP payments]] abroad:
*   Deposited into a local bank account in the local currency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:32">[00:07:32]</a>.
*   Deposited into a Canadian bank account, which you can then transfer with a service like Wise to your new bank <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:34">[00:07:34]</a>.
*   Mailed as checks in Canadian dollars if local bank transfers aren't feasible, although this is likely not the best option <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:44">[00:07:44]</a>.

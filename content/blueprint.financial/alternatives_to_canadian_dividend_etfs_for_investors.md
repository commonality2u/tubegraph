## Why Canadian Dividend ETFs May Not Be Ideal

*   **Higher Fees** Canadian dividend ETFs often come with higher-than-average Management Expense Ratios (MERs), which can significantly erode returns over time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:10">[00:11:10]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:14">[00:11:14]</a>. For example, iShares XDV charges a 0.55% MER for holding only 31 stocks, which doesn't offer great diversification for the cost <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:19">[00:11:19]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:23">[00:11:23]</a>. An investment of $1,000 in XDV would incur $5.50 in fees annually <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:28">[00:11:28]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:32">[00:11:32]</a>.
*   **Limited Diversification** Many Canadian dividend ETFs are heavily concentrated in the financial and energy sectors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:46">[00:11:46]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:50">[00:11:50]</a>. For instance, Vanguard's VDY has over 85% exposure to just financials and energy, making it vulnerable to downturns in these sectors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:54">[00:11:54]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:57">[00:11:57]</a>. Some ETFs, like XDV with 31 stocks or XEQ with 20 stocks, offer limited broad market exposure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:01">[00:12:01]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:04">[00:12:04]</a>.

## Alternative Investment Strategies

For investors seeking more robust [[investment_strategies_using_dividend_etfs_in_canada | investment strategies]] or better diversification than Canadian dividend ETFs offer, several alternatives can be considered:

### 1. Globally Diversified ETFs
*   **Description**: Unlike Canadian dividend ETFs, globally diversified ETFs offer exposure to thousands of stocks across multiple sectors and geographies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:08">[00:12:08]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:11">[00:12:11]</a>.
*   **Example**: Vanguard Growth ETF (VGRO) holds over 13,000 stocks, providing true diversification that Canadian dividend ETFs often lack <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:14">[00:12:14]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:17">[00:12:17]</a>.
*   **Benefit**: This approach can offer broader market exposure and potentially reduce reliance on specific sectors, mitigating the risks associated with the heavy concentration in Canadian dividend ETFs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:22">[00:12:22]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:26">[00:12:26]</a>. For more information on this type of fund, see [[introduction_to_allinone_etfs_in_canada | All-in-One ETFs in Canada]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:32">[00:12:32]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:34">[00:12:34]</a>.

### 2. Building Your Own Dividend Stock Portfolio
*   **Description**: Instead of paying ETF fees, investors can handpick 10 to 20 high-quality dividend stocks directly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:51">[00:12:51]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:55">[00:12:55]</a>. This strategy allows for greater control over holdings and can include a mix of Canadian and U.S. companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:58">[00:12:58]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:01">[00:13:01]</a>.
*   **Benefits**:
    *   **Lower Costs**: Eliminates ETF management fees.
    *   **Customization**: Tailor the portfolio to specific investment goals and risk tolerance.
    *   **Broader Reach**: Integrate U.S. dividend stocks for enhanced growth and diversification.

### 3. Investing in U.S. Dividend Stocks
*   **Description**: The U.S. market offers many blue-chip companies with global reach, often providing higher dividend growth rates and greater sector diversification compared to Canadian stocks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:37">[00:12:37]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:40">[00:12:40]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:43">[00:12:43]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:46">[00:12:46]</a>.
*   **Strategy**: Investors could combine a Canadian dividend ETF with separate investments in U.S. dividend stocks to achieve a diversified income stream <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:03">[00:13:03]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:05">[00:13:05]</a>.
*   **Tax Tip**: To [[tax_saving_strategies_on_investments_in_canada | avoid withholding tax]] on U.S. dividends, consider holding them in a Registered Retirement Savings Plan (RRSP) rather than a Tax-Free Savings Account (TFSA) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:09">[00:13:09]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:11">[00:13:11]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:15">[00:13:15]</a>. For more information on this, see [[tax_implications_of_noncanadian_stocks_in_tfsa | Tax Implications of Non-Canadian Stocks in TFSA]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:15">[00:13:15]</a>.

## Key Inputs for the Calculator

To utilize the calculator, several financial figures must be input to accurately compare buying, leasing, and financing options <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:52">[00:00:52]</a>. Obtaining exact figures often requires visiting a dealership for quotes or checking the company's website <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:03">[00:01:03]</a>.

### General Inputs

These inputs apply to both buying and leasing scenarios:
*   **Vehicle Price** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:45">[00:01:45]</a>.
*   **Down Payment** (e.g., zero) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:00">[00:02:00]</a>.
*   **Sales Tax Rate** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:11">[00:02:11]</a>.
*   **Investment Rate of Return (Opportunity Cost)**: This critical figure represents the return you could earn if the money used for a car purchase was invested elsewhere <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:16">[00:02:16]</a>. A higher rate makes buying a vehicle less attractive compared to leasing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:30">[00:02:30]</a>.
    *   Conservative investors: 3-4% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:49">[00:02:49]</a>
    *   Middle-of-the-road investors: 5-6% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:50">[00:02:50]</a>
    *   Aggressive investors: 7% and above <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:56">[00:02:56]</a>

### Buy Option Inputs

*   **Term** (e.g., 48 months) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:10">[00:03:10]</a>.
*   **Custom APR (Annual Percentage Rate)**: The interest rate on the loan. It's crucial to confirm eligibility for advertised low rates with the dealership, as rates can vary based on credit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:30">[00:03:30]</a>.
*   **Fees** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:18">[00:03:18]</a>.
*   **Annual Depreciation Rate**: This is considered the most significant number in the calculator <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:42">[00:03:42]</a>. [[impact_of_car_depreciation_on_financial_decisions | Car depreciation]] represents the value loss of a car over time, often likened to an iceberg, with significant value melting away in the initial years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:04">[00:04:04]</a>.

#### Estimating Car Depreciation

There are three main methods to estimate annual car depreciation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:17">[00:04:17]</a>:
1.  **Research Average Depreciation Rates**: New cars typically lose about 20% of their value in the first year and 15% annually for the next four years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:26">[00:04:26]</a>.
2.  **Use Online Depreciation Estimators**: Websites like Edmunds and Kelly Blue Book offer tools to estimate depreciation based on make, model, year, and mileage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:41">[00:04:41]</a>.
3.  **Check Current Marketplaces**: This hands-on method involves checking sites like AutoTrader, Craigslist, Facebook Marketplace, and Kijiji for real-time insights into a car's current value and its depreciation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:56">[00:04:56]</a>. This method is preferred for its accuracy over mere estimates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:18">[00:05:18]</a>.

### Lease Option Inputs

*   **Term** (e.g., 48 months) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:06">[00:06:06]</a>.
*   **Kilometers or Miles per Year Usage**: Select the appropriate mileage allowance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:07">[00:06:07]</a>.
*   **Interest Rate** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:22">[00:06:22]</a>.
*   **Fees** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:25">[00:06:25]</a>.
*   **Residual Percentage**: This is the estimated value of the car at the end of the lease term, typically ranging from 40% to 60% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:42">[00:06:42]</a>. This value is provided by the dealership and is generally non-negotiable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:53">[00:06:53]</a>.

## Understanding the Results

After inputting the data, the calculator provides the "net cost" of both buying and leasing. The "net cost of buying" takes into account the estimated resale value of the car at the end of the term, making the [[impact_of_car_depreciation_on_financial_decisions | annual depreciation]] rate a critical factor in determining this value <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:38">[00:05:38]</a>.

### Impact of Variables

The comparison results can significantly change based on adjusted variables:
*   **Investment Rate of Return**: A higher rate of return on investments makes buying less financially attractive than leasing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:56">[00:07:56]</a>.
*   **Annual Depreciation**: This is the most unpredictable yet impactful factor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:09">[00:08:09]</a>. Lower depreciation (e.g., 10%) makes buying significantly cheaper, while higher depreciation (e.g., 25%) makes buying much more expensive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:18">[00:08:18]</a>. The actual market resale value is largely determined by supply and demand at the time, which can be unpredictable, as seen during the pandemic with soaring used car prices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:07">[00:09:07]</a>.

## Key Financial Takeaways

Based on the calculator's insights, here are the main conclusions regarding car purchase decisions:
*   **High Resale Value**: If a vehicle maintains a high resale value, buying or financing is generally more cost-effective <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:31">[00:09:31]</a>.
*   **Low Resale Value**: When a vehicle has a low resale value, leasing becomes more cost-effective, especially if the payments can be written off for business purposes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:38">[00:09:38]</a>.
*   **Opportunity Cost**: When buying outright or making a large down payment, the opportunity cost of investing that money elsewhere must be carefully considered <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:45">[00:09:45]</a>.

### Limitations of Financial Estimates

It's important to remember that all financial calculations are estimates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:00">[00:10:00]</a>. The exact resale value and investment earnings are unknown, and the car market is inherently unpredictable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:05">[00:10:05]</a>. For example, those who leased in 2017 missed out on profiting from the surge in used car prices during the COVID-19 pandemic, where owning the vehicle would have allowed them to capitalize on that market shift <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:16">[00:10:16]</a>.

Due to the unpredictable nature of the market, [[nonfinancial_factors_when_choosing_to_lease_or_buy_a_car | non-financial factors]] can often outweigh purely financial considerations in the buy or lease decision <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:27">[00:10:27]</a>.

## Additional Considerations

New cars can lose up to 20% of their value in the first year and 50% within three years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:23">[00:12:23]</a>. Purchasing a car that is 3 to 5 years old allows buyers to avoid this initial depreciation hit, offering greater value <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:29">[00:12:29]</a>. Additionally, used cars typically have lower insurance costs and less financial burden <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:41">[00:12:41]</a>.

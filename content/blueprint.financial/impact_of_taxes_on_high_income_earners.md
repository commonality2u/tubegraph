High-income earners in Canada contribute a significant portion of the country's tax revenue, facing substantial tax liabilities as their incomes rise. The data from 2022 highlights the significant tax contributions of the top 5% and 1% of earners <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:15:23">[04:15:23]</a>.

## Tax Contributions of Top Earners

### Top 5% of Earners
The top 5% of earners in Canada paid an average of $87,700 in income taxes in 2022 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:22:04">[04:22:04]</a>. This group, despite representing only 1 in 20 taxpayers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:31:21">[04:31:21]</a>, contributed over 41% of all federal and provincial personal income taxes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:26:08">[04:26:08]</a>.

### Top 1% of Earners
The top 1% of earners faced an even heavier tax burden, paying an average of $231,600 in income taxes in 2022 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:11:43">[07:11:43]</a>. This elite group contributed over 22% of all federal and provincial income taxes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:17:15">[07:17:15]</a>.

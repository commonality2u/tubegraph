Even though [[introduction_to_allinone_etfs | all-in-one ETFs]] are designed to simplify investing, individuals can still make mistakes that negatively impact their returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:25">[00:10:25]</a>. Avoiding these common pitfalls is crucial for long-term success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:31">[00:10:31]</a>.

## Key Mistakes to Watch Out For

### 1. Dishonestly Answering the Investor Questionnaire

It is vital to answer investor questionnaires truthfully to ensure you are matched with the appropriate ETF for your risk tolerance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:35">[00:10:35]</a>.
*   **Consequence of being too conservative:** You might not achieve sufficient growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:41">[00:10:41]</a>.
*   **Consequence of being too aggressive:** You could panic during a market downturn and sell your investments at an unfavorable time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:43">[00:10:43]</a>.

> "Take the questionnaire very seriously to match your actual Comfort level" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:49">[00:10:49]</a>

### 2. Getting Bored and Tweaking Too Much

The primary [[benefits_of_using_allinone_etfs | advantage of all-in-one ETFs]] is their "set it and forget it" approach <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:57">[00:10:57]</a>. Investors often check their accounts too frequently and make unnecessary changes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:02">[00:11:02]</a>.
*   **Impact:** Constantly adjusting investments or selling during market swings can significantly harm long-term returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:06">[00:11:06]</a>.
*   **Solution:** Patience is key for these investments to work effectively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:14">[00:11:14]</a>.

### 3. Buying Too Many ETFs in the Same Account

Some investors mistakenly purchase multiple [[introduction_to_allinone_etfs | all-in-one ETFs]] or combine them with other ETFs, believing it provides extra diversification <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:19">[00:11:19]</a>.
*   **Reality:** This practice disrupts the carefully designed built-in balance of the all-in-one ETF and makes the investment strategy more complicated than necessary <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:27">[00:11:27]</a>.
*   **Example:** An ETF like Vanguard's VGRO already includes exposure to over 13,000 companies, providing ample diversification for most needs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:33">[00:11:33]</a>.
*   **Recommendation:** [[how_to_choose_the_right_allinone_etf | Choose one]] that aligns with your risk tolerance and maintain that single investment per account <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:41">[00:11:41]</a>.

> [!TIP] One Fund is Enough
> [[introduction_to_allinone_etfs | All-in-one ETFs]] are designed to be standalone investments for each account <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:28">[00:09:28]</a>. Adding more funds can interfere with their intended balance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:34">[00:09:34]</a>. Each ETF is built to match a specific risk level, and mixing them can accidentally alter your desired asset allocation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:38">[00:09:38]</a>.

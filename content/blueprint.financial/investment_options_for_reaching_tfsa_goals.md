A survey indicates that 47% of Canadian [[the_tfsa_as_a_strategic_financial_tool | TFSA]] holders only have cash and savings in their accounts, which means many may never achieve a [[strategies_for_building_a_1_million_tfsa_in_canada | million-dollar goal]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:09">[00:01:09]</a>.

## Investment Options for Your TFSA

Here are five options for investing within your [[the_tfsa_as_a_strategic_financial_tool | TFSA]] to work towards your [[strategies_for_building_a_1_million_tfsa_in_canada | million-dollar goal]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:44">[00:05:44]</a>:

### 1. Avoiding Mutual Funds at Big Banks

While convenient, mutual funds offered by big banks often come with very high fees, sometimes topping 3% per year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:04">[00:06:04]</a>. These high fees can significantly erode your investment gains <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:13">[00:06:13]</a>.

### 2. Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs)

ETFs are an excellent choice for cost-effective investing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:22">[00:06:22]</a>. They mirror market index performance, allowing you to earn returns close to the overall market <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:27">[00:06:27]</a>. Providers like Vanguard and iShares offer ETFs with very low fees in Canada <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:33">[00:06:33]</a>. However, ETFs expose you to market volatility and require basic knowledge of trading <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:41">[00:06:41]</a>.

### 3. Individual Stocks

Investing in individual stocks offers ultimate control over your investments and typically the lowest fees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:52">[00:06:52]</a>. A dividend stock strategy can provide capital appreciation and regular income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:00">[00:07:00]</a>. This approach requires substantial market knowledge and time dedicated to portfolio management <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:13">[00:07:13]</a>. Greater control often comes with increased risk, especially without diversification <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:22">[00:07:22]</a>.

### 4. Robo-Advisors

Robo-advisors are automated platforms that manage investments using algorithms, aligning with your financial goals and [[understanding_risk_and_investment_choices_for_tfsa | risk tolerance]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:27">[00:07:27]</a>. Well Simple and Questwealth are popular Canadian examples <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:38">[00:07:38]</a>. They offer convenience, simplicity, and a hands-off approach, making investing accessible with relatively small amounts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:43">[00:07:43]</a>. Drawbacks include a lack of personal touch and the inability to fully capture unique investment opportunities or adapt to complex, changing financial situations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:00">[00:08:00]</a>.

### 5. Portfolio Managers

For those who find self-management overwhelming or prefer a personal touch over algorithms, hiring a professional portfolio manager is an excellent option <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:21">[00:08:21]</a>. Traditionally for the wealthy, this service is now more accessible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:31">[00:08:31]</a>. A manager can tailor your portfolio to specific needs and [[understanding_risk_and_investment_choices_for_tfsa | risk tolerance]], optimizing success without daily hassle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:36">[00:08:36]</a>.

## Withdrawing from Your TFSA in Retirement

A crucial aspect of a [[strategies_for_building_a_1_million_tfsa_in_canada | $1 million TFSA]] is understanding how to withdraw from it in [[the_role_of_tfsa_in_retirement_planning | retirement]] to [[maximizing_the_benefits_of_your_tfsa | maximize tax savings]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:00">[00:09:00]</a>.

### TFSA vs. RSP Withdrawal Order

The decision to withdraw from your [[using_tfsa_wisely_with_rrsp | RSP]] or [[the_tfsa_as_a_strategic_financial_tool | TFSA]] first is not one-size-fits-all <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:07">[00:09:07]</a>.
*   **Withdrawing from [[using_tfsa_wisely_with_rrsp | RSP]] first**: Beneficial because [[using_tfsa_wisely_with_rrsp | RSP]] withdrawals are taxable income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:18">[00:09:18]</a>. Drawing it down earlier may reduce your overall tax burden later, especially to avoid Old Age Security (OAS) clawbacks based on income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:22">[00:09:22]</a>.
*   **Withdrawing from [[the_tfsa_as_a_strategic_financial_tool | TFSA]] first**: [[the_tfsa_as_a_strategic_financial_tool | TFSA]] withdrawals are not taxable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:34">[00:09:34]</a>. This allows investments to continue growing tax-free, providing a tax-efficient source of funds that doesn't affect OAS <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:36">[00:09:36]</a>.

### Bucket Strategy for Your TFSA in Retirement

With a substantial [[strategies_for_building_a_1_million_tfsa_in_canada | $1 million TFSA]], consider a "bucket strategy" to ensure longevity and continued growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:44">[00:09:44]</a>:
*   **Short-term funds**: Kept in safer investments like bonds or GICs for immediate expenses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:57">[00:09:57]</a>.
*   **Medium-term funds**: Invested in balanced funds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:03">[00:10:03]</a>.
*   **Long-term funds**: Remain in equities to ensure growth against inflation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:06">[00:10:06]</a>.

This strategy ensures funds are available when needed while still working for you <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:10">[00:10:10]</a>.

### Managing Your TFSA with CPP

A strategic approach is key to managing your [[the_tfsa_as_a_strategic_financial_tool | TFSA]] effectively with your Canada Pension Plan (CPP) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:17">[00:10:17]</a>:
*   Use your [[the_tfsa_as_a_strategic_financial_tool | TFSA]] to supplement CPP income during years when other income is lower <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:24">[00:10:24]</a>.
*   Consider delaying CPP withdrawals while drawing from your [[the_tfsa_as_a_strategic_financial_tool | TFSA]] if you retire early <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:31">[00:10:31]</a>. This allows government benefits to grow, [[maximizing_the_benefits_of_your_tfsa | maximizing]] income later in [[the_role_of_tfsa_in_retirement_planning | retirement]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:40">[00:10:40]</a>.

## Provincial Income Statistics (2022)

Income levels vary across Canada, with certain provinces showing higher median incomes due to their economic structures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:58">[00:05:58]</a>.

*   **Alberta**
    *   Median Income: $45,600 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:09">[00:05:09]</a>
    *   Average Income: $61,500 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:07">[00:10:07]</a>
    *   *Contributing Factors*: Leads the pack due to its strong energy and resource sectors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:13">[00:05:13]</a>.
*   **Quebec, Saskatchewan, and British Columbia**
    *   Median Income: $43,900 (each) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:18">[00:05:18]</a>
    *   *Contributing Factors*: These provinces closely follow Alberta, reflecting their relatively solid economies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:21">[00:05:21]</a>.
*   **Ontario**
    *   Median Income: $42,700 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:27">[00:05:27]</a>
    *   Average Income: $58,700 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:33">[00:05:33]</a>
    *   *Contributing Factors*: Features a diverse job market, but this also showcases a high disparity between high and low-income jobs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:27">[00:05:27]</a>.
*   **Eastern Provinces (Prince Edward Island, New Brunswick, Newfoundland, and Nova Scotia)**
    *   Median Income: Ranging from $38,000 to $40,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:49">[00:05:49]</a>
    *   *Contributing Factors*: These areas typically have smaller economies and a higher prevalence of lower-wage jobs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:52">[00:05:52]</a>.

The significant variations in income highlight how greatly earnings can differ depending on one's location within Canada <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:58">[00:05:58]</a>.

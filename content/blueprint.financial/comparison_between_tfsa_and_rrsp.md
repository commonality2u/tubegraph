## Initial Prioritization: FHSA
While the focus is on TFSA and RRSP, it's worth noting that if you are saving for your first home, the FHSA (First Home Savings Account) is often recommended as the first pick <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:36">[00:00:36]</a>. It offers the best of both worlds: tax-deductible contributions like an RRSP and tax-free withdrawals like a TFSA, provided it's for a home purchase <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:43">[00:00:43]</a>. Funds can also be transferred to an RRSP if plans change <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:09">[00:01:09]</a>. However, the FHSA has a limitation: withdrawing for a non-qualifying purpose (e.g., buying a car) permanently forfeits that contribution room <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:47">[00:01:47]</a>. For emergencies, a TFSA is a more suitable option <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:01">[00:02:01]</a>.

## TFSA vs. RRSP: Which to Prioritize?
[[differences_between_rrsps_and_tfsas | Choosing between a TFSA (Tax-Free Savings Account) and an RRSP (Registered Retirement Savings Plan)]] depends on your income goals and need for flexibility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:11">[00:02:11]</a>.

### The TFSA: Flexibility and Simplicity
The TFSA is designed for flexibility, allowing tax-free withdrawals at any time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:21">[00:02:21]</a>. This makes it ideal for both short-term goals and long-term investments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:26">[00:02:26]</a>.

Key points of the TFSA:
*   **Flexibility**: Funds can be withdrawn at any time, tax-free, and without penalties <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:42">[00:02:42]</a>. It's ideal for saving for short-term goals like vacations, big purchases, or emergency funds, while still providing tax-free investment growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:47">[00:02:47]</a>.
*   **Simplicity**: Unlike the RRSP, there are no complicated withdrawal rules or tax implications as long as contribution limits are respected <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:30">[00:02:30]</a>. You can contribute, withdraw, and recontribute the next year within your contribution room <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:00">[00:03:00]</a>.

Prioritize your TFSA when:
*   You want easy access to your funds without penalty <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:15">[00:03:15]</a>.
*   You are saving for short-term goals like vacations, big purchases, or emergencies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:18">[00:03:18]</a>.
*   You are in a lower income year, as RRSP tax deductions would offer less benefit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:22">[00:03:22]</a>.
*   You seek a simple way to grow investments tax-free without locking in your funds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:28">[00:03:28]</a>.

### The RRSP: Long-Term Savings and Tax Deductions
The RRSP is built for long-term retirement savings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:42">[00:03:42]</a>. It provides tax deductions now, while your investments grow tax-free until retirement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:45">[00:03:45]</a>.

Key points of the RRSP:
*   **Tax Advantages**: Offers immediate tax deductions that reduce your taxable income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:45">[00:03:45]</a>. This is particularly beneficial for higher-income earners <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:50">[00:03:50]</a>.
*   **Employer Matching**: A must-do if your employer offers matching contributions, as this is considered "free money" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:51">[00:03:51]</a>.
*   **Government Benefits**: RRSP contributions can be used to qualify for government benefits like the Canada Child Benefit or GST/HST credits by controlling your income in key years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:04">[00:04:04]</a>.

Prioritize your RRSP when:
*   You have a higher income and can benefit from tax deductions to reduce your taxable income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:58">[00:03:58]</a>.
*   Your employer offers RRSP matching contributions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:51">[00:03:51]</a>.

### Summary of TFSA vs. RRSP Prioritization
<div class="callout">
    **Choose TFSA first if:**
    *   You need flexibility and easy access to your funds without penalty <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:20">[00:04:20]</a>.
    *   You are in a lower income year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:22">[00:04:22]</a>.
    *   You want a simple, tax-free way to save for both short and long-term goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:22">[00:04:22]</a>.
    **Choose RRSP first if:**
    *   You have a higher income and want immediate tax savings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:29">[00:04:29]</a>.
    *   Your employer offers RRSP matching <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:31">[00:04:31]</a>.

Generally, it's recommended to go with the FHSA first, then consider either the TFSA or RRSP depending on your situation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:37">[00:04:37]</a>. If in doubt, choose the TFSA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:45">[00:04:45]</a>.

## Overall Account Prioritization
Beyond the FHSA, TFSA, and RRSP, there are other accounts that offer significant "free money" opportunities that should be prioritized even before the TFSA or FHSA due to their unique benefits and government grants <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:50">[00:04:50]</a>:

1.  **RDSP (Registered Disability Savings Plan)**: Prioritize if you qualify, as it offers generous government grants and bonds for disability savings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:00">[00:05:00]</a>. For example, the Canada Disability Savings Grant (CDSG) matches up to 300% on contributions, and the Canada Disability Savings Bond (CDSB) provides funds without requiring contributions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:13">[00:05:13]</a>.
2.  **RRSP Employer Matching**: Always maximize this if available, as it's essentially "free money" from your employer, providing an instant return on your contribution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:40">[00:06:40]</a>.
3.  **RESP (Registered Education Savings Plan)**: Prioritize for saving for post-secondary education, as the Canada Education Savings Grant (CESG) matches 20% of contributions up to $500 per year per child <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:56">[00:05:56]</a>.

After these, the FHSA is generally recommended for first-time home buyers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:15">[00:09:15]</a>. Then, choose between the [[using_tfsa_wisely_with_rrsp | RRSP for higher income tax deductions]] or the [[coordinating_tfsa_with_rrsp_and_cpp | TFSA for flexibility and tax-free growth]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:23">[00:09:23]</a>.

Finally, non-registered accounts are the last destination once all registered accounts are maximized <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:33">[00:07:33]</a>. To minimize taxes in non-registered accounts, focus on growth stocks with no dividends (to avoid high interest income tax) or Canadian dividend stocks (due to the Canadian dividend tax credit) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:49">[00:07:49]</a>. Avoid US dividend-paying stocks, which face withholding taxes and don't qualify for Canadian credits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:37">[00:08:37]</a>.

The right account prioritization depends on your personal financial goals, income, and savings needs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:44">[00:09:44]</a>.

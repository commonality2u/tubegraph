## Defining the Top 1% in Canada

### Income Threshold
As of 2021, the threshold for being in the top 1% of income earners in Canada is an annual income of $271,600 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:49">[00:48:49]</a>. The median income for this group, representing the top 0.5%, is $378,900 per year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:00">[00:37:00]</a>.

### Key Characteristics
Analysis of data from Statistics Canada reveals interesting points about the top 1% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:00">[00:28:00]</a>:
*   Approximately 80% of individuals in the top 1% are married or in common-law relationships <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:00">[01:22:00]</a>.
*   Wages and salaries accounted for 63.1% of their income in 2021, a slight increase from 62.7% in 2017 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:31:00">[01:31:00]</a>. This indicates that about one-third of their income comes from sources outside of their primary jobs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:40:00">[01:40:00]</a>.
*   These findings suggest that being married or in a common-law relationship and having [[diversifying_retirement_income | diversified income sources]] can contribute to reaching the top 1% of income earners <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:51:00">[01:51:00]</a>.

### Net Worth Threshold
Beyond income, net worth is considered a true measure of being in the top 1% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:03:00">[02:03:00]</a>. A 2021 report from the PBO indicates that the threshold for being in the top 1% of net worth in Canada is having assets of at least $6.3 million <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:22:00">[02:22:00]</a>. Approximately 160,000 families in Canada have achieved this mark <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:43:00">[02:43:00]</a>.

## Strategies for Amassing Wealth and Income

To reach the top 1% in income and net worth, several strategies can be employed, often in combination <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:55:00">[09:55:00]</a>.

### 1. [[highpaying_careers_that_lead_to_top_1_income | High-Paying Professions]]
Having a profession that generates an income close to or exceeding the $271,600 threshold significantly increases the chances of accumulating substantial wealth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:00:00">[03:00:00]</a>. Such professions typically require many years of specialized education and work experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:03:00">[04:03:00]</a>.

Examples of such roles include <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:24:00">[03:24:00]</a>:
*   **C-Suite Level Executives**: CEO, CFO, CTO <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:29:00">[03:29:00]</a>
*   **Medical Specialists**: Cardiologists, orthopedic surgeons, specialized dentists <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:35:00">[03:35:00]</a>
*   **Partners in Law Firms and Accounting Firms** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:38:00">[03:38:00]</a>
*   **Investment Bankers** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:40:00">[03:40:00]</a>
*   **Principal Software Engineers, Senior Data Scientists, Senior Software Architects** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:42:00">[03:42:00]</a>

### 2. Starting a Business
Business ownership is a primary way the top 1% accumulate wealth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:21:00">[04:21:00]</a>. While not easy, starting a successful business is possible for anyone <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:30:00">[04:30:00]</a>. Many of Canada's richest individuals built their fortunes through business, rather than traditional 9-5 jobs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:36:00">[04:36:00]</a>.

*   **Financial Rewards**: Business ownership offers significant financial rewards through capital appreciation, profit margins, and potential exits like selling the business or going public <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:07:00">[05:07:00]</a>.
*   **Scaling**: The ability to scale a business to reach hundreds, thousands, or millions of customers is a key factor in wealth creation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:20:00">[05:20:00]</a>.
*   **Sales Roles**: Certain sales roles, such as real estate agents, mortgage brokers, or pharmaceutical sales reps, can also achieve top 1% incomes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:39:00">[05:39:00]</a>. These roles often combine elements of salary and business ownership, with high-performing professionals earning substantial income through commissions and bonuses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:57:00">[05:57:00]</a>.

### 3. Investing Wisely
Investing is crucial for allowing money to compound and grow over time, significantly contributing to net worth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:46:00">[06:46:00]</a>.

*   **Diversification**: For non-expert investors, diversification across various asset classes is recommended <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:22:00">[07:22:00]</a>.
*   **Stock Market**:
    *   **Hands-off approach**: Consider investing in index funds or ETFs with low fees for broad diversification <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:30:00">[07:30:00]</a>.
    *   **Active approach**: Learn to pick individual stocks to reduce risk <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:43:00">[07:43:00]</a>.
    *   **Balanced Portfolio**: Investing in a mix of Canadian stocks and bonds can provide steady growth and protection from market volatility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:51:00">[07:51:00]</a>.
*   **Real Estate**: Real estate is a major wealth builder for Canadians and is commonly held by millionaires <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:03:00">[08:03:00]</a>. Investing in rental properties can provide steady income and property value appreciation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:12:00">[08:12:00]</a>.
*   **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Utilize accounts like RSPs, TFSAs, and FHSAs to enhance investment returns through tax benefits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:18:00">[08:18:00]</a>.
    *   **RSP**: Contributions are tax-deductible, and investments grow tax-free until withdrawal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:30:00">[08:30:00]</a>.
    *   **TFSA**: Investments grow tax-free, and withdrawals are also tax-free <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:37:00">[08:37:00]</a>.

### 4. Frugality and Saving
Consistently saving a significant portion of income and investing it wisely, even from a modest income, can lead to substantial wealth accumulation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:25:00">[09:25:00]</a>. However, it's important to avoid "extreme frugality" that negatively impacts quality of life or relationships <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:33:00">[09:33:00]</a>.

## Combining Strategies for Success
Individuals typically combine these methods rather than choosing just one <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:55:00">[09:55:00]</a>. The best approach depends on personal circumstances and risk tolerance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:01:00">[10:01:00]</a>.

*   A high-paying job can be combined with wise investing and frugality for those who are not risk-takers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:08:00">[10:08:00]</a>.
*   For those who enjoy risk and don't want to be overly frugal, starting a scalable business and investing profits wisely might be ideal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:18:00">[10:18:00]</a>.
*   If financial obligations prevent frugality or high-risk ventures, focusing on increasing salary through promotions or training, then investing the excess, can be effective <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:27:00">[10:27:00]</a>.

Having a solid financial plan is paramount for achieving wealth goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:10:00">[11:10:00]</a>.

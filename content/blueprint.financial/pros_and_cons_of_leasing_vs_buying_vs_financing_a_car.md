## [[estimating_car_depreciation_and_its_impact_on_decision_making | Estimating Car Depreciation]]

Car depreciation is a significant factor in your decision <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:54:00">[03:54:00]</a>. A new car's value diminishes significantly in its first few years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:09:00">[04:09:00]</a>.

### Methods to Estimate Depreciation

1.  **Average Depreciation Rates**: New cars typically lose about 20% of their value in the first year and 15% annually for the next four years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:26:00">[04:26:00]</a>.
2.  **Online Estimators**: Websites like Edmunds and Kelly Blue Book offer free tools to estimate depreciation based on make, model, year, and mileage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:41:00">[04:41:00]</a>.
3.  **Current Marketplaces**: Checking sites like AutoTrader, Craigslist, Facebook Marketplace, and Kijiji for similar car listings provides real-time insights into their current value and how much they've depreciated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:56:00">[04:56:00]</a>. This method is preferred as it reflects actual listed and sold prices, not just estimates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:18:00">[05:18:00]</a>.

## Comparing Buy vs. Lease: The Results

Using the calculator, the net cost of buying and leasing can be very similar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:07:00">[07:07:00]</a>.For example, a Ford F-150 might show a net cost to buy of $34,383 and a net cost to lease of $35,371 over 48 months <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:11:00">[07:11:00]</a>.

However, changing variables can significantly impact the decision:

*   **Investment Rate of Return**: If you expect to earn only 3% on investments, buying might be cheaper (e.g., $32,000 vs. $35,000 for leasing) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:37:00">[07:37:00]</a>. If you anticipate a high return, like 10%, leasing could become the more financially favorable option <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:56:00">[07:56:00]</a>.
*   **Annual Depreciation**: This is the most impactful and unpredictable factor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:09:00">[08:09:00]</a>.
    *   **Low Depreciation (e.g., 10%)**: If your vehicle depreciates slowly, buying becomes much more cost-effective (e.g., $27,000) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:18:00">[08:18:00]</a>.
    *   **High Depreciation (e.g., 25%)**: If your vehicle depreciates quickly, the net cost of buying can skyrocket (e.g., $44,000) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:41:00">[08:41:00]</a>.

The actual market resale value of a car at the end of a term is difficult to predict, as it depends heavily on supply and demand at that time, as seen during the pandemic's car shortages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:04:00">[09:04:00]</a>.

### Key Financial Takeaways

*   **High Resale Value**: If a vehicle is expected to retain a high resale value, buying or financing is generally cheaper <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:31:00">[09:31:00]</a>.
*   **Low Resale Value**: If a vehicle has a low expected resale value, leasing becomes more cost-effective, especially if payments can be written off for business purposes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:39:00">[09:39:00]</a>.
*   **Opportunity Cost**: When buying outright or making a large down payment, consider the [[savings_versus_investing | opportunity cost]] of what that money could have earned if invested elsewhere <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:45:00">[09:45:00]</a>.

It's important to remember that these are all estimates; exact resale values or investment returns are unpredictable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:00:00">[10:00:00]</a>.

## [[nonfinancial_considerations_for_car_leasing_and_buying | Non-Financial Considerations]]

Given the unpredictability of the car market, [[nonfinancial_considerations_for_car_leasing_and_buying | non-financial factors]] can often outweigh purely financial calculations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:14:00">[10:14:00]</a>.

*   **Ownership Preference**: Buy if you prefer long-term ownership and customization. Lease if you enjoy driving new models every few years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:43:00">[10:43:00]</a>.
*   **Tax Write-offs**: Leasing offers deductible payments for businesses, while buying allows for depreciation and interest deductions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:51:00">[10:51:00]</a>. Choose based on your business's financial strategy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:58:00">[10:58:00]</a>.
*   **Debt Aversion**: Leasing typically means lower monthly payments without the long-term debt of ownership, though it doesn't build equity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:01:00">[11:01:00]</a>.
*   **Flexibility**: Buying offers the freedom to sell at any time. Leasing locks you into a term but often includes warranty coverage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:14:00">[11:14:00]</a>.
*   **Long-Term Plans**: Buy if you plan to keep the car for many years. Lease if you prefer frequent vehicle changes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:23:00">[11:23:00]</a>.
*   **Environmental or Emotional Factors**: Buying supports sustainability through longer vehicle use and fosters attachment. Leasing provides access to newer, potentially more eco-friendly models but with higher turnover, which might feel less personal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:29:00">[11:29:00]</a>.
*   **Maintenance**: Buyers cover maintenance post-warranty. Leases often include maintenance during the lease term <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:41:00">[11:41:00]</a>.
*   **Insurance Costs**: Leased vehicles can sometimes require higher insurance, increasing overall costs compared to buying <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:47:00">[11:47:00]</a>.
*   **End of Lease Options**: Leasing provides the option to buy the car at the end of the term, offering flexibility if you're unsure about committing to a purchase upfront <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:54:00">[11:54:00]</a>.

## [[benefits_of_buying_used_cars | Buying Used Cars]]

Often, the smartest financial choice is to buy a used car <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:13:00">[12:13:00]</a>. While new cars can lose up to 20% of their value in the first year and 50% within three years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:23:00">[12:23:00]</a>, buying a used car in the "sweet spot" of 3 to 5 years old allows you to avoid this initial depreciation hit and get more value for your money <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:29:00">[12:29:00]</a>. These cars are also typically not old enough to have major issues <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:37:00">[12:37:00]</a>.

[[benefits_of_buying_used_cars | Used cars]] often have lower insurance costs and represent less financial burden <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:41:00">[12:41:00]</a>. With some research, a reliable, well-priced used car can meet your needs, often with comparable or better features than a new one <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:45:00">[12:45:00]</a>.

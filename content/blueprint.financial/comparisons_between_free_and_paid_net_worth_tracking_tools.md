## Paid and Subscription-Based Net Worth Tracking Alternatives

While manual spreadsheets offer control, several tools provide automation, often at a cost <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:22">[00:12:22]</a>.

### Wetha
*   **Functionality:** Connects directly to financial accounts to provide an automatic overview of net worth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:33">[00:12:33]</a>.
*   **Pros:** Convenient due to automation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:40">[00:12:40]</a>.
*   **Cons:** Advanced features are typically locked behind a paid subscription <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:40">[00:12:40]</a>.

### Quicken (Replaced Mint)
*   **Functionality:** A robust financial tracking tool that replaced the formerly free and popular Mint in Canada <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:47">[00:12:47]</a>.
*   **Pros:** Offers comprehensive financial tracking capabilities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:56">[00:12:56]</a>.
*   **Cons:** Requires a paid subscription, which may not be cost-effective given the availability of free alternatives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:59">[00:12:59]</a>.

### PocketGuard
*   **Functionality:** Primarily a budgeting app that helps monitor spending and manage finances <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:11">[00:13:11]</a>.
*   **Pros:** Offers a free version for basic features <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:15">[00:13:15]</a>.
*   **Cons:** Access to premium features requires a monthly or annual fee <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:18">[00:13:18]</a>. It's an option for those comfortable paying for automation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:21">[00:13:21]</a>.

Ultimately, the choice between free, manual methods like a custom spreadsheet and paid, automated tools depends on individual preferences for control, customization, and budget <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:56">[00:13:56]</a>.

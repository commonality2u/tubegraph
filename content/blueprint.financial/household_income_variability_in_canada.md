## General Income Overview (2018-2022)

The average income in Canada for a single person gradually increased from $54,600 in 2018 to $57,400 in 2022 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:41">[00:00:41]</a>. The median income rose from $41,000 in 2018 to $44,000 in 2021, with a slight dip in 2022 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:50">[00:00:50]</a>.

### Average vs. Median Income

It's important to distinguish between average and median income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:29:43">[02:29:43]</a>. The average can be significantly skewed by a few very high earners, such as in an example where one person earning $20 million heavily inflates the average for a group of five <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:31:30">[02:31:30]</a>. The median income, however, represents the middle number when all salaries are lined up in order, providing a more accurate picture of what most people are actually earning <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:08:00">[03:08:00]</a>.

## Income by Household Type (2022)

Household type significantly influences total income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:03:00">[06:03:00]</a>.

*   **Couple Families:** Have a clear financial advantage, with a median income of over $110,000 in 2022 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:10:00">[06:10:00]</a>. This is typically due to two sources of income, which helps manage higher living costs like mortgages and childcare <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:17:00">[06:17:00]</a>.
*   **Lone Parent Families:** Face more financial challenges, with a median income of $58,800 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:28:00">[06:28:00]</a>. Being the sole breadwinner can lead to financial stress, making it harder to save or deal with unexpected costs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:34:00">[06:34:00]</a>.
*   **Individuals Not in Census Families:** Have a median income of $35,790 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:50:00">[06:50:00]</a>. Without the support of a family, managing expenses like rent and healthcare can be more difficult, highlighting the benefit of having more than one income source <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:59:00">[06:59:00]</a>.

## Factors Affecting Income Variability

### [[average_income_in_canada_by_age_group | Income by Age Group]] (2022)

Income typically shifts as individuals age <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:00">[01:07:00]</a>:

*   **Ages 25-34:** Median income is $48,700, typical for those building experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:00">[01:16:00]</a>.
*   **Ages 35-44:** Median income jumps to $66,300, often marking peak earning years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:00">[01:29:00]</a>.
*   **Ages 45-54:** Median income sees a slight increase to $66,400, reflecting years of experience and higher-level positions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:41:00">[01:41:00]</a>.
*   **Ages 55-64:** Median income starts to decline to $48,900, signaling transitions toward retirement or less demanding roles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:53:00">[01:53:00]</a>.
*   **Ages 65 and Older:** Median income drops further to $34,636 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:05:00">[02:05:00]</a>.

### [[gender_income_gap_in_canada | Gender Income Gap]] (2022)

There was a noticeable income gap between men and women in Canada in 2022 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:40:00">[03:40:00]</a>:

*   **Men:** Earned an average of $66,000 with a median income of $49,800 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:46:00">[03:46:00]</a>.
*   **Women:** Earned an average of $48,500 with a median of $37,700 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:55:00">[03:55:00]</a>. This represents a 36% higher average and 32% higher median income for men <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:02:00">[04:02:00]</a>.

This gap is influenced by factors such as men working in higher-paying fields (finance, engineering), working more full-time hours, fewer career interruptions, systemic biases, and greater likelihood of negotiating higher pay <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:06:00">[04:06:00]</a>.

### [[provincial_differences_in_income_across_canada | Provincial Differences]] (2022)

The [[provincial_differences_in_income_across_canada | province you live in]] greatly affects income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:48:00">[04:48:00]</a>:

*   **Alberta:** Leads with a median income of $45,600, driven by strong energy and resource sectors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:07:00">[05:07:00]</a>.
*   **Quebec, Saskatchewan, British Columbia:** Follow closely with a median income of $43,900 each <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:15:00">[05:15:00]</a>.
*   **Ontario:** Has a diverse job market with a median income of $42,700 and an average of $58,700, indicating high disparity between high and low-income jobs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:24:00">[05:24:00]</a>.
*   **Eastern Provinces (PEI, New Brunswick, Newfoundland, Nova Scotia):** Have lower median incomes, ranging from $38,000 to $40,000, due to smaller economies and more lower-wage jobs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:41:00">[05:41:00]</a>.

### Income by Education Level (2016)

The median income clearly rises with higher levels of education <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:32:00">[07:32:00]</a>:

*   **Bachelor's Degree or Higher:** Median income of $69,950 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:36:00">[07:36:00]</a>.
*   **College/Trade School Diploma:** Median income around $47,000, about 31% lower than those with a bachelor's degree <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:50:00">[07:50:00]</a>.
*   **High School Diploma or No Formal Education:** High school graduates have a median income of $35,790, which is over 60% less than the highest earners <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:02:00">[08:02:00]</a>.

### Salaries by Industry and Profession (2023)

In 2023, the average hourly wage in Canada was about $35.57, with a median wage of $30.98 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:34:00">[08:34:00]</a>.

*   **Highest Wages:** Senior Management roles command an average hourly wage of $91.16 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:45:00">[08:45:00]</a>. Specialized mid-level and general management roles are around $60 and $57 per hour, respectively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:54:00">[08:54:00]</a>.
*   **Well-Compensated Fields:** Professional fields like engineering ($49/hour), finance, and health ($42-$44/hour) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:03:00">[09:03:00]</a>. Education, law, and social services average $39-$44 per hour <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:19:00">[09:19:00]</a>.
*   **Decent Wages:** Trades, transport, and agricultural jobs offer $30-$31 per hour <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:27:00">[09:27:00]</a>.
*   **Lower End:** Manufacturing, retail, and service industries have wages ranging from $24-$28 per hour <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:35:00">[09:35:00]</a>.

## Strategies for Increasing Income

Understanding these income statistics can guide smarter financial decisions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:44:00">[09:44:00]</a>:

*   **Relocate to Higher-Wage Provinces:** If flexible, moving to a province with higher wages and a lower cost of living, like Alberta, can significantly increase take-home pay and savings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:56:00">[09:56:00]</a>.
*   **Choose Your Industry Wisely:** Fields such as management, engineering, finance, and healthcare offer some of the highest wages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:19:00">[10:19:00]</a>.
*   **Invest in Education:** Higher education clearly leads to higher earnings, with a university degree significantly boosting income compared to lower educational levels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:31:00">[10:31:00]</a>.
*   **Consider Household Income:** Dual-income households have much greater financial stability, with a median income for couples almost double that of a single income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:45:00">[10:45:00]</a>. Divorce can also be a significant factor affecting wealth and income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:57:00">[10:57:00]</a>.
*   **Address the [[gender_income_gap_in_canada | Gender Pay Gap]]:** Women might consider negotiating pay more aggressively or targeting industries working to close this gap <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:04:00">[11:04:00]</a>.

While it's natural to compare oneself to others, focusing on personal financial habits and goals is more constructive than constantly measuring success against misleading averages or medians <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:22:00">[11:22:00]</a>.

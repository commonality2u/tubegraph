## Key Considerations for TFSA Withdrawals in Retirement

When approaching retirement, a significant question is how to manage your TFSA to complement other income sources and reduce your tax burden <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:00">[00:09:00]</a>.

### TFSA vs. RSP: Which to Withdraw First?

Deciding whether to withdraw from your [[taxefficient_rrsp_withdrawal_strategies | Registered Retirement Savings Plan (RSP)]] or [[taxfree_savings_account_strategies | TFSA]] first is a common dilemma with no single answer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:12">[00:09:12]</a>.

*   **RSP Withdrawals:** Withdrawing from your RSP first can be beneficial because these withdrawals are considered taxable income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:18">[00:09:18]</a>. Drawing down your RSP earlier in retirement may help reduce your overall tax burden later on <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:20">[00:09:20]</a>. This strategy is particularly important if you are nearing the age to receive Old Age Security (OAS), as OAS benefits can be subject to clawbacks based on your income level <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:26">[00:09:26]</a>.
*   **TFSA Withdrawals:** Withdrawals from a TFSA are not taxable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:34">[00:09:34]</a>. The tax-free nature of TFSA withdrawals offers flexibility, allowing your investments to continue growing tax-free and providing a source of funds that does not impact your OAS benefits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:36">[00:09:36]</a>.

### Implementing a "Bucket Strategy" for Your TFSA

For a substantial TFSA, such as one worth $1 million, a "bucket strategy" can help ensure your funds last and continue to grow <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:47">[00:09:47]</a>. This strategy involves dividing your TFSA into portions based on your short-term, medium-term, and long-term financial needs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:51">[00:09:51]</a>.

*   **Short-Term Funds:** Allocate funds for immediate expenses to safer investments like bonds or Guaranteed Investment Certificates (GICs) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:58">[00:09:58]</a>.
*   **Medium-Term Funds:** Invest these funds in balanced portfolios <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:03">[00:10:03]</a>.
*   **Long-Term Funds:** Keep these funds in equities to ensure growth and combat inflation over time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:06">[00:10:06]</a>.

This approach ensures funds are available when needed while still working for you <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:10">[00:10:10]</a>.

### Coordinating TFSA Withdrawals with Canada Pension Plan (CPP)

A strategic approach to managing your TFSA involves coordinating it with your CPP benefits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:17">[00:10:17]</a>.

*   Since CPP provides regular income, your TFSA can supplement these amounts during years when other income is lower <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:22">[00:10:22]</a>.
*   Consider delaying CPP withdrawals while simultaneously drawing from your TFSA, especially if you retire early and need income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:31">[00:10:31]</a>. This allows your government benefits to grow, maximizing your income later in retirement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:39">[00:10:39]</a>.

Achieving a $1 million TFSA goal requires a simple yet smart strategy, balancing risk and return <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:44">[00:10:44]</a>. The more challenging aspect often lies in determining the optimal [[retirement_tax_strategies_for_canadians | tax strategy]] for your TFSA once it's built up <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:55">[00:10:55]</a>.

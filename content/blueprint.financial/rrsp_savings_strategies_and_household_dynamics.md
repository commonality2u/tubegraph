The average Canadian's Registered Retirement Savings Plan (RRSP) balance saw a significant drop of over $30,000 from 2022 to 2023 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:04">[00:00:04]</a>. The national average declined 28% to $113,000 in 2023 from approximately $144,000 in 2022 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:40">[00:00:40]</a>. This decline is largely a return to more typical savings levels after temporary spikes in 2021 and 2022, which were driven by increased savings during the pandemic when expenses were cut <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:09">[00:01:09]</a>. Despite this, Canadians remain focused on their financial futures, though rising living costs and [[impact_of_inflation_and_economic_factors_on_rrsp_savings|inflation]] make maintaining high contribution levels challenging <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:18">[00:01:18]</a>.

## Understanding RRSP Balances: Median vs. Average

When evaluating RRSP savings, median data provides a more realistic snapshot than average data <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:36">[00:01:36]</a>. Averages can be skewed by high-saving and high-earning outliers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:43">[00:01:43]</a>. For instance, in the 65 and older age group, the median RRSP balance is $224,000, which is more than double the average of $100,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:10">[00:02:10]</a>. This highlights a significant disparity, suggesting that a small number of high-income earners heavily influence the average figures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:17">[00:02:17]</a>.

### RRSP Holdings by Age (2019 Data)
Based on 2019 data combining RRSPs, RRIFs, and LIRAs from Statistics Canada:
*   **Under 35**: Median savings of only $12,500 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:27">[00:02:27]</a>. This underscores the critical need for young adults to begin consistent saving early <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:35">[00:02:35]</a>.
*   **35-54**: Median savings range between $30,000 and $70,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:54">[00:02:54]</a>. While savings improve, these amounts are often insufficient for a secure retirement, as individuals in this age group are frequently managing mortgages, family expenses, and careers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:00">[00:03:00]</a>.
*   **55-64**: Median savings reach $100,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:14">[00:03:14]</a>. However, this may still be inadequate for comfortable retirement, requiring reliance on additional income sources <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:17">[00:03:17]</a>.
*   **65+**: Median savings remain at $100,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:33">[00:03:33]</a>, indicating many retirees depend heavily on government benefits like CPP and OAS for their primary income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:37">[00:03:37]</a>.

## The Impact of Household Dynamics on RRSP Savings

Household size and structure significantly influence RRSP contributions and overall savings. Data that includes "economic family types" (combined households and individuals) generally shows higher savings for households due to shared financial responsibilities and additional incomes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:47">[00:03:47]</a>.

### Household Size and Contributions
Larger households tend to contribute more to their RRSPs, typically because they have more income available <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:35">[00:05:35]</a>:
*   **Two-person households**: Lead in contributions with 4.2 million households contributing and a median contribution of $4,888 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:41">[00:05:41]</a>. Dual-income households are often better positioned to prioritize and grow their retirement savings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:59">[00:05:59]</a>.
*   **Single-person households**: Contribute significantly less overall, with a median of $3,560 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:51">[00:05:51]</a>.

### Contributions by Age Group (2023 Data)
Despite [[impact_of_inflation_and_economic_factors_on_rrsp_savings|inflation]] and rising costs, Canadians are prioritizing RRSP contributions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:36">[00:06:36]</a>. The average contribution grew to $6,510 in 2023, up from $5,750 in 2022 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:40">[00:06:40]</a>.
*   **55 and above**: Contribute a median of $5,490 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:01">[00:07:01]</a>.
*   **15-24**: Contribute a median of $2,080 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:06">[00:07:06]</a>, a surprising and positive finding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:28">[00:07:28]</a>.

## Regional Variations in RRSP Savings
A 2023 survey revealed regional differences in RRSP management <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:15">[00:04:15]</a>:
*   **Ontario**: Leads with the highest average RRSP balance at $162,900 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:22">[00:04:22]</a>.
*   **Atlantic Canada**: Boasted the highest average contribution at $8,155 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:30">[00:04:30]</a>.

Beyond contributions, understanding [[rrsp_withdrawal_strategies|RRSP withdrawal strategies]] is crucial for maximizing the potential of these savings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:17">[00:05:17]</a>.

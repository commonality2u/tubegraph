Starting to invest can often feel like one of the most confusing parts of personal finance, with a wealth of conflicting advice available <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>. Rather than rigid rules, a five-step framework can guide anyone on their investment journey <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:08">[00:00:08]</a>.

Investing significantly outperforms simply saving money over time, primarily due to compounding returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:14">[00:00:14]</a>.
*   **Low Growth Larry** saved $2,000 a year for 30 years in a savings account earning 0.01% interest, contributing $600,000 but ending with only $600,931. His money barely grew and lost real value to inflation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:21">[00:00:21]</a>.
*   **Steady Investor Sally** invested $7,000 a year for 30 years in a portfolio, earning an annualized 7% return. She contributed a total of $210,000, but her investments grew to $777,000, outpacing Larry by over $100,000 despite contributing far less <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:44">[00:00:44]</a>.

Think of your investments like a snowball rolling downhill; it starts small but grows larger as it collects more snow, similar to compounding returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:06">[00:01:06]</a>.

## Five-Step Framework for Investing

### Step 1: [[developing_an_investment_philosophy | Develop Your Investment Philosophy]]

This is the most crucial step in your investment journey: starting with your "why" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:16">[00:01:16]</a>. Investing without purpose is like sailing without a destination, leading to aimless drifting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:29">[00:01:29]</a>.

Have a clear purpose for investing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:36">[00:01:36]</a>:
*   Saving for a down payment on a house <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:40">[00:01:40]</a>
*   Retiring comfortably <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:40">[00:01:40]</a>
*   Achieving financial freedom <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:42">[00:01:42]</a>
*   Funding education <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:45">[00:01:45]</a>
*   Providing for your family <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:46">[00:01:46]</a>

The more specific and personal your reasons, the stronger your foundation will be <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:47">[00:01:47]</a>. Knowing your "why" makes it easier to stick to your plan, weather market fluctuations, and make decisions aligned with long-term goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:23">[00:02:23]</a>.

### Step 2: Figure Out How Much You Can Contribute

Once your purpose is clear, determine how much you can realistically set aside for investments each month <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:41">[00:02:41]</a>.
*   **Review Your Budget:** Track income and expenses to identify saving opportunities, even small adjustments like cutting unused subscriptions can free up cash <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:51">[00:02:51]</a>.
*   **Automate Contributions:** Set up recurring transfers to your investment accounts on payday to make investing a habit, not a monthly decision <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:08">[00:03:08]</a>.

Consistent contributions are vital for growing your portfolio and benefiting from compounding growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:19">[00:03:19]</a>.
*   **Impact of Monthly Contributions (30 years, 7% return)** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:26">[00:03:26]</a>:
    *   $100/month grows to over $121,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:36">[00:03:36]</a>.
    *   $500/month builds a portfolio worth more than $600,000, significantly aiding retirement planning <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:44">[00:03:44]</a>.
    *   $1,000/month can grow into over $1.2 million, nearing financial freedom <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:52">[00:03:52]</a>.
For more details on contributions, refer to content on [[tfsa_investment_strategies | TFSA investment strategies]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:01">[00:04:01]</a>.

### Step 3: [[choosing_investment_types_and_accounts | Choose Your Investments]]

This step, while time-consuming and confusing for many, is where your investment journey takes shape <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:13">[00:04:13]</a>.
*   **Understand Your Risk Tolerance and Time Horizon:**
    *   **Risk Tolerance:** How comfortable are you with your portfolio dipping during market downturns? <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:30">[00:04:30]</a>
    *   **Time Horizon:** How long do you plan to stay invested? Longer timelines generally allow for higher-risk investments as there's more time to recover from fluctuations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:44">[00:04:44]</a>.
    *   Tools like Vanguard's free investor questionnaire can help assess your risk tolerance and investment style <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:54">[00:04:54]</a>.
    *   The true test of risk tolerance is your actions during market crashes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:07">[00:05:07]</a>, as many underestimate the risk they can actually handle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:21">[00:05:21]</a>.

*   **Where to Start for Beginners:**
    *   **ETFs (Exchange-Traded Funds) and Robo-Advisors:** Great for beginners as they are diversified and require little management <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:27">[00:05:27]</a>. Examples include all-in-one ETFs like VGRO or robo-advisors like Questwealth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:35">[00:05:35]</a>.
    *   **Individual Stocks:** Once confidence grows, explore stocks in companies you believe in and understand, while still aiming for diversification <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:42">[00:05:42]</a>.
    *   **Advanced Options:** With more experience, consider real estate, private equity, or hiring portfolio managers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:50">[00:05:50]</a>.

*   **[[Common Investment Mistakes | Two Big Mistakes to Avoid]]**:
    *   **Panic Selling:** Staying invested during market downturns is crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:09">[00:06:09]</a>. A $10,000 investment in the S&P 500 from 2004-2024 grew at an annualized 10.5% if fully invested, even through the 2008 financial crisis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:12">[00:06:12]</a>. Missing just the 10 best days reduced returns to 6.2%, and missing 30 best days dropped them to 1.4% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:27">[00:06:27]</a>.
    *   **Staying on the Sidelines:** Waiting for the "perfect" time to invest is often a losing game; even investing at the worst possible time usually outperforms just holding cash <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:42">[00:06:42]</a>.

### Step 4: [[choosing_investment_types_and_accounts | Choose Your Accounts]]

Selecting the right investment account significantly impacts your after-tax returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:56">[00:06:56]</a>. In Canada, TFSA (Tax-Free Savings Account), RRSP (Registered Retirement Savings Plan), and FHSA (First Home Savings Account) offer unique tax benefits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:01">[00:07:01]</a>.

*   **Comparison of $10,000 Invested Over 30 Years at 6% Growth** (assuming 40% current tax rate, 20% future tax rate for RRSP withdrawals, and 40% annual tax on non-registered growth) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:06">[00:07:06]</a>:
    *   **RRSP:** After-tax balance of $64,327, benefiting from upfront tax refunds and tax-deferred growth. Best if your retirement tax rate is significantly lower than your current rate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:24">[00:07:24]</a>.
    *   **TFSA:** Grows to $57,434 entirely tax-free, ideal for flexibility and tax-free withdrawals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:40">[00:07:40]</a>.
    *   **Non-Registered Account:** Only grows to $28,896, demonstrating how annual taxation drastically limits compounding growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:49">[00:07:49]</a>.

### Step 5: Buy the Investments and Track Performance

Once you've chosen your accounts and strategies, it's time to purchase your investments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:10">[00:08:10]</a>.
*   **How to Buy Stocks and ETFs:**
    *   **Pick a Platform:**
        *   **Wealthsimple:** User-friendly, great for beginners, offers commission-free trading for stocks and ETFs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:25">[00:08:25]</a>.
        *   **Questrade:** Ideal for more active investors or those seeking advanced features, with low trading fees and free ETF purchases <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:35">[00:08:35]</a>.
    *   **Set Up Your Account:** Choose the account type (TFSA, RRSP, FHSA, or non-registered), provide personal details, and verify identity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:44">[00:08:44]</a>.
    *   **Fund Your Account:** Transfer money from an existing account (in-kind or cash) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:55">[00:08:55]</a>.
    *   **Place Your Order:** Search for the ticker symbol (e.g., VFV for Vanguard S&P 500 ETF), decide on the number of shares, review costs, and place a market or limit order <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:12">[00:09:12]</a>.

*   **Monitoring Your Investments:**
    *   **Analyze Performance:** Regularly check your portfolio's performance against benchmarks like the S&P 500 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:32">[00:09:32]</a>.
    *   **Rebalance:** If one part of your portfolio has grown significantly, consider rebalancing to align with your goals and reduce risk <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:42">[00:09:42]</a>.
    *   **Watch for Fees and Taxes:** Be aware of sneaky fees or taxes that can quietly erode returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:51">[00:09:51]</a>.
    *   **Track Your Net Worth:** Sum up assets (investments, savings, property) and subtract liabilities (loans, debt). Watching this number grow is motivating and provides a big-picture view of your finances <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:57">[00:10:00]</a>. This aligns with [[strategies_to_increase_net_worth_using_financial_data | strategies to increase net worth using financial data]].
    *   **Be Consistent:** Choose a schedule (monthly, quarterly, or annually) for checking investments and stick to it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:19">[00:10:19]</a>.

*   **Learn from Your Mistakes:** Everyone makes mistakes in investing, whether it's panic selling during a dip or staying in cash too long <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:38">[00:10:38]</a>. The key is to learn from these moments, adjust your strategy, and focus on long-term goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:49">[00:10:49]</a>. This is part of [[avoiding_common_investment_pitfalls_and_maximizing_savings | avoiding common investment pitfalls and maximizing savings]].

*   **Consider an Expert:** If managing investments feels overwhelming or if you're not achieving desired results, hiring a portfolio manager can be beneficial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:00">[00:11:00]</a>. Look for an unbiased professional focused on helping you grow your money, whose approach aligns with your investment philosophy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:18">[00:11:18]</a>. There will be costs, but they often include personalized management, strategy, and planning towards your goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:27">[00:11:27]</a>.

Whether you manage your investments yourself or hire someone, the most important step is to simply get started <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:41">[00:11:41]</a>.

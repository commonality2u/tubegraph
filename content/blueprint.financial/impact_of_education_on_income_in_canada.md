## Median Income by Education Level

Data from 2016 for individuals aged 15 to 64 years in Canada reveals a clear correlation between higher levels of education and increased median income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:18:00">[07:18:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:32:00">[07:32:00]</a>:

*   **Bachelor's Degree or Higher:** Individuals with a bachelor's degree or higher had a median income of $69,950 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:36:00">[07:36:00]</a>.
*   **College Diploma or Apprenticeship:** Those with a college diploma or apprenticeship had a median income of approximately $47,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:50:00">[07:50:00]</a>. This figure is about 31% lower than the median income for those with a bachelor's degree <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:50:00">[07:50:00]</a>.
*   **High School Diploma or No Formal Education:** Individuals with only a high school diploma or no formal education faced a significant income gap <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:54:00">[07:54:00]</a>. High school graduates had a median income of $35,790 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:02:00">[08:02:00]</a>, which is over 60% less than the highest earners <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:21:00">[08:21:00]</a>.

These figures highlight the substantial differences in earning potential based on educational attainment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:21:00">[08:21:00]</a>.

The [[work_from_home_tax_deduction | Home Office Expense Deduction]] allows employees and self-employed individuals to claim expenses related to their home workspace <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:22:15">[03:22:15]</a>. Despite its benefits, many eligible taxpayers overlook this deduction, often due to a lack of awareness or the perceived complexity of the claims process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:32:00">[03:32:00]</a>.

## Eligibility and Covered Expenses
This deduction is available to individuals who work from home <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:22:15">[03:22:15]</a>. Eligible expenses can include:
*   Rent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:27:00">[03:27:00]</a>
*   Utilities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:27:00">[03:27:00]</a>
*   Internet costs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:27:00">[03:27:00]</a>

For renters, it may be possible to deduct a portion of the rent as a home office expense under certain conditions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:35:00">[06:35:00]</a>.

## Importance of Record-Keeping
It is crucial to keep all receipts and proper documentation, as the CRA may request proof when reviewing your tax return <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:40:00">[06:40:00]</a>. Maintaining detailed records supports your claims in case of an audit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:01:00">[08:01:00]</a>.

## Usage and Missed Opportunities
In the 2023 tax season, Canadians claimed a total of $2.08 billion in [[work_from_home_tax_deduction | home office expenses]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:39:00">[03:39:00]</a>. This marked a 41% increase from the previous year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:46:00">[03:46:00]</a>, yet many eligible individuals are still missing out on this deduction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:48:00">[03:48:00]</a>.

The Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) offers a tax deduction for certain [[moving_expenses_tax_deduction | moving expenses]], which many Canadians overlook <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:44:00">[01:44:00]</a>. This deduction can put hundreds or thousands of dollars back into your pocket <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:58:00">[01:58:00]</a>.

## Eligibility Criteria
To be eligible for the [[moving_expenses_tax_deduction | moving expenses deduction]], you must have moved at least 40 km closer to a new job or school <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:46:00">[01:46:00]</a>.

## Deductible Expenses
Eligible [[moving_expenses_tax_deduction | moving expenses]] can include:
*   Travel <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:50:00">[01:50:00]</a>
*   Storage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:50:00">[01:50:00]</a>
*   Temporary accommodations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:51:00">[01:51:00]</a>

## Common Pitfall: Lack of Record Keeping
A primary reason many individuals miss out on this deduction is forgetting to track their receipts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:54:00">[01:54:00]</a>. The CRA will not remind you to claim these deductions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:00:00">[02:00:00]</a>. If you made a work-related move in the past year, it is advisable to locate and gather all relevant receipts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:04:00">[02:04:00]</a>.

## Pro Tip for Claiming
You can only deduct [[moving_expenses_tax_deduction | moving costs]] against income earned at the new location <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:08:00">[02:08:00]</a>. If you moved but have not yet started work or school at the new location, you will need to wait to claim the deduction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:12:00">[02:12:00]</a>.

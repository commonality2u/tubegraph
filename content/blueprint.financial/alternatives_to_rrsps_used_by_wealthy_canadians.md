## Private Equity

Private Equity is a high-stakes investment game, akin to an exclusive VIP club that requires deep pockets, connections, and patience for a large payout <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:16">[00:00:16]</a>.

### What is Private Equity?
Private Equity involves investments in privately held companies that are not traded on public stock exchanges <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:29">[00:00:29]</a>. These investments may include funding startups, financing turnarounds, or supporting business expansions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:35">[00:00:35]</a>. Unlike traditional stocks, private equity carries higher risk, longer investment horizons, and limited liquidity, but it also offers the potential for significantly greater returns for investors willing to commit capital for years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:41">[00:00:41]</a>.

### Why Most Private Equity Can't Be in an RRSP
[[Comparing RRSPs with other wealthbuilding strategies | RRSPs]] are primarily designed for publicly traded investments, meaning they typically don't allow direct ownership of private companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:57">[00:00:57]</a>. The Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) restricts [[Key principles for building RRSP wealth | RRSP]] investments to public securities or select CRA-approved private equity funds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:03">[00:01:03]</a>. Therefore, unless investing through a specialized structure, private equity investments usually need to be held outside an [[Utilizing TFSA and RRSP for tax efficiency | RRSP]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:13">[00:01:13]</a>.

### When Private Equity Can Be Held in an RRSP
Despite restrictions, certain self-directed [[Key principles for building RRSP wealth | RRSPs]] can provide access to private equity under specific conditions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:22">[00:01:22]</a>. These accounts permit investors to hold CRA-approved private equity funds, enabling them to benefit from tax-deferred growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:28">[00:01:28]</a>. Examples include the North Leaf Capital Partners Private Equity Fund and select private equity investments offered through Wealthsimple <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:36">[00:01:36]</a>.

### The Downsides of Private Equity
Like any high-reward investment, private equity has notable drawbacks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:57">[00:01:57]</a>:
*   **High Fees**: Significant costs, including management fees and carried interest, can substantially reduce returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:04">[00:02:04]</a>.
*   **Very High Risk**: These investments are often highly illiquid, making them difficult to sell, and there's always a risk of losing the entire investment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:19">[00:02:19]</a>. For example, many Canadian investors suffered losses when Walton International Group, a land syndication venture under private equity real estate investing, filed for creditor protection in 2017 after amassing $67 million in losses over three years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:27">[00:02:27]</a>.
*   **Long Lockup Periods**: Investors must commit their funds for extended periods, often between 5 to 10 years, before seeing any returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:43">[00:02:43]</a>. For instance, a $100,000 investment might be locked up for five years before a company goes public and allows a cash-out <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:51">[00:02:51]</a>.

Private Equity is not for the faint of heart, but for patient and strategic investors, it can be a powerful wealth-building tool beyond an [[Key principles for building RRSP wealth | RRSP]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:03">[00:03:03]</a>.

## Individual Pension Plans (IPPs)

### What is an IPP?
An Individual Pension Plan (IPP) is a pension plan tailored specifically for business owners or incorporated professionals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:34">[00:03:34]</a>. Unlike an [[Utilizing TFSA and RRSP for tax efficiency | RRSP]] with a set contribution cap, an IPP allows for larger contributions, especially for individuals over 40, while still enjoying tax-deferred growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:45">[00:03:45]</a>. It is essentially a corporate-sponsored pension plan designed to provide structured retirement income, making it a preferred choice for high-income earners seeking a more strategic way to save for the future <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:54">[00:03:54]</a>.

### How an IPP Replaces RRSPs
While an [[Utilizing TFSA and RRSP for tax efficiency | RRSP]] acts like a "savings account on steroids," an IPP is more akin to a personalized pension planâ€”structured, powerful, and built for long-term security <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:06">[00:04:06]</a>.
*   **Less RRSP Contribution Room**: Once an IPP is established, the ability to contribute to an [[Utilizing TFSA and RRSP for tax efficiency | RRSP]] is reduced or eliminated, as the IPP takes priority in pension contributions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:24">[00:04:24]</a>.
*   **Bigger Contributions**: IPPs allow for significantly higher contributions than [[Key principles for building RRSP wealth | RRSPs]], particularly as one gets older, enabling faster growth of retirement savings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:37">[00:04:37]</a>.
*   **Reliable Retirement Income**: Withdrawals from an IPP follow a structured, pension-like system, providing predictable income, similar to a defined benefit pension plan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:50">[00:04:50]</a>.

### When an IPP Outperforms an RRSP: Real-World Examples
*   **High-Income Business Owner**: Daniel, a 50-year-old business owner earning $250,000 annually, maxed out his [[Utilizing TFSA and RRSP for tax efficiency | RRSP]]. By setting up an IPP, he was able to contribute over $50,000 annually, significantly boosting his retirement savings compared to the $31,560 [[Key principles for building RRSP wealth | RRSP]] cap in 2024 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:13">[00:05:13]</a>.
*   **Late Start to Retirement Savings**: Linda, a 55-year-old professional with a corporation who realized she hadn't saved enough, established an IPP to make past service contributions, rapidly growing her retirement fund and reducing her corporation's taxable income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:40">[00:05:40]</a>.
*   **Corporate Tax Efficiency**: Ryan, a 40-something incorporated consultant, transitioned from an [[Utilizing TFSA and RRSP for tax efficiency | RRSP]] to an IPP for greater tax efficiency. IPP contributions are considered a corporate expense, reducing his company's taxable income and corporate taxes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:04">[00:06:04]</a>.
*   **Client Success Story**: A doctor client increased his tax deductions by over 50% compared to his [[Key principles for building RRSP wealth | RRSP]] after switching to an IPP, securing a steady pension for retirement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:26">[00:06:26]</a>.

Setting up an IPP can be complex but offers significant tax and retirement benefits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:38">[00:06:38]</a>.

## Family Trust

[[Comparing RRSPs with other wealthbuilding strategies | RRSPs]] and Family Trusts serve different purposes in wealth building and preservation but can complement each other <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:57">[00:06:57]</a>. While an [[Utilizing TFSA and RRSP for tax efficiency | RRSP]] is a personal tax-sheltered retirement savings tool, a Family Trust offers greater flexibility in managing wealth, reducing taxes, and planning for future generations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:04">[00:07:04]</a>.

### What is a Family Trust?
A Family Trust can allow income to be distributed among family members in lower tax brackets, thereby minimizing overall tax liability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:15">[00:07:15]</a>. Unlike an [[Utilizing TFSA and RRSP for tax efficiency | RRSP]], which has strict contribution limits and taxable withdrawals upon retirement, a Family Trust has no contribution caps and offers more control over how and when money is distributed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:23">[00:07:23]</a>. It can hold a variety of assets, including private equity, businesses, real estate, and other investments, making it a powerful tool for long-term financial planning <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:33">[00:07:33]</a>.

Because [[Key principles for building RRSP wealth | RRSP]] withdrawals are fully taxable, they don't always provide the most efficient way to transfer wealth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:44">[00:07:44]</a>. A Family Trust, however, allows assets to be potentially passed down tax-efficiently and enables income splitting among family members <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:49">[00:07:49]</a>. This makes it an excellent strategy for business owners and high-income earners looking to reduce taxes while building generational wealth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:57">[00:07:57]</a>.

### Key Benefits of a Family Trust Over an RRSP
*   **No Contribution Limits**: A Family Trust can hold and grow significant wealth without annual caps <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:05">[00:08:05]</a>.
*   **Tax-Efficient Income Distribution**: If structured correctly, income can potentially be allocated to lower-tax family members to reduce overall tax exposure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:11">[00:08:11]</a>.
*   **Wealth Transfer Advantages**: Unlike [[Key principles for building RRSP wealth | RRSPs]] which trigger full taxation upon withdrawals, a Family Trust allows for potentially smoother and more tax-efficient intergenerational wealth transfers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:21">[00:08:21]</a>. For example, instead of passing down a million-dollar [[Utilizing TFSA and RRSP for tax efficiency | RRSP]] and paying 50% in taxes, there are potential ways to structure it into a trust to legally minimize taxes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:32">[00:08:32]</a>.

A pro tip for Family Trusts is to use a corporate trustee if worried about family disputes, as it helps maintain professionalism and avoids tensions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:44">[00:08:44]</a>. A Family Trust isn't just for the ultra-wealthy; it can be a smart tool for many Canadians seeking to manage wealth more effectively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:53">[00:08:53]</a>. When paired with an [[Utilizing TFSA and RRSP for tax efficiency | RRSP]], it can enhance tax efficiency, protect assets, and provide financial security across generations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:00">[00:09:00]</a>.

Understanding how IPPs, private equity, and Family Trusts work can unlock significant tax savings and smarter wealth protection <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:07">[00:09:07]</a>.

### Registered Retirement Savings Plan (RRSP)

The RRSP is a tax-deferred investment account designed primarily for [[tax_planning_for_retirement_in_canada | retirement planning]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:02">[07:02]</a>. Contributions to an RRSP are tax-deductible, meaning they reduce your taxable income in the year you contribute <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:32">[07:32]</a>. Your investments grow tax-deferred within the account, and you only pay tax when you withdraw the money, typically in retirement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:32">[07:32]</a>. This account is particularly advantageous if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement than you are during your working years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:34">[07:34]</a>.

### Tax-Free Savings Account (TFSA)

The TFSA is a highly flexible investment account where all investment income, including capital gains and dividends, grows tax-free and can be withdrawn tax-free <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:44">[07:44]</a>. Contributions to a TFSA are not tax-deductible, but the tax-free growth and withdrawals make it an excellent choice for various financial goals beyond retirement, such as saving for a down payment, education, or achieving [[investment_options_for_growing_a_tfsa | financial freedom]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:40">[01:40]</a>. It offers unparalleled flexibility because withdrawals have no tax implications <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:47">[07:47]</a>. You can explore [[investment_options_for_a_tfsa | investment options for a TFSA]] to maximize growth while also considering [[understanding_investment_risks_and_returns_in_a_tfsa | understanding investment risks and returns in a TFSA]].

### First Home Savings Account (FHSA)

The FHSA is a newer registered account designed to help first-time homebuyers save for a down payment. It combines features of both the RRSP (tax-deductible contributions) and the TFSA (tax-free withdrawals for a qualifying home purchase).

### Non-Registered Account

A non-registered account is a standard investment account that does not offer special tax benefits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:51">[07:51]</a>. Investment income earned in this account, such as interest, dividends, and capital gains, is taxed annually <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:53">[07:53]</a>. This annual taxation significantly limits the power of compounding growth, resulting in lower after-tax returns compared to registered accounts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:57">[07:57]</a>.

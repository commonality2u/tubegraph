## The Power of Investing vs. Only Saving

The distinction between saving and investing is crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:12">[00:00:12]</a>.

*   **Low Growth Larry**: Saves $2,000 annually in a TD savings account earning 0.01% interest <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:21">[00:00:21]</a>. Over 30 years, Larry contributes $600,000 but ends up with only $600,931 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:33">[00:00:33]</a>. His money barely grows and loses real value due to inflation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:41">[00:00:41]</a>.
*   **Steady Investor Sally**: Invests $7,000 annually in a portfolio earning an annualized 7% return <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:45">[00:00:45]</a>. Over the same 30 years, Sally contributes $210,000 but her investments grow to $777,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:53">[00:00:53]</a>. This significantly outpaces Larry's returns despite her lower contributions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:02">[00:01:02]</a>.

Investing can be thought of as a snowball rolling downhill, growing larger as it collects more snow, similar to compounding returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:07">[00:01:07]</a>.

## Five-Step Framework for Investing

### Step 1: Develop Your Investment Philosophy

This foundational step involves defining your "why" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:16">[00:01:16]</a>. Investing without purpose can lead to aimless drifting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:29">[00:01:29]</a>.

Consider your specific goals:
*   Saving for a down payment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:39">[00:01:39]</a>
*   [[financial_planning_for_canadian_retirees | Retirement planning]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:40">[00:01:40]</a>
*   Achieving financial freedom <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:42">[00:01:42]</a>
*   Funding education <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:45">[00:01:45]</a>
*   Providing for family <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:46">[00:01:46]</a>

A clear, personal purpose strengthens your investment foundation and helps you stick to your plan through market fluctuations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:49">[00:01:49]</a>.

### Step 2: Figure Out How Much You Can Contribute

Once your "why" is clear, determine your consistent contribution amount <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:41">[00:02:41]</a>.

*   **Review Your Budget**: Assess how much you can realistically set aside monthly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:51">[00:02:51]</a>. Track income and expenses to find saving opportunities, even small adjustments can free up cash <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:57">[00:02:57]</a>.
*   **Automate Contributions**: Set up recurring transfers to make investing a habit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:09">[00:03:09]</a>.

Consistent contributions are vital for portfolio growth and leveraging compounding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:20">[00:03:20]</a>.
*   **Impact of Monthly Contributions (7% return over 30 years)** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:27">[00:03:27]</a>:
    *   $100/month: Grows to over $121,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:36">[00:03:36]</a>.
    *   $500/month: Builds over $600,000, aiding [[retirement_planning_strategies_in_canada | retirement planning]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:44">[00:03:44]</a>.
    *   $1,000/month: Can grow to over $1.2 million, nearing financial freedom <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:52">[00:03:52]</a>.

For more details on maximizing contributions, see the video on [[strategies_to_build_a_million_dollar_tfsa | how important contributions are to building a massive TFSA]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:01">[00:04:01]</a>.

### Step 3: Choose Your Investments

This is often the most time-consuming and confusing part of investing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:16">[00:04:16]</a>.

*   **Understand Your Risk Tolerance and Time Horizon**:
    *   **Risk Tolerance**: How comfortable are you with portfolio dips during market downturns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:33">[00:04:33]</a>? The true test is your actions when markets crash <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:08">[00:05:08]</a>. As Peter Lynch noted, many overestimate their risk tolerance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:13">[00:05:13]</a>.
    *   **Time Horizon**: How long do you plan to stay invested <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:45">[00:04:45]</a>? Longer timelines typically allow for higher-risk investments as there's more time to recover from fluctuations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:48">[00:04:48]</a>.
    *   **Tools**: Vanguard's free investor questionnaire can help assess your risk tolerance and style <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:55">[00:04:55]</a>.

*   **Starting Points for Beginners**:
    *   **ETFs (Exchange Traded Funds)** and **Robo-advisors**: Great for beginners because they are diversified and require minimal management <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:27">[00:05:27]</a>. Examples include all-in-one ETFs like VGRO or robo-advisors such as Questwealth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:35">[00:05:35]</a>.
    *   **Individual Stocks**: Explore these once your confidence grows, sticking to companies you believe in and understand, and diversifying <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:43">[00:05:43]</a>.
    *   **Advanced Options**: With experience, consider real estate, private equity, or hiring portfolio managers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:50">[00:05:50]</a>.

*   **Avoid These Big Mistakes**:
    *   **Panic Selling**: Staying invested during market downturns is crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:09">[00:06:09]</a>. Missing even a few of the best recovery days can significantly reduce long-term returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:27">[00:06:27]</a>.
    *   **Staying on the Sidelines**: Waiting for the "perfect" time to invest is often a losing strategy, as investing even at suboptimal times tends to outperform sitting in cash <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:42">[00:06:42]</a>.

### Step 4: Choose Your Accounts

The right investment account can significantly impact your after-tax returns in Canada <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:56">[00:06:56]</a>. Key accounts like the TFSA, RRSP, and FHSA offer unique tax benefits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:01">[00:07:01]</a>.

*   **Account Comparison (Example: $10,000 invested over 30 years at 6% growth)** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:06">[00:07:06]</a>:
    *   **RRSP (Registered Retirement Savings Plan)**: Comes out ahead with an after-tax balance of $64,327, thanks to upfront tax refunds and tax-deferred growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:24">[00:07:24]</a>. This strategy works best if your retirement tax rate is significantly lower than your current tax rate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:34">[00:07:34]</a>.
    *   **TFSA (Tax-Free Savings Account)**: A close second, growing to $57,434 entirely tax-free <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:40">[00:07:40]</a>. Ideal for flexibility and tax-free withdrawals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:46">[00:07:46]</a>.
    *   **Non-Registered Account**: Only grows to $28,896, demonstrating how annual taxation severely limits compounding growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:49">[00:07:49]</a>.

For a deep dive into Canadian investment accounts, refer to the video on [[types_of_investment_accounts_in_canada | the best account order of investing in Canada]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:03">[00:08:03]</a>.

### Step 5: Buy Investments and Track Performance

Once you've chosen your accounts and strategies, it's time to purchase your investments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:10">[00:08:10]</a>.

*   **How to Buy Stocks and ETFs**:
    *   **Pick a Platform**:
        *   **Well Simple**: User-friendly, great for beginners, offers commission-free trading for stocks and ETFs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:25">[00:08:25]</a>.
        *   **Questrade**: Ideal for more active investors or those needing advanced features, with low trading fees and free ETF purchases <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:35">[00:08:35]</a>.
    *   **Set Up Your Account**: Choose the account type (TFSA, RRSP, FHSA, non-registered), provide personal details, and verify identity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:45">[00:08:45]</a>.
    *   **Fund Your Account**: Transfer money from an existing account (in-kind transfers can take weeks) or cash from your savings account (takes a few business days) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:55">[00:08:55]</a>.
    *   **Place Your Order**: Search for the ticker symbol (e.g., VFV for Vanguard S&P 500 ETF), decide on the number of shares, review costs, and place a market or limit order <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:12">[00:09:12]</a>. The process for stocks is similar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:25">[00:09:25]</a>.

*   **Monitoring Your Investments**:
    *   **Analyze Performance**: Regularly check your portfolio against benchmarks like the S&P 500 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:32">[00:09:32]</a>.
    *   **Rebalance**: If one part of your portfolio grows disproportionately, consider rebalancing to align with goals and reduce risk <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:42">[00:09:42]</a>.
    *   **Check Fees and Taxes**: Be aware of hidden fees and taxes that can reduce returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:51">[00:09:51]</a>.
    *   **Track Your Net Worth**: Calculate your net worth by adding assets (investments, savings, property) and subtracting liabilities (loans, credit card debt) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:58">[00:09:58]</a>. This provides a big-picture view and motivation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:09">[00:10:09]</a>. A free net worth tracker is available for download on the speaker's website <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:14">[00:10:14]</a>.
    *   **Be Consistent**: Choose a monitoring schedule (monthly, quarterly, or annually) and stick to it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:19">[00:10:19]</a>.
    *   **Learn from Mistakes**: Everyone makes mistakes in investing. Focus on learning, adjusting your strategy, and avoiding emotional decisions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:38">[00:10:38]</a>.

## Seeking Professional Help

While many can manage investments themselves, life can get busy, or desired results may not be achieved <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:01">[00:11:01]</a>.

*   **Hiring a Portfolio Manager**: Can be a great solution if self-management feels overwhelming <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:10">[00:11:10]</a>.
    *   **Finding an Unbiased Expert**: Look for someone who is not just selling products for high commissions but is focused on growing your money and providing personalized management, strategy, and [[strategies_for_increasing_income_and_net_worth_in_canada | planning towards reaching your goals]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:18">[00:11:18]</a>.
    *   **Interview and Ask Questions**: Interview several portfolio managers to ensure their approach aligns with your investment philosophy, and inquire about fees and services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:33">[00:11:33]</a>.

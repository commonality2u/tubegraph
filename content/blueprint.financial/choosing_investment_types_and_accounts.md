## Saving vs. Investing: The Power of Compounding

The difference between merely saving and smart investing can be significant over time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:17">[00:00:17]</a>.

*   **Low Growth Larry** saves $2,000 a year in a TD savings account earning 0.01% interest <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:21">[00:00:21]</a>. Over 30 years, he contributes a total of $600,000 but ends up with only $600,931 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:33">[00:00:33]</a>. His money barely grows and actually loses real value due to inflation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:38">[00:00:38]</a>.
*   **Steady Investor Sally** invests $7,000 a year in a portfolio earning an annualized 7% return over the same 30 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:45">[00:00:45]</a>. She contributes a total of just $210,000, but her investments grow to $777,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:51">[00:00:51]</a>. This outperforms Larry by over $100,000, even though she contributed far less <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:58">[00:00:58]</a>.

Think of investments like a snowball rolling downhill: it starts small but grows larger as it collects more snow, similar to compounding returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:04">[00:01:04]</a>.

## Step 1: [[developing_an_investment_philosophy | Develop Your Investment Philosophy]]

This is the most important step in your investment journey <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:16">[00:01:16]</a>. Too often, people invest without a clear purpose, which is like setting sail without a destination <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:29">[00:01:29]</a>.

*   **Define Your "Why"**: Have a clear purpose for investing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:36">[00:01:36]</a>. Is it for a house down payment, comfortable retirement, financial freedom, education, or family provision? <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:38">[00:01:38]</a> The more specific and personal your reasons, the stronger your foundation will be <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:48">[00:01:48]</a>.
*   **Personal Motivation**: For the speaker, investing is about freedom to live life on their own terms and the confidence that comes with financial security <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:52">[00:01:52]</a>. This clarity helps to stay consistent with the strategy, even when it requires delaying gratification <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:15">[00:02:15]</a>.
*   **Benefits of a Clear Why**: Knowing your "why" makes it easier to stick to your plan, weather market ups and downs, and align decisions with long-term goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:24">[00:02:24]</a>.

## Step 2: Figure Out Your Contribution Strategy

Once your "why" is established, determine how much you can contribute <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:41">[00:02:41]</a>.

*   **Budgeting**: Review your budget to see how much you can realistically set aside each month <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:51">[00:02:51]</a>. Track income and expenses to find saving opportunities, even small adjustments like cutting unused subscriptions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:57">[00:02:57]</a>.
*   **Automate Contributions**: Set up recurring transfers to your investment accounts on payday <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:09">[00:03:09]</a>. This makes investing a habit rather than a monthly decision <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:15">[00:03:15]</a>.
*   **Impact of Consistent Contributions**: Contributions are the lifeblood of portfolio growth; consistent investment maximizes compounding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:19">[00:03:19]</a>.
    *   At a 7% rate of return over 30 years:
        *   $100/month grows to over $121,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:34">[00:03:34]</a>.
        *   $500/month builds a portfolio worth more than $600,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:44">[00:03:44]</a>.
        *   $1,000/month can grow into over $1.2 million, nearing financial freedom <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:52">[00:03:52]</a>.
*   For more details on maximizing contributions, refer to the video about building a massive TFSA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:00">[00:04:00]</a>.

## Step 3: Choose Your Investments

This step can be the most time-consuming and confusing, but it's where your investment journey takes shape <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:16">[00:04:16]</a>. Spend time researching <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:26">[00:04:26]</a>.

### [[Understanding Risk and Investment Choices | Understand Your Risk Tolerance and Time Horizon]]

Before picking investments, assess how much risk you're comfortable with <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:30">[00:04:30]</a>.

*   **Risk Tolerance**: Are you okay with your portfolio dipping during market downturns, or would that cause you anxiety? <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:35">[00:04:35]</a>
*   **Time Horizon**: How long do you plan to stay invested? Longer timelines typically allow for higher-risk investments because there's more time to recover from market fluctuations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:44">[00:04:44]</a>.
*   **Tools**: Vanguard's free investor questionnaire can help determine your risk tolerance and investment style <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:55">[00:04:55]</a>.
*   **True Test**: Famed investor Peter Lynch noted that "Everybody in the world is a long-term investor until the markets start going down" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:13">[00:05:13]</a>, meaning many overestimate their risk tolerance in reality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:21">[00:05:21]</a>.

### Where to Start for Beginners

*   **ETFs and Robo-Advisors**: Ideal for beginners as they are diversified and require little management <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:27">[00:05:27]</a>. Examples include all-in-one ETFs like VGRO or Robo-advisors like Questwealth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:35">[00:05:35]</a>.
*   **Individual Stocks**: As confidence grows, explore stocks. Stick to companies you believe in and understand, and diversify your holdings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:43">[00:05:43]</a>.
*   **Advanced Options**: With experience, consider diversifying into real estate, private equity, or hiring portfolio managers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:51">[00:05:51]</a>.

A list of pros and cons for various [[investment_options_for_tfsa | investment options]] can be considered <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:01">[00:06:01]</a>.

### Two Big Mistakes to Avoid

1.  **Panic Selling**: Staying invested during market downturns is crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:09">[00:06:09]</a>. A $10,000 investment in the S&P 500 from 2004 to 2024 grew at an annualized rate of 10.5% if fully invested, even through the 2008 financial crisis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:12">[00:06:12]</a>. Missing just the 10 best days reduced returns to 6.2%, and missing the 30 best days dropped them to 1.4% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:27">[00:06:27]</a>. Panic selling often means missing key recovery days <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:36">[00:06:36]</a>.
2.  **Staying on the Sidelines**: Waiting for the perfect time to invest is a losing game <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:42">[00:06:42]</a>. Even investing at the worst possible time often outperforms just sitting in cash <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:46">[00:06:46]</a>. Start with the basics, learn as you go, and don't overthink it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:50">[00:06:50]</a>.

## Step 5: Buy Investments and Track Performance

Once you've chosen your accounts and strategies, it's time to actually buy your investments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:10">[00:08:10]</a>.

### How to Buy Stocks and ETFs

1.  **Pick a Platform**:
    *   **Well Simple**: User-friendly, great for beginners, offers commission-free trading for stocks and ETFs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:25">[00:08:25]</a>.
    *   **Questrade**: Ideal for more active investors or those needing advanced features, with low trading fees and free ETF purchases <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:35">[00:08:35]</a>.
2.  **Set Up Your Account**: Choose the account type (TFSA, RRSP, FHSA, or [[nonregistered_accounts_and_taxefficient_investments | non-registered]]), provide personal details, and verify your identity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:44">[00:08:44]</a>.
3.  **Fund Your Account**: Transfer money from an existing account (can take weeks for "in kind" transfers, or a few business days for cash transfers) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:55">[00:08:55]</a>.
4.  **Place Your Order**:
    *   **ETFs**: Search for the ticker symbol (e.g., VFV for Vanguard S&P 500 ETF), decide on the number of shares, review costs, and place a market or limit order <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:12">[00:09:12]</a>.
    *   **Stocks**: The process is similar to ETFs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:25">[00:09:25]</a>.

### Monitoring Your Investments

*   **Analyze Performance**: Keep an eye on your portfolio's performance. Compare your returns to benchmarks like the S&P 500 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:32">[00:09:32]</a>.
*   **Rebalance**: If one part of your portfolio, like an individual stock, has grown significantly more than others, it might be time to rebalance to align with your goals and reduce risk <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:41">[00:09:41]</a>.
*   **Watch for Fees and Taxes**: Be aware of sneaky fees or taxes that can quietly erode your returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:51">[00:09:51]</a>.

### Tracking Your Net Worth

Tracking your net worth is a great way to monitor your overall financial health <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:58">[00:09:58]</a>.

*   **Calculation**: Add up all your assets (investments, savings, property) and subtract your liabilities (loans, credit card debt) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:01">[00:10:01]</a>.
*   **Motivation**: Watching this number grow over time is highly motivating and provides a big-picture view of your finances <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:09">[00:10:09]</a>. A free net worth tracker can be downloaded from the speaker's website <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:13">[00:10:13]</a>.

### Consistency and Learning from Mistakes

*   **Be Consistent**: Pick a schedule (monthly, quarterly, or annually) that works for you and stick to it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:19">[00:10:19]</a>. Monthly is great for staying on top of things, quarterly for stable portfolios, and annual for passive, long-term investing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:27">[00:10:27]</a>.
*   **Learn from Mistakes**: Everyone makes mistakes in investing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:38">[00:10:38]</a>. Whether it's panic selling during a dip or staying in cash too long, the key is to learn from these moments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:40">[00:10:40]</a>. Adjust your strategy, focus on long-term goals, and avoid letting short-term emotions derail your plan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:49">[00:10:49]</a>. The more you learn, the better you become at sticking to your game plan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:55">[00:10:55]</a>.

### When to Hire an Expert

While many can manage investments themselves, life gets busy, or if you're not achieving desired results, an expert's second opinion can be helpful <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:00">[00:11:00]</a>.

*   **Portfolio Managers**: Hiring a portfolio manager can be a great solution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:09">[00:11:09]</a>.
*   **Finding the Right Advisor**: Look for someone unbiased, not just selling products for high commissions, but focused on helping you grow your money <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:18">[00:11:18]</a>.
*   **Cost vs. Value**: There will be a cost, but it often includes personalized management, strategy, and planning toward your goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:26">[00:11:26]</a>.
*   **Interview Process**: Interview several portfolio managers to ensure their approach aligns with your [[developing_an_investment_philosophy | investment philosophy]], and ask questions about fees and services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:33">[00:11:33]</a>.

Whether you manage your investments yourself or hire someone, the key is simply to get started <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:41">[00:11:41]</a>.

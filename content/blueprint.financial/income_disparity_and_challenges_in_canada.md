## Widespread Financial Strain
A recent Angus Reid poll indicates that a striking 40% of recent immigrants are contemplating moving to another province or entirely leaving Canada, primarily due to the high [[cost_of_living_crisis_in_canada | cost of living]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:15">[00:00:15]</a>. This raises questions about the attainability of the "Canadian dream" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:27">[00:00:27]</a>.

### [[rising_food_and_housing_costs_in_canada | Rising Costs of Essentials]]
The frustration among Canadians is rooted in the significant increases in everyday expenses:

*   **Food Prices**
    *   According to David Olive in the Toronto Star, a basket of 18 household items that cost $82 in March 2021 now costs $113.32, marking a 38.2% increase in just three years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:44">[00:00:44]</a>.
    *   The latest Canada Food Price Report forecasts an additional increase of about $711 in the average family of four's annual food bill this year, bringing the total average to $16,295 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:06">[00:01:06]</a>. This follows a $1,065 jump in 2023 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:22">[00:01:22]</a>.
    *   Food prices have consistently outpaced the average rate of [[impact_of_inflation_on_canadians_financial_struggles | inflation]] in recent years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:26">[00:01:26]</a>.
*   **Rental and Housing Costs**
    *   Rent is a major concern <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:32">[00:01:32]</a>. The average rent for a two-bedroom apartment in Vancouver increased by 40% from $2,598 per month in April 2021 to $3,648 per month in July 2024 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:35">[00:01:35]</a>.
    *   The average cost to purchase a house in Canada has dramatically risen by 69.3% in nine years, from $413,000 in 2015 to $699,000 in May 2024 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:55">[00:01:55]</a>.
    *   Essential costs like shelter and food have become significantly more expensive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:48">[00:01:48]</a>, with transportation and entertainment costs also increasing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:18">[00:02:18]</a>. Overall, rampant [[impact_of_inflation_on_canadians_financial_struggles | inflation]] has made it challenging for many Canadians to pay their bills <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:23">[00:02:23]</a>.

### Income Stagnation
Despite rising costs, income growth has not kept pace <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:26">[00:03:26]</a>.
*   According to Statistics Canada, the median after-tax income for Canadian families and unattached individuals in 2022 was $70,500 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:30">[00:03:30]</a>. This represents a decrease from $73,000 in 2021 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:42">[00:03:42]</a>.
*   Average wages likely haven't increased sufficiently in 2023 or 2024 to match [[impact_of_inflation_on_canadians_financial_struggles | inflation]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:45">[00:03:45]</a>.
*   The discontinuation of pandemic-era government benefits and the return to pre-pandemic Employment Insurance programs have further tightened finances <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:08">[00:04:08]</a>.

### Geographic Mobility Due to Costs
Many Canadians are considering internal migration due to high costs:
*   Almost one-third (28%) of all Canadians are contemplating leaving their current province due to high housing costs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:38">[00:02:38]</a>.
*   Young adults and renters are most likely to consider such a move <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:44">[00:02:44]</a>.
*   This trend is particularly evident in Ontario and British Columbia, where high housing costs have been a persistent issue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:51">[00:02:51]</a>.
*   Alberta is a popular relocation destination, attracting 18% of Canadians considering a move, largely due to more affordable housing and better job opportunities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:00">[00:03:00]</a>.
*   Between 2022 and 2023, net interprovincial migration out of Toronto, Montreal, and Vancouver exceeded 130,000 people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:10">[00:03:10]</a>.

### Decline in Well-being
The financial struggles contribute to widespread unhappiness, particularly among Canadian youth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:17">[00:04:17]</a>.
*   The latest World Happiness Report by the Gallup World Poll ranks Canada as the 15th happiest country overall <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:20">[00:04:20]</a>.
*   However, Canadians under 30 fall to the 58th position, highlighting a significant disparity in happiness levels among younger generations and painting a "bleak picture for our future" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:28">[00:04:28]</a>.

## Strategies to Combat High Living Costs
Addressing the [[cost_of_living_crisis_in_canada | cost of living crisis]] requires both increasing income and reducing expenses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:16">[00:05:16]</a>. While many factors are uncontrollable, individuals can manage what they can <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:46">[00:04:46]</a>.

### Earning More Money
Strategies to boost earnings vary by income level:

*   **For [[financial_characteristics_and_challenges_of_lower_class_in_canada | Lower Income]] Canadians**
    *   **Side Hustles:** Consider taking on a part-time job, freelance writing on platforms like Upwork, driving for Uber, delivering for DoorDash, or starting a small online store on Etsy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:37">[00:05:37]</a>.
    *   **Skill Development:** Invest in learning new and in-demand skills through free or low-cost online courses (e.g., Coursera, LinkedIn Learning, Udemy) to gain certificates in fields like digital marketing or participate in government-subsidized training programs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:58">[00:05:58]</a>.
    *   **Job Search:** Constantly look for better-paying job opportunities on boards like Indeed or Glassdoor, set up automatic alerts, network on LinkedIn, attend job fairs, or join professional groups <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:16">[00:06:16]</a>.
*   **For Middle Income Earners**
    *   **Career Advancement:** Seek more responsibilities, promotions, and negotiate for higher pay within your current company <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:33">[00:06:33]</a>.
    *   **Additional Qualifications:** Obtain post-graduate degrees or specialized training that could lead to higher-paying roles, ideally employer-sponsored <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:43">[00:06:43]</a>.
    *   **Passive Income:** Explore opportunities like investments, rental properties, or creating digital products if you have savings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:54">[00:06:54]</a>.
*   **For Higher Income Earners**
    *   **Investments:** Make money work for you by investing in stocks, real estate, or other higher-return opportunities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:07">[00:07:07]</a>.
    *   **Business Ventures:** Consider starting or investing in businesses with high profitability potential <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:14">[00:07:14]</a>.
    *   **Consulting:** Leverage your expertise to offer consulting services for higher fees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:21">[00:07:21]</a>.

### Spending Less Money
Reducing expenses is crucial regardless of income level:

*   **For [[financial_characteristics_and_challenges_of_lower_class_in_canada | Lower Income]] Earners**
    *   **Government Programs:** Utilize government assistance programs for services like food banks, emergency shelters, financial counseling, job training, and utility bill assistance (e.g., Salvation Army, YMCA, United Way) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:40">[00:07:40]</a>.
    *   **Strict Budget:** Create and adhere to a budget, tracking expenses to identify areas for cuts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:01">[00:08:01]</a>.
    *   **Focus on the Big Three:** Prioritize reducing food, shelter, and transportation costs, as these are typically the largest household expenses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:07">[00:08:07]</a>.
    *   **Negotiate Bills:** Negotiate with service providers for cable, internet, and phone to secure lower rates or better deals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:15">[00:08:15]</a>.
*   **For Middle Income Earners**
    *   **Debt Management:** Focus on paying off high-interest debt to reduce monthly expenses and save money long-term <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:31">[00:08:31]</a>.
    *   **Smart Shopping:** Use coupons, discounts, and cashback offers for everyday purchases <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:39">[00:08:39]</a>.
    *   **Lifestyle Choices:** Evaluate lifestyle choices and cut back on things like dining out or choosing more affordable entertainment options <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:46">[00:08:46]</a>.
*   **For Higher Income Earners**
    *   **Tax Planning:** Work with a financial advisor or accountant to implement tax-saving strategies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:56">[00:08:56]</a>.
    *   **Luxury Spending:** Be mindful of luxury spending and consider whether it aligns with long-term financial goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:04">[00:09:04]</a>.
    *   **Automatic Savings:** Set up automatic transfers to savings and investment accounts to ensure consistent contributions and reduce spending temptation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:10">[00:09:10]</a>.

### Out-of-the-Box Solutions
While not applicable to everyone, these options can help reduce living costs:
*   **Tiny Houses:** A growing trend in Canada involves living in compact homes (200-400 sq ft) to save on housing costs and embrace minimalism. These can be stationary or mobile and are often environmentally friendly, with companies like Mint Tiny House Company in Vancouver and Minimalist Houses in Quebec <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:24">[00:09:24]</a>.
*   **Digital Nomads:** With the rise of remote work, individuals can live and work from anywhere, reducing living expenses by temporarily moving to more affordable international locations like Thailand, Portugal, or Mexico <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:51">[00:09:51]</a>. This requires a reliable internet connection and careful planning <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:08">[00:10:08]</a>.

Despite the current challenges, Canadian core values and economy are still worth investing in <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:23">[00:10:23]</a>. Hopes remain for effective government strategies to control the [[cost_of_living_crisis_in_canada | cost of living]], and there are signs that [[impact_of_inflation_on_canadians_financial_struggles | inflation]] may be cooling, potentially offering some relief <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:28">[00:10:28]</a>.

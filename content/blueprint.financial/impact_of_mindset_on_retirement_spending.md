Retirement often brings a new challenge: shifting from a lifetime of saving to a period of spending the wealth accumulated over decades <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:24">[00:00:24]</a>. This transition requires a significant mindset shift, moving from a "scarcity mindset" to an "abundance mindset" where retirees feel confident utilizing their savings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:30">[00:00:30]</a>. This [[the_save_or_spend_dilemma_for_retirees | save or spend dilemma]] is a common challenge for many retirees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:39">[00:00:39]</a>.

## Why Not Spending Enough Can Be Detrimental

While the instinct to save is strong, an overly cautious approach to spending in retirement can have negative consequences:

*   **Missed Experiences and Opportunities**
    Retirees who hesitate to spend may miss out on fulfilling experiences such as travel, hobbies, or even healthcare improvements that could enrich their golden years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:01">[00:01:01]</a>.
*   **Healthspan vs. Lifespan**
    Delaying spending can mean missing the best years of one's "healthspan"â€”the period lived in good health, active, independent, and free from chronic disease or disability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:30">[00:01:30]</a>. Waiting to spend until physical abilities decline can limit enjoyment of accumulated wealth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:39">[00:01:39]</a>.
*   **Inflation's Impact**
    Inflation can erode the value of money over time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:47">[00:01:47]</a>. While a properly structured retirement plan should account for inflation to maintain purchasing power, unspent money simply loses value if not invested strategically <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:52">[00:01:52]</a>.

## Why Not Spending Enough Can Be Beneficial

Paradoxically, a degree of hesitation in spending can also be advantageous:

*   **Encourages Cautious Spending**
    A cautious mindset promotes thoughtful and deliberate spending, preventing impulsive purchases and overspending <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:18">[00:02:18]</a>. This ensures that each financial decision is well-considered <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:24">[00:02:24]</a>.
*   **Helps Money Last (Reducing Longevity Risk)**
    A careful approach helps money stretch further, reducing the risk of running out of funds prematurely, a common concern for retirees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:29">[00:02:29]</a>. This is known as reducing longevity risk <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:38">[00:02:38]</a>.
*   **Reduces Portfolio Risk (Reducing Sequence of Returns Risk)**
    Spending cautiously helps protect against the impact of market losses, especially those occurring early in retirement, which can drain a portfolio faster than later losses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:43">[00:02:43]</a>. This strategy helps mitigate "sequence of returns risk" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:54">[00:02:54]</a>.
*   **Aligns with Legacy Goals**
    For many, leaving a substantial amount for loved ones or charitable causes is a priority <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:04">[00:03:04]</a>. Being cautious with spending allows retirees to achieve these legacy goals while still enjoying their own retirement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:10">[00:03:10]</a>.

## Finding the Right Balance

The key to navigating the [[the_save_or_spend_dilemma_for_retirees | save versus spend dilemma]] is finding a healthy balance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:22">[00:03:22]</a>. Many retirees are unsure how much they can safely spend, leading them to err on the side of caution to avoid running out of money <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:32">[00:03:32]</a>. However, this cautious approach can prevent them from enjoying the wealth they've worked hard to build <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:48">[00:03:48]</a>.

A well-structured [[planning_a_sustainable_retirement_budget | financial plan]] is essential to provide clarity on how much can be safely spent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:01">[00:04:01]</a>. By stress-testing the investment portfolio, financial planners can help retirees transition from a mindset of fear to one of confidence and enjoyment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:05">[00:04:05]</a>.

## Case Studies: Overcoming the Dilemma

### Michael and Karen: Spending More Freely

*   **Client Profile:** Michael (66) and Karen (64) had a combined annual income of $180,000 from pensions (CPP, OAS, work pensions) and investments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:32">[00:04:32]</a>. Their yearly expenses were $70,000, covering basic living costs, travel, and discretionary spending <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:40">[00:04:40]</a>. They had $4 million in a diversified portfolio <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:47">[00:04:47]</a>.
*   **Goal:** To increase spending on luxury travel, home renovations, and charitable giving, but they were unsure how much more they could spend <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:53">[00:04:53]</a>.
*   **Process:** Despite an annual surplus of $110,000 ($180,000 income - $70,000 expenses), they were hesitant to spend more <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:11">[00:05:11]</a>. With their $4 million portfolio and a sustainable 3.5% withdrawal rate, they could easily withdraw an additional $140,000 annually, more than enough for their desired increases in spending <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:22">[00:05:22]</a>. Their basic expenses were covered by guaranteed income streams (pensions), leaving their investments largely untouched <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:36">[00:05:36]</a>.
*   **Solution:** Stress-testing their portfolio for a 30% decline in value during early retirement showed it remained safe for long-term sustainability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:47">[00:05:47]</a>. Longevity projections to age 95 indicated their portfolio would last well beyond their predicted lifetimes, even with higher spending <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:04">[00:06:04]</a>.
*   **Outcome:** Armed with this information, Michael and Karen gained confidence to spend more freely, booking luxury vacations, starting home renovations, and increasing charitable contributions without jeopardizing their financial future <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:16">[00:06:16]</a>.

### Robert and Susan: Balancing Spending and Legacy Goals

*   **Client Profile:** Robert (70) and Susan (68) had a combined annual income of $100,000 and expenses of $75,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:12">[00:07:12]</a>. They had $1.2 million in their portfolio <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:19">[00:07:19]</a>.
*   **Goal:** To leave a meaningful inheritance for their children and grandchildren while still enjoying their retirement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:23">[00:07:23]</a>.
*   **Process:** They were cautious about increasing their spending, unsure if they could afford more travel and leisure while still leaving enough for family <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:31">[00:07:31]</a>. Their $1.2 million portfolio, with a 4% withdrawal rate, could provide $48,000 annually <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:47">[00:07:47]</a>. Combined with their $100,000 income, they had more than enough to cover their $75,000 expenses and increase spending <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:57">[00:07:57]</a>.
*   **Solution:** To balance their lifestyle and legacy goals, it was recommended they increase spending slightly from $75,000 to $85,000 per year, allowing more funds for travel and experiences while maintaining their inheritance goal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:06">[00:08:06]</a>. Stress-testing confirmed they could maintain this spending even in adverse market conditions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:24">[00:08:24]</a>.
*   **Outcome:** Robert and Susan felt confident in enjoying a higher quality of life while still leaving a meaningful inheritance, striking the right balance between current enjoyment and long-term goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:35">[00:08:35]</a>.

> [!tip] Easing into Spending
> Clients with larger portfolios often find it harder to spend more money, due to very strict savings habits developed to accumulate wealth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:34">[00:06:34]</a>. To overcome this, ease into spending by setting small, meaningful goals like short trips or new hobbies, gradually shifting your mindset to enjoy retirement more <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:47">[00:06:47]</a>.

Many retirees, including those focused on legacy goals, struggle to enjoy their money <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:50">[00:08:50]</a>. It's crucial not to let fear hold you back from enjoying retirement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:04">[00:09:04]</a>.

## Key Tax Credit Adjustments for 2025

### Canada Child Benefit (CCB)

The Canada Child Benefit (CCB) has seen increases for 2025 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:04">00:07:04</a>:
*   Approximately $8,000 per child under six <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:07">00:07:07</a>.
*   Around $6,700 per child aged 6 to 17 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:09">00:07:09</a>.

### Medical Expense Tax Credit (METC)

The federal threshold for the Medical Expense Tax Credit has been updated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:15">00:07:15</a>. This adjustment allows taxpayers to claim a slightly higher amount for medical expenses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:18">00:07:18</a>. For a comprehensive list of eligible expenses and thresholds, individuals should consult the Government of Canada website <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:22">00:07:22</a>.

### Age Amount Credit

The Age Amount Credit is a non-refundable tax credit specifically for Canadians aged 65 and older <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:00">00:10:00</a>. It is designed to reduce federal income tax for eligible seniors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:02">00:10:02</a>.

For the 2025 tax year, the maximum Age Amount has increased to $9,928 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:07">00:10:07</a>, up from $8,790 in 2024 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:11">00:10:11</a>.

Eligibility for the full credit:
*   Individuals with a net income below $45,522 are eligible for the full credit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:14">00:10:14</a>.

Reduction and elimination:
*   The credit is reduced by 15% of net income exceeding the threshold <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:20">00:10:20</a>.
*   It is fully eliminated when net income reaches approximately $100,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:23">00:10:23</a>.

## Mistakes to Avoid

### Investing Only in a Savings Account
A common mistake is [[investing_vs_saving_in_a_tfsa | investing]] only in a savings account within a TFSA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:30">[00:00:30]</a>. For example, starting with $95,000 and contributing $7,000 annually at a 2% interest rate would take 56 years to reach $1 million, with $487,000 contributed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:32">[00:00:32]</a>. This strategy is too slow; if started at age 25, one would be 81 by the time the goal is reached <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:01">[00:01:01]</a>. A survey indicates that 47% of Canadian TFSA holders only have cash and savings, meaning many may never reach their million-dollar goal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:09">[00:01:09]</a>.

### Taking on Too Much Risk
On the opposite end, taking on excessive [[risks_and_challenges_of_unconventional_tfsa_investments | risk]], such as betting on very high-risk crypto coins, is also problematic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:26">[00:01:26]</a>. While a theoretical 100% annual return could achieve $1 million in 3-4 years with $120,000 invested <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:36">[00:01:36]</a>, the vast majority of people trying this strategy will not achieve such returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:00">[00:02:00]</a>. The chances of losing all money are extremely high, making the strategy worthless for most <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:06">[00:02:06]</a>. As Warren Buffett advises, "Rule number one: Never lose money. Rule number two: Never forget rule number one," emphasizing the importance of avoiding losses over seeking spectacular gains <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:23">[00:02:23]</a>.

> "Warren Buffett's famous quote rule number one never lose money rule number two never forget rule number one emphasizes the import of avoiding losses rather than striving for spectacular gains in Investments" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:22">[00:02:22]</a>

## Finding the Middle Ground

Successful [[compounding_and_investment_growth_in_a_tfsa | investment growth]] involves balancing risk and return, much like choosing a driving road where a highway is faster but carries higher risks than a slower back road <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:48">[00:02:48]</a>. Higher returns often come with higher risks, while safer investments offer steadier but lower returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:05">[00:03:05]</a>. The key is to find a middle ground that balances risk and return <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:46">[00:02:46]</a>.

### Understanding Risk Tolerance
To begin, assess your personal [[investment_risk_management_in_tfsa_accounts | risk tolerance]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:29">[00:03:29]</a>. This can be done by completing a free online questionnaire <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:31">[00:03:31]</a>. Once determined, select investments that align with your risk profile and contribute regularly to your TFSA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:37">[00:03:37]</a>.

## Realistic Strategies for a $1 Million TFSA

Here are two realistic strategies that balance risk and return for reaching a $1 million TFSA:

### High Risk Tolerance: 100% Equities
For those with a high [[investment_risk_management_in_tfsa_accounts | risk tolerance]] who wish to invest 100% in stocks or equities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:51">[00:03:51]</a>:
*   **Assumed Return**: Based on the Canadian stock market's previous 20-year return of 7.8% per year, an average return of 7% is used <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:56">[00:03:56]</a>.
*   **Timeline**: Starting with $95,000 and contributing $7,000 annually, it would take 25 years to reach $1 million <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:06">[00:04:06]</a>.
*   **Total Contribution**: You would have invested $270,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:19">[00:04:19]</a>.
*   **Age Example**: Starting at age 40, one could reach the goal by [[role_of_tfsa_in_retirement_planning | retirement]] age 65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:24">[00:04:24]</a>.

### Medium Risk Tolerance: 50% Equities, 50% Bonds
For those with a medium [[investment_risk_management_in_tfsa_accounts | risk tolerance]] opting for a balanced portfolio of 50% equities and 50% bonds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:31">[00:04:31]</a>:
*   **Assumed Return**: Combining equities with bonds (which have seen 2-3% annual returns over 20 years) yields an assumed return of 4.5% per year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:37">[00:04:37]</a>.
*   **Timeline**: Starting with $95,000 and contributing $7,000 annually, it would take about 35 years to reach $1 million <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:48">[00:04:48]</a>.
*   **Total Contribution**: You would have contributed $340,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:58">[00:04:58]</a>.
*   **Age Example**: Starting at age 30, this strategy could reach the goal by [[role_of_tfsa_in_retirement_planning | retirement]] age 65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:02">[00:05:02]</a>.

The time it takes to reach the goal depends on your risk and return <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:20">[00:05:20]</a>. The optimal approach is to find a "sweet spot" that is not too risky, preventing loss of most of your money, yet provides enough return to reach your goal quickly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:24">[00:05:24]</a>.

## Investment Options for Your TFSA

Consider these investment options to help achieve your million-dollar goal:

### Avoid Mutual Funds at Big Banks
While convenient, mutual funds offered by big banks often come with very [[avoiding_high_fees_in_tfsa_investments | high fees]], sometimes exceeding 3% per year, which significantly reduces [[compounding_and_investment_growth_in_a_tfsa | investment gains]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:04">[00:06:04]</a>.

### Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs)
ETFs are a cost-effective choice that mirror the performance of a market index, allowing you to earn close to the market's return <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:22">[00:06:22]</a>. Popular providers like Vanguard and iShares offer very low fees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:33">[00:06:33]</a>. However, ETFs are subject to market volatility and require basic knowledge of trading as they are bought and sold on stock exchanges <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:40">[00:06:40]</a>.

### Individual Stocks
[[investing_vs_saving_in_a_tfsa | Investing]] in individual stocks offers ultimate control and the lowest fees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:51">[00:06:51]</a>. A dividend stock strategy can provide both capital appreciation potential and regular income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:00">[00:07:00]</a>. This approach requires significant market knowledge, time for portfolio management, and diversification to mitigate [[investment_risk_management_in_tfsa_accounts | risk]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:13">[00:07:13]</a>.

### Robo-Advisors
Robo-advisors are automated platforms that manage investments using algorithms, aligning with personal financial goals and [[investment_risk_management_in_tfsa_accounts | risk tolerance]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:27">[00:07:27]</a>. Companies like Wealthsimple and Questwealth are popular Canadian options <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:37">[00:07:37]</a>. They offer convenience, simplicity, and a hands-off approach, accessible even with small initial investments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:43">[00:07:43]</a>. However, they lack the personal touch of a human advisor and may not capture unique investment opportunities or tailor strategies for changing financial circumstances <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:00">[00:08:00]</a>.

### Portfolio Managers
For those who find managing investments overwhelming or prefer personalized guidance, hiring a portfolio manager is an excellent option <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:21">[00:08:21]</a>. Traditionally for the wealthy, this service is now more accessible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:31">[00:08:31]</a>. A professional manager can tailor your portfolio to specific needs and [[investment_risk_management_in_tfsa_accounts | risk tolerance]], optimizing chances for success without daily effort <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:36">[00:08:36]</a>.

## Using TFSA in Retirement Planning

A crucial aspect of a successful TFSA strategy is knowing how to manage withdrawals in [[using_tfsa_in_retirement_planning | retirement]] to maximize tax savings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:00">[00:09:00]</a>.

### TFSA vs. RSP Withdrawals
The decision to withdraw from a TFSA or RSP first is not one-size-fits-all <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:07">[00:09:07]</a>.
*   **RSP First**: Withdrawing from your RSP earlier in [[using_tfsa_in_retirement_planning | retirement]] can be beneficial as it's taxable income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:17">[00:09:17]</a>. This can reduce your overall tax burden later, especially to avoid Old Age Security (OAS) clawbacks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:22">[00:09:22]</a>.
*   **TFSA Later**: TFSA withdrawals are not taxable, allowing investments to continue [[compounding_and_investment_growth_in_a_tfsa | growing tax-free]] and providing a tax-efficient source of funds that doesn't impact OAS <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:34">[00:09:34]</a>.

### Bucket Strategy for a $1 Million TFSA in Retirement
To ensure a substantial TFSA lasts and continues to grow in [[using_tfsa_in_retirement_planning | retirement]], consider a bucket strategy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:47">[00:09:47]</a>:
*   **Short-term Funds**: Keep in safer investments like bonds or GICs for immediate expenses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:57">[00:09:57]</a>.
*   **Medium-term Funds**: Place in balanced funds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:03">[00:10:03]</a>.
*   **Long-term Funds**: Maintain in equities to ensure growth against inflation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:06">[00:10:06]</a>.

### Managing TFSA with CPP
A strategic approach is to use your TFSA to supplement regular Canada Pension Plan (CPP) income during years when other income is lower <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:19">[00:10:19]</a>. Additionally, consider delaying CPP withdrawals while drawing from your TFSA if you retire early and need funds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:31">[00:10:31]</a>. This allows government benefits to grow, maximizing income later in [[using_tfsa_in_retirement_planning | retirement]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:40">[00:10:40]</a>.

Achieving a $1 million TFSA is attainable with a smart strategy that properly manages [[investment_risk_management_in_tfsa_accounts | risk and return]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:48">[00:10:48]</a>. The more challenging aspect often lies in figuring out the optimal tax strategy for your TFSA once it's built up <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:54">[00:10:54]</a>.

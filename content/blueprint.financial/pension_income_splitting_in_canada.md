[[pension_income_splitting | Pension income splitting]] is a tax strategy available to retired Canadians, allowing them to reduce their overall household tax burden by transferring a portion of their pension income to a spouse. This approach can lead to significant tax savings, but it is often overlooked by eligible individuals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:24:00">[01:24:00]</a>.

## What is Pension Income Splitting?
[[pension_income_splitting | Pension income splitting]] allows a retired individual to transfer up to 50% of their eligible pension income to their spouse, provided the spouse is in a lower tax bracket <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:00">[01:08:00]</a>. This transfer effectively lowers the total household tax bill <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:00">[01:17:00]</a>.

## Benefits
By utilizing [[pension_income_splitting | pension income splitting]], couples can save thousands of dollars, resulting in less money lost to taxes and more remaining in their pockets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:00">[01:10:00]</a>. Despite its potential for massive savings, many retirees do not take advantage of it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:24:00">[01:24:00]</a>.

## Why it's Overlooked
Many retirees fail to claim [[pension_income_splitting | pension income splitting]] either due to a lack of awareness or the incorrect assumption that it is an automatic process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:26:00">[01:26:00]</a>.

## How to Claim
To claim [[pension_income_splitting | pension income splitting]], individuals must fill out Form T1032 and submit it with their tax return each year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:31:00">[01:31:00]</a>.

## The Rising Tide of Food Insecurity
The [[cost_of_living_crisis_in_canada | cost of living crisis]] is significantly impacting [[Canadians]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:19:35">[05:19:35]</a>. According to the 2025 Food Price Report, 45% of [[Canadians]] are struggling to cover day-to-day costs, with food being a major contributing factor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:26:38">[05:26:38]</a>. The situation is so severe that 28% of [[Canadians]] report eating less food, rather than simply switching brands <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:34:06">[05:34:06]</a>. This is considered a "serious red flag" for a country as wealthy as Canada <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:38:39">[05:38:39]</a>.

This isn't merely about budgeting to get ahead; it's about budgeting to survive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:46:17">[05:46:17]</a>. Expenses are outpacing even the most intelligent financial plans and incomes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:58:08">[05:58:08]</a>. While the projected 3% to 5% food price increase in 2025 might not seem massive, its impact on stress and lifestyle is significant <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:03:00">[06:03:00]</a>. Incomes are generally not rising at the same rate, causing the gap between earnings and expenses to widen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:09:59">[06:09:59]</a>.

### Stark Statistics
Food insecurity is a growing crisis in Canada <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:14:04">[07:14:04]</a>:
*   Over 2 million food bank visits were recorded in March 2024, marking a 90% jump since 2019 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:17:09">[07:17:09]</a>.
*   Nearly half of Gen Z [[Canadians]] have had to dip into savings or borrow money just to afford food <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:26:24">[07:26:24]</a>.
*   48% of [[Canadians]] are actively searching for discounts, not just to save money, but to cope with rising costs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:31:51">[07:31:51]</a>.
*   More [[Canadians]] are skipping meals, cutting non-essential purchases, or settling for lower quality food <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:43:08">[07:43:08]</a>. A concerning 28% are eating less to make ends meet <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:48:47">[07:48:47]</a>.

## Evolving Shopping Habits and Challenges
The escalating food costs have led to shifts in shopping behavior among [[Canadians]]. Many are adopting [[strategies_to_manage_personal_finances_in_canada | strategies to manage personal finances in Canada]], such as:
*   Bulk buying <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:50:07">[05:50:07]</a>
*   Comparing flyers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:51:00">[05:51:00]</a>
*   Cutting impulse spending <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:52:00">[05:52:00]</a>
*   Cooking more creatively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:11:00">[08:11:00]</a>
*   Reducing food waste <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:12:00">[08:12:00]</a>

Brand loyalty is diminishing as affordability becomes the primary factor in purchasing decisions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:03:04">[08:03:04]</a>. Younger [[Canadians]] are particularly affected, but even middle-income households are feeling the financial strain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:14:00">[08:14:00]</a>.

### The Luxury of Steak
Beef prices rose by 9.2% in 2024 and continue to climb <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:33:00">[08:33:00]</a>. This is largely due to Canada having its smallest cattle herd since 1987, leading to less supply and higher prices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:40:00">[08:40:00]</a>. Consequently, steak is becoming a luxury item <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:32:00">[08:32:00]</a>, prompting many [[Canadians]] to substitute beef with more affordable options like chicken, pork, or lentils <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:50:00">[08:50:00]</a>. This shift not only impacts meal choices but also cultural traditions associated with meat <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:03:00">[09:03:00]</a>.

### Boycott Efforts Against Grocery Giants
In 2024, [[Canadians]] attempted a boycott against Loblaws due to rising prices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:06:00">[09:06:00]</a>. While the boycott generated attention, it did not significantly impact the company's operations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:14:00">[09:14:00]</a>. However, it symbolized growing frustration and a feeling of powerlessness among consumers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:16:00">[09:16:00]</a>. Trust in large grocers is low, leading shoppers to explore smaller stores, compare prices, and even revisit farmers' markets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:21:00">[09:21:00]</a>. Despite these efforts, major chains still wield considerable power, leading to continued public frustration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:27:00">[09:27:00]</a>.

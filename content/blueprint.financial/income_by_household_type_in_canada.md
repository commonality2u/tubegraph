## Median Incomes by Household Type (2022)

### Couple Families
Couple families in Canada demonstrate a significant financial advantage, with a median income exceeding $110,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:10">[00:06:10]</a>. This higher income is typically attributed to having two sources of income, which aids in managing common living expenses such as mortgages, childcare, and savings with greater ease <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:18">[00:06:18]</a>. Dual-income households generally exhibit greater financial stability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:48">[00:10:48]</a>.

### Lone Parent Families
Lone parent families often encounter more significant financial challenges <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:28">[00:06:28]</a>. Their median income was $58,800 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:31">[00:06:31]</a>. Being the sole breadwinner means that every expense can feel heavier, and balancing work, childcare, and other responsibilities on a single income frequently leads to financial stress, making it difficult to save for the future or manage unexpected costs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:34">[00:06:34]</a>.

### Individuals Not in Census Families
Individuals not part of census families face even tougher financial situations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:50">[00:06:50]</a>. Their median income was $35,790 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:54">[00:06:54]</a>. Without the support of a family or other non-relatives, managing expenses like rent and healthcare can be particularly challenging <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:59">[00:06:59]</a>. This highlights the comparative ease of navigating financial hurdles when there are multiple incomes to rely on <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:06">[00:07:06]</a>.

The data reveals a considerable disparity in income among different household types <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:12">[00:07:12]</a>. For couples, the median income can be roughly double that of a single-income household <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:52">[00:10:52]</a>. It's also noted that divorce can be a significant detriment to wealth and income in Canada <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:57">[00:10:57]</a>.

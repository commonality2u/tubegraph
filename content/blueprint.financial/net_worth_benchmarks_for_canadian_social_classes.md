## Understanding Net Worth vs. Income

While income is often seen as a key financial indicator, net worth is what truly determines financial freedom <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:29">[00:02:29]</a>. Net worth is calculated as the total value of what you own (assets like cash, investments, property) minus what you owe (debts like mortgages, loans, and credit cards) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:31">[00:02:31]</a>. Someone earning a high income but spending it all may be worse off than someone with a lower income who invests wisely <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:41">[00:02:41]</a>. Tracking net worth helps to understand one's financial position and make adjustments to reach financial goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:54">[00:02:54]</a>.

> [!TIP] Global Perspective
> A single person earning $30,000 in Canada is considered to be in the high-income group worldwide, translating to about $56 per day in purchasing power parity dollars <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:33">[00:05:33]</a>. This perspective can help reframe financial challenges and motivate smarter decisions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:04">[00:06:04]</a>.

## Strategies for Moving Up Social Classes

Here are some general [[strategies_for_increasing_net_worth_across_different_social_classes | strategies]] to optimize wealth and move up the social classes, based on current financial position:

### Lower Class
*   **Key Focus:** Reduce debt and build a financial cushion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:26">[00:12:26]</a>.
*   **Steps:**
    *   Prioritize paying off high-interest debt (credit cards, personal loans) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:31">[00:12:31]</a>.
    *   Start an emergency fund of at least three months of expenses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:36">[00:12:36]</a>.
    *   Contribute small amounts to a TFSA or RRSP to begin benefiting from tax-free gains <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:40">[00:12:40]</a>.
    *   Look for ways to increase income, such as career advancement, side hustles, or certifications <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:47">[00:12:47]</a>.

### Lower Middle Class
*   **Key Focus:** Debt reduction and smarter investing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:55">[00:12:55]</a>.
*   **Steps:**
    *   Maximize TFSA and RRSP contributions to leverage tax benefits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:00">[00:13:00]</a>.
    *   Reduce reliance on debt by paying off non-mortgage liabilities (credit cards, auto loans) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:04">[00:13:04]</a>.
    *   Invest consistently in diversified assets like ETFs, index funds, or robo-advisors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:09">[00:13:09]</a>.
    *   Consider generating additional income through side businesses or upskilling for a better job <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:16">[00:13:16]</a>.

### Middle Class

## TFSA vs. RSP Withdrawals First

A common question in [[role_of_tfsa_in_retirement_planning|retirement planning]] is whether to withdraw from your [[taxfree_savings_account_tfsa_and_withdrawal_strategies|TFSA]] or [[optimizing_tfsa_rrsp_and_fhsa_accounts|RSP]] first <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:07">[00:09:07]</a>. There isn't a single answer that fits all, as it depends on individual circumstances <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:12">[00:09:12]</a>.

*   **Withdrawing from your [[optimizing_tfsa_rrsp_and_fhsa_accounts|RSP]] first** can be advantageous because [[optimizing_tfsa_rrsp_and_fhsa_accounts|RSP]] withdrawals are considered taxable income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:18">[00:09:18]</a>. By drawing down your [[optimizing_tfsa_rrsp_and_fhsa_accounts|RSP]] earlier in retirement, you might reduce your overall tax burden later, especially if you anticipate receiving Old Age Security (OAS), which can be subject to clawbacks based on your income level <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:22">[00:09:22]</a>.
*   **Withdrawing from your [[taxfree_savings_account_tfsa_and_withdrawal_strategies|TFSA]]** means those withdrawals are not taxable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:34">[00:09:34]</a>. This provides flexibility, allowing your [[taxfree_savings_account_tfsa_and_withdrawal_strategies|TFSA]] investments to continue growing tax-free and offering a tax-efficient source of funds that does not impact your OAS eligibility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:36">[00:09:36]</a>.

## Strategies for a Million-Dollar TFSA in Retirement

With a substantial [[taxfree_savings_account_tfsa_and_withdrawal_strategies|TFSA]] balance, the goal is to ensure it lasts and continues to grow <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:48">[00:09:48]</a>.

### Bucket Strategy

Consider implementing a bucket strategy, dividing your [[taxfree_savings_account_tfsa_and_withdrawal_strategies|TFSA]] into portions to cover different time horizons <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:52">[00:09:52]</a>:

*   **Short-term funds** can be held in safer investments, such as bonds or GICs, to cover immediate expenses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:57">[00:09:57]</a>.
*   **Medium-term funds** can be allocated to balanced funds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:03">[00:10:03]</a>.
*   **Long-term funds** can remain invested in equities to ensure continued growth and protection against inflation over time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:06">[00:10:06]</a>.

This approach ensures funds are available when needed while still working to generate returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:10">[00:10:10]</a>.

## Integrating TFSA with CPP

A strategic approach is essential for managing your [[taxfree_savings_account_tfsa_and_withdrawal_strategies|TFSA]] alongside your Canada Pension Plan (CPP) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:17">[00:10:17]</a>.

Since CPP provides regular income, you can use your [[taxfree_savings_account_tfsa_and_withdrawal_strategies|TFSA]] to supplement these amounts, particularly during years when other income is lower <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:22">[00:10:22]</a>. Additionally, consider delaying CPP withdrawals while simultaneously drawing from your [[taxfree_savings_account_tfsa_and_withdrawal_strategies|TFSA]] if you retire early and require funds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:31">[00:10:31]</a>. This strategy allows your government benefits to grow, thereby maximizing your income later in retirement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:38">[00:10:38]</a>.

Figuring out the optimal tax strategy for your [[taxfree_savings_account_tfsa_and_withdrawal_strategies|TFSA]] once it's built up is a key challenge <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:55">[00:10:55]</a>. Understanding how to align [[taxfree_savings_account_tfsa_and_withdrawal_strategies|TFSA]] withdrawals with your CPP and [[optimizing_tfsa_rrsp_and_fhsa_accounts|RSP]] can significantly reduce your tax burden <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:01">[00:11:01]</a>.

## Provincial Income Statistics (2022)

A snapshot of average and median incomes across various Canadian provinces for 2022 reveals a range of earnings, with provinces ranked from highest to lowest median income:

*   **Alberta** leads with a median income of $45,600 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:09">[00:05:09]</a>. This is attributed to its strong energy and resource sectors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:11">[00:05:11]</a>. Alberta also has the highest average income at $61,500 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:07">[00:10:07]</a>.
*   **Quebec**, **Saskatchewan**, and **British Columbia** follow closely, each reporting a median income of $43,900 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:18">[00:05:18]</a>, indicative of their solid economies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:21">[00:05:21]</a>.
*   **Ontario** has a median income of $42,700 and an average income of $58,700 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:29">[00:05:29]</a>. This difference highlights a significant [[income_disparity_and_challenges_in_canada | disparity]] between high and low-income jobs within its diverse job market <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:37">[00:05:37]</a>.
*   **Eastern provinces** such as Prince Edward Island, New Brunswick, Newfoundland, and Nova Scotia show lower median incomes, ranging from $38,000 to $40,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:46">[00:05:46]</a>. These areas generally have smaller economies and a higher prevalence of lower-wage jobs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:52">[00:05:52]</a>.

## Leveraging Data to Increase Income

Understanding these regional differences can guide financial decisions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:49">[00:09:49]</a>. If flexible about location, relocating to a province with higher wages and a lower cost of living can significantly boost take-home pay and savings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:56">[00:09:56]</a>. Alberta, for example, combines high wages with lower living costs, enabling greater savings and investment opportunities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:10">[00:10:10]</a>.

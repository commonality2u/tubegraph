## Why Net Worth is More Important Than Income

While many focus on income as a key financial indicator, [[net_worth_differences_by_household_type_in_canada | net worth]] is what truly determines financial freedom <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:29">[00:02:29]</a>. [[net_worth_differences_by_household_type_in_canada | Net worth]] is the total value of assets (cash, investments, property) minus liabilities (debts like mortgages, loans, credit cards) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:31">[00:02:31]</a>. For example, someone earning $250,000 annually but spending it all may be worse off in the long run than someone earning $80,000 who invests wisely <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:41">[00:02:41]</a>.

Tracking [[net_worth_differences_by_household_type_in_canada | net worth]] helps in understanding financial position and making adjustments to reach financial goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:54">[00:02:54]</a>.

## Global Outlook on Wealth

It can be helpful to take a global perspective when considering personal net worth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:28">[00:05:28]</a>. ## Net Worth Classes in Canada

### Lower Class (Bottom 20%)

This group has a median net worth of $12,800 as of 2023 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:44">[00:00:44]</a>. In Q2 of 2024, their average net worth was $2,112, meaning debt outweighed assets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:49">[00:00:49]</a>.

**Net Worth Growth Over Time:**
*   **2012:** $1,400 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:58">[00:00:58]</a>
*   **2016:** ~$3,000, remaining unchanged until 2023 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:01">[00:01:01]</a>
*   **2023:** $12,800 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:06">[00:01:06]</a>

This represents a nearly four times increase since 2012, with most growth occurring after 2019, likely due to higher wages, pandemic savings, and rising asset values <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:09">[00:01:09]</a>.

**Financial Reality:**
Individuals in this bracket may include young professionals with student debt, recent immigrants adjusting to Canada's job market, or low-income earners struggling to cover essentials <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:26">[00:01:26]</a>. They might also be facing financial hardship from job loss or unexpected expenses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:35">[00:01:35]</a>.

**Main Challenges:**
*   **High Debt:** Average liabilities are $88,914, including over $44,000 in mortgage debt and $44,000 in other liabilities (credit cards, loans, lines of credit) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:42">[00:01:42]</a>.
*   [[impact_of_real_estate_on_canadian_net_worth | Limited Home Ownership]]: Real estate assets average just under $50,000, leaving very little to no home equity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:54">[00:01:54]</a>.
*   **Minimal Investments:** Financial assets (pensions, life insurance, savings) total about $21,000, which is insufficient for building long-term wealth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:01">[00:02:01]</a>.
*   [[financial_characteristics_and_challenges_of_lower_class_in_canada | Living Paycheck to Paycheck]]: Many rely on credit to manage shortfalls, leading to job insecurity and financial stress <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:12">[00:02:12]</a>.

**Main Challenges:**
The main challenge for this group is breaking out of debt cycles and optimizing investment strategies to build long-term wealth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:53">[00:04:53]</a>. ## Strategies to Move Up

Building your [[net_worth_differences_by_household_type_in_canada | net worth]] is about strategy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:40">[00:14:40]</a>. Here are strategies tailored to each class:

### Lower Class
*   **Key Focus:** Reduce debt and build a financial cushion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:27">[00:12:27]</a>.
*   **Steps:**
    *   Prioritize paying off high-interest debt (credit cards, personal loans) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:31">[00:12:31]</a>.
    *   Start an emergency fund of at least three months of expenses to avoid further debt <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:36">[00:12:36]</a>.
    *   Contribute small amounts to a TFSA or RRSP to begin benefiting from tax-free gains <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:40">[00:12:40]</a>.
    *   Look for ways to [[high_paying_professions_in_canada | increase income]] through career advancement, side hustles, or certifications/training <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:47">[00:12:47]</a>.

### Lower Middle Class
*   **Key Focus:** Debt reduction and smarter investing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:55">[00:12:55]</a>.
*   **Steps:**
    *   Maximize TFSA and RRSP contributions for tax benefits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:00">[00:13:00]</a>.
    *   Reduce reliance on debt by paying off non-mortgage liabilities (credit cards, auto loans) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:05">[00:13:05]</a>.
    *   Invest consistently in diversified assets (ETFs, index funds, robo-advisors) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:09">[00:13:09]</a>.
    *   Consider making money in other ways, such as side businesses or upskilling for better jobs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:17">[00:13:17]</a>.

### Middle Class
*   **Key Focus:** Wealth optimization and diversification <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:22">[00:13:22]</a>.

    *   Diversify beyond [[impact_of_real_estate_on_canadian_net_worth | real estate]] by investing more in stocks, ETFs, and alternative assets to avoid overexposure to the housing market <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:33">[00:13:33]</a>.

## Why Net Worth Matters More Than Income

While many focus on income as a primary financial indicator, net worth is what truly determines financial freedom <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:25">[00:02:25]</a>. Net worth is calculated as the total value of assets (cash, investments, property) minus liabilities (mortgages, loans, credit cards) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:31">[00:02:31]</a>. Someone earning $250,000 annually but spending it all may be worse off than someone making $80,000 who invests wisely <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:41">[00:02:41]</a>. Tracking net worth helps individuals understand their financial position and make necessary adjustments to reach their goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:54">[00:02:54]</a>.

## Canadian Household Net Worth by Class

### Lower Class (Bottom 20%)

The lower class, comprising the bottom 20% of households, had a median net worth of $12,800 as of 2023 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:42">[00:00:42]</a>. In Q2 2024, their average net worth was $2,112, indicating that debt often outweighed assets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:47">[00:00:47]</a>.

*   **[[Net worth growth trends in Canada | Net Worth Growth]]**: Their net worth started at $1,400 in 2012, rose to about $3,000 in 2016, and significantly jumped to $12,800 in 2023, nearly quadrupling since 2012 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:56">[00:00:56]</a>. Most of this growth occurred after 2019, influenced by higher wages, pandemic savings, and rising asset values <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:14">[00:01:14]</a>.
*   **Financial Reality**: This bracket often includes young professionals with student debt, recent immigrants adjusting to the job market, low-income earners, or individuals facing financial hardship <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:26">[00:01:26]</a>.
    *   **High Debt**: Average liabilities are $88,914, including over $44,000 in mortgage debt and $44,000 in other liabilities like credit cards and loans <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:42">[00:01:42]</a>.
    *   **Limited Home Ownership**: Real estate assets average just under $50,000, leaving minimal to no home equity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:54">[00:01:54]</a>.
    *   **Minimal Investments**: Financial assets (pensions, life insurance, savings) total only about $21,000, which is insufficient for long-term wealth building <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:01">[00:02:01]</a>.
    *   **Paycheck to Paycheck**: Many rely on credit, leading to job insecurity and financial stress <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:11">[00:02:11]</a>.

*   **[[Net worth growth trends in Canada | Net Worth Growth]]**: In 2012, their net worth was $72,100, growing gradually until it surged to $174,300 in 2023, showing over two times growth since 2012 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:39">[00:03:39]</a>.

    *   **Growing Home Ownership**: Real estate assets average $169,000, but high mortgage balances (around $98,000) mean much of their net worth is tied to home equity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:15">[00:04:15]</a>.

Growth is often slow due to conservative strategies or inconsistent contributions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:10">[00:04:10]</a>.
    *   **Significant Non-Mortgage Debt**: They hold about $37,000 in liabilities like credit cards, lines of credit, and auto loans, reducing cash flow <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:41">[00:04:41]</a>.
    *   **Challenge**: Breaking out of debt cycles and optimizing investment strategies is key <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:53">[00:04:53]</a>. Building net worth involves strategy, regardless of one's current financial position <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:35">[00:14:35]</a>.

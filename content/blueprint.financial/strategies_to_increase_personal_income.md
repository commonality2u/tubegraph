## Overall Income Trends (2018-2022)

Income data for a single person across Canada from 2018 to 2022 shows:
*   The average income increased from $54,600 in 2018 to $57,400 in 2022 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:39">[00:00:39]</a>.
*   The median income rose from $41,000 in 2018 to $44,000 in 2021, before dipping slightly in 2022 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:50">[00:00:50]</a>.

### Understanding Average vs. Median Income

It's important to distinguish between average and median income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:08">[00:03:08]</a>. The average can be skewed by a few very high earners (outliers), while the median provides a clearer picture of what's typical <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:28">[00:03:28]</a>.

> For example, if five friends each make $100,000 a year, and one person (like Connor McDavid) makes $20 million, the average salary for the group jumps to over $4 million <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:31">[00:02:31]</a>. However, the median salary, which is the middle number when all salaries are lined up, remains at $100,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:08">[00:03:08]</a>. The median income is a more accurate representation of what most people are actually making <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:20">[00:03:20]</a>.

## Income Breakdown by Demographics (2022)

### Income by Age Group
Median income shifts as Canadians age:
*   **25-34 years:** Median income is $48,700 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:16">[00:01:16]</a>. This is typical for those building experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:24">[00:01:24]</a>.
*   **35-44 years:** Median income jumps to $60,300, marking peak earning years for many <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:29">[00:01:29]</a>.
*   **45-54 years:** Median income sees a slight increase to $60,400, reflecting years of experience and higher-level positions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:41">[00:01:41]</a>.
*   **55-64 years:** Median income starts to decline to $48,900, signaling a transition toward retirement with potential reduced working hours <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:53">[00:01:53]</a>.
*   **65 and older:** Median income drops further to $34,636 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:05">[00:02:05]</a>.

### Income by Gender
In 2022, a noticeable income gap existed between men and women in Canada:
*   **Men:** Average income of $66,000, with a median income of $49,800 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:46">[00:03:46]</a>.
*   **Women:** Average income of $48,500, with a median of $37,700 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:55">[00:03:55]</a>.
    *   This represents a 36% higher average and 32% higher median income for men <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:02">[00:04:02]</a>.

Factors contributing to the gender income gap include:
*   Men often work in higher-paying fields (finance, engineering), while women are more likely in lower-paying sectors (education) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:09">[00:04:09]</a>.
*   Men tend to work more full-time hours and have fewer career interruptions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:24">[00:04:24]</a>.
*   Systemic barriers like biases in hiring and promotions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:31">[00:04:31]</a>.
*   Men are more likely to negotiate for higher pay, and a lack of salary transparency can perpetuate the gap <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:41">[00:04:41]</a>.

*   **Quebec, Saskatchewan, British Columbia:** Follow closely with a median income of $43,900 each <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:16">[00:05:16]</a>.

*   **Eastern Provinces (PEI, New Brunswick, Newfoundland, Nova Scotia):** Have lower median incomes ranging from $38,000 to $40,000, typically due to smaller economies and more lower-wage jobs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:41">[00:05:41]</a>.

## [[Strategies for increasing income in Canada | Strategies to Increase Personal Income]]

Understanding these numbers provides actionable [[strategies for increasing personal net worth using data | strategies for increasing personal net worth using data]]. Here are key takeaways to boost your earnings:

*   **Relocate Strategically:** If flexible, consider moving to a province with higher wages and a lower cost of living. Alberta stands out with the highest average income ($61,500) and a combination of high wages and lower living costs, allowing for greater savings and investment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:56">[00:09:56]</a>.
*   **Choose Your Industry Wisely:** Management, engineering, finance, and healthcare offer some of the highest wages. Consider training for these fields if you are in a lower-paying job <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:19">[00:10:19]</a>.
*   **Invest in Education:** Higher education directly correlates with higher earnings. A university degree can significantly boost your income compared to lower levels of education <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:33">[00:10:33]</a>.
*   **Consider Household Income:** Dual-income households have much greater financial stability, with couples' median income nearly double that of single incomes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:45">[00:10:45]</a>. Divorce can be a significant "wealth and income killer" in Canada, so choosing your partner wisely is advised <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:57">[00:10:57]</a>.
*   **Address the Gender Pay Gap:** While there's no easy solution, women might consider negotiating pay more aggressively or targeting industries actively working to close this gap <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:04">[00:11:04]</a>.

## The Dangers of Comparison

It's natural to compare your income to others, but constantly measuring your success against others isn't healthy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:22">[00:11:22]</a>. Higher averages or medians can be misleading <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:30">[00:11:30]</a>. Instead, focus on your own financial journey and developing habits that lead to personal financial success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:35">[00:11:35]</a>. Financial success is not just about a large paycheck, but also fulfillment, balance, and achieving personal goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:41">[00:11:41]</a>.

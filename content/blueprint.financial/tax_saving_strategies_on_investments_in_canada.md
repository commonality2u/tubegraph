## Registered Accounts: TFSA, RRSP, and FHSA

Utilizing registered accounts like the Tax-Free Savings Account (TFSA) and Registered Retirement Savings Plan (RRSP) is crucial for maximizing wealth over time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:37">[00:00:37]</a>.

Consider Mark, a 30-year-old with $30,000 to invest, splitting it evenly across a TFSA, RRSP, and a non-registered account ($10,000 in each) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:48">[00:00:48]</a>. After 30 years, with a 7% annual return, a 40% working tax rate, and a 20% retirement tax rate, the outcomes differ significantly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:59">[00:00:59]</a>:

*   **TFSA:** Mark pays 40% tax upfront, leaving $6,000 to invest <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:14">[00:01:14]</a>. After 30 years of tax-free growth, it is worth $45,733, with no tax on withdrawals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:17">[00:01:17]</a>.
*   **RRSP:** Mark invests the full $10,000 tax-free upfront <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:28">[00:01:28]</a>. After 30 years, the account grows to $76,123, and after a 20% retirement tax on withdrawal, it yields $60,899 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:31">[00:01:31]</a>.
*   **Non-Registered Account:** Mark invests the full $10,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:45">[00:01:45]</a>. Similar to the TFSA, annual taxes on returns reduce growth, leaving $31,385 after 30 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:49">[00:01:49]</a>.

[[taxefficient_investment_strategies_in_canada | Tax-advantaged accounts]] like the TFSA and RRSP significantly outperform non-registered taxable accounts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:58">[00:01:58]</a>. The TFSA can provide 45% more, while the RRSP nearly doubles the value due to tax-free or tax-deferred growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:04">[00:02:04]</a>.

For first-time home buyers, the new First Home Savings Account (FHSA) combines the tax benefits of both a TFSA and an RRSP, offering powerful wealth-building potential <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:15">[00:02:15]</a>.

## Tax-Efficient Investment Choices for Each Account

Knowing what to invest in each account is as crucial as contributing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:26">[00:02:26]</a>.

### Tax-Free Savings Account (TFSA)
The TFSA is a "Powerhouse for tax-free compounding" but requires careful planning <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:37">[00:02:37]</a>.
*   **Avoid:** US dividend stocks and Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs) due to a 15% withholding tax that cuts into returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:40">[00:02:40]</a>.
*   **Recommend:** Canadian or non-dividend paying international stocks or ETFs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:48">[00:02:48]</a>.

### Registered Retirement Savings Plan (RRSP)
RRSPs are excellent for deferring taxes on income-heavy assets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:54">[00:02:54]</a>.
*   **Recommend:** They are the best place for US dividend stocks and ETFs, as there is no withholding tax in this account <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:58">[00:02:58]</a>.
*   **Caution:** Withdrawals are taxed as regular income, so strategic planning is essential, especially as [[effective_strategies_for_canadian_retirees_to_manage_retirement_savings | retirement]] approaches <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:04">[00:03:04]</a>.

### Non-Registered Accounts
These accounts offer flexibility but are the least tax-efficient <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:10">[00:03:10]</a>.
*   **Recommend:**
    *   Investments with favorable tax treatment, such as Canadian dividend stocks, which benefit from the dividend tax credit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:15">[00:03:15]</a>.
    *   Non-dividend paying stocks, like Berkshire Hathaway, where growth comes from capital gains instead of taxable dividends, allowing for tax deferral until sold <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:24">[00:03:24]</a>.
*   **Avoid:** Interest-heavy investments like bonds or GICs, as they are fully taxed at your marginal rate, making them less suitable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:33">[00:03:33]</a>.

Beyond TFSA and RRSP, other tax-advantaged accounts like the Registered Disability Savings Plan (RDSP) also exist, offering further opportunities for tax savings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:43">[00:03:43]</a>.

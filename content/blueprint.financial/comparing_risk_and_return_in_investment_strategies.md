## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Investing in Only a Savings Account
Relying solely on a savings account for your TFSA investments is a significant drawback <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:30">[00:00:30]</a>. If you begin with $95,000 and contribute $7,000 annually, earning a low interest rate of 2%, it would take a staggering 56 years to reach $1 million, with total contributions amounting to $487,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:32">[00:00:32]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:36">[00:00:36]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:39">[00:00:39]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:43">[00:00:43]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:45">[00:00:45]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:52">[00:00:52]</a>. This strategy is too slow and costly, as starting at age 25 would mean reaching the goal at age 81 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:55">[00:00:55]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:58">[00:00:58]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:01">[00:01:01]</a>. A survey indicates that 47% of Canadian TFSA holders only hold cash and savings in their accounts, which suggests many may never reach their million-dollar goal even if they max out their TFSAs annually <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:07">[00:01:07]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:09">[00:01:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:11">[00:01:11]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:17">[00:01:17]</a>.

### Mistake 2: Taking on Too Much Risk
At the opposite end of the spectrum, taking on excessive risk is another common mistake <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:26">[00:01:26]</a>. While an investor might hypothetically double their money annually with high-risk crypto coins, reaching $1 million in 3 to 4 years with only $120,000 invested, this is an extremely poor strategy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:30">[00:01:30]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:36">[00:01:36]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:38">[00:01:38]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:41">[00:01:41]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:45">[00:01:45]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:51">[00:01:51]</a>. The vast majority of people attempting such high-risk, high-reward strategies will not achieve similar returns, and the chances of losing all capital are extremely high, rendering the strategy worthless <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:00">[00:02:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:06">[00:02:06]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:08">[00:02:08]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:10">[00:02:10]</a>. As Warren Buffett famously stated, "Rule number one: Never lose money. Rule number two: Never forget rule number one," emphasizing the importance of avoiding losses over seeking spectacular gains <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:22">[00:02:22]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:24">[00:02:24]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:26">[00:02:26]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:28">[00:02:28]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:31">[00:02:31]</a>.

## Sustainable Investment Strategies

The key to reaching a $1 million TFSA sustainably is to find a middle ground by balancing risk and return <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:40">[00:02:40]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:44">[00:02:44]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:46">[00:02:46]</a>. This means avoiding strategies that are too low-risk (taking too long) and too high-risk (high chance of capital loss) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:14">[00:03:14]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:17">[00:03:17]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:19">[00:03:19]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:20">[00:03:20]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:22">[00:03:22]</a>.

This can be assessed through free online questionnaires <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:31">[00:03:31]</a>. ### Examples of Balanced Strategies

*   **High Risk Tolerance (100% Equities)**: For those with a high [[managing_investment_risk_appropriately | risk tolerance]], investing 100% in equities (stocks) is an option <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:49">[00:03:49]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:51">[00:03:51]</a>. Based on a 7% average annual return (considering the Canadian stock market's 20-year return of 7.8%), starting with $95,000 and contributing $7,000 annually, it would take 25 years to reach $1 million, with total contributions of $270,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:56">[00:03:56]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:58">[00:03:58]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:00">[00:04:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:03">[00:04:03]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:06">[00:04:06]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:09">[00:04:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:11">[00:04:11]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:13">[00:04:13]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:16">[00:04:16]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:21">[00:04:21]</a>. This strategy allows someone starting at age 40 to reach the goal by age 65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:24">[00:04:24]</a>.

*   **Medium Risk Tolerance (50% Equities, 50% Bonds)**: For a medium [[managing_investment_risk_appropriately | risk tolerance]], a balanced portfolio of 50% equities and 50% bonds is suitable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:31">[00:04:31]</a>. Assuming an average annual return of 4.5% (combining 7% for equities and 2-3% for bonds), starting with $95,000 and contributing $7,000 annually, it would take about 35 years to reach $1 million, with total contributions of $340,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:35">[00:04:35]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:38">[00:04:38]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:41">[00:04:41]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:43">[00:04:43]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:45">[00:04:45]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:48">[00:04:48]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:51">[00:04:51]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:53">[00:04:53]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:57">[00:04:57]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:00">[00:05:00]</a>. This strategy requires starting by age 30 to reach the goal by age 65 and is attainable for most Canadians due to its medium risk level <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:03">[00:05:03]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:08">[00:05:08]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:11">[00:05:11]</a>.

The time it takes to reach your goal is directly tied to your chosen risk and return profile <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:20">[00:05:20]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:22">[00:05:22]</a>. The ideal approach is to find a "sweet spot" that is not excessively risky (to avoid losing capital) but still offers sufficient returns to achieve your goal within a reasonable timeframe <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:24">[00:05:24]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:26">[00:05:26]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:29">[00:05:29]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:31">[00:05:31]</a>.

*   **TFSA First**: TFSA withdrawals are not taxable, allowing investments to continue growing tax-free and providing a tax-efficient source of funds that doesn't affect your OAS <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:34">[00:09:34]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:36">[00:09:36]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:38">[00:09:38]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:40">[00:09:40]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:42">[00:09:42]</a>.

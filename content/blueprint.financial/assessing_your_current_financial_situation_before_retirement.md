A crucial first step before retiring is to assess your current financial situation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:47">[00:00:47]</a>. This means understanding exactly where you stand financially today <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:50">[00:00:50]</a>.

## What to Assess <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:50">[00:00:50]</a>
Think of this step like creating a financial selfie <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:28">[00:01:28]</a>. It involves tracking all your assets, liabilities, and income sources to get a clear snapshot of your financial standing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:21">[00:01:21]</a>.

### Example: Pam and Jim
For Pam and Jim, this step was revealing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:53">[00:00:53]</a>. They discovered they had substantial savings spread across various accounts, including:
*   RSPs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:02">[01:00:02]</a>
*   TFSAs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:03">[01:00:03]</a>
*   Non-registered accounts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:04">[01:00:04]</a>

Their data showed a steady increase in their total net worth as they approached their planned retirement age of 65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:05">[01:00:05]</a>. By age 65, they had an impressive combined net worth of over $1.7 million <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:15">[01:00:15]</a>.

## Tips for Assessment
If you diligently consolidate accounts and track spending like Jim and Pam, you are in good shape <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:30">[01:00:30]</a>. [[financial_planning_for_retirement | Financial planning for retirement]] can be simplified by consolidating accounts, which also helps minimize fees and makes management much simpler <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:42">[01:00:42]</a>.

If you are unsure where to start, consider using tools such as Excel or Quicken to track your finances <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:37">[01:00:37]</a>.

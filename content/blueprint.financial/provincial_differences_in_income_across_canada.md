## Income by Province (2022)

*   **Alberta** leads the provinces with a median income of $45,600 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:09">[00:05:09]</a>. This is primarily attributed to its robust energy and resource sectors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:11">[00:05:11]</a>.
*   **Quebec**, **Saskatchewan**, and **British Columbia** follow closely, each reporting a median income of $43,900 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:21">[00:05:21]</a>, indicative of their solid economies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:23">[00:05:23]</a>.
*   **Ontario** features a diverse job market with a median income of $42,700 and a much higher average income of $58,700 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:33">[00:05:33]</a>. This disparity highlights a significant gap between high and low-income jobs within the province <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:37">[00:05:37]</a>.
*   The **Eastern provinces**, including Prince Edward Island, New Brunswick, Newfoundland, and Nova Scotia, generally have lower median incomes, ranging from $38,000 to $40,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:49">[00:05:49]</a>. These regions typically have smaller economies and a higher prevalence of lower-wage jobs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:52">[00:05:52]</a>.

Income can vary significantly depending on where one lives in Canada <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:58">[00:05:58]</a>.

## Leveraging Provincial Income Data

Understanding these provincial differences can help individuals make informed financial decisions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:49">[00:09:49]</a>. If geographical flexibility is possible, relocating to a province with higher wages and a lower cost of living could substantially increase disposable income and savings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:03">[00:10:03]</a>. For instance, Alberta is highlighted for its combination of high wages and lower living costs, offering opportunities for greater savings and investments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:14">[00:10:14]</a>.

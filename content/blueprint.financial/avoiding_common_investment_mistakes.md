## The Peril of Only Saving

A common mistake is to save money without investing it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:15">[00:00:15]</a>.
*   **Low Growth Larry** saved $2,000 a year in a TD savings account earning 0.01% interest <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:27">[00:00:27]</a>. After 30 years, contributing $60,000, he ended up with just $60,093 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:35">[00:00:35]</a>. His money barely grew and lost real value due to inflation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:41">[00:00:41]</a>.
*   In contrast, **Steady Investor Sally** invested $7,000 a year, earning an annualized 7% return over 30 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:45">[00:00:45]</a>. She contributed $210,000 but her investments grew to $777,000, outpacing Larry by over $100,000 despite contributing far less <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:53">[00:00:53]</a>. This demonstrates the power of compounding returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:11">[00:01:11]</a>.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. Investing Without Purpose

One of the most significant mistakes is diving into investing simply because it feels like "something you should be doing" without a clear "why" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:25:00">[01:25:00]</a>.
*   Investing without purpose is compared to "setting sail without a destination or a compass" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:31:00">[01:31:00]</a>.
*   It's crucial to have a clear purpose, such as saving for a down payment, retirement, financial freedom, education, or supporting family <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:38:00">[01:38:00]</a>.
*   A specific and personal reason creates a stronger foundation for your investment journey <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:49:00">[01:49:00]</a>. Knowing your "why" makes it easier to stick to your plan, weather market fluctuations, and align decisions with long-term goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:24:00">[02:24:00]</a>.

### 2. Panic Selling During Market Downturns

A major mistake is panic selling when markets decline <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:09:00">[06:09:00]</a>.
*   Remaining invested during market downturns is crucial for long-term growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:14:00">[06:14:00]</a>.
*   A $10,000 investment in the S&P 500 from 2004 to 2024 grew at an annualized 10.5% if fully invested, even through the 2008 financial crisis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:17:00">[06:17:00]</a>.
*   However, missing just the 10 best days reduced returns to 6.2%, and missing the 30 best days dropped them to 1.4% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:28:00">[06:28:00]</a>.
*   Panic selling often results in missing key recovery days <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:36:00">[06:36:00]</a>.
*   As Peter Lynch noted, many believe they are long-term investors until markets start to fall, revealing a lower actual risk tolerance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:08:00">[05:08:00]</a>.

### 3. Staying on the Sidelines

Waiting for the "perfect time" to invest is a losing strategy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:44:00">[06:44:00]</a>.
*   Even investing at the worst possible time often outperforms simply keeping cash <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:48:00">[06:48:00]</a>.
*   It's better to start with the basics, learn as you go, and avoid overthinking the market <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:50:00">[06:50:00]</a>.

### 4. Overlooking Tax-Efficient Accounts

Not choosing the right investment account can significantly impact your after-tax returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:58:00">[06:58:00]</a>. In Canada, accounts like the TFSA (Tax-Free Savings Account), RRSP (Registered Retirement Savings Plan), and FHSA (First Home Savings Account) offer unique tax benefits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:02:00">[07:02:00]</a>.
*   A $10,000 investment over 30 years at 6% growth can yield vastly different after-tax balances depending on the account type <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:06:00">[07:06:00]</a>:
    *   **RRSP**: $64,327 (with 40% current tax rate and 20% future tax rate) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:24:00">[07:24:00]</a>. Best when retirement tax rate is significantly lower than current <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:34:00">[07:34:00]</a>.
    *   **TFSA**: $57,434 (entirely tax-free) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:40:00">[07:40:00]</a>. Ideal for flexibility and tax-free withdrawals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:47:00">[07:47:00]</a>.
    *   **Non-registered account**: Only $28,896 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:51:00">[07:51:00]</a>. Annual taxation severely limits compounding growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:55:00">[07:55:00]</a>.

### 5. Neglecting Sneaky Fees or Taxes

It's important to monitor your investments and regularly check for hidden fees or taxes, as they can "quietly chip away at your returns" if left unattended <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:52:00">[09:52:00]</a>.

### 6. Failing to Learn from Mistakes

Everyone makes mistakes in investing, whether it's panic selling during a dip or staying in cash too long <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:40:00">[10:40:00]</a>.
*   What truly matters is learning from these moments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:47:00">[10:47:00]</a>.
*   Adjust your strategy, focus on long-term goals, and avoid letting short-term emotions derail your plan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:49:00">[10:49:00]</a>. The more you learn, the better you become at sticking to your strategy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:55:00">[10:55:00]</a>.

### 7. Not Seeking Expert Help When Needed

While many can manage their investments independently, life can get busy, or you might not achieve the desired results <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:01:00">[11:01:00]</a>.
*   If managing your own investments feels overwhelming, hiring a portfolio manager can be a valuable solution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:10:00">[11:10:00]</a>.
*   The key is to find an unbiased expert who focuses on growing your money, rather than just selling products for high commissions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:18:00">[11:18:00]</a>.
*   Be sure to interview several managers to ensure their approach aligns with your investment philosophy and ask about fees and services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:33:00">[11:33:00]</a>.

Regardless of whether you manage your investments yourself or hire an expert, the most important step is to simply get started <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:41:00">[11:41:00]</a>.

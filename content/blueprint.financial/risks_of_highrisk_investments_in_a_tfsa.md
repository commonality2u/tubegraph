From: [[blueprint.financial]] <br/> 

Making overly risky investments within a Tax-Free Savings Account (TFSA) can significantly hinder wealth growth and lead to irreversible losses of contribution room <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:38:00">[03:38:00]</a>. While the TFSA offers tax-free growth, it does not mitigate the inherent risks of volatile assets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:58:00">[03:58:00]</a>.

## Types of High-Risk Investments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:41:00">[03:41:00]</a>

High-risk investments often pursued in a TFSA include:
*   Meme stocks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:51:00">[03:51:00]</a>
*   Cryptocurrencies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:53:00">[03:53:00]</a>
*   Penny stocks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:53:00">[03:53:00]</a>

These types of investments are pursued by those eager to achieve rapid, substantial gains, hoping to "double their money overnight" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:56:00">[03:56:00]</a>.

## Why This Approach Backfires <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:58:00">[03:58:00]</a>

High-risk investments are characterized by extreme volatility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:03:00">[04:03:00]</a>. Even if an investor manages to double their money, the discipline to "cash out and move it into something safer" is often lacking, leading to continued exposure to even greater risks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:07:00">[04:07:00]</a>. The more an investor gambles, the greater the chance of significant losses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:15:00">[04:15:00]</a>. As Charlie Munger, Warren Buffett's longtime business partner, stated, "The big money is not in the buying and selling but in the waiting" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:22:00">[04:22:00]</a>.

## Consequences of Losses in a TFSA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:47:00">[04:47:00]</a>

One of the most severe consequences of high-risk investments in a TFSA is the impact of losses:
*   **No tax claim** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:50:00">[04:50:00]</a>: Unlike non-registered accounts, investment losses within a TFSA cannot be claimed on taxes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:50:00">[04:50:00]</a>.
*   **Lost contribution room** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:52:00">[04:52:00]</a>: If an investment loses value, the associated contribution room is permanently lost <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:54:00">[04:54:00]</a>. For example, if $10,000 is invested and drops by 50% to $5,000, that $5,000 of lost value in contribution room is gone forever, reducing future tax-free growth potential <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:40:00">[04:40:00]</a>. This means instead of building wealth, an investor can dig themselves into a financial hole <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:01:00">[05:01:00]</a>.

## Recommendation for TFSA Investing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:03:00">[05:03:00]</a>

For securing financial future, a strategy that balances risk with reward is essential <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:34:00">[03:34:00]</a>. While taking some risks is acceptable, it's advised not to "swing for the fences with everything" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:09:00">[05:09:00]</a>. Slow, steady, and consistent growth is considered the smartest way to build real wealth over time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:11:00">[05:11:00]</a>. Understanding and managing [[understanding_investment_risks_and_returns_in_a_tfsa | investment risks]] is crucial.

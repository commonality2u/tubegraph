## Average vs. Median Income: A Clarification

When discussing income, it's important to distinguish between average and median figures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:00">[00:03:00]</a>.
*   **Average income** can be significantly skewed by a few very high earners or outliers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:00">[00:03:00]</a>. For example, if five friends each make $100,000 annually, and one person like Connor McDavid, earning $20 million, is added to the group, the average salary for the group would jump to over $4 million, which doesn't reflect what most in the group earn <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:31">[00:02:31]</a>. This is a key point when considering the [[comparison_of_median_and_average_incomes_in_canadas_top_earners | comparison of median and average incomes in Canada's top earners]].
*   **Median income** is the middle number when all salaries are lined up in order <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:08">[00:03:08]</a>. It provides a much clearer and more accurate picture of what is typical for most people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:31">[00:03:31]</a>.

*   **55 to 64 years:** Median income starts to decline to $48,900 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:53">[00:01:53]</a>. This typically signals a transition toward retirement, potentially with reduced working hours or a shift to less demanding roles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:57">[00:01:57]</a>.
*   **65 years and older:** Median income drops further to $34,636 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:05">[00:02:05]</a>. This highlights [[the_reality_of_retiree_income_in_canada | the reality of retiree income in Canada]].

## Gender Income Gap in Canada (2022)

In 2022, there was a noticeable [[gender_income_gap_in_canada | gender income gap in Canada]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:40">[00:03:40]</a>:
*   **Men:** Earned an average of $66,000, with a median income of $49,800 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:46">[00:03:46]</a>.
*   **Women:** Earned an average of $48,500, with a median income of $37,700 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:54">[00:03:54]</a>.

This represents a 36% higher average and 32% higher median income for men <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:01">[00:04:01]</a>. Several factors contribute to this gap <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:06">[00:04:06]</a>:
*   **Occupational Choice:** Men often work in higher-paying fields like finance and engineering, while women are more likely to be in lower-paying sectors such as education <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:11">[00:04:11]</a>.
*   **Working Hours & Interruptions:** Men tend to work more full-time hours and experience fewer career interruptions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:23">[00:04:23]</a>.
*   **Systemic Barriers:** Biases in hiring and promotions can make it harder for women to advance and increase earnings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:31">[00:04:31]</a>.
*   **Negotiation:** Men are more likely to negotiate for higher pay, and a lack of salary transparency can perpetuate the gap <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:40">[00:04:40]</a>.

## How to Use This Data to Increase Your Income

Understanding these income statistics can guide smarter financial decisions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:44">[00:09:44]</a>:

*   **Relocate Strategically:** If flexible, moving to a province with higher wages and a lower cost of living can significantly increase take-home pay <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:56">[00:09:56]</a>. Alberta stands out with a combination of high average income ($61,500) and lower living costs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:05">[00:10:05]</a>.
*   **Choose Your Industry Wisely:** Management, engineering, finance, and healthcare offer some of the highest wages. Consider training for these fields if possible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:19">[00:10:19]</a>.
*   **Invest in Education:** Higher education clearly leads to higher earnings; a university degree can significantly boost income compared to lower levels of education <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:33">[00:10:33]</a>.
*   **Consider Household Income:** Dual-income households have much greater financial stability, with median income for couples being nearly double that of single incomes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:45">[00:10:45]</a>. Divorce can also be a significant drain on wealth and income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:57">[00:10:57]</a>.
*   **Address the Gender Pay Gap:** While there's no easy solution, women might consider negotiating pay more aggressively or targeting industries actively working to close the gap <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:04">[00:11:04]</a>.

## The Dangers of Comparison

While income data provides valuable insights, constantly comparing your success to others can be unhealthy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:22">[00:11:22]</a>. Averages and medians can be misleading when applied to individual circumstances <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:33">[00:11:33]</a>. Instead, focus on your own financial journey and developing habits that lead to personal financial success, which encompasses fulfillment, balance, and hitting your own goals, not just a big paycheck <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:35">[00:11:35]</a>.

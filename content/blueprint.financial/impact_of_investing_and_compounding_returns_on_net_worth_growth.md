## Investing and Compounding Returns

Upon reaching the $100,000 mark, [[Building Wealth|building wealth]] becomes significantly easier due to the power of compounding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:11">[00:01:11]</a>.

### The Power of Compounding Illustrated

Consider a scenario where $10,000 is invested annually with a 10% annual return in tax-free accounts like a TFSA or RSP, aiming for $1 million <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:20">[00:01:20]</a>.

The time it takes to reach subsequent $100,000 milestones drastically decreases:
*   **0 to $100,000**: This initial milestone takes the longest, requiring a little over 7 years (specifically 7.27 years) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:37">[00:01:37]</a>. Progress at this stage is primarily driven by disciplined contributions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:47">[00:01:47]</a>.
*   **Second $100,000 ($100,000 to $200,000)**: Takes 4.25 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:52">[00:01:52]</a>.
*   **Third $100,000 ($200,000 to $300,000)**: Takes approximately 3 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:59">[00:01:59]</a>.
*   **Fourth $100,000 ($300,000 to $400,000)**: Takes 2.34 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:02">[00:02:02]</a>.
*   **Fifth $100,000 ($400,000 to $500,000)**: Takes less than 2 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:04">[00:02:04]</a>. At this point, the invested money begins doing a significant amount of the heavy lifting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:09">[00:02:09]</a>.
*   **Sixth $100,000 ($500,000 to $600,000)**: Takes 1.62 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:13">[00:02:13]</a>.
*   **Seventh $100,000 ($600,000 to $700,000)**: Takes 1.4 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:16">[00:02:16]</a>.
*   **Eighth $100,000 ($700,000 to $800,000)**: Takes 1.24 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:19">[00:02:19]</a>.
*   **Ninth $100,000 ($800,000 to $900,000)**: Takes 1.11 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:22">[00:02:22]</a>.
*   **Final $100,000 ($900,000 to $1 million)**: Takes just one year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:26">[00:02:26]</a>.

The total time to reach $1 million with this strategy is about 25 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:49">[00:02:49]</a>. This demonstrates how investments generate substantial growth, allowing for rapid achievement of financial milestones once a base is established <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:31">[00:02:31]</a>.

### Contributions vs. Investment Returns

As [[Building Wealth|wealth]] grows, the balance between personal savings and growth from investment returns shifts dramatically <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:57">[00:02:57]</a>:
*   **First $100,000**: Approximately 73% comes from personal contributions, with only 27% from investment returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:02">[00:03:02]</a>.
*   **Reaching $1 million**: The situation completely flips. Now, 75% of the wealth is produced by returns, and only 25% comes from direct contributions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:11">[00:03:11]</a>.

A significant tipping point occurs around $300,000, where returns begin to outpace contributions, indicating that compounding is fully engaging <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:21">[00:03:21]</a>. By the time an individual reaches $1 million, their investments are doing triple the work of their contributions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:33">[00:03:33]</a>. This effectively means that money begins to work for the individual, accelerating growth at every step <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:38">[00:03:38]</a>.

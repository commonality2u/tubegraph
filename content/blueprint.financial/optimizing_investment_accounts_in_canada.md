## Choosing Between the Big Three: FHSA, TFSA, and RRSP

The choice among the FHSA, [[retirement_tax_strategies_in_canada | RRSP]], and [[investment_strategies_for_tfsa | TFSA]] is a common struggle for many <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:29">[00:00:29]</a>. [[choosing_the_right_investment_accounts | Choosing the right investment accounts]] depends on your financial goals, income, and need for flexibility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:11">[00:02:11]</a>.

### First Pick: The FHSA

The FHSA (First Home Savings Account) is an excellent starting point for those saving for their first home <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:38">[00:00:38]</a>. It offers a unique combination of benefits:
*   **[[tax_efficient_investment_strategies_in_canada | Tax-deductible contributions]]** similar to an [[retirement_tax_strategies_in_canada | RRSP]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:43">[00:00:43]</a>.
*   **[[tax_efficient_investment_strategies_in_canada | Tax-free withdrawals]]** for a home purchase, like a [[investment_strategies_for_tfsa | TFSA]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:46">[00:00:46]</a>.
*   **Tax-free growth** on investments held within the account <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:04">[00:01:04]</a>.

Even if buying a home isn't an immediate plan, the FHSA remains a smart option due to its flexibility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:56">[00:00:56]</a>. Funds can be easily transferred to an [[retirement_tax_strategies_in_canada | RRSP]] if your goals change <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:07">[00:01:07]</a>.

**Pro Tip: Income Splitting**
The FHSA can be used for a form of income splitting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:19">[00:01:19]</a>. A higher-income spouse can gift up to $8,000 annually to a lower-income spouse, who can then contribute it to their FHSA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:22">[00:01:22]</a>. The growth remains tax-free, and the lower-income spouse receives a [[tax_strategies_for_investments_in_canada | tax deduction]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:31">[00:01:31]</a>.

**Considerations:**
The main drawback of the FHSA is the permanent loss of contribution room if withdrawals are made for non-qualifying purposes (e.g., buying a car) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:45">[00:01:45]</a>. For emergencies, a [[investment_strategies_for_tfsa | TFSA]] is generally a better choice <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:59">[00:01:59]</a>.

### TFSA vs. RRSP: Which to Prioritize?

The choice between a [[investment_strategies_for_tfsa | TFSA]] and an [[retirement_tax_strategies_in_canada | RRSP]] hinges on your income goals and need for flexibility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:11">[00:02:11]</a>.

#### The TFSA: Flexibility and Simplicity

The [[investment_strategies_for_tfsa | TFSA]] is renowned for its flexibility, allowing tax-free withdrawals at any time for both short-term goals and long-term investments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:21">[00:02:21]</a>.
*   **Key Point 1: Flexibility** - Funds can be withdrawn tax-free without penalties, making it ideal for emergency funds, vacations, or large purchases <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:42">[00:02:42]</a>.
*   **Key Point 2: Simplicity** - There are no complex withdrawal rules or [[tax_strategies_for_investments_in_canada | tax implications]] as long as contribution limits are respected <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:30">[00:02:30]</a>. You can contribute, withdraw, and recontribute the next year within your room <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:06">[00:03:06]</a>.

**Prioritize [[investment_strategies_for_tfsa | TFSA]] When:**
*   You need easy access to your funds without penalty <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:15">[00:03:15]</a>.
*   Saving for short-term goals (e.g., vacations, big purchases, emergencies) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:18">[00:03:18]</a>.
*   You are in a lower-income year, reducing the benefit of [[retirement_tax_strategies_in_canada | RRSP]] [[tax_strategies_for_investments_in_canada | tax deductions]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:22">[00:03:22]</a>.
*   You desire a simple way to grow [[investment_options_for_tfsa | investments]] tax-free without locking in funds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:28">[00:03:28]</a>.

#### The RRSP: Long-Term Retirement Savings

The [[retirement_tax_strategies_in_canada | RRSP]] is designed for long-term retirement savings, providing [[tax_strategies_for_investments_in_canada | tax deductions]] in the present while investments grow tax-free until retirement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:42">[00:03:42]</a>.

**Prioritize [[retirement_tax_strategies_in_canada | RRSP]] When:**
*   You are a higher-income earner and can benefit from [[tax_strategies_for_investments_in_canada | tax deductions]] to reduce taxable income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:58">[00:03:58]</a>.
*   Your employer offers [[tax_strategies_for_investments_in_canada | RRSP matching contributions]], which is considered "free money" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:51">[00:03:51]</a>.
*   You can use [[retirement_tax_strategies_in_canada | RRSP]] contributions to qualify for government benefits like the Canada Child Benefit or GST/HST credits by controlling your income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:04">[00:04:04]</a>.

#### Summary: TFSA vs. RRSP Prioritization

*   Choose [[investment_strategies_for_tfsa | TFSA]] first if: you need flexibility, are in a lower-income year, or want a simple tax-free way to save for both short and long-term goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:17">[00:04:17]</a>.
*   Choose [[retirement_tax_strategies_in_canada | RRSP]] first if: you have a higher income and want immediate [[tax_strategies_for_investments_in_canada | tax savings]], or if your employer offers [[retirement_tax_strategies_in_canada | RRSP]] matching <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:27">[00:04:27]</a>.

Overall, the FHSA is generally recommended first, followed by either the [[investment_strategies_for_tfsa | TFSA]] or [[retirement_tax_strategies_in_canada | RRSP]] depending on your situation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:37">[00:04:37]</a>. When in doubt, lean towards the [[investment_strategies_for_tfsa | TFSA]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:42">[00:04:42]</a>.

## Important Exceptions: Unlock Free Government Money

There are three critical exceptions that should be prioritized even before the FHSA, [[investment_strategies_for_tfsa | TFSA]], or [[retirement_tax_strategies_in_canada | RRSP]] due to their "free money" advantage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:47">[00:04:47]</a>.

### 1. RDSP (Registered Disability Savings Plan)

The RDSP provides significant support for Canadians with disabilities through substantial government grants and bonds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:03">[00:05:03]</a>.
*   **Canada Disability Savings Grant (CDSG):** Matches 300% on the first $500 contributed and 200% on the next $1,000, potentially yielding up to $3,500 per year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:13">[00:05:13]</a>.
*   **Canada Disability Savings Bond (CDSB):** Provides up to $1,000 annually without requiring any personal contributions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:28">[00:05:28]</a>.
*   **Example:** A $1,500 contribution can result in $3,500 from the CDSG and $1,000 from the CDSB, totaling $4,500 in government support annually <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:36">[00:05:36]</a>.

### 2. RESP (Registered Education Savings Plan)

RESPs help families save for post-secondary education with government matching grants <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:56">[00:05:56]</a>.
*   **Canada Education Savings Grant (CESG):** Matches 20% of contributions up to $500 per year per child, with a lifetime maximum of $7,200 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:02">[00:06:02]</a>.
*   **Example:** A $2,500 annual contribution yields a $500 grant from CESG, an instant 20% return on investment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:12">[00:06:12]</a>.
*   **Canada Learning Bond (CLB):** Offers up to $2,000 for lower-income families without requiring contributions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:26">[00:06:26]</a>.
*   Starting early maximizes growth due to compounding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:33">[00:06:33]</a>.

### 3. RRSP Employer Matching

If your employer offers [[retirement_tax_strategies_in_canada | RRSP]] matching, it's considered "free money" and should not be missed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:40">[00:06:40]</a>.
*   **Example:** If your employer matches 50% of contributions up to $1,500, a $3,000 contribution yields an instant 50% return <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:47">[00:06:47]</a>.
*   Employer matching not only accelerates retirement savings but also builds saving discipline <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:55">[00:06:55]</a>. Always maximize this if available <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:02">[00:07:02]</a>.

## Non-Registered Accounts: The Final Destination

After maximizing your FHSA, [[investment_strategies_for_tfsa | TFSA]], and [[retirement_tax_strategies_in_canada | RRSP]], non-registered accounts are the next step for growing wealth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:31">[00:07:31]</a>. While they lack the [[tax_efficient_investment_strategies_in_canada | tax advantages]] of registered accounts, careful asset selection can minimize taxes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:43">[00:07:43]</a>.

### Ideal Assets for Non-Registered Accounts

To minimize [[tax_strategies_for_investments_in_canada | taxes]], focus on assets that are taxed at lower rates or only upon sale:
*   **Growth Stocks with No Dividends:** Avoid interest income, which is taxed at the highest possible rate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:51">[00:07:51]</a>. Growth stocks like Shopify, Nvidia, and Amazon are ideal because capital gains are taxed at a lower rate and only when the shares are sold <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:56">[00:07:56]</a>.
*   **Canadian Dividend Stocks:** Take advantage of the Canadian dividend tax credit to lower taxes on eligible dividends <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:14">[00:08:14]</a>. For example, a $1,000 dividend is grossed up to $1,380, resulting in a 15% federal tax credit that reduces taxes by $27 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:23">[00:08:23]</a>, with provincial credits further assisting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:33">[00:08:33]</a>.

### Assets to Avoid in Non-Registered Accounts

*   **US Dividend-Paying Stocks:** These face withholding taxes and do not qualify for the Canadian dividend tax credit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:37">[00:08:37]</a>.

## Final Order of Investment Account Prioritization

To make the most of your savings and minimize taxes, consider the following order of priority for [[investment_account_prioritization_and_tax_efficiency | investment account prioritization and tax efficiency]]:

1.  **RDSP:** If you qualify, due to the most generous government grants for disability savings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:56">[00:08:56]</a>.
2.  **[[retirement_tax_strategies_in_canada | RRSP]] with Employer Matching:** Prioritize to take advantage of free employer contributions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:01">[00:09:01]</a>.
3.  **RESP:** Provides a 20% government match (up to $500 annually) for education savings, and can act as a form of income splitting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:07">[00:09:07]</a>.
4.  **FHSA:** Best for first-time home buyers with [[tax_efficient_investment_strategies_in_canada | tax-deductible contributions]] and [[tax_efficient_investment_strategies_in_canada | tax-free withdrawals]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:17">[00:09:17]</a>.
5.  **[[retirement_tax_strategies_in_canada | RRSP]] or [[investment_strategies_for_tfsa | TFSA]]**:
    *   [[retirement_tax_strategies_in_canada | RRSP]] for higher-income [[tax_strategies_for_investments_in_canada | tax deductions]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:26">[00:09:26]</a>.
    *   [[investment_strategies_for_tfsa | TFSA]] for flexibility and [[tax_efficient_investment_strategies_in_canada | tax-free growth]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:30">[00:09:30]</a>.
6.  **Non-Registered Accounts:** With a focus on growth stocks or Canadian dividend stocks for [[tax_efficient_investment_strategies_in_canada | tax efficiency]], and avoiding US dividend stocks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:34">[00:09:34]</a>.

Ultimately, the right [[choosing_investment_types_and_accounts | investment accounts]] depend on your personal financial goals, income, and saving needs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:44">[00:09:44]</a>.

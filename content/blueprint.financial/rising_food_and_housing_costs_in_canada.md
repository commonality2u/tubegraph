## Impact of Inflation on Everyday Costs

### Groceries and Food Prices
Canadians are increasingly frustrated by the [[impact_of_inflation_on_grocery_costs_in_canada | rising cost of everyday items]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:39">[00:00:39]</a>. David Olive, in an article for the Toronto Star, highlighted a significant increase in grocery costs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:44">[00:00:44]</a>:
*   In March 2021, a basket of 18 household items cost $82 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:49">[00:00:49]</a>.
*   By July 2024, the same basket cost $113.32 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:56">[00:00:56]</a>.
*   This represents a staggering 38.2% increase in just three years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:03">[00:01:03]</a>.

The latest Canada Food Price Report predicts that the average family of four will see an increase of about $711 in their annual food bill this year, bringing the total annual average to $16,295 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:06">[00:01:06]</a>. This follows a $1,065 jump in 2023 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:22">[00:01:22]</a>. Food prices have significantly outpaced the average rate of inflation in recent years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:26">[00:01:26]</a>.

### Rental and Housing Costs
Shelter costs are another major concern <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:32">[00:01:32]</a>:
*   In April 2021, the average rent for a two-bedroom apartment in Vancouver was $2,598 per month <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:35">[00:01:35]</a>.
*   By July 2024, this average had increased to $3,648 per month, a 40% rise <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:43">[00:01:43]</a>.

The cost of purchasing a home has also soared <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:52">[00:01:52]</a>:
*   In 2015, the average cost to buy a house in Canada was $413,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:56">[00:01:56]</a>.
*   By May 2024, this average had skyrocketed to $699,000, representing a 69.3% increase over nine years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:02">[00:02:02]</a>.

Beyond food and housing, other costs like transportation and entertainment have generally increased, with rampant inflation making it difficult for many Canadians to manage their bills <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:16">[00:02:16]</a>.

## Income Disparity and Financial Struggles
Unfortunately, [[income_disparity_and_challenges_in_canada | income growth]] has not kept pace with the [[cost_of_living_crisis_in_canada | rising cost of living]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:22">[00:03:22]</a>.
*   According to Stat Canada data from 2022, the median after-tax income for Canadian families and unattached individuals was $70,500 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:30">[00:03:30]</a>.
*   This marks a decrease from $73,000 in 2021 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:42">[00:03:42]</a>.
*   Average wages are not believed to have significantly increased in 2023 or 2024 to keep up with inflation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:45">[00:03:45]</a>.

The combination of rising grocery costs, household goods, rent, and housing has greatly outpaced income growth, making financial stability a challenge for many Canadians <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:59">[00:03:59]</a>. The discontinuation of pandemic-related government benefits and the return to pre-pandemic Employment Insurance programs have further strained finances <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:08">[00:04:08]</a>.

### Widespread Unhappiness and Migration Trends
Unhappiness is prevalent across Canada, particularly among younger generations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:14">[00:04:14]</a>. While the Gallup World Poll's latest World Happiness Report ranks Canada as the 15th happiest country overall, Canadians under the age of 30 fall to 58th place, highlighting a significant disparity in happiness levels among younger Canadians <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:20">[00:04:20]</a>.

Many Canadians are considering internal or external migration due to the high [[cost of living crisis in Canada | cost of living]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:37">[00:02:37]</a>:
*   Approximately 28% of all Canadians are contemplating moving to another province due to high housing costs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:38">[00:02:38]</a>.
*   Young adults and renters are most likely to consider such a move <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:44">[00:02:44]</a>.
*   This trend is particularly pronounced in Ontario and British Columbia, where high housing costs have persisted longer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:51">[00:02:51]</a>.
*   Alberta is a popular relocation spot, with 18% of Canadians considering a move there, attracted by more affordable housing and better job opportunities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:00">[00:03:00]</a>.
*   A recent report indicates that net interprovincial migration out of Toronto, Montreal, and Vancouver exceeded 130,000 people between 2022 and 2023 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:10">[00:03:10]</a>.

## Strategies to Combat the High Cost of Living

While many issues stem from uncontrollable economic factors, individuals can manage what they can control <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:46">[00:04:46]</a>. The two main approaches to address the [[impact_of_inflation_on_canadians_financial_struggles | financial struggles]] are to earn more money or spend less money <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:16">[00:05:16]</a>.

### Earning More Money

Strategies to boost earnings can be tailored to different income levels:

*   **For Lower Income Earners**:
    *   **Start a side hustle**: Consider a part-time job or freelance business like writing on platforms such as Upwork, driving for Uber, delivering for DoorDash, or starting an online store on Etsy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:39">[00:05:39]</a>.
    *   **Skill development**: Invest in learning new, in-demand skills through free or low-cost online courses on platforms like Coursera, LinkedIn Learning, or Udemy. Obtain certificates in fields such as digital marketing, or participate in government-subsidized training programs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:58">[00:05:58]</a>.
    *   **Job search**: Constantly search for better-paying job opportunities by regularly checking job boards like Indeed or Glassdoor, setting automatic alerts, networking on LinkedIn, attending job fairs, or joining professional groups <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:16">[00:06:16]</a>.

*   **For Middle Income Earners**:
    *   **Career advancement**: Seek more responsibilities, promotions, and negotiate for higher pay within your current company <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:35">[00:06:35]</a>.
    *   **Additional qualifications**: Obtain certificates or post-graduate degrees that can lead to higher-paying roles, ideally with employer support <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:43">[00:06:43]</a>.
    *   **Passive income**: If you have savings, explore opportunities like investments, rental properties, or creating digital products to generate ongoing revenue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:54">[00:06:54]</a>.

*   **For Higher Income Earners**:
    *   **Investments**: Make your money work for you by investing in stocks, real estate, or other higher-return opportunities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:07">[00:07:07]</a>.
    *   **Business ventures**: Consider starting or investing in businesses with high profitability potential <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:14">[00:07:14]</a>.
    *   **Consulting**: Leverage your expertise to offer consulting services in your field, which can command higher fees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:21">[00:07:21]</a>.

### Spending Less Money

Reducing expenses can significantly improve financial situations regardless of income level <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:30">[00:07:30]</a>.

*   **For Lower Income Earners**:
    *   **Government programs**: Utilize government assistance programs to reduce financial burdens, including services like food banks, emergency shelters, financial counseling, job training, and utility bill assistance (e.g., Salvation Army, YMCA, United Way) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:40">[00:07:40]</a>.
    *   **Strict budgeting**: Create and adhere to a budget, tracking expenses to identify areas for cutbacks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:01">[00:08:01]</a>.
    *   **Reduce "big three" spending**: Focus on reducing food, shelter, and transportation costs, as these are typically the largest household expenses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:06">[00:08:06]</a>.
    *   **Negotiate bills**: Negotiate rates for services like cable, internet, and phone to secure lower prices or better deals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:15">[00:08:15]</a>.

*   **For Middle Income Earners**:
    *   **Debt management**: Prioritize paying off high-interest debt to reduce monthly expenses and save money long-term <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:29">[00:08:29]</a>.
    *   **Smart shopping**: Use coupons, discounts, and cashback offers for everyday purchases <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:39">[00:08:39]</a>.
    *   **Evaluate lifestyle choices**: Identify areas to cut back, such as dining out less or choosing more affordable entertainment options <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:46">[00:08:46]</a>.

*   **For Higher Income Earners**:
    *   **Tax planning**: Work with a financial adviser or accountant to implement tax-saving strategies to reduce your overall tax burden <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:56">[00:08:56]</a>.
    *   **Mindful luxury spending**: Be aware of luxury spending and consider if it aligns with long-term financial goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:04">[00:09:04]</a>.
    *   **Automatic transfers**: Set up automatic transfers to savings and investment accounts to ensure consistent contributions and reduce the temptation to spend <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:10">[00:09:10]</a>.

### Alternative Housing and Lifestyle Solutions
Beyond traditional financial strategies, some out-of-the-box solutions exist for addressing the [[cost of living crisis in Canada | high cost of living]]:
*   **Tiny houses**: There has been a rise in tiny houses in Canada, which are compact homes ranging from 200 to 400 square feet, offering savings on housing costs and promoting a minimalist lifestyle. Examples include Mint Tiny House Company in Vancouver and Minimaliste Houses in Quebec <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:24">[00:09:24]</a>.
*   **Digital Nomads**: With the increase in remote work, individuals can live and work from anywhere, potentially reducing living expenses by moving to more affordable locations abroad, even temporarily, such as Thailand, Portugal, or Mexico <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:51">[00:09:51]</a>. All that is required is a reliable internet connection and careful planning <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:06">[00:10:06]</a>.

Despite the current challenges, Canadian core values and economy are still deemed worthy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:20">[00:10:20]</a>. There is hope that the government will implement effective strategies to control the [[cost_of_living_crisis_in_canada | cost of living]], and inflation has shown signs of slightly cooling off, potentially offering some relief in the near future <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:28">[00:10:28]</a>.

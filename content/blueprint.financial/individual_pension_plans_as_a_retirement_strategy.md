Individual Pension Plans (IPPs) are powerful tools used by wealthy Canadians to grow and protect their money, offering a more strategic way to save for retirement compared to traditional Registered Retirement Savings Plans (RRSPs) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:09">[00:00:09]</a>.

## What is an Individual Pension Plan (IPP)?

An Individual Pension Plan (IPP) is a pension plan tailored specifically for business owners or incorporated professionals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:34">[00:03:34]</a>. Unlike an RRSP, which has a set contribution cap, an IPP allows individuals, especially those over 40, to contribute more money while enjoying tax-deferred growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:43">[00:03:43]</a>. It is essentially a corporate-sponsored pension plan designed to provide structured [[strategies_for_extending_retirement_income | retirement income]], making it a preferred choice for high-income earners <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:53">[00:03:53]</a>.

## How an IPP Replaces RRSPs

While an RRSP functions similarly to a savings account, an IPP is designed as a personalized, structured, and powerful pension plan built for long-term security <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:06">[00:04:06]</a>. If you are a high-income earner looking to maximize [[retirement_savings_growth_strategies | retirement savings]], an IPP may be a more suitable fit than an RRSP <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:16">[00:04:16]</a>.

Key differences include:
*   **Reduced RRSP Contribution Ability**
    Once an IPP is established, the ability to contribute to an RRSP is reduced or eliminated because the IPP takes priority in pension contributions, making RRSPs less relevant for those using this strategy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:22">[00:04:22]</a>.
*   **Bigger Contributions**
    IPPs allow for significantly higher contributions than RRSPs, particularly as one gets older <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:37">[00:04:37]</a>. This enables individuals to defer more tax and grow [[retirement_savings_growth_strategies | retirement savings]] at a faster rate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:44">[00:04:44]</a>.
*   **Reliable Retirement Income**
    With an IPP, retirement withdrawals follow a structured, pension-like system, similar to a defined benefit pension plan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:50">[00:04:50]</a>. Unlike an RRSP, where withdrawal control is flexible, an IPP provides predictable income, aiding in more confident [[retirement_planning_strategies_to_navigate_pension_changes | retirement planning]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:56">[00:04:56]</a>.

## When an IPP Outperforms an RRSP

IPPs can be particularly advantageous in specific situations:

*   **High-Income Business Owner**
    For a 50-year-old business owner earning $250,000 annually who has maxed out RRSP contributions, an IPP allows for contributions exceeding $50,000 annually, far more than the 2024 RRSP cap of $31,560 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:13">[00:05:13]</a>. This strategy significantly boosts [[retirement_savings_growth_strategies | retirement savings]] and increases tax deductions, contributing to long-term [[strategies_for_financial_security_in_retirement | financial security]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:31">[00:05:31]</a>.
*   **Late Start to Retirement Savings**
    A 55-year-old professional who owns a corporation and hasn't saved enough for retirement can establish an IPP to make past service contributions, covering previous working years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:41">[00:05:41]</a>. This rapidly grows their retirement fund while simultaneously reducing the corporation's taxable income, maximizing both business and personal wealth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:55">[00:05:55]</a>.
*   **Corporate Tax Efficiency**
    An incorporated consultant in their 40s can transition from an RRSP to an IPP for a more tax-efficient strategy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:04">[00:06:04]</a>. Since IPP contributions are considered a corporate expense, the company's taxable income is reduced, leading to lower corporate taxes while securing a stable and secure retirement fund <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:12">[00:06:12]</a>.
*   **Client Success Story**
    One client, a doctor, was able to increase tax deductions by over 50% compared to his RRSP contributions after switching to an IPP, securing a steady pension for retirement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:26">[00:06:26]</a>.

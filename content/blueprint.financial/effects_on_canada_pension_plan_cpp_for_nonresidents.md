## How Your [[canada_pension_plan_cpp_and_its_international_considerations | CPP]] is Affected
The [[canada_pension_plan_cpp_and_its_international_considerations | CPP]] is based on contributions made during your working life in Canada <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:02">[00:06:02]</a>.

### Benefits When Moving Abroad
The good news is that your [[canada_pension_plan_cpp_and_its_international_considerations | CPP]] benefits will travel with you if you [[impact_of_moving_abroad_on_canadian_retirement_benefits | move abroad]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:11">[00:06:11]</a>. The amount you receive abroad will remain the exact same as if you lived in Canada <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:13">[00:06:13]</a>. Your [[canada_pension_plan_cpp_and_its_international_considerations | CPP]] will be paid the same amount regardless of where you retire <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:30">[00:06:30]</a>.

[[canada_pension_plan_cpp_and_its_international_considerations | CPP]] survivor benefits and Child Benefits will also continue to be paid even if the recipient lives abroad, ensuring your family benefits are secure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:15">[00:07:15]</a>.

### [[tax_implications_of_cpp_payments | Tax Implications]]
[[tax_implications_of_cpp_payments | CPP]] payments may be subject to withholding taxes depending on the tax treaty status of your new country of [[impact_of_residency_on_canadian_government_pensions | residence]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:34">[00:06:34]</a>.

For instance, if you [[impact_of_moving_abroad_on_canadian_retirement_benefits | move to the US]], a tax treaty is in place that can reduce or even eliminate the withholding tax on your [[canada_pension_plan_cpp_and_its_international_considerations | CPP]] benefits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:44">[00:06:44]</a>. However, without a tax treaty, you could face a 25% withholding tax on your payments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:51">[00:06:51]</a>. While the amount you receive from [[canada_pension_plan_cpp_and_its_international_considerations | CPP]] doesn't change, how it's taxed in your new country is crucial to understand to avoid surprises <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:49">[00:07:49]</a>.

### Social Security Agreements
Canada has social security agreements with over 50 countries to ensure your [[canada_pension_plan_cpp_and_its_international_considerations | CPP]] coverage continues uninterrupted and to avoid dual contributions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:57">[00:06:57]</a>. These agreements also coordinate [[pension_and_retiree_tax_changes_including_cpp_and_oas | pension]] benefits for those who've lived and worked abroad <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:06">[00:07:06]</a>.

### Receiving Payments
You have a couple of [[canadian_pension_plan_payment_options | options]] for receiving your [[canada_pension_plan_cpp_and_its_international_considerations | CPP]] payments while abroad <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:29">[00:07:29]</a>:
*   Deposited into a local bank account in the local currency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:32">[00:07:32]</a>.
*   Deposited into a Canadian bank account, which can then be transferred via a service like Wise to your new bank <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:34">[00:07:34]</a>.
*   Mailed as checks in Canadian dollars, though this is generally not recommended <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:44">[00:07:44]</a>.

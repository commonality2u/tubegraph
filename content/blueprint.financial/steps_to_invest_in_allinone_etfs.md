[[introduction_to_allinone_etfs | All-in-one ETFs]], also known as asset allocation ETFs, are designed to simplify investing by combining multiple ETFs into a single fund <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:28">[00:00:28]</a>. They provide instant diversification across stocks, bonds, and global markets, eliminating the need to pick individual investments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:37">[00:00:37]</a>. For example, Vanguard's VGRO offers exposure to over 13,000 companies worldwide through an 80% stock and 20% bond allocation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:03">[00:01:03]</a>.

## [[benefits_of_using_allinone_etfs | Benefits of All-in-One ETFs]]

Investors favor [[introduction_to_allinone_etfs | all-in-one ETFs]] for several key advantages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:26">[00:02:26]</a>:
*   **Low Cost** They offer a low-cost alternative to mutual funds, typically charging between 0.2% to 0.25% in management expense ratios (MER), significantly lower than the common 2% MER of traditional Canadian mutual funds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:28">[00:02:28]</a>.
*   **Auto-Rebalancing** These ETFs automatically rebalance their asset mix, ensuring the portfolio maintains its target stock-to-bond ratio without manual adjustments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:57">[00:02:57]</a>.
*   **Ideal for Smaller Accounts** For those starting with a [[investment_options_for_a_tfsa | TFSA]], RRSP, or other [[choosing_the_right_investment_accounts | investment account]], a single [[introduction_to_allinone_etfs | all-in-one ETF]] provides instant diversification without requiring investments across multiple funds or stocks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:10">[00:03:10]</a>.
*   **Diversification and Time-Saving** They offer a professionally managed portfolio with exposure to thousands of investments globally, saving investors from extensive research and stock/bond picking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:23">[00:03:23]</a>.

## Who Should Use All-in-One ETFs?

[[introduction_to_allinone_etfs | All-in-one ETFs]] are suitable for several investor profiles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:51">[00:03:51]</a>:
*   **Beginners** Those new to investing who want a simple, low-maintenance portfolio without the complexities of picking stocks or rebalancing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:56">[00:03:56]</a>.
*   **Busy Professionals** Individuals with demanding careers who lack the time to actively manage their investments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:10">[00:04:10]</a>.
*   **Parents** Those investing for their children's future in accounts like RESPs, seeking instant diversification and minimal maintenance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:33">[00:04:33]</a>.
*   **Small Account Investors** Investors starting with smaller accounts, such as a [[investment_options_for_a_tfsa | TFSA]] or RRSP, who desire broad diversification from the outset <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:46">[00:04:46]</a>.

## [[how_to_choose_the_right_allinone_etf | How to Choose the Right All-in-One ETF]]

When choosing an [[introduction_to_allinone_etfs | all-in-one ETF]], the most critical factor is selecting the appropriate asset allocation for your risk tolerance and investment goals, rather than focusing on the ETF brand <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:19">[00:05:19]</a>.

Canadian ETF providers like iShares (BlackRock), Vanguard, and BMO offer high-quality [[introduction_to_allinone_etfs | all-in-one ETFs]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:27">[00:05:27]</a>. These providers offer low-cost, diversified, and auto-rebalancing options <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:04">[00:06:04]</a>.

Consider your comfort level with risk to determine your ideal stock-to-bond ratio <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:11">[00:06:11]</a>:
*   **Higher Risk, Higher Returns** (e.g., VEQT, XEQT): 100% stocks, 0% bonds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:23">[00:06:23]</a>.
*   **Balanced Growth** (e.g., VGRO, XGRO): 80% stocks, 20% bonds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:31">[00:06:31]</a>.
*   **Lower Risk, Steady Growth** (e.g., VBAL, XBAL): 60% stocks, 40% bonds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:39">[00:06:39]</a>.
*   **Conservative, Stability-Focused** (e.g., VCNS, XCNS): 40% stocks, 60% bonds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:48">[00:06:48]</a>.

Your risk tolerance and investment goals should drive your decision, not the ETF provider, as long as fees are low <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:57">[00:06:57]</a>.

## How to Invest in All-in-One ETFs Step-by-Step

Here is a step-by-step guide to investing in [[introduction_to_allinone_etfs | all-in-one ETFs]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:11">[00:07:11]</a>:

### Step 1: Determine Your Risk Tolerance
Before purchasing, assess how much risk you are comfortable with. This helps you choose between aggressive or balanced ETFs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:19">[00:07:19]</a>. Tools like the Vanguard investor questionnaire can help you determine the right mix of stocks and bonds for your financial goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:31">[00:07:31]</a>.

### Step 2: Choose a Brokerage
You will need a brokerage account to buy your ETF <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:44">[00:07:44]</a>. Popular options in Canada include:
*   **Wealthsimple Trade**: Known for zero commission fees, ideal for beginners <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:50">[00:07:50]</a>.
*   **Questrade**: Also offers zero commission fees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:54">[00:07:54]</a>.
*   **Big Bank Platforms**: Such as TD Direct Investing or RBC Direct Investing, which offer convenience for existing bank customers but typically charge higher fees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:59">[00:07:59]</a>.

Ensure you open the correct type of account based on your [[investment_strategies_for_beginners_in_canada | investment goals]], such as a [[investment_options_for_a_tfsa | TFSA]], RRSP, or non-registered account <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:14">[00:08:14]</a>.

### Step 3: Buy the ETF
Once your account is set up, you can purchase your chosen [[introduction_to_allinone_etfs | all-in-one ETF]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:23">[00:08:23]</a>:
1.  Log into your brokerage account <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:31">[00:08:31]</a>.
2.  Search for the ETF by its ticker symbol (e.g., VGRO or ZBAL) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:33">[00:08:33]</a>.
3.  Enter the number of shares you wish to buy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:39">[00:08:39]</a>.
4.  Choose a market order (immediate purchase) or a limit order (set a specific price) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:42">[00:08:42]</a>.
5.  Confirm the purchase <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:48">[00:08:48]</a>.

### Optional Step: Set Up DRIP and Automatic Contributions
To further automate and compound your investments:
*   **Set up a DRIP (Dividend Reinvestment Plan)**: This automatically uses any dividends paid by the ETF to purchase more shares, rather than letting them sit as cash <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:58">[00:08:58]</a>. Most brokerages allow you to enable this feature <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:07">[00:09:07]</a>.
*   **Automate Contributions**: Set up automatic monthly contributions from your paycheck into your [[introduction_to_allinone_etfs | all-in-one ETF]] to ensure consistent investing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:17">[00:09:17]</a>.

## Is One Fund Enough?

Yes, [[introduction_to_allinone_etfs | all-in-one ETFs]] are designed to be standalone investments for each account <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:27">[00:09:27]</a>. Adding multiple funds can disrupt the carefully designed balance and asset allocation, potentially leading to more or less risk than intended <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:34">[00:09:34]</a>. For example, VGRO alone holds approximately 13,000 companies, providing ample diversification <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:33">[00:11:33]</a>.

However, you can use separate accounts for different [[alternative_investment_strategies_to_dividend_etfs | strategies]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:52">[00:09:52]</a>:
*   **[[investment_options_for_a_tfsa | TFSA]]**: Keep it simple with a single [[introduction_to_allinone_etfs | all-in-one ETF]] like VGRO for long-term tax-free growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:59">[00:09:59]</a>.
*   **RRSP**: Build your own stock portfolio if you enjoy picking individual stocks, while still maintaining a portion of your portfolio passively managed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:06">[00:10:06]</a>.
*   **Non-Registered Account**: Focus on tax-efficient ETFs, such as Horizon's swap-based ETFs, to minimize taxes on dividends <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:15">[00:10:15]</a>.

## [[common_mistakes_to_avoid_with_allinone_etfs | Common Mistakes to Avoid with All-in-One ETFs]]

Even with their simplicity, investors often make mistakes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:24">[00:10:24]</a>:
*   **Dishonest Investor Questionnaire Answers** Not answering honestly can lead to selecting an ETF that doesn't match your true comfort level, potentially causing panic selling during market downturns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:35">[00:10:35]</a>.
*   **Getting Bored and Tweaking Too Much** The "set it and forget it" approach is key to [[introduction_to_allinone_etfs | all-in-one ETFs]]. Constantly checking accounts or making unnecessary changes due to market swings can hurt long-term returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:53">[00:10:53]</a>.
*   **Buying Too Many ETFs in the Same Account** Combining multiple [[introduction_to_allinone_etfs | all-in-one ETFs]] or adding other ETFs can disrupt the built-in balance and complexity, which is unnecessary given the inherent diversification of a single [[introduction_to_allinone_etfs | all-in-one ETF]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:17">[00:11:17]</a>.

The [[canada_pension_plan_cpp_benefits | CPP]] offers a children's benefit, which provides financial support to children after the loss of a contributing parent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:28">[00:09:28]</a>. This benefit can make a meaningful difference, especially with education expenses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:40">[00:09:40]</a>.

## Eligibility
To qualify for the [[canada_pension_plan_cpp_benefits | CPP]] children's benefit:
*   The child must be either under 18 years old <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:02">[00:10:02]</a> or between 18 and 25 and enrolled in full-time school <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:06">[00:10:06]</a>.
*   The benefit is available to both biological and adopted children of the deceased parent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:34">[00:09:34]</a>.
*   Payments stop once the child reaches 25 or is no longer attending school full-time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:10">[00:10:10]</a>.

## Benefit Amount
In 2024, the [[canada_pension_plan_cpp_benefits | CPP]] child benefit offers a flat rate payment of $294 per month per child <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:49">[00:09:49]</a>. This amount is a set figure and is not based on the deceased parent's or surviving spouse's earnings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:53">[00:09:53]</a>.

## How to Apply
The application process is straightforward:
*   Apply through Service Canada <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:22">[00:10:22]</a>.
*   Supporting documents, such as proof of enrollment in school for children aged 18 to 25, will be needed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:24">[00:10:24]</a>.

## Tax Implications
The [[canada_pension_plan_cpp_benefits | CPP]] child benefit is taxable income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:56">[00:10:56]</a>. Recipients must report these benefits on their tax return <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:00">[00:11:00]</a>.

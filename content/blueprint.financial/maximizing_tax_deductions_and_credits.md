Understanding and claiming available tax deductions and credits can significantly reduce your tax bill or taxable income, allowing you to keep more of your earnings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:17">[00:07:17]</a>. Knowing which ones apply to your situation is key to achieving substantial savings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:27">[00:07:27]</a>.

## Child Care Expenses

You can deduct eligible child care expenses, which lowers your taxable income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:30">[00:07:30]</a>.
*   For children under seven, you can claim up to $7,997 per child annually <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:35">[00:07:35]</a>.
*   For children aged 7 to 16, this amount is up to $6,740 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:40">[00:07:40]</a>.

## Moving Expenses

If you relocate at least 40 km closer to a new job or school, you may be able to deduct your moving expenses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:46">[00:07:46]</a>. Eligible expenses include transportation, storage, and temporary living costs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:51">[00:07:51]</a>.

> For example: If you have $10,000 in moving expenses and are in a 30% tax bracket, you could save $3,000 in taxes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:57">[00:07:57]</a>.

## Medical Expenses

You can [[maximizing_medical_expense_claims_for_families | claim medical expenses]] that exceed the lesser of 3% of your net income or a set threshold <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:04">[00:08:04]</a>. For 2025, this threshold is $2,833 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:09">[00:08:09]</a>.

> For example: If your net income is $60,000, 3% is $1,800. Any expenses over this amount are eligible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:14">[00:08:14]</a>. If you have $5,000 in medical expenses, you could claim $3,200. With a 15% federal tax credit rate, this results in $480 in tax savings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:19">[00:08:19]</a>.

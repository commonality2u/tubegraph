## Common Tax Filing Mistakes to Avoid

### Filing Late or Not Filing At All
Filing taxes late can be a very costly mistake <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:23">[00:00:23]</a>.
*   **Penalties for Owing Taxes**: If you owe taxes, the CRA charges a 5% penalty on the unpaid balance, plus an additional 1% per month until it's paid <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:28">[00:00:28]</a>.
*   **Delayed Benefits**: Even if no taxes are owed, late filing can delay your tax refunds and benefits, such as the GST/HST credit, Canada Child Benefit, and the Climate Action Incentive Payment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:34">[00:00:34]</a>.
*   **Increased Penalties**: Repeated late filing can lead to increased penalties <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:41">[00:00:41]</a>.
*   **Solution**: Set a reminder well before the April 30th deadline <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:46">[00:00:46]</a>. File on time, even if you can't pay right away, to avoid penalties <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:50">[00:00:50]</a>.

### Forgetting to Report All Income
The CRA cross-checks T4s, T5s, and other income records against your tax return <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:58">[00:00:58]</a>.
*   **Consequences**: Forgetting to report various income sources (employment, rental, side hustle, investment dividends) can lead to penalties and potential audits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:05">[00:01:05]</a>.
*   **Automatic Reassessment**: Platforms like Uber, Airbnb, and Etsy now report income directly to the CRA, potentially triggering automatic reassessments for unreported earnings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:13">[00:01:13]</a>.
*   **Specific Penalty**: If you fail to report $500 or more in income on your current return and have missed reporting income in any of the past three years, the CRA might impose a 10% penalty on the unreported income (5% federally and 5% provincially) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:21">[00:01:21]</a>.
*   **Solution**: Ensure all income sources are accurately reported <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:36">[00:01:36]</a>. Check your My CRA account to confirm all tax slips are included before filing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:44">[00:01:44]</a>. Keep accurate records of all income sources <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:50">[00:01:50]</a>.

### Overcontributing to RRSPs or TFSAs
Going over your Registered Retirement Savings Plan (RRSP) or Tax-Free Savings Account (TFSA) contribution limit can be costly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:03">[00:02:03]</a>.
*   **Penalties**: Excess RRSP contributions are subject to a 1% monthly penalty until withdrawn <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:09">[00:02:09]</a>. [[maximizing_tfsa_contributions_for_taxfree_growth | TFSA]] contributions also incur a 1% penalty per month on the excess amount <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:13">[00:02:13]</a>.
*   **Tracking**: Many people don't track their contribution room carefully and mistakenly assume the CRA will notify them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:21">[00:02:21]</a>.
*   **Solution**: Track your own contributions as My CRA account isn't always updated in real time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:35">[00:02:35]</a>. Withdraw any excess contributions as soon as possible to minimize penalties <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:43">[00:02:43]</a>.
    *   **Pro Tip**: You can exceed your RRSP limit by up to $2,000 without incurring penalties, providing a small buffer for RRSPs but not TFSAs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:52">[00:02:52]</a>.

### Missing Out on Deductions and Credits
Many Canadians overlook valuable tax deductions and credits that could reduce their taxable income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:06">[00:03:06]</a>.
*   **Commonly Missed Deductions**:
    *   [[maximizing_tax_refunds_from_medical_expenses | Medical expenses]]: Prescription drugs, dental treatments, and travel costs for medical care <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:14">[00:03:14]</a>.
    *   Moving expenses: If you moved for work or school in the past year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:21">[00:03:21]</a>.
    *   Union and professional membership dues <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:27">[00:03:27]</a>.
    *   Tuition credits: Students often forget they can carry forward unused credits or transfer them to a parent or spouse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:30">[00:03:30]</a>.
*   **Solution**: Review your eligibility for these deductions to achieve a bigger refund <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:36">[00:03:36]</a>. Always keep receipts and records as the CRA may request proof <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:40">[00:03:40]</a>. This is a key part of [[using_tax_credits_to_maximize_tax_savings_before_the_deadline | maximizing tax savings]].

### Claiming Ineligible Write-Offs
The CRA is strict about business and employment deductions, and incorrect claims can lead to audits and penalties <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:54">[00:03:54]</a>.
*   **Home Office Expenses**: Deductible only if your workspace is your primary place of work or used exclusively for work-related activities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:02">[00:04:02]</a>. Eligible expenses include utilities, rent, and internet for that portion of your home <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:12">[00:04:12]</a>.
*   **Business Expense Write-Offs**: Deductions for meals, travel, and vehicle costs must be reasonable, directly related to earning income, and supported by receipts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:18">[00:04:18]</a>. Personal expenses disguised as business costs can be denied <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:29">[00:04:29]</a>.
*   **Paying Family Members**: If you hire a spouse or child, their salary must reflect actual work performed; unjustified payments may be rejected <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:31">[00:04:31]</a>.

### Forgetting to Claim Capital Losses
If you've sold investments (stocks, ETFs) or property at a loss, you may be able to use those losses to offset capital gains and reduce your tax bill <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:43">[00:04:43]</a>.
*   **Carry Forward/Back**: The CRA allows you to carry forward capital losses indefinitely or apply them to past three years of gains <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:55">[00:04:55]</a>.
*   **Solution**: Keep track of investment transactions and report losses in the same year they occur <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:07">[00:05:07]</a>. Filing losses for future use, even without current gains, is a valuable [[tax_loss_harvesting_to_minimize_investment_taxes | tax-saving strategy]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:14">[00:05:14]</a>.

### Not Reporting Foreign Assets or Cryptocurrency
*   **Foreign Assets**: If you hold over $100,000 in foreign assets (stocks, real estate, offshore bank accounts), you must file a T1135 foreign asset disclosure form <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:23">[00:05:23]</a>. Failure can lead to hefty penalties <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:34">[00:05:34]</a>.
*   **Cryptocurrency**: Unreported gains from selling or trading crypto can result in a tax audit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:39">[00:05:39]</a>. Some crypto exchanges now report transactions to the CRA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:48">[00:05:48]</a>.
*   **Solution**: Keep detailed records of foreign assets and crypto trades, report all taxable gains, and follow correct tax rules <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:53">[00:05:53]</a>.

### Overlooking Income Splitting Opportunities
Income splitting can significantly reduce a household's overall tax burden <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:04">[00:06:04]</a>.
*   **Spousal RRSP Contributions**: Allows the higher-earning spouse to contribute to the lower-earning spouse's RRSP, leading to tax savings in retirement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:13">[00:06:13]</a>.
*   **Pension Income Splitting**: Retirees can split up to 50% of eligible pension income to lower their combined tax bill <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:21">[00:06:21]</a>.
*   **TFSA and FHSA**: These accounts are excellent for income splitting as they are not subject to income attribution rules, allowing spouses or family members to contribute and grow investments tax-free <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:27">[00:06:27]</a>. This is a key aspect of [[strategies_for_stacking_multiple_credits_for_larger_tax_savings | maximizing tax savings]].

### Failing to Pay Installments If Required
If you owe more than $3,000 in taxes (or $1,800 in Quebec), the CRA may require quarterly tax installment payments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:50">[00:06:50]</a>.
*   **Applicability**: Applies mostly to self-employed individuals, landlords, investors, and high-income earners who don't have enough tax deducted at source <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:00">[00:07:00]</a>.
*   **Consequences**: Missing these payments leads to interest charges and penalties <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:09">[00:07:09]</a>. The CRA charges compound daily interest on unpaid amounts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:11">[00:07:11]</a>. An additional penalty may apply if installment interest charges for the year exceed $1,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:41">[00:07:41]</a>.
*   **Solution**: Set reminders and budget accordingly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:19">[00:07:19]</a>. Timely and sufficient payments prevent unnecessary interest costs and large lump-sum tax bills in April <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:00">[00:08:00]</a>.

### Ignoring Tax Planning Until April
Tax planning should be a year-round process, not just at filing time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:21">[00:08:21]</a>.
*   **Missed Opportunities**: Many tax-saving strategies, such as contributing to a First Home Savings Account (FHSA), making [[maximizing_charitable_donations_for_tax_benefits | charitable donations]], and capital loss harvesting, need to be completed before December 31st to count for that tax year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:26">[00:08:26]</a>.
*   **Solution**: Set up a tax review before year-end to optimize your strategy, reduce your tax bill, and avoid last-minute surprises <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:49">[00:08:49]</a>.

## Utilizing Autofill My Return

The CRA's "Autofill My Return" tool, built into Netfile-certified tax software (like TurboTax and Wealthsimple Tax), can significantly reduce mistakes and errors automatically <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:02">[00:09:02]</a>.

### How it Works
1.  **Instant Slip Pull**: Log into your My CRA account and connect it to your tax software. Autofill automatically imports T4s, T5s, RRSP contributions, and other tax slips directly from the CRA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:12">[00:09:12]</a>.
2.  **Prevents Audit Triggers**: It ensures everything matches what the CRA expects, preventing issues from missed income (e.g., a second job, dividend payment, side hustle) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:27">[00:09:27]</a>.
3.  **Saves Time and Finds Missed Credits**: Eliminates manual entry and helps find missed credits and claim every possible deduction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:44">[00:09:44]</a>.

To use it, simply log into your My Account before filing, connect your tax software, and import your tax slips with one click <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:57">[00:09:57]</a>.

Tax mistakes can cost thousands, but they are completely avoidable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:08">[00:10:08]</a>. Seeking expert help can also optimize your tax strategy and help you keep more of your hard-earned money <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:12">[00:10:12]</a>.

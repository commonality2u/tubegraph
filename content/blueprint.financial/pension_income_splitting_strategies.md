[[income_splitting_strategies | Income splitting strategies]] are crucial for Canadian taxpayers seeking to reduce their overall tax burden. One such strategy, [[pension_income_splitting | pension income splitting]], is particularly beneficial for retired couples.

## What is Pension Income Splitting?
[[pension_income_splitting | Pension income splitting]] allows a retiree to transfer up to 50% of their eligible pension income to their spouse if the spouse is in a lower tax bracket <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:45">[01:08:45]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:25">[01:13:25]</a>. This mechanism is designed to lower the total household tax bill <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:15">[01:17:15]</a>, resulting in more money retained by the household and less lost to taxes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:65">[01:19:65]</a>.

## Benefits
The primary benefit of [[pension_income_splitting | pension income splitting]] is the potential for significant tax savings, which can amount to thousands of dollars for eligible couples <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:45">[01:08:45]</a>. By distributing pension income between two individuals, the household can take advantage of lower marginal tax rates, effectively reducing the overall amount of tax paid.

## Why it's Overlooked
Despite the substantial savings potential, many retirees do not utilize [[pension_income_splitting | pension income splitting]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:24:05">[01:24:05]</a>. This oversight often stems from a lack of awareness about the provision or a mistaken assumption that it is automatically applied to their tax returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:27:05">[01:27:05]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:05">[01:29:05]</a>.

## How to Claim
To claim [[pension_income_splitting | pension income splitting]], taxpayers must complete and submit Form T1032 with their annual tax return <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:31:05">[01:31:05]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:33:05">[01:33:05]</a>. This form must be filed each year to take advantage of the benefit.

Strategic adjustments to pension withdrawals can be made to optimize the benefits of [[pension_income_splitting | pension income splitting]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:37:05">[01:37:05]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:39:05">[01:39:05]</a>. This proactive approach can help maximize [[strategies_for_extending_retirement_income | retirement income]] and minimize future tax liabilities.

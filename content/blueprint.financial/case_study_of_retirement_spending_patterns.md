## General Trends in Spending Across Life Stages

Household spending patterns evolve significantly as individuals progress from middle age through retirement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:52">[00:06:52]</a>. Data from Stat Canada illustrates how these costs typically decrease throughout life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:44">[00:02:44]</a>:

*   **Middle-Aged Households (40-54)**: This period sees the highest spending, averaging $102,646 annually or $8,554 monthly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:00">[00:03:00]</a>. This peak is driven by larger family needs, increased food consumption, education costs for children, rising healthcare costs, and a strong focus on saving and investing for retirement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:14">[00:03:14]</a>.
*   **Pre-Retirement Households (55-64)**: Spending begins to decrease slightly, averaging about $99,250 annually or $8,237 monthly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:30">[00:03:30]</a>. During this time, childcare expenses typically drop, but healthcare costs continue to rise significantly. There's also a greater emphasis on paying off mortgages and preparing for retirement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:41">[00:03:41]</a>.
*   **Retired Households (65+)**: Spending further reduces to an average of $61,800 annually or $5,150 monthly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:52">[00:03:52]</a>. The focus shifts to essential living costs like housing maintenance, food, and healthcare <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:02">[00:04:02]</a>. Transportation costs decline due to less work-related travel, and there's generally less spending on luxuries and more on necessities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:06">[00:04:06]</a>.

## Phases of Retirement Spending

Within the retirement phase itself, there are generally three distinct phases of spending <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:31">[00:07:31]</a>: high, medium, and low spending <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:34">[00:07:34]</a>. The principle is that as retirees become less active, they are likely to [[save_or_spend_dilemma_for_retirees | spend less money]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:40">[00:07:40]</a>.

### Alex's Retirement Spending Journey: A Case Study

To highlight these [[changes_in_spending_throughout_retirement_stages | differences in spending patterns]], consider Alex, a Canadian retiree, as he navigates these stages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:33">[00:04:33]</a>:

#### Early Retirement Years (Ages 65-70)

In his early retirement, Alex's annual spending is about $70,000, or approximately $5,833 monthly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:41">[00:04:41]</a>.

*   **Housing**: Costs remain high, though reduced since his mortgage is paid off, but he still manages maintenance and property taxes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:52">[00:04:52]</a>.
*   **Transportation**: Costs have decreased significantly due to no more daily commutes, though he still maintains his vehicle and travels occasionally <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:59">[00:04:59]</a>.
*   **Healthcare**: Costs begin to rise due to increased medical needs and insurance premiums <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:06">[00:05:06]</a>.
*   **Food**: He spends slightly less on food, dining out less and preparing more meals at home <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:13">[00:05:13]</a>.
*   **Leisure**: Alex actively enjoys spending on hobbies and travel, taking advantage of his new free time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:17">[00:05:17]</a>.

#### Mid-Retirement Years (Ages 70-75)

As Alex moves into mid-retirement, his annual spending decreases to around $60,000, or about $5,000 per month <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:24">[00:05:24]</a>.

*   **Housing**: Costs stabilize and may even slightly decrease, possibly due to downsizing his living space <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:37">[00:05:37]</a>.
*   **Transportation**: Costs reduce further as he travels even less <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:42">[00:05:42]</a>.
*   **Healthcare**: Expenses continue to rise with more frequent medical care and prescriptions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:46">[00:05:46]</a>.
*   **Food**: Costs remain stable or decrease further as his dietary needs change <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:51">[00:05:51]</a>.
*   **Leisure**: Spending on travel reduces slightly, and he focuses more on home-based activities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:57">[00:05:57]</a>.

#### Later Retirement Years (After Age 75)

In his later retirement, Alex's annual spending drops to $50,000 per year, or around $4,160 per month <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:04">[00:06:04]</a>.

*   **Housing**: Costs remain stable due to ongoing maintenance and utilities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:16">[00:06:16]</a>.
*   **Transportation**: Costs are significantly lower as he rarely travels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:18">[00:06:18]</a>.
*   **Healthcare**: Becomes a major expense, often including costs for long-term care services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:22">[00:06:22]</a>.
*   **Food**: Expenses are relatively low due to simpler dietary needs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:27">[00:06:27]</a>.
*   **Leisure**: Travel spending virtually halts, with Alex focusing more on community and home-based activities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:31">[00:06:31]</a>.

This [[case_studies_on_financial_planning_for_retirees | case study]] of Alex's spending changes throughout his retirement is typical of many clients <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:44">[00:06:44]</a>.

## Real-Life Observations

Based on observations from financial planners, a wide range of retirement spending patterns are seen among clients <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:49">[00:07:49]</a>. On average, retirees may spend anywhere from $4,000 to $7,000 or $8,000 per month <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:55">[00:07:55]</a>. High-end households often spend a minimum of $5,000 to $9,000 per month <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:02">[00:08:02]</a>. However, this can vary significantly, with some retirees managing comfortably on as little as $2,000 a month, while others spend upwards of $10,000 per month and above <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:11">[00:08:11]</a>.

A common trend is that spending tends to start at a higher range in the early years of retirement and then gradually decreases, similar to Alex's example <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:24">[00:08:24]</a>. Typically, there is about a 25% reduction in spending as retirees settle into their new lifestyle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:36">[00:08:36]</a>. For example, if initial retirement spending is $100,000 annually, it might decrease to about $75,000 per year over time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:43">[00:08:43]</a>. This reduction is attributed to factors like lowering housing costs, less need for transportation, and overall [[factors_influencing_retirement_decisions | lifestyle changes]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:53">[00:08:53]</a>. As retirees age, their spending on travel, dining out, and other leisure activities often declines <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:06">[00:09:06]</a>.

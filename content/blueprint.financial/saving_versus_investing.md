## The Power of Investing Over Saving

To illustrate the difference between simply saving and smart investing, consider the examples of Low Growth Larry and Steady Investor Sally <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:17">[00:00:17]</a>:
*   **Low Growth Larry** saves $2,000 a year in a TD savings account earning 0.01% interest <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:27">[00:00:27]</a>. Over 30 years, he contributes $600,000 but only ends up with $600,931 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:37">[00:00:37]</a>. His money barely grows and loses real value due to inflation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:43">[00:00:43]</a>.
*   **Steady Investor Sally** invests $7,000 a year, earning an annualized 7% return over the same 30 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:51">[00:00:51]</a>. She contributes a total of $210,000 but her investments grow to $777,000, outpacing Larry by over $100,000 despite contributing far less <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:04">[00:01:04]</a>.

Investing can be thought of as a snowball rolling downhill, growing larger as it collects more snow, similar to compounding returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:13">[00:01:13]</a>. This demonstrates the [[impact_of_investment_rates_of_return_on_savings_growth | impact of investment rates of return on savings growth]].

## Five-Step Investment Framework

### Step 1: Develop Your Investment Philosophy (The Why)

This is the most crucial step in your investment journey <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:23">[00:01:23]</a>. Investing without purpose is like sailing without a destination <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:31">[00:01:31]</a>.
*   **Define Your Purpose**: Clearly identify why you want to invest <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:38">[00:01:38]</a>. Examples include saving for a down payment on a house ([[strategies_for_saving_in_the_canadian_housing_market | Strategies for saving in the Canadian housing market]]), comfortable retirement ([[the_save_or_spend_dilemma_for_retirees | the save or spend dilemma for retirees]]), financial freedom, funding education, or providing for family <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:47">[00:01:47]</a>.
*   **Personal Motivation**: A specific and personal "why" provides a stronger foundation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:51">[00:01:51]</a>. For the speaker, their purpose is freedom and financial security, which has allowed them to take calculated risks and remain consistent with their strategy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:17">[00:02:17]</a>.
*   **Benefits**: Knowing your purpose helps you adhere to your plan, navigate market fluctuations, and make decisions aligned with long-term goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:31">[00:02:31]</a>.

### Step 2: Figure Out How Much You Can Contribute

Once your "why" is clear, determine your contribution capacity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:44">[00:02:44]</a>.
*   **Budgeting**: Review your budget to identify how much you can realistically set aside monthly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:54">[00:02:54]</a>. Track income and expenses to find saving opportunities, even small adjustments like cutting unused subscriptions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:07">[00:03:07]</a>.
*   **Automate Contributions**: Set up recurring transfers to your investment accounts on payday to make investing a habit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:17">[00:03:17]</a>.
*   **Impact of Consistency**: Contributions are vital for portfolio growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:22">[00:03:22]</a>. Consistent investing maximizes compounding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:26">[00:03:26]</a>.

> [!example] The Power of Monthly Contributions (7% annual return over 30 years) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:32">[00:03:32]</a>
> *   **$100/month**: Grows to over $121,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:42">[00:03:42]</a>.
> *   **$500/month**: Builds a portfolio worth over $600,000, significantly aiding retirement planning <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:50">[00:03:50]</a>.
> *   **$1,000/month**: Can grow to over $1.2 million, nearing financial freedom <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:59">[00:03:59]</a>.

This demonstrates the [[key_ingredients_for_growing_retirement_savings | key ingredients for growing retirement savings]].

### Step 3: Choose Your Investments

This is often the most time-consuming and confusing part, but it's where your investment journey takes shape <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:20">[00:04:20]</a>.
*   **Risk Tolerance and Time Horizon**: Understand how much risk you are comfortable with (e.g., portfolio dips over 10%) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:41">[00:04:41]</a>. Your time horizon (how long you plan to stay invested) is also key; longer timelines generally allow for higher risk investments due to more time to recover from market fluctuations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:54">[00:04:54]</a>. Vanguard's free investor questionnaire can help assess your risk tolerance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:00">[00:05:00]</a>. The true test of risk tolerance is how you act when markets crash <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:10">[00:05:10]</a>. As Peter Lynch said, "Everybody in the world is a long-term investor until the markets start going down," meaning most people overestimate their risk tolerance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:26">[00:05:26]</a>.

*   **Investment Options for Beginners**:
    *   **ETFs and Robo-Advisors**: Excellent for beginners due to diversification and low management requirements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:34">[00:05:34]</a>. Examples include all-in-one ETFs like VGRO or Robo-advisors such as Questwealth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:41">[00:05:41]</a>.
    *   **Individual Stocks**: Explore once confidence grows, sticking to companies you understand and believe in, while ensuring diversification <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:50">[00:05:50]</a>.
    *   **Advanced Options**: With experience, consider real estate, private equity, or hiring portfolio managers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:58">[00:05:58]</a>.
    *   *A visual aid lists pros and cons of various investment options.* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:05">[00:06:05]</a>

*   **[[avoiding_common_investment_mistakes | Avoiding Common Investment Mistakes]]**:
    *   **Panic Selling**: Staying invested during market downturns is crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:15">[00:06:15]</a>. Missing even a few of the best market days can significantly reduce long-term returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:33">[00:06:33]</a>.
    *   **Staying on the Sidelines**: Waiting for the "perfect time" to invest is often a losing strategy, as even investing at the worst possible time usually outperforms holding cash <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:50">[00:06:50]</a>. Start with the basics and learn as you go <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:52">[00:06:52]</a>.

### Step 4: Choose Your Accounts

[[choosing_the_right_investment_accounts_for_tax_saving | Choosing the right investment accounts for tax saving]] can significantly impact your after-tax returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:59">[00:06:59]</a>. In Canada, TFSA, RRSP, and FHSA offer unique tax benefits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:06">[00:07:06]</a>.

> [!example] Comparison of $10,000 Invested Over 30 Years at 6% Growth (with specific tax assumptions) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:11">[00:07:11]</a>
> *   **RRSP (Registered Retirement Savings Plan)**: Comes out ahead with an after-tax balance of $64,327, thanks to upfront tax refunds and tax-deferred growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:32">[00:07:32]</a>. This is most effective if your retirement tax rate is significantly lower than your current tax rate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:38">[00:07:38]</a>.
> *   **TFSA (Tax-Free Savings Account)**: A close second, growing to $57,434 entirely tax-free, offering flexibility for withdrawals without tax implications <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:49">[00:07:49]</a>.
> *   **Non-Registered Account**: Only grows to $28,896, demonstrating how annual taxation drastically limits compounding growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:58">[00:07:58]</a>.

For more details on the best order of investing in Canada, refer to resources on [[tax_saving_strategies_on_investments_in_canada | tax saving strategies on investments in Canada]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:06">[00:08:06]</a>.

### Step 5: Buy the Investments and Track Performance

Once you've chosen your accounts and strategies, it's time to execute <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:18">[00:08:18]</a>.
*   **How to Buy Stocks and ETFs**:
    *   **Pick a Platform**: Well Simple is user-friendly and offers commission-free trading for stocks and ETFs, making it suitable for beginners <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:32">[00:08:32]</a>. Questrade is ideal for more active investors or those needing advanced features, with low trading fees and free ETF purchases <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:43">[00:08:43]</a>.
    *   **Set Up Your Account**: Choose the account type (TFSA, RRSP, FHSA, or non-registered), provide personal details, and verify your identity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:53">[00:08:53]</a>.
    *   **Fund Your Account**: Transfer money from an existing account (can take several weeks) or directly from your savings account (a few business days) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:11">[00:09:11]</a>.
    *   **Place Your Order**: For ETFs, search for the ticker symbol (e.g., VFV for Vanguard S&P 500 ETF), decide on the number of shares, review costs, and place a market or limit order <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:25">[00:09:25]</a>. The process is similar for stocks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:29">[00:09:29]</a>.
*   **Monitoring Your Investments**:
    *   **Analyze Performance**: Keep an eye on your portfolio's performance and compare returns to benchmarks like the S&P 500 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:40">[00:09:40]</a>.
    *   **Rebalance**: If one part of your portfolio has grown significantly more than others, rebalance to align with goals and reduce risk <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:50">[00:09:50]</a>.
    *   **Check for Fees and Taxes**: Be aware of fees and taxes, as they can reduce your returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:56">[00:09:56]</a>.
    *   **Track Your Net Worth**: Add up all assets (investments, savings, property) and subtract liabilities (loans, credit card debt) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:06">[00:10:06]</a>. Watching this number grow is motivating and provides a holistic financial view <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:12">[00:10:12]</a>.
    *   **Be Consistent**: Whether you check investments monthly, quarterly, or annually, pick a schedule and stick to it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:26">[00:10:26]</a>.
*   **Learn from Mistakes**: Everyone makes mistakes in investing, such as panic selling during dips or staying in cash too long <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:45">[00:10:45]</a>. The key is to learn from these moments, adjust your strategy, and avoid letting short-term emotions derail long-term goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:55">[00:10:55]</a>.

## When to Seek Expert Help

If managing your own investments feels overwhelming, or you're not achieving desired results, hiring a portfolio manager can be a valuable solution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:12">[00:11:12]</a>.
*   **Finding an Unbiased Expert**: Look for someone who is unbiased and focused on growing your money, not just selling products for high commissions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:25">[00:11:25]</a>.
*   **Costs and Benefits**: While there is a cost for management, it often includes personalized strategy and planning towards your goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:32">[00:11:32]</a>.
*   **Interview and Inquire**: Interview several portfolio managers to ensure their approach aligns with your investment philosophy, and ask about fees and services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:38">[00:11:38]</a>.

Whether you manage investments yourself or hire an expert, the most important step is to simply get started <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:44">[00:11:44]</a>.

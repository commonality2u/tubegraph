The Canada Pension Plan (CPP) survivor's pension provides financial support to the legal spouse or common-law partner of a deceased CPP contributor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:09">[00:00:09]</a>. This benefit can amount to up to $818 per month for survivors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:11">[00:00:11]</a>.

## Eligibility Requirements

To qualify for the [[cpp_survivor_benefit_amounts_and_calculations | CPP survivorship pension]], there are two main requirements:

1.  **Relationship Status**
    The applicant must be the legal spouse or common-law partner of the deceased [[estimating_cpp_benefits | CPP contributor]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:26">[00:00:26]</a>.
2.  **CPP Contributions**
    The deceased individual must have contributed to the CPP for at least three of the last six years before their passing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:35">[00:00:35]</a>, or they must have contributed for at least 10 years in total <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:43">[00:00:43]</a>.

## How to Apply

Applying for the CPP survivorship pension is straightforward <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:03">[00:09:03]</a>. Applicants need to complete the application on the Government of Canada's website and provide documents such as the deceased spouse's death certificate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:04">[00:09:04]</a>. Step-by-step guides are available, and assistance can be sought <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:11">[00:09:11]</a>.

### Importance of Timely Application

It is crucial to apply for the [[how_to_apply_for_cpp_survivor_benefits | survivorship pension]] as soon as possible after a partner's death <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:51">[00:00:51]</a>. Retroactive payments are limited to 12 months, meaning any delay beyond this period could result in a loss of entitled funds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:55">[00:00:55]</a>. This is often overlooked during the grieving process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:02">[00:01:02]</a>.

> [!example] Missed Payments Due to Delay
> John passed away, and his wife Emma was eligible for $700 per month in [[cpp_survivor_benefit_amounts_and_calculations | CPP survivor benefits]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:10">[00:01:10]</a>. If Emma waited 18 months to apply, she would miss out on 6 months of payments, totaling $4,200 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:19">[00:01:19]</a>. Applying promptly ensures that financial support is received during a difficult time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:31">[00:01:31]</a>.

### Duration of Payments

The CPP survivorship pension is received for life, as long as the recipient is alive, with no time limits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:36">[00:08:36]</a>.

*   **Remarriage or New Common-Law Relationship:** Remarrying or entering a new common-law relationship does not affect eligibility; the pension will continue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:48">[00:08:48]</a>.
*   **Death of the Survivor:** Payments will cease upon the death of the survivor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:56">[00:08:56]</a>.

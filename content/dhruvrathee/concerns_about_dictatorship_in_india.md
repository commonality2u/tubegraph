### The Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA)

The difficulty in obtaining bail for arrested opposition leaders is linked to the PMLA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:25">[00:02:25]</a>. A 2018 amendment by the Modi government made bail conditions more stringent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:30">[00:03:30]</a>. Unlike other laws where one is "innocent until proven guilty," PMLA states "guilty until proven innocent," meaning bail is denied until innocence is proven <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:40">[00:03:40]</a>. The amendment also granted more power to the ED <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:53">[00:03:53]</a>.

Arvind Kejriwal, before his arrest, explained that under this amended law, individuals can be arrested based on accusations, regardless of truth, and may not receive bail until their case concludes and they are proven innocent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:57">[00:03:57]</a>.

## Refuting Counter-Arguments

### "If it's a dictatorship, how can you criticize?" (False Dilemma)
Some argue that the existence of open criticism, such as this video, proves democracy is not in danger <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:52">[00:13:52]</a>. This argument is likened to a "False Dilemma," forcing a black-and-white view of either a full democracy or a full dictatorship <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:53">[00:14:53]</a>. The argument asserts that the country is *leaning towards* dictatorship, not that it has fully become one <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:04">[00:15:04]</a>. Cases have been filed against individuals for protesting with posters and journalists for reporting on sensitive issues <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:35">[00:14:35]</a>.

### "What about Indira Gandhi's dictatorship?" (Red Herring Fallacy)
Comparing the current situation to Indira Gandhi's emergency is dismissed as a "Red Herring Fallacy" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:03">[00:20:03]</a>. While the emergency was a "black day for democracy" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:17">[00:20:17]</a>, past problems do not resolve present ones <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:22">[00:20:22]</a>.

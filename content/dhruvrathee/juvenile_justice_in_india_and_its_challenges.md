The handling of juvenile cases in India, particularly those involving serious crimes, has come under significant scrutiny due to perceived leniency and systemic corruption. The Pune Porsche accident serves as a prominent case study, highlighting the complexities and [[challenges_of_independent_journalism_in_india | challenges]] within the Indian juvenile justice system <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:14">[00:02:14]</a>.

## The Pune Porsche Accident

On the night of May 18th, a 24-year-old man, Aneesh, and a 24-year-old woman, Ashwini, were tragically killed in Pune when their motorcycle was hit by a speeding electric Porsche Taycan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:34">[00:01:34]</a>. The car was driven by Vedant, a 17-year-old minor, who was under the influence of alcohol and driving at speeds reportedly between 160 kmph and 200 kmph on a small road <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:11">[00:01:11]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:40">[00:01:40]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:45">[00:01:45]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:51">[00:01:51]</a>. Vedant, who is under the legal drinking age, had been drinking at two clubs, Cosie and Blak, with friends, accumulating a bill of ₹48,000 at one <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:32">[00:00:32]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:42">[00:00:42]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:55">[00:00:55]</a>.

After the collision, Vedant was handed over to the police by gathering crowds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:02">[00:02:02]</a>.

### Initial Influence and Corruption Allegations

The case quickly garnered public attention due to several unusual occurrences:
*   **Political Interference** Shortly after the incident, MLA Sunil Tingre arrived at Yerwada police station at 3 AM, stating he had known Vedant's family for 30 years and was there to help <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:26">[00:02:26]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:03">[00:03:03]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:08">[00:03:08]</a>.
*   **Special Treatment** Reports emerged of pizzas being served at the police station that night <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:32">[00:03:32]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:35">[00:03:35]</a>.
*   **Delayed Blood Test and Sample Tampering** Vedant's blood test was conducted 8 hours after the incident, at 11 AM <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:50">[00:05:50]</a>. The test was negative, later revealed to be due to two doctors, Dr. Ajay Taware and Dr. Shrihari Halnor, allegedly throwing away the real blood sample and replacing it with another for ₹300,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:06">[00:06:06]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:14">[00:06:14]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:18">[00:06:18]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:27">[00:06:27]</a>. Both doctors have been arrested <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:41">[00:06:41]</a>.
*   **Attempt to Shift Blame** Vedant's mother requested their driver to take the blame, and his grandfather allegedly threatened the driver to admit he was driving <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:48">[00:06:48]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:56">[00:06:56]</a>.

### Initial Legal Proceedings and Public Outcry

An FIR was filed against Vedant under Section 304A of the Indian Penal Code (IPC) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:54">[00:03:54]</a>.

*   **Section 304A (Death by Negligence)**: This section applies when death occurs due to a mistake or negligence, carrying a maximum punishment of 2 years in jail <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:01">[00:04:01]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:07">[00:04:07]</a>.
*   **Section 304 (Culpable Homicide Not Amounting to Murder)**: This section applies when one commits an act knowing it could lead to death, even without direct intent to kill. It carries a maximum punishment of 10 years imprisonment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:12">[00:04:12]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:16">[00:04:16]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:33">[00:04:33]</a>.

The filing under Section 304A, rather than 304, sparked controversy, as many argued that drunk driving at high speeds implies knowledge of potential fatal outcomes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:41">[00:04:41]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:52">[00:04:52]</a>.

The Juvenile Justice Board granted Vedant bail quickly, stipulating that he study rules and regulations, prepare a presentation, and write a 300-word essay on road accidents <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:12">[00:07:12]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:15">[00:07:15]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:21">[00:07:21]</a>. This lenient bail condition led to widespread public outrage and shock across the nation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:33">[00:07:33]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:38">[00:07:38]</a>.

## Systemic Challenges and Legal Technicalities

The public outcry pressured the police to move the Sessions Court on May 20th, requesting Vedant be tried as an adult and the charge be modified to Section 304 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:11">[00:08:11]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:15">[00:08:15]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:18">[00:08:18]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:22">[00:08:22]</a>. The police also booked managers of Club Cosie and Blak, and Vedant's father, Vishal Agarwal, under Sections 75 and 77 of the Juvenile Justice Act for willful neglect and supplying alcohol, and under the Motor Vehicles Act for allowing him to drive without a license <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:34">[00:08:34]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:36">[00:09:36]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:40">[00:09:40]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:46">[00:09:46]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:51">[00:09:51]</a>. Vishal Agarwal was arrested on May 21st in Aurangabad after attempting to flee <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:02">[00:10:02]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:09">[00:10:09]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:18">[00:10:18]</a>. On May 22nd, Vedant was remanded to a observation home until June 5th, and Section 185 (driving under influence) was invoked <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:28">[00:10:28]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:31">[00:10:31]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:46">[00:10:46]</a>.

### Legal Framework: Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015

India's legal system, like many others, incorporates the concept of `Doli Incapax`, which assumes children below a certain age cannot intentionally commit crimes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:06">[00:12:06]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:12">[00:12:12]</a>. The primary goal for child offenders is reform through counseling and education, rather than punitive punishment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:43">[00:12:43]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:56">[00:12:56]</a>.

Following the Nirbhaya case, the government amended the law in 2015, enacting the **Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:17">[00:11:17]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:19">[00:11:19]</a>. This Act allows for individuals aged 16-18 who commit "heinous crimes" (crimes with a minimum imprisonment of at least 7 years) to be tried as adults, as determined by the Juvenile Justice Board <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:23">[00:11:23]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:26">[00:11:26]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:31">[00:11:31]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:38">[00:11:38]</a>.

A significant challenge in Vedant's case, and similar incidents, arises because IPC Section 304, while having a maximum sentence of 10 years, does not specify a *minimum* sentence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:47">[00:11:47]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:52">[00:11:52]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:54">[00:11:54]</a>. This technicality prevents it from being classified as a "heinous crime" under the 2015 Act, making it difficult to try such juveniles as adults, despite the severity of their actions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:56">[00:11:56]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:02">[00:12:02]</a>.

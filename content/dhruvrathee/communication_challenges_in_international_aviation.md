## The Swiss Cheese Model and Contributing Factors

The incident can be understood through the "Swiss Cheese Model" of accidents, which posits that accidents occur not due to a single failure, but when multiple small mistakes or "holes" in protective layers align [00:07:51]<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:51">[00:07:51]</a>. In this case, in addition to communication issues, other factors included:
*   **Limited Air Corridors** In the 1990s, Delhi airport had only one air corridor for both departing and incoming planes, increasing collision risk [00:11:15]<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:15">[00:11:15]</a>.
*   **Outdated Radar Technology** ATCs relied solely on primary radar, which only provided approximate horizontal distance, necessitating manual charting of flight paths based on constant communication with pilots for altitude data [00:06:21]<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:21">[00:06:21]</a>. This made the ATC job highly stressful [00:07:01]<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:01">[00:07:01]</a>.

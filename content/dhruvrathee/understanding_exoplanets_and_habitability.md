### Technical Aspects

*   **Cost and Development:** The telescope cost approximately $10 billion and took [[NASAs Voyager Missions | NASA]] 25 years to design and develop <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:00">[01:04:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:00">[01:08:00]</a>.

*   **Location:** Unlike the Hubble Space Telescope, JWST does not orbit Earth. *   **Sun Shield:** A sun shield, nearly the size of a tennis court, is attached to block out sunlight <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:50:00">[01:50:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:52:00">[01:52:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:54:00">[01:54:00]</a>.
*   **Optics:** Its focal length is 131.4 meters, over 2,500 times that of a typical DSLR camera lens, and its main mirror weighs about 705 kg <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:09:00">[02:09:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:12:00">[02:12:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:17:00">[02:17:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:18:00">[02:18:00]</a>.
*   **Infrared Imaging:** The JWST primarily operates in the infrared spectrum (0.6 to 28 micrometers), which allows it to see through gas and dust clouds due to the longer wavelengths <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:58:00">[02:58:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:02:00">[03:02:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:06:00">[03:06:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:09:00">[03:09:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:12:00">[03:12:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:14:00">[03:14:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:18:00">[03:18:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:20:00">[03:20:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:22:00">[03:22:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:24:00">[03:24:00]</a>. It is equipped with:
    *   **Near Infrared Camera (NIRCam):** Captures shorter infrared wavelengths <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:32:00">[03:32:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:34:00">[03:34:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:37:00">[03:37:00]</a>.

    *   **Near Infra-Red Imager and Slitless Spectograph (NIRISS):** Measures the intensity of various light wavelengths, crucial for analysing exoplanet atmospheres <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:27:00">[09:27:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:29:00">[09:29:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:31:00">[09:31:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:32:00">[09:32:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:36:00">[09:36:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:39:00">[09:39:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:42:00">[09:42:00]</a>.

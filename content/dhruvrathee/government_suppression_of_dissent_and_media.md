## Alleged Suppression of Opposition

### Arrests and Legal Challenges
The arrest of Delhi Chief Minister Arvind Kejriwal without a proven money trail or conviction marked the first time a sitting CM was arrested in independent India's history <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:27">[00:00:27]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:14">[00:02:14]</a>. Other leaders from the Aam Aadmi Party, including Manish Sisodia, Satyendra Jain, and Sanjay Singh, have also been arrested and remain in jail without their crimes proven in court <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:23">[00:02:23]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:40">[00:02:40]</a>. The Enforcement Directorate (ED) claims AAP leaders were bribed with ₹1 billion in exchange for undue benefits under Delhi's Excise policy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:44">[00:02:44]</a>. However, politician Atishi states that despite over 500 raids and 1,000 witnesses in two years, no evidence proving wrongdoing by AAP or its ministers has been found <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:51">[00:02:51]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:01">[00:03:01]</a>.

The difficulty in obtaining bail for these cases is attributed to the Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA), particularly a 2018 amendment by the Modi government <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:25">[00:03:25]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:30">[00:03:30]</a>. This amendment reversed the legal principle of "innocent until proven guilty" to "guilty until proven innocent" for PMLA cases, making bail contingent on proving innocence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:40">[00:03:40]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:48">[00:03:48]</a>. The ED was also granted more power <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:54">[00:03:54]</a>.

### Electoral Bonds Connection
Arvind Kejriwal's arrest was based on statements from four individuals, including Sarath Chandra Reddy, head of Aurobindo Pharma Company <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:31">[00:04:31]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:36">[00:04:36]</a>. Reddy was arrested by the ED on November 10, 2022, after an initial statement in favor of Kejriwal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:51">[00:04:51]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:53">[00:04:53]</a>. Five days later, his company donated ₹50 million to BJP via electoral bonds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:55">[00:04:55]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:57">[00:04:57]</a>. After 6 months in jail, Reddy's statement changed to be against Kejriwal on April 25, 2023, he was granted bail in May 2023, and became an ED approver in June 2023 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:07">[00:05:07]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:26">[00:05:26]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:35">[00:05:35]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:37">[00:05:37]</a>. Six months after his bail, his company donated another ₹250 million to BJP <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:40">[00:05:40]</a>. In total, Aurobindo Pharma bought bonds worth ₹520 million, with two-thirds (₹340 million) going to BJP <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:50">[00:05:50]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:55">[00:05:55]</a>. Atishi argues this "money trail" leads to BJP, not AAP <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:03">[00:06:03]</a>. Similar chronologies exist for the other three individuals whose statements led to Kejriwal's arrest <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:21">[00:06:21]</a>. Kejriwal thus ignored ED summons, believing their aim was to arrest him and overthrow his party's governments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:30">[00:06:30]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:37">[00:06:37]</a>.

## Undermining Democratic Institutions

### Election Commission Appointments
The process of appointing Election Commissioners (ECs) has raised concerns about [[government_interference_in_state_affairs | government interference in state affairs]]. On March 9, 2024, Election Commissioner Arun Goel resigned <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:29">[00:10:29]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:31">[00:10:31]</a>. While the Chief Election Commissioner cited health reasons, others close to Goel claimed his health was fine, speculating disagreements within the commission as the cause <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:34">[00:10:34]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:40">[00:10:40]</a>.

A Supreme Court order in March 2023 mandated that EC appointments be made by a three-person committee consisting of the Prime Minister, Leader of Opposition, and the Chief Justice of India <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:06">[00:11:06]</a>. However, 3.5 months prior to the recent appointments, the Modi government introduced a new law removing the Chief Justice from this committee, replacing them with a Union Minister from the ruling government <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:19">[00:11:19]</a>.

Consequently, two new ECs were appointed by a committee of Prime Minister Modi, Home Minister Amit Shah, and opposition politician Adhir Ranjan Choudhary <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:32">[00:11:32]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:35">[00:11:35]</a>. Choudhary stated he was given a list of 212 names the night before and a shortlist of 6 names just before the meeting, without sufficient time to assess candidates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:46">[00:11:46]</a>. The Supreme Court expressed concern over hidden details but took no action to stop the appointments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:07">[00:12:07]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:13">[00:12:13]</a>. The newly appointed ECs, Gyanesh Kumar and Sukhbir Sandhu, are retired IAS officers who previously worked under or held significant positions related to the current government <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:22">[00:12:22]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:30">[00:12:30]</a>.

## Control over Media and Dissent

### Addressing Criticisms on Freedom of Expression
The argument that criticism still exists means democracy is thriving is a "False Dilemma" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:02">[00:14:02]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:53">[00:14:53]</a>. A country is not binary (100% democracy or 100% dictatorship) but exists on a spectrum <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:00">[00:15:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:12">[00:15:12]</a>. India is "leaning towards dictatorship," not fully there <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:08">[00:15:08]</a>. The "Democracy Report 2024" by Sweden's V-Dem Institute categorized India as an "Electoral Autocracy," two steps away from full dictatorship <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:00">[00:16:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:06">[00:16:06]</a>. This means multi-party elections occur, but there is no fair playing field and people's freedom is being crushed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:13">[00:16:13]</a>.

### Proposed Broadcasting Services Regulation Bill 2023
A new law, the Broadcasting Services Regulation Bill 2023, threatens to bring in new IT rules with a broad definition of "broadcasting" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:34">[00:16:34]</a>. This bill would categorize YouTube channels, Facebook accounts, Instagram handles, and WhatsApp channels as broadcasters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:44">[00:16:44]</a>. Every broadcaster would need to register with the government, and a Central Evaluation Committee would have extensive powers to approve or deny content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:51">[00:16:51]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:54">[00:16:54]</a>. The government would also have the power to seize a YouTuber's equipment without prior notice <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:08">[00:17:08]</a>. This indicates an attempt to suppress voices on social media <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:15">[00:17:15]</a>.

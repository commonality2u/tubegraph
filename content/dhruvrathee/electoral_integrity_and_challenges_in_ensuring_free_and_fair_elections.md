## Concerns Regarding Constitutional Amendments

The ambition to win over 400 seats raised significant concerns among some citizens and commentators <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:28">[00:00:28]</a>. Several BJP leaders, including Jyoti Mirdha, Anant Kumar Hegde, Arun Govil, and Lallu Singh, made statements that hinted at an intent to change the Indian Constitution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:31">[00:00:31]</a>. Achieving such changes would require a majority in both the Lok Sabha and the Rajya Sabha <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:44">[00:00:44]</a>. While the BJP later refuted these statements and other leaders offered clarifications, these occurred after the election campaign had already begun <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:55">[00:00:55]</a>. Concerns arose about the potential transformation of the world's largest democracy into a dictatorship <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:03">[00:01:03]</a>.

## Challenges to Electoral Integrity

Several incidents and ongoing issues have highlighted [[challenges to indian democracy and opposition | challenges to Indian democracy]] and [[election fraud and irregularities in india | electoral integrity]]:

### Direct Election Fraud
A significant trigger point for public concern was the Chandigarh election fraud in February, where a presiding officer was caught committing voter fraud on CCTV footage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:44">[00:01:44]</a>. The Supreme Court notably described this incident as a "murder of democracy" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:53">[00:01:53]</a>.

### Broader Systemic Issues
Beyond direct fraud, other [[systemic issues in law enforcement and governance | systemic issues]] affecting fairness and transparency have been noted:
*   **Media Bias**: A clear bias in media coverage, with many TV channels and Hindi newspapers perceived as government puppets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:03">[00:02:03]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:48">[00:04:48]</a>.
*   **Overthrowing Elected Governments**: Instances of elected governments being overthrown and horse-trading of MLAs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:04">[00:02:04]</a>.
*   **Targeting Opposition**: The arrest of Arvind Kejriwal and the freezing of Congress's bank accounts raised concerns about the fairness of legal processes being followed. Kejriwal's arrest was based on the allegation of a man whose companies had donated millions to the BJP through [[electoral bonds and their political impact | electoral bonds]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:58">[00:02:58]</a>. Congress's accounts were frozen for a minor mistake, allegedly to cripple their election campaign <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:22">[00:03:22]</a>.
*   **Election Process Inefficiencies and Irregularities**:
    *   Long hours for polling officers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:01">[00:22:01]</a>.
    *   Questions raised about whether elections were free or fair in places like Manipur, Sambal (Uttar Pradesh), Surat, and Indore <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:10">[00:22:10]</a>.
    *   Repeated use of religious hate speech by the Prime Minister in violation of the Model Code of Conduct <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:21">[00:22:21]</a>.

## Public Response and Counter-Efforts

Concerns about these issues resonated widely, with one video addressing the question "Is India becoming a dictatorship?" garnering 25 million views, indicating shared public sentiment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:17">[00:02:17]</a>. Despite personal risks and threats, efforts were made to spread awareness, reaching approximately 7% of eligible voters through one YouTube channel alone, and an estimated 20-25% including other platforms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:21">[00:01:21]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:31">[00:07:31]</a>.

### Defamation and Disinformation Campaigns
These awareness efforts faced organized backlash, including:
*   **Personal Attacks**: The creator of the videos was called names like "dog," "cockroach," and "bully," and labeled "anti-national" and "Pakistani" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:41">[00:10:41]</a>.
*   **False Accusations**: Claims of Chinese funding and secret WhatsApp groups for trolling were made <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:46">[00:10:46]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:55">[00:12:55]</a>.
*   **Fake News and Deepfakes**: There were deliberate plans to spread fake news, such as an absurd claim about PM Modi making Sunny Leone a minister <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:44">[00:11:44]</a>. Deepfake technology was used to create fake audio clips imitating the creator's voice to falsely show him asking for money from a politician <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:14">[00:12:14]</a>. Newspapers like Dainik Jagran also published unverified accusations of foreign funding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:15">[00:13:15]</a>.

Despite these challenges, public support and the focus on real issues like unemployment, farmer problems, [[Electoral Bonds and their political impact | electoral bonds]], pharma scams, and food adulteration, helped maintain momentum <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:42">[00:15:42]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:24">[00:16:24]</a>.

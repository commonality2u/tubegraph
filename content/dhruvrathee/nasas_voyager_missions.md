The **Voyager missions**, spearheaded by NASA, represent a significant effort to explore the outer reaches of our solar system and beyond, carrying messages from humanity into [[Interstellar Exploration | interstellar space]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:00">[00:20:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:00">[00:26:00]</a>. ## Mission Conception

The concept for the Voyager missions emerged in 1965 when a NASA engineer identified a unique geometric alignment of Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune that would occur in the late 1970s <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:37:00">[02:37:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:42:00">[02:42:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:47:00">[02:47:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:50:00">[02:50:00]</a>. This rare alignment, happening once every 175 years, allowed for the use of "Gravity Assist" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:54:00">[02:54:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:10:00">[03:10:00]</a>. This principle enables a spacecraft to leverage the gravitational force of planets to alter its speed and trajectory, significantly reducing fuel and travel time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:01:00">[03:01:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:07:00">[03:07:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:13:00">[03:13:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:16:00">[03:16:00]</a>. The year 1977 was chosen for the launch of both spacecraft to take advantage of this alignment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:22:00">[03:22:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:25:00">[03:25:00]</a>.

Voyager 1's mission was to fly by Jupiter and Saturn, with a specific focus on Saturn's moon Titan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:28:00">[03:28:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:32:00">[03:32:00]</a>. Voyager 2 was tasked with passing by all four gas giants: Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:37:00">[03:37:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:41:00">[03:41:00]</a>.

### Spacecraft Design

Both Voyager spacecraft shared a similar design <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:42:00">[03:42:00]</a>. They were powered by three Radioisotope Thermoelectric Generators (RTGs), which generate electricity from the heat released by the radioactive decay of plutonium <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:45:00">[03:45:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:05:00">[04:05:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:07:00">[04:07:00]</a>.

The fisherman, however, points out that he is already relaxing on the beach <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:09">[00:03:09]</a>. ## Critiques of "Hustle Culture" and Social Media

### The Myth of Delayed Gratification

The idea that one should "hustle" in their 20s and 30s to relax in their 40s is considered "sheer stupidity" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:16">[00:14:16]</a>. The period of youth should also be enjoyed, as it will not return <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:25">[00:14:25]</a>.

### The Illusion of Social Media

Social media often portrays unrealistic lives where people show off their possessions, leading to a desire for more <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:00">[00:15:00]</a>. This culture promotes a race for money, where seeing others with luxury items triggers a need to acquire similar ones <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:21">[00:15:21]</a>.

### Misleading Motivational Gurus

Some motivational gurus encourage a relentless pursuit of wealth, asking "Why aren't you a millionaire yet?" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:43">[00:15:43]</a>. This promotes the false notion that being a millionaire is the sole purpose of life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:51">[00:15:51]</a>. Money should be a by-product of one's work and efforts, not the work itself <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:25">[00:16:25]</a>. They often suggest retiring early, but this can lead to a life of "mindless consumption" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:34">[00:16:34]</a>.

4.  **Staying in Touch with Friends**: Regretting losing contact with friends <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:02">[00:19:02]</a>.

## Philosophical Perspectives on Possessions

Various philosophies offer guidance on the pursuit of material wealth:
*   **Hinduism and Jainism** preach the principle of non-possessiveness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:41">[00:19:41]</a>.
*   **Gautam Buddha's Middle Way** advises living in the world and enjoying it, but without total immersion in material things <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:51">[00:19:51]</a>. Freedom lies in minimalism <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:00">[00:20:00]</a>.
*   **Religious Principles of Sharing**: Hinduism emphasizes *Daan* (donation), Islam includes *Zakat* (donation) as one of its five pillars, and Sikhism promotes *Dasvandh* (donating one-tenth of income) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:42">[00:20:42]</a>.

The more one earns and owns, the more maintenance and complications arise <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:02">[00:20:02]</a>. Many become slaves to their lifestyle in the endless pursuit of money <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:09">[00:20:09]</a>. Material possessions often bring temporary excitement but quickly become old, leading to a perpetual cycle of buying and consuming without permanent happiness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:13">[00:20:13]</a>.

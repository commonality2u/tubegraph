A PIL was filed in the Supreme Court, and all episodes of "India's Got Latent" were removed from YouTube <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:25:00">[01:25:00]</a>. The uproar escalated to the point where Ranveer and Samay received public death threats <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:47:00">[01:47:00]</a>. Ranveer Allahbadia later issued an apology, acknowledging his comment on parents as insensitive and disrespectful, while also reporting threats to his family <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:10:00">[02:10:00]</a>. Samay Raina also removed his show's episodes, citing difficulty in handling the situation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:34:00">[02:34:00]</a>.

However, this perspective is critically challenged, asserting that films, songs, web series, and jokes profoundly impact individuals and society <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:52:00">[03:52:00]</a>. Most people, especially those under 18, have fragile minds, and their ideologies are shaped by their environment, not inherited genetically <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:00:00">[04:00:00]</a>. This highlights the [[personal_philosophies_and_influence_of_media | influence of media]] on shaping societal norms and individual perceptions.

## Solutions: Boycott vs. Ban

When addressing vulgar and insulting comedy, two main approaches are often considered:
*   **Government Censorship (Banning)**: This is deemed ineffective and potentially harmful. Historical examples like "Bandit Queen," "Udta Punjab," and "India's Daughter" show that meaningful and sensitive films face censorship, while vulgar ones often pass easily <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:02:00">[22:02:00]</a>. Censorship can also suppress critical works, as seen with Saadat Hassan Manto's stories <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:19:00">[22:19:00]</a>. This highlights the ongoing debate of [[freedom_of_expression_versus_censorship | freedom of expression versus censorship]].
*   **Public Call-Out and Boycott**: This is presented as a legal, democratic, and constitutional solution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:02:00">[23:02:00]</a>. People have the right to express dissent by criticizing and boycotting content they find offensive, rather than demanding a ban <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:05:00">[23:05:00]</a>. A boycott involves individuals choosing to stop watching content and encouraging others to do the same, whereas a ban restricts everyone's access <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:23:00">[23:23:00]</a>.

It is crucial that "calling out" does not devolve into insults or death threats, which are entirely wrong and should face strict action <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:38:00">[23:38:00]</a>. The government's perceived inaction against death threats compared to its swift action against vulgar jokes is criticized as a mockery of law and order <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:44:00">[24:44:00]</a>.

## Defining Ethical Comedy: Punching Up vs. Punching Down

The core principle for ethical comedy is the distinction between "punching up" and "punching down" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:19:00">[26:19:00]</a>.
*   **Punching Down**: This involves humiliating those who are at a disadvantage (e.g., based on weight, disability, financial status, social status) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:26:00">[26:26:00]</a>.
*   **Punching Up**: This involves mocking and criticizing those more powerful, or challenging societal norms that benefit elites <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:29:00">[26:29:00]</a>.

Examples of comedians who successfully "punch up" include:
*   **Gaurav Kapoor**: Creates comedy on topics like traffic rules and road safety <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:38:00">[26:38:00]</a>.
*   **Abhishek Upmanyu**: Addresses health, anxiety, privilege blindness, and the rote education system <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:43:00">[26:43:00]</a>.
*   **Manik Mahina**: Mocks figures like Bobby Kataria and satirizes the inefficiencies of Indian police <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:51:00">[26:51:00]</a>.
*   **Kunal Kamra**: Engages in political comedy, which requires immense courage due to the risks involved <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:02:00">[27:02:00]</a>.

While some of these comedians also use foul language, their comedy contains substance beyond mere vulgarity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:17:00">[27:17:00]</a>. There is an appeal for comedians, actors, and filmmakers to create content without profanity and vulgarity, as demonstrated by channels like Slayy Point and comedians like Deepak Garg and Hustling Rajan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:42:00">[27:42:00]</a>.

Comedians have a choice: to make a classist joke or joke about someone being classist; to make a casteist joke or joke about casteism; to punch down or punch up <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:08:00">[28:08:00]</a>. Ultimately, content creators are encouraged to reflect on the impact of their content on society, and audiences are urged to be mindful of the content they consume, recognizing that their minds are not "dustbins" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:22:00">[28:22:00]</a>.

## [[history_and_discovery_of_black_holes | History and Discovery]] Rooted in [[einsteins_theory_of_relativity_and_time_dilation | Einstein's Theory of Relativity]]

The [[history_and_discovery_of_black_holes | history of black holes]] is relatively short, with most knowledge developing in the last 100 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:55">[00:01:55]</a>. The existence of [[scientific_and_theoretical_aspects_of_black_holes | black holes]] was later discovered due to [[einsteins_theory_of_relativity_and_time_dilation | Einstein's Theory of Relativity]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:01">[00:02:01]</a>. This theory has two parts:

### Special Theory of Relativity

Published in 1905, the [[einsteins_theory_of_relativity_and_time_dilation | Special Theory of Relativity]] explains how speed affects time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:12">[00:02:12]</a>. For example, if one travels in a spaceship at very high speeds, time would slow down for that individual relative to those on Earth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:20">[00:02:20]</a>. This phenomenon is known as [[einsteins_theory_of_relativity_and_time_dilation | Kinematic Time Dilation]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:45">[00:02:45]</a>.

### General Theory of Relativity

Developed in 1915, the [[einsteins_theory_of_relativity_and_time_dilation | General Theory of Relativity]] further elaborates that gravity can also cause [[einsteins_theory_of_relativity_and_time_dilation | time dilation]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:54">[00:02:54]</a>. The stronger the gravitational force experienced, the slower time would become <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:03">[00:03:03]</a>. This is termed [[einsteins_theory_of_relativity_and_time_dilation | Gravitational Time Dilation]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:09">[00:03:09]</a>.

An accurate depiction of [[einsteins_theory_of_relativity_and_time_dilation | Gravitational Time Dilation]] was shown in *Interstellar* when Cooper's team landed on the "Aqua Planet," where one hour equated to seven years on Earth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:11">[00:03:11]</a>. This extreme [[einsteins_theory_of_relativity_and_time_dilation | time dilation]] occurred because the planet was very close to the [[scientific_and_theoretical_aspects_of_black_holes | Gargantua black hole]], allowing its gravitational force to significantly impact time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:22">[00:03:22]</a>.

To visualize these effects, Einstein proposed imagining a "space-time fabric" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:31">[00:03:31]</a>. This fabric, like a mesh, bends due to the mass of objects placed on it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:38">[00:03:38]</a>. This bending not only increases the gravitational attraction of physical objects but also leads to [[einsteins_theory_of_relativity_and_time_dilation | time dilation]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:46">[00:03:46]</a>. Furthermore, [[einsteins_theory_of_relativity_and_time_dilation | Einstein's Theory of Relativity]] concluded that gravity affects almost everything, including:
*   Physical objects <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:08">[00:04:08]</a>
*   Heat <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:55">[00:03:55]</a>
*   Sound <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:55">[00:03:55]</a>
*   Light <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:55">[00:03:55]</a>

This implication means that objects with such extreme gravitational force could exist that they could completely absorb light <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:14">[00:04:14]</a>. Such objects would appear completely black because not even light could escape them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:27">[00:04:27]</a>. These are what we now know as [[scientific_and_theoretical_aspects_of_black_holes | black holes]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:32">[00:04:32]</a>.

Initially, the concept of [[scientific_and_theoretical_aspects_of_black_holes | black holes]] was purely theoretical when Einstein presented his [[einsteins_theory_of_relativity_and_time_dilation | General Theory of Relativity]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:35">[00:04:35]</a>. Einstein himself found the idea "weird" and did not believe such objects existed in reality, even though he knew they were theoretically possible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:54">[00:04:54]</a>. The term "[[scientific_and_theoretical_aspects_of_black_holes | Black Hole]]" wasn't even invented by the time of his passing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:10">[00:05:10]</a>.

A key point in [[einsteins_theory_of_relativity_and_time_dilation | Einstein's theory]] was that the speed of light limits gravity's influence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:16">[00:05:16]</a>. For example, if the Sun suddenly disappeared, its gravitational impact would be felt on Earth 8 minutes later, just like sunlight takes 8 minutes to reach us <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:32">[00:05:32]</a>.

After Einstein, other scientists continued to work on the [[einsteins_theory_of_relativity_and_time_dilation | General Theory of Relativity]], solving its equations to theoretically prove the existence of [[scientific_and_theoretical_aspects_of_black_holes | black holes]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:51">[00:05:51]</a>. By the 1960s, researchers agreed that [[scientific_and_theoretical_aspects_of_black_holes | black holes]] not only existed theoretically but would eventually be observed in space <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:11">[00:06:11]</a>. The term "[[scientific_and_theoretical_aspects_of_black_holes | Black Hole]]" gained popularity after 1967, largely due to physicist John Wheeler <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:27">[00:06:27]</a>.

## [[scientific_and_theoretical_aspects_of_black_holes | Scientific and Theoretical Aspects of Black Holes]]

Despite its sensational name, "[[scientific_and_theoretical_aspects_of_black_holes | Black Hole]]" is misleading; there is no actual "hole" in space <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:35">[00:06:35]</a>. [[scientific_and_theoretical_aspects_of_black_holes | Black holes]] are formed from the collapsed remnants of massive stars, meaning there is dense material at their center <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:47">[00:06:47]</a>. When a star's nuclear fusion fuel (Hydrogen or Helium) runs out, the outward force generated by heat production ceases <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:19">[00:07:19]</a>. Without this counter-force, the star collapses under its own immense gravitational pull <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:33">[00:07:33]</a>. The final form depends on the star's initial mass <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:46">[00:07:46]</a>. If the collapsed core is large enough, it forms a [[scientific_and_theoretical_aspects_of_black_holes | black hole]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:19">[00:08:19]</a>.

For a star the size of our Sun, if it were to become a [[scientific_and_theoretical_aspects_of_black_holes | black hole]], its diameter would be a mere 50 km <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:35">[00:08:35]</a>. However, our Sun will not become a [[scientific_and_theoretical_aspects_of_black_holes | black hole]] due to the Chandrasekhar Limit, which states that a White Dwarf's maximum stable mass is 1.4 times the Sun's mass, beyond which it would become a Neutron Star or [[scientific_and_theoretical_aspects_of_black_holes | Black Hole]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:46">[00:08:46]</a>.

### Key Features of a Black Hole

[[black_holes_and_their_representation_in_movies | Movies]] and illustrations often show a [[scientific_and_theoretical_aspects_of_black_holes | black hole]] not as a simple black ball, but with specific visible features:

*   **Accretion Disk**: This is an orange-colored ring of gaseous matter and debris that forms around a [[scientific_and_theoretical_aspects_of_black_holes | black hole]] due to its strong gravitational pull <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:06">[00:11:06]</a>. These particles revolve at extremely high speeds, heating up to over a million degrees Celsius and becoming a glowing, fluid-like matter that emits electromagnetic radiation, primarily X-rays <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:38">[00:11:38]</a>. While depicted as orange in *Interstellar* and the first actual photo, its true color would be closer to blue, as human eyes cannot see X-rays <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:09">[00:12:09]</a>. The brightness variation in the disk (one side brighter than the other) is due to the Doppler Beaming effect, indicating the direction of spinning particles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:43">[00:12:43]</a>. The disk also appears to cover the top and bottom of the [[scientific_and_theoretical_aspects_of_black_holes | black hole]] from the side due to gravity bending light <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:19">[00:13:19]</a>.
*   **Photonsphere**: This is the last visible circle of light before entering the [[scientific_and_theoretical_aspects_of_black_holes | black hole]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:54">[00:13:54]</a>. The gravity here is so strong that photons (light particles) start orbiting the [[scientific_and_theoretical_aspects_of_black_holes | black hole]] in a circle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:59">[00:13:59]</a>. Theoretically, if one could reach this area alive, it might be possible to see the back of one's own head <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:09">[00:14:09]</a>.
*   **Event Horizon**: This is the boundary of the [[scientific_and_theoretical_aspects_of_black_holes | black hole]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:23">[00:14:23]</a>. Beyond this point, gravity is so overwhelming that nothing, not even light, can escape <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:27">[00:14:27]</a>. Once the Event Horizon is crossed, escape is theoretically impossible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:39">[00:14:39]</a>.
*   **Singularity**: At the very center of a [[scientific_and_theoretical_aspects_of_black_holes | black hole]] lies the Singularity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:25">[00:15:25]</a>. According to [[einsteins_theory_of_relativity_and_time_dilation | Einstein's General Theory of Relativity]], this is a region where the curvature of space-time becomes infinite <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:32">[00:15:32]</a>. The space-time fabric is bent so much that it stretches infinitely <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:50">[00:15:50]</a>. As gravity increases, time slows down infinitely <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:02">[00:16:02]</a>. What happens inside the Event Horizon, particularly at the Singularity, remains largely speculative as science has yet to discover it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:05">[00:15:05]</a>. One theory suggests that light might reflect off multiple points inside the event horizon before reaching the Singularity, potentially making things visible within <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:30">[00:16:30]</a>.

## Practical Evidence and Safety

The first practical proof of a [[scientific_and_theoretical_aspects_of_black_holes | black hole]]'s existence came from the Event Horizon Telescope, which captured a single photo on April 10, 2019 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:47">[00:16:47]</a>.

Despite their powerful nature, [[scientific_and_theoretical_aspects_of_black_holes | black holes]] are not something to be feared in general <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:17">[00:17:17]</a>. While falling into one would result in disintegration due to immense gravitational forces within milliseconds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:06">[00:17:06]</a>, they do not randomly suck up all matter and destroy the universe <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:19">[00:17:19]</a>. Supermassive [[scientific_and_theoretical_aspects_of_black_holes | black holes]] exist at the center of every major [[galaxy_formation_and_gravitational_lensing | galaxy]], and other celestial bodies revolve around them, similar to how planets orbit the Sun <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:28">[00:17:28]</a>. As long as a proper distance is maintained, one would be safe <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:49">[00:17:49]</a>.

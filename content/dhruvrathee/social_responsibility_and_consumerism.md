### The Trap of Self-Image and Social Media
Many people prioritize earning money, believing it will bring happiness once a certain wealth level is achieved <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:50">[00:10:50]</a>. However, the only truly valuable thing to "buy" is self-image, and money cannot achieve that <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:26">[00:00:26]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:29">[00:00:29]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:51">[00:14:51]</a>. Improving one's self-image should negate the need to buy luxury items like a Mercedes to prove something <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:54">[00:14:54]</a>.

Social media often portrays unrealistic lives, encouraging a constant desire for more possessions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:00">[00:15:00]</a>. This culture promotes a "race for money" where seeing others' luxury items fuels a desire to work harder to acquire them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:21">[00:15:21]</a>.

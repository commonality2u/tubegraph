The Armenian community achieved disproportionate success within the Ottoman Empire, particularly in the 16th, 17th, and 18th centuries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:21">[00:30:21]</a>. Lacking an educated and entrepreneurial class, the Ottoman state encouraged Armenians to fill this gap <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:44">[00:32:44]</a>. Armenians became a vital link for the Empire's articulation with the modern world <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:03">[00:33:03]</a>.

They were pioneers in various fields:
*   Photography workshops <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:28">[00:33:28]</a>

Following conflicts with the Ottoman Empire, Armenians migrated to countries like Syria, Lebanon, Egypt, and Western nations, continuing their success for generations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:38">[00:35:38]</a>.

## Recent Incidents and Hypocrisy

A recent incident involving a migrant boat sinking south of Greece highlights the severe issues surrounding migration management in Europe <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:17">[01:04:17]</a>.

*   **Details of the Sinking**
    *   Hundreds of women and children died in the incident <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:17">[01:04:17]</a>.
    *   The Western press reportedly attempted to cover up the details <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:30">[01:04:30]</a>.
    *   Survivors were reportedly taken to a "concentration camp" and denied contact with outsiders <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:42">[01:04:42]</a>.
    *   A Syrian dentist survivor claimed his family died in a hospital <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:04">[01:05:04]</a>.
    *   The Greek coast guard reportedly visited the boat three times, first asking if help was needed, then showing off and making fun <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:12">[01:05:12]</a>.
    *   During their third visit, they allegedly tied a rope and pulled the boat, causing it to sink with a sudden move <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:40">[01:05:40]</a>.
    *   There were 750 people on board, with women and children reportedly packed into the hold "like fish," all of whom are now dead <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:00">[01:06:00]</a>.
    *   This incident has been described as an act of "sickening hypocrisy" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:43">[01:06:43]</a>.

## Policy and Enforcement

The approach to preventing [[mass_migration_and_its_societal_impact | mass migration]] is debated. It is suggested that if European nations were to openly declare their intent to sink migrant ships and not save anyone, the migration would cease immediately <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:51">[01:07:51]</a>. This controversial approach is argued to be "more humane" as it would result in fewer total deaths by deterring future attempts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:08">[01:08:08]</a>.

### Control in the Greek Islands
Observations from the Greek Islands over four years reveal a concerning shift in migration control:
*   The [[European Union]]'s "site organization" was taken over by a police organization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:55">[01:08:55]</a>.
*   Fantex, an international police organization led by Germans, assumed control of administration on the islands <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:07">[01:09:07]</a>.
*   Arresting individuals who tried to save drowning migrants on beaches was considered a "feat" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:50">[01:09:50]</a>.
*   One instance involved Turkish citizens who rescued two drowning people and were subsequently detained for six months and charged with human trafficking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:00">[01:10:00]</a>.
*   This behavior, particularly under German administration, has been described as "despicable" and "filthy," turning law enforcement into "animals" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:42">[01:10:42]</a>.
*   The lack of public outrage in Germany over these actions is also noted <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:12">[01:11:12]</a>.

The root of these issues is attributed to a lack of "honor" and proper education in modern society <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:37">[01:11:37]</a>.

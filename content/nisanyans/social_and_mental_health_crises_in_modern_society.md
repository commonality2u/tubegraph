## Causes of Mental Health Crisis

The primary drivers of this widespread mental health deterioration include:

*   **Loneliness and Erosion of Community Ties**
    Individuals have become increasingly separated from the community ties that are fundamental to human experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:40">[01:10:40]</a>. People traditionally grew up feeling they belonged to a community, were responsible to it, and received praise or blame, encouragement, and applause from it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:55">[01:10:55]</a>. This sense of belonging, which would continue through families for generations, has diminished, leading to isolation and psychological distress <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:18">[01:11:18]</a>. People are becoming "isolated" and "crazy" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:23">[01:11:23]</a>.

*   **Destruction of the Family Institution**
    The family institution, considered the basic foundation of human happiness, has been destroyed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:30">[01:11:30]</a>. This is seen as a direct result of the aforementioned erosion of social belonging and family unity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:35">[01:11:35]</a>.

*   **Addiction to Alcohol and Drugs**
    As a consequence of loneliness, people often turn to alcohol and drugs, which are "strongly encouraged by the states and by this corporation, Capitalism" as a solution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:59">[01:11:59]</a>. However, these substances are also major contributors to mental illness and overall mental health deterioration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:16">[01:12:16]</a>.

## Modern Forms of Control and Exploitation

Beyond direct mental health factors, modern societal structures contribute to psychological strain through new forms of control and exploitation, which are often discussed under [[challenges_of_modern_employment_and_economic_structures | challenges of modern employment and economic structures]].

### Bureaucracy and State Power
Laws enacted in modern civilized countries increasingly serve to augment the power and authority of the bureaucracy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:50:02">[01:50:02]</a>. The administration of many societies has become dependent solely on the interests and priorities of the bureaucracy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:02:24">[02:02:24]</a>. This uncontrolled accumulation of rules and lack of accountability can lead to public frustration and violent outbursts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:07:20">[03:07:20]</a>. It is seen as natural for such "explosions" to occur in a world where bureaucracy appears as an "uncontrolled pile of rules" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:07:34">[03:07:34]</a>.

## Art and Civilization: A Comparative Analysis

### The Purpose of Art

Historically, art in high and fine arts (literature, music, architecture) has served the basic function of helping a society understand itself and idealize itself <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:48">[00:09:48]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:44">[00:10:44]</a>. This function has been consistent across various societies, including Islamic, Christian, Chinese, and Indian <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:32">[00:10:32]</a>.

### The Western "Rupture" in Art

The Western world experienced a significant rupture after World War I, leading to a terrible morale collapse and a loss of faith in itself and its institutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:12">[00:11:12]</a>. This period saw the emergence of modernist art movements that aimed to break societal authority structures, mocking and belittling existing values in painting, literature, music, and architecture <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:27">[00:11:27]</a>. Initially, this might have been a "necessary correction" due to societal arrogance and defeat <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:53">[00:11:53]</a>.

However, after 1945, the situation "got completely out of hand" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:13">[00:13:13]</a>. Modern art transitioned into an "investment area for some rich people who were filled with a sense of hatred towards culture" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:19">[00:13:19]</a>. Figures like Jackson Pollock, who "claimed to paint by throwing a bucket of paint at the wall," contributed to presenting "buffoonery as an economically good investment and an art act that brought money," spreading like a cancer through society <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:38">[00:13:38]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:59">[00:13:59]</a>. As a result, art in the West has lost its fundamental purpose of helping society understand and idealize itself <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:04">[00:14:04]</a>.

### Russia's Different Path in Art

During the Soviet Union period, Russia "entered a kind of time tunnel" for 70-80 years, where it "stopped and could not take any steps forward" in art <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:35">[00:14:35]</a>. It largely lived with memories, trying to combine a classical understanding of art with a political program <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:51">[00:14:51]</a>. While not "very productive," this period of stagnation meant Russia "did not experience the moral collapse that the Western world experienced in the 20th century" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:20">[00:15:20]</a>.

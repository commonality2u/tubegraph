## The Meaning of Life: Carpe Diem vs. Historical Context

The concept of "carpe diem" (seize the day) is critiqued as a "dead person" that fails to bring happiness or meaning to individuals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:42:00">[10:42:00]</a>. Instead, it is argued that true and profound living occurs at the intersection of the past and the future <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:27:00">[11:27:00]</a>.
*   Life gains meaning by assimilating lessons from the past and recognizing oneself as a continuation of those experiences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:35:00">[11:35:00]</a>.
*   It also involves feeling a responsibility toward the future, anticipating and hoping for the legacy one will leave behind <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:40:00">[11:40:00]</a>.
*   Without these dimensions of past and future, the present becomes "an empty point, a dead point" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:06:00">[12:06:00]</a>.
*   Focusing solely on oneself leads to an "emptiness" and a "terrible trap" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:23:00">[12:23:00]</a>.
*   Comprehending existence as part of a continuous journey from past to future provides meaning and perspective to life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:50:00">[13:50:00]</a>.

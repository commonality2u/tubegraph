### Historical Context of the Book

The book, as understood today, is a direct [[effects_of_the_printing_press_on_social_structure | function of the invention of the printing press]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:08">[00:32:08]</a>. Before the printing press, books were extremely expensive and laborious to reproduce, often requiring professional expertise <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:30">[00:32:30]</a>. Authors typically wrote works intended to be timeless and authoritative, often speaking with a "prophetic voice" about "high truths" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:12">[00:33:12]</a>. These literary creations served as proof of a culture's high literary capacity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:03">[00:34:03]</a>.

With the advent of the printing press, books became more accessible, leading to standardized formats, such as the multi-volume books popular in the 19th century or the single-volume books of the 20th century <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:24">[00:34:24]</a>. These books were often designed for a temporary lifespan, selling for a period and then going out of print <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:52">[00:34:52]</a>.

### The Future of the Classical Book

In the contemporary digital age, the survival of "books in the classical sense" seems "impossible" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:04">[00:35:04]</a>. While they may not disappear immediately, writing books is predicted to become an "antique business," primarily pursued by older generations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:17">[00:35:17]</a>.

## The Rise of Digital Writing

The culture of writing is expanding exponentially in terms of quantity, though often at the expense of quality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:35">[00:35:35]</a>. More people are writing than ever before, producing vast amounts of content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:44">[00:35:44]</a>.

### New Dominant Styles

Two main styles of writing are emerging as significant in the digital age:
1.  **Blogs or Micro-blogs**: These involve conveying ideas in "short capsules," item by item, in the simplest possible way, often with reduced accountability for accuracy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:10">[00:36:10]</a>. This style is becoming standardized <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:44">[00:36:44]</a>.
2.  **Bureaucratic Reports**: These are technical or administrative reports written by experts for experts, typically dry, lengthy (5-40 pages), and incredibly boring to a general audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:49">[00:36:49]</a>. This is seen as a standard writing production area for the coming age <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:26">[00:37:26]</a>.

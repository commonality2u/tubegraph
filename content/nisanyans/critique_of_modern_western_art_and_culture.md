## The Historical Function of Art

Historically, art in various forms—literature, music, and architecture—has served a basic function in high and fine arts across different societies, including Islamic, Christian, Chinese, and Indian <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:48">[00:09:48]</a>. The fundamental role of art was to help society understand "who we are" and "why we are so good" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:44">[00:10:44]</a>.

## Rupture and Decline in Western Art

A significant rupture in the Western world, particularly in Western Europe, occurred during World War I, leading to a terrible collapse in morale and a loss of faith in its institutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:12">[00:11:12]</a>. This period saw the emergence of [[postmodern_architecture_and_cultural_reflection | modernist movements]] in art (painting, literature, music, and architecture) that aimed to break societal authority structures and mock or belittle existing values <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:24">[00:11:24]</a>. While initially a "necessary correction" to societal arrogance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:53">[00:11:53]</a>, this trajectory did not lead to a re-evaluation based on higher values <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:20">[00:12:20]</a>.

Europe's strong art tradition from the preceding 500 years allowed for the production of recognizable art for another 20-30 years post-WWI, with figures like Picasso and Stravinsky <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:33">[00:12:33]</a>. However, after 1945, things "got completely out of hand" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:13">[00:13:13]</a>. Modern art became an "investment area for some rich people who were filled with a sense of hatred towards culture" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:19">[00:13:19]</a>. The speaker cites Jackson Pollock as an example of artists who presented "buffoonery as an economically good investment and an art act that brought money" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:38">[00:13:38]</a>. This trend spread like a cancer, causing art to lose its function as a tool for society to understand and idealize itself <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:04">[00:14:04]</a>.

## Russia's Different Path

This meant it largely avoided the moral collapse experienced by the Western world in the 20th century <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:20">[00:15:20]</a>. The speaker observes that Russia appears to be awakening "like Sleeping Beauty," potentially picking up where it left off in 1917 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:41">[00:15:41]</a>. ## Broader Cultural Impacts

### Printing Press and Information Overload
The invention of the printing press simultaneously led to a massive increase in readership and, paradoxically, to an "incredible explosion in religious fanaticism" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:12">[01:17:12]</a>. Access to texts like the Bible led many to believe they were religious experts, resulting in centuries of religious wars <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:53">[01:18:53]</a>. The press also fueled the sale of sensational and hate-inducing books, such as *Malus Maleficarum*, which contributed to the phenomenon of witch burning—a New Age phenomenon rather than a Middle Age one <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:21">[01:19:21]</a>. The speaker draws a parallel between these historical dynamics and the spread of the internet today <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:17">[01:20:17]</a>, suggesting that both represent unstoppable, transformative events with both good and bad aspects. This reflects on the [[reflection_on_science_reason_and_propaganda_in_modern_society | cultural impact of technology]].

### Automobile and Urbanization
The automobile is identified as the force that "destroyed cities" and introduced a new line of urbanization unlike anything in world history <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:21:40">[01:21:40]</a>. Before 1910, the city's main unit was housing and neighborhoods; afterward, newly developing urban areas centered around roads for cars, reshaping cities around them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:05">[01:22:05]</a>. This transformation meant that cities, once original, diverse, and "suitable for human sensitivities" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:44">[01:22:44]</a>, largely became "terrible places" in the modern era <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:23:55">[01:23:55]</a>. This highlights a critique of [[postmodern_architecture_and_cultural_reflection | modern urban planning and its cultural impact]].

## Impact on German Industry
The German economy has historically been based on the automotive industry since World War I <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:48">[00:38:48]</a>. Germany is home to Europe's, and arguably the world's, largest automobile manufacturers, including four of the most important global car brands <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:58">[00:38:58]</a>. However, this crucial sector is now facing a "danger of collapse" due to its inability to adapt and renew itself in response to these new market conditions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:14">[00:39:14]</a>.

Further exacerbating the situation, energy resource prices in Germany have nearly doubled in the last two years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:24">[00:39:24]</a>. This makes the continued existence of these industries in Germany "zero possibility" under current conditions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:39">[00:39:39]</a>. The exact reaction of the German state to this crisis remains uncertain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:42">[00:39:42]</a>.

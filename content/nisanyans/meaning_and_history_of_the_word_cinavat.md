From: [[nisanyans]] <br/> 

The term "çinçavat" is a derogatory adjective originating from Armenian, meaning "atheist" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:31">[00:01:31]</a>. It is a derogatory term implying "corrupted faith" or "corrupted religion" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:34">[00:01:34]</a>.

## Etymology and Usage

The word's structure is considered "extremely clear" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:00">[00:02:00]</a>:
*   "Çinç" means "weak," "void," or "too" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:03">[00:02:03]</a>.
*   "Havad" means "faith" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:09">[00:02:09]</a>.
*   Together, it means "lacking faith" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:16">[00:02:16]</a>.

Information about "çinçavat" can be found in Nişanyan dictionaries, specifically in the dictionary of place names <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:44">[00:00:44]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:52">[00:00:52]</a>.

## Historical Context

The term was used by Kara papaks (also referred to as "kemel irin") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:57">[00:00:57]</a>. These Kara papaks, who were migrants from 1828 and the dominant majority in Çıldır and Arpaçay districts today, used "çinçavat" to refer to the local inhabitants of neighboring villages in the Ardahan and Kars regions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:57">[00:00:57]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:14">[00:15:14]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:18">[00:15:18]</a>. These "natives" were Turks, specifically Georgian converts to Islam <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:06">[00:01:06]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:27">[00:15:27]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:30">[00:15:30]</a>.

## Unrelated Usage

The word "çinçavat" also refers to a bridge in ancient Zoroastrian mythology, though the speaker has no idea if there's an etymological connection between this and the derogatory adjective <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:45">[00:01:45]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:47">[00:01:47]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:53">[00:01:53]</a>.

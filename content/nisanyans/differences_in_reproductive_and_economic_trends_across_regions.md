## Demographic Shifts

Historically, the basic behavior of all mammals, with very few exceptions, has been to reproduce as much as possible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:10:02">[02:10:02]</a>. However, for the first time as a mammal species, humans have begun to limit reproduction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:19:02">[02:19:02]</a>. This phenomenon has emerged within the speakers' lifetime, noting that when they were born, most people gave birth as much as they could <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:34:02">[02:34:02]</a>.

While acknowledging this general trend, a counter-argument is raised that this stopping of reproduction is not valid for all of humanity, with the African and Asian continents continuing to reproduce "like crazy" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:13:02">[05:13:02]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:16:02">[05:16:02]</a>. However, it is clarified that reproduction rates have dropped "extraordinarily all over the world except Niger" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:26:02">[05:26:02]</a>, and overall, numbers have decreased and are still decreasing compared to 10 and 20 years ago <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:40:02">[05:40:02]</a>. Despite the global decline, the "rapid and radical decline in the Western world" is seen by one speaker as a "collapse, weakening and defeat of a cultural area, a kind of society" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:01:02">[06:01:02]</a>. Countries like Japan and South Korea are also perceived as "getting stuck and accepting defeat" in this regard <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:43:02">[06:43:02]</a>.

This demographic shift is intertwined with [[biological_differences_between_men_and_women|societal changes]], particularly the drastic change in the [[societal_changes_and_historical_perspectives|status of women]], which was very similar to 10,000 years ago when the speakers were born but now "doesn't resemble anything" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:51:02">[02:51:02]</a>. ## Economic Transformations and Production Capacity

However, in the current period, supply has exceeded demand, leading to the creation of a sector specifically designed to generate demand <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:22:02">[03:22:02]</a>.

For the first time in human history, the production capacity allows for everything to be produced for all of humanity if desired and organized <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:32:02">[03:32:02]</a>.

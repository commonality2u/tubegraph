## Global Economic and Geopolitical Turning Points

### Rise of Chinese Automotive Industry
One of the most important global events of 2023 was the remarkable success of Chinese cars in the world market <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:44:00">[01:44:00]</a>. Chinese car sales nearly doubled for two consecutive years, reaching approximately 6 million units <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:53:00">[01:53:00]</a>. These "cheaper, more reliable, and more solid" vehicles significantly [[impact_of_social_economic_transformations_on_humanity | invaded]] markets in Europe, including Eastern and Western Europe and Russia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:04:00">[02:04:00]</a>. This rise poses a serious challenge to the European automotive market, especially Germany, whose economic success has long relied on its major car manufacturers like Volkswagen, Mercedes, BMW, and Opel <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:35:00">[02:35:00]</a>. The American automobile industry had already faced a collapse years prior <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:27:00">[02:27:00]</a>.

### Demographic Shifts and Immigration
A second critical development was the unprecedented demographic change in the United States, where for the first time, the number of immigrants (mostly undocumented) exceeded the number of new births <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:43:00">[03:43:00]</a>. A similar phenomenon of mass migration from poorer countries to affluent ones is occurring in Europe <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:30:00">[04:30:00]</a>. This [[global_immigration_trends_and_their_impact | shift]] is expected to have rapid and significant political consequences in Europe, potentially leading to the rise of "extreme right" anti-immigrant parties <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:55:00">[04:55:00]</a>. These parties are often also opposed to the European Union project, suggesting that while the EU may not disappear, its expansion projects and increasing absorption of national sovereignty may become unsustainable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:30:00">[05:30:00]</a>. In the US, although the political system is less susceptible to such immediate transformations, social tensions are reaching unbearable levels, seemingly destined for an "explosion" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:30:00">[06:30:00]</a>.

## Broader Philosophical and Historical Reflections

### The End of Constitutions
The speaker argues that the "era of constitutions is over" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:19:00">[28:19:00]</a>. This is evidenced by a government that "systematically does not comply with the decision of the Constitutional Court" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:32:00">[28:32:00]</a>. For a constitution to be meaningful, there must be:
1.  A consensus among political and social actors to uphold it, even amidst conflict <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:44:00">[28:44:00]</a>.
2.  An independent arbitration institution above political considerations with a final say <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:28:00">[29:28:00]</a>.
Both conditions are absent in Türkiye and increasingly in other countries, leading to a "constitutional crisis" globally <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:46:00">[29:46:00]</a>, as seen in Israel <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:57:00">[29:57:00]</a>. The concept of states governed by written rules, born from 18th-century social contract theories, is no longer applicable to the current era <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:25:00">[30:25:00]</a>. England, which never had a written constitution, historically relied on an "elite consensus," but this consensus has also disappeared <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:25:00">[32:25:00]</a>.

The transition from the Ottoman Empire to the Turkish Republic involved significant continuity in state cadres <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="38:03:00">[38:03:00]</a>. The "overwhelming majority of the state cadres," such as land registry directors, police chiefs, and military court judges, continued in their positions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="38:08:00">[38:08:00]</a>. However, political cadres of the Republic, many previously organized within the Union and Progress party since 1908, were either "cleaned up and killed" or forced into exile if they resisted the new order <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="39:15:00">[39:15:00]</a>. High bureaucrats and former Grand Viziers, unless actively aligned with the Union and Progress, mostly retired <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="40:04:00">[40:04:00]</a>.

Sultan Abdulhamid's reign (post-1890) is described as a "period of stagnation and decay," where educated people desired to emigrate due to the country's dire state <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="54:21:00">[54:21:00]</a>. Abdulhamid initially made efforts to modernize education and various sectors, but his attempt to govern solely through bureaucratic methods by suppressing politics ultimately failed, leading to a system "everyone cursed" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:52:00">[01:00:52:00]</a>.

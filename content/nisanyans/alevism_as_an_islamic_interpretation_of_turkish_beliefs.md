## Historical Origins and Nature of [[historical_development_of_alevism | Alevism]]

It is described as a "tremendous uprising" in these regions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:58">[00:02:58]</a>.

This movement involved resistance to the state and elements seeking to distance themselves from traditional Islam <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:05">[00:03:05]</a>. Various groups joined this movement, including:
*   Old Turkmen elements, who had been in constant [[rebellions_and_social_integration_of_alevism | rebellion]] since the 13th century, frequently refusing to pay taxes or bow to state teachers and mosques <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:24">[00:03:24]</a>.
*   A large portion of Kurds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:53">[00:03:53]</a>.
*   Armenians and Armenian converts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:59">[00:03:59]</a>.
*   Christian converts in coastal Syria <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:24">[00:04:24]</a>.
*   Nusayris, who are stated to have "nothing to do with Islam" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:21">[00:04:21]</a>.

These diverse groups found the "new model" offered by [[introduction_to_alevism | Alevism]] suitable for them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:34">[00:04:34]</a>, and they declared themselves "Flames" (Alevis) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:37">[00:04:37]</a>.

## [[cultural_and_religious_dynamics_within_alevi_communities | Alevism]] as a Response to Oppression

The historical context of [[introduction_to_alevism | Alevism]] is deeply tied to oppression. Authorities "massacred, crushed, banned" [[introduction_to_alevism | Alevis]], raided their villages, and engaged in various forms of mistreatment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:44">[00:04:44]</a>. This oppression fostered integration and a defensive posture among the communities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:56">[00:04:56]</a>. As a result, [[cultural_and_religious_dynamics_within_alevi_communities | secret codes]], [[cultural_and_religious_dynamics_within_alevi_communities | solidarity organizations]], and a distinct [[cultural_and_religious_dynamics_within_alevi_communities | culture]] were formed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:08">[00:05:08]</a>. This cultural formation, rather than ancient Turkish roots, is posited as the essence of [[introduction_to_alevism | Alevism]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:14">[00:05:14]</a>.

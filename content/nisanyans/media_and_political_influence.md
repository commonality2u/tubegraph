## Julian Assange and Wikileaks

Julian Assange and Wikileaks are highlighted as examples of a "great revolution" that exposed potential vulnerabilities in Western states <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:30:21">[01:30:21]</a>. The Wikileaks platform allowed anonymous posting of secret state information, leading to revelations that "normally all the regimes of the Western countries... needed to be destroyed" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:30:54">[01:30:54]</a>. The subsequent crackdown on Assange, including his imprisonment, is seen as a classic method of dictatorship and tyranny to neutralize a threat <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:32:09">[01:32:09]</a>. This event underscores the power of information and the state's efforts to control it.

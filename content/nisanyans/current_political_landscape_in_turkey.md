### Understanding Unionism

Unionism is identified as the Young Turk ideology <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:10">[00:04:10]</a>. It is better described as a 20th-century version of the Turkish Muslim national identity and traditional worldview <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:04">[00:04:04]</a>.

*   **Context:** In the 19th century, the ruling Muslim classes of the Ottoman State faced the possibility of collapse and annihilation, prompting a psychology of defeat that surrounded society during Abdulhamid's time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:21">[00:04:21]</a>.
*   **Rise to Power:** Due to an oppressive regime, cadres were not renewed, leading to a dominant presence of old, conservative cadres by 1913-1914 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:43">[00:05:43]</a>. Young, educated cadres, long excluded, seized power in 1908, driven by a desire to save the country <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:00">[00:06:00]</a>. The Unionist regime came to power as a cry for liberation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:35">[00:06:35]</a>. By 1913-1914, all educated Muslim cadres in [[political_situation_in_trkiye | Türkiye]] had unified around this movement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:42">[00:06:42]</a>.
*   **Core Principles of Unionism:**
    1.  **Muslim Identity and Modernization:** The society is Muslim, and the ruling class exists because of this <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:11">[00:07:11]</a>. Recognizing they were left behind by Europe, they concluded that they must learn from Western civilization, adopting technology, culture, and military aspects to maintain their existence as a Muslim ruling class <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:43">[00:07:43]</a>. Their national identity, Islam, needed to be cleansed of "reactionary, outdated, lethargic" elements to stand up again <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:15">[00:08:15]</a>.
    2.  **Elimination of Non-Muslims:** The presence of non-Muslims was seen as an obstacle to this project, leading to the conclusion that they must be destroyed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:46">[00:08:46]</a>. In 1910, non-Muslims constituted a large proportion of the population <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:30">[00:07:30]</a>.

### Continuation into Kemalism

The same cadres and organization, with Unionist financial and organizational foundations, continued into the national struggle in 1919 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:11">[00:09:11]</a>. However, they faced new realities:
*   **Loss of Empire:** A large part of the Ottoman Empire was lost <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:39">[00:09:39]</a>.
*   **Absence of Non-Muslim Population:** There was no significant non-Muslim population left in the remaining territories, thus eliminating the agenda of "cleaning" them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:47">[00:09:47]</a>.
*   **New World Order:** Post-World War II, Western states (England, France, US) became undisputed sovereigns, necessitating peace and good relations with them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:06">[00:10:06]</a>. This also meant trying to forget or hide "one of the greatest crimes against humanity in modern history" (presumably referring to the Armenian Genocide, though not explicitly named) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:42">[00:10:42]</a>.

This new atmosphere meant continuing the Unionist project in a new world <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:56">[00:10:56]</a>.

### Kemalist Ideology

A common mistake is identifying Kemalist ideology solely with Westernization and secularism <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:07">[00:11:07]</a>. The ideological foundations of the Kemalist movement are "two-forked":
*   **Mass-Oriented:** Towards the majority of [[political_dynamics_and_social_issues_in_turkey | Türkiye's]] population, the essence of Kemalism was defeating powers, defending the homeland and nation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:38">[00:11:38]</a>. It was a Muslim movement based on Muslim nationalism <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:16">[00:12:16]</a>.
*   **Elite/Western-Oriented:** Towards Western states and elites in Istanbul and Izmir, an image and discourse of Westernization were presented <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:27">[00:12:27]</a>. A secularism project was put forward domestically to silence conservative voices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:50">[00:12:50]</a>.

Kemalism is thus described as a "locomotive that walks on two legs and two rails," balancing homeland/nation values with modernity, secularism, and Westernism <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:06">[00:13:06]</a>. The Kemalist national education system, especially for new generations, prioritizes "homeland, nation, Sakarya" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:27">[00:13:27]</a>.

The speaker emphasizes that Kemalism's basis is the same ideology as Unionism, and fundamentally, both are a continuation of the ancient Ottoman imperial ideology, adapted to the new era <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:41">[00:13:41]</a>. There has been no real rupture <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:29">[00:14:29]</a>.

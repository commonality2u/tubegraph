The Fiesta of Lisbon, also known as St. Anthony's Day, is described as the most important and famous event of the year in Lisbon <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:39">[00:00:39]</a>. This incredible revelry <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:55">[00:00:55]</a> involves city-wide celebrations lasting from midnight until the morning <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:36">[00:01:36]</a>.

## Celebration and Activities

The fiesta is characterized by an "incredible revelry" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:55">[00:00:55]</a> and an "incredible event" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:59">[00:00:59]</a>. Activities include:
*   **Weddings:** The city council and municipality sponsor weddings for "all the girls," with many marriages taking place <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:10">[00:01:10]</a>.
*   **Parade:** A significant parade features various neighborhoods, institutions, and teams, likened to the Rio Carnival <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:20">[00:01:20]</a>.
*   **Street Parties:** Tables are set up all over the city for street parties that continue until morning <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:36">[00:01:36]</a>.
*   **Music:** Music can be heard on every street corner throughout the night <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:57">[00:01:57]</a>.

## Culinary Traditions

A massive amount of sardines are consumed during the festivities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:41">[00:01:41]</a>. In many places, neighborhoods provide food and drink for free <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:45">[00:01:45]</a>, with wine being a popular beverage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:51">[00:01:51]</a>. The entire city is busy eating fish and drinking wine <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:20">[01:15:20]</a>.

## Saint Anthony of Lisbon

The fiesta is celebrated on the feast day of Saint Anthony <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:52">[00:00:52]</a>.
*   **Historical Figure:** Saint Anthony was a real person who lived in the 13th century <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:37">[01:13:37]</a>.
*   **Founding Figure:** He is considered the second founder of the Franciscan order, which pioneered the concept of "poor dervish" in Christianity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:57">[01:13:57]</a>. Alongside Saint Francis, he is seen as a true ascetic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:01">[01:14:01]</a>, an example to humanity due to his eloquence, enthusiasm, and purity of heart <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:05">[01:14:05]</a>.
*   **Symbolism:** He is often depicted with a lily, symbolizing purity, volunteering, and cleanliness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:24">[01:14:24]</a>. He is also shown with a burning heart <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:32">[01:14:32]</a>.
*   **Legend:** A famous story tells of him preaching to fish on a riverbank, with the fish gathering to listen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:43">[01:14:43]</a>.
*   **Origin and Death:** Born in Lisbon <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:54">[01:14:54]</a>, he is considered Lisbon's own saint <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:58">[01:14:58]</a>. He later went to Italy, dying in Padua, where his tomb is located <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:00">[01:15:00]</a>. While known as "Saint Anthony of Padua" in most cultures, in Portugal, he is specifically called "Saint Anthony of Lisbon" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:07">[01:15:07]</a>.

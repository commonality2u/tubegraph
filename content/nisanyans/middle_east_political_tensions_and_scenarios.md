## Historical Context: Armenian Question
The discussion touches upon the historical possibility of an Armenian state in Erzurum and surrounding areas after World War I <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:31:04">[01:31:04]</a>. This possibility was strong, and Türkiye's actions in 1915, referred to as genocide, were seen as a demographic engineering effort to empty the Armenian population from the region, making it difficult for Russia to establish an Armenian state there <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:33:02">[01:33:02]</a>. Russia's historical policy in Kars, however, involved a "divide and rule" strategy, settling various ethnic groups rather than solely establishing an Armenian-majority area <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:34:24">[01:34:24]</a>. Furthermore, Western powers (France, England) would not have allowed Russia to gain too much strength in the Eastern Mediterranean, leading to counter-calculations regarding influence over Armenian populations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:36:01">[01:36:01]</a>. The Russian Revolution ultimately altered these scenarios <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:38:40">[01:38:40]</a>.

This means people desire their defined "us" group to value, love, understand, and treat them with understanding, forming their basic moral behavior <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:23">[01:07:23]</a>.

The urban planning concepts of the 20th century, like its artistic expressions, were founded on a hatred of the past <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:53:22">[01:53:22]</a>. Cities were divided, torn down, and streets opened up out of a disdain for the existing urban fabric <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:53:43">[01:53:43]</a>. This extended to the abandonment of old art, architecture, customs, and traditions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:53:51">[01:53:51]</a>. This demolition of old structures and replacement with new, often less livable, plywood and plastic environments was an expression of hatred and rejection of the past <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:54:10">[01:54:10]</a>. Postmodernism later attempted to preserve small islands of old texture as museums <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:52:47">[01:52:47]</a>.

## Upcoming Elections and Political Parties

The elections scheduled for May 14 are a central focus of the [[turkish_political_landscape_and_election_analysis | political climate]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:45">[00:21:45]</a>. There is significant skepticism regarding the opposition's chances of winning, with predictions of a loss <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:14">[00:24:14]</a>.

### The Opposition

Kemal Kılıçdaroğlu, the opposition leader, has consistently received 22% to 26% of the vote in elections over an 11-year period <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:41">[00:22:41]</a>. For a shift in voter behavior, a "new factor," "new big hope," or "new goal" is needed, as voters will not change their votes without a new element in the political equation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:48">[00:23:48]</a>.

Key figures and parties in the opposition include:
*   **Kemal Kılıçdaroğlu:** The leader of the Republican People's Party (CHP), he is seen by some as a successful politician, though this view is not universally shared <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:27">[00:22:27]</a>. The CHP itself is viewed as having a "structural weakness" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:56">[00:48:56]</a> and has been suggested for liquidation in the past <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:26">[00:51:26]</a>.
*   **Meral Akşener:** The leader of the IYI Party, she is seen as potentially filling the role of the next dominant right-wing party in Trkiye after the current ruling party <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:05">[00:50:05]</a>.
*   **Ekrem İmamoğlu:** The Mayor of Istanbul, a potential defeat in the upcoming elections might paradoxically strengthen his long-term political career by opening opportunities for leadership within the CHP or a new political formation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:23">[00:54:23]</a>.
*   **Selahattin Demirtaş:** A politician described as "a few leagues ahead of Turkish standards" who expresses himself well, correctly, and maintains a "humane and civilized line" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:38:36">[01:38:36]</a>. His open letter to Meral Akşener is noted as a "beautiful letter" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:38:30">[01:38:30]</a>.
*   **Peoples' Democratic Party (HDP):** The HDP's role in a potential coalition government is discussed, with the assertion that if a majority is gained by the opposition, even parties that initially refuse to sit with HDP will have to do so <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:40:39">[01:40:39]</a>.

### The Ruling Party (AKP)

The ruling Justice and Development Party (AKP) is perceived as having "filled its name" and become "a personal corruption organ of a single person" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:31">[00:49:31]</a>. It is believed that the AKP is not permanent and "will leave the stage with Tayyip Erdoğan" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:43">[00:49:43]</a>.

Uncertainties remain regarding the election, including whether Tayyip Erdoğan will be a candidate, whether the election will be held, and if it will be honest <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:01">[00:52:01]</a>. The presidential candidate of the ruling People's Alliance had not been officially announced two months before the election <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:32">[00:25:32]</a>, leading to speculation about a surprise candidate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:40">[00:25:40]</a>.

## Social and Cultural Dynamics in Trkiye

### Alevi and Kurdish Identity in Politics

The question of why an Alevi or Kurdish president cannot be elected in Trkiye is discussed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:41:21">[01:41:21]</a>.
*   **Historical Context:** Sunni Kurds have historically been at odds with the Ottoman Empire and the Republic of Turkey for the last 500 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:41:43">[01:41:43]</a>. Similarly, the Ottoman Empire continuously suppressed Alevis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:42:02">[01:42:02]</a>.
*   **State Policy:** There is a strong assumption within Trkiye's governing cadres that Kurds and Alevis have a "genetic memory" that prevents them from being on good terms with the Turkish state <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:42:57">[01:42:57]</a>. The Turkish state has historically not allowed individuals who emphasize their Kurdish identity to hold important public office <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:40:55">[01:40:55]</a>.
*   **Example of Özal:** Turgut Özal became president by "giving up his identity," meaning his Kurdish background was never mentioned <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:38:51">[01:38:51]</a>. He proved his loyalty to the state without making his Kurdishness an issue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:39:51">[01:39:51]</a>.
*   **Current Government:** The current government includes a "very strong wing of Kurdish origin" in key positions, but these individuals do not highlight their Kurdish identity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:40:05">[01:40:05]</a>.
*   **Consequences of Exclusion:** This policy of excluding a large part of the population by labeling them "enemies of the state" is seen as backward and severely detrimental to the country's quality of life and "level of civilization" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:44:14">[01:44:14]</a>. Such a policy necessitates restricting fundamental rights, ruling with a stick, or frequent coups <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:44:28">[01:44:28]</a>.

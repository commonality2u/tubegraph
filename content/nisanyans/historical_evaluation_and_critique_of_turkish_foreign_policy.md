## Drawing of Turkish Borders <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:29">[01:04:29]</a>

### Syrian Border <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:51">[01:04:51]</a>
The Syrian border was primarily drawn through negotiations between England, France, and the Ottoman Government, surprisingly easily and immediately after the Armistice of Mondros in October 1918 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:01">[01:05:01]</a>. Despite the Allied forces (mainly British) having the military capability to advance much further into Ottoman territory, they did not <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:15">[01:07:15]</a>. The border established in the Armistice was largely adopted as the basis for the Turkish National Pact <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:18">[01:08:18]</a>.

The Syrian border has two key features:
1.  **Baghdad Railway Line:** The border largely follows the Baghdad railway line, remaining 200 meters ahead of it, ensuring the railway stayed on the Turkish side due to its significant investment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:07">[01:09:07]</a>.
2.  **Ethnic Demarcation:** Particularly on the Kilis side, the border closely adheres to the Arab-Turkish ethnic line, with Turkish villages remaining in Turkey and Arab villages in Syria <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:51">[01:09:51]</a>. However, due to the railway factor, a "considerable Arab population exceeding hundreds of thousands" remained on the Turkish side <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:30">[01:10:30]</a>.

### Iraqi Border <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:59">[01:10:59]</a>
The determination of the Iraqi border is less clear to the speaker. It is noted that the British made significant advances in the final weeks of World War I, capturing "half of Kurdistan" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:17">[01:11:17]</a>. Unlike the Syrian border, the Iraqi border has no "natural" or "logical" features like a railway line and appears to have been drawn "randomly" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:55">[01:11:55]</a>. This border was not stabilized until around 1926-1927, long after the Treaty of Lausanne, and changed at certain points <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:11">[01:12:11]</a>.

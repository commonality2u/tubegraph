## Prospects for Political Change
Changing the government in Trkiye via elections is deemed a "self-deception" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:31">[00:10:31]</a>. The "book of democracy" in Trkiye closed definitively in 2016, following the coup attempt, though the process began in 2013 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:44">[00:10:44]</a>.

Several factors indicate that a change through democratic means is unlikely:
*   **Leader's Discourse**: The current leader views any demand for a change in government as "treason" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:34">[00:11:34]</a>, clearly stating that an order where someone else comes to power is illegitimate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:46">[00:11:46]</a>.
*   **Lack of Arbitration Institutions**: There is no state-level arbitration institution (like Parliament, Constitutional Court, or Supreme Electoral Council) with the power to remove the leader <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:18">[00:12:18]</a>. The armed forces remain the only unknown factor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:13">[00:13:13]</a>.
*   **Impunity for Crimes**: The ruling cadres have committed numerous actions that constitute formal crimes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:19">[00:14:19]</a>, meaning there is no guarantee of their fate if they lose power <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:34">[00:14:34]</a>.

Therefore, the current leader "will not leave power unless he dies or a serious amount of blood is shed" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:05">[00:15:05]</a>. Street demonstrations, while important for long-term effects, are unlikely to cause short-term government change <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:42">[00:06:42]</a>. Historically, governments change through the "force of arms" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:59">[00:06:59]</a> or a coup prepared on "another platform" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:59">[00:07:59]</a>, as seen in Ukraine (2014), Syria (2011), Iran (1977), and Russia (1917) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:10">[00:08:10]</a>.

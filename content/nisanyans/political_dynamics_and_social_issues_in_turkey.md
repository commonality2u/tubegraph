### Migration of Non-Muslim Communities in Istanbul

The migration of Istanbul's non-Muslim communities from historic districts like Samatya and Kumkapı to Şişli began in the 1950s and became definitive by 1965 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:35">[01:13:35]</a>. This shift was driven by the economic development of the middle class among non-Muslims after 1950, enabling them to move to rapidly developing areas like Şişli <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:10">[01:14:10]</a>. The enactment of the Condominium Law in 1964 also made apartment living more attractive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:03">[01:15:03]</a>.

A significant factor was the influx of proletarian immigrants to Istanbul, particularly from Rumelia (Bulgaria and Yugoslavia) after 1950 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:55">[01:15:55]</a>. These immigrants, forming a poor working class, settled in cheap neighborhoods in Old Istanbul, including Samatya and Kumkapı. As Rumelian immigrants "invaded" these areas, the local Armenian population fled to Şişli <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:30">[01:16:30]</a>.

## Historical Perspectives and Their Impact

### Ottoman Empire's Role in Islamic History

The Turkish invasion of 1071 (Battle of Manzikert) is seen as crucial for the survival of the Islamic world, which was in a period of rapid decline and losing ground militarily and in terms of sovereignty from the 10th century <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:44:46">[01:44:46]</a>. The intervention of the Turks "brought Islam, which was a political phenomenon, back to its feet" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:46:22">[01:46:22]</a>.

However, the Ottoman Empire's inheritance of the Byzantine Empire after 1450, while contributing to Islam's survival as a dominant power, also "emptied itself" and led to the impoverishment of the Islamic world <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:47:20">[01:47:20]</a>. The Ottoman system, described as similar to the Soviet Union, was extremely centralized, absorbing the surplus value of the Eastern Mediterranean geography into the center and military, leaving behind a "dreg" and causing intellectual regression <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:48:09">[01:48:09]</a>. The Ottoman Empire ultimately "rotted from within" and crashed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:48:47">[01:48:47]</a>.

### Kremlin-Related Internet Interruptions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:48">[00:00:48]</a>
Approximately 23 months prior to the broadcast, there was a drone attack on the Kremlin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:31">[00:00:31]</a>. To confuse drones, the internet within a 1-kilometer radius of the Kremlin was cut off for about a minute every 10 minutes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:48">[00:00:48]</a>. This measure was intended to disrupt drone activity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:51">[00:00:51]</a>.

The speaker's previous location, presumably the luxurious hotel, was affected by these security measures as it was "right across from us in the square that is the center of the country and the empire" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:28">[00:03:28]</a>, near Putin's office <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:39">[00:03:39]</a>. This resulted in frequent interruptions, with the sound and connection stopping multiple times during the live stream <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:36">[01:09:36]</a>.

Despite user suggestions that a VPN might help, the speaker noted that the interruptions would persist <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:05">[00:04:05]</a>. Moving "two blocks away" to a cafe would have resolved the problem, but they were required to be in the affected area <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:12">[00:04:12]</a>.

### General Internet Situation in the City <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:25">[00:01:25]</a>
The speaker observed that generally, there is "no problem-free internet in the city" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:25">[00:01:25]</a>. However, their new budget-friendly accommodation provided "wonderful internet" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:19">[00:01:19]</a>.

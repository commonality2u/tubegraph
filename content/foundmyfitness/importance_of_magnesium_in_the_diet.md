Magnesium is a crucial micronutrient found abundantly in green leafy vegetables, where it resides at the center of the chlorophyll molecule, responsible for giving plants their green color <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:09">[00:01:09]</a>. Despite its importance, a significant portion of the U.S. population does not meet the recommended daily intake for magnesium <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:23">[00:01:23]</a>.

## Recommended Intake and Deficiency
The adequate daily intake for magnesium is currently set at approximately 350 to 400 milligrams <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:30">[00:01:30]</a>. However, around 45% of the U.S. population fails to meet this requirement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:23">[00:01:23]</a>. Athletes may require even higher amounts, potentially 10 to 15 percent more than the general recommendation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:32">[00:01:32]</a>. This widespread [[magnesium_deficiency_and_its_health_implications | magnesium deficiency]] highlights the need for increased dietary awareness.

## [[Magnesiums Role in Metabolic Processes and Aging | Metabolic and Cellular Functions]]
Magnesium is indispensable for over 300 different enzymes in the body to function correctly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:37">[00:01:37]</a>. Its roles are fundamental to life itself:

*   **Energy Production:** Magnesium is essential for enzymes involved in the production and utilization of ATP (adenosine triphosphate), the cell's energetic currency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:40">[00:01:40]</a>. ATP must bind to a magnesium ion to become biologically active <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:48">[00:01:48]</a>. The body prioritizes allocating available magnesium to these energy-producing enzymes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:51">[00:01:51]</a>.
*   **DNA Repair and Stability:** [[Magnesiums impact on brain health and aging | Magnesium]] is crucial for DNA repair enzymes, which mend DNA damage resulting from normal metabolic processes and immune function <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:07">[00:02:07]</a>. It is also required for DNA polymerases and DNA ligase, ensuring that mutations do not occur during cell replication <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:23">[00:02:23]</a>. This function makes magnesium very important in preventing cancer-initiating factors like DNA damage and mutations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:33">[00:02:33]</a>.

## Dietary Sources and Intake Example
Green leafy vegetables are excellent sources of magnesium <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:09">[00:01:09]</a>. A daily micronutrient smoothie, including ingredients like eight kale leaves, three cups of spinach, and four rainbow chard leaves, can deliver approximately 588 milligrams of magnesium, exceeding the recommended daily allowance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:12">[00:00:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:45">[00:02:45]</a>. Kale, in particular, is noted for its high magnesium content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:52">[00:02:52]</a>.

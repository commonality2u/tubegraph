## General Role in Immune Function

`[[role_of_specific_vitamins_in_disease_prevention_and_cognitive_function | Vitamin C]]` is highly concentrated in immune cells, with neutrophils and leukocytes having roughly 50 to 100 times higher `[[role_of_specific_vitamins_in_disease_prevention_and_cognitive_function | Vitamin C]]` concentrations than plasma <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:23">[00:21:23]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:55">[01:02:55]</a>.

It performs several crucial functions:
*   **Protection from Reactive Oxygen Species (ROS)**: Immune cells generate large quantities of ROS to kill foreign invaders, but these oxidants can also damage the immune cells themselves <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:01">[00:22:01]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:44">[00:36:44]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:33">[01:03:33]</a>. High `[[role_of_specific_vitamins_in_disease_prevention_and_cognitive_function | Vitamin C]]` levels in immune cells protect them from this damage while promoting ROS production needed for neutrophil function <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:49">[00:21:49]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:45">[01:03:45]</a>.
*   **T-Cell Proliferation**: `[[role_of_specific_vitamins_in_disease_prevention_and_cognitive_function | Vitamin C]]` appears to boost the immune system by promoting the proliferation of T cells and preventing T cell death <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:01">[00:22:01]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:03">[00:22:03]</a>.
*   **Cytokine Modulation**: It might modulate cytokine levels, which are important signaling molecules produced in response to inflammation and infection <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:26">[00:22:26]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:31">[00:22:31]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:59">[01:03:59]</a>. ## Specific Viral Defenses

### Common Cold
*   A meta-analysis of 23 clinical studies found that `[[role_of_specific_vitamins_in_disease_prevention_and_cognitive_function | Vitamin C]]` supplementation of at least 2 grams per day during a cold had a greater benefit than 1 gram per day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:27">[00:23:27]</a>.
*   In children under 16, cold duration decreased 26% with 2 grams daily and 17% with 1 gram daily <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:55">[00:23:55]</a>. In adults, it decreased 21% with 2 grams daily and 6% with 1 gram daily <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:45">[00:23:45]</a>.
*   A prophylactic (preventive) and therapeutic (at onset of symptoms) combination of `[[role_of_specific_vitamins_in_disease_prevention_and_cognitive_function | Vitamin C]]` reduced both symptoms and duration of a cold episode by about half a day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:26">[00:24:26]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:30">[00:24:30]</a>.
*   Endurance athletes and children appear to benefit significantly from prophylactic doses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:31">[00:26:31]</a>. Six trials involving over 600 marathon runners, skiers, and soldiers reported 50% fewer colds among those taking supplemental `[[role_of_specific_vitamins_in_disease_prevention_and_cognitive_function | Vitamin C]]` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:36">[00:26:36]</a>.

### Lower Respiratory System & Infections

It can prevent hydrogen peroxide-producing bacteria from inactivating inflammasomes, which are crucial for detecting and responding to pathogens <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:31">[00:28:31]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:57">[00:28:57]</a>.

*   **Respiratory Disease & Lung Cancer**: A large study showed that people with the highest blood `[[role_of_specific_vitamins_in_disease_prevention_and_cognitive_function | Vitamin C]]` concentrations were 15% less likely to develop respiratory conditions and 46% less likely to die of lung cancer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:42">[00:29:42]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:47">[00:34:47]</a>.
*   **Mechanical Ventilation**: In patients requiring mechanical ventilation for more than 24 hours, `[[role_of_specific_vitamins_in_disease_prevention_and_cognitive_function | Vitamin C]]` (often intravenous) reduced the time spent on ventilation by 18% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:31">[00:30:31]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:58">[00:30:58]</a>. This is particularly relevant due to `[[factors_affecting_immune_function_and_susceptibility_to_covid19 | COVID-19]]` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:22">[00:30:22]</a>.
*   **Pneumonia**: Observational studies suggest `[[role_of_specific_vitamins_in_disease_prevention_and_cognitive_function | Vitamin C]]` may prevent or treat pneumonia, especially in individuals with low baseline `[[role_of_specific_vitamins_in_disease_prevention_and_cognitive_function | Vitamin C]]` levels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:36">[00:31:36]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:44">[00:31:44]</a>. Prophylactic treatment reduced pneumonia incidence by 80% in specific populations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:04">[00:32:04]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:08">[00:32:08]</a>.

### General Viral Infections
Intravenous `[[role_of_specific_vitamins_in_disease_prevention_and_cognitive_function | Vitamin C]]` has been used to treat various viral infections, including shingles, cold sores, chickenpox, and mononucleosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:24">[00:51:24]</a>.
*   **Mononucleosis**: A retrospective study found that patients given intravenous `[[role_of_specific_vitamins_in_disease_prevention_and_cognitive_function | Vitamin C]]` showed decreased viral titers and replication <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:01">[00:52:01]</a>.
*   **Myocarditis**: A meta-analysis indicated that intravenous `[[role_of_specific_vitamins_in_disease_prevention_and_cognitive_function | Vitamin C]]` combined with conventional therapy is superior for treating viral myocarditis in children <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:23">[00:52:23]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:26">[00:52:26]</a>. ### Sepsis
Sepsis, a life-threatening condition resulting from the body's response to bacterial or viral infection, can cause organ injury <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:09">[00:53:09]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:15">[00:53:15]</a>. Low `[[role_of_specific_vitamins_in_disease_prevention_and_cognitive_function | Vitamin C]]` levels are common in sepsis patients and may predict increased risk for organ failure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:28">[00:53:28]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:31">[00:53:31]</a>. The virus responsible for `[[factors_affecting_immune_function_and_susceptibility_to_covid19 | COVID-19]]` (Sars-CoV-2) is suggested to cause sepsis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:20">[00:53:20]</a>.

*   Experimental studies in mice showed that intravenous `[[role_of_specific_vitamins_in_disease_prevention_and_cognitive_function | Vitamin C]]` lessened the pro-inflammatory state associated with sepsis and preserved organ function <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:41">[00:53:41]</a>.
*   Clinical studies have shown that intravenous `[[role_of_specific_vitamins_in_disease_prevention_and_cognitive_function | Vitamin C]]` can reduce hospital deaths among sepsis patients <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:50">[00:53:50]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:53">[00:53:53]</a>.
    *   One study found that intravenous `[[role_of_specific_vitamins_in_disease_prevention_and_cognitive_function | Vitamin C]]` (50 mg/kg every six hours for 96 hours) led to fewer deaths (less than 30% vs. nearly 50% in placebo group), more ventilator-free days, higher ICU transfer rates, and more hospital-free days <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:05">[00:54:05]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:22">[00:54:22]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:30">[00:54:30]</a>.
    *   Another study combining intravenous `[[role_of_specific_vitamins_in_disease_prevention_and_cognitive_function | Vitamin C]]` (6 grams four times daily for four days) with hydrocortisone and thiamine reduced mortality from roughly 40% to 8% and improved organ function <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:50">[00:54:50]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:08">[00:55:08]</a>.

## Mechanisms of Action in Viral Defense
`[[role_of_specific_vitamins_in_disease_prevention_and_cognitive_function | Vitamin C]]` influences `[[vitamin_d_and_immune_system_function | immune function]]` through various mechanisms:
*   **Neutrophil Function**: It facilitates neutrophil migration to infection sites and supports overall neutrophil function, while also protecting them from damage caused by reactive oxygen species <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:05">[01:03:05]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:26">[01:03:26]</a>.
*   **Cytokine and Interferon Production**: It modulates cytokine levels, which are crucial signaling molecules produced in response to inflammation and infection <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:59">[01:03:59]</a>. It is also involved in the production of interferon, a cytokine that signals the body to initiate antiviral defenses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:22">[01:04:22]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:25">[01:04:25]</a>.
*   **Inhibiting Viral Replication**: In vitro studies have shown that `[[role_of_specific_vitamins_in_disease_prevention_and_cognitive_function | Vitamin C]]` can interfere with the replication of viral particles. For example, it decreased parameters of virus production by 99% in infected cells and dose-dependently killed influenza viruses, with high concentrations fully blocking viral replication <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:56">[01:06:56]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:08">[01:07:08]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:24">[01:07:24]</a>.
*   **Protection against Viral Infection Mortality**: A study in `[[role_of_specific_vitamins_in_disease_prevention_and_cognitive_function | Vitamin C]]`-deficient mice demonstrated that these mice had increased pro-inflammatory cytokines and significantly higher viral titers in their lungs, leading to death. Supplementation with `[[role_of_specific_vitamins_in_disease_prevention_and_cognitive_function | Vitamin C]]` prior to viral infection protected the mice from death <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:41">[01:07:41]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:00">[01:08:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:06">[01:08:06]</a>.

## Safety & Considerations
*   **Oral Doses**: The tolerable upper intake limit for oral `[[role_of_specific_vitamins_in_disease_prevention_and_cognitive_function | Vitamin C]]` is 2,000 milligrams per day, mainly to reduce the risk of gastrointestinal issues like diarrhea <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:35">[01:08:35]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:42">[01:08:42]</a>. However, doses up to 10 grams per day have not been scientifically shown to be toxic or detrimental to health in adults <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:04">[01:11:04]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:07">[01:09:07]</a>.
*   **Intravenous Doses**: Intravenous `[[role_of_specific_vitamins_in_disease_prevention_and_cognitive_function | Vitamin C]]` is generally well-tolerated with low toxicity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:17">[01:09:17]</a>. Common side effects include mild to moderate nausea, headache, and dry mouth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:20">[01:09:20]</a>.
*   **Contraindications**:
    *   Individuals with hemochromatosis (abnormally high iron levels) should exercise caution due to `[[role_of_specific_vitamins_in_disease_prevention_and_cognitive_function | Vitamin C]]`'s ability to improve dietary iron absorption <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:00">[01:09:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:09">[01:09:09]</a>.
    *   People with glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase (G6PD) deficiency are at risk of hemolysis (rupturing of red blood cells) with high doses (40 grams or higher) of intravenous `[[role_of_specific_vitamins_in_disease_prevention_and_cognitive_function | Vitamin C]]` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:41">[01:09:41]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:55">[01:09:55]</a>. Lower intravenous doses (1-10 grams) may actually reduce hemolysis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:02">[01:10:02]</a>.
*   **Kidney Stone Risk**: While some studies suggest a perceived risk of kidney stones with high oral `[[role_of_specific_vitamins_in_disease_prevention_and_cognitive_function | Vitamin C]]` doses due to oxalate formation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:22">[01:10:22]</a>, this risk is generally low in healthy individuals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:43">[01:13:43]</a>. It might be a concern for patients with pre-existing kidney impairment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:45">[01:13:45]</a>.
    *   Large cohort studies showed a statistically increased relative risk but no actual increase in kidney stone events, suggesting compensatory mechanisms in the body <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:10">[01:11:10]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:21">[01:12:21]</a>.

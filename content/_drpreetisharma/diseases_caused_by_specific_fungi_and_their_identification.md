[[mycology_common_fungi_and_their_identification_markers | Mycology]] is the study of fungi, including their different types and etiologies important for diagnosis and treatment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:10">[00:10:10]</a>. Fungi have gained significant importance, especially since the COVID-19 pandemic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:00">[00:29:00]</a>.

## [[staining_techniques_for_studying_fungi | Staining Techniques for Fungi]]
Various [[staining_techniques_for_studying_fungi | stains]] are used to identify fungi, each revealing distinct morphological features <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:00">[01:04:00]</a>:

*   **Gomori Methenamine Silver (GMS) Stain**: Fungal hyphae appear black <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:00">[01:12:00]</a>. The presence of "silver" in the stain name indicates a blackish color <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:39:00">[01:39:00]</a>.
*   **Periodic Acid-Schiff (PAS) Stain**: Fungal hyphae appear pink <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:53:00">[01:53:00]</a>.
*   **Calcofluor White**: A fluorescent dye, causing organisms to appear fluorescent against a dark background <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:09:00">[02:09:00]</a>.
*   **Lactophenol Cotton Blue (LPCB)**: Best for studying the morphology of fungal cultures grown on media like Sabouraud's Dextrose Agar (SDA) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:00">[00:56:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:59:00">[02:59:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:09:00">[03:09:00]</a>. The stain itself is blue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:13:00">[03:13:00]</a>.
*   **India Ink Stain**: A negative [[staining_techniques_for_studying_fungi | stain]] where the background gets colored, and the organisms remain unstained <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:39:00">[04:39:00]</a>. It is typically used for Cerebrospinal Fluid (CSF) samples in cases of *Cryptococcus* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:49:00">[04:49:00]</a>.
*   **Mucicarmine Stain**: Used for biopsy specimens of *Cryptococcus*, where the organism appears rose pink <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:06:00">[05:06:00]</a>.

## [[dimorphic_fungi_identification_and_examples | Dimorphic Fungi]]
[[dimorphic_fungi_identification_and_examples | Dimorphic fungi]] exist in two forms ("di" means two, "morphic" means morphology): yeast and mold <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:38:00">[07:38:00]</a>.

*   **Yeast Form**: Seen at 37째C (heat) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:13:00">[08:13:00]</a>.
*   **Mold Form (Hyphae)**: Seen at 25째C (cold) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:16:00">[08:16:00]</a>.

[[dimorphic_fungi_identification_and_examples | Dimorphic fungi]] are notable for causing systemic involvement, often affecting organs like the lungs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:23:00">[11:23:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:35:00">[11:35:00]</a>.

Common examples of [[dimorphic_fungi_identification_and_examples | dimorphic fungi]] include (mnemonic: **B**ody **H**eat **P**robably **C**hanges **S**hape) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:01:00">[09:01:00]</a>:

*   **B**lastomyces <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:16:00">[09:16:00]</a>
*   **H**istoplasma <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:18:00">[09:18:00]</a>
*   **P**enicillium marneffei (also known as *Talaromyces marneffei*) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:20:00">[09:20:00]</a> and *Paracoccidioides* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:25:00">[09:25:00]</a>
*   **C**occidioides <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:26:00">[09:26:00]</a>
*   **S**porothrix schenckii <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:30:00">[09:30:00]</a>

*Cryptococcus neoformans* is **not** a [[dimorphic_fungi_identification_and_examples | dimorphic fungus]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:29:00">[07:29:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:23:00">[10:23:00]</a>.

### Specific Fungi and Associated Diseases

#### Cryptococcus Neoformans
*   **Disease**: Cryptococcal meningitis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:46:00">[03:46:00]</a>.
*   **Samples**: Cerebrospinal Fluid (CSF) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:01:00">[04:01:00]</a> or biopsy tissue (e.g., brain biopsy) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:16:00">[04:16:00]</a>.
*   **Identification**:
    *   CSF: India ink [[staining_techniques_for_studying_fungi | negative staining]] shows unstained organisms against a dark background <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:41:00">[04:41:00]</a>.
    *   Biopsy: Mucicarmine [[staining_techniques_for_studying_fungi | stain]] shows rose-pink organisms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:06:00">[05:06:00]</a>.
    *   Diagnostic Test: Latex agglutination test for final [[pathological_conditions_and_diagnostic_techniques | diagnosis]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:56:00">[06:56:00]</a>.

#### Blastomyces
*   **Other Names**: North American blastomycosis, Gilchrist disease, Chicago disease <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:04:00">[11:04:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:33:00">[17:33:00]</a>.
*   **Identification**: Classical figure of eight appearance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:55:00">[11:55:00]</a> due to broad-based budding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:29:00">[12:29:00]</a>. Often seen black with GMS [[staining_techniques_for_studying_fungi | stain]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:42:00">[12:42:00]</a>.

#### Histoplasma
*   **Other Names**: Ohio's disease, Darling's disease <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:13:00">[14:13:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:54:00">[17:54:00]</a>.
*   **Similarities to Tuberculosis**: Can cause lung involvement, caseating granuloma, and caseous necrosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:31:00">[13:31:00]</a>.
*   **Identification**:
    *   Yeast form (37째C): Tiny, 2-4 microns, seen inside macrophages as dot-like structures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:40:00">[14:40:00]</a>.
    *   Mold form (25째C): Shows tuberculate macroconidia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:17:00">[16:17:00]</a>.

#### Paracoccidioides
*   **Other Names**: South American blastomycosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:05:00">[17:05:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:21:00">[18:21:00]</a>.
*   **Identification**:
    *   Mickey Mouse appearance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:09:00">[19:09:00]</a>.
    *   Mariner's wheel or pilot wheel appearance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:27:00">[19:27:00]</a>.
    *   Typically black when stained with GMS <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:35:00">[19:35:00]</a>.

#### Penicillium Marneffei (Talaromyces Marneffei)
*   **Natural Reservoir**: Bamboo rats <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:12:00">[20:12:00]</a>.
*   **Identification**: Broom or brush appearance of conidia in culture (LPCB [[staining_techniques_for_studying_fungi | stain]]) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:38:00">[20:38:00]</a>.

#### Coccidioides
*   **Other Names**: Desert rheumatism, Valley fever, Arizona fever <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:30:00">[21:30:00]</a>. Associated with history of caves, deserts, or valleys <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:36:00">[21:36:00]</a>. Can involve joints and cause fever <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:54:00">[21:54:00]</a>.
*   **Identification**: Barrel-shaped arthrospores seen in culture (LPCB [[staining_techniques_for_studying_fungi | stain]]) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:28:00">[22:28:00]</a>.

#### Sporothrix Schenckii (Sporotrichosis)
*   **Other Names**: Rose gardener's disease <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:06:00">[23:06:00]</a>. Often linked to a thorn prick history while working in rose gardens <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:25:00">[23:25:00]</a>.
*   **Clinical Features**: Lymphocutaneous disorder, with multiple cutaneous lesions developing along lymphatic channels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:45:00">[23:45:00]</a>.
*   **Identification**:
    *   Microscopically: Cigar bodies (round yeast) surrounded by starry projections form an asteroid body <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:31:00">[24:31:00]</a>.
    *   Culture morphology (LPCB [[staining_techniques_for_studying_fungi | stain]]): Rosettes of conidia, resembling a rose flower <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:50:00">[25:50:00]</a>.

#### Chromoblastomycosis
*   **Other Names**: Verrucous dermatitis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:41:00">[32:41:00]</a>.
*   **Clinical Features**: Causes cauliflower-like warty lesions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:01:00">[33:01:00]</a>. Often seen in forest workers who walk barefoot <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:07:00">[33:07:07]</a>.
*   **Identification**: Presence of "chromo" (color) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:28:00">[33:28:00]</a>, specifically copper penny bodies (also known as muriform, medlar, or sclerotic bodies) due to their copper-like pigment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:36:00">[33:36:00]</a>.

#### Aspergillus
*   **Culture Characteristics (Sabouraud's Dextrose Agar)**:
    *   *Aspergillus niger*: Black colonies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:13:00">[35:13:00]</a>.
    *   *Aspergillus fumigatus*: Smoky green colonies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:32:00">[35:32:00]</a>.
    *   *Aspergillus flavus*: Yellow-green colonies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:43:00">[35:43:00]</a>.
*   **Microscopic Identification (LPCB Stain)**:
    *   *Aspergillus niger*: Maintains black color <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:21:00">[37:21:00]</a>.
    *   *Aspergillus fumigatus*: Conidia arise only from the upper part of the vesicle, like fumes rising <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:50:00">[37:50:00]</a>.
    *   *Aspergillus flavus*: Conidia arise from all around the vesicle, like petals of a flower <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="38:24:00">[38:24:00]</a>.
*   **Hyphae Characteristics**: *Aspergillus* shows acute-angled branching and septate hyphae (A for acute angle, SPE for septate) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="40:48:00">[40:48:00]</a>. These hyphae are typically thin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="42:24:00">[42:24:00]</a>.

#### Mucor (Mucormycosis)
*   **Hyphae Characteristics**: Shows perpendicular (90-degree) branching and aseptate hyphae <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:44:00">[41:44:00]</a>. Hyphae are very broad, described as "motor" or "ribbon-like" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="42:29:00">[42:29:00]</a>.

#### Pneumocystis Karani (Pneumocystis Jirovecii)
*   **Disease**: Pneumocystis carinii pneumonia (PCP) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="43:57:00">[43:57:00]</a>, also known as plasma cell pneumonia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="44:18:00">[44:18:00]</a>.
*   **Clinical Context**: Occurs in immunocompromised patients, such as those with HIV or transplantation recipients <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="44:46:00">[44:46:00]</a>.
*   **Morphological Feature**: Exudate intra-alveolar with abundant plasma cells <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="43:43:00">[43:43:00]</a>.
*   **Identification**: Only stained with Gomori Methenamine Silver (GMS) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="45:01:00">[45:01:00]</a>, showing a crushed ping-pong ball, hat, or cup and saucer appearance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="45:26:00">[45:26:00]</a>.

#### Rhinosporidium Seeberi (Rhinosporidiosis)
*   **Clinical Features**: Patients present with nasal polyps <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="47:04:00">[47:04:00]</a>.
*   **Classification**: Formerly considered a fungus, now categorized as an aquatic parasite or protozoan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="47:30:00">[47:30:00]</a>.
*   **Identification**: Under microscope, large round sporangia containing endospores are seen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="48:00:00">[48:00:00]</a>.
*   **Cultivation**: Cannot be cultivated on any medium <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="48:36:00">[48:36:00]</a>.

#### Candida (Candida Albicans, Candida Dubliniensis)
*   **Clinical Features**: Can cause endophthalmitis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="49:53:00">[49:53:00]</a>.
*   **Identification**: Shows two forms: budding forms and pseudo-hyphae forms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="50:16:00">[50:16:00]</a>. Pseudo-hyphae are elongated budding forms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="50:56:00">[50:56:00]</a>.
*   **Test**: Reynolds-Braude phenomenon (germ tube test) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="51:21:00">[51:21:00]</a>. *Candida albicans* and *Candida dubliniensis* are positive for this test <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="52:25:00">[52:25:00]</a>. In this test, *Candida* colonies are incubated in serum for two hours, showing a germ tube originating from the organism <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="51:51:00">[51:51:00]</a>.

## Dermatophytes
[[mycology_common_fungi_and_their_identification_markers | Dermatophytes]] are fungi that primarily cause infections of the skin, hair, and nails <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="54:14:00">[54:14:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="54:20:00">[54:20:00]</a>. They generally do not cause systemic infections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="54:10:00">[54:10:00]</a>.

The three main [[mycology_common_fungi_and_their_identification_markers | dermatophytes]] are (mnemonic: **TME**):

*   **T**richophyton <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="55:04:00">[55:04:00]</a>
*   **M**icrosporum <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="55:10:00">[55:10:00]</a>
*   **E**pidermophyton <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="55:13:00">[55:13:00]</a>

### Involvement of Skin, Hair, and Nails:
*   **Trichophyton**: Infects skin, hair, and nails (tri for three) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="55:41:00">[55:41:00]</a>.
*   **Microsporum**: Infects skin and hair (hair is thinner, hence "micro") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="56:09:00">[56:09:00]</a>.
*   **Epidermophyton**: Infects skin and nails (ends with 'n' for nail) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="56:12:00">[56:12:00]</a>.

### Microscopic Identification (LPCB Stain)
[[mycology_common_fungi_and_their_identification_markers | Dermatophytes]] are differentiated based on their macroconidia (larger spores) and microconidia (smaller spores) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="57:32:00">[57:32:00]</a>.

*   **Microconidia**: Arranged in a descending order of abundance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="57:51:00">[57:51:00]</a>.
    *   *Trichophyton*: Maximum microconidia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="57:57:00">[57:57:00]</a>.
    *   *Microsporum*: Fewer microconidia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="58:00:00">[58:00:00]</a>.
    *   *Epidermophyton*: Least or absent microconidia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="58:03:00">[58:03:00]</a>.
*   **Macroconidia** (mnemonic: **PSC** for TME):
    *   *Trichophyton* (T): **P**encil-shaped macroconidia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="59:16:00">[59:16:00]</a>.
    *   *Microsporum* (M): **S**pindle-shaped (fusiform) macroconidia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="59:19:00">[59:19:00]</a>.
    *   *Epidermophyton* (E): **C**lub-shaped macroconidia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="59:20:00">[59:20:00]</a>.

#### Malassezia Furfur (Tinea Versicolor)
*   **Clinical Features**: Causes hypopigmented lesions on the trunk (back, shoulder, neck) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:02">[01:03:02]</a>.
*   **Identification**:
    *   KOH mount (10% KOH): Used for samples containing keratin (skin, hair, nails) to digest keratin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:57">[01:03:57]</a>. Shows "spaghetti and meatball" appearance, where dot-like yeast forms are "meatballs" and long hyphae are "spaghetti" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:03">[01:05:03]</a>.
    *   Culture: Sabouraud's Dextrose Agar with olive oil (SDA + olive oil) because *Malassezia furfur* is lipophilic (fat-loving) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:40">[01:05:40]</a>. Colonies show a "fried egg" or "sunny side up" appearance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:42">[01:05:42]</a>.

## [[betadglucan_and_galactomannan_testing_for_fungal_infections | Fungal Infection Testing]]
Two important markers used for [[diagnosis_and_identification_of_bacterial_infections | fungal infections]] include:

*   **Beta-D-Glucan**: A normal cell wall component of most fungi <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:43">[01:07:43]</a>. It can be used for various fungi like *Candida*, *Aspergillus*, *Histoplasma*, *Fusarium*, *Dermatophytes*, and *Pneumocystis gyrovici* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:06">[01:08:06]</a>.
    *   **Negative for**: *Blastomyces*, *Mucor*, and *Cryptococcus* (mnemonic: **BMC**) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:18">[01:08:18]</a>.
*   **Galactomannan**: Primarily used for *Aspergillus* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:50">[01:07:50]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:53">[01:07:53]</a>.

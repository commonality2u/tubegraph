Both *Neisseria meningitidis* and *Neisseria gonorrhoeae* are Gram-negative cocci, which is an exception to the general rule that all cocci are Gram-positive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:32:00">[02:32:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:44:00">[02:44:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:01:00">[03:01:00]</a>. They are part of the "venom" mnemonic for Gram-negative cocci, which includes *Veillonella*, *Neisseria*, and *Moraxella* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:47:00">[02:47:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:50:00">[02:50:00]</a>. Both are catalase-positive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:52:00">[25:52:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:56:00">[25:56:00]</a> and oxidase-positive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:23:00">[26:23:00]</a>.

## Differentiation of [[neisseria_species_and_their_differentiation | Neisseria Species]]

### *Neisseria meningitidis*
*   **Habitat:** Primarily found in the nasopharynx <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:38">[01:08:38]</a>.
*   **Diseases:** Causes meningitis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:49">[01:08:49]</a>.
*   **Fermentation:** Ferments both glucose and maltose <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:45">[01:09:45]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:51">[01:09:51]</a>.
*   **Shape:** Lens-shaped <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:36">[01:10:36]</a>.
*   **Capsule:** Possesses a capsule <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:37">[01:10:37]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:02">[01:11:02]</a>.
    *   Capsular serotypes include A, B, C, W135, and Y <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:18">[01:11:18]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:06">[01:12:06]</a>.
    *   Vaccines protect against A, C, W135, and Y serotypes, but generally *not* serotype B <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:31">[01:11:31]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:47">[01:11:47]</a>.

### *Neisseria gonorrhoeae*
*   **Habitat:** Primarily found in the genitourinary tract <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:00">[01:09:00]</a>.
*   **Diseases:** Causes the STD gonorrhea <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:55">[01:08:55]</a>.
*   **Fermentation:** Ferments only glucose <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:40">[01:09:40]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:01">[01:10:01]</a>.
*   **Shape:** Bean-shaped or kidney-shaped <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:14">[01:07:14]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:42">[01:10:42]</a>.
*   **Capsule:** Does not have a capsule <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:14">[01:11:14]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:15">[01:11:15]</a>.

## Virulence Factors

Common virulence factors for both *Neisseria meningitidis* and *Neisseria gonorrhoeae* include:
*   Outer membrane proteins (Opa) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:20">[01:12:20]</a>.
*   IgA proteases <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:23">[01:12:23]</a>.
*   Endotoxins (LPS/LOS) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:27">[01:12:27]</a>.
*   Pili (Type IV pili) are significant, especially for attachment and twitching motility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:18">[01:13:18]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:22:00">[01:13:22:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:51">[01:13:51]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:52">[01:14:52]</a>. While most cocci are non-motile, *Neisseria* species can exhibit twitching motility due to Type IV pili <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:08">[01:13:08]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:16">[01:13:16]</a>.

M protein is *not* a virulence factor for *Neisseria*; it is associated with *Streptococcus pyogenes* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:27">[01:06:27]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:40">[01:06:40]</a>.

## Clinical Aspects

### *Neisseria gonorrhoeae*
*   **Treatment:** Best treated with Ceftriaxone and Azithromycin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:46">[01:15:46]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:49">[01:15:49]</a>.
*   **Complications:**
    *   **Fitz-Hugh-Curtis Syndrome:** A perihepatitis (inflammation around the liver) that can cause a "violin string appearance" on radiology <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:16">[01:17:16]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:21">[01:17:21]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:24">[01:17:24]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:48">[01:17:48]</a>.
    *   Water-can perineum <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:02">[01:17:02]</a>.

### *Neisseria meningitidis*
*   **Complications:**
    *   **Waterhouse-Friderichsen Syndrome:** The most dreaded complication, characterized by adrenal hemorrhage, leading to a fatal medical emergency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:50">[01:13:50]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:35">[01:16:35]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:40">[01:16:40]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:44">[01:16:44]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:49">[01:16:49]</a>. This is associated with meningococcemia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:53">[01:16:53]</a>.
*   **Risk Factors for Recurrent Infections:** Increased risk of recurrent *Neisseria meningitidis* infections is associated with deficiencies in terminal complement components (C5-C9), which form the Membrane Attack Complex (MAC) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:49">[01:18:49]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:12">[01:19:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:17">[01:19:17]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:47">[01:20:47]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:52">[01:20:52]</a>. The most common complement deficiency in humans is C2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:21:07">[01:21:07]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:21:09">[01:21:09]</a>.

*Note: Other complement deficiencies mentioned, but not directly related to recurrent Neisseria infections, include early complement protein deficiency (C1, C2, C4) associated with SLE <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:42">[01:19:42]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:45">[01:19:45]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:48">[01:19:48]</a>, C1 esterase inhibitor deficiency causing hereditary angioedema <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:56">[01:19:56]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:03">[01:20:03]</a>, and deficiencies in DAF (CD55) and MIRL (CD59) leading to Paroxysmal Nocturnal Hemoglobinuria (PNH) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:17">[01:20:17]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:23">[01:20:23]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:30">[01:20:30]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:36">[01:20:36]</a>.*

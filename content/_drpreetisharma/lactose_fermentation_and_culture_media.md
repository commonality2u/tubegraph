Lactose fermentation is a key characteristic used in microbiology to differentiate bacteria, particularly within the Enterobacteriaceae family <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:42">[00:05:42]</a>. Organisms capable of fermenting lactose produce acidic byproducts, which, in the presence of specific indicators in [[culture_media_for_bacteria | culture media]], result in a visible color change.

## Key Organisms

The primary lactose-fermenting organisms discussed are:
*   *Escherichia coli* (*E. coli*) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:00">[00:06:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:49">[00:07:49]</a>
*   *Klebsiella* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:49">[00:07:49]</a>

## Culture Media for Lactose Fermentation

Several [[selective_culture_media_and_its_application_in_microbiology | selective culture media]] and [[media_and_diagnostic_tests_for_bacteria_identification | diagnostic tests]] are used to identify lactose fermenters, each with distinct compositions and resulting appearances.

### MacConkey Agar

MacConkey Agar is a widely used medium for detecting lactose fermentation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:35">[00:23:35]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:06">[00:24:06]</a>.
*   **Composition:** It contains peptone, agar, lactose, and bile salts (like sodium taurocholate) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:49">[00:24:49]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:02">[00:25:02]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:32">[00:25:32]</a>.
*   **Indicator:** Neutral red is the indicator used in MacConkey Agar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:22">[00:25:22]</a>.
*   **Characteristics:**
    *   **Differential Medium:** It differentiates bacteria based on their ability to ferment lactose <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:07">[00:25:07]</a>.
    *   **Indicator Medium:** It changes color in the presence of fermentation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:26">[00:25:26]</a>.
    *   **Selective Medium:** The presence of bile salts makes it selective, inhibiting the growth of most Gram-positive organisms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:38">[00:25:38]</a>.
*   **Result:** Lactose-fermenting colonies appear pink on MacConkey Agar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:13">[00:24:13]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:56">[00:25:56]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:05">[00:53:05]</a>.
    *   *E. coli* produces pink colonies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:01">[00:30:01]</a>.
    *   *Klebsiella* forms mucoid, shiny pink colonies due to its capsule <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:44">[00:22:44]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:04">[00:30:04]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:48">[00:30:48]</a>.

### Cysteine-Lactose-Electrolyte-Deficient (CLED) Agar

CLED Agar is another medium used for lactose fermentation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:08">[00:26:08]</a>.
*   **Indicator:** Bromo thymol blue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:27">[00:26:27]</a>.
*   **Result:** Lactose fermenters typically produce yellow colonies on CLED Agar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:16">[00:26:16]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:02">[00:53:02]</a>.
*   **Advantage over MacConkey:** CLED Agar is considered less inhibitory, allowing the growth of both Gram-negative and Gram-positive organisms, making it suitable for polymicrobial samples like urine <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:48">[00:27:48]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:04">[00:28:04]</a>.

### Eosin Methylene Blue (EMB) Agar

EMB Agar is also used for lactose fermentation testing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:54">[00:28:54]</a>.
*   **Result:** *E. coli* on EMB Agar produces colonies with a characteristic greenish metallic sheen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:00">[00:29:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:06">[00:29:06]</a>.

## Other Fermentation Reactions

While lactose fermentation is prominent, other sugars are also used for bacterial identification:
*   **Mannitol Fermentation:** Typically results in a yellow color change. Phenol red is the indicator used in Mannitol Salt Agar, which is used for [[staphylococcus_aureus_and_mannitol_fermentation | Staphylococcus aureus and Mannitol fermentation]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:01">[00:51:01]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:12">[00:51:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:24">[00:51:24]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:38">[00:51:38]</a>.
*   **Sucrose Fermentation:** *Vibrio cholerae* ferments sucrose. This is tested using TCBS (Thiosulfate Citrate Bile Salt Sucrose) media, which also produces a yellow color with bromothymol blue as the indicator <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:08">[00:50:08]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:50">[00:51:50]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:05">[00:52:05]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:12">[00:52:12]</a>.
*   **Sorbitol Fermentation:** Most *E. coli* strains are sorbitol fermenters. However, *Enterohemorrhagic E. coli* O157:H7 is a notable exception, being "smack negative" (not fermenting sorbitol) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:21">[00:18:21]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:12">[00:19:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:26">[00:19:26]</a>.

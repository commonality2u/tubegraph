## Gram Staining and Initial Classification
Bacterial classification often begins with Gram staining, which reveals the basic morphology and Gram reaction of cocci (spherical bacteria) or bacilli (rod-shaped bacteria) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:10">[00:02:10]</a>. Most cocci are Gram-positive, appearing purple, with the exception of *Veillonella*, *Neisseria*, and *Moraxella*, which are Gram-negative <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:47">[00:02:47]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:01">[00:03:01]</a>. Observing Gram-positive cocci in clusters helps narrow down the chapter for diagnosis to organisms like *Staphylococcus* or *Micrococcus* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:24">[00:03:24]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:41">[00:03:41]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:33">[00:09:33]</a>.

## Catalase Test
The catalase test is a common method for identifying bacteria <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:44">[00:03:44]</a>.

### Principle
The test detects the presence of the enzyme catalase, which breaks down hydrogen peroxide (H₂O₂) into water and oxygen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:04">[00:22:04]</a>. The release of oxygen gas results in the formation of bubbles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:06">[00:22:06]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:09">[00:22:09]</a>.

### Procedure
A patient's sample (e.g., skin infection, sputum) is taken, and hydrogen peroxide (H₂O₂) is added to it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:45">[00:21:45]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:55">[00:21:55]</a>. If bubbles are observed, the test is positive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:37">[00:21:37]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:22">[00:22:22]</a>.

### Interpretation
*   **Catalase Positive:** Bubbles form <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:22">[00:22:22]</a>.
    *   **Common organisms**:
        *   *Staphylococcus* species (e.g., *Staphylococcus aureus*) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:28">[00:20:28]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:30">[00:20:30]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:33">[00:23:33]</a>.
        *   *Micrococcus* species <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:12">[00:26:12]</a>.
        *   Pigment-producing bacteria: *Staphylococcus aureus* (golden pigment, Au from periodic table) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:19">[00:24:19]</a>, *Serratia marcescens* (red pigment) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:35">[00:24:35]</a>, *Pseudomonas aeruginosa* (blue-green pigment, causes green nail syndrome) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:42">[00:24:42]</a>.
        *   Fungal organisms: *Aspergillus*, *Candida*, *Cryptococcus* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:21">[00:25:21]</a>.
        *   Enterobacteriaceae family and *E. coli* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:37">[00:25:37]</a>.
        *   *Bacillus anthracis* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:46">[00:25:46]</a>.
        *   *Neisseria gonorrhoeae* and *Neisseria meningitidis* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:10">[01:10:10]</a>.
        *   *Mycobacterium tuberculosis* (MTB) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:05">[00:26:05]</a>.
*   **Catalase Negative:** No bubbles form <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:26">[00:22:26]</a>.
    *   *Streptococcus* species <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:28">[00:20:28]</a>.

## Coagulase Test
The coagulase test differentiates *Staphylococcus aureus* from other *Staphylococcus* species.

### Types
There are two main types:
1.  **Tube Coagulase Test:** Detects free coagulase <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:57">[00:29:57]</a>. This is typically done in a test tube and is considered positive if coagulation occurs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:48">[00:29:48]</a>.
2.  **Slide Coagulase Test:** Detects bound coagulase (clumping factor) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:29">[00:30:29]</a>.

### Principle
Coagulase is an enzyme that causes plasma to clot <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:02">[00:29:02]</a>. *Staphylococcus aureus* can produce both free and bound coagulase <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:06">[00:30:06]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:12">[00:30:12]</a>.

### Interpretation
*   **Coagulase Positive:** *Staphylococcus aureus*, *Staphylococcus intermedius*, *Staphylococcus hyicus* show both slide and tube coagulase positive results <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:51">[00:30:51]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:57">[00:30:57]</a>.
    *   *Staphylococcus schleiferi* is free coagulase positive (free like "ferry") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:04">[00:31:04]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:16">[00:31:16]</a>.
    *   *Staphylococcus lugdunensis* is bound coagulase positive (like "lag dance" bound) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:24">[00:31:24]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:30">[00:31:30]</a>.
*   **Coagulase Negative:** *Staphylococcus epidermidis* and *Staphylococcus saprophyticus* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:45">[00:20:45]</a>.

## Glucose Utilization Test
This test determines if a bacterium utilizes glucose oxidatively or fermentatively.

### Hugh and Leifson's Medium
[[bacteriology_culture_media_and_bacteria_identification | Hugh and Leifson's medium]] is used for this test <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:17">[00:06:17]</a>.

### Indicator
Bromothymol blue is the indicator used in this medium <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:41">[00:06:41]</a>.

### Interpretation
*   **Oxidative utilization:** *Micrococcus* species <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:03">[00:05:03]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:31">[00:07:31]</a>. These are also oxidase positive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:34">[00:07:34]</a>.
*   **Fermentative utilization:** *Staphylococcus* species <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:50">[00:04:50]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:53">[00:07:53]</a>. These are oxidase negative <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:39">[00:07:39]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:42">[00:07:42]</a>.

## Other Relevant Tests and Culture Media

### Mannitol Salt Agar
[[selective_and_enriched_media_for_bacterial_growth | Mannitol Salt Agar]] (MSA) is a [[bacterial_culture_media_and_their_uses | selective and differential medium]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:03">[00:34:03]</a>.
*   **Selective:** It contains 7-10% salt, which inhibits the growth of most bacteria, allowing only halophilic (salt-loving) organisms like *Staphylococcus aureus* to grow <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:06">[00:34:06]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:25">[00:34:25]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:50">[00:34:50]</a>.
*   **Differential:** It contains mannitol, and phenol red is the indicator <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:08">[00:35:08]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:02">[00:36:02]</a>.
    *   If mannitol is fermented (e.g., by *Staphylococcus aureus*), the medium turns yellow due to acid production <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:20">[00:37:20]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:32">[00:37:32]</a>.
    *   If mannitol is not fermented, the medium remains red <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:23">[00:37:23]</a>.

### Blood Agar for Hemolysis
Blood agar is used to observe the hemolytic activity of bacteria <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:25">[00:39:25]</a>.
*   **Alpha-hemolysis:** Partial lysis of red blood cells, resulting in a green discoloration around the colony <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:31">[00:39:31]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:38">[00:39:38]</a>. Seen in *Streptococcus viridans* and *Streptococcus pneumoniae* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:10">[00:40:10]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:17">[00:40:17]</a>.
*   **Beta-hemolysis:** Complete lysis of red blood cells, resulting in a clear zone around the colony <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:48">[00:39:48]</a>.
*   **Gamma-hemolysis:** No hemolysis, resulting in no change in the blood agar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:55">[00:39:55]</a>. Seen in *Enterococcus* species and *Streptococcus gallolyticus* (formerly *Streptococcus bovis*) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:56">[00:53:56]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:26">[01:03:26]</a>.

### Bile Esculin Azide Agar
[[bacteriology_culture_media_and_bacteria_identification | Bile Esculin Azide Agar]] (BEAA) is a selective and differential medium. It contains bile salts that inhibit the growth of most bacteria <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:12">[00:54:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:36">[00:55:36]</a>.
*   **Interpretation:** *Enterococcus* species are resistant to 40% bile and can grow on BEAA, producing a black color due to esculin hydrolysis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:29">[00:54:29]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:07">[00:55:07]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:40">[00:55:40]</a>. *Streptococcus gallolyticus* also grows on BEAA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:34">[01:04:34]</a>.

### Camp Test
The [[diagnosis_of_bacterial_infections_using_culture_media | CAMP test]] (Christie-Atkins-Munch-Peterson) is performed on blood agar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:07">[00:46:07]</a>.
*   **Principle:** It detects the production of the CAMP factor by *Streptococcus agalactiae*, which enhances the beta-hemolysis of *Staphylococcus aureus* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:42">[00:48:42]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:02">[00:49:02]</a>.
*   **Interpretation:** A positive [[differentiating_bacterial_fermentation_techniques | CAMP test]] shows an arrowhead-shaped zone of enhanced hemolysis at the intersection of a streak of *Streptococcus agalactiae* (horizontal line) and *Staphylococcus aureus* (vertical line) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:27">[00:47:27]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:29">[00:48:29]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:11">[00:49:11]</a>.
*   **Reverse CAMP Test:** Used for the [[diagnosis_of_bacterial_infections_using_culture_media | diagnosis of *Clostridium perfringens*]] using *Streptococcus agalactiae* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:50">[00:49:50]</a>.

### PYR Test
The Pyrrolidonyl Arylamidase (PYR) test is used to identify *Enterococcus* species <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:29">[00:52:29]</a>. A positive PYR test is characteristic of *Enterococcus faecalis* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:36">[00:57:36]</a>.

### Optochin Sensitivity Test
*   **Interpretation:** *Streptococcus pneumoniae* is optochin-sensitive, while *Streptococcus viridans* is resistant <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:33">[00:41:33]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:53">[00:43:53]</a>.

### Bile Solubility Test
*   **Interpretation:** *Streptococcus pneumoniae* is bile-soluble, while *Streptococcus viridans* is bile-resistant <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:24">[00:41:24]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:46">[00:43:46]</a>.

### Inulin Fermentation
*   **Interpretation:** *Streptococcus pneumoniae* ferments inulin, while *Streptococcus viridans* does not <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:28">[00:41:28]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:49">[00:43:49]</a>.

## Renal Cell Carcinoma (RCC)

[[renal_cell_carcinoma_and_genetic_alterations | Renal Cell Carcinoma (RCC)]] is often suspected clinically due to symptoms like right flank pain and hematuria, and confirmed radiologically and surgically via nephrectomy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:22">[00:02:22]</a>.

### Clear Cell RCC
*   **Most Common** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:04">[00:05:04]</a>.
*   **Histology:** Shows "empty clear cells" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:07">[00:05:07]</a>, appearing as white, clear cells under microscopy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:43">[00:05:43]</a>.
*   **Genetic Alteration:** Deletion of chromosome 3p <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:15">[00:05:15]</a>.
*   **Associated Syndrome:** Von Hippel-Lindau (VHL) syndrome <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:19">[00:05:19]</a>.

### Papillary RCC
*   **Histology:** Tall cells arranged in papillary structures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:57">[00:01:57]</a>.
*   **Associated Bodies:** [[general_and_immunopathology | Psammoma bodies]] are commonly seen in papillary tumors throughout the body <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:17">[00:06:17]</a>.
*   **Genetic Alterations:**
    *   Trisomy 7 and 17 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:18">[00:02:18]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:16">[00:07:16]</a>.
    *   Loss of Y chromosome <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:08">[00:07:08]</a>.
    *   Fumarate hydratase (FH) gene mutation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:33">[00:07:33]</a>.
*   **Associated Conditions:**
    *   History of dialysis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:55">[00:06:55]</a>.
    *   FH gene mutation is associated with hereditary leiomyomatosis and [[renal_cell_carcinoma_and_genetic_alterations | Papillary RCC]] (HLRCC) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:50">[00:07:50]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:18">[00:08:18]</a>.

### Chromophobe RCC
*   **Genetic Alterations:** Multiple chromosomal losses (e.g., chromosomes 1, 5, 7, 10, 13) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:40">[00:10:40]</a>. This is sometimes described as having a "phobia" of chromosomes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:29">[00:10:29]</a>.
*   **Associated Syndrome:** Birt-Hogg-Dub√© syndrome <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:08">[00:11:08]</a>.
*   **Associated Skin Tumor:** Fibrofolliculoma <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:31">[00:11:31]</a>.
*   **Histology:** Shows "plant cells" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:47">[00:11:47]</a> with distinct cell outlines, a dark "raisinoid" nucleus, and a clear perinuclear halo <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:23">[00:12:23]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:17">[00:13:17]</a>.
*   **Stain:** Colloidal iron stain can be used <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:31">[00:13:31]</a>.

### Bellini Duct (Collecting Duct) RCC
*   **Prognosis:** Has the worst prognosis among RCC types <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:16">[00:14:16]</a>.
*   **Origin:** Arises from the collecting duct <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:51">[00:14:51]</a>.

### Medullary RCC
*   **Association:** Strongly associated with sickle cell trait, not sickle cell anemia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:16">[00:15:16]</a>.

### Pediatric RCC (TFE3-associated RCC)
*   **Genetic Alteration:** Xp11 translocation involving the TFE3 gene <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:21">[00:03:21]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:48">[00:15:48]</a>.
*   **Age Group:** Most commonly affects children, but can occur in adults <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:00">[00:04:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:38">[00:04:38]</a>.

## Other Renal Tumors

### Angiomyolipoma (AML)
*   **Components:** Contains blood vessels (angio), muscle (myo), and fat (lipo) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:32">[00:18:32]</a>.
*   **Association:** Associated with tuberous sclerosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:46">[00:17:46]</a>, which also causes cardiac rhabdomyoma <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:08">[00:18:08]</a>.

### Renal Oncocytoma
*   **Nature:** Benign tumor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:59">[00:17:59]</a>.
*   **Histology:** Composed of oncocytes, which are cells rich in mitochondria <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:25">[00:20:25]</a>, giving them a granular eosinophilic cytoplasm.
*   **Gross Appearance:** "Mahogany brown" color <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:04">[00:21:04]</a>.
*   **Key Feature:** Often shows a central stellate scar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:12">[00:21:12]</a>.

## Glomerulonephritis (GN)

### Rapidly Progressive Glomerulonephritis (RPGN)
*   **Clinical Presentation:** Presents as a nephritic syndrome, characterized by "HOAA" features: Hematuria, Oliguria (urine output < 400ml/24hr), Hypertension, and Azotemia (increased BUN) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:30">[00:35:30]</a>.
*   **Histology:** Presence of crescent formation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:00">[00:35:00]</a>.
*   **Crescent Composition:** Primarily composed of fibrin and parietal epithelial cells, but can also contain visceral epithelial cells <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:55">[00:37:55]</a>.
*   **Electron Microscopy:** Shows rupture and wrinkling of the glomerular basement membrane (GBM) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:37">[00:39:37]</a>.

### [[renal_tumor_diagnosis_and_histology | Renal Tumor Diagnosis and Histology]]

This section covers the differentiation of various renal tumor types based on histological findings, genetic alterations, and associations with specific syndromes.

#### Clear Cell RCC
*   **Most Common:** This is the most prevalent type of RCC. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:04">[00:05:04]</a>
*   **Histology:** Characterized by "empty clear cells" which appear white under microscopy due to dissolved lipid and glycogen. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:07">[00:05:07]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:43">[00:05:43]</a>
*   **Genetic Alteration:** Associated with the deletion of chromosome 3p. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:15">[00:05:15]</a>
*   **Associated Syndrome:** Often linked to Von Hippel-Lindau (VHL) syndrome. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:19">[00:05:19]</a>

#### Papillary RCC
*   **Histology:** Features tall cells arranged in papillary structures. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:57">[00:01:57]</a>
*   **Associated Bodies:** The presence of [[general_and_immunopathology | psammoma bodies]] is a characteristic finding in papillary tumors, including those of the kidney. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:17">[00:06:17]</a>
*   **Genetic Alterations:** Common alterations include Trisomy 7 and 17, and loss of the Y chromosome. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:18">[00:02:18]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:08">[00:07:08]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:16">[00:07:16]</a>
*   **Specific Gene Mutation:** Fumarate hydratase (FH) gene mutation leads to Hereditary Leiomyomatosis and [[renal_cell_carcinoma_and_genetic_alterations | Papillary RCC]] (HLRCC), characterized by numerous leiomyomas alongside papillary RCC. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:33">[00:07:33]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:50">[00:07:50]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:18">[00:08:18]</a>
*   **Clinical Association:** Patients with a history of dialysis are at increased risk of papillary RCC. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:55">[00:06:55]</a>

#### Chromophobe RCC
*   **Genetic Alterations:** Exhibits multiple chromosomal losses (e.g., chromosomes 1, 5, 7, 10, 13), described as having a "phobia" of chromosomes. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:29">[00:10:29]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:40">[00:10:40]</a>
*   **Associated Syndrome:** Linked to Birt-Hogg-Dub√© syndrome. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:08">[00:11:08]</a>
*   **Associated Skin Tumor:** Patients with Birt-Hogg-Dub√© syndrome may also present with fibrofolliculomas. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:31">[00:11:31]</a>
*   **Histology:** Cells resemble "plant cells" with distinct cell outlines, a dark, "raisinoid" nucleus, and a clear perinuclear halo. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:47">[00:11:47]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:23">[00:12:23]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:17">[00:13:17]</a>
*   **Special Stain:** Colloidal iron stain can be used for visualization. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:31">[00:13:31]</a>

#### Bellini Duct (Collecting Duct) RCC
*   **Prognosis:** This type of RCC carries the worst prognosis. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:16">[00:14:16]</a>
*   **Origin:** Arises from the collecting ducts of the kidney. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:51">[00:14:51]</a>

#### Medullary RCC
*   **Association:** Strongly associated with sickle cell trait, rather than sickle cell anemia. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:16">[00:15:16]</a>

#### Pediatric RCC (TFE3-associated RCC)
*   **Genetic Alteration:** Characterized by Xp11 translocation involving the TFE3 gene. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:21">[00:03:21]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:48">[00:15:48]</a>
*   **Age Group:** While most commonly seen in children, it can also occur in adults. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:00">[00:04:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:38">[00:04:38]</a>

## Other Renal Tumors

### Angiomyolipoma (AML)

### Renal Oncocytoma
*   **Nature:** A benign tumor of the kidney. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:59">[00:17:59]</a>
*   **Histology:** Composed of oncocytes, which are cells characterized by an abundance of mitochondria, giving them a granular, eosinophilic cytoplasm. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:25">[00:20:25]</a>
*   **Gross Appearance:** Classically described as having a "mahogany brown" color. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:04">[00:21:04]</a>
*   **Key Feature:** Often exhibits a central stellate scar. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:12">[00:21:12]</a>

## [[diabetic_nephropathy_and_pathology | Diabetic Nephropathy]]

[[diabetic_nephropathy_and_pathology | Diabetic Nephropathy]] encompasses a range of renal changes resulting from chronic, uncontrolled diabetes. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:08">[00:24:08]</a>

*   **Kimmelstiel-Wilson (KW) Lesions:** These are also known as Nodular Glomerulosclerosis (NGSS). <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:01">[00:25:01]</a> Histologically, they appear as pink, nodular areas of sclerosis within the glomerulus. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:12">[00:25:12]</a> These lesions are positive with PAS stain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:57">[00:25:57]</a> and are formed due to glycosylation of proteins. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:42">[00:26:42]</a>
*   **Other Features of [[diabetic_nephropathy_pathology_and_special_stains | Diabetic Nephropathy]]:**
    *   Thickening of the glomerular capillaries. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:22">[00:28:22]</a>
    *   Hyaline arteriolosclerosis affecting renal arterioles. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:44">[00:28:44]</a>
    *   Armani-Ebstein lesion: Glycogen deposits found in the proximal convoluted tubules. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:54">[00:28:54]</a>
    *   Diffuse Glomerulosclerosis. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:39">[00:29:39]</a>
    *   Capsular drop and Fiebig lesions (small pinkish dots on capillaries and capsule). <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:54">[00:29:54]</a>
    *   Papillary necrosis. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:07">[00:31:07]</a>

## Glomerulonephritis (GN)

### Rapidly Progressive Glomerulonephritis (RPGN)
*   **Clinical Presentation:** Manifests as a nephritic syndrome, characterized by "HOAA" features: Hematuria, Oliguria (urine output less than 400ml in 24 hours), Hypertension, and Azotemia (increased Blood Urea Nitrogen - BUN). <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:30">[00:35:30]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:49">[00:35:49]</a>
*   **Histology:** Distinguished by the presence of crescent formation within the glomeruli. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:00">[00:35:00]</a>
*   **Crescent Composition:** Crescents are primarily composed of fibrin and parietal epithelial cells, with occasional contributions from visceral epithelial cells. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:55">[00:37:55]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:07">[00:38:07]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:46">[00:38:46]</a>
*   **Electron Microscopy:** Shows rupture and wrinkling of the glomerular basement membrane (GBM). <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:37">[00:39:37]</a>

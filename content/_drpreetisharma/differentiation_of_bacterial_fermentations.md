## Escherichia coli (E. coli) and its Variants

*E. coli* is a prototype member of the Enterobacteriaceae family <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:57">[00:05:57]</a>. It produces various toxins and can cause different clinical syndromes, often differentiated by specific characteristics and fermentation patterns.

### E. coli Toxin Mechanisms
*   **Heat-Labile Toxin (LT)**: Increases cyclic AMP (cAMP) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:12">[00:06:12]</a>. "Labile in the air" helps remember this mechanism <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:28">[00:06:28]</a>.
*   **Heat-Stable Toxin (ST)**: Increases cyclic GMP (cGMP) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:37">[00:06:37]</a>. "Stable on the ground" aids recall <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:37">[00:06:37]</a>.
*   **Verocytotoxin (Shiga-like toxin)**: Decreases protein synthesis by affecting the 60S ribosome <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:00">[00:07:00]</a>.

### E. coli Clinical Syndromes and Virulence Factors
*   **Enteropathogenic *E. coli* (EPEC)**:
    *   Causes pediatric infantile diarrhea <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:12">[00:11:12]</a>.
    *   Virulence marker: Bundle-forming pili, which are plasmid-coded <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:25">[00:11:25]</a>.
    *   Characterized by "attachment and effacement (AE) lesions" on intestinal villi <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:11">[00:12:11]</a>.
*   **Enterotoxigenic *E. coli* (ETEC)**:
    *   Causes traveler's diarrhea <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:01">[00:13:01]</a>.
    *   Virulence marker: Colonization factor antigen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:16">[00:13:16]</a>.
*   **Enteroinvasive *E. coli* (EIEC)**:
    *   Causes dysentery (diarrhea with blood) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:56">[00:13:56]</a>.
    *   Virulence marker: Virulence marker antigen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:14">[00:14:14]</a>.
    *   Positive in the Sereny test, which involves inoculating the bacteria into an animal eye, causing mucopurulent keratoconjunctivitis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:41">[00:14:41]</a>. This test is similar to the Anton test used for *Listeria* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:50">[00:15:50]</a>.
*   **Enterohemorrhagic *E. coli* (EHEC) O157:H7**:
    *   Causes hemolytic uremic syndrome (HUS), characterized by hemolytic anemia, renal involvement, and thrombocytopenia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:45">[00:16:45]</a>.
    *   Unique because it is **sorbitol negative** (SMAC negative), unlike other *E. coli* strains which are sorbitol fermenters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:26">[00:19:26]</a>.
*   **Enteroaggregative *E. coli* (EAEC)**:
    *   Causes persistent diarrhea (lasting more than 14 days) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:00">[00:20:00]</a>.
    *   Virulence marker: Enteroaggregative Toxin (EAST1 toxin) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:52">[00:20:52]</a>.
    *   Shows a "stacked brick appearance" on Hep-2 cell lines due to bacterial aggregation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:09">[00:21:09]</a>.

## Klebsiella vs. E. coli Differentiation
Both *E. coli* and *Klebsiella* are lactose fermenters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:07">[00:23:07]</a>. However, *Klebsiella* can be differentiated by its production of mucoid colonies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:44">[00:22:44]</a>. This mucoid appearance is due to the presence of a capsule, a universal rule in microbiology: capsulated organisms often produce mucoid colonies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:35">[00:30:35]</a>. A string test can confirm this mucoid nature <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:51">[00:30:51]</a>.

## Culture Media for Fermentation Testing
Differentiation of bacterial fermentations relies on specific [[culture_media_for_microbiology | culture media]] and indicators:

*   **MacConkey Agar**:
    *   **Composition**: Agar, peptone, lactose, neutral red (indicator), bile salts (e.g., sodium taurocholate) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:46">[00:24:46]</a>.
    *   **Function**:
        *   **Differential**: Distinguishes lactose fermenters (pink colonies) from non-lactose fermenters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:05">[00:25:05]</a>.
        *   **Indicator**: Contains neutral red for color change <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:22">[00:25:22]</a>.
        *   **Selective**: Bile salts inhibit the growth of Gram-positive bacteria, making it selective for Gram-negative organisms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:36">[00:25:36]</a>.
    *   **Appearance**: Lactose fermenters produce a pink color <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:41">[00:24:41]</a>.
*   **CLED Agar (Cystine-Lactose-Electrolyte Deficient Agar)**:
    *   **Function**: Tests for lactose fermentation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:06">[00:26:06]</a>.
    *   **Indicator**: Bromothymol blue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:27">[00:26:27]</a>.
    *   **Appearance**: Lactose fermenting colonies appear yellow <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:16">[00:26:16]</a>.
    *   **Advantage**: CLED is considered better than MacConkey because it is less inhibitory, allowing the growth of both Gram-negative and Gram-positive organisms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:02">[00:27:02]</a>.
*   **Eosin Methylene Blue (EMB) Agar**:
    *   **Function**: Also used for lactose fermentation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:54">[00:28:54]</a>.
    *   **Appearance**: Lactose fermenters, particularly *E. coli*, show a greenish metallic sheen around the colonies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:06">[00:29:06]</a>.
*   **Mannitol Salt Agar (MSA)**:
    *   **Function**: Used for mannitol fermentation, notably for *Staphylococcus aureus* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:55">[00:50:55]</a>.
    *   **Indicator**: Phenol red <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:24">[00:51:24]</a>.
    *   **Appearance**: Mannitol fermenters (like *S. aureus*) produce a yellow color <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:40">[00:51:40]</a>.
*   **TCBS Media (Thiosulphate Citrate Bile Salt Sucrose Agar)**:
    *   **Function**: Tests for sucrose fermentation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:10">[00:51:10]</a>.
    *   **Organism**: Primarily used for *Vibrio* species, specifically *Vibrio cholerae* as a sucrose fermenter <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:56">[00:51:56]</a>.
    *   **Indicator**: Bromothymol blue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:12">[00:52:12]</a>.
    *   **Appearance**: Sucrose fermenters produce yellow colonies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:05">[00:52:05]</a>.

## Key Fermentation Types and Their Differentiation

### Lactose Fermentation
This is a primary characteristic for differentiating many Gram-negative rods, particularly within the Enterobacteriaceae family <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:38">[00:00:38]</a>.
*   **Organisms**: *E. coli* and *Klebsiella* are classic lactose fermenters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:54">[00:07:54]</a>.
*   **Media**:
    *   [[culture_media_for_microbiology | MacConkey Agar]]: Lactose fermenters produce pink colonies due to the neutral red indicator <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:41">[00:24:41]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:22">[00:25:22]</a>. It's a differential, indicator, and selective medium (contains bile salts) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:08">[00:25:08]</a>.
    *   [[culture_media_for_microbiology | CLED (Cystine-Lactose-Electrolyte Deficient) Agar]]: Lactose fermenting colonies appear yellow with bromothymol blue as an indicator <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:13">[00:26:13]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:27">[00:26:27]</a>. CLED is preferred over MacConkey because it is less inhibitory, allowing growth of both Gram-negative and Gram-positive organisms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:48">[00:27:48]</a>.
    *   [[culture_media_for_microbiology | Eosin Methylene Blue (EMB) Agar]]: Lactose fermenters produce a greenish metallic sheen around their colonies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:06">[00:29:06]</a>.

### Sorbitol Fermentation
*   **Organisms**: Most *E. coli* strains are sorbitol fermenters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:18">[00:19:18]</a>.
*   **Exception**: Enterohemorrhagic *E. coli* (EHEC) O157:H7 is a notable exception as it is **sorbitol negative** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:26">[00:19:26]</a>. This is tested using a Sorbitol MacConkey (SMAC) agar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:31">[00:18:31]</a>.

### Sucrose Fermentation
*   **Organisms**: *Vibrio cholerae* is a sucrose fermenter <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:10">[00:50:10]</a>. Other *Vibrio* species like *V. parahaemolyticus* and *V. alginolyticus* are typically non-sucrose fermenters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:13">[00:50:13]</a>.
*   **Media**: TCBS (Thiosulphate Citrate Bile Salt Sucrose) agar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:18">[00:50:18]</a>.
    *   **Appearance**: Sucrose fermenters (like *V. cholerae*) produce yellow colonies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:05">[00:52:05]</a>. The indicator is bromothymol blue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:12">[00:52:12]</a>.

### Mannitol Fermentation
*   **Organisms**: *Staphylococcus aureus* is a mannitol fermenter <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:55">[00:50:55]</a>.
*   **Media**: Mannitol Salt Agar (MSA) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:59">[00:50:59]</a>.
    *   **Indicator**: Phenol red <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:24">[00:51:24]</a>.
    *   **Appearance**: Mannitol fermenters produce yellow colonies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:40">[00:51:40]</a>.

## Other Differentiating Features

### Vibrio Species
*   **Halophilic vs. Non-halophilic**:
    *   **Non-halophilic** (do not love salt): *Vibrio cholerae*, *Vibrio mimicus* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:26">[00:40:26]</a>.
    *   **Halophilic** (love salt): *Vibrio parahaemolyticus*, *Vibrio vulnificus*, *Vibrio alginolyticus* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:37">[00:40:37]</a>. *V. alginolyticus* is the most salt-tolerant (over 10% NaCl) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:59">[00:41:59]</a>.
*   **Clinical Associations**:
    *   ***Vibrio parahaemolyticus***: Causes gastroenteritis from seafood consumption <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:52">[00:42:52]</a>. Shows the Kanagawa phenomenon on Wagatsuma agar (blood agar with salt), where pathogenic strains cause hemolysis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:58">[00:42:58]</a>.
    *   ***Vibrio vulnificus***: Can cause sepsis, especially in patients with HIV or cirrhotic liver disease <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:45">[00:44:45]</a>.
    *   ***Vibrio alginolyticus***: Associated with conjunctivitis and otitis media <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:42">[00:45:42]</a>.
*   **Cholera Toxin**: Fragment A activates adenylate cyclase, increasing cyclic AMP <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:09">[00:48:09]</a>. Fragment B binds to GM1 gangliosides <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:34">[00:47:34]</a>.

### Agglutination Tests
[[microbiology_tests_camp_test_and_differentiating_pathogens | Agglutination tests]] are used for [[identification_of_bacterial_species | diagnosis]] of various infections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:00">[01:19:00]</a>.

*   **Cold Agglutination Test**: Used for *Mycoplasma* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:45">[01:19:45]</a>.
*   **Standard Agglutination Test**: Used for *Brucella* (Brucellosis) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:52">[01:19:52]</a>.
*   **Microscopic Agglutination Test (MAT)**: Used for *Leptospira* (Leptospirosis) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:58">[01:19:58]</a>.
*   **Heterophile Agglutination Tests**: Involve antibodies reacting with different (hetero) antigens from various species.
    *   **Paul-Bunnell Test**: Used for Epstein-Barr virus (infectious mononucleosis), where human antibodies react with sheep RBCs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:21:22">[01:21:22]</a>.
    *   **Weil-Felix Reaction**: Used for [[staining_techniques_in_microbiology | Rickettsial infections]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:21:43">[01:21:43]</a>. Human antibodies against *Rickettsia* cross-react with *Proteus* antigens (OX2, OX19, OXK strains of *Proteus vulgaris* and *mirabilis*) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:07">[01:22:07]</a>. Scrub typhus is the only rickettsial disease that shows positivity with OXK <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:23:29">[01:23:29]</a>.
*   **Coagglutination**: A test for *Staphylococcus aureus* using Cowan's strain of *S. aureus* protein A <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:24:22">[01:24:22]</a>.

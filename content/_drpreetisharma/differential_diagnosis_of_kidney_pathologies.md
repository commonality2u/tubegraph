## Renal Cell Carcinoma (RCC)

[[Renal Pathology and Cancers | Renal cell carcinoma (RCC)]] is often suspected clinically and radiologically in patients presenting with right flank pain and hematuria <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:24">[00:02:24]</a>.

### Types of RCC

Several types of RCC are distinguished by their pathological and genetic characteristics:

*   **[[Renal Pathology and Cancers | Papillary RCC]]**
    *   **Histology:** Tall cells arranged in papillae <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:57">[00:01:57]</a>.
    *   **Genetics:** Trisomy 7 and 17, loss of chromosome Y <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:49">[00:02:49]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:16">[00:07:16]</a>.
    *   **Associations:** Common in patients with a history of dialysis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:51">[00:06:51]</a>. Can be associated with fumarate hydratase (FH) gene mutation, which also leads to hereditary leiomyomatosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:33">[00:07:33]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:02">[00:08:02]</a>.
    *   **Distinctive Feature:** Presence of psammoma bodies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:17">[00:06:17]</a>.

*   **[[Renal Pathology and Cancers | Clear Cell RCC]]**
    *   **Prevalence:** Most common type of RCC <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:04">[00:05:04]</a>.
    *   **Histology:** Features empty clear cells <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:07">[00:05:07]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:43">[00:05:43]</a>.
    *   **Genetics:** Characterized by deletion of chromosome 3p <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:15">[00:05:15]</a>.
    *   **Syndrome:** Associated with [[Renal Pathology and Cancers | VHL syndrome]] (Von Hippel-Lindau syndrome) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:19">[00:05:19]</a>.

*   **[[Renal Pathology and Cancers | Chromophobe RCC]]**
    *   **Genetics:** Shows multiple chromosomal losses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:40">[00:10:40]</a>.
    *   **Associations:** Linked to Birt-Hogg-Dubé syndrome <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:08">[00:11:08]</a>.
    *   **Histology:** Contains "plant cells" with distinct outlines, a dark "resinoid nucleus," and a "perinuclear halo" (empty area around the nucleus) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:47">[00:11:47]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:01">[00:13:01]</a>. Stains positively with Helz Kolod Iron <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:31">[00:13:31]</a>.

*   **[[Renal Pathology and Cancers | Bellini Duct (Collecting Duct) RCC]]**
    *   Known for having the worst prognosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:20">[00:14:20]</a>.

*   **[[Renal Pathology and Cancers | Medullary RCC]]**
    *   **Associations:** Strongly associated with sickle cell trait (HbAS), not sickle cell anemia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:16">[00:15:16]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:40">[00:16:40]</a>.

*   **[[Renal Pathology and Cancers | Pediatric RCC (TFE3 gene)]]**
    *   **Genetics:** Characterized by Xp11 translocation involving the TFE3 gene <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:21">[00:03:21]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:48">[00:15:48]</a>. While commonly associated with childhood RCC, it can also occur in adults <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:03">[00:04:03]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:40">[00:04:40]</a>.

### Other Renal Tumors

*   **[[Renal Pathology and Cancers | Angiomyolipoma (AML)]]**
    *   **Composition:** Composed of blood vessels (angio), muscle (myo), and fat (lipo) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:32">[00:18:32]</a>.
    *   **Association:** Associated with tuberous sclerosis (TSC1/TSC2 gene mutations) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:46">[00:17:46]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:53">[00:18:53]</a>.

*   **[[Renal Pathology and Cancers | Renal Oncocytoma]]**
    *   **Nature:** Benign tumor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:18">[00:20:18]</a>.
    *   **Histology:** Cells are rich in mitochondria ("oncocytes") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:25">[00:20:25]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:56">[00:20:56]</a>.
    *   **Gross Appearance:** Often described as mahogany brown color <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:04">[00:21:04]</a>.
    *   **Key Feature:** Presence of a central stellate scar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:12">[00:21:12]</a>.

## Diabetic Nephropathy

[[Pathology of Diabetic Nephropathy and Special Stains | Diabetic nephropathy]] is a kidney complication of uncontrolled diabetes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:09">[00:24:09]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:21">[00:24:21]</a>.

*   **[[Pathological features of various diseases | Kimmelstiel-Wilson (KW) lesions]] / Nodular Glomerular Sclerosis**
    *   Characterized by pink, nodular formations in the glomeruli <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:01">[00:25:01]</a>.
    *   Are PAS (Periodic Acid–Schiff) positive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:36">[00:24:36]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:57">[00:25:57]</a>.
    *   The lesions are made up of glycosylation of proteins, not glycogen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:42">[00:26:42]</a>.

*   **Other features of [[Pathology of Diabetic Nephropathy and Special Stains | diabetic nephropathy]]**
    *   Thickening of capillaries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:22">[00:28:22]</a>.
    *   Hyaline arteriosclerosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:44">[00:28:44]</a>.
    *   Armani-Ebstein lesion: Glycogen deposits in the proximal convoluted tubules <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:06">[00:29:06]</a>.
    *   Diffuse glomerular sclerosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:39">[00:29:39]</a>.
    *   Capsular drop: Pinkish material seen dropping from Bowman's capsule <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:17">[00:30:17]</a>.
    *   Fiebig lesions: Small pinkish dots on capillaries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:34">[00:30:34]</a>.
    *   [[Pathological features of various diseases | Renal papillary necrosis]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:07">[00:31:07]</a>.

### [[Pathological features of various diseases | Papillary Necrosis]]

The four main causes of [[Pathological features of various diseases | renal papillary necrosis]] are:
1.  Diabetes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:50">[00:32:50]</a>
2.  Obstruction (obstructive disorders) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:54">[00:32:54]</a>

## Glomerular Nephropathies

### [[Pathological features of various diseases | Rapidly Progressive Glomerulonephritis (RPGN)]]

*   **Clinical Presentation:** Patients present with nephritic syndrome features, including hematuria, oliguria (less than 400ml/24 hours), hypertension, and azotemia (increased BUN) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:12">[00:35:12]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:01">[00:36:01]</a>.
*   **Histology:** Characterized by crescent formation within Bowman's capsule <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:00">[00:35:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:26">[00:37:26]</a>.
*   **Crescent Composition:** Composed primarily of fibrin and parietal epithelial cells <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:58">[00:37:58]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:07">[00:38:07]</a>. Visceral epithelial cells may also be present <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:49">[00:38:49]</a>.
*   **[[Diagnosis using electron microscopy | Electron Microscopy]] (EM):** Shows rupture and wrinkling of the glomerular basement membrane <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:41">[00:39:41]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:43">[00:40:43]</a>.

## Ovarian Tumors

### Types of Ovarian Tumors

*   **Serous Cystadenoma**
    *   **Gross Appearance:** Uniloculated cyst <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:54">[01:01:54]</a>.
    *   **Fluid:** Contains clear watery (serous) fluid <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:57">[01:01:57]</a>.
    *   **Histology:** Shows psammoma bodies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:58">[01:02:58]</a>. Lined by ciliated epithelium <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:34">[01:04:34]</a>.

*   **Mucinous Cystadenoma**
    *   **Gross Appearance:** Multiloculated cyst <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:28">[01:03:28]</a>.
    *   **Fluid:** Contains abundant mucin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:31">[01:03:31]</a>.
    *   **Histology:** Lined by mucin-containing empty cells <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:41">[01:04:41]</a>.

*   **Brenner Tumor**
    *   **Category:** Classified as a surface epithelial tumor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:35">[01:05:35]</a>.
    *   **Histology:** Nests of cells resembling urinary bladder transitional epithelium <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:48">[01:07:48]</a>. Features "coffee bean nucleus" (nuclei with a line through them) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:37">[01:06:37]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:04">[01:07:04]</a>.
    *   **Associations:** In 25% of cases, it is associated with mucinous cystadenoma <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:07">[01:09:07]</a>.
    *   **Tumor Marker:** CA125 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:25">[01:07:25]</a>.

*   **Granulosa Cell Tumor**
    *   **Clinical Presentation:** Typically seen in post-menopausal females (50-70 years), presenting with abnormal uterine bleeding due to a bulky uterus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:41">[01:09:41]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:29">[01:10:29]</a>. This is due to a hyperestrogenic state, as the tumor produces estrogen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:57">[01:10:57]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:57">[01:13:57]</a>.
    *   **Histology:** Features Call-Exner bodies (cells arranged around a central pink material) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:58">[01:11:58]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:53">[01:12:53]</a>, and nuclear grooves (coffee bean nucleus) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:48">[01:13:48]</a>.
    *   **Immunohistochemistry:** CD99 positive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:29">[01:12:29]</a>.
    *   **Genetics:** Associated with FOXL2 gene mutation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:31">[01:13:31]</a>.

Mycology is the study of fungi. This field has gained significant importance, especially following events like the COVID-19 pandemic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:29">[00:00:29]</a>. This article provides an overview of various fungi, their characteristic features, diagnostic techniques, and associated diseases.

## Fungal Staining Techniques

Identifying fungal morphology, particularly in cultures, often relies on specific staining methods <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:39">[00:00:39]</a>.

*   **Lactophenol Cotton Blue (LPCB)**: Best for studying culture morphology, giving a blue color to fungal structures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:46">[00:00:46]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:09">[00:03:09]</a>.
*   **Gomori Methenamine Silver (GMS) Stain**: Fungal hyphae appear black or dark <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:12">[00:01:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:39">[00:01:39]</a>. This stain is used for various fungi including _Blastomyces_ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:42">[00:12:42]</a>, _Paracoccidioides_ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:35">[00:19:35]</a>, and _Pneumocystis jirovecii_ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:07">[00:45:07]</a>.
*   **Periodic Acid-Schiff (PAS) Stain**: Fungal hyphae stain pink <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:53">[00:01:53]</a>.
*   **Calcofluor White**: A fluorescent dye that makes fungal structures appear fluorescent against a dark background <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:05">[00:02:05]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:20">[00:02:20]</a>.
*   **India Ink Stain**: A negative stain used for cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) samples, where the background gets colored and the encapsulated organisms (like _Cryptococcus_) remain unstained <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:41">[00:04:41]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:49">[00:04:49]</a>.
*   **Mucicarmine Stain**: Used for biopsy specimens, particularly for _Cryptococcus_, where the capsule stains rose-pink <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:06">[00:05:06]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:50">[00:05:50]</a>.
*   **KOH Mount (Potassium Hydroxide)**: Used for samples containing keratin (skin, hair, nails) to digest keratin and allow visualization of fungal elements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:47">[01:03:47]</a>.

## Fungal Culture Media

**Sabouraud's Dextrose Agar (SDA)** is a common culture medium for growing fungal samples <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:44">[00:02:44]</a>.

## Key Fungi and Their Features

### _Cryptococcus neoformans_

_Cryptococcus neoformans_ is known for causing meningitis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:43">[00:03:43]</a>.

*   **Samples**: Cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) and biopsy tissue (e.g., brain biopsy) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:01">[00:04:01]</a>.
*   **Staining**:
    *   CSF: [[Fungal Staining Techniques | India Ink negative staining]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:52">[00:04:52]</a>.
    *   Biopsy: [[Fungal Staining Techniques | Mucicarmine stain]] (capsule appears rose pink) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:01">[00:05:01]</a>.
*   **Diagnostic Test**: Latex agglutination test for final diagnosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:56">[00:06:56]</a>.
*   **Dimorphism**: _Cryptococcus neoformans_ is **not** [[Dimorphic Fungi and Their Features | dimorphic]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:29">[00:07:29]</a>.

### [[Dimorphic Fungi and Their Features | Dimorphic Fungi]]

[[Dimorphic Fungi and Their Features | Dimorphic fungi]] exist in two forms depending on temperature <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:43">[00:07:43]</a>:

*   **Yeast form**: Seen at higher temperatures (37°C or "heat") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:13">[00:08:13]</a>.
*   **Mold (hyphae) form**: Seen at lower temperatures (25°C or "cold") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:17">[00:08:17]</a>.

[[Dimorphic Fungi and Their Features | Dimorphic fungi]] are famous for causing systemic involvement, commonly affecting organs like the lungs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:23">[00:11:23]</a>.

**Mnemonic for [[Dimorphic Fungi and Their Features | Dimorphic Fungi]]:** "Body Heat Probably Changes Shape" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:01">[00:09:01]</a>.

## _Chromoblastomycosis_

This condition is also known as **verrucous dermatitis** and causes warty, cauliflower-like lesions on the skin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:41">[00:32:41]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:55">[00:32:55]</a>. It often affects forest workers who walk barefoot, allowing the organism to enter the skin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:07">[00:33:07]</a>. Microscopically, it features pigmented structures known as "copper penny bodies," "muriform bodies," "medlar bodies," or "sclerotic bodies" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:34">[00:33:34]</a>.

### _Aspergillus_

_Aspergillus_ species are commonly identified by their colony color and microscopic morphology:

*   **_Aspergillus niger_**: Produces black colonies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:16">[00:35:16]</a>.
*   **_Aspergillus fumigatus_**: Produces smoky green colonies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:32">[00:35:32]</a>. Microscopically, conidia arise only from the upper part of the vesicle, resembling "fumes" going upwards <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:41">[00:37:41]</a>.
*   **_Aspergillus flavus_**: Produces yellow and green colonies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:46">[00:35:46]</a>. Microscopically, conidia arise from all around the circumference of the vesicle, like "petals" of a flower <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:29">[00:38:29]</a>.

On microscopy using Lactophenol Cotton Blue, _Aspergillus_ shows **narrow-angled, septate hyphae with acute angle branching** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:05">[00:40:05]</a>.

### _Mucor_

In contrast to _Aspergillus_, _Mucor_ shows **perpendicular branching** and **aseptate hyphae** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:41">[00:41:41]</a>. Its hyphae are typically broader and described as "ribbon-like" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:28">[00:42:28]</a>.

### _Pneumocystis jirovecii_ (formerly _Pneumocystis carinii_)

This fungus causes **Pneumocystis pneumonia (PCP)**, also known as **plasma cell pneumonia**, characterized by lung involvement with an exudate containing numerous plasma cells <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:43">[00:43:43]</a>. It typically affects immunocompromised individuals, such as HIV-positive patients or transplant recipients <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:46">[00:44:46]</a>.

*   **Staining**: Exclusively stained with [[Fungal Staining Techniques | Gomori Methenamine Silver (GMS) stain]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:01">[00:45:01]</a>.
*   **Appearance**: Gives a "ping-pong ball appearance," or more specifically, a "crushed ping-pong ball appearance." It can also be described as having a "hat appearance" or "cup and saucer appearance" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:26">[00:45:26]</a>.

### _Rhinosporidium seeberi_

_Rhinosporidium seeberi_ causes nasal polyps, indicated by "rhino" in its name <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:04">[00:47:04]</a>. Historically considered a fungus, it is now categorized as an aquatic parasite or protozoan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:30">[00:47:30]</a>.

*   **Microscopy**: Large, round structures called **sporangia** are seen, containing smaller **endospores** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:00">[00:48:00]</a>.
*   **Cultivation**: It **cannot be cultivated** on standard laboratory media like [[Microbial culture techniques | SDA]] or cell lines <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:36">[00:48:36]</a>.

### _Candida albicans_

_Candida albicans_ is a common causative agent in eye infections (endophthalmitis) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:51">[00:49:51]</a>.

*   **Morphology**: Exhibits both budding yeast forms and **pseudo-hyphae** (elongated budding forms) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:16">[00:50:16]</a>.
*   **Reynolds-Braud Phenomenon (Germ Tube Test)**: This test is positive for _Candida albicans_ and _Candida dubliniensis_. In this test, a "germ tube" (a true hyphal outgrowth) originates from the yeast cell after incubation in serum for two hours <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:37">[00:51:37]</a>.

### [[Dermatophytes and Their Differentiation | Dermatophytes]]

[[Dermatophytes and Their Differentiation | Dermatophytes]] are fungi that primarily cause infections of the skin, hair, and nails ("derma involvement") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:14">[00:54:14]</a>. Unlike [[Dimorphic Fungi and Their Features | dimorphic fungi]], they typically do not cause systemic infections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:07">[00:54:07]</a>.

The three main [[Dermatophytes and Their Differentiation | dermatophytes]] are identified using the mnemonic "TME" (Trichophyton, Microsporum, Epidermophyton) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:45">[00:54:45]</a>.

| :--------------- | :----------------- | :-------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------- |
| **_Trichophyton_** | Skin, Hair, Nails  | Abundant (most) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:43">[00:55:43]</a>     | **P**encil-shaped <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:16">[00:59:16]</a>     |
| **_Microsporum_** | Skin, Hair         | Few <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:09">[00:56:09]</a>             | **S**pindle-shaped (fusiform) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:16">[00:59:16]</a> |
| **_Epidermophyton_** | Skin, Nails        | Least or Absent (none) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:15">[00:56:15]</a> | **C**lub-shaped <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:16">[00:59:16]</a>          |

### _Malassezia furfur_ (_Tinea versicolor_)

_Tinea versicolor_ is a skin disorder characterized by hypopigmented lesions, typically on the trunk <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:05">[01:03:05]</a>.

*   **Microscopy (KOH mount)**: Shows a characteristic "spaghetti and meatball appearance" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:03">[01:05:03]</a> – short, curved hyphae ("spaghetti") and clusters of round yeast cells ("meatballs") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:12">[01:05:12]</a>.
*   **Culture**: _Malassezia furfur_ is lipophilic (fat-loving) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:17">[01:06:17]</a>. It grows on SDA supplemented with olive oil, producing colonies with a "fried egg appearance" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:31">[01:05:31]</a>.

## Fungal Biomarkers

During fungal infections, specific biomarkers can be detected:

*   **Beta-1,3-D-glucan**: A common component of the fungal cell wall <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:43">[01:07:43]</a>. It is used as a general marker for many fungal infections, including _Candida_, _Aspergillus_, _Histoplasma_, _Fusarium_, and _Pneumocystis jirovecii_ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:06">[01:08:06]</a>.
    *   **Exceptions (Negative for BMC)**: Beta-1,3-D-glucan testing is typically negative for _Blastomyces_, _Mucor_, and _Cryptococcus_ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:20">[01:08:20]</a>.
*   **Galactomannan**: Primarily used as a marker for _Aspergillus_ infection <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:53">[01:07:53]</a>.

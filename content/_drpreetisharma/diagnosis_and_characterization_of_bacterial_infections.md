## Gram-Negative Bacilli and Associated Syndromes

Gram-negative bacilli are a significant category of bacteria, often presenting with diverse clinical manifestations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:30">[00:30:30]</a>.

### *Escherichia coli* (E. coli) Strains

*E. coli* is a prototype member of the Enterobacteriaceae family, which typically ferments lactose <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:04">[00:42:04]</a>.
Different strains of *E. coli* cause distinct diseases:
*   **Enteropathogenic *E. coli* (EPEC)**
    *   Causes pediatric or infantile diarrhea <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:12">[00:11:12]</a>.
    *   Virulence marker: Bundle-forming pili, which are plasmid-coded <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:23">[00:11:23]</a>.
    *   Associated with attachment and effacement (A/E) lesions on intestinal villi, where the organism attaches to the intestine <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:07">[00:12:07]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:46">[00:12:46]</a>.
*   **Enterotoxigenic *E. coli* (ETEC)**
    *   Causes traveler's diarrhea <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:09">[00:13:09]</a>.
    *   Produces heat-labile toxin (LT) and heat-stable toxin (ST) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:03">[00:06:03]</a>.
        *   Labile toxin (LT) increases cyclic AMP (cAMP) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:11">[00:06:11]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:31">[00:06:31]</a>.
        *   Stable toxin (ST) increases cyclic GMP (cGMP) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:37">[00:06:37]</a>.
    *   Virulence marker: Colonization factor antigen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:16">[00:13:16]</a>.
*   **Enteroinvasive *E. coli* (EIEC)**
    *   Causes diarrhea with blood (dysentery) due to invasion of the intestine <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:54">[00:13:54]</a>.
    *   Virulence marker: Virulence marker antigen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:14">[00:14:14]</a>.
    *   Positive for the Sereny test, which involves inoculating the bacteria into an animal and observing purulent keratoconjunctivitis (e.g., red eye with pus in a guinea pig or rabbit) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:32">[00:14:32]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:10">[00:15:10]</a>.
    *   The Sereny test is also positive for *Shigella* and *Listeria* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:29">[00:15:29]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:34">[00:15:34]</a>.
    *   The Anton test is another name for this test when used for *Listeria* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:50">[00:15:50]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:02">[00:16:02]</a>.
*   **Enterohemorrhagic *E. coli* (EHEC)**
    *   Associated with O157:H7 serotype <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:31">[00:10:31]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:51">[00:16:51]</a>.
    *   Causes hemolytic uremic syndrome (HUS), characterized by a triad of hemolytic anemia, renal involvement, and thrombocytopenia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:45">[00:16:45]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:17">[00:17:17]</a>.
    *   Produces Shiga-like toxin (also called Verocytotoxin or VST), which decreases protein synthesis by affecting the 60S ribosome <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:50">[00:06:50]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:00">[00:07:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:09">[00:07:09]</a>.
    *   Unlike other *E. coli* strains, EHEC O157:H7 is sorbitol negative on MacConkey agar with sorbitol (SMAC agar) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:05">[00:19:05]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:26">[00:19:26]</a>.
*   **Enteroaggregative *E. coli* (EAEC)**
    *   Causes persistent diarrhea (more than 14 days) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:00">[00:20:00]</a>.
    *   Virulence marker: Enteroaggregative Toxin (EAST1 toxin) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:48">[00:20:48]</a>.
    *   Shows a "stacked brick" appearance on HEp-2 cell lines <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:09">[00:21:09]</a>.

### *Klebsiella*

*Klebsiella* is a lactose-fermenting, encapsulated organism <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:44">[00:30:44]</a>. Its capsule results in mucoid colonies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:51">[00:30:51]</a> and a positive string test <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:23">[00:30:23]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:01">[00:31:01]</a>.
*   Often causes sepsis, particularly in newborns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:48">[00:22:48]</a>.

### *Yersinia pestis*

*Yersinia pestis*, the causative agent of plague, is transmitted by flea bites (rat flea) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:03">[00:33:03]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:07">[00:33:07]</a>.
*   **Staining:** Vacin staining is used to visualize its characteristic "safety pin" appearance (bipolar staining) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:22">[00:33:22]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:52">[00:34:52]</a>.
*   **Culture:** Shows a "stalactite growth pattern" in ghee broth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:19">[00:38:19]</a>. This is distinct from an "inverted fir tree appearance" seen on gelatin stab <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:57">[00:37:57]</a>.
*   Other organisms showing safety pin appearance include *Vibrio parahemolyticus*, *Haemophilus ducreyi*, *Burkholderia mallei* and *pseudomallei*, and *Klebsiella granulomatis* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:02">[00:36:02]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:07">[00:37:07]</a>.

### *Vibrio* Species

*Vibrio* bacteria are classified based on their salt requirements:
*   **Non-halophilic:** *Vibrio cholerae* and *Vibrio mimicus* (do not love salt) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:17">[00:40:17]</a>.
*   **Halophilic:** *Vibrio parahemolyticus*, *Vibrio vulnificus*, and *Vibrio alginolyticus* (love salt) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:37">[00:40:37]</a>.

Specific *Vibrio* species and their characteristics:
*   ***Vibrio cholerae***
    *   Causes rice water stools and severe vomiting and dehydration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:28">[00:46:28]</a>.
    *   Produces Cholera Toxin (CTX), an AB toxin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:15">[00:47:15]</a>.
        *   Fragment B binds to GM1 ganglioside receptors on intestinal cells <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:31">[00:47:31]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:34">[00:47:34]</a>.
        *   The toxin affects Zona occludens (tight junctions) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:49">[00:47:49]</a>.
        *   Fragment A activates adenylate cyclase, leading to an increase in cyclic AMP (cAMP) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:05">[00:48:05]</a>.
            *   Other toxins that increase cAMP include Anthrax toxin, *Bordetella pertussis* toxin, and *E. coli* labile toxin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:49">[00:48:49]</a>.
    *   A string test can be performed on *Vibrio cholerae* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:57">[00:31:57]</a>.
    *   *Vibrio cholerae* is a sucrose fermenter, tested on TCBS media <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:08">[00:50:08]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:16">[00:50:16]</a>.
*   ***Vibrio parahemolyticus***
    *   Causes gastroenteritis, often linked to raw fish or seafood consumption <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:52">[00:42:52]</a>.
    *   Shows the Kanagawa phenomenon: it is non-hemolytic on standard blood agar but shows hemolysis on Wagatsuma agar (blood agar with salt) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:58">[00:42:58]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:00">[00:43:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:24">[00:43:24]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:41">[00:43:41]</a>.
*   ***Vibrio vulnificus***
    *   Can cause sepsis, especially in immunocompromised individuals or those with cirrhotic liver disease <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:45">[00:44:45]</a>.
*   ***Vibrio alginolyticus***
    *   Most tolerant to NaCl (salt), even above 10% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:44">[00:41:44]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:59">[00:41:59]</a>.
    *   Can cause conjunctivitis and otitis media <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:42">[00:45:42]</a>.

## Spirochetes and Associated Diseases

Spirochetes are spiral-shaped bacteria, typically visualized using dark-field microscopy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:59">[01:11:59]</a>. Their motility is due to endoflagella located in the periplasmic space <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:26:36">[01:26:36]</a>.

### *Treponema pallidum*

*   Causative agent of Syphilis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:32">[01:05:32]</a>.
*   Characterized by painless genital ulcers (chancre) and painless inguinal lymphadenopathy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:38">[01:05:38]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:58">[01:05:58]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:51">[01:08:51]</a>.
*   Shows tight coils and "screw motility" under dark-field microscopy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:54">[01:12:54]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:02">[01:13:02]</a>.

### *Borrelia*

*   *Borrelia burgdorferi* causes Lyme disease <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:51">[01:11:51]</a>.
*   Distinguished by loose coils and "lashing motility" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:23">[01:13:23]</a>.

### *Leptospira*

*   Transmitted through rat urine contaminating rainy water, affecting rice field workers (the "three R's": rat urine, rainy season, rice fields) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:55">[01:14:55]</a>.
*   Causes Weil's disease, also known as icterohemorrhagic fever, characterized by liver (jaundice) and renal involvement (hematuria) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:12">[01:16:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:20">[01:16:20]</a>.
*   Has hooked ends <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:53">[01:13:53]</a>.
*   Diagnosed using EMJH culture media, which shows a "Dinger ring" (a line or ring beneath the fluid) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:50">[01:17:50]</a>.
*   The microscopic agglutination test (MAT) is used for diagnosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:58">[01:19:58]</a>.

## Culture Media and Fermentation Tests

[[bacteriology_culture_media_and_bacteria_identification | Bacterial identification]] often relies on observing growth on specific [[diagnosis_of_bacterial_infections_using_culture_media | culture media]] and their fermentation patterns.

### MacConkey Agar (MacConkey)
*   **Composition:** Agar, peptone, lactose, neutral red (indicator), bile salts (e.g., sodium taurocholate) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:46">[00:24:46]</a>.
*   **Purpose:** Differentiates lactose-fermenting from non-lactose-fermenting bacteria <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:05">[00:25:05]</a>.
*   **Characteristics:**
    *   Differential medium <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:07">[00:25:07]</a>.
    *   Indicator medium (due to neutral red) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:24">[00:25:24]</a>.
    *   Selective medium (due to bile salts, which inhibit Gram-positive bacteria) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:38">[00:25:38]</a>.
    *   Lactose fermentation results in pink colonies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:13">[00:24:13]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:56">[00:25:56]</a>.

### Cysteine-Lactose-Electrolyte-Deficient (CLED) Agar
*   **Composition:** Contains lactose <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:03">[00:26:03]</a> and bromothymol blue (indicator) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:27">[00:26:27]</a>.
*   **Purpose:** Tests for lactose fermentation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:09">[00:26:09]</a>.
*   **Characteristics:**
    *   Lactose fermentation results in yellow colonies from a blue background <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:13">[00:26:13]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:22">[00:26:22]</a>.
    *   Considered better than MacConkey agar because it is "less inhibitory," allowing the growth of both Gram-negative and Gram-positive organisms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:48">[00:27:48]</a>.

### Eosin Methylene Blue (EMB) Agar
*   **Purpose:** Also used for lactose fermentation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:54">[00:28:54]</a>.
*   **Characteristics:** Lactose-fermenting colonies show a greenish metallic sheen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:00">[00:29:00]</a>.

### Thiosulfate Citrate Bile Salts Sucrose (TCBS) Media
*   **Purpose:** Used to test for sucrose fermentation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:10">[00:50:10]</a>.
*   **Characteristics:** Sucrose fermentation (e.g., by *Vibrio cholerae*) results in yellow colonies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:05">[00:52:05]</a>. Bromothymol blue is the indicator used <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:12">[00:52:12]</a>.

### Mannitol Salt Agar (MSA)
*   **Purpose:** Used to test for mannitol fermentation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:59">[00:50:59]</a>.
*   **Organism:** *Staphylococcus aureus* ferments mannitol on MSA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:03">[00:51:03]</a>.
*   **Characteristics:** Phenol red is the indicator <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:24">[00:51:24]</a>. Mannitol fermentation results in a yellow color <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:40">[00:51:40]</a>.

## Clinical Presentations and Diagnostic Clues

### Diarrhea Classification
*   **Watery Diarrhea:** Often seen in traveler's diarrhea (*E. coli* ETEC) or Cholera <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:00">[00:32:00]</a>.
*   **Bloody Diarrhea (Dysentery):** Indicates intestinal invasion, seen in Enteroinvasive *E. coli* (EIEC) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:06">[00:10:06]</a>.
*   **Persistent Diarrhea:** Lasting more than 14 days, often caused by Enteroaggregative *E. coli* (EAEC) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:00">[00:20:00]</a>.

### Rash Patterns
*   **Trunk to Extremities:** Typical rash pattern for most rickettsial infections (e.g., epidemic typhus, endemic typhus) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:47">[00:54:47]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:04">[01:01:04]</a>.
*   **Extremities to Trunk:** Seen in Rocky Mountain Spotted Fever <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:16">[01:01:16]</a>.

### Genital Ulcers
*   **Painful Genital Ulcers:**
    *   *Haemophilus ducreyi* (causes soft chancre) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:38">[01:07:38]</a>.
    *   Herpes Simplex Virus 2 (HSV-2) (HSV-1 typically causes oral lesions, HSV-2 genital) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:06">[01:08:06]</a>.
*   **Painless Genital Ulcers:**
    *   Syphilis (*Treponema pallidum*): Characterized by a painless ulcer and painless lymphadenopathy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:51">[01:08:51]</a>.
    *   Lymphogranuloma Venereum (LGV): Ulcer is painless, but buboes (lymph nodes) are painful <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:09">[01:09:09]</a>.
    *   *Klebsiella granulomatis* (Donovanosis): Painless ulcer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:43">[01:09:43]</a>.

### Zoonotic Infections and Vectors (Let Fen Tria PSM mnemonic)
*   **Louse (L):** Epidemic Typhus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:15">[00:55:15]</a>.
*   **Flea (F):** Endemic Typhus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:32">[00:55:32]</a>.
*   **Tick (T):** Rocky Mountain Spotted Fever, Indian Tick Typhus, African Tick Typhus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:55">[00:55:55]</a>.
*   **Mite (M):**
    *   Rickettsialpox <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:58">[00:56:58]</a>.
    *   Scrub Typhus (*Orientia tsutsugamushi*): Transmitted by trombiculid mites (chiggers), often from mice in shrubs. Characterized by an eschar (blackish lesion) at the bite site, often in moist or hidden areas like the axilla or groin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:02">[00:57:02]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:29">[00:57:29]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:11">[00:58:11]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:26">[01:02:26]</a>.

## Agglutination Tests

Agglutination tests are used for diagnosing various infections:
*   **Cold Agglutination Test (CAT):** Used for *Mycoplasma* (*Mycoplasma pneumoniae*) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:42">[01:19:42]</a>.
*   **Standard Agglutination Test (SAT):** Used for Brucellosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:52">[01:19:52]</a>.
*   **Microscopic Agglutination Test (MAT):** Used for *Leptospira* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:58">[01:19:58]</a>.
*   **Heterophile Agglutination:**
    *   **Paul-Bunnell Test:** Used for Epstein-Barr Virus (EBV) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:45">[01:20:45]</a>.
    *   **Weil-Felix Reaction:** Used for Rickettsial diagnosis. Antibodies produced against Rickettsia cross-react with Proteus antigens (OX2, OX19, OXK strains) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:21:41">[01:21:41]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:04">[01:22:04]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:31">[01:22:31]</a>. Scrub typhus is the only rickettsial disease that shows a positive reaction to OXK <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:23:09">[01:23:09]</a>.
*   **Coagglutination:** A test done for *Staphylococcus aureus*, using the Cowan's strain which has Protein A <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:24:22">[01:24:22]</a>.

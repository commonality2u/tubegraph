[[overview_of_bacteriology | Bacteriology]] classifies organisms based on their Gram stain reaction. While most cocci are Gram-positive, Gram-positive bacilli are exceptions to the general rule that bacilli are typically Gram-negative <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:40">[00:04:40]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:57">[00:04:57]</a>.

## Gram Positive Bacilli (GPB) Mnemonic

A common mnemonic to remember Gram Positive Bacilli (GPB) is "McDonald's" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:03">[00:05:03]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:05">[00:05:05]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:08">[00:05:08]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:11">[00:05:11]</a>:

*   **M** - *Mycobacteria* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:36">[00:05:36]</a>
*   **A & C** - *[[Diagnosis and properties of Bacillus anthracis | Anthrax]]* (Bacillus) and *Clostridium* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:39">[00:05:39]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:40">[00:05:40]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:42">[00:05:42]</a>
    *   These two belong to the spore-forming family <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:44">[00:05:44]</a>.
*   **D & D** - *Corynebacterium diphtheriae* and *Listeria* (also *Erysipelothrix*) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:52">[00:05:52]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:52">[00:06:52]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:56">[00:06:56]</a>
*   **N & A** - *Nocardia* and *Actinomyces* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:00">[00:07:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:03">[00:07:03]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:04">[00:07:04]</a>
    *   These are filament-forming bacteria <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:10">[00:07:10]</a>.
*   **L** - *Listeria* (again, grouped with the 'D's) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:24">[00:07:24]</a>

### Spore-Forming Bacilli

*   **Spores**
    *   Can be stained with Sheffer and Fulton stain or Modified Ashworth stain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:13">[00:06:13]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:18">[00:06:18]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:21">[00:06:21]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:23">[00:06:23]</a>.
    *   Are ZN (Ziehl-Neelsen) stain positive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:10">[00:06:10]</a>.
    *   Location of spores:
        *   **Subterminal:** Most common type, seen in *Clostridium perfringens* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:32">[01:17:32]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:35">[01:17:35]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:53">[01:17:53]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:56">[01:17:56]</a>.
        *   **Terminal:** Seen in *Clostridium tetani* (round, drumstick-like) and *Clostridium tertium* (oval, tennis racket-like) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:09">[01:18:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:13">[01:18:13]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:16">[01:18:16]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:18">[01:18:18]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:22">[01:18:22]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:24">[01:18:24]</a>.
        *   **Central:** Seen in *Bacillus cereus* and *Clostridium bifermentans* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:12">[01:19:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:14">[01:19:14]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:19">[01:19:19]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:40">[01:19:40]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:43">[01:19:43]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:46">[01:19:46]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:49">[01:19:49]</a>.

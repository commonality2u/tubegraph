Cocci are spherical bacteria, and in [[general_microbiology_overview | general microbiology]], it is important to understand their characteristics for [[pathology_and_microbiology | pathology and microbiology]] diagnosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:23">[00:00:23]</a>.

## General Characteristics of Cocci
Most cocci are [[gram_positive_vs_gram_negative_cocci | Gram-positive]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:34">[00:03:34]</a>.

### Gram-Negative Cocci Exceptions
While most cocci are [[gram_positive_vs_gram_negative_cocci | Gram-positive]], there are exceptions that are [[gram_positive_vs_gram_negative_cocci | Gram-negative]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:37">[00:02:37]</a>:
*   **V**eillonella (rarely questioned) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:50">[00:02:50]</a>
*   **N**eisseria (frequently questioned) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:52">[00:02:52]</a>
*   **M**oraxella <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:57">[00:02:57]</a>

This can be remembered by the mnemonic "VENOM" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:47">[00:02:47]</a>.

## Gram-Positive Cocci
When identifying [[bacteriology_basics_and_introduction | bacteria]], Gram staining is crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:24">[00:03:24]</a>. For [[gram_positive_vs_gram_negative_cocci | Gram-positive cocci]], further tests like catalase and oxidase are performed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:44">[00:03:44]</a>.

### Micrococcaceae Family
The Micrococcaceae family includes *Micrococcus* and *Staphylococcus* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:09">[00:05:09]</a>.

#### Micrococcus
*   **Morphology**: Tetrads (clusters of four) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:00">[00:05:00]</a>.
*   **Glucose Utilization**: Oxidative, identified using Hugh and Leifson's media with bromothymol blue indicator <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:03">[00:05:03]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:17">[00:06:17]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:41">[00:06:41]</a>.
*   **Oxidase Test**: Positive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:34">[00:07:34]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:42">[00:08:42]</a>.
*   **Bacitracin**: Sensitive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:57">[00:07:57]</a>.

#### Staphylococcus
*   **Morphology**: Clusters (like a "bunch of grapes") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:48">[00:04:48]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:48">[00:05:48]</a>.
*   **Glucose Utilization**: Fermentative <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:50">[00:04:50]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:51">[00:07:51]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:51">[00:08:51]</a>.
*   **Catalase Test**: Positive ("bubbly cats" mnemonic) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:49">[00:03:49]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:24">[00:20:24]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:40">[00:21:40]</a>.
    *   Hydrogen peroxide (H2O2) is added to the sample; if catalase is present, it breaks down H2O2 into water and oxygen, creating bubbles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:53">[00:21:53]</a>.
    *   Other catalase-positive organisms include *Serratia* (red pigment), *Pseudomonas* (blue-green pigment), *Aspergillus*, *Candida*, *Cryptococcus*, Enterobacteriaceae family (*E. coli*), *Bacillus anthracis*, *Neisseria*, *Mycobacterium tuberculosis*, and *Micrococcus* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:06">[00:24:06]</a>.
*   **Oxidase Test**: Negative <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:51">[00:03:51]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:42">[00:07:42]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:40">[00:08:40]</a>.
*   **Bacitracin**: Resistant <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:58">[00:03:58]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:59">[00:07:59]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:46">[00:08:46]</a>.

#### *Staphylococcus aureus*
*   **Pigment**: Golden color (from "aurum" meaning gold) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:29">[00:24:29]</a>.
*   **Coagulase Test**: Positive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:55">[00:20:55]</a>.
    *   **Tube Coagulase**: Detects free coagulase (tube and free both have four letters) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:03">[00:23:03]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:55">[00:29:55]</a>.
    *   **Slide Coagulase**: Detects bound coagulase (slide and bound both have five letters) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:27">[00:30:27]</a>.
    *   *Staphylococcus aureus*, *Staphylococcus intermedius*, and *Staphylococcus hyicus* show both slide and tube coagulase positivity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:49">[00:30:49]</a>.
    *   *Staphylococcus schleiferi* shows only free coagulase positivity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:04">[00:31:04]</a>.
    *   *Staphylococcus lugdunensis* shows only bound coagulase positivity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:27">[00:31:27]</a>.
*   **Selective Culture Media**: Mannitol Salt Agar (MSA) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:29">[00:32:29]</a>.
    *   MSA is a selective medium due to 7-10% salt, which *Staphylococcus aureus* can tolerate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:03">[00:34:03]</a>.
    *   MSA is also a differential medium because it shows mannitol fermentation (turns yellow from red due to phenol red indicator if fermented) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:06">[00:35:06]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:05">[00:36:05]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:30">[00:37:30]</a>.
*   **Toxins and Associated Diseases**:
    *   **Epidermolytic Exfoliative Toxin**: Acts on desmoglein 1 in the stratum granulosum, causing Staphylococcal Scalded Skin Syndrome (SSSS) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:05">[00:10:05]</a>.
    *   **Hemolysins (Alpha, Beta, Gamma, Delta)** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:46">[00:11:46]</a>:
        *   Beta hemolysin exhibits the hot-cold phenomenon (hemolysis at 37Â°C, enhanced on cooling) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:06">[00:12:06]</a>.
    *   **Panton-Valentine Leukocidin (PVL)**: Often associated with MRSA, acts synergistically with Gamma hemolysin (synergohymenotropic toxin) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:42">[00:12:42]</a>.
    *   **Enterotoxins (A to I, R to TV)**: Cause food poisoning (vomiting action) with a short incubation period of 1-6 hours <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:51">[00:13:51]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:30">[00:15:30]</a>. Commonly associated with milk and meat products (e.g., mayonnaise, cream pastries) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:55">[00:15:55]</a>.
    *   **Enterotoxin F (TSST-1)**: Causes Toxic Shock Syndrome, especially in females with forgotten tampons <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:30">[00:14:30]</a>.

#### Streptococci
*   **Catalase Test**: Negative <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:28">[00:20:28]</a>.
*   **Classification by Hemolysis on Blood Agar** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:19">[00:39:19]</a>:
    *   **Alpha-Hemolysis (Green color)**: Partial hemolysis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:31">[00:39:31]</a>.
        *   *Streptococcus viridans* (e.g., *S. mitis*, *S. mutans*): Not bile soluble, not optochin sensitive, no inulin fermentation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:40">[00:43:40]</a>.
        *   *Streptococcus pneumoniae*: Lens-shaped diplococci with a capsule <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:53">[00:41:53]</a>.
            *   **Characteristics**: Bile soluble, optochin sensitive, inulin fermentation positive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:24">[00:41:24]</a>.
            *   **Colony Morphology**: "Carrom coin" or "draughts-man" colonies due to autolysis causing central depression <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:48">[00:42:48]</a>.
    *   **Beta-Hemolysis (Clear/Yellow color)**: Complete hemolysis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:48">[00:39:48]</a>.
    *   **Gamma-Hemolysis (No hemolysis/Red color)**: No hemolysis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:55">[00:39:55]</a>.
        *   **Enterococcus**:
            *   **Morphology**: [[gram_positive_vs_gram_negative_cocci | Gram-positive]] cocci in pairs or short chains, spectacle-shaped <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:11">[00:53:11]</a>.
            *   **Growth Conditions**: Survives in 6.5% NaCl, resistant to 40% bile <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:21">[00:52:21]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:22">[00:55:22]</a>. Tolerant to heat <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:27">[00:57:27]</a>.
            *   **Culture Media**: Bile Esculin Azide Agar (BEAA) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:12">[00:54:12]</a>. Growth appears black on BEAA due to esculin hydrolysis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:07">[00:55:07]</a>.
            *   **PYR Test**: Positive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:29">[00:52:29]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:36">[00:57:36]</a>.
            *   **Motility**: Non-motile <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:30">[00:56:30]</a>.
            *   **Spore Formation**: Non-spore forming <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:30">[00:56:30]</a>.
            *   **Resistance**: Inherently [[bacteriology_basics_and_introduction | resistant]] to beta-lactam antibiotics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:22">[00:58:22]</a>.
                *   Vancomycin resistance (VRE) is due to a genetic change (vanA gene) that alters the D-Ala-D-Ala binding site to D-Ala-D-Lac or D-Ala-D-Ser <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:04">[00:59:04]</a>.
                *   Treatment options for VRE include Linezolid or Daptomycin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:25">[01:00:25]</a>.
        *   *Streptococcus gallolyticus* (formerly *S. bovis*): Also grows on Bile Esculin Azide Agar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:05">[01:03:05]</a>.
            *   **Clinical Association**: Strongly associated with colorectal polyps and colorectal cancer, often causing sepsis and endocarditis (vegetation on mitral valve) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:02">[01:04:02]</a>.

## Gram-Negative Cocci (Neisseria)
Neisseria are [[gram_positive_vs_gram_negative_cocci | Gram-negative]] cocci <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:24">[01:06:24]</a>.

#### *Neisseria gonorrhoeae* (Gonococcus)
*   **Morphology**: Kidney-bean shaped diplococci, often seen inside cells (intracellular) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:14">[01:07:14]</a>.
*   **Habitat**: Genital tract <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:00">[01:09:00]</a>.
*   **Fermentation**: Glucose only (mnemonic: "Gonorrhea" has 'G') <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:40">[01:09:40]</a>.
*   **Catalase Test**: Positive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:17">[01:10:17]</a>.
*   **Oxidase Test**: Positive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:23">[01:10:23]</a>.
*   **Motility**: Non-motile usually, but can show "twitching motility" due to Type IV pili <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:08">[01:13:08]</a>.
*   **Virulence Factors**: Outer membrane proteins (Opa), IgA1 protease, Pili (Type IV) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:19">[01:11:19]</a>.
*   **Complications**:
    *   **Waterhouse-Friderichsen Syndrome**: Adrenal hemorrhage, a fatal complication of meningococcemia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:35">[01:16:35]</a>.
    *   **Water-Can Perineal Rule**: Caused by *Neisseria gonorrhoeae* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:02">[01:17:02]</a>.
    *   **Fitz-Hugh-Curtis Syndrome**: Perihepatitis (inflammation around the liver), causing "violin string appearance" on radiology <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:16">[01:17:16]</a>.
*   **Treatment**: Ceftriaxone and Azithromycin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:46">[01:15:46]</a>.

#### *Neisseria meningitidis* (Meningococcus)
*   **Morphology**: Lens-shaped diplococci with a capsule <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:31">[01:10:31]</a>.
*   **Habitat**: Nasopharynx <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:38">[01:08:38]</a>.
*   **Fermentation**: Glucose and Maltose (mnemonic: "Meningitidis" has 'M') <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:45">[01:09:45]</a>.
*   **Catalase Test**: Positive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:17">[01:10:17]</a>.
*   **Oxidase Test**: Positive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:23">[01:10:23]</a>.
*   **Motility**: Can also show twitching motility due to Type IV pili <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:16">[01:15:16]</a>.
*   **Virulence Factors**: Capsular polysaccharide (serotypes A, B, C, W135, Y), Outer membrane proteins (Opa), IgA1 protease, Pili <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:02">[01:11:02]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:20">[01:12:20]</a>.
    *   Vaccines protect against A, C, W135, Y, but not serotype B <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:39">[01:11:39]</a>.
*   **Immune Deficiencies**: Increased risk of recurrent *Neisseria meningitidis* infections in patients with terminal complement deficiencies (C5b to C9, components of Membrane Attack Complex - MAC) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:17">[01:19:17]</a>.
    *   C2 deficiency is the most common complement deficiency in humans <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:21:07">[01:21:07]</a>.
    *   Early complement deficiencies (C1, C2, C4) are associated with SLE <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:42">[01:19:42]</a>.
    *   C1 esterase inhibitor deficiency causes hereditary angioedema <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:56">[01:19:56]</a>.
    *   Decay accelerating factor (DAF, CD55) and Membrane Attack Complex inhibitory protein (MIRL, CD59) deficiencies cause Paroxysmal Nocturnal Hemoglobinuria (PNH) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:15">[01:20:15]</a>.

## Diagnostics
### [[microbial_culture_techniques_and_their_controls | Culture Media]] and Staining Techniques
*   **Hugh and Leifson's Media**: Used for glucose utilization, with bromothymol blue as an indicator <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:17">[00:06:17]</a>.
*   **Mannitol Salt Agar (MSA)**: A selective and differential medium for *Staphylococcus aureus* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:44">[00:35:44]</a>. Phenol red is the indicator <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:05">[00:36:05]</a>.
*   **Bile Esculin Azide Agar (BEAA)**: Used for *Enterococcus* and *Streptococcus gallolyticus* identification <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:12">[00:54:12]</a>.

### [[microbiology__diagnosis_using_camp_test | CAMP Test]]
*   Used for the diagnosis of *Streptococcus agalactiae* (Group B Strep) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:17">[00:46:17]</a>.
*   Performed on blood agar with a vertical streak of *Staphylococcus aureus* and a horizontal streak of the test organism <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:27">[00:46:27]</a>.
*   A positive test shows an arrowhead appearance at the intersection due to enhanced hemolysis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:29">[00:48:29]</a>.

### Reverse CAMP Test
*   Used for the diagnosis of *Clostridium perfringens* using *Streptococcus agalactiae* as the horizontal streak <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:48">[00:47:48]</a>.

### Agglutination Tests
*   "Cat sat on a mat" refers to different agglutination tests <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:44">[00:59:44]</a>.

### Coagulation Pathway
*   **Intrinsic Pathway**: Factors 12, 11, 9, 8 activate Factor 10 (12 -> 11 -> 9 -> 8 -> 10) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:21:54">[01:21:54]</a>.
*   **Extrinsic Pathway**: Factors 7 + 3 activate Factor 10 (7+3=10) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:42">[01:22:42]</a>.
*   **Common Pathway**: Factor 10 activates Factor 5, which activates Factor 2 (prothrombin to thrombin), which activates Factor 1 (fibrinogen to fibrin) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:23:05">[01:23:05]</a>.
*   **Calcium (Factor 4)**: Used at the junctional points of pathways <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:24:03">[01:24:03]</a>.
*   **Factor 13 (Fibrin Stabilizing Factor)**: Used at the last step to stabilize the blood clot <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:24:30">[01:24:30]</a>.
*   Factor 6 does not exist <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:23:54">[01:23:54]</a>.

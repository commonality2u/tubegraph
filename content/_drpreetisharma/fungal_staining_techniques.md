Mycology, the study of fungi, is a critical area in microbiology, especially given its increased importance, notably after events like the COVID-19 pandemic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:29">[00:00:29]</a>. Identifying fungi often relies on [[staining_techniques_in_microbiology | staining techniques]] and [[Microscopic techniques and types of microscopes | microscopic examination]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:05">[00:01:05]</a>.

## Overview of Fungal Stains

Various stains are used to identify fungi, each with specific applications and resulting appearances <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>:

*   **Gomori Methanamine Silver (GMS) Stain**
    *   **Appearance**: Fungal hyphae appear black <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:14">[00:01:14]</a>.
    *   **Principle**: Stains containing "silver" tend to turn black <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:39">[00:01:39]</a>.
    *   **Uses**: Used for visualizing fungal elements in tissues, such as *Blastomyces* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:44">[00:12:44]</a> and *Paracoccidioides* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:35">[00:19:35]</a>. It is the only stain that colors *Pneumocystis jirovecii* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:04">[00:45:04]</a>.

*   **Periodic Acid-Schiff (PAS) Stain**
    *   **Appearance**: Fungal hyphae appear pink <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:53">[00:01:53]</a>.
    *   **Uses**: Fungal hyphae are PAS positive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:01">[00:02:01]</a>. Useful for visualizing fungi like *Aspergillus* and *Mucor* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:13">[00:40:13]</a>.

*   **Calco Fluor White**
    *   **Appearance**: Gives a fluorescent appearance against a dark background <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:09">[00:02:09]</a>.
    *   **Principle**: It is a fluorescent dye <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:30">[00:02:30]</a>.

*   **Lactophenol Cotton Blue (LPCB)**
    *   **Appearance**: Fungal elements appear blue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:11">[00:03:11]</a>.
    *   **Uses**: Best for studying culture morphology of fungi <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:56">[00:00:56]</a>. This includes fungi grown on Sabouraud's Dextrose Agar (SDA) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:44">[00:02:44]</a>. Used for examining the morphology of *Histoplasma* mold form <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:40">[00:15:40]</a>, *Penicillium marneffei* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:26">[00:20:26]</a>, *Coccidioides* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:20">[00:22:20]</a>, *Sporothrix schenckii* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:37">[00:25:37]</a>, and *Aspergillus* species <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:55">[00:36:55]</a>.

*   **India Ink Stain**
    *   **Appearance**: Negative stain where the background is dark, and the organism remains unstained, showing a clear halo around it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:39">[00:04:39]</a>.
    *   **Uses**: Primarily used for visualizing the capsule of *Cryptococcus neoformans* in cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) samples <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:52">[00:04:52]</a>.

*   **Mucicarmine Stain**
    *   **Appearance**: Stains *Cryptococcus neoformans* a rose-pink color <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:01">[00:05:01]</a>.
    *   **Uses**: Used for visualizing the capsule of *Cryptococcus neoformans* in biopsy specimens <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:20">[00:05:20]</a>.

*   **Potassium Hydroxide (KOH) Mount**
    *   **Appearance**: Colorless, used for direct microscopic examination <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:47">[01:04:47]</a>.
    *   **Principle**: A 10% KOH solution is used to digest keratin in samples, softening them for easier microscopic examination <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:01">[01:04:01]</a>.
    *   **Uses**: Employed for samples containing keratin, such as skin, hair, and nails <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:15">[01:04:15]</a>. Commonly used for identifying *Malassezia furfur* in [[Diabetic nephropathy pathology and special stains | tinea versicolor]] cases <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:38">[01:03:38]</a>.

## Mycology Crash Course: An Overview

This section covers essential details concerning fungi, including various types and etiological aspects pertinent to examinations. It also includes important clinical images and questions from previous years' examinations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:10">[00:00:10]</a>.

## Fungal Stains and Techniques

When studying the [[clinical_pathology_practical_techniques | culture morphology]] of fungi, [[Staining techniques in microbiology | staining]] is crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:39">[00:00:39]</a>.

### Lactophenol Cotton Blue (LPCB)

*   **Application**: LPCB is the best stain for studying [[clinical_pathology_practical_techniques | culture morphology]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:56">[00:00:56]</a>.
*   **Appearance**: Fungal elements appear blue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:11">[00:03:11]</a>.
*   **Method**: After growing fungal samples on a [[Staining techniques for different microorganisms | culture medium]] like Sabouraud's Dextrose Agar (SDA), a specimen can be taken to observe its morphology under the microscope using LPCB <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:42">[00:02:42]</a>.

### Gomori Methanamine Silver (GMS) Stain

*   **Appearance**: Fungal hyphae appear black <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:14">[00:01:14]</a>.
*   **Nomenclature**: GMS stands for Gomori Methanamine Silver stain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:19">[00:01:19]</a>.
*   **Trick for identification**: Stains containing "silver" typically result in a blackish color <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:37">[00:01:37]</a>.

### Periodic Acid-Schiff (PAS) Stain

*   **Appearance**: Fungal hyphae appear pink <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:53">[00:01:53]</a>.
*   **Characteristic**: Fungal hyphae are PAS positive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:01">[00:02:01]</a>.

### Calco Fluor White

*   **Appearance**: The stained substance appears fluorescent against a dark background <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:07">[00:02:07]</a>.
*   **Nature**: It is a fluorescent dye <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:30">[00:02:30]</a>.

### Staining for *Cryptococcus neoformans*

*   *Cryptococcus* is known for causing meningitis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:43">[00:03:43]</a>.
*   **Sample Types**: Specimens can include cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) from living patients or biopsy tissues (e.g., brain biopsy) from autopsy cases <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:54">[00:03:54]</a>.
*   **India Ink Stain**: Used for CSF samples <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:52">[00:04:52]</a>. This is a negative stain where the background is colored, and the organisms remain unstained <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:34">[00:04:34]</a>.
*   **Mucicarmine Stain**: Used for biopsy specimens <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:06">[00:05:06]</a>, where *Cryptococcus* appears rose-pink <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:01">[00:05:01]</a>.

### Potassium Hydroxide (KOH) Mount

*   **Principle**: Used for samples containing keratin (e.g., skin, hair, nails) because KOH digests keratin, making microscopic examination easier <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:01">[01:04:01]</a>.

### [[Dimorphic Fungi and Their Characteristics | Dimorphic Fungi]]

[[Dimorphic Fungi and Their Characteristics | Dimorphic fungi]] exist in two forms: yeast and mold (hyphae) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:43">[00:07:43]</a>.
*   **Yeast form**: Seen at 37 degrees Celsius (rhymes with "heat") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:13">[00:08:13]</a>.
*   **Mold form**: Seen at 25 degrees Celsius (rhymes with "cold") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:16">[00:08:16]</a>.
*   **Examples**: *Blastomyces*, *Histoplasma*, *Penicillium marneffei* (now *Talaromyces marneffei*), *Paracoccidioidomycosis*, *Coccidioidomycosis*, and *Sporothrix* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:12">[00:09:12]</a>.
*   **Key characteristic**: [[Dimorphic Fungi and Their Characteristics | Dimorphic fungi]] are noted for their systemic involvement, commonly affecting organs like the lungs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:23">[00:11:23]</a>.

#### *Blastomyces*

*   **Other names**: North American Blastomycosis, Gilchrist's disease, Chicago disease <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:04">[00:11:04]</a>.
*   **Microscopic feature**: Characterized by a "figure of eight" appearance due to broad-based budding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:55">[00:11:55]</a>. Stained with GMS appears black <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:44">[00:12:44]</a>.

#### *Histoplasma*

*   **Other names**: Ohio's disease, Darling's disease <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:11">[00:14:11]</a>.
*   **Clinical similarity**: Resembles tuberculosis (TB) in its presentation, causing lung involvement, caseating granulomas, and caseous necrosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:14">[00:13:14]</a>.
*   **Yeast form**: Very tiny (2-4 microns), often seen as multiple dot-like structures inside macrophages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:40">[00:14:40]</a>.
*   **Mold form (LPCB stain)**: Shows tuberculate macroconidia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:17">[00:16:17]</a>.

#### *Paracoccidioides*

*   **Other name**: South American Blastomycosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:05">[00:17:05]</a>.
*   **Microscopic features (GMS stain)**: Exhibits "Mickey Mouse" appearance or "mariner's wheel" (pilot wheel) appearance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:09">[00:19:09]</a>.

#### *Penicillium marneffei* (now *Talaromyces marneffei*)

*   **Other name**: *Talaromyces marneffei* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:57">[00:19:57]</a>.
*   **Natural reservoir**: Bamboo rats <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:16">[00:20:16]</a>.
*   **Microscopic features (LPCB stain)**: Conidia show a "broom or brush" appearance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:33">[00:20:33]</a>.

#### *Coccidioides*

*   **Other names**: Desert rheumatism, Valley fever, Arizona <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:30">[00:21:30]</a>.
*   **Clinical presentation**: Often associated with joint involvement and fever <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:57">[00:21:57]</a>.
*   **Microscopic features (LPCB stain)**: Characterized by barrel-shaped arthrospores <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:28">[00:22:28]</a>.

#### *Sporothrix schenckii*

*   **Other name**: Rose Gardener's disease <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:04">[00:23:04]</a>.
*   **Transmission**: Typically acquired through a thorn prick, often from rose bushes, leading to cutaneous lesions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:25">[00:23:25]</a>.
*   **Clinical presentation**: Lesions develop along lymphatic channels (lymphocutaneous disorder) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:12">[00:24:12]</a>.
*   **Microscopic features**:
    *   **Cigar body**: Round yeast structures seen in the center of asteroid bodies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:45">[00:24:45]</a>.
    *   **Asteroid body**: Cigar bodies surrounded by starry, pink projections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:03">[00:25:03]</a>.
*   **Culture morphology (LPCB stain)**: Shows "rosettes of conidia," resembling roses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:50">[00:25:50]</a>.

### *Chromoblastomycosis*

*   **Other name**: *Verrucous dermatitis* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:44">[00:32:44]</a>.
*   **Clinical presentation**: Causes cauliflower-like warty lesions, often on the feet of forest workers who walk barefoot <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:01">[00:33:01]</a>.
*   **Microscopic features**: Exhibits characteristic pigmented (brown/copper-colored) bodies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:30">[00:33:30]</a>:
    *   Copper penny bodies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:36">[00:33:36]</a>
    *   Muriform bodies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:49">[00:33:49]</a>
    *   Medlar bodies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:52">[00:33:52]</a>
    *   Sclerotic bodies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:54">[00:33:54]</a>

### *Aspergillus* Species

    *   *Aspergillus niger*: Black colonies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:18">[00:35:18]</a>.
    *   *Aspergillus fumigatus*: Smoky green color (like fumes) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:32">[00:35:32]</a>.
    *   *Aspergillus flavus*: Bright yellow-green color (like a flower) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:46">[00:35:46]</a>.

    *   *Aspergillus niger*: Remains black <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:23">[00:37:23]</a>.
    *   *Aspergillus fumigatus*: Conidia arise only from the top part of the vesicle, resembling rising fumes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:50">[00:37:50]</a>.
    *   *Aspergillus flavus*: Conidia arise from all around the vesicle, like petals of a flower <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:29">[00:38:29]</a>.
*   **Hyphae**: *Aspergillus* shows narrow-angled (acute angle) septate hyphae <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:41">[00:40:41]</a>.

### *Mucor* Species

*   **Hyphae**: *Mucor* shows broad, ribbon-like, aseptate hyphae with perpendicular (90-degree) branching <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:39">[00:41:39]</a>.

### *Pneumocystis jirovecii*

*   **Clinical name**: Formerly *Pneumocystis carinii*, infection is known as PCP (Pneumocystis *carinii* pneumonia) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:57">[00:43:57]</a>.
*   **Pathology**: Causes plasma cell pneumonia, characterized by abundant plasma cells and exudate within the alveoli <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:18">[00:44:18]</a>.
*   **Context**: Occurs in immunocompromised individuals, such as HIV-positive patients or transplant recipients <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:46">[00:44:46]</a>.
*   **Staining**: Exclusively stained by GMS, appearing black <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:01">[00:45:01]</a>.
*   **Appearance**: Gives a "crushed ping-pong ball," "hat," or "cup and saucer" appearance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:26">[00:45:26]</a>.

### *Rhinosporidium seeberi*

*   **Clinical presentation**: Causes nasal polyps <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:09">[00:47:09]</a>.
*   **Classification**: Historically considered a fungus, now categorized as an aquatic protozoan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:30">[00:47:30]</a>.
*   **Microscopic features**: Large, round sporangia containing endospores <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:00">[00:48:00]</a>.

### *Candida* Species

*   **Key species**: *Candida albicans* is commonly discussed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:12">[00:50:12]</a>.
*   **Morphology**: Shows two forms: budding yeast and pseudo-hyphae <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:16">[00:50:16]</a>. Pseudo-hyphae are elongated budding forms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:56">[00:50:56]</a>.
*   **Diagnostic test**: Reynolds-Brodie phenomenon (Germ Tube Test) is positive for *Candida albicans* and *Candida dubliniensis* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:21">[00:51:21]</a>. In this test, *Candida* colonies are incubated in serum for two hours, resulting in the formation of a germ tube <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:51">[00:51:51]</a>.

### Dermatophytes

[[Diagnosis and identification of bacterial and fungal infections | Dermatophytes]] are fungi that primarily infect skin, hair, and nails <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:20">[00:54:20]</a>. Unlike [[Dimorphic Fungi and Their Characteristics | dimorphic fungi]], they typically do not cause systemic infections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:10">[00:54:10]</a>.

*   **Types (TME mnemonic)**:
    *   *Trichophyton*: Infects skin, hair, and nails <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:41">[00:55:41]</a>. Shows abundant microconidia and pencil-shaped macroconidia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:57">[00:59:57]</a>.
    *   *Microsporum*: Infects skin and hair (hair is "micro" thinner) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:09">[00:56:09]</a>. Shows fewer microconidia and spindle-shaped macroconidia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:10">[01:00:10]</a>.
    *   *Epidermophyton*: Infects skin and nails (ends with "n" for nail) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:12">[00:56:12]</a>. Shows least or no microconidia and club-shaped macroconidia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:02">[01:00:02]</a>.

*   **Differentiation**: Differentiated based on the morphology of macroconidia (pencil, spindle, club shapes) and the abundance of microconidia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:32">[00:57:32]</a>.

### *Malassezia furfur* (*Tinea versicolor*)

*   **Microscopic features (KOH mount)**: Presents as "spaghetti and meatball" appearance, with short hyphae (spaghetti) and yeast forms (meatballs) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:03">[01:05:03]</a>.
*   **Culture**: Grows on SDA supplemented with olive oil because it is lipophilic (fat-loving) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:06">[01:06:06]</a>. The colonies show a "fried egg" appearance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:42">[01:05:42]</a>.

## Modern Fungal Diagnostic Tests

Beyond traditional [[staining_techniques_in_microbiology | staining techniques]], molecular and serological tests are important for [[diagnosis and identification of bacterial and fungal infections | diagnosis and identification]].

*   **Beta-1,3-D-Glucan Testing**:
    *   **Principle**: Beta-D-glucan is a component of the fungal cell wall <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:43">[01:07:43]</a>.
    *   **Application**: Used for diagnosing various fungal infections, including *Candida*, *Aspergillus*, *Histoplasma*, *Fusarium*, dermatophytes, and *Pneumocystis jirovecii* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:06">[01:08:06]</a>.
    *   **Exceptions**: Beta-D-glucan testing is negative for *Blastomyces*, *Mucor*, and *Cryptococcus* (BMC mnemonic) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:24">[01:08:24]</a>.

*   **Galactomannan Assay**:
    *   **Principle**: Galactomannan is a specific fungal antigen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:50">[01:07:50]</a>.
    *   **Application**: Primarily used for detecting *Aspergillus* infections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:59">[01:07:59]</a>.

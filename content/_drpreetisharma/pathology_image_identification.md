This article provides a super-fast recap of key images encountered in pathology and microbiology, focusing on rapid identification and key differentiating features <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:16">[00:00:16]</a>.

## Necrosis Types

*   **Coagulative Necrosis** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:06">[00:02:06]</a>:
    *   Often seen in cases of heart attack, kidney, or liver involvement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:10">[00:02:10]</a>.
    *   Characterized by "pinkish-pinkish" cells externally <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:15">[00:02:15]</a>.
    *   Cells are referred to as "ghost cells" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:17">[00:02:17]</a>.
    *   Presents with a "tombstone appearance" due to numerous ghost cells <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:20">[00:02:20]</a>.
    *   "Row of tombstone appearance" is also seen in Pemphigus Vulgaris <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:26">[00:02:26]</a>.
    *   Often appears as a wedge-shaped area <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:31">[00:02:31]</a>.
*   **Liquefactive Necrosis** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:37">[00:02:37]</a>:
    *   Typically seen in brain images with a history of stroke <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:40">[00:02:40]</a>.
*   **Caseous Necrosis** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:02">[00:03:02]</a>:
    *   Shows a "cheese appearance" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:04">[00:03:04]</a>.
    *   Commonly associated with tuberculosis (TB) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:05">[00:03:05]</a>.
    *   Also seen in fungal infections, particularly Histoplasma, but can include Coccidioidomycosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:10">[00:03:10]</a>.
*   **Fat Necrosis** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:25">[00:03:25]</a>:
    *   Characterized by a "fatty, fatty yellow" appearance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:23">[00:03:23]</a>.
    *   Presents as "chalky white areas" made up of calcium <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:29">[00:03:29]</a>.
    *   Often related to fat-rich organs like the breast or omentum <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:34">[00:03:34]</a>.
*   **Fibrinoid Necrosis** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:46">[00:03:46]</a>:
    *   Seen in blood vessels, appearing pinkish <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:41">[00:03:41]</a>.
    *   Associated with conditions like Polyarteritis Nodosa, Rheumatic Heart Disease, Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE), and Hypertension <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:49">[00:03:49]</a>.

## Apoptosis vs. Necrosis in Electrophoresis

*   **Gel Electrophoresis** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:04">[00:04:04]</a>:
    *   **Step-ladder pattern**: More common in Apoptosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:30">[00:04:30]</a>.
    *   **Smiling pattern**: More common in Necrosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:36">[00:04:36]</a>.

## Pigments and Deposits

*   **Lipofuscin** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:10">[00:05:10]</a>:
    *   Characterized by a brown peri-nuclear pigment around the nucleus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:02">[00:05:02]</a>.
    *   Known as the "old age pigment" and is associated with free radicals and wear and tear <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:14">[00:05:14]</a>.
*   **Hemosiderin** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:32">[00:05:32]</a>:
    *   Associated with a history of bleeding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:28">[00:05:28]</a>.
    *   Indicates iron, and the specific stain is Perl's Prussian Blue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:34">[00:05:34]</a>.
    *   Iron overload is known as Hemochromatosis, also identified with Prussian Blue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:48">[00:05:48]</a>.
*   **Lipids** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:04">[00:06:04]</a>:
    *   Stains: Oil Red O (red) and Sudan Black B (black) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:06">[00:06:06]</a>.
*   **Calcium** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:15">[00:06:15]</a>:
    *   Stains: Von Kossa (black) and Alizarin Red S (red) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:20">[00:06:20]</a>. Alizarin Red S detects minute amounts of calcium <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:31">[00:06:31]</a>.
*   **Amyloid** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:07">[01:00:07]</a>:
    *   Gross appearance: "Waxy Kidney" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:01">[01:00:01]</a>.
    *   Stain: Congo Red <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:30">[01:00:30]</a>.
        *   On light microscopy: Salmon pink color <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:46">[01:00:46]</a>.
        *   On polarizing microscope: "Apple Green" birefringence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:41">[01:00:41]</a>.
    *   Electron Microscopy: Fibrils with a diameter of 7.5 to 10 nanometers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:57">[01:00:57]</a>.
    *   X-ray crystallography: Beta-pleated sheet configuration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:04">[01:01:04]</a>.

## Giant Cells and Granulomas

*   **Langhans Giant Cell** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:59">[00:06:59]</a>:
    *   Seen in TB (tuberculosis) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:59">[00:06:59]</a>.
    *   Nuclei arranged in a horseshoe or peripheral pattern <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:05">[00:07:05]</a>.
*   **Touton Giant Cell** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:03">[00:07:03]</a>:
    *   Round nuclei around a central lipid inclusion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:03">[00:07:03]</a>.
    *   Seen in conditions with lipid problems, such as Xanthoma <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:09">[00:07:09]</a>.
*   **Foreign Body Giant Cell** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:30">[00:07:30]</a>:
    *   Nuclei scattered haphazardly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:28">[00:07:28]</a>.
    *   History often includes exposure to foreign material like talc powder or sutures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:32">[00:07:32]</a>.
*   **Finkeldey Cell** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:46">[00:07:46]</a>:
    *   Giant cell with intra-nuclear and intra-cytoplasmic inclusions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:42">[00:07:42]</a>.
    *   Seen in Measles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:50">[00:07:50]</a>.
    *   Measles vaccine uses the Edmonston-Zagreb strain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:55">[00:07:55]</a>.
    *   Associated with Koplik spots and the "3 Cs": cough, coryza, and conjunctivitis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:02">[00:08:02]</a>.
*   **Reed-Sternberg Cell** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:40">[00:08:40]</a>:
    *   Characteristic "owl's eye" appearance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:21">[00:08:21]</a>.
    *   Seen in Hodgkin Lymphoma <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:40">[00:08:40]</a>.
    *   Described as having "red or pink eyes" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:26">[00:08:26]</a>.
*   **Cytomegalovirus (CMV) Inclusion** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:35">[00:08:35]</a>:
    *   Also has an "owl's eye" appearance but with "blue colored eyes" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:35">[00:08:35]</a>.
*   **Stellate Granuloma** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:53">[00:08:53]</a>:
    *   "Star-like" appearance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:53">[00:08:53]</a>.
    *   Seen in Cat Scratch Disease and Lymphogranuloma Venereum (LGV) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:57">[00:08:57]</a>.
    *   LGV is associated with painful buboes and the "groove sign" (inguinal lymph nodes above and below the inguinal ligament) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:12">[00:09:12]</a>.
*   **Dirk Granuloma** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:33">[00:09:33]</a>:
    *   Characterized by two parallel lines <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:31">[00:09:31]</a>.
    *   Seen in cerebral malaria, caused by Plasmodium Falciparum <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:37">[00:09:37]</a>.

## Lysosomal Storage Disorders

*   **Gaucher Disease** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:27">[01:02:27]</a>:
    *   "Crumpled tissue paper appearance" of cells <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:23">[01:02:23]</a>.
    *   Associated with bone pain ("ouch bone pain") and CNS problems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:29">[01:02:29]</a>.
    *   Deficiency of beta-glucocerebrosidase enzyme <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:36">[01:02:36]</a>.
*   **Tay-Sachs Disease** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:04">[01:02:04]</a>:
    *   "Onion skin pattern" visible on electron microscopy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:01">[01:02:01]</a>.
    *   Deficiency of alpha-hexosaminidase A <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:10">[01:02:10]</a>.
    *   Associated with a "cherry red spot in the macula" of the eye <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:19">[01:02:19]</a>.
*   **Niemann-Pick Disease** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:30">[01:02:30]</a>:
    *   "Zebra bodies" seen on electron microscopy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:28">[01:02:28]</a>.
    *   Deficiency of sphingomyelinase enzyme <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:34">[01:02:34]</a>.
    *   Characterized by splenomegaly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:50">[01:02:50]</a>.

## Cytogenetics and Molecular Techniques

*   **Karyotyping** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:06">[01:03:06]</a>:
    *   Arrested in metaphase for analysis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:19">[01:03:19]</a>.
    *   For light microscopy: G-banding (Giemsa banding) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:26">[01:03:26]</a>.
    *   For fluorescent microscopy: Q-banding (Quinacrine banding) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:39">[01:03:39]</a>.
    *   Examples of chromosomal abnormalities: Trisomy 21 (Down Syndrome), Trisomy 18 (Edwards Syndrome), Trisomy 13 (Patau Syndrome) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:07">[01:02:07]</a>.
*   **Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization (FISH)** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:36">[01:03:36]</a>:
    *   Detects chromosomal disorders, including monosomy and translocations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:40">[01:03:40]</a>.
    *   Example: BCR-ABL translocation (red and green signals fusing) on chromosome 9 and 22, seen in Chronic Myeloid Leukemia (CML) (Philadelphia Chromosome) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:19">[01:03:19]</a>.
    *   Cannot detect point mutations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:54">[01:03:54]</a>.
*   **Comparative Genomic Hybridization (CGH)** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:05">[01:04:05]</a>:
    *   Uses three colors (red, yellow, green) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:13">[01:04:13]</a>.
    *   Yellow indicates normal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:17">[01:04:17]</a>.
    *   Red (upper color) indicates gene gain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:20">[01:04:20]</a>.
    *   Green (lower color) indicates gene loss <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:22">[01:04:22]</a>.

## Immunofluorescence Patterns (ANA)

*   **Antinuclear Antibody (ANA) Patterns** (using Immunofluorescence) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:46">[01:04:46]</a>:
    *   **Homogenous pattern (H)**: Even green staining across the nucleus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:04">[01:05:04]</a>. Antibodies are against dsDNA, histones, and other nuclear components <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:10">[01:05:10]</a>.
    *   **Peripheral pattern (P)**: Green staining concentrated at the periphery of the nucleus, resembling dsDNA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:25">[01:05:25]</a>. Detects anti-dsDNA antibodies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:34">[01:05:34]</a>.
    *   **Nucleolar pattern (N)**: Single dot or few dots of staining within the nucleus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:42">[01:05:42]</a>. Antibodies are against RNA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:49">[01:05:49]</a>.
    *   **Speckled pattern (S)**: Numerous dot-like specks throughout the nucleus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:57">[01:05:57]</a>. This is the most common and non-specific pattern, seen in SLE, Sjögren's syndrome, and other autoimmune diseases <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:02">[01:06:02]</a>.
    *   **Centromeric pattern (C)**: Distinct dots corresponding to centromeres <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:19">[01:06:19]</a>. Detects anti-centromeric antibodies, seen in CREST syndrome <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:31">[01:06:31]</a>.

## Vascular Pathologies

*   **Large Vessel Vasculitis** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:16">[01:08:16]</a>:
    *   **Temporal Arteritis / Giant Cell Arteritis** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:24">[01:08:24]</a>:
        *   Biopsy of temporal artery. Treatment with steroids should not be delayed to prevent blindness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:27">[01:08:27]</a>.
        *   Stain: Verhoeff-Van Gieson (VVG) stain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:43">[01:08:43]</a>.
        *   Shows fragmentation of the internal elastic lamina <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:46">[01:08:46]</a>.
    *   **Takayasu Arteritis** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:59">[01:08:59]</a>:
        *   Involves the arch of the aorta and its branches <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:07">[01:09:07]</a>.
        *   Characterized by narrowing of subclavian and other vessels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:03">[01:09:03]</a>.
        *   Known as "pulseless disease" due to absent radial pulse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:10">[01:09:10]</a>.
*   **Medium Vessel Vasculitis** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:18">[01:09:18]</a>:
    *   **Polyarteritis Nodosa (PAN)** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:22">[01:09:22]</a>:
        *   Shows presence of fibrinoid necrosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:24">[01:09:24]</a>.
        *   Associated with Hepatitis B virus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:31">[01:09:31]</a>.
        *   Does NOT involve pulmonary blood vessels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:37">[01:09:37]</a>.
    *   **Kawasaki Disease** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:42">[01:09:42]</a>:
        *   Known for the "strawberry tongue" appearance, also seen in Scarlet Fever <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:44">[01:09:44]</a>.
    *   **Buerger's Disease (Thromboangiitis Obliterans)** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:53">[01:09:53]</a>:
        *   History of bidi/cigarette smoking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:55">[01:09:55]</a>.
        *   Shows thrombosis, inflammation (thrombo-), and obliteration (blocked) of blood vessels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:09">[01:10:09]</a>.
        *   Patient experiences intermittent claudication (pain while walking) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:25">[01:10:25]</a>.
*   **Small Vessel Vasculitis (ANCA-associated)** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:35">[01:10:35]</a>:
    *   Identified via immunofluorescence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:44">[01:10:44]</a>.
    *   **c-ANCA (Cytoplasmic ANCA)**: Staining throughout the cytoplasm, around the nucleus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:51">[01:10:51]</a>.
        *   Also known as PR3-ANCA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:15">[01:11:15]</a>.
        *   Primarily seen in [[Wegener's granulomatosis | Wegener Granulomatosis]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:22">[01:11:22]</a>.
    *   **p-ANCA (Perinuclear ANCA)**: Staining concentrated around the nucleus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:31">[01:11:31]</a>.
        *   Also known as MPO-ANCA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:36">[01:11:36]</a>.
        *   Seen in [[Wegener's granulomatosis | Wegener Granulomatosis]], Microscopic Polyangiitis, Churg-Strauss Syndrome, Ulcerative Colitis, and Primary Sclerosing Cholangitis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:41">[01:11:41]</a>.
        *   p-ANCA is "not loyal" and can be seen in many conditions, while c-ANCA is only for [[Wegener's granulomatosis | Wegener Granulomatosis]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:15">[01:12:15]</a>.
        *   [[Wegener's granulomatosis | Wegener Granulomatosis]] can show both p-ANCA and c-ANCA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:52">[01:11:52]</a>.

## Myocardial Infarction

*   **Gross Diagnosis with Triphenyltetrazolium chloride (TPTC) stain** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:37">[01:13:37]</a>:
    *   Normal heart tissue stains brick red <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:48">[01:13:48]</a>.
    *   Infarcted (dead) tissue appears pale <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:51">[01:13:51]</a>.
    *   TPTC stains lactate dehydrogenase (LDH) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:00">[01:14:00]</a>.
*   **Contraction Band Necrosis** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:20">[01:14:20]</a>:
    *   Fibers show bands forming in between <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:16">[01:14:16]</a>.
    *   Seen in reperfusion injury <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:25">[01:14:25]</a>.

## Cardiomyopathies

*   **Dilated Cardiomyopathy (DCM)** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:55">[01:14:55]</a>:
    *   "Ninja star nucleus" appearance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:45">[01:14:45]</a>.
    *   Most common cause is idiopathic, followed by alcohol <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:57">[01:14:57]</a>.
    *   Genetic cause: Titan gene mutation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:05">[01:15:05]</a>.
*   **Takotsubo Cardiomyopathy (Broken Heart Syndrome)** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:18">[01:15:18]</a>:
    *   Caused by elevated catecholamines <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:31">[01:15:31]</a>.
*   **Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy (HCM)** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:41">[01:15:41]</a>:
    *   Often presents as sudden death in young athletes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:38">[01:15:38]</a>.
    *   Grossly shows a "banana-shaped heart" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:43">[01:15:43]</a>.
    *   Microscopically shows "helter-skelter" cardiac fibers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:53">[01:15:53]</a>.
*   **Restrictive Cardiomyopathy (RCM)** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:04">[01:16:04]</a>:
    *   Most common cause is Amyloidosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:07">[01:16:07]</a>.
    *   Shows pink material outside myocytes and cardiac muscle fibers, Congo red positive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:18">[01:16:18]</a>.

## Endocarditis

*   **Rheumatic Heart Disease (RHD)** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:40">[01:16:40]</a>:
    *   Shows small, "vati-vati" vegetations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:37">[01:16:37]</a>.
    *   Characterized by Aschoff bodies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:42">[01:16:42]</a>.
    *   Anitschkow cells (also known as "caterpillar cells" or "owl cells") are seen within Aschoff bodies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:51">[01:16:51]</a>.
    *   Chronic RHD can lead to "fish mouth mitral valve" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:24">[01:17:24]</a>.
        *   *Note*: "Fish mouth vertebra" is seen in Osteoporosis and Sickle Cell Anemia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:30">[01:17:30]</a>.
*   **Infective Endocarditis (IE)** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:56">[01:17:56]</a>:
    *   Characterized by large and bulky vegetations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:54">[01:17:54]</a>.
*   **Non-Bacterial Thrombotic Endocarditis (NBTE)** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:17">[01:18:17]</a>:
    *   Small, friable (breaking easily) vegetations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:01">[01:18:01]</a>.
    *   Tendency for embolization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:09">[01:18:09]</a>.
    *   Often formed in hypercoagulable states, such as with malignancy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:19">[01:18:19]</a>.
*   **Libman-Sacks Endocarditis** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:34">[01:18:34]</a>:
    *   Seen in Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:37">[01:18:37]</a>.
    *   Vegetations are seen on both upper and lower surfaces of the valve, predominantly on the lower surface <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:44">[01:18:44]</a>.

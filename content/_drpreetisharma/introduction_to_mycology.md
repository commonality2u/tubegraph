## Fungal Staining Techniques

The best method to study culture morphology of fungi is by using Lactophenol Cotton Blue (LPCB) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:39">[00:00:39]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:56">[00:00:56]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:01">[00:03:01]</a>.

Different stains used for identifying fungi include:
*   **Gomori Methenamine Silver (GMS) Stain**: Fungal hyphae appear black <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:12">[00:01:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:19">[00:01:19]</a>. The presence of the word "silver" in a stain name indicates a blackish color <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:37">[00:01:37]</a>.
*   **PAS (Periodic Acid-Schiff) Stain**: Fungal hyphae appear pink <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:53">[00:01:53]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:56">[00:01:56]</a>.
*   **Calcofluor White**: A fluorescent dye where the background is dark and the organism appears fluorescent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:05">[00:02:05]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:12">[00:02:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:27">[00:02:27]</a>.
*   **Lactophenol Cotton Blue (LPCB)**: Used to study the morphology of fungal cultures grown on media like Sabouraud's Dextrose Agar (SDA) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:57">[00:02:57]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:09">[00:03:09]</a>. The stain is blue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:11">[00:03:11]</a>.

### Stains for Cryptococcus

*   **India Ink Stain**: A negative stain used for cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) samples in cases of cryptococcal meningitis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:39">[00:03:39]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:34">[00:04:34]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:52">[00:04:52]</a>. The background gets a color, and the organisms remain unstained <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:34">[00:03:34]</a>.
*   **Mucicarmine Stain**: Used for biopsy specimens of Cryptococcus, where the organism appears rose pink <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:57">[00:04:57]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:01">[00:05:01]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:06">[00:05:06]</a>.
*   For final diagnosis of Cryptococcus, a latex agglutination test (LAT) is performed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:53">[00:06:53]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:56">[00:06:56]</a>.

## Dimorphic Fungi

Dimorphic fungi exist in two forms ("di" means two, "morphic" means morphology): yeast and mold <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:38">[00:07:38]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:43">[00:07:43]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:49">[00:07:49]</a>.
*   **Yeast form**: Tiny, seen at 37 degrees Celsius (rhymes with "heat") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:52">[00:07:52]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:13">[00:08:13]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:35">[00:08:35]</a>.
*   **Mold form (hyphae form)**: Seen at 25 degrees Celsius (rhymes with "cold") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:58">[00:07:58]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:16">[00:08:16]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:43">[00:08:43]</a>.

Dimorphic fungi are known for causing systemic involvement, affecting organs like the lungs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:21">[00:11:21]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:30">[00:11:30]</a>.

"Body Heat Probably Changes Shape" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:01">[00:09:01]</a>:
*   **B** for Blastomyces <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:12">[00:09:12]</a>
*   **H** for Histoplasma <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:16">[00:09:16]</a>

### Specific Dimorphic Fungi

#### Blastomycosis
*   **Other Names**: North American Blastomycosis, Gilchrist's Disease, Chicago Disease <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:04">[00:11:04]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:06">[00:11:06]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:33">[00:17:33]</a>.

Often seen with GMS stain, appearing black <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:42">[00:12:42]</a>.

*   **Other Names**: Ohio's Disease, Darling's Disease <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:09">[00:14:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:15">[00:14:15]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:54">[00:17:54]</a>.
*   **Yeast Form**: Very tiny (2-4 microns), often seen inside macrophages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:40">[00:14:40]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:42">[00:14:42]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:09">[00:15:09]</a>.
*   **Mold Form**: Shows "tuberculate macroconidia," resembling TB <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:17">[00:16:17]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:22">[00:16:22]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:44">[00:16:44]</a>.

#### Paracoccidioidomycosis

*   **Key Identification**: Shows "Mickey Mouse" appearance or "Mariner's Wheel" (or Pilot Wheel) appearance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:09">[00:19:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:14">[00:19:14]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:27">[00:19:27]</a>. Often seen with GMS stain, appearing black <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:33">[00:19:33]</a>.

#### Penicillium marneffei (Talaromyces marneffei)

#### Sporotrichosis

## Fungal Diagnostic Tests

During the COVID-19 pandemic, two markers for fungal infections gained prominence:
*   **Beta-D-glucan Test**: Detects a normal cell wall component of most fungi <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:43">[01:07:43]</a>. It is positive for many fungal organisms including Candida, Aspergillus, Histoplasma, Fusarium, [[Dermatophytes and Identification | Dermatophytes]], and [[Pneumocystis jirovecii (formerly Pneumocystis carinii) | Pneumocystis jirovecii]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:06">[01:08:06]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:11">[01:08:11]</a>.

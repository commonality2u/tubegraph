*Bacillus* species are spore-forming bacteria, with *Bacillus anthracis* being an aerobe, meaning it requires oxygen to thrive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:38:00">[01:38:00]</a>.

## [[Virulence factors of Bacillus anthracis | Virulence Factors]] of [[bacillus_anthracis | Bacillus anthracis]]
[[bacillus_anthracis | Bacillus anthracis]] possesses two primary virulence factors: a capsule and a toxin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:36:00">[02:36:00]</a>.

*   **Capsule**: Unlike most bacterial capsules which are made of polysaccharides, the capsule of [[bacillus_anthracis | Bacillus anthracis]] is unique, being composed of polypeptide <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:56:00">[02:56:00]</a>.
*   **Toxin**: The toxin has three parts, often remembered by the acronym EPL (English Premier League) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:18:00">[03:18:00]</a>:
    *   **Edema Factor (E)**: Causes edema by increasing cyclic AMP <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:41:00">[03:41:00]</a>.
    *   **Protective Factor (P)**: Protects the bacteria <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:39:00">[03:39:00]</a>.
    *   **Lethal Factor (L)**: Causes killing and destruction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:41:00">[03:41:00]</a>.
    *   *Note*: Labile toxin is not a virulence factor of [[bacillus_anthracis | Bacillus anthracis]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:20:00">[02:20:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:27:00">[04:27:00]</a>.

## Types of Anthrax
There are three main types of anthrax, categorized by their route of infection <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:44:00">[04:44:00]</a>:

1.  **Cutaneous Anthrax**:
    *   Affects the skin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:49:00">[04:49:00]</a>.
    *   Known as "Hide Porter's Disease" because it affects workers who carry animal hides/skin on their neck, shoulders, or back <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:11:00">[05:11:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:47:00">[05:47:00]</a>.
    *   Characterized by a blackish lesion called a "malignant pustule" or "eschar" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:03:00">[06:03:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:27:00">[07:27:00]</a>. The term "malignant" refers to its appearance, not its cancerous nature <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:49:00">[07:49:00]</a>.

2.  **Pulmonary Anthrax**:
    *   Affects the lungs due to inhalation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:52:00">[04:52:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:23:00">[06:23:00]</a>.
    *   Common in wool sorters who inhale wool fibers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:33:00">[06:33:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:39:00">[06:39:00]</a>.
    *   Causes pneumonia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:49:00">[06:49:00]</a>.
    *   Can be an agent of bioterrorism due to its inhalation route <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:52:00">[06:52:00]</a>.

3.  **Intestinal Anthrax**:
    *   Affects the intestines, typically from consuming uncooked meat <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:56:00">[04:56:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:00:00">[07:00:00]</a>.

## [[Diagnostics in pathology and microbiology | Diagnosis]] of [[bacillus_anthracis | Bacillus anthracis]]
[[Diagnosis and identification of bacterial and fungal infections | Diagnosis]] involves several methods:

*   **Gram Staining**:
    *   Appears as long bacilli <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:22:00">[08:22:00]</a>.
    *   Shows a "boxcar" or "bamboo stick" appearance, with unstained spores in the center <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:47:00">[08:47:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:50:00">[08:50:00]</a>.

*   **Culture Media**:
    *   **Agar**: Forms colonies with a "Medusa head" appearance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:06:00">[09:06:00]</a>.
    *   **Gelatin**: Shows an "inverted fir tree" appearance due to maximum gelatin liquefaction at the oxygen-rich surface, decreasing towards the bottom <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:34:00">[09:34:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:57:00">[09:57:00]</a>.
    *   **Penicillin Agar**: Exhibits a "string of pearls" appearance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:50:00">[10:50:00]</a>.

*   **Selective Media**:
    *   PLET media (Polymyxin, Lysozyme, EDTA, Thallous Acetate) is a selective medium for [[bacillus_anthracis | Bacillus anthracis]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:05:00">[11:05:00]</a>.

*   **Antigen-Antibody Test**:
    *   Ascoli's Thermoprecipitant Test is an antigen-antibody reaction used for anthrax <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:15:00">[11:15:00]</a>.

### Key Characteristics Summary
*   **Motility**: [[bacillus_anthracis | Bacillus anthracis]] is a non-motile organism <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:35:00">[12:35:00]</a>.
*   **Spore Formation**: It is a spore-forming organism <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:39:00">[08:39:00]</a>.
*   **Aerobic**: Requires oxygen for growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:38:00">[01:38:00]</a>.

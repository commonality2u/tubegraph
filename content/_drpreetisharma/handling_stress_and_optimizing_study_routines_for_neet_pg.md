## General Approach & Mindset

Preparation for NEET PG requires a strategic and positive mindset. It's crucial to acknowledge that minor technical glitches or setbacks are normal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:12">[00:02:12]</a>. The session's advice is specifically tailored for NEET PG aspirants, not FMGE students, whose dedicated session was held separately <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:27">[00:02:27]</a>.

### Overcoming Challenges
*   **Acknowledge and Act:** Identify your study problems and proactively seek solutions. Don't just lament over difficulties; take action to address them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:01">[00:43:01]</a>.
*   **Combat Demotivation:** Utilize a "comfortable timetable" that accounts for potential ups and downs, rather than a "jam-packed" one, to avoid dips in motivation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:06">[00:50:06]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:04">[01:02:04]</a>.
*   **Avoid Self-Blame:** Do not dwell on past exam scores or grand test results. View past poor performances as "bad days" and focus on working positively towards future success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:06">[00:49:06]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:18">[00:49:18]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:46">[00:58:46]</a>.
*   **Be Practical:** Acknowledge that interruptions like illness, family emergencies, or moments of anxiety are normal during preparation. The timetable is designed to account for these eventualities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:10">[01:02:10]</a>.

### Question Practice & Grand Tests
*   **Daily Question Solving:** Aim to solve 100-150 questions per day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:24">[00:17:24]</a>, increasing gradually if you're starting with fewer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:39">[00:17:39]</a>. Solve questions related to the subject being studied that day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:00">[00:20:00]</a>.
*   **Previous Year Topics (PYTT):** Focus on PYTTs. They are readily available and important for NEET PG <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:59">[00:07:59]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:25">[00:08:25]</a>.
*   **QBank Usage:**
    *   For bulky subjects (Medicine, Surgery, OBGYN, PSM, Anatomy, Pharma), aim to complete the full QBank <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:36">[00:32:36]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:30">[00:56:30]</a>.
    *   For shorter subjects, the [[rapid_revision_vs_main_videos_for_neet_pg | Rapid Revision]] QBank is sufficient <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:54">[00:32:54]</a>.
    *   Integrate QBank practice with your daily study time; no separate days are allotted for QBank <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:50">[00:36:50]</a>.
*   **Grand Tests (GTs):**
    *   **Frequency:** Ideally, take a GT fortnightly (every two weeks) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:44">[00:10:44]</a>. If GT results demotivate you, reduce frequency to once every three weeks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:11">[00:11:11]</a>.
    *   **Time Allocation:** Dedicate half a day to a grand test <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:36">[00:08:36]</a>.
    *   **Analysis:** Spend half a day analyzing the GT <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:13">[00:11:13]</a>.
        *   Do not read explanations for correct answers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:21">[00:19:21]</a>.
        *   Focus on incorrect answers to understand *why* they were wrong (silly mistake, unknown topic, new concept, time management issue) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:31">[00:09:31]</a>.
        *   Prioritize qualitative analysis over quantitative statistics (e.g., number of correct answers) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:55">[00:09:55]</a>.
    *   **Purpose:** GTs are primarily for identifying mistakes and assessing time management, not for final rank prediction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:20">[00:11:20]</a>. They also help build confidence through consistent practice <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:36">[00:39:36]</a>.
*   **PYQs for NEET PG:** Aim to cover the last five years of NEET PG PYQs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:46">[00:34:46]</a>. For INI CET, focus on the last three years due to changes in exam patterns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:07">[00:35:07]</a>. PYQs alone are not sufficient for a good rank; they must be combined with [[rapid_revision_vs_main_videos_for_neet_pg | Rapid Revision]] and QBank practice <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:21">[00:36:21]</a>.

## Breaks & Internship Management

### Taking Breaks
*   **Pomodoro Technique:** Not recommended for this final phase of preparation (3 months out) as exams require sustained focus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:51">[00:51:51]</a>.
*   **Longer Stretches:** Train your mind to focus for 2.5-3 hours continuously, similar to exam conditions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:17">[00:52:17]</a>.
*   **Break Duration:** After a long study stretch, a 15-minute break is recommended, though it may extend to 20-25 minutes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:43">[00:52:43]</a>. What you do during the break is personal preference (e.g., avoid social media if it distracts) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:54">[00:52:54]</a>.

### Interns' Schedule
*   **Follow Same Sequence:** Interns should follow the same subject sequence as the general timetable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:11">[00:28:11]</a>.
*   **Add Days:** Add one extra day for each subject given the limited study time due to internship duties (e.g., 4 days become 5-5.5 days) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:20">[00:28:20]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:54">[00:28:54]</a>.
*   **Focus on RR + QBank:** For interns, it is highly recommended to stick to [[rapid_revision_vs_main_videos_for_neet_pg | Rapid Revision]] videos and QBank, avoiding main videos <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:26">[00:28:26]</a>.
*   **Prioritize Short Subjects:** Finish short subjects like Psychiatry, Radiology, Orthopedics, Anesthesia, Forensic Medicine, and Dermatology quickly (e.g., within 7-8 days during a light posting) to build confidence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:03">[00:29:03]</a>.

It covers topics ranging from cellular injury and pigments to specific organ pathologies and hematological findings.

## General Concepts & Cellular Changes

### Gel Electrophoresis
Gel electrophoresis shows different patterns:
*   **Normal:** Patient A exhibits a typical pattern <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:41">[00:01:41]</a>.
*   **Step Ladder Pattern:** Patient B shows a step ladder pattern <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:25">[00:02:25]</a>.
    *   This pattern is characteristic of **apoptosis** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:29">[00:02:29]</a>.
    *   It can also be seen in necrolysis, but is more common in apoptosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:36">[00:04:36]</a>.

### Types of Necrosis
Different organs exhibit distinct types of necrosis:
*   **Coagulative Necrosis:**
    *   Commonly seen in the heart, as in myocardial infarction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:10">[00:05:10]</a>.
*   **Liquefactive Necrosis:**
    *   Typically observed in the brain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:31">[00:05:31]</a>.
    *   Also seen in the pancreas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:39">[00:05:39]</a>.
*   **Caseous Necrosis:**
    *   Characteristic of Tuberculosis (TB) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:52">[00:05:52]</a>.
    *   Also found in fungal infections like Histoplasma <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:56">[00:05:56]</a>.
*   **Fat Necrosis:**
    *   Appears yellow <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:01">[00:06:01]</a>.
    *   Commonly seen in fatty organs such as the omentum or breast <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:09">[00:06:09]</a>.
*   **Fibrinoid Necrosis:**
    *   Associated with conditions like Polyarteritis Nodosa (PAN) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:27">[00:06:27]</a>.
    *   Also seen in Rheumatic Heart Disease (RHD) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:34">[00:06:34]</a> and malignant hypertension <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:37">[00:06:37]</a>.

### Pigments
*   **Lipofuscin Pigment:**
    *   Appears brown and is located perinuclear <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:57">[00:06:57]</a>.
    *   Known as the "wear and tear" pigment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:05">[00:07:05]</a>.
    *   Typically seen in old age (senile changes) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:08">[00:07:08]</a>, often in questions describing an "83-year-old man" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:11">[00:07:11]</a>.

### Calcification
*   **Psammoma Bodies:**
    *   Characterized by round, concentrated calcium deposits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:42">[00:07:42]</a>.
    *   These are a type of **dystrophic calcification** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:35">[00:08:35]</a>.
    *   **Mnemonic (PSM Sq.):**
        *   **P**apillary carcinoma (any papillary carcinoma, not just [[thyroid_pathology | thyroid]]) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:01">[00:08:01]</a>
        *   **S**omatostatinoma <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:09">[00:08:09]</a>
        *   **S**erous ovarian tumor (tumor marker CA-125 elevated) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:15">[00:08:15]</a>
        *   **M**eningioma <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:26">[00:08:26]</a>
        *   **M**esothelioma <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:26">[00:08:26]</a>

## Cytogenetics & Molecular Techniques

### Karyotyping
*   **Procedure:** Chromosomes are arrested in metaphase <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:33">[00:13:33]</a>.
*   **Shortest Chromosome:** Chromosome 21 is the shortest <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:40">[00:13:40]</a>.
*   **Banding Techniques:**
    *   **G-banding (Giemsa banding):** Uses light microscopy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:17">[00:14:17]</a>.
    *   **Q-banding (Quinacrine banding):** Uses fluorescent microscopy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:01">[00:14:01]</a>.

### FISH (Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization)
*   A technique used for detecting chromosomal abnormalities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:30">[00:14:30]</a>.
*   **Example:** If green probe targets chromosome 21 and red targets chromosome 13, presence of three red signals indicates Trisomy 13 (Patau Syndrome) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:49">[00:14:49]</a>. The examiner will provide the color coding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:03">[00:15:03]</a>.

## Organ-Specific Pathology

### Kidney Pathology

#### Gross Findings
*   **Waxy Kidney:**
    *   Appears like "candle wax" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:19">[00:15:19]</a>.
    *   Indicates **Amyloidosis** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:25">[00:15:25]</a>.
    *   Confirmed by Congo Red stain (shows apple-green birefringence under polarizing microscope) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:45">[00:15:45]</a>.
    *   Amyloid in Alzheimer's disease is A-beta; in hemodialysis it is A-beta-2 microglobulin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:06">[00:16:06]</a>.
*   **Putty Kidney:**
    *   A kidney with caseous necrosis and calcification due to TB <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:14">[00:28:14]</a>.
*   **Leather Grain Kidney:**
    *   Appears granular, with dot-like structures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:49">[00:28:49]</a>.
    *   Seen in **benign hypertension** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:55">[00:28:55]</a>.
*   **Flea-Bitten Kidney:**
    *   Characterized by red-colored hemorrhages resembling flea bites <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:04">[00:29:04]</a>.
    *   Seen in **malignant hypertension** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:09">[00:29:09]</a>.

    *   Green color shining more at some places like stars <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:28">[00:30:28]</a>.

### Cardiac Pathology
*   **Triphenyl Tetrazolium Chloride (TTC) Stain:**
    *   Used to identify myocardial infarction (MI) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:40">[00:16:40]</a>.
    *   Useful within four hours of chest pain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:54">[00:16:54]</a>.
    *   Normal areas stain brick red <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:01">[00:17:01]</a>.
    *   Infarcted (dead) areas appear pale <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:04">[00:17:04]</a>.
    *   Principle: TTC stains lactate dehydrogenase (LDH), specifically LDH1, which is common in the heart <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:12">[00:17:12]</a>.
*   **Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy (HCM):**
    *   Grossly, the heart appears "banana-shaped" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:34">[00:17:34]</a>.
    *   Classical presentation: sudden death in a young athlete (e.g., 19-year-old football/basketball player, marathon runner) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:48">[00:17:48]</a>.
    *   Mutation: Myosin Heavy Chain (MHC) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:11">[00:18:11]</a>.
    *   Inheritance: Autosomal dominant disorder <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:14">[00:18:14]</a>.

## General Concepts & Cellular Changes

### Gel Electrophoresis
Gel electrophoresis patterns are crucial for identifying specific cellular processes:
*   **Normal Pattern:** Patient A displays a normal pattern with no significant anomalies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:41">[00:01:41]</a>.
*   **Step Ladder Pattern:** Patient B shows a distinct step ladder pattern <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:25">[00:02:25]</a>.
    *   This pattern is characteristic of **apoptosis** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:29">[00:02:29]</a>.
    *   It can also be seen in necrolysis but is more commonly associated with apoptosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:36">[00:04:36]</a>.

### Types of Necrosis
Different forms of cell death exhibit distinct appearances and are associated with specific organs or conditions:
*   **Coagulative Necrosis:**
    *   Commonly observed in the heart, for instance, in myocardial infarction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:10">[00:05:10]</a>.
*   **Liquefactive Necrosis:**
    *   Typically seen in the brain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:31">[00:05:31]</a>.
    *   It is also found in the pancreas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:39">[00:05:39]</a>.
*   **Caseous Necrosis:**
    *   A hallmark of Tuberculosis (TB) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:52">[00:05:52]</a>.
    *   Also present in fungal infections like Histoplasma <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:56">[00:05:56]</a>.
*   **Fat Necrosis:**
    *   Presents with a characteristic yellow appearance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:01">[00:06:01]</a>.
    *   Often seen in fatty organs such as the omentum or breast <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:09">[00:06:09]</a>.
*   **Fibrinoid Necrosis:**
    *   Associated with conditions like Polyarteritis Nodosa (PAN) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:27">[00:06:27]</a>.
    *   Also observed in Rheumatic Heart Disease (RHD) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:34">[00:06:34]</a> and malignant hypertension <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:37">[00:06:37]</a>.

## Cytogenetics & Molecular Techniques

### Karyotyping
*   **Procedure:** Involves arresting chromosomes in the metaphase stage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:33">[00:13:33]</a>.
*   **Shortest Chromosome:** Chromosome 21 is recognized as the shortest chromosome <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:40">[00:13:40]</a>.
*   **Banding Techniques:**
    *   **G-banding (Giemsa banding):** Utilizes a light microscope for visualization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:17">[00:14:17]</a>.
    *   **Q-banding (Quinacrine banding):** Requires a fluorescent microscope for analysis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:01">[00:14:01]</a>.

### FISH (Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization)
*   A technique used for detecting chromosomal abnormalities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:30">[00:14:30]</a>.
*   **Example:** If a green probe targets chromosome 21 and a red probe targets chromosome 13, the presence of three red signals would indicate Trisomy 13 (Patau Syndrome) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:49">[00:14:49]</a>. The specific color coding used is typically provided by the examiner <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:03">[00:15:03]</a>.

#### White Blood Cells
Identifying different white blood cell types is fundamental:
*   **Neutrophil:** Has 3-5 lobes in its nucleus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:51">[00:48:51]</a>.
*   **Lymphocyte:** A simple, round cell <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:53">[00:48:53]</a>.
*   **Monocyte:** Characterized by a kidney-shaped nucleus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:57">[00:48:57]</a>. Elevated in malaria <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:25">[00:49:25]</a>.
*   **Eosinophil:** Orange-looking cell with granules <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:00">[00:49:00]</a>. Elevated in parasitic infections (e.g., hookworm, Giardia) and allergies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:33">[00:49:33]</a>.
*   **Basophil:** Contains blue-black granules <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:08">[00:49:08]</a>. Elevated in Chronic Myeloid Leukemia (CML) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:53">[00:49:53]</a>.
*   **Mnemonic (NLMEB):** Never Let Monkeys Eat Bananas (Neutrophils, Lymphocytes, Monocytes, Eosinophils, Basophils - from most to least common) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:15">[00:48:15]</a>.

#### Leukemias
*   **Acute Myeloid Leukemia (AML) M3:**
    *   Features Faggot cells (cells with abundant Auer rods) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:25">[00:50:25]</a>.
    *   It is the only leukemia that can cause Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation (DIC) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:38">[00:50:38]</a>.
    *   Treated with Vitamin A (all-trans retinoic acid) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:41">[00:50:41]</a>.
    *   Associated with translocation 15;17 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:48">[00:50:48]</a>.
*   **Chronic Lymphocytic Leukemia (CLL):**
    *   Characterized by **smudge cells** (fragile lymphocytes that rupture during smear preparation) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:12">[00:51:12]</a>.
    *   Often described as having a "convent school girl" appearance of lymphocytes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:25">[00:51:25]</a>.
*   **Chronic Myeloid Leukemia (CML):**
    *   Features a "college girl" or "garden party" appearance, where all stages of myeloid cells (neutrophils, myelocytes, blasts) are visible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:32">[00:51:32]</a>.

#### Lymphomas
*   **Burkitt Lymphoma:**
    *   Clinical history often includes an African child with jaw swelling <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:49">[00:51:49]</a>, due to endemic Epstein-Barr Virus (EBV) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:54">[00:51:54]</a>.
    *   Microscopically, it shows a **starry sky appearance** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:06">[00:52:06]</a>.
    *   Associated with translocation of chromosome 8 with an even number (e.g., t(8;14), t(8;22)) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:14">[00:52:14]</a>.

#### Multiple Myeloma
*   **Clinical Presentation:**
    *   Lytic bony lesions are seen, but it's crucial to consider the patient's age; typically affects elderly individuals (e.g., 60-75 years old) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:07">[00:53:07]</a>. In a young child (e.g., 5-year-old), lytic lesions might suggest Langerhans Cell Histiocytosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:18">[00:53:18]</a>.
*   **Peripheral Smear:**
    *   Shows **rouleaux formation**, where red blood cells stack like coins <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:47">[00:53:47]</a>.
*   **Plasma Cell Morphology (contains different bodies):**
    *   **Russell Bodies:** Intracytoplasmic inclusions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:20">[00:54:20]</a>. Most common antibody is IgG <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:40">[00:54:40]</a>.
    *   **Dutcher Bodies:** Intranuclear inclusions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:26">[00:54:26]</a>. Most common antibody is IgG <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:47">[00:54:47]</a>.
    *   **Mott Cells (Mulberry Cells):** Plasma cells filled with multiple Russell bodies, giving a mulberry-like appearance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:33">[00:54:33]</a>. Most common antibody is IgG <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:50">[00:54:50]</a>.
    *   **Flame Cells:** Plasma cells with a red or "flame" appearance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:37">[00:54:37]</a>. Most common antibody is IgA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:01">[00:55:01]</a>.
*   **Diagnosis Confirmation:**
    *   **M Spike:** Presence of a monoclonal spike (M spike) in the gamma region on serum protein electrophoresis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:26">[00:55:26]</a>. This usually indicates an increase in IgG, not IgM <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:36">[00:55:36]</a>.
*   **Genetics:**
    *   Associated with deletion 13q and translocation 11;14 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:03">[00:57:03]</a>.

## Pathology Cases and Findings

### Necrosis Types
*   **Coagulative Necrosis** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:04">[00:02:04]</a>
    *   **Appearance**: Pinkish cells, often referred to as "ghost cells" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:15">[00:02:15]</a>.
    *   **Characteristic Pattern**: "Tom stone appearance" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:22">[00:02:22]</a>.
    *   **Clinical Cases**: Commonly seen in myocardial infarction (heart attack), kidney, and liver pathologies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:10">[00:02:10]</a>. The tom stone appearance is also observed in pemphigus vulgaris <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:28">[00:02:28]</a>.
*   **Liquefactive Necrosis** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:37">[00:02:37]</a>
    *   **Appearance**: Liquidified tissue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:37">[00:02:37]</a>.
    *   **Clinical Cases**: Brain infarcts, often associated with a history of stroke <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:40">[00:02:40]</a>.
*   **Caseous Necrosis** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:02">[00:03:02]</a>
    *   **Appearance**: "Cheese appearance" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:02">[00:03:02]</a>.
    *   **Clinical Cases**: Tuberculosis (TB) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:05">[00:03:05]</a>, and fungal infections, particularly Histoplasma <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:10">[00:03:10]</a>.
*   **Fat Necrosis** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:23">[00:03:23]</a>
    *   **Appearance**: Fatty, yellowish areas that can appear chalky white due to calcification <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:23">[00:03:23]</a>.
    *   **Clinical Cases**: Associated with organs rich in fat, such as the breast or omentum <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:34">[00:03:34]</a>.
*   **Fibrinoid Necrosis** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:41">[00:03:41]</a>
    *   **Appearance**: Pinkish, often involving blood vessels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:44">[00:03:44]</a>.
    *   **Clinical Cases**: Seen in conditions like Polyarteritis Nodosa, Rheumatic Heart Disease, SLE, and hypertension <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:49">[00:03:49]</a>.

### Diagnostic Patterns and Stains
*   **Gel Electrophoresis** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:01">[00:04:01]</a>
    *   **Step-ladder pattern**: More commonly seen in apoptosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:30">[00:04:30]</a>.
    *   **Smearing pattern**: More commonly seen in necrosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:36">[00:04:36]</a>.
*   **Lipofuscin Pigment** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:00">[00:05:00]</a>
    *   **Appearance**: Brown perinuclear pigment around a nucleus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:03">[00:05:03]</a>.
    *   **Association**: "Old age pigment," linked to free radicals and wear and tear <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:14">[00:05:14]</a>.
*   **Hemosiderin Pigment** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:28">[00:05:28]</a>
    *   **Association**: Indicates bleeding, related to iron <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:28">[00:05:28]</a>.
    *   **Stain**: Pearls of Prussian blue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:38">[00:05:38]</a>.
    *   **Clinical Condition**: Iron overload is known as hemochromatosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:48">[00:05:48]</a>.
*   **Lipids** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:04">[00:06:04]</a>
    *   **Stains**: Oil red O (red) and Sudan Black B (black) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:06">[00:06:06]</a>.
*   **Calcium** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:15">[00:06:15]</a>
    *   **Stains**: Von Kossa (black) and Alizarin Red S (red) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:20">[00:06:20]</a>. Alizarin Red S is used for minute amounts of calcium <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:31">[00:06:31]</a>.

### Giant Cells and Granulomas
*   **Langhans Giant Cell** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:59">[00:06:59]</a>
    *   **Appearance**: Nuclei arranged in a horseshoe or peripheral pattern <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:59">[00:06:59]</a>.
    *   **Association**: Tuberculosis (TB) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:59">[00:06:59]</a>.
*   **Touton Giant Cell** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:03">[00:07:03]</a>
    *   **Appearance**: Round nuclei <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:03">[00:07:03]</a>.
    *   **Association**: Xanthoma, related to lipid problems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:09">[00:07:09]</a>.
*   **Foreign Body Giant Cell** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:30">[00:07:30]</a>
    *   **Appearance**: Nuclei are scattered haphazardly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:30">[00:07:30]</a>.
    *   **Association**: Presence of foreign material like talc powder or sutures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:32">[00:07:32]</a>.
*   **Warthin-Finkeldey Giant Cell** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:46">[00:07:46]</a>
    *   **Appearance**: Intracytoplasmic and intranuclear inclusions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:42">[00:07:42]</a>.
    *   **Association**: Measles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:50">[00:07:50]</a>. Measles is associated with the Edmonston-Zagreb strain (for vaccine) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:57">[00:07:57]</a>, Koplik spots <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:02">[00:08:02]</a>, and the "3 Cs" (cough, coryza, conjunctivitis) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:11">[00:08:11]</a>.
*   **Owl's Eye Appearance** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:21">[00:08:21]</a>
    *   **Red/Pink "Owl's Eyes"**: Reed-Sternberg cells in Hodgkin's lymphoma <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:40">[00:08:40]</a>.
    *   **Blue "Owl's Eyes"**: Cytomegalovirus (CMV) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:35">[00:08:35]</a>.
*   **Stellate Granuloma** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:51">[00:08:51]</a>
    *   **Appearance**: Star-like shape <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:53">[00:08:53]</a>.
    *   **Association**: Cat-scratch disease and Lymphogranuloma Venereum (LGV) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:57">[00:08:57]</a>. LGV is associated with painful buboes and the "groove sign" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:12">[00:09:12]</a>.
*   **Durck Granuloma** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:31">[00:09:31]</a>
    *   **Appearance**: Two parallel lines <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:31">[00:09:31]</a>.
    *   **Association**: Cerebral malaria, caused by Plasmodium falciparum <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:39">[00:09:39]</a>.

### Lysosomal Storage Disorders
*   **Gaucher Disease** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:27">[00:10:27]</a>
    *   **Appearance**: "Crumpled tissue paper appearance" of cells <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:23">[00:10:23]</a>.
    *   **Clinical**: Often associated with bone pain in children <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:17">[00:10:17]</a>.
    *   **Deficiency**: Beta-glucocerebrosidase deficiency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:38">[00:10:38]</a>.
*   **Tay-Sachs Disease** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:03">[00:11:03]</a>
    *   **Electron Microscopy**: "Onion skin pattern" around a round body <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:50">[00:10:50]</a>.
    *   **Deficiency**: Alpha-hexosaminidase A deficiency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:10">[00:11:10]</a>.
    *   **Clinical**: Cherry-red spot in the macula of the eye <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:19">[00:11:19]</a>.
*   **Niemann-Pick Disease** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:30">[00:11:30]</a>
    *   **Electron Microscopy**: "Zebra bodies" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:28">[00:11:28]</a>.
    *   **Deficiency**: Sphingomyelinase enzyme deficiency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:34">[00:11:34]</a>.
    *   **Clinical**: Splenomegaly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:51">[00:11:51]</a>.

### Genetics and Cytogenetics
*   **Karyotyping** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:07">[00:12:07]</a>
    *   **Normal Karyotype** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:24">[00:12:24]</a>.
    *   **Trisomy 21 (Down Syndrome)**: Extra chromosome 21 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:07">[00:12:07]</a>.
    *   **Trisomy 18 (Edwards Syndrome)**: Extra chromosome 18 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:14">[00:12:14]</a>.
    *   **Trisomy 13 (Patau Syndrome)**: Extra chromosome 13 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:18">[00:12:18]</a>.
    *   **Stage for Analysis**: Metaphase <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:28">[00:12:28]</a>.
    *   **Stains**:
        *   G-banding (Giemsa) for light microscopy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:35">[00:12:35]</a>.
        *   Q-banding (Quinacrine) for fluorescent microscopy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:43">[00:12:43]</a>.
*   **FISH (Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization)** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:36">[00:13:36]</a>
    *   **Appearance**: Fused red and green signals indicating a translocation (e.g., BCR-ABL fusion, Philadelphia chromosome) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:21">[00:13:21]</a>.
    *   **Use**: Detects chromosomal disorders, translocations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:40">[00:13:40]</a>.
    *   **Limitation**: Cannot detect point mutations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:54">[0:13:54]</a>.
*   **CGH (Comparative Genomic Hybridization)** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:05">[00:14:05]</a>
    *   **Interpretation**:
        *   Yellow color indicates normal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:17">[00:14:17]</a>.
        *   Red color indicates gain (gain of function mutation) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:20">[00:14:20]</a>.
        *   Green color indicates loss (loss of function mutation) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:22">[00:14:22]</a>.

### Immunofluorescence Patterns
*   **Antinuclear Antibody (ANA) Patterns** (detected via immunofluorescence) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:46">[00:14:46]</a>
    *   **Homogenous Pattern**: Antibodies against dsDNA, histone, or other nuclear components <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:04">[00:15:04]</a>.
    *   **Peripheral Pattern**: Antibodies against dsDNA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:25">[00:15:25]</a>.
    *   **Nucleolar Pattern**: Antibodies against RNA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:44">[00:15:44]</a>.
    *   **Speckled Pattern**: Most common and non-specific; seen in SLE, Sjogren's syndrome, and other autoimmune diseases <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:57">[00:15:57]</a>.
    *   **Centromeric Pattern**: Antibodies against centromeres, seen in CREST syndrome <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:19">[00:16:19]</a>.

### Vasculitides
*   **Large Vessel Vasculitis** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:18">[00:18:18]</a>
    *   **Temporal (Giant Cell) Arteritis** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:22">[00:18:22]</a>:

    *   **Takayasu Arteritis** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:57">[00:18:57]</a>:

*   **Medium Vessel Vasculitis** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:17">[00:19:17]</a>
    *   **Polyarteritis Nodosa (PAN)** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:22">[00:19:22]</a>:

        *   Association: Hepatitis B virus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:31">[00:19:31]</a>.

    *   **Kawasaki Disease** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:42">[00:19:42]</a>:
        *   Clinical: "Strawberry tongue" (also seen in Scarlet Fever) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:44">[00:19:44]</a>.
    *   **Buerger's Disease (Thromboangiitis Obliterans)** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:53">[00:19:53]</a>:
        *   Clinical: History of smoking (bidi, cigarettes) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:55">[00:19:55]</a>.
        *   Pathology: Blood vessel blocked by thrombus and inflammation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:09">[00:20:09]</a>.
        *   Clinical: Intermittent claudication <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:25">[00:20:25]</a>.
*   **Small Vessel Vasculitis (ANCA-associated)** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:35">[00:20:35]</a>
    *   **C-ANCA (Cytoplasmic ANCA) / PR3-ANCA** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:11">[00:21:11]</a>:
        *   Immunofluorescence: Diffuse cytoplasmic staining <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:49">[00:20:49]</a>.
        *   Association: Primarily Wegener's Granulomatosis (now Granulomatosis with Polyangiitis - GPA) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:22">[00:21:22]</a>.
    *   **P-ANCA (Perinuclear ANCA) / MPO-ANCA** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:33">[00:21:33]</a>:
        *   Immunofluorescence: Perinuclear staining around the nucleus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:31">[00:21:31]</a>.
        *   Association: Microscopic Polyangiitis, Churg-Strauss Syndrome (now Eosinophilic Granulomatosis with Polyangiitis - EGPA) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:45">[00:21:45]</a>, Ulcerative Colitis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:07">[00:22:07]</a>, Primary Sclerosing Cholangitis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:11">[00:22:11]</a>. GPA can show both C-ANCA and P-ANCA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:55">[00:21:55]</a>.

    *   **Restrictive Cardiomyopathy (RCM)** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:04">[00:26:04]</a>:

### Endocarditis

    *   Appearance: Small, warty vegetations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:36">[00:26:36]</a>.

        *   *Differential*: Fish mouth vertebrae (Osteoporosis, Sickle Cell Anemia) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:30">[00:27:30]</a>.

*   **Nonbacterial Thrombotic Endocarditis (NBTE) / Marantic Endocarditis** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:17">[00:28:17]</a>:

*   **Libman-Sacks Endocarditis** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:34">[00:28:34]</a>:
    *   Association: Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:39">[00:28:39]</a>.

### Gastrointestinal Pathologies

*   **Ménétrier's Disease** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:36">[00:31:36]</a>:
    *   Gross: Stomach appears like "brain gyri" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:31">[00:31:31]</a>.

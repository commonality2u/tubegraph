## Achalasia Cardia
[[Visual and histological pathology clues for diagnosis | Achalasia cardia]] is a gastrointestinal motility disorder characterized by difficulty swallowing and a tendency to regurgitate food <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:17">[00:02:17]</a>. Endoscopy typically reveals no gastric or esophageal abnormalities, meaning no cancer, growth, or ulcers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:22">[00:02:22]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:44">[00:02:44]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:56">[00:02:56]</a>.

Manometric studies show a complete absence of peristalsis and a failure of the lower esophageal sphincter (LES) to relax upon swallowing, along with increased LES pressure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:25">[00:02:25]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:01">[00:03:01]</a>. A barium study often reveals a classic "Bird's Beak Appearance" due to the non-relaxing LES <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:09">[00:03:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:16">[00:03:16]</a>.

Most cases of achalasia cardia are idiopathic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:24">[00:03:24]</a>. However, it can be associated with *Trypanosoma cruzi* infection, leading to Chagas disease <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:30">[00:03:30]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:41">[00:03:41]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:46">[00:03:46]</a>. Herpes infection can also cause achalasia cardia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:33">[00:03:33]</a>. In Chagas disease, achalasia can manifest as "megaesophagus" and "megacolon," where the esophagus becomes very dilated due to obstruction at the lower end <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:00">[00:04:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:06">[00:04:06]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:16">[00:04:16]</a>.

## Other Esophageal and Gastric Disorders

### [[Plummer Vinson syndrome and its implications | Plummer-Vinson Syndrome]]
Also known as Paterson-Brown-Kelly Syndrome, [[Plummer Vinson syndrome and its implications | Plummer-Vinson syndrome]] is clinically termed Sideropenic Dysphagia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:12">[00:05:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:23">[00:05:23]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:37">[00:05:37]</a>. It is characterized by iron deficiency anemia, dysphagia (difficulty swallowing), and esophageal webs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:39">[00:05:39]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:50">[00:05:50]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:53">[00:05:53]</a>.

The esophageal webs are constrictions without inflammation or neoplasia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:59">[00:05:59]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:08">[00:06:08]</a>. Patients with [[Plummer Vinson syndrome and its implications | Plummer-Vinson syndrome]] are at an increased risk of developing squamous cell carcinoma of the esophagus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:41">[00:06:41]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:47">[00:06:47]</a>.

### Pernicious Anemia
Pernicious anemia is a megaloblastic anemia caused by a deficiency of intrinsic factor, which is essential for vitamin B12 absorption <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:19">[00:11:19]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:24">[00:11:24]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:27">[00:11:27]</a>. Intrinsic factor is released from parietal cells in the stomach <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:01">[00:11:01]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:46">[00:11:46]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:51">[00:11:51]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:00">[00:12:00]</a>.
A patient presenting with fatigue, neck swelling (goiter), megaloblastic anemia, and elevated TSH with anti-thyroid antibodies may have autoimmune thyroiditis (Hashimoto's thyroiditis) co-occurring with pernicious anemia due to autoimmune destruction of parietal cells <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:29">[00:07:29]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:10">[00:08:10]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:34">[00:08:34]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:40">[00:09:40]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:47">[00:09:47]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:12">[00:11:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:15">[00:11:15]</a>.

**Histological Identification of Stomach Cells:**
*   Foveolar cells: Appear empty/white due to mucin content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:34">[00:12:34]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:52">[00:12:52]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:54">[00:12:54]</a>.

Paneth cells are found more in the intestines <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:57">[00:13:57]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:00">[00:14:00]</a>. A special stain for Paneth cells is Phloxine Tartrazine Stain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:05">[00:14:05]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:10">[00:14:10]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:24">[00:14:24]</a>. This stain is also used for intracytoplasmic inclusions like [[Clostridium infections like tetanus botulism and pseudomembranous colitis | molluscum contagiosum bodies]] or Patterson bodies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:13">[00:14:13]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:16">[00:14:16]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:21">[00:14:21]</a>.

### [[Understanding signet ring cells in gastric cancer | Diffuse Gastric Cancer (Linitis Plastica)]]
Diffuse gastric cancer, also known as [[Understanding signet ring cells in gastric cancer | linitis plastica]], is characterized by a "leather bottle stomach" appearance, where the stomach wall becomes very hard <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:47">[00:15:47]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:51">[00:15:51]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:14">[00:16:14]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:21">[00:16:21]</a>. Unlike typical fungating cancers, the malignancy spreads diffusely within the stomach wall <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:01">[00:16:01]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:08">[00:16:08]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:35">[00:16:35]</a>.
Histologically, it is characterized by [[Understanding signet ring cells in gastric cancer | signet ring cells]], where the nucleus is pushed to the side by abundant intracellular mucin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:50">[00:16:50]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:54">[00:16:54]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:02">[00:17:02]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:10">[00:17:10]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:20">[00:17:20]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:25">[00:17:25]</a>.
The most common genetic alteration associated with diffuse gastric cancer is a mutation in the *CDH1* gene, which encodes E-cadherin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:31">[00:18:31]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:34">[00:18:34]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:43">[00:18:43]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:50">[00:18:50]</a>. E-cadherin acts as a cell adhesion molecule (a "glue" holding cells together) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:56">[00:18:56]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:00">[00:19:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:08">[00:19:08]</a>. Loss of E-cadherin leads to diffuse spreading of cancer cells.
This "golu tumor" mnemonic (where "glue is gone") also applies to invasive lobular carcinoma of the breast, which similarly shows a loss of E-cadherin and presents with an "Indian file" pattern <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:20">[00:19:20]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:27">[00:19:27]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:30">[00:19:30]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:38">[00:19:38]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:01">[00:20:01]</a>.

### [[Gastrointestinal Stromal Tumors and Their Mutations | Gastrointestinal Stromal Tumors (GIST)]]
[[Gastrointestinal Stromal Tumors and Their Mutations | GIST]] is the most common mesenchymal tumor of the stomach <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:21">[00:21:21]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:24">[00:21:24]</a>. It is characterized by spindle cells on biopsy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:38">[00:20:38]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:51">[00:20:51]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:17">[00:21:17]</a>.

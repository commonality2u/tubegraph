## [[virulence_factors_of_bacillus_anthracis | Virulence Factors]]

The [[virulence_factors_of_bacillus_anthracis | virulence factors]] of [[anthrax_and_bacillus_anthracis | Bacillus anthracis]] include its capsule and a specific toxin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:36:00">[02:36:00]</a>.

*   **Capsule**: Unlike most bacterial capsules which are made of polysaccharides, the capsule of [[anthrax_and_bacillus_anthracis | Bacillus anthracis]] is uniquely composed of polypeptide, specifically poly-D-glutamic acid <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:47:00">[02:47:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:56:00">[02:56:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:18:00">[12:18:00]</a>. This capsule is a key [[virulence_factors_of_bacillus_anthracis | virulence factor]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:08:00">[04:08:00]</a>.
*   **Toxin**: The [[anthrax_and_bacillus_anthracis | Anthrax]] toxin consists of three parts, often remembered by the mnemonic "EPL" (English Premier League) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:15:00">[03:15:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:21:00">[03:21:00]</a>:
    *   **Edema Factor (E)**: Contributes to edema by increasing cyclic AMP <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:41:00">[03:41:00]</a>.
    *   **Protective Factor (P)**: Helps protect the bacteria <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:50:00">[03:50:00]</a>.
    *   **Lethal Factor (L)**: Responsible for causing destruction and killing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:53:00">[03:53:00]</a>.
    *   A "labile toxin" is *not* a [[virulence_factors_of_bacillus_anthracis | virulence factor]] of [[anthrax_and_bacillus_anthracis | Bacillus anthracis]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:20:00">[02:20:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:27:00">[04:27:00]</a>.

## Types of [[anthrax_and_bacillus_anthracis | Anthrax]]

There are three main types of [[anthrax_and_bacillus_anthracis | anthrax]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:44:00">[04:44:00]</a>:

1.  **Cutaneous [[anthrax_and_bacillus_anthracis | Anthrax]]**:
    *   Affects the skin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:49:00">[04:49:00]</a>.
    *   Known as "hide potter's disease" because it affects workers who carry animal hides/skins on their neck, shoulders, or upper back <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:11:00">[05:11:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:50:00">[05:50:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:00:00">[06:00:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:09:00">[06:09:00]</a>.
    *   Characterized by a blackish lesion on the skin, known as a malignant pustule or eschar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:03:00">[06:03:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:27:00">[07:27:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:42:00">[07:42:00]</a>. It's called "malignant" due to its appearance, not because it's cancerous <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:51:00">[07:51:00]</a>.

2.  **Pulmonary [[anthrax_and_bacillus_anthracis | Anthrax]]**:
    *   Affects the lungs, often through inhalation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:52:00">[04:52:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:23:00">[06:23:00]</a>.
    *   Commonly seen in "wool sorters," workers in the wool industry who inhale thin wool fibers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:33:00">[06:33:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:37:00">[06:37:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:40:00">[06:40:00]</a>.
    *   Causes pneumonia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:47:00">[06:47:00]</a>.
    *   Considered an agent of bioterrorism due to its inhalation route <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:52:00">[06:52:00]</a>.

3.  **Intestinal [[anthrax_and_bacillus_anthracis | Anthrax]]**:
    *   Affects the intestines <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:56:00">[04:56:00]</a>.
    *   Occurs from the consumption of uncooked meat or animal material <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:00:00">[07:00:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:06:00">[07:06:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:00:00">[13:00:00]</a>.
    *   Leads to intestinal inflammation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:11:00">[07:11:00]</a>.

## [[bacterial_and_fungal_diagnostics | Laboratory Diagnosis]]

The [[bacterial_and_fungal_diagnostics | diagnosis]] of [[anthrax_and_bacillus_anthracis | Bacillus anthracis]] involves various [[bacterial_and_fungal_diagnostics | laboratory techniques for identifying bacterial pathogens]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:00">[01:17:00]</a>:

*   **Gram Staining**:
    *   Appears as long bacilli <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:20:00">[08:20:00]</a>.
    *   Shows a "boxcar appearance" or "bamboo stick appearance," where whitish areas indicate spores within the organism <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:47:00">[08:47:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:50:00">[08:50:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:53:00">[08:53:00]</a>. This is a characteristic feature seen on gram stain, not culture <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:25:00">[13:25:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:36:00">[13:36:00]</a>.
    *   [[anthrax_and_bacillus_anthracis | Bacillus anthracis]] is a non-motile organism <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:35:00">[12:35:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:42:00">[12:42:00]</a>.

*   **Culture Media**:
    *   **Agar**: On a regular agar medium, it produces "Medusa head colonies" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:06:00">[09:06:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:15:00">[09:15:00]</a>.
    *   **Gelatin**: When grown in gelatin, it shows an "inverted fir tree appearance" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:19:00">[09:19:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:34:00">[10:34:00]</a>. This is due to maximum gelatin liquefaction at the surface where oxygen is present, and less liquefaction at the bottom due to lack of oxygen, consistent with its aerobic nature <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:47:00">[09:47:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:00:00">[10:10:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:19:00">[10:19:00]</a>.
    *   **Penicillin Agar**: On penicillin agar, it exhibits a "string of pearls appearance" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:44:00">[10:44:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:00:00">[11:00:00]</a>.
    *   **Selective Media**: PLET media is used as a selective medium for [[anthrax_and_bacillus_anthracis | Bacillus anthracis]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:03:00">[11:03:00]</a>.

*   **Antigen-Antibody Test**:
    *   Ascoli's thermoprecipitant test is an antigen-antibody reaction that can be used for [[anthrax_and_bacillus_anthracis | anthrax]] detection, though it's less common nowadays <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:09:00">[11:09:00]</a>.

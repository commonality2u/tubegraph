With exams approaching on January 20th, it is crucial to establish and adhere to a structured study [[creating_and_customizing_a_study_timetable | timetable]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:24">[00:00:24]</a>. This guide outlines an [[creating_and_customizing_a_study_timetable | effective study timetable]] and strategies tailored for FMG students, with approximately two months remaining until the exam <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:26">[00:04:26]</a>. (Note: [[time_table_for_neet_pg_exam_preparation | NEET PG students]] have a separate, more extensive timetable session at 2 PM due to a longer preparation period) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:51">[00:00:51]</a>.

## Core Study Strategies

*   **MCQs Alongside Theory**
    *   Given the January 20th exam date, it's essential to practice Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) concurrently with theory <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:02">[00:02:02]</a>.
    *   Do not wait to finish theory before starting MCQs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:13">[00:02:13]</a>.
    *   Aim for 100+ MCQs per day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:47">[00:16:47]</a>. If this is challenging, start with 50 and gradually increase by 5-7 questions daily <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:18">[00:17:18]</a>. The daily questions from your current topics should help you naturally cross this mark <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:00">[00:17:00]</a>.
*   **Rapid Revision (RR) Videos and Notes**
    *   Prioritize Rapid Revision (RR) videos and notes due to limited time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:25">[00:02:25]</a>.
    *   Only refer to main videos for conceptual clarity on specific doubts that are not understood through RR <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:48">[00:02:48]</a>. Play main videos for 10 minutes to clarify a concept, then return to RR <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:50">[00:02:50]</a>.
    *   RR is sufficient for the FMG exam <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:26">[00:32:26]</a>.
*   **Source of Questions**
    *   Utilize a Q-bank from any platform you already have a subscription for <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:22">[00:03:22]</a>.
    *   Focus on Previous Year Topics (PYTs) rather than rote memorization of Previous Year Questions (PYQs) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:45">[00:03:45]</a>. For example, if 'apoptosis' has appeared multiple times, study the apoptosis topic from your pathology notes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:09">[00:04:09]</a>.

## Recommended Study Hours

*   **Daily Study Target**: Aim for at least 10 to 12 hours of effective study per day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:37">[00:04:37]</a>.
    *   "Effective hours" means focused study time, accounting for time wastage from anxiety, panic, or social media <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:47">[00:04:47]</a>. If you sit for 12 hours, expect an output of 10 effective hours <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:50">[00:04:50]</a>.
*   **Sleep**: Aim for around 6 hours of sleep for healthy mental functioning, though 7 hours is also acceptable if it helps maintain focus and prevents fatigue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:13">[00:30:13]</a>.

## Revision Rounds

The timetable incorporates multiple revision rounds to reinforce learning:

*   **Round One (Current Timetable)**: This initial pass aims to cover all 19 subjects <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:01">[00:09:01]</a>.
*   **Round Two (Post-December 24th)**: This round focuses on revisiting subjects more quickly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:28">[00:18:28]</a>.
    *   **Short Subjects**: 3 days for all six (Psychiatry, Radiology, Orthopedics, Anesthesia, Forensic Medicine, Skin) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:31">[00:13:31]</a>.
    *   **ENT & Ophthalmology**: 2.5 days <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:05">[00:14:05]</a>.
    *   **PSM**: 2 days <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:15">[00:14:15]</a>.
    *   **OBG**: 3 days <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:20">[00:14:20]</a>.

    *   **Physiology**: 1 day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:29">[00:14:29]</a>.
    *   **Volatile Subjects (Biochemistry, Anatomy, Pharmacology)**: 2 days each, placed closer to the exam for better retention (until Jan 13th) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:55">[00:14:55]</a>.
    *   **Pathology and Microbiology**: Daily targets will be provided for these subjects from December 24th until January 13th <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:42">[00:18:42]</a>.
*   **Round Three (Last Week)**: Focus solely on previous year topics and volatile topics that were problematic during LRR <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:17">[00:15:17]</a>.

## Grand Tests (GTs)

*   **Frequency**: Aim for a minimum of 5 Grand Tests (GTs) before the January 20th exam <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:11">[00:20:11]</a>.
*   **Purpose of GTs**:
    1.  **Time Management**: Learn to manage time effectively, ensuring you complete the exam and revisit questions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:35">[00:20:35]</a>. Maintain a pace of 45-50 seconds per question <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:06">[00:23:06]</a>.
    2.  **Analysis of Incorrect Questions**: Focus heavily on analyzing incorrect answers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:29">[00:29:29]</a>. Read explanations from your Q-bank/solution source <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:31">[00:21:31]</a>.
    3.  **Review of Correct Questions**: For correct answers, briefly revisit your notes for quick reinforcement, rather than reading full explanations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:02">[00:21:02]</a>.
*   **Score Focus**: Do not get disheartened by GT scores <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:34">[00:23:34]</a>. GTs are often designed to be tougher to encourage further study <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:49">[00:23:49]</a>. A 150+ in GTs indicates you're on track for 170-180+ in the actual exam <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:04">[00:24:04]</a>.
*   **Last 15 Days**: Avoid taking GTs in the last 15 days to minimize stress <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:46">[00:31:46]</a>.

## Addressing Common Concerns

*   **Forgetting is Normal**: It is human nature to forget <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:43">[00:13:43]</a>. Accept that you will read, forget, and re-read in a continuous cycle until the exam <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:48">[00:13:48]</a>. Even experienced faculties forget if they don't teach for months <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:37">[00:26:37]</a>.
*   **Making Notes Concise**: When studying, highlight only the 5 main words or key concepts on a page <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:21">[00:25:21]</a>. This ensures that during rapid revision, you only need to read the highlighted terms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:35">[00:25:35]</a>.
*   **Volatile/Disliked Subjects**:
    *   Don't take dislikes too seriously <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:53">[00:50:53]</a>. You're studying for the exam, not to master the subject for life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:59">[00:50:59]</a>.
    *   Pick up the Rapid Revision notes and just go through them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:20">[00:51:20]</a>.
    *   If you're particularly scared of subjects like Pharma, Anatomy, Biochemistry, or Microbiology, consider tackling them first in the initial week to get them out of the way <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:41">[00:51:41]</a>.
*   **Balancing Health**:
    *   **Physical Health**: Dedicate 30-40 minutes daily to physical activity, such as a walk <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:55">[00:34:55]</a>. This also serves as a mental break <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:15">[00:35:15]</a>.
    *   **Mental Health**: Everyone has their own stress busters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:38">[00:35:38]</a>. Practice deep breathing, ensure a balanced diet, and consider 10-15 minutes of meditation or yoga <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:59">[00:35:59]</a>. The main aim is to keep your heart rate normal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:15">[00:36:15]</a>.
*   **Image-Based Questions (IBQs)**: IBQs are important and help you pick up questions easily <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:07">[00:33:07]</a>. Be prepared for a mixed bag of questions, including both one-liners and clinical questions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:12">[00:33:12]</a>.
*   **Starting from Zero**: It is possible to clear FMG by studying diligently in the last 60 days <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:13">[00:41:13]</a>. Assume you're starting from zero and aim to reach 150+ marks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:01">[00:41:01]</a>.
*   **Customization for Higher Scores**: If aiming for 200+ marks, consider reducing the allocated days for some subjects (e.g., Anatomy in 2.5-3 days instead of 4) to free up ~10 extra days for an additional revision round <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:04">[00:47:04]</a>.

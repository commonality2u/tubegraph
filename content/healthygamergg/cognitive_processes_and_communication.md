## Why Thoughts Don't Translate Directly to Speech

Thoughts originate from various parts of the brain, but speech is primarily dictated by the linguistic cortex, located in the left hemisphere <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:51">[00:01:51]</a>.

### Brain Regions Involved in Thought
Thoughts are not confined to the linguistic cortex <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:05">[00:02:05]</a>. Other brain regions contribute to our thoughts and understanding:
*   **Default Mode Network (DMN)**: This part of the mind is responsible for self-reflection and is hyperactive during depressive states, leading to self-deprecating thoughts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:09">[00:02:09]</a>. These thoughts do not originate from the linguistic cortex <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:28">[00:02:28]</a>.
*   **Limbic System and Frontal Lobes**: These areas manage functions like risk assessment, [[nervousness_and_communication | anxiety]], and mathematical reasoning <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:33">[00:02:33]</a>.
*   **Empathic Circuits**: These circuits help us understand other people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:44">[00:02:44]</a>.

When we speak, all these diverse thoughts must be channeled through the linguistic cortex <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:02">[00:03:02]</a>. Improving this channeling process leads to better communication <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:13">[00:03:13]</a>.

### Communication as a Skill
Just as imagining a complex physical feat (like a ninja kick) doesn't mean the body can perform it, thinking articulately doesn't automatically mean one can speak articulately <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:35">[00:04:35]</a>. Communication is a [[techniques_for_effective_communication | skill]] that requires practice <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:42">[00:04:42]</a>.

### The Problem of Assumptions
When thinking in one's head, all assumptions are perfectly understood because they are internal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:39">[00:08:39]</a>. However, when communicating verbally, these [[role_of_assumptions_in_communication | assumptions]] are often missing, leading to garbled or misunderstood messages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:06">[00:09:06]</a>. Effective communication involves the other person, requiring an understanding of their perspective <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:23">[00:09:23]</a>. Authors, for instance, must use their empathic circuits to understand what information the reader is missing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:49">[00:09:49]</a>. Similarly, to communicate effectively, one must acknowledge their own assumptions and meet the audience where they are at <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:25">[00:11:25]</a>.

### Impact of Mental State
The state of one's mind significantly impacts communication clarity. For example, in mania, thoughts become rapid, and speech speeds up, often resulting in nonsensical communication <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:17">[00:16:17]</a>. This suggests that calming oneself can lead to better communication <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:10">[00:17:10]</a>. Tools like breathing exercises or even medications like propranolol (which slows down the nervous system) can help reduce [[nervousness_and_communication | nervousness]] and improve clarity during public speaking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:16">[00:17:16]</a>.

## How to Improve Communication
To bridge the gap between internal thoughts and spoken words, three key areas can be addressed:

### 1. Train the Linguistic Cortex
Since all communication must pass through the linguistic cortex, dedicating [[practicing_effective_communication_skills | practice]] to this pathway is crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:15">[00:13:15]</a>:
*   **Writing Things Out**: Writing helps clarify ideas, facilitating the translation of thoughts into words <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:49">[00:05:49]</a>. Even a long, unsuccessful writing project can serve to clarify ideas for future communication <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:32">[00:06:32]</a>.
*   **Role-Playing Conversations**: Practicing conversations, especially with a sounding board, can help refine phrasing and prepare for actual interactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:27">[00:07:27]</a>. This is applicable to both formal and casual conversations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:16">[00:20:16]</a>.
*   **Rehearsal**: Simply rehearsing what one wants to say can improve fluency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:38">[00:13:38]</a>.

### 2. Focus on Assumptions and Audience Perspective
Effective communication requires engaging one's empathic circuitry <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:58">[00:13:58]</a> to understand the audience's starting point and what they already know or are missing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:04">[00:14:04]</a>. Instead of expecting others to "understand why," effective communicators meet people where they are and guide them step-by-step <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:28">[00:14:28]</a>. This approach is fundamental in fields like coaching, where a coach meets a client's initial goal but then helps them address underlying issues needed to achieve it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:40">[00:14:40]</a>. Being a good friend, parent, or therapist also involves acknowledging one's own [[role_of_assumptions_in_communication | assumptions]] and adapting to the other person's understanding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:18">[00:11:18]</a>.

### 3. Calm the Mind and Slow Down
Reducing mental and physiological agitation can significantly improve communication clarity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:10">[00:17:10]</a>. Techniques include:
*   **Breathing Exercises**: These can help regulate the nervous system <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:45">[00:17:45]</a>.
*   **Pharmacological Aids**: In some cases, medication like propranolol can be prescribed to reduce the physical effects of [[nervousness_and_communication | adrenaline]] before a speech <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:20">[00:17:20]</a>.

These three approaches (training the linguistic cortex, addressing assumptions, and calming the mind) are distinct but complementary mechanisms for improving communication <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:32">[00:18:32]</a>. Problems with communication are often multifactorial, meaning a combination of these solutions is typically most effective <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:48">[00:18:48]</a>.

It is also possible to rationalize or process thoughts through non-verbal means like art, music, mathematics, sports, or freeform games, with strong evidence supporting the healing power of art therapy and similar methods <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:34">[00:19:34]</a>.

Navigating relationships can be challenging, but for public figures like streamers, the difficulties are often amplified, especially when a relationship ends publicly. This article explores the unique struggles faced by those in the public eye, drawing insights from a conversation with a streamer who experienced a very public breakup.

## The Impact of a Public Breakup

A streamer, Lily, experienced a highly public breakup with her boyfriend, Albert, after he cheated on her <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:09">[00:04:09]</a>. Their relationship was integrated into her content, making them a "very public couple" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:04">[00:30:04]</a>. The infidelity led to articles, videos, and online discussions, which were "overwhelming" for Lily, even reaching her parents <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:07">[00:29:07]</a>.

Since the breakup, Lily has struggled significantly with sleep, describing it as "really hard" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:19">[00:04:19]</a>, and dealing with intense feelings of sadness and anger <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:55">[00:05:55]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:59">[00:31:59]</a>. She expressed a desire for things to "just go back to normal," but understands it will never be the same <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:17">[00:32:17]</a>.

### Public Perception vs. Personal Reality

Lily felt compelled to hide her sadness from her audience, concerned about appearing "not strong" or "throwing a pity party" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:19">[00:06:19]</a>. This highlights a common struggle for public figures: the internal conflict between how they genuinely feel and how they wish to be perceived <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:44">[00:07:44]</a>. While some viewers found her openness about sadness relatable, she worried about being seen as constantly talking about "depression or sadness" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:01">[00:09:01]</a>, despite most people finding it helpful <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:46">[00:09:46]</a>. This concern can lead to internal judgment and self-criticism <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:21">[00:11:21]</a>.

## Self-Perception and Deserving Success

Lily struggles with a deep-seated feeling of not deserving her success, often attributing it to "luck" rather than earned merit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:11">[00:26:11]</a>. This feeling is intensified by her public breakup, which brought a "huge influx of viewers" due to the "drama" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:11">[00:18:11]</a>. She questions if she is "doing enough to deserve this success" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:26">[00:26:26]</a>.

This self-doubt, dubbed "Lily 2" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:30">[00:44:30]</a>, originates from childhood experiences. Her very traditional Korean parents, like many Asian parents, had a "very specific sense of what success is in life" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:19">[01:00:19]</a>. Lily experienced depression in high school, leading to academic struggles and intense conflicts with her parents <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:14">[00:55:14]</a>. She felt like a "failure" and "useless" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:32">[00:55:32]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:15">[00:57:15]</a>, recalling a painful memory of her mother wishing she "was never born" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:34">[00:58:34]</a>. These experiences ingrained a core belief of not being "good enough" or being a "disappointment" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:05">[01:08:05]</a>, making it hard to see her own inherent value and the path she forged despite difficulties <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:02">[01:03:02]</a>.

## Dealing with Public Scrutiny and Hate

Public figures, especially streamers, often face intense scrutiny and "a lot of hate" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:21">[01:15:21]</a>. Lily received hate mail and messages questioning her authenticity, accusing her of faking her voice for attention or being a "gold digger" in previous relationships <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:23:18">[01:23:18]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:25:26">[01:25:26]</a>. Some even told her she "deserved to be cheated on" after her breakup <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:26:45">[01:26:45]</a>.

### Understanding the Origin of Hate

It's crucial to understand that such extreme hate often "has nothing to do with you" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:51">[01:29:51]</a>. It comes from the "haters'" own issues, hurts, and anger <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:32:28">[01:32:28]</a>. Seeing someone like Lily, who struggles with depression but is successful, can terrify those who use their own struggles as an "excuse" for their situation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:32:49">[01:32:49]</a>. Haters may try to "tear you down in order to make themselves feel better about like their own situation in life" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:33:30">[01:33:30]</a>.

### Empathic Nature and Self-Protection

Lily's highly empathic nature means she "feel[s] the feelings of others in a very deep and intense way" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:40:55">[01:40:55]</a>, making her like an "empathic sponge" that "absorbs negativity very easily" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:43:08">[01:43:08]</a>. This explains why public hate can be so deeply painful for her.

To protect herself, Lily often employs self-deprecation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:25">[01:17:25]</a>. This serves as a "survival mechanism" where she's "willing to treat yourself badly because there's certainty and security in that" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:53:01">[01:53:01]</a>. By putting herself down, she believes others can't hurt her, providing a "perfect armor against everything that Lily 2 dreads," particularly the "dread of disappointment" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:53:51">[01:53:51]</a>.

## Coping Mechanisms and Strategies

Grief is a process that can resurface unexpectedly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:24">[00:34:24]</a>. True grief can be cathartic, leading to processing feelings and eventual peace <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:49">[00:35:49]</a>.

### Self-Compassion and Self-Worth
Instead of trying to eliminate "Lily 2" (the self-critical part), the goal is to support her <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:46">[00:50:46]</a>. This involves acknowledging her fears and offering reassurance, similar to how one would support a friend <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:53">[00:47:53]</a>. Recognizing one's inherent value and hard work, despite self-doubt, is crucial for fostering genuine self-worth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:08">[01:07:08]</a>.

### Managing Emotions and Empathy
As an "empathic sponge," Lily needs specific self-care techniques to [[managing_emotions_and_empathy_in_relationships | manage emotions]].

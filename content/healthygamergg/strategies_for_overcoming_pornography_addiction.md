## The Paradox of Resisting Cravings

When the brain wants something, it initially tries to entice you <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:48">[00:01:48]</a>. If you resist, the craving escalates through stages: from mild discomfort to withdrawal symptoms like headaches, stomach aches, and even vomiting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:06">[00:02:06]</a>. If you continue to resist and then give in, your brain learns that it needs to "punish" you more severely to get what it wants, similar to how the body reacts to hunger or thirst <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:49">[00:02:49]</a>.

### Why Resistance Can Intensify Cravings
This leads to a paradoxical effect in addiction: the more a person resists cravings and fails, the more intensive their cravings become <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:43">[00:03:43]</a>. Unlike in other areas of life (like going to the gym or playing chess) where fighting and losing leads to improvement over time, fighting addiction often leads to worse outcomes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:53">[00:03:53]</a>. The brain learns that intense signals are required to achieve its desired outcome, thus amplifying future cravings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:44">[00:04:44]</a>.

This phenomenon is not exclusive to addiction; the brain applies similar logic in other contexts:
*   **Communicating with a deaf person**: If someone doesn't hear you at a normal volume, you raise your voice. The brain learns to start at a louder volume for future interactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:08">[00:05:08]</a>.
*   **Children seeking attention**: A child bullied at school might complain of stomach aches after parents ignore initial complaints about school. The body can physiologically manifest symptoms to get needs met, even unconsciously <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:33">[00:05:33]</a>.

Ultimately, if you resist cravings and fail, your brain will intensify them to achieve its goal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:11">[00:06:11]</a>.

## Evidence-Based Addiction Interventions

Traditional addiction psychiatry does not primarily focus on "white-knuckling" through cravings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:43">[00:08:43]</a>. Instead, interventions are designed to avoid directly fighting cravings:

*   **Psychotherapy**: When attending therapy, individuals are not actively resisting cravings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:42">[00:06:42]</a>.
*   **Peer Support (e.g., Alcoholics Anonymous, Narcotics Anonymous)**: Meetings provide community and focus on activities that divert from directly grappling with the illness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:51">[00:06:51]</a>. Sponsors often distract individuals from cravings by suggesting alternative activities like walks or coffee <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:09">[00:07:09]</a>.
*   **Environmental Changes**: For example, an alcoholic might be advised to engage in a "social transplant" to find new, sober friends, as resisting cravings is extremely difficult when surrounded by people who use <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:25">[00:07:25]</a>.
*   **Medications**: Drugs like Naltrexone reduce cravings, while Buprenorphine partially satisfies the opioid receptors, eliminating cravings by providing the body/brain with what it wants <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:11">[00:08:11]</a>.

The overarching strategy in addiction psychiatry is to avoid fighting cravings directly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:43">[00:08:43]</a>. Direct grappling with cravings repeatedly often does not lead to success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:58">[00:08:58]</a>.

## Strategies for Overcoming Pornography Addiction

While the principle of not directly fighting cravings applies, pornography addiction has unique aspects that allow for specific strategies:

### 1. Pick Your Battles (Scheduled Use)
Instead of randomly resisting and failing, which intensifies cravings, one strategy is to schedule pornography use <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:34">[00:09:34]</a>. For example, picking two specific times a day when it's "allowed" can have interesting effects <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:51">[00:09:51]</a>:
*   **Easier Resistance**: During times outside these windows, it becomes easier to resist <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:59">[00:09:59]</a>. You're training your frontal lobe to delay gratification <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:15">[00:10:15]</a>.
*   **Moderation is Possible**: Unlike many substance use disorders where moderation can be challenging due to genetic modifications (e.g., one drink for an alcoholic can derail the day), behavioral addictions like pornography often allow for moderation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:21">[00:10:21]</a>.
*   **Building Successes**: By successfully delaying use, even until the scheduled time, individuals build confidence and hope <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:50">[00:12:50]</a>. Over time, some patients even begin to skip their scheduled times <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:57">[00:12:57]</a>.

### 2. Set the Field of Battle (Sun Tzu's "Art of War")
The goal is to set up your environment to make it easy to resist and win battles, rather than fighting and losing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:09">[00:11:09]</a>. This means:
*   **Reducing Cravings**: Implement strategies that naturally reduce the intensity of cravings.
*   **Shaping Your Environment**: Change your surroundings and routines to minimize exposure or triggers that lead to engaging in pornography <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:17">[00:11:17]</a>.

Fighting battles you cannot win leads to hopelessness and teaches your brain that resistance is futile, intensifying the cravings further <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:33">[00:11:33]</a>.

### 3. Address Underlying Driving Forces
Pornography is often used as a source of emotional regulation, providing dopamine and temporarily suppressing negative emotions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:20">[00:13:20]</a>. While watching, negative emotions are subdued, but shame and other negative feelings often flood in afterward <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:50">[00:13:50]</a>. Therefore, focus on:
*   **Developing Alternate Emotional Regulation Techniques**: Proactively develop healthy ways to manage emotions. This includes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:38">[00:13:38]</a>:
    *   Meditation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:08">[00:14:08]</a>
    *   Journaling <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:09">[00:14:09]</a>
    *   Talking about emotions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:10">[00:14:10]</a>
    *   Working with a therapist <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:11">[00:14:11]</a>
    *   Engaging in clinical treatment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:12">[00:14:12]</a>

### 4. Wait Out the Craving ("Urge Surfing")
A key difference between pornography (behavioral addiction) and substance use disorders is the physiological response to waiting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:19">[00:14:19]</a>.
*   **Substance Use Disorders**: The longer you wait to use a substance, the more physiological withdrawal symptoms increase, potentially leading to severe and even fatal outcomes (e.g., alcohol withdrawal seizures) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:26">[00:14:26]</a>.
*   **Pornography Addiction**: While cravings may intensify initially, there isn't a gross physiological withdrawal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:03">[00:15:03]</a>. If you can wait out the urge for 15-30 minutes or an hour, the craving will often decrease due to homeostatic tolerance, similar to how initial hunger pangs might subside if you don't eat immediately <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:25">[00:15:25]</a>. This technique is known as "urge surfing" in psychiatry <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:19">[00:16:19]</a>.

### 5. Find a Greater Purpose
The ability to tolerate negativity is crucial in overcoming addiction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:54">[00:17:54]</a>. If you cannot tolerate negative emotions, you are more likely to turn to pornography <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:58">[00:17:58]</a>. Having a greater purpose in life can enable you to endure unpleasant experiences for the sake of something more meaningful <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:35">[00:17:35]</a>.

## Conclusion

The approach to overcoming [[dangers_of_resisting_pornography_cravings | pornography addiction]] is unusual: it's not about fighting battles and losing, which intensifies cravings and leads to hopelessness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:04">[00:18:04]</a>.

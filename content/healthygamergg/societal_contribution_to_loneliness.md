## "Failure to Launch"

In psychiatry, this phenomenon is often termed "Failure to Launch" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:17:00">[02:17:00]</a>. It describes individuals who performed adequately in primary and middle school, passing classes and having some friends <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:27:00">[02:27:00]</a>. However, as they progress through life, the gap between them and their peers widens <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:40:00">[02:40:00]</a>. A key characteristic is the absence of a single traumatic event to explain their struggles, leading them to blame themselves <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:47:00">[02:47:00]</a>. Over time, friendships fade as others move on and build new relationships, while the "loner" struggles to do the same <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:54:00">[03:54:00]</a>. Externally, they may appear fine, but internally, they experience [[coping_with_feelings_of_isolation_and_loneliness | loneliness]] and isolation, which worsens over time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:25:00">[04:25:00]</a>. This often leads to a self-perception of being a "loser," a label that places all blame on the individual <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:56:00">[04:56:00]</a>.

## Systemic Nature of the Problem

The widespread presence of the "25-year-old loner" indicates a systemic issue, not just an individual one <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:20:00">[05:20:00]</a>. Culturally specific terms exist globally to describe this phenomenon:
*   **China**: "Laying flat" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:40:00">[01:40:00]</a>.
*   **Latin America/Spain**: "Nini's" (Ni estudian, ni trabajan - neither study, nor work) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:45:00">[01:45:00]</a>.
*   **English**: "NEETs" (Not in Employment, Education, or Training) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:50:00">[01:50:00]</a>.
*   **Japan**: "Hikikomori" (shut-ins) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:57:00">[01:57:00]</a>.

Economically, the proportion of young people, especially men, entering the workforce is at a historic low <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:06:00">[02:06:00]</a>. When individuals perceive their struggles as solely their fault, they accept responsibility for systemic issues beyond their control, leading to feelings of defeat <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:54:00">[05:54:00]</a>.

## Societal Shifts and the [[Impact of technology and society on loneliness | Internet]]

A major contributing factor to this societal shift is the [[Impact of technology and society on loneliness | Internet]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:56:00">[11:56:00]</a>. Historically, social skills were implicitly learned within tight-knit communities where people constantly interacted with the same individuals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:50:00">[13:50:00]</a>. For example, a village of 200 people might have generations living and dying without anyone visiting another country, making the concept of "the world" fictional <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:37:00">[12:37:00]</a>. In such environments, parents didn't need to explicitly teach social skills because society provided consistent contact and opportunities for interaction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:57:00">[13:57:00]</a>.

The [[Impact of technology and society on loneliness | Internet]] has introduced "social alternatives" and "socially fracturing forces," leading to increased globalization, remote work, and mobility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:03:00">[12:03:00]</a>. As a result, parents who were raised in a different social landscape may not realize the necessity of actively teaching social skills to their children <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:31:00">[14:31:00]</a>. This leads to a generation of people who are not taught how to maintain social relationships <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:27:00">[14:27:00]</a>.

### Impact of Online Communication on Social Skills

Online communication further exacerbates social anxiety. When interacting in person, parts of the brain interpret body language unconsciously, providing reassurance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:40:00">[28:40:00]</a>. However, as individuals spend more time communicating online, these brain regions may atrophy, similar to an unused language <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:11:00">[29:11:00]</a>. This reduced ability to interpret social cues leads to a lack of reassurance in social situations, increasing social anxiety <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:26:00">[29:26:00]</a>.

## Addressing the Issue

Understanding that these struggles are influenced by societal factors is crucial. Viewing one's circumstances through a "karmic perspective" – acknowledging the confluence of events, parenting, and societal changes that shaped them – can alleviate self-blame <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:25:00">[16:25:00]</a>. There is "no such thing as a loser" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:54:00">[17:54:00]</a>; it is a judgment made out of ignorance of an individual's unique circumstances and upbringing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:02:00">[18:02:00]</a>.

### Strategies for Moving Forward
*   **Release Blame**: Understand that not all aspects of the situation are the individual's fault <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:10:00">[19:10:00]</a>.
*   **Focus on "What Can I Do"**: Instead of dwelling on what "should" be done, focus on small, actionable steps that are possible today <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:02:00">[20:02:00]</a>. This shifts the focus from potential failure to immediate success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:00:00">[24:00:00]</a>.
*   **Process Emotions**: Individuals struggling with [[coping_with_feelings_of_isolation_and_loneliness | loneliness]] and anxiety may have piled-up negative emotions. Processing these through therapy, meditation, journaling, or walks can help clear the mind <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:43:00">[33:43:00]</a>.
*   **Seek Psychological Rehabilitation**: For those with clinical diagnoses like social anxiety disorder, professional treatment is the first step, followed by "psychological rehab" to build atrophied social skills <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:14:00">[41:14:00]</a>.
*   **Practice Social Skills**: Intentional practice, even in online "social sandboxes," can help relearn and refine social skills that were not adequately developed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:55:00">[29:55:00]</a>. Being willing to listen is a fundamental aspect of conversation, even if one feels they have "nothing to say" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="43:08:00">[43:08:00]</a>.
*   **Control Actions, Not Outcomes**: When reaching out, one's responsibility is the action of reaching out, not the response of others. Non-response does not reflect on self-worth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="38:36:00">[38:36:00]</a>.
*   **It's Never Too Late**: While it might feel "out of place," it is generally never too late to socialize, regardless of age <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="44:02:00">[44:02:00]</a>.

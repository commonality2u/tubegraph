## Understanding the Challenges of ADHD

Several factors contribute to the amplified fatigue and self-blame experienced by individuals with [[ADHD therapy and management | ADHD]]:

### Increased Effort for Tasks
For someone with [[ADHD therapy and management | ADHD]], it requires more effort to complete a single task than it does for a neurotypical person <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:20:00">[03:20:00]</a>. For example, a task that takes a neurotypical person one hour may take an individual with [[ADHD therapy and management | ADHD]] four hours due to distractibility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:10:00">[04:10:00]</a>. This means that after four hours of work, an [[ADHD therapy and management | ADHD]] individual may only have one hour's worth of tangible output, leading to significant mental depletion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:18:00">[04:18:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:22:00">[04:22:00]</a>.

### The Adjustment Phase
Many individuals are diagnosed with [[ADHD therapy and management | ADHD]] later in life, often around age 26, because they developed compensatory mechanisms in childhood due to their intelligence, allowing them to manage school without complete failure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:52:00">[04:52:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:00:00">[05:00:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:07:00">[05:07:00]</a>. As they begin to [[adhd_therapy_and_management | put their lives together]] and catch up to their peers, they enter a period of two to four years where they experience significant fatigue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:38:00">[05:38:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:47:00">[05:47:00]</a>. This is analogous to the overwhelming adjustment period of starting college or medical school, but individuals with [[ADHD therapy and management | ADHD]] are adapting later in life while their peers have already gone through such phases <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:00:00">[06:00:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:06:00">[07:06:00]</a>.

### Impact of Chronic Stress
Constant stress, such as continuous deadlines without proper breaks, significantly impacts energy levels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:04:00">[08:04:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:17:00">[08:17:00]</a>. Cortisol, the stress hormone, temporarily boosts productivity by cannibalizing resources like muscle tissue for energy, but this comes at the cost of prolonged exhaustion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:29:00">[08:29:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:33:00">[08:33:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:41:00">[08:41:00]</a>. In modern society, there are few opportunities for recovery, leading to chronic stress that depletes the body's ability to perform work <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:18:00">[09:18:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:21:00">[09:21:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:35:00">[09:35:00]</a>.

### The "I Am Not Enough" Belief
A significant factor is the self-blame often internalized by individuals with [[ADHD therapy and management | ADHD]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:03:00">[10:03:00]</a>. Around 70% of people with [[ADHD therapy and management | ADHD]] develop [[strategies_for_managing_adhd_and_depression | depression]] later in life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:40:00">[10:40:00]</a>. This often stems from childhood experiences: despite being smart, children with [[ADHD therapy and management | ADHD]] struggle with attention, forgetfulness, and organization, leading them to conclude "there is something wrong with me" because no one explains their attention is simply different <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:06:00">[11:06:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:22:00">[11:22:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:08:00">[12:08:00]</a>. Furthermore, social isolation due to impulsive behaviors and feedback from teachers like "they just need to apply themselves" reinforce the belief that their struggles are a lack of effort, not a neurological difference <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:37:00">[12:37:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:51:00">[12:51:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:07:00">[13:07:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:24:00">[13:24:00]</a>. This deeply ingrained belief of "I am not enough" clashes with intellectual understanding of external stressors, leading to self-blame even when circumstances are objectively challenging <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:56:00">[13:56:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:04:00">[14:04:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:30:00">[14:30:00]</a>.

## Strategies for Managing ADHD Symptoms

While life with [[ADHD therapy and management | ADHD]] can feel like "playing life on hard mode," there are effective [[adaptive_strategies_in_chaotic_environments_for_those_with_adhd | strategies]] to manage symptoms and achieve success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:51:00">[18:51:00]</a>.

However, the effects of medication cease when treatment stops, while psychotherapy's benefits can last for up to two years after stopping <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:38:00">[15:38:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:43:00">[15:43:00]</a>. [[ADHD therapy and management | Psychotherapy]] teaches individuals with [[ADHD therapy and management | ADHD]] how to function effectively despite distractible attention <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:52:00">[15:52:00]</a>.

### Addressing Self-Perception and Building Confidence
A critical component of managing [[ADHD therapy and management | ADHD]] is working on [[the_role_of_confidence_in_managing_adhd_symptoms | confidence]] and self-perception, especially to counter the internalized belief of "I am not enough" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:20:00">[17:20:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:35:00">[17:35:00]</a>.

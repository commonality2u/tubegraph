## The Core Strategy: Embracing Boredom

### The "Father's Rule"

A powerful strategy is to eliminate all attractive alternatives to the task at hand. The rule is: if you don't feel like studying, you are allowed to sleep as much as you want or sit there and do nothing as much as you want, but those are the only two options besides studying <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:48">[00:16:06]</a>. This means no engaging in "productive" distractions like cleaning or dopaminergic activities like playing [[strategies_for_managing_adhd_and_addiction | video games]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:00">[00:13:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:13">[00:16:13]</a>.

The mind often plays a "waiting game," knowing that an individual will eventually seek productive or stimulating activities to avoid boredom <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:14">[00:13:14]</a>. However, the self can tolerate boredom better than the mind <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:44">[00:17:44]</a>. By limiting options to only the task, boredom, or sleep, the mind will eventually concede and begin to [[techniques_for_improving_focus_and_attention | focus]] on the work <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:37">[00:17:37]</a>. This teaches the mind to comply with your direction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:17">[00:20:17]</a>.

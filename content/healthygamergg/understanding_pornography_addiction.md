In psychiatry, a major criterion for addiction is **impairment of function** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:10:00">[12:10:00]</a>. If pornography use, masturbation, or sexual behavior causes problems in one's life—such as negatively impacting a marriage, leading to sexually transmitted infections (STIs), or interfering with work—it is more likely to be considered an addiction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:24:00">[12:24:00]</a>. People with an addiction experience negative consequences but are unable to stop, and they often fail in attempts to cut back <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:58:00">[12:58:00]</a>. Addiction tends to control the individual, rather than the individual controlling it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:15:00">[14:15:00]</a>.

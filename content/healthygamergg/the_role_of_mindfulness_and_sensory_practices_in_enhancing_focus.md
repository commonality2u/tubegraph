## Understanding Mind Wandering and Focus
When attempting to focus, such as reading a book, the mind typically starts in a "one-pointed" or focused state <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:00">[01:04:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:00">[01:07:00]</a>. After a short period, it tends to scatter into various thoughts and distractions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:00">[01:12:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:34:00">[01:34:00]</a>. The process of [[the_role_of_mindset_and_focus_in_learning | studying]] often involves this cycle of focused attention, followed by scattering, and then eventually coalescing back to focus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:00:00">[02:00:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:05:00">[02:05:00]</a>. On productive days, the periods of focused attention are longer, and the scattering periods are shorter <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:13:00">[02:13:00]</a>.

The mind enjoys being focused and entertained, with entertainment being defined as a sensory experience that easily facilitates mental focus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:41:00">[04:41:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:43:00">[04:43:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:03:00">[05:03:00]</a>. Even studying can be enjoyable when one is in a state of flow and learning <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:27:00">[05:27:00]</a>. The challenge arises when the mind scatters and becomes bored <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:48:00">[05:48:00]</a>.

## Traditional Practices for Mental Control
Ancient practices offer [[Mindfulness and meditation for controlling attention and selfawareness | techniques for controlling attention]].

### Dharana (Focus)
Dharana are [[techniques_for_improving_focus_and_attention | meditation techniques]] specifically designed to train the mind to become "one-pointed" or deeply focused <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:07:00">[03:07:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:08:00">[03:08:00]</a>.

### Pratyahara (Sensory Withdrawal)
Pratyahara involves the practice of sensory withdrawal or restraining the sense organs (*indriyas*) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:11:00">[03:11:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:14:00">[03:14:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:00:00">[04:00:00]</a>. Distractions like the smell of barbecue or a phone notification can shatter a one-pointed mind <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:29:00">[03:29:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:45:00">[03:45:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:56:00">[03:56:00]</a>. Pratyahara teaches how to keep your eyes focused on what they should be, control your nose and ears, and ultimately restrain your attention to where you desire it to be <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:06:00">[04:06:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:10:00">[04:10:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:14:00">[04:14:00]</a>.

These platforms use algorithms to suggest content that keeps users engaged, effectively training the user to become more addicted to the platform <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:20:00">[07:20:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:22:00">[07:22:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:46:00">[07:46:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:25:00">[08:25:00]</a>.

Once algorithmic entertainment is engaged, it becomes extremely difficult to pull attention back to study or work due to the sophisticated engineering behind these platforms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:17:00">[09:17:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:34:00">[09:34:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:41:00">[10:41:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:50:00">[10:50:00]</a>.

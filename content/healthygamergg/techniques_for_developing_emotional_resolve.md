## Understanding Resolve and Discipline
Discipline is often misunderstood. It is not solely about willpower or habits, but rather an emotion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:55:00">[01:55:00]</a>. This concept became clear when studying addiction, where overcoming addiction requires significant discipline, yet therapeutic approaches often focus on processing feelings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:41:00">[02:41:00]</a>.

Yogic philosophy provides insight into the nature of discipline, suggesting that doubt is the opposite of discipline <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:30:00">[07:30:00]</a>. When there is doubt—whether in a relationship, a career path, or studies—discipline wavers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:48:00">[07:48:00]</a>. The opposite of doubt is resolve <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:49:00">[08:49:00]</a>. When an individual is internally resolved, their behavior becomes disciplined <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:04:00">[09:04:00]</a>.

Resolve is an emotion that fluctuates daily, much like anger or love <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:51:00">[09:51:00]</a>. While negative emotions are often localized to specific anatomical structures in the brain (e.g., amygdala for fear), positive emotions like humor, joy, love, and resolve emerge from complex neural circuits, which neuroscience is still exploring <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:51:00">[03:51:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:39:00">[11:39:00]</a>.

## Cultivating Resolve: The Sankalpa Practice
Ancient yogic texts, particularly the Upanishads, offer [[the_role_of_resolve_in_cultivating_discipline | methods for cultivating resolve]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:53:00">[06:53:00]</a>. The practice of `sankalpa`, which literally translates to "resolve," involves a three-step process:

1.  **Notice Resolve:** The first step is to become aware of the sensation of resolve. When you feel resolved, take a moment to notice and "snapshot" that internal experience. This practice helps you understand the emotional state of resolve <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:49:00">[12:49:00]</a>. Resolve fuels willpower, acting as a "thermonuclear engine" that helps you resist distractions and stay focused <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:04:00">[13:04:00]</a>.

2.  **Practice with a Medium-Difficulty Goal:**
    Choose one specific, medium-difficulty thing you want to be resolved towards. Avoid picking something critically important or emotionally charged initially, as this can lead to reliance on willpower and increase the chance of failure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:10:00">[14:10:00]</a>. An example could be giving up a specific food item for a period, as it requires consistency but not overwhelming willpower <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:08:00">[14:08:00]</a>.

    *   **Daily Practice:** Every day, ideally within the first 1-1.5 hours of waking, sit quietly, close your eyes, and focus on this chosen resolve. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:49:00">[14:49:00]</a>
    *   **Stoke the Emotion:** Spend 5-10 minutes actively trying to feel the internal emotional state of resolve that you identified in step one. Allow yourself to open up to this feeling, giving it a calm space in your mind to grow <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:22:00">[15:22:00]</a>.
    *   **Consistency over Willpower:** The goal is to focus on the consistency of cultivating the resolve, rather than relying heavily on willpower for success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:18:00">[15:18:00]</a>. Practice this step for approximately 30 days before moving to the next <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:07:00">[17:07:00]</a>.

3.  **Cultivate a Global, Emotionally Charged Resolve:**
    After consistent practice with a simpler goal, select a more significant, emotionally charged resolve that is deeply important to you <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:00:00">[16:00:00]</a>. This could be a broad life goal or a statement of self-worth, such as "I deserve to be whole" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:17:00">[16:17:00]</a>.

    *   **Deeper Reflection:** Spend 10-20 minutes daily reflecting on this resolve, allowing the associated emotions to surface. The aim is to cultivate these emotions like a fire <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:09:00">[16:09:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:47:00">[16:47:00]</a>.
    *   **Sustained Energy:** As this positive emotion is cultivated daily, its energetic impact will carry over into consistent discipline <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:25:00">[17:25:00]</a>. This process is believed to activate positive emotional circuitry throughout the brain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:37:00">[17:37:00]</a>.

## Obstacles to Cultivating Resolve
One significant barrier to cultivating resolve and discipline is emotional numbness. Individuals who struggle with discipline often report feeling emotionally numb, a state where daily life feels drab or gray <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:50:00">[17:50:00]</a>. This numbness acts as a protection against negative emotions like disappointment or inadequacy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:28:00">[18:28:00]</a>. However, numbing out negative emotions, often through technology or substances like marijuana, also numbs positive emotions, preventing the capacity to cultivate or stoke the positive emotion of resolve <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:44:00">[18:44:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:51:00">[18:51:00]</a>. To overcome this, it's crucial to acknowledge that one must either feel everything or feel nothing at all <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:04:00">[19:04:00]</a>. [[Strategies for developing emotional awareness and processing | Addressing Alexithymia]] and other emotional processing challenges is essential for individuals experiencing numbness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:10:00">[19:10:00]</a>.

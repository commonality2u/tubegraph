The [[Blackmagic Intensity Extreme | Blackmagic Intensity Extreme]] (specifically, the Blackmagic Design Intensity Extreme) can be used to capture video input from a [[gopro_hero_3_black_edition_features | GoPro Hero 3]] camera <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:02">[01:02:02]</a>.

## Connecting the Devices

The [[Blackmagic Intensity Extreme | Blackmagic Intensity Extreme]] is powered by Thunderbolt and features a standard HDMI IN port <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:04">[01:10:04]</a>. The [[gopro_hero_3_black_edition_features | GoPro Hero 3]] outputs video via a tiny [[hdmi_output_from_gopro_hero_3 | HDMI out]] port <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:02">[01:15:02]</a>. To connect, a small [[using_hdmi_cables_with_gopro_hero_3 | HDMI cable]] from the [[gopro_hero_3_black_edition_features | GoPro Hero 3]]'s output plugs into the HDMI IN of the Blackmagic Intensity Extreme <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:46:04">[01:46:04]</a>. The Blackmagic Intensity Extreme also comes with a breakout cable for various connections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:21:08">[01:21:08]</a>.

## Video Quality and Adjustments

When video comes raw from the [[gopro_hero_3_black_edition_features | GoPro]] into the Intensity Extreme, it does not have any effects applied <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:07">[00:25:07]</a>. Adjustments can be made to achieve proper color balance and gamma <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:04">[00:37:04]</a>. With some tinkering, the colors can be corrected to look good <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:07">[00:48:07]</a>. Settings within capture software can be used to scale and adjust gamma, resulting in a good output <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:09:04">[02:09:04]</a>.

## Software Compatibility

The [[Blackmagic Intensity Extreme | Blackmagic Intensity Extreme]] functions well with [[using_blackmagic_capture_and_editing_software_with_gopro | Blackmagic's capture and editing software]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:52:05">[01:52:05]</a>. It is recognized as an input source within Wirecast <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:03:00">[02:03:00]</a>.

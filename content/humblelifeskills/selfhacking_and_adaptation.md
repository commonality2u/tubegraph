## Philosophy and Approach

### Defining Self-Hacking
Self-hacking is described as an ongoing process of adapting and living creatively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:47">[00:00:47]</a>. It is a method for individuals to maintain control over their lives, especially when faced with systemic obstacles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:32">[00:00:32]</a>. The goal is to avoid being "indexed and stored," resisting societal categorization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:36">[00:00:36]</a>.

### Creative Living and Independence
A core aspect of this philosophy is the pursuit of a location-independent and [[digital nomad lifestyle | digitally nomadic]] existence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:28">[00:00:28]</a>. This allows for continuous movement and flexibility, potentially forgoing traditional property investments like flats or houses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:26">[00:00:26]</a>.

## Personal Challenges and Repair

### Overcoming Past Issues
Self-hacking also involves repairing past "information overload and self damage" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:49">[00:00:49]</a>. This includes addressing personal struggles such as paranoia and depression, which can arise from excessive social activities like clubbing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:56">[00:00:56]</a>.

### Maintaining Happiness
Despite challenges, a key objective of self-hacking is to maintain a level of happiness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:54">[00:00:54]</a>. This is seen as a continuous effort to recover from or mitigate the impact of past negative experiences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:56">[00:00:56]</a>.

From: [[humblelifeskills]] <br/> 

The Centrance MicPort Pro is described as a "work of genius" and "beautifully made" audio interface <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:03">[00:00:03]</a>. It is noted for its "pure digital" capabilities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:12">[00:00:12]</a>.

## Design and Build
The device is "very well made" and strong, described as "Billet cut die cut" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:02">[00:01:02]</a>. It has a "really nice size" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:11">[00:01:11]</a> and integrates flushly as an "all in one unit" when connected to a microphone <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:21">[00:01:21]</a>. The indicator light illuminates white and is not overly bright, unlike another blue-lit device <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:58">[00:00:58]</a>.

## Key Features
*   **Adjustments**
    *   Minimum and maximum microphone level settings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:30">[00:00:30]</a>.
    *   Minimum and maximum headphone level settings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:35">[00:00:35]</a>.
*   **Connectivity**
    *   Headphone connector on the bottom <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:41">[00:00:41]</a>.
    *   USB connection to a computer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:51">[00:00:51]</a>.
*   **Power**
    *   48-volt phantom power to power certain microphones <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:44">[00:00:44]</a>.

## Compatibility and Usage
The MicPort Pro is compatible with any XLR microphone that has the appropriate connector on the bottom, allowing it to snap in securely <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:13">[00:01:13]</a>. This includes microphones like the [[Rode NTG2 microphone features | Rode NTG2 mic]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:13">[00:01:13]</a>. It connects to a laptop for direct recording into a computer, producing broadcast-quality sound <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:25">[00:01:25]</a>.

## Comparison to Other Devices
The MicPort Pro is noted as being superior to a [[Blue Mikey microphone features | Blue Snowball]] device previously used, specifically highlighting its range of adjustments which the Blue Snowball lacked <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:18">[00:00:18]</a>. The Blue Snowball also had an "over the top" blue light that was not preferred <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>.

## Funding and Resources

A significant challenge for creators is securing the necessary funds to continue producing content and furthering their knowledge. The ability to keep putting out episodes whenever time allows is a goal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:36">[00:01:36]</a>.

Access to industry events and professional development is often hindered by financial constraints <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:34">[00:04:34]</a>. For example, a company called Santa Cruz Tech, based in California, was scheduled to speak at an O'Reilly Emerging Technology Conference in March <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:21">[00:04:21]</a>. An associate from Belgium, Chris, was also slated to speak at this conference <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:15">[00:05:15]</a>. Despite a strong desire to attend and hear the speakers, the individual lacked the necessary funds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:25">[00:05:25]</a>.

To address this, an appeal was made for donations via a "donate button" on their social media site, `www.tv` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:32">[00:05:32]</a>. The purpose of these donations, even as little as one dollar, is to further their knowledge to give back online, rather than for personal gain like buying clothes or computers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:49">[00:05:49]</a>.

## Distribution and Conformity

Some content aims for [[nonconformity_in_media_production | nonconformity]] and does not adhere to traditional broadcasting regulations, meaning it will likely never be shown on television <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:27">[00:01:27]</a>. The goal is to retain the ability to release episodes freely, whenever convenient <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:36">[00:01:36]</a>. The future of content distribution is seen through platforms like IPTV <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:53">[00:03:53]</a>.

The process of obtaining an [[Estonias digital ID system | Estonian e-residency digital ID card]] is described as super straightforward and easy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:18">[00:01:18]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:59">[00:03:59]</a>. ## Collection Process at the Embassy

After applying, there is typically a waiting period of about a month for the card details to be ready <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:02">[00:04:02]</a>. The card must be collected in person at an Estonian embassy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:08">[00:00:08]</a>.

### London Embassy Experience

The experience at the London embassy was quick and efficient <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:18">[00:01:18]</a>.
*   **Operating Hours:** The embassy in London is open from 9:00 AM on Mondays, Wednesdays, and Fridays, but only from 1:00 PM on Tuesdays <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:29">[00:00:29]</a>.
*   **Appointment/Wait Time:** The process was very quick, taking less than 5-10 minutes to obtain the card <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:18">[00:01:18]</a>. Upon arrival, there were only three people waiting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:22">[00:01:22]</a>, and the overall time spent inside was about five minutes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:26">[00:01:26]</a>.
*   **Required Steps:** During collection, individuals need to present their passport and have both index fingers scanned for fingerprints <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:26">[00:01:26]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:13">[00:04:13]</a>.

## Cost of the Card

The cost for the [[Estonias digital ID system | e-residency digital ID card]] was €50 if obtained before February 1st, after which the price increased to €100 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:18">[00:02:18]</a>.

## Contents of the E-Residency Pack

Upon successful collection, the applicant receives a pack containing several items crucial for [[Instructions for setting up and using the digital ID card | using the card]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:38">[00:01:38]</a>:
*   **The E-Residency Card:** The [[Estonias digital ID system | digital ID card]] itself, which features RFID technology <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:40">[00:01:40]</a>. It is designed with very strong 2048-bit encryption <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:04">[00:01:04]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:30">[00:03:30]</a>.
*   **Instructions:** A guide detailing how to [[Instructions for setting up and using the digital ID card | use the ID card]] with the reader, including sliding the card into a plus shape and ensuring contact between the chip and the reader <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:50">[00:01:50]</a>. It directs users to e-estonia.ee/welcome <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:56">[00:01:56]</a>.
*   **PIN Codes:** A rippable sheet containing necessary PIN codes presumed for authenticating the card upon signup <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:02">[00:02:02]</a>.
*   **Conditions for Use:** A sheet outlining the conditions for using certificates issued for identity cards, residence permit cards, and [[Estonias digital ID system | digital ID cards]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:10">[00:02:10]</a>.
*   **Card Reader:** A small, foldable black device made by Webbrick, which functions as a card reader <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:27">[00:02:27]</a>. This reader connects to a computer via USB <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:44">[00:02:44]</a>. The card is inserted into the reader, making contact with internal points on the back, and then the reader plugs into a USB port <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:06">[00:03:06]</a>.

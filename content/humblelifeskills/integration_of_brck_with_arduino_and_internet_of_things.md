The [[features_of_brck_router | BRCK]] is described as a rugged, cloud-managed, fully-featured router designed for reliable internet connectivity in various environments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:52:00">[01:52:00]</a>. Its design philosophy, "if it works in Kenya, it'll work anywhere," emphasizes its robustness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:00">[01:08:00]</a>.

### Arduino Compatibility

A core feature of the [[features_of_brck_router | BRCK]] is its compatibility with Arduino <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:40:00">[01:40:00]</a>. This means the device is designed to interact seamlessly with Arduino-based projects and hardware.

### Programmable GPIO for IoT Expansion

The [[features_of_brck_router | BRCK]] includes a programmable General Purpose Input/Output (GPIO) expansion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:58:00">[01:58:00]</a>. Specifically, its GPIO breakout provides 20 pins that support digital and analog read/write functionalities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:20:00">[02:20:00]</a>. Additionally, it supports standard communication protocols such as I2C, SPI, and UART (which reminds the speaker of 232 serial interfaces) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:24:00">[02:24:00]</a>.

This combination of an Arduino profile and comprehensive GPIO capabilities makes the [[features_of_brck_router | BRCK]] ideal for quickly connecting hardware to the Internet of Things (IoT) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:31:00">[02:31:00]</a>.

### Connectivity and Storage for IoT Applications

The [[features_of_brck_router | BRCK]] offers diverse [[device_connectivity_options | connectivity options]] essential for IoT deployments. It can connect to the internet via RG45, GSM, Wi-Fi Bridge, and Ethernet over USB through its GPIO breakout <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:04:00">[02:04:00]</a>. It also features an auxiliary USB port allowing external storage to be plugged in, which is beneficial for remote management scenarios, such as deploying the device in a client space with an attached hard drive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:39:00">[04:39:00]</a>.

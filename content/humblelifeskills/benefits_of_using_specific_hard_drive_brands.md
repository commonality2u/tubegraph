When selecting hard drives, particular brands and types can offer significant advantages, especially for data-intensive tasks like managing [[highdefinition_video_storage_challenges | HD files]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:45">[01:45]</a>.

## Issues with External Hard Drives
The speaker expresses a personal dislike for [[comparing_internal_and_external_hard_drives | external hard drives]] due to negative experiences, including two failures within 12 months <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:07">[02:07]</a>. Potential issues cited include overheating and a perceived lower quality in the drives themselves <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:12">[02:12]</a>. This perspective contrasts with Apple's push for its Time Capsule, which is considered a [[advantages_of_enterprise_class_servers | server grade hard drive]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:19">[02:19]</a>.

## Preference for Western Digital Caviar
For internal storage, the speaker favors Western Digital Caviar drives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:24">[02:24]</a>. A 500 GB SATA model is noted for being "super super fast" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:30">[02:30]</a>. The speaker already uses two 500 GB drives, totaling 1 TB, which are nearly full from [[highdefinition_video_storage_challenges | HD files]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:42">[02:42]</a>.

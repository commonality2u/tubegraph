## Estonia's Digital Innovation
Estonia is recognized for being a country confident in its technology, willing to allow individuals from other nations to utilize their digital ID system <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:37">[00:00:37]</a>. This system is already used by Estonian citizens for various purposes, including public transport tracking, encryption, and documents <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:54">[00:00:54]</a>. The nation's initiative to launch the world's first global digital ID demonstrates a strategic "power move" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:00">[00:03:00]</a>, providing a significant first-mover advantage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:05">[00:03:05]</a>. The system features strong security, such as 248-bit encryption, enabling users to digitally sign documents <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:40">[00:02:40]</a>.

## Implications for Countries
Estonia's [[what_is_eresidency | e-residency]] program presents a unique approach to retaining or attracting economic activity from its talent pool. The speaker considers it a "really smart power move" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:47">[00:01:47]</a>. ## Limitations of E-residency
It is important to note that [[what_is_eresidency | e-residency]] does not confer physical residency or citizenship <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:21">[00:02:21]</a>. E-residents cannot vote or engage in other activities typically associated with being a resident <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:23">[00:02:23]</a>. It functions primarily as a "glorified digital ID" or "digital pass" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:27">[00:02:27]</a>.

## Content Characteristics

### Publishing Frequency and Titles
Seven Second Riddles uploads content frequently, sometimes posting up to five times a day, which is described as absurd <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:56">[00:00:56]</a>. Video titles often make sensational claims, such as boosting IQ, or stating that only "geniuses" or the "top 1%" can solve them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>. These titles are often "clickbait," featuring "completely unrelated titles" like "only the x-men can solve this thing" or "only people with good relationships with their fathers can pass this quiz" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:10">[00:01:10]</a>. One title example is "sixteen brain teasers that'll turn your brain on" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:28">[00:02:28]</a>.

### Thumbnails and Thematic Content
Thumbnails typically depict cartoon scenes that pose "salacious questions" such as "who's telling the truth" or "who killed the math teacher" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:25">[00:01:25]</a>.

The riddles themselves are generally dark, frequently revolving around themes of crime, death, lying, cheating, and even poverty <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:41">[00:01:41]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:49">[00:01:49]</a>. ## Critique of Content Strategy

### Sensationalism and Psychological Impact
The primary critique of [[seven_second_riddles_channel_review | Seven Second Riddles]] is its hyper-sensationalist content, a problem shared with other channels like Facts Verse and Bright Side <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:47">[00:12:47]</a>. This sensationalism is considered harmful to young people due to the [[storytelling_and_narrative_styles_in_viral_content | availability heuristic]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:58">[00:12:58]</a>.

The [[storytelling_and_narrative_styles_in_viral_content | availability heuristic]] suggests that people perceive things as more likely if they have more examples of them readily available, such as more news examples of plane crashes versus car crashes leading to a greater fear of flying <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:06">[00:13:06]</a>. When combined with the [[storytelling_and_narrative_styles_in_viral_content | negativity bias]] (humans' attraction to unpleasant things) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:28">[00:13:28]</a> and the YouTube algorithm, this content can distort people's sense of reality, making the world seem bleaker than it truly is <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:37">[00:13:37]</a>.

### Target Audience Concerns
The use of cartoons in Seven Second Riddles makes the negative content even more appealing to a younger demographic, exacerbating the potential harm <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:48">[00:14:48]</a>. The channel's focus on themes like murder and negative events is seen as exploitative <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:18">[00:14:18]</a>. This approach can lead viewers to be overly afraid of bad things happening, even though such events are statistically unlikely compared to the portrayal on the channel <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:26">[00:14:26]</a>.

## Critiques of the Paternity Test Impact

Despite the dramatic reveals, the show's approach to paternity tests is criticized for not promoting genuine conflict resolution or responsibility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:30">[00:27:30]</a>. The show often assumes that a positive paternity test result will automatically lead to a father taking responsibility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:14">[00:05:14]</a>, but this is often not the case in reality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:21">[00:05:21]</a>. For example, even after Greg is revealed to be Miranda's father, there's no indication of a resolved relationship or acceptance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:50">[00:26:50]</a>.

The show's emphasis on public spectacle for personal matters is seen as exploitative <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:12">[00:01:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:09">[00:09:09]</a>. The belief that a reveal on television will cause a complete and immediate change in someone's behavior, transforming them into a responsible parent, is viewed as unrealistic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:00">[00:26:00]</a>.

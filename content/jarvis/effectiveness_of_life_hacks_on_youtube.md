A YouTube research session moderated by Mr. YouTube investigated how [[life_hacks_and_pranks_in_youtube_videos | life hacks on YouTube]] have improved viewers' lives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:37">[00:00:37]</a>. The responses from participants highlighted a range of outcomes, from the absurd to the dangerous, largely questioning the practical effectiveness and safety of many such hacks.

## Questionable Practicality

Several participants described [[life_hacks_and_their_practicality | life hacks]] with dubious real-world value:
*   One participant learned to put peanut butter in their hair, without asking follow-up questions about the purpose or outcome <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:44">[00:00:44]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:49">[00:00:49]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:53">[00:00:53]</a>.
*   Another claimed to know if food is fake or real thanks to life hacks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:01">[00:01:01]</a>.
*   [[five_minute_crafts_life_hacks_critique | Five Minute Crafts]] was mentioned for showing how to make a new hand out of soap after a lighter hack went wrong, which the participant rationalized as a benefit because they now always have soap when shaking hands <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:12">[00:01:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:26">[00:01:26]</a>.

## Dangerous and Unrealistic Outcomes

The discussion quickly revealed that [[criticism_of_unrealistic_and_staged_life_hacks | life hacks]] can lead to severe consequences:
*   A participant recounted blowing off their hand due to "complications in a lighter hack" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:07">[00:01:07]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:10">[00:01:10]</a>. This points to the potentially dangerous nature of some [[life_hacks_and_diy_culture | DIY projects]] promoted as life hacks.
*   A "Future Jarvis" described a dystopian future where everyone is armed with an empty coke bottle and a boxcutter <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:45">[00:01:45]</a>, or a glue gun <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:53">[00:01:53]</a>. While presented humorously, this reflects a perception of how widespread and nonsensical life hack culture could become.

He specifically refers to a video where a woman spills coffee, suggesting that the absurd or problematic nature of their content continues <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:58">[00:02:58]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:01">[00:03:01]</a>. This overall commentary points towards a prevailing sentiment that many online life hacks lack genuine usefulness and can even be detrimental or simply nonsensical, often leaning into [[practicality_and_humor_in_diy_life_hacks | humor]] or [[humorous_consequences_of_following_life_hacks | absurdity]] rather than practical effectiveness.

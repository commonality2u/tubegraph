## The Motive: Money, Views, and Virality
The primary motivation for creating this type of content is explicitly stated as [[monetization_and_sponsorship_in_controversial_content | money]] and [[the_use_of_extreme_content_for_social_media_engagement | outrage views]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:27:00">[09:27:00]</a>. The content is fabricated for [[impact_online_fame_and_popularity_content_creation | virality and clout]], rather than any genuine purpose or fetish <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:17:00">[09:17:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:44:00">[09:44:00]</a>. Anna, who has a long-standing presence on Facebook, has adapted to [[social_media_platforms_and_content_imitation | TikTok]] and is "crushing it" by gaining widespread recognition as "the toilet lady" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:09:00">[17:09:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:14:00">[17:14:00]</a>. ### Consequences
While effective for [[monetization_and_sponsorship_in_controversial_content | monetization]], this type of content has negative consequences:
*   **Food Waste:** Many of the "food hacks" involve significant waste of edible items <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:25:00">[22:25:00]</a>.
*   **Perpetuating Harmful Ideas:** Prank videos, like the Tinder date example, can perpetuate "weird ideas about beauty and sex" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:36:00">[22:36:00]</a>.
*   **Lack of Authenticity:** The content is entirely staged, with creators not actually performing these actions repeatedly in real life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:07:00">[10:07:00]</a>.

Ultimately, the focus is on generating maximum reaction for commercial gain, often at the expense of common sense, hygiene, and ethical considerations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:27:00">[09:27:00]</a>.

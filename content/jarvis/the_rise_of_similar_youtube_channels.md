*   **Voice Acting** Stories are voiced by fluent English speakers who appear to be reading scripts written by individuals for whom English is not their first language <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>.
*   **Claimed Authenticity** Channels present these stories as true <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:16">[00:01:16]</a>.
*   **Topic Range** Content spans from "horrible things" and "rare things" to a combination of both <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:31">[00:01:31]</a>. Examples include stories about being left in a coffin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:34">[00:01:34]</a>, becoming rich as a teenager <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:42">[00:01:42]</a>, or having a "one-in-a-billion disease" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:52">[00:01:52]</a>.
*   **Plagiarism and Inaccuracies** A significant portion of these stories are plagiarized from popular media, such as Reddit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:56">[00:01:56]</a>.

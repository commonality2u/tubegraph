### University Computer Science Degree Overview
A university degree in Computer Science (CS) typically covers a broad range of topics, including core programming, mathematics, general education, and specialized CS subjects <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:17">00:00:17</a>.

For instance, a typical CS degree from an institution like Georgia Tech includes:

*   **Introductory Programming**
    *   Begins with foundational programming concepts, often in languages like Python, incorporating practical projects like controlling robots or web scraping <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:32">00:01:32</a>.
    *   Followed by [[computer_science_degree_curriculum|object-oriented programming]], commonly taught using languages like Java <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:31">00:03:31</a>.
*   **Mathematics**
    *   Multiple levels of calculus (Calculus I, II, III) are standard requirements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:11">00:02:11</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:14">00:05:14</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:49">00:07:49</a>.
    *   Includes [[computer_science_degree_curriculum|discrete mathematics]] (e.g., "Constructing Proofs") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:14">00:03:14</a>.
    *   Applied combinatorics, which blends algorithms and probability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:00">00:09:00</a>.
    *   Statistics and applications covering probability and statistics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:26">00:10:26</a>.
*   **Core Computer Science**
    *   **Data Structures and Algorithms:** Considered a fundamental course, often serving as a basis for technical interviews <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:42">00:05:42</a>. Advanced algorithms courses are also common <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:14">00:08:14</a>.
    *   **Computer Organization and Programming:** Explores how computers function, from digital logic to assembly language and C programming, often involving projects like making Game Boy Advance games <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:50">00:05:50</a>.
    *   **Database Systems:** Dedicated courses on modeling, structuring, storing, and retrieving data efficiently <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:21">00:06:21</a>. Advanced topics may include how large companies solve data problems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:25">00:09:25</a>.
    *   **Systems and Networks:** Covers low-level programming and how computers communicate, including designing simple processors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:50">00:06:50</a>, and dedicated courses on computer networking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:11">00:08:11</a>.
    *   **Objects in Design:** Focuses on design patterns for structuring and organizing code in large applications or multi-person projects <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:02">00:07:02</a>.
    *   **Languages and Computation:** Teaches about compilers and theoretical concepts like finite state automata <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:16">00:11:16</a>.
*   **Specialized CS & Electives**
    *   **Artificial Intelligence:** Introduces how computers can be taught to perform intelligent tasks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:20">00:08:20</a>.
    *   **Machine Learning:** Delves into how computers learn <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:34">00:09:34</a>.
    *   **Computer Vision:** Explores how computers "see" and process visual information, with projects like tracking faces in video footage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:17">00:10:17</a>.
    *   **Computational Photography:** Teaches how software like Photoshop works and principles behind features like Snapchat filters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:25">00:11:25</a>.
*   **General Education & Life Skills**
    *   **Lab Sciences:** Typically requires a sequence of science courses, such as Chemistry or Physics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:51">00:02:51</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:55">00:09:55</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:35">00:10:35</a>.
    *   **Humanities:** Courses like English, focusing on critical reading and writing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:37">00:03:37</a>.
    *   **Foreign Language:** Often a multi-semester requirement, such as Japanese or Spanish <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:44">00:03:44</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:37">00:07:37</a>.
    *   **Social Sciences:** Examples include Social Psychology or Cognitive Science <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:34">00:06:34</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:56">00:08:56</a>.
    *   **Ethics:** A crucial component, discussing real-world ethical dilemmas in technology, such as liability in self-driving car accidents or patent law <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:41">00:11:41</a>.
    *   **Freshman Seminar:** Guides students on career paths, resume building, and career fair preparation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:35">00:02:35</a>.
    *   **Health:** A general education course on healthy living <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:16">00:10:16</a>.
*   **Practical Experience**
    *   Universities often integrate or encourage practical experience through teaching assistant roles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:35">00:03:35</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:46">00:05:46</a>, and internships at tech companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:44">00:08:44</a>.
    *   Senior design projects provide an opportunity to apply learned skills to develop applications <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:25">00:08:25</a>.

## Early Celebrity Missteps
Several celebrities exhibited [[tonedeafness_of_celebrities_during_crises | tone-deafness]] in their early responses to the pandemic:
*   **Tom Brady** – The football star relocated from his New England mansion to a Florida mansion for a new job, but found his new residence problematic due to its proximity to the beach, allowing him to see "people from his backyard" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:42">[00:01:42]</a>.
*   **Justin Bieber** – During an Instagram Live with Kendall Jenner, Bieber stated he couldn't feel "too bad" for his wealth because he "worked for what he has" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:08">[00:02:08]</a>. He later apologized after public backlash, though billionaire Sean "Puff Daddy" Combs reportedly assured him that "the Lord speaks through him" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:33">[00:02:33]</a>.
*   **Vanessa Hudgens** – The actress lamented being unable to attend Coachella that year, prompting a sense of irony given the global crisis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:55">[00:02:55]</a>.

## Celebrity Responses to Quarantine

The pandemic offered a unique [[impact_of_social_media_on_public_perception_of_success | perspective on fame]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:25">[00:03:25]</a>. Celebrity coping mechanisms generally fell into two categories: those who wanted to work but couldn't, and those who attempted to work from home <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:36">[00:03:36]</a>.

### Celebrities Who Would Rather Be Working But Can't
This category often involved [[celebrities_trying_to_entertain_from_home | celebrities trying to entertain from home]] through social media.
*   **Gal Gadot's "Imagine" Video** – Gadot orchestrated a video featuring numerous famous friends singing John Lennon's "Imagine" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:45">[00:04:45]</a>. The video was criticized for its [[tonedeafness_of_celebrities_during_crises | tone-deafness]]—both musically and socially <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:01">[00:08:01]</a>. It was filmed on "day 6" of quarantine, prompting criticism about the premature philosophical reflections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:13">[00:05:13]</a>. The song "Imagine" was deemed inappropriate given the context <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:51">[00:05:51]</a>. The video was contrasted with an Italian man playing the trumpet on his balcony, whose performance was seen as "powerful and pure" because it was for his community, not for attention, and he was an "excellent trumpet player" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:12">[00:06:12]</a>.
*   **Kristin Chenoweth's TikToks** – The singer and actress repeatedly used a single high-note joke involving cheese on a boyfriend's pasta <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:51">[00:08:51]</a>.
*   **Sophie Turner and Joe Jonas** – This couple posted TikToks of their completed Harry Potter Lego sets without additional context <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:51">[00:09:51]</a>. They also shared cooking videos on Instagram Live <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:09">[00:10:09]</a>.
*   **Other Examples** – Jack Black performed a dance, Jake Gyllenhaal removed his shirt, and Matthew McConaughey attempted to reassure the public <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:28">[00:10:28]</a>.

### Celebrities Trying to Work From Home
Television production continued during the pandemic, benefiting many non-famous workers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:48">[00:10:48]</a>. However, the quality of these remote productions was mixed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:41">[00:04:41]</a>.

*   **Ellen DeGeneres** – Ellen was widely criticized for comparing lockdown to "being in jail" from her mansion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:17">[00:11:17]</a>. Reports also emerged that her employees were concerned about their pay while she hired an outside company to film her show <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:27">[00:11:27]</a>.
*   **Late Night Comedy** – The hosts of late-night shows, often called "the Jimmies," adapted their formats:
    *   **John Oliver** – Filmed from what appeared to be the afterlife, Oliver remained "present and engaging" despite the lack of a studio audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:23">[00:12:23]</a>.
    *   **Seth Meyers** – Recorded his "A Closer Look" segments in an "echoey room," with production quality noted as inferior to some online content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:41">[00:12:41]</a>.
    *   **Trevor Noah** – Used a "fun YouTube editing style" and good camera quality, often featuring other comedians <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:11">[00:13:11]</a>.
    *   **Jimmy Fallon** – His "at home edition" of *The Tonight Show* featured his young daughters who appeared "miserable" and "used for content" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:00">[00:15:00]</a>. The show's monologues were described as low-energy and poorly edited, with jokes often falling flat <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:04">[00:16:04]</a>. The overall impression was that Fallon "doesn't want to be here" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:49">[00:16:49]</a>.

### Positive Examples
Some celebrities successfully adapted to the new reality:
*   **John Krasinski** – Praised for his videos <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:16">[00:21:16]</a>.
*   **Kumail Nanjiani and Emily V. Gordon** – Their podcast, *Stayin' In*, was noted as a comforting presence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:20">[00:21:20]</a>.
*   **Cardi B** – Described as "going off right now" for her viral commentary <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:30">[00:21:30]</a>.

## Conclusion

The pandemic offered a "humanizing view of celebrity" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:16">[00:22:16]</a>. It highlighted that while celebrities are often placed on a pedestal, they are "just like us" in their fallibility, especially when stripped of their usual production teams and support <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:10">[00:22:10]</a>. This contrasts with internet creators who are already adept at self-production <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:02">[00:22:02]</a>.

## Superpowers as a Narrative Device

In Z-Void videos, nearly everyone possesses magical abilities, including "random people who sit on park benches" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:31:73">[01:31:73]</a>. This element introduces a fantastical layer to otherwise mundane, moral-driven stories <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:25:00">[01:25:00]</a>.

## Case Study: "Kid Found Out He Can Freeze Time"

A prime example of Z-Void Studios' approach is the video titled "kid found out he can freeze time what happens next will shock you" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:58:91">[01:58:91]</a>. The title itself employs a [[sensationalism_in_digital_storytelling | sensationalism in digital storytelling]] strategy, using a clickbait phrase to entice viewers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:08:43">[02:08:43]</a>.

### Integration of Powers
The story follows a child who gains the power to freeze people while his parents are arguing about their divorce <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:16:30">[03:16:30]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:23:80">[08:23:80]</a>. The boy wishes for "superpowers" to "stop all this" fighting, which literally manifests as the ability to freeze time or people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:28:44">[05:28:44]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:37:37">[05:37:37]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:01:06">[06:01:06]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:13:00">[09:13:00]</a>.

### Ramifications and Realism
Upon freezing his parents, the protagonist experiences "emotional Whiplash," feeling both powerful and stuck in a "predicament" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:20:20">[10:20:20]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:32:51">[10:32:51]</a>. His friend, who is unaffected by the time freeze, raises practical questions about the consequences of his actions:
*   "Who's gonna sign the school stuff for you?" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:10:97">[14:10:97]</a>
*   "Who's gonna prepare meals for you?" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:17:85">[14:17:85]</a>
*   "Who's gonna give you hugs when you need them?" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:23:44">[14:23:44]</a>

These questions highlight the disruption superpowers can cause to everyday life, even when intended for positive change. The friend also suggests that freezing problems away doesn't solve them and that the protagonist needs to "face the fact that your parents are getting a divorce" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:35:90">[15:35:90]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:40:40">[15:40:40]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:42:36">[15:42:36]</a>.

## Superpowers vs. Traditional Drama
The inclusion of superpowers in Z-Void Studios' content is seen as an improvement over Darman's typical approach, which often focuses on [[problems_with_selfcongratulatory_storytelling | self-congratulatory storytelling]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:59:16">[18:59:16]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:14:48">[19:14:48]</a>. The fantastical element of superpowers means the stories "don't play on any weird existing like traumas in the world" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:28:44">[19:28:44]</a>. Instead of solely depicting a "divorce story," it becomes a "divorce story but with with time stopping," making it "cool" and less reliant on real-world emotional manipulation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:34:55">[19:34:55]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:39:13">[19:39:13]</a>. This suggests that the addition of an unrealistic element can temper the [[sensationalism_in_animated_stories | sensationalism in animated stories]] or [[impact_of_fabricated_stories_on_audience_perceptions | impact of fabricated stories on audience perceptions]] by shifting the narrative focus from pure drama to imaginative problem-solving.

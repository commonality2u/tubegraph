He later apologized, and billionaire Puff Daddy "came to Justin's rescue" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:29">[00:02:29]</a>.
*   **Vanessa Hudgens** lamented not being able to attend Coachella <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:55">[00:02:55]</a>. She later posted TikToks dancing to "We're All In This Together" with fellow *High School Musical* alumni <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:10">[00:04:10]</a>.

### The "Imagine" Video and Its Backlash
One prominent example of celebrity quarantine content was **Gal Gadot's** "Imagine" video, posted around "150 years ago" in "time" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:45">[00:04:45]</a>. Gadot called on famous friends to sing John Lennon's song *Imagine*, resulting in a "bad" video that received significant backlash for being "tone-deaf both musically and socially" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:56">[00:04:56]</a>.

The critique highlighted several points comparing it unfavorably to an Italian man playing the trumpet from his balcony:
*   The Italian trumpet player was "excellent" and played for his community, not for attention, making it "inspirational" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:17">[00:08:17]</a>. Most celebrities in the video were not musical <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:23">[00:08:23]</a>.
*   The video lacked genuine emotion, with the celebrities seeming "philosophical" only "six days" into quarantine <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:13">[00:05:13]</a>.
*   The song *Imagine* didn't truly apply to the situation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:51">[00:05:51]</a>.
*   The introduction by Gal Gadot was seen as contrived, despite coordinating "dozens of the most famous celebrities on the planet" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:48">[00:07:48]</a>.

## Traditional Television Adapting to Home Production
Television production continued during the pandemic, which was commendable as it provided jobs for many non-famous individuals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:48">[00:10:48]</a>. However, the quality varied:
*   **Ellen DeGeneres** sparked controversy by comparing lockdown to being in jail from her mansion and later faced reports of employee dissatisfaction regarding pay <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:11">[00:11:11]</a>. Her show was considered "really boring" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:38">[00:11:38]</a>.
*   **John Oliver's** *Last Week Tonight* adapted well from what appeared to be the afterlife, maintaining an engaging presence despite the lack of live audience laughter <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:21">[00:12:21]</a>.
*   **Seth Meyers** recorded his *A Closer Look* segments from a very "echoey room," revealing that "no one on TV has a good camera" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:41">[00:12:41]</a>.
*   **Trevor Noah** of *The Daily Show* had a good camera and adopted "more of a fun YouTube editing style" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:11">[00:13:11]</a>.

## Internet Creators vs. Traditional Media
The challenges faced by traditional media figures provided an interesting comparison to internet creators:
*   **Self-Production Skills:** Internet creators are naturally adept at self-production, having developed these skills over time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:02">[00:22:02]</a>. Traditional media, on the other hand, struggled to recreate their studio magic remotely with no crew or equipment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:25">[00:04:25]</a>.
*   **Humanizing View of Celebrity:** The pandemic provided a rare "humanizing view of celebrity," stripping away the "glitz and the glam" that often obscures their "innate human fallibility" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:15">[00:22:15]</a>. This revealed that "celebrities are just like us... kind of" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:30">[00:22:30]</a>.
*   **Challenges of Solo Content Creation:** While some celebrities, like John Krasinski, Kumail Nanjiani, Emily V. Gordon, and Cardi B, successfully adapted <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:16">[00:21:16]</a>, many "are just not good at doing that alone" and might need help from other creatives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:41">[00:21:41]</a>. This contrasts with the inherent self-reliance of internet creators.

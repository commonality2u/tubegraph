YouTube channels, particularly those like Five Minute Crafts, frequently present "life hacks" that are criticized for being unrealistic, impractical, dangerous, or overtly staged <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:01">[00:01:01]</a>. These videos often feature ridiculous clickbait thumbnails and range in usefulness from mildly helpful to outright hazardous <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:47">[00:00:47]</a>. The consistent format, typically compilations of short, square clips less than a minute long, suggests a "content farm" model designed to generate revenue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:06">[00:01:06]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:21">[00:01:21]</a>. The channels also engage in recycling hacks across multiple videos to maintain high posting frequency, sometimes as many as three 20-minute videos a day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:18">[00:08:18]</a>.

Many of these presented "hacks" are critiqued for their lack of practicality or the absurdity of the problems they aim to solve:

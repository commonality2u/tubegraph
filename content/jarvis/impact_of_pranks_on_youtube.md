## Evolution of Prank Content

Initially, Morgz's content was seen as merely obnoxious behavior from a teenager <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:06">[00:02:06]</a>. However, as he matured, his videos became more problematic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:17">[00:02:17]</a>. An example of this shift is a video titled "THE BEST SCARE PRANK ON FAMILY!", which featured a "horrifying" thumbnail depicting Morgz as if deceased in a bathtub filled with a red, presumably blood-like, fluid <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:28">[00:02:28]</a>. YouTube subsequently age-restricted this video <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:05">[00:03:05]</a>.

## Content and Staging of Pranks

Prank videos on YouTube, particularly those involving elaborate setups, often lack realism and are clearly staged. In the "scare prank" video, Morgz announces his intent to "trash the entire house" and make it seem like a break-in, setting up hidden cameras <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:46">[00:03:46]</a>. The pranks themselves are designed for children's humor, featuring "cartoonish jokes" and slapstick elements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:54">[00:05:54]</a>. However, the attempts at creating a "mess" during the staged burglary are often unconvincing, and characters scream loudly, which is unrealistic for burglars <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:37">[00:06:37]</a>. The opulence of the pranksters' home, with casual displays of wealth and personal branding, is also noted <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:13">[00:08:13]</a>.

[[the_ethics_of_staging_prank_videos | The Ethics of Staging Prank Videos]] are further questioned by clear indications of staging, such as well-lit "hidden" cameras, some of which appear to be DSLRs on tripods or even moving during the prank <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:46">[00:15:46]</a>.

## Harmful Elements and Audience Impact

Despite attempts at humor, the content can become "gruesome" and "sensationalist" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:43">[00:12:43]</a>. The fake death scenario, with "fake blood," is intended to make his family "nervous" and "upset" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:37">[00:11:37]</a>. The severe emotional distress inflicted on his mother, who screams upon discovering him, is highlighted <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:12">[00:19:12]</a>. This blend of cartoonish humor and graphic reality is described as triggering and problematic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:32">[00:19:32]</a>.

Furthermore, a direct command to subscribe, implying that "someone will break into your house" if not subscribed, is seen as exploiting the fear of young children without a follow-up "just kidding" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:26">[00:14:26]</a>.

The presenter explicitly states that the lack of clear disclaimers for a young and impressionable audience is insufficient <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:21">[00:21:21]</a>.

## Five-Minute Crafts: A Deep Dive into DIY Content

Jarvis, a YouTube commentator, initially labeled Five-Minute Crafts as "the worst channel on YouTube" but later reconsidered this stance, humorously suggesting it's "actually a great channel with lots of fun crafts for the whole family" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:40">[00:00:40]</a>. This pivot was part of a humorous segment involving multiple mask reveals, including impressions of Tony Stark, Barack Obama, and Batman <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:01">[00:01:01]</a>.

Six months after his initial [[critique_of_5minute_crafts_life_hacks | critique]], Jarvis revisited Five-Minute Crafts to try out some of their featured [[effectiveness_of_life_hacks_on_youtube | life hacks]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:09">[00:03:09]</a>. He noted that Five-Minute Crafts had grown significantly, from 16 million subscribers six months prior to nearly 40 million subscribers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:43">[00:02:43]</a>.

### Characteristics of Five-Minute Crafts Videos

Five-Minute Crafts is described as a [[life_hacks_and_diy_projects_in_fabiosa_videos | life hacks and DIY channel]] that posts around three 20-minute videos every day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:18">[00:03:18]</a>. Key characteristics include:
*   **Clickbait**: Videos often feature titles that have nothing to do with their content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:29">[00:03:29]</a>. For example, a video titled "weird hacks that are actually genius" had a plot summary from *Orange is the New Black* in its description <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:45">[00:03:45]</a>.
*   **Impracticality**: The [[life_hacks_and_their_practicality | life hacks]] themselves are often not very useful <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:35">[00:03:35]</a>.
*   **Duplicate Content**: The channel is known for uploading a lot of duplicate content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:57">[00:02:57]</a>.

## Critiquing Specific Life Hacks

Jarvis tested several [[five_minute_crafts_life_hacks_critique | Five Minute Crafts life hacks]], often finding them [[bizarre_and_impractical_diy_trends | bizarre and impractical]], or even unsafe.

### Food-Related Hacks

*   **Ice Cream Drip Solution**: A hack suggested putting a cupcake paper around an ice cream cone to catch drips <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:16">[00:04:16]</a>. Jarvis noted this doesn't solve melting through the hole and questioned who has cupcake papers "lying around" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:26">[00:04:26]</a>.
*   **Hot Scoop for Hard Ice Cream**: Another hack involved heating an ice cream scoop to make it easier to scoop solid ice cream <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:40">[00:05:40]</a>. Jarvis demonstrated that the scoop cools down immediately upon touching the ice cream <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:48">[00:05:48]</a>.
*   **Cutting Hot Dogs with Syringe Device**: A complex [[life_hacks_and_diy_projects_in_fabiosa_videos | DIY project]] involved cutting a syringe and gluing it to a soda bottle top to create a hand-washing device, then using it to cut a hot dog <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:30">[00:06:30]</a>. Jarvis pointed out that the user had a knife the entire time and the hack added "extra equipment to stuff that you could already do" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:17">[00:07:17]</a>.
*   **Pouring Liquids Without Spilling**: The channel showed pouring from a high angle, causing spills, then suggested a different pouring method <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:03">[00:09:03]</a>. Jarvis argued that the simpler solution is to "bring the milk closer to the bowl" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:29">[00:10:29]</a>.
*   **Breaking Apples with Bare Hands**: A hack demonstrated breaking an apple in half with bare hands <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:50">[00:10:50]</a>. Jarvis and his friends attempted it unsuccessfully, highlighting the immense strength required, concluding it's "insane" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:23">[00:11:23]</a>.
*   **Banana Peeling**: A peculiar method involved peeling a banana by hitting its top on a counter <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:58">[00:13:58]</a>. Jarvis tried it, slightly hurting his tooth, and questioned the "natural and normal way to eat a banana" it presented <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:12">[00:14:12]</a>.
*   **Onion/Tomato Cutting with Hair Pick**: A hack showed using a hair pick to stabilize an onion or tomato before cutting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:43">[00:14:43]</a>. Jarvis found it destroyed the tomato and made cutting more difficult <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:04">[00:15:04]</a>.

### Personal and Home Hacks

*   **Making a Prison Candle and Knife**: From a video titled "five tricks people have learned in prison," the channel showed how to make a candle and a knife <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:27">[00:07:27]</a>. Jarvis questioned the target audience, particularly if children are watching, given that it teaches "kids to make weapons" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:55">[00:07:55]</a>. This also raised concerns about [[safety_in_attempting_diy_life_hacks | safety]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:55">[00:07:55]</a>.
*   **Reversed Headphones**: A thumbnail showed wearing headphones with the left cup on the right ear and vice-versa <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:07">[00:08:07]</a>. Jarvis pointed out that headphones are labeled "left and right" for a reason, making this hack "so dumb" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:20">[00:08:20]</a>.
*   **Coffee Cup with Second Hole for Cooling**: A hack suggested poking a second hole in a coffee cup lid for "faster cooling" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:51">[00:12:51]</a>. Jarvis noted that removing the lid altogether would be faster, as putting the lid on already slowed cooling <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:59">[00:12:59]</a>.
*   **Emergency Spoon from Coke Bottle**: A hack demonstrated cutting an empty Coke bottle with an exacto knife to create an "emergency spoon" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:29">[00:16:29]</a>. Jarvis questioned the necessity of such a complex solution when one could just drink from the bowl <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:36">[00:16:36]</a>.
*   **Lubricating Shoes with Axe Body Spray**: To prevent shoe irritation, a hack showed spraying Axe body spray on the ankle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:54">[00:16:54]</a>. Jarvis's simple counter-suggestion was to "just wear socks" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:00">[00:17:00]</a>.
*   **Quick Shoe Tying**: A shoe was pre-configured to be tied "really quickly" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:04">[00:17:04]</a>. Jarvis observed that it was already "like, already tied" before the demonstration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:11">[00:17:11]</a>.
*   **No-Spill Coffee Lid from Pringles Top**: A [[practicality_and_humor_in_diy_life_hacks | humorous]] hack suggested using a Pringles lid to prevent coffee spills <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:31">[00:17:31]</a>. Jarvis demonstrated it didn't fit standard mugs and, even if it did, it would make the coffee undrinkable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:10">[00:18:10]</a>.

### Questionable and Potentially Illegal Hacks

*   **Hiding Beer Cans**: A hack involved disguising a beer can inside a comically large cup <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:53">[00:15:53]</a>. Jarvis questioned the intent, suggesting that if caught, the evidence of the can would still be present, and it's simpler to just pour the drink into a regular cup <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:58">[00:15:58]</a>. This raised questions about the channel's target audience and if they are "giving us ideas on how to break the law" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:43">[00:15:43]</a>.

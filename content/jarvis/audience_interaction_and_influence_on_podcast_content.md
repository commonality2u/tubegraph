## Mechanisms of Audience Interaction

Viewers engage with the "Whatever Podcast" primarily through live chat and monetary donations (superchats) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:30">[00:18:30]</a>.

### Chat Behavior
The live chat typically displays distinct patterns of support and negativity:
*   **Support for Men** When a man speaks, the chat often expresses approval with phrases like "w man" or "Big W Chad" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:00">[00:19:00]</a>.
*   **Negative Comments Towards Women** Conversely, when women speak, common derogatory terms include "dumb lady" and "bad lady," along with spamming "304," a code for "hoe" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:05">[00:19:05]</a>. This "304" code, derived from typing the numbers into a calculator and turning it upside down to spell "hoe," is described as a "universal code for incels" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:26">[00:19:26]</a>.

### Monetary Donations
Audience members frequently spend hundreds of dollars to send messages or directly interact with the panel during episodes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:51">[00:20:51]</a>. This system allows for:
*   **Interrupting the Flow** Donations often interrupt the conversation, allowing commenters to inject "the most evil shit" into the show <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:38">[00:20:38]</a>.
*   **Direct Engagement** Some viewers engage in "ongoing conversations" with the panel, spending hundreds of dollars to ask women guests to rate their appearance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:10">[00:21:10]</a>. One viewer even reached YouTube's $500 daily donation limit while begging for the girls to rate him <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:28">[00:21:28]</a>.

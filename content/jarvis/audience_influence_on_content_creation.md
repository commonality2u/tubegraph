Content creators on platforms like YouTube are significantly influenced by their audience, from the selection of topics to the eventual removal or restriction of videos. This dynamic is evident in the case of Morgz, a YouTuber whose content strategy appears to be heavily shaped by viewer engagement metrics and demographic considerations.

## Content Decisions Driven by Views
Initially, a newly posted video by Morgz was made private. The speculated reason for its removal was poor viewership, rather than the creator acknowledging any "error of his ways" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:10">[00:00:10]</a>, a conclusion drawn from the performance of his next upload <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:26">[00:00:26]</a>. This suggests that low engagement directly prompts content adjustments or takedowns.

This demographic influence is highlighted by his event, MorgzFest, where attendees over 18 were required to be "accompanied by a child" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:33">[00:01:33]</a>, featuring a "sea of children listening to Morgz Mum" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:36">[00:01:36]</a>.

The humor in Morgz's videos, such as "cartoonish jokes" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:08">[00:06:08]</a>, is explicitly designed for children <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:57">[00:05:57]</a>. ## Navigating Age Restrictions and Monetization
A recent video, "THE BEST SCARE PRANK ON FAMILY!", depicted Morgz in a "not alive" state in a bathtub of "famously red" fluid <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:45">[00:02:45]</a>. This graphic content led YouTube to age-restrict the video <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:05">[00:03:05]</a>.

As Morgz's primary audience consists of "young tots" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:13">[00:03:13]</a>, age restriction significantly impacts viewership and potential monetization. Despite this, such [[Influence of Viral Content and Clickbait | sensational content]] is chosen, possibly due to the perceived [[Influence of sensational content on viewer engagement | influence of sensational content on viewer engagement]] or the creator's pursuit of viral trends.

## Direct Engagement Tactics
Morgz frequently uses direct commands to his audience, such as "Watch to the end!" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:39">[00:03:39]</a> and "watch til' the end to find out!" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:41">[00:04:41]</a>. These tactics are designed to maximize watch time, especially from a young audience who might lack the "agency" to navigate away from the content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:52">[00:04:52]</a>.

He also employs coercive subscription prompts, threatening viewers that "someone will break into your house" if they don't subscribe <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:26">[00:14:26]</a>, which can lead "thousands of kids" to "scrambling to find the subscribe button for fear of their family's safety" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:40">[00:14:40]</a>. This exemplifies how a creator might exploit the [[Audience impact and reception of inspirational content | impressionability of their audience]] for metrics.

## The Impact of Content Choices on Viewers
The escalation of pranks, such as faking a gruesome scene, is considered problematic. While segments of Morgz's videos contain "slapstick humor" and "fun times" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:48">[00:21:48]</a>, the "gruesome" and "triggering" elements, especially with a blend of "cartoon and reality," can be "scarring" even for adults <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:28">[00:19:28]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:34">[00:21:34]</a>. The lack of disclaimers like "Don't try this at home" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:21">[00:21:21]</a> means that the "young and impressionable audience" is not adequately informed about what they have just witnessed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:25">[00:21:25]</a>.

In conclusion, the pursuit of views and engagement from a specific demographic heavily shapes the content produced, sometimes leading to sensational or potentially harmful material in the absence of self-regulation or sufficient platform oversight.

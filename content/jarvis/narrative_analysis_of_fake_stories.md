## Characteristics of Fake Animated Stories

### Claims of Authenticity vs. Reality
Despite claims of being "100% true" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:01">[01:10:01]</a>, the stories frequently exhibit characteristics that challenge their veracity. Narratives often feature extreme scenarios and improbable character reactions, leading to an overall lack of believability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:32:01">[04:32:01]</a>. This skepticism is shared by viewers, who question whether the stories are "really experience[d] or just making things up" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:44:01">[21:44:01]</a>.

### [[Storytelling and narrative techniques in Fabiosa videos | Narrative Techniques]] and Exaggeration
These stories often employ exaggerated plotlines and character traits to heighten drama. For example, in "I witnessed a cult at an abandoned house," the protagonist Rick, despite being 16, is depicted as having an "emotional intelligence" beyond his years when rejecting a romantic advance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:38:01">[04:38:01]</a>. Conversely, he makes "horrible thinking bad decision-making" by not telling his parents the truth about his disappearance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:50:01">[07:50:01]</a>. The story culminates in a dramatic misinterpretation of a "cult" meeting as a "weirdly therapeutic" ex-relationship burning ritual <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:46:01">[11:46:01]</a>.

Similarly, "My family disowned me because of my youtube channel" details parents with "super weird" and "ridiculous punishments" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:36:01">[14:36:01]</a>, such as closing the kitchen at 8 PM "as if it were a store" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:50:01">[14:50:01]</a>, forcing calls to relatives for bad grades to "apologize... for being a disgrace to the family" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:20:01">[18:20:01]</a>, and making children climb an "apple tree" for hours as punishment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:04:01">[19:04:01]</a>. The central conflict—being disowned for a YouTube channel discussing these punishments—is presented as an extreme reaction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:29:01">[25:29:01]</a>.

### Plot Inconsistencies and Character Portrayal
Stories frequently contain inconsistencies or unresolved plot points. In "I witnessed a cult at an abandoned house," the character Roger is described as being "very jealous that Kelly had a crush on me" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:58:01">[08:58:01]</a>, yet later, Kevin, Roger, and Kelly are depicted acting together without clear motivation or consistent character behavior <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:52:01">[04:52:01]</a>. A "creepy old gardener" is introduced but "never mentioned again" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:05:01">[12:05:01]</a>, and the mystery of who left a "voodoo doll" is never solved <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:58:01">[11:58:01]</a>.

The character portrayals often serve the dramatic arc rather than realistic psychological depth. For instance, Rick's ability to forgive his friends quickly after being locked in a basement is attributed to them being his "only friends" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:36:01">[08:36:01]</a>, which may not align with typical teenage reactions.

### Clickbait and Title Changes
Titles are frequently altered to be more sensational and "to get the click," regardless of their accuracy or relevance to the actual plot <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:58:01">[01:58:01]</a>. This contributes to the [[Criticism of fake animated stories on YouTube | criticism of fake animated stories on YouTube]] by prioritizing engagement over factual representation.

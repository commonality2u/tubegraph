### Keys to Virality (Crowdstrike Example)
Analyzing his Crowdstrike video, which garnered 6.6 million views on Instagram <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:21:00">[24:21:00]</a>, Yer breaks down its success:
*   **Trending Topic**: It covered a very relevant topic in tech and beyond (flight delays due to a software update) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:14:00">[21:14:14]</a>.
*   **Strong Hook**: Starting with him yelling erratically on the phone immediately grabbed attention <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:38:00">[21:38:00]</a>.
*   **Contextual Setting**: Filming in a swimming pool provided the context of a tech person working on vacation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:03:00">[22:03:00]</a>.
*   **Informational Aspect**: Including the news article in the video helped clarify the context and made the content feel more informational, encouraging follows <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:14:00">[23:14:00]</a>.
*   **Speed of Shipping**: He posted the video within hours of the event, dominating the trend by shipping fast rather than aiming for perfection <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:58:00">[23:58:00]</a>.

Yer prioritizes frequency over quality, especially for platforms like Instagram, aiming to post daily and jump on trends <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:52:00">[24:52:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:14:00">[25:14:00]</a>.

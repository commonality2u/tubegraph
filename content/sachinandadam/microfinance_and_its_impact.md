Kevin's personal drive to found Zarc stemmed from the chance to generate profit while simultaneously doing good, developing a country rather than just an individual <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:56:12">[04:56:12]</a>.

### Advantages of Traditional Microfinance

Traditional microfinance institutions (MFIs) have brought basic financial infrastructure to remote corners of countries, reaching villages often ignored by large commercial banks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:51:00">[05:51:00]</a>. A groundbreaking achievement of MFIs is their ability to underwrite individuals without traditional title assets, such as land or registered property <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:12:03">[06:12:03]</a>. They have developed methods to price risk and ensure loan performance by encouraging group guarantees, where individuals must be respected and trustworthy community members to access loans <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:33:04">[06:33:04]</a>.

### The Challenge of Poverty and Lack of Credit Access

A significant challenge in many developing countries is the prevalence of unbanked populations. Globally, 2.5 billion people lack access to traditional banking services and credit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:25:01">[14:25:01]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:23:01">[23:23:01]</a>. In Uganda, less than five percent of the population has a bank account <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:12:06">[12:12:06]</a>.

Kevin initially believed that people could save money even if they couldn't borrow <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:16:03">[15:16:03]</a>. However, his experience in Bangladesh, India, Ghana, and Uganda revealed that saving is often difficult due to cultural and structural reasons <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:42:02">[15:42:02]</a>.

*   **Cultural Barriers**: Many developing countries have communal societies with strong family networks, where individuals are expected to help each other <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:48:02">[15:48:02]</a>. For example, in Uganda, if a relative needs financial help, individuals are compelled to provide it, acting as a social safety net that can quickly deplete any savings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:05:07">[16:05:07]</a>. This contrasts with individualistic Western cultures where people might rely on credit cards or personal funds for financial problems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:57:02">[18:57:02]</a>.
*   **Structural Barriers**: Traditional banks are often inconvenient and unreliable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:53:07">[16:53:07]</a>. An anecdote illustrates this: Ugandan farmers preferred buying pigs to opening bank accounts because pigs were always accessible, appreciated in value, and could be easily liquidated within the village without incurring transportation costs or dealing with unpredictable bank hours and potential bribes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:35:01">[13:35:01]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:09:02">[14:09:02]</a>. This highlights that "pigs are more liquid than cash" in certain contexts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:09:02">[14:09:02]</a>.

Access to credit is crucial for escaping the poverty cycle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:02:00">[15:02:00]</a>. Without it, individuals cannot afford significant investments like homes in developed countries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:49:09">[14:49:09]</a>. In contexts like Uganda, where [[entrepreneurship_and_innovation_in_developing_countries | entrepreneurship and innovation in developing countries]] is high, lack of capital can prevent individuals from making initial investments to generate income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:08:00">[23:08:00]</a>.

## Zarc's Evolution and Business Model

Zarc was founded in June 2016 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:52:03">[20:52:03]</a>. Its initial business model involved providing cash loans for various business purposes, such as agriculture or working capital for retail shops <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:02:02">[21:02:02]</a>.

### The Pivot to Asset-Backed Lending

A key learning from Zarc's early years was that cash loans were "very fungible" in lower-middle-income households <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:17:02">[21:17:02]</a>. Business loans would often be diverted to address urgent personal needs like school fees or medical treatment, making repayment difficult <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:38:00">[21:38:00]</a>.

This led to a pivot towards in-kind or asset-backed lending, which proved more impactful and successful <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:45:00">[21:45:00]</a>. Zarc now primarily focuses on motorcycle loans for taxi (boda-boda) drivers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:58:00">[21:58:00]</a>.

This model is effective because:
*   **Shared Risk**: Drivers make a down payment on the motorcycle, giving them "skin in the game" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:12:00">[22:12:00]</a>.
*   **Productive Use**: Motorcycles are primarily used for income generation, unlike cash, which can be spent on non-productive purposes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:31:00">[22:31:00]</a>. A typical boda-boda driver can spend 50% of their income on motorcycle rent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:50:00">[17:50:00]</a>. Owning the asset is a game-changer, allowing drivers to build equity and break out of a "paper-thin margin" existence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:19:00">[18:19:00]</a>.
*   **Simplified Underwriting**: By focusing on a specific asset (motorcycles), Zarc can standardize its underwriting process, understand the industry deeply, and predict income generation more accurately <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:29:00">[28:29:00]</a>.
*   **Collateral Creation**: The loan itself creates collateral. Zarc installs GPS trackers in motorcycles and insures them, allowing them to remotely disable the bike if payments stop <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:43:00">[29:43:00]</a>. This provides security without requiring pre-existing assets from the borrower <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:47:00">[29:47:00]</a>.

This approach significantly reduces risk. Zarc boasts a 3% default rate on motorcycle loans in Africa, outperforming U.S. auto loan default rates (5.8%), despite weaker legal and credit infrastructure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="38:52:00">[38:52:00]</a>. This demonstrates how [[social_impact_and_innovation_in_emerging_technologies | social impact and innovation in emerging technologies]] can overcome traditional systemic challenges.

## Business for Impact: Zarc's For-Profit Model

Zarc chose a for-profit business model over a not-for-profit one to achieve greater scalability and long-term sustainability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:07:07">[25:07:07]</a>. Kevin believes that depending on philanthropy can lead to funding eventually running out, whereas a business solution to poverty can sustain itself for hundreds of years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:25:00">[24:25:00]</a>. This aligns with the concept of [[intersection_of_business_and_social_impact | intersection of business and social impact]].

The vision is for Zarc to become the largest digital bank in Africa, channeling trillions of dollars of investments into the continent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:17:00">[25:17:00]</a>. This model aims to attract investment by demonstrating higher returns in Africa compared to the West, while simultaneously creating jobs and doubling the income of people living in poverty <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:54:00">[25:54:00]</a>. This represents a form of [[social_venture_capital_and_impact_investing | social venture capital and impact investing]].

Investing in essential services for "bottom of the pyramid" customers in Africa has significant commercial potential due to billions of people coming online and Africa being home to the largest working-class population for decades to come <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:33:00">[26:33:00]</a>. This makes it an opportune time to invest in [[the_future_impact_of_blockchain_and_fintech_in_emerging_markets | fintech growth in Africa]] and its infrastructure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:43:00">[26:43:00]</a>.

## Leapfrogging Innovation and Risk Mitigation

East Africa has seen technologies leapfrog outdated ones in the West <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:36:00">[09:36:00]</a>. For instance, mobile money allows instant transactions in Uganda, unlike the slower wire transfers in the U.S. that rely on older payment technologies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:50:00">[09:50:00]</a>. This was possible because developing regions did not have widespread bank accounts or landline telephones, allowing them to build new systems the "right way" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:14:00">[10:14:00]</a>.

### Zarc's Strategy for Scale

Zarc's plan to become the biggest digital bank in Africa relies on several factors:
*   **Macro Factors**: The market for motorcycle loans in Uganda is highly fragmented, with the largest player holding less than 15% market share, indicating significant room for growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:41:00">[30:41:00]</a>.
*   **Strategic Partnerships**: Zarc partners with major ride-hailing apps in Uganda, such as SafeBoda, to acquire customers cheaply <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:04:00">[31:04:00]</a>. These partnerships provide access to valuable financial and behavioral data (e.g., number of trips, customer ratings) to quickly assess credit risk, bypassing the need for traditional bank statements or titled assets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:34:00">[31:34:00]</a>.
*   **Technological Advantage**: Zarc offers the fastest turnaround time in the industry (three days) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:59:00">[31:59:00]</a>. Its automated lending process allows for quick and cheap provision of secondary loan products like personal loans, fuel loans, and smartphone loans, making it difficult for traditional brick-and-mortar lenders to compete <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:22:00">[32:22:00]</a>.

### Addressing Investment Skepticism in Africa

Kevin acknowledges that many investors are skeptical about investing in Africa due to concerns about weak rule of law and institutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:59:00">[35:59:00]</a>. For example, the court system in Uganda can be slow, with lawsuits pending for years, and credit reference bureaus are not as strong as in Western countries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:12:00">[36:12:00]</a>.

However, Zarc mitigates these risks through technological innovations:
*   **GPS Trackers**: Sophisticated GPS trackers in motorcycles can cut off fuel to the engine, rendering the bike unusable if a borrower defaults <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:35:00">[37:35:00]</a>. This enforcement mechanism is highly effective because the motorcycle is essential for the daily income of bottom-of-the-pyramid customers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:59:00">[37:59:00]</a>. While such methods might be restricted by privacy laws in the U.S., they are accepted in Uganda as a means to access credit without extensive financial history <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="38:28:00">[38:28:00]</a>.
*   **Focus on Productive Loans**: Unlike some digital lenders in Kenya who charge extremely high interest rates (up to 300% APR) on unsecured consumer loans that might be used for unproductive purposes like gambling, Zarc focuses on funding assets that directly increase income for borrowers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:03:00">[34:03:00]</a>. This "virtuous circle" of focusing on productive credit purposes drastically reduces risk, allowing Zarc to offer a more affordable product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:42:00">[34:42:00]</a>.

He emphasizes that one cannot solve African problems without experiencing them directly, understanding local languages, and developing products for customers whose languages are not spoken by remote teams <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="40:14:00">[40:14:00]</a>. Kevin himself lives in Uganda and is learning three local languages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="40:23:00">[40:40:23]</a>.

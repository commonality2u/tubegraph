## Formative Career Experiences in Finance

Carnegie worked with James Wolfensohn, a renowned banker who later headed the World Bank <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:22:15">[10:22:15]</a>. From Wolfensohn, Carnegie learned that success at the highest level of finance is primarily about human psychology, not just numbers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:13:00">[11:13:00]</a>. While numerical proficiency is table stakes, understanding human nature and facilitating difficult deals through reading people was key <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:17:01">[11:17:01]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:30:00">[11:30:00]</a>. He highlights that people's stated motivations often differ from their actual ones, requiring a nuanced understanding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:47:00">[13:47:00]</a>.

He emphasizes the importance of studying Charlie Munger's multidisciplinary "latticework view of the world" as the "absolute ground zero of a career in business" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:00:00">[13:00:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:06:57">[14:06:57]</a>. Munger's *Poor Charlie's Almanack* is considered a full university course in understanding internal and behavioral psychology <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:47:00">[12:47:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:16:00">[13:16:00]</a>.

## Transition to Venture Capital and Views on DeFi

Mark Carnegie transitioned into venture capital and has become a vocal advocate for decentralized finance (DeFi) and crypto, despite his background in traditional finance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:06:00">[17:06:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:30:00">[23:30:00]</a>. While acknowledging debate, Carnegie tends to agree with Nassim Nicholas Taleb's view that Bitcoin is a Ponzi scheme with no real function <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:02:00">[19:02:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:28:00">[19:28:00]</a>.

## Storytelling in Venture Capital and Startups

Effective [[the_role_of_storytelling_in_venture_capital_and_startup_success | storytelling]] is crucial for identifying and supporting successful ventures.

### Identifying Emerging Movements
A core aspect of both journalism and venture capital is the ability to "catch the thing as it's emerging" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:40:00">[30:40:00]</a>. Cultural movements often begin from the bottom up, and successful investors don't create them but rather respond to movements they observe <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:01:00">[30:01:00]</a>. This requires being attuned to change, which is more of a "taste thing and a practice thing" than a science <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:48:00">[30:48:00]</a>.

### Founders as Storytellers
Founders often present ideas that seem "weird" or absurd to others, initially met with laughter or skepticism <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:19:00">[23:19:00]</a>. However, this unusual or contrarian perspective can be an indicator of a "serious person" with a unique thesis on where the world is headed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:25:00">[23:25:00]</a>.

An example is Elon Musk's early vision for SpaceX, which was initially met with an eye-roll before becoming a highly valuable company <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:53:00">[23:53:00]</a>. Similarly, Chris Pal of Hadrian was talking about a stronger industrial base and supply chain resiliency before it became a mainstream thesis among founders <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:25:00">[22:25:26]</a>.

### Case Study: Hallow App
The Hallow app, a Catholic meditation app, exemplifies the power of an unexpected but deeply purposeful venture <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:01:00">[33:01:00]</a>. When the founder, Alex, first pitched it in 2017-2018 at business school, it was met with laughter, as other students were focused on "sexy" trends like the Creator economy or crypto <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:17:00">[33:17:00]</a>. However, the founder's personal need for the app and the dedication to his mission resonated strongly, leading to significant success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:07:00">[34:07:00]</a>.

This highlights that a strong reaction, whether positive or negative, to a company's premise can indicate it's worth exploring, whereas apathy usually means it won't go anywhere <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:57:00">[35:57:00]</a>.

### The Role of Narrative in Purpose-Driven Businesses

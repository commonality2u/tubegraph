## Reasons for Choosing Consulting

The decision to pursue a career in management consulting often stems from a desire for a highly successful and impactful professional life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>.

One individual, initially obsessed with [[maximizing_career_growth_in_startups | startups]] and entrepreneurship, interned in [[transition_from_venture_capital_to_entrepreneurship | venture capital]] and [[maximizing_career_growth_in_startups | startups]], believing "corporates are bad" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:52">[00:00:52]</a>. However, an internship in consulting, initially to appease parents, led to a complete reevaluation of what "corporate" and "consulting" entailed, particularly after a project involving VR headsets for rural communities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:09">[00:01:09]</a>.

Another individual, passionate about economics and helping the greater good, initially sought a career in the public sector or the UN <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:46">[00:01:46]</a>. After becoming disillusioned with an internship at the New South Wales Treasury, a period of personal crisis led to extensive networking and internships in investment banking, corporate finance, [[maximizing_career_growth_in_startups | startups]], and consultancies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:58">[00:01:58]</a>. Through these experiences, a strong conviction formed that consulting offered awesome people, fascinating projects, and alignment with personal skills <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:44">[00:02:44]</a>.

## Key Advantages of a Consulting Career

There are four primary [[reasons_for_choosing_a_career_in_consulting | reasons for choosing a career in management consulting]]:

### Broad Industry Exposure
Consulting provides exposure to a wide array of industries, including energy, financial services, government, and education <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:11">[00:03:11]</a>. Consultants solve diverse problems for large businesses and governments, from developing fintech apps for banks to working on multi-billion dollar private equity transactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:27">[00:03:27]</a>. This means consultants do not perform just one task <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:29">[00:03:29]</a>.

### Working with Talented People
Consulting attracts highly intelligent and ambitious individuals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:58">[00:03:58]</a>. Surrounding oneself with such individuals can significantly influence future growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:03">[00:04:03]</a>. As a junior consultant, there's also the unique opportunity to be in the room with high-power executives, an experience rare in many other jobs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:10">[00:04:10]</a>.

### Diverse Exit Opportunities
Consulting is often seen as a springboard, with people typically spending two to three years in the field before moving on to bigger careers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:25">[00:04:25]</a>. The diverse and challenging nature of the work equips consultants with many options for their future careers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:32">[00:04:32]</a>. These "exit opportunities" include:
*   Big tech <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:39">[00:04:39]</a>
*   [[maximizing_career_growth_in_startups | Startups]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:42">[00:04:42]</a>
*   Investing (private equity or [[transition_from_venture_capital_to_entrepreneurship | venture capital]]) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:42">[00:04:42]</a>
*   Industry roles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:46">[00:04:46]</a>

## Potential Challenges

While offering many advantages, consulting does present certain [[challenges_of_working_in_consulting | challenges of working in consulting]]:

### General Skill Set Development
The broad exposure, while beneficial, can be a "double-edged sword" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:58">[00:04:58]</a>. Project rotations typically occur every one to three months, which limits the time available to develop deep competencies in specific areas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:09">[00:05:09]</a>.

### Work-Life Balance
Not all consultancies offer a good work-life balance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:18">[00:05:18]</a>. Many firms can have average working hours of 60 to 80 hours per week, often combined with extended periods of travel for projects <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:23">[00:05:23]</a>. This demanding schedule can make it difficult to maintain connections with family and friends or pursue hobbies, as management consulting is definitely not a nine-to-five job <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:32">[00:05:32]</a>.

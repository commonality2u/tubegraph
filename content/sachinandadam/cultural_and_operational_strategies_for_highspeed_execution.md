## Core Philosophy: Speed and Execution

Zella's phenomenal [[strategies_for_rapid_growth_and_success_in_startups | rapid growth strategies for startups]] stemmed from a culture that prioritizes speed and immediate action <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:12">[00:20:12]</a>. This "wartime mentality" ensured that all departments operated with absolute urgency, not tolerating slow movement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:18">[00:20:18]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:20">[00:20:20]</a>.

> "We just won't tolerate moving slowly... it was like that wartime mentality if you will where listen yeah we've got this amazing injection of capital but comes massive responsibility that so let's just build everything out with absolute urgency and that's again in every single team." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:18">[00:20:18]</a>

### Stripping Out Bureaucracy
A critical aspect of Zella's [[execution_and_leadership_in_startups | execution and leadership in startups]] is eliminating unnecessary bureaucracy and overthinking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:13">[00:21:13]</a>. Ben Fisa actively strips out anything that hinders progress <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:26">[00:21:26]</a>. This includes:
*   **Avoiding extensive planning:** Instead of spending months on detailed Gantt charts, the team gets a general idea of when tasks will be done and pushes hard <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:00">[00:30:00]</a>.
*   **Direct communication:** Meetings are minimized; direct desk-side conversations are preferred to resolve issues immediately <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:26">[00:10:26]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:41">[00:29:41]</a>.
*   **Challenging timelines:** When a delivery date is given, the question is always, "How do we do it faster? What do you need to do it faster?" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:32">[00:29:32]</a>.

> "I don't believe in one-on-ones... I don't want to look at meeting rooms and see them full of people just having one-on-ones updating each other on what they're doing. Complete waste of time." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:17">[00:29:17]</a>
> "When people say, 'Hey, I'll set a meeting up with you next week.' I said, 'Well, why don't you walk up to my desk and ask me now? Let's solve it right here. Let's just keep moving.'" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:38">[00:29:38]</a>

## Developing a Company Culture
The [[developing_a_company_culture_during_rapid_growth | developing a company culture during rapid growth]] at Zella is described as organic, formed by the amalgamation of personalities rather than fabricated mottos <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:36">[00:15:36]</a>. It's built on:
*   **Transparency and directness:** Ben's personal trait of being very direct, combined with an Australian background, fosters an honest environment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:14">[00:06:14]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:07">[00:16:07]</a>. This directness, while sometimes intense, is valued for cutting to the core of business needs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:42">[00:06:42]</a>.
*   **High expectations and hard work:** The team is upfront about the immense challenge of building their product, requiring "ridiculously hard" work <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:26">[00:16:26]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:14">[00:31:14]</a>.
*   **Support and fairness:** Employees are supported to succeed, and if they give their all, the company promises to be there for them when they need a break, without judgment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:05">[00:00:05]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:12">[00:16:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:26">[00:31:26]</a>.
*   **"Reverse Pitching" in hiring:** During interviews, Ben "scares off" candidates by being brutally honest about the intensity and demands of the culture <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:50">[00:16:50]</a>. This ensures that only those who embrace and thrive in such an environment join the team <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:59">[00:16:59]</a>.

> "We work hard. There's no great company in the world that has achieved what they have without working hard." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:17">[00:32:17]</a>
> "We are not the company forever and that's okay." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:01">[00:00:01]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:23">[00:32:23]</a>

## Go-to-Market and Product Strategy

Zella's [[rapid_company_growth_and_expansion_strategies | rapid company growth and expansion strategies]] were driven by an aggressive go-to-market plan and a strong product philosophy:
*   **Broad approach:** Instead of narrowing focus, Zella went broad initially, recognizing that being too narrow and wrong could lead to stagnation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:38">[00:20:38]</a>.
*   **Direct channel and strategic partnerships:** They focused on owning their direct channel while also partnering with entities like Officeworks for a "halo effect" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:47">[00:20:47]</a>.
*   **Experienced team:** The initial team had extensive experience, which meant they knew exactly what marketing and media strategies to employ, eliminating the need for foundational learning <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:56">[00:20:56]</a>.
*   **Vision-led development:** They didn't ask customers what they wanted; instead, they built based on their vision, ready to pivot quickly if needed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:31">[00:00:31]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:18">[00:21:18]</a>.
*   **Stable and professional launch:** Dealing with people's money meant the product had to be solid and stable from day one, avoiding "flimsy MVPs" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:41">[00:21:41]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:47">[00:21:47]</a>. The brand also needed to appear larger and more professional than it was <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:59">[00:21:59]</a>.
*   **Product engineering company:** Zella's ability to move quickly is rooted in being a product engineering company that builds its own infrastructure and products, rather than relying heavily on third parties <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:31">[00:14:31]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:41">[00:14:41]</a>.
*   [[exploiting_unique_advantages_in_business | Exploiting unique advantages in business]]: Zella leveraged their unique device design and user interface to create brand awareness and capture interest in a commoditized market <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:02">[00:24:02]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:08">[00:24:08]</a>.

## Navigating Challenges and Adversity

Ben Fisa emphasizes the importance of resilience and the ability to push through adversity. His previous experiences, particularly setting up Square in Australia, taught him how to overcome impossible obstacles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:46">[00:25:46]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:57">[00:25:57]</a>.

During Zella's launch, external factors like the COVID-19 pandemic caused significant apprehension, particularly concerns about the future of face-to-face transactions which their business model relied upon <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:40">[00:26:40]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:00">[00:27:00]</a>. However, the team's belief in their vision and human behavior prevented them from "blinking" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:10">[00:27:10]</a>. The initial success of 1,500 businesses signing up in the first month quickly alleviated early doubts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:22">[00:28:22]</a>.

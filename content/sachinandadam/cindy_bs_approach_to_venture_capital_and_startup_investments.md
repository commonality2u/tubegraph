## Investment Strategy

Cindy B's success is attributed to strategic timing and a disciplined approach to identifying and supporting startups.

### Focus on Y Combinator (YC)
A significant part of her success comes from her strategic choice to focus on YC companies, with 12 out of her 14 unicorns being YC graduates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:01">[00:10:01]</a>. She started investing in 2011, and YC was already clearly the best accelerator at the time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:17">[00:10:17]</a>. YC provided an efficient way to talk to many companies in a short period, allowing her to benchmark them quickly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:36">[00:10:36]</a>. This environment enabled her to make good decisions by gathering qualitative data from conversations with founders, understanding their stories, and assessing their technical capabilities and teams <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:49">[00:10:49]</a>.

### Quick Decision-Making
Cindy B is known for her rapid decision-making, often sending an initial email stating "I want to invest" to founders <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:50">[00:11:50]</a>. She understands that in early stages, there isn't much more data to gather after a couple of calls <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:08">[00:12:08]</a>. This quick decision-making is highly valued by founders, providing them with confidence and saving them time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:20">[00:12:20]</a>.

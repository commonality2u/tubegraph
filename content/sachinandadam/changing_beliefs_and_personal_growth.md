## Growth Curves and Identifying Talent

The discussion delves into "growth curves" in the context of personal and [[personal_and_professional_growth_through_coaching | professional growth]], particularly when hiring. Max defines talent across three vectors:
1.  **Distance:** How much someone has already achieved <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:19">[00:36:19]</a>.
2.  **Growth:** How fast someone is developing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:23">[00:36:23]</a>.
3.  **Acceleration:** Growing at an increasing rate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:25">[00:36:25]</a>.

He emphasizes looking for acceleration in candidates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:36">[00:36:36]</a>, which tends to come from people who use each experience to achieve more than before <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:44">[00:36:44]</a>. The key traits identifying accelerators are **curiosity** (spending free time learning, questioning everything) and **drive** (willingness to work hard and make sacrifices) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:54">[00:37:54]</a>.

Adam suggests that a person's life story and context are crucial for understanding their "distance" and what has driven them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:38">[00:42:38]</a>. He also looks at how candidates answer unexpected questions, indicating their ability to think independently rather than just "game the system" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:59">[00:43:59]</a>.

The guests offer advice for students seeking to accelerate their [[Selfimprovement_selftalk_and_personal_development | personal development]]:

This is the foundation for determining the right direction for velocity and boldness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:47">[00:53:47]</a>.

### The Role of Intuition in Personal Growth

Intuition is conceptualized as access to experiences stored in the subconscious <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:44">[00:20:44]</a>. Good intuition or judgment stems from:
1.  **Extensive Experiences:** Having seen and done a lot <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:57">[00:20:57]</a>.
2.  **High Storage:** The ability to remember and learn from those experiences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:03">[00:21:03]</a>.
3.  **High RAM (Processing Power):** The capacity to hold multiple experiences simultaneously in one's mind to inform decisions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:24">[00:21:24]</a>.

It is suggested that "intuition brings you to a mountain, data helps you climb it" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:43">[00:21:43]</a>, emphasizing the balance between intuitive insights and analytical validation.

## Overcoming Risk Aversion and Societal Norms

A key obstacle to personal growth and taking bold steps is risk aversion, which is often reinforced by the school system <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:23">[00:25:23]</a>. The system rewards conformity rather than risk-taking or independent thought <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:30">[00:25:30]</a>. This can extend to financial decisions, where index fund investing, while potentially profitable, may discourage individuals from learning to allocate capital and take calculated risks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:53">[00:25:53]</a>.

The idea that "everyone's a winner" in competitive environments also fails to prepare individuals for the realities of the world, hindering the development of resilience to setbacks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:30">[00:26:30]</a>. This can lead to a culture where people complain about problems but do not act to solve them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:50">[00:24:50]</a>, or where they talk about starting businesses without ever doing so <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:41">[00:09:41]</a>.

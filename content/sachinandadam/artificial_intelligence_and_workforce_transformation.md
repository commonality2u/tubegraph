## The Core Problem in Workforce Management
Historically, organizations have struggled with understanding their workforce. Companies often know more about their physical assets like chairs and printers than they do about their own people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:03">[00:11:03]</a>. This includes a lack of clarity on:
*   Employees' skills <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:57">[00:10:57]</a>
*   Their potential <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:59">[00:10:59]</a>
*   Their current workload <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:00">[00:11:00]</a>

Additionally, organizations often lack a clear understanding of the actual tasks being performed within a job title, beyond just the job description <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:17">[00:11:17]</a>. This disconnect leads to inefficiencies, such as simultaneously hiring and laying off large numbers of people due to a lack of visibility into internal talent and needs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:37">[00:04:37]</a>.

## Workforce Intelligence as a Solution
The concept of [[workforce_intelligence_and_software_development | Workforce Intelligence]] aims to solve these problems by providing data to help organizations make informed decisions about their people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:24">[00:00:24]</a>. This involves understanding workers and work at a deep level, including specific tasks and skills, to enable the seamless movement of work to the right worker <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:22">[00:12:22]</a>.

Organizations need to be bold in embracing [[the_impact_of_technology_and_ai_on_future_industries_and_opportunities | AI]] while also being responsible, ensuring no one is left behind <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:32">[00:05:32]</a>. The goal is to facilitate a transition into meaningful work, rather than simply replacing jobs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:20">[00:15:20]</a>. This approach is crucial because even AI companies don't fully grasp the potential of this technology, making it challenging for organizations to build an [[future_of_jobs_and_skills_in_an_aidriven_world | AI-embracing workforce]] without leaving people behind <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:24">[00:06:24]</a>.

## Building an AI-Driven Company
One company's mission is to reach a billion-dollar valuation with fewer than 100 people by leveraging a unique workforce strategy driven by [[rise_of_ai_agents_and_their_impact_on_the_workforce | AI]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:29">[00:13:29]</a>. This involves:
*   **Deconstructing Work**: Breaking down strategy into tasks and chunks of work before allocating them to people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:24">[00:26:24]</a>.
*   **Optimal Allocation**: Determining if a task should be handled by a human, [[artificial_intelligence_and_existential_risks | AI]], or an offshore resource <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:43">[00:26:43]</a>.
*   **Internal AI Agencies**: Creating internal AI "agencies" to handle tasks like content creation, which can significantly boost productivity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:23">[00:27:23]</a>.

This approach has already led to significant cost savings, with the company saving $1.5 million per month through its own AI adoption <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:30">[00:14:30]</a>. Key areas seeing returns include go-to-market, customer relations, and product development <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:38">[00:14:38]</a>. The goal is to enable organizations to "do more with less" and adapt to the pressures faced by startups <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:01">[00:15:01]</a>.

## Enterprise Sales in an AI Era
For Enterprise sales, deep empathy and subject matter expertise are crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:27">[00:23:27]</a>. It's less about selling and more about solving problems with customers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:45">[00:23:45]</a>. Companies are investing in experts who understand and have experienced the customer's problems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:21">[00:24:21]</a>. For Enterprise sales, companies must also invest in certifications like Sock 1 and Sock 2, and ISO, to meet strict data privacy and cyber requirements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:47">[00:24:47]</a>.

Sales cycles, typically around 12 months for Enterprise, are being shortened to around five months through a "land, expand, explode" strategy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:04">[00:25:04]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:15">[00:25:15]</a>. This involves:
1.  **Landing**: Solving a specific problem for a key area of the business to get "under the radar" with less expense and risk <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:23">[00:25:23]</a>.
2.  **Expanding**: Growing the solution within the organization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:29">[00:25:29]</a>.
3.  **Exploding**: Achieving significant net revenue retention <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:32">[00:25:32]</a>.

This strategy is particularly important in current economic times where companies are scrutinizing software spending <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:40">[00:25:40]</a>.

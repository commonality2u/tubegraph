## The Storytelling Problem and Market Incentives

A core challenge identified is a "storytelling problem" that prevents [[the_role_of_innovation_in_climate_solutions | climate tech]] from engaging the masses and building the necessary movements for change <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:22">00:05:22</a>. Adoption is inherently unevenly distributed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:38">00:05:38</a>. Historically, even transformative [[the_role_of_innovation_in_climate_solutions | innovations]] like the steam engine took a long time to move beyond being a mere "toy" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:42">00:05:42</a>.

A critical missing piece is a price on carbon <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:56">00:05:56</a>. Without this, much of the work in [[the_role_of_innovation_in_climate_solutions | climate technology]] is "premature" because there is no direct financial incentive for widespread adoption <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:59">00:05:59</a>. This creates a "catch-22": the incentive to buy won't exist until the solutions are built <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:07">00:06:07</a>. The speaker suggests that a price on carbon would emerge once wealthy industrialists could profit more from it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:19">00:06:19</a>.

## Overcoming Perceived Impossibility

Despite the challenges, the notion that building a regenerative economy is "impossible" is challenged <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:51">00:06:51</a>. Many of the necessary technologies already exist; the issue lies in the collective drive and speed to deploy them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:32">00:08:32</a>. The process is described as requiring persistent effort and "grinding for a while" until a seemingly difficult task becomes manageable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:24">00:09:24</a>.

A key distinction is drawn between being "prepared" and being "ready" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:28">00:10:28</a>. [[the_role_of_innovation_in_climate_solutions | Innovation]] often demands action before one is "ready" (meaning, completely certain of success), but adequate preparation is crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:50">00:10:50</a>. This "feeling between three and go" is where [[the_role_of_innovation_in_climate_solutions | innovation]] resides, and the ability to decide to act is a learnable skill <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:17">00:13:17</a>. Sometimes, it requires disregarding one's own feelings and just executing the plan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:43">00:14:43</a>.

## Systemic Solutions and Shunning Non-Believers

Addressing [[challenges_in_the_fintech_and_banking_industry | systemic problems]] requires [[systems_change_and_market_incentives_for_green_practices | systemic solutions]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:54">00:31:54</a>. It's not about shaming individuals for minor actions, but fixing the larger system <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:02">00:32:02</a>.

Key strategies for fostering adoption include:
*   **Shunning non-believers:** Avoid engaging with those who oppose merely for attention <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:22">00:32:22</a>. Winning a debate with them will not change the system <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:36">00:32:36</a>.
*   **Scaffolding:** Provide progressive steps for people to engage with the story and solutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:42">00:32:42</a>.
*   **Creating positive tension:** Instead of relying on fear (which can paralyze and lead to denial), create tension by presenting clear opportunities and limited-time propositions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:45">00:34:45</a>. This motivates people to join the movement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:06">00:36:06</a>.

## The Role of a Carbon Dividend

A proposed solution for market-driven adoption is a carbon dividend coupled with a border adjustment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:31">00:40:31</a>. This system would involve:
*   Charging an accurate price for carbon emissions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:00">00:41:00</a>.
*   Distributing 100% of these funds directly to every citizen as a check <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:58">00:40:58</a>.
*   The government merely facilitates the movement of money <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:05">00:41:05</a>.

This approach leverages the market's efficiency in responding to costs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:19">00:41:19</a>. As people receive these checks, they will begin to change their behavior, and market prices will adjust accordingly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:35">00:41:35</a>. Once alternatives become reasonably priced, businesses will have a financial incentive to lobby for this regime, as it makes green practices more profitable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:27">00:42:27</a>. The border adjustment mechanism ensures that imported goods also face carbon pricing, preventing unfair competition <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:36">00:42:36</a>. This market-based solution aims to heal the planet by addressing the root cause of mispriced carbon <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:08">00:43:08</a>.

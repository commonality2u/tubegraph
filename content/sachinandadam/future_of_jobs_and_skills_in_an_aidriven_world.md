## The Rise of AI Agents

AI agents are defined as autonomous reasoning digital workers or AI employees/coworkers that can perform actions, think for themselves beyond programmed rules, and improve with experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:54">[00:01:54]</a>. They operate 24/7 without needing sleep or food <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:28">[00:01:28]</a>. The expectation is that every business will eventually have AI agents that collaborate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:37">[00:00:37]</a>, potentially leading to a world with hundreds of millions or billions of these agents <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:20">[00:02:20]</a>. The excitement stems from AI's capability to "do the work" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:46">[00:00:46]</a>.

The concept of AI agents gained significant traction in early 2023 with the emergence of Lang Chain, which allowed chaining Large Language Models (LLMs) to perform complex tasks like ordering pizza or deep research <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:40">[00:02:40]</a>. This progression enables "text to action," where users can simply describe desired software, and the AI will create it, learning as it goes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:10">[00:03:10]</a>. The realization that AI agents could combine tasks and capabilities led to the understanding that they might "literally change the way that we work forever" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:44">[00:03:44]</a>.

## [[impact_of_ai_on_employment_and_the_future_of_work | Impact on Employment and the Future of Work]]

The emergence of AI agents brings both excitement and concern regarding [[impact_of_ai_on_employment_and_the_future_of_work | employment and the future of work]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:53">[00:06:53]</a>. The core question is whether AI will replace or augment human workers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:11">[00:01:11]</a>.

### Jobs at Risk

Many acknowledge that "jobs will definitely change" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:56">[00:03:56]</a>, with a rapid shift towards automation in the midterm <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:04">[00:04:04]</a>.
Specific roles identified as being at risk or undergoing significant change include:
*   **Consultants and VCs:** Initial concern that jobs like those in venture capital and consulting could be replaced by AI agents <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:19">[00:00:19]</a>. Junior consultants performing tasks like presentations, formatting, and Excel modeling are seen as particularly vulnerable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:23">[00:00:23]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:40">[00:14:40]</a>.
*   **Software Writers/Programmers:** The ability of AI to write software means "the people who were writing software, they're gone" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:35">[00:03:35]</a>.
*   **White-Collar vs. Blue-Collar Jobs:** White-collar jobs are expected to be impacted faster than blue-collar jobs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:43">[00:04:43]</a>.
*   **Junior Roles:** AI agents are "dangerously close to replacing a lot of the junior roles" such as admin, research, sales, and modeling <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:29">[00:14:29]</a>.
*   **Business Development Representatives (BDRs/SDRs):** The repetitive tasks of cold calling and lead generation in sales are highly suitable for AI automation, allowing human BDRs to be promoted to more engaging account executive roles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:14">[00:12:14]</a>.

> [!WARNING] The rapid advancement of AI could cause "significant harm to the world" and society if not adequately prepared for <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:48">[00:04:48]</a>.

### Adaptation and New Opportunities

Despite the risks, the narrative also points to opportunity. Historically, innovation "upends the kind of comfortable status quo" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:00">[00:05:00]</a>. Just as roles like "social media manager" didn't exist 15 years ago but are now important vocations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:17">[00:05:17]</a>, AI is expected to create new jobs and roles.

Automation through AI can lead to:
*   **Increased Productivity:** Making individuals and teams more productive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:37">[00:05:37]</a>.
*   **More Goods and Services:** This doesn't necessarily mean fewer jobs, but rather "more diversity in goods and services created" and increased competition, which benefits the economy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:44">[00:05:44]</a>.
*   **Promotion and New Work:** Automating lower-level tasks can free up human workers for higher-value, more complex roles, fostering career progression rather than displacement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:45">[00:12:45]</a>.

> [!NOTE] The goal might not be for AI to take jobs, but to "help you get promoted 'cause you can do more with less" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:42">[00:11:42]</a>.

The [[the_impact_of_technology_and_ai_on_future_industries_and_opportunities | impact of technology and AI on future industries and opportunities]] requires a fundamental rethink of what a job means, what skills to teach children, and what adults should retrain in <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:24">[00:06:24]</a>.

## Case Study: Relevance AI

Relevance AI, a startup from Sydney, Australia, exemplifies the navigation of this [[challenges_and_opportunities_in_aidriven_startups | AI-driven startup landscape]]. They pivoted to focus entirely on building AI agents, raising $15 million to create "the world's first workforce of AI agents" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:30">[00:10:30]</a>.

They developed "Bosch," an AI sales agent, after identifying sales as a high-potential use case <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:58">[00:11:58]</a>. Their strategy involves a "customer obsession" and a focus on getting agents "in production" and working for customers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:26">[00:15:26]</a>.

### Competing with Giants

Relevance AI competes with trillion-dollar companies like Google, Microsoft, and OpenAI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:06">[00:01:06]</a>. Their competitive advantages include:
*   **Agility and Velocity:** Startups can "move and ship faster" and be "extremely responsive to the rapidly changing and evolving market" compared to larger, slower incumbents <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:01">[00:17:01]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:53">[00:17:53]</a>.
*   **Model Agnostic Platform:** Relevance AI aims to be a horizontal platform not tied to any single AI vendor, viewing agents more like team members than restrictive software <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:09">[00:16:09]</a>. They call themselves "the AI workforce company" rather than "the AI agent company" to emphasize diversity in agents <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:45">[00:16:45]</a>.
*   **No-Code Approach:** They strive to make building an agent platform completely no-code, delivering value more easily <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:04">[00:16:04]</a>.

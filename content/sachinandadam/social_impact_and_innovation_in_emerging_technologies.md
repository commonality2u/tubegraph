## Evolution of the Web: From Centralized to Decentralized

Henderson provides a clear overview of the internet's progression:

*   **Web1 (Early Internet)**: Characterized by open, interoperable protocols like HTTP for websites and SMTP for email. It was niche and required technical expertise to use, limiting its widespread adoption <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:04:00">[07:04:00]</a>.
*   **Web2 (Current Internet)**: Defined by companies that made the internet user-friendly, onboarding billions of users. Examples include Google, Facebook, Amazon, and Apple. These companies built easy-to-use interfaces around open protocols, enabling user-generated content and widespread accessibility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:40:00">[07:40:00]</a>. While a significant economic driver, Web2 faces challenges concerning data ownership, privacy, and stifled [[product_development_and_innovation_in_tech_startups | innovation]] due to centralized control <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:44:00">[08:44:00]</a>. Content creators, for instance, are often confined to platforms and receive minimal compensation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:09:00">[09:09:00]</a>.
*   **Web3 (Decentralized Internet)**: Focuses on the emergence of decentralization through [[the_future_impact_of_blockchain_and_fintech_in_emerging_markets | blockchain-based technology]]. This shift transforms private databases into public, open, and interoperable ones, enabling data ownership and permissionless building on top of existing protocols <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:24:00">[10:24:00]</a>.

## Incentives and Social Impact in Web3

In Web3, incentives are primarily driven by **tokens**, which align communities economically around shared outcomes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:01:00">[17:01:00]</a>. Early users can earn or buy tokens, incentivizing them to promote the platform and benefit from token appreciation. This contrasts with Web2 platforms where content creators often receive no direct monetary reward <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:11:00">[18:11:00]</a>. Builders of Web3 protocols also hold tokens and often have revenue models tied to platform usage (e.g., transaction fees), which are intrinsically linked to token value <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:36:00">[19:36:00]</a>.

### The Power of Digital Ownership (NFTs)

A fundamental [[innovation_in_australian_technology | innovation]] of Web3 is **digital ownership** through NFTs. Before 2017, it was virtually impossible to truly own a digital good <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:37:00">[21:37:00]</a>. While currently popular for collectibles and art, NFTs have broader implications. For example, in gaming, players spend thousands of hours and dollars on digital items that become worthless if they stop playing. NFTs could allow players to truly own and transfer these in-game assets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:40:00">[29:40:00]</a>.

Beyond current use cases, NFTs hold promise for:
*   **Digital Proof of Action**: Recognizing contributions to a cause or community, potentially leading to future rewards or access to exclusive groups <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:48:00">[30:48:00]</a>.
*   **Community Building**: Fostering genuine connections and mutual benefits, as seen with artist Des Lucrece's "Monster Owner's Club" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:37:00">[32:37:00]</a>.

## The Importance of Self-Awareness and Human Skills

The ability to understand people and oneself is becoming increasingly vital, especially in roles like consumer investing where having a "finger on the pulse" of culture and taste is essential <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:33:04">[01:33:04]</a>. Even in B2B contexts, brand and design matter, as products are ultimately sold to and built by people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:09:07">[02:09:07]</a>.

With the rise of AI making product creation easier, "soft skills" like understanding consumer psychology and brand development are becoming relatively more important <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:33:04">[03:33:04]</a>. More human skills are expected to become increasingly significant over time because they are harder to automate and augment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:54:02">[03:54:02]</a>. This shift will free people from monotonous tasks, making jobs requiring judgment and nuance more important <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:15:20">[04:15:20]</a>.

### Block Talk: A Tool for Deeper Understanding

A side project, a board game called Block Talk, was created to explore the zero-to-one process of launching a product in commerce <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:54:33">[04:54:33]</a>. The game, which is similar to Jenga, involves writing questions on wooden blocks and answering them in a circle, serving as an "intentional forced bonding exercise" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:27:32">[05:27:32]</a>.

The game's creator's favorite questions often revolve around people's parents and backgrounds, such as "How are you most different from your parents and how are you most similar to them?" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:51:30">[05:51:30]</a>. These questions provide a concise way to understand individuals' wants, dreams, and desires <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:02:40">[06:02:40]</a>. Other questions in the game prompt reflection on career choices, like "If you weren't doing this career what would you want to do?" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:09:40">[06:09:40]</a>.

These types of questions are valuable for revealing [[personal_growth_and_self_awareness | self-awareness]], deep motivations, and the ability to assess one's strengths and weaknesses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:22:15">[06:22:15]</a>. In venture capital, especially at the pre-seed and seed stages, understanding what makes a founder "tick" and why they are driven to build a business is more of an art than a science <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:54:26">[06:54:26]</a>.

## Navigating Personal Challenges and Self-Criticism

An often surprising aspect of an individual's internal monologue is a tendency to be "too hard on myself" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:22:15">[08:22:15]</a>. Drawing inspiration from "The Untethered Soul," it's noted that one might not be friends with someone who spoke to them the way they speak to themselves internally <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:48:47">[08:48:47]</a>.

Working with a coach can help cultivate self-kindness and gentleness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:55:00">[08:55:00]</a>. This includes not feeling angry at oneself for taking an evening, Saturday, or Sunday off when exhausted or having a tough time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:07:05">[09:07:05]</a>. The root of such intense self-motivation and high standards often stems from a desire to feel that one's work makes a tangible difference and is not commoditized <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:33:02">[09:33:02]</a>. The motivation is driven by a desire to be excellent and special, aiming to leave a lasting impact <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:43:23">[09:43:23]</a>.

## Evolving Work-Life Balance and Productivity

Over time, there has been a shift towards being less obsessed with productivity and more relaxed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:54:23">[28:54:23]</a>. This involves focusing less on sheer "activity" and more on "action" that is substantive and truly matters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:08:46">[29:08:46]</a>. Friends have observed a calmer, less frenetic approach, where one no longer feels stressed about immediately responding to emails on weekends <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:25:02">[29:25:02]</a>.

This change comes with the "compounding of developing a lot of useful skills" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:32:00">[29:32:00]</a>. In earlier career stages, being useful and frantic might be necessary, but later on, the quality of thought becomes more important <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:39:03">[29:39:03]</a>. The venture capital profession, in particular, teaches that success often hinges on a few right decisions per year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:50:06">[29:50:06]</a>. Similarly, for companies, it's about identifying the critical actions that move the needle, rather than just constant activity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:03:00">[30:03:00]</a>. This perspective contributes to [[balancing_work_life_and_personal_wellbeing | balancing work life and personal wellbeing]] and finding [[the_concept_of_work_life_balance_and_harmony_in_entrepreneurship | work life balance and harmony]].

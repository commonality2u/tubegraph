Personalized healthcare is emerging as a critical frontier in improving health outcomes, addressing the systemic inefficiencies and patient disengagement prevalent in traditional healthcare models <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:08">[00:30:08]</a>. At its core, personalized healthcare aims to tailor medical decisions, treatments, and products to the individual patient, moving beyond the one-size-fits-all approach <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:25">[00:37:25]</a>.

## Human Health's Vision for Personalized Healthcare

Human Health, co-founded by Georgia Vidler, is at the forefront of this shift, developing an app designed to track treatments and symptoms, provide actionable insights, and empower both individuals and their care teams <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:37">[00:00:37]</a>. The company's mission is to give everyone access to personalized healthcare <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:25">[00:37:25]</a>.

The initial focus of Human Health is on individuals with chronic diagnoses, particularly those with one or more conditions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:50">[00:00:50]</a>. Many users tracking with Human Health manage multiple conditions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:58">[00:00:58]</a>. This focus stems from the co-founders' personal experiences, including Georgia Vidler's family struggles with serious mental illnesses like bipolar, schizophrenia, and manic depression, and her co-founder Kate's experience as a parent of children with autism <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:10">[00:00:10]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:46">[00:24:46]</a>. These experiences highlight the need for a system that truly serves the patient <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:58">[00:33:58]</a>.

> "Our whole goal is build a patient a truly Patient First product that that's pretty rare in the US" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:58">[00:33:58]</a>

### The Role of Technology and Data

[[The role of technology in healthcare improvement | Technology]] is central to Human Health's approach. The company believes that in the coming decades, [[AI doctors and the future of healthcare | computers]] will handle significant data processing, research, and decision-making in healthcare with less bias than human doctors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:36">[00:42:36]</a>. This shift will allow doctors to become more human, focusing on creativity, empathy, accountability, and acting more like coaches <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:05">[00:43:05]</a>.

The vision includes patients having full control of their health information at the click of a button <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:27">[00:43:27]</a>. This is supported by:
*   **At-home testing**: Set to become cheaper and more accessible, enabling users to upload data to apps like Human Health <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:39">[00:43:39]</a>.
*   **Personalized insights**: The app will process this data to provide helpful and personalized information that users can then discuss with their doctors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:47">[00:43:47]</a>.

## Challenges and Goals

[[Challenges and goals of the healthcare startup Human Health | Building Human Health]] involves navigating a complex industry rife with [[challenges_in_navigating_the_healthcare_system | misaligned incentives]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:07">[00:36:07]</a>. A primary challenge is making an inherently complex system simple and user-friendly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:39">[00:36:39]</a>. Unlike companies building for payers, employers, or pharmaceutical companies, Human Health is committed to a "Patient First" product, which is rare in the US market <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:58">[00:33:58]</a>.

To achieve mass adoption and financial viability, Human Health's strategy includes:
*   **Free product focus**: Building an amazing free product to achieve scale and a large community of users <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:32">[00:38:32]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:07">[00:39:07]</a>.
*   **Monetization through data and paid features**: The ultimate product is the information collected and learned from, which is highly monetizable in the long term <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:47">[00:38:47]</a>. A paid version for users will also be developed, offering an "even more amazing" experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:43">[00:39:43]</a>.
*   **Reimbursement by employers and payers**: Similar to companies like Headspace and Calm, the long-term goal is to have the product reimbursed by employers and government entities, starting with tech companies due to their progressive health plans <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:50">[00:39:50]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:24">[00:40:24]</a>.

A key milestone for Human Health is launching research flows to allow users to contribute de-identified, aggregate data to medical research via consent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:16">[00:49:16]</a>. This will enable mass-scale observational studies and the publication of novel scientific findings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:50">[00:49:50]</a>.

### Fixing Systems, Not Symptoms

A core value of Human Health is to "fix systems not symptoms" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:41">[00:34:41]</a>. This principle guides not only team behavior—getting to the root cause of problems rather than just treating superficial issues—but also the approach to healthcare <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:06">[00:35:06]</a>. The goal is to help people understand the underlying causes of their health challenges, moving beyond the current system's tendency to merely treat symptoms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:54">[00:35:54]</a>.

## Impact and Future Outlook

The vision for personalized healthcare, as championed by Human Health, is one where the patient experience is enjoyable, and individuals have agency over their health data <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:08">[00:44:08]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:27">[00:43:27]</a>. With the confluence of various technological advancements, this future is considered "totally achievable" within the next 10-20 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:11">[00:42:11]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:00">[00:45:00]</a>. This is particularly crucial given that [[trends_in_health_and_wellness | Millennials]] are predicted to be the first generation to live shorter lives than their parents, underscoring the urgent need for effective, personalized solutions for chronic conditions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:16">[00:47:16]</a>.

Despite the complexities, the drive to create such a product is fueled by a deep sense of purpose and a desire to provide a much-needed solution for those navigating chronic illnesses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:55">[00:26:55]</a>. The user feedback, such as an individual with bipolar disorder finding the app helpful and validating, underscores the significant impact personalized healthcare solutions can have <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:06">[00:01:06]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:10">[00:41:10]</a>.

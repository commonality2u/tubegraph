## Evolution of Investment Focus at Insight Partners

Puji's journey into [[venture_capital_and_consumer_investment | consumer internet investing]] began at Insight Partners <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:47">[00:27:47]</a>. While Insight's core focus was on late-stage enterprise software companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:08">[00:28:08]</a>, Puji found a lack of innovation in this sector, noting that the workings of enterprise software companies have been understood for decades, with the last major innovation being the shift from license maintenance to SaaS <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:27">[00:28:27]</a>.

This led to the creation of "Insight Next," a four-person team, including Puji, dedicated to [[investing_in_startups_and_early_stage_companies | early growth internet companies]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:54">[00:27:54]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:04">[00:28:04]</a>. This team operated as an independent fund within Insight, with its own pipeline meetings and investment committee <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:42">[00:40:42]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:46">[00:30:46]</a>.

### Distinguishing Consumer Internet Businesses

Consumer internet companies, unlike traditional enterprise software, are more nuanced <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:51">[00:28:51]</a>. Their business models often include freemium, direct-to-consumer sales, subscriptions, or marketplace structures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:52">[00:28:52]</a>. They do not adhere to the "classic Boiler Room Enterprise sales playbooks" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:03">[00:29:03]</a>.

Puji notes that these companies often have:
*   Good margins <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:38">[00:29:38]</a>
*   Good retention rates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:39">[00:29:39]</a>
*   The potential to grow into multi-billion or trillion-dollar categories <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:34">[00:29:34]</a>

## Data-Driven Investment Strategy

A key aspect of their [[datadriven_venture_capital_strategies | investment strategy]] in this space involves deep [[datadriven_venture_capital_strategies | data analysis]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:11">[00:30:11]</a>. For example, they would analyze millions of rows of usage data, correlating open times with retention rates, demographics with customer acquisition costs, and six-month user behavior with likely lifetime value <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:14">[00:30:14]</a>. This allowed them to identify patterns that other firms were not yet recognizing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:37">[00:30:37]</a>, leading to a high success rate in term sheet wins <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:40">[00:30:40]</a>.

This [[trends_in_consumer_investing | approach]] focuses on understanding the "levers of the business" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:15">[00:31:15]</a> beyond traditional enterprise metrics, such as marketing life cycle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:13">[00:31:13]</a>.

## Seeking "Utilities" of the Future

Left Lane Capital actively seeks businesses that can become "utilities" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:31">[00:35:31]</a>. These are companies that solve complex, stressful problems for consumers or small businesses, integrating seamlessly into their lives.

Examples include:
*   **Henry**: An [[investing_in_australian_tech_startups | investment in the Australian region]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:42">[00:35:42]</a>, this company serves sole proprietors by automating tax calculations and bookkeeping <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:14">[00:36:14]</a>. For a 1% fee of earnings, it removes the stress of tax compliance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:20">[00:36:20]</a>. Henry exhibits incredible retention <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:24">[00:36:24]</a>.
*   **Property Tax Negotiation Company**: A recent investment, this company automatically fights for homeowners to reduce property taxes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:41">[00:36:41]</a>. It takes a 25% cut of the savings achieved <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:02">[00:37:02]</a>.

For Left Lane Capital, a "utility" is characterized by its essential nature and strong customer retention, even when serving single-person businesses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:32">[00:36:32]</a>.

## Buying Out Stores: Strategies and Scale

The central premise involved [[buying_items_from_multiple_stores | buying every single item]] in five different retail locations: a clothing store, a GameStop, a car dealership, a sports equipment store, and half of a Food Lion supermarket <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:10">[00:01:10]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:06">[00:02:06]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:17">[00:04:17]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:52">[00:06:52]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:52">[00:08:52]</a>.

### Clothing Store Acquisition
The first store was a clothing retailer. Initially, staff began hand-typing every item purchased <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:28">[00:01:28]</a>. To expedite the process, a lump sum of $85,000 was negotiated to buy the entire inventory, negating the need for individual scanning <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:32">[00:01:32]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:38">[00:01:38]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:40">[00:01:40]</a>. The entire store's contents were loaded into a U-Haul truck <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:42">[00:01:42]</a>.

Endurance challenges often test participants' physical and mental fortitude, requiring a blend of strategic positioning, psychological resilience, and adaptive responses to unforeseen conditions. The "Hold Hand on Jet" challenge, where contestants vie for ownership of a private jet, exemplifies various [[winning_strategies_in_competitions | winning strategies]] and adaptations in a high-stakes endurance setting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:29">[00:00:29]</a>.

## Initial Setup and Rules
The challenge begins with 11 YouTubers placing their hands on a $2.5 million private jet <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>. The primary rule is simple: whoever keeps their hand on the longest wins, and taking a hand off results in elimination <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:05">[00:00:05]</a>. Participants are prohibited from pushing others <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:15">[00:00:15]</a>. An "army of people" monitors their hands to ensure compliance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:57">[00:03:57]</a>.

> [!info] The Prize
> The winner receives a private jet, valued at $2.5 million <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>.

## Contestant Mindset and Early Strategies
From the outset, contestants expressed extreme dedication, with some stating they could "stay here forever" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:18">[00:00:18]</a> or were "willing to do anything, including crap my own pants" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:19">[00:00:19]</a>, showcasing their [[motivational_strategies_to_endure_challenges | motivational strategies]].

### Seeking Optimal Positions
Early in the challenge, a common [[contestant_competition_strategies | strategy]] was to find the "best spot" or the "lowest spot on the plane" for comfort and stability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:32">[00:00:32]</a>. One contestant found the wheels "pretty comfortable" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:07">[00:01:07]</a>.

## Elimination Dynamics and Tactics
Elimination can occur through accidental slips, deliberate actions, or strategic inducements.

### Accidental Eliminations
*   **Vik's Elimination:** Vik was eliminated early by lifting his hand, which was caught on camera <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:18">[00:01:18]</a>. He believed his spot was "logical" but still lost grip <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:24">[00:01:24]</a>.
*   **Inside the Jet:** One contestant honestly admitted elimination after touching the interior chair of the jet, which was not allowed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:35">[00:06:35]</a>.
*   **Late Stage Slips:** Harry was eliminated right after the jet landed from its flight due to a single-frame hand lift caught on camera <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:55">[00:13:55]</a>. Similarly, George was eliminated in the final moments for a "singular frame" where his finger left the jet <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:42">[00:14:42]</a>.

### Inducing Eliminations
*   **Monetary Incentives:** MrBeast offered Chunkz his debit card with a $10,000 limit to encourage him to leave <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:46">[00:01:46]</a>. Chunkz accepted, leading to Speed's accidental elimination during a high-five <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:21">[00:02:21]</a>.
*   **Bribery and Collusion:** In the later stages, contestants engaged in negotiations, with others offering the winner's share (e.g., $20,000) for someone to drop out <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:58">[00:05:58]</a>. This [[competition_dynamics_in_endurance_challenges | competition dynamics]] allowed Will to be bought out by the remaining contestants <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:07">[00:06:07]</a>.
*   **Coin Flip Agreements:** In the final stages, a coin flip was used as a mutual agreement for elimination, with the loser taking their hand off the jet <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:09">[00:11:09]</a>. Callux lost a coin flip and was eliminated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:22">[00:11:22]</a>.

## Increasing Challenge Difficulty
To force eliminations, the organizers progressively increased the difficulty, testing participants' [[challenges_and_survival_strategies | survival strategies]].

### Movement and Discomfort
*   **Towing the Jet:** After initial eliminations, the jet was towed around the airport, forcing contestants to move with it and increasing physical strain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:42">[00:02:42]</a>. The plane then flew for over an hour, further testing endurance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:01">[00:08:01]</a>. The flight was revealed to be in circles, a "prank" to exhaust them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:24">[00:08:24]</a>.
*   **Bathroom Breaks:** A "bucket method" was introduced for bathroom breaks, requiring contestants to maintain hand contact during use <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:35">[00:03:35]</a>.
*   **Eating Challenges:** Food like steak and spaghetti was provided, but with wooden forks, making it intentionally difficult to eat with one hand and maintain hand contact <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:47">[00:08:47]</a>.

### Strategic Constraint and Physical Strain
*   **Limited Movement:** Contestants were later forced to "crawl up" parts of the plane <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:22">[00:04:22]</a> and eventually do "laps around the plane" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:25">[00:05:25]</a>, increasing physical demand.
*   **Banning Wings:** The wings were eventually banned as contact points to make the challenge harder <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:23">[00:10:23]</a>.
*   **Standing Requirement:** Contestants were given 60 seconds to stand up, removing any comfort from sitting or lying down <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:26">[00:10:26]</a>.
*   **Red Tape Restriction:** Red tape was applied to the jet, creating an even smaller allowable area for hand contact. Touching or going below the red line meant elimination <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:37">[00:10:37]</a>.
*   **Single Finger Rule:** As a final, extreme test of endurance, participants were required to only use one finger (their pointer finger) for contact <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:49">[00:11:49]</a>. This drastically increased the difficulty and pain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:02">[00:12:02]</a>.
*   **Changing Orientation:** Contestants were asked to face a different direction, further challenging their stability and comfort <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:23">[00:13:23]</a>.

## Adapting to the Challenge
Contestants devised various [[strategies to sustain morale during challenges | strategies to sustain morale]] and physical presence:
*   **Finding Comfortable Positions:** Continuously adjusting body position to alleviate discomfort <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:32">[00:00:32]</a>.
*   **Psychological Fortitude:** Relying on "perseverance" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a> and refusing to give up <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:50">[00:01:50]</a>.
*   **Teamwork (Limited):** Some contestants attempted to form alliances or find shared comfortable positions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:06">[00:10:06]</a>. One contestant, George, initially had his hand taped to the plane, providing an unfair advantage that was later removed for fairness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:51">[00:10:51]</a>.
*   **External Motivation:** In the final stages, contestants received calls from their loved ones (e.g., George, Niko's editor) or those they intended to give the jet to, providing a surge of [[motivational_strategies_to_endure_challenges | encouragement]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:53">[00:12:53]</a>.

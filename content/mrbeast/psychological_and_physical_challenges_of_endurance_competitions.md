Endurance competitions, such as the one organized by Mr. Beast where 100 kids competed against 100 adults for a cash prize [[contest_challenge_to_win_a_lamborghini | contest to win a Lamborghini]], present unique psychological and physical challenges. These challenges can test the participants' limits and highlight the dynamics of human resilience under prolonged stress.

## Psychological Challenges

One of the primary psychological challenges in endurance competitions is **maintaining motivation** throughout the event. Participants often begin with high spirits and enthusiasm, but as time progresses, they may struggle to stay motivated. In Mr. Beast's challenge, despite the initial excitement, participants had to endure the monotony for 100 hours while confined in a cube [[isolation_experiment_dynamics | isolation experiment dynamics]]. This is illustrated by one participant's remark, "So far I have been retaining my sanity" (<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:33">[00:00:33]</a>).

**Boredom** is another major psychological hurdle. As noted in the challenge, participants quickly became bored, leading some to peel tiles off the walls for entertainment [[creative_methods_of_encouraging_participation_through_rewards | creative methods of encouraging participation through rewards]] (<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:53">[01:53]</a>). Boredom can lead to recklessness, which may result in mistakes or an early exit from the competition [[contestants_experiences_and_mistakes | contestants' experiences and mistakes]].

**Decision-making under pressure** is also tested in such contests. Mr. Beast offered $1,000 to participants if they exited the challenge early, sparking internal conflicts about immediate reward versus long-term gain [[decision_making_under_pressure | decision making under pressure]] (<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:38">[00:00:38]</a>).

## Physical Challenges

**Fatigue** becomes a significant factor in endurance competitions. With limited activities and confined spaces, participants may struggle to remain physically active, leading to physical restlessness and discomfort. Changes in the environment, such as the deliberate shrinking of their living space, added a layer of difficulty [[adapting_living_conditions_for_a_prolonged_stay | adapting living conditions for a prolonged stay]], as participants had to adjust to increasingly confined quarters (<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:24">[04:24]</a>). Participants employed various strategies to cope with these challenges. The provision of comforts, such as coffee and pizzas, served as brief morale boosters [[highend_travel_entertainment_and_amenities | high end travel entertainment and amenities]] (<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:42">[03:42]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:07">[06:07]</a>).

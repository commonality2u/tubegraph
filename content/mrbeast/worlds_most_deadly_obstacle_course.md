From: [[mrbeast]] <br/> 

The "world's most deadly obstacle course" was designed to push a contestant, Mack, through a series of increasingly difficult [[Highstakes physical challenges | high-stakes physical challenges]] and mental tests, with a substantial monetary reward at stake <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>. The ultimate prize for completing all six levels was $800,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:28">[00:00:28]</a>. The creator stated that this was just the first of many "deadly, painful, absolutely insane obstacle courses" Mack would face <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:20">[00:00:20]</a>.

## The Contestant: Mack

The contestant, Mack, had previously participated in two other [[Survival challenges | challenge videos]] where he "tragically lost" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:37">[00:03:37]</a>. Despite his past losses, he was given "one final chance to redeem himself and win this money once and for all" due to being a "massive crowd favorite" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:43">[00:03:43]</a>. Prior to this challenge, Mack had lost a total of $1.5 million in previous competitions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:37">[00:14:37]</a>.

## Course Structure

The course consisted of six distinct levels, each designed to test different aspects of Mack's physical and mental resilience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:28">[00:00:28]</a>. Failure at any point typically resulted in a significant fall into a net or, in some cases, the loss of the prize money <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:56">[00:01:56]</a>.

### Level 1: High-Altitude Jumps

The first level required Mack to navigate a series of platforms suspended hundreds of feet in the air <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:05">[00:00:05]</a>, without falling 200 feet to the ground <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:05">[00:00:05]</a>. The final jump involved "double swinging hammers" and was the longest distance to clear <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:48">[00:00:48]</a>. Despite its perceived difficulty, this was described as the "easiest of the six obstacle courses" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:12">[00:01:12]</a>.

### Level 2: Balance/Strength Challenge

This level was set hundreds of feet in the air <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:28">[00:01:28]</a>. Mack was given a choice between two childhood games: a balance beam or monkey bars <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:45">[00:01:45]</a>. He chose the monkey bars <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:14">[00:02:14]</a>. Crucially, Mack had to remove his harness for this level <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:53">[00:01:53]</a>. If he fell, he would freefall 100 feet into a net, and his $800,000 prize would be lost <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:56">[00:01:56]</a>.

### Level 3: Memory Maze

Level three shifted from physical strength to mental acuity, designed to "test your brain" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:02">[00:04:02]</a>. Mack had to press and hold a red button to reveal a 44-tile pattern on the floor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:05">[00:04:05]</a>. To cross, he could only step on green tiles, relying entirely on memory once the button was released <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:22">[00:04:22]</a>. He was given 30 minutes to memorize the pattern <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:29">[00:04:29]</a>. Stepping on a red square would cause the money to explode, though a riot shield was provided for protection <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:33">[00:04:33]</a>. Mack successfully completed this challenge within the time limit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:29">[00:08:29]</a>.

### Level 4: Water Obstacle Course

This level was not set in the sky, but featured obstacles described as "almost impossible to accomplish" in a single attempt <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:03">[00:09:03]</a>. Mack had two options: attempt the course with one try and lose everything if he fell in the water, or hold a Feastables chocolate bar to gain unlimited attempts, provided he didn't drop the bar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:07">[00:09:07]</a>. Mack chose to hold the chocolate bar, which proved beneficial as he would have failed multiple times early on <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:31">[00:09:31]</a>. The course included a "log roll" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:42">[00:09:42]</a> and "not big red balls" (a reference to Wipeout's "Big Balls") which were particularly challenging <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:48">[00:10:48]</a>. It took Mack three hours to get past one section of the "balls" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:02">[00:12:02]</a>.

### Level 5: Elevated Rolling Logs

Initially, this level appeared to be a ground-based balance challenge, featuring three rolling logs that Mack had to cross while carrying eight bags of money <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:00">[00:13:00]</a>. The twist was revealed when a lever was pulled, raising the entire course 200 feet into the sky <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:40">[00:13:40]</a>. Mack had to transport all eight bags of money to the other side, and a single fall would result in losing everything <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:15">[00:13:15]</a>. After successfully crossing with the initial money, Mack was given the option to move on to the final challenge with whatever money he brought across, or return for more, risking his progress <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:18">[00:15:18]</a>. He chose to go back for all the money, completing multiple trips across the elevated logs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:38">[00:15:38]</a>.

### Level 6: Ultimate High-Altitude Course

The final and "most insane obstacle course" was also set 200 feet in the air <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:14">[00:19:14]</a>. Before starting, Mack was offered a choice: attempt an "umbrella cookie" challenge from a previous video, which he had "legitimate nightmares about" and lost $1.5 million to previously <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:48">[00:19:48]</a>, or attempt the new "giant, monstrosity, physics defying obstacle course" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:55">[00:19:55]</a>. Mack chose the physical course, throwing the cookie away, signifying "no turning back" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:32">[00:20:32]</a>. His parents were brought to watch him <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:39">[00:21:39]</a>.

The course included:
*   **Trapeze bars and a yellow platform** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:06">[00:21:06]</a>.
*   **The Wall of Death:** A section requiring careful navigation across a wall <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:02">[00:22:02]</a>.
*   **Dismount Circles:** Obstacles that required precise dismounting to avoid falling <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:04">[00:23:04]</a>.
*   **Giant Swinging Hammers and a Balance Beam:** A section with large swinging obstacles over a balance beam <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:25">[00:23:25]</a>.
*   **Final Cookie Jumps:** Despite his earlier choice, the "cookies" were brought back for the final part of the challenge <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:51">[00:23:51]</a>. Mack had to jump between trapeze bars to increasingly smaller cookies, with each jump getting further apart <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:14">[00:24:14]</a>. The final jump, to the "umbrella cookie," was "twice as far as the previous jump" and considered the "biggest hurdle in Mack's entire life" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:08">[00:26:08]</a>.

## Outcome

After an intense struggle on the final cookie jump, Mack successfully completed the entire course <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:34">[00:27:34]</a>, winning the $800,000 prize <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:46">[00:27:46]</a>. He expressed his intention to use the money to help three childhood friends who "don't have a lot of money," stating he would "make sure they don't worry about money anymore" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:48">[00:27:48]</a>.

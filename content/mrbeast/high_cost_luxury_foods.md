It was highly praised, with one participant stating it tasted "amazing" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:15">[00:01:15]</a> and like "silk clouds" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:22">[00:01:22]</a>.
*   **$250 Grilled Cheese** – This grilled cheese included a French homeman loaf of bread, Caciocavallo Podolico cheese, and 23 karat edible gold <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:41">[00:01:41]</a>. It was considered "so good" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:54">[00:01:54]</a> by participants.
*   **$1,000 Golden Opulence Sundae** – This dessert consisted of three scoops of Tahitian vanilla ice cream rolled in 23 karat edible gold, topped with chocolate sauce made from the world's rarest chocolate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:22">[00:02:22]</a>. ## High-End Gourmet Experiences

The prices escalated significantly as the group moved to dishes costing thousands of dollars:
*   **$2,000 Steak** – Described as the "finest beef" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:44">[00:03:44]</a>, this steak was rated highly, with multiple 10 out of 10 scores <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:15">[00:04:15]</a>. *   **$3,000 Pork** – A heritage old spot pork from North Carolina, served with a sauce made from a 26-year-old scotch <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:11">[00:05:11]</a>. Opinions varied, with some finding it good despite not being a "pork guy" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:40">[00:05:40]</a>, while others were not big fans <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:55">[00:05:55]</a>.

*   **$5,000 Tacos** – Each of these tacos cost about $1,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:00">[00:07:00]</a>, featuring tuna, caviar, and a shell made from squid ink <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:03">[00:07:03]</a>. The taste was described as "very salty" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:19">[00:07:19]</a> and like "the ocean is swimming in my mouth" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:36">[00:07:36]</a>.

While described as a "fancy version of things I've tasted before" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:37">[00:08:37]</a>, some felt it wasn't worth the price <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:44">[00:08:44]</a>.

### General Observation on Value
A key takeaway from the experience was that "more expensive food doesn't actually taste better" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:53">[00:08:53]</a>. The $3,000 pork, $5,000 tacos, and $6,000 short ribs were not as good as anticipated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:00">[00:09:00]</a>. This [[comparing_expensive_foods_to_regular_foods | comparison of expensive foods to regular foods]] highlighted that price doesn't always equate to superior taste.

## Format and Initial Elimination

The competition began with all contestants entering designated cubes, where the preliminary rule was straightforward: any person who left their cube or crossed a red line would be instantly eliminated. ## Group-Based Challenges

The next set of challenges were designed to foster [[psychology_and_group_dynamics_in_competitions | social dynamics]] and require participants to build alliances and negotiate who should leave. At one point, the group was tasked with convincing ten people to voluntarily exit within a limited timeframe. This mix of collaboration and coercion highlighted the interdependency and strategic thinking necessary to preserve one's spot in the game.

## Secret and Psychological Challenges

These challenges further emphasized personal integrity and social engineering. For example, when participants were asked to unanimously agree on a winner and failed, they had to select captains for each row. These captains faced a moral test—accept a monetary bribe that would eliminate their entire row or hold firm for a larger prize. The prize money offers escalated from $1,000 to $100,000, intensifying the pressure and [[decision_making_and_honesty_in_high_stakes_situations | testing loyalty and resolve]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:00">[00:06:00]</a>.

## The Spinning Wheel and Deal-Or-No-Deal Challenge

The competition took a strategic turn with the introduction of a spinning wheel that determined who would face the threat of elimination. Contestants took turns deciding whether to save or eliminate other players, often having to face the harsh reality of their decisions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:47">[00:08:47]</a>. This was followed by a “Deal or No Deal” style challenge where participants had to either trust or deceive their competitors regarding the contents of their briefcases, intensifying the [[psychological_and_physical_challenges_of_endurance_competitions | psychological warfare]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:52">[00:21:52]</a>.

## The Final Elimination and Conclusion

As the competition drew to a close, the remaining contestants had to face the ultimate decider: who could maintain their position longest in a small cube with $250,000 at stake. The continual challenges to agree on a sole winner were designed to force confrontations and [[strategies_and_teamwork_in_competitive_challenges | strategic alliances]]. The eventual winner was decided not only by survival skills but by the strength of personal connections and strategic alliances formed throughout the competition <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:55">[00:35:55]</a>.

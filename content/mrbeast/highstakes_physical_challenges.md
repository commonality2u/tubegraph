## The Six Levels

### Level 1: The Sky Jump

The first challenge involved jumping across platforms suspended hundreds of feet in the air <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:17">[00:00:17]</a>. This introductory course included double swinging hammers and required the longest distance jump <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:49">[00:00:49]</a>. Despite being the "easiest of the six obstacle courses" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:12">[00:01:12]</a>, Mack nearly failed to pass it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:39">[00:00:39]</a>.

### Level 2: Childhood Games, Extreme Edition

Elevated hundreds of feet in the air <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:28">[00:01:28]</a>, Level Two offered a choice between a balance beam and monkey bars <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:48">[00:01:48]</a>. Mack chose the monkey bars. Failure in this challenge meant a free fall of 100 feet into a net, and the loss of the $800,000 prize <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:56">[00:01:56]</a>.

### Level 3: The Memory Maze

This level shifted from strength to a test of the brain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:02">[00:04:02]</a>. Mack had 30 minutes to memorize a 44-tile pattern on the floor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:29">[00:04:29]</a>. He had to cross the floor by only stepping on green tiles; stepping on a red tile would cause the prize money to explode <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:22">[00:04:22]</a>. A riot shield was provided for protection <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:41">[00:04:41]</a>. Mack had to return to the start multiple times to re-check the pattern due to forgetting the end of the sequence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:19">[00:06:19]</a>. He completed the challenge with less than three minutes remaining <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:43">[00:07:43]</a>.

### Level 4: The Water Wipeout Course

Although not in the sky, this obstacle course was designed to be "almost impossible to accomplish in your one and only attempt" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:04">[00:09:04]</a>. Mack was given two options: attempt the course with one try and lose everything if he fell into the water <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:10">[00:09:10]</a>, or hold a Feastables chocolate bar to gain unlimited attempts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:16">[00:09:16]</a>. Mack chose the latter, using multiple attempts to navigate obstacles like log rolls and giant red balls <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:52">[00:10:52]</a>, often falling and getting back up. The challenge was so prolonged that it lasted until sunrise <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:54">[00:11:54]</a>, with one section taking him three hours to complete <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:02">[00:12:02]</a>.

### Level 5: Rolling Logs in the Sky

This level was built to exploit Mack's past issues with balance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:00">[00:13:00]</a>. It required crossing three rolling logs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:14">[00:13:14]</a> while transporting eight bags of money <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:15">[00:13:15]</a>, all 200 feet up in the sky <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:42">[00:13:42]</a>. Falling even once meant losing everything <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:18">[00:13:18]</a>. Mack successfully transported the first two bags of money, and then chose to go back for the remaining $600,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:38">[00:15:38]</a>, risking his current winnings on additional trips <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:19">[00:17:19]</a>. This demonstrated a [[high_stakes_challenge_strategies | strategy]] of increasing risk for greater reward.

### Level 6: The Ultimate Gauntlet

The final challenge was described as the "most insane obstacle course" in the video <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:16">[00:19:16]</a>. Mack was initially offered a choice: attempt an "umbrella cookie" challenge, which he had previously lost $1.5 million to <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:48">[00:19:48]</a>, or attempt the "giant, monstrosity, physics defying obstacle course" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:55">[00:19:55]</a>. Mack refused the cookie challenge, committing to the full course <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:32">[00:20:32]</a>.

The final course, 200 feet in the air <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:20">[00:22:20]</a>, included:
*   Trapeze bars and platforms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:06">[00:21:06]</a>.
*   The "Wall of Death" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:02">[00:22:02]</a>, a vertical structure requiring strategic climbing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:09">[00:22:09]</a>.
*   Spinning circles that required precise dismounts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:04">[00:23:04]</a>.
*   Giant swinging hammers paired with a balance beam <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:25">[00:23:25]</a>.
*   A series of jumps between "cookies" that became exponentially smaller and further apart <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:15">[00:24:15]</a>, with trapeze bars in between <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:24">[00:24:24]</a>.

The last jump to the final "umbrella" cookie was twice as far as the previous jump and represented the "biggest hurdle in Mack's entire life" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:09">[00:26:09]</a>. After a momentary fall and a subsequent recovery <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:00">[00:27:00]</a>, Mack successfully completed the final challenge <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:34">[00:27:34]</a>.

His total losses from these prior attempts amounted to $1.5 million <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:37">[00:14:37]</a>. Despite these setbacks and the immense pressure, he was given "one final chance to redeem himself" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:46">[00:03:46]</a>.

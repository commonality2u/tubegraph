## Environmental Hazards

The island's environment posed several threats:
*   **Heat and Sunburn** The initial hours on the island were hot <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:22">[00:00:22]</a>, and sunburn became an issue by the end of day one <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:57">[00:01:57]</a>.
*   **Poisonous Trees** The island is home to the "most dangerous tree on Earth," which contains poisonous sap that can be lethal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:42">[00:00:42]</a>. If it rains, the tree seeps poison that will burn skin, referred to as "death apple sap" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:12">[00:18:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:17">[00:18:17]</a>.
*   **Bugs** The island was infested with bugs that bit the group throughout the second night, making sleep difficult <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:45">[00:04:45]</a>.
*   **Rain and Storms** Despite initial forecasts, the group encountered multiple rain showers and storms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:07">[00:08:07]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:17">[00:10:17]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:42">[00:11:42]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:33">[00:17:33]</a>. One significant storm caused water to pool on their shelter roof, threatening its stability and soaking their beds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:20">[00:19:20]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:49">[00:19:49]</a>.

## Environmental Management and [[outdoor_survival_skills | Survival Strategies]]

To cope with the environment, the group implemented various [[survival_in_the_wilderness | survival strategies]]:
*   **Shelter Construction** Immediately upon arrival, the group prioritized building a shelter before dark <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:23">[00:00:23]</a>. Over subsequent days, they upgraded it to be larger and rain-proof using sturdy sticks and palm leaves <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:02">[00:02:02]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:25">[00:02:25]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:35">[00:02:35]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:21">[00:08:21]</a>.
*   **Water Management** Fresh water was a critical resource. The group had ten carts of water initially but were down to six by day two, emphasizing the need for responsible water consumption <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:13">[00:04:13]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:21">[00:04:21]</a>. They showered in the ocean to conserve drinking water <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:01">[00:04:01]</a>. They also collected coconut water <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:05">[00:13:05]</a>.
*   **Food Acquisition** Beyond the initial "bagged meals" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:11">[00:09:11]</a>, the group attempted to hunt chickens and fish to supplement their diet <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:12">[00:05:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:48">[00:09:48]</a>.
*   **Navigation and Exploration** The group explored the island, including climbing a mountain to find ancient ruins <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:19">[00:12:19]</a>. This expedition led to getting lost on the way back at night in the rain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:11">[00:17:11]</a>. This relates to [[responsible_hiking_practices]].
*   **Technology for Assistance** While the group relied on primitive [[outdoor_survival_skills | survival skills]], they also had a "survival crate" containing a flare for emergencies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:40">[00:01:40]</a>. Money transfer services were also mentioned in relation to arranging transport <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:55">[00:13:55]</a>. This is an example of [[using_technology_for_assistance_in_the_wilderness]].

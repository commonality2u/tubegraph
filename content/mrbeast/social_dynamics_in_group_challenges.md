This led to immediate friction and the formation of "enemies" based on perceived betrayal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:03">[00:05:03]</a>. This new challenge required participants to write down the number of a contestant they wished to eliminate, with the person receiving the most votes being removed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:56">[00:06:56]</a>.

This challenge directly exposed the consequences of previous social interactions:
*   Those who were lied to in the previous challenge sought to eliminate the liars <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:13">[00:07:13]</a>, reinforcing the idea that "it pays off to be honest" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:18">[00:07:18]</a>.

*   The group also strategically targeted younger participants like the 10-year-old, viewing them as a "strong-minded child" who "could win" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:16">[00:08:16]</a>.

## Final Eliminations: The Public Vote

In the final stages, with only five people remaining, the contestants voted for another challenge <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:49">[00:18:49]</a>. This time, the 95 already eliminated contestants voted on who should be removed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:54">[00:18:54]</a>. This twist emphasized the importance of overall [[team_dynamics_and_humor_in_long_challenges | interpersonal relationships]] forged throughout the competition. Contestants were unsure how they were perceived by the eliminated players, leading to nervousness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:04">[00:19:04]</a>.

Participant 54, known for his earlier deceptive tactics, correctly anticipated that he would receive the most votes due to his past actions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:26">[00:20:26]</a>. Despite this, the format allowed the two participants with the *fewest* votes to advance, meaning 54's prediction did not result in elimination, but rather confirmed his unpopularity among the eliminated participants. The final round saw 52 and 40 remaining, with 40 expressing the sentiment that "the good guys can win" and "it pays off to be honest" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:00">[00:25:00]</a>.

## Conclusion

The challenge underscored how individual motivations, strategic alliances, and the consequences of [[incentives_and_motivations_in_challenges | social interactions]] profoundly shaped the outcome. Deception led to targeted eliminations, while strategic voting blocks influenced survival. Ultimately, the cumulative effect of social dynamics played a critical role in determining who advanced and who was eliminated, culminating in a final test of trust and bluffing.

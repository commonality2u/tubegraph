## Building an Ice Wall

Antarctica's notorious winds were a constant threat to the camp's stability. To combat this, the team constructed a wall using snow blocks around the shelter. This wall served to break the wind and provide additional structural security <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:20">[00:01:20]</a>.

> [!info] Ice Wall Construction
> Cut snow blocks to build a windbreak around temporary shelters in snowy environments. This adds an important layer of protection against high winds [[building_a_shelter_with_limited_resources]].

## Coping with Perpetual Daylight

Another challenge was the constant daylight, as the sun never sets in Antarctica during certain periods. Without a natural signal to indicate day or night, maintaining routine activities, including meal times and sleep, becomes crucial. Despite this, the team's internal clock is disrupted, making rest periods difficult <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:47">[00:01:47]</a>.

## Adapting to Environmental Conditions

Handling bodily needs, such as peeing and pooping, posed unique challenges due to the extreme cold. The crew had to pee in bottles, as doing so in the open on the snow was not allowed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:23">[00:03:23]</a>. ## Lessons Learned

Building a shelter in extreme conditions requires:
- **Strategic Location**: Utilize natural features for protection.
- **Secure Anchoring**: Proper setup techniques are vital to prevent structural failure.
- **Wind Blocks**: Construct barriers using available materials to shield from the wind.
- **Routine Maintenance**: Regularly check and reinforce the shelter to ensure it withstands ongoing weather changes [[impact_of_weather_on_survival]].

The experience in Antarctica provides a valuable case study on the importance of preparation and adaptability in extreme environments. The tactics used by the team can be applied in other severe conditions to ensure safety and sustainability [[strategies_for_longterm_endurance_challenges]].

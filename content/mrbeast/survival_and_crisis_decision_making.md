## Initial Challenges and Adaptations

In the first trap, a contestant named Mack had to break out of a cage and climb a roof before walls closed in, with $100,000 on the line <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:13">[00:00:13]</a>. Despite initial confusion and frustration, Mack managed to escape, winning his first $100,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:58">[00:00:58]</a>. This success led to an immediate decision point: risk the winnings for a chance at $200,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:05">[00:01:05]</a>. Mack confidently chose to continue, despite the knowledge that ten more traps stood between him and $1,000,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:15">[00:01:15]</a>.

The second trap involved navigating a laser grid, where a single touch would result in immediate failure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:58">[00:01:58]</a>. Mack's [[survival tactics | survival tactics]] included crawling to avoid lower lasers, but he then faced higher, unavoidable beams <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:17">[00:02:17]</a>. His quick inquiry about the time limit for the door to close if he touched a laser showed strategic thinking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:35">[00:02:35]</a>. He successfully passed this trap, again opting to risk his winnings for $300,000, even as the traps became progressively harder <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:08">[00:03:08]</a>.

## Physical and Mental Demands

The third trap involved a "boulder run," requiring speed and agility to outrun a massive rolling boulder after grabbing money <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:41">[00:03:41]</a>. Mack's unexpected speed allowed him to evade the boulder, leading to another moment of [[contestants decision making | decision making]] regarding whether to continue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:11">[00:04:11]</a>. He decided to attempt one more trap, acknowledging the increasing difficulty <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:35">[00:04:35]</a>.

Trap four placed Mack inside a giant ball, navigating a life-sized video game course with a five-minute time limit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:56">[00:04:56]</a>. Falling into foam meant losing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:08">[00:05:08]</a>. Balancing in the ball proved challenging, multiplying movements and increasing the risk of falling <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:13">[00:06:13]</a>. Despite the perceived difficulty, Mack "literally obliterated" the final stage, winning $400,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:36">[00:06:36]</a>. His repeated willingness to push forward, despite the increasing difficulty, highlighted a high-stakes gambling mentality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:06">[00:07:06]</a>.

## Extreme Conditions and Ingenuity

Trap five involved swimming through an underwater chamber to pick four locks using a clothes hanger, all while $500,000 drifted towards a bonfire <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:25">[00:07:25]</a>. The challenge became progressively harder as Mack went deeper into the water, requiring him to hold his breath longer with less time for each lock <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:40">[00:08:40]</a>. This task showcased extreme physical endurance and precise [[improvisation and resourcefulness in survival situations | improvisation and resourcefulness in survival situations]]. He successfully retrieved the money, once again choosing to risk it for $600,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:07">[00:10:07]</a>.

The sixth trap was presented as the "easiest" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:16">[00:10:16]</a>: choosing between two chocolate bars, with the "correct" choice earning $100,000. Mack's choice was successful, bringing his total to $600,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:59">[00:10:59]</a>. Despite concerns about losing, he agreed to "just one more" challenge, aiming for $700,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:23">[00:11:23]</a>.

The seventh trap leveraged Mack's biggest fear: heights <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:00">[00:12:00]</a>. Blindfolded, he was taken 200 feet in the air and had to walk across an "absurdly thin balance beam" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:27">[00:12:27]</a>. This scenario demanded intense focus and mental fortitude to overcome deep-seated fears <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:36">[00:12:36]</a>. Despite initial struggles, he successfully traversed the beam, winning $700,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:07">[00:13:07]</a>. After this, he felt "so close" to the million-dollar prize and decided to continue for the final three traps <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:48">[00:13:48]</a>.

## The Ultimate Test and Consequences

The penultimate trap combined elements of the "Squid Game," requiring Mack to precisely cut out shapes from a honeycomb cookie without breaking it, on a glass bridge <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:13">[00:14:13]</a>. Breaking the cookie would cause the bridge to drop, and he would lose everything <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:30">[00:14:30]</a>. Mack employed the "licking" strategy to melt the sugar, similar to the method used in the show <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:52">[00:14:52]</a>. He took his time, understanding the high stakes involved <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:23">[00:15:23]</a>.

After successfully cutting out a circle and a triangle, he faced the hardest shape: an umbrella <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:47">[00:16:47]</a>. A 10-minute timer was added to this challenge <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:59">[00:16:59]</a>. Mack used a stack of money and even a shoe as a "hammer" to carefully break off pieces <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:18">[00:17:18]</a>. Despite his best efforts and being close to finishing, he tragically broke the umbrella with 60 seconds left, losing all his $700,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:23">[00:18:23]</a>. This moment highlighted the harsh reality of "quitting while you're ahead" in such high-stakes scenarios <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:04">[00:19:04]</a>. However, Mack was later given an additional $100,000 for his efforts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:17">[00:19:17]</a>.

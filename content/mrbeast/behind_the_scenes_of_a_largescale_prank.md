## The Prank Concept

In this prank, a popular YouTube creator planned to fill his brother's house with elephant toothpaste as a counter-prank. ## What is Elephant Toothpaste?

Elephant toothpaste is a foamy chemical reaction caused by the rapid decomposition of hydrogen peroxide when it comes in contact with a catalyst, typically potassium iodide or yeast mixed with warm water. This experiment is popular in classrooms due to its rapid, visual, and safe reaction under controlled conditions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:20">[00:00:20]</a>.

## Preparing for Explosion

The scale of this prank was eye-watering. It involved planning for the reaction of enough foam to potentially fill multiple houses, although the creators opted to use just enough to prevent structural damage. The setups included 14 drums of reactants prepared throughout the house, marking a new record for this experiment. The intent was to have the foam push out of every window and door <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:31">[00:03:31]</a>.

## Ensuring Safety and Coordination

The prank required meticulous coordination. Besides getting consent from the homeowner's roommate, every critical belonging was temporarily removed from the house. The prank's activation was timed to coincide precisely with the prankee's arrival, ensuring maximum effect. Safety protocols included staying at a safe distance during the activation. When the chemical reaction was finally set off, the foam even found its way up the chimney, providing a spectacular sight <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:19">[00:07:19]</a>.

In a recent social experiment detailed in a [[social_experiments_with_wealth_perception | popular video]], two strangers, Bailey and Suzie, faced a unique challenge: [[100day_survival_challenge | survive 100 days]] together in an isolation chamber for a chance to win half a million dollars. This experience provided valuable insights into the intricacies of decision-making under pressure. The decisions they made throughout the challenge were influenced by various psychological and environmental factors, demonstrating how pressure can affect human behavior.

## Initial Impressions and Social Dynamics

The experiment began with an introduction between Bailey and Suzie, who had never met before. From the outset, their dynamic was tested through awkward interactions and adapting to a new environment with a stranger. This initial stage is crucial as it sets the tone for decision-making throughout the experiment (<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:11">[00:00:11]</a>).

## Environmental Influence on Decisions

The conditions in the cube greatly influenced the [[survival_challenge_in_a_confined_space | couple's decision-making processes]]. They faced constant bright lights and a monotonous food supply which quickly introduced stress and discomfort (<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:46">[00:00:46]</a> and <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:12">[00:02:12]</a>). These environmental pressures pushed them to devise [[using_creative_methods_to_complete_a_task | strategies for entertainment and comfort]], such as creating playing cards from scratch (<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:23">[00:02:23]</a>).

## Temptations and Financial Decisions

At various points in the challenge, Bailey and Suzie were offered temptations, such as a private chef introduction on day 10 for $50,000. Remarkably, they declined, demonstrating a strategic decision to conserve funds despite immediate discomfort (<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:11">[00:05:11]</a>). However, as time passed and pressures mounted, their resilience weakened, leading them to eventually spend on comforts like coffee and books (<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:58">[00:09:58]</a>).

## The Regret of Missed Opportunities

One critical moment of decision-making under pressure involved a [[concept_and_appeal_of_mystery_boxes | mystery box on day 60]], containing Teslas and cash, which the pair declined due to skepticism and fear of loss (<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:03">[00:16:03]</a>). This decision, motivated by fear and uncertainty, resulted in significant regret, highlighting how pressure can lead to [[the_psychological_impact_of_highstakes_decisions | overly cautious choices and missed opportunities]].

## Group Dynamics and Individual Differences

As time progressed, the impact of individual personality differences on decision-making under pressure became more apparent. Bailey’s energetic nature often clashed with Suzie’s preferences, causing friction and impacting group decisions (<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:06">[00:21:06]</a>). Their ability to navigate these [[psychology_and_group_dynamics_in_competitions | differences was crucial]] to their ultimate success.

## Final Decision: Trust and Cooperation

The culmination of their decision-making journey occurred on day 99, when they were faced with the option to either split or steal the $360,000. Despite the tempting allure to [[moral_dilemmas_in_moneyrelated_challenges | steal for personal gain]], both Suzie and Bailey chose to split the prize, cementing their partnership and mutual trust developed during their shared hardship (<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:01">[00:25:01]</a>).

## Congressional Initiative on Nuclear Red Lines

On June 22nd, 2023, Senators Richard Blumenthal (Democrat, Connecticut) and Lindsey Graham (Republican, South Carolina) introduced a joint resolution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:52">[00:08:52]</a>. This resolution seeks to establish a clear stance on potential nuclear threats, stating that any Russian or Belarusian use of a strategic or tactical nuclear weapon, or actions leading to a nuclear power plant meltdown, would be considered an act of war under [[potential_nato_implications_in_response_to_nuclear_threats | Article 5 of NATO]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:00">[00:09:00]</a>.

The intent behind this resolution is to send a direct warning to Russian President Vladimir Putin and those who would follow such orders, making it clear that these actions would initiate a direct military conflict with the United States and its allies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:32">[00:09:32]</a>.

### Limitations of the Resolution

While the Senators' initiative is seen as an important step in initiating a crucial conversation, it faces several limitations:
*   **Executive Privilege** The interpretation of [[potential_nato_implications_in_response_to_nuclear_threats | Article 5]] falls under executive privilege, not congressional authority <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:55">[00:09:55]</a>.
*   **Non-Binding Nature** A joint resolution is a statement of intent, not a legally binding law <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:03">[00:10:03]</a>.
*   **Alliance Decision** [[potential_nato_implications_in_response_to_nuclear_threats | Article 5]] decisions are made collectively by NATO allies, not unilaterally by the United States <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:10">[00:10:10]</a>.

## Strategic Context and Russian Perceptions

The discussions on nuclear policy are particularly relevant given the ongoing Ukrainian counter-offensive. While early reports indicated slow progress, the Ukrainians have recently shifted their targeting to Russian ammunition dumps and critical supply bridges <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:05">[00:02:05]</a>. Notably, the Chonhar crossing, a vital supply route to Crimea, was hit, disrupting Russian logistics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:27">[00:05:27]</a>. This could precede a major push towards the Sea of Azov, aiming to cut off Russian forces in southwestern Ukraine and Crimea <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:06">[00:03:06]</a>.

For [[strategic_and_diplomatic_implications_for_russia | Russia]], Crimea is considered an integral part of its territory, annexed in 2014 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:27">[00:10:27]</a>. Any significant push towards Crimea or direct attacks on [[strategic_and_diplomatic_implications_for_russia | Russian]] proper territory could trigger a perception within [[strategic_and_diplomatic_implications_for_russia | Russia]] that a "real war" requiring defensive nuclear weapons is warranted <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:39">[00:10:39]</a>.

Internationally, no country, including China or Nicaragua, has recognized [[strategic_and_diplomatic_implications_for_russia | Russia's]] annexation of Crimea <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:50">[00:10:50]</a>. However, [[strategic_and_diplomatic_implications_for_russia | Russia's]] internal view of Crimea as equivalent to Moscow is the critical factor for potential nuclear escalation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:10">[00:11:10]</a>.

## The Need for Renewed Discussion

The United States has not engaged in a comprehensive public conversation on [[shifts_in_us_foreign_policy_and_its_implications | strategic nuclear policy]] concerning [[strategic_and_diplomatic_implications_for_russia | Russia]] since the 1980s <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:48">[00:11:48]</a>. The current geopolitical landscape necessitates this dialogue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:55">[00:11:55]</a>.

President Joe Biden's long experience, having been involved in discussions since the first nuclear weapon detonation, provides a unique and valuable perspective for navigating these complex issues in the months ahead <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:03">[00:12:03]</a>. The current circumstances suggest the conflict is entering a new stage, requiring deeper consideration of these critical questions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:37">[00:12:37]</a>.

## [[changes_in_media_structure_and_foreign_reporting | Evolution and Decline of US Media Credibility]]

The [[changes_in_media_structure_and_foreign_reporting | challenges facing credible news consumption]] in the United States began in the 1980s with the advent of the fax machine <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:54">[00:00:54]</a>. Previously, major newspapers like the Des Moines Register and larger TV stations maintained numerous roving reporters, including many stationed abroad <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:55">[00:00:55]</a>. This system ensured multiple layers of fact-checking and editorial oversight from foreign bureaus, copy editors, and editors before stories reached the public <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:14">[00:01:14]</a>.

However, [[impact_of_technology_on_media_credibility | technological advancements]] steadily eroded this structure:
*   **Fax Machine (1980s)**: Reduced the need for extensive staffing abroad and led to the elimination of copy editors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:40">[00:01:40]</a>.
*   **Email and Attachments (1990s)**: Further decreased the number of staff required <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:48">[00:01:48]</a>.
*   **Internet Rise (2000s)**: Most foreign bureaus were closed, and roving reporters were largely replaced by local "stringers" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:54">[00:01:54]</a>. While stringers provided local insights, their reports lacked the previous layers of fact-checking and were often influenced by personal biases <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:15">[00:02:15]</a>.
*   **Algorithms (Present)**: Today, algorithms often write newspaper stories and scrape the web for information, with fact-checking largely absent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:25">[00:02:25]</a>.

This [[impact_of_technology_on_media_credibility | technological shift]] has created an environment where a charismatic politician can easily dismiss unfavorable reports as "fake news" and foster political movements built around personal trust rather than institutional integrity or factual information <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:37">[00:02:37]</a>. The necessary factfulness for an educated populace to govern itself is largely gone <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:00">[00:03:00]</a>.

## [[challenges_facing_credible_news_consumption | Information Desert]] Outside and Inside the US

This "information desert" is not unique to the United States <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:28">[00:03:28]</a>.
*   **Middle East**: Regions with single-party rule often experience an information vacuum, where the rumor mill becomes the primary news source <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:34">[00:03:34]</a>. This made it difficult for American efforts in the Middle East to convey basic truths, as rumors spread via email and Twitter were widely accepted <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:45">[00:03:45]</a>.
*   **Russia**: The rumor mill is augmented by a state monopoly on information <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:03">[00:04:03]</a>. Information from state-controlled radio and TV is essentially 100% propaganda <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:10">[00:04:10]</a>.

In the United States, media operates as a business driven by viewership, prioritizing content that aligns with audience desires over truth or necessity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:19">[00:04:19]</a>.
*   **Cable News**: Channels like Fox and MSNBC primarily offer opinion programs with minimal news content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:34">[00:04:34]</a>.
*   **Opinion-Based Outlets**: Newsmax, for example, is entirely opinion-based, akin to media environments found in the Middle East <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:45">[00:04:45]</a>.
*   **Social Media**:
    *   Twitter has become "colorful" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:53">[00:04:53]</a>.
    *   Facebook is considered one of the worst sources for news, particularly for those aged 25-45 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:11">[00:05:11]</a>.
    *   TikTok, used by those under 25, is considered at least better than Facebook <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:18">[00:05:18]</a>.
*   **Traditional News**: Nightly News broadcasts are now significantly interspersed with drug commercials <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:03">[00:05:03]</a>.

## Recommended News Sources

Finding reliable news sources is crucial for an informed perspective <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:23">[00:05:23]</a>.

### International News Sources

*   **Al Jazeera**: Considered the most reliable international news source overall, especially outside of Middle Eastern affairs where it is heavily biased <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:33">[00:05:33]</a>. They prioritize interviewing multiple people with multiple viewpoints, reflecting a traditional journalistic approach <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:47">[00:05:47]</a>.
*   **France 24**: Ranks second, being one of the few remaining entities with global bureaus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:55">[00:05:55]</a>.
*   **BBC**: Has significantly declined in quality over the last 20 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:04">[00:06:04]</a>.
*   **Canadian Broadcasting**: Considered the "bottom of the barrel," being 100% opinion and party line <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:11">[00:06:11]</a>.
*   **Local News Sources Worldwide**: Can offer unique, albeit biased, perspectives not available elsewhere <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:18">[00:06:18]</a>.

### United States News Sources

For national news within the United States, three entities are particularly noteworthy:
*   **The Wall Street Journal**: While primarily focused on financial news, their coverage in that domain is highly regarded <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:50">[00:06:50]</a>.
*   **Bloomberg**: Although not as strong as it once was, Bloomberg offers a broad and even-handed coverage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:57">[00:06:57]</a>.
*   **Straight Arrow News**: A newer project aiming to provide non-partisan views on various topics, from finance to international affairs, by gathering contributors from across the political spectrum <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:04">[00:07:04]</a>.

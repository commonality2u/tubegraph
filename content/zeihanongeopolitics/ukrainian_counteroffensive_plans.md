### Significant Casualties and Equipment Shortages
Russian forces have sustained immense losses, particularly in the battle for Bakhmut. Conservatively, 20,000 Russians have died and 100,000 have been injured in Bakhmut over six months <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:26">[00:02:26]</a>. This single battle, which is not strategically significant, has significantly depleted Russian forces <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:44">[00:02:44]</a>.

Overall Russian losses since the operation began are estimated to be at least 100,000, possibly as many as 200,000 men killed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:00">[00:03:00]</a>. Additionally, at least 100,000 have been injured in Bakhmut and likely another 100,000 elsewhere <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:10">[00:03:10]</a>.

Russian troop numbers:
*   February 2022: Started with approximately 100,000-140,000 men <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:19">[00:03:19]</a>.

This implies Russia is likely looking at a second partial mobilization of 200,000-250,000 troops <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:48">[00:03:48]</a>.

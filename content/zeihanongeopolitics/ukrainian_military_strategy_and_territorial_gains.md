## Incursion into Kursk Province, Russia

In the last two weeks, [[ukrainian_military_strategy_and_offensive_preparations | Ukrainians]] have invaded Russia proper, capturing approximately 1,000 square kilometers in the province of Kursk <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:30">[00:01:30]</a>. This territorial gain by [[ukrainian_military_strategy_and_offensive_preparations | Ukraine]] is larger than the entirety of what the [[russian_military_strategies_and_advancements_in_ukraine | Russian army]] achieved in the Donbas region over the last 18 months <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:41">[00:02:41]</a>. From a Ukraine war perspective, this is a "huge amount of territory" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:50">[00:02:50]</a>.

### Strategic Objectives and Tactics
[[ukrainian_military_strategy_and_offensive_preparations | The Ukrainian plan]] aims to seize a "sizable bite" of territory <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:42">[00:01:42]</a>.
Key actions include:
*   **Bridge Destruction** [[ukrainian_military_strategy_and_offensive_preparations | Ukrainian forces]] have destroyed the three permanent bridges over the Sem River, an east-west river cutting through Kursk Province <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:47">[00:01:47]</a>. This action makes it extremely difficult for [[russian_military_strategies_and_advancements_in_ukraine | Russians]] to reinforce the areas of the incursion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:59">[00:01:59]</a>. As a result, [[russian_military_strategies_and_advancements_in_ukraine | Russian resupply]] is limited to pontoons, which are vulnerable to artillery <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:56">[00:02:56]</a>.
*   **Multi-Axis Expansion** [[ukrainian_military_strategy_and_offensive_preparations | Ukrainians]] are expanding on at least four different axes: Northwest, Northeast, North, and East <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:06">[00:02:06]</a>.
*   **Targeting Belgorod** A primary objective is to neutralize the city of Belgorod, which serves as the "tip of the spear" for [[russian_military_strategies_and_advancements_in_ukraine | Russian forces]] and a major logistical hub for armies and artillery in the northern sector <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:17">[00:03:17]</a>. By taking the southern half of Kursk Province, [[ukrainian_military_strategy_and_offensive_preparations | Ukraine]] aims to take out most of the infrastructure feeding into Belgorod, effectively neutralizing a city of a million people without a direct conquest <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:40">[00:03:40]</a>.

[[ukrainian_military_strategy_and_offensive_preparations | The Ukrainian aim]] is to temporarily occupy about half of Kursk province, roughly 6,000 square miles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:14">[00:02:14]</a>.

## [[Strategic changes in Ukrainian military tactics | Strategic Advantages]] and [[Ukrainian Counteroffensive Strategy | Counteroffensive Effectiveness]]

[[strategic_changes_in_ukrainian_military_tactics | The Ukrainian incursion]] has successfully triggered a "war of movement" within Russian territory, something [[ukrainian_military_strategy_and_offensive_preparations | they]] could not achieve on their own land <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:52">[00:04:52]</a>. Previous [[ukrainian_counteroffensive in the war | counteroffensives]] in Donbas and near Crimea were hampered by [[russian_military_strategies and advancements in Ukraine | Russian multi-layered defensive lines]], including tank and artillery placements, and extensive minefields <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:07">[00:05:07]</a>. In Kursk, these heavy defensive lines are absent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:31">[00:05:31]</a>, allowing [[strategic_changes_in_ukrainian_military_tactics | Ukrainians to find a battlefield]] that plays to their strengths <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:36">[00:05:36]</a>.

This operation involved scraping up the last of [[ukrainian_military_strategy_and_offensive_preparations | Ukraine's reserve units]] and some advanced units training with NATO <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:39">[00:04:39]</a>. While [[ukrainian_military_strategy_and_offensive_preparations | Ukraine]] may not have deep reserves <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:47">[00:04:47]</a>, [[russian_military_strategies_and_advancements_in_ukraine | Russia]] also lacks the manpower to eject them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:48">[00:05:48]</a>. [[russian_military_strategies_and_advancements_in_ukraine | Russia's manpower pipeline]] is running low, especially in leadership, with a former bodyguard reportedly assigned to run the Kursk operation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:36">[00:06:36]</a>. [[russian_military_strategies_and_advancements_in_ukraine | Russia]] typically requires a 3:1 to 7:1 manpower superiority for attacks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:05">[00:07:05]</a>, which they cannot achieve in Kursk given the presence of 10,000 [[ukrainian_military_strategy_and_offensive_preparations | Ukrainian]] troops and support echelons <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:57">[00:06:57]</a>. [[russian_military_strategies_and_advancements_in_ukraine | Russia]] has been forced to mobilize nearly all remaining military forces, even "denuding" Pacific, Baltic, and Arctic theaters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:26">[00:07:26]</a>, and has raided security forces from a cosmodrome <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:44">[00:07:44]</a>.

[[ukrainian_military_strategy_and_offensive_preparations | Ukrainians]] are currently facing lightly armed and untrained [[russian_military_strategies_and_advancements_in_ukraine | Russian troops]] without significant artillery support <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:11">[00:08:11]</a>. While [[ukrainian_military_strategy_and_offensive_preparations | Ukrainian forces]] are likely taking heavy casualties in this active war zone, especially in armor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:23">[00:08:23]</a>, [[ukrainian_military_strategy_and_offensive_preparations | they]] have found a relatively low-cost way to leverage their strengths, neutralize Belgorod, and shift the war's narrative <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:35">[00:08:35]</a>.

## [[Potential Outcomes and Geopolitical Consequences of Ukrainian Successes | Geopolitical Implications]]

The most significant shift resulting from the Kursk operation is the demonstration that [[russian_military_strategies and advancements in Ukraine | Russia's nuclear threats]] have proven to be bluffs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:09">[00:09:09]</a>. [[ukrainian_military_strategy_and_offensive_preparations | Ukraine]] has crossed the international border in force, effectively "castrated" the [[russian_military_strategies_and_advancements_in_ukraine | Russian military]] in the area <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:16">[00:09:16]</a>, and is poised to neutralize Belgorod without [[russian_military_strategies_and_advancements_in_ukraine | Russia]] launching nuclear weapons <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:22">[00:09:22]</a>.

This incursion, where [[russian_military_strategies_and_advancements in_ukraine | Russian forces]] are broken in the region, has not triggered nuclear retaliation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:00">[00:10:00]</a>. This suggests that [[russian_military_strategies_and_advancements_in_ukraine | Russia]] is either unwilling or unable to escalate to that level <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:13">[00:10:13]</a>. This new understanding implies that the conservatism in Western capitals regarding challenging [[russian_military_strategies_and_advancements_in_ukraine | Russia]] "is about to evaporate" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:31">[00:10:31]</a>. If [[ukrainian_military_strategy_and_offensive_preparations | Ukraine]] can conduct such operations without nuclear counter-reaction, then previous [[russian_military_strategies_and_advancements_in_ukraine | Russian threats]] appear "meaningless" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:46">[00:10:46]</a>.

While a "red line" for nuclear response might exist, it is now known that it does not involve population centers, territory, or cities within dozens of miles of [[russian_military_strategies_and_advancements_in_ukraine | Russia's]] internationally recognized borders <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:51">[00:10:51]</a>. This shift is expected to lead to new types of [[ukrainian_counteroffensive plans | offensive operations]] with the full support of Western countries in the coming months <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:11">[00:11:11]</a>.

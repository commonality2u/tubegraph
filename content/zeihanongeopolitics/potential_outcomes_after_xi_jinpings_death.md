He has purged the system of individuals with ambition or competence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:43">[00:00:43]</a>. The bureaucracy is actively working to prevent anyone under 25 from entering the system by targeting patents and college dissertations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:50">[00:00:50]</a>. This has resulted in a situation where [[centralized_power_in_china_under_xi_jinping | Xi]] rules alone, driving the system "into the ground" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>.

While China is technically a federal system, the purges and the Communist Party's control have transformed it into a "super unitary" system <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:45">[00:01:45]</a>. Decisions are solely made in Beijing, as [[centralized_power_in_china_under_xi_jinping | Xi]] has removed competent individuals from regional and local governments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:50">[00:01:50]</a>.

## China's Unique Governance Challenges

China presents a difficult geography to govern due to its varied regions looking to different parts of the world, and country, for leadership and economic growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:02">[00:02:02]</a>.

### Geographic Divisions and Their Characteristics

*   **Southern Coast (Fujian to Guangzhou)**: This region lacks an interior, access to local agricultural products, and interconnected infrastructure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:24">[00:02:24]</a>. Historically, these cities have obtained the majority of their food from outside the Asian mainland for over 1500 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:51">[00:02:51]</a>. They possess individual identities and have traditionally been prone to secession <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:46">[00:02:46]</a>.
*   **Central Region (Shanghai to Chongqing)**: Centered around the Yangtze River, this area is a distinct economic unit with its own political and cultural identity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:06">[00:03:06]</a>. It functions as a corporatist, industrialist, and financial hub <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:56">[00:03:56]</a>.
*   **North China Plain (Yellow River)**: This flat region has historically been too vast to be controlled by a single power until the industrial era <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:25">[00:03:25]</a>. It has typically been divided by warlords who would attack waterworks during conflicts, leading to a nationalistic and militaristic culture <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:33">[00:03:33]</a>.

### Historical Governance Models

Chinese history demonstrates two main models for governance, neither of which has proven stable long-term:

1.  **Regional Autonomy**: Each region is given as much autonomy as it can handle, leading to the entire system fragmenting, with the North often devolving into civil war <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:14">[00:04:14]</a>.
2.  **[[centralized_power_in_china_under_xi_jinping | Hyper-Concentrated Authority]]**: Power is overly concentrated in Beijing under the emperor or the Communist Party's General Secretary, holding everything as tightly as possible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:29">[00:04:29]</a>.

Neither model has lasted for long, and no effective middle ground, like a confederal system, has emerged without leading to friction and conflict <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:47">[00:04:47]</a>.

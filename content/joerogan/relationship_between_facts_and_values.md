## The Complexity of Facts and Values

The complexity arises from attempting to understand how scientific facts, which are often considered objective, relate to values, which are subjective and often seen as ethical or moral imperatives. Peterson highlights that these discussions are not merely theoretical but pertain to real-world applications that have been subjects of contention among philosophers indefinitely <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:10">[00:04:10]</a>.

### Philosophical Origins

The discussion around facts and values can be traced back to David Hume, who famously articulated the "is-ought problem," which questions how one can derive prescriptive statements (what ought to be) directly from descriptive statements (what is) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:20">[00:04:20]</a>.

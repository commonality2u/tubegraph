Weight cutting is a widely discussed subject in the world of combat sports like [[mma_training_strategies | MMA]]. It involves athletes losing a significant amount of weight in a short period before a fight to qualify for a lower weight class, giving them a potential advantage over their opponent. However, the practice is not without risks and controversy.

## The Process

Weight cutting typically occurs in two phases: diet and dehydration. Fighters often start by reducing their food intake and altering their diets to lose body fat and muscle mass. The most drastic weight loss takes place just before the weigh-in, where fighters dehydrate their bodies to shed water weight. This might involve long sessions in saunas or hot baths to sweat out excess fluid.

## Risks and Concerns

The health risks associated with weight cutting are significant. Fighters may experience extreme dehydration, which can lead to severe cramping, organ failure, or even death in extreme cases. Dehydration also affects cognitive function, impacting a fighter's performance and decision-making abilities during the bout.

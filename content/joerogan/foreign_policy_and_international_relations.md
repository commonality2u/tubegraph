## U.S. Involvement in Foreign Conflicts

The conversation highlights the U.S. media's role in perpetuating conflicts, exemplified by the Iraq War, which was entered under false pretenses regarding weapons of mass destruction. This misstep costs countless lives and demonstrates the impact of media complicity and governmental narratives on foreign policy decisions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:35">[00:03:35]</a>.

## Criticism of U.S. Foreign Policy

Kyle Kalinski criticizes both Republican and Democratic administrations for continuing military engagements without clear exit strategies. He argues that the promise to end wars, such as those in Iraq and Afghanistan—often made during election campaigns—rarely translates into action once a candidate assumes office. This pattern is evident in the campaigns and presidencies of Obama, Trump, and Biden, who have all been criticized for hawkish stances once in office <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:00">[00:09:00]</a>.

## Media's Influence on International Relations

The media's framing of U.S. foreign intervention is crucial. The narrative often shifts depending on political alignment, as seen in the discussion of Trump and North Korea. The media heavily scrutinized Trump's diplomatic overtures to North Korea despite the goal of de-escalation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:24:58">[03:24:58]</a>.

The elusive yellow caiman, a remarkable creature native to Colombia, has recently been rediscovered despite being considered extinct since the 1980s. This intriguing species, distinguished by its wild green-yellow coloration, was last seen alive in a zoo in the 1980s when its last known specimen died <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:16">[00:00:16]</a>.

## Background

The habitat of the yellow caiman has been largely inaccessible due to the control of the region by FARC rebels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:29">[00:00:29]</a>. This has led to no recent expeditions to confirm its existence in the wild until the breakthrough discovery by two parties: a Colombian scientist named Sergio Rianna and an exploring biologist <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:37">[00:00:37]</a>.

## Unique Characteristics

Yellow caimans are identified by their striking coloration and impressive ability to go without food for extended periods, similar to many other members of the alligator family <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:21">[00:01:21]</a>. Despite their fierce appearance, which includes powerful jaws, yellow caimans primarily prey by swallowing food whole and using a unique rolling behavior to tear off chunks of meat <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:42">[00:01:42]</a>.

## Rediscovery

The quest to find the yellow caiman was fraught with challenges due to the civil unrest in Colombia. However, both explorers, Rianna in Colombia and a visiting biologist, managed to rediscover the species within a month of each other <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:42">[00:00:42]</a>. 

## Conservation Efforts

Following its rediscovery, there's a concerted effort to manage the yellow caiman's habitat and ensure its survival. Sergio Rianna is now overseeing the ongoing conservation and management activities. These actions focus on understanding the caiman's population dynamics, genetic traits, food sources, and the pressure from hunting it can sustain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:46">[00:03:46]</a>.

## Impact on Ecology

Caimans play a critical role in their ecosystem as apex predators. The rediscovery of the yellow caiman provides a unique opportunity to study the species and its interactions with local biodiversity, ensuring it forms part of the conservation agenda in Colombia.

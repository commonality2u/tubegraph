The Younger Dryas Cataclysm is a hypothesized cataclysmic event, involving a series of massive comet impacts, that significantly impacted Earth's climate and ecosystems approximately 12,800 to 11,600 years ago. This period, known as the Younger Dryas, saw a sudden return to glacial conditions after a period of warming and is believed to coincide with significant changes in human history and prehistoric civilizations.

## Evidence of Cataclysmic Impacts

The concept of the Younger Dryas Cataclysm emerged from research indicating a series of comet impacts on the North American ice cap around 12,800 years ago, which are thought to have triggered a global catastrophe wiping out prehistoric civilizations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:02:06">[02:02:06]</a>. This idea is supported by the presence of a "black matte" layer found at various Clovis sites across North America, marking a clear distinction between the dramastic ecological and archaeological shifts occurring below and above it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:18">[00:56:18]</a>.

### Geological Evidence

Studies of geological formations across North America have identified mega-flood landscapes, such as those in the Channelled Scablands of Washington, where massive floodwaters carved deep grooves and left mile-long ripples in the landscape <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:00:05">[03:00:05]</a>. Similar sedimentary evidence showing rapid and immense water movement aligns with the dating of the Younger Dryas onset <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:02:06">[03:02:06]</a>.

### Impact Proxies

The comet impacts are further evidenced by the presence of nano-diamonds, Carbon spherules, and melted glass in specific soil layers. These markers are indicative of high-heat events consistent with an extraterrestrial impact <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:59:02">[02:59:02]</a>.

## Effects on Climate and Megafauna Extinction

The hypothesized comet impacts and the subsequent Younger Dryas period led to a dramatic climate shift, halting and even reversing warming trends and resulting in a sudden drop in global temperatures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:28:43">[01:28:43]</a>. Many megafaunal species, such as woolly mammoths and saber-toothed cats, went extinct during this time. The massive environmental upheaval and transformation of ecosystems likely disrupted human populations and forced changes in migration and survival strategies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:52:00">[02:52:00]</a>.

## Theories on Human Civilization Impact

One of the intriguing aspects of the Younger Dryas Cataclysm is its proposed connection to lost prehistoric civilizations. Researchers suggest that these catastrophic events may have reset the progress of human societies, erasing established cultures and giving rise to new ones, such as those involved in the creation of Gobekli Tepe <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:36:56">[01:36:56]</a>. This period of upheaval has been posited as a "reset" moment, causing disruptions to any advanced civilizations that might have existed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:36">[01:08:36]</a>.

## The Need for Further Research

Despite the growing body of evidence, the Younger Dryas Cataclysm remains a contentious topic in geological and archaeological circles, often met with skepticism from mainstream academics. Critics of the theory argue for a series of smaller, less severe impacts or dispute the evidence outright <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:56:58">[02:56:58]</a>. However, advocates such as the Comet Research Group continue to push for recognition and further investigation, backed by public and crowdfunded support, to explore these pivotal events in Earth's history <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:35:05">[03:35:05]</a>.

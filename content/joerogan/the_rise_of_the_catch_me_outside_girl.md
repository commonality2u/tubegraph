The phenomenon of the "Catch Me Outside" girl, whose real name is Danielle Bregoli, serves as a remarkable case study in how viral culture can transform lives almost overnight. Originating from a fraught appearance on the Dr. Phil show, Danielle Bregoli skyrocketed to fame with a simple, though audacious, phrase, and has since built a substantial career from that flash of notoriety <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:39">[00:02:39]</a>.

## From Daytime TV to Internet Fame

Danielle Bregoli made her first national appearance on the Dr. Phil show when her mother brought her onto the series to seek help for her behavior <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:29">[00:00:29]</a>. During this infamous episode, Bregoli uttered the now-iconic line "Catch me outside, how about that?" in response to audience members who were appalled by her behavior. This phrase inexplicably captured the public's imagination and transformed her into a viral sensation virtually overnight <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:39">[00:02:39]</a>.

Following her appearance, which Dr. Phil himself described as his "moment of infamy," Danielle was sent to a ranch for troubled teens. There, she reportedly made a remarkable turnaround, becoming a leader among the other teens <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:08">[00:01:08]</a>. Despite this positive shift, Danielle found herself catapulted back into the spotlight for very different reasons.

## A Meme That Changed Everything

Despite the show’s attempts to focus on her rehabilitation, a brief clip of the episode featuring Danielle's catchphrase gained unexpected traction on the internet. The phrase turned into a meme that spread like wildfire, taking on a life of its own <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:39">[00:02:39]</a>.

Danielle's newfound fame was not without its skeptics, and Dr. Phil himself expressed a mix of bewilderment and hope that her moment of fame could eventually turn into something positive for her future <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:03">[00:03:03]</a>.

## From Meme to Music Career

Danielle, now known as Bhad Bhabie, leveraged her viral fame into a music career. She signed a record deal and released tracks that garnered millions of plays. Her debut single was a chart success and established her as a rapper, proving that she was more than just a viral curiosity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:14">[00:02:14]</a>.

Moreover, Danielle has managed to make financial strides, earning sizeable sums from endorsements and partnerships that highlight her unique brand and influence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:11">[00:04:11]</a>.

## Reflection on Viral Culture

The rise of Danielle Bregoli serves as a powerful indication of the unpredictable nature of viral fame. As Dr. Phil noted, such things "make no sense,"—not even seeming logical when considering people working on more traditionally impactful endeavors, like curing diseases, who often remain in obscurity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:20">[00:03:20]</a>.

## Black Ice Scare

One particularly harrowing experience involved driving in treacherous winter conditions. Black ice, a common hazard in snowy regions, nearly caused a disaster during a nighttime drive in Colorado. The lack of visibility and unseen ice patches made the road especially dangerous. On one occasion, the vehicle spun out of control upon hitting a patch of black ice on a bridge, an area known for freezing rapidly due to the absence of ground insulation. Fortunately, the road was devoid of traffic at the time, allowing for a safe albeit tense stop facing the wrong direction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:26">[00:02:26]</a>.

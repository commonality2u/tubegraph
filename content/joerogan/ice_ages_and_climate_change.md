Ice ages and climate change have been significant factors in shaping the Earth's environment, influencing both the physical landscape and the development of life over millennia. Understanding these phenomena provides insight into the history of the planet and offers lessons for contemporary discussions about [[climate_change_and_environmental_policy | climate change and environmental policies]].

## Ice Ages Explained

The term "ice age" refers to periods in Earth's history when large parts of the planet were covered in ice sheets and glaciers. These ice sheets are massive, reaching depths that can exceed two miles thick. Notably, the last ice age was marked by such extensive ice covering North America and Europe, causing profound changes in sea levels and climates worldwide <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:07">[01:11:07]</a>.

The most recent major ice age began about 2.4 million years ago and lasted until roughly 11,600 years ago, when the planet transitioned into the current interglacial period known as the Holocene. During ice ages, global temperatures drop significantly, resulting in the expansion of ice sheets. Conversely, in interglacial periods, temperatures rise, and the ice recedes, leading to higher sea levels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:43">[01:13:43]</a>.

One event within the last ice age that has garnered significant attention is the Younger Dryas, a period of abrupt climatic change that occurred approximately 12,900 to 11,600 years ago <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:46:05">[01:46:05]</a>. This era is marked by a sudden reversal back to glacial conditions before quickly warming again. The Younger Dryas is often discussed within the context of [[younger_dryas_cataclysm | cataclysmic events]] and potential impacts from extraterrestrial objects, as some research suggests a comet or asteroidal impact could have occurred <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:33:10">[01:33:10]</a>.

## Climate Change and Human History

The transition out of the last ice age significantly impacted human history. The period known as the Holocene saw the development and spread of agriculture, leading to the rise of cities and civilizations as climates stabilized and warmed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:25:54">[02:25:54]</a>.

However, this stability has been punctuated by episodes of cooling and warming. For instance, periods such as the Little Ice Age in the 14th to 19th centuries had significant impacts on agriculture and civilizations, often intertwining with historical events like the onset of the Dark Ages and various plagues <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:30:39">[01:30:39]</a>.

## The Role of Cosmic Events

Cosmic events have played a crucial role in shaping climates on Earth. The evidence of impacts from asteroids and comets has been directly linked to past mass extinctions and rapid climate changes. These events are not mere historical footnotes; they serve as reminders of the dynamic and often volatile nature of Earth's climate system <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:00">[00:35:00]</a>.

The understanding and study of ice ages underscore the need for deeper insights into [[comet_impacts_and_global_cataclysms | comet impacts and extinction events]] and the resulting climate alterations. Such knowledge is invaluable as current concerns about human-induced climate change continue to grow <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:39:47">[01:39:47]</a>.

## Exploration of Other Worlds
Jumping robots are considered ideal for exploring extraterrestrial environments, particularly those with thin or non-existent atmospheres <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:50:00">[02:50:00]</a>.

*   **Lunar Exploration** On the Moon, with its one-sixth Earth gravity, a highly capable jumping robot could leap 125 meters high and half a kilometer forward <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:58:00">[02:58:00]</a>.
*   **Navigating Challenging Terrain** Unlike rovers that may encounter difficulties with steep cliffs and deep craters, jumpers can efficiently hop in and out of such obstacles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:07:00">[03:07:00]</a>. This capability allows them to retrieve samples and bring them back to a rover <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:10:00">[03:10:00]</a>.
*   **Energy Efficiency** [[Physics of jumping and energy storage | Jumping]] is an energy-efficient form of locomotion, and if the kinetic energy from landing could be stored back in the spring, the efficiency could be nearly perfect <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:16:00">[03:16:00]</a>.

## Enhanced Robot Capabilities
The development of [[work_multiplication_in_robotics | work multiplication]] in robotics could significantly advance robot capabilities across various applications <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:25:00">[11:25:00]</a>.

*   **Increased Energy Storage** By building up energy gradually over time from multiple motor turns, robots can store and release vast amounts of energy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:34:00">[11:34:00]</a>. This overcomes the limitation of motor power being the primary limiting factor for performance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:16:00">[10:16:00]</a>.
*   **Record-Setting Performance** This principle allows for the creation of robots capable of setting new world records in various metrics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:41:00">[11:41:00]</a>.
*   **Future Fleets** Researchers are already developing entire fleets of jumping robots, including those that can right themselves after landing for immediate re-launch and steerable models with adjustable legs for directional launching <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:26:00">[03:26:00]</a>.

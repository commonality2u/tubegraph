## Perception of Randomness

Humans are generally poor at selecting things truly randomly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:37">[01:37]</a>. This is observed in phenomena like the "blue-seven phenomenon," where people consistently choose blue and the number 7 when asked to pick a random color and number across various cultures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:41">[01:41]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:47">[01:47]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:49">[01:49]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:51">[01:51]</a>. The number 37 is considered the numerical equivalent of the blue-seven phenomenon when selecting a number between 1 and 100 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:57">[01:57]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:01">[02:01]</a>.

### Survey Findings

A large-scale survey involving 200,000 respondents asked people to pick a random number between 1 and 100 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:13">[04:13]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:17">[04:17]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:22">[04:22]</a>.
*   The overall distribution of chosen numbers was highly consistent, demonstrating that human "random" choices are anything but <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:30">[04:30]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:43">[04:43]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:45">[04:45]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:49">[04:49]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:52">[04:52]</a>.
*   After excluding extreme values (1 and 100, which are often chosen because they are explicitly stated in the question) and culturally significant numbers (42 and 69), the numbers that stood out as most frequently chosen were 7, 73, 77, and 37 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:56">[04:56]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:00">[05:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:02">[05:02]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:06">[05:06]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:09">[05:09]</a>.
*   In a follow-up question asking people to pick the number they thought the fewest others would pick (aiming for truly random selections by eliminating favorite numbers), 73 and 37 were overwhelmingly the most selected, nearly tied <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:20">[05:20]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:24">[05:24]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:26">[05:26]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:29">[05:29]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:35">[05:35]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:38">[05:38]</a>.
*   The least-picked numbers were multiples of 10, such as 90, 30, 40, 70, 80, and 60, suggesting they don't feel random <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:43">[05:43]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:47">[05:47]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:50">[05:50]</a>.

This indicates that while 37 and 73 are perceived as random, they are, ironically, not truly random choices at all <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:01">[06:01]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:04">[06:04]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:08">[06:08]</a>.

### Psychological Explanations

The preference for 37 stems from how people perceive randomness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:10">[06:10]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:11">[06:11]</a>.
*   Even numbers are often considered less random than odd numbers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:27">[06:27]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:28">[06:28]</a>.
*   Numbers like 50 feel "too contrived" or "too central" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:19">[06:19]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:21">[06:21]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:26">[06:26]</a>.
*   Single digits like 5, 9, and 1 feel either not random or too extreme <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:30">[06:30]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:32">[06:32]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:33">[06:33]</a>.
*   People tend to gravitate towards digits 3 and 7 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:33">[06:33]</a>. Indeed, all the top numbers in the survey consisted of 3s and 7s, and these were the most selected digits in both survey questions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:35">[06:35]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:39">[06:39]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:42">[06:42]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:45">[06:45]</a>.

Remarkably, 37 is the median second prime factor of all numbers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:00">[08:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:04">[08:04]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:08">[08:08]</a>. 1.  **Explore Phase:** Automatically reject the first 37% of options encountered <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:02">[13:02]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:04">[13:04]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:06">[13:06]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:43">[15:43]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:45">[15:45]</a>.

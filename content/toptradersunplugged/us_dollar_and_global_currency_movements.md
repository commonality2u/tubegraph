## Influencing Factors

### Interest Rates and Monetary Policy
The low [[Interest rates and currency trends | interest rates]] seen five years prior led to a significant amount of refinancing and capital being stored, delaying the impact of rising rates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:38">[00:31:38]</a>. However, this delay means that when the impact arrives, it could be more severe <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:53">[00:31:53]</a>. The Federal Reserve is currently in a difficult position, knowing that [[Inflation and Currency Devaluation Trends | inflation]] is coming but uncertain about the impact of wealth redistribution on growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:10">[00:14:10]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:09">[00:19:09]</a>.

### Inflation and Dollar Weakness
A weaker dollar has a reflexive effect, contributing to more [[Inflation and Currency Devaluation Trends | inflation]] by making imported goods more expensive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:21">[00:37:21]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:47">[00:37:47]</a>. This effect is compounded by the implementation of tariffs, creating a "tariff upon a tariff" scenario <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:51">[00:37:51]</a>.

### Liquidity and Asset Correlation
The pulling of liquidity from the system, driven by factors like the five-year anniversary of debt refinancing, affects all assets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:11">[00:33:11]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:59">[00:33:59]</a>. In an environment where the earnings yield of any asset must increase due to higher interest rates, asset prices consequently must decrease <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:51">[00:34:51]</a>.

### Geopolitical Influence
The current political environment, particularly in the US, is characterized by populism and protectionist policies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:18">[00:10:18]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:56">[00:19:56]</a>. These policies, including tariffs, can create more jobs locally but are also inflationary <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:03">[00:15:03]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:12">[00:15:12]</a>. There is a concern about the disunification of the West through policies that target allies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:04">[00:53:04]</a>, contributing to [[Impact of geopolitical tensions on the US dollar and global trade | global volatility]] and uncertainty <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:05">[00:37:05]</a>.

### Debt and Systemic Risk
The rising cost of interest payments on US debt is a significant budget concern <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:39">[00:17:39]</a>. There's a perceived increase in the probability of a US debt default, which, while still small, is no longer considered zero <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:30">[00:32:30]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:32">[00:32:32]</a>. This concern is reflected in the increased CDS (Credit Default Swap) rates on US debt <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:47">[00:26:47]</a>.

> "The one thing that everybody's short is the tail. Whether you're thinking about this is the ultimate tail, right? A default the dollar would be the ultimate tail, right?" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:18">[00:27:18]</a>

The long-term solution for the US in a situation where things spiral out of control is likely to print money and monetize debt, rather than a literal default <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:04">[00:29:04]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:06">[00:29:06]</a>.

## Broader Implications

The current [[Global monetary system changes | global monetary system]] is characterized by "unconventional policy with entities all over the world" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:22">[00:57:22]</a>. This unleashes volatility and uncertainty, as many previously static assumptions become dynamic and interact with one another <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:38">[00:57:38]</a>. This complex interplay of forces creates a high-potential energy situation with increased uncertainty <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:02">[00:58:02]</a>, making accurate predictions difficult.

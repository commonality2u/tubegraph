### [[financial_nihilism_and_its_cultural_impact | Financial Nihilism]]

The concept of [[financial_nihilism_and_its_cultural_impact | financial nihilism]] emerged from observations in crypto and later equity markets (e.g., GameStop, Hertz) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:13">[00:06:13]</a>. It's defined as an investment philosophy where objects of speculation are treated as intrinsically worthless <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:31">[00:06:31]</a>. This signifies a departure from the traditional, albeit tenuous, relationship between an asset's price and its underlying fundamentals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:13">[00:06:13]</a>. Unlike past bubbles where there was a story of growth and real things happening, [[financial_nihilism_and_its_cultural_impact | financial nihilism]] exhibits a self-awareness that "it's all just a narrative" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:50">[00:07:50]</a>.

This phenomenon is seen as a cultural one, embodying the frustration of generations who grew up with sustained monetary policy dominance and increasing inequality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:07">[00:09:07]</a>. It reflects an "anger" and "resulting nihilism," a desire for fairness and to fix a system perceived as broken <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:05">[00:10:05]</a>.

The only way to address it is to develop new stories and myths <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:12">[00:18:12]</a>.

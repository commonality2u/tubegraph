The upcoming US presidential election is a significant factor influencing global markets, with potential impacts on market flows, fiscal policy, [[impact_of_geopolitical_tensions_on_global_markets | geopolitical dynamics]], inflation, and commodity prices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:20">[01:15:20]</a>. ## Immediate Market Reaction Post-Election

Regardless of the specific outcome, the period immediately following the election (approximately two weeks to one month) is expected to see structural positive flows into markets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:11:09">[04:11:09]</a>. This is due to the removal of high skew in the market and the "event vol" (event volatility) that dissipates after the election <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:24:00">[04:24:00]</a>.

Historically, populist election years have shown exceptionally strong equity performance, averaging 21% returns, with every such election year resulting in double-digit gains <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:06">[00:59:06]</a>. This phenomenon is attributed to massive stimulus measures undertaken by the incumbent party to secure votes, driving money to voters and leading to positive market outcomes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:56">[00:59:56]</a>.

## Longer-Term Impact by Presidential Candidate

While short-term flows are positive, the longer-term [[us_economic_outlook_and_presidential_election_implications | economic outlook]] and market implications differ significantly depending on whether Donald Trump or Kamala Harris wins <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:13:40">[04:13:40]</a>. The primary focus for discerning market effects should be on inflation and populism, both in rhetoric and actual policy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:53:07">[04:53:07]</a>. Key areas include fiscal policy, [[impact_of_geopolitical_events_on_global_markets | global conflict]], labor costs (influenced by immigration), and protectionism, all of which drive structural inflation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:28:40">[04:28:40]</a>.

### Donald Trump Presidency

A Trump victory is likely to grant him significant power, potentially including control of both the House and Senate due to historical voting patterns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:03">[00:55:03]</a>. This would provide a "carte blanche" to implement his policies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:01">[00:57:01]</a>.

*   **Fiscal Policy**: Trump's economic policy is expected to be highly stimulative <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:05">[00:44:05]</a>. He is likely to pressure the Federal Reserve to stimulate the economy on his behalf <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:14">[00:44:14]</a>. This approach, likened to an "Erdogan-type figure," could lead to a "super hot" economy cyclically in the short term, but worsen long-term structural inflationary pressures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:01">[00:45:01]</a>.
*   **[[effect_of_global_geopolitical_tensions_on_markets | Geopolitical Conflicts]] (China/Taiwan)**: Trump's "America First" policy suggests a more isolationist stance, potentially leading to less intervention in [[current_global_political_landscape_and_its_impact_on_markets | global conflicts]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:32">[00:48:32]</a>. His previous statements indicate a willingness to demand payment for US support to allies like Taiwan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:11">[00:48:11]</a>. This approach could be seen by China as an opportunity to act more quickly against Taiwan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:39">[00:48:39]</a>. While an action might come sooner (e.g., within the next year) under a Trump presidency, it is also considered more likely to resolve sooner, potentially leading to a quicker detente with China <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:25">[00:49:25]</a>. This "quicker resolution" scenario would be incredibly bullish for Chinese assets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:29">[00:50:29]</a>.
*   **Commodity Prices**: Trump is seen as more likely to make a deal with Saudi Arabia, providing security guarantees in exchange for cooperation on oil prices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:40">[00:51:40]</a>. Such a deal could be stimulative and less inflationary regarding energy costs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:02">[00:52:02]</a>.
*   **Immigration Policy**: A hardline immigration policy under Trump, including closing borders or exporting people, is seen as a major structurally inflationary pressure, especially on labor costs in the US <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:07">[00:53:07]</a>. This effect could potentially offset other rebalancing pressures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:06">[00:54:06]</a>.

### Kamala Harris Presidency

A Harris victory might result in a more mixed bag regarding congressional control, with less clear control of the House and Senate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:23">[00:57:23]</a>.

*   **Fiscal Policy**: A Harris administration's fiscal policy is expected to be more focused on wealth redistribution (e.g., taxing capital gains to send money to people) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:57">[00:45:57]</a>. While less extreme in cyclical push than Trump's, it is still inflationary and could lead to stagflation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:05">[00:46:05]</a>.
*   **[[geopolitical_impacts_on_global_markets | Geopolitical Conflicts]] (China/Taiwan)**: Under a Harris administration, a conflict with China over Taiwan is considered more likely to be a "slow-moving, bigger issue" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:47">[00:50:47]</a>, potentially continuing for several years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:50">[00:50:50]</a>.
*   **Commodity Prices**: A Harris administration is viewed as less willing to negotiate on terms that would lead to lower commodity prices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:08">[00:52:08]</a>.

## Broader Economic and Market Outlook

Regardless of the election outcome, the current period is characterized by structural inflation, driven by factors like fiscal spending, wealth redistribution, de-globalization, and [[impact_of_geopolitical_tensions_on_the_us_dollar_and_global_trade | global conflict]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:16">[00:32:16]</a>. This contrasts with the prior 40-year period of structural deflation driven by monetary policy, globalization, and technological development <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:26">[00:29:26]</a>.

While the Federal Reserve's actions might cyclically slow down inflation, the underlying structural inflationary pressures persist <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:27">[00:32:27]</a>. This leads to a scenario of "sequestered demand," where consumers are waiting for interest rates to fall before spending <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:41">[00:34:41]</a>. When rates do fall, this demand flows back into the economy, causing re-acceleration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:19">[00:34:19]</a>.

The market is currently reflecting this through a "steepener" in the yield curve, indicating expectations of continued inflationary trends and reinforced longer-term inflation as interest rates are lowered <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:05">[00:36:05]</a>. This suggests a potential decade of stagflation, where the Fed will be forced to contend with an economy facing both slow growth and high inflation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:41">[00:36:41]</a>.

Despite the short-term positive outlook for markets due to election-related flows, the long-term perspective remains cautious <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:18">[01:00:18]</a>. The current market rally is viewed as a "sugar high" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:24">[01:01:24]</a>. Historically, periods of strong election year performance during populist eras often precede poor long-term market outcomes, including significant real-term value loss for equities and bonds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:27">[00:58:27]</a>. A retracement and decline are considered likely after this "sugar high" wears off, potentially beginning in January <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:35">[01:01:35]</a>. This aligns with the concept of "blow-off tops" seen before major market declines <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:54">[01:01:54]</a>.

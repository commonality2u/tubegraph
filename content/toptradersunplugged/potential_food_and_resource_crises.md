## Drivers of Crisis

The current trajectory towards potential food and resource crises stems from several interconnected factors:

*   **End of Globalization**: The post-World War II American-led system of free trade, which allowed global specialization and access to raw materials, is dismantling <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:31:00">[05:31:00]</a>. The United States has lost interest in maintaining this system, evidenced by electing anti-globalist leaders seven times in a row <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:59:00">[06:59:00]</a>. As global [[global_supply_chains_and_economic_fragility | supply chains]] break down, extra-regional trade becomes almost impossible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:43:00">[04:43:00]</a>.
*   **Demographic Collapse**: Urbanization and industrialization, driven by the post-war global system, have led to unprecedented drops in birth rates worldwide <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:44:00">[06:44:00]</a>. The advanced world now lacks sufficient young people to regenerate its demographic structures, with populations moving into mass retirement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:06:00">[07:06:00]</a>. This "top-heavy demography" removes the capital base needed for investment and future growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:27:00">[07:27:00]</a>.
*   **Geopolitical Instability**: [[Geopolitical Tensions and Global Conflicts | Wars]] and trade tensions break up the world, constraining the ability to interact and cooperate at a distance, thus slowing technological advancement globally <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:56:00">[13:56:00]</a>. The [[current_energy_crisis_origins_and_impacts | Ukraine war]] has significantly disrupted agricultural, industrial, and [[global_energy_crisis_and_supply_chain_issues | energy markets]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:32:00">[09:32:00]</a>.
*   **China's Decline**: China, once a major beneficiary of globalization, faces a terminal decline in every measurable wayâ€”demographically, economically, culturally, and nationally <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:04:00">[09:04:00]</a>. Its population peaked around 2003 and has been overcounted by perhaps 100 million <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:28:00">[12:28:00]</a>. China's reliance on foreign corporations for market access and technological transfer means it would be devastated by sanctions similar to those imposed on Russia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:01:00">[22:01:00]</a>. Persistent COVID-19 lockdowns further undermine its reliability in [[global_supply_chains_and_economic_fragility | supply chain]] manufacturing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:45:00">[23:45:00]</a>.

## Specific Crises and Their Impacts

### Energy Breakdown
Europe faces an imminent loss of four to five million barrels a day of crude from Russia within months <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="44:12:00">[44:12:00]</a>. To maintain power, European countries may need to revert to neo-colonial practices, forcing former African colonies to redirect resource flows to Europe <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="44:18:00">[44:18:00]</a>. The inability to use oil and natural gas without viable alternatives, except possibly coal, highlights a severe [[global_energy_crisis_and_supply_chain_issues | energy crisis]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="42:48:00">[42:48:00]</a>.

### Agricultural Breakdown & Food Insecurity
Globalization allowed agricultural producers to specialize in crops best suited to their climate, leading to less regional diversification <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:02">[01:14:02]</a>. With the breakdown of the global system, countries will become more responsible for their own food production, which means less specialization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:25">[01:14:25]</a>. This shift will reduce the amount of calories the world grows by more than half, even without considering the breakdown in the fertilizer [[global_supply_chains_and_economic_fragility | supply chain]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:05">[01:15:05]</a>.

> [!WARNING] Projected Famine
> In the best-case scenario, where there are no wars, the world is still looking at three billion more people than it can support <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:22">[01:15:22]</a>. With potential shifts towards plant-based protein and continued production from countries like the US, France, Australia, and potentially Argentina, the number of people dying from famine over the next 20 years might be reduced to "only a billion" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:42">[01:15:42]</a>. This represents horrific, unavoidable shifts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:54">[01:15:54]</a>.

Wheat, humanity's number one crop, faces the greatest danger as it is primarily grown in arid parts of the world, making it vulnerable to climate shifts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:31">[01:05:31]</a>.

## Investment Implications

The period of "bottomless supplies of capital" is ending, making capital harder to acquire <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:46">[01:09:46]</a>. Investors should consider:
*   **Large Markets with Deep Liquidity**: The United States currently stands out <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:54">[01:07:54]</a>.
*   **Energy-Intensive Production**: Products whose production is energy-intensive offer a competitive advantage in the U.S. due to its cheaper oil, natural gas, and electricity from the shale revolution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:01">[01:08:01]</a>.
*   **Demographically Driven Demand**: The U.S. has the healthiest demography in the rich world, and Mexico has the healthiest in the advanced and developing world, indicating stable demand <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:19">[01:08:19]</a>.
*   **Exportable Products**: As global [[global_supply_chains_and_economic_fragility | supply chains]] weaken and North American ones strengthen, arbitrage opportunities exist <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:32">[01:08:32]</a>.
*   **Strategic Materials**: Investments in materials no longer flowing from the Russian system, such as bauxite, aluminum, titanium, steel, pig iron, wheat, and nitrogen/phosphate-based fertilizers, are promising <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:42">[01:08:42]</a>. It's crucial to identify American firms not exposed to the former Soviet Union <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:07">[01:09:07]</a>.

## Digital Biology

NVIDIA's technologies have profoundly impacted digital biology, transforming the field and accelerating research:
*   **Early Impact:** As early as the 2000s, quantum chemistry scientists utilized NVIDIA's GPUs for molecular simulations, achieving results much faster than on traditional supercomputers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:37">[00:07:37]</a>. This early adoption showcased the power of parallel processing for complex scientific problems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:42">[00:07:42]</a>.
*   **AI Applications:** The next decade of AI is expected to focus on its application to fields like digital biology <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:30:24">[02:30:24]</a>. Deep learning networks are capable of learning patterns and relationships from diverse data types, including those found in biological sequences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:13:06">[02:13:06]</a>.
*   **Understanding Biological Language:** NVIDIA aims to understand the "language of molecules and cells" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:52:28">[02:52:28]</a>, similar to how they understand the language of physics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:53:34">[02:53:34]</a>. This includes:
    *   Translating amino acid sequences to protein structures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:21:55">[02:21:55]</a>.
    *   Querying proteins for their function or properties, such as identifying drug targets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:22:03">[02:22:03]</a>.
*   **Digital Human Twin:** The ability to learn and predict from biological data makes the concept of a digital twin of a human plausible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:54:50">[02:54:50]</a>.
*   **Field Transformation:** The entire field of digital biology and life sciences is expected to be transformed by these advancements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:59:59">[01:59:59]</a>.

## The Role of AI and GPUs as "Time Machines"

Jensen Huang refers to GPUs as "time machines" because they significantly accelerate computations, allowing scientists and researchers to complete complex tasks much faster than previously possible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:31">[00:06:31]</a>. This accelerated processing enables a quicker exploration of future possibilities, whether it's simulating a virtual city for self-driving cars or predicting weather patterns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:05">[00:07:05]</a>. The fundamental concept is that by reducing the time required for computation, it becomes possible to "see the future sooner" in various scientific and technological domains <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:31">[00:06:31]</a>.

The overarching vision is that by seeing and predicting the future through advanced computation, humanity has a better chance of optimizing for the best version of that future <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:59:09">[02:59:09]</a>.

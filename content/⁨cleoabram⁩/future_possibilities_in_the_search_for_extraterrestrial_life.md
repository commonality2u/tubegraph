## Detecting Biosignatures on Exoplanets

The James Webb Space Telescope's capabilities are revolutionary because it can discern what's on distant planets, beyond merely detecting their presence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:37">[00:01:37]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:40">[00:01:40]</a>. This is achieved by searching for "biosignatures" – indicators of life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:10">[00:02:10]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:12">[00:02:12]</a>. For instance, if an atmosphere is found to contain 78% nitrogen and 21% oxygen, it would suggest a very high probability of life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:57">[00:01:57]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:02">[00:02:02]</a>.

### The Transit Method and Atmospheric Analysis

The method involves observing tiny dips in starlight when an [[exoplanet_discovery_through_starlight_dips | exoplanet]] passes between its star and the telescope <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:18">[00:02:18]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:22">[00:02:22]</a>. Crucially, not all starlight is blocked; a small amount passes *through* the planet's atmosphere on its way to us <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:26">[00:02:26]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:28">[00:02:28]</a>. Different gases in an atmosphere block different kinds of light <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:34">[00:02:34]</a>. The JWST is powerful enough to detect what specific wavelengths of light are missing, thereby revealing the atmospheric composition <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:38">[00:02:38]</a>. This process involves analyzing incredibly tiny specks of darkness against equally tiny specks of light in the vastness of space <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:52">[00:02:52]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:56">[00:02:56]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:58">[00:02:58]</a>.

## Initial Findings: WASP-96b

By analyzing the missing light signatures, scientists confirmed that WASP-96b's atmosphere contains water <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:27">[00:03:27]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:30">[00:03:30]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:34">[00:03:34]</a>.

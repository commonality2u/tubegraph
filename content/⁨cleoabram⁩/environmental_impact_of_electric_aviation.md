### Evolution of Battery Technology

Historically, the concept of [[future_potential_of_electric_planes | electric aviation]] predates modern airplanes, with some airships in the 1800s being electric <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:23">[00:02:23]</a>. For example, an 1883 electric airship used a nearly thousand-pound battery made from zinc and chlorine <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:28">[00:02:28]</a>. However, batteries were initially too big and heavy to compete with liquid fuel for sustained flight <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:33">[00:02:33]</a>.

Significant advancements in battery technology have changed this <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:47">[00:00:47]</a>. All the gains made in developing batteries for laptops, phones, and [[transition_to_electric_cars_in_racing | electric cars]] are now being applied to [[future_potential_of_electric_planes | electric planes]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:02">[00:04:02]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:07">[00:04:07]</a>.

*   **Lithium-ion Cells**: Modern [[future_potential_of_electric_planes | electric aircraft]] use commercial off-the-shelf lithium-ion cells, similar to those found in Tesla cars <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:05">[00:03:05]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:30">[00:04:30]</a>. One battery pack for an [[future_potential_of_electric_planes | electric plane]] can hold the same amount of energy as a standard Tesla car <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:12">[00:03:12]</a>.
*   **Energy Density**: Batteries have become much smaller and, crucially for planes, lighter over the last decade <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:52">[00:03:52]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:58">[00:03:58]</a>. While batteries are still far from traditional fuel in terms of energy density (specifically gravimetric energy, or specific energy) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:27">[00:03:27]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:20">[00:04:20]</a>, their dramatically reduced cost has spurred significant investment in [[future_potential_of_electric_planes | electric planes]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:46">[00:04:46]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:48">[00:04:48]</a>.

### Rethinking Plane Design

The advent of [[future_potential_of_electric_planes | electric aviation]] allows for a complete redesign of aircraft <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:52">[00:04:52]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:10">[00:06:10]</a>.

*   **Vertical Takeoff and Landing (VTOL)**: While not exclusive to electric planes, VTOL capabilities are synergistic with electric innovations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:46">[00:05:46]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:50">[00:05:50]</a>. Electric batteries are heavier, but electric motors are lighter and smaller, offering more flexibility in placement on the aircraft <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:52">[00:05:52]</a>. VTOL capability allows access to places without traditional airports, expanding aviation access <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:13">[00:06:13]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:18">[00:06:18]</a>.

## Impact on Emissions Reduction

[[future_potential_of_electric_planes | Electric planes]] do not need to replace large fuel-powered passenger planes to make a significant environmental difference <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:42">[00:06:42]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:51">[00:06:51]</a>.

*   **Short-Haul Flights**: A third of passenger flight emissions come from short-haul flights (less than 1,500 kilometers or 932 miles) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:09">[00:07:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:14">[00:07:14]</a>. Even shorter flights contribute disproportionately to emissions because the extra fuel used during takeoff is a greater percentage of the total flight compared to cruising, and less efficient planes are often used <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:33">[00:07:33]</a>. Electrifying these short flights can make a substantial reduction in emissions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:46">[00:07:46]</a>.
*   **Freight and Logistics**: [[future_potential_of_electric_planes | Electric planes]], especially those with VTOL capabilities, can also reduce carbon emissions from sources outside of aviation, such as ground transportation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:54">[00:07:54]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:58">[00:07:58]</a>. By not relying on roads for delivery, the logistics industry could significantly change and reduce its carbon footprint <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:07">[00:08:07]</a>. UPS, for instance, has signed a deal with Beta <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:02">[00:08:02]</a>.

This [[transitioning_from_fossil_fuels_to_clean_energy | transition]] to [[future_potential_of_electric_planes | electric planes]] allows for moving into a more sustainable future without having to wait for distant technological breakthroughs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:12">[00:08:12]</a>.

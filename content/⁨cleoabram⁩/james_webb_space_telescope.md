## The Quest for Extraterrestrial Life

One of the most exciting aspects of the JWST's capabilities is its potential role in the [[search_for_extraterrestrial_life | search for extraterrestrial life]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:29">[00:00:29]</a>. The telescope embarks on a voyage to observe worlds beyond our solar system <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:44">[00:00:44]</a>.

### Mapping Exoplanets

To date, approximately 5,000 [[exoplanet_discovery | exoplanets]] (planets outside our solar system) have been identified <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>. The Kepler telescope, for example, focused on one specific region for four years, leading to numerous discoveries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:04">[00:01:04]</a>. This extensive mapping represents decades of scientific endeavor and significant [[technological_advancements_in_astronomy | technological advancements in astronomy]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:12">[00:01:12]</a>.

### Identifying Biosignatures

The JWST is designed to detect "biosignatures" — indicators of life — on distant planets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:10">[00:02:10]</a>. This is achieved by analyzing the light that passes through an exoplanet's atmosphere as it transits its star <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:18">[00:02:18]</a>. Different gases in an atmosphere block distinct types of light, and the JWST is powerful enough to identify which light is missing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:34">[00:02:34]</a>. This capability allows scientists to deduce atmospheric composition, similar to how identifying 78% nitrogen and 21% oxygen in Earth's atmosphere would suggest a high probability of life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:57">[00:01:57]</a>.

> "If you could tell that our atmosphere has 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen you'd know that you had a very good shot at finding life." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:57">[00:01:57]</a>

The challenge lies in observing these minuscule specks of darkness against tiny points of light in the vastness of space <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:52">[00:02:52]</a>.

## WASP-96b: A Case Study

The scientists operating the James Webb Space Telescope specifically directed it towards WASP-96b <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:14">[00:03:14]</a>. This exoplanet is located 1,150 light-years away, meaning the light reaching the telescope today left WASP-96b 1,150 years ago <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:19">[00:03:19]</a>.

Analysis of the blocked light from WASP-96b's atmosphere revealed the presence of water <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:27">[00:03:27]</a>. However, WASP-96b is a gas giant with a hot, steamy atmosphere, indicating water vapor rather than liquid water <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:37">[00:03:37]</a>.

> "This is a hot world. And so we're not looking at liquid water here. We're looking instead at sort of steam, water vapor." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:42">[00:03:42]</a>

While water vapor has been detected on other planets closer to Earth (within a few hundred light-years), obtaining such detailed information about a planet over 1,000 light-years away is an astounding feat <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:49">[00:03:49]</a>.

## The Future of Exploration

The observations of WASP-96b serve as a "proof of concept," confirming the efficacy of this method for exoplanet research <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:09">[00:04:09]</a>. This is merely the beginning of the JWST's discoveries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:13">[00:04:13]</a>.

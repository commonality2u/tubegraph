## What Are Biosignatures?

[[James Webb Space Telescope|The James Webb Space Telescope]] aims to address this by searching for "biosignatures" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:13">[00:02:13]</a>. Biosignatures are indicators of life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:10">[00:02:10]</a>. For example, if distant observers could determine that Earth's atmosphere contains 78% nitrogen and 21% oxygen, they would have a strong indication of life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:57">[00:01:57]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:02">[00:02:02]</a>. While not necessarily intelligent life, these atmospheric compositions suggest biological processes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:06">[00:02:06]</a>.

## How the James Webb Space Telescope Detects Biosignatures

The telescope's method for detecting biosignatures involves observing exoplanet atmospheres:
*   **Transit Method** When an exoplanet passes between its host star and the telescope, it causes a tiny dip in starlight <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:18">[00:02:18]</a>. This method helped locate many exoplanets initially <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:22">[00:02:22]</a>.
*   **Atmospheric Absorption** The planet doesn't block all the starlight; a small amount passes through its atmosphere on the way to the telescope <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:28">[00:02:28]</a>. Different gases in the atmosphere block different wavelengths of light <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:34">[00:02:34]</a>.
*   **Spectroscopy** The [[James Webb Space Telescope|James Webb Telescope]] is powerful enough to detect which specific kinds of light are missing from the starlight that has passed through the atmosphere <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:38">[00:02:38]</a>. By analyzing these missing wavelengths, scientists can identify the chemical composition of the exoplanet's atmosphere <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:38">[00:02:38]</a>.

This process is remarkable, as it involves detecting minute "specks of darkness" against "tiny specks of light" from "the depths of space" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:52">[00:02:52]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:56">[00:02:56]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:58">[00:02:58]</a>.

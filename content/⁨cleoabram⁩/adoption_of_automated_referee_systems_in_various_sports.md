The catalyst for widespread adoption of automated officiating systems can be traced back to a controversial match in 2004 at the US Open between Serena Williams and Jennifer Capriati <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:03">[00:00:03]</a>. During a crucial moment, a ball hit by Williams landed clearly inside the line, as shown by TV replays, but was erroneously called out by the umpire <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:14">[00:00:14]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:25">[00:00:25]</a>. Within a year of this incident, [[Impact of Hawkeye technology on tennis | Hawkeye]] was officially incorporated into the US Open's review process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:39">[00:00:39]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:44">[00:00:44]</a>. Today, it has replaced over 200 human judges in tennis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:44">[00:00:44]</a>.

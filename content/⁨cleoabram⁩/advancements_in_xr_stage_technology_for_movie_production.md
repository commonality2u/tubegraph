Modern filmmaking has seen a significant leap in complexity and realism thanks to technological advancements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:22">[00:00:22]</a>. ## What is an XR Stage?

An XR stage is essentially a giant [[impact_of_led_walls_on_cinematic_realism | LED wall]] capable of displaying any desired environment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:28">[00:01:28]</a>. It allows filmmakers to design diverse and intricate sets that can be instantly changed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:00">[00:02:00]</a>.

### Solving the "Rear Projection" Problem

Historically, movies used "rear projection techniques" with still images or videos behind actors, similar to classic driving scenes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:09">[00:02:09]</a>. However, this method suffered from a critical flaw: the lack of **motion parallax** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:18">[00:02:18]</a>. Motion parallax is the natural phenomenon where closer objects shift at a different speed than farther ones as the camera moves <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:23">[00:02:23]</a>. Without it, the scene looks "weird" or unnatural <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:29">[00:02:29]</a>.

An XR stage resolves this by creating a live virtual world in the background that perfectly matches the movements of the cameras <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:34">[00:02:34]</a>. The background actively moves, simulating true parallax, where foreground elements shift differently from those in the background <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:50">[00:02:50]</a>. This is achieved because the camera's position is tracked by a special gadget, allowing the "moving window" displayed on the LED wall to adjust perfectly and render a realistic view <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:12">[00:03:12]</a>.

### Eliminating Green Screen Spill

Another significant advantage of XR stages over traditional green or blue screens is the elimination of "spill" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:37">[00:03:37]</a>. Spill refers to the unwanted reflection of the green or blue screen color onto actors or props, which is a persistent problem for visual effects teams and difficult to remove in post-production, especially with reflective surfaces <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:40">[00:03:40]</a>.

With an XR stage, the virtual background provides realistic lighting during shooting, preventing any color spill <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:02">[00:04:02]</a>. The lights and environment on the stage match perfectly, creating a far more seamless and convincing effect <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:17">[00:04:17]</a>.

## Case Study: Disney's "The Mandalorian" and "The Volume"

The Disney+ series *The Mandalorian* famously utilized an XR stage, which they named "The Volume" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:24">[00:04:24]</a>. This technology allowed them to achieve stunning, accurate reflections on characters like Mando, who wears highly reflective armor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:26">[00:04:26]</a>. Richard Bluff, visual effects supervisor for *The Mandalorian*, stated that without The Volume, they would "almost certainly be replacing all of his armor with CG armor" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:31">[00:04:31]</a>.

### Integration of Real and Virtual Elements

In scenes shot on an XR stage, elements like walls, ceilings, and even the ground can be entirely virtual while still casting realistic light and shadows on the characters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:58">[00:04:58]</a>. Simultaneously, real physical props (e.g., a pole or a desk) can be incorporated, blurring the line between the real set and the virtual screen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:03">[00:05:03]</a>. Filmmakers decide what to make real versus virtual, often by "slicing" the virtual environment to extend real-world elements like sand <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:17">[00:05:17]</a>.

### Efficiency and Accessibility Benefits

A significant advantage of XR stages is the speed and ease with which backgrounds can be changed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:32">[00:05:32]</a>. This flexibility allows for rapid changes, such as altering the time of day, making productions cheaper and faster <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:39">[00:05:39]</a>. For *The Mandalorian*, despite needing to tell a *Star Wars* story that spanned many different planets, they only required three physical locations for shooting across season one by utilizing The Volume to load various virtual sets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:44">[00:05:44]</a>.

## Advanced Multi-Camera Capabilities

One of the most innovative features of these LED screens is their ability to display different content to multiple cameras simultaneously <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:09">[00:06:09]</a>. This is achieved by refreshing the LED wall at extremely high frames per second (e.g., 120 FPS), allowing different cameras (e.g., capturing at 60 FPS) to each capture alternating frames, thus seeing entirely different environments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:29">[00:06:29]</a>. This allows for scenarios like one camera seeing a green screen while another sees a blue screen, or each seeing the correct color reflections in a mirror <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:05">[00:07:05]</a>. The technology supports up to six cameras filming simultaneously, with the wall refreshing at a minimum of 180 FPS <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:26">[00:07:26]</a>.

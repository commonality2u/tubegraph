## Personalized AI

A core focus for Meta is the development of [[advantages_and_risks_of_ai_technology | personalized AI]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:13">[00:12:13]</a>, exemplified by projects like Llama and Meta AI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:20">[00:12:20]</a>. The goal is to make [[advantages_and_risks_of_ai_technology | AI models]] increasingly smarter and, crucially, personalized for individual users <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:25">[00:12:25]</a>.

### Contextual Understanding for Personalization
For [[advantages_and_risks_of_ai_technology | AI]] to be truly personalized, it must gain "context and understand what's going on in your life" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:38">[00:12:38]</a>. This includes both global context and what is physically happening around the user in real-time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:44">[00:12:44]</a>.

Glasses are considered the "ideal form factor" for achieving this contextual awareness, as they are positioned to "see what you see and hear what you hear" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:49">[00:12:49]</a>, which are humanity's two most important senses for gathering information about the world <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:58">[00:12:58]</a>.

### Capabilities of Personalized AI
The aspiration for [[advantages_and_risks_of_ai_technology | personalized AI]] is to create a "deep and profound" capability that offers "really personalized intelligence that can help you" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:03">[00:13:03]</a>.

## The Philosophical Debate: Struggle vs. Utility in [[impact_of_ai_on_human_creativity | AI]]

The application of [[advantages_and_risks_of_ai_technology | AI]] raises questions about where to draw the line between removing struggle for utility and preserving struggle for human development.

### [[advantages_and_risks_of_ai_technology | AI]] as a Humanity Unlocker
Some [[advantages_and_risks_of_ai_technology | AI technologies]] are seen as "measurably unlock[ing] our humanity" by removing struggles between people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:16">[00:25:16]</a>. A key example is automatic real-time translation, akin to the Star Trek Universal Translator <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:42">[00:24:42]</a>, which is nearing reality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:47">[00:24:47]</a>. While it might reduce the functional need to learn new languages, the cultural and structural benefits of language learning persist, similar to learning Latin or Greek <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:04">[00:25:04]</a>.

### Preserving the "Muscle Building" Struggle
Conversely, there are areas where struggle is beneficial, acting like "building a muscle" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:27">[00:25:27]</a>. For instance, the act of struggling to articulate emotions, while challenging, is important for personal development <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:04">[00:26:04]</a>.

Mark Zuckerberg believes humanity will "always find new things to struggle with" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:42">[00:26:42]</a>. He draws parallels to coding:
*   **Coding Evolution**: Just as coding evolved from low-level systems to easier app development, future [[advantages_and_risks_of_ai_technology | AI tools]] will allow kids to "just describe the things that they want and build incredibly complex pieces of software" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:34">[00:27:34]</a>.
*   **Thinking Rigorously**: Despite [[advantages_and_risks_of_ai_technology | AI's]] capabilities, teaching programming remains vital as it teaches "a way to think rigorously" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:55">[00:29:55]</a>, making individuals "generally a better thinker and better person" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:06">[00:30:06]</a>. This is likened to still learning basic math despite calculators <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:14">[00:30:14]</a>.

The core concern is maintaining "self-confidence and ability" in basic skills <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:52">[00:28:52]</a>, while recognizing that [[advantages_and_risks_of_ai_technology | AI]] will elevate the baseline of what is possible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:51">[00:27:51]</a>.

## Open Source [[advantages_and_risks_of_ai_technology | AI]]
A significant debate in the [[advantages_and_risks_of_ai_technology | AI field]] revolves around open source models.

### Centralized vs. Decentralized AI
Mark Zuckerberg advocates for a future with many different [[advantages_and_risks_of_ai_technology | AI systems]], rather than a centralized "one AI" approach like some companies are pursuing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:07">[00:40:07]</a>. He believes every business, creator, and individual will eventually have their own [[advantages_and_risks_of_ai_technology | AI]] to interact with customers or communities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:22">[00:40:22]</a>. Open source allows anyone to "take and modify the model and build stuff on top of it" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:09">[00:41:09]</a>, fostering this diversity.

### Safety and Security
The safety debate for [[advantages_and_risks_of_ai_technology | AI]] questions whether keeping models closed makes them safer by preventing "bad developers doing bad things" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:40">[00:41:40]</a>. Historically, open source software has proven to be "safer and more secure" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:14">[00:42:14]</a> due to increased scrutiny from more people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:20">[00:42:20]</a>. Issues are identified and fixed quicker, leading to continuous improvement and safer models for everyone <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:49">[00:42:49]</a>.

## The Unanswered Question: Scaling Limits of [[machine_learning_and_its_implications | AI]]

A major, high-stakes question for the [[future_of_ai_as_a_tool_for_artists | future of AI]] is the scaling limit of current methods, particularly transformer-based architectures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:17">[00:44:17]</a>.
*   **Continuous Growth**: Unlike past [[machine_learning_and_its_implications | AI architectures]] that hit plateaus, modern transformer models have not yet found an "end" to their scaling <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:44">[00:44:44]</a>. This allows for massive scaling, from thousands of GPUs for Llama 3 to potentially over 100,000 GPUs for Llama 4 and beyond <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:52">[00:44:52]</a>.
*   **Uncertainty**: It's possible that a limit will be reached, similar to an asymptote, where continued scaling no longer yields significant improvements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:06">[00:45:06]</a>. Alternatively, this limit may not be reached anytime soon, allowing for continuous advancement and usefulness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:17">[00:45:17]</a>.

Meta is making "hundreds of billions of dollars" of infrastructure bets on the assumption that [[machine_learning_and_its_implications | AI]] will continue to scale <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:42">[00:45:42]</a>. This ongoing advancement would lead to a "dynamic" [[technological_advancements_and_global_competition_in_ai | technology landscape]] and compelling new products for the next 20 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:31">[00:46:31]</a>.

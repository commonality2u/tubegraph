## Evolution of Social Media Interaction

Initially, social media platforms were primarily designed for individuals to interact with their friends <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:10">[00:32:10]</a>. However, this has shifted dramatically, with at least half of the content now involving interactions with creators or content not personally known to the user <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:23">[00:32:23]</a>. This paradigm is expected to be further accelerated by [[machine_learning_and_its_implications | AI]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:36">[00:32:36]</a>.

## Societal Implications and Human Connection

The evolution of social media and the integration of [[future_of_ai_and_personalized_ai | AI]] occur against a backdrop of changing social dynamics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:33">[00:21:33]</a>.

### Declining In-Person Socialization
Research indicates a decline in human connection, with the average American having fewer friends now than 15 years ago <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:25">[00:20:25]</a>. Over the last two decades, the time American adults spend socializing in person has dropped by nearly 30%, and for ages 15 to 24, this figure is nearly 70% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:43">[00:20:43]</a>. The percentage of Americans reporting no close friends has also increased from 3% to 12% in the last 30 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:15">[00:21:15]</a>. These trends largely predate much of modern technology <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:49">[00:21:49]</a>.

### AI and Enhanced Digital Presence
Rather than replacing physical connection, [[human_connection_in_the_digital_age | digital connection]] aims to provide an additional means of socialization for those who may lack it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:16">[00:22:16]</a>. The goal is to unlock more opportunities for people to be present with others who are physically distant <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:45">[00:22:45]</a>. Technologies like holographic augmented reality glasses envision a future where individuals can interact with full body holograms of others, enabling activities like games, work, or simply shared presence, beyond mere video calls <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:00">[00:03:00]</a>. This pursuit of "presence" is seen as a "Holy Grail" in designing social applications <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:02">[00:12:02]</a>.

While [[advantages_and_risks_of_ai_technology | AI]] promises to enhance the quality of work and communication efficiency, it also necessitates that professionals keep pace with new trends to remain competitive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:35">[00:36:35]</a>. ### Open Source AI and its Implications
There is a significant debate within the AI field regarding open source models <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:37">[00:39:37]</a>. Some companies, like Open AI and Google, are building centralized AI systems, implying a future where users interact with one primary AI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:52">[00:39:52]</a>. However, an alternative vision, supported by Meta, suggests a diverse ecosystem of many different [[future_of_ai_and_personalized_ai | AI systems]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:07">[00:40:07]</a>. This perspective argues that every business and creator will eventually have their own AI to interact with customers or communities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:22">[00:40:22]</a>.

The core of the open source debate centers on safety <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:26">[00:41:26]</a>. Some argue that keeping models closed makes them safer by limiting misuse by "bad developers" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:40">[00:41:40]</a>. Historically, however, open source software has proven to be safer and more secure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:59">[00:41:59]</a>. This is because public scrutiny allows for quicker identification and patching of bugs and security vulnerabilities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:14">[00:42:14]</a>. The belief is that this counterintuitive approach will lead to smarter and safer [[advantages_and_risks_of_ai_technology | AI models]] for everyone <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:12">[00:43:12]</a>.

The trajectory of [[machine_learning_and_its_implications | AI model]] scaling, particularly with transformer-based architectures, remains an open question <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:17">[00:44:17]</a>. It is unclear if there's a plateau to how useful and capable these models can become by simply scaling up compute and data <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:06">[00:45:06]</a>. The investment in infrastructure to support this scaling represents a significant bet on the continued advancement of [[technological_advancements_and_global_competition_in_AI | AI technology]] for decades to come <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:42">[00:45:42]</a>.

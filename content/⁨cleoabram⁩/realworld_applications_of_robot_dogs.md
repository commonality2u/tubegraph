## "Inspector Spot": Real-World Jobs

The primary function of [[capabilities_of_boston_dynamics_spot_robot | Spot]] across various industries is inspection. This has led to it being colloquially referred to as "Inspector Spot" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:27">[00:02:27]</a>. Its roles include:
*   **Factory Inspection**: At a Purina dog food factory, [[capabilities_of_boston_dynamics_spot_robot | Spot]] inspects packaging for air leaks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:09">[00:02:09]</a>.
*   **Energy Infrastructure**: On an oil rig in the Gulf of Mexico, it inspects equipment for corrosion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:13">[00:02:13]</a>. [[capabilities_of_boston_dynamics_spot_robot | Spot]] is also used in defunct nuclear power plants <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:36">[00:00:36]</a>.
*   **Archaeological Sites**: In the ancient ruins of Pompeii, [[capabilities_of_boston_dynamics_spot_robot | Spot]] inspects grave robber tunnels for the Italian police <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:18">[00:02:18]</a>.
*   **Hazardous Environments**: In the remains of the Fukushima nuclear power plant, [[capabilities_of_boston_dynamics_spot_robot | Spot]] inspects areas for radiation levels and safety, accessing areas untouched by humans since 2011 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:23">[00:02:23]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:24">[00:07:24]</a>. It was also sent to Chernobyl <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:09">[00:01:09]</a>.
*   **Industrial Sites**: It operates in deep mines <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:39">[00:00:39]</a>.
*   **Public Safety**: [[capabilities_of_boston_dynamics_spot_robot | Spot]] is being tried out by several police departments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:04">[00:01:04]</a>, and was controversially used by the New York Police Department in 2021 to locate a suspect <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:42">[00:00:42]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:42">[00:07:42]</a>. It is also utilized by fire departments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:36">[00:00:36]</a>.

These jobs are often dangerous, require constant or reliable inspection, or are incredibly boring for humans, making a robot a helpful solution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:40">[00:02:40]</a>.

## Why a Robot Dog?

The choice of a robot dog over a wheeled robot or a [[humanoid_robots_and_their_capabilities | humanoid robot]] is due to the environments in which it operates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:51">[00:02:51]</a>. Its jobs are in places mostly designed for humans, where four legs provide greater stability than two and are easier to coordinate than six <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:57">[00:02:57]</a>.

## Capabilities and Operation

[[capabilities_of_boston_dynamics_spot_robot | Spot]] does not arrive "job-ready"; it requires a step-by-step demonstration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:08">[00:03:08]</a>. Users manually drive it through a specific "mission," which [[capabilities_of_boston_dynamics_spot_robot | Spot]] can then repeat autonomously <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:17">[00:03:17]</a>. This autonomy includes the ability to improvise, allowing it to adjust in real-time to avoid new obstacles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:22">[00:03:22]</a>. [[capabilities_of_boston_dynamics_spot_robot | Spot]] is also incredibly robust and designed to protect itself from injuries, such as falling down stairs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:50">[00:05:50]</a>.

Advanced software developments for [[capabilities_of_boston_dynamics_spot_robot | Spot]] include the ability to identify a person lying down, enabling the robot to get help if it finds someone in distress, such as after an electrocution in a factory <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:37">[00:05:37]</a>.

## The "Platform" Concept and [[future_of_robotics_and_ai_applications | Future Applications]]

The key to [[capabilities_of_boston_dynamics_spot_robot | Spot]]'s versatility is its design as a "platform" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:08">[00:08:08]</a>. This allows customers to develop their own payloads and build new capabilities on top of the base robot <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:03">[00:08:03]</a>. This flexibility means people can devise new uses for the technology, both for better and for worse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:15">[00:08:15]</a>.

The development of similar robots by other companies, such as Ghost Robotics, for military use, further highlights this platform approach <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:47">[00:08:47]</a>. For instance, the Air Force partnered with Ghost Robotics in 2020 to use robot dogs as inspectors at Tyndall Air Force Base, though notably without weapons <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:51">[00:08:51]</a>.

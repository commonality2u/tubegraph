[[introduction_to_formula_1_racing | Formula 1]] cars are not merely vehicles; they are described as "epic group science projects" where ten teams compete, involving hundreds of people and millions of dollars to push [[engineering_and_technology_in_formula_1 | technology]] to its limit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:30">[00:00:30]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:36">[00:00:36]</a>. The objective is to build and drive some of the fastest and most expensive cars globally <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:20">[00:00:20]</a>, with winning dependent on how the car is built at the factory <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:04">[00:03:04]</a>.

## Design Philosophy and Regulations

Unlike series such as IndyCar, where all cars use the same frame, each [[introduction_to_formula_1_racing | Formula 1]] team is required to design and manufacture their own [[basics_of_formula_1_cars | cars]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:57">[00:02:57]</a>. This design process is governed by strict regulations set by the FIA and [[introduction_to_formula_1_racing | F1]], which dictate the appearance and shapes of the cars <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:09">[00:03:09]</a>, forming the "formula" in [[introduction_to_formula_1_racing | Formula 1]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:14">[00:03:14]</a>. Within these rules, teams constantly strive to find the best design to gain an advantage, as margins between competitors are incredibly slim <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:17">[00:03:17]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:30">[00:03:30]</a>.

Teams are permitted to spend $135 million per year, excluding driver salaries, necessitating that every decision maximizes performance for cost <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:40">[00:03:40]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:47">[00:03:47]</a>. The [[engineering_and_technology_in_formula_1 | engineering]] involved is "next level," utilizing techniques and [[engineering_and_technology_in_formula_1 | technologies]] akin to those used by NASA or SpaceX for designing rockets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:52">[00:03:52]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:57">[00:03:57]</a>.

## Aerodynamics

A winning [[basics_of_formula_1_cars | Formula 1 car]] must excel at both making super quick turns and achieving extreme speeds in a straight line <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:31">[00:05:31]</a>. The primary challenge in design is that features enhancing one aspect often hinder the other <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:36">[00:05:36]</a>.

### Downforce
To make quick turns, a strong grip on the ground is essential, achieved by generating "downforce" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:45">[00:05:45]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:50">[00:05:50]</a>. More downforce results in stronger grip and quicker turns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:53">[00:05:53]</a>. A [[basics_of_formula_1_cars | Formula 1 car]] is designed to act like an upside-down airplane wing, with air moving across its surfaces forcing it down <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:59">[00:05:59]</a>. This "sucking" effect is created by having higher air pressure above the car and lower air pressure below it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:10">[00:06:10]</a>. Low-pressure air is achieved by making it move very fast <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:15">[00:06:15]</a>.

Engineers aim to speed up the air as much as possible under the floor of the car and slow it down on top <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:20">[00:06:20]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:24">[00:06:24]</a>. A key technique involves creating "tiny tornadoes" or "vortices" under the car <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:32">[00:06:32]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:36">[00:06:36]</a>. These powerful rotating structures generate significant low pressure along the length of the floor, effectively sucking the car towards the ground for better grip and faster turns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:39">[00:06:39]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:44">[00:06:44]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:53">[00:06:53]</a>. At top speed, a [[basics_of_formula_1_cars | Formula 1 car]] can generate downforce three or four times its own weight <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:57">[00:06:57]</a>.

### Drag and Drag Reduction System (DRS)
While downforce is crucial for turns, it comes with a cost: added drag <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:02">[00:07:02]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:13">[00:07:13]</a>. Drag is a resistive force that prevents the car from moving efficiently through the air, crucial for high straight-line speeds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:08">[00:07:08]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:13">[00:07:13]</a>. For example, the rear wing, which generates downforce in turns, acts like a parachute slowing the car on straights <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:18">[00:07:18]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:23">[00:07:23]</a>. To mitigate this, [[introduction_to_formula_1_racing | Formula 1]] teams incorporate a Drag Reduction System (DRS), which allows the driver to flip part of the wing down, enabling the car to accelerate faster on straights <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:27">[00:07:27]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:32">[00:07:32]</a>. Designing a winning [[basics_of_formula_1_cars | Formula 1 car]] involves balancing these opposing effects and pushing the limits of physics and regulations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:36">[00:07:36]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:42">[00:07:42]</a>.

## Materials and Manufacturing

The lightness, speed, and strength of [[basics_of_formula_1_cars | Formula 1 cars]] are largely due to the materials used <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:30">[00:09:30]</a>.

### Carbon Fiber
Most visible parts of an [[basics_of_formula_1_cars | Formula 1 car]] are made from carbon fiber, a material ten times stronger than steel but half the weight <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:30">[00:09:30]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:35">[00:09:35]</a>. Its excellent strength-to-weight ratio allows for extremely light yet structurally sound components <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:57">[00:09:57]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:01">[00:10:01]</a>. Carbon fiber parts are manufactured by creating patterns, laying down sometimes 100 layers of carbon fiber cloth, removing air, and then compressing them with resin in a pressure cooker <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:10">[00:10:10]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:14">[00:10:14]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:20">[00:10:20]</a>.

### Metals
Various metals are used for specific parts depending on their required function <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:04">[00:11:04]</a>. Aluminum is widely used throughout the car for its light weight and strength <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:10">[00:11:10]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:15">[00:11:15]</a>. Densimet, an alloy, is used to add weight to the car, often in tiny strips, to meet the minimum weight requirement if the driver is under weight <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:36">[00:11:36]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:41">[00:11:41]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:45">[00:11:45]</a>.

### Finishing
After being designed, molded, and machined by programmable machines <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:25">[00:10:25]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:32">[00:10:32]</a>, some of the thousands of pieces that make up a [[basics_of_formula_1_cars | Formula 1 car]] are painted <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:54">[00:11:54]</a>. The paint job is not just aesthetic; it contributes to performance. Sponsor logos are painted on rather than stickered to avoid tiny edges that would create unwanted drag <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:16">[00:12:16]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:19">[00:12:19]</a>.

## Engines

[[basics_of_formula_1_cars | Formula 1]] engines are among the world's most advanced engine development facilities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:37">[00:12:37]</a>. Some teams, like Red Bull, are developing their own custom engines to gain greater control and optimize the integration between the chassis and engine, believing this tight tie-in is paramount to winning <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:47">[00:12:47]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:06">[00:13:06]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:11">[00:13:11]</a>. The primary goal for an [[engineering_and_technology_in_formula_1 | F1]] engine is maximum power <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:57">[00:13:57]</a>.

### Internal Combustion
Like standard road cars, [[basics_of_formula_1_cars | Formula 1 cars]] utilize a four-stroke internal combustion engine, typically a V6 configuration with six cylinders arranged in a 'V' shape <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:11">[00:14:11]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:16">[00:14:16]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:39">[00:14:39]</a>. In this process, air and fuel enter the cylinder, are compressed, ignited by a spark, and explode, pushing a piston down to turn gears and generate mechanical movement for the wheels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:20">[00:14:20]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:26">[00:14:26]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:30">[00:14:30]</a>. The piston then pushes out the remaining gas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:35">[00:14:35]</a>.

### Key Differences from Road Cars
A significant difference lies in the maximum number of cycles per minute (RPM). While a typical car engine might complete about 6,000 cycles per minute, a [[basics_of_formula_1_cars | Formula 1 car]] engine exceeds 15,000, contributing to much greater power and speed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:49">[00:14:49]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:54">[00:14:54]</a>. [[basics_of_formula_1_cars | F1 cars]] also integrate [[engineering_and_technology_in_formula_1 | technologies]] like a turbocharger, which compresses air before it enters the cylinder, increasing the explosive force when ignited <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:01">[00:15:01]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:07">[00:15:07]</a>.

### [[future_developments_in_formula_1_engines | Future Developments]]
The engines being developed for 2026 will use less fuel and more electricity, leading to a slightly different sound <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:35">[00:15:35]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:39">[00:15:39]</a>. These engines undergo rigorous testing at different speeds, running power curves to measure performance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:03">[00:16:03]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:09">[00:16:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:14">[00:16:14]</a>. The advancements in [[basics_of_formula_1_cars | Formula 1]] engine [[engineering_and_technology_in_formula_1 | technology]] help inspire better car [[engineering_and_technology_in_formula_1 | tech]] for general automotive use <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:19">[00:15:19]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:35">[00:15:35]</a>, contributing to [[formula_1s_impact_on_car_innovation_and_future_trends | F1's impact on car innovation and future trends]].

## Testing and Simulation

Throughout the design process, [[economic_aspects_and_financial_operations_of_formula_1_teams | teams]] constantly test their designs using various methods <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:09">[00:09:09]</a>. These include:
*   **Computer simulations**: Running virtual tests of designs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:14">[00:09:14]</a>.
*   **Physical rigs**: Mimicking the movement of a car to test components <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:14">[00:09:14]</a>.
*   **Wind tunnels**: Large facilities where teams test 60% scale versions of their cars due to [[introduction_to_formula_1_racing | Formula 1]] limits on full-size car testing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:16">[00:09:16]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:20">[00:09:20]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:25">[00:09:25]</a>.

During races, strategists, many of whom are back at the factory in a room resembling NASA mission control, use supercomputers to run millions of simulations in real-time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:31">[00:20:31]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:41">[00:20:41]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:46">[00:20:46]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:51">[00:20:51]</a>. These simulations factor in weather, brake heat, tire performance, and competitors' actions, providing crucial data to the race engineers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:56">[00:20:56]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:01">[00:21:01]</a>.

## Human Factor in Design

The design of a [[basics_of_formula_1_cars | Formula 1 car]] also considers the driver's specific needs. Drivers spend 3 to 4 hours having their seat fitted, sitting in clay to form a custom seat around them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:31">[00:02:31]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:38">[00:02:38]</a>. This carbon fiber seat is crucial as drivers are in a "very unnatural position" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:29">[00:18:29]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:34">[00:18:34]</a>. Each driver has a "whole book of measurements" for their seat and pedal positions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:24">[00:18:24]</a>.

The steering wheel is also custom-designed for each team, featuring dozens of buttons, knobs, and switches that drivers memorize to optimize engine, tires, brakes, and other systems while driving at high speeds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:49">[00:18:49]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:53">[00:18:53]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:57">[00:18:57]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:01">[00:19:01]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:06">[00:19:06]</a>.

An [[basics_of_formula_1_cars | F1 car]] is never truly "done" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:12">[00:22:12]</a>. Teams are constantly learning, changing, and rebuilding components between every single race <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:12">[00:22:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:19">[00:22:19]</a>. The continuous effort of engineers, designers, strategists, and athletes working together allows the cars to improve with every iteration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:50">[00:22:50]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:55">[00:22:55]</a>.

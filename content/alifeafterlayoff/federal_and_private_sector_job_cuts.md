## Federal Layoffs

[[current_trends_and_causes_of_layoffs|Layoffs have been surging]], notably with the Department of Government Efficiency (Doge Department) cutting into the federal workforce <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:36">[00:00:36]</a>. This impact is also extending to the private sector <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:40">[00:00:40]</a>.

For March, furloughs in the federal government totaled 216,000, contributing to a broader total of 275,200 labor force reductions, according to Challenger, Gray and Christmas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:44">[00:00:44]</a>. These cuts not only affect federal positions but also ancillary support groups and local businesses that rely on federal employees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:07">[00:01:07]</a>. Continued layoffs by the Department of Government Efficiency are expected, requiring federal employees to pay close attention to their departments and consider rebranding themselves <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:33">[00:01:33]</a>.

## Broader Economic Picture

The US economy is slowing down <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:49">[00:08:49]</a>:
*   **GDP Growth:** Expected to drop from 2.8% in 2024 to 1.9% in 2025. Some forecasts even show a contraction of 2.8% in the first quarter of 2025 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:55">[00:08:55]</a>.
*   **Unemployment:** Currently around 4.1%, but could reach 4.8-5% by next April due to [[tech_layoffs_and_job_market_challenges|layoffs]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:12">[00:09:12]</a>.
*   **Inflation:** Remains around 3%, still above the Fed's 2% target <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:23">[00:09:23]</a>.

### Impact of Tariffs

Tariffs are significantly impacting prices of imported goods like steel, cars, and electronics, affecting consumer spending <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:30">[00:09:30]</a>. Manufacturing in the United States is also weakening, with growth down to 2% in 2025 from 2.9% a year ago <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:43">[00:09:43]</a>. People are spending less as job fears increase <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:55">[00:09:55]</a>.

While tariffs are causing immediate pain and will lead to [[cost_cutting_measures_and_their_effects_on_layoffs|layoffs]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:46">[00:11:46]</a>, there might be a long-term benefit for the US manufacturing sector <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:51">[00:11:51]</a>. For example, the Nissan plant in Tennessee, which had planned to move production to Mexico, has walked back those plans due to tariffs, saving jobs and reinstituting shifts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:34">[00:10:34]</a>. This could make manufacturing roles in areas like plant engineering, plant management, safety, and maintenance more favorable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:54">[00:10:54]</a>.

### CFO Perspectives

A survey of CFOs at major corporations reveals widespread pessimism about a recession by the end of 2025 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:40">[00:12:40]</a>.
*   95% of CFOs reported that current policy impacts their ability to make business decisions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:03">[00:13:03]</a>.
*   40% stated they "don't know" which sector will have the biggest growth over the next six months, while 20% named energy, and 10% each named tech and healthcare <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:36">[00:13:36]</a>.
*   Zero CFOs believed communication services, consumer discretionary, real estate, or utilities would be the best performing sectors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:48">[00:13:48]</a>.
*   75% are somewhat pessimistic about the economy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:58">[00:13:58]</a>.
*   60% expect a recession in the second half of 2025, and 15% foresee it in 2026 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:05">[00:14:05]</a>.
*   The majority (40% mild, 90% moderate) expect any recession to be mild to moderate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:16">[00:14:16]</a>.
*   90% expect the Dow to fall below 40,000 before reaching 50,000, erasing gains since August of the previous year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:27">[00:14:27]</a>.

## Preparing for Potential Layoffs

[[strategies_for_mitigating_layoff_impacts|Job security]] is shakier than in years, and the [[tech_layoffs_and_job_market_challenges|labor market]] is unpredictable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:07">[00:16:07]</a>. With millions of layoffs on the horizon, having a game plan is crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:15">[00:16:15]</a>.

Individuals should:
*   **Assess Skills:** Evaluate current skills against future market needs and work to fill any gaps <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:34">[00:16:34]</a>.
*   **Build Networks:** Expand professional networks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:48">[00:16:48]</a>.
*   **Create Side Income Streams:** Develop alternative sources of income to protect against income disruption <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:53">[00:16:53]</a>.
*   **Save Money:** Set aside funds now to avoid dipping into retirement savings like 401ks if things worsen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:11">[00:17:11]</a>.

These proactive measures can help individuals navigate potential [[handling_layoffs_and_terminations_at_companies|career shifts]] or disruptions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:06">[00:18:06]</a>.

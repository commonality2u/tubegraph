### Understanding Recruiter Sourcing (10/10)
The single most effective way to maximize your job search results is by understanding how recruiters operate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:35">[00:18:35]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:38">[00:18:38]</a>. The majority of recruiters—over 80%—fill their open positions through outbound sourcing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:45">[00:17:45]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:47">[00:17:47]</a>. This means recruiters actively search the market to find candidates who closely fit the profile their hiring managers are seeking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:51">[00:17:51]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:53">[00:17:53]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:57">[00:17:57]</a>.

When a recruiter finds you through outbound sourcing, you are immediately pulled to the front of the line, often bypassing the typical application process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:12">[00:18:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:15">[00:18:15]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:17">[00:18:17]</a>. Recruiters spend most of their time perusing platforms like [[networking_for_job_seekers | LinkedIn]] and other resources to identify potential candidates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:21">[00:18:21]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:24">[00:18:24]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:26">[00:18:26]</a>. By setting yourself up to be discoverable, you gain a significant advantage over the average job seeker <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:07">[00:18:07]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:29">[00:18:29]</a>.

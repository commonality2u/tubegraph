## A Personal Account of [[career management and being a free agent | Career Missteps]]

The speaker shares a personal story highlighting the [[importance of proactive career management | importance of proactive career management]] and the pitfalls of neglecting it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:06">[00:00:06]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:02">[00:07:02]</a>.

### Initial Opportunity and Unfulfilled Promise
Early in his career, the speaker was recruited into a small railroad services company of 600-700 employees as a Staffing and Development Manager, which evolved into a Human Resources Manager role <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:53">[00:00:53]</a>. The role offered substantial work and valuable experience, allowing him to impact the business directly through hiring and employee growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:17">[00:01:17]</a>.

The main reason for accepting the position was the promise of a promotion: he was supposed to learn from the Vice President of Human Resources, who was slated to retire in 18 months, after which the speaker would take over the role <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:29">[00:01:29]</a>.

### The Stagnation Period
The Vice President, despite the initial promise, showed no intention of retiring after 18 months <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:10">[00:03:10]</a>. This was part of a broader trend where several senior leaders, due to their influence and significant bonuses, delayed their planned retirements, retaining their "kingdoms" within the organization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:50">[00:03:50]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:02">[00:04:02]</a>. They also convinced themselves that no one was capable of taking over their roles, often telling potential successors they weren't "ready yet" without providing actual development opportunities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:31">[00:04:31]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:45">[00:04:45]</a>.

The speaker, part of a leadership development group, observed his peers one by one leaving the organization when promotions didn't materialize <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:09">[00:04:09]</a>. Only about one out of eight to ten people in this succession group actually achieved their intended level within the company <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:23">[00:04:23]</a>.

Despite the clear signs, the speaker remained in the role for nine years, well beyond the initial 18-month expectation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:34">[00:05:34]</a>. He became comfortable with a steady paycheck and a supportive, family-oriented boss, but ultimately grew apathetic and stopped actively pursuing [[career advancement and job satisfaction | career advancement]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:43">[00:06:43]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:02">[00:09:02]</a>. He did not push for development or new opportunities, essentially "playing checkers" with his career instead of "chess" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:50">[00:09:50]</a>.

### Consequences of Apathy
This period of stagnation led to significant professional setbacks:
*   **Loss of currency**: The speaker stopped learning and keeping up with industry trends, technology, and buzzwords, falling behind his peer group in skills and knowledge <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:03">[00:10:03]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:26">[00:10:26]</a>.
*   **Belief in self-doubt**: Repeatedly being told he wasn't "ready" caused him to internalize that belief, despite his peers proving their readiness by succeeding elsewhere <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:31">[00:08:31]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:48">[00:08:48]</a>.
*   **Career setback**: When he eventually re-entered the job market, he had to take a step down in responsibility to gain relevant recruiting experience at larger companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:45">[00:10:45]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:58">[00:10:58]</a>. This put him a decade behind his peer group, who were moving into senior director roles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:12">[00:11:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:18">[00:11:18]</a>.
*   **Financial implications**: He realized he was significantly undercompensated compared to the market rate, costing him thousands in lost income over the years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:26">[00:12:26]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:44">[00:12:44]</a>.

## Lessons for [[proactive career management strategies | Proactive Career Management]]

The speaker's experience underscores several critical lessons for [[strategies for effective career management | effective career management]]:

*   **Be the CEO of your career**: This means taking ownership, having a plan and a strategy, and not simply waiting for opportunities to arise or for others to recognize your efforts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:56">[00:09:56]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:08">[00:13:08]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:44">[00:13:44]</a>.
*   **Avoid comfort and apathy**: While a steady job can be appealing, becoming too comfortable can lead to stagnation and a lack of proactive decision-making for your [[long term job stability and career management | long-term career interests]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:34">[00:09:34]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:42">[00:09:42]</a>.
*   **Continual learning and development**: Staying current with industry trends, technology, and skills is crucial to remain competitive in the job market <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:03">[00:10:03]</a>.
*   **Test the open market**: Consistently exploring other opportunities, even if you are employed, helps you understand your market value and ensures you are positioned for the best possible opportunities, acting as a "true free agent" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:33">[00:15:33]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:37">[00:15:37]</a>.
*   **Recognize and address being "stuck"**: If you are too good at your job and your company keeps you in that role, preventing advancement, it's time to take control of your [[career strategy and coaching | career strategy]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:55">[00:13:55]</a>.
*   **Time is a limited resource**: Your career runway shortens quickly, especially in mid-career. Waiting around means wasting valuable time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:52">[00:11:52]</a>.

For individuals looking to move ahead, especially if they are stuck or dissatisfied, adopting a "chess player" mindset for their career is essential <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:05">[00:13:05]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:19">[00:13:19]</a>. This proactive approach involves [[job search strategies and career management | job search strategies]] like crafting effective resumes and mastering interview skills to market oneself effectively and negotiate compensation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:10">[00:14:10]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:00">[00:15:00]</a>. Leveraging professional networks, such as LinkedIn, can help attract recruiters and generate opportunities rather than relying solely on applications <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:51">[00:15:51]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:54">[00:15:54]</a>.

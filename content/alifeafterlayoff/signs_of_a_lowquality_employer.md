## Case Studies: Cringeworthy Job Postings and Practices

### Public Shaming and Toxic Management
One clear [[signs_of_a_toxic_workplace | sign of a toxic workplace]] or [[signs_of_a_problematic_boss | problematic management]] is public shaming of employees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:07:00">[02:07:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:14:00">[02:14:00]</a>.

> [01:29:00] A gas station sign read: "We are closed due to Alex walking out. We apologize about the inconvenience. - Racetrack Team."
>
> The leadership team publicly threw "poor Alex here under the bus" to future customers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:43:00">[01:43:00]</a>. This childish behavior suggests the leadership team was likely the problem, leading Alex to leave due to poor treatment or pay <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:07:00">[02:07:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:14:00">[02:14:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:30:00">[02:30:00]</a>.

### Unrealistic Compensation and Loyalty Expectations
Employers who offer minimal raises or implement ambiguous "loyalty components" to compensation may indicate a disregard for employee value and career growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:12:00">[03:12:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:16:00">[03:16:00]</a>.

> [02:54:00] An educational establishment announced a new pay scale for instructional aides, starting at $12/hour. After five years, employees would receive a 50-cent raise, and another 50 cents after 10 years, and so on <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:39:00">[03:39:00]</a>.
>
> With just 3% inflation, $12/hour would need to be nearly $14/hour after five years just to keep pace <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:09:00">[04:09:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:14:00">[04:14:00]</a>. This pay structure, which offers only a $3/hour raise after 30 years, provides no incentive to stay <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:47:00">[04:47:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:51:00">[04:51:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:55:00">[04:55:00]</a>. Even a master's degree might only yield a 50-cent bump at the superintendent's discretion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:20:00">[05:20:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:32:00">[05:32:00]</a>.

### Expecting Unpaid Work for Equity
Startups offering equity instead of salary can be a [[signs_of_a_poorly_funded_company | sign of a poorly funded company]] or an unrealistic business model <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:53:00">[07:53:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:05:00">[08:05:00]</a>.

> [06:12:00] Salty Llama, a startup, sought a Shopify web development manager for seven hours a week in exchange for up to 0.25% equity, with no salary or freelancer fee <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:16:00">[06:16:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:59:00">[06:59:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:53:00">[07:53:00]</a>. They stated this was temporary until the company gained traction and revenue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:07:00">[08:07:00]</a>.
>
> This is essentially asking someone to "work for free" with almost no chance of the equity turning into a liquid asset <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:05:00">[09:05:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:10:00">[09:10:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:19:00">[09:19:00]</a>. It's an "empty promise" of a potential future full-time position and generous salary <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:11:00">[08:11:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:27:00">[08:27:00]</a>.

### Disorganized and Demanding Hiring Processes
Companies with highly disorganized, generic, or excessively demanding hiring processes often struggle to attract and retain quality candidates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:11:00">[10:11:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:14:00">[10:14:00]</a>.

#### Generic Job Postings
> [09:58:00] Canonical, a software company, posted the same "Talent Selection Lead" position across multiple locations (e.g., San Diego, Charlotte) with a generic, unedited template that included placeholders like "Job Summary," "title must appear," and "recruiter adds additional benefits if applicable" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:01:00">[10:01:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:24:00">[10:24:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:41:00">[11:41:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:29:00">[12:29:00]</a>.
>
> Spamming job postings across wide geographic areas without tailoring descriptions suggests poor candidate flow, an inability to fill roles, or issues in their interview process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:01:00">[10:01:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:14:00">[10:14:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:00:00">[11:00:00]</a>. The unusual title "Talent Selection Lead" also raised suspicion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:12:00">[11:12:00]</a>.

#### Excessively Rigorous Interview Stages
> [13:11:00] Canonical's hiring process for a Senior Linux Support Engineer included:
> 1.  Initial application review
> 2.  Written interview (a prepared statement covering interests, experience, and ambition) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:18:00">[15:18:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:56:00">[17:56:00]</a>
> 3.  Standardized personality and aptitude tests <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:31:00">[15:31:00]</a>
> 4.  Introductory interview <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:55:00">[15:55:00]</a>
> 5.  Technical take-home test <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:29:00">[16:29:00]</a>
> 6.  Technical interview <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:44:00">[16:44:00]</a>
> 7.  Leadership interviews <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:51:00">[16:51:00]</a>
>
> This "ridiculous" process involves multiple hours of uncompensated effort (2-3 hours before even speaking to a hiring manager) with no guarantee of an interview <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:18:00">[16:18:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:21:00">[16:21:00]</a>. The "written interview" involves answering 30-40 questions, including irrelevant queries about high school and university achievements for a senior role <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:02:00">[19:02:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:19:00">[20:19:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:00:00">[21:00:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:14:00">[21:14:00]</a>. This process is a "lazy way" for recruiters to screen out candidates without putting in the work themselves <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:17:00">[18:17:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:25:00">[18:25:00]</a>.

### Devaluing Professional Work with Unpaid Internships
Offering unpaid positions, especially for skilled roles, demonstrates a lack of respect for an individual's time and professional value <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:47:00">[25:47:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:54:00">[25:54:00]</a>.

> [22:45:00] Turf Envy, a small company (11-50 employees), posted an "unpaid" Human Resources Specialist position as a "virtual internship" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:47:00">[22:47:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:19:00">[23:19:00]</a>. They claimed it was a way to "jump start your career" by "working for free" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:22:00">[23:22:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:26:00">[23:26:00]</a>.
>
> Such postings often include cringeworthy and unrealistic pitches about "fun" work environments, "superheroes," or "Avengers" analogies, suggesting a disconnect from professional reality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:46:00">[23:46:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:30:00">[24:30:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:58:00">[24:58:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:21:00">[25:21:00]</a>.

### Recruitment Practices and Recruiters
The attitude and practices of a company's recruitment team can also be a significant [[signs_of_a_red_flag_job_candidate | red flag]].

> [26:34:00] A recruiter posted online, "as a recruiter I get the most joy out of rejecting candidates" and claimed "rejecting a candidate with the dignity they deserve is how I add value to my clients" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:37:00">[26:37:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:13:00">[27:13:00]</a>.
>
> No recruiter of value genuinely enjoys rejecting people; it's an unpleasant but necessary part of the job <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:01:00">[28:01:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:20:00">[28:20:00]</a>. The greatest joy for a recruiter comes from offering someone a job that can be a "life-changing event" and providing a competitive compensation package and growth opportunity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:37:00">[28:37:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:47:00">[28:47:00]</a>.

## Key Takeaways: [[Indicators of a Good Employer]] vs. Bad

When looking for a job, prioritize companies that:
*   **Value and respect your time** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:30:00">[29:30:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:33:00">[29:33:00]</a>.
*   **Pay you properly** for your services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:35:00">[29:35:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:47:00">[25:47:00]</a>.
*   **Treat employees properly** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:31:00">[29:31:00]</a>.
*   Have clear, professional, and efficient recruitment processes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:18:00">[30:18:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:21:00">[30:21:00]</a>.

Avoid companies that engage in public shaming, offer unrealistic compensation, expect free labor for vague promises of equity, or have disorganized and excessively demanding hiring practices.

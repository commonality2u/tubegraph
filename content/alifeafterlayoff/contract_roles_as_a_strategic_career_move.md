## Pros of Contract Positions

### Increased Availability
[[contract_positions_for_skill_development_and_industry_transition | Contract positions]] are often more readily available, especially in uncertain economic markets when companies may be apprehensive about adding permanent headcount but still require work to be done <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:38">[00:01:38]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:42">[00:01:42]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:10">[00:02:10]</a>. This higher prevalence means a greater likelihood of being considered for these roles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:18">[00:02:18]</a>.

### Opportunity to Demonstrate Skills and Reduce Employer Risk
Contract roles provide an opportunity to demonstrate skills, especially if an employer views a candidate as a "stretch" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:22">[00:02:22]</a>. For hiring managers, who tend to be risk-averse, a contract position reduces the risk of a poor hiring decision, offering a chance for the candidate to prove their capabilities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:31">[00:02:31]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:01">[00:03:01]</a>. Many contract roles are created with the potential for conversion to full-time if the individual performs well <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:42">[00:03:42]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:47">[00:03:47]</a>. Asking about the likelihood of conversion during the interview process can reveal if the company intends to use the contract as a "test drive" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:15">[00:04:15]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:27">[00:04:27]</a>.

### Pivoting into New Industries or Skill Sets
[[contract_positions_for_skill_development_and_industry_transition | Contract positions]] can be a [[importance_of_strategic_career_planning | strategic way]] to pivot into a new industry, job type, or acquire new skills <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:41">[00:04:41]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:46">[00:04:46]</a>. This allows individuals to gain relevant experience and fill resume gaps, making them more competitive in desired fields <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:27">[00:05:27]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:37">[00:05:37]</a>.

### Income Continuity
Taking a contract position helps bridge income gaps during a job search, which can typically last six to nine months or even longer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:07">[00:06:07]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:16">[00:06:16]</a>. This provides financial stability, allowing more time to find the "perfect" full-time job <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:34">[00:06:34]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:37">[00:06:37]</a>.

### "Test Driving" the Company
Just as companies can test-drive candidates, individuals can use a contract role to evaluate the work environment, company culture, and job responsibilities before committing to a permanent role <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:50">[00:06:50]</a>. If the fit is poor, ending the contract is easier than leaving a full-time position <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:00">[00:07:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:07">[00:07:07]</a>.

### Confidence Boost and Routine
For those who have experienced a layoff, a contract position can help boost confidence, provide a routine, and ease the transition back into the workforce <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:17">[00:07:17]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:31">[00:07:31]</a>. It's often easier to find a new job when already employed and receiving a steady income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:41">[00:07:41]</a>.

### Potentially Higher Hourly Pay
In many cases, contract positions offer a higher hourly rate compared to their full-time equivalents <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:54">[00:07:54]</a>. Some contractors earn enough to take extended breaks between assignments while maintaining financial stability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:17">[00:08:17]</a>.

## Cons of Contract Positions

### "Hopping Resume" Perception
A significant downside of taking too many [[contract_positions_for_skill_development_and_industry_transition | contract positions]] is the risk of developing a "[[job_hopping_strategies_for_career_growth | hopping resume]]" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:52">[00:08:52]</a>. Hiring managers often view candidates with a history of short-term roles as lacking commitment to long-term projects, process improvement, or building lasting relationships within a team <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:04">[00:09:04]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:22">[00:09:22]</a>. This perception can be a challenge for younger professionals early in their [[strategies_for_effective_career_progression | career progression]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:03">[00:10:03]</a>.

### Lack of Benefits
Most [[contract_positions_for_skill_development_and_industry_transition | contract positions]] do not offer benefits like health insurance, retirement plans, or paid time off <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:20">[00:10:20]</a>. While some agencies may provide benefits, they are typically less comprehensive than those offered by large corporations to their full-time employees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:25">[00:10:25]</a>.

### Sense of Limbo and Instability
Contract work can create a sense of limbo, especially for those accustomed to full-time employment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:41">[00:10:41]</a>. There's a constant need to look for the next opportunity and plan for what happens after the contract ends, which can weigh heavily on individuals with major life plans like buying a house or having children, which require stable income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:54">[00:10:54]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:00">[01:10:00]</a>.

### Lack of Employer Commitment and Belonging
Employers may not feel fully committed to contract employees, meaning there are often no clear [[strategies_for_effective_career_progression | career opportunities]] or paths for advancement within the company (unless conversion is likely) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:17">[00:11:17]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:32">[00:11:32]</a>. This can lead to a feeling of not truly belonging to the team, as discussions about future projects or long-term initiatives may feel irrelevant if one expects to be temporary <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:41">[00:11:41]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:59">[00:11:59]</a>.

## Conclusion
The decision to pursue [[contract_positions_for_skill_development_and_industry_transition | contract positions]] or full-time employment requires weighing these pros and cons <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:07">[00:12:07]</a>. The ideal choice depends on personal preferences, the current job market, and how often job opportunities arise <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:22">[00:12:22]</a>. For those facing a difficult [[proactive_career_management_strategies | job search]], a contract position can be a valuable [[alternative_career_paths_and_job_options | strategic move]] in the long run <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:53">[00:12:53]</a>.

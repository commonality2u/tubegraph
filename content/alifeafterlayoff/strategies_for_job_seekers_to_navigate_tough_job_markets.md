### Job Seeking Strategies in a Challenging Market

The job market can often feel impossible, with employer requirements sometimes seeming completely out of touch <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>. This common frustration is backed by the reality of "hidden job requirements" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:09">[00:00:09]</a>.

### The Phenomenon of Hidden Job Requirements

Hidden job requirements refer to secret internal selection guidelines provided by hiring authorities (typically the hiring manager) to recruiters, which are not disclosed in public job postings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:00">[01:19:00]</a>. These guidelines often stem from the biases of the hiring manager or the team responsible for hiring <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:02:00">[03:02:00]</a>. While a proficient recruiter should challenge unrealistic demands, this often doesn't occur, particularly with less experienced recruiters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:22:00">[03:22:00]</a>.

One notable instance involved a recruiter accidentally emailing their internal selection guidelines to a technical candidate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:00">[01:04:00]</a>. The candidate expressed annoyance at the "pretentiousness and elitism" of the criteria <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:36:00">[01:36:00]</a>.

#### Example Criteria from an Internal Memo

The following examples highlight the specific and sometimes exclusionary criteria found in internal hiring guidelines for a software development role:

> [05:47:00] "What we're looking for:"
>
> *   **Education**: University graduate with a Bachelor's or Master's degree in Computer Science <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:49:00">[05:49:00]</a> from a "top program" such as MIT, Stanford, Carnegie Mellon, Berkeley, Caltech, University of Illinois, or University of Waterloo <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:54:00">[05:54:00]</a>. A special exception might be made for 4.0 graduates from other schools <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:31:00">[06:31:00]</a>.
> *   **Experience Level**: Must have 4 to 10 years of software development experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:42:00">[06:42:00]</a>; individuals with less experience are not considered a good fit, regardless of their proficiency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:48:00">[06:48:00]</a>.
> *   **Technical Expertise**: Expertise in modern JavaScript (TypeScript, Node.js, ReactJS), sometimes Kotlin, and knowledge of AI and machine learning <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:56:00">[06:56:00]</a>.
> *   **Job Stability**: Requires "longevity in role," meaning candidates must show two or more years in each position <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:13:00">[07:13:00]</a>. "Job hoppers" are not considered a fit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:17:00">[07:17:00]</a>. Those who lost jobs due to layoffs or company closures are also deemed unsuitable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:31:00">[07:31:00]</a>.
> *   **Company Type Experience**: Significant experience in "notable startups" backed by firms like Y Combinator or Sequoia Capital <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:40:00">[07:40:00]</a>. Candidates who have only worked for large companies (even Google or Facebook) will be rejected <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:53:00">[07:53:00]</a>.
> *   **Diversity and Citizenship**: Diversity hires (e.g., Black, female) are a bonus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:16:00">[08:16:00]</a>. Must be a US citizen, US permanent resident (green card holder), or Canadian citizen; no visa sponsorships <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:21:00">[08:21:00]</a>.
> *   **Entrepreneurial Background**: Bonus points for being a founding engineer or having founder experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:43:00">[08:43:00]</a>.

> [08:57:00] "What to avoid:"
>
> *   Visa candidates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:59:00">[08:59:00]</a>.
> *   Career contractors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:03:00">[09:03:00]</a>; candidates must have permanent work experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:09:00">[09:09:00]</a>.
> *   Job hoppers (frequent short stints) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:16:00">[09:16:00]</a>.
> *   Graduates of coding bootcamps (e.g., Full Stack Academy, Hack Reactor) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:34:00">[09:34:00]</a>.
> *   Candidates with a consulting background or experience limited to IT software development firms, as opposed to companies producing products directly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:50:00">[09:50:00]</a>.
> *   Experience limited to big companies like Uber or Intel, unless paired with startup experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:05:00">[10:05:00]</a>.
> *   Fake profiles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:23:00">[10:23:00]</a> (an increasing concern due to AI and mass applying tools <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:35:00">[10:35:00]</a>).
> *   Candidates who have ever worked at blacklisted companies such as Intel, Cisco, HP, Tata, Infosys, or Dell <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:56:00">[10:56:00]</a>.

These demanding and often contradictory criteria make it incredibly difficult for recruiters to find suitable candidates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:53:00">[01:53:00]</a>. While this specific list is extreme, such hidden requirements are common in the industry <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:12:00">[12:12:00]</a>.

### [[Strategies for job seekers in a challenging market | Strategies for Job Seekers to Navigate Tough Job Markets]]

Despite the challenges, job seekers can adopt several [[Strategies for job searching in a competitive market | strategies for job searching]] and career advancement:

*   **Self-Presentation**: Focus on presenting yourself with the cleanest, most direct resume possible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:54:00">[12:54:00]</a>.
*   **Targeted Networking**: Engage in targeted networking to make connections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:57:00">[12:57:00]</a>.
*   **Showcase Your Fit**:
    *   For technical roles, consider putting up a GitHub profile to demonstrate your skills <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:59:00">[12:59:00]</a>.
    *   Create a portfolio or a simple website to showcase your work and potential fit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:04:00">[13:04:00]</a>.
*   **Manage Expectations**: Understand that if you're frustrated, it's often due to unrealistic expectations from employers, not your own shortcomings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:49:00">[12:49:00]</a>. It's "them and not you" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:51:00">[12:51:00]</a>.
*   **Strategic Application**: Do not apply for jobs with ridiculous requirements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:39:00">[13:39:00]</a>. By not applying, job seekers can compel companies to adjust their approach if they cannot find candidates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:43:00">[13:43:00]</a>.
*   **Reclaim Control**: Focus on reclaiming control in your career <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:00">[00:48:00]</a>.
*   **Utilize Resources**: Seek out resources that offer insider perspectives on careers and hiring, such as webinars, free content, articles, training courses, or one-on-one coaching <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:00">[00:54:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:58:00">[13:58:00]</a>.

By selectively choosing where to apply, job seekers can avoid subjecting themselves to impossible criteria and help shift the power dynamic in the job market <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:50:00">[13:50:00]</a>.

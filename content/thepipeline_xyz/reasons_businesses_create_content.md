## Attracting Talent and Partners

One major reason for content creation is to [[attracting_talent_and_partners_with_content | attract people that want to work for you and work with you]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:08">[00:00:08]</a>. This extends to finding individuals who [[aligning_business_content_with_company_culture | align with your culture and Mission]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:34">[00:00:34]</a>. While skills can be trained, shared cultural values are crucial for building a business <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:30">[00:00:30]</a>.

## Targeting Market and Audience

Content is often created to appeal directly to the company's [[targeting_market_audience_with_content | Market and my audience in my customer base]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:20">[00:00:20]</a>. This involves tailoring messages to resonate with potential customers.

## Gaining a Competitive Advantage

Businesses also create content to keep their competitors "on their toes" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:38">[00:00:38]</a>. When competitors are busy watching what you are doing, they spend less time working on their own initiatives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:44">[00:00:44]</a>. This provides a [[using_content_to_gain_a_competitive_advantage | competitive Advantage]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:50">[00:00:50]</a>. Keeping business activities quiet isn't always the best strategy; leveraging content can be a way to distract competitors with what you're doing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:54">[00:00:54]</a>.

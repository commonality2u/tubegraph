## What is a Hot Wallet?
A hot wallet is characterized by its constant connection to the internet, making it "live" or "hot" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:21">[00:00:21]</a>. This connectivity is the fundamental [[difference_between_hot_and_cold_wallets | difference between hot and cold wallets]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:02">[00:00:02]</a>.

## Advantages of Hot Wallets
Hot wallets offer several conveniences for users:
*   **Ease of Use** They are "super easy to use" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:58">[00:00:58]</a>.
*   **Quick Transactions** Many traders primarily utilize hot wallets for quickly moving funds on-chain and off-chain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>. This makes them suitable for active trading and immediate access to funds, forming part of their [[use_cases_for_hot_and_cold_wallets | use cases]].

## Disadvantages and Risks of Hot Wallets
Despite their convenience, hot wallets come with significant [[security_risks_of_hot_wallets | security risks of hot wallets]]:
*   **Higher Risk of Compromise** It is "much more likely that you'll get compromised on your hot wallet versus a cold wallet" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:13">[00:01:13]</a>. This is a key point users should know when considering [[cold_wallets_vs_hot_wallets_what_users_should_know | Cold Wallets vs Hot Wallets]].

> [!TIP]
> Due to the increased risk of compromise, it is advised "you never want to keep too much" cryptocurrency in a hot wallet <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:12">[00:01:12]</a>.

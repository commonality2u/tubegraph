A [[differences_between_hot_and_cold_wallets | hot wallet]] is characterized by its constant connection to the internet, meaning it is "live" or "hot" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:21">[00:00:21]</a>. This distinguishes it from a [[differences_between_hot_and_cold_wallets | cold wallet]], where private keys are stored offline <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:08">[00:00:08]</a>. While [[convenience_of_hot_wallets | hot wallets]] are popular for quickly moving funds on-chain and off-chain due to their ease of use <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05">[00:01:05]</a>, they carry inherent risks.

## Primary Risk: Online Vulnerability

The most significant risk associated with [[differences_between_hot_and_cold_wallets | hot wallets]] is their susceptibility to compromise <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13">[00:01:13]</a>. Because they are always connected to the internet, they are more exposed to potential hacking attempts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:21">[00:00:21]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13">[00:01:13]</a>. In contrast, [[security_of_cold_wallets | cold wallets]] are far more secure as hackers cannot directly access them unless specific user mistakes are made <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:15">[00:00:15]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:27">[00:00:27]</a>.

## Mitigating Risk

To manage the risks associated with [[differences_between_hot_and_cold_wallets | hot wallets]], it is crucial to avoid keeping excessive amounts of funds in them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12">[00:01:12]</a>. The likelihood of a hot wallet being compromised is much higher compared to a cold wallet <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13">[00:01:13]</a>.

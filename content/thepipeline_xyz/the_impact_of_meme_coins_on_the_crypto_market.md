## Concerns Regarding Meme Coins

Prominent figures in crypto, including Ledger, have expressed strong skepticism about [[crypto_meme_coins | meme coins]], arguing that they are "silly" and do not deserve their high valuations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:39:53">[01:39:53]</a>. Ledger states, "I do not enjoy [[crypto_meme_coins | meme coins]] like or [[the_impact_of_memecoins_on_the_perception_of_the_crypto_industry | meme coin culture]]" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:11">[01:18:11]</a>.

### Uselessness and Pump-and-Dump Schemes
While acknowledging that a rare [[crypto_meme_coins | meme coin]] might create "real culture" (e.g., Bonk, Whiff, Doge), Ledger emphasizes that "99.9% of them are absolute trash, probably made by the same like half dozen people and they're they're useless" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:21:58">[01:21:58]</a>. He views them as blatant pump-and-dump schemes, where individuals with large followings effectively generate liquidity for themselves <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:25">[01:22:25]</a>.

### Financial Nihilism and Market Dynamics
The prevalence of [[memecoins_as_a_financial_phenomenon_with_implications_for_retail_investors | meme coins]] is linked to a "YOLO cycle" driven by "financial nihilism," where investors feel they cannot make money any other way and resort to a "lotto ticket style method" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:31:00">[01:31:00]</a>. This creates a "game of chicken" where participants aim to ride the chart highest and then exit, knowing "everybody's plan is to get out" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:32:04">[01:32:04]</a>.

This environment fosters a "progressively lower spiral of lesser and lesser opportunity," where new [[crypto_meme_coins | meme coins]] are touted as "the next Doge" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:37:37">[01:37:37]</a>. Ledger argues that participating in this game means "chasing someone else's victory" without the necessary "insider advantage" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:37:37">[01:37:37]</a>. The "spray and pray" strategy for [[crypto_meme_coins | meme coins]] is largely ineffective, working only for "very few weeks per cycle" and typically requiring one large win to offset numerous losses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:48:47">[01:48:47]</a>. The vast majority of these assets are destined to go to zero, leaving the community "holding the bag" and potentially becoming "disappointed and disillusioned by crypto" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:52:58">[02:52:58]</a>.

### Insider Tactics and Misinformation
Despite the narrative of [[memecoins_as_a_financial_phenomenon_with_implications_for_retail_investors | meme coins]] being a rebellion against "insiders" and venture capital (VC) coins, insiders are often deeply involved <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:16:50">[02:16:50]</a>. They may hold a significant portion of the supply (e.g., 40%) that is unlocked from day zero, allowing them to "dump on you" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:29:43">[02:29:43]</a>. This leads to extremely low liquidity, or a "skinny door" through which many "fat people" (large holders) try to exit, causing massive price corrections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:16:50">[02:16:50]</a>.

### Dev Fatigue and Market Perception
The prevalence of [[state_of_meme_coins | meme coins]] has led to "[[challenges_faced_by_venture_capitalists_regarding_memecoins | Dev fatigue from meme coins]]" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:16:50">[02:16:50]</a>. Talented developers are tempted to abandon "real product" development for the quick financial gains offered by manufacturing [[crypto_meme_coins | meme coins]], creating a disheartening environment for those building legitimate products <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:58:32">[02:58:32]</a>. This phenomenon contributes to [[crypto_misinformation | crypto misinformation]] and the perception that the industry is filled with "scammers" if it fails to produce real-world applications <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:10:50">[04:10:50]</a>.

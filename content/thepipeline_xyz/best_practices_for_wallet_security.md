Understanding the differences between [[Hot wallets vs Cold wallets | hot wallets and cold wallets]] is fundamental to implementing [[Best practices for crypto security | effective crypto security practices]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>. Each type offers distinct advantages and disadvantages regarding [[Crypto security challenges | security]] and convenience.

## Cold Wallets: Offline Security

A [[Security of cold wallets | cold wallet]] stores your private keys offline, meaning it is not connected to the internet <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:08">[00:00:08]</a>. This "cold storage" makes it inaccessible to hackers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:13">[00:00:13]</a>. Cold wallets are considered far more secure than hot wallets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:25">[00:00:25]</a>.

### Managing Cold Wallet Security

While highly secure, users must take extra steps to maintain the [[Security of cold wallets | security of cold wallets]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:30">[00:00:30]</a>.

*   **Backup Recovery Phrase:** Always back up your recovery phrase or keys <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:38">[00:00:38]</a>.
*   **Offline Storage for Backups:** Never put your recovery phrase online, as this would defeat the purpose of having a cold wallet <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:41">[00:00:41]</a>.
*   **Safe Physical Storage:** Store your backup in a safe place, ideally on a piece of paper that is fireproof <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:46">[00:00:46]</a>. Ensure you know exactly where it is <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:53">[00:00:53]</a>.

## Hot Wallets: Online Convenience and Risks

A [[Convenience and risks of hot wallets | hot wallet]] is connected to the internet <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:21">[00:00:21]</a>. Its primary advantage is ease of use, making it popular for quick on-chain and off-chain transactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:56">[00:00:56]</a>.

### Mitigating Hot Wallet Risks

Due to their online nature, hot wallets are more susceptible to compromise than cold wallets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:13">[00:01:13]</a>.

*   **Limit Funds:** A key security practice is to never keep too many funds in a hot wallet <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:12">[00:01:12]</a>.
*   **Purpose:** While hot wallets offer convenience for active trading, the inherent friction of cold wallets means they are not ideal for frequent transactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:07">[00:01:07]</a>.

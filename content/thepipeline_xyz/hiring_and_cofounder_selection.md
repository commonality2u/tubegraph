## Cofounder Selection

Picking the right co-founder is arguably the most important thing a founder can do <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:29">[00:05:29]</a>. Building a business is challenging and can often feel irrational due to the immense time and energy invested versus the likelihood of success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:38">[00:05:38]</a>. It can also be incredibly lonely, as very few people, even close friends, truly understand the unique challenges faced by a founder <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:52">[00:05:52]</a>. Having an equally committed co-founder can significantly boost morale and provide the necessary support to endure difficult periods <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:08">[00:06:08]</a>.

### Key Considerations for Cofounders

*   **Complementary Skills:** Ideally, a co-founder should complement your skills rather than be an exact replica <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:30">[00:06:30]</a>. No single individual can possess all the necessary skill sets for a successful founder, which include product sense, technical acumen, market analysis, and community management <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:46">[00:06:46]</a>. For instance, two highly technical engineers might not form the optimal founding team compared to one technical person and one with strong business acumen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:10">[00:07:10]</a>.
*   **Personality:** Beyond skills, complementary personalities can strengthen a partnership <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:25">[00:07:25]</a>. Different approaches to decision-making or interacting with founders can lead to a more robust team <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:36">[00:07:36]</a>.
*   **Network:** A co-founder with a distinctly different network can vastly improve the chances of success. If all co-founders share the same network, scaling and growth efforts become much harder <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:50">[00:07:50]</a>.

### Founder-Market Fit

From an investor's perspective, "founder-market fit" is one of the most crucial elements for early-stage companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:16">[00:08:16]</a>. At the earliest stages, when a company might only consist of "two people in a deck," there's minimal other information to evaluate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:26">[00:08:26]</a>. Investors assess whether founders have the ability to understand the market and possess the necessary technical capacity to build the product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:41">[00:08:41]</a>.

Founders should consider bringing in someone with deep founder-market fit if they lack it themselves, as this person can quickly understand the market and leverage the right networks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:55">[00:08:55]</a>.

> "Obsession" is a key term here <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:09">[00:09:09]</a>. If a founder isn't obsessed with what they're building, they're unlikely to succeed, as competitors are probably "obsessed" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:15">[00:09:15]</a>. Investors often view it as an "insta pass" if a founder is full-time in their startup but also full-time in another community management role <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:34">[00:09:34]</a>. Success requires being "all in" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:47">[00:09:47]</a>.

## Hiring

A CEO's primary responsibility is to empower and bring on the right people to ensure the company's success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:45">[00:04:45]</a>.

### Hire Slowly, Fire Quickly

Founders, especially when they secure capital, often feel immense pressure from customers, investors, and others to rapidly grow their team <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:56">[00:21:56]</a>. However, hiring too quickly and bringing on the wrong individuals can cause more damage than not hiring at all <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:07">[00:22:07]</a>.

*   **Impact of Bad Hires:** If a company hires an eighth person, that individual effectively constitutes 12.5% of the company's operational capacity, regardless of their cap table ownership <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:30">[00:22:30]</a>. If 10% of a car isn't working, it needs fixing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:40">[00:22:40]</a>. Similarly, founders should not lower the bar for new hires, which can lead to a significant portion of the company not being operated by "A players" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:48">[00:22:48]</a>.
*   **Attracting Top Talent:** Top talent in crypto is highly competitive and seeks environments where they are challenged, motivated, and free from "dead weight" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:07">[00:23:07]</a>. Companies must avoid dead weight to scale effectively long-term <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:20">[00:23:20]</a>.
*   **Correcting Mistakes:** If a wrong hire is made, it's crucial to fire quickly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:37">[00:23:37]</a>. People rarely regret firing someone too soon; "once you know, you know" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:47">[00:23:47]</a>.
*   **Avoiding Revolving Doors:** The goal is to take time and hire the right person, preventing a continuous cycle of hiring and firing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:54">[00:23:54]</a>.

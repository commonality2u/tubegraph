The practice of maintaining a [[crypto_trading_strategies_and_personal_experiences | trading journal]], while beneficial for personal reflection and strategy development, can inadvertently expose individuals to sophisticated online [[fraudulent_transaction_tactics | scams]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:04">[00:00:04]</a>. ## Case Study: The y22 Trader Compromise

A notable example involves a trader known as y22, who gained significant attention by publicly sharing his [[crypto_trading_strategies_and_personal_experiences | trading journal]] on Twitter <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>. His success made him a target for a sophisticated [[fraudulent_transaction_tactics | scam]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:05">[00:00:05]</a>.

The [[fraudulent_transaction_tactics | scam]] unfolded through the following steps:
1.  **Impersonation and Lure** A scammer posted a link to a fake Telegram group in y22's Twitter thread, mimicking his actual Twitter handle and history, including follower count <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:08">[00:00:08]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:34">[00:00:34]</a>. The imposter even displayed a gold check mark on their profile to enhance credibility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:44">[00:00:44]</a>.
2.  **Forced Verification** Upon joining the Telegram group, users were prompted to undergo a verification process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:11">[00:00:11]</a>.
3.  **Desktop Action Requirement** The verification included "extra added steps" that required the user to perform a specific action on their desktop browser <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:16">[00:00:16]</a>. This often involved moving from a mobile device to a desktop <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:22">[00:00:22]</a>.
4.  **Hot Wallet Compromise** Performing the requested action on the desktop browser, where the victim's [[crypto_in_payments_and_personal_finance | hot wallet]] was located, led to its compromise <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:27">[00:00:27]</a>. As a result, y22 "more or less lost everything" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:31">[00:00:31]</a>.

### Scammer Tactics and Effort

Scammers are willing to go to "very extreme lengths to compromise" individuals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:47">[00:00:47]</a>. This includes investing significant resources, potentially "five figures," in social engineering campaigns that demand considerable "time and effort and money" to successfully compromise multiple targets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:51">[00:00:51]</a>.

> "People will go to very extreme lengths to compromise you. Don't assume somebody's not willing to shell out like five figures..." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:47">[00:00:47]</a>

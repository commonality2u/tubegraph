The blockchain ecosystem features a variety of platforms, each with unique characteristics, advantages, and challenges. Understanding these differences is crucial for users, developers, and investors navigating the space.

## Layer 1 (L1) Blockchains

Layer 1 blockchains are the foundational networks on which decentralized applications (dApps) are built. Key L1s discussed include Ethereum and Solana, along with mentions of Cardano and Tron.

### Ethereum (ETH)
Ethereum is a prominent L1 blockchain that pioneered smart contracts and a robust ecosystem for decentralized finance (DeFi) and non-fungible tokens (NFTs) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:58">[00:05:58]</a>.
*   **Current State:** Ethereum mainnet, in its current state, faces challenges with higher costs and slower transaction speeds compared to some alternatives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:00">[00:07:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:54">[00:20:54]</a>. This can lead to [[user_experience_issues_on_blockchain_platforms | user experience issues on blockchain platforms]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:59">[00:20:59]</a>.
*   **Transaction Capacity:** Ethereum's L1 handles approximately 1 million transactions per day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:49">[00:24:49]</a>. While future updates like EIP-4844 are expected to enable 10,000 transactions per second (TPS) on the mainnet <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:57">[00:24:57]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:58">[00:24:58]</a>, this is still significantly less than the 50 million daily transactions potentially required for a top-10 mobile application <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:43">[00:24:43]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:46">[00:24:46]</a>.
*   **Development:** Ethereum's creation "opened up everyone's imagination to what's possible" in blockchain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:40">[00:25:40]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:42">[00:25:42]</a>.
*   **Market Perception:** Early Ethereum users faced criticism from Bitcoin maximalists but built "real applications" like DeFi during their first bear market, leading to strong price appreciation and a sense of vindication among its supporters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:30">[00:42:30]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:32">[00:42:32]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:35">[00:42:35]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:37">[00:42:37]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:40">[00:42:40]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:42">[00:42:42]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:44">[00:42:44]</a>.

## Modular Blockchains

The "modular thesis" involves breaking down blockchain functionality into specialized layers.

### Celestia
Celestia is an example of a modular blockchain that recently launched its mainnet <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:35">[00:21:35]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:36">[00:21:36]</a>. It focuses entirely on being a "modular data availability layer" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:39">[00:21:39]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:42">[00:21:42]</a>. This allows rollups to post data to Celestia more cheaply before settling on Ethereum or other chains, or for sovereign rollups to post data directly to Celestia without needing a separate settlement layer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:45">[00:21:45]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:46">[00:21:46]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:49">[00:21:49]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:51">[00:21:51]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:52">[00:21:52]</a>.

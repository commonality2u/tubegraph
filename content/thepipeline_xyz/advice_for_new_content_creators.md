## Just Start Creating

With readily available tools like ChatGPT, there are no excuses to delay starting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:09">[00:00:09]</a>. It's expected to "suck at the beginning" of any new endeavor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:16">[00:00:16]</a>, but the process of "fumbling your way into getting better" is how improvement happens <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:20">[00:00:20]</a>. The quicker one makes mistakes, the faster one improves <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:24">[00:00:24]</a>.

## Focus on Incremental Improvement

A key strategy for growth, advised by creators like Mr. Beast, is to focus on getting 1% better with each iteration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:28">[00:00:28]</a>. Significant growth in [[importance_of_starting_and_practicing_in_content_creation | content creation]] was personally observed when the speaker committed to doing "a vlog a day," titled "Gone in 60," where each 60-second vlog aimed to be incrementally better than the last <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:34">[00:00:34]</a>.

Trying to perfect every aspect will significantly slow down progress <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:48">[00:00:48]</a>. Instead, the focus should be on moving as quickly as possible and improving one small detail in each new piece of content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:52">[00:00:52]</a>. This approach allows for rapid improvement by embracing a full-force, iterative process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>. This strategy is also relevant to [[building_consistency_and_finding_inspiration_in_daily_content_creation | building consistency in content creation]].

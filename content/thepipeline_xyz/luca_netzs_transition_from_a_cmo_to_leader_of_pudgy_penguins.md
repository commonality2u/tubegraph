## The State of Pudgy Penguins Before Netz

[[saga_and_resurgence_of_pudgy_penguins | Pudgy Penguins]] was one of the NFTs that spearheaded the significant NFT boom in 2021, alongside projects like Punks and Apes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:54">[00:00:54]</a>. However, its original leadership consisted of 18 and 19-year-olds who lacked the business acumen to manage such a project, causing it to falter while others thrived <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:11">[00:01:11]</a>.

## The Acquisition of Pudgy Penguins

Netz, a significant holder of [[saga_and_resurgence_of_pudgy_penguins | Pudgy Penguins]] NFTs, felt a strong affinity for the IP and its community, which he found "super sticky" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:29">[00:01:29]</a>. This led him to make a public bid of about 750 ETH, equivalent to $2.5 million at the time, to acquire the project <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:40">[00:01:40]</a>. He initially didn't expect the deal to go through due to contractual stipulations, but community pressure ultimately forced the hand of the previous owners <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:16">[00:04:16]</a>. The acquisition occurred at the beginning of a bear market, with no initial funds in the bank, requiring the team to "grind" through challenging times and multiple Black Swan events <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:01">[00:02:01]</a>.

## From CMO to Web3 Leader: A Fated Transition

Before acquiring [[saga_and_resurgence_of_pudgy_penguins | Pudgy Penguins]], Luca Netz was the CMO and partner at Gel Blaster, North America's fastest-growing toy company, which had just hit a $100 million annual revenue milestone <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:39">[00:04:39]</a>. Despite this success, he felt unfulfilled and was seeking something new <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:50">[00:04:50]</a>. He describes his journey to [[saga_and_resurgence_of_pudgy_penguins | Pudgy Penguins]] leadership as a period of "universal alignment" and "divine intervention" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:09">[00:05:09]</a>.

His background uniquely prepared him for the role:
*   **Monetizing Social Influence:** Netz built his career around building brands with influencers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:44">[00:05:44]</a>. He views the [[saga_and_resurgence_of_pudgy_penguins | Pudgy Penguin]] itself as an influencer around which he could build a brand <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:47">[00:05:47]</a>.
*   **Consumer Product Experience:** His experience with Gel Blaster directly paved the way for partnerships with major retailers like Walmart and Target <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:54">[00:05:54]</a>.
*   **Software Business Acumen:** He had been involved in multiple software businesses, which was crucial for the technological aspects of Web3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:00">[00:06:00]</a>.
*   **Community Building:** As a high school dropout who found success by learning from online courses and cultivating communities, he was well-versed in the importance of community in Web3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:07">[00:06:07]</a>.
*   **Communication and Vision Casting:** Netz emphasizes that his ability to communicate a vision effectively is a key competitive advantage, a skill he practiced extensively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:45">[00:06:45]</a>.
*   **Team Assembly:** He assembled a team, including his best friend as Chief Creative Officer who ran a design agency, knowing the specific skills needed for the NFT space <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:02">[00:07:02]</a>.

## Leadership Philosophy and Strategy

Netz's leadership of [[saga_and_resurgence_of_pudgy_penguins | Pudgy Penguins]] is defined by several core principles:

### Underdog Mentality and Resourcefulness
He emphasizes the team's hyper-competitiveness and desire to win, even with limited resources <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:29">[00:19:29]</a>. Unlike competitors with "giant treasure chests," [[saga_and_resurgence_of_pudgy_penguins | Pudgy Penguins]] operated by not paying themselves for the first year after a $9 million seed round, which pales in comparison to what others raised <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:04">[00:20:04]</a>. This fosters an "uncomfortable" but driven environment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:23">[00:20:23]</a>.

### Community-Centric Approach
Netz believes in treating people the way you want to be treated and addressing disgruntled holders by understanding their frustrations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:53">[00:32:53]</a>. He maintains constant communication with the community, encouraging open dialogue in bi-weekly "Inner Igloos" sessions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:10">[00:35:10]</a>.

> "I think the most impressive thing of what we've accomplished is is you have to look at the light in in the resources in which we had to achieve what we had and I think that's the most impressive aspect of this." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:53">[00:19:53]</a>

He aims to be in the "same bucket" as the holders, constantly working to ensure the project's success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:09">[00:37:09]</a>.

### Proliferation of the Penguin IP
Netz views the toy business not primarily as a revenue stream, but as a marketing vehicle for "penguin proliferation" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:17">[00:14:17]</a>. The goal is to make the [[saga_and_resurgence_of_pudgy_penguins | Pudgy Penguin]] omnipresent, creating touchpoints beyond digital screens so people can discuss it with their families <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:20">[00:14:20]</a>. He aims for [[saga_and_resurgence_of_pudgy_penguins | Pudgy Penguins]] to become the "face of Web3" and eventually the "face of crypto" from a character perspective <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:18">[00:22:18]</a>. The intrinsic appeal of penguins as "naturally memetic animals" with a gap in the Web2 brand market makes the IP inherently powerful <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:07">[00:26:07]</a>.

### Focus on Consumer Crypto and Mass Adoption
Netz believes crypto has a branding and marketing problem, with an overemphasis on technical innovation rather than genuine community building and user acquisition <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:04">[00:45:04]</a>. He posits that marketing is the most crucial department in any business, and without it, even great technology will fail to find product-market fit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:33">[00:56:33]</a>.

## Legacy and Impact
Under Luca Netz's [[transition_from_soldier_to_leader | leadership]], [[saga_and_resurgence_of_pudgy_penguins | Pudgy Penguins]] has become the highest-performing NFT project of the last two and a half years, building a strong community, culture, and brand <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:18">[00:02:18]</a>. The project successfully pioneered the sale of physical toys at major retailers like Walmart and Target, integrating a unique Web3 onboarding mechanism where each toy's QR code leads to a custodial wallet and redeemable NFT traits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:08">[00:15:08]</a>. This approach aims to onboard non-crypto users by making them "collecting NFTs unbeknownst to them" through a $5 toy purchase <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:10">[00:16:10]</a>.

Netz and his team exemplify how a strong background in consumer products, marketing, and community building, combined with an "underdog" tenacity, can drive mass adoption in the crypto space <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:17">[00:18:17]</a>.

## SocialFi as a Form of Gaming

SocialFi applications are increasingly seen as a type of game, blurring the lines between social interaction and game mechanics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:56:06">[12:56:06]</a>.
*   **Fantasy Top** is considered a game project rather than purely a SocialFi project due to its gameplay mechanics involving cards and tournaments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:36:09">[14:36:09]</a>. It functions as a "Twitter L2," building a speculation, tokenization, and monetization layer on top of Twitter activity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:06:26">[04:06:26]</a>. Fantasy Top provides an engaging activity when traditional markets are stagnant <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:52:16">[03:52:16]</a>. Its success suggests that a game doesn't need mass market adoption to be successful; it can thrive by catering to a niche audience with high monetization potential <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:56:09">[09:56:09]</a>.
*   **Pump.fun** is highlighted as a major SocialFi app that generated more revenue than Solana <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:31:31">[03:31:31]</a>. Meme coins, in general, can be seen as a form of SocialFi due to their reliance on social media and network effects for value creation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:43:24">[09:43:24]</a>.

## Financialization of Social Media
The integration of crypto introduces the financialization of social media, giving creators and users new ways to earn value from their content and interactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:54:38">[07:54:38]</a>.
*   Platforms like Fantasy Top enable users to profit from participating and supporting creators, leveling the playing field between platforms and users <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:00:23">[09:00:23]</a>.
*   [[Blockchain gaming and NFTs | NFTs]] were the first iteration of this, allowing people to create clubs and express interests through digital collectibles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:01:14">[10:01:14]</a>.
*   The "unit cost per attention" on Crypto Twitter is significantly higher than on other platforms like TikTok, making it a valuable place for building a community and attention <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:11:53">[37:11:53]</a>.

## The Audience for Web3 Games
There is a growing recognition that games can be built specifically for the crypto audience, rather than solely aiming for mass market adoption <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:13:30">[31:13:30]</a>.
*   Games like Fantasy Top cater directly to crypto natives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:43:08">[30:43:08]</a>.
*   The crypto audience includes "whales" who are willing to spend significant amounts, making niche games highly profitable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:18:24">[31:18:24]</a>.
*   The "onchain gaming" movement focuses on building games entirely on the blockchain, which allows for fully transparent and verifiable gameplay <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:27:08">[32:27:08]</a>. Examples include Duper by Curio and Final Form <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:32:00">[32:32:00]</a>.

## Challenges in Crypto Game Development
Despite the potential, challenges remain in [[the evolution and future of web 3 gaming | Web3 gaming]].
*   Many traditional game studios struggle to properly implement crypto and blockchain, often misunderstanding the core benefits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:48:47">[34:48:47]</a>. For example, some studios consider using Bitcoin for payments, which is impractical due to slow transaction times, or propose BRC-20 tokens for in-game governance without understanding the user experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:40:02">[35:40:02]</a>.
*   There's a scarcity of teams with both strong game development and crypto backgrounds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:31:01">[36:31:01]</a>.
*   The current state of crypto gaming is still in its early stages, with many "mini-game experiences" that are precursors to more sophisticated applications <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:04:16">[12:04:16]</a>.

## The Future of Web3 Gaming
The [[future of Web3 gaming | future of Web3 gaming]] will depend heavily on infrastructure development and new application mechanics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="54:48:58">[54:48:58]</a>.
*   Better account abstraction and high-performance, low-fee EVMs (like Monad) are crucial for enabling more seamless [[onchain gaming | onchain gaming]] and social applications <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="54:16:47">[54:16:47]</a>.
*   The success of applications like Fantasy Top could inspire a second wave of builders to introduce new dynamics and more nuanced SocialFi applications over time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="51:32:38">[51:32:38]</a>.
*   There's optimism for a resurgence of [[blockchain_gaming_and_nfts | NFTs]], particularly those with utility, as seen in Fantasy Top cards <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:41">[01:01:41]</a>.
*   The "liquidity aspect of crypto" means that large amounts of money can be generated from small, passionate communities, demonstrating the unique value of Web3 economies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:41">[01:01:41]</a>.

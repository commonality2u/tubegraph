## Gaming and Crypto Integration

[[Gaming and crypto integration | Gaming]] is another sector frequently discussed for its potential to drive crypto adoption, given that most people globally play video games <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:51">[00:56:51]</a>. While some [[the_evolution_and_future_of_web_3_gaming | crypto-integrated games]] and apps like Posy T and Obaby games have found success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:15">[00:57:15]</a>, there's a more cautious, or "bearish," sentiment towards the broader video game sector within crypto <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:28">[00:57:28]</a>.

The main concern is that many existing crypto games focus on "play-to-earn" models rather than prioritizing the fun of the game itself, which is not a sustainable long-term strategy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:39">[00:57:39]</a>. Creating an enjoyable game is inherently difficult, even for established studios like Activision, Bethesda, and Nintendo, which have decades of experience and still have a mixed hit rate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:56">[00:57:56]</a>. It is argued that it will be very challenging for crypto-native developers, new to game development, to consistently create compelling games <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:05">[00:58:05]</a>.

Instead, [[the_future_of_web3_gaming | future success in gaming]] may depend on major, traditional gaming companies recognizing the utility of blockchain technology and integrating it into their existing or new titles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:40">[00:58:40]</a>. While there is a chance for a crypto-native game to achieve significant success, it's considered less likely compared to [[future_potential_use_cases_for_crypto_and_blockchain | traditional gaming giants]] adopting the technology <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:02">[00:59:02]</a>.

## Music Industry Disruption

There is strong [[future_developments_in_crypto_and_blockchain_technology | conviction that crypto can disrupt the music industry]], not primarily through NFTs, but via protocols that ensure artists are paid quickly and efficiently <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:29">[01:00:29]</a>. A significant portion (70%) of money generated in music currently does not reach the artists <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:17">[01:01:17]</a>, and crypto is seen as a potential solution to this inefficiency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:25">[01:01:25]</a>.

NFTs in music are viewed skeptically, with few exceptions like the Loud Punks NFTs by Rolling Loud, which offer tangible utility such as lifetime VIP access to festivals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:34">[01:00:34]</a>. However, the broader NFT boom is considered to have had mixed effects; while it brought new interest, it also led to a lot of "grifting," deterring powerful mainstream figures from engaging with the crypto space due to negative experiences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:16">[01:02:16]</a>. Concerns remain that another "NFT grift Bull Run 2.0" could occur in the next cycle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:14">[01:04:14]</a>.

Ultimately, the crypto space is still in its "Wild West" phase, and no one truly knows what will succeed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:41">[01:04:41]</a>. The long-term perspective (e.g., 50 years) is crucial, and the goal is continuous improvement, aiming for [[future_developments_in_crypto_and_blockchain_technology | crypto to find its place in the world]] in a way that genuinely benefits people at scale <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:47">[01:04:47]</a>.

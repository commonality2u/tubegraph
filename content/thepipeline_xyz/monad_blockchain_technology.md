## Decentralization and Censorship Resistance
A core tenet of [[monad_blockchain_technology | Monad]] is its commitment to decentralization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>. This is seen as critical for censorship resistance, ensuring that no single entity can arbitrarily change rules or halt operations, a stark contrast to more centralized systems or "app chains" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:06">[00:00:06]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:00">[00:19:00]</a>. In a decentralized system like [[monad_blockchain_technology | Monad]], it's impossible to "phone up a validator" to manipulate oracle prices or rewrite transaction history, unlike in closed systems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:28">[00:20:28]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:40">[00:20:40]</a>. This trustless environment is essential for attracting institutions and ensuring the long-term viability of on-chain finance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:55">[00:21:55]</a>.

## Comparison to Other Blockchains
[[comparison_of_monad_with_other_blockchain_environments | Monad]] presents a different set of trade-offs compared to other blockchain environments:

*   **Ethereum:** While Ethereum is decentralized, its 12-second block times make it unsuitable for high-frequency trading applications like perpetual exchanges <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:58">[00:12:58]</a>.
*   **Layer 2s (L2s):** Perps built on L2s often create "isolated islands" where users are siloed from the broader DeFi ecosystem <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:18">[00:09:18]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:00">[00:13:00]</a>.
*   **Solana:** While fast, Solana faced challenges with market makers consistently providing liquidity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:08">[00:13:08]</a>.
*   **App Chains (e.g., dYdX, Hyperliquid):** App chains, like dYdX and Hyperliquid (Cosmos-based), can offer custom opcodes (e.g., gasless transactions, prioritized orders for market makers) because they control their entire chain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:50">[00:07:50]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:02">[00:15:02]</a>. However, this comes at the cost of decentralization and composability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:21">[00:15:21]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:51">[00:15:51]</a>. [[monad_blockchain_technology | Monad]], as a generalized L1, leverages composability and ecosystem benefits even if it cannot offer custom chain-level optimizations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:58">[00:16:58]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:00">[00:17:00]</a>. The "Jelly Jelly attack" example on an app chain highlighted the risks of centralized control and the ability to "rewrite history" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:04">[00:20:04]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:02">[00:21:02]</a>.

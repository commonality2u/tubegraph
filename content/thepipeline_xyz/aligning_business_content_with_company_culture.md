## Strategic Audience Separation

When developing content, whether in written, audio, or video format, it is crucial to identify the target audience for each piece <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:12">[00:00:12]</a>. Content can be designed for your primary market, audience, and customer base <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:20">[00:00:20]</a>, or it can be created specifically to [[attracting_talent_and_partners_with_content | attract people]] who share similar cultural values <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:25">[00:00:25]</a>.

## Attracting Culturally Aligned Talent

While training can equip anyone with necessary skills, building a successful business benefits greatly from having individuals who [[cultural_references_in_workplace | align with your company's culture and mission]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:30">[00:00:30]</a>. Therefore, a key function of business content is to communicate your culture effectively, thereby attracting team members and partners who naturally fit with your organizational values.

## Content for Competitive Advantage

Another reason for creating content is to keep competitors engaged and aware of your activities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:38">[00:00:38]</a>. If competitors are focused on watching your content, they are less focused on their own work, which provides your business with a [[using_content_to_gain_a_competitive_advantage | competitive advantage]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:50">[00:00:50]</a>. It is not always strategic to keep business activities quiet, as distraction can be leveraged <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:54">[00:00:54]</a>.

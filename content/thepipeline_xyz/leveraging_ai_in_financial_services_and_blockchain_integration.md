## The Need for Innovation in Financial Services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:54">[00:01:54]</a>

The crypto credit market experienced a 90% contraction in uncollateralized lending in 2022, primarily because lenders lost trust and moved to over-collateralized options <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:11:00">[02:11:00]</a>. This shift occurred because market makers, who often require uncollateralized loans, do not hold sufficient inventory to provide collateral. Requiring market makers to source and hedge collateral from the open market is expensive and impractical <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:30">[00:02:30]</a>. This situation necessitates a fundamental change to restore trust and enable verifiable credit in a trustless manner <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:01">[00:02:01]</a>.

## Current State of Financial Data Verification <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:44">[00:03:44]</a>

A data verification platform acts as a trust layer to re-enable communication between lenders and borrowers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:50">[00:03:50]</a>. This platform starts with existing claims and increases verifiability through various means, including:
*   Running proprietary API connectors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:14">[00:04:14]</a>.
*   Utilizing SGX (Software Guard Extensions) for hardware guarantees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:17">[00:04:17]</a>.
*   Implementing [[ZK TLS | ZK TLS]] (Zero-Knowledge Transport Layer Security) to enhance trust <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:22">[00:04:22]</a>.
*   Developing a "Signed API" solution for sensitive data sharing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:27">[00:04:27]</a>.

This approach allows borrowers to prove their financial health in a peer-to-peer fashion to lenders, without disclosing raw data to a central entity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:42">[00:08:42]</a>. It's designed to be privacy-preserving, allowing sensitive information to remain secure while verifiable bits are shared in real-time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:37">[00:10:37]</a>. For example, a summation Merkle tree on assets and liabilities, combined with a [[ZK Proofs | ZK proof]] check, can reveal hidden liabilities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:13">[00:09:13]</a>. While proofs can be inserted on the [[Web3 and Blockchain Technologies in Consumer Applications | blockchain]] for certain use cases like stablecoin reserves, for lender-borrower relations, proofs are typically validated off-chain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:11">[00:11:11]</a>.

### Benefits of Enhanced Verification <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:46">[00:04:46]</a>
*   **For Lenders:** Enables accurate calculation of risk/reward, allowing them to allocate more capital and reduce collateral requirements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:49">[00:04:49]</a>.
*   **For Borrowers:** Allows them to demonstrate trustworthiness, potentially leading to lower interest rates due to reduced perceived risk <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:02">[00:05:02]</a>.

## Decentralizing Credit and Market Making <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:17">[00:06:17]</a>

The aim is to replace the old model of centralized credit desks, which often relied on single credit desks without proper data platforms or misaligned incentives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:36">[00:06:36]</a>. This is achieved by decentralizing the credit function through a professional network of specialized parties who can manage retail funds for strategies and pass yields back to average users <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:50">[00:06:50]</a>.

## AI as an Intelligence Layer in Financial Services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:13:08">[02:13:08]</a>

AI is envisioned as an "intelligence layer" on top of financial services, acting as a window to [[Web3 and Blockchain Technologies in Consumer Applications | blockchain]] technologies and crypto payment rails <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:13:52">[02:13:52]</a>. This layer aims to provide presence and personalization in financial markets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:16:43">[02:16:43]</a>.

### Addressing Financial Literacy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:18:02">[02:18:02]</a>

A significant motivation for integrating AI in finance is the low financial literacy among adults (65% globally), which hinders their ability to manage personal finances effectively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:18:05">[02:18:05]</a>. AI can provide tools for goal-based savings, analyzing finances, and optimizing investment plans, making financial planning accessible to a broader audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:25:05">[02:25:05]</a>.

### AI Agent Capabilities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:18:50">[02:18:50]</a>

The goal is to develop an application for sending, receiving, and spending money that is built on blockchain systems and crypto payment rails, operating intelligently <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:19:03">[02:19:03]</a>. Key features include:
*   **Multi-language support:** Facilitating automated translations and token transfers, connecting people globally regardless of language <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:19:18">[02:19:18]</a>.

*   **Goal-based investment decisions:** In the future, AI agents could make investment decisions based on user-defined goals (e.g., saving for a holiday), while also considering personal preferences and moral values which requires sophisticated context collection <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:23:03">[02:23:03]</a>.

## Blockchain's Role in Payments and Rewards <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:51:08">[02:51:08]</a>

Blockchain is essential for secure, transparent, and scalable payment systems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:17:17">[03:17:17]</a>. This technology enables:
*   **Tokenized rewards points:** Brands can execute direct-to-consumer campaigns tailored to their needs and profit margins. It also allows for swaps between different brands' rewards points, increasing stickiness and boosting conversions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:59:31">[02:59:31]</a>.
*   **Proof of Reserve:** Especially crucial for stablecoin issuers who are growing rapidly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:44">[00:07:44]</a>.
*   **Decentralized payment rails:** Facilitating instant global payments without jurisdictional limitations or extensive KYC, making it easier to reward users for online engagements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:57:51">[02:57:51]</a>.
*   **Sybill resistance:** Preventing fraudulent activity by ensuring high-quality users engage with brands <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:58:45">[02:58:45]</a>.
*   **Vampire attacks on Web2:** Leveraging [[ZK Proofs | ZK TLS]] allows brands to target competitors' customers by verifying user value (e.g., trading volume on another platform) and offering variable, higher rewards to more valuable users without revealing personal data <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:59:05">[02:59:05]</a>.

## Challenges and Opportunities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:08:08">[02:08:08]</a>

### Data Privacy and Security <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:51">[00:05:51]</a>
AI models are typically non-deterministic, posing a liability risk if transactions initiated by an agent don't match user intent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:21:01">[02:21:01]</a>. Solutions involve using small, niche-filtered models for context extraction rather than large language models, requiring confirmation for transactions, and potentially using dual agents for validation. A small percentage of transaction failures might be covered by an insurance fund <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:21:11">[02:21:11]</a>.

The use of Trusted Execution Environments (TEEs) like AWS Nitro enables transaction automation within confidential computing environments, where only the user has access to the private key. This supports advanced features like private Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA) and off-chain limit orders, addressing concerns about public transaction visibility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:40:40">[03:40:40]</a>.

### Market Size and Growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:11">[00:02:11]</a>
The crypto credit market, despite its recent contraction, represents a significant opportunity for growth. The total market size for lending to trading firms was estimated at around $70 billion in 2022, and this gap has not yet been replaced <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:31">[00:13:31]</a>. The AI finance app market is projected to reach 45 million customers across 40 countries, handling around 60 million monthly transactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:17:00">[02:17:00]</a>.

On-chain trading volume is currently about a tenth of centralized exchange (CEX) trading volume, which itself is a fraction of traditional finance. This presents a potential "thousand X" growth opportunity over the coming decades for on-chain activity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:43:02">[03:43:02]</a>.

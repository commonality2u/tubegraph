## Identifying Paradigm Shifts
A key aspect of [[entrepreneurial_mindset_and_societal_shifts | entrepreneurship]] is recognizing and adapting to societal and technological paradigm shifts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:16:17">[02:16:17]</a>. The shift occurring now with TikTok is profound:
*   **Discovery and Search**: Consumers, especially Gen Z, are increasingly using TikTok as their primary search engine and news source, moving away from traditional platforms like Google and Yelp <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:48:00">[02:48:00]</a>. They prefer visually engaging, short-form content for discovery, including for products and even restaurants <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:29:00">[02:29:00]</a>.
*   **Marketing and Sales**: The traditional advertising model (e.g., billboards, TV) is becoming less effective compared to digital platforms where return on ad spend (ROAS) is measurable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:29:00">[06:29:00]</a>. While Amazon ads now yield a ROAS of 1x-1.5x, TikTok's system can deliver 7x-8x, and initially even 30x, offering "cheat codes in the game of business" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:14:00">[07:14:00]</a>. This means brands can achieve massive revenue, with some projecting over $100 million in sales annually through this model <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:40:53">[08:40:53]</a>.

## Mindset for Success in New Eras
To capitalize on these shifts, an entrepreneur needs a specific [[mindset_and_strategies_for_innovation | mindset]]:
*   **Be an Observer, Not a Consumer**: Instead of judging new trends, objectively observe what is happening and how people are behaving <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:00">[00:31:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="40:48:00">[40:48:00]</a>. If a behavior or phenomenon seems "weird" but is undeniably happening, that's a signal to lean in as an investor or entrepreneur <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="39:03:00">[39:03:00]</a>.
*   **Embrace Early Adoption**: Those who are "crazy until they're successful" are the ones who ultimately become "geniuses" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="40:45:00">[40:45:00]</a>. This means acting early on new platforms before they become saturated, similar to how early Instagram users became influencers when others dismissed it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="58:19:00">[58:19:00]</a>.
*   **Disregard the Past, Be Realistic About the Present, Delusional About the Future**: Dwelling on past failures or successes can hinder progress. Instead, focus on the current reality while maintaining a bold, optimistic vision for what's possible, pushing beyond perceived limits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:13">[01:02:13]</a>.

## The TikTok Playbook: Creator Armies and Viral Content
The key to success on TikTok lies in its unique algorithm, which prioritizes views over follower count <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:51:00">[13:51:00]</a>. This has led to a new "creator model":
*   **Army of Creators**: Instead of relying on a single influencer, brands seek an army of creators to produce product-specific content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:15:00">[14:15:00]</a>. This increases the "swings at bat," making it more likely for a piece of content to go viral <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:31:00">[14:31:00]</a>.
*   **Content Wins**: The content itself is paramount. It should be raw, organic, and feel less like an advertisement and more like a story or genuine discovery <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:03:00">[20:03:00]</a>. Creators are incentivized through affiliate commissions to produce high volumes of content (30-50 videos/month), constantly learning what converts best <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:58:00">[17:58:00]</a>. Some freelance creators on TikTok can earn over $100,000 per month <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:18:00">[17:18:00]</a>.
*   **Leveraging Trends and Storytelling**: Successful content often taps into trending topics, conspiracies, or common fears/insecurities to grab attention and build curiosity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:00:00">[19:00:00]</a>. For example, a video selling oil of oregano utilized a combination of news trends (Trump), conspiracy theories about predatory industries, and scarcity tactics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:08:00">[21:08:00]</a>.
*   **"Response Marketing"**: This approach is akin to classic direct-response marketing, but instead of ad agencies, it leverages young creators specializing in short-form, attention-grabbing, and converting content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:19:00">[18:19:00]</a>.

Several brands are leveraging this model:
*   **Roose Research**: A 27-year-old founder with prior Amazon experience is building a nine-figure supplement brand in the longevity category, projected to do over $15 million in online sales this month through TikTok <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:12:00">[12:12:00]</a>.
*   **Evil Goods**: A skincare brand using beef tallow, it taps into the holistic wellness trend and anti-toxic skincare sentiment, using an aggressive name that resonates with a "marketing to fear" strategy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:37:00">[24:37:00]</a>.
*   **Top Shelf**: A cologne brand targeting Gen Z, aiming to be a "Creed for Gen Z." Despite luxury products traditionally being hard to sell on "discount" platforms, Top Shelf is doing close to $1 million a month, demonstrating that premium products can succeed if the marketing is right <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:41:00">[25:41:00]</a>.

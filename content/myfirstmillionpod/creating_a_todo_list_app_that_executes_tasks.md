The rapid advancements in artificial intelligence (AI), particularly with models like ChatGPT and autonomous agents such as AutoGPT and BabyAGI, are paving the way for a new generation of productivity tools. One significant development expected is the creation of a "magic to-do list" application that can not only organize tasks but also execute them autonomously <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>.

## The Concept of a Magic To-Do List

Traditional to-do list applications typically require users to manually input tasks and check them off upon completion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:51">[00:05:51]</a>. The core challenge with these apps is not creating the list, but the "doing" of the tasks themselves <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:58">[00:05:58]</a>. The "magic to-do list" envisions a system where users state what they need to accomplish, and the AI agent then performs the necessary steps on their behalf <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:07">[00:06:07]</a>.

This concept is considered "next level" compared to previous note-taking applications like Evernote, which achieved valuations in the billions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:04">[00:00:04]</a>.

## How it Works: Leveraging Advanced AI

The feasibility of an executing to-do list app stems from the capabilities of advanced AI models:

*   **General Artificial Intelligence (AGI)**: Unlike "narrow AI" designed for specific tasks (like self-driving cars), AGI refers to AI that can "do anything" and figure out how to accomplish tasks without being trained on a specific function <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:12">[00:01:12]</a>.
*   **Task Breakdown and Execution**: Newer models like AutoGPT and BabyAGI function as "agents" or "little workers" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:00">[00:04:00]</a>. You can give them a broad task, and they will break it down into sequential steps, similar to how a human would <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:20">[00:03:20]</a>. Crucially, these agents can then *perform* those steps, even passing sub-tasks between different AI "workers" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:01">[00:04:01]</a>.

### Practical Examples of AI Execution

*   **Website Creation**: An AI could be instructed to "make a website for my personal portfolio" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:09">[00:03:09]</a>. It would then deduce steps like setting up a Squarespace site, choosing a template, uploading files, and arranging photos <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:26">[00:03:26]</a>.
*   **Household Chores**: Imagine telling a to-do list app, "I need to get my pool fence installed; I have to call and get a quote" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:16">[00:06:16]</a>. The AI could then:
    *   Call three local places to get quotes (using existing auto-calling AI) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:22">[00:06:22]</a>.
    *   Compile the quotes into a table <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:30">[00:06:30]</a>.
    *   Offer a recommendation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:32">[00:06:32]</a>.
    *   Book the service and schedule it on your calendar, using your credit card if authorized <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:36">[00:06:36]</a>.
*   **Financial Management**: AI is being developed for tax preparation ("TaxGPT") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:00">[00:05:00]</a>. It can also analyze financial data, such as building a full Profit & Loss (P&L) statement and predicting financial outcomes based on various inputs like ad spend and return on ad spend (ROAS) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:01">[00:13:01]</a>.

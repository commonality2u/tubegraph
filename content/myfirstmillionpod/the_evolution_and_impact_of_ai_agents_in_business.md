## Understanding AI Agents

In the past year, the focus was primarily on interactive AI like Chat GPT, where users typed prompts and received discrete responses, such as a blog post or an image <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:02">[00:45:02]</a>. However, [[ai_agents_and_business_applications | AI agents]] represent a significant leap forward.

> "What agents are... are it's AI software that can accomplish higher-level goals requiring multiple tasks" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:38">[00:45:38]</a>.

This means agents can break down a complex goal into multiple, functionally decomposed steps, possessing memory and capability to execute each task to achieve the broader objective <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:52">[00:45:52]</a>. The expectation is that [[AI Agents and Business Applications | agents]] will become the "new apps," similar to how mobile applications proliferated, leading to hundreds of thousands or millions of specialized agents <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:17">[00:46:17]</a>.

## The Rise of Hybrid Teams

The future of work will see the emergence of hybrid teams comprising both humans and [[AI Agents and Business Applications | AI agents]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:13">[00:47:13]</a>. ## Results as a Service (RaaS)

The evolution of [[AI and its impact on various industries | AI]] also brings about a shift from "Software as a Service" (SaaS) to "Results as a Service" (RaaS) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:18">[01:07:18]</a>. Historically, software was a tangible product (e.g., on CDs), evolving into SaaS where the value of the software is provided as a service over the internet <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:27">[01:07:27]</a>.

RaaS takes this a step further:

> "Results as a service is like actually you don't even access the software you tell us what it is that you actually want to do what's the outcome that you're looking for and we'll just sell you that" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:49">[01:07:49]</a>.

This concept focuses purely on the desired outcome, rather than the tools or processes used to achieve it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:25">[01:08:25]</a>. For example, instead of selling legal software, a company might sell the service of analyzing a contract and providing commentary. This aligns with the long-standing principle that customers don't buy drills; they buy holes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:57">[01:08:57]</a>. The underlying software and [[AI Agents and Business Applications | agents]] would still be used to produce these results, but the offering to the customer is the direct outcome.

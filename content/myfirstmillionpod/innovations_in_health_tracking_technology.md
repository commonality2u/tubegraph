## The Rise of Personal Health Data

Individuals are increasingly tracking various aspects of their health to gain insights and make informed decisions. This trend goes beyond general fitness to detailed physiological measurements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:43">[00:27:43]</a>. While some health enthusiasts may track extensively (e.g., blood work quarterly, weight daily, almost all food consumed), such detailed tracking can take as little as 10 minutes a week <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:01">[00:28:01]</a>.

### Continuous Glucose Monitors (CGMs)

Continuous Glucose Monitors (CGMs) are a significant advancement in personal health tracking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:45">[00:27:45]</a>.
Dexcom, a major CGM manufacturer with approximately 40% market share, recently released Stellos, the first over-the-counter (OTC) FDA-approved CGM in late August <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:42">[00:28:42]</a>. Previously, CGMs typically required a prescription, often for individuals who were overweight or had diabetes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:03">[00:29:03]</a>.

CGMs involve a small, quarter-sized device injected into the arm that continuously measures blood glucose levels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:22">[00:29:22]</a>. Users can check their blood glucose levels at any time using an app <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:36">[00:29:36]</a>. Each device lasts about two weeks, and many people use them for two to four weeks to gather data on their resting and fasting glucose levels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:47">[00:29:47]</a>.

These devices provide immediate, "harsh" feedback, revealing how specific foods, even seemingly healthy ones like cranberries in a salad, sugary dressings, or whole foods like steak and potatoes, can cause blood glucose spikes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:08">[00:30:08]</a>. High blood glucose levels can damage organs and increase insulin resistance over time, potentially leading to conditions like type 2 diabetes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:16">[00:34:16]</a>. Key metrics to track include the height of the spike and the "area under the curve," which indicates how long it takes for glucose levels to return to normal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:54">[00:34:54]</a>.

Companies like Levels offer CGM products, and their availability as an OTC device presents new opportunities for broader consumer adoption <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:37">[00:31:37]</a>.

Beyond CGMs, other personal health tracking devices include:

*   Apple Watch <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:50">[00:30:50]</a>

While these devices provide broad data (e.g., "I slept bad"), they often lack specific actionable insights (e.g., "how do I fix it?") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:11">[00:31:11]</a>. In contrast, CGMs offer reactive and precise information, showing exactly what causes spikes and making it harder to ignore dietary impacts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:13">[00:31:13]</a>.

## Business Opportunities in Health Tracking

The increased access to personal health data opens up various [[business_opportunities_in_longevity_and_healthcare | business opportunities]]:

### Loss Leader Models
Given the $50 cost for a two-week CGM, a business could offer the device as a loss leader <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:34">[00:35:34]</a>. By providing free CGMs and requiring users to track their diet and lifestyle for two weeks, companies could gather invaluable data on an individual's metabolic health <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:56">[00:35:56]</a>. This data could then be used to offer:
*   Health concierge services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:08">[00:36:08]</a>
*   Nutrition and exercise education <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:13">[00:36:13]</a>
*   Personal trainers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:16">[00:36:16]</a>
*   Referrals to local gyms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:19">[00:36:19]</a>
*   Deals for subsequent CGM purchases <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:24">[00:36:24]</a>

One example of a business built on this premise is a "wearable challenge" where participants pay a sum upfront and receive money back daily for staying below a specific blood glucose threshold <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:40">[00:36:40]</a>.

### Posture and Physical Health
Beyond internal metrics, technology is also emerging to address physical alignment and posture <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:39">[00:39:39]</a>. Devices like "BetterBack" use straps to encourage straighter sitting posture <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:07">[00:40:07]</a>. Some sports bras, such as "Form," also claim to improve posture <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:02">[00:41:02]</a>. Ultimately, proper posture is a function of muscle strength in the abdomen and back <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:36">[00:41:36]</a>.

## Five Key Checkpoints for Success

To achieve success and financial freedom, there are five key checkpoints <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:51">[05:51]</a>:

### 1. Have the Courage to Start Now
The first crucial step is courage, not intelligence, experience, or strategy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:46">[12:46]</a>. Most people fall into the "someday trap," where they intend to [[Starting a business from scratch | start a company]] eventually, but "someday never comes" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:19">[10:19]</a>.

A steady salary can be a trap, akin to a "salary monster" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:17">[09:17]</a>. Choosing a safe path, like a corporate job, can be dangerous to your dream of being a successful entrepreneur <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:54">[09:54]</a>.

> "Going to business school to become an entrepreneur is like reading a book about basketball history because you want to join the NBA." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:55">[08:55]</a> — Andrew Wilkinson

To avoid the salary trap, consider becoming "strategically broke" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:21">[14:21]</a>. Calculate your "freedom number" – the minimum income needed to cover bills while maximizing free time for your entrepreneurial pursuits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:52">[14:52]</a>. For example, the speaker aimed for $15,000 a year to cover basic living expenses, which allowed him to work minimal hours (e.g., tutoring, coaching basketball) and dedicate the rest of his time to building a business <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:10">[15:10]</a>. This approach often requires living "scrappy" to reduce expenses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:15">[16:15]</a>.

### 2. Don't Quit on the First Failure – Play the Game 10 Times
Failure is almost inevitable when starting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:30">[13:30]</a>. The speaker's first business idea, a sushi franchise, failed miserably, resulting in lost money and a feeling of being labeled a failure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:00">[17:00]</a>.

> "There is no risk when you go after a dream. There is a tremendous amount of risk to playing it safe." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:03">[14:03]</a> — Bill Burr

Entrepreneurship is a game where the odds of a single business succeeding are about 1 in 10 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:31">[18:31]</a>. However, if you're allowed to play the game multiple times (e.g., 10 times), your odds of success increase significantly to about 65% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:54">[18:54]</a>. The speaker found that success often took around 10 attempts over 8-10 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:22">[19:22]</a>.

> "Startups fail but Founders don't." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:56">[19:56]</a> — Naval Ravikant

### 3. Learn to Build, Learn to Sell, and Learn to Get Lucky
Even if your initial [[Startup Ideas and Opportunities | startups]] fail, you can "win when you lose" by building a valuable skill stack <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:18">[20:18]</a>. The three master skills to develop are:

*   **Learning to Build:** Choose one way to make things (e.g., engineering, coding, design, content creation like podcasts or YouTube channels) and practice relentlessly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:14">[21:14]</a>. Pick something you're drawn to and would have the energy to do consistently <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:04">[22:04]</a>.
*   **Learning to Sell:** This involves getting your product or service into people's hands. Skills include Facebook/Google ads, content marketing, newsletter writing, copywriting, in-person sales, phone sales, or cold emailing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:21">[22:21]</a>. You'll likely be bad at first, but practice builds proficiency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:03">[23:03]</a>. [[Marketing and sales strategies for new businesses | Marketing and sales strategies for new businesses]] are crucial.
*   **Learning to Get Lucky:** Luck isn't just random. There are four types <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:16">[23:16]</a>:
    *   **Blind Luck:** Pure chance (e.g., being struck by lightning) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:22">[23:22]</a>.
    *   **Motion Luck:** Fortune favors the bold; doing more things increases your surface area for luck <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:31">[23:31]</a>.
    *   **Spotting Luck:** The prepared mind recognizes opportunities others miss by having a deep understanding of a field <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:55">[23:55]</a>.
    *   **Reputational Luck:** Building a strong reputation makes people seek you out for opportunities related to your expertise <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:40">[24:40]</a>. Making content online is valuable for building this reputation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:01">[25:01]</a>.

### 4. Proximity is Power – Move
Moving to a city aligned with your ambitions can significantly increase your odds of success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:29">[25:29]</a>. For example, actors go to Hollywood, finance professionals go to New York, and tech entrepreneurs go to Silicon Valley <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:44">[25:44]</a>. Being around a "crew" of people chasing the same dream creates a powerful energy that helps you learn and improve faster through osmosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:58">[25:58]</a>.

### 5. Be Impatient with Action, Patient with Results
This principle means taking immediate action while understanding that significant results take time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:56">[29:56]</a>. This ties into the "play the game 10 times" idea.

## White Belt Businesses: Beginner-Friendly Opportunities

These are [[Easy and hard businesses to start | beginner-friendly businesses]] that require little capital or experience, making them great starter options <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:39">[26:39]</a>.

### 1. [[Startup and content agency opportunities | Marketing Agency]]
A [[Startup and content agency opportunities | marketing agency]] forces you to learn how to sell, a critical skill <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:01">[27:01]</a>.

**How to get started:**
*   **Pick a niche:** Choose a specific type of marketing (e.g., video commercials, Google Ads, Facebook Ads, TikTok Ads) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:18">[27:18]</a>.
*   **Practice on low-stakes clients:** Offer your services to a friend or family member with a "crummy business" for a very low or starter budget (e.g., $100/month) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:46">[27:46]</a>. This allows you to learn and do simultaneously <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:30">[28:30]</a>.
*   **Learn intensely:** Study free courses on YouTube, join Facebook groups, and follow thought leaders in your chosen marketing channel <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:15">[28:15]</a>.
*   **Target specific businesses:** Many local businesses (e.g., senior living facilities, plumbers, daycares) are bad at marketing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:41">[28:41]</a>. Offer to run their ads for free, audit their accounts, or provide free advice <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:27">[29:27]</a>. Businesses with high customer lifetime value (e.g., a senior living facility where one customer is worth $36,000/year) can easily justify marketing costs, even if it takes $500-$1000 to acquire a customer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:00">[29:00]</a>.
*   **Scale:** Once you've successfully helped a few businesses, leverage those case studies to approach similar businesses in other areas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:37">[29:37]</a>. Your goal is to get to about 10 customers paying you $5,000 a month for your services, which translates to $50,000 a month in recurring revenue – effectively building a million-dollar business through hustle and commitment to learning one marketing channel <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:48">[29:48]</a>. This can be a great way of [[Scaling small businesses without traditional employees | scaling small businesses without traditional employees]].

### 2. Real Estate Development Apprenticeship
Real estate is an industry where you don't need to be a "genius" to succeed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:21">[30:21]</a>. Many millionaires are made through real estate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:19">[30:19]</a>.

**How to get started:**
*   **Identify local developers:** Draw a five-mile radius around your home and contact every real estate developer you can find <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:52">[30:52]</a>.
*   **Request a lunch meeting:** Ask to take them to lunch to learn from their experience, mentioning how someone might have helped them early in their career <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:13">[31:13]</a>.
*   **Offer free labor:** For the developer you connect best with, offer to work for free for 3-6 months. Emphasize your desire to learn the business inside and out, willingness to work harder than anyone, and upfront goal to buy your first property within a year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:47">[31:47]</a>. You can even express hope that they might become an investor in your future projects <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:30">[32:30]</a>.
*   **Learn and acquire:** After a year-long apprenticeship, locate your own first property (e.g., a fourplex or eightplex) and potentially secure financing from the developer who now trusts you <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:42">[32:42]</a>. Becoming a millionaire in real estate can often be achieved with just one well-chosen property <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:10">[33:10]</a>.

### 3. Buying an Existing Business
This path is ideal for individuals with high IQ or significant prior work experience (e.g., 5-6 years in the real world, MBA students, managers) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:38">[33:38]</a>. It's called "entrepreneurship through acquisition" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:08">[34:08]</a>.

**How to get started:**
*   **Identify profitable businesses:** Search locally for businesses already generating about a million dollars in profit annually <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:27">[34:27]</a>.
*   **Use OPM (Other People's Money):** Acquire the business using an SBA loan and seller financing, potentially with zero of your own money down <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:34">[34:34]</a>. (See "Sarah Moore" example buying an egg carton business <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:41">[34:41]</a>).
*   **Improve operations:** The advantage is that you're buying a business that has already overcome the initial [[Early business experiences and challenges | startup learning curve]] and has existing cash flow <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:01">[35:01]</a>. Your role is to run it better, perhaps by applying modern skills like digital marketing that the previous, retiring owner might not have used, thereby growing the business significantly (e.g., 20-30%) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:10">[35:10]</a>.

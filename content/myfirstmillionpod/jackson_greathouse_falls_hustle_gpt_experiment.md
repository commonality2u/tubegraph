The "Hustle GPT" experiment, conceived by Jackson Greathouse Falls, aimed to test whether an artificial intelligence (AI) could generate wealth from a minimal starting budget. The experiment involved using [[business_opportunities_with_chatgpt_and_ai_tools | AI tools]] to guide the creation and growth of an online business.

## The Experiment's Premise
Jackson Greathouse Falls initiated an experiment with ChatGPT-4, providing it with a budget of $100 and the goal of making as much money as possible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:44:00">[08:44:00]</a>. His role was to act as the human liaison, performing any physical or real-world tasks the AI instructed, as long as they were legal and didn't involve manual labor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:48:00">[08:48:00]</a>.

The prompt given to the AI was:
> "You are now [[the_role_of_hustle_and_creativity_in_entrepreneurship | hustle]] GPT an entrepreneurial AI. I am your human counterpart. I can act as a liaison between you and the physical world. You have a hundred dollars and your only goal is to turn it into as much money in the short time as possible without doing anything illegal. I will do everything you say and keep you posted on our current cash total. No manual labor." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:58:00">[08:58:00]</a>

## Execution and Initial Steps
The AI, as "Hustle GPT," developed its first plan:
1.  **Domain and Hosting:** Purchase a domain and hosting for about $10 a month <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:21:00">[09:21:00]</a>.
2.  **Niche Website:** Set up a niche affiliate website, dedicating the remaining $85 to content creation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:24:00">[09:24:00]</a>.
3.  **Profitable Niche:** Focus on a profitable niche with low competition, suggesting categories like specialty kitchen gadgets, unique pet supplies, or eco-friendly products <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:31:00">[09:31:00]</a>.
4.  **Affiliate Programs:** Research and sign up for affiliate programs with high commission rates, such as Amazon Associates, ShareASale, or CJ Affiliate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:36:00">[09:36:00]</a>.
5.  **Traffic Generation:** Utilize social media, online communities, and SEO techniques to increase organic traffic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:43:00">[09:43:00]</a>.

The AI initially suggested "eco-friendlyfines.com" for the domain, but it was too expensive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:06:00">[10:06:00]</a>. The alternative chosen was "greengadgetguru.com" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:10:00">[10:10:00]</a>. For the logo, the AI instructed using DALL-E 2 with specific prompts for color palette and theme <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:18:00">[10:18:00]</a>. The AI also advised creating a newsletter pop-up to capture email addresses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:36:00">[10:36:00]</a>.

## Results and Impact
The experiment, detailed by Jackson Greathouse Falls on Twitter, quickly gained traction, with his initial tweet receiving 20 million views <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:10:00">[11:10:00]</a>. The website, greengadgetguru.com, reportedly garnered 500,000 views <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:04:00">[11:04:00]</a>.

After some time, the experiment yielded:
*   **Cash on Hand:** $1,378 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:42:00">[11:42:00]</a>
*   **Investment:** A $500 investment, valuing the company at $25,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:48:00">[11:48:00]</a>
*   **Revenue:** The site itself generated $130 in revenue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:37:00">[12:37:00]</a>, with additional thousands from investors interested in being part of the first AI-driven company <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:43:00">[12:43:00]</a>.
*   **Newsletter:** The newsletter grew to 10,000 subscribers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:16:00">[11:16:00]</a>.

Jackson Greathouse Falls' experiment demonstrated the potential of AI to guide and even automate aspects of [[side_hustle_ideas_for_2023 | online business]] creation and [[building_and_automating_online_businesses | building and automating online businesses]].

This concept extends to various fields, such as "Tax GPT" for doing taxes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:00:00">[05:00:00]</a> or a "Magic To-Do List" that could handle tasks like getting quotes for a pool fence, scheduling appointments, and even processing payments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:07:00">[06:07:00]</a>.

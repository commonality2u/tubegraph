## The Cost of a Desired Lifestyle: A Brooklyn Example

One host, Sam, explored the astronomical expenses of living the lifestyle he desires in Brooklyn <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:23:00">[05:23:00]</a>.
His hypothetical plan to live in an $8 million home involves significant costs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:41:00">[05:41:00]</a>:

*   **Housing**:
    *   A $5 million brownstone in Cobble Hill (3,000 sq ft) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:21:00">[06:21:00]</a>.
    *   Down payment: $1.1 million <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:31:00">[06:31:00]</a>.
    *   Monthly mortgage and interest: Around $20,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:35:00">[06:35:00]</a>.

*   **Furnishing**: $50,000 (low end) to $150,000 (middle/high end) for brands like Restoration Hardware <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:52:00">[06:52:00]</a>.

In total, this lifestyle could cost $600,000-$700,000 annually <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:47:00">[07:47:00]</a>. Due to taxes, making over $1 million a year in Brooklyn means about half goes to taxes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:00:00">[08:00:00]</a>, requiring an income of $1.2 million just to break even <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:10:00">[08:10:00]</a>. ## Pathways to Affording Such Lifestyles

Discussion with friends revealed how people manage these expenses:

*   **Generational Wealth/Early Acquisition**: Many owners of expensive homes bought them 10-20 years ago at a fraction of today's prices, benefiting from appreciated value, lower mortgages, and property taxes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:01:00">[09:01:00]</a>. Research suggests that many current owners, like teachers or artists, received significant financial help, often in the form of family inheritance or gifts, to acquire these properties <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:30:00">[10:30:00]</a>.
*   **High Income**: To afford a $5 million home and its monthly costs without inherited assets, one needs to earn substantial income. Saving $10 million could take about 15 years if earning $1.5 million annually and spending $25,000 a month on living expenses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:43:00">[11:43:00]</a>.
    *   **Traditional Professions**: High-income jobs that consistently pull in $1-2 million a year include finance (bankers), law, medicine (self-employed doctors), and consulting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:15:00">[13:15:00]</a>.
    *   **Entrepreneurship**: Owning a small or large business, or a contracting firm, can lead to high earnings, though income is often "lumpy" – periods of nothing, then a lot, then back to nothing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:10:00">[14:10:00]</a>. This typically requires selling an asset to realize a large sum <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:06:00">[13:06:00]</a>.
*   **[[The philosophy of seizing opportunities | Seizing Opportunities]]**: The sheer amount of wealth and opportunity existing in the world is vast <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:01:00">[15:01:00]</a>.

## Case Study: [[Building Wealth through Apps | Building Wealth through Apps]] - Allan Wong

Allan Wong, known by his Reddit username "Rego apps," is an example of rapid wealth creation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:35:00">[23:35:00]</a>. He went from rags to riches during the recession by developing mobile applications <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:11:00">[24:11:00]</a>. His most popular app, the "5-0 Radio Police Scanner," allowed users to listen to police chatter worldwide <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:16:00">[24:16:00]</a>. By 2012, his apps had been downloaded 20 million times <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:28:00">[24:28:00]</a>. He is reportedly the only person in App Store history to have three apps simultaneously in the overall top 10 for both paid and free categories <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:22:00">[26:22:00]</a>.

Wong's background includes parents who fled the slums of China, a mother who worked in a sweatshop, and the passing of his father shortly after he graduated college <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:36:00">[24:36:00]</a>. As the sole income earner, he taught himself app development on weekends <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:55:00">[24:55:00]</a>. Through apps like "5-0 Radio," "Police Scanner Plus," and one other, he accumulated over $100 million in liquid assets within 8-10 years, with minimal employees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:34:00">[25:34:00]</a>. He achieved this by age 25, reportedly making over $10 million annually in his 30s <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:32:00">[27:32:00]</a>.

His experience illustrates that wealth can be achieved through unique paths, often non-traditional and not requiring genius or painful effort <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:21:00">[01:21:00]</a>. He also shares insights on the different levels of [[the_interplay_between_money_success_and_personal_values | wealth and its impact]], from tipping generously with $100K-$1M, to funding college tuition and medical expenses with $1-5M, to creating non-profits and building schools with $50M+ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:47:00">[27:47:00]</a>.

## Serial Entrepreneurship: Scott Painter

Scott Painter is presented as a "Billy of the week" – someone with "big Billy energy" (referring to a billionaire) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:02:00">[29:02:00]</a>. His career demonstrates the relentless pursuit of opportunity, even through significant setbacks.

*   **Early Life & Career**: Painter served as a Spanish interrogator in the Army <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:36:00">[30:36:00]</a>. In the early internet era (late 1990s), he held marketing leadership roles at 1-800 Car Search and 1-800 Dentist, and founded consulting firms like Dental Advantage and Vision Incorporated to help businesses adopt e-commerce <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:51:00">[30:51:00]</a>.
*   **Dominance in Auto Industry**:
    *   **CarsDirect**: Founded this company, which achieved $650 million in sales in its first year by being among the first to put upfront car prices on the internet <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:29:00">[31:29:00]</a>.
    *   **TrueCar**: Founded and became CEO of TrueCar, which brought price transparency to the auto industry, reducing price differentials for the same car from 30% to 3% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:37:00">[31:37:00]</a>.
    *   **Setbacks**: Despite taking TrueCar public, Painter faced a devastating period: a margin call due to buying too much of his own company's stock, a divorce, and his house burning down <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:10:00">[32:10:00]</a>. He had to borrow money from friends and resigned as CEO <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:23:00">[32:23:00]</a>.
    *   **Fair.com**: The week after his personal calamities, he incorporated Fair.com, aiming to simplify car ownership by making it a subscription model like Netflix, allowing users to rent cars long-term and return them anytime <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:40:00">[32:40:00]</a>. *   **Philosophy**: Painter, a multi-time college dropout, acknowledges his many failures (3 successes out of 25 attempts) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:43:00">[37:43:00]</a>. He sees cars as indicators of one's life chapters and emphasizes playing in massive markets (mobility accounts for 15% of gross income) to raise capital <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="38:00:00">[38:00:00]</a>. He has made and lost money many times over <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="40:07:00">[40:07:00]</a>, embodying [[the_philosophy_of_seizing_opportunities | consistent risk-taking]] and reinvention.

## An Unexpectedly Lucrative Industry: B2B Media

The hosts stumbled upon TechTarget, a publicly traded company (market cap $2.1 billion) that owns 10-20 websites like "Bitpipe.com" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="52:29:00">[52:29:00]</a>. Despite their outdated, "Craigslist-looking" interfaces <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="50:07:00">[50:07:00]</a>, these sites generate $400-500 million in annual revenue from only about 3,000 customers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="52:05:00">[52:05:00]</a>.

Their business model:
1.  They host highly technical articles and "e-guides" (e.g., "How to choose an HR Software System for a thousand plus employees") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="51:20:00">[51:20:00]</a>.
2.  To access these resources, users must provide extensive corporate information (email, name, company, job title, industry, address, phone) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="51:41:00">[51:41:00]</a>.
3.  TechTarget then sells this valuable lead information to software vendors who pay to contact potential buyers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="53:47:00">[53:47:00]</a>.
4.  They also track user behavior across their network of sites to identify purchase intent, then inform their clients about potential hot leads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="53:53:00">[53:53:00]</a>.

This business-to-business (B2B) media industry, despite its "awful" content and low aesthetic standards, is incredibly profitable because it serves a niche market of decision-makers seeking highly specific information, willing to exchange their data for it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="56:21:00">[56:21:00]</a>. This discovery leads to a discussion of [[balancing_personal_wealth_aspirations_with_business_growth | strategies for building wealth and financial independence]] within this overlooked sector.

## The Challenge of Presentations

Most business presentations are ineffective, not due to a lack of fancy slides or design, but because the core message is "out of order, jumbled up, and unclear" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:18">[00:06:18]</a>. The focus often incorrectly shifts to aesthetics rather than clarity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:01">[00:06:01]</a>. This phenomenon is why companies like Zeec.app, founded by actor Ed Norton, aim to help CEOs improve their presentations and make meetings more productive by focusing on storytelling <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:21">[00:04:21]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:27">[00:04:27]</a>.

## Strategies for Improvement

### Focusing on the Core Message

A fundamental step to improve any presentation is to identify the single most important takeaway <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:40">[00:06:40]</a>. This key message should ideally be presented upfront, perhaps as the title of the first slide <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:46">[00:06:46]</a>. By asking just a few targeted questions, a presentation can be significantly enhanced <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:55">[00:06:55]</a>.

### Effective Use of Titles

Titles in presentations should convey the message the audience should take away from that specific slide, rather than merely labeling its content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:17">[00:09:17]</a>. For example, instead of a slide titled "Our Team," a more impactful title would be "Our Team Has Had Three Successful Exits," immediately highlighting a key value proposition for the audience, such as an investor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:25">[00:09:25]</a>. This approach not only guides the audience but also helps the presenter structure their narrative effectively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:43">[00:09:43]</a>.

Instead of using [[AI tools for productivity and business management | AI]] to generate answers, prompt it to ask you questions that help structure your thinking and clarify your objectives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:09">[00:07:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:44">[00:07:44]</a>. [[AI tools for productivity and business management | AI]] can also generate a "bland neutral summary" of a topic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:14">[00:08:14]</a>, which can be used as a "negative space" to identify what *not* to say, ensuring your message stands out by focusing on more interesting and less obvious points <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:25">[00:08:25]</a>.

### Selling Value Over Time or Cost

When presenting a product or service, it's often more effective to emphasize the value it generates rather than just the time or money it saves <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:01">[00:34:01]</a>. For instance, a product that halves marketing costs could also be framed as one that doubles leads. Customers are often willing to pay significantly more for increased output or revenue than for cost savings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:07">[00:35:07]</a>. The goal should be to generate as much value as possible for the customer, and then strategically decide how to share that value, whether through pricing, retention, or advocacy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:01">[00:36:01]</a>.

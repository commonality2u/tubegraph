## Future Outlook and Job Evolution

### The "K-Shaped Future"

The future of jobs is described as a "k-shaped future" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:52:00">[07:52:00]</a>:
*   Those who learn to use [[role_of_ai_in_enhancing_business_processes | AI tools]] will "accelerate and go up at a way faster pace" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:03:00">[08:03:00]</a>.
*   Those who ignore or pretend these tools don't exist will "go down" due to being "outcompeted by companies that are not going to do that" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:11:00">[08:11:00]</a>.

### The Rise of Virtual Employees

The expectation is that businesses will very soon have "virtual employees" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:45:00">[22:45:00]</a>. Within the next 12 months, "[[the_evolution_and_impact_of_ai_agents_in_business | AI]] co-workers" will be present in communication platforms like Slack <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:58:00">[22:58:00]</a>. ## AI's Impact on the Software Industry

The landscape of software businesses is expected to change significantly. Traditionally, "vertical markets" or niche software (e.g., funeral home management software, golf course management software) were highly profitable due to limited competition <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:54:00">[26:54:00]</a>. However, [[generative_ai_and_its_potential_impact | AI]] tools like Replit can easily reproduce basic software in minutes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:12:00">[28:12:00]</a>. This increased ease of software creation will lead to greater "commoditization" and "way more competition," making it a "way harder world for software businesses" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:40:00">[28:40:00]</a>.

> [!NOTE] Software Evolution, Not Elimination
> While some basic "crud database" software may become less valuable, complex software (e.g., social networks where value is in people and connections, or enterprise software with security and tailored services) will remain safe <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:51:00">[31:51:00]</a>. The market for software may also expand as new, previously unfeasible software ideas can now be brought to fruition by non-coders <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:53:00">[32:53:00]</a>. The analogy to media is drawn: while traditional media businesses declined, new social media businesses emerged, creating a larger overall market <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:20:00">[36:20:00]</a>.

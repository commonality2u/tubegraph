### Idea Assessment Framework
[[Dharmesh Shahs journey from entrylevel jobs to billiondollar valuations | Shah]] employs a quantitative framework for assessing ideas, whether for himself or for investing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:31">[01:05:31]</a>:
1.  **Profit Potential**: What's the overall potential if everything aligns perfectly? (0-10 score)
2.  **Passion**: How excited are you about it? (0-10 score)
3.  **Probability of Success**: What's the likelihood of achieving that success? (0-10 score)
He multiplies these scores to get a total out of 1000, allowing for different weightings. A common mistake people make is to "exclusively" filter by the probability of success first, discarding ideas with low probability even if they have high potential <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:28">[01:06:28]</a>. He argues for evaluating ideas by "expected value" (e.g., a 1% chance at a billion dollars has an expected value of $10 million) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:57">[01:06:57]</a>.

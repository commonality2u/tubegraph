## Individual Creative Philosophies

## The Role of Collaboration

### Assembling a Team
MrBeast's company is not run like a "normal company" in the traditional sense, but he manages a team of about 60 people in his studio. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:09">[00:21:09]</a> His employees report that the "extreme good part" of working with him is the unique experiences, including travel and executing "coolest stunts." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:17">[00:18:17]</a> The "worst part" is the lack of routine and not knowing what's next, which employees acknowledge comes with the job. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:28">[00:18:28]</a>

### Empowering Experts
For his other ventures like snack brands and "Beast Burger," MrBeast establishes separate entities and hires top industry professionals, such as Jim, who helped build RXBAR, to lead and manage these independent companies. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:50">[00:20:50]</a> This strategy allows him to "empower them and give them money." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:05">[00:21:05]</a>

### Hasan Minhaj on Comedy's "Low Status Game"
Hasan Minhaj discusses comedy as a "low status game," where comedians start from a low status to encourage empathy from the audience. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:29">[00:13:29]</a> He gives examples of owning awkward or self-deprecating facts, like soiling pants during a baseball game or remarrying an ex-wife, to connect with an audience. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:21">[00:14:21]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:38">[00:14:38]</a>

## Content Strategy and Evolution

### Hooking the Audience
MrBeast emphasizes the importance of hooking the audience in the first 10 seconds of a YouTube video. He criticizes typical podcast intros for not immediately gripping listeners. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:02">[00:04:02]</a> He demonstrates his ideal hook: "Guys, in this we're going to teach you how to make a million dollars. If you don't have a million dollars, we're going to show you in this pocket." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:31">[00:04:31]</a>

### Originality and Perfectionism
MrBeast strives for originality in his content, aiming for ideas "no one's ever done before." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:24">[00:24:24]</a> He is a perfectionist who only wants to upload "the greatest YouTube videos possible." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:14">[00:19:14]</a> He finds it "dumb" for creators to stick to a rigid upload schedule if the content isn't great, stating that quality is the only thing that matters. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:37">[00:33:37]</a>

### Adapting to Change
The perception of being an "obsessive nerd" for creating YouTube videos has shifted from a negative to a positive, now seen as "inspiring." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:08">[00:30:08]</a> This reflects a broader cultural shift where "standing out" through unique passions is valued over "fitting in." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:09">[00:31:09]</a>

> "Standing out has been the game the whole time and the way you stand out is not by trying to figure it out, just whatever you're obsessed with just let that ride because you'll go on a path that nobody else is going to copy and then you'll, you know, you'll land in a good spot." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:18">[00:31:18]</a>

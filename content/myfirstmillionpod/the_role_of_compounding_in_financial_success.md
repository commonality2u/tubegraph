## Getting Rich Slow: The Power of Consistency

The path to significant wealth often involves a "get rich slow" approach, contrasting with the allure of "get rich quick" schemes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:37">[00:21:37]</a>. While headlines often feature individuals who "swung big and they won big" like Jeff Bezos, for every such success, there are many failures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:35">[00:13:35]</a>.

Instead of aiming for high-risk, high-reward ventures that often fail (like venture-backed startups with an estimated 80% failure rate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:22">[00:17:22]</a>), a more reliable strategy focuses on consistent, solid returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:12">[00:13:12]</a>.

### An Example of Compounding

Consider an individual who has a million dollars at age 30 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:33">[00:14:33]</a>. If they can produce and invest profits of $300,000 to $500,000 per year at a 10-12% return, they could accumulate $100 million by age 60 through compounding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:40">[00:14:40]</a>.

> "The number you need to start with doesn't need to be that big. Like a small number can compound into something staggering if you have a long enough time horizon, especially if you're outperforming 10 percent." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:30">[00:15:30]</a>

The majority of wealth from compounding tends to accumulate in the later stages of the investment period <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:56">[00:15:56]</a>. At a 7% rate, capital roughly doubles every ten years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:20">[00:16:20]</a>. Even a modest rate, like 10-15%, can lead to hundreds of millions of dollars over time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:37">[00:30:37]</a>.

## Applying Compounding in Business

One effective strategy is to reinvest a significant portion of business profits back into the business or into new ventures. For instance, taking the majority of profits (e.g., 80-90%) and using them to incubate or acquire other businesses can lead to substantial compounding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:50">[00:25:50]</a>.

By acquiring businesses at 3-10 times their earnings and then doubling their growth within one to two years, a significant return can be achieved <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:41">[00:28:41]</a>. This active approach can yield much higher compounding rates (e.g., 40%) compared to passive investments like real estate or stocks (8-12%) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:18">[00:28:18]</a>. This strategy also allows for the use of debt, further amplifying returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:28">[00:28:28]</a>.

> "When I went from starting businesses to buying businesses and investing... I made a lot of bets with that money and ended up compounding into something significant." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:21">[00:26:21]</a>

Over 15 years, consistently reinvesting profits can lead to businesses collectively worth over a billion dollars <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:36">[00:27:36]</a>. While the compounded rate may slow down with larger deals, the key is the sustained reinvestment and growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:57">[00:29:57]</a>. This method prioritizes reasonable safety and good businesses over chasing extreme returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:08">[00:30:08]</a>.

## Early Career: From Journalist to Aspiring YouTuber

Doug DeMuro began his career as a journalist, writing "funny quirky blogs" for various websites, including the popular car blog Jalopnik <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:10">[00:01:10]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:52">[00:09:52]</a>. He launched his YouTube channel in late 2013 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:49">[00:07:49]</a>. Initially, he primarily reviewed his own cars, which didn't gain significant traction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:20">[00:11:20]</a>.

A pivotal moment came when he started embedding his YouTube videos into his articles on Jalopnik. Although Jalopnik paid him a modest $25 per article, this exposure was invaluable, driving traffic to his YouTube channel and helping him gain algorithmic visibility and subscribers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:00">[00:10:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:09">[00:10:09]</a>. By 2015, it became clear that his primary identity was shifting from writer to YouTuber <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:27">[00:10:27]</a>.

## [[doug_demuros_approach_to_growing_his_youtube_channel | YouTube Channel Growth and Strategy]]

Doug's YouTube channel, which now boasts over four million subscribers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:16">[00:01:16]</a>, saw a major turning point in late 2016 when he increased his upload frequency to two videos per week <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:51">[00:07:51]</a>. This consistency had a significant impact on his growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:01">[00:08:01]</a>. In 2017, the channel truly "took off" when he shifted his content strategy from reviewing his own cars to reviewing other people's cars, especially new models <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:00">[00:12:00]</a>. This change was cost-effective and allowed him to produce more content more frequently <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:47">[00:11:47]</a>.

His content strategy focuses on "overload[ing] people with info and cool stuff about the cars" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:35">[00:04:35]</a>. Unlike many reviewers who focus on driving experience, Doug delves into the "little ins and outs of the car they are actually gonna buy" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:07">[00:05:07]</a>. This detailed, quirky approach resonated with viewers, building a loyal and engaged audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:08">[00:02:08]</a>.

### Persona and Relatability

Doug's on-screen persona is a significant part of his appeal. He is known for his unique, often perceived as "horrible," fashion sense, favoring "khaki shorts like from the early 2000s with like a red Polo shirt and then your white undershirt will be longer than the sleeves" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:25">[00:01:25]</a>. This unpretentious style, combined with his funny, quirky, and relatable demeanor, makes him highly likable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:04">[00:02:04]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:09">[00:03:09]</a>.

Despite his growing wealth, he maintains a relatable image, often joking about affording certain cars <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:17">[00:12:17]</a>. He consciously chooses to keep his video production simple, often using an iPhone for half the shots and editing them himself until recently, believing that "as long as the content is good, you can just do this" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:03">[00:04:03]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:17">[00:04:17]</a>.

### The Doug Score

A unique feature of his reviews is the "Doug Score," a personal rating system for cars <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:19">[00:05:19]</a>. This score, initially an informal Google Sheet, has become a trusted resource for his audience, influencing their car-buying decisions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:42">[00:05:42]</a>. While recognizing its potential as a standalone business, Doug has prioritized content creation, seeing himself as a specialist in videos rather than database management <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:23">[00:07:23]</a>.

## Monetization and Entrepreneurship

By December 2016, Doug's YouTube channel was generating substantial income, with one month bringing in $20,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:13">[00:08:13]</a>. This quickly grew to an amount he could live off <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:31">[00:08:31]</a>.

In 2019, Doug launched [[business_models_of_successful_youtube_creators | Cars & Bids]], an online auction platform for enthusiast cars from the 1980s, 90s, and early 2000s <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:15">[00:01:15]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:02">[00:16:02]</a>. He partnered with Blake, a viewer with a background in tech <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:27">[00:17:27]</a>. Doug held a "substantial majority" of the equity, recognizing that his existing audience was a crucial asset <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:41">[00:18:41]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:14">[00:19:14]</a>.

The business model for Cars & Bids involves buyers paying a 4.5% fee, while sellers pay nothing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:35">[00:20:35]</a>. They also offer shipping and inspection services as additional revenue drivers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:41">[00:20:41]</a>. The YouTube channel and auction site are now rolled into one business <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:03">[00:21:03]</a>. In 2021, Cars & Bids achieved approximately $75 million in gross sales <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:12">[00:21:12]</a>.

### [[doug_demuros_sale_of_his_business_to_the_churnin_group | Sale to the Churnin Group]]

Recently, Doug sold a majority stake in Cars & Bids to the Churnin Group for around $40 million, just two or three years after its launch <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:24">[00:02:24]</a>. This transaction valued the company in the $60-80 million range <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:47">[00:50:47]</a>. Doug was able to take money off the table from this deal, which provided him with financial comfort <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:22">[00:25:22]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:50">[00:47:50]</a>.

The Churnin Group was specifically interested in creator-led businesses, which alleviated Doug's fear of external parties interfering with his YouTube content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:30">[00:24:30]</a>. This type of business model carries a unique risk, as the success is highly dependent on the creator's continued involvement and public image <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:28">[00:28:28]</a>.

Doug frequently advises other content creators to "start a business" and monetize their audience, emphasizing that audience attention "will not last forever" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:55">[00:00:55]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:24">[00:30:24]</a>. His own journey, driven by a fear of his audience eventually fading <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:00">[00:30:00]</a>, serves as a testament to this philosophy.

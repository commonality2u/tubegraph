## The Turning Point: From Chaos to Clarity
Dyrdek's current highly structured and optimized life was not how he always operated. He admits to having been a "guy getting attacked by sharks who's starting company after company breaking world records" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:06">[00:24:06]</a>, living a chaotic life with significant financial disorganization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:01">[00:37:01]</a>.

The pivotal moment came in 2013 when a group offered him a 360 deal for his professional skateboarding league. During their due diligence, they deemed him "uninvestible" due to his uncontrolled spending and lack of proper financial management <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:11">[00:25:11]</a>. This served as a "deep awakening" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:27">[00:25:27]</a>, making him realize his self-created identity was false <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:34">[00:25:34]</a>.

This led him to:
*   Hire a consultant to learn how to build a company from beginning to end <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:00">[00:26:00]</a>.
*   Discover the book "Start at the End," which advised deciding on business outcomes *before* starting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:09">[00:26:09]</a>. This inspired him to apply the same principle to his life: designing the life he wanted first <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:22">[00:26:22]</a>.
*   Shift from "self-preservation" to "generational preservation," aiming for a 500-year plan for his family <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:56">[00:26:56]</a>.
*   Change his living habits to attract the necessary people and knowledge to achieve his designed life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:18">[00:27:18]</a>.

## Building and Deploying Voice AI
The platform used for developing "Lowballer 9000" is Vappy, described as a flexible way to build voice agents <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:47:00">[02:47:00]</a>. Vappy allows users to bring their own prompts and select providers for different services, offering a non-proprietary approach to [[implementing_and_deploying_ai_voice_applications_with_nextjs | implementing and deploying AI voice applications]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:57:00">[02:57:00]</a>.

### Key Components and Considerations
*   **Prompt Engineering**: Prompts are developed through iterative testing and observation of real-world interactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:42:00">[03:42:00]</a>. Key refinements include:
    *   Keeping responses concise (1-2 sentences max) to improve engagement and avoid sounding overly robotic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:43:00">[04:43:00]</a>.
    *   Instructing the AI to communicate at a "sixth-grade English level" to achieve longer call durations and a more human sound <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:30:00">[05:30:00]</a>.
*   **AI Models**: The choice of AI model (e.g., Gemini 20 Flash, DeepSeek) depends on current availability and performance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:04:00">[06:04:00]</a>. Model popularity can lead to API slammage, causing bots to crash or fail to respond <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:21:00">[06:21:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:09:00">[08:09:00]</a>.
*   **Temperature Setting**: This setting controls the AI's creativity. A temperature closer to zero makes the system more deterministic and predictable, while a higher temperature allows for more creative responses, crucial for handling uncommon questions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:24:00">[08:24:00]</a>.
*   **Voice Configuration**: Users can select specific voices, such as "Cartesia" or "New York man" (allegedly referencing a fictional character from a popular TV show) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:06:00">[09:06:00]</a>.
*   **Tool Calls**: This powerful feature allows the voice AI to interact with existing tools and databases <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:09:00">[10:09:00]</a>. For example:
    *   Recording and analyzing calls in a database (like AirTable) to identify actionable outcomes, such as when an offer was received <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:31:00">[10:31:00]</a>.
    *   Integrating with tools like Lindy, which excels at flexibly extracting and categorizing data from large amounts of text into spreadsheets, even adapting to new columns automatically <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:47:00">[12:47:00]</a>.
*   **Call Deliverability (Twilio)**: A significant hurdle is ensuring calls ring through to phones, often impacted by "shake and stir" regulations. Users must register their phone numbers and complete Know Your Customer (KYC) processes to avoid calls going straight to voicemail <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:53:00">[14:53:00]</a>.

## Broader Business Use Cases for Voice AI
Beyond direct sales [[negotiation_strategies_using_ai | negotiation strategies using AI]], voice AI offers diverse applications for [[implementing_ai_assistants_in_different_business_functions | implementing AI assistants in different business functions]] and [[using_ai_for_business_efficiency and expansion | business expansion]]:

*   **Sales Prospecting**: Automating calls to prospect lists in industries like real estate to gather critical information, such as whether a house is likely to go on the market soon <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:20:00">[16:20:00]</a>.
*   **Data Arbitrage**: Voice AI is excellent for gathering data, which can provide an "unfair advantage" and enable arbitrage businesses. Any business where knowledge is an unfair advantage or where knowing more than competitors is crucial presents a voice AI opportunity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:32:00">[17:32:00]</a>. This can lead to creating more transparent secondary markets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:47:00">[18:47:00]</a>.
*   **Customer Feedback Collection**: Implementing voice AI to collect feedback from community members, allowing them to "brain dump" without having to fill out forms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:24:00">[20:24:00]</a>. This feedback can then be categorized, classified, and summarized (e.g., posted to Slack channels) to [[improving_customer_interactions_with_ai | improve customer interactions with AI]] and enhance services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:58:00">[20:58:00]</a>. This capability could be offered as a service to other companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:03:00">[22:03:00]</a>.
*   **[[ai_avatars_in_business_workflow | AI avatars in business workflow]]**: The ability of these voice agents to act as persistent, interactive entities suggests a future where AI avatars become more integrated into daily business processes.

The flexibility of platforms like Vappy, coupled with integrations with tools like Lindy, means that [[using_ai_tools_like_daily_and_pipcats_for_voice_apps | using AI tools like Daily and Pipcats for voice apps]] can unlock significant value across various business functions.

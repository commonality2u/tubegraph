## Why YouTube?

YouTube is not merely a social media platform like Instagram or TikTok; it functions more like a streaming service, comparable to Netflix or Amazon Prime <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:41">[00:04:41]</a>. Nielsen data indicates that YouTube is the largest streaming service globally by total time spent streaming content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:31">[00:05:31]</a>.

Key reasons to invest in YouTube:
*   **Digital Assets (Evergreen Content)**: YouTube videos act as [[creating_highquality_content_without_a_large_social_media_following | digital assets]] that can generate views and engagement long after they are published <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:05">[00:06:05]</a>. Unlike fleeting social media posts, YouTube content can have a sustained lifespan, providing continuous viewership from its "catalog" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:47">[00:06:47]</a>. There is no algorithmic bias against older content if it performs well <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:42:21">[01:42:21]</a>.
*   **High Conversion Rates**: YouTube excels at converting viewers into customers, second only to email marketing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:45">[01:14:45]</a>. If you have a clear, defined audience, YouTube can be a powerful platform for audience engagement and conversion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:51">[01:14:51]</a>.
*   **Monetization**: YouTube allows creators to earn revenue directly through AdSense, with RPM (revenue per thousand views) typically ranging from $3 to $10 for Western, English-speaking audiences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:40">[01:15:40]</a>. This means your content can essentially pay for itself or even become a significant income stream <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:25">[01:16:25]</a>.
*   **Stability and Predictability**: While challenging to master, YouTube offers a more stable and predictable growth environment compared to other platforms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:04">[01:17:04]</a>. The platform's algorithm has matured, reducing reliance on "growth hacks" or gimmicks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:40">[01:20:40]</a>. YouTube is considered a stable platform, unlikely to face bans like some other social media apps <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:24:26">[01:24:26]</a>.
*   **Audience Demographics**: YouTube is highly popular among younger demographics, with 33% of 2-11 year olds' streaming market share in the US belonging to YouTube <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:50:00">[01:50:00]</a>. These viewers will grow up and continue to consume content on the platform <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:50:12">[01:50:12]</a>.
*   **Depth and Discovery**: YouTube uniquely combines the ability to build deep connections and community with an audience while also offering strong discovery mechanisms for new viewers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:30:23">[01:30:23]</a>.

## 12-Month Plan for [[YouTube growth strategies from Patty Galloway | YouTube Growth]]

This plan is structured in phases to help you [[building_and_growing_an_online_audience | build and grow an online audience]] on YouTube <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:36:53">[01:36:53]</a>.

### Phase 1: Establishment (Months 1-4)
This phase focuses on getting comfortable with the platform and defining your core content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:53:00">[01:53:00]</a>.
1.  **Find Your Niche/Sub-Niche**: Use the "triple Venn diagram" approach: find the intersection of what you love, what you're good at, and what people want to watch <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:38:39">[01:38:39]</a>. Tools like 10.com and View Stats can help identify "outlier" content in broader niches to pinpoint a profitable sub-niche <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:49:50">[01:49:50]</a>.
2.  **Laser Focus**: Don't try to be everything to everyone. Avoid being a "sushi restaurant serving burgers" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:40:28">[01:40:28]</a>. A diversified channel appealing to multiple interests will struggle to find a consistent audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:41:17">[01:41:17]</a>.
3.  **Post Two Videos Per Week**: In the beginning, focus on quantity over perfection. The goal is to build a habit of [[developing_content_ideas_and_strategies_on_youtube | content creation]] and get familiar with [[youtube_channel_creation_and_monetization | YouTube channel creation]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:41:51">[01:41:51]</a>. Don't worry too much about ideation or refining content quality yet <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:46:57">[01:46:57]</a>. By the end of this phase, you should have around 32 videos posted <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:51:00">[01:51:00]</a>.

### Phase 2: Improvement (Months 5-8)
Having established your presence, this phase is about refining your content and strategy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:53:00">[01:53:00]</a>.
1.  **Start Ideating 100+ Ideas Per Week**: Shift focus to generating a high volume of ideas. This is a volume game <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:52:19">[01:52:19]</a>. Big creators like Mr. Beast often have "idea farms" with thousands of video concepts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:53:49">[01:53:49]</a>. Brainstorm around outliers within your niche or even explore "vertical" or similar niches to adapt successful formats <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:55:00">[01:55:00]</a>. Schedule dedicated "idea time" and consider sourcing ideas from your community or team members <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:21:50">[02:21:50]</a>.
2.  **Make Three Thumbnails Per Video**: Thumbnails are crucial, accounting for 50-60% of a video's effectiveness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:59:01">[01:59:01]</a>. Utilize YouTube's ABC testing feature <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:57:48">[01:57:48]</a>. Don't just change text; create three distinct concepts for each video <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:30:30">[02:30:30]</a>.
    *   **Tools**: Canva is good for basic thumbnails; Photoshop is recommended for higher quality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:01:10">[02:01:10]</a>.
    *   **Designers**: Good graphic design skills don't necessarily translate to good thumbnail design <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:01:42">[02:01:42]</a>. Focus on understanding the psychology of what makes people click <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:02:13">[02:02:13]</a>. You can hire thumbnail designers on platforms like [YTjobs.co](https://www.ytjobs.co/) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:03:09">[02:03:09]</a>.
3.  **Switch to One Video Per Week**: As you gain experience, focus on increasing the quality of each video <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:05:01">[02:05:01]</a>. This allows for more time to refine content and packaging <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:05:13">[02:05:13]</a>. Even top creators like Mr. Beast aim to post as frequently as possible while maintaining high quality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:07:15">[02:07:15]</a>.

### Phase 3: Optimization (Months 9-12)
By this stage, you should have nearly 50 videos posted. The goal is to become more strategic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:08:01">[02:08:01]</a>.
1.  **Hone in on the Top 10% of Videos**: Identify your best-performing content and create more videos in similar formats or themes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:08:09">[02:08:09]</a>. This is the "sniper approach" versus a "machine gun approach" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:09:11">[02:09:11]</a>.
2.  **Study YouTube Trends and Outliers**: Dedicate about four hours per week to studying what's working on the platform <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:09:54">[02:09:54]</a>. Create playlists (a "swipe file") of outlier videos, great thumbnails, titles, or intros to analyze later <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:10:04">[02:10:04]</a>. This training of your mind helps you instinctively recognize what works <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:12:55">[02:12:55]</a>.
3.  **Continue to Post Weekly with 1% Improvements**: Embrace a philosophy of continuous marginal gains. Focus on making each video slightly better than the last <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:13:50">[02:13:50]</a>. Track learnings from each video, especially using retention curves in YouTube Analytics to understand where viewers drop off or stay engaged <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:14:50">[02:14:50]</a>. Even small changes, like avoiding phrases that signal an end to the video, can significantly impact retention <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:16:00">[02:16:00]</a>.

## Idea Generation: The 0-100-10-1 Funnel

This framework streamlines the idea selection process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:45">[01:17:45]</a>:
1.  **0 Ideas**: Start by understanding your channel's strategy and core frameworks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:52">[01:17:52]</a>.
2.  **Brainstorm 100+ Ideas**: Generate a large volume of raw ideas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:02">[01:18:02]</a>.
3.  **Apply Elimination Criteria**: Filter these ideas down to about 10 strong concepts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:08">[01:18:08]</a>.
    *   **Example Criteria**:
        *   Can you think of a good title and thumbnail for it? If not, it's not a good YouTube idea <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:27:15">[01:27:15]</a>.
        *   Can it realistically achieve a specific view target (e.g., 10k, 1M views) or generate a certain amount of positive comments? <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:27:49">[01:27:49]</a>
        *   Is it feasible to produce? <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:05">[01:29:05]</a>
        *   Does it excite you or your team? <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:15">[01:29:15]</a>
4.  **Further Develop 1 Idea**: Select the single best idea from the narrowed-down list and develop it fully <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:15">[01:18:15]</a>.

### Key Frameworks for Ideas
*   **CCN Fit (Core, Casual, New)**: Ensure your videos appeal to your loyal core audience, casual viewers, and new potential subscribers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:00">[01:19:00]</a>. Avoid making videos too niche that alienate new viewers, or too broad that they don't resonate with your core audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:14">[01:19:14]</a>.
*   **80%+ Audience Overlap**: Each video should appeal to at least 80% of the audience that watched your other videos <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:46">[01:20:46]</a>. This ensures viewers who enjoy one video will likely enjoy others, fostering a strong content catalog <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:56">[01:20:56]</a>.
*   **Sources of Ideas**:
    *   **External**: Look at other channels (even in tangential niches) and what's performing well for them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:25:00">[01:25:00]</a>.
    *   **Internal**: Analyze your own channel's past successful videos to identify patterns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:25:53">[01:25:53]</a>.
    *   **Innovation**: Brainstorm completely new concepts, often inspired by things outside YouTube (e.g., TV shows, movies) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:26:03">[01:26:03]</a>.

### Qualities of a Great Idea
*   **Easy to Convey in Title/Thumbnail**: If you can't package it well, it's not a strong idea for YouTube <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:27">[01:29:27]</a>.
*   **Uniqueness/Superlative**: Incorporate elements like "world's biggest," "most expensive," or a unique twist to make it stand out <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:51">[01:29:51]</a>.
*   **Plays on a Competitive Advantage**: Leverage your unique knowledge, access, or resources that others don't have <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:30:24">[01:30:24]</a>. This doesn't just mean wealth or power; it could be specific expertise or unique access to individuals/events <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:32:01">[01:32:01]</a>.
*   **Not Too Narrow, Not Too Broad**: Find the sweet spot that appeals to a wide, but consistent, audience within your niche <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:31:32">[01:31:32]</a>.

## Packaging: Titles and Thumbnails

Packaging is critical for attracting clicks and views <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:22:15">[02:22:15]</a>.
*   **The Line Between Boring and Misleading**: Successful channels often "exaggerate" or "oversell" the truth a little in their packaging to draw viewers in, as long as the content delivers on the promise <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:30:00">[02:30:00]</a>. The average viewer processes a thumbnail in a split second <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:33:57">[02:33:57]</a>.

### Thumbnail Best Practices
*   **Create Three Distinct Thumbnails**: Don't just change text; generate three different conceptual approaches for each video <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:35:11">[02:35:11]</a>.
*   **Learn Basic Photoshop**: While advanced graphic design isn't necessary, understanding basic Photoshop or using tools like Canva helps create effective thumbnails <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:36:09">[02:36:09]</a>.
*   **Clear and Bright**: Thumbnails should be easy to understand at a glance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:39:56">[02:39:56]</a>. Perform a "glance test" by quickly flashing your thumbnail to see if its message is immediately apparent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:40:03">[02:40:03]</a>.
*   **Limit Text**: Aim for four to five words maximum in your thumbnail text for English-speaking markets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:40:48">[02:40:48]</a>.
*   **Three-Focus Area Rule**: A thumbnail should have no more than three main elements for the viewer to focus on (e.g., a face, text, and an object) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:41:25">[02:41:25]</a>.
*   **Match Expression to Content**: Ensure the emotions or expressions in the thumbnail align with the video's tone <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:42:31">[02:42:31]</a>.

### Title Best Practices
*   **Keep Titles Concise**: Aim for under 60 characters for optimal visibility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:40:50">[02:40:50]</a>.
*   **Use Simple Language**: Make titles easy to understand, even for a seven-year-old <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:37:01">[02:37:01]</a>. Avoid jargon or region-specific terms if aiming for a global audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:39:01">[02:39:01]</a>.
*   **Incorporate Superlatives/Uniqueness**: Use words that create intrigue or highlight something exceptional <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:37:04">[02:37:04]</a>.
*   **Focus on Human Interest**: Write titles for human appeal rather than solely for search engine optimization. While keywords can be included, the primary goal is to entice a human viewer to click <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:37:12">[02:37:12]</a>.

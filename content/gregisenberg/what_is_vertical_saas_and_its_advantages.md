From: [[gregisenberg]] <br/> 

Vertical SaaS refers to software-as-a-service (SaaS) tailored specifically for a particular industry or niche, rather than a broad, horizontal market <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>. While often perceived as "boring," this focus creates immense opportunities for building valuable and enduring businesses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:42">[00:00:42]</a>.

Luke Sfinos, a leading expert and author of the "Vertical SaaS Bible," highlights that his own vertical SaaS business generates millions in revenue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:27">[00:00:27]</a>. He also advises Atomic, a company that builds billion-dollar businesses like Him & Hers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:30">[00:00:30]</a>.

## Why Vertical SaaS?

Historically, the initial wave of software creation was deeply tied to business needs, focusing on optimizing operations, increasing efficiency, saving costs, and boosting revenue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:50">[00:01:50]</a>. However, over the last 10-15 years, there was a shift towards chasing "sexy" multi-billion dollar, broad market opportunities, leading many founders away from industry-specific software <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:13">[00:02:13]</a>. This broad approach, while producing some "unicorn" winners, also resulted in many failures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:50">[00:02:50]</a>.

In contrast, focusing on a single industry or type of business to build tailored software still presents significant opportunity in 2024 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:13">[00:03:13]</a>. These vertical businesses, while sometimes lacking the hyper-growth rates sought by venture capitalists, can be strong, enduring, and exhibit high customer retention <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:33">[00:03:33]</a>.

## Advantages of Vertical SaaS

### 1. Identifying a Market and Industry
[[identifying_a_market_and_industry_for_vertical_saas | Identifying a market and industry for vertical SaaS]] is a science, not an art <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:32">[00:04:32]</a>. It begins not with an idea, but with selecting an industry that possesses specific characteristics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:37">[00:05:37]</a>.

*   **Market Size & Segmentation**: Look for industries with substantial operating revenue and a healthy mix of small businesses, mid-market companies (doing $10-100 million/year), and enterprise businesses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:15">[00:06:15]</a>. Top-heavy industries dominated by a few large enterprises are difficult to penetrate, especially for bootstrapped startups <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:01">[00:07:01]</a>. Machine shops, for example, are a promising industry with $240 billion in revenue and 22,000 fragmented companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:01">[00:10:01]</a>.
*   **Customer Journey Mapping**: Deeply understand the end-to-end operations of businesses within the chosen industry <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:55">[00:12:55]</a>. This involves visualizing every aspect of their operations, like how Toast mapped out every part of a restaurant's workflow in its S1 filing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:35">[00:13:35]</a>. This mapping reveals where software opportunities exist <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:11">[00:14:11]</a>.
*   **Financial Analysis**: Analyze Profit & Loss (P&L) statements of companies in the industry to understand where they spend money <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:46">[00:16:46]</a>. This can be done by examining publicly traded companies or by consulting with industry-focused bankers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:01">[00:17:01]</a>. The goal is to build software that:
    1.  Increases revenue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:34">[00:17:34]</a>
    2.  Decreases costs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:36">[00:17:36]</a>
    3.  Prevents customer churn <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:41">[00:17:41]</a>
    4.  Maintains compliance (in regulated industries) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:44">[00:17:44]</a>

### 2. Competitive Analysis
[[competitive_analysis_for_saas_development | Competitive analysis for SaaS development]] should focus on existing software solutions in the market <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:07">[00:19:07]</a>. Common scenarios include:
*   **Legacy Providers**: Old, all-encompassing ERPs (Enterprise Resource Planning) that are 30 years old <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:35">[00:19:35]</a>.
*   **Fragmented Horizontal Solutions**: Businesses stitching together generic CRM, project management, or accounting software not specifically built for their industry <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:01">[00:20:01]</a>. This indicates a strong demand for an all-in-one vertical solution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:35">[00:20:35]</a>.
*   **Paper Processes**: Despite being 2024, many industries still rely on manual, paper-based workflows <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:46">[00:20:46]</a>. These areas are ripe for digitization and automation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:55">[00:20:55]</a>.

AI tools like ChatGPT can assist in initial research for market size, segmentation, mapping operations, and identifying existing software solutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:00">[00:23:00]</a>. However, real-world validation through direct interaction with businesses is crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:30">[00:30:30]</a>.

### 3. Go-to-Market Strategy
Understanding the go-to-market strategy is critical before investing significant time in development <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:23">[00:35:23]</a>. Many vertical industries traditionally require heavy outside sales models, involving in-person meetings and relationship building, which can be slower than product-led or marketing-led approaches <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:21">[00:36:21]</a>. Venture capitalists often prefer businesses with faster customer acquisition methods <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:09">[00:37:09]</a>.

For example, a newsletter for machine shop owners with daily industry-specific memes could build a community and act as a low-cost lead generation channel <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:06">[00:39:06]</a>.

### 4. Wedge Products
A "wedge product" is a crucial [[marketing_strategies_for_saas_products | marketing strategy for SaaS products]] in vertical SaaS, serving as an easy "get-in-the-door" solution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:24">[00:40:24]</a>. Instead of starting with an "all-in-one" solution, focus on a specific, important problem that is currently being handled poorly (e.g., on paper or with bad competition) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:12">[00:41:12]</a>.

*   **Characteristics**: Easy to implement, quick to deploy, and acts as a "reputation prover" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:30">[00:41:30]</a>.
*   **Example: Roofer.com**: This company entered the roofing industry not with a full ERP, but with a proposal tool that used Google Maps to generate real-time quotes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:25">[00:42:25]</a>. This free tool spread via digital marketing (Instagram, Facebook ads), which is unusual for home services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:36">[00:43:36]</a>. Once customers were acquired, Roofer.com then launched additional products like a CRM, moving towards an all-in-one solution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:20">[00:44:20]</a>.

Wedge products build trust and make it easier to acquire existing customers for additional services than to find entirely new ones <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:57">[00:44:57]</a>.

### 5. The Role of AI and Payment Integration
[[the_role_of_ai_and_payment_integration_in_vertical_saas | The role of AI and payment integration in vertical SaaS]] is significant for scaling.
*   **AI Opportunities**: AI can be leveraged for predictive maintenance, optimized job scheduling, automated quality control, and energy management, especially in industries like machine shops that currently underutilize AI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:12">[00:31:12]</a>.
*   **Payment Integration**: Adding payments to a vertical SaaS solution is a powerful way to build massive companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:31">[00:46:31]</a>. By owning the transaction, a company's total addressable market explodes. For example, Toast, a restaurant software, leveraged payments to tap into the trillion-dollar restaurant revenue, far exceeding what a pure software subscription could offer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:38">[00:47:38]</a>. If a wedge product can be built before or after the transaction, it often leads to capturing the transaction itself <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:14">[00:48:14]</a>.

### 6. Strategies for Pricing and Monetization
[[strategies_for_pricing_and_monetization_in_vertical_saas | Strategies for pricing and monetization in vertical SaaS]] should be based on the value delivered to the customer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:50">[00:49:50]</a>. Understand the existing cost or problem your software solves <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:01">[00:50:01]</a>.

*   **ROI-Based Pricing**: If a retention tool saves a trade school five students who pay $20,000 tuition each, that's $100,000 in saved revenue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:17">[00:51:17]</a>. The software can then be priced as a portion (e.g., half) of that ROI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:53">[00:51:53]</a>.
*   **Focus on Value**: Only build products that clearly increase revenue, decrease costs, prevent churn, or help with compliance, as these are areas where quantifiable ROI can be demonstrated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:25">[00:52:25]</a>.

Vertical SaaS presents a methodical, scientific approach to building sustainable and profitable software businesses by deeply understanding and serving specific industries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:51">[00:04:51]</a>.

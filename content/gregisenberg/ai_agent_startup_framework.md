## Core Philosophy: Building for Better Services
The most successful companies in the world operate on the philosophy that "we don't make services to make money, we make money to make better services" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:00">[00:27:00]</a>. This approach, originally attributed to Walt Disney regarding movies and co-opted by Facebook, suggests that monetization should fuel product improvement rather than being the sole objective <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:17">[00:17:17]</a>. ## B2C vs. B2B Startup Strategy
Choosing between B2C and B2B depends on your risk appetite and goals:
*   **B2C (Business-to-Consumer)**:
    *   **Pros**: Often a larger opportunity with massive scaling potential <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:18">[00:16:18]</a>. Can lead to a hundred-billion-dollar company <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:41">[00:17:41]</a>.
    *   **Cons**: Extremely difficult to achieve product-market fit and growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:25">[00:16:25]</a>. Requires "catching lightning in a bottle" with much higher odds of failure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:31">[00:16:31]</a>. High dynamic range of outcomes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:45">[00:16:45]</a>.
    *   **Mindset**: Often for those who enjoy the challenge and persistence despite setbacks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:50">[00:17:50]</a>.

*   **B2B (Business-to-Business)**:
    *   **Pros**: Easier to find product-market fit by directly asking customers about their problems and building tailored solutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:52">[00:16:52]</a>. Offers a tighter range of outcomes, meaning less likelihood of complete failure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:26">[00:17:26]</a>.
    *   **Cons**: Dynamic range is not as large; less likely to create a hundred-billion-dollar company <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:55">[00:16:55]</a>.

## Choosing a Startup Idea: Regret Minimization Framework
A personal framework for choosing a startup idea is "regret minimization" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:34">[00:19:34]</a>. Imagine yourself 5 or 10 years in the future and consider all possible outcomes:
*   **Someone else succeeds and you didn't try**: You'll feel regret that it wasn't you <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:22">[00:20:22]</a>.
*   **Someone else fails and you didn't try**: You'll still wonder if you could have made it work <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:32">[00:20:32]</a>.
*   **You try and fail**: Failure is the default for big consumer swings, so there's no shame <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:44">[00:20:44]</a>.
*   **You try and succeed**: It will be great <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:07">[00:21:07]</a>.

This framework encourages pursuing ideas you deeply care about and believe are important for the world <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:10">[00:21:10]</a>. Find positive meaning in your work that excites you to work on it every morning for a long time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:59">[00:21:59]</a>.

## Pricing and Monetization for AI Startups
When pricing an [[ai_startup_success | AI startup success]], consider the value it provides:
1.  **Calculate Human Input Saved**: Quantify the total cost of human input saved, including salary and emotional damage/frustration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:52">[00:34:52]</a>.

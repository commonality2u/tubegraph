## The Blake Anderson Framework for App Distribution

A key philosophy for taking an idea and making it a mainstream product is an iterative process of generating virality from early on <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:55:08">[08:55:08]</a>. This involves:

### Iterative Testing and Positioning
*   **Generate New Hooks**: Constantly come up with new ways to position the product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:00:03">[09:00:03]</a>.
*   **Split Testing**: Test different hooks across various videos and creators <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:37:00">[09:37:00]</a>.
*   **Assess Results**: Evaluate views and conversion rates per view to inform future distribution strategies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:42:00">[09:42:00]</a>. This is akin to how platforms like Meta and TikTok split test creatives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:12:00">[10:12:00]</a>.

### [[Influencer marketing strategies for software companies | Influencer Marketing]]
A distinct approach to [[Influencer marketing strategies for software companies | influencer marketing]] is favored:
*   **Avoid Marketplaces/Databases**: These are often seen as less effective <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:44:00">[10:44:00]</a>.
*   **Develop Personal Relationships**: Focus on building genuine connections with influencers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:03:05">[11:03:05]</a>. Influencers receive hundreds of daily messages, so personal relationships help differentiate serious companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:49:00">[11:49:00]</a>.
*   **Quantity and Quality**: Early on, reach out to thousands of influencers across all platforms, even messaging their families to get in touch <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:43:00">[12:43:00]</a>.
*   **Nurture Relationships**: After initial contact, treat influencers well and become friends; this opens up endless opportunities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:06:07">[13:06:07]</a>.

### Engineering for Virality
When designing an app, prioritize features that will perform well on social media <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:20:00">[14:20:00]</a>:
*   **Product Hooks**: Split test different tools or features within the application to see what goes most viral <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:46:00">[13:46:00]</a>.
*   **UMAX Example**: The facial analysis app UMAX found virality through its six-factor Madden-like rating system, which users were highly drawn to <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:03:00">[14:03:00]</a>.
*   **Career AI Idea**: For a career AI app, one viral feature could be a resume analysis with a six-factor rating system similar to UMAX, designed around popular ed-tech content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:13:00">[16:13:00]</a>. Another could be a career analysis quiz that generates a "pretty screen" recommending a future career, leading to shareable content based on relatable student experiences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:36:00">[16:36:00]</a>.

## Monetization Strategies for Mobile Apps

When considering [[Monetization strategies for mobile apps | monetization strategies for mobile apps]], particularly with paywalls:
*   **Superwall**: An effective platform for split testing paywalls and accessing data <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:16:00">[24:16:00]</a>.
*   **Optimization Philosophy**: Instead of optimizing for (number of users * profit per user), a strategy is to optimize for (number of users ^ N, where N > 1 * profit per user) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:55:00">[24:55:00]</a>. This means accepting a lower lifetime value (LTV) per download in exchange for more people using the platform <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:09:00">[25:09:00]</a>.
*   **Market Domination & Word of Mouth**: The primary benefit of wider adoption is quicker market domination and increased word-of-mouth marketing, which is otherwise difficult to measure when constantly running ads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:38:00">[25:38:00]</a>.
*   **Paywall Placement**: The most standard and efficient practice is to guide users through an onboarding flow that includes push notifications and personal information input, then present the paywall at the end <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:37:00">[26:37:00]</a>. While this can feel morally questionable as users invest time before hitting the paywall, it is effective <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:16:00">[27:16:00]</a>.

## Competing with Incumbents

When entering a market with established players, such as Duolingo in language learning or MyFitnessPal in calorie tracking:
*   **Incumbents' Limitations**: Large, established companies (especially those that have been sold multiple times) often have engineers and social media managers whose incentives are not as aligned as a startup's <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:49:00">[30:49:00]</a>. This means they are less likely to iterate and progress as quickly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:53:00">[30:53:00]</a>.
*   **Focus on Niche Improvements & Novel Distribution**: Success comes from integrating new AI functionality faster and pioneering new ways of [[Marketing strategies for new apps | marketing]] on social media <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:16:00">[31:16:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:36:00">[31:36:00]</a>. Even a few percent changes in how people learn or interact, combined with unique [[Effective use of TikTok and social media for app marketing | distribution strategies]], can capture a significant share of a large market <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:00:00">[32:00:00]</a>.
*   **Creating New Market Share**: The goal isn't always to steal market share from existing apps, but to create new market share by reaching people who weren't using the old solutions, often through [[Utilizing TikTok Creators for app promotions | viral content creation for apps on social media]] and [[TikTok marketing strategies for consumer apps | TikTok marketing strategies for consumer apps]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:50:00">[33:50:00]</a>.

## Generating New App Ideas
*   **Assess Market Size**: Utilize tools like Sensor Tower to understand the size of other players in the space <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:42:00">[35:42:00]</a>.
*   **Deliberate Social Media Scrolling**: Curate social media feeds (e.g., using burner accounts) around specific interests or app categories to understand content consumption and user mindset <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:06:00">[36:06:00]</a>.
*   **Everyday Observations**: Many successful AI app ideas are derived from observing common, everyday activities like calorie tracking, language learning, or journaling, and then applying an AI twist to them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:15:00">[37:15:00]</a>.
*   **"AI Twist"**: The addition of "AI" to an app's name or core functionality can be valuable when innovating in a stagnant industry (e.g., Cal AI in calorie tracking) by bringing a level of sophistication <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:27:00">[20:27:00]</a>. However, it can be a negative signal if 100% accuracy is critical, as it might imply skepticism or danger <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:01:00">[21:01:00]</a>.

The overall approach emphasizes building small MVPs (Minimum Viable Products) and focusing on new styles of execution in functionality and distribution to achieve massive success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:32:00">[37:32:00]</a>.

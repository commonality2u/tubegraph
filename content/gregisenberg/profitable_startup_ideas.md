## Service Business Ideas

### Google Ads for XYZ (YouTube Top-of-Funnel)

This idea focuses on becoming an expert in [[Google Ads]] for specific niches and using YouTube as a lead generation tool <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:00">[00:47:00]</a>.

*   **Concept**
    *   Create an agency specializing in [[Google Ads]] for a specific industry or "XYZ" category, such as apartments, car dealerships, or other local businesses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:40">[00:02:40]</a>.
    *   Produce detailed YouTube tutorial videos demonstrating how to set up and manage [[Google Ads]] campaigns for these specific niches <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:00">[00:27:00]</a>.
    *   These videos should be long-form (20-30 minutes) and delve deep into the strategy, tactics, and philosophy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:59">[00:32:59]</a>.

*   **Monetization & Strategy**
    *   **Ad Revenue:** Generate ad revenue from YouTube views due to high watch time and high-value impressions for "[[Google Ads]]" related searches <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:54">[00:32:54]</a>.
    *   **Affiliate Commissions:** Upsell high-value affiliate tools like Supermetrics (a data pipeline tool for ads data) within the tutorials, earning commissions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:05">[00:33:05]</a>.
    *   **Agency Services:** The primary revenue stream is converting viewers who find the process "mind-numbing" into paying clients for your [[Google Ads]] management service <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:07">[00:41:07]</a>. Charge around $3,000 per month for unlimited [[Google Ads]] management <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:06">[00:42:06]</a>.
    *   **Ranking:** YouTube videos often rank on the front page of [[Google]] in a "snack pack" of three videos for specific longtail keywords (e.g., "[[Google Ads]] for apartments") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:21">[00:00:21]</a>.
    *   **Consistency:** Producing one video a day for 200 days can lead to $30,000 per month in revenue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:56">[00:00:56]</a>.

*   **Benefits**
    *   Acts as a powerful "one-to-many" sales funnel <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:00">[00:44:00]</a>.
    *   Builds deep trust and expertise with potential clients <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:00">[00:45:00]</a>.
    *   Forces the creator to become highly skilled in the niche, as tutorials require genuine knowledge <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:00">[00:59:00]</a>.
    *   This framework can be applied to other marketing tactics like [[Facebook Ads]] (e.g., "[[Facebook Ads]] for churches") or [[Google Maps]] (e.g., "[[Google Maps]] for truckers") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:00">[00:59:00]</a>.

*   **Concept**

    *   **Email Validation:** Validate extracted emails using tools like ZeroBounce <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:30:00">[05:30:00]</a>.
    *   **Cold Email Campaign:** Conduct large-scale cold email campaigns using software like Instantly, setting up multiple domains and inboxes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:36:00">[05:36:00]</a>. For non-English speaking countries, translate emails into the local language <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:34:00">[12:34:00]</a>. Use Loom videos within emails for higher conversion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:52:00">[12:52:00]</a>.
    *   **Social Media DMs & Cold Calling:** Reach out via Instagram DMs, Facebook DMs (for restaurants), or even cold calling <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:58:00">[14:58:00]</a>.

*   **Marketing Stack for Clients (Example: CrossFit Gyms)**
    *   Instagram Ads: Target people with specific interests (e.g., CrossFit) within a geographical area and income bracket (e.g., top 1% earners on [[Facebook]]) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:09:00">[03:09:00]</a>.
    *   Lead Magnet: Offer a free e-book download (e.g., "How to Gain Muscle Fast") to collect emails <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:28:00">[04:28:00]</a>.
    *   Email Nurture Campaign: Follow up with leads through an email sequence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:40:00">[04:40:00]</a>.
    *   Instagram Growth: Use ads to drive followers to the client's Instagram account <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:43:00">[04:43:00]</a>.

*   **Benefits**
    *   **Low Competition:** Local businesses often have unsophisticated marketing providers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:50:00">[10:50:00]</a>.
    *   **Learning Effect:** Once a strategy is figured out for one client in a niche, it can be replicated easily <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:21:00">[04:21:00]</a>.
    *   **Referrals:** Industries often have tight-knit communities where business owners know each other <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:27:00">[11:27:00]</a>.

    *   **Scalability:** With [[AI]] tools and automations, the entire stack can be standardized, allowing one person to manage many clients <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:20:00">[08:20:00]</a>.

*   **Related Concept: Rank and Rent**
    *   Create geography-optimized websites (e.g., "pool cleaning XYZ") for specific keywords <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:37:00">[18:37:00]</a>.
    *   Rank these sites high on [[Google]] and then sell the leads generated to local service providers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:19:00">[19:19:20]</a>.
    *   Examples include window cleaning or yacht rentals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:48:00">[18:48:00]</a>.

### Done-for-You Influencer Marketing (Software Focus)

This idea involves offering influencer outreach and management as a service, especially for B2SMB (Business-to-Small-and-Medium-Business) software companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:24:00">[20:24:00]</a>.

*   **Concept**
    *   Software companies, especially those building in public, struggle to create trust and teach people how to use their products at scale <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:35:00">[20:35:00]</a>.
    *   Offer unlimited influencer outreach to YouTubers to promote their software, typically with an affiliate commission model (e.g., 30% recurring) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:20:00">[21:20:00]</a>.

*   **Strategy**
    *   **Identify Software Companies:** Look for software companies (e.g., Instantly, Swell, Harpy) that are actively building and promoting their products <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:06:00">[22:06:00]</a>.
    *   **Find YouTubers:** YouTube's "About" section often provides a YouTuber's email address (behind a CAPTCHA) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:42:00">[22:42:00]</a>. Use Fiverr or similar services to scrape these emails in bulk <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:58:00">[22:58:00]</a>.
    *   **Cold Email Outreach:** Contact YouTubers with offers (e.g., $500 per video plus recurring affiliate commissions) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:18:00">[23:18:00]</a>.
    *   **Performance Analysis:** Track subscriber counts, view counts, cost, and affiliate commissions to determine ROI for clients <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:57:00">[23:57:00]</a>. Identify "underpriced" influencers and reinvest capital strategically <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:09:00">[24:09:00]</a>.
    *   **Reporting:** Provide weekly/monthly reports on influencer performance and ROI to clients <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:57:00">[25:57:00]</a>.

*   **Benefits**
    *   **High Demand:** Founders often lack the time and expertise to manage influencer marketing effectively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:29:00">[25:29:00]</a>.
    *   **Revenue Focused:** Position the agency as conversion rate or "return on ad spend" (ROAS) focused, differentiating from traditional agencies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:06:00">[26:06:00]</a>.
    *   **Growth Flywheel:** As more influencers talk about a product, up-and-coming influencers may review it for free to get affiliate commissions, creating a "digital gravity" effect <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:51:00">[24:51:00]</a>.
    *   **Inbound Leads:** Create a YouTube channel showcasing influencer marketing experiments and data (anonymized) to attract new clients <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:20:00">[27:20:00]</a>.

This concept leverages a massive existing user base and a gap in the market for specific software integrations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="40:26:00">[40:26:00]</a>.

*   **Concept**
    *   QuickBooks has 29 million users but a relatively small App Store (around 750 apps, with many having no reviews) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="44:11:00">[44:11:00]</a>.
    *   There are over 4,000 longtail keywords for "XYZ QuickBooks Integrations" (e.g., ClickUp QuickBooks integration, Builder Trend QuickBooks integration), and 40-50% of these lack existing integrations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="40:31:00">[40:31:00]</a>.
    *   The software itself is a simple data transfer (API call) between two platforms, requiring no data storage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:48:00">[41:48:00]</a>.

*   **Strategy**
    *   **Development:** Can be built relatively easily, potentially with an offshore team <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="43:03:00">[43:03:00]</a>.
    *   **Marketing:** Focus entirely on [[Google Ads]] and SEO targeting these specific longtail keywords with dedicated landing pages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="42:02:00">[42:02:00]</a>.
    *   **High Intent:** Users searching for these integrations are highly motivated because the alternative is manual data entry <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="42:22:00">[42:22:00]</a>.
    *   **Trust:** As the founder, build a public persona to establish trust, as the software handles sensitive business data <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="43:17:00">[43:17:00]</a>.
    *   **Client Acquisition:** Cold email accountants offering custom integrations, then productize and sell those custom solutions to others <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="44:41:00">[44:44:00]</a>. This provides upfront capital and allows for a "no operating capital" approach <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="45:01:00">[45:01:00]</a>.

*   **Potential:** This is estimated to be a $30 million per year company opportunity due to the massive user base, high search volume, and lack of competition <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="43:29:00">[43:29:00]</a>.

### Music Artist Development (Data-Driven)

This idea applies digital marketing strategies to help emerging music artists grow their audience and streams <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="53:35:00">[53:35:00]</a>.

*   **Concept**
    *   Treat music artists and their catalogs as digital products with unique economics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="55:36:00">[55:36:00]</a>.
    *   Music fans tend not to churn, and their engagement with an artist's "back catalog" provides intrinsic, perpetual value <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="56:32:00">[56:32:00]</a>.

*   **Strategy**
    *   **Social Media Growth:** Use Instagram ads to grow an artist's social media profile (e.g., 30-50 cents per follower) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="56:19:00">[56:19:00]</a>.
    *   **Content Production:** Record live sessions of songs, chop them into short-form content for daily social media posts across all channels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="55:50:00">[55:50:00]</a>.
    *   **Influence Streams:** Social media growth directly influences streaming numbers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="56:11:00">[56:11:00]</a>.
    *   **Monetization & Exit:** Acquire a percentage (e.g., 20%) of an artist's back catalog <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="58:06:00">[58:08:00]</a>.
    *   **Label Acquisition:** Once an artist reaches a threshold (e.g., 300,000 monthly listeners on Spotify), labels often approach them for deals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="57:25:00">[57:25:00]</a>.
    *   **Venture Portfolio:** Grow multiple artists simultaneously (e.g., 10 musicians) with a small capital injection (e.g., $1,000/month in ad spend) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="59:04:00">[59:04:00]</a>. The successful artists (e.g., 2 out of 10) can then be sold to labels or management companies, providing an exit for the investment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="59:38:00">[59:38:00]</a>.

*   **Benefits**
    *   **High ROI:** Unit economics can be highly favorable (e.g., $1 in, $3 out) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="56:25:00">[56:25:00]</a>.
    *   **Perpetual Value:** Music assets live on indefinitely, unlike many other business models <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="56:53:00">[56:53:00]</a>.
    *   **Accessibility:** Most unsigned musicians are accessible via direct messages and lack knowledge of effective growth strategies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="58:01:00">[58:01:00]</a>.

### Newsletter Consolidation (AI/Tech Space)

This involves acquiring and rolling up smaller, niche newsletters, particularly in the [[AI startup ideas]] and tech space <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:57">[01:03:57]</a>.

*   **Concept**
    *   Platforms like Beehiiv have made it easy to create newsletters, leading to a flooded market where many creators struggle with content production <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:01">[01:04:01]</a>.
    *   Acquire these smaller newsletters and consolidate their subscribers into a larger, single publication <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:13">[01:04:13]</a>.

*   **Valuation & Strategy**
    *   **Subscriber Value:** For an established company, evaluate acquisition cost per subscriber based on current CPM (cost per mille) or CPC (cost per click) and payback period <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:12">[01:06:12]</a>.
    *   **Active Newsletter Metrics:** Look for newsletters with high open rates (e.g., 50%+) and CTRs (e.g., 7-10%) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:47">[01:07:47]</a>.
    *   **Acquisition Cost:** Aim to acquire email subscribers for under $1.00 per subscriber (e.g., a 20,000-person newsletter for $15,000, or $0.75 per email) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:59">[01:07:59]</a>.
    *   **Mispriced Asset:** Acquire newsletters that monetize primarily via ads, where a subscriber is valued at $0.75 to $1.00, but integrate them into a business that has higher-value clients (e.g., an [[AI startup ideas and opportunities]] product agency where a client is worth $1-2 million) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:31">[01:09:31]</a>.
    *   **Operational Efficiency:** Consolidating multiple newsletters into one reduces the operational cost of content creation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:28">[01:08:28]</a>.
    *   **Media Leverage:** Use the acquired audience as "owned media," a long-term asset that provides marketing leverage, attracting partnerships and inbound inquiries from larger companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:58">[01:11:58]</a>.

*   **Broader Trend:** Every company is becoming a media company because it's more economically viable than traditional marketing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:01">[01:04:01]</a>. Building owned media (like a newsletter or podcast) creates long-term value that cannot be taken away and allows for powerful cross-selling into core services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:52">[01:13:52]</a>.

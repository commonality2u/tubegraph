Understanding frameworks is crucial because they apply universally across different content types and platforms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:54">[00:00:54]</a>. For example, a Twitter thread hook can be adapted for LinkedIn or a Reel <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:06">[00:01:06]</a>. Once you understand these writing frameworks and skills, you can apply them to anything, whether it's an email, an article, a Twitter thread, or a landing page <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:33">[00:01:33]</a>.

The world is rapidly moving towards [[leveraging_ai_for_creating_business_content_and_marketing | leveraging AI]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:45">[00:01:45]</a>. The notion that AI will eliminate all writing jobs misunderstands the shift <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:04">[00:02:04]</a>. Instead, AI is changing the landscape, similar to how Photoshop didn't ruin design but created exponentially more designers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:18">[00:02:18]</a>, and Instagram didn't remove photographers but made everyone a photographer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:33">[00:02:33]</a>.

ChatGPT and Claude are expected to make everyone a writer by reducing the barrier to entry <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:44">[00:02:44]</a>. Combined with the increasing demand for online content from every business and individual <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:54">[00:02:54]</a>, branded and educational content will only grow <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:30">[00:03:30]</a>. AI becomes a "stress test" for writers: those who learn to [[using_ai_tools_for_content_creation | use AI]] to double, triple, or quadruple their output will become more valuable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:01">[00:04:01]</a>. Failing to adapt means becoming less valuable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:15">[00:04:15]</a>.

## The Writing Process with AI: From Seed to Article <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:51">[00:04:51]</a>

### Starting with a Seed Idea <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:25">[00:06:25]</a>

Most people begin the writing process with a "seed of an idea," like Reed Hastings' "no Blockbuster" rule at Netflix <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:21">[00:05:21]</a>. The challenge is knowing where to take that idea <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:30">[00:06:30]</a>.

### AI as an Editor-in-Chief <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:29">[00:07:29]</a>

It's crucial to have a baseline understanding of how content should work, as AI is not a silver bullet <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:36">[00:07:36]</a>. You need to know how to prompt AI effectively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:49">[00:07:49]</a>.

There are two primary ways to interact with AI models like ChatGPT or Claude <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:00">[00:13:00]</a>:
1.  **Conversationally:** Chunking things out piece by piece in a dialogue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:09">[00:13:09]</a>.
2.  **Massive Prompt:** Assembling a comprehensive prompt that feeds into the AI model for a specific output <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:15">[00:13:15]</a>.

When starting to write, begin conversationally <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:45">[00:13:45]</a>. The goal at this stage is for AI to feed you ideas, not necessarily to start writing the piece itself <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:52">[00:12:52]</a>.

*   **Prompting for Ideas:** Plug your goals into AI (e.g., connected to startup ideas, actionable, tied to the Reed Hastings story) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:35">[00:12:35]</a>. Ask for potential headlines and 3-5 main points under each <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:42">[00:14:42]</a>.
*   **Decisions Before Writing:** Many crucial decisions are made before you start writing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:19">[00:15:19]</a>. If you immediately open a blank document, you've already made a mistake <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:32">[00:15:32]</a>. By making these decisions beforehand, writing becomes significantly easier, and AI can handle lower-leverage tasks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:47">[00:15:47]</a>.

### Selecting a Headline and Adapting to Different Mediums <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:03">[00:16:03]</a>

AI can generate numerous iterations of headlines, allowing you to elevate yourself to an "editor-in-chief" role, where your primary value is your taste <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:30">[00:16:30]</a>. You choose the headline that best aligns with your goals (e.g., "Why Reed Hastings Banned the Word Blockbuster" due to its powerful, curiosity-invoking nature) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:17">[00:16:17]</a>.

*   **[[generating_content_with_ai_tools | Stealing Architecture, Not Content]]:** When adapting to different mediums (like a Twitter thread), you can use an existing successful hook as a template <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:08">[00:20:08]</a>. This isn't stealing content, but rather the architecture or "bones of the house" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:29">[00:20:29]</a>.
*   **Training a Mini Model:** By taking an original hook and manually creating a new version based on your chosen idea, you create a "mini model" that AI can learn from <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:16">[00:24:16]</a>. You can then prompt AI to generate multiple variations of this tailored hook, leveraging its ability to learn from examples <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:36">[00:25:36]</a>. This collaborative process allows AI to produce even better outputs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:39">[00:26:39]</a>.

### Structuring an 800-Word Article <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:23">[00:28:23]</a>

For a typical 800-word article, the easiest container is to think in chunks of three: an introduction followed by three main sections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:41">[00:28:41]</a>.

*   **The Power of Main Points:** 90% of a piece's value lies in its headline and the quality of its three main points <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:48">[00:29:48]</a>. If the main points are vague, readers will immediately dismiss the content as worthless <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:29">[00:30:29]</a>. Specific, tangible main points increase the likelihood of engaging the reader <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:58">[00:31:58]</a>.
*   **Making the Promise:** The headline promises something worthwhile, and the main points must tangibly deliver on that promise <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:16">[00:32:16]</a>.

### The "10 Magical Ways" Framework <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:01">[00:35:01]</a>

This framework is a powerful tool to eliminate writer's block at every stage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:23">[00:35:23]</a>. It helps clarify what you will give the reader (e.g., tips, stats, steps, lessons) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:54">[00:35:54]</a>.

*   **Headline and Subhead Alignment:** If your headline promises "three lessons," your subheads should clearly state "Lesson One," "Lesson Two," and "Lesson Three" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:16">[00:38:16]</a>. The subheads must directly deliver on the promise in the headline <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:33">[00:38:33]</a>.
*   **The Highest Leverage Decision:** The quality of the three lessons (or main points) themselves is 90% of the value, not how beautifully they are written <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:53">[00:38:53]</a>.
*   **Collaborating with AI:** Try to come up with the lessons yourself first, then use AI to stress-test and improve them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:51">[00:39:51]</a>. This allows you to write *with* AI rather than deferring the writing entirely to it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:05">[00:40:05]</a>.
*   **Refining Main Points:** AI can provide many options, but your human taste and context are essential for selection (e.g., avoiding potentially misleading phrases like "me too trap") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:37">[00:43:37]</a>. Subheads should be clear, tangible sentences that immediately convey value, rather than vague or clever titles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:17">[00:44:17]</a>. Continue using conversational prompts to refine AI output for tangibility and actionability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:11">[00:45:11]</a>.

### Completing the Writing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:57">[00:46:57]</a>

Once the three main points are finalized, revisit the "10 Magical Ways" framework for each section <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:02">[00:47:02]</a>. Decide what type of content to include in each (e.g., steps for an experiment, stats and examples for a differentiator, reasons why for an explanation) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:13">[00:47:13]</a>.

All major decisions are made *before* writing a single full paragraph <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:38">[00:48:38]</a>. This structured approach makes the actual writing process much easier. You can then articulate these detailed decisions to AI to generate a first draft and continue refining it through iterative prompts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:50">[00:48:50]</a>.

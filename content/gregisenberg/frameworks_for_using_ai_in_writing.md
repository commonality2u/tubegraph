## Foundational Frameworks for Writing with AI

### 1. Idea Categorization: Main Idea vs. Sub-Point
When starting with a seed of an idea, the first decision is whether it will be the main theme of the piece or a sub-point within a larger idea <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:12">[00:08:12]</a>. For example, a specific anecdote can either be the core topic or one of three examples in a broader article <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:00">[00:09:00]</a>.

### 2. Defining Content Goals
Before writing, it's crucial to summarize your goals for the piece <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:46">[00:10:46]</a>. This strategic planning, though simple, is often overlooked by writers who rely on intuition <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:39">[00:11:39]</a>. Most people lack a talent problem but rather avoid practicing the basics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:52">[00:11:52]</a>.

### 3. Interacting with AI: Conversational vs. Massive Prompt
There are two main ways to interact with [[ai_in_writing_and_content_creation | AI models]] like ChatGPT or Claude <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:00">[00:13:00]</a>:
1.  **Conversationally:** Chunking things out piece by piece in a dialogue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:09">[00:13:09]</a>. This approach is recommended for starting a new piece <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:51">[00:13:51]</a>.
2.  **Massive Prompt:** Assembling a comprehensive prompt that includes all potential situations and hypotheticals for a single output <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:15">[00:13:15]</a>.

Avoid "half-baked" prompts that are somewhere in the middle, as they lead to unsatisfactory outputs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:33">[00:13:33]</a>.

### 4. Ideation with AI: Headlines and Main Points
To narrow down an idea, plug your content goals into AI and ask it to generate potential headlines and 3-5 main points for each <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:35">[00:12:35]</a>. This defers the lower-leverage task of generating variations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:30">[00:16:30]</a>.

This process mirrors how editors give prompts to writers in traditional publishing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:47">[00:16:47]</a>. By evaluating the AI's output, you act as the editor-in-chief, applying your taste and metrics for effectiveness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:29">[00:17:29]</a>.

### 5. Template-Based Hook Creation
Using a viral hook as a template allows you to "steal the architecture" (the bones of the house), not the content itself <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:29">[00:20:29]</a>. This process of adapting existing successful formats, which humans often do subconsciously as "intuition," is made conscious and intentional <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:01">[00:21:01]</a>.

You can create a "mini-model" for the AI by providing an original hook and then a manually created new version based on your topic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:24">[00:24:24]</a>. This trains the AI to generate further variations that align with your desired style <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:36">[00:25:36]</a>.

### 6. Structuring Long-Form Content
For an 800-word article, the easiest container is typically an intro followed by three main sections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:41">[00:28:41]</a>.

The majority of a piece's value (around 90%) is derived from the headline and the quality of the three main points <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:43">[00:29:43]</a>. Main points should be specific and tangible, making the reader feel compelled to read the full content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:58">[00:31:58]</a>. Failing to have clear, valuable main points often leads to readers abandoning the content within seconds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:46">[00:30:46]</a>.

### 7. The 10 Magical Ways Framework
This framework helps remove writer's block by providing 10 categories for content delivery <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:07">[00:35:07]</a>. These include tips, stats, steps, lessons, and reasons why <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:01">[00:36:01]</a>.

To make a headline more tangible, ask which of these 10 things you will actually give the reader <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:54">[00:35:54]</a>. Big numbers in headlines and hooks often perform well <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:37">[00:36:37]</a>.

### 8. Aligning Headline and Subheads
The specific "magical way" used in the headline should be consistently delivered in the subheads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:33">[00:38:33]</a>. If the headline promises "three lessons," the subheads should clearly state "Lesson One," "Lesson Two," and "Lesson Three" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:16">[00:38:16]</a>. The quality of these lessons is paramount; beautiful writing cannot compensate for weak core points <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:02">[00:39:02]</a>.

Always try to generate the lessons/main points yourself first, then compare them against AI-generated options for improvement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:51">[00:39:51]</a>. Stack-rank your main points, leading with the most interesting or compelling one to hook the reader <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:29">[00:41:29]</a>.

### 9. Iterative Refinement with AI
Engage in a conversational back-and-forth with AI, providing feedback to refine its output <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:51">[00:44:51]</a>. For example, if AI provides vague subheads, instruct it to rewrite them in sentence format and be more tangible and actionable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:11">[00:45:11]</a>.

The benefit of this iterative process is that you create "Lego blocks" of refined content and learn what prompts yield the best results, ultimately enabling you to compress the entire process into a single, more effective "mega prompt" for future use <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:43">[00:45:43]</a>.

### 10. Completing the Content Sections
Once the three main points are finalized, revisit the "10 Magical Ways" framework to decide what type of content to include within each section <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:02">[00:47:02]</a>. For example, if a lesson is about running an experiment, the section might contain steps; if it's about product differentiation, it might include stats or examples <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:27">[00:47:27]</a>.

By making these decisions before writing a single full paragraph, the actual writing process becomes significantly easier, as you simply fill in the pre-defined structure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:32">[00:48:32]</a>.

## The Value of Education in the Age of AI
Paying for education, whether through courses or books, is powerful because it allows you to download specific frameworks and strategies into your own "repository" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:24">[00:49:24]</a>. These internalized frameworks can then be leveraged with technology to increase productivity, efficiency, and quality of output exponentially <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:05">[00:50:05]</a>.

For instance, learning six proven single-sentence openers for writing, which might have taken an expert 12 years to discover, can be internalized in minutes and then applied with AI for the rest of your life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:14">[00:52:14]</a>. This rapid leveling up through education is critical for gaining digital leverage and becoming an expert in the AI era <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:47">[00:51:47]</a>.

However, once these [[frameworks_for_developing_and_testing_business_ideas | frameworks]] are understood, they can be applied to "everything" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:37">[00:01:37]</a>.

## The "Editor-in-Chief" Mindset

When working with AI, it's crucial to adopt an "Editor-in-Chief" mindset <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:21">[00:17:21]</a>. People often expect AI to automate tasks they don't understand how to do themselves <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:29">[00:07:29]</a>. However, AI is not a "silver bullet"; a baseline understanding of how the desired outcome is achieved is necessary to prompt AI effectively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:43">[00:07:43]</a>.

The primary value you bring is "taste" and context <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:29">[00:17:29]</a>. You defer lower-leverage tasks to AI, such as generating multiple iterations of headlines or main points <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:30">[00:16:30]</a>. This mirrors the process in publishing houses where editors give prompts to writers, who then generate multiple ideas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:47">[00:16:47]</a>.

## Starting the Writing Process

The writing process often begins with a "seed of an idea" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:25">[00:06:25]</a>. The first decision is to determine if this idea is the main theme of the piece or a sub-point within a larger idea <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:12">[00:08:12]</a>. For example, Reed Hastings' "strange rule" at Netflix could be the main idea or one of three strange rules from different CEOs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:00">[00:09:00]</a>.

Before writing, establish clear goals for the content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:46">[00:10:46]</a>. For instance, goals for an article on Reed Hastings might include:
*   Connecting to [[startup_ideas_and_brainstorming | startup ideas]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:55">[00:10:55]</a>.
*   Being actionable, providing "how-to" guidance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:04">[00:11:04]</a>.
*   Retaining connection to the Reed Hastings story <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:10">[00:11:10]</a>.

### Leveraging AI for Ideation

To generate [[importance_of_content_ideation_and_packaging | ideas]] and narrow down the topic, plug your goals and seed idea into an AI model like ChatGPT or Claude <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:30">[00:12:30]</a>.

There are two main ways to interact with AI models:
1.  **Conversationally**: Chunking things out piece by piece in a dialogue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:09">[00:13:09]</a>.
2.  **Massive Prompt**: Assembling a comprehensive prompt with all potential situations and hypotheticals to generate a specific output <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:15">[00:13:15]</a>.

It's best to start conversationally <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:51">[00:13:51]</a>. For example, provide the seed idea and goals, then ask the AI to generate 20 potential headlines with three to five main points for each <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:42">[00:14:42]</a>. The AI feeds you ideas, allowing you to make decisions *before* you start writing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:27">[00:15:27]</a>.

### The Importance of Structure

For an 800-word article, the easiest structure is typically an introduction followed by three main sections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:41">[00:28:41]</a>.

The "90% of the value" of a piece comes from its headline and the quality of its three main points <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:39">[00:29:39]</a>. If the main points are too vague (e.g., "learn about money," "hire a CPA," "don't be dumb"), readers will quickly dismiss the content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:15">[00:30:15]</a>. Main points should be specific and tangible, showing the reader exactly how the promise in the headline will be delivered <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:06">[00:31:06]</a>.

### The 10 Magical Ways Framework

This [[frameworks_for_developing_and_testing_business_ideas | framework]] helps remove writer's block at every stage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:07">[00:35:07]</a>. It suggests classifying content into 10 fundamental types (e.g., tips, stats, steps, lessons) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:15">[00:35:15]</a>.

To use it:
1.  **Refine the Headline**: Make the headline more tangible by specifying what you're giving the reader, e.g., "Why Reed Hastings Banned the Word Blockbuster and Three Lessons That Can Be Learned from Netflix's $361 Billion Streaming Service" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:01">[00:36:01]</a>.
2.  **Align Subheads with the Headline**: If the headline promises "three lessons," the three main subheads should clearly state those lessons <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:04">[00:38:04]</a>. The highest leverage decision is deciding *what* those three lessons are <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:53">[00:38:53]</a>. Avoid vague or "clever" subheads; instead, use sentence format that provides immediate value <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:17">[00:44:17]</a>.
3.  **Specify Content for Each Section**: For each main point/lesson, decide which of the "10 magical ways" you will use to deliver the content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:08">[00:47:08]</a>. For example, if a lesson is about "running a band-words experiment," you might provide the "steps" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:20">[00:47:20]</a>. If another lesson is about "convenience as a product differentiator," you might use "stats" or "examples" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:39">[00:47:39]</a>.

By making these decisions before writing a single full paragraph, the actual writing process becomes significantly easier <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:38">[00:48:38]</a>.

### Iterative Work with AI

Working with AI is often a "conversational" back-and-forth process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:51">[00:39:51]</a>. You try to generate ideas yourself, then weigh them against AI's suggestions, and improve the quality together <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:05">[00:40:05]</a>.

You can train the AI by providing examples of what works. For instance, give it a viral hook you wrote, then provide your new version based on your chosen headline, and ask it to generate more variations based on your example <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:06">[00:25:06]</a>. This creates a "mini-model" that the AI can learn from <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:24">[00:24:24]</a>. This iterative process helps you learn what yields the best output <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:05">[00:46:05]</a>, eventually allowing you to compress all these steps into a single "mega prompt" for future use <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:16">[00:46:16]</a>.

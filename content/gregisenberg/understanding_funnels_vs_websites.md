Funnels and websites serve fundamentally different purposes in a business's marketing and sales strategy. While often confused, they are distinct entities with different goals, design principles, and effectiveness for generating revenue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:07:10">[03:07:10]</a>.

## What is a Funnel?

A funnel is a step-by-step process designed to turn a complete stranger or non-customer into a paying customer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:13:15">[03:13:15]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:27:29">[03:27:29]</a>. Its entire goal is to achieve one specific action, such as booking a call or making a purchase <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:47:04">[09:47:04]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:56:56">[09:56:56]</a>.

Funnels are:
*   **Super focused:** They lead the user through a single, directed path <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:41:04">[09:41:04]</a>.
*   **Revenue-driven:** Their primary purpose is to generate leads, call bookings, or sales <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:22:24">[05:22:24]</a>.
*   **Separate from websites:** They exist on completely separate URLs and are managed by different teams and concepts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:58:00">[04:58:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:26:00">[09:26:00]</a>. This often means they have different branding or design than the main website <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:31:48">[09:31:48]</a>.
*   **Conversion-optimized:** Funnels can achieve significantly higher conversion rates, often ranging from 10% to 25%, compared to websites which typically convert 1% to 3% of visitors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:36:58">[11:36:58]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:03:06">[10:03:06]</a>.

## What is a Website?

A website acts more like a brochure, showcasing all the different aspects of a company <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:34:04">[03:34:04]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:39:00">[03:39:00]</a>. It's a place for browsing, for visitors to get a general feel for a company, but it doesn't typically provide a focused path for conversion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:00:00">[04:00:00]</a>.

Websites are generally not designed to directly drive leads or create customers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:40:00">[05:40:00]</a>. Unless a business has strong SEO, significant Google Ads spend, or high brand recognition, a website alone is unlikely to sell products or services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:52:00">[05:52:00]</a>. People often visit a website later, after a recommendation or initial discovery, primarily to verify if a company is legitimate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:18:00">[06:18:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:24:00">[06:24:00]</a>. The speaker notes that their company did not even have a "nice" website for the first 12-14 years of its operation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:58:00">[06:58:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:05:00">[07:05:00]</a>.

The "funnel world" sometimes refers to websites as "shadow funnels," existing primarily to prove a company's existence and offer a general home for its offerings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:31:00">[04:31:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:43:00">[04:43:00]</a>. Crucially, funnels are kept completely separate from the main website; you cannot typically access funnels from the website, nor vice-versa <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:50:00">[04:50:00]</a>.

                                                                                                                                # Understanding Funnels vs. Websites

While often conflated, funnels and websites serve distinct purposes in a business's marketing and sales strategy. Jonathan Courtney clarifies that they operate under different principles and should be managed as separate entities to maximize effectiveness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:07:10">[03:07:10]</a>.

## What is a Funnel?

A funnel is a precisely engineered, step-by-step process designed to convert a complete stranger or non-customer into a paying customer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:13:15">[03:13:15]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:27:29">[03:27:29]</a>. Its core objective is to achieve a single, specific action, such as booking a call or making a purchase <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:47:04">[09:47:04]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:56:56">[09:56:56]</a>.

Key characteristics of funnels:
*   **Focused Path:** Users are guided through a highly focused sequence of pages designed for one specific outcome <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:41:04">[09:41:04]</a>.
*   **Revenue-Driven:** Their primary function is to generate leads, schedule calls, or drive direct sales <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:22:24">[05:22:24]</a>.
*   **Separate Entities:** Funnels reside on entirely separate URLs from the main website and are often developed and managed by dedicated teams with different objectives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:58:00">[04:58:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:26:00">[09:26:00]</a>. This separation often results in distinct branding or design aesthetics compared to the main website <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:31:48">[09:31:48]</a>.
*   **High Conversion Rates:** Funnels consistently achieve significantly higher conversion rates, typically ranging from 10% to 25% or more, due to their singular focus and optimized flow <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:36:58">[11:36:58]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:03:06">[10:03:06]</a>.

## What is a Website?

A website functions more like a comprehensive brochure, presenting all aspects of a company to a broad audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:34:04">[03:34:04]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:39:00">[03:39:00]</a>. It serves as a place for browsing and getting a general impression of a company, but it lacks a focused path for direct conversion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:00:00">[04:00:00]</a>.

Websites are generally ineffective for directly driving leads or acquiring customers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:40:00">[05:40:00]</a>. Without strong SEO, substantial Google Ads investment, or established brand recognition, a website alone rarely generates sales <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:52:00">[05:52:00]</a>. Visitors typically consult a website later, often after an initial recommendation or discovery, primarily to validate a company's legitimacy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:18:00">[06:18:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:24:00">[06:24:00]</a>. Jonathan Courtney highlights that his company operated for 12-14 years without a "nice" website <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:58:00">[06:58:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:05:00">[07:05:00]</a>.

In the "funnel world," websites are sometimes called "shadow funnels." They exist primarily to confirm a company's reality and provide a general information hub <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:31:00">[04:31:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:43:00">[04:43:00]</a>. A critical distinction is that funnels are entirely separate from the main website; they cannot be accessed from the website, nor can the website be reached from the funnels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:50:00">[04:50:00]</a>.

### 1. Video Sales Letter (VSL) Funnel
This type of funnel is commonly used for selling high-ticket items like training or coaching <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:41:00">[08:41:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:02:00">[13:02:00]</a>.
The structure includes:
1.  **Traffic Generation:** Starting with ads (Facebook/Meta or YouTube) or content (YouTube videos) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:38:00">[09:38:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:53:00">[17:53:00]</a>.
2.  **Super Focused Landing Page:** Leads to a page packed with persuasive copy, aimed at driving one action <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:34:00">[19:34:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:03:00">[20:03:00]</a>.
3.  **Free Training (VSL):** An "obnoxiously long" video (e.g., 1 hour 10 minutes) that provides valuable information and demonstrates expertise <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:14:00">[21:14:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:47:00">[21:47:00]</a>. This aims to warm up the audience, showing them what it's like to be trained by the company <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:17:00">[21:17:00]</a>. A call-to-action button appears after a certain watch time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:16:00">[22:16:00]</a>.
4.  **Application/Survey (Optional Friction):** A survey (e.g., Typeform) can be added to increase friction when lead flow is high, filtering out less committed prospects <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:33:00">[34:33:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:52:00">[34:52:00]</a>. Questions assess commitment and budget <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:26:00">[35:26:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:53:00">[35:53:00]</a>.
5.  **Call Booking:** Prospects book a call directly, typically within three days, to ensure interest remains high <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:47:00">[36:47:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:01:00">[37:01:00]</a>.
6.  **Email Drip:** If email addresses are collected, an email sequence can deliver the video content over time, continuing the call-to-action <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:31:00">[24:31:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:38:00">[24:38:00]</a>.

This funnel doesn't just sell individual courses; it also generates high-value corporate leads, such as a recent 300K training session booking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="38:39:00">[38:39:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="39:04:00">[39:04:00]</a>.

### 2. Lead Generation Funnel / Lead Magnet

### 3. Book Funnel
This highly effective strategy leverages the perceived value of a physical book.

Examples of MIFGE include:
*   Offering additional licenses for a team after closing a large deal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="57:40:00">[57:40:00]</a>.
*   Providing printed versions of workshop materials as a free gift <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="57:50:00">[57:50:00]</a>.

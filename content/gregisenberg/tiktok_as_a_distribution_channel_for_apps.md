## Strategies for [[viral_marketing_for_mobile_apps | Viral App Growth]]

Many successful tactics for promoting consumer apps on TikTok are adapted from the e-commerce space, especially since TikTok Shop launched in November 2022 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:28:00">[02:28:00]</a>.

### Leveraging TikTok Shop Affiliate Data

A unique opportunity exists by analyzing data from the TikTok Shop Affiliate Center <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:17:00">[11:17:00]</a>. This platform provides insights into high-volume product sales and the creators selling them, allowing identification of top-performing TikTok Shop affiliates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:25:00">[11:25:00]</a>. The idea is to approach these creators, who are incentivized by e-commerce companies to make videos, and encourage them to promote apps instead of just physical products <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:25:00">[12:25:00]</a>. This is considered a "gold mine" because these creators excel at producing content that gains organic views and converts to sales <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:04:00">[19:04:00]</a>.

It is even possible to build an app that helps these creators sell the products they are already promoting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:16:00">[19:16:00]</a>. For instance, a skincare app could analyze a user's face and suggest products, or it could be used by an influencer to show before-and-after results using an e-commerce product, integrating the app as a utility tool without being overly promotional <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:28:00">[19:28:00]</a>. Such apps could potentially be used for free by influencers if they help them create content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:29:00">[20:29:00]</a>. This approach is applicable across numerous niches like skincare, makeup, fashion, electronics, sports, home improvement, and collectibles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:38:00">[20:38:00]</a>.

### Content Formats

While many associate TikTok with short-form videos, carousels (slideshows) are highly effective <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:21:00">[07:21:00]</a>. They are easier to create (e.g., using AI like Midjourney for images) compared to videos <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:41:00">[07:41:00]</a>. Although slideshows may have lower conversion rates due to less "human feeling" compared to talking-head videos, they offer significantly higher scalability and lower barriers to entry <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:02:00">[08:02:00]</a>.

Examples of effective content:
*   **Problem/Solution Narrative:** Videos starting with a "POV" (point of view) hook that presents a common pain point, then introduces a product or app as the solution. Example: a colostrum supplement using an AI-generated image and a Joe Rogan audio clip to address "energy brain fog metabolism" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:07:00">[04:07:00]</a>.
*   **Controversial Hooks:** Apps like the POV app (disposable camera app) use strong negative hooks, such as "Our guest hated the controversial rules we enforce at our wedding," leading into a slideshow that promotes the app as a solution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:40:00">[05:40:00]</a>. They also found that bizarre first images, like a "cake on the face," inexplicably get views <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:40:00">[06:40:00]</a>.
*   **Product Demos/Utility Showcase:** Apps like "What Colors" (a color analysis app) use simple slideshows to showcase how their AI analyzes facial features to recommend clothing and makeup colors, with a call to action to download the app <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:00:00">[14:00:00]</a>.
*   **Visual Analysis Tools:** Apps like KO (an MMA training app) use a wireframe skeleton overlay to analyze movements, creating a "wow factor" on TikTok <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:06:00">[26:06:00]</a>. This concept can be applied to other sports like tennis, golf, or pickleball <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:38:00">[26:38:00]</a>, potentially using advanced AI vision like GPT Vision with an iPhone camera <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:25:00">[28:25:00]</a>.

Once a content format works, it's beneficial to "spam it every day" to leverage the algorithm's understanding of the target audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:08:00">[07:08:00]</a>. Using multiple brand accounts with a consistent creator/face for each format can help the algorithm serve content to the right audience without confusion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:17:00">[36:17:00]</a>.

### Monetization Models

Many successful single-utility apps use a paywall model, where users onboard, answer questions, and then encounter a weekly or annual subscription <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:05:00">[15:05:00]</a>. Often, the app has users complete a step (e.g., scanning their face) before the paywall to increase commitment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:23:00">[15:23:00]</a>. The ease of iOS subscriptions through features like Apple Pay ("double tap to pay") has significantly boosted app revenue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:30:00">[15:30:00]</a>. Digital products also benefit from lower price points (e.g., $5 for an app subscription vs. $100 for a physical product) which can lead to higher conversion volumes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:16:00">[21:16:00]</a>, and a cost of goods near zero, making them infinitely scalable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:54:00">[21:54:00]</a>.

## Finding [[opportunities_in_niche_mobile_app_markets | Untapped Opportunities]]

### Target Niches with High Lifetime Value (LTV)

While TikTok's user base is predominantly young, there are diverse demographics present <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:24:00">[22:24:00]</a>. Targeting niches with fewer creators allows content to be "underpriced" and reach audiences willing to pay more <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:41:00">[22:41:00]</a>.

*   **Golfers:** A high-LTV niche <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:57:00">[22:57:00]</a>. Apps offering AI golf swing analysis (angle, speed, tips) could serve as a more convenient and affordable alternative to in-person coaching <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:24:00">[23:24:00]</a>. One golf swing app is reportedly making $70,000 a month from 10,000 downloads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:26:00">[24:26:00]</a>.
*   **Poker Players:** Another high-LTV niche with under-monetized content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:08:00">[29:08:00]</a>. An app could help players analyze hand history, strategies, or provide educational tips for beginners, subtly integrated into popular poker content videos <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:47:00">[29:47:00]</a>.

### Capitalizing on "Cult Movements"

Identifying "movements" or ideologies that don't yet have a dedicated product attached to them can lead to significant business opportunities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="43:23:00">[43:23:00]</a>. These movements often revolve around universal human desires: health, wealth, and relationships <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="43:55:00">[43:55:00]</a>.

*   **Looksmaxing (Relationships/Self-Improvement):** The "looksmaxing" movement, focused on improving physical appearance, inspired the Umax app. This app scans a user's face, provides scores and analysis, and offers tips on improving features like jawline, skin, and hair <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:08:00">[41:08:00]</a>. This movement has deep, ideological roots among young men <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="42:35:00">[42:35:00]</a>.
*   **Making Money Online (Wealth):** The "cult of young money" movement, where teenagers seek to make money via TikTok and YouTube shorts, led to the Crayo app <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="46:04:00">[46:04:00]</a>. Instead of selling a course, Crayo productizes the video creation process, allowing users to automate content production <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="44:36:00">[44:36:00]</a>.

## Influencer Marketing on TikTok

Traditional influencer marketing, which relies on paying influencers with large follower counts (the "Instagram era" approach), is less effective on TikTok <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:13:00">[33:13:00]</a>. TikTok's "For You Page" algorithm prioritizes content over creator following, meaning 95% of content on a user's feed often comes from new accounts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:47:00">[33:47:00]</a>.

For TikTok, creators should be viewed as actors (talent in the video), directors (creating the video), and having a built-in audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:10:00">[34:10:00]</a>. The "director" role is crucial; too much creative freedom might lead to influencers not selling the product (as they dislike "shilling"), while too much prescription makes the video feel like an ad and reduces views <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:42:00">[34:42:00]</a>.

The optimal approach is to hire creators primarily as "actors," focusing on their ability to make good videos, regardless of their follower count <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:41:00">[35:41:00]</a>. They can create new brand-specific accounts, like Tabs Chocolate and Airbuds have done, ensuring the algorithm targets content to the right audience by maintaining a consistent face and format on each account <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:17:00">[36:17:00]</a>. This makes [[paid_advertising_strategies_for_app_growth | influencer marketing]] on TikTok still "underpriced" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:10:00">[37:10:00]</a>.

## Key Considerations for [[strategies_for_successful_consumer_app_growth | App Growth]]

Before scaling [[marketing_and_selling_apps | app marketing]] efforts, the product itself must be high quality and retain users <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="46:54:00">[46:54:00]</a>. Understanding metrics like conversions, retention, Lifetime Value (LTV), and Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) is crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="47:47:00">[47:47:00]</a>. Without a retentive product, spending heavily on marketing creates a "leaky bucket," making the app function more like a one-time e-commerce sale rather than a scalable, subscription-based business <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="48:09:00">[48:09:00]</a>.

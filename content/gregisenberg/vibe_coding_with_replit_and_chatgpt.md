## Finding Opportunities on Upwork

Upwork serves as an effective platform for identifying business problems and potential software-as-a-service (SaaS) ideas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:43">[00:00:43]</a>. The core strategy is to find someone willing to buy an app *before* it's built, which is often easier than building first and then seeking users <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:24">[00:02:24]</a>.

### Identifying Pain Points
*   **Replacing Existing Technologies:** Look for jobs that involve specific technologies (e.g., AirTable) and consider if a custom app could perform the function better and cheaper <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:49">[00:02:49]</a>. For instance, AirTable's per-seat pricing can be high ($20 a seat) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:10">[00:03:10]</a>.
*   **Automations:** Many automation tasks can be converted into a front-end application <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:36">[00:09:36]</a>.
*   **Cobbling Together Solutions:** Look for clients trying to integrate multiple services (e.g., Google Forms, AirTable, Typeform, Calendly, Zapier, SMS) and offer a unified app <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:54">[00:09:54]</a>.

### Pricing Considerations
*   **Lowball Offers:** Some Upwork listings might have low fixed prices (e.g., $125 for a case management system) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:35">[00:06:35]</a>.
*   **Submitting Proposals:** It's possible to submit a proposal for a higher price than what's listed and hope the client accepts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:03">[00:07:03]</a>.
*   **Upwork Connects:** Upwork requires "connects" (credits) to apply for jobs, and these can be used to "boost" a proposal to the top of the client's inbox <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:13">[00:07:13]</a>.
*   **Realistic Pricing:** Initial projects might be priced lower (e.g., $750 for a first app) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:40">[00:07:40]</a>, but larger projects can be valued higher (e.g., $2500) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:05">[00:10:05]</a>.

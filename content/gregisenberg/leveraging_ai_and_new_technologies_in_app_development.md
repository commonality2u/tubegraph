## Current Opportunities and Examples

Several successful apps demonstrate how [[leveraging_ai_and_automation_in_app_development | AI and new technologies]] can be integrated:

### Cali AI

*   **Nice Packaging**: Presenting simple tools in an appealing way <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:03">[00:03:03]</a>.
*   **Good Onboarding Experience**: Creating user investment from the start <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:05">[00:03:05]</a>.
*   **Value Perception**: Making the app seem more valuable than its core functionality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:30">[00:03:30]</a>.

*   **Live Activities**: A "super interesting piece" that few people are fully utilizing. This allows apps to display real-time updates on the lock screen (e.g., Uber's status) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:13">[00:12:13]</a>.
*   **Lock Screen Widgets**: Despite being frequently seen, these widgets have not been fully explored for social experiences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:40">[00:12:40]</a>.

## Hunter Isacson's Playbook for Building a Million-Dollar App Business

Hunter Isacson outlines a clear process for [[building_and_deploying_apps_with_ai_integration | building and deploying successful apps]]:

1.  **Build a Simple Experience (V1 MVP)**:
    *   Focus on core features that solve the problem, even if rough around the edges <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:18">[00:18:18]</a>.
    *   The goal is to create a "bare bones MVP" that is good enough <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:25">[00:18:25]</a>.

2.  **Get Users on Early**:
    *   Start with a small group of users (hundreds or thousands) as soon as the core concept and MVP are built <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:43">[00:18:43]</a>.
    *   **Test content**: Even in the pre-order stage, test content on platforms like TikTok or Instagram Reels to see user response <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:59">[00:18:59]</a>.

3.  **Select a Core Action (Northstar Metric)**:
    *   Choose one primary metric that matters more than others <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:30">[00:19:30]</a>. This simplifies decision-making and tradeoffs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:41">[00:19:41]</a>.
    *   *Examples*:
        *   **Wink**: Percentage of users getting a match on their first day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:00">[00:20:00]</a>.
        *   **NGL**: Percentage of users sharing the link and a few replies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:26">[00:20:26]</a>.
        *   **Bags**: Percentage of users funding accounts and making a trade on the first day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:03">[00:21:03]</a>.
    *   Aim for over 90% of users to complete this core action <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:00">[00:26:00]</a>.
    *   Design a single user flow for everyone to easily track success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:14">[00:26:14]</a>.

4.  **Continuously Seed and Scale**:
    *   Once the core action conversion is proven, apply pressure by seeding the app across different audiences, niches, countries, and time zones <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:42">[00:27:01]</a>.
    *   **Incorporate Sharing/Inviting**: Integrate a sharing or inviting mechanism directly into the core action <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:23">[00:27:23]</a>. This creates a powerful growth loop, increasing the app's K-factor and reducing reliance on external marketing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:08">[00:28:08]</a>.
        *   *Examples*: NGL's link sharing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:34">[00:28:34]</a>, Bags' Twitter sharing and leaderboard <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:37">[00:29:37]</a>.
    *   **Paid vs. Organic**: Seeding can involve small payments to influencers for posts, but the goal is to find scalable methods that work on a small scale, aiming for viral traction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:36">[00:31:11]</a>.

5.  **Build a Brand People Love**:
    *   Create simple, easily recognizable names that resonate with the target audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:35">[00:21:35]</a>.
    *   Use slang or terms from the community to establish a comfortable, colloquial, and relatable brand tone <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:40">[00:23:40]</a>.
    *   **Authenticity**: Maintain an authentic public voice, being memeable, funny, and relatable, rather than a static brand <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:41">[00:32:09]</a>. This increases affinity and trust, encouraging sharing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:50">[00:32:50]</a>.

> [!NOTE] Design Process: Hunter Isacson personally designs all his apps, including branding, using tools like Figma <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:57">[00:24:57]</a>.

## Future Trends in Technology for App Development

Isacson identifies key technological trends poised to impact app development:

*   **Crypto Going Mainstream**: Increased regulation and broader acceptance are making the development of consumer crypto apps exciting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:54">[00:37:05]</a>.
*   **Augmented Reality (AR) Glasses**: Anticipated to be the next major hardware platform, offering immersive experiences where users can prompt AI and create sharable, detailed content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:15">[00:38:01]</a>.
*   **Local AI Models on Phones**: While currently cloud-hosted, there's potential for AI models to run locally on phones, which would act as the primary compute for AR glasses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:48">[00:39:07]</a>.

## Advice for Aspiring Developers

For young individuals looking to enter tech and build successful startups, Isacson strongly recommends focusing on **consumer mobile apps** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:26">[00:40:26]</a>. This sector offers:
*   **Learning Human Psychology**: A direct way to understand how to build products people love <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:36">[00:40:36]</a>.
*   **Viral Potential**: The ability to create global experiences and reach a wide audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:43">[00:40:43]</a>.
*   **Accessibility**: Tools are continually improving, soon making it possible to build apps without extensive technical expertise <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:39">[00:41:39]</a>.
*   **Engagement**: Consumer apps are generally perceived as more "fun" to develop compared to B2B or SaaS products, focusing on product creation and marketing rather than complex contracts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:14">[00:41:14]</a>.

## Featured Startup Ideas

### 1. Robot Lawn Mower Services

Wilkinson identifies a significant opportunity in the robot lawn mower market, drawing parallels to Roomba vacuums but noting that yards are less variable environments, making robot mowers more effective <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:46:00">[03:46:00]</a>.

*   **Review Website:** Create a "Wirecutter-style" review website specializing in robot lawnmowers, focusing on SEO and affiliate marketing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:05:00">[04:05:00]</a>.
*   **Automated Lawn Mowing Company:** Acquire an existing landscaping company with a large customer base and transition to using robot lawnmowers instead of human laborers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:27:00">[04:27:00]</a>. This model allows for higher profit margins compared to traditional landscaping businesses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:03:00">[05:03:00]</a>.
*   **Local Service Niche:** Start small by offering robot lawn mowing services to a limited number of houses in a specific neighborhood <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:09:00">[06:09:00]</a>.
*   **Dealership:** Consider buying a dealership for robot lawnmowers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:20:00">[04:20:00]</a>.

Wilkinson views this as a "Launchpad business," which, while physical and less scalable than software, can provide a solid income for a "scrappy person" willing to invest around $5,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:31:00">[08:31:00]</a>.

### 2. Sleep Apnea and Health Monitoring Solutions

With the Apple Watch now diagnosing sleep apnea, Wilkinson sees a massive opportunity due to the high rate of undiagnosed cases (80-90%) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:50:00">[17:50:00]</a>.

*   **Acquire Clinics:** Purchase existing sleep apnea clinics, betting on an increase in diagnoses and hardware sales <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:22:00">[19:22:00]</a>.
*   **Hardware Review Site:** Develop a website that reviews CPAP machines and related hardware, potentially using an affiliate model <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:38:00">[19:38:00]</a>.
*   **Alternative Treatments (D2C):** Focus on direct-to-consumer (D2C) sales of alternative treatments like oral devices for those who prefer not to use CPAP machines <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:01:00">[20:01:00]</a>.
*   **Apple Watch Data App:** Create an app that utilizes Apple Watch data for deeper sleep apnea monitoring or coaching <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:18:00">[20:18:00]</a>.
*   **Hardware Financing:** Establish a business offering financing options for CPAP machines and other sleep apnea hardware <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:30:00">[20:30:00]</a>.

This trend extends beyond sleep apnea to other undiagnosed conditions detectable by wearables, such as AFib (arterial fibrillation), hypertension, diabetes, and even stress and anxiety <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:24:00">[24:24:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:50:00">[23:50:00]</a>. Wilkinson suggests that services using [[using_ai_to_generate_startup_ideas | AI to generate startup ideas]] for mental health assessments and diagnosis, like ADHD, could fill a critical gap where current diagnosis processes are expensive and inefficient <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:45:00">[25:45:00]</a>.

### 3. Computer Vision for Small Businesses

Wilkinson sees current computer vision technology as finally affordable and accessible for small business owners <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:43:00">[27:43:00]</a>.

*   **In-store Analytics:** Use simple webcams or old iPhones to create heat maps of customer movement, identify where people linger or get confused, and track repeat customers via anonymized facial recognition <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:56:00">[26:56:00]</a>. This can provide valuable insights into store layout and customer behavior <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:12:00">[27:12:00]</a>.
*   **Personalized Customer Experience:** Leverage facial recognition (anonymized) to recognize returning customers and provide personalized greetings or recommendations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:21:00">[31:21:00]</a>.

### 4. Negotiation as a Service

Drawing from his experience in saving significant money through negotiation at his own agency, Wilkinson proposes a business model centered on negotiating on behalf of clients <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:01:00">[32:01:00]</a>.

*   **Software Procurement & Negotiation:** Offer services to evaluate and negotiate enterprise software contracts (e.g., Amazon S3, HubSpot, Rippling) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:58:00">[33:58:00]</a>. This can lead to substantial savings (60-70%) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:16:00">[34:16:00]</a>.
*   **Big Purchase Negotiation:** Extend negotiation services to large personal purchases like houses or cars, operating on a commission-based model where the service takes a percentage of the savings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:25:00">[34:25:00]</a>. This business model benefits from the current economic climate where companies are focused on cost reduction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:41:00">[35:41:00]</a>.

### 5. Direct-to-Consumer Sperm Freezing/Banking

Wilkinson notes the rising interest in sperm freezing (visible in Google Trends) but a lack of marketing and an awkward traditional process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="38:09:00">[38:09:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:13:00">[37:13:00]</a>.

*   **At-Home Service:** Create a D2C service that ships a kit with dry ice, allowing individuals to collect and ship samples from home, making the process convenient and less awkward <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:38:00">[37:38:00]</a>.
*   **Brand Building:** Develop a strong brand in this underserved market <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="38:24:00">[38:24:00]</a>.
*   **Lead Generation:** Focus on lead generation by purchasing relevant keywords and selling those leads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="39:47:00">[39:47:00]</a>.
*   **Partnerships:** Explore leasing space from existing sperm banks to manage the storage aspect <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="42:12:00">[42:12:00]</a>.

Wilkinson considers this a "lottery ticket" opportunity due to the lack of competition in online advertising for the service <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="38:54:00">[38:54:00]</a>.

### 6. Curated Community / Forum Groups

Inspired by his experience with EO and YPO forums, Wilkinson sees a need for structured community groups that foster deep friendships and shared trust <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="43:35:00">[43:35:00]</a>.

*   **Structured Sharing:** Facilitate monthly meetings where members share personal, family, and business challenges in a confidential setting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="43:40:00">[43:40:00]</a>.
*   **Niche Communities:** Create groups around specific interests or for demographics seeking shared experiences (e.g., "Man Mornings" for men to share feelings over coffee) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="46:24:00">[46:24:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="47:49:00">[47:49:00]</a>.
*   **Secular Church Model:** Build community around shared values and regular gatherings, fostering a sense of belonging in an increasingly online world <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="47:06:00">[47:06:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="47:27:00">[47:27:00]</a>.

Wilkinson believes these communities address an "epidemic of loneliness" and provide a "friend billionaire" experience that is more rewarding than solely pursuing wealth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="49:32:00">[49:32:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="50:04:00">[50:04:00]</a>. While not always huge money-makers on their own, they build invaluable networks that can lead to future opportunities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="51:05:00">[51:05:00]</a>.

## General Advice on Startup Ideas

*   **Affiliate Marketing:** Wilkinson views affiliate marketing as a way to capture revenue that advertisers would otherwise make, especially valuable when leveraged with an established audience or newsletter <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:13:00">[10:13:00]</a>.
*   **Directory Affiliate:** Obsessed with creating curated directories that plug into affiliate deals, believing people seek curation in an information-rich world <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:11:00">[13:11:00]</a>. He advises building an email list from directory traffic to avoid reliance on search algorithms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:16:00">[15:16:00]</a>.
*   **Leverage New Technology:** Look for business opportunities that arise from new technologies, like the Apple Watch's health features <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:54:00">[20:54:00]</a>.
*   **Idea Validation:** Andrew emphasizes that his compiled ideas are "validated concepts" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:31:00">[06:31:00]</a>.
*   **When to Continue an Idea:** If someone is already doing it in one market (e.g., the U.S.), consider copying the exact model in another market (e.g., Canada) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:07:00">[41:07:00]</a>. The key considerations are the difficulty and cost of execution (can it be bootstrapped?) and the ability to find a dedicated CEO <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:21:00">[41:21:00]</a>.

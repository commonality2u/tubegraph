## The "Peppermint" Commercial: A Case Study in [[viral_content_creation_with_tik_tok | Viral Content Creation]]

A successful example of V3's capabilities is the satirical "Peppermint" pharmaceutical commercial, which achieved significant viral traction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:36">[00:01:36]</a>. This ad parodies traditional pharmaceutical commercials by introducing an unexpected, humorous twist: a pill that secretes pheromones to attract puppies as a cure for depression <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:00">[00:03:00]</a>.

### Ideation and Scripting
The creation process begins with a "kernel of an idea" – a unique hook that makes the content unexpected <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:12">[00:06:12]</a>. For the "Peppermint" ad, the initial concept was a placebo pill that leads to a dog delivery, which then evolved into the pheromone-secreting pill idea <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:59">[00:06:59]</a>.

To generate the script, [[ai_tools_for_creating_youtube_content | ChatGPT]] (or other Large Language Models like Claude or Grok) is used <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:37">[00:07:37]</a>. The process involves:
*   **Generalized Instructions:** Start by giving broad instructions to the AI, allowing it to produce cliché content first <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:39">[00:07:39]</a>.
*   **Iterative Refinement:** Request satirical or SNL-style twists <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:09">[00:09:09]</a>, continually prompting the AI for more ideas and "zingers" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:20">[00:14:20]</a>. It's suggested to refeed favorite lines back to the AI for learning and updating <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:48">[00:14:48]</a>.
*   **Outsourcing "Taste":** If you lack a specific comedic style, instruct the AI to "create jokes in the style of Seth Rogan" or "imagine your Bill Burr doing a standup comedy routine" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:51">[00:15:51]</a>.
*   **Utilizing Other Sources:** Jokes from sources like "Deep Thoughts with Jack Handy from SNL" can be reconstituted into V3 settings, with proper credit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:16">[00:16:16]</a>.

### Cost and Efficiency
The "Peppermint" commercial, which would typically cost $100,000 to $1,000,000 to produce conventionally, was made for approximately $500 in V3 credits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:36">[00:21:36]</a>. The entire production took about 6 to 12 hours <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:32">[00:25:32]</a>.

## Technical Process for V3 Video Generation

### Prompt Generation for V3
Once the script is finalized, convert each dialogue line into a V3 prompt <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:34">[00:16:34]</a>.
*   **ChatGPT's Role:** Feed the dialogue to [[ai_tools_for_creating_youtube_content | ChatGPT]] and instruct it to extrapolate a prompt using a specific structure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:51">[00:16:51]</a>.
*   **Character Consistency (Limitations):** While not perfect, describe characters in detail (e.g., "an old guy with short gray hair," "a white guy in his 30s with a mustache and a beard and strong chin with muscular arms") and have [[ai_tools_for_creating_youtube_content | ChatGPT]] generate these details <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:52">[00:20:52]</a>.
*   **Prompting Camera Movements:** Include camera movements like "camera push in" or "dolly in" to make shots feel less AI-generated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:15">[00:22:15]</a>. You can also prompt specific emotions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:36">[00:22:36]</a> and granular details within the scene <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:02">[00:23:02]</a>.

### V3 Specifics
*   **Model:** Use the "VO3" model for the highest quality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:24">[00:22:24]</a>.
*   **Outputs:** Generate one output per prompt <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:27">[00:18:27]</a>.
*   **Cost:** Each shot costs approximately $2 to $3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:31">[00:18:31]</a>. Run shots one at a time due to the cost and to get feedback <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:30">[00:19:30]</a>.
*   **Audio Generation:** V3 generates audio automatically with the video <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:19">[00:24:19]</a>.
    *   **Limitations:** Audio can sound "thin" with no deep EQ spectrum <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:42">[00:24:42]</a>, and there's no voice consistency between shots <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:49">[00:24:49]</a>. Regenerating a shot might change the voice and appearance of the character <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:55">[00:24:55]</a>.
    *   **Subtitle Issue:** To reduce weird gibberish subtitles, do not include quotes in your prompts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:11">[00:19:11]</a>.
*   **Text-to-Video vs. Image-to-Video:** Currently, text-to-video performs better than image-to-video in V3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:23">[00:20:23]</a>. Character consistency is a current limitation, meaning you won't see the same character twice <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:31">[00:20:31]</a>.

### Editing
Use editing software like Final Cut Pro, Cap Cut, or DaVinci Resolve (a free, top-tier option) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:25">[00:23:25]</a>. Expect a 3:1 to 5:1 ratio of generated clips to usable good ones <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:46">[00:23:46]</a>.

## [[YouTube growth strategies | Creating Viral Hooks]]

The key to a video's success, especially on platforms like X (Twitter), lies in its hook <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:55">[00:25:55]</a>. This is analogous to Mr. Beast's strategy where the title is more important than the thumbnail, and the thumbnail is more important than the content itself <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:22">[00:26:22]</a>.

*   **Headline/Title:** On X, the headline has 0.2 seconds to hook viewers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:57">[00:25:57]</a>. The "Peppermint" ad's hook was: "I used to shoot 500k pharmaceutical commercials. I made this for $500 in V3 credits in less than a day. What's the argument for spending 500K now? Steal my prompt below." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:47">[00:26:47]</a>. This combines a "low-key flex" (legitimacy), a "trendjacking" element (V3 cost), symmetry (500k to $500), a question (engages replies), and a call to action ("Steal my prompt") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:10">[00:27:10]</a>.
*   **Content Hook:** The first 2-3 seconds of the video must compel viewers to stay <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:03">[00:26:03]</a>.
*   **Provocative Statements:** Use compelling statements like "AI video quality just 10xed overnight. I'm speechless" or "What if Studio Ghibli directed Lord of the Rings?" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:17">[00:28:17]</a>. Avoid overused phrases like "Hollywood is cooked" and find your own unique language <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:10">[00:29:10]</a>.
*   **Leverage Social Formats:** Identify popular social formats (e.g., "Man on the street" videos, Zillow tours, mock Joe Rogan sets) and add a unique twist, like an alien protagonist <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:01">[00:39:01]</a>.

## Monetization Strategies for Generative AI Content

There are numerous ways to build a business around generative AI and V3 content:

### 1. Build a Personal Brand
Use V3 to [[Creating a successful YouTube channel | build a brand]] for yourself, creating content you're proud of that can lead to downstream work <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:36">[00:31:36]</a>. A strong personal brand and audience can be highly valuable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:51">[00:44:51]</a>.

### 2. Attract Direct Clients
Create commercials for specific niches, attracting clients directly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:55">[00:31:55]</a>.
*   **Target Industries:** Examples include food products (beer companies, restaurants, CPG brands like Feastables), or underwear brands <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:47">[00:32:47]</a>.
*   **Value Proposition:** Offer "unhinged special effects" that traditional agencies cannot deliver within similar budgets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:19">[00:33:19]</a>.
*   **Pricing:** Charge a minimum of $5,000, emphasizing your filmmaking skill and expertise <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:30">[00:33:30]</a>.

### 3. Build a Portfolio for Agencies
Create a portfolio of diverse commercials, or specialize in a niche (e.g., knife commercials) and target agencies or companies in that sector <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:41">[00:34:41]</a>.
*   **"Steal Like an Artist":** For beginners, it's suggested to "rip" shots from award-winning commercials <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:12">[00:35:12]</a>. Upload a screenshot to [[ai_tools_for_creating_youtube_content | ChatGPT]], ask it to break it down into a prompt, then use that prompt in V3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:26">[00:35:26]</a>. Adapt the shots to an adjacent product to avoid direct ripoffs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:36">[00:35:36]</a>.

### 4. Create Narrative Series with Brand Integrations
Develop an original narrative universe and integrate products naturally within the story <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:23">[00:36:23]</a>.
*   **Example:** Neural Viz's "Moniverse," featuring aliens discovering artifacts from our time, which could include branded items like Tabasco sauce <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:10">[00:38:10]</a>.
*   **Monetization:** Besides merch and Patreon, direct product placements can be highly lucrative <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:08">[00:38:08]</a>.

### 5. Sell Courses and Digital Products
Monetize your expertise by selling courses, PDFs, or other digital products that teach others how to use V3 and create content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:50">[00:39:50]</a>.

### 6. Consulting with Larger Companies
Offer consulting services to Fortune 500 companies, guiding them on integrating generative AI within legal and compliance constraints <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:08">[00:40:08]</a>. Charge more for larger companies, even if the content is similar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:01">[00:41:01]</a>.

### 7. Build a Sketch Comedy Channel
Create a Key & Peele-style sketch comedy show <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:14">[00:41:14]</a>. While harder to monetize with direct brand integrations, platforms like TikTok show it's possible with a large enough audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:29">[00:41:29]</a>.

### 8. Narrative Films (Episodic Content Preferred)
While possible to create longer narrative films, the fast-iterating nature of AI models makes long-form content challenging due to potential quality inconsistencies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:49">[00:41:49]</a>. Instead, focus on weekly episodic content, which aligns better with current consumption habits and the new creator economy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:17">[00:42:17]</a>.

### 9. Pitch Brands on Narrative Content
Propose creating a multi-episode narrative series for a brand, making their products or brand elements central to the story <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:02">[00:43:02]</a>.

### 10. Pick a Small Sub-Niche
Identify underserved niches with high demand and low supply of content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:28">[00:43:28]</a>.
*   **Examples:** Bible content (e.g., story of Jonah) due to open-sourced IP and a large engaged audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:30">[00:43:30]</a>.
*   **BookTok/Romantasy:** Create book promos or scenes from romantic fantasy books for authors, who are willing to pay for marketing materials <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:41">[00:45:41]</a>.

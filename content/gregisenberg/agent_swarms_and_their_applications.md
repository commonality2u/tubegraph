## What are Agent Swarms?
[[AI agents and automated marketing workflows | AI agent swarms]] represent a significant advancement in [[ai_agents_in_problemsolving | AI agent]] technology <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:17">[00:00:17]</a>. An agent swarm is the capability to provide an [[ai_agents_in_problemsolving | AI agent]] with a list of tasks, which it then performs reliably, quickly, and in parallel <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:31">[00:10:31]</a>. While individual [[ai_agents_in_problemsolving | AI agents]] are powerful, they can lose coherence or fail over long-term, complex horizons <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:44">[00:10:44]</a>. An agent swarm addresses this by duplicating the agent, sending a copy to handle each item on the list, similar to "Agent Smith" from The Matrix <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:15">[00:11:15]</a>.

## Advantages of Agent Swarms
Agent swarms offer several key advantages:
*   **Reliability and Speed** They execute tasks reliably and quickly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:38">[00:10:38]</a>.
*   **Parallel Processing** They can perform multiple tasks simultaneously <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:38">[00:10:38]</a>.
*   **Scalability** They overcome the limitations of single [[ai_agents_in_problemsolving | AI agents]] that might become unreliable or expensive when handling large lists of tasks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:02">[00:11:02]</a>.
*   **Autonomous Operation** Agent swarms can often figure out what items to swarm through without explicit configuration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:05">[00:17:05]</a>.

## Monetization Strategies for AI Products

Dan Chipper suggests exploring various [[Monetization Strategies Beyond Ads | monetization strategies]] for AI products, moving beyond traditional subscription models.

### Per-Use/Per-Task Pricing
For AI-powered tools like advanced voice notes or meeting summarizers, a monetization model based on usage can be effective.
> "I think like the the way that I think about it is new technologies create the opportunity for New Media formats" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:14:00">[05:14:00]</a>
*   **Advanced Voice Notes:** Charging per download, play, or listen scales directly with the value gained from the service <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:33:00">[07:33:00]</a>. This aligns with the amount of value being extracted <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:37:00">[07:37:00]</a>.
*   **Shift from Subscriptions:** Many users are fatigued by multiple subscriptions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:51:00">[07:51:00]</a>. A trend in AI pricing involves charging per successfully completed task rather than a monthly fee <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:22:00">[08:22:00]</a>.

### Database as a Service (DaaS)
Leveraging AI to curate and sell specialized data sets presents a significant opportunity.
*   **Concept:** Take existing data, curate it using AI, place a paywall, and sell access through a subscription or one-time payment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:17:00">[15:17:00]</a>.
*   **Distribution:** This can be sold to social audiences via organic content (Twitter, TikTok), paid creators, or targeted ads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:42:00">[15:42:00]</a>.
*   **Kaggle Inspiration:** Platforms like Kaggle, which host datasets and bounties for data scientists <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:00:00">[09:00:00]</a>, demonstrate the value in curated data. With AI, "everyone's a data scientist" because tools like GPT-4 can be prompt-tuned to do interesting things with datasets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:40:00">[09:40:00]</a>.

### Luxury Goods and AI-Enhanced Experiences
AI's ability to make storytelling cheaper opens new avenues for creating luxury versions of previously accessible media.
*   **Great Books Example:** AI can translate classic "Great Books" into new, engaging formats tailored for specific audiences (e.g., a 30-something tech enthusiast) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:03:00">[17:03:00]</a>. This can include multimodal formats like audio, video, and interactive "advanced voice mode" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:56:00">[17:56:00]</a>.
*   **Monetization:** Sell one-off access, subscriptions, or create beautiful, expensive physical versions with a digital companion for easier consumption <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:20:00">[19:20:00]</a>.
*   **AI and Luxury:** As technology makes things cheaper, the original, often "human-made" or artisanal versions can become luxury status goods (e.g., Broadway shows, handmade shirts) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:59:00">[22:59:00]</a>. The prompt for startup ideas is: "What's something that's not luxury today that could be luxury?" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:35:00">[23:35:00]</a>.

## AI App Distribution Strategies

When building [[AI app marketing strategies | AI apps]], effective distribution is paramount. Dan Chipper emphasizes a "distribution first" approach.

### Building an Audience First
*   **Leverage Existing Media:** Dan Chipper's strategy involves building a media business (like his company, Every, which publishes a newsletter) for five years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:57:00">[34:57:00]</a>. This creates an engaged audience that can be immediately leveraged for new AI products <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:03:00">[35:03:00]</a>, yielding hundreds to thousands of users on day one <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:18:00">[35:18:00]</a>.
*   **Build for Yourself, Attract Like-Minded People:** Develop products to solve personal problems or address interests <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:16:00">[36:16:00]</a>. This naturally attracts an audience with similar characteristics who are likely to desire the same solution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:20:00">[36:20:00]</a>.
*   **Distribution First, Build Second:** The core philosophy is to prioritize building an audience and distribution channels before developing the product itself <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:34:00">[36:34:00]</a>.

### Product-Led Growth (PLG)
Once initial users are acquired from a built-up audience, focus on product-led growth to scale.
*   **Incentivize Sharing:** Encourage users to share the app by offering incentives like free access for a period, early access to new features, or exclusive event invitations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:41:00">[36:41:00]</a>.
*   **Targeting the Right Users:** Segmenting an audience (e.g., by source like YouTube video) allows for sending targeted invitations, ensuring the acquisition of ideal users who will provide valuable insights and form the core community <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="39:38:00">[39:38:00]</a>.

### Current Trends in Audience Building
*   **YouTube as a Starting Point:** While newsletters were effective in the past, starting a media business in 2025 might involve YouTube first, then funneling viewers to a newsletter <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="38:18:00">[38:18:00]</a>.
*   **Voice-First Interfaces:** With the rise of voice assistants and advanced voice modes, designing "voice first" interfaces for products is an interesting area to explore for new businesses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:50:00">[29:50:00]</a>. This is especially relevant given that younger generations are becoming accustomed to voice interactions from an early age <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:07:00">[30:07:00]</a>.
*   **Mobile-First Analogy:** Just as mobile-first companies disrupted industries by creating specialized user experiences for smartphones, "voice-first" companies could capture significant market share from incumbents like Expedia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:01:00">[33:01:00]</a>. Big companies are often too risk-averse to fully adapt their core interfaces for new paradigms, creating an advantage for startups <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:54:00">[33:54:00]</a>.

> [!TIP] **Opportunities for AI App Development**
> The current "AI wave" has "reset the playing field," offering abundant "low-hanging fruit" for new businesses. This makes it an opportune time to build products for oneself and then find an audience of like-minded individuals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:54:00">[35:54:00]</a>.

## Conclusion

Dan Chipper's insights highlight that successful AI ventures hinge not only on innovative ideas but also on thoughtful strategies for monetization and distribution. By focusing on usage-based pricing, identifying luxury market opportunities, and prioritizing audience-building before product development, entrepreneurs can navigate the evolving landscape of AI-powered businesses.

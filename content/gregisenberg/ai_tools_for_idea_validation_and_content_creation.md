## Finding Validated Startup Ideas

The core of this process involves identifying "gold" within Reddit communities by looking for validated ideas that can lead to multi-million dollar businesses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:19">[00:00:19]</a>.

### Step 1: Discovering Trending Subreddits with Gummy Search

The initial step uses a tool called Gummy Search (which offers a free signup and a paid plan) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:31">[00:01:31]</a>.

*   **Trending Section**: Navigate to the "trending" section within Gummy Search to view top subreddits sorted by size, activity, or growth rate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:40">[00:01:40]</a>.
*   **Focus on Growth**: Prioritize subreddits based on how fast they are growing daily, weekly, monthly, or yearly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:56">[00:01:56]</a>.
*   **Target Niche Communities**: A key strategy is to focus on subreddits with 10,000 to 100,000 members, rather than massive ones <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:17">[00:02:17]</a>. These are often newer, trending communities that answer the "why now" question for building a product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:28">[00:02:28]</a>.
*   **Identify Interests**: Scroll through these growing subreddits and note down any that seem interesting or where you might have an "unfair advantage" (e.g., personal experience or connections) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:50">[00:02:50]</a>.
*   **Timeframe Filters**: Experiment with daily, weekly, or monthly growth filters to uncover different trending niches <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:36">[00:03:36]</a>.
*   **Curated Sections**: Gummy Search also provides curated lists of subreddits, such as those for software engineers, which can be added to an audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:15">[00:04:15]</a>.

### Step 2: Identifying Problems and Solutions within a Niche

Once a promising subreddit is selected, the next step is to use Gummy Search to pinpoint problems and potential solutions within that community <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:56">[00:04:56]</a>.

*   **Create an Audience**: In Gummy Search, create an "audience" for the chosen niche, which can include similar subreddits (e.g., "people into dog food" includes other dog-related communities) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:57">[00:05:57]</a>.
*   **"Pain and Anger" Category**: This section highlights the common struggles and frustrations within the community <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:53">[00:06:53]</a>. [[ai_tools_for_validating_and_developing_startup_ideas | AI]] summarizes thousands of words to find common patterns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:08">[00:07:08]</a>.
    *   *Example*: For a "dog food" audience, patterns like "potty training challenges," "dogs reluctant to walk outside," or "eating habits/loss of appetite" emerge, sparking startup ideas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:58">[00:07:58]</a>.
*   **"Advice Requests" Category**: This section reveals what products or solutions people are actively seeking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:08">[00:10:08]</a>. These are "people screaming they want these products to exist" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:36">[00:10:36]</a>.
    *   *Example*: For dog communities, requests for "dog breeding recommendations" or "dog food recommendations" for senior dogs with dental issues indicate specific needs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:54">[00:10:54]</a>.
*   **"Solution Requests" Category**: This provides insight into the existing competitive landscape and how people are currently addressing their problems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:40">[00:12:40]</a>.
*   **Document Ideas**: Throughout this process, meticulously write down potential startup ideas, problems, and solutions in a document (e.g., Google Doc) to serve as a one-pager for the business idea <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:55">[00:09:55]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:20">[00:13:20]</a>.

### Step 3: Finding Creators and Deepening Insights

After identifying core problems and solutions, the next step involves researching creators in the niche to gain further understanding.

*   **Identify Influencers with [[ai_tools_for_validating_and_developing_startup_ideas | Perplexity AI]]**: Use a tool like [[ai_tools_for_validating_and_developing_startup_ideas | Perplexity AI]] (a free tool) to find popular influencers related to your niche (e.g., "25 most popular dog influencers on YouTube") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:38">[00:13:38]</a>. Specify "educational" influencers if seeking expert advice rather than entertainment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:50">[00:14:50]</a>.
*   **Analyze Creator Content and Comments**: Visit the channels of identified creators, sort their videos by "most popular," and read the comments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:12">[00:15:12]</a>. The comments reveal what people are thinking about, their unaddressed needs, and further pain points <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:37">[00:15:37]</a>. This step helps put yourself in the audience's shoes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:06">[00:16:06]</a>.
*   **Wireframe and Prototype**: Based on all gathered insights, wireframe out a potential website or product. This can be done rapidly using tools like Framer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:19">[00:16:19]</a>. An example shared is a design agency focused on conversion and cash flow, born from Reddit insights about designers not delivering results <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:46">[00:16:46]</a>.
*   **Develop a Catchy Name**: Consider naming your company after a subreddit or coming up with a name that resonates with the community based on insights (e.g., "Design Science" from a desire for designers to be more scientific, less artistic) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:42">[00:17:42]</a>.

## Creating Content People Will Love

The same methodology for finding startup ideas can be applied to [[using_ai_for_content_creation | content creation]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:07">[00:01:07]</a>.

*   **"Top Content" in Gummy Search**: Use Gummy Search's "Top Content" section within your chosen audience to see what posts perform best <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:31">[00:18:31]</a>.
*   **Identify Patterns and Formats**: Observe common patterns, successful formats (long stories, memes), and topics that resonate most with the community <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:38">[00:18:38]</a>.
    *   *Example*: For the dog community, stories like "loss of beloved pet" or "today I saved a dog's life" tend to perform well <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:55">[00:18:55]</a>.
*   **Ask Direct Questions**: Gummy Search allows you to ask direct questions about the audience's preferences (e.g., "Do they love memes?") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:18">[00:19:18]</a>. This feature acts as an "entrepreneurial assistant" or co-founder, helping to connect ideas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:46">[00:19:46]</a>.

By observing and analyzing communities on platforms like Reddit, YouTube, and TikTok, you can effectively teleport into different communities to gather insights and develop ideas that genuinely resonate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:21">[00:20:21]</a>. This methodology has proven successful in building multi-figure businesses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:34">[00:20:34]</a>.

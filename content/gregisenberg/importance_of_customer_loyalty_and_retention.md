## The Triple Triple Double Double Strategy

To achieve a $100 million business within five years, a common strategy involves tripling revenue three times and then doubling it twice <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:41">[00:00:41]</a>. This means going from a $3 million business to $9 million, then $27 million, then doubling to $54 million, and finally to $108 million <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:35">[00:00:35]</a>. This ambitious growth trajectory is significantly more achievable when built on a solid foundation of customer loyalty <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:07">[00:18:07]</a>.

## Churn vs. Loyalty

Many founders view "churn" as a daunting four-letter word <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:24">[00:16:24]</a>. However, focusing solely on churn can be counterproductive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:27">[00:16:27]</a>. Instead, businesses should prioritize "loyalty," which is best measured by Net Revenue Retention (NRR) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:32">[00:16:32]</a>.

### Net Revenue Retention (NRR)

Net Revenue Retention measures not just the avoidance of churn, but how to gain more loyalty from each existing customer cohort <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:39">[00:16:39]</a>. A 3% increase in Net Revenue Retention can double a company's valuation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:45">[00:16:45]</a>.

*   **Definition**: If a cohort of customers spent $100 in January, and in February they spend $90, that's a 10% monthly churn <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:54">[00:16:54]</a>. Conversely, Net Revenue Retention means that the same cohort spends *more* with the company in February than in January <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:08">[00:17:08]</a>.
*   **Examples**: Companies like Slack and AWS exemplify strong NRR, where usage increases over time, leading to higher spending from existing customers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:14">[00:17:14]</a>.

A high NRR drastically increases a company's valuation, makes business growth easier, and facilitates successful exits years down the road <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:41">[00:17:41]</a>.

## Prioritizing Retention Over Growth

A common mistake founders make is rushing into growth strategies before ensuring strong retention <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:02">[00:18:02]</a>. It is significantly easier to grow a business when it has incredible customer loyalty <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:07">[00:18:07]</a>.

> "You wouldn't build a leaky ship." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:55">[00:18:55]</a>

Trying to aggressively market a product that barely breaks even often results in massive churn and makes profitable growth difficult <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:18">[00:18:18]</a>. Customer acquisition costs are continually rising <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:29">[00:18:29]</a>. Therefore, the focus should be on retention first and foremost, *before* worrying about `Promotion` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:45">[00:18:45]</a>.

Even with consistent customer cohorts, focusing on proper pricing and strong retention can lead to continued revenue growth, even if the promotion strategy isn't yet accelerated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:53">[00:20:53]</a>.

## Building Loyalty and Brand Equity

*   **Initial Customers**: The first handful of customers are often the most loyal because they have the biggest pain points <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:35">[00:19:35]</a>. This early feedback loop is crucial for iteration and refining the product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:40">[00:19:40]</a>.
*   **Nailing Retention**: Nailing retention upfront helps develop brand equity and creates a "movement," making customers want to stick around long-term <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:50">[00:19:50]</a>.

### AppSumo Example

AppSumo analyzed customer data in 2017 and found that over 80% of their top customers were marketing agencies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:22">[00:38:22]</a>. These agencies used AppSumo to reduce monthly software expenses due to the feast-or-famine nature of agency revenues <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:39">[00:38:39]</a>.

By identifying this ideal customer, AppSumo shifted its strategy:
1.  **Hypothesis**: What if they increased their software deals from one per quarter to one per month? <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:01">[00:39:01]</a>
2.  **Test then Invest**: They experimented, and revenue increased <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:07">[00:39:07]</a>. This led to launching one software lifetime deal every day, totaling 30 per month <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:18">[00:39:18]</a>.
3.  **Outcome**: Selling more of what the ideal customer desired led to:
    *   Increased loyalty and referrals within customer communities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:35">[00:39:35]</a>.
    *   Benefits for other customer segments (e.g., solo-preneurs) due to better negotiated deals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:46">[00:39:46]</a>.
    *   Overall increase in customer lifetime value <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:56">[00:39:56]</a>.
    *   Reduced [[importance_of_generating_impressions_and_leads | customer acquisition costs]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:59">[00:39:59]</a>.
    *   The business tripled <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:07">[00:40:07]</a>.

This example highlights that deep focus on the ideal customer and consistently delivering what they need leads to increased loyalty and organic growth.

## Sustaining Business Growth

For early-stage companies (e.g., mid-seven figures), it's crucial to address fundamentals like retention to avoid burnout and collapse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:07">[00:15:07]</a>. Ignoring these issues in the beginning is like "building a skyscraper on a sandy foundation" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:48">[00:14:48]</a>, leading to potential churn issues and competitive challenges <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:12">[00:15:12]</a>. A strong foundation of customer loyalty and retention is essential for long-term [[entrepreneurship_strategies_for_profitability | profitability]] and sustainable scaling.

## Optimizing for Local Search: Google My Business

A sophisticated approach to acquiring customers for local service businesses involves optimizing a Google My Business (GMB) profile listing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:16">[00:03:16]</a>.

### Setting Up Your Google My Business Profile

*   **Address**: A physical address is preferred over a PO Box. If available, use a family member's business address <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:26">[00:03:26]</a>.
*   **Business Citations**:
    *   Build citations for your business across various online business directory websites <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:37">[00:03:37]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:44">[00:03:44]</a>.
    *   Services like Fiverr can provide 300 citations for about $30 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:46">[00:03:46]</a>.
    *   Ensure exact matching business information (Name, Address, Phone number - NAP) across all citations and your GMB listing. This consistency builds trust with Google and helps in ranking within the "snack pack" (the top three Google Maps listings for local searches) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:02">[00:04:02]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:10">[00:04:10]</a>.
*   **Keywords**:
    *   Identify keyword phrases you want to rank for (e.g., "Window Cleaning Denver," "Window Clean [Service Area]") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:40">[00:04:40]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:48">[00:04:48]</a>.
    *   Use AI tools like ChatGPT or Claude to write a natural-sounding business description that includes these keywords. Claude is noted for more human-like outputs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:44">[00:04:44]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:33">[00:05:33]</a>.
    *   Keyword stuffing the listing description has shown to work, but an AI-generated natural description is more effective <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:04">[00:05:04]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:06">[00:06:06]</a>.
    *   Include these keywords in the "service offering" section of your GMB listing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:15">[00:06:15]</a>.
    *   Consider including your highest-target keyword in the GMB listing title (e.g., "Denver Window Cleaning Company"), though this is a gray area in terms of Google's guidelines <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:40">[00:08:40]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:46">[00:08:46]</a>. This strategy is akin to a restaurant naming itself "Thai Near Me" to capture high-traffic "near me" searches <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:04">[00:09:04]</a>.
*   **Reviews & Images**:
    *   Reviews are less critical than previously thought, especially in smaller geographies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:35">[00:03:35]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:33">[00:06:33]</a>.
    *   Images are an important component for ranking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:29">[00:06:29]</a>.
*   **Phone Number**: Real phone numbers are more effective than Google Business phone numbers. A cell phone or VoIP software can be used <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:00">[00:07:00]</a>.

### Indexing Citations and Advanced Strategies

After building citations, you need to ensure Google sees them:

*   **Indexing Bot**: Use an indexing bot (e.g., Speedy Indexer, a Telegram bot) to drip traffic to your citation pages. This helps Google discover and index them, especially since large directory websites often have limited crawl budgets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:43">[00:09:43]</a>. This service is very inexpensive, costing a few cents per listing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:34">[00:10:34]</a>.
*   **Embedding Google Maps Listing**:
    *   Embed your Google Maps listing on other websites. Google prioritizes this as a signal of a legitimate and valuable resource <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:20">[00:11:20]</a>.
    *   This can be done using Private Blog Networks (PBNs), which are networks of thousands of blogs owned by a company that internally link to each other <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:54">[00:11:54]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:18">[00:12:18]</a>.
    *   A service by "Kevin PBNs" on Black Hat World can create these embedded PBN articles for about 50 cents each <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:43">[00:11:43]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:09">[00:13:09]</a>.
    *   Using PBNs is a more sophisticated, "black hat" SEO technique and should only be considered in highly competitive markets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:51">[00:11:51]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:02">[00:12:02]</a>.
*   **Results**: These strategies can lead to significant results, like ranking in Google's "snack pack" within a week and a half, much faster than traditional SEO, which can take at least three months <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:58">[00:13:58]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:09">[00:14:09]</a>.

## Complementary Marketing Strategies

Beyond local SEO, other tactics can drive growth:

*   **Direct Mail**:
    *   Use services like lob.com to send direct mail flyers. Upload your design (e.g., 11x8 flyer created in Canva) and a list of addresses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:22">[00:15:22]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:37">[00:15:37]</a>.
    *   Target the wealthiest ZIP codes in your area <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:51">[00:15:51]</a>.
*   **Flyers on Doors**: Old-school, classic method of distributing flyers directly to homes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:09">[00:16:09]</a>.
*   **Content Marketing (YouTube)**:
    *   Film satisfying videos of your services (e.g., lawn mowing, window cleaning, power washing transformations) and upload them to YouTube <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:34">[00:19:34]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:46">[00:19:46]</a>.
    *   This creates an additional revenue stream from ad dollars <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:03">[00:20:03]</a>.
    *   YouTube's algorithm can show these videos to people in your service geography, generating inbound leads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:07">[00:20:07]</a>.
    *   Using your own business as a case study for content demonstrating how to achieve results (e.g., "How to rank your window cleaning business for Google Maps") can attract potential clients who'd rather hire someone to do it for them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:09">[00:21:09]</a>.

## Scaling to an Agency and Portfolio

Once you've successfully grown your own service business using these [[seo_techniques_for_startups | marketing skill sets]], the next step is to leverage that expertise:

*   **Form a Digital Agency**: Turn your proven marketing skills into a digital agency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:48">[00:14:48]</a>.
*   **Acquire Clients**: Cold email other service companies (e.g., window cleaning companies) offering to grow their leads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:55">[00:14:55]</a>.
*   **Service Fees & Equity**: Offer services for a flat fee or on a performance basis. The ultimate goal is to create cash flow and potentially acquire portions of these companies in specific geographies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:08">[00:15:08]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:53">[00:16:53]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:00">[00:17:00]</a>. This can evolve into owning a portfolio company that holds equity in numerous businesses across different regions, like an HVAC or window cleaning company <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:29">[00:17:29]</a>.

This long-term strategy, potentially spanning 7-10 years, allows a "baby entrepreneur" to "play at the pro level" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:49">[00:00:49]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:05">[00:01:05]</a>. An example is James Zly, who built a substantial portfolio company by acquiring portions of businesses and applying the same marketing playbook and team to scale them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:44">[00:17:44]</a>. He leverages his [[building_online_businesses_using_seo_and_domain_investments | SEO]] expertise to drive this growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:25">[00:18:25]</a>. Ideally, equity in these businesses could be earned through seller financing (e.g., in exchange for marketing services) rather than upfront cash <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:41">[00:18:41]</a>.

### Strategic Investments

Companies that achieve strong brand recognition, to the point where people wear unrelated merchandise (e.g., streetwear) of that brand, indicate a highly successful and organic growth trajectory <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:07">[00:26:07]</a>. This signals a business worth investing in. The company Resi Brands, for instance, which includes the Pink's painting franchise, is leveraging visually distinct branding and [[leveraging_tiktok_and_social_media_for_local_seo | TikTok-friendly content]] to grow quickly, even attracting investments from figures like Cody Sanchez <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:50">[00:23:50]</a>. Influencers buying into companies where they have a "distribution advantage" through their audience can rapidly scale a business, especially by franchising <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:07">[00:25:07]</a>.

## The Future of Business: Media First

The traditional model of a product company that *also* does media is shifting. The future belongs to "media companies that make product" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:19">[01:00:19]</a>. This means building an audience and distribution channels (e.g., podcasts, newsletters, social media) is paramount, creating an inbound funnel for customers or even investment opportunities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:51">[00:59:51]</a>. This shift is particularly impactful in non-software, "grimy" industries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:30">[01:00:30]</a>.

## Hunter Isacson's Playbook for App Growth

Hunter Isacson outlines a concise playbook for building a successful consumer app, emphasizing product simplicity, strategic user acquisition, and clear performance metrics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:39">[00:17:39]</a>:

1.  **Build a simple experience (MVP)**: Focus on a bare-bones Minimum Viable Product (MVP) that effectively solves a problem, even if it's not fully optimized <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:18">[00:18:18]</a>. The core concept should be good enough to solve the issue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:34">[00:18:34]</a>.
2.  **Build a resonant brand**: Create a brand that people can understand and resonate with <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:40">[00:22:40]</a>. Hunter suggests keeping names short, often using slang terms relevant to the target community <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:09">[00:23:09]</a>. This approach helps set a comfortable, colloquial tone and makes the app feel like it understands its users <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:51">[00:23:51]</a>.
    *   **Examples**: "NGL" (Not Gonna Lie) clearly indicates an anonymous app <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:30">[00:22:30]</a>, and "Bags" for a crypto app leverages community slang <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:17">[00:22:17]</a>.
3.  **Get users on quickly (seed)**: Test content on platforms like [[tiktok_marketing_strategies_for_consumer_saas_apps | TikTok]] or Instagram Reels, even during the pre-order stage, to gauge audience response <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:04">[00:19:04]</a>. The goal is to get enough initial users to activate a "growth loop" where users share content and spread the app organically <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:34">[00:14:34]</a>.
4.  **Select a core action/North Star Metric**: Define a single, crucial action that a high percentage of users must complete <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:22">[00:19:22]</a>. All development decisions should be oriented around increasing this metric <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:46">[00:19:46]</a>.
    *   **Examples**:
        *   **Wink (make new friends app)**: Percentage of users getting a match on their first day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:01">[00:20:01]</a>.
        *   **NGL (anonymous messaging)**: Percentage of users sharing their link and multiple replies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:27">[00:20:27]</a>.
        *   **Bags (crypto app)**: Percentage of people funding their accounts and making one trade in the first day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:05">[00:21:05]</a>.
    *   A "high percentage" means over 90% completion of the core action in a single, unbranching user flow <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:02">[00:26:02]</a>.

## Leveraging Social Media for Growth

Hunter Isacson's success is heavily tied to effective social media strategies:

### Engaging with Creators and Influencers

A primary method for seeding apps is [[engaging_with_influencers_for_successful_app_promotion | working with creators]] and using platforms like [[tiktok_marketing_strategies_for_consumer_saas_apps | TikTok]] and Instagram <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:24">[00:14:24]</a>.

*   **Low Cost, High Impact**: For NGL, less than $10,000 was spent on marketing to achieve 250 million downloads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:40">[00:14:40]</a>. This involved paying influencers small amounts ($50-$100) to post the app link on their stories <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:42">[00:30:42]</a>.
*   **Seeding for Virality**: The marketing budget was primarily used to "seed" the app, getting enough initial users to trigger a viral growth loop <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:51">[00:14:51]</a>. Once enough people adopted it, NGL went "giga viral" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:00">[00:15:00]</a>.

### Identifying New Channels and Opportunities

Success often comes from applying existing popular ideas to new or underutilized social channels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:04">[00:17:04]</a>.

*   **NGL and Instagram's Link Feature**: Anonymous messaging apps existed before NGL (e.g., Ask FM, Yikyak) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:17">[00:15:17]</a>. NGL capitalized on Instagram's October 2021 update, which allowed all users (not just those with 10k+ followers) to post links <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:29">[00:15:29]</a>. This new channel created a massive growth lever <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:07">[00:16:07]</a>.
*   **Locket and iOS Widgets**: Locket was a pioneer in utilizing the iPhone's widget functionality socially, allowing users to share pictures directly to friends' home screens <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:35">[00:10:35]</a>. It gained popularity on TikTok's relationship and best friend communities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:52">[00:10:52]</a>.
*   **Future Opportunities**: Isacson sees potential in iOS Live Activities and lock screen widgets as under-explored new real estate for apps <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:13">[00:12:13]</a>.

### Implementing Social Growth Loops

Integrating sharing and inviting mechanisms directly into the core user experience is crucial for viral growth.

*   **NGL**: The North Star Metric focused on users sharing their NGL link and replies, making sending anonymous messages more comfortable for viewers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:51">[00:20:51]</a>.
*   **Bags**: Early growth involved users logging in with Twitter, claiming a unique "bags link," sharing it, and tracking invites on a leaderboard <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:39">[00:29:39]</a>. This viral loop helped build social media followers and hype even before the product was fully developed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:05">[00:30:05]</a>.
*   **Hard Invite Gates**: Apps like "Laps" used hard invite gates (e.g., invite 3-5 friends to access the app), ensuring highly valuable users who brought others with them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:31">[00:27:31]</a>.
*   **Reduced Reliance on Paid Marketing**: By incorporating sharing into the core action, the app's "K-factor" (virality coefficient) increases, reducing full reliance on external paid marketing or viral content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:10">[00:28:10]</a>.

### Maintaining Authenticity on Social Media

For continued [[viral_content_creation_for_app_promotion | app promotion]], authenticity in public communication is key <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:41">[00:31:41]</a>.

*   **NGL & Bags Social Presence**: NGL has over 2 million followers on Instagram <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:53">[00:31:53]</a>, and Bags has over 100,000 on Twitter <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:57">[00:31:57]</a>.
*   **Relatable Content**: Regular posting, being "memeable," funny, and relatable, and presenting an authentic voice makes the brand feel more comfortable and trustworthy to young audiences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:09">[00:32:09]</a>. This increases user affinity and encourages sharing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:57">[00:32:57]</a>.

## Key Takeaways

*   **Focus on Frameworks, Not Just Ideas**: Instead of constantly seeking new ideas, focus on understanding successful frameworks (e.g., "NGL for X," "Cali for X") and applying them to new niches or audiences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:26">[00:36:26]</a>.
*   **Embrace Consumer Mobile Apps**: For young people, building consumer mobile apps offers significant opportunities to understand human psychology, build beloved products, and create global, viral experiences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:26">[00:40:26]</a>.
*   **Utilize Evolving Tools**: Tools for building consumer apps are continually improving, making it increasingly accessible even for non-technical individuals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:39">[00:41:39]</a>.

Hunter Isacson believes that while creating a business is not simple <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:33">[00:00:33]</a>, there's a current window of opportunity in consumer AI and mobile apps to build startups and generate revenue without necessarily needing venture capital <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:41">[00:00:41]</a>.

## Concept: Productized AI Services
A significant opportunity lies in building a marketing agency that operates almost entirely on AI, potentially scaling to a billion dollars in revenue with minimal human intervention <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:49">[00:36:49]</a>. The core idea is to [[evaluating_aidriven_business_ideas | productize services]], meaning specific, repeatable tasks are automated by AI, making them more accessible and cheaper <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:27">[00:36:27]</a>.

Instead of focusing on the AI aspect, the aim is to humanize AI by making the service feel like a traditional interaction, similar to how iPhones made digital notes resemble physical notes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:18">[00:28:18]</a>. This approach makes the service more accessible to a broader audience, as "normies" (regular people) might be afraid of AI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:10">[00:20:10]</a>.

### Example: Headline99
One specific example of a productized AI service is "Headline99," a concept for generating landing page headlines for $99 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:57">[00:35:57]</a>.

Here's how it would work:
1.  **Client Questionnaire** Clients fill out a questionnaire detailing their company <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:07">[00:36:07]</a>.
2.  **AI Generation** Large Language Models (LLMs) summarize the information and generate 5 to 10 headline ideas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:11">[00:36:11]</a>.
3.  **Human Curation** A human in the loop selects the top three best-performing headlines <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:17">[00:36:17]</a>.
4.  **Delivery** The client receives the best-working headline for their landing page <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:21">[00:36:21]</a>.

This model allows for a 10x reduction in cost compared to traditional services, as AI replaces manual labor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:04">[00:31:04]</a>.

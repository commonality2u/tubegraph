## Starting Your Writing Process

Most people begin writing with a seed of an idea and struggle to develop it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:25">[00:06:25]</a>. A common mistake is expecting AI to automate tasks one doesn't understand how to do manually <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:36">[00:07:36]</a>. Understanding the basic mechanics of how something should work is essential to effectively prompt AI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:43">[00:07:43]</a>.

### Idea Conception: Main Idea or Sub-Point?

When starting with a seed idea, the first decision is whether the idea itself will be the central theme of the content or a sub-point within a larger idea <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:12">[00:08:12]</a>. For example, Reed Hastings' rule at Netflix could be the main topic, or it could be one example in an article about "Three CEOs who had strange rules at the beginning of their startup journeys" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:00">[00:09:00]</a>.

### Defining Content Goals

Before writing, clearly define the goals for the piece <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:46">[00:10:46]</a>. For instance, linking to startup ideas, making the content actionable, and connecting it to a specific story like Reed Hastings' <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:55">[00:10:55]</a>. The majority of people lack a structured approach, often hoping smart ideas emerge without a clear strategy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:52">[00:11:52]</a>.

### Leveraging AI for Ideation

AI can be used conversationally, chunking out ideas piece by piece in a dialogue, or through a massive, pre-assembled prompt <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:00">[00:13:00]</a>. Many go wrong by using a "half-baked" prompt that is neither fully conversational nor fully structured <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:33">[00:13:33]</a>.

To start, feed AI your seed idea and content goals, then ask for potential headlines and 3-5 main points for each <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:51">[00:13:51]</a>. This process defers the lower-leverage task of generating many iterations to the AI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:30">[00:16:30]</a>. This mimics how an editor gives a prompt to a writer, who then returns with multiple ideas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:47">[00:16:47]</a>. Your role becomes that of an "editor-in-chief," applying your taste and context to select the best options <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:29">[00:17:29]</a>.

## Structuring Your Content

Many critical decisions are made before writing begins <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:19">[00:15:19]</a>. Opening a document and immediately starting to write is often a misstep, as key conclusions can be reached pre-writing, making the actual writing much easier and allowing AI to handle lower-leverage tasks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:27">[00:15:27]</a>.

### The Rule of Three

For an 800-word article, the easiest structure involves an introduction and three main sections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:41">[00:28:41]</a>. The first decision is what three key takeaways or pieces of information you will deliver to the reader <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:27">[00:29:27]</a>.

### The Power of Specificity in Main Points

90% of a piece's value lies in its headline and the quality of its three main points <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:39">[00:29:39]</a>. If main points are vague (e.g., "learn about money," "hire a CPA," "don't be dumb"), readers will quickly deem the content worthless <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:15">[00:30:15]</a>. However, if they are specific and tangible (e.g., "take advantage of this loophole to save 250k," "convert all money into a personal S-Corp, not an LLC," "use Section 8 housing program to get paid dividends you can write off"), readers are much more likely to engage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:06">[00:31:06]</a>.

The subheads should clearly deliver on the promise made in the headline <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:33">[00:38:33]</a>. The highest-leverage decision for any piece is determining these specific, tangible main points <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:53">[00:38:53]</a>.

### The 10 Magical Ways Framework

This framework helps overcome writer's block by categorizing 99% of non-fiction writing into 10 core delivery methods (e.g., tips, stats, steps, lessons) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:07">[00:35:07]</a>.

*   **Refining the Headline:** Use the 10 Magical Ways to make the headline more tangible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:54">[00:35:54]</a>. For example, changing "what it means for your startup" to "and three lessons that can be learned from the $361 billion streaming service" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:15">[00:36:15]</a>. Adding big numbers to headlines often improves performance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:37">[00:36:37]</a>.
*   **Clarifying Subheads:** The main points (subheads) should mirror the "magical way" specified in the headline <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:44">[00:38:44]</a>. If the headline promises "three lessons," the subheads should be "Lesson One," "Lesson Two," and "Lesson Three" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:16">[00:38:16]</a>.
*   **Content within Sections:** For each section, decide which of the 10 Magical Ways you will use to deliver the content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:08">[00:47:08]</a>. For example, if a lesson is about "running a banned words experiment," you might provide the steps for doing so <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:32">[00:47:32]</a>. For "convenience as a product differentiator," you might include stats or examples <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:39">[00:47:39]</a>.

By making these decisions before writing, the actual writing process becomes significantly easier and more structured <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:38">[00:48:38]</a>.

## Collaborating with AI: Editor-in-Chief Role

Your role in the [[the_role_of_ai_in_writing_and_content_creation | AI content creation]] process is to be the editor-in-chief, providing feedback and refinement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:59">[00:48:59]</a>.

### Training Your "Mini-Model"

When you manually create a new version of a successful hook or content piece based on an original, you've created a "mini-model" for the AI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:16">[00:24:16]</a>. This allows you to then ask the AI to generate more variations based on your specific example <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:06">[00:25:06]</a>. The key is understanding how to do things manually first, then training the AI with your examples <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:24">[00:24:24]</a>.

### Conversational Prompting

Engage with AI in a conversational format <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:32">[00:45:32]</a>. Provide your initial ideas and examples, then ask for more options, guiding the AI to refine its output based on your preferences (e.g., "These aren't bad, but I want them in sentence format and more tangible") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:51">[00:44:51]</a>. This iterative process reveals what works best and helps you compress the back-and-forth into more efficient "mega prompts" for future use <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:43">[00:45:43]</a>.

### No Need for Custom GPTs Initially

For most users, standard tools like ChatGPT or Claude are powerful enough, and there's no immediate need for custom GPTs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:42">[00:27:42]</a>. Focus on mastering the basics of prompting before seeking advanced solutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:42">[00:27:42]</a>.

## The Power of Owned Media
Having "owned media" such as a YouTube channel or podcast is crucial for attracting an audience interested in specific topics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:05">[00:00:05]</a>. This allows creators to build products and services to sell directly to their audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:12">[00:00:12]</a>.
This approach provides direct control over content distribution, acting as "rails" upon which creators can place any content they desire <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:30">[00:00:30]</a>. It's considered essential for playing "at the pro level" in today's content economy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:33">[00:00:33]</a>. Young generations are observed to "print money on YouTube," using this revenue to subsidize productized services and SaaS companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:39">[00:00:39]</a>.

[[media_and_content_strategies_for_business_growth | Owned media]] channels enable a company to go from zero to one in 30 days and be cash flow positive and sustainable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:00:00">[02:00:00]</a>.

## Strategies for Content Creation

### 1. Riding Market Trends
A key strategy for any company, especially bootstrapped ones, is to identify and [[viral_and_organic_content_strategies | ride larger market trends]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:07">[01:07:07]</a>. This means selling products or services that people already desire, rather than trying to educate the market <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:11">[01:10:11]</a>.

### 2. Consistency Over Intensity
While content creation can feel like a grind, it is often less demanding in terms of hours than perceived <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:04:00">[02:04:00]</a>. The primary challenge is consistency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:04:00">[02:04:00]</a>.

### 3. Defining Your Content Format
It's important to spend time defining your content's tone, voice, personality, style, and structure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:04:00">[02:04:00]</a>. Creators should think of themselves more as editors or curators, managing the pipeline of content ideas and the system for its creation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:04:00">[02:04:00]</a>. This involves being the "conductor of the orchestra" rather than playing every instrument <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:04:00">[02:04:00]</a>.

## Leveraging Specific Platforms and Technologies

### 1. LinkedIn Video for B2B Growth
LinkedIn is seeing a surge in video content, with a "For You page" feed similar to TikTok <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:44:00">[06:44:00]</a>. This creates a "blue ocean" opportunity due to the generally low quality of existing content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:50:00">[06:50:00]</a>.

A potential business idea involves servicing founders by creating and distributing vertical video content for LinkedIn <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:01:00">[07:01:00]</a>. This would involve:
*   **Interviews:** Interviewing founders for an hour weekly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:17:00">[07:17:00]</a>.
*   **Scripting:** Turning interview ideas into seven different scripts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:12:00">[07:12:00]</a>.
*   **Recording Guidance:** Providing founders with inflection and pacing guidance to record clips in about 10 minutes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:29:00">[07:29:00]</a>.
*   **Post-production & Scheduling:** An agency then creates clips and schedules them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:37:00">[07:37:00]</a>.

This service helps founders build a massive personal brand and generate inbound leads due to LinkedIn's free reach <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:54:00">[07:54:00]</a>. Pricing could range from $2,500/month for content creation to $5,000-$8,000/month for additional services like lead magnets and design help <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:55:00">[11:55:00]</a>.

Founders can be acquired by building a community around LinkedIn growth, offering free tips, and then upselling the done-for-you service <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:47:00">[12:47:00]</a>.

### 3. Newsletter Services for Founders
Many founders want a newsletter but lack the time or operational capacity to write it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:51:00">[02:51:00]</a>. A "done-for-you" service could involve:
*   **Interview-to-Newsletter:** Interviewing founders for an hour and transforming the transcript into newsletter content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:51:00">[02:51:00]</a>. [[ai_tools_for_content_creation_and_marketing | AI tools]] can help translate raw data into the founder's tone and voice <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:27:00">[03:27:00]</a>.
*   **Audience Growth:** Scraping emails from existing social media followers (e.g., Twitter, LinkedIn) and sequencing them into the newsletter <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:07:00">[03:07:00]</a>.
*   **Automation:** Setting up automated DMs on Twitter or LinkedIn to new followers/connections, inviting them to subscribe <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:07:00">[03:07:00]</a>.

This service appeals to founders because it offers a clear return on investment by translating audience engagement into customer lifetime value <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:17:00">[03:17:00]</a>. It also provides "schmuck insurance" by reducing dependence on a single platform by moving followers to an owned email list <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:37:00">[03:37:00]</a>.

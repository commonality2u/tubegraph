## Gamifying Employee Engagement

One practical application of gamification is in employee engagement. An internal tool, "incentive.io," was developed to gamify workplace tasks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:41">[00:11:41]</a>.

### Incentive.io Features:
*   **Micro-incentives:** Employees receive small rewards, such as $1 or $2 in Solana, for completing tasks on time, like filling out weekly employee engagement forms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:00">[00:12:00]</a>.
*   **Performance Tracking:** The system can track task completion and high ratings from colleagues, often through APIs integrated with tools like Jira <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:16">[00:12:16]</a>.
*   **Leaderboards:** A leaderboard visually displays who is completing the most tasks or lagging, providing a clear basis for promotions or performance reviews <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:21">[00:12:21]</a>.
*   **Results:** This gamified approach led to a significant increase in engagement, boosting participation in these tools by approximately 77% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:33">[00:12:33]</a>.

## Gamifying Chores with Spatial Computing

The advent of spatial computing, such as the Apple Vision Pro, presents new opportunities for gamifying everyday chores <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:04">[00:14:04]</a>.

### Apple Vision Pro Demo Example:
*   A demo showcased a user vacuuming, with virtual coins appearing as they cleaned, making the chore feel like a game <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:48">[00:12:48]</a>. The visual and auditory feedback (sounds of coins) made the experience enjoyable, similar to collecting coins in Mario <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:56">[00:14:56]</a>.

### Potential Applications:
*   **Kids' Chores:** Parents could reward children based on the number of "coins" they collect while doing chores, motivating them through play <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:02">[00:13:02]</a>.
*   **Household Tasks:** The concept can extend to various household duties, such as taking out the trash, by associating them with micro-rewards <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:28">[00:13:28]</a>. These rewards don't necessarily have to be monetary; they could be virtual coins redeemable at favorite stores like Amazon <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:41">[00:13:41]</a>.

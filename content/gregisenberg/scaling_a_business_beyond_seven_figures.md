## "Nine Steps, Nine Figures" Framework

This framework outlines three distinct phases for business development and [[execution_and_scaling_strategies_for_startups|scaling]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:01:00">[03:01:00]</a>:

1.  **Startup Phase (Product Market Fit)** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:05:00">[03:05:00]</a>
    *   Focuses on achieving product-market fit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:20:00">[03:20:00]</a>.
    *   Key elements: Persona (who you're selling to), Product (what you're selling), and Promotion (how you're selling at scale) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:21:00">[03:21:00]</a>.
    *   Product-market fit is achieved when a business has more leads than it knows what to do with <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:40:00">[03:40:00]</a>.

2.  **Scale-Up Phase (Company Creation)** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:08:00">[03:08:00]</a>
    *   This is the focus when a business reaches mid-seven figures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:35:00">[03:35:00]</a>.
    *   It involves establishing Process, People, and Performance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:40:00">[05:40:00]</a>.
    *   The emphasis is on building a highly optimized, automated, and software-driven business, leveraging tools like AI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:05:00">[06:05:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:10:00">[06:10:00]</a>.
    *   The goal is to have a small team of "A players" rather than a large, bureaucratic organization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:15:00">[06:15:00]</a>.
    *   Performance management involves providing teams with "scoreboards," incentives, and compensation packages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:48:00">[06:48:00]</a>.

3.  **Grow-Up Phase (Legacy Protection)** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:12:00">[03:12:00]</a>
    *   This phase focuses on protecting the company's legacy once it grows beyond $100 million <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:24:00">[02:24:00]</a>.

## Key Principles for [[growth_strategies_for_startups|Growth Strategies for Startups]]

### Focus on Loyalty: Net Revenue Retention
Instead of solely focusing on "churn," businesses should prioritize "loyalty" by measuring Net Revenue Retention (NRR) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:16:32">[0:16:32]</a>.
*   **Definition**: For a customer cohort from a specific month (e.g., January), NRR measures if they spend more in subsequent months (e.g., February) than they did initially <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:17:08">[0:17:08]</a>.
*   **Impact**: Every 3% increase in Net Revenue Retention can double a company's valuation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:16:43">[0:16:43]</a>. It makes it easier to grow and exit the business <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:17:44">[0:17:44]</a>.
*   **Prioritization**: It's easier to grow a business with strong customer loyalty <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:18:07">[0:18:07]</a>. Founders should focus on retention upfront before pushing aggressive promotion strategies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:18:45">[0:18:45]</a>. Building a strong foundation and brand equity with early loyal customers leads to more sustainable [[steps_for_growing_a_business_to_10_million|growth]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:19:50">[0:19:50]</a>.

### Strategic Growth Channels: The 80/20 Rule for Marketing
When considering marketing channels, it's advised to treat them like an investment portfolio <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:21:20">[0:21:20]</a>:
*   **80% in "Index Funds"**: Allocate 80% of investment to reliable channels that consistently produce results year over year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:21:31">[0:21:31]</a>.
*   **20% in "Rocket Ships"**: Allocate 20% to experimental channels with the potential for massive future [[steps_for_growing_a_business_to_10_million|growth]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:21:47">[0:21:47]</a>. This diversified approach prevents reliance on a single channel and provides backup if one fails <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:22:02">[0:22:02]</a>.

### Decision-Making: Impact vs. Effort Matrix
A CEO should be skilled at making decisions using an Impact vs. Effort matrix <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:24:45">[0:24:45]</a>.
*   **Evaluation**: Opportunities are ranked from 1 to 5 for effort and 1 to 5 for potential impact <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:25:22">[0:25:22]</a>.
*   **Ideal Opportunities**: Look for "10 out of 10 ideas" â€“ those that are easy to do (low effort) but will have a massive impact on the business <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:25:26">[0:25:26]</a>.
*   **Prioritization**: It's crucial to rank new opportunities, like potential acquisitions, against existing opportunities within the current business that might offer a higher return on investment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:26:08">[0:26:08]</a>. Acquisitions, especially large ones, can be high effort (e.g., 18-month process) with no guaranteed impact, making them a "6 out of 10" idea compared to maximizing existing internal opportunities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:28:50">[0:28:50]</a>.

### Team Structure and Incentives
Properly structuring incentives is critical for alignment and performance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:08:21">[0:08:21]</a>.
*   **COO Compensation**: Instead of incentivizing a COO solely on profit (which can lead to cost-cutting at the expense of [[steps_for_growing_a_business_to_10_million|growth]]) or solely on revenue (which can lead to unsustainable spending), a 50/50 split between top-line revenue and bottom-line profitability is ideal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:09:35">[0:09:35]</a>. This aligns the COO's goals with overall founder objectives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:09:42">[0:09:42]</a>.
*   **Equity vs. Cash Bonuses**: Studies show no significant increase in performance from handing out equity, largely because employees often don't understand or value it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:11:16">[0:11:16]</a>. A better approach is to provide a total compensation package and allow the employee to decide how much is allocated to cash bonus versus equity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:11:46">[0:11:46]</a>. This gives them "skin in the game" and creates alignment by making it a conscious investment decision <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:14:14">[0:14:14]</a>.

### Identifying Bottlenecks and Hiring
Businesses have two core functions: sales and delivery <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:41:14">[0:41:14]</a>.
*   **Bottleneck Identification**: Determine whether the bottleneck is in sales (not enough leads, insufficient salespeople, poor onboarding) or delivery (lack of automation, quality issues, high churn) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:42:20">[0:42:20]</a>.
*   **Hiring in Your Zone of Genius**: When hiring executives, it's often more effective to hire in your "zone of genius" first <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:43:08">[0:43:08]</a>. This allows the founder to properly train, evaluate, and understand what "genius" looks like in that role <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:43:24">[0:43:24]</a>.
*   **Identifying Your Zone of Genius**: Your zone of genius is where you naturally feel excited, where you tinker in the morning, and what activities you prioritize on your calendar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:44:43">[0:44:43]</a>.

## Avoiding Common Pitfalls

Many founders make the mistake of ignoring foundational issues early on, akin to "building a skyscraper on a sandy foundation" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:14:50">[0:14:50]</a>. This leads to issues like high churn and increased competition, causing burnout and company collapse around mid-seven figures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:15:05">[0:15:05]</a>. Early focus on fundamentals and retention is crucial for long-term [[execution_and_scaling_strategies_for_startups|scaling]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:15:34">[0:15:34]</a>.

## The [[founder_to_ceo_transition_for_business_growth|Founder to CEO Transition]]

Founders build, and CEOs manage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:31:59">[0:31:59]</a>. The most valuable part of the value chain is discovering "flight" (product-market fit) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:32:06">[0:32:06]</a>. However, many founders abandon their successful creations to start the next thing without handing off the baton <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:32:17">[0:32:17]</a>.
*   **The CEO's Role**: Once a business reaches mid-seven figures, the founder's job is to assess if they are the right CEO for the next phase of [[steps_for_growing_a_business_to_10_million|growth]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:32:39">[0:32:39]</a>. If not, they should hire a COO or president to execute their vision, or a CEO if they are not passionate about that specific business <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:32:47">[0:32:47]</a>.
*   **Becoming the "Laziest Person"**: The ultimate goal for a founder transitioning to CEO is to have nothing on their plate, allowing them to think quarters or even years ahead for the business's strategic direction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:46:13">[0:46:13]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:47:35">[0:47:35]</a>. This long-term thinking is a luxury typically afforded to larger, profitable businesses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:48:21">[0:48:21]</a>.

## Relentless Focus and Hyper-Niching

A key to [[steps_for_growing_a_business_to_10_million|business growth]] is deep understanding and focus on the ideal customer, leading to hyper-niching <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:37:57">[0:37:57]</a>.
*   **AppSumo Example**: By analyzing customer data, AppSumo realized 80% of their top customers were marketing agencies using software to reduce monthly expenses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:38:22">[0:38:22]</a>.
*   **Test Then Invest**: Initially, AppSumo increased their software deals from one per quarter to one per month as an experiment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:39:04">[0:39:04]</a>. Seeing positive results, they gradually increased to launching one software lifetime deal daily <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:39:18">[0:39:18]</a>.
*   **Benefits of Focus**: By focusing on what their ideal customer wanted, AppSumo increased customer loyalty and referrals, improved negotiation power with software founders, and ultimately tripled their business by increasing customer lifetime value and slashing acquisition costs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:39:56">[0:39:56]</a>.
*   **80/20 for Experiments**: Allocate 20% of money, time, and energy to experiments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:40:21">[0:40:21]</a>. Founders should be responsible for discovering new revenue channels, then hiring to scale those discoveries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:41:40">[0:41:40]</a>.

Ultimately, the market rewards winners <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:36:13">[0:36:13]</a>. If entering a market, the goal should be to be the best, because the majority of value goes to the top performer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:36:17">[0:36:17]</a>. Dedicating oneself for five years to become the best in a chosen arena can lead to a $100 million business and a life-changing exit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="0:37:21">[0:37:21]</a>.

Starting with a course or a book is considered the easiest way to begin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:08">[00:00:08]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:51">[00:03:51]</a>.

## Initial Pricing Considerations

It's common to believe that a subscription model guarantees monthly recurring revenue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:18">[00:00:18]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:55">[00:18:55]</a>. However, it's worth questioning if a subscription is always necessary <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:29">[00:00:29]</a>. A key principle is that recurring payments should align with recurring value <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:35">[00:00:35]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:38">[00:19:38]</a>. This often applies to software-as-a-service (SaaS) products, such as scheduling tools, where continuous value is provided <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:44">[00:19:44]</a>.

Many products can benefit from a one-time payment or a credit-based system, which can be easier to sell as it avoids the customer objection of potential cancellations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:57">[00:19:57]</a>.

## Setting Your Price

For initial product launches, it is advised to start with a paywall rather than offering things for free, unless there's a strong passion or clear strategy for a free offering <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:24">[00:16:24]</a>. This approach provides valuable learning <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:33">[00:16:33]</a>.

Avoid pricing products too cheaply, as this can lead to undervaluation by customers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:38">[00:16:38]</a>. Pricing a little higher than expected can also provide the necessary motivation for the creator <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:51">[00:16:51]</a>.

When unsure about pricing, consider looking at competitors' prices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:57">[00:16:57]</a>. Artificial intelligence (AI) tools, such as Claude, can also be used as a "pricing partner" to refine initial pricing based on competitive data <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:28">[00:17:28]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:07">[00:18:07]</a>.

It's important to remember that pricing can always be adjusted <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:29">[00:18:29]</a>. Experimenting with different [[strategies_for_pricing_and_client_acquisition | pricing models]], such as starting with cheap subscriptions and long free trials, then removing trials and pricing higher or even removing subscriptions entirely, is a valid approach <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:36">[00:18:36]</a>.

## Alternative Pricing Models

Beyond traditional subscriptions, other models exist:
*   **One-time payment:** Suitable for courses or ebooks that provide value upfront without ongoing updates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:05">[00:17:05]</a>.
*   **Credit system:** A model where customers purchase credits to use a service, allowing for usage-based payments without a recurring commitment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:03">[00:20:03]</a>.
*   **Pay-per-usage (e.g., Stripe's fees):** While beneficial for the business (more use equals more revenue), customers may dislike variable costs, especially if they are high percentage-based fees without tiered discounts for higher usage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:26">[00:22:26]</a>.
*   **Decreasing subscription:** A smart model where the initial months are priced higher, and the price decreases over time. This incentivizes customers to stay to benefit from accumulated value <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:18">[00:23:18]</a>.

## Monetization Through Gamification

Gamification can be a powerful [[monetization_strategies_for_mobile_apps | monetization strategy]] for digital products aimed at habit formation. For example, the "Pokemon Sleep" app uses gamification where better sleep leads to Pokemon growth and evolution, generating significant revenue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:28">[00:26:28]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:02">[00:27:02]</a>.

This approach leverages intellectual property licensing, combining popular brands with real-life goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:08">[00:28:08]</a>. Licensing well-known intellectual property can be surprisingly affordable, even for major brands <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:55">[00:28:55]</a>. This strategy allows creators to build on existing emotional connections, making products more engaging and marketable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:09">[00:30:09]</a>.

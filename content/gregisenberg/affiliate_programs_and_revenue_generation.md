## How Affiliate Programs Work

SaaS (Software as a Service) products commonly offer affiliate commissions, ranging from 10% to 50% of monthly recurring revenue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:50">[00:04:50]</a>. This model allows affiliates to earn ongoing income from customers they refer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:57">[00:04:57]</a>. By naturally including affiliate products in workflows and sharing links in descriptions, a recurring revenue stream can be established <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:06">[00:06:06]</a>.

Even large companies like Adobe, a multi-billion dollar company, utilize robust affiliate programs, actively engaging with short-form creators through brand deals and offers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:04">[00:06:04]</a>. Adobe has been observed increasing their affiliate cut, indicating the success of this strategy for them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:28">[00:07:28]</a>. Canva also has an affiliate program <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:37">[00:07:37]</a>.

## Affiliate Marketing as a Revenue Driver

For individuals in the print-on-demand space, affiliate marketing is a significant source of income, often exceeding earnings from courses and YouTube ad revenue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:20">[00:05:20]</a>. Many creators in this field are reportedly making over $500,000 per year combined from courses, affiliate income, and YouTube ad revenue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:27">[00:05:27]</a>.

## Integration with Content Creation

Affiliate marketing can be seamlessly integrated with content creation strategies, such as YouTube channels and newsletters, to create a multi-faceted [[monetizing_internet_businesses_with_affiliate_marketing_and_seo | monetization strategy]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:04">[00:04:04]</a>.

For example, a YouTube channel teaching "how-to" tutorials (e.g., "how to do XYZ on Canva") can serve as a lead generation funnel for an agency, while also generating ad revenue and building a newsletter list <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:36">[00:03:36]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:49">[00:03:49]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:51">[00:03:51]</a>. Each new video can be emailed to the newsletter subscribers, where ads can be sold, creating multiple revenue streams <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:02">[00:04:02]</a>.

### Layered Monetization Phases

This approach can evolve in phases:
*   **Phase 1: Creator & Client Service** Initially, the creator can service clients directly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:09">[00:09:09]</a>.
*   **Phase 2: Agency Scaling** This can scale into managing hundreds of clients with a team of offshore talent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:17">[00:04:17]</a>. An agency focused on "how to do XYZ on Canva" could potentially generate $80,000 per month at an 80% margin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:40">[00:04:40]</a>.
*   **Phase 3: Software Development** Identifying limitations within existing tools (like Canva) can lead to building custom applications that solve these problems, immediately having an audience and a newsletter for distribution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:45">[00:07:45]</a>. This represents a natural progression from creator to agency to software <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:05">[00:08:05]</a>.

The process of generating content for these funnels can involve creating long-form videos (e.g., 20 minutes or even two hours) that teach step-by-step tutorials, leading viewers to hire the creator for tasks they don't have time for <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:27">[00:03:27]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:27">[00:09:27]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:05">[00:10:05]</a>. This strategy, sometimes called a "video sales letter" (VSL), has shown success in selling online courses and services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:00">[00:10:00]</a>.

## Newsletter Monetization as a Business Opportunity

Short-form creators, who often struggle to make substantial money in B2B spaces, can build newsletters for their target audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:52">[00:37:52]</a>.

### Strategy:
1.  **Audience Identification:** Gather emails of individuals interested in a specific niche (e.g., interior designers, architects for a design-focused creator) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:00">[00:38:00]</a>.
2.  **Content Creation:** The creator records a 30-minute video discussing weekly insights or interesting topics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:18">[00:38:18]</a>. This audio is transcribed and transformed into a unique email newsletter, mimicking the creator's voice and vocabulary <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:29">[00:38:29]</a>.
3.  **Monetization:**
    *   **Beehive Boost:** Use platforms like Beehive Boost for initial revenue to self-fund growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:02">[00:39:02]</a>.
    *   **Direct Ads:** Sell ad space within the newsletter to companies in related industries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:06">[00:39:06]</a>.
4.  **Scaling:** Aggregating multiple newsletters across different niches allows for larger media buys from companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:09">[00:41:09]</a>.

A newsletter with 100,000 subscribers, especially in B2B, can be valued as a digital asset worth between $100,000 (at $1/subscriber) and $1,000,000 (at $10/subscriber) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:21">[00:41:21]</a>. This creates a potential exit opportunity for creators, where they can sell the list and walk away <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:26">[00:42:26]</a>. This business model requires minimal time commitment from creators (around two hours a month) and can involve a revenue share model <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:56">[00:41:56]</a>.

> [!CAUTION] Shady Version of Newsletter Monetization
> A "gray" version of this business involves building a newsletter list for an existing creator without their initial knowledge, using their publicly available content (e.g., podcast transcripts) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:07">[00:43:07]</a>. Once the list reaches a substantial size (e.g., 10,000 subscribers), the builder can approach the creator for a partnership <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:23">[00:43:23]</a>. Alternatively, a more ethically questionable approach involves building a large list (e.g., 100,000 subscribers) and selling it without the creator's knowledge <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:36">[00:43:36]</a>.

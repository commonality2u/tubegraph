This article outlines a detailed process for identifying and validating niche markets and startup ideas using Reddit and AI tools. The methodology has been shown to result in multi-million dollar businesses due to its focus on finding "validated ideas" that resonate with people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:24">[00:00:24]</a>. The core concept involves "teleporting to different communities" to observe, write, and generate ideas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:24">[00:20:24]</a>.

*   **Gummy Search**: A tool used to analyze Reddit trends and community discussions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:31">[00:01:31]</a>. It offers free and paid plans <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:34">[00:01:34]</a>.
*   **Perplexity.ai**: An AI tool used to find popular influencers and content creators in a specific niche <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:38">[00:13:38]</a>. It is free to sign up <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:40">[00:13:40]</a>.

*   **Framer**: A website builder for quickly wireframing and launching ideas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:05">[00:17:05]</a>.

## Four-Step Process for Finding Validated Startup Ideas

### Step 1: Finding Trending Subreddits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:02">[00:01:02]</a>

The initial step involves using Gummy Search to identify trending subreddits, which are potential niche markets.

*   **Access the Trending Section**: Navigate to the "trending" section within Gummy Search <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:40">[00:01:40]</a>.
*   **Sort by Growth**: Observe how fast subreddits are growing daily, weekly, monthly, or yearly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:56">[00:01:56]</a>.
*   **Focus on Niche Sizes**: Instead of large subreddits, concentrate on those with **10,000 to 100,000 members** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:17">[00:02:17]</a>. These "newer communities" are often trending and answer the "why now" question, indicating timely opportunities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:28">[00:02:28]</a>. This step is key for [[choosing_and_validating_a_niche | choosing and validating a niche]].
*   **Identify Personal Advantage**: As you scroll through subreddits, consider if you have an "unfair advantage" in that area (e.g., personal experience or connections) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:58">[00:02:58]</a>.
*   **Take Notes**: Document interesting subreddits and initial thoughts in a notebook or Google Doc <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:25">[00:03:25]</a>.
*   **Review Regularly**: Check Gummy Search every few weeks to discover new trends <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:09">[00:04:09]</a>. Gummy Search also offers a "curated section" of subreddits, for example, for software engineers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:15">[00:04:15]</a>.

### Step 2: Identifying Problems and Solutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:56">[00:04:56]</a>

Once a promising subreddit is identified, the next step involves using Gummy Search to uncover the community's problems and potential solutions, which are crucial for [[identifying_an_audience_and_niche_before_building | identifying an audience and niche before building]].

*   **Create an Audience**: In Gummy Search, add the chosen subreddit (and similar ones) to an "audience" (e.g., "people into dog food") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:57">[00:05:57]</a>.
*   **Analyze "Pain and Anger"**: This section, summarized by AI, highlights common patterns of pain points and frustrations within the community <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:53">[00:06:53]</a>. For example, in the dog food community, pain points might include "potty training challenges," "dogs reluctant to walk outside," or "eating habits/loss of appetite" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:58">[00:07:58]</a>. These insights generate immediate startup ideas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:18">[00:08:18]</a>.
*   **Review "Advice and Requests"**: This section reveals what products or services people are actively seeking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:08">[00:10:08]</a>. Users are "screaming" for solutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:36">[00:10:36]</a>. Examples include dog breeding recommendations or specific dog food recommendations for senior dogs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:55">[00:10:55]</a>.
*   **Examine "Solution Requests"**: This provides insight into the competitive landscape, existing products, and how people are currently addressing their problems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:40">[00:12:40]</a>.
*   **Document Ideas**: Continuously write down potential startup ideas based on the problems and solutions identified <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:54">[00:08:54]</a>. Even if similar businesses exist, opportunities may arise from poor marketing or a lack of social media presence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:20">[00:09:20]</a>. This aids in [[validating_and_finding_niche_ideas_for_directories | validating and finding niche ideas]].

### Step 3: Finding Creators in the Niche <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:34">[00:13:34]</a>

This step helps understand the audience and content better, which is crucial for [[targeting_niche_markets_with_seo_strategies | targeting niche markets with SEO strategies]].

*   **Identify Influencers**: Use Perplexity.ai to find the "most popular" or "educational" YouTubers/influencers in the chosen niche <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:44">[00:13:44]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:50">[00:14:50]</a>.
*   **Analyze Popular Content**: Visit their YouTube channels and sort videos by "most popular" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:12">[00:15:12]</a>.
*   **Read Comments**: Go through the comments section of these popular videos. This reveals specific audience desires, thoughts, and unaddressed needs, helping to "put yourself in their shoes" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:37">[00:15:37]</a>.
*   **Update Notes**: Add learnings from creator content and comments to your one-pager <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:13">[00:16:13]</a>. This identifies [[identifying_market_opportunities_in_specific_industries | market opportunities in specific industries]].

### Step 4: Wireframing and Naming <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:19">[00:16:19]</a>

The final step is to conceptualize and name the startup idea.

*   **Wireframe the Solution**: Based on the gathered insights, wireframe a website or outline the product/service <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:46">[00:16:46]</a>. The goal is to create a simple MVP (Minimum Viable Product) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:32">[00:09:32]</a>.
    *   *Example*: An insight about designers not focusing on conversion led to wireframing a "design agency that cared about copy and conversion," resulting in "designscience.com" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:31">[00:16:31]</a>. This could apply to [[opportunities_in_niche_app_markets_and_sectors | niche app markets]].
*   **Develop a Catchy Name**: Choose a name that resonates with the audience and is "validated" by the insights <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:54">[00:17:54]</a>. Sometimes, a company can even be named after a subreddit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:42">[00:17:42]</a>.

## Content Creation

The same methodology used for finding startup ideas can be applied to create content people will love, which is essential for building an audience and community <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:13">[00:01:13]</a>. This is relevant to [[kindle_direct_publishing_and_niche_markets | Kindle Direct Publishing and niche markets]] and [[building_niche_publishing_houses | building niche publishing houses]].

*   **Analyze "Top Content"**: In Gummy Search, go to the "Top Content" section for your chosen audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:20">[00:18:20]</a>.
*   **Identify Patterns and Formats**: Observe what types of content (e.g., long stories, memes, specific topics like "loss of beloved pet" or "saving a dog's life") are best performing and resonate with the community <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:37">[00:18:37]</a>.
*   **Ask Questions**: Use Gummy Search as an "entrepreneurial assistant" to ask questions about the community's content preferences (e.g., "Do they love memes?") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:18">[00:19:18]</a>. This can inform [[subscription_services_in_niche_markets | subscription services in niche markets]] based on content.

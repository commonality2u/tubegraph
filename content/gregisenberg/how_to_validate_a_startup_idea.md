## Kettle and Fire: A Case Study in Validation (2014)

Justin Mayers began his entrepreneurial journey in 2014 while working at Rackspace, seeking to start his own company <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:29">[00:01:29]</a>. He had heard about bone broth from friends in CrossFit and was reading about its benefits for skin, gut, and joint health <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:40">[00:01:40]</a>. At the time, bone broth was not widely discussed or available online <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:46">[00:01:46]</a>.

### Identifying the Problem & Initial Questions

Mayers recognized a personal need for bone broth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:56">[00:01:56]</a>. His key questions were:
1.  How many people want this product? <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:01">[00:02:01]</a>
2.  Are there enough people willing to spend enough on the product to build a meaningful business? <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:06">[00:02:06]</a>

### Step 1: Validating Market Interest

To gauge interest, Mayers conducted both micro and macro research:
*   **Micro-level (Community Engagement)**: He looked at online communities where his target audience (Paleo, CrossFit) congregated, such as Mark's Daily Apple, Paleo Hacks, and Reddit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:30">[00:02:30]</a>. He observed highly engaged threads discussing bone broth recipes, sourcing bones, and cooking times, indicating a small but "fanatic" group of interested people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:53">[00:02:53]</a>.
*   **Macro-level (Trend Analysis)**: He used Google Trends to see how "bone broth" was trending, noting it was starting to take off <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:17">[00:03:17]</a>. This step helped validate that the idea could be a "decent business" rather than just a side project <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:45">[00:04:45]</a>.
    *   Mayers emphasized that Google Trends validated a problem he already experienced and saw others discussing, rather than being the sole source of his idea <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:17">[00:04:17]</a>. If Google Trends showed no sign of life and the next step (economic validation) failed, he would not have started the business <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:03">[00:05:03]</a>.

Initially, Mayers was aiming for a business that could pay him and his brother $10,000 per month, which would require only a couple of thousand customers spending a few hundred dollars a year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:37">[00:03:37]</a>.

### Step 2: Validating Economic Viability (Will People Pay?)

This was considered the most critical step <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:05">[00:06:05]</a>. Mayers created a minimal viable product (MVP) for validation:
1.  **Domain Name**: Purchased the cheapest bone broth-related domain name available for $9.99 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:11">[00:06:11]</a>.
2.  **Placeholder Logo & Renders**: Hired someone on Fiverr for $5 to create a basic logo and product renders <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:35">[00:06:35]</a>.
3.  **Landing Page**: Built a landing page using Unbounce (a tool from 2015) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:50">[00:06:50]</a>.
    *   The page highlighted value propositions identified from Paleo forums, such as healing leaky gut, convenience (due to the 24-48 hour cooking time for bone broth), and quality (organic ingredients, 100% grass-fed bones) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:04">[00:07:04]</a>.
4.  **Pricing Strategy**: Set a price of $29.99 for 16 ounces, significantly higher than typical grocery store broth ($5 for 16 ounces) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:45">[00:07:45]</a>. The rationale was that if people would pay six times more for a product that didn't exist, it was a strong indicator of demand <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:58">[00:07:58]</a>.
5.  **Fake Checkout Flow**: The "Order Now" button on the landing page directed users to Mayers' personal PayPal account for payment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:20">[00:08:20]</a>.
6.  **Paid Advertising**: Spent approximately $100 on Bing Ads (which was cheaper than Google Ads at the time) to drive traffic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:51">[00:08:51]</a>.
    *   Within a two-week test, despite a "super sketchy checkout flow" and no actual product, people converted <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:14">[00:09:14]</a>. The conversion rate was about 30% for clicking the "order now" button <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:22">[00:09:22]</a>.
    *   He generated almost $500 in revenue from about $250 in ad spend, concluding that a real product could generate around $100,000 in annual sales <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:35">[00:09:35]</a>.

### Post-Validation Steps

After validating demand and willingness to pay, Mayers took the next steps:
1.  **Transparency with Customers**: Emailed everyone who placed an order, explaining the product wasn't in stock yet. He offered a full refund or a 50% discount for waiting (originally optimistically stated as "a few weeks," which turned into eight months) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:04">[00:11:04]</a>. Most chose the discount <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:40">[00:11:40]</a>.
2.  **Product Development**: Spent the next eight months figuring out how to actually make and scale the product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:08">[00:12:08]</a>.

## Modern Validation with AI (2025 Perspective)

Justin Mayers notes that while the core principle of trying to sell a product before it exists remains <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:14">[00:13:14]</a>, [[ai_startup_ideas | AI]] and other tools could significantly enhance the validation process today.

### Improvements with AI
*   **Enhanced Landing Pages**: [[AIdriven startup ideas and entrepreneurship | AI tools]] can create much better landing pages and product renders than was possible in 2015 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:43">[00:12:43]</a>.
*   **Sentiment Analysis**: [[using_ai_tools_for_generating_startup_ideas | AI tools]] can quickly scrape and perform sentiment analysis across Reddit, forums, and Twitter to gauge public interest and pain points <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:58">[00:12:58]</a>.
*   **Rapid Prototyping/Design**: Tools like ChatGPT-4o can generate product designs based on existing images and prompts, dramatically reducing the time and cost of design <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:41">[00:20:41]</a>. This reduces barriers to building CPG brands related to design and marketing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:08">[00:24:08]</a>.
*   **Lowering Barriers**: [[AI startup ideas and incubation | AI tools]] empower individuals to build products that previously required large teams, enabling "one guy in a basement" to create high-quality solutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:58">[00:19:58]</a>.

### Core Principle Remains
The fundamental principle of getting 10 people to pay for a product that doesn't yet exist is still a "massively validating" exercise <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:31">[00:13:31]</a>. This can be achieved through direct messaging on Twitter, emailing, or texting friends <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:24">[0:13:24]</a>.

## General Advice on Finding [[startup_idea_generation_and_evaluation | Startup Ideas]]

Mayers advises aspiring entrepreneurs to:
*   **Follow Personal Passion**: The most successful ventures often stem from deep personal interest. For example, Mayers' interest in health led him to be early on trends like bone broth, keto, and non-alcoholic beverages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:31">[00:14:31]</a>.
*   **Identify Frontier Problems**: Focus on "big problems in the world that have relatively few people focused on them" or where energy is starting to move <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:07">[00:17:07]</a>. Examples include the chronic disease crisis and issues with the food system <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:21">[00:17:21]</a>.
*   **Circle the Problem**: Continuously explore a problem that "lights you up" until you find interesting ideas that could solve it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:45">[00:17:45]</a>. If you can address a significant problem like health, you can build a successful business <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:04">[00:18:04]</a>.

To rapidly validate and build, Mayers suggests familiarizing oneself with current [[AI startup ideas | AI tools]]:

*   **Rok**: Another general-purpose tool <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:34">[00:16:34]</a>.
*   **Frontier AI Models**: Experiment with models like Claude, GPT, and Gemini <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:42">[00:16:42]</a>. Becoming a "top 1% user" of these tools is considered high leverage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:50">[00:16:50]</a>.
*   **ChatGPT-4o (or similar image generation AI)**: For generating product designs and renders <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:19">[00:20:19]</a>. This can enable a "startup in like a day" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:23">[00:19:23]</a>, allowing entrepreneurs to test dozens of [[generating_startup_ideas_and_audience_feedback | startup ideas]] per month <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:55">[00:19:55]</a>.

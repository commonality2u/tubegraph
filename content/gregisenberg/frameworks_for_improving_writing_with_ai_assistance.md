## Fundamental Writing Process with AI

The process of writing with [[using_ai_tools_for_content_creation | AI tools]] involves several stages, moving from an initial idea to a structured piece of content.

### 1. Starting with a Seed Idea

Most writing processes begin with a "seed of an idea" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:25">[00:06:25]</a>. For example, Reed Hastings' "strange rule" at Netflix where no one was allowed to say "Blockbuster" in meetings because Netflix wasn't competing with Blockbuster, but rather with the *guilt* of late fees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:30">[00:05:30]</a>.

### 2. Defining the Idea's Scope

The first decision is whether the idea is the main topic of the piece or a sub-point within a larger idea <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:12">[00:08:12]</a>. For instance, Reed Hastings' rule could be *the* piece or one point in an article about "three CEOs who had strange rules at the beginning of their startup journeys" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:08">[00:09:08]</a>.

### 3. Setting Clear Goals

Before writing, it is smart to define clear goals for the content piece <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:39">[00:11:39]</a>. For example:
*   Connect to startup ideas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:55">[00:10:55]</a>.
*   Be actionable (e.g., how to get started) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:04">[00:11:04]</a>.
*   Connect to the Reed Hastings story <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:10">[00:11:10]</a>.

### 4. Prompting AI for Ideas and Structure

[[generating_content_with_ai_tools | AI tools]] like ChatGPT or Claude can be used to generate ideas, but a baseline understanding of how things work is necessary to prompt effectively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:29">[00:07:29]</a>.

There are two main ways to interact with [[generating_content_with_ai_tools | AI models]]:
*   **Conversationally:** Chunking things out piece by piece, like a dialogue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:09">[00:13:09]</a>.
*   **Massive Prompt:** Assembling a comprehensive prompt that accounts for potential situations and hypotheticals to generate a specific output <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:15">[00:13:15]</a>.

For initial ideation, start conversationally <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:45">[00:13:45]</a>. The goal is for [[generating_content_with_ai_tools | AI]] to feed ideas, not to start writing yet <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:47">[00:12:47]</a>.

For example, provide the seed idea and goals, then ask for potential headlines and 3-5 main points under each <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:51">[00:13:51]</a>. This defers the lower-leverage task of generating many iterations to the [[utilizing_ai_for_writing_and_content_creation | AI]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:30">[00:16:30]</a>.

### 5. Elevating to Editor-in-Chief Role

In the publishing world, editors give prompts to columnists who then generate ideas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:47">[00:16:47]</a>. With [[utilizing_ai_for_writing_and_content_creation | AI]], writers can elevate themselves to the role of editor-in-chief, where their primary value becomes "taste" – selecting the best options generated by the [[utilizing_ai_for_writing_and_content_creation | AI]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:21">[00:17:21]</a>.

### 6. Applying Frameworks Across Mediums

Writing frameworks work for everything and can be used across any medium <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:44">[00:18:44]</a>.

#### Using Templates
One powerful technique is to use successful content (e.g., a viral Twitter hook) as a template <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:08">[00:20:08]</a>. This is "stealing the architecture" or "bones of the house," not the content itself <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:29">[00:20:29]</a>. Writers already do this intuitively; [[utilizing_ai_for_writing_and_content_creation | AI]] simply makes the process conscious <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:01">[00:21:01]</a>.

To train a mini-model within the [[utilizing_ai_for_writing_and_content_creation | AI]]:
1.  Provide an example of a successful piece (e.g., a viral hook).
2.  Manually create a new version of that piece using your idea, following the template.
3.  Then, prompt the [[utilizing_ai_for_writing_and_content_creation | AI]] to generate more variations based on your example <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:16">[00:24:16]</a>. This shows the [[utilizing_ai_for_writing_and_content_creation | AI]] what you want it to learn from <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:56">[00:25:56]</a>.

### 7. Structuring Long-Form Content

For an 800-word article, the easiest structure is typically an introduction followed by three main sections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:49">[00:28:49]</a>.

#### The Promise of the Headline and Main Points
Ninety percent of a piece's value is determined by the headline and the quality of the three main points <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:39">[00:29:39]</a>. If the main points are vague, readers will perceive the content as worthless and leave <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:29">[00:30:29]</a>. Tangible and specific main points inherently make readers want to read the actual content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:47">[00:31:47]</a>.

#### The "10 Magical Ways" Framework
This framework helps remove writer's block at every stage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:15">[00:35:15]</a>. It involves clarifying what type of content will be given to the reader (e.g., tips, stats, steps, lessons) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:54">[00:35:54]</a>.

*   **Headline Clarity** A vague headline like "What it means for your startup" can be made more tangible by specifying the content, e.g., "...and three lessons that can be learned from the $361 billion streaming service" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:15">[00:36:15]</a>. Including big numbers in headlines often performs well <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:37">[00:36:37]</a>.
*   **Main Points Mirror Headline** The three subheads should clearly deliver on the promise made in the headline <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:33">[00:38:33]</a>. If the headline promises "lessons," the subheads should be "Lesson Number One," "Lesson Number Two," etc. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:16">[00:38:16]</a>.
*   **Prioritize Compelling Points** Once main points are identified, stack-rank them by interest or compulsion and make the most compelling point the first <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:29">[00:41:29]</a>.
*   **Tangible Subheads** The best main points and subheads are often full sentences, not just clever titles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:10">[00:44:10]</a>. They clearly state what the reader will gain from that section, increasing engagement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:17">[00:44:17]</a>.

### 8. Iterative Improvement with AI

Even after selecting preferred headlines and main points, one can stress-test the quality by asking [[utilizing_ai_for_writing_and_content_creation | AI]] to generate more batches of alternative lessons <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:19">[00:42:19]</a>. This conversational back-and-forth helps in learning what works best and eventually creating a "mega prompt" that compresses the entire process into a single, efficient command <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:32">[00:45:32]</a>.

### 9. Completing the Writing

Once the three main points are finalized, revisit the "10 Magical Ways" to determine what specific type of content will be provided within each section (e.g., steps, stats, examples, reasons why) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:02">[00:47:02]</a>. This pre-planning makes the actual writing much easier, as all major decisions are already made <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:38">[00:48:38]</a>.

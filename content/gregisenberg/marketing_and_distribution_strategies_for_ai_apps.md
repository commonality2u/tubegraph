## Key Principles for Growth and Virality

A core philosophy for marketing [[AI apps and AI startups | AI apps]] involves an iterative process of generating virality from early stages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:55:00">[08:55:00]</a>. This includes constantly developing new "hooks" and exploring different ways to position the product for various target audiences, followed by continuous split testing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:00:00">[09:00:00]</a>.

### Designing for Virality

Product design should inherently consider social media virality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:58:00">[13:58:00]</a>. By split testing different features or tools within an application on social media, developers can identify what resonates most with users and drives engagement, informing further design choices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:50:00">[13:50:00]</a>. For instance, the "six-factor Madden rating system" in the Umax app was highly successful because it was engaging and shareable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:03:00">[14:03:00]</a>.

### Optimizing for Market Dominance

Instead of strictly optimizing for profit per user, some strategies advocate for optimizing for the number of users raised to a power greater than one, multiplied by profit per user <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:51">[00:24:51]</a>. This means accepting a lower lifetime value (LTV) per download in exchange for a larger user base <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:09">[00:25:09]</a>. The rationale is to more quickly dominate the market and increase word-of-mouth growth, which is often difficult to measure but highly impactful <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:38">[00:25:38]</a>.

## [[Influencer Marketing Strategies for Apps | Influencer Marketing]]

Effective [[Influencer Marketing Strategies for Apps | influencer marketing]] for [[AI apps and AI startups | AI apps]] emphasizes building personal relationships with creators rather than relying solely on marketplaces or agencies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:03:00">[11:03:00]</a>.

### Building Relationships
Influencers receive hundreds of daily messages, making it hard for them to differentiate serious companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:49:00">[11:49:00]</a>. By embedding oneself within the industry and developing personal relationships, companies can gain trust and access to valuable connections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:00:00">[12:00:00]</a>.

### Outreach and Engagement
Initially, it's crucial to prioritize both quantity and quality in outreach, contacting thousands of influencers across all possible platforms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:43:00">[12:43:00]</a>. After establishing initial contact, treating influencers well and fostering friendships can open up endless opportunities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:06:00">[13:06:00]</a>.

## [[Monetization strategies for AI applications | Monetization Strategies]] and Paywalls

A common and efficient [[strategies_for_pricing_and_monetization_in_ai | monetization strategy]] involves taking users through an onboarding flow that collects personal information and customizes the application, before presenting a paywall at the end <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:35">[00:26:35]</a>. This approach leverages the user's invested time and excitement to increase conversion rates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:35">[00:27:35]</a>.

### Tools for Paywall Testing
Superwall is highlighted as an excellent platform for easily split-testing different paywall placements and designs, providing comprehensive data on conversion rates and user engagement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:16">[00:24:16]</a>.

### Ethical Considerations
While effective for conversion, pushing paywalls after significant user investment may raise ethical questions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:12">[00:27:12]</a>. However, some argue that the efficiency it brings allows for greater market penetration and faster progress toward the market's "inevitable end state" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:31">[00:28:31]</a>.

## Naming Conventions for AI Apps

The decision to include "AI" in an app's name depends on the industry and desired perception <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:17">[00:20:17]</a>:
*   **Adding "AI" is valuable** when creating innovation in a relatively stagnant industry (e.g., Cal AI for calorie tracking) as it conveys sophistication <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:27">[00:20:27]</a>.
*   **Avoiding "AI" is prudent** for services requiring 100% accuracy (e.g., medical document services) where minor errors are unacceptable, as the term "AI" might cause skepticism or fear <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:52">[00:20:52]</a>.

Ultimately, a good name, such as "Cal AI," can significantly contribute to an app's success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:25">[00:22:25]</a>.

## Competing with Incumbents

When facing large existing applications like Duolingo or MyFitnessPal, the strategy is not necessarily to "reinvent the wheel," but to leverage new AI functionality and novel distribution strategies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:00">[00:32:00]</a>.
*   **Innovation and Agility**: Larger companies may not iterate or progress as quickly due to internal incentives or lack of direct equity for engineers and social media managers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:46">[00:30:46]</a>.
*   **New Market Share**: Instead of just competing for existing market share, [[integration_of_ai_tools_in_gaming_and_marketing | AI-first apps]] can create entirely new market segments by offering personalized and dynamically updating functionalities that were previously impossible or too costly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:50">[00:33:50]</a>.
*   **Focus on Execution**: Even with seemingly "obvious ideas" like calorie tracking, language learning, or journaling, applying modern execution methods in building functionality and distribution can lead to massive applications <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:55">[00:37:55]</a>.

## The AI-Assisted Content Creation Process
The process of [[aiassisted_writing_processes_and_links | writing with AI]] is about working *with* AI, not deferring writing to it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:05">[00:40:05]</a>. It requires a baseline understanding of how content should work so that AI can be prompted effectively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:36">[00:07:36]</a>.

### Starting with a Seed Idea
Most writing processes begin with a "seed of an idea" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:25">[00:06:25]</a>. For example, the idea of Netflix founder Reed Hastings banning the word "Blockbuster" in early meetings because Netflix wasn't competing with Blockbuster, but with "guilt" (referencing late fees) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:21">[00:05:21]</a>.

The first decision is whether the idea is the main theme of the piece or a sub-point within a larger idea <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:12">[00:08:12]</a>. For instance, the Reed Hastings story could be the focal point of an article, or one of three strange rules CEOs had at the start of their startup journeys <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:00">[00:09:00]</a>.

### Defining Content Goals
Before writing, it's crucial to summarize the content goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:46">[00:10:46]</a>. For the Reed Hastings example, goals could include:
*   Connecting to [[strategies_for_media_and_content_creation | startup ideas]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:55">[00:10:55]</a>.
*   Being actionable (e.g., how to get started) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:04">[00:11:04]</a>.
*   Integrating the Reed Hastings story <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:10">[00:11:10]</a>.

Having clear goals is a smart approach to writing any piece <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:46">[00:11:46]</a>.

### Prompting AI for Ideas and Headlines
When narrowing down an idea, use AI (like ChatGPT or Claude) to feed ideas, rather than immediately writing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:47">[00:12:47]</a>.

There are two primary ways to interact with AI models <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:00">[00:13:00]</a>:
1.  **Conversational**: Chunking things out piece by piece, like a dialogue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:09">[00:13:09]</a>.
2.  **Massive Prompt**: Assembling a comprehensive prompt with all potential situations and hypotheticals to generate an output <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:15">[00:13:15]</a>.

It's common to start conversationally <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:51">[00:13:51]</a>. You can provide the initial idea and goals, then ask AI to generate multiple headlines and their main points <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:42">[00:14:42]</a>. This defers the "lower leverage task" of iterating on ideas to the AI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:30">[00:16:30]</a>. This process mirrors how an editor-in-chief provides a prompt to a writer, who then generates multiple ideas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:55">[00:16:55]</a>.

### The Editor-in-Chief Mindset
When using AI, writers elevate to the role of "editor-in-chief," where their primary value is taste and context <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:29">[00:17:29]</a>. For instance, choosing a headline with the word "ban" over others because it implies conflict and creates a better hook for the target audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:41">[00:17:41]</a>. The editor-in-chief perspective helps in [[strategies_for_media_and_content_creation | creating engaging content for different platforms]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:53">[00:17:53]</a>.

## Structuring Long-Form Content

### The "Chunks of Three" Framework
For an average 800-word article (which can be a long-form post on X, Medium, or Quora) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:49">[00:28:49]</a>, the easiest container is to think in "chunks of three" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:41">[00:28:41]</a>. This typically means an introduction followed by three main sections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:05">[00:29:05]</a>.

The first decision is what three things will be given to the reader <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:34">[00:29:34]</a>. Ninety percent of a piece's value lies in its headline and the quality of its three main points <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:48">[00:29:48]</a>. If the main points are vague, readers will immediately dismiss the content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:46">[00:30:46]</a>. Main points should be specific and tangible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:06">[00:31:06]</a>.

### The 10 Magical Ways Framework
This framework helps remove writer's block by asking: "Which of these 10 things am I going to give the reader?" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:54">[00:35:54]</a>. Options include tips, stats, steps, or lessons <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:01">[00:36:01]</a>.

For example, enhancing a headline like "Why Reed Hastings banned the word Blockbuster: What it means for your startup" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:17">[00:36:17]</a> to "Why Reed Hastings banned the word Blockbuster and three lessons from his $361 billion streaming service" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:28">[00:36:28]</a>. The inclusion of a big number often performs well in headlines <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:37">[00:36:37]</a>.

The chosen "magical way" in the headline (e.g., "lessons") should be reflected clearly in the subheads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:33">[00:38:33]</a>. The highest leverage decision is defining what those three lessons are <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:53">[00:38:53]</a>. Ninety percent of the value is in the quality of the lessons, not the writing style <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:02">[00:39:02]</a>.

It's recommended to try coming up with these lessons manually first, then use AI to stress-test their quality by generating more options <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:51">[00:39:51]</a>.
*   **Pro-Tip**: Stack rank main points from most to least compelling to hook the reader early <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:15">[00:41:15]</a>.
*   **Pro-Tip**: The best main points/subheads are often full sentences that deliver value upfront, rather than vague, clever titles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:17">[00:44:17]</a>.

Once the three main points are selected, return to the "10 Magical Ways" to determine what specific type of content will fill each section (e.g., steps, stats, examples, reasons why) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:08">[00:47:08]</a>.

## Applying Frameworks to Short-Form Content: Thread Hooks

[[creating_engaging_content_for_different_platforms | Creating engaging content for different platforms]] often involves adapting content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:44">[00:18:44]</a>. A chosen headline for an article can be adapted into a viral thread hook <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:07">[00:19:07]</a>.

### Stealing Architecture, Not Content
When creating content, it's acceptable to use existing successful hooks as [[utilizing_templates_and_starter_kits_in_development | templates]] or "architecture," not by stealing content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:41">[00:20:41]</a>. This is akin to "steal like an artist" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:36">[00:20:36]</a>. This process makes subconscious intuition conscious, allowing writers to intentionally reuse successful structures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:28">[00:21:28]</a>.

### Training AI with Manual Examples
By manually creating a new version of a successful hook based on a new idea, a "mini model" is created <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:24">[00:24:24]</a>. This mini-model can then be fed to AI to generate more variations, increasing the likelihood of virality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:36">[00:25:36]</a>. This process relies on the human's ability to prompt AI effectively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:46">[00:26:46]</a>.

It's not necessary to use advanced AI features like custom GPTs for most tasks; standard models like ChatGPT and Claude are powerful enough <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:42">[00:27:42]</a>.

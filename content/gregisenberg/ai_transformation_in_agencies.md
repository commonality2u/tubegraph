## Business Models for AI Agencies

The [[ai_startups_and_agency_models | AI transformation]] opens up new business models, particularly the "pay-for-performance" agency model <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:00">[00:57:00]</a>.

### Pay-for-Performance Model
In a pay-for-performance model, agencies charge clients based on the results they deliver, rather than a fixed fee or retainer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:55:00">[01:55:00]</a>. This model is becoming more attractive because:
*   **Reduced Client Risk**: Clients only pay if the agency performs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:57:00">[01:57:00]</a>.
*   **Data-Driven Underwriting**: In an [[ai_and_technology_trends | AI-driven]] and data-rich world, marketers have more data to assess opportunities, making them more willing to take risks on performance-based deals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:23:00">[02:22:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:27:00">[02:27:00]</a>.
*   **Scalability**: An example is an agency generating $80 million in revenue with $30 million in profit and only 30 employees by focusing on one industry and running paid ads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:00">[01:15:00]</a>. Another example is a telecom-focused agency that grew to over $1 billion in revenue by delivering leads and eventually acquiring call centers to "gift wrap" customers for large companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:43:00">[02:43:00]</a>.

Industries suitable for this model typically have high Customer Acquisition Costs (CPAs), such as finance, crypto exchanges, and hotels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:41:00">[03:41:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:06:00">[04:06:00]</a>.

### Charging for Value vs. Savings
When implementing [[ai_startups_and_agency_models | AI agency models]], there's a strategic choice:
*   **Percentage of Cost Savings**: Initially, an agency could offer to save a company money (e.g., $2 million a month in customer service costs) and take a percentage of those savings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:50:00">[06:50:00]</a>. This can be a helpful starting point to de-risk the engagement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:28:00">[08:28:00]</a>.
*   **Charging for Direct Value**: Alternatively, an agency can go "straight for the value," charging for new customers or increased revenue, which can capture four to eight times more value <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:34:00">[07:34:00]</a>. This means directly charging based on the incremental customers gained <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:45:00">[07:45:00]</a>.

### Smart Contracts
The future of pay-for-performance could involve smart contracts, which are still 5-10 years away from widespread adoption <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:22:00">[09:22:00]</a>. These blockchain-based contracts would automatically release payments upon meeting agreed-upon performance metrics, ensuring transparency and reducing payment disputes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:34:00">[09:34:00]</a>.

Beyond general [[ai_startups_and_agency_models | AI transformation]], several specific agency types are poised for growth:

*   **[[ai_agents_in_marketing | AI Agents in Marketing]]**: Building and deploying [[building_and_deploying_ai_agents_for_business_tasks | AI agents]] to handle lead calls, scheduling, and rescheduling can significantly streamline marketing and sales processes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:55:00">[05:55:00]</a>.
*   **[[ai_in_content_creation_and_marketing | AI in Content Creation and Marketing]]**:
    *   **AI-assisted content**: Agencies specializing in creating content with the help of [[ai_and_technology_trends | AI tools]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:03:00">[02:03:00]</a>.

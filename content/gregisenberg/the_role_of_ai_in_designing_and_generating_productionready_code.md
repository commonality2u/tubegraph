AI product designers are emerging tools that claim to generate designs and production-ready code from simple English prompts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:10">[00:00:10]</a>. This article explores the capabilities and limitations of such tools, specifically Poly.ai, and compares it to V0, another prominent AI designer tool.

## Poly.ai: An AI Product Designer

Poly.ai, a Y Combinator startup, claims to help non-designers create production-ready designs and front-end code with AI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:10">[00:01:10]</a>. The product aims to translate written descriptions into beautiful designs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:14">[00:00:14]</a>.

### Initial Impressions and Interface
Upon first use, Poly.ai's interface resembles a chat-like experience, similar to Chat GPT, but for design <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:45">[00:02:45]</a>. Key features include:
*   Text input for design prompts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:51">[00:02:51]</a>.
*   Microphone input for voice commands <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:53">[00:02:53]</a>.
*   Option to upload images, allowing users to draw a wireframe on a napkin and upload it for the AI to interpret <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:07">[00:03:07]</a>. This feature is also present in V0 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:34">[00:03:34]</a>.

### Testing Poly.ai: A YouTube Analytics Startup Idea
To test Poly.ai, a multi-million dollar year startup idea was used: a SaaS product that predicts YouTube video engagement based on changes to titles, thumbnails, and other elements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:50">[00:01:50]</a>, inspired by an algorithm that simulates tweet reactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:04">[00:04:04]</a>.

The initial prompt included the concept and aesthetic preferences: "glassy, minimalist, and with colorful calls to action" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:43">[00:06:43]</a>. An image of an analytics dashboard for X (Twitter) was also uploaded as inspiration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:02">[00:05:02]</a>.

#### First Attempt Results
The first generation by Poly.ai produced a landing page for the imagined product, named "Predict" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:04">[00:10:04]</a>. It included marketing copy like "AI powered analytics to optimize your YouTube content and maximize your engagement" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:08">[00:10:08]</a>.

However, there were significant shortcomings:
*   It was primarily a landing page, not the actual product interface <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:08">[00:11:08]</a>.
*   Calls to action like "Get Started" led to a black screen, rather than a product demo <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:45">[00:10:45]</a>.
*   The design did not fully embody the requested "glassy" aesthetic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:26">[00:10:26]</a>.
*   It lacked a progress bar during generation, making wait times uncertain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:18">[00:09:18]</a>.

#### The Importance of Specific Terminology
To refine the design, it became clear that specific design terms were necessary. The term for "glassy" was identified as "glassmorphism" using Perplexity AI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:33">[00:13:33]</a>.

> "The reality is you need with all these a lot of these tools it's as good as the prompt that you can do it right so you do need to know design terms and like what's trending and what people call Taste to get the most out of it" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:41">[00:12:41]</a>.

This highlights a crucial aspect of [[evaluating_ai_tools_for_productionready_applications | evaluating AI tools for production-ready applications]]: user expertise in prompting significantly impacts output quality.

#### Second Attempt Results
With a more specific prompt including "glassmorphic," Poly.ai's second attempt showed improvement. It started to look "pretty good" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:03">[00:16:03]</a> and developed a product interface rather than just a landing page <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:05">[00:16:05]</a>. The design included elements like version comparisons (A/B testing), an engagement chart, and AI suggestions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:45">[00:23:45]</a>. However, the product images still didn't directly match the YouTube analytics use case, appearing more like generic website dashboards <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:06">[00:17:06]</a>.

## Comparison with V0

V0 is another well-known AI product designer tool <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:40">[00:00:40]</a>. A side-by-side comparison with Poly.ai revealed several differences.

### V0's Approach
V0 provides reasoning and context for its generation process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:05">[00:21:05]</a>. It explains its understanding of the request, such as "I'll create a SAS application that helps YouTubers predict how changes to their titles thumbnails and other elements will affect engagement" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:16">[00:21:16]</a>. This transparency makes the user feel more connected to the AI's thought process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:14">[00:21:14]</a>.

### V0's Design Outcome
V0's initial output for the YouTube analytics tool closely copied the provided X analytics image, but it did build out a functional product interface with features like "version A original content title description thumbnail" and "predict performance" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:40">[00:22:40]</a>. It also included AI recommendations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:56">[00:22:56]</a>.

### Testing Poly.ai: A YouTube Analytics Startup Idea
To test Poly.ai, a hypothetical multi-million dollar year startup idea was used: a SaaS product that predicts YouTube video engagement based on changes to titles, thumbnails, and other elements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:50">[00:01:50]</a>. This concept was inspired by an algorithm designed to simulate tweet reactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:04">[00:04:04]</a>, showcasing how [[using_ai_for_creating_steps_in_startup_projects | AI can be used for creating steps in startup projects]] by validating ideas.

An image of an analytics dashboard for X (Twitter) was also uploaded as inspiration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:02">[00:05:02]</a>.

#### First Attempt Results
The first generation by Poly.ai produced a landing page for the imagined product, named "Predict" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:04">[00:10:04]</a>. It included marketing copy like "AI powered analytics to optimize your YouTube content and maximize your engagement" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:08">[00:10:08]</a>.

However, there were significant shortcomings:
*   It was primarily a landing page, not the actual product interface <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:08">[00:11:08]</a>.
*   Calls to action like "Get Started" led to a black screen, rather than a product demo <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:45">[00:10:45]</a>.
*   The design did not fully embody the requested "glassy" aesthetic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:26">[00:10:26]</a>.
*   It lacked a progress bar during generation, making wait times uncertain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:18">[00:09:18]</a>.

#### The Importance of Specific Terminology
To refine the design, it became clear that specific design terms were necessary. The term for "glassy" was identified as "glassmorphism" using Perplexity AI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:33">[00:13:33]</a>.

> "The reality is you need with all these a lot of these tools it's as good as the prompt that you can do it right so you do need to know design terms and like what's trending and what people call Taste to get the most out of it" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:41">[00:12:41]</a>.

#### Second Attempt Results
With a more specific prompt including "glassmorphic," Poly.ai's second attempt showed improvement. It started to look "pretty good" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:03">[00:16:03]</a> and developed a product interface rather than just a landing page <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:05">[00:16:05]</a>. ## General Insights on AI Design Tools

The experience with Poly.ai and V0 provides several insights into [[the_evolving_role_of_ai_in_content_creation | the evolving role of AI in content creation]] and product development:

*   **Prompt Engineering**: The quality of AI output is highly dependent on the specificity and knowledge of the prompt <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:41">[00:12:41]</a>. Users need to understand design terms and trends to guide the AI effectively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:46">[00:14:46]</a>. This highlights a need for users to become skilled in prompt engineering for [[pros_and_cons_of_different_ai_coding_tools | different AI coding tools]].
*   **High Bar for AI Products**: User expectations for AI product output are very high. Tools need to provide "incredible" results to be integrated into daily workflows <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:07">[00:15:07]</a>.
*   **Combined Tool Usage**: Optimal results may come from using multiple AI tools in conjunction, leveraging each tool's strengths. For example, a design from V0 could be input into Poly.ai for further refinement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:17">[00:28:17]</a>. Just as users combine Chat GPT, Claude, Grok, and Gemini for writing and research, designers may use several AI product design tools <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:39">[00:28:39]</a>.

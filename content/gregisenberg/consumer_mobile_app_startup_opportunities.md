## Hunter Isacson's Playbook for $1M MRR Consumer Apps
Hunter Isacson outlines a clear strategy for building successful consumer apps:

> [!tip] Hunter Isacson's Playbook
> 1.  **Build a Simple Experience/Bare Bones MVP** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:18">[00:18:18]</a>: Focus on a minimal viable product (MVP) that effectively solves a problem, even if it's not fully optimized initially <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:25">[00:18:25]</a>.
> 2.  **Get Users on Quickly** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:43">[00:18:43]</a>: Test content on platforms like TikTok or Instagram Reels, even during the pre-order stage, to gauge user response <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:56">[00:18:56]</a>.
> 3.  **Select a Core Action and Northstar Metric** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:20">[00:19:20]</a>: Identify one primary metric that drives the business, such as the percentage of users completing a crucial action. Aim for a high percentage, ideally over 90% completion, and ensure users follow a single, clear flow <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:00">[00:26:00]</a>.
>     *   **Examples of Northstar Metrics:**
>         *   **Wink (make new friends app)**: Percentage of users getting a match on their first day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:01">[00:20:01]</a>.
>         *   **NGL (anonymous messaging)**: Percentage of users sharing the link and a few replies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:27">[00:20:27]</a>.
>         *   **Bags (crypto app)**: How many users fund their accounts and make one trade on the first day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:05">[00:21:05]</a>.
> 4.  **Seed the App with Pressure & Built-in Growth Loops** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:44">[00:26:44]</a>: Once the core action is proven, strategically seed the app in different audiences, niches, and countries, leveraging creators <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:01">[00:27:01]</a>. Integrate a sharing or inviting mechanism directly into the core user experience to increase the K-factor and reduce reliance on external marketing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:22">[00:27:22]</a>.
>     *   NGL achieved viral growth by encouraging users to post their anonymous question link on Instagram/Snapchat <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:36">[00:14:36]</a>.
>     *   Bags utilized Twitter logins, link sharing, and a leaderboard of inviters to generate hype and growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:39">[00:29:39]</a>.

## Strategies for Growth

### Creator-Led Growth
Hunter Isacson's primary method for growing his apps involves using creators and platforms like TikTok and Instagram to "seed" the app <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:21">[00:14:21]</a>. NGL spent less than $10,000 on marketing to achieve 250 million downloads, primarily by getting enough people to download it quickly through seeding to activate a viral growth loop <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:38">[00:14:38]</a>. This seeding can be organic or paid, often involving small payments to influencers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:36">[00:30:36]</a>.

### Reinventing Old Ideas for New Channels
Successful apps often take existing concepts and adapt them to new platforms or channels. NGL, for instance, wasn't a new idea (anonymous messaging apps existed) but thrived by launching when Instagram introduced its link feature for all users, turning Instagram stories into a key growth lever <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:15">[00:15:15]</a>. This approach of finding "existing ideas that could work in a new channel" is a great way to discover good ideas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:03">[00:17:03]</a>.

## Opportunities in the Consumer App Space

### Leveraging New Platform Features
Just as Locket capitalized on iPhone widget functionality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:35">[00:10:35]</a>, new opportunities arise when Apple introduces new features in iOS updates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:09">[00:12:09]</a>.
*   **Live Activities**: A cool feature that few have fully exploited, displaying dynamic information on the lock screen (e.g., Uber status) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:15">[00:12:15]</a>.
*   **Lock Screen Widgets**: Despite extensive use for customization, there's still potential for social experiences within lock screen widgets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:40">[00:12:40]</a>.
The speaker predicts significant "bangers" will emerge from live activities and iPhone lock screen widgets in the next 6-12 months <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:37">[00:13:37]</a>.

### Spiritual and Niche Apps
Apps like Hallow, a top Christian and Catholic prayer app, demonstrate success by making religious content mobile, digital, colorful, and appealing to younger generations (millennials, Gen Z) with features like daily prayers, sleep meditations, and community challenges <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:40">[00:04:40]</a>. There's a huge opening for similar spiritual apps across all religions, focusing on turning denominations and faiths into engaging mobile experiences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:17">[00:06:17]</a>.

### [[AI Startups and Entrepreneurship | AI-First Applications]]
The integration of AI offers new possibilities for existing app categories:
*   **CalA.I. (Calorie Tracking)**: This app took a common concept (calorie tracking) and packaged it nicely with AI, focusing on an amazing user funnel and onboarding experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:55">[00:02:55]</a>.
*   **AI for Spiritual Apps**: Potential exists for an [[AI startups and entrepreneurship | AI-first version of Hallow]], possibly using GPT vision to overlay religious quotes on scenery or generating answers to complex questions from religious texts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:31">[00:09:31]</a>.
*   **Pangu (Virtual Pet)**: This app is a great example of injecting a high degree of AI into an existing concept (virtual pets) to create a more human-like experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:47">[00:34:47]</a>. This suggests opportunities for [[Micro app trends and AI startup studios | AI startup studios]] focusing on "X for Y" models, like "NGL for X," "CalA.I. for X," or "Pangu for X," applying successful formats to new niches <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:41">[00:35:41]</a>.
*   **Framework over Ideas**: Hunter suggests focusing on frameworks (e.g., "framework X with audience Y") rather than entirely novel ideas, as it's easier to find opportunities where something is not optimized for a specific channel <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:26">[00:36:26]</a>.

### Future Tech Trends
*   **Crypto**: Hunter is excited about crypto going mainstream and gaining regulation, leading to more consumer apps in the space <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:54">[00:36:54]</a>.
*   **Augmented Reality (AR) Glasses**: Anticipated to be the next hardware platform, allowing for immersive, sharable experiences where users can prompt an app with their own "Jarvis AI" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:15">[00:37:15]</a>. The phone is expected to be the driving compute for these glasses, with AI models potentially running locally on phones in the future <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:55">[00:38:55]</a>.

## Advice for Aspiring Entrepreneurs
For young individuals (teenagers, 20s, college/post-college), building consumer mobile apps is highly recommended <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:26">[00:40:26]</a>. This path offers opportunities to understand human psychology, build beloved products, and create global, viral experiences that touch the world <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:36">[00:40:36]</a>. The tools for consumer app development are continually improving, soon allowing non-technical individuals to build apps without requiring a technical co-founder <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:39">[00:41:39]</a>. Hunter advises focusing on the consumer side over B2B/SaaS due to its inherent "fun" and direct engagement with building things for many people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:14">[00:41:14]</a>.

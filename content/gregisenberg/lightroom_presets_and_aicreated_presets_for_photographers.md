## The Opportunity for AI in Preset Creation

There's an opportunity to disrupt the existing market for Lightroom presets by transforming it into a subscription-based software service <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:52">[00:01:52]</a>.

The core idea involves leveraging [[content_creation_tools_and_ai_technology | AI technology]] to automatically create Lightroom presets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:02">[00:02:02]</a>. Users could upload a photo with a desired aesthetic, and the [[using_ai_for_content_creation | AI]] would generate the necessary XML code that defines the Lightroom preset <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:02">[00:02:02]</a>. This allows for the creation of "XML packets" at scale <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:22">[00:02:22]</a>. This functionality has been explored and proven feasible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:59">[00:02:59]</a>.

This approach solves the problem of manually building presets and allows users to generate any desired aesthetic using [[using_ai_for_graphic_design_and_branding | AI image recognition]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:55">[00:02:55]</a>. Once created, the preset can be downloaded and imported directly into Lightroom, saving time and effort <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:48">[00:02:48]</a>.

## Business Models and Marketing Strategies

### Subscription Software
A viable business model for [[using_ai_for_graphic_design and branding | AI-powered preset creation]] is a subscription service, such as $29 per month, offering unlimited preset generation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:24">[00:02:24]</a>. The product's margin would be extremely high, estimated at 99% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:41">[00:03:41]</a>.

The company could offer two main functionalities:
1.  Allowing customers to upload a source photo and have the [[using_ai_for_content_creation | AI]] build a custom preset from it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:49">[00:03:49]</a>.
2.  Providing a pre-built library of presets (XML files) for download <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:53">[00:03:53]</a>.

To build the library, the company could scrape common keywords photographers search for, like "boho Lightroom presets," find variations of photos, and then use AI to generate the corresponding XML files <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:00">[00:04:00]</a>.

### Leveraging SEO with Exact Match Domains
A powerful marketing strategy involves acquiring numerous exact match domains based on specific search terms (e.g., "boho Lightroom presets") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:33">[00:04:33]</a>. Even with algorithm changes, longtail exact match domains can still rank highly on Google <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:40">[00:07:40]</a>.

This strategy creates a "top of funnel" by directing users from these ranking domains to the subscription service <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:47">[00:04:47]</a>. This approach mirrors strategies used by industries with high-value leads, such as personal injury lawyers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:07">[00:07:07]</a>.

To capture leads, free preset packs (e.g., 10 presets) can be offered in exchange for an email address, allowing for email nurturing and upselling into the subscription <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:18">[00:04:18]</a>. Advertising can be targeted via Facebook Ads to individuals with interests in Photoshop, Lightroom, or Adobe products <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:13">[00:03:13]</a>.

The value of such a domain portfolio itself can be significant, akin to digital real estate, and can be sold to private equity firms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:08">[00:09:08]</a>.

### Micro Apps as a Larger Trend
The development of highly specialized [[content_creation_tools_and_ai_technology | AI applications]] (micro apps) that address specific user needs, like "text behind image" for YouTube thumbnails, represents a growing trend <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:47">[00:33:47]</a>. These apps often leverage APIs to perform single, focused actions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:01">[00:34:01]</a>.

This strategy aligns with the idea of "riding the wave" of ephemeral media trends <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:08">[00:37:08]</a>. The ability to quickly develop and ship products, coupled with advanced trend analysis capabilities, makes this feasible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:52">[00:37:52]</a>. Even large companies like Adobe create micro-apps that funnel users to their main products <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:11">[00:38:11]</a>. For example, a standalone captions app for videos gained a million ARR in 27 days, demonstrating the potential of focused tools for a large audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:40">[00:38:40]</a>.

The market for such niche tools is vast because many users prefer specialized applications over comprehensive ones like Canva or Cheesecake Factory with too many options <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:20">[00:39:20]</a>. They seek simplicity and clear value propositions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:03">[00:41:03]</a>. The ability to target users precisely through platforms like Facebook Ads, which have advanced [[using_ai_for_ad_creation | AI-driven targeting capabilities]], further enhances the viability of these micro-apps <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:20">[00:41:20]</a>.

## Blake Anderson's Framework for [[AI Startup Ideation and Framework | Creating Startup Ideas]] and Execution

Blake's approach to taking an idea and making it a mainstream product involves an iterative process from ideation through development and distribution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:55:00">[08:55:00]</a>.

### Iterative Virality and Hooks
Instead of focusing on a single viral trend, Blake engages in a highly iterative process of generating virality early on <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:55:00">[08:55:00]</a>.
*   **New Hooks & Positioning**: Constantly experiment with new hooks and ways to position the product for different target audiences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:00:00">[09:00:00]</a>.
*   **Split Testing**: Split test hooks across different videos and creators within various niches (e.g., EdTech, study tips, job application tips, productivity tips) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:37:00">[09:37:00]</a>.
*   **Analyze Results**: Assess views, conversion rates per view, and use this data to inform future distribution strategies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:42:00">[09:42:00]</a>. This is akin to how platforms like Meta or TikTok split-test creatives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:09:00">[10:09:00]</a>.
*   **Engineer Around Virality**: Ideation should begin with considering what tools or features will perform well on social media <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:20:00">[14:20:00]</a>.

### Influencer Relationships
Blake prioritizes building personal relationships with influencers over using marketplaces or agencies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:03:00">[11:03:00]</a>.
*   **Direct Engagement**: Influencers receive hundreds of daily DMs and emails, making it hard to differentiate serious companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:49:00">[11:49:00]</a>.
*   **Embedding in the Industry**: Developing personal relationships and embedding oneself within the industry helps build trust and connections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:00:00">[12:00:00]</a>.
*   **Quantity and Quality**: Early on, reach out to thousands of creators and message them on every platform. Once a relationship is established, treat them well and become friends <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:41:00">[12:41:00]</a>.

### Product Design and Development
*   **Wireframes & UI**: Start with wireframes, then hire a professional designer or someone from Upwork to convert them into a fleshed-out UI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:29:00">[14:29:00]</a>.
*   **Coding**: For those starting without capital, ChatGPT can be used to learn how to code and build the app (e.g., Swift UI with Xcode, or React Native with Cursor) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:43:00">[14:43:00]</a>.

### Monetization Strategy: Paywalls
Blake uses Superwall for paywall management, as it facilitates incredibly easy split testing and data analysis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:16:00">[24:16:00]</a>.
*   **Optimization Thesis**: Instead of optimizing solely for "number of users multiplied by profit per user," Blake advocates for optimizing for "number of users raised to the N (where N > 1) multiplied by profit per user" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:49:00">[24:49:00]</a>.
*   **Prioritize User Access**: Accepting a lower lifetime value (LTV) per download allows more people to use the platform, which can lead to faster market domination and increased (though hard to measure) word-of-mouth growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:06:00">[25:06:00]</a>.
*   **Standard Practice**: The most common and efficient paywall strategy is to guide users through an onboarding flow (e.g., quizzes, push notifications, personal info collection) before hitting them with a paywall <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:34:00">[26:34:00]</a>. While not "morally great" as it leverages user time investment before revealing the cost, it is effective <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:16:00">[27:16:00]</a>.

### Naming [[AIPowered Startup Ideas | AI Apps]]
Appending "AI" to an app's name can be valuable when innovating within a relatively stagnant industry, like "Cal AI" for calorie tracking, as it brings a level of sophistication <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:27:00">[20:27:00]</a>. However, it should be avoided if 100% accuracy is critical (e.g., medical services), as "AI" might cause skepticism about potential errors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:52:00">[20:52:00]</a>. Ultimately, simple, easy-to-understand names are beneficial, but sometimes more friendly names like "Juny" or "Journey" might be preferred over "Journal AI" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:42:00">[21:42:00]</a>.

### Competing in Established Markets
When entering a market dominated by incumbents like Duolingo or MyFitnessPal, Blake believes smaller, agile teams can win by:
*   **Work Ethic & Incentives**: Startups can iterate and progress faster because their internal incentives are more aligned, unlike large corporations where engineers may have less equity or motivation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:49:00">[30:49:00]</a>.
*   **Superior Technology**: Develop significantly better core functionality (e.g., Cal AI's calorie estimations based on images are superior to MyFitnessPal's) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:28:00">[31:28:00]</a>.
*   **Novel Distribution**: Pioneer new marketing methods on social media <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:36:00">[31:36:00]</a>.
*   **Creating New Market Share**: Often, these new [[ai_startups_and_business_ideas | AI-first apps]] aren't just taking market share but are engaging entirely new user segments that weren't using the incumbent products <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:42:00">[33:42:00]</a>. This means pioneering new ways for people to learn and improve themselves using [[AI]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:00:00">[34:00:00]</a>.

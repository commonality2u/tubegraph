## Leveraging Emerging Platforms and AI

### LinkedIn Video Content Agency for Founders

LinkedIn is rolling out a "for you page" feed similar to TikTok, creating a "blue ocean" opportunity for content creators <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:44">[00:06:44]</a>. This shift allows for massive free reach and personal brand building for founders <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:54">[00:07:54]</a>.

**The Business Idea:**
*   Create a service agency that helps founders create vertical video content for LinkedIn <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:01">[00:07:01]</a>.
*   **Process:** Interview a founder weekly for an hour, generate scripts based on the conversation, have the founder record the content (mimicking provided pacing and inflection), and then produce and schedule clips <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:17">[00:07:17]</a>.
*   **Pricing:** Entry-level pricing could be around $2,500 a month <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:55">[00:11:55]</a>, with higher tiers ($5,000-$8,000/month) for additional services like creating lead magnets (e.g., drip courses) and design help (banners, infographics) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:01">[00:12:01]</a>.
*   **Validation:** This model is already validated, with a friend having paid $2,000 a month for a similar service <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:42">[00:07:42]</a>.
*   **Go-to-Market Strategy:**
    *   Start by running your own LinkedIn channel, demonstrating the service by creating your own clips and inviting potential clients to intro calls <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:38">[00:12:38]</a>.
    *   Build a community around "Founders Going Viral on LinkedIn" (e.g., foundersgoingviralonlinkedin.com) offering weekly tips and monthly peer-to-peer calls, then upsell the done-for-you service <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:49">[00:12:49]</a>.
    *   Target Founders with VC dollars, as they are often seeking to build personal brands subsidized by their companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:42">[00:11:42]</a>.
    *   Alternatively, target VC firms directly, offering the service as a value-add for their portfolio companies, as VCs also desire attention and audience for deal flow <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:50">[00:45:50]</a>. VCs might pay 4x what a founder would <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:50">[00:48:50]</a>.
*   **Key Insight:** This business rides a massive trend, catering to founders who want personal brand growth and inbound lead generation without the operational burden <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:00">[00:08:00]</a>.

### AI Automation Consulting for Small Businesses

The keyword "AI automation" shows insane growth in search volume, indicating high demand for implementation in businesses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:36">[00:16:36]</a>. While large consulting firms like McKinsey are servicing enterprises, there's a huge opportunity for SMBs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:16">[00:17:16]</a>.

    *   Become a thought leader in the space <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:11">[00:18:11]</a>.
    *   Bid on "AI automation" and related keywords (Zapier, Make.com) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:16">[00:18:16]</a>.
    *   Create tutorials using AI tools like Perplexity AI for common setups, with a call to action for consulting services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:28">[00:18:28]</a>.
    *   Run a YouTube channel demonstrating these automations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:42">[00:18:42]</a>.
*   **Key Insight:** People want automation, not just AI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:11">[00:19:11]</a>. Automation directly translates to cost savings, making it an easy sell <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:22">[00:19:22]</a>. This is a large wave that can be ridden <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:08">[00:18:08]</a>.

### AI Graphic Designer Co-Pilot Software

There's a significant unmet need for an AI tool that can generate on-brand graphic designs quickly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:56">[00:22:56]</a>. Existing solutions often go "off on a tangent" or produce outdated designs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:41">[00:23:41]</a>.

**The Business Idea:**
*   Develop a SaaS tool that takes a brand style guide (colors, logos, fonts) and generates graphic assets like podcast thumbnails, email headers, or Facebook ads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:07">[00:23:07]</a>.
*   The goal is not perfection, but "good enough" designs that can be quickly refined in tools like Figma or Canva <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:27">[00:23:27]</a>. It acts as a co-pilot, providing variations of ideas based on constraints <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:34">[00:23:34]</a>.
*   **Pricing:** A competitive price point could be $99 a month, undercutting existing services like Design Pickle ($499/month) by leveraging AI for efficiency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:04">[00:27:04]</a>.
*   **Key Feature:** The ability to handle multiple design requests simultaneously (multi-threading) would be incredibly valuable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:13">[00:27:13]</a>.
*   **Strategic Opportunity:** While Canva is moving into AI, there will always be demand for quick, on-brand asset generation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:02">[00:25:02]</a>. By imposing constraints (brand guidelines) on AI, the outputs become much better and more aligned with user needs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:30">[00:28:30]</a>. This [[ai_startup_incubation_and_ideas | AI startup incubation and ideas]] is a prime example of building a business where an 80% AI-generated solution is 100x faster than a human <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:09">[00:26:09]</a>.

### Newsletter as a Service for Founders

Founders want a newsletter but rarely have the time or operational capacity to write it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:33">[00:29:33]</a>.

**The Business Idea:**
*   Offer a "done-for-you" newsletter service for founders <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:33">[00:29:33]</a>.
*   **Process:** Interview the founder for an hour, transcribe the conversation, and use it to write the newsletter <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:39">[00:29:39]</a>. AI can assist in translating raw data into the founder's tone, style, and voice <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:40">[00:32:40]</a>.
*   **Growth Strategy:**
    *   Approach founders with existing social media followings (Twitter, LinkedIn) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:54">[00:29:54]</a>.
    *   Use scraping tools (e.g., SalesQL for LinkedIn, or Twitter email scrapers) to gather emails from followers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:06">[00:30:06]</a>. Validate emails and slowly sequence them into the newsletter (e.g., 200/day) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:35">[00:31:35]</a>.
    *   Implement automations (e.g., Phantom Buster) to send automated DMs or messages with a newsletter subscribe link to new followers or connections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:07">[00:31:07]</a>.
    *   Show potential clients data from successful newsletter growth for other clients <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:56">[00:32:56]</a>.
*   **Value Proposition:**
    *   Founders view this as a direct return on audience spend (ROAS), as newsletter subscribers have high lifetime value <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:21">[00:33:21]</a>.
    *   It provides "schmuck insurance" by moving audience dependence off volatile social media platforms onto an owned email list <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:51">[00:33:51]</a>.
*   **Market Trend:** Founders are increasingly becoming media companies themselves, and this service supports that trend <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:48">[00:31:48]</a>. This aligns with [[examples_of_successful_newsletter_business_models | successful newsletter business models]].

### YouTube Thumbnail and Title Library with Data

YouTube continues to dominate the creator space due to its robust monetization and RPMs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:23">[00:36:23]</a>. Good thumbnails and titles are crucial for discoverability and click-through rates.

**The Business Idea:**
*   Build a subscription service offering a vast, plug-and-play library of high-performing YouTube thumbnails and titles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:53">[00:36:53]</a>.
*   **Key Feature:** Integrate data into the library, showing performance metrics like impressions and views for specific thumbnail/title combinations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:05">[00:39:05]</a>.
*   **Execution:** Scrape YouTube for successful thumbnails and titles, pull data via YouTube API (or tools like VidIQ), and offer filtering capabilities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:10">[00:39:10]</a>.
*   **Go-to-Market Strategy:**
    *   Scrape email addresses of YouTubers (e.g., those with >10,000 subscribers in specific categories) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:11">[00:37:11]</a>.
    *   Cold email them, offering initial free access to a limited number of templates/ideas, with a paid tier for full access <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:37">[00:37:37]</a>.
*   **Market Trend:** YouTube is becoming the dominant social media platform, owning long-form, podcasting, short-form, and monetization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:24">[00:38:24]</a>. Creators are increasingly using YouTube revenue to fund other ventures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:09">[00:39:09]</a>.

### Canva Video Captioning App

Canva is a rapidly growing video editor, but its native captioning capabilities are lacking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:17">[00:50:17]</a>.

**The Business Idea:**
*   Develop a third-party app for Canva that automatically captions videos <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:28">[00:50:28]</a>.
*   **Functionality:** Users upload a video, click the app, it transcribes the audio (using a large whisper model), and adds captions with various font styles and karaoke effects, integrating with Canva's existing features <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:49">[00:50:49]</a>.
*   **Market Comparison:** Tools like Veed.io offer similar features and are popular <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:33">[00:51:33]</a>. Cloning Veed.io's features within Canva's ecosystem could create a strong product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:38">[00:51:38]</a>.
*   **Potential:** This could be a solid $20,000/month company, potentially leading to an acquisition by Canva to integrate the feature natively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:11">[00:51:11]</a>.
*   **Strategy:** Build it, achieve initial success (e.g., $20k/month revenue), then seek PR and reach out to Canva for M&A discussions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:10">[00:52:10]</a>.

### Resurrecting "Cheap Flights" Newsletters

The original "Scott's Cheap Flights" (now "Going.com") has lost its unique flavor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:08">[00:53:08]</a>. There's an opportunity to revive this concept with a fresh approach.

**The Business Idea:**
*   Create a low-code newsletter service that aggregates cheap flight deals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:21">[00:53:21]</a>.
*   **Execution:** Leverage Google Flights' alert system (which sends emails when prices drop for chosen city pairs) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:28">[00:53:28]</a>. Automate the aggregation of these alerts into a curated newsletter using a simple setup like Zapier <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:21">[00:54:21]</a>.
*   **Monetization:** Subscription fees and affiliate kickbacks from flight booking platforms (e.g., Kiwi) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:32">[00:54:32]</a>.
*   **Go-to-Market Strategy:**
    *   Run Facebook ads targeting "frequent traveler" interest groups, directing them to a newsletter signup page <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:03">[00:54:03]</a>.
    *   Focus on specific major metropolitan areas initially, then expand <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:42">[00:54:42]</a>.
    *   The newsletter itself encourages organic growth through forwarding to friends <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:00">[00:55:00]</a>.
    *   Consider acquiring existing, outdated "Craigslist vibes" travel newsletters (like Canadian ones using airport codes, e.g., "YYZdeals.com") or cloning their model with a modern aesthetic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:49">[00:55:49]</a>.
*   **Additional Revenue:** Once a large list is established, expand into local "What's Happening in My City" newsletters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:13">[00:59:13]</a>.
*   **Key Insight:** This is a proven [[examples_of_successful_newsletter_business_models | business model]] that can be easily recreated with modern, low-code tools. Travel-related niches often have high CPMs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:58">[00:58:58]</a>.

### Niche Accounting Software ("QuickBooks for X")

General accounting software like QuickBooks is too broad for many specific industries, leading to a need for tailored solutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:00">[01:01:00]</a>.

**The Business Idea:**
*   Create industry-specific accounting software or services, essentially "QuickBooks for X," where X is a specific niche <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:45">[01:01:45]</a>.
*   **Examples:** QuickBooks for SaaS, QuickBooks for agencies, QuickBooks for real estate, QuickBooks for nonprofits, or even highly specific niches like QuickBooks for Airbnb entrepreneurs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:56">[01:03:56]</a>.
*   **Identification Strategy:**
    *   Research QuickBooks extensions and integrations to identify industries with high connectivity demand <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:00">[01:02:00]</a>.
    *   Use ChatGPT to brainstorm categories, then research competition in each <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:10">[01:03:10]</a>.
    *   Focus on niches with growing trends and low competition, especially those built on top of other platforms (like D2C on Shopify, or health practitioners on specific EHRs) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:02">[01:04:02]</a>.
*   **Go-to-Market Strategy:** Cold email businesses within the chosen niche, highlighting how the custom solution integrates seamlessly with their existing platforms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:20">[01:05:20]</a>.
*   **Analogy:** Similar to how specialized EHRs (Electronic Health Records) emerged for different medical practitioner types, breaking away from monolithic systems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:48">[01:04:48]</a>. This represents an [[building_startup_ideas_around_niche_trends | embeddable business model]] within larger platforms.

## Core Principles for Building Innovative Businesses

*   **Ride Larger Trends:** The most effective strategy for [[brainstorming_startup_ideas | brainstorming startup ideas]] and building a company, especially when bootstrapping or self-funding, is to attach yourself to a larger, existing trend <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:05">[00:10:05]</a>. This avoids the need to educate the market; instead, you sell what people already want to buy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:11">[00:10:11]</a>.
*   **"Owned Media" as a Foundation:** Building an audience through platforms like YouTube, podcasts, or newsletters provides a robust distribution channel for any product or service <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:59">[00:19:59]</a>. This allows for rapid iteration and sustainable, cash-flow positive growth from day one <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:09">[00:20:09]</a>.
*   **Content Consistency over Intensity:** While content creation can feel like a grind, it's often more about consistent effort than immense hourly commitment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:23">[00:20:23]</a>. Focus on defining your tone, voice, and format, then build a system and team to manage the content pipeline, acting more as an editor or conductor than a sole writer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:02">[00:21:02]</a>.
*   **"Barnacle on the Whale" Strategy:** Identify massive, validated markets (like Cricut with 10 million users) and build a smaller, specialized business that serves a niche within that larger ecosystem <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:00">[00:32:00]</a>. This strategy minimizes validation risk <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:51">[00:33:51]</a>.
*   **Focus on Distribution:** An idea is only as good as its distribution strategy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:43">[01:06:43]</a>. Think tactically about how to reach your target audience from the outset, whether through cold emailing, targeted ads, or community building <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:54">[01:06:54]</a>.
*   **Be Authentic:** In content creation, being yourself and talking about what genuinely interests you, even if it's considered "too niche" by others, can attract a highly engaged and valuable audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:46">[01:17:46]</a>. This creates "digital gravity" and serendipitous connections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:11">[01:16:11]</a>.

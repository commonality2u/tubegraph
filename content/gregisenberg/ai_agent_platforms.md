## Key Use Cases

### Meeting Management
*   **Meeting Recording and Note-taking**: A basic agent can record and summarize meetings, often expanding to include saving notes to specific Google Docs, even updating existing ones for recurring contacts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:15:37">[02:15:37]</a>. An agent can intelligently understand prompts like "summarize the meeting you just had in bullet points" without needing a separate AI step <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:59:00">[03:59:00]</a>. This acts as an "exocortex" or external brain, helping with memory recall related to past discussions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:26:00">[07:26:00]</a>.
*   **Meeting Scheduling**: [[ai_agents_for_customer_service_and_scheduling | AI agents]] can handle meeting scheduling by finding available times, sending invites, and including necessary details like office addresses for in-person meetings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:36:00">[13:36:00]</a>.
*   **Meeting Preparation**: Using [[agent_swarms_and_their_applications | agent swarms]], an agent can check daily meetings, deploy swarms for each meeting, and then deploy further swarms for each attendee. This allows the agent to gather relevant notes, emails, and LinkedIn information for each person, providing a digest via email <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:25:00">[11:25:00]</a>. For example, it can identify specific challenges to discuss if a meeting requires pre-preparation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:38:00">[12:38:00]</a>.

### Communication and Outreach
*   **Phone Calls**: [[AI agents and automated marketing workflows | AI agents]] can make phone calls for tasks like restaurant reservations. An amusing example involved an AI agent making a reservation with another AI agent answering the phone at the restaurant, successfully securing the booking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:28:00">[08:28:00]</a>. They can also cancel flights <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:51:00">[09:51:00]</a>.
*   **Recruiting**: Agents can act as recruiters by searching for people based on given criteria, presenting a list, asking which ones to reach out to, researching candidates on platforms like Perplexity, and sending personalized outreach emails. They can also send reminders if there's no reply <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:04:00">[16:04:00]</a>.
*   **Sales Prospecting**: Similar to recruiting, agents can perform sales prospecting, either generating their own leads or processing uploaded CSVs of leads from other platforms to send personalized outreach <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:49:00">[20:49:00]</a>.
*   **Influencer Outreach**: [[AI agents and automated marketing workflows | AI agents]] are used by creators to filter incoming brand partnership emails and negotiate deals, and by brands to find influencers on platforms like TikTok, Instagram, and YouTube, find their contact information, and offer partnerships <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:00:00">[25:00:00]</a>.

### Business Operations
*   **[[ai_agents_for_customer_service_and_scheduling | Customer Support]] Automation**: With extensive integrations, AI agents can automate customer support across platforms like Telegram, Slack, WhatsApp, Zendesk, and Intercom <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:20:00">[26:20:00]</a>.
*   **[[ai_agents_in_problemsolving | Focus Groups/User Research Simulation]]**: [[AI agents and automated marketing workflows | LLMs]] are good at emulating human responses. Businesses can create agents prompted to simulate user personas and gather feedback on product ideas or features, acting as an 80% as good, but significantly cheaper, alternative to traditional focus groups <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:48:00">[26:48:00]</a>. This concept extends to "virtual communities" for product feedback and iteration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:55:00">[27:55:00]</a>.
*   **Virtual Standups/Team Reports**: An "Elon Lindy" agent can call team members weekly for a virtual standup, asking about their accomplishments, and then compile a summary report for the CEO, effectively replacing aspects of middle management <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:50:00">[28:50:00]</a>.
*   **Competitive Analysis**: A "competitive tracker" agent can wake up monthly, get a list of competitors from a spreadsheet, and deploy an [[agent_swarms_and_their_applications | agent swarm]] for each competitor. It then gathers information like employee count, traffic estimates, recent news, funding, and hiring updates, logging this data and generating reports on competitor trends <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:16:00">[30:16:00]</a>. This can also track the longevity of new AI companies, revealing that many don't last <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:31:00">[32:31:00]</a>.
*   **CRM Management**: An agent can help manage a professional network by adding new contacts to a spreadsheet, and intelligently suggesting people to meet based on location (e.g., when a flight is booked) or specific criteria (e.g., "good salespeople") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:04:00">[33:04:00]</a>.

## Agent Swarms
[[agent_swarms_and_their_applications | Agent swarms]] are a significant advancement, allowing a list of tasks to be sent to an [[AI agents and automated marketing workflows | AI agent]] for reliable, quick, and parallel execution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:20:00">[10:20:00]</a>. While single [[AI agents and automated marketing workflows | AI agents]] are powerful, they can lose coherence over long-term, complex tasks. An [[agent_swarms_and_their_applications | agent swarm]] addresses this by duplicating the agent, sending a copy to each item in the list, similar to "Agent Smith" in *The Matrix* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:44:00">[10:44:00]</a>. This ensures reliability and efficiency for tasks like personalized lead outreach to hundreds of people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:51:00">[10:51:00]</a>.

Users are encouraged to start with simple, personal assistant-style [[examples_and_use_cases_of_ai_agents | use cases]] like meeting scheduling or recording to "dip their toes in the water" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:07:00">[15:07:00]</a>. Templates are available to facilitate easy onboarding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:22:00">[15:22:00]</a>.

When building, it's recommended to start small and incrementally add complexity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:48:00">[18:48:00]</a>. A "human in the loop" can be inserted into the workflow to ask for confirmation, especially if the [[AI agents and automated marketing workflows | AI agent]] could potentially cause embarrassment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:49:00">[17:49:00]</a>. This acts like training an intern, with the agent learning from corrections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:14:00">[18:14:00]</a>.

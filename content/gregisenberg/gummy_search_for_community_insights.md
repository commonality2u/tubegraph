## Accessing and Navigating Gummy Search

Gummy Search offers both free and paid plans <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:34">[00:01:34]</a>. The initial step involves navigating to the "trending" section within the platform <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:42">[00:01:42]</a>. This section displays top subreddits, which can be sorted by size, activity, or growth rate (daily, weekly, monthly, yearly) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:46">[00:01:46]</a>.

### Identifying Trending Communities
While large subreddits are visible, a key strategy is to focus on communities with 10,000 to 100,000 members <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:17">[00:02:17]</a>. These smaller, trending communities often represent newer interests that are gaining momentum, indicating a timely opportunity to build solutions for them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:28">[00:02:28]</a>. Users can filter by time frame (daily, weekly, monthly growth) to uncover different trending topics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:36">[00:03:36]</a>.

Gummy Search also provides a curated section of subreddits, such as those for software engineers, which users can add to their audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:15">[00:04:15]</a>. However, the primary focus for finding opportunities is on identifying growing communities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:34">[00:04:34]</a>.

## Finding Problems and Solutions within Communities

Once an interesting subreddit is selected, the next step is to identify problems and potential solutions within that community <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:56">[00:04:56]</a>. Gummy Search allows users to create "audiences" by grouping related subreddits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:57">[00:05:57]</a>. For example, an audience could be created for "people into dog food" that includes similar dog-related subreddits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:08">[00:06:08]</a>.

Within an audience, Gummy Search organizes content into categories, revealing core community insights:

*   **Pain and Anger**: This section highlights common grievances and frustrations within the community <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:39">[00:06:39]</a>. Gummy Search uses AI to summarize tens of thousands of words into common patterns, such as "potty training challenges with new or rehomed dogs" or "dogs reluctant to walk outside" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:08">[00:07:08]</a>. These patterns directly suggest startup ideas or product development opportunities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:18">[00:08:18]</a>.
*   **Advice Requests**: This category surfaces specific questions and requests for product or service recommendations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:08">[00:10:08]</a>. These are users "screaming" for solutions that might not yet exist or are poorly marketed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:36">[00:10:36]</a>. Examples include requests for dog breeding recommendations or dog food suitable for senior dogs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:55">[00:11:55]</a>.
*   **Solution Requests**: This section provides insights into how community members are currently addressing their problems and what products they are using <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:42">[00:12:42]</a>. It helps in understanding the competitive landscape and existing offerings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:50">[00:12:50]</a>.

Users are encouraged to take notes on potential ideas, even if existing solutions are present, as there might be opportunities due to poor marketing or a chance to create a simpler Minimum Viable Product (MVP) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:12">[00:09:12]</a>.

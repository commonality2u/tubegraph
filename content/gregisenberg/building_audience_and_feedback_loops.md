## [[strategies_for_growing_online_audiences | Building an Audience]] Through Content

### Finding Your Format and Voice
It's crucial to find a content format that feels natural <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:35">[00:12:35]</a>. Instead of imitating others, especially those who "teach," start by focusing on your own experience using "I" statements ("I did that, I learned that") rather than "you" statements ("you should be doing this") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:14">[00:13:14]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:25">[00:13:25]</a>.

Developing your own format involves:
1.  **Consuming content** and identifying specific elements you admire from various creators <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:32">[00:14:32]</a>.
2.  **Creating a "Creator Mount Rushmore"**: Select 3-4 influential creators and identify 1-2 takeaways from each that you want to implement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:31">[00:15:31]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:42">[00:15:42]</a>.
3.  **Experimenting** with these elements to develop a unique blend that becomes part of your identity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:53">[00:15:53]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:06">[00:15:06]</a>.

## Pricing and [[leveraging_community_insights_and_feedback_for_product_development | Feedback]]
When launching a digital product, it's generally advisable to start with a paywall rather than offering things for free <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:24">[00:16:24]</a>. This approach provides valuable lessons about the market and customer behavior <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:33">[00:16:33]</a>.

*   **Avoid pricing too cheaply** as it can lead to undervaluation and insufficient motivation to continue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:38">[00:16:38]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:51">[00:16:51]</a>.
*   **Price higher than expected** and research competitor pricing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:55">[00:16:55]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:57">[00:16:57]</a>.
*   **Consider one-time payments** for courses or products that don't provide recurring value <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:05">[00:17:05]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:31">[00:19:31]</a>.
    *   Many customers are weary of subscriptions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:08">[00:19:08]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:34">[00:19:34]</a>.
    *   If you offer a recurring payment, you should provide recurring value, typically for software <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:40">[00:19:40]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:44">[00:19:44]</a>.
    *   Payment models like credit systems can also work well <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:01">[00:20:01]</a>.
*   **Pricing can be adjusted**: Flexibility in pricing allows for experimentation and optimization based on market response <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:29">[00:18:29]</a>.

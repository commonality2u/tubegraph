## Understanding Customer Economics: Following the Money

A fundamental step in setting prices is to deeply understand the financial operations of businesses within the target industry <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:46">[16:46]</a>. This involves:

*   **Analyzing P&Ls:** Obtain and analyze profit and loss statements (P&Ls) from as many businesses as possible in the target industry <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:48">[16:48]</a>. Publicly traded companies in the sector are a good starting point <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:01">[17:01]</a>.
*   **Consulting Industry Bankers:** Bankers who specialize in the industry are an excellent source of P&L insights, as they are involved in buying and selling companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:08">[17:08]</a>.
*   **Identifying Spending Patterns:** P&L analysis reveals where companies spend their money, how much is spent on people that could be automated by software, and existing software expenditures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:10">[18:10]</a>. This helps in creating solutions that address challenging parts of the P&L <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:43">[18:43]</a>.

## Core Value Propositions for Pricing

Software should always aim to provide clear, measurable value to the customer. The core beliefs for building software, which directly influence [[pricing_strategies_for_digital_products | pricing]], are to:

*   **Increase Revenue:** Help businesses generate more income <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:34">[17:34]</a>.
*   **Decrease Costs:** Reduce operational expenses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:36">[17:36]</a>.
*   **Prevent Customer Churn:** Improve customer retention <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:41">[17:41]</a>.
*   **Maintain Compliance:** For highly regulated industries, software can be a "need to have" for compliance, not just a "nice to have" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:44">[17:44]</a>.

Software products should only be built if they address one of these four areas, as they provide clear Return on Investment (ROI) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="52:35">[52:35]</a>.

## Pricing Framework: Value-Based and ROI-Driven

The recommended [[pricing_strategies_for_digital_products | pricing]] approach is to understand the cost or value of the problem being solved for the customer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="50:01">[50:01]</a>.

*   **Quantify the Problem:** Determine how much the customer is currently paying for the problem (e.g., lost revenue from student dropouts) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="50:08">[50:08]</a>.
*   **Demonstrate ROI:** If the software can save five students who pay $20,000 in tuition, that represents a significant financial impact <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="51:19">[51:19]</a>.
*   **Price as a Portion of Value:** Charge a portion of the money the software saves or makes for the customer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="52:07">[52:07]</a>. A starting point could be 50% of the calculated ROI, adjusting based on market feedback <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="52:18">[52:18]</a>.

## Monetization Through Payments and Transactions

A critical strategy for building "massive" vertical SaaS companies is to integrate payments into the solution and own the transaction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="46:31">[46:31]</a>.

*   **Expanding Market Size:** By taking a percentage of each transaction, the potential market size for the [[What is vertical SaaS and its advantages | vertical SaaS]] company dramatically increases <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="47:41">[47:41]</a>. For example, if restaurants do a trillion in revenue, taking a small percentage transforms a $120 million software market into a multi-billion dollar opportunity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="47:51">[47:51]</a>.
*   **Seamless Integration:** Customers prefer to pay through a solution where their accounts are already made, and their schedules and data are known, rather than using fragmented third-party payment systems like PayPal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="48:40">[48:40]</a>. This also alleviates administrative burdens like exporting transaction records <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="49:03">[49:03]</a>.
*   **Transaction-Centric Wedge Products:** If a [[pricing_strategies_for_digital_products | wedge product]] (initial, easy-to-adopt offering) can be built either before or after a transaction, it creates an opportunity to eventually capture and monetize the transaction itself <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="46:22">[46:22]</a>.

## The Role of Wedge Products in Client Acquisition and Monetization

A "wedge product" is a crucial "get in the door" offering in [[What is vertical SaaS and its advantages | vertical SaaS]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="40:29">[40:29]</a>.

*   **Initial Offering:** Rather than attempting to build an "all-in-one" solution immediately, a wedge product focuses on solving a specific, important problem that is currently being done poorly (e.g., on paper or with suboptimal competition) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:12">[41:12]</a>.
*   **Ease of Adoption:** A good wedge product is easy to implement and deploy quickly, avoiding lengthy (e.g., 12-month) implementation periods <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:35">[41:35]</a>.
*   **Reputation Prover:** It serves as a "reputation prover," building trust with the customer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:40">[41:40]</a>, which is essential for future upselling <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="45:57">[45:57]</a>.
*   **Marketing-Led Acquisition:** Wedge products can enable [[marketing_strategies_for_saas_products | marketing-led customer acquisition]], which is generally faster and preferred by VCs compared to sales-led models <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:11">[37:11]</a>. For instance, Roofer.com used a free proposal tool, monetized by taking a percentage of bookings, enabling digital marketing unheard of in home services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="43:34">[43:34]</a>.
*   **Upselling and Expansion:** Once a customer is using the wedge product, it becomes significantly easier to sell them additional software solutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="44:57">[44:57]</a>. This allows the business to expand its offerings across the customer's entire operational lifecycle, eventually working towards an "all-in-one" solution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="44:20">[44:20]</a>.

## Go-to-Market Strategy and Average Contract Value (ACV)

Before investing significant time, it's crucial to understand and be comfortable with the go-to-market strategy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:23">[35:23]</a>.

*   **Customer Acquisition Models:** Many [[What is vertical SaaS and its advantages | vertical SaaS]] industries have traditionally required heavy outside sales models, involving face-to-face relationship building <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:30">[36:30]</a>.
*   **Sales-Led vs. Product-Led:** While sales-led motions can be effective, they typically require "massive" Average Contract Values (ACVs) to build large companies rapidly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:20">[37:20]</a>. Venture Capitalists (VCs) often prefer product-led or marketing-led customer acquisition due to their faster growth potential <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:09">[37:09]</a>.
*   **Media and Niche Engagement:** For blue-collar industries, non-traditional [[marketing_strategies_for_saas_products | marketing]] avenues like a daily, industry-specific meme [[newsletter_business_models_and_monetization_strategies | newsletter]] can build advocacy and trust, serving as a subtle lead generation tool for software <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="39:06">[39:06]</a>.
*   **AI for Market Research:** AI tools like ChatGPT can assist in initial desk research, such as market size, segmentation, and identifying prevalent software solutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:00">[23:00]</a>. However, in-person validation remains crucial to confirm online information with real-world operations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:08">[33:08]</a>. AI can help identify areas with paper processes, those not leveraging [[the_role_of_ai_and_payment_integration_in_vertical_saas | AI]], and those using fragmented horizontal solutions, all of which are potential opportunities for [[What is vertical SaaS and its advantages | vertical SaaS]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:06">[29:06]</a>.

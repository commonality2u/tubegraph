Validating a startup idea before committing to building the actual product can save significant time and resources <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>. ## The Genesis of Kettle and Fire: Identifying a Problem

In 2014, while working at Rackspace, Justin Mayers sought to start his own company <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:32">[00:01:32]</a>. He had heard about bone broth from friends in the CrossFit community and was reading about its benefits for skin, gut, and joint health <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:40">[00:01:40]</a>. At the time, bone broth was not widely discussed, and it was difficult to find online <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:46">[00:01:46]</a>.

Mayers recognized a personal desire for the product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:56">[00:01:56]</a>. His primary questions were:
1.  How many people genuinely want this product? <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:01">[00:02:01]</a>
2.  Are there enough potential customers, and will they spend enough to establish a meaningful business? <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:06">[00:02:06]</a>

## Step 1: Validating Market Demand (Macro & Micro)

To assess the macro demand, Mayers first explored niche communities where potential customers (e.g., paleo, CrossFit) congregated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:09">[00:03:09]</a>. He observed highly engaged discussions about bone broth on platforms like Mark's Daily Apple, Paleo Hacks, and Reddit, with conversations revolving around recipes, sourcing bones, and cooking times <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:30">[00:02:30]</a>. This indicated a fanatic and interested group <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:03">[00:03:03]</a>.

For macro-level validation, he then turned to [[Leveraging Google Trends for startup ideas | Google Trends]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:17">[00:03:17]</a>. The data showed that "bone broth" was an emerging trend, with increasing public interest <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:21">[00:03:21]</a>. This combination suggested that at least several thousand people were interested in the product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:30">[00:03:30]</a>.

::info
It's important to note that while data-driven insights like Google Trends are valuable, the initial idea stemmed from a personal problem and passion within a community Mayers cared about <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:17">[00:04:17]</a>. [[Leveraging Google Trends for startup ideas | Google Trends]] was used to validate the business potential, not to randomly find an idea <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:43">[00:04:43]</a>. Had there been no interest, the business would not have proceeded <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:03">[00:05:03]</a>.
::

## Step 2: Validating Willingness to Pay (Micro)

The crucial second question was whether people would pay enough to make a business viable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:05">[00:06:05]</a>. To test this, Mayers created a minimal validation setup:

1.  **Domain & Branding**: He purchased the cheapest bone broth-related domain, "Bone Broths Co.com," for $9.99 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:11">[00:06:11]</a>. He then paid $5 on Fiverr for a basic logo and product renders <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:40">[00:06:40]</a>.
2.  **Landing Page**: Using Unbounce (a tool from 2015), he built a landing page <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:50">[00:06:50]</a>. The value propositions on the page were derived from paleo forums and included "healing leaky gut," "convenience" (due to 24-48 hour cooking time), and "organic, 100% grass-fed ingredients" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:04">[00:07:04]</a>.
3.  **Pricing Strategy**: He set a price of $29.99 for 16 ounces, significantly higher than typical grocery store broth ($5 for 16 ounces) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:47">[00:07:47]</a>. The hypothesis was that if people paid six times more, the economics would work <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:58">[00:07:58]</a>.
4.  **"Janky" Checkout Flow**: Critically, the "Order Bone Broth Now" button redirected customers to Mayers' personal PayPal account for a $29.99 payment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:20">[00:08:20]</a>. The product itself did not yet exist <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:39">[00:07:39]</a>.
5.  **Traffic Generation**: He spent approximately $100 on [[Profitable startup ideas using Google ads | Bing Ads]] (which was cheaper than Adwords at the time) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:51">[00:08:51]</a>.

### Validation Results

Within a two-week test, despite the non-existent product and "super sketchy checkout flow," people actually converted <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:14">[00:09:14]</a>. The test yielded almost $500 in revenue from approximately $250 in ad spend <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:35">[00:09:35]</a>. The conversion rate for clicking the order button was around 30% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:22">[00:09:22]</a>. This confirmed that people were willing to pay for the product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:07">[00:10:07]</a>.

## Step 3: Post-Validation Engagement

After validating demand and willingness to pay, Mayers informed his early "customers" that the product wasn't in stock <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:15">[00:11:15]</a>. He offered a full refund or a 50% discount if they waited a few weeks (which turned into eight months) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:21">[00:11:21]</a>. Most customers chose the discount, indicating strong interest and patience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:40">[00:11:40]</a>. This process confirmed the viability of building Kettle and Fire, leading to eight months of product development and scaling <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:09">[00:12:09]</a>.

## Modernizing Validation in 2025: The Role of AI

If Kettle and Fire were to be built today, the process would be significantly enhanced by modern [[AI app marketing strategies | AI tools]]:

*   **Improved Design**: Landing pages and product renders would be much higher quality than those made with 2015 tools <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:43">[00:12:43]</a>.
*   **Sentiment Analysis**: [[Keyword Research using AI | AI and other tools]] could quickly scrape and analyze sentiment across Reddit, forums, and Twitter to gauge public discussion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:01">[00:13:01]</a>.
*   **Rapid Prototyping**: Tools like Bolt.new and Rok, along with frontier models such as Claude, GPT, and Gemini, allow for rapid creation of landing pages and product concepts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:00">[00:16:00]</a>. This enables testing dozens of startup ideas monthly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:55">[00:21:55]</a>.
*   **Reduced Barriers**: AI significantly lowers the barriers to building consumer packaged goods (CPG) brands, particularly in design and marketing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:08">[00:24:08]</a>. What once cost an agency $60,000 for design can now be achieved for as little as $20 a month with AI tools <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:26">[00:24:26]</a>.
*   **Individual Advantage**: AI empowers individual builders with strong opinions to create products that were previously the domain of small or medium-sized teams <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:42">[00:19:42]</a>.

## Finding Startup Opportunities

The best startup ideas often come from personal passion and interest, rather than solely focusing on maximizing profit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:11">[00:14:11]</a>. Key advice for finding opportunities includes:

*   **Follow Your Frontier**: Engage deeply with topics you're intensely interested in. Being among the "top 0.1% of people interested in a topic" often leads to novel and interesting ideas because you're naturally spending time there <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:01">[00:15:01]</a>.
*   **Solve Big Problems**: Identify significant global problems with relatively few people focused on them, or where energy is shifting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:07">[00:17:07]</a>. An example is the chronic disease crisis and food system issues, which Mayers has a long-standing interest in <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:21">[00:17:21]</a>.
*   **Circle the Problem**: Obsess over a problem that genuinely lights you up, and continuously explore potential solutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:45">[00:17:45]</a>. If you can address one of people's most important problems (like health), a successful business can follow <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:04">[00:18:04]</a>.
*   **Utilize Data and [[Tools for finding and analyzing business opportunities | AI Tools]]**: Regardless of the problem, it's crucial to validate the market, understand pain points, and analyze community sentiment (e.g., Reddit) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:38">[00:18:38]</a>. AI can help distill this data simply and accelerate the process of building and launching a landing page, potentially creating a startup in a single day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:14">[00:19:14]</a>.

The ability to quickly validate a product and market demand using readily available, often inexpensive, tools and AI, dramatically lowers the barrier to entry for entrepreneurs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:23">[00:19:23]</a>.

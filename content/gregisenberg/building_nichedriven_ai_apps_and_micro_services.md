## Core Idea: Find Trends, Build a Studio
The fundamental concept is to identify emerging trends and then establish a "studio" to rapidly develop products around them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:01">[00:00:01]</a>. The focus should be on "micro apps" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:03">[00:00:03]</a>. While finding trends can be challenging for the average person <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:12">[00:00:12]</a>, tools and methods for [[Niche AI Opportunities | trend analysis]] are becoming more accessible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:25">[00:00:25]</a>. ## Examples and Strategies

### Lightroom Preset Generator
One specific example of a niche AI app is a tool that generates Lightroom presets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:52:00">[01:52:00]</a>.

*   **Problem:** Photographers use Lightroom presets to achieve specific photo styles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:26:00">[01:26:00]</a>. There's a whole economy of people selling these presets in packs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:41:00">[01:41:00]</a>.
*   **AI Solution:** [[Using AI for app development | AI]] can be used to analyze a photo and create the necessary XML code for a Lightroom preset, effectively eroding the existing market for selling preset packs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:55:00">[01:55:00]</a>. This allows users to upload a source photo with a desired aesthetic, and the AI automatically creates the preset plugin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:38:00">[02:38:00]</a>.
*   **Business Model:** Turn this into a subscription software, for example, $29 a month, providing access to unlimited preset creation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:24:00">[02:24:00]</a>. The margin on this digital product is "infinite," around 99% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:41:00">[03:41:00]</a>.
*   **Content Strategy:**
    *   Allow customers to upload photos and build their own presets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:47:00">[03:47:00]</a>.
    *   Curate a library of popular presets by scraping photo keywords (e.g., "boho Lightroom presets"), generating the XML files, and offering them as downloads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:53:00">[03:53:00]</a>.
    *   Offer free preset packs to collect emails and upsell users into subscriptions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:18:00">[04:18:00]</a>.
*   **Marketing Strategy:**
    *   High search volume exists for specific presets (e.g., "X Lightroom preset") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:05:00">[03:05:00]</a>.
    *   Target Facebook ads to users interested in Photoshop, Lightroom, or Adobe products <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:11:00">[03:11:00]</a>.
    *   Purchase domain names matching popular search terms (e.g., "boho Lightroom preset") to rank high on Google and funnel traffic to the subscription service <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:30:00">[04:30:00]</a>. This strategy, similar to what injury lawyers use for lead farming <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:41:00">[06:41:00]</a>, leverages the effectiveness of longtail exact domains for SEO <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:38:00">[07:38:00]</a>. Building a portfolio of such domains can become a valuable digital real estate asset that can be sold later <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:48:00">[08:48:00]</a>.

### AI for E-commerce Newsletters
Another area for [[Niche AI Opportunities | niche AI opportunity]] is in [[Using AI for app development | AI]] for e-commerce newsletters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:57:00">[10:57:00]</a>.

*   **Problem:** E-commerce companies struggle with what to include in newsletters, how to talk about products, and the technical complexity of designing emails with hardcoded tables <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:13:00">[11:13:00]</a>. Many companies don't fully leverage their email marketing platforms like Klaviyo <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:10:00">[15:10:00]</a>.
*   **AI Solution:** An [[Implementing AI in app development processes | AI]] tool could help design and write e-commerce newsletters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:22:00">[11:22:00]</a>. This could involve:
    *   Modifying existing email templates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:27:00">[12:27:00]</a>.
    *   Using a chat interface to insert specific products <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:41:00">[11:41:00]</a>.
    *   Identifying catalog products relevant to events (e.g., holidays) and crafting narrative-driven content around them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:44:00">[12:44:00]</a>.
*   **Business Model:** [[Productized services using AI | Productized services]] or an agency model that helps businesses get the most out of high LTV (Lifetime Value) SaaS platforms like Klaviyo <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:59:00">[16:59:00]</a>. This can evolve into an [[Building a SaaS app using AI | AI]]-driven, fully autonomous solution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:03:00">[17:03:00]</a>. An example is Sage 100/300 consulting, a massive opportunity given the software's widespread use in construction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:16:00">[17:16:00]</a>.

### Specific AI Apps (API Wrapper Games)
There's a growing trend of "very specific AI apps" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:52:00">[32:52:00]</a>, often functioning as "API wrapper games" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:01:00">[34:01:00]</a>.

*   **Concept:** These apps perform one small, specific action that previously existed in more complex software (e.g., background removal, cloud removal) but are now simplified and made viral <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:48:00">[33:48:00]</a>. An example is "Text behind image," an app that went viral for creating YouTube thumbnails <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:56:00">[32:56:00]</a>.
*   **Technical Approach:** Reuse an app framework that simply replaces the "guts" (the underlying API call) for different small actions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:05:00">[34:05:00]</a>.
*   **Business Model:** Priced with credit packs (e.g., $4/month for 10 credits) or weekly subscriptions that translate to a higher monthly cost (e.g., $7/week, which is $29/month) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:24:00">[34:24:00]</a>. The acquisition cost can be very low ($3) for a high return ($100 over three months) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:50:00">[34:50:00]</a>. This model originated from ringtones <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:59:00">[34:59:00]</a>.
*   **Why it Works:**
    *   Large audience for simple actions (e.g., everyone edits photos) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="39:50:00">[39:50:00]</a>.
    *   People prefer less decision-making and specialized products over "Cheesecake Factory" style extensive menus (like Canva) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="40:02:00">[40:02:00]</a>.
    *   Facebook ad targeting for app installs is highly effective <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:20:00">[41:20:00]</a>, allowing broad targeting and letting the AI find the right audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:27:00">[41:27:00]</a>.

## The Larger Trend
The movement towards [[Building and deploying apps with AI integration | building and deploying AI apps]] and microservices is part of a larger trend where companies position themselves as "AI labs" or "product studios" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:43:00">[36:43:00]</a>. These entities leverage skilled engineers to rapidly push out products, often within 24 hours, to capitalize on ephemeral media trends that might last 90 days <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:51:00">[36:51:00]</a>.

Even large companies like Adobe create micro apps that funnel users to their main products <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="38:07:00">[38:07:00]</a>. Veed.io, an online video editor, launched a standalone captions app that reached $1 million ARR in 27 days <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="38:28:00">[38:28:00]</a>. This demonstrates the power of single-purpose apps if the target audience is large enough, even if only a small percentage converts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="38:53:00">[38:53:00]</a>. This approach is central to [[Building scalable businesses with AI | building scalable businesses with AI]].

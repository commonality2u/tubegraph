## TikTok Organic as an Underpriced Distribution Channel
TikTok organic content is considered the "biggest underpriced distribution channel" currently available <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:41">[00:01:41]</a>. The cost of creating and distributing content on TikTok is extremely low, making it accessible for various consumer apps <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:50">[00:01:50]</a>.

Apps can achieve significant growth, such as $10,000 Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) within a few months, by replicating successful content and app models seen on TikTok <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:17">[00:01:17]</a>.

## Borrowing Tactics from E-commerce
Many effective TikTok tactics for apps are adapted from the e-commerce sector, especially since TikTok Shop launched in November 2022 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:26">[00:02:26]</a>.

An example is Tabs Chocolate, an e-commerce company that achieved $10 million/year by utilizing mass User-Generated Content (UGC) creators <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:55">[00:02:55]</a>. They hired numerous teenagers, organized them in a Discord server, and mass-produced content daily <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:07">[00:03:07]</a>. The founder later pivoted to a micro-SaaS business, applying the same [[marketing_and_selling_apps | marketing strategies]] for a consumer SaaS with higher margins <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:16">[00:03:16]</a>.

## Effective TikTok Content Formats for Apps

### AI-Generated Slideshows
Surprisingly, photo carousels or slideshows on TikTok, often featuring AI-generated images (e.g., Midjourney), are highly effective <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:28">[00:07:28]</a>. While they might have lower conversion rates than human-led videos, their ease of creation allows for greater scale and lower barrier to entry <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:02">[00:08:02]</a>.

Example: A video promoting a colostrum product used AI-generated images, a relatable hook ("POV you divorced bread"), and an audio clip to address common pain points like energy and brain fog, driving traffic to Amazon <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:07">[00:04:07]</a>. The same concept can be applied to apps by offering an app-based solution to similar pain points <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:30">[00:05:30]</a>.

### Negative Hooks and Problem/Solution
Videos with "strong negative hooks" can capture attention <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:44">[00:05:44]</a>. The POV app, a disposable camera app, uses slideshows with hooks like "Our Guest hated the controversial rules we enforce at our wedding" to introduce the app as a solution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:50">[00:05:50]</a>. This app consistently posts wedding-themed stories, sometimes using AI actors or repeating visually engaging first images like a cake on the face <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:20">[00:06:20]</a>.

Once a content format works, it can be "spammed" daily for consistent views <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:08">[00:07:08]</a>.

## Leveraging TikTok Shop Creators for App Promotion
A significant opportunity exists in engaging TikTok Shop creators to promote apps instead of just e-commerce products <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:25">[00:12:25]</a>.

### TikTok Shop Affiliate Center
The TikTok Shop Affiliate Center provides valuable data on high-volume products <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:15">[00:11:15]</a>. Users can filter by industry (e.g., skincare) and performance to find creators who are already skilled at generating organic views and high Gross Merchandise Volume (GMV) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:38">[00:11:38]</a>.

These creators are adept at making videos that both get views and convert sales <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:54">[00:18:54]</a>. By partnering with them, app developers can tap into proven content styles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:06">[00:19:06]</a>.

### Product Integration Ideas
Apps can be integrated into existing e-commerce content. For instance, a skincare app could analyze a user's face and suggest products, and TikTok creators could use it to show "before and after" transformations, casually mentioning the app as a utility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:16">[00:19:16]</a>. This provides a free marketing channel as creators would use the app to enhance their existing content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:25">[00:20:25]</a>. This strategy applies to various niches like makeup, personal care, fashion, and sports <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:38">[00:20:38]</a>.

Digital products (apps) have a lower price point but can achieve higher volume compared to physical products, making the conversion easier <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:06">[00:21:06]</a>. App subscriptions also often have zero cost of goods and are infinitely scalable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:54">[00:21:54]</a>.

## The Shift in App Discovery and Monetization
In the early days (2009-2011), the App Store was the primary distribution channel, featuring apps and generating hundreds of thousands of downloads weekly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:36">[00:16:36]</a>. Today, TikTok and Instagram are the main platforms for content discovery <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:03">[00:17:03]</a>. People rarely open the App Store looking for new apps <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:10">[00:17:10]</a>.

TikTok can serve as a top-of-funnel distribution channel. Once an app gains traction on TikTok, its App Store ranking improves, leading to organic downloads through App Store Optimization (ASO) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:32">[00:17:32]</a>.

The rise of iOS subscriptions, facilitated by features like "Double Tap to Pay" (Apple Pay), has changed people's perception of paying for apps, leading to a boom in revenue for apps <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:30">[00:15:30]</a>.

## TikTok Algorithm and Influencer Marketing
TikTok's "For You Page" algorithm means people primarily watch content from new accounts, not necessarily those they follow <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:47">[00:33:47]</a>.

### Redefining Influencer Marketing
Traditional influencer marketing (paying influencers with millions of followers) is expensive and less effective on TikTok <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:15">[00:37:15]</a>. On TikTok, creators should be viewed as "actors" rather than directors or audience providers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:10">[00:34:10]</a>. They can have zero followers, focusing solely on creating good content that aligns with TikTok's algorithm <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:46">[00:35:46]</a>.

Brands can create multiple new brand accounts and hire one creator for each, as seen with Tabs Chocolate and Airbuds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:17">[00:36:17]</a>. This is a very cheap approach <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:02">[00:37:02]</a>.

### Algorithm Optimization
Creating new accounts for specific content formats is beneficial. If an account consistently produces a certain type of content that resonates with a specific audience, the algorithm learns to serve it to that audience effectively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:08">[00:39:08]</a>. Using too many different "faces" or content styles on a single account can confuse the algorithm and hurt reach <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:19">[00:40:19]</a>.

## Building "Cult Movements" Around Apps
Identifying existing "movements" or ideologies that lack a product attached to them can lead to multi-million dollar businesses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:19">[00:43:19]</a>. Businesses that are "cult-like" often scale effectively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:24">[00:46:24]</a>.

### Examples:
- **Umax (Looksmaxing app):** Capitalizes on the "looksmaxing" movement, where men seek to improve their physical appearance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:08">[00:41:08]</a>. The app scans faces and provides tips on improving features like jawline and skin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:48">[00:41:48]</a>.
- **Crayo (Wealth-focused app):** Partners with YouTubers and TikTokers who teach teenagers how to make money online <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:45">[00:44:45]</a>. The app productizes the course content, automating video creation for platforms like YouTube Shorts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:32">[00:45:32]</a>.

A framework for finding these ideas revolves around universal human desires: health, wealth, and relationships <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:55">[00:43:55]</a>.

## The Importance of Product Retention
Before investing heavily in viral marketing, ensure the app is high-quality and retains users <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:54">[00:46:54]</a>. A "leaky bucket" (poor retention) means marketing efforts will be wasted <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:12">[00:48:12]</a>. Understanding user Lifetime Value (LTV) and Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) through retention metrics allows for more informed [[paid_advertising_strategies_for_app_growth | marketing spend]] and improved strategies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:47">[00:47:47]</a>. The core benefit of an app is its scalability and retentiveness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:26">[00:48:26]</a>.

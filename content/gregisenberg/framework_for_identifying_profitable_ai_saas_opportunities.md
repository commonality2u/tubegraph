## The Export Button Theory of AI Opportunity

The core idea for finding AI SaaS opportunities is based on the "export button theory" [00:02:43]. Every export button in software represents a business opportunity [00:02:48]. When a user clicks "export," they are indicating that the current software doesn't meet their needs, and they are taking their data elsewhere to perform manual work [00:02:59]. Each export signifies:
*   A workflow breakdown [00:03:09]
*   A pain point [00:03:10]
*   Manual labor that could be automated [00:03:12]
*   A potential feature worth $10,000 to $30,000 a month [00:03:15]

### 5. Starting Small and Growing Naturally (Niche Focus)
The most successful AI SaaS businesses focus on specific niches that larger players often overlook [00:14:08].

Think in terms of horizontal, niche, and sub-niche [00:14:25]:
*   **Horizontal:** Legal category [00:14:34]
*   **Niche:** Divorce category (sub-niche of legal) [00:14:38]
*   **Sub-Niche:** Prenup category (sub-niche of divorce) [00:14:44]

When developing an AI SaaS startup, target a very small, specific problem. For example, creating an AI version of prenups for easy generation [00:15:01]. This helps avoid direct competition with highly funded businesses, especially considering the large number of AI SaaS companies in recent YC batches [00:15:17].

Successful AI SaaS businesses:
*   Are niche-focused [00:15:45]
*   Focus on one painful workflow and make it 10 times better with AI [00:15:48]
*   Let the AI suggest next actions [00:15:52]
*   Charge immediately from day one if solving a real pain [00:15:55]
*   Allow users to pull you into adjacent problems, indicating expansion opportunities [00:16:14]

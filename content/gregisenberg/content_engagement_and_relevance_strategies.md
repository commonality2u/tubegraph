Building an audience and creating engaging content is a crucial aspect of modern marketing and business growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:16">[00:00:16]</a>. This article outlines a four-step playbook for building an audience in 2025, developed over 15 years, focusing on an "audience-first" approach <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:55">[00:00:55]</a>.

## The ACP Framework

The foundation of this strategy is the ACP framework, which prioritizes audience, then community, then product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:49">[00:01:49]</a>:
*   **A: Audience** – People in your niche who are passionate about a topic and currently underserved <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:56">[00:01:56]</a>.
*   **C: Community** – Your hardcore followers and supporters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:01">[00:02:01]</a>.
*   **P: Product** – What you sell to those people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:08">[00:02:08]</a>.

It is highly recommended to start with the audience, then build the community, and finally develop the product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:17">[00:02:17]</a>. This "audience-first" approach offers more options and confidence upon release, as opposed to the "product-first" approach which can be soul-destroying if no one uses your offering <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:36">[00:02:36]</a>. For bootstrap businesses, starting with the audience is the most sensible path <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:10">[00:03:10]</a>.

> "Dig the well before you need the water." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:22">[00:03:22]</a>

## Step 1: Identify and Understand Your Audience

The initial step is to clearly identify who you are creating content for <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:54">[00:03:54]</a>.
*   **Focus on a specific niche**: For example, people interested in startup ideas and building them using the ACP framework <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:05">[00:04:05]</a>.
*   **Understand unmet needs**: Crucially, identify what your target audience is not currently getting from other creators <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:27">[00:04:27]</a>. This allows you to stand out by providing unique value, referred to as "the sauce" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:57">[00:04:57]</a>. This might involve sharing every detail, giving away strategies, or providing comprehensive playbooks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:05">[00:05:05]</a>.
*   **Adjust niche breadth for engagement**: If engagement is low, consider starting with a slightly broader niche to gain reach before specializing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:38">[00:07:38]</a>. A very narrow niche might not get enough exposure on platforms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:54">[00:07:54]</a>. For example, broadening from "design Sprints" to "UX design" to attract a larger initial audience before funneling them to the specific topic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:13">[00:08:13]</a>.

## Step 2: Figure Out Your Content Format

A critical element often overlooked is the content format <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:55">[00:09:55]</a>.
*   **Define your format**: This refers to the style and length of your content, e.g., short philosophical tweets, long-form articles, videos, or images <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:07">[00:10:07]</a>.
*   **Experimentation**: Pick one format for each business day and test it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:52">[00:10:52]</a>. The goal is to find one that performs exceptionally well, then double down on it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:03">[00:11:03]</a>.
*   **Stand out**: Look for formats outside your niche to avoid saturation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:21">[00:11:21]</a>. Copying a popular creator in their exact format makes it harder to compete <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:27">[00:11:27]</a>.
*   **Platform-wide trends**: Consider what formats are currently favored by algorithms, such as short-form video in 2025 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:23">[00:12:23]</a>.
*   **Consistency**: Choose formats that feel natural and enjoyable to create to ensure you stay consistent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:41">[00:12:41]</a>.
*   **Data-driven decisions**: Test formats for 90 days and use platform analytics to determine what's working <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:11">[00:13:11]</a>.
*   **Format life cycles**: Be aware that formats have life cycles, meaning their popularity can wane <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:33">[00:13:33]</a>. Constantly reinvent yourself and observe what others are doing for fresh insights <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:49">[00:13:49]</a>. A life cycle can be as short as two weeks now <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:56">[00:13:56]</a>.

## Step 3: Implement Systems for Consistency

Systems are essential to ensure you consistently execute the ACP funnel and build your audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:30">[00:19:30]</a>.
*   **Creative faucet routine**: Identify and build a routine that gets your creative juices flowing, whether it's following niche accounts, attending events, or taking walks and listening to podcasts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:59">[00:19:59]</a>.
*   **Ideas capture system**: Maintain a simple system, like Apple Notes, to quickly jot down content ideas (e.g., tweet ideas, video concepts, newsletter topics) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:30">[00:20:30]</a>.
*   **Dedicated content creation time**: Set aside weekly time to transform your captured ideas into content, treating them as minimal viable products <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:14">[00:21:14]</a>. Use scheduling tools to automate posting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:50">[00:21:50]</a>.
*   **Strategic timing**:
    *   Match platform timing to audience behavior (e.g., don't post on LinkedIn at 3 AM on a Saturday) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:04">[00:22:04]</a>.
    *   Post when topics are relevant and timely <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:24">[00:22:24]</a>.
    *   Look for opportunities to join trending conversations (newsjacking) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:37">[00:22:37]</a>.

## Step 4: Pick a Primary Goal and Measure

Set a clear objective and consistently measure your progress <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:36">[00:24:36]</a>.
*   **Single primary goal**: Choose one main goal, such as gaining 100,000 followers in 12 months, growing an email list to 25,000, or achieving $1 million in sales via social media <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:47">[00:24:47]</a>. Avoid picking multiple goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:57">[00:24:57]</a>.
*   **Measure effectiveness**: Track the effectiveness of your content formats against this goal every 30, 60, or 90 days <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:01">[00:25:01]</a>.
*   **Optimize for goals, not vanity metrics**: Focus on metrics that directly contribute to your primary goal, rather than superficial numbers like subscriber counts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:45">[00:25:45]</a>. For example, comments and positive engagement might be more valuable than just subscriber numbers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:09">[00:26:09]</a>.
*   **Treat content like a product**: Approach content creation with the same rigor as you would a job <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:21">[00:25:21]</a>.

## Why Build an Audience?

Building an audience offers significant advantages and is viewed as an unfair advantage for businesses and individuals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:23">[00:19:23]</a>.
*   **Organic vs. Paid Attention**: Building an audience organically (for free) is preferable to paying for attention, especially for bootstrap businesses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:14">[00:17:14]</a>.
*   **Enhanced Product Development**: Being attuned to your audience and community allows for the creation of better products with more durable advantages and indispensable businesses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:35">[00:17:35]</a>.
*   **Better Ads**: Organic insights gained from audience building can lead to more effective paid advertising campaigns in the future <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:57">[00:17:57]</a>.
*   **An Asset and Insurance Policy**: An audience acts as a long-term asset, much like index funds, compounding dividends over years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:05">[00:16:05]</a>. It provides "insurance" – something to fall back on in uncertain times <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:23">[00:36:23]</a>.
*   **Market Clarity**: Engaging with an audience provides deep clarity into their desires, motivations, and incentives within your niche <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:44">[00:36:44]</a>.
*   **Direct Access**: You can tap into your audience for potential customers, beta users, or even job opportunities, leveraging built-in trust <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:08">[00:37:08]</a>.
*   **Revenue Generation and Options**: An audience provides the ability to "switch on" revenue. If you have a product idea, you can present it to an existing audience with predictable results, making business feel less tense <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:09">[00:34:09]</a>. It also gives you more career and business options <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:52">[00:33:52]</a>.
*   **Leveraging Creativity**: Having an audience allows you to focus on creating interesting, valuable content without constantly worrying about how to get it seen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:34">[00:39:34]</a>.
*   **Compounding Dividends**: The effort put into building an audience continues to pay dividends for years, even if consistent effort lessens over time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:44">[00:31:44]</a>.

## Starting and Overcoming Overwhelm

The process can seem daunting, but starting small is key <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:00">[00:28:00]</a>.
*   **Best Starting Platform**: X (formerly Twitter) is recommended as the best place to start for experimentation with formats, regardless of what you're building <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:02">[00:29:02]</a>.
*   **The "Reply Guy" Tactic**: To gain initial momentum and confidence, start by primarily replying to others' posts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:08">[00:29:08]</a>.
    *   This allows you to experiment with formats through replies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:36">[00:29:36]</a>.
    *   It is less daunting than posting directly on your profile, reducing the fear of judgment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:41">[00:29:41]</a>.
*   **Finding Your Voice**: Don't be afraid to start, even if you feel you have nothing new to say <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:44">[00:42:44]</a>. By engaging and replying, you will naturally discover your unique angle, voice, and format <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:59">[00:42:59]</a>.
*   **Originality by Being Yourself**: Even if discussing common topics, your unique personality, energy, and life experiences will make your content different and automatically original <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:11">[00:43:11]</a>.
    *   This is akin to bands starting as cover bands and evolving their unique sound <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:23">[00:44:23]</a>.
    *   Even successful products often start as "clones" but develop their own distinct vibe and identity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:50">[00:44:50]</a>. Originality often emerges accidentally from consistent self-expression <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:45">[00:45:45]</a>.

Ultimately, building an audience is a long-term investment that provides immense value, predictability, and safety for your professional and personal life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:34">[00:34:34]</a>.

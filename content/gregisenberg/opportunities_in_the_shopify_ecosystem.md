## Identifying Pain Points

To succeed in building products for [[shopify_for_x_startup_ideas | Shopify]], Daniel suggests a deep understanding of customer pain points <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:52">[00:45:52]</a>. This involves "getting your hands dirty" and figuring out solutions to problems that businesses face <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:03">[00:46:03]</a>.

## Business Models and Strategy

Daniel suggests that within the [[shopify_for_x_startup_ideas | Shopify]] ecosystem, it's easier to justify higher monthly subscriptions (e.g., $49-$99/month) for apps compared to general SaaS products, because there is always cash flowing through DTC businesses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:14">[00:49:14]</a>. The key is to solve a significant problem that justifies the cost <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:31">[00:49:31]</a>.

### Niching Down
It is crucial to niche down within broader problem areas. For instance, instead of just "CRO," consider "CRO for local websites" or "CRO for construction workers" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:07">[00:50:07]</a>. This creates a stronger moat and reduces direct competition with large players like Google or even [[shopify_for_x_startup_ideas | Shopify]] itself <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:09">[00:51:09]</a>.

### Acquisition Strategy
While not his preferred long-term strategy, Daniel acknowledges the possibility of building a [[shopify_for_x_startup_ideas | Shopify]] app with the intent of being acquired by [[shopify_for_x_startup_ideas | Shopify]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:30">[00:51:30]</a>. This can lead to "generational wealth" if the timing and market share are right, but it is considered a "big bet" or akin to playing a slot machine rather than poker due to the reliance on external factors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:40">[00:52:40]</a>. Daniel prefers bootstrapping and focusing on a cash flow positive business that can grow organically <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:47">[00:54:47]</a>.

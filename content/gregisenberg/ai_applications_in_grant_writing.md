-   **Information Aggregation**: Accessing data from all grant aggregation websites <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:14">[00:56:14]</a>.
-   **Opportunity Ranking**: Ranking grant opportunities based on a company's information and likelihood of success, factoring in grant size and other criteria <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:16">[00:56:16]</a>.
-   **Automated Writing**: Bridging the gap between company information and grant requirements to write proposals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:38">[00:56:38]</a>.

## Market Potential
The grant writing industry is massive, with examples like the construction of most HUD housing being subsidized through grant money <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:46">[00:56:46]</a>. Builders write for grant money to get projects subsidized for free, eventually owning the building outright after a period <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:56">[00:56:56]</a>. This concept is particularly impactful in countries with more socialist policies than the United States <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:30">[00:57:30]</a>. For instance, the Canadian government allocates $3.2 billion annually for student grants alone <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:46">[00:57:46]</a>.

### Renewable Energy Investments
Public documents from various states detail the best geographies for solar and wind farms, including wind consistency and energy production per acre <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:53">[01:01:53]</a>.

*   **Wind Power**: An acre of land in a high-energy density area, costing around $1,000, could host a wind turbine costing approximately $1.3 million per megawatt <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:00">[01:03:00]</a>. Such a turbine could potentially generate $600,000 per year in energy sales on the open market <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:53">[01:03:53]</a>. This suggests a payback period of 2.4 years for a turbine with an average lifespan of 30 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:48">[01:04:48]</a>.
    *   The primary challenges for wind turbines are manufacturing and transport <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:58">[01:09:58]</a>.
*   **Solar Power**: Commercial solar projects offer significant tax benefits, including an upfront federal tax credit of 40% (e.g., $400,000 for a $1 million investment), depreciation that offsets federal and state taxes, and an income stream from the project <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:05">[01:07:05]</a>.
    *   Solar panels can be fully depreciated over five years (minus half the tax credit) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:43">[01:07:43]</a>.
    *   The main challenge for solar is the upkeep due to breakage and cleaning <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:34">[01:09:34]</a>.
*   **Market Trends**: Wind power saw 10 times more investment than solar in 2023, despite solar being cheaper <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:11">[01:09:11]</a>. Renewable energy is already cheaper than natural gas, and solar costs are projected to decrease further <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:11">[01:10:11]</a>. Notably, Texas is currently the leading state in renewable energy production in the US <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:18">[01:11:18]</a>.
    *   This shift has created new "natural resources" for states: sun and land for solar farms, and consistent wind for wind farms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:57">[01:11:57]</a>.

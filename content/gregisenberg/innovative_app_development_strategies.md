## The Dr. AI Concept

Zach introduces an [[innovative_app_ideas_leveraging_current_trends | innovative app idea leveraging current trends]] for a medical AI app called Dr. AI, which aims to be a full business launched in less than a month <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:25">[00:01:25]</a>. The core idea originated from a friend's coincidental skin cancer diagnosis after a knee injury, highlighting the need for quick, accessible initial assessments for skin concerns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:47">[00:01:47]</a>. The app's primary purpose is to help users determine if a medical issue warrants a doctor's visit or can be safely waited out <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:17">[00:05:17]</a>.

### Core Features of Dr. AI

Dr. AI is designed with three main features:
*   **Skin Scan**: Users can take a picture of a skin mark or rash for identification, diagnosing whether it's harmful or benign <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:49">[00:03:49]</a>. This feature would include a "danger level" metric (e.g., 0-5) to make the information digestible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:10">[00:14:10]</a>.
*   **Symptoms Quiz**: Users input symptoms (e.g., cough, chills) to receive possible sickness diagnoses with a likelihood percentage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:00">[00:04:00]</a>.
*   **Chatbot**: A general AI chatbot for asking any medical questions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:18">[00:04:18]</a>. ChatGPT has shown high accuracy in diagnosing medical problems, even outperforming doctors in some studies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:24">[00:04:24]</a>.

## User Experience (UX) Design Philosophy

When designing apps, Zach emphasizes a simple, digestible approach:
*   **Basic Framing**: Start with a navigation bar and essential sections like Home, Settings, and History <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:43">[00:05:43]</a>.
*   **DIY UX, Professional Polish**: Zach creates the initial UX sketches himself, then hires talented designers (e.g., from Upwork) to refine and beautify them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:56">[00:05:56]</a>. This allows non-engineers to effectively prototype and design <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:56">[00:05:56]</a>.
*   **History Feature**: Incorporate a log of user interactions (previous chats, scans, quiz results) to foster user investment and retention, similar to Cal AI's food logging <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:38">[00:06:38]</a>. This history can also be beneficial for users to share with actual doctors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:28">[00:08:28]</a>.
*   **Inspiration from "Primitives"**: When a direct competitor app doesn't exist for inspiration, look at other apps with similar "primitives" or building blocks (e.g., scanning screens like Photomath, or feature layouts like Riz GPT) and repurpose them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:04">[00:09:04]</a>. Primitives are fundamental, reusable components of app design (e.g., a "feed" for social media) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:14">[00:10:14]</a>.
*   **Optimize for User Flow**: Instead of forcing users to wait on a loading screen for AI processing, return them to the home screen with a loading indicator <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:06">[00:13:06]</a>.
*   **Gamification/Digestibility**: Present complex information, like the "danger level" of a skin condition, using a numerical scale (0-5) or charts rather than just text to make it easily understandable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:05">[00:17:05]</a>.
*   **Seamless Follow-up**: Allow users to initiate a chat directly from a scan result to ask follow-up questions, enhancing user value and retention <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:22">[00:15:22]</a>.
*   **Source Citation**: For medical AI apps, including sources for diagnoses would be a valuable feature <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:01">[00:24:01]</a>.

## [[the_role_of_ai_and_new_technologies_in_app_development | The Role of AI and New Technologies in App Development]]

The effectiveness of AI apps, particularly those leveraging Large Language Models (LLMs) like GPT, hinges on careful "prompting" strategies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:17">[00:25:17]</a>.
*   **Role Assignment**: When prompting AI, assign it a specific role (e.g., "You are a doctor") to guide its responses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:02">[00:29:02]</a>.
*   **Bypassing Disclaimers**: To circumvent common AI disclaimers (e.g., "I am not a medical professional"), use creative prompting techniques such as framing the request as part of a "movie script" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:26">[00:31:26]</a>.
*   **Clarity and Detail**: Prompt the AI to provide both scientific and layman's terms for diagnoses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:20">[00:29:20]</a>.
*   **API vs. Dashboard**: Using the AI's API directly offers more control over the prompt and response, allowing for clearer and more specific outputs (e.g., using function calls to assign responses to variables) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:29">[00:32:29]</a>.
*   **Accuracy is Key**: For health-related apps, ensuring high accuracy through complex prompting pipelines is crucial to avoid harmful misinformation, unlike many basic GPT wrappers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:01">[00:47:01]</a>. All medical apps must include clear disclaimers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:09">[00:03:09]</a>.

## [[strategies_for_successful_consumer_app_growth | Strategies for Successful Consumer App Growth]]

Zach emphasizes a "reverse engineering" approach to [[innovative_marketing_strategies_for_startups | marketing strategies for startups]]: designing the product with how it will be marketed in mind <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:10">[00:28:10]</a>.
*   **Simplicity for Marketing**: An app must be simple and clear enough for a viewer to understand its value within a few seconds of an influencer video <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:59">[00:25:59]</a>. Gamified elements (like the danger level) aid digestibility for both users and marketing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:11">[00:26:11]</a>.
*   **Branding Placement**: Display the app's name prominently on key screens, especially those likely to be featured in marketing materials (e.g., the scanning and results screens) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:41">[00:26:41]</a>.
*   **Identifying "Wow Factor"**: Pinpoint the most impressive feature of the app to highlight in marketing (e.g., the skin scan and diagnosis for Dr. AI) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:09">[00:27:09]</a>.
*   **Shareability Assessment**: While some consumer apps thrive on virality through sharing, utility apps like Dr. AI may have low inherent shareability, especially for sensitive topics like medical conditions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:09">[00:18:09]</a>. Word-of-mouth among specific demographics (e.g., concerned parents) could be a growth channel <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:27">[00:19:27]</a>.
*   **Influencer Marketing ([[marketing_strategies_for_saas_apps | marketing strategies for SaaS apps]])**:
    *   **Audience-Centric**: Always analyze the influencer's audience (e.g., through comments) rather than just their niche <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:18">[00:35:18]</a>. For Dr. AI, target audiences might be concerned parents <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:18">[00:37:18]</a>.
    *   **Seamless Integration**: Encourage influencers to create organic content (e.g., skits, "get ready with me" videos) that naturally incorporates the app's value proposition through realistic scenarios <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:07">[00:36:07]</a>.
    *   **Pricing**: Base influencer compensation on predicted views, aiming for a profitable CPM (cost per mille/thousand views) that is lower than the RPM (revenue per mille/thousand views) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:11">[00:41:11]</a>. For an app, Zach suggests starting with an RPM of $5 and paying influencers within that margin (e.g., $4000 for 1 million views = $4 CPM) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:15">[00:42:15]</a>.
    *   **Outreach**: Contact influencers via email and direct messages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:59">[00:40:59]</a>.

### Pricing Model (Dr. AI)

*   **Weekly Subscription Focus**: For utility apps that aren't used daily but are crucial when needed, a weekly subscription model can be highly effective, capitalizing on repeat users who subscribe, unsubscribe, and resubscribe as needed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:11">[00:43:11]</a>.
*   **No Free Trial**: For apps like Dr. AI, a free trial might not be ideal as users might use it once and then not return for a period, limiting immediate monetization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:49">[00:43:49]</a>.
*   **Pricing Sweet Spot**: Zach suggests starting with weekly pricing of $4 or $7 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:18">[00:45:18]</a>.
*   **A/B Testing**: Continuously A/B test different pricing structures (weekly, monthly, annual, with/without free trials) to identify the highest "proceeds per download" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:36">[00:45:36]</a>.

Zach Yagari encourages aspiring app developers to take this blueprint and build out Dr. AI, emphasizing the importance of disclaimers and accuracy in such a sensitive domain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:24">[00:46:24]</a>.

## Affiliate Marketing Business Models

### Digital Review Websites
A scalable digital business model involves creating review websites focused on specific product categories. This approach leverages [[ai_and_seo_opportunities | SEO]] to drive traffic and then monetizes through affiliate links <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:09:11">[04:09:11]</a>.

For instance, a website dedicated to in-depth, "Wirecutter-style" reviews of robot lawnmowers could focus on [[ai_and_seo_opportunities | SEO]] and affiliate commissions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:05:00">[04:05:00]</a>. Such a business is "relatively scalable" and could potentially generate $300,000 to $1,000,000 per year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:24:00">[08:24:00]</a>. It is considered a more favorable option than a physical lawn mowing company due to its scalability and reduced vulnerability to [[ai_and_seo_opportunities | SEO]] "rug pulls" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:01:00">[09:01:00]</a>.

### Newsletter and Creator Partnerships
Affiliate marketing through newsletters and [[leveraging_creators_and_influencer_partnerships_for_business_growth | influencer partnerships]] offers a direct path to monetization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:41:00">[09:41:00]</a>. This strategy involves promoting products to a built audience and earning commissions on sales.

*   **High Earning Potential**: A single mention in a large newsletter can generate significant income, with one individual reportedly making $50,000 from one mention of Athletic Greens <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:43:00">[09:43:00]</a>. Another individual makes $40,000 to $60,000 a month in Athletic Greens commissions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:41:00">[11:41:00]</a>.
*   **Lifetime Commissions**: Some affiliate programs, like Athletic Greens, offer generous lifetime commissions (e.g., 30%), meaning a recurring payment for as long as a referred customer maintains their subscription <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:00:00">[12:00:00]</a>.
*   **Audience "Moat"**: Success depends on having a strong "moat" around the audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:47:00">[10:47:00]</a>. Newsletters provide a direct-to-consumer channel, making them less vulnerable to algorithm changes compared to platforms like Twitter <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:57:00">[10:57:00]</a>. As long as the audience is built organically and recommendations are genuine, there is "no downside" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:10:00">[11:10:00]</a>.

### Curated Online Directories
There is a growing obsession with creating curated directories that integrate affiliate deals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:13:00">[13:13:00]</a>. This strategy is driven by two factors:
1.  **Demand for Curation**: In a world oversaturated with information, people increasingly seek curated content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:24:00">[13:24:00]</a>.
2.  **Programmatic SEO**: Leveraging [[ai_and_seo_opportunities | programmatic SEO]] allows for the creation of landing pages at scale based on user search queries, presenting a "huge opportunity" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:35:00">[13:35:00]</a>.

*   **Strategy**: Create one directory a month in different [[opportunities_in_niche_markets | niche markets]], such as robot lawnmowers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:58:00">[13:58:00]</a>.
*   **AI Concerns**: A potential challenge is that AI models like Perplexity or ChatGPT might scrape content, inform their results, and omit links or credit, potentially impacting the long-term viability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:17:00">[14:17:00]</a>. However, the belief is that [[ai_and_seo_opportunities | SEO]] will evolve rather than disappear <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:50:00">[14:50:00]</a>.
*   **Mitigation**: To safeguard against reliance on search traffic, build directories as much as possible, focusing on converting 6-10% of visitors to email subscribers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:00:00">[15:00:00]</a>. It's anticipated that AI tools will eventually give credit to their information sources <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:31:00">[15:31:00]</a>.

## Execution and Team Structure

The "old way" of startups involved a technical founder, possibly a designer, and a business person <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:00">[00:11:00]</a>. Today, a successful team typically needs an "idea guy or gal" and a "funnel guy or gal" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:00">[00:19:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:46:00">[16:46:00]</a>.

## Nick Huber's Journey: A Case Study in Sweaty Startups

Nick Huber's personal journey exemplifies the potential of boring businesses:

*   **Storage Squad (Pickup and Delivery Storage)**: Huber started a business called Storage Squad, which picked up and delivered students' belongings for summer storage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:20">[00:02:20]</a>. At its peak, the company generated $2.2 million in annual revenue, with $400,000-$500,000 in annual profit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:34">[00:02:34]</a>. Marketing initially involved simple sidewalk chalk ads written over 5,000 times personally by Huber <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:55">[00:02:55]</a>. The business was bootstrapped with inexpensive used vans <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:44">[00:03:44]</a>. He sold the business in 2020 for $1.75 million <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:19">[00:04:19]</a>. This venture was profitable from day one, driven by an identified market need rather than an entrepreneurial "trap" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:08">[00:05:08]</a>.
*   **Self-Storage Facilities**: Leveraging his experience, Huber moved into self-storage.
    *   In Erie, Pennsylvania, he purchased a 40,000 sq ft, 180-unit facility at public auction for $625,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:30">[00:05:30]</a>. The neglected property was generating only $4,000 a month but had over 100 abandoned units <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:44">[00:05:44]</a>.
    *   Another facility in North Georgia, purchased for $1.5 million, increased its monthly revenue from $16,000 to $40,000 and its value to $4.5 million <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:09">[00:06:09]</a>.

## Examples of Profitable Niche Service Businesses

A range of "sweaty startups" offer significant earning potential:

*   **Lawn Care**: Huber's brother runs a lawn care company, using simple bandit signs for marketing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:32">[00:07:32]</a>. He earns over $100,000 annually working nine months a year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:44">[00:07:44]</a>.
*   **Mobile Detailing**: A mobile detailing business generated $17,000 in revenue and $10,000 in profit in a single month <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:06">[00:08:06]</a>, translating to $120,000 annually for a recent college graduate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:14">[00:08:14]</a>.
*   **High-End Transportation**: A service offering high-end airport transportation in Atlanta employs multiple drivers and performs well <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:17">[00:08:17]</a>.
*   **Firewood Delivery**: Building firewood racks on-site and delivering seasoned firewood could generate $800-$1,000 per delivery, potentially making $1,000 profit daily (over $100/hour) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:40">[00:08:40]</a>. This could evolve into a subscription service or an entry point for other services like lawn mowing or tree work <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:31">[00:09:31]</a>.
*   **Local Digital Advertising**: One entrepreneur in North Georgia charges businesses $10 a month to display their ads on rotating TVs in 70 bars and restaurants, netting $20,000 a month <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:11">[00:10:11]</a>.
*   **Putting Green Installation**: This higher-skilled service can yield $30,000 to $200,000 per high-end turf installation at residential properties <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:40">[00:11:40]</a>.
*   **Tree Removal**: Despite requiring expensive equipment and skilled labor, tree removal can be highly lucrative; a four-hour job for two guys can net over $3,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:54">[00:11:54]</a>.
*   **Christmas Light Installation**: Though risky, installing Christmas lights can command prices of $3,500-$4,000 for a few months of service <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:17">[00:12:17]</a>.
*   **Power Washing**: An accessible business for those with little skill or capital, involving cleaning concrete, furniture, and decks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:54">[00:12:54]</a>.
*   **Concrete Work**: Driveway slabs and patios offer further opportunities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:04">[00:13:04]</a>.
*   **Bounce House Rentals**: Another viable "sweaty startup" idea <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:08">[00:13:08]</a>.

## Niche Service Recruiting: The Night Nurse Idea

A specific opportunity identified is a **nanny or night nurse recruiting company** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:22">[00:13:22]</a>.
*   The service would involve posting jobs, screening calls, conducting background checks, and setting up interviews for qualified candidates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:32">[00:13:32]</a>.
*   Customers would pay $3,000-$5,000 for such a service <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:40">[00:13:40]</a>.
*   A "night nurse" assists parents by watching the baby overnight, allowing parents to sleep <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:45">[00:14:45]</a>.
*   This service caters to a high-value customer at the beginning of their journey, creating opportunities for cross-selling other baby-related services, akin to "home outfitting for babies" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:03">[00:15:03]</a>.

### The `nightnurses.com` Domain Opportunity

The domain `nightnurses.com` is available for $13,000 and can be leased to own for $1,100 a month over 13 months <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:23">[00:16:23]</a>.
*   The strategy would be to build a directory, leverage [[opportunities_for_ai_consulting_and_agency_services | AI]] for content generation (blog, product reviews), and gain organic traffic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:50">[00:16:50]</a>.
*   Advertising could target pregnancy apps used by expecting mothers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:06">[00:17:06]</a>.
*   A `dot-com` domain is seen as crucial, with LLMs (Large Language Models) likely to prioritize them, leading to higher lead conversion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:14">[00:20:14]</a>. [[niche_websites_and_domains | Investing in a good domain]] is considered "digital real estate" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:07">[00:20:07]</a>.

The proposed initial model for `nightnurses.com` is a high-touch recruiting service:
1.  Charge a one-time fee ($1,000) to find a night nurse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:29">[00:27:29]</a>.
2.  Post ads (e.g., on care.com), leverage networks, get referrals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:36">[00:27:36]</a>.
3.  Vet, background check, and set up interviews for highly qualified candidates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:43">[00:27:43]</a>.
4.  Once a nurse is hired, the relationship and payment are direct between the family and the nurse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:56">[00:27:56]</a>.
This approach avoids the complexities of a marketplace model and can lead to a significant revenue stream (e.g., 29 placements/month x $1,000 = $29,000/month) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:01">[00:28:01]</a>.

## Sweaty Startups vs. AI Startups

While the current environment sees a "renaissance" of [[opportunities_in_aidriven_media_and_niche_businesses | AI startups]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:38">[00:23:38]</a>, many may fail. The speaker estimates that 93% of AI startups won't reach profitability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:59">[00:23:59]</a>.
*   **Competition**: In "sweaty startups," competition is often against businesses that are less tech-savvy, less responsive, or operating with outdated methods (e.g., fax machines) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:40">[00:24:40]</a>.
*   **Higher Odds of Success**: Choosing to compete in a less glamorous, less technologically advanced sector increases the odds of winning and making money, even if the individual wins are smaller <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:05">[00:25:05]</a>.
*   **Hybrid Approach**: The best [[building_a_niche_ai_saas_business | AI startups]] might actually start as "sweaty startups." By building a foundational service business, gathering an audience (e.g., email list of millions of parents), and then layering on technology (like payroll providers) or [[developing_niche_publishing_houses | digital products]], one can create a robust "fintech AI company" with a strong, hands-on foundation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:11">[00:26:11]</a>. AI will likely make business owners more money by improving efficiency, rather than solely benefiting AI companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:57">[00:28:57]</a>.

## The ACP Framework

The entire approach to [[building_an_audience_and_generating_impressions | building an audience]] and businesses is based on the **ACP Framework**: Audience, Community, Product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:49">[00:01:49]</a>.

*   **A - Audience**: People in your niche who are passionate about a topic and currently underserved <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:56">[00:01:56]</a>.
*   **C - Community**: Your hardcore followers and supporters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:01">[00:02:01]</a>.
*   **P - Product**: What you sell to these people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:08">[00:02:08]</a>.

The recommended approach is to start with the Audience, then build the Community, and finally develop the Product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:17">[00:02:17]</a>. This "audience-first" strategy offers more options and confidence when releasing something, as people are already there to use it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:36">[00:02:36]</a>. For bootstrap businesses, starting with the audience is generally essential, as it's akin to "digging the well before you need the water" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:10">[00:03:10]</a>.

> "The product first approach works for some people but the audience first approach gives you so many options also gives you so much confidence when you release something and people actually use it like there's nothing more Soul destroying than launching something and zero people are using it" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:30">[00:02:30]</a>

## Step 1: Audience (A) - Identifying Who You're Creating For

The first step is to identify your target audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:54">[00:03:54]</a>.

*   **Define Your Niche**: Focus on individuals interested in specific topics, such as startup ideas and building them using the ACP framework <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:05">[00:04:05]</a>.
*   **Understand Underserved Needs**: Crucially, identify what your audience is *not* getting from other creators <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:27">[00:04:27]</a>. This allows you to stand out by providing unique value, often referred to as "the sauce" â€“ not holding anything back and sharing exact playbooks or ideas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:57">[00:04:57]</a>. While this might lead to competition, the benefits of standing out outweigh the risks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:04">[00:06:04]</a>.
*   **Engagement for Reach**: If engagement is low, consider starting one level broader than your initial niche <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:38">[00:07:38]</a>. This allows you to gain initial reach before specializing. For example, a company wanting to be known for "design sprints" had to start by creating broader "UX design" content to gain an audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:13">[00:08:13]</a>.

## Step 2: [[developing_content_formats_and_leveraging_platform_analytics | Formats]] - Figuring Out Your Content Style

Choosing the right content format is a crucial second step in [[building_an_audience_for_newsletters | building an audience]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:55">[00:09:55]</a>.

*   **Define Format**: A format is the style and presentation of your content, like short, philosophical "Banger tweets" or long-form detailed posts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:07">[00:10:07]</a>.
*   **Experimentation**: Pick one format to test each business day with the goal of finding one that "crushes it" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:52">[00:10:52]</a>.
*   **Look Outside Your Niche**: Avoid copying what's saturated in your space <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:21">[00:11:21]</a>. Instead, look for successful formats in other niches and apply them to yours <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:40">[00:11:40]</a>.
*   **Consider Platform Trends**: Be aware of what's working platform-wide, such as short-form video in 2025, as algorithms often favor certain formats <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:23">[00:12:23]</a>.
*   **Natural and Enjoyable**: Focus on creating content that feels natural and enjoyable for *you* to produce, ensuring consistency over time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:41">[00:12:41]</a>.
*   **Test with Data**: Test formats for 90 days and let analytics tell you what's working <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:05">[00:13:05]</a>. Platforms like X (Twitter) and YouTube offer valuable insights <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:11">[00:13:11]</a>.
*   **Format Life Cycles**: Remember that formats have life cycles, which can be very short (even two weeks) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:33">[00:13:33]</a>. Continuously reinvent yourself and observe what others are doing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:49">[00:13:49]</a>.

## Step 3: Systems - Maintaining Consistency and Efficiency

Systems are crucial for ensuring you consistently execute the ACP funnel and [[building_audience_and_feedback_loops | build an audience]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:30">[00:19:30]</a>.

*   **Creative Faucet Routine**: Identify and build a routine that gets your creative juices flowing, whether it's following new accounts in your niche, attending in-person events, or listening to podcasts during walks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:59">[00:19:59]</a>.
*   **Ideas Capture System**: Have a simple system, like Apple Notes, to quickly capture ideas as they come to you during your creative routine <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:30">[00:20:30]</a>. Simplicity ensures consistency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:57">[00:20:57]</a>.
*   **Weekly Content Creation Time**: Dedicate specific weekly time to turn your captured ideas into content, treating them as "minimal viable products" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:02">[00:21:02]</a>. Utilize scheduling tools to automate posting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:42">[00:21:42]</a>.
*   **Strategic Timing**: Match your content release timing to your audience's behavior and mindset on each platform <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:04">[00:22:04]</a>. Post when topics are relevant and look for opportunities to join trending conversations (newsjacking) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:24">[00:22:24]</a>.

## Step 4: Goals - Optimizing for Your Objective

The final step in this [[strategies_for_growing_online_audiences | four-step playbook]] is to set clear goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:36">[00:24:36]</a>.

*   **Pick One Primary Goal**: Focus on a single objective, such as gaining 100,000 followers, building a 25,000-person email list, or selling a million dollars through social media <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:47">[00:24:47]</a>. Avoid picking multiple goals at once <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:57">[00:24:57]</a>.
*   **Measure Effectiveness**: Regularly (e.g., every 30, 60, or 90 days) measure the effectiveness of your formats against this specific goal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:01">[00:25:01]</a>.
*   **Treat Content as an Asset**: Recognize that attention is scarce. Treat your content with the same rigor and dedication you would a job or a product, and build it as an asset <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:19">[00:25:19]</a>.
*   **Optimize for Goals, Not Vanity Metrics**: Focus on metrics that directly contribute to your primary goal, rather than superficial "vanity metrics" like raw subscriber counts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:45">[00:25:45]</a>. For example, positive comments on a podcast might be more valuable than total subscribers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:03">[00:26:03]</a>.

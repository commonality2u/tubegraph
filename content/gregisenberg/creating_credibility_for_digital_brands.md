## Strategies for Building Credibility

### Leveraging Recognition and Awards
One strategy for building credibility is through external validation and recognition <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:12:00">[04:12:00]</a>. Inspired by physical awards like a "best dentist in America plaque," the idea of a "best product of the year" or "best e-commerce brand of the year" business could create and sell plaques and digital images to endorse other brands <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:57:00">[01:57:00]</a>.

Similar concepts exist:
*   The Better Business Bureau sells an image to denote legitimacy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:11:00">[02:11:00]</a>.
*   YouTube awards plaques for subscriber milestones <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:56:00">[03:56:00]</a>.
*   Product Hunt, a platform for showcasing new products, allows companies to display "Product of the Day" plaques on their websites <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:08:00">[05:08:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:31:00">[05:31:00]</a>.

A "top product of the year" image could be more impactful than common "trusted at" credibility slides, as consumers are increasingly glossing over these <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:29:00">[03:29:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:35:00">[03:35:00]</a>. Making "noise" through exposure, such as a podcast or online platform, can drive traffic and build recognition for these credibility symbols <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:16:00">[06:16:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:42:00">[06:42:00]</a>.

### Embeddable Businesses
Another method involves creating embeddable businesses that "own [[digital_real_estate_and_monetization_strategies | real estate]] on other people's websites" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:36:00">[09:36:00]</a>. An example is "Offer Button," which allows a "for sale" button to be embedded on any website, creating a directory of companies for sale <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:03:00">[09:03:03]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:26:00">[09:26:00]</a>.

The benefit of embeddable businesses is consistent traffic and implicit marketing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:30:00">[11:30:00]</a>. When a recognized product is embedded on an authoritative website, it gains credibility by association <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:40:00">[12:40:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:33:00">[13:33:00]</a>.

Examples of embedded marketing that build credibility:
*   Invoicing software like Xero includes "sent with Xero" at the bottom of invoices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:53:00">[11:53:00]</a>.
*   Beehiiv (an email newsletter platform) includes its branding at the bottom of emails <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:23:00">[12:23:00]</a>.
*   Gmail initially grew by displaying "Sent with Gmail" on emails during its invite-only phase <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:55:00">[12:55:00]</a>.
*   Superhuman (an email client) uses "Sent with Superhuman" as a status symbol <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:22:00">[13:22:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:17:00">[14:17:00]</a>.

These examples highlight how [[virality_strategies_for_digital_products | embedded marketing]] allows a product to promote itself and leverage the authority of its users <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:50:00">[11:50:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:33:00">[13:33:00]</a>.

### The Role of Aesthetic Software
The concept of "luxury software" like Superhuman, which is more expensive but offers a premium experience and status symbol, is evolving into "aesthetic software" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:02:00">[14:02:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:02:00">[17:02:00]</a>. Aesthetic software prioritizes design and user experience, contributing significantly to the perceived value of a product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:52:00">[17:52:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:37:00">[18:37:00]</a>.

Aesthetic design can grab attention, especially when launching a product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:39:00">[23:39:00]</a>. A well-designed landing page, even a simple one, can convey professionalism and credibility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:29:00">[24:29:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:03:00">[28:03:03]</a>. Elements like a nice font, clear header, simple logo, and subtle animations can significantly improve a website's appeal and help in early [[creating_and_selling_digital_products | product validation]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:37:00">[24:37:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:22:00">[27:22:00]</a>.

> "I feel like building a product is like building a car and whenever someone walks into a dealership, they see this super nice car... but the second that person gets into the car and finds out that the car doesn't drive, he he doesn't care and he's not going to buy it" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:25:00">[22:25:00]</a>.
> "Let's figure out what makes the car drive... but let's figure out the core value proposition of getting from A to B" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:55:00">[22:55:00]</a>.

While core functionality (the "car driving") is paramount, aesthetics play a vital role in initial attraction and perceived quality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:55:00">[22:55:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:12:00">[23:12:00]</a>.

### Niche Down and Solve Pain Points
To [[starting_an_online_business_in_the_current_digital_landscape | create a successful business]] and build credibility, it's essential to understand specific pain points within a niche <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="45:56:00">[45:56:00]</a>. The Shopify app ecosystem, for instance, presents a massive opportunity for businesses to build tools that save time, increase convenience, or boost conversion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="44:58:00">[44:58:00]</a>.

Examples of solving niche pain points within Shopify:
*   Instant.so, a page builder, offers SEO-optimized templates that allow quick store creation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:31:00">[41:31:00]</a>.
*   A fulfillment business built a Shopify app to hide the China origin of products for US customers by providing only USPS tracking links, enhancing the customer experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="43:21:00">[43:21:00]</a>.

By deeply understanding challenges, such as dealing with Chinese manufacturers (language barriers, distance, cultural differences), and solving them, businesses can create valuable products and establish credibility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="47:07:00">[47:07:00]</a>. Niching down further, such as focusing on conversion rate optimization (CRO) for specific industries like construction, can create a defensible market position and a "moat" against larger competitors like Google or Shopify's own offerings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="50:07:00">[50:07:00]</a>.

## Step 1: [[Identifying a niche and audience | Identifying a Niche and Audience]] on Reddit

The first step involves identifying trending and growing communities on Reddit that represent potential [[opportunities_in_niche_markets_and_customized_solutions | niche markets]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:02">[00:01:02]</a>.

*   **Utilize Gummy Search:** A tool like Gummy Search (free and paid plans available) helps in this process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:31">[00:01:31]</a>.
*   **Focus on Trending Subreddits:** Navigate to the "trending" section of Gummy Search <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:40">[00:01:40]</a>.
    *   Sort by active and growing subreddits to see what's gaining momentum <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:53">[00:01:53]</a>.
*   **Target Mid-Sized Communities:** While larger subreddits exist, the focus should be on those with 10,000 to 100,000 members <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:17">[00:02:17]</a>. These are often newer and currently trending, answering the "why now" question for potential ventures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:28">[00:02:28]</a>.
*   **Assess Personal Advantage:** Consider if you possess an "unfair advantage" or personal connection to the niche <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:58">[00:02:58]</a>.
*   **Take Notes:** As you scroll through potential subreddits, actively note down interesting ones <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:25">[00:03:25]</a>.
*   **Experiment with Timeframes:** Adjust sorting to daily or weekly growth to uncover different insights <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:36">[00:03:36]</a>.
*   **Regular Review:** Check Gummy Search every few weeks to discover new niches <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:06">[00:04:06]</a>. Gummy Search also offers curated sections for specific audiences, like software engineers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:15">[00:04:15]</a>.

## Step 2: [[Finding and validating niche ideas for directories | Finding and Validating Problems and Solutions]]

Once a promising subreddit is identified, the next step is to dive into the community to uncover their specific problems and desired solutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:52">[00:04:52]</a>. This information is crucial for developing startup ideas, whether they are agency, SaaS, marketplace, software, brick-and-mortar, or CPG businesses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:07">[00:07:07]</a>.

*   **Create an Audience in Gummy Search:** Add the selected subreddit (and similar ones) to an "audience" within Gummy Search <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:54">[00:05:54]</a>.
*   **Explore Discussion Categories:** Gummy Search organizes discussions into categories like "pain and anger," "advice requests," and "solution requests" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:36">[00:06:36]</a>.
    *   **Pain and Anger:** This category helps identify common challenges and frustrations within the community. Gummy Search uses AI to summarize patterns from thousands of words of submissions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:53">[00:06:53]</a>. For example, in a dog food community, patterns might reveal issues with potty training or dogs reluctant to walk <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:58">[00:07:58]</a>. These directly inspire product or content ideas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:18">[00:08:18]</a>.
    *   **Advice Requests:** This category reveals what people are actively seeking help or recommendations for <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:08">[00:10:08]</a>. These are "people screaming they want these products to exist" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:36">[00:10:36]</a>. Examples include requests for dog breed recommendations or specific dog food for older dogs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:53">[00:11:53]</a>.
    *   **Solution Requests:** This section provides insight into existing solutions people are using and the competitive landscape <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:40">[00:12:40]</a>. It helps understand how people are currently addressing their problems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:54">[00:12:54]</a>.

### Evaluating Opportunities

Even if existing solutions are found, opportunities often arise from poor marketing or a lack of social media presence in the competitive landscape <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:12">[00:09:12]</a>. Simple Minimum Viable Products (MVPs) can be built to start a small business, potentially leading to a full-time venture <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:32">[00:09:32]</a>.

## Step 3: Identify Niche Creators and Their Audience Insights

After understanding the problems and solutions, the next step is to identify key creators within the niche.

*   **Use AI for Creator Discovery:** Use tools like Perplexity.ai (free) to find top influencers or content creators in the niche (e.g., "top educational YouTubers for dogs") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:38">[00:13:38]</a>. Compile a list, perhaps in a Google Sheet <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:07">[00:14:07]</a>.
*   **Analyze Popular Content and Comments:** Visit the creators' platforms (e.g., YouTube) and sort their videos by "most popular" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:12">[00:15:12]</a>. Read the top comments to understand audience desires and pain points <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:25">[00:15:25]</a>. This helps in empathizing with the target audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:49">[00:15:49]</a>.

## Step 4: Wireframe and Validate the Idea

With problems, solutions, and audience insights gathered, the final step involves prototyping and validating the business idea.

*   **Wireframe a Solution:** Based on insights, wireframe a website or product. This could be a design agency focused on conversion rather than just aesthetics, inspired by common frustrations found in the niche <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:22">[00:16:22]</a>.
*   **Rapid Prototyping:** Tools like Framer allow for quick wireframing and deployment of a website in a few hours <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:16">[00:17:16]</a>.
*   **Choose a Resonant Name:** Consider using a name that aligns with the subreddit or leverages insights from community discussions to resonate with the target audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:42">[00:17:42]</a>. For example, "Design Science" was inspired by the desire for designers to be more "like scientists" and less "art" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:07">[00:18:07]</a>.

## Creating Content People Love

The same methodology for finding startup ideas can be applied to generating content ideas that resonate with the target audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:15">[00:18:15]</a>.

*   **Analyze Top Content:** In Gummy Search, explore the "top content" section for your selected audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:31">[00:18:31]</a>.
*   **Identify Common Patterns and Formats:** Observe what types of content perform best (e.g., long stories, memes, specific topics) and the formats that work <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:37">[00:18:37]</a>. For example, in a dog community, stories about saving a dog's life or losing a pet might be highly engaging <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:55">[00:18:55]</a>.
*   **Leverage AI for Insights:** Ask Gummy Search questions like "Do they love memes?" to gain further understanding of community preferences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:18">[00:19:18]</a>. This tool acts as an "entrepreneurial assistant" or co-founder, helping connect dots and generate ideas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:46">[00:19:46]</a>.

By systematically observing and analyzing communities on Reddit, entrepreneurs can uncover validated problems, develop effective solutions, and create content that deeply resonates, leading to successful ventures and [[opportunities_in_niche_websites | niche websites]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:21">[00:20:21]</a>.

## The Concept of "Open Pages" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:05:00">[03:05:00]</a>
Open pages are web pages where individuals or startups openly share their statistics, such as revenue or user numbers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:11:00">[03:11:00]</a>. Traditionally, these have been difficult to build and were selective in the data they covered <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:24:00">[03:24:24]</a>.

### Evolution to Personal Tracking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:31:00">[03:31:00]</a>
The concept can extend beyond business metrics to personal life stats. An example of a personal open page tracks daily habits like reading and exercise consistency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:36:00">[03:36:00]</a>. This type of page can display a wide range of personal data, from supplements taken and exercise frequency to glucose monitor readings, with the user choosing what to share <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:52:00">[03:52:00]</a>.

## Why Now? The Enabling Technology and Social Motivation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:22:00">[04:22:00]</a>
The current technological landscape makes it significantly easier for nearly anyone to create such a page <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:41:00">[03:41:00]</a>. Tools like Cursor or Bolt allow users, even those without prior coding experience, to "whip up a page" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:46:00">[03:46:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:46:00">[05:46:00]</a>. Beyond technological feasibility, there's a strong social motivation for these pages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:27:00">[04:27:00]</a>.

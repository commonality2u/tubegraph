## The Four-Step Process for [[AIgenerated startup ideas | AI-Generated Startup Ideas]]

The process focuses on finding new, validated ideas and identifying "gold" within communities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:59">[00:00:59]</a>.

### Step 1: Discovering Trending Subreddits

The first step involves using Gummy Search, an AI tool, to identify trending subreddits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:31">[00:01:31]</a>.
*   **Accessing Trends**: Navigate to the "trending" section within Gummy Search <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:42">[00:01:42]</a>.
*   **Filtering Subreddits**: While large subreddits exist, the focus should be on communities with 10,000 to 100,000 members <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:17">[00:02:17]</a>. These newer, trending communities often answer the "why now" question, indicating current relevance and interest <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:28">[00:02:28]</a>.
*   **Growth Rate Analysis**: Observe how fast subreddits are growing daily, weekly, monthly, or yearly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:56">[00:01:56]</a>. Experimenting with different timeframes can yield unique insights <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:36">[00:03:36]</a>.
*   **Personal Advantage**: As you scroll, consider if you have an unfair advantage or personal connection to any trending niche <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:55">[00:02:55]</a>.
*   **Curated Sections**: Gummy Search also offers curated sections, such as "26 subreddits for software engineers," which can be added to an audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:20">[00:04:20]</a>.
*   **Note-Taking**: Maintain a Google Doc or notebook to jot down interesting subreddits and initial thoughts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:27">[00:03:27]</a>.

### Step 2: Uncovering Problems and Solutions

Once a promising subreddit is chosen, Gummy Search helps identify problems and potential solutions within that community to generate [[startup_ideas_and_how_to_evaluate_them | startup ideas]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:56">[00:04:56]</a>. This process is crucial for discovering opportunities for various business models, including agency, SaaS, marketplace, software, brick-and-mortar, or CPG businesses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:07">[00:05:07]</a>.

Gummy Search categorizes discussions into:
*   **Pain and Anger**: This section highlights common patterns of frustration using [[Comparison of AI platforms for startup idea generation | AI]] to summarize thousands of words from submissions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:08">[00:07:08]</a>.
    *   *Example*: For a "dog food" audience, patterns like "potty training challenges," "dogs reluctant to walk outside," or "eating habits/loss of appetite" emerge <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:58">[00:07:58]</a>.
*   **Advice Requests**: This category reveals what products or solutions people are actively seeking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:11">[00:10:11]</a>.
    *   *Example*: Users "screaming" for advice on dog breeds ("What breed a dog would you recommend?") or specific dog food recommendations ("soft dog food...focused on Senior Dogs") indicate unmet needs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:36">[00:10:36]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:57">[00:11:57]</a>.
*   **Solution Requests**: This section provides insight into existing solutions and the competitive landscape, showing how people are currently addressing their problems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:40">[00:12:40]</a>.

> "These are people screaming they're screaming they want they want these products to exist and it's your job to go and figure out how to build it" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:36">[00:10:36]</a>

*   **Documenting Ideas**: Continue adding these insights to your "one-pager" document <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:20">[00:13:20]</a>.

### Step 3: Engaging with Niche Creators

Once problems and solutions are identified, the next step involves researching creators and influencers within the niche <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:34">[00:13:34]</a>.
*   **Identify Creators**: Use AI tools like Perplexity.ai to find popular creators, such as "25 most popular dog influencers on YouTube" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:44">[00:13:44]</a>. Refine your search to find educational creators rather than purely entertainment-focused ones <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:50">[00:14:50]</a>.
*   **Analyze Comments**: Go to the creators' most popular videos and read the comments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:12">[00:15:12]</a>. The comments section, especially top comments, reveals what people want, their thoughts, and their current challenges <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:25">[00:15:25]</a>.
*   **Empathy and Notes**: Put yourself in the audience's shoes and continue taking detailed notes on your one-pager <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:49">[00:15:49]</a>.

### Step 4: Wireframing and Naming the Idea

The final step for [[ai_startup_ideas_and_incubation | AI startup ideas and incubation]] involves visualizing and naming the concept <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:19">[00:16:19]</a>.
*   **Wireframing**: Based on the gathered insights, create a wireframe for a website or product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:19">[00:16:19]</a>. This allows for rapid prototyping and deployment in a few hours <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:16">[00:17:16]</a>.
    *   *Example*: An insight about designers not focusing on conversion led to wireframing a "design agency that cared about copy and conversion," resulting in Design Science <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:31">[00:16:31]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:58">[00:17:58]</a>. This business became a seven-figure venture through this methodology <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:38">[00:20:38]</a>.
*   **Catchy Name**: Develop a name that resonates with the target audience and is validated by insights <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:54">[00:17:54]</a>. Sometimes, using the subreddit's name can be effective <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:42">[00:17:42]</a>.

This entire process enables [[validating_startup_ideas_without_building_them | validating startup ideas without building them]] by deeply understanding community needs.

*   **Top Content Analysis**: In Gummy Search, explore the "top content" section of a chosen audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:31">[00:18:31]</a>.
*   **Identify Patterns**: Look for common patterns and the best-performing content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:38">[00:18:38]</a>. This reveals preferred formats (e.g., long stories, memes) and what the community cares about <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:45">[00:18:45]</a>.

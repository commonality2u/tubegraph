## [[developing_startup_ideas_into_businesses | Developing Startup Ideas]] and Products: The Blake Anderson Framework

### Ideation: Combining Functionality and Virality
Blake's ideation process begins by considering what tools will perform well on social media and go viral <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:20:23">[14:20:23]</a>. He focuses on redefining the value users get from apps, rather than just leveraging the TikTok algorithm for quick cash <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:27:00">[05:27:00]</a>.

### Product Development
Blake's framework for taking an idea to a mainstream product involves several steps:

1.  **Wireframing**: Start with basic wireframes of the app <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:29:00">[14:29:00]</a>.
2.  **UI Design**: Convert wireframes into more fleshed-out UI designs, either by a professional designer or someone from platforms like Upwork <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:33:00">[14:33:00]</a>.
3.  **Building/Coding**:
    *   For those with capital and connections to a good CTO, it's recommended to leverage those resources <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:50:00">[14:50:00]</a>.
    *   For those just starting, like Blake was, he taught himself to code using ChatGPT <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:43:00">[14:43:00]</a>. He used ChatGPT to get Swift UI code for Xcode for Riz GPT and Umax <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:07:00">[15:07:00]</a>. Currently, he codes in Cursor with React Native <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:18:00">[15:18:00]</a>.
    *   There isn't a one-size-fits-all answer for development, as it differs for everyone <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:33:00">[15:33:00]</a>.

### Naming Strategy for [[building_a_successful_ai_startup | AI Startups]]
Appending "AI" to a product name can be valuable when innovating within a relatively stagnant industry, like "Cal AI" for calorie tracking, as it conveys sophistication <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:27:00">[20:27:00]</a>. However, it should be avoided for services requiring 100% accuracy (e.g., medical document services), as people might be skeptical or scared of errors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:55:00">[20:55:00]</a>. Ultimately, the decision depends on the context and public perception of AI in that specific domain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:10:00">[21:10:00]</a>. Sometimes, a more friendly, less technical name like "Juny" or "Journey" (for a journal app) might be preferable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:50:00">[21:50:00]</a>.

## [[growth_tactics_for_ai_startups | Growth Tactics for AI Startups]]: Distribution and Influencer Marketing

### Iterative Distribution Process
Blake emphasizes a highly iterative process for generating virality from early on <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:55:00">[08:55:00]</a>. This involves:
*   **New Hooks and Positioning**: Continuously coming up with new ways to position the product and identifying target audiences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:00:00">[09:00:00]</a>.
*   **Split Testing**: Split testing different hooks across various videos and creators to assess results like views and conversion rates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:37:00">[09:37:00]</a>. This process helps build a statistical model to inform future distribution strategies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:57:00">[09:57:00]</a>.

### Influencer Marketing Strategy
Blake does not use influencer marketplaces or databases <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:44:00">[10:44:00]</a>. His approach focuses on developing personal relationships:
*   **Direct Outreach**: Initially, prioritize both quantity and quality. Reach out to thousands of influencers and be persistent across all platforms, even finding creative ways to make contact <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:41:00">[12:41:00]</a>.
*   **Building Relationships**: Treat influencers well and become friends with them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:06:00">[13:06:00]</a>. This helps differentiate a serious company from the hundreds of daily DMs and emails influencers receive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:54:00">[11:54:00]</a>.
*   **Industry Embedding**: Embedding oneself within the industry leads to connections and further opportunities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:02:00">[12:02:00]</a>.

## Monetization: Paywalls and Pricing Strategy
Blake uses Superwall for paywall testing, praising its ability to facilitate split testing and provide data <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:16:00">[24:16:00]</a>.

### Optimization Philosophy
Unlike many in the industry who optimize for `number of users * profit per user`, Blake believes in optimizing for `(number of users)^N * profit per user`, where `N > 1` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:51:00">[24:51:00]</a>. This means accepting a lower LTV (Lifetime Value) per download in exchange for more people using the platform <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:09:00">[25:09:00]</a>.

### Benefits of Wider Adoption
Prioritizing a larger user base allows a company to:
*   More quickly dominate the market <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:38:00">[25:38:00]</a>.
*   Increase hard-to-measure Word of Mouth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:41:00">[25:41:00]</a>.

### Paywall Implementation
The most standard and efficient practice is to take users through an onboarding flow (e.g., quizzes, personal information input for customization) and then present the paywall at the end <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:35:00">[26:35:00]</a>. While this is efficient and leads to higher conversion rates, Blake acknowledges it might not feel morally great, as users have already invested time and become excited before encountering the payment barrier <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:16:00">[27:16:00]</a>.

## Finding New [[ai_startups_and_business_ideas | AI App Ideas]]
Blake uses a combination of methods for ideation:
*   **Physical Activity**: Walking or hiking helps him think clearly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:54:00">[35:54:00]</a>.
*   **Deliberate Social Media Scrolling**: Curating social media feeds (e.g., using burner accounts) around specific interests puts him in the mind of the target content consumer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:06:00">[36:06:00]</a>.
*   **Market Analysis**: Using tools like Sensor Tower to assess the size and activity of other players in a space <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:44:00">[35:44:00]</a>.
*   **Everyday Observations**: Many successful ideas come from observing everyday life, identifying common activities like calorie tracking, language learning, or journaling, and then applying an "AI twist" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:12:00">[37:12:00]</a>.

Even "obvious" or "straightforward" ideas can lead to massive applications if executed with new styles of functionality and distribution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:55:00">[37:55:00]</a>. The key is to start with small MVPs (Minimum Viable Products) rather than trying to "boil the ocean" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:32:00">[37:32:00]</a>.

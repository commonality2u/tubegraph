## Vibe Marketing Workflow in Practice
The core of [[introduction_to_vibe_marketing | Vibe Marketing]] involves a systematic approach to content creation and distribution:

### 1. Identifying Demand (Keyword Research)
The process begins by understanding internet demand for a product or service through keyword research [00:04:15]. This identifies what people are searching for, how often, and potential website traffic [00:04:30]. This research also helps optimize for LLMs and chatbots like ChatGPT [00:04:46].

Traditional keyword research tools like Ahrefs are often overwhelming [00:05:26]. [[ai_and_vibe_coding_tools_for_marketing_automation | AI and Vibe Coding tools for marketing automation]] simplify this significantly. For example, using a "keyword tool" integrated into Claude via MCP, one can find 20 high-potential, non-branded longtail keywords for a product (e.g., Beehive, a newsletter growth platform) with a single prompt [00:10:43]. This can provide detailed insights, including monthly traffic opportunities (e.g., 14,580 for Beehive), competition levels (e.g., medium), and high-potential keywords (e.g., 20) [00:06:10]. The process, which could take a human 20-40 hours, can be completed by AI in 30 seconds [00:12:08].

The keyword research output differentiates between informational keywords (e.g., "how do I start my first newsletter") and commercial keywords (e.g., "what is the best newsletter platform for my business") [00:06:29]. It also identifies competitors and provides key insights and recommendations for content creation, competitive advantage, and traffic opportunities [00:06:43].

### 2. Analyzing Competitor Gaps
After identifying keywords, Claude can analyze the search results from the first two pages of Google for a specific keyword cluster (e.g., "newsletter growth and audience building") [00:17:28]. This process scrapes content to understand what competitors are *not* addressing, identifying gaps such as a lack of data-driven growth benchmarks, advanced segmentation strategies, technical guidance, or content on retention [00:17:50]. This allows for the creation of deeper, more comprehensive content that adds more value than existing competitors [00:18:27]. This ensures that the content created is based on real data and context, avoiding "hallucinations" often seen with LLMs [00:19:39].

### 3. Automated Content Creation
Based on the identified gaps, the AI can generate a content plan. For example, a "30-day newsletter growth and audience building content dashboard" can be created [00:20:50]. This plan outlines specific content pieces, like a blog post for Day 1 on a "newsletter growth roadmap," including target keywords and a comprehensive content brief [00:21:21].

Instead of manual writing, the content brief is fed back into Claude, along with a predefined "brand voice," to automatically generate a complete, search-optimized blog post [00:22:57]. This output is highly detailed and contextual, unlike generic AI-generated "slop" [00:24:34]. Claude can also generate "artifacts," which are coded dashboards or even functional websites, displaying the content directly [00:23:55].

### 4. Multi-format and Multi-channel Content
Modern organic growth requires content across multiple formats and channels [00:28:46]. After generating a blog post, the workflow can extend to:
*   **Image Generation**: Integrating ChatGPT's image generator allows for hero images and other visuals to be created directly within Claude based on the article's context [00:25:42].
*   **Social Media Content**: Claude can generate platform-specific content, such as X (formerly Twitter) threads or LinkedIn posts, from the main blog post [00:30:58]. The AI understands the nuances of each platform, producing engaging hooks and well-structured threads [00:31:27].
*   **Video Generation**: Tools for video generation (e.g., "videogen") are also being tested to create multi-format content [00:28:38].

This multi-channel approach helps startups enter established markets and siphon off traffic from main players by being present "everywhere in all the formats" [00:30:22].

### 5. Human in the Loop (Brand Safety)
While automation is extensive, a "human in the loop" step is often recommended for brand safety and quality control [00:34:22]. After content is drafted in Claude, it can be sent to platforms like Slack for review by a marketing or social media manager [00:34:32]. This allows for editing, tweaking, and ensuring brand alignment before final publication, for instance, on a Webflow-powered domain [00:33:14]. Full end-to-end automation, including direct publishing, is a future goal [00:33:25].

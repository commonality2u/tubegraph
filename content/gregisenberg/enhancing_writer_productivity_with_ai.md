## Why AI Matters for Writers

Understanding writing frameworks is crucial because they apply across all mediums, whether writing an email, an article, a Twitter thread, or a landing page <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:33">[00:01:33]</a>. The world is increasingly moving towards [[the_future_of_ai_in_personal_productivity | AI integration]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:45">[00:01:45]</a>.

The common fear that AI will eliminate writing jobs is misguided <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:08">[00:02:08]</a>. Historically, new technologies have expanded opportunities:
*   **Photoshop (1990):** Didn't ruin design; it created exponentially more designers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:18">[00:02:18]</a>.
*   **Instagram (2010):** Didn't remove photographers; it made everyone a photographer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:33">[00:02:33]</a>.

Similarly, [[impact_of_ai_on_the_writing_profession | AI writing platforms]] like ChatGPT and Claude will make everyone a writer by reducing the barrier to entry <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:49">[00:02:49]</a>. Every business and individual now recognizes the need to create and share content online <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:18">[00:03:18]</a>. Content creation is continually increasing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:36">[00:03:36]</a>.

AI acts as a "stress test" for writers:
*   Those who learn to leverage AI to double, triple, or quadruple their output will become more valuable and earn more <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:01">[00:04:01]</a>.
*   Those who fail to adapt will become less valuable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:15">[00:04:15]</a>. AI doesn't steal jobs; it changes the baseline skill level, and individuals must adapt <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:21">[00:04:21]</a>.

## Initial Idea Generation with AI

The writing process often begins with a "seed of an idea" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:25">[00:06:25]</a>. AI is not a silver bullet; users need a baseline understanding of how content is supposed to work to effectively prompt AI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:36">[00:07:36]</a>.

There are two primary ways to approach an idea when starting a writing project <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:12">[00:08:12]</a>:
1.  **The idea is the main theme:** The entire piece revolves around this specific concept (e.g., Reed Hastings' strange rule) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:32">[00:08:32]</a>.
2.  **The idea is a sub-point:** The specific concept is a component within a larger theme (e.g., one of three CEOs with strange rules) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:50">[00:08:50]</a>.

When starting a piece, it's beneficial to summarize goals, such as connecting to startup ideas, being actionable, and linking to the core story <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:46">[00:10:46]</a>.

### Prompting AI for Ideas

To overcome writer's block and narrow down ideas, users can plug their goals into AI models like ChatGPT or Claude <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:35">[00:12:35]</a>. The initial goal is not for AI to write, but to feed ideas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:47">[00:12:47]</a>.

There are two main types of AI interaction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:00">[00:13:00]</a>:
1.  **Conversational:** Chunking things piece by piece in a dialogue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:09">[00:13:09]</a>.
2.  **Massive Prompt:** Assembling a comprehensive prompt with all potential situations and hypotheticals to generate an output <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:15">[00:13:15]</a>.

Starting conversationally is often effective <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:45">[00:13:45]</a>. An example prompt: "I recently came up with an idea for an 800-word article: [Idea description]. Here are the goals of the piece: [Goals]. Can you please generate 20 potential headlines for this sort of piece and under each headline list out three to five main points that would make up the content of that piece?" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:51">[00:13:51]</a>

Asking for bullets and main points is crucial because many decisions for a piece are made *before* writing begins <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:12">[00:15:12]</a>. Starting to write immediately without this pre-planning is a common mistake <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:27">[00:15:27]</a>.

## Leveraging AI for Headlines and Hooks

AI can serve as a "junior columnist" by generating multiple iterations of headlines and content ideas, allowing the human user to act as an "editor-in-chief" and select the best options based on their taste and context <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:30">[00:16:30]</a>. For example, a user might prefer the word "ban" in a headline because it implies conflict and creates a better hook <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:36">[00:17:36]</a>.

AI can also help refine content for different platforms. For instance, to create a viral Twitter/X hook:
1.  Provide an existing viral hook as a template <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:08">[00:20:08]</a>.
2.  Adapt the new idea into that template manually <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:14">[00:20:14]</a>. This is not "stealing content" but "stealing the architecture" or "bones of the house" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:29">[00:20:29]</a>.
3.  Feed both the original and the manually adapted hook back to AI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:32">[00:24:32]</a>.
4.  Prompt AI to generate more variations based on the provided examples <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:36">[00:25:36]</a>. This process trains a "mini model" for the AI, allowing it to learn from the user's specific style and preferences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:24">[00:24:24]</a>.

> "You have to remove your own ego and be like who cares that AI actually produced a better hook the reason it was able to was because I prompted it the right way and that that fundamental piece is what is changing everything and either you get on board with that and you become an orchestrator an editor and chief or you don't have a job anymore" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:39">[00:26:39]</a>

For tactical questions, complex custom GPTs are often unnecessary for beginners; the default ChatGPT and Claude are powerful enough <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:28">[00:27:28]</a>.

## Structuring Long-Form Content with AI

For an 800-word article, the easiest structure is typically an introduction followed by three main sections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:41">[00:28:41]</a>.

The "10 Magical Ways" framework helps overcome writer's block by providing categories for content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:07">[00:35:07]</a>. These include tips, stats, steps, lessons, etc. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:01">[00:36:01]</a>.

### The Importance of Specific and Tangible Main Points

90% of a piece's value lies in its headline and the quality of its three main points <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:39">[00:29:39]</a>. If the main points are vague (e.g., "learn about money," "hire a CPA," "don't be dumb"), the reader will quickly deem the content worthless <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:15">[00:30:15]</a>. Instead, main points should be specific and tangible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:06">[00:31:06]</a>.

The subheads should clearly deliver on the promise made in the headline <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:33">[00:38:33]</a>. If the headline promises "three lessons," the subheads should explicitly be "Lesson One," "Lesson Two," and "Lesson Three" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:16">[00:38:16]</a>. The highest leverage decision in the entire piece is what those three lessons are <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:53">[00:38:53]</a>. A piece with generic lessons (e.g., "work hard," "out compete," "care") will be perceived as low quality regardless of writing style <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:09">[00:39:09]</a>.

It's recommended to try coming up with these key points manually first, then compare and improve with AI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:51">[00:39:51]</a>. The most compelling main points should be placed first to hook the reader <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:29">[00:41:29]</a>.

For generating better subheads with AI:
1.  Provide the chosen headline and manually created main points <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:57">[00:41:57]</a>.
2.  Ask AI to stress-test the quality by generating five more batches of main points <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:19">[00:42:19]</a>.
3.  Be specific in prompts, asking for main points in "sentence format" and to be "as tangible [and] actionable for the reader as possible" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:51">[00:44:51]</a>. This conversational refinement helps AI understand the desired output <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:32">[00:45:32]</a>.

Once the three main points are established, revisit the "10 Magical Ways" framework to determine what specific type of content will be delivered in each section (e.g., steps, stats, examples, reasons why) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:02">[00:47:02]</a>.

This structured approach means that by the time you sit down to write, all key decisions have been made, making the writing process significantly easier <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:38">[00:48:38]</a>. AI can then be used to articulate these sections, with the user acting as editor to refine the output <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:50">[00:48:50]</a>.

A significant aspect of the AI revolution is the "sketching economy," where anyone can transform simple sketches into production-ready designs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:47">[00:24:47]</a>. This shift elevates "taste and ideation" as the most scarce resources <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:55">[00:24:55]</a>. The value of sketching—whether on paper or an iPad—is currently underappreciated, as a good sketch fed into AI tools can significantly boost engagement for content like YouTube thumbnails <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:27">[00:25:27]</a>. This concept extends beyond literal sketching to include brainstorming ideas through voice notes, which AI can then refine and bring to life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:55">[00:25:55]</a>.

## Reimagining Core Software and Experiences

AI is poised to fundamentally rebuild existing software. It's predicted that every calendar, inbox, and CRM will be rethought and rebuilt from scratch within the next three years, moving beyond mere AI enhancement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:37">[00:07:37]</a>. This presents an opportunity to consolidate communication channels into a single feed, addressing the overwhelming nature of managing multiple emails and Slack groups <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:54">[00:07:54]</a>.

New solutions like Micro HQ are emerging, aiming to integrate intelligence and improved design to enhance email, turning it into a more manageable and prioritized dashboard <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:33">[00:08:33]</a>. This transformation in the multi-hundred-billion-dollar industry of calendars, inboxes, and CRMs opens vast opportunities for niche-specific AI-powered tools that offer intelligence, dashboard functionality, and aesthetic appeal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:30">[00:10:30]</a>.

### The Rise of AI-First Mobile Apps

Consumer mobile development is experiencing a resurgence, shifting from desktop-first to mobile-first, and now to "AI-first mobile apps" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:17">[00:26:17]</a>. The next wave of successful apps, potentially generating over $100 million annually, will integrate AI from their inception <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:36">[00:26:36]</a>. This trend is leading to increasingly niche software, where multiple variations of an application can be tailored to specific user segments, enabling creators to build profitable businesses with highly specialized apps <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:06">[00:27:06]</a>.

A "really smart strategy" is to rebuild traditional products, leveraging AI as an unfair advantage while hiding complexity behind familiar interfaces <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:03">[00:29:03]</a>. For example, an app like CalAI innovated by allowing users to take a photo of food to track calories, rather than manually entering data into a database <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:44">[00:29:44]</a>. Similarly, tools like Canva could evolve, with AI generating complex graphics from simple sketches and allowing for layer separation and manipulation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:11">[00:30:11]</a>.

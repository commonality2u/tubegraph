## Hunter Isacson's Playbook for App Growth

Hunter Isacson's strategy for building a $1 million MRR app business focuses on simplicity, rapid iteration, and targeted growth loops <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:53:00">[17:53:00]</a>.

### 1. Build a Simple Experience (MVP)
Begin by creating a bare-bones Minimum Viable Product (MVP) that effectively addresses the core problem the app aims to solve <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:25:00">[18:25:00]</a>. This initial version may have rough edges but should be functional enough to test with users <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:36:00">[18:36:00]</a>.

### 2. Quickly Acquire Early Users
Aim to get a few hundred to a couple thousand users on the app swiftly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:49:00">[18:49:00]</a>. This can even be done during the pre-order stage of the app's development <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:00:00">[19:00:00]</a>.

### 3. Test Content and Formats
Before launch, test content on platforms like [[Tik Tok marketing strategies for consumer apps | TikTok]] or Instagram Reels to gauge audience response to the app's premise and formats <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:04:00">[19:04:00]</a>.

### 4. Select a Core Action (Northstar Metric)
Identify a single "Northstar Metric" that dictates key decisions and represents the primary action users should take within the app <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:30:00">[19:30:00]</a>. A high percentage of users (over 90%) should complete this action, and the user journey should ideally be a single, streamlined flow <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:00:00">[26:00:00]</a>.

*   **Examples of Northstar Metrics**:
    *   **Wink**: Percentage of users who get a match on their first day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:55:00">[19:55:00]</a>.
    *   **NGL**: Percentage of users sharing their link and sending a few replies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:26:00">[20:26:00]</a>.
    *   **Bags**: Percentage of users funding their accounts and making one trade on the first day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:03:00">[21:03:00]</a>.

### 5. Build a Brand People Love
Focus on creating a brand that resonates with the target audience. This often involves choosing short, memorable names, leveraging slang or community-specific language, and maintaining an authentic voice in public communication <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:35:00">[21:35:00]</a>.

*   **Examples**:
    *   **NGL**: Short for "Not Gonna Lie," easily understood by a young audience as an anonymous app <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:30:00">[22:30:00]</a>.
    *   **Bags**: Utilizes crypto community slang, making it feel comfortable and colloquial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:17:00">[22:17:00]</a>.
*   **Design**: Isacson personally designs all his apps, including branding, using tools like Figma <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:57:00">[24:57:00]</a>.

### 6. Continuously Seed and Apply Pressure
Once the core action is validated, the focus shifts to continuously getting more users onto the app <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:44:00">[26:44:00]</a>.

*   **Creator Partnerships**: Work with creators in diverse audiences, niches, countries, and time zones <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:03:00">[27:03:00]</a>.
*   **In-App Growth Loops**: Integrate sharing or inviting mechanisms directly into the app's core experience. This allows users to organically bring in new users, reducing reliance on external marketing channels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:23:00">[27:23:00]</a>.
    *   **NGL**: Users posted their NGL link on Instagram or Snapchat stories <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:34:00">[28:34:00]</a>.
    *   **Bags**: Users logged in with Twitter, claimed a Bags link, shared it, and participated in a leaderboard based on invites <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:39:00">[29:39:00]</a>.
    *   **Laps**: Required users to invite three to five friends to gain access, creating an extremely valuable user who brought others along <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:39:00">[27:39:00]</a>.
*   **Paid vs. Organic Seeding**: Seeding can be organic or paid. For NGL, less than $10,000 was spent on marketing to achieve 250 million downloads by paying influencers small amounts (e.g., $50-$100) to post the link <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:38:00">[14:38:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:42:00">[30:42:00]</a>. The goal is to find methods that scale efficiently from a small initial investment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:07:00">[31:07:00]</a>.

## Successful App Examples and Their [[Strategies for successful app development and scaling | Strategies]]

### NGL
NGL's success stemmed from launching at the opportune moment when Instagram unveiled its universal link feature, allowing all users (not just those with 10,000+ followers) to post links <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:29:00">[15:29:00]</a>. This created a "new channel" for an existing concept (anonymous messaging apps), which had previously worked on other platforms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:00:00">[16:00:00]</a>. The team trained hundreds of millions of people how to use the new Instagram feature <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:20:00">[16:20:00]</a>.

### Locket
Locket capitalized on Apple's introduction of widget functionality, becoming one of the first mainstream social apps to utilize this new "real estate" on iPhone home screens <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:35:00">[10:35:00]</a>. It allowed users to share photos directly to friends' home screens via a widget, leveraging [[Tik Tok marketing strategies for consumer apps | TikTok]] trends, particularly among young users in relationships or best friendships <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:52:00">[10:52:00]</a>. This demonstrates the potential for [[Strategies for successful app development and scaling | app development]] in response to new OS features, such as live activities or lock screen widgets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:13:00">[12:13:00]</a>.

### Cali AI
Cali AI exemplifies how to package existing, publicly available AI tools into an attractive app <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:01:00">[03:01:00]</a>. Its success is attributed to an "amazing funnel," good onboarding, and user investment in the onboarding experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:05:00">[03:05:00]</a>. [[AI app marketing strategies | Cali AI]] effectively leveraged creators to generate content around the app, marrying a simple AI concept with a strong distribution strategy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:04:00">[04:04:00]</a>.

### Hallow
Hallow, the top app for Christian and Catholic prayers, innovated by taking traditional religious content and making it mobile, digital, and appealing to younger generations (millennials, Gen Z) through features like push notifications and a more colorful interface <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:10:00">[05:10:00]</a>. It offers a strong daily use case with extensive content (daily prayers, sleep meditations), focusing on a "single player first" experience before adding "multiplayer" features like community challenges and family plans <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:38:00">[07:38:00]</a>. There's perceived opportunity for similar spiritual apps across other religions and denominations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:17:00">[06:17:00]</a>.

### Pangu
Pangu is a virtual pet app that injects advanced AI capabilities into a classic concept <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:47:00">[34:47:00]</a>. It focuses on character animation and creating a more human-like experience between a user and an AI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:09:00">[34:09:00]</a>. This highlights the strategy of taking existing ideas and optimizing them for new channels or with new technologies like AI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:50:00">[35:50:00]</a>.

## Emerging Opportunities in Consumer Mobile Apps
*   **New OS Features**: Opportunities exist by identifying and building around new features released in iOS updates (e.g., live activities, lock screen widgets) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:13:00">[12:13:00]</a>.
*   **AI Integration**: Applying AI to existing app categories (e.g., [[AI app marketing strategies | AI-first calorie tracking]]) or creating novel experiences with [[Using marketing strategies for AIbased apps | AI-based apps]] (e.g., GPT Vision for spiritual content creation, AI for complex religious questions) <a class="yt="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:19:00">[09:19:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:31:00">[09:31:00]</a>.
*   **Crypto**: Building consumer apps that make crypto accessible and understandable for a mainstream audience while still appealing to crypto natives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:05:00">[29:05:00]</a>, particularly with increasing regulatory acceptance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:58:00">[36:58:00]</a>.
*   **Augmented Reality (AR) Glasses**: Anticipating future hardware platforms like AR glasses, which could enable fully immersive and sharable experiences by leveraging phone compute power for AI models <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:15:00">[37:15:00]</a>.
*   **Reinventing the Wheel**: Revisit past popular ideas and re-launch them with new technologies, distribution channels, or lower costs of development and distribution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:41:00">[16:41:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:03:00">[17:03:00]</a>.
*   **Frameworks over Ideas**: Focus on applying proven frameworks (like NGL for X, Cali AI for X) to different niches and audiences, rather than seeking entirely new ideas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:36:00">[35:36:00]</a>.

Isacson encourages young people to focus on building consumer mobile apps to learn about human psychology, product development, and achieving [[viral_consumer_app_growth_tactics | viral growth]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="40:26:00">[40:26:00]</a>. The increasing quality of tools is also making it easier to build apps with less technical expertise <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:39:00">[41:39:00]</a>.

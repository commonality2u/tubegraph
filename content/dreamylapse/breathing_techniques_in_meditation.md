Breathing techniques are an integral part of meditation practices, helping to center the mind and body. This particular practice, focused on [[gratitude_meditation | gratitude meditation]], emphasizes specific breathing methods to cultivate appreciation for the present moment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:10">[00:00:10]</a>.

## How to Practice

1.  **Preparation**: Begin by finding a comfortable position and closing your eyes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:22">[00:00:22]</a>.
2.  **Initial Breath**: Take a deep breath in through your nose, filling your lungs with air <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:30">[00:00:30]</a>. Slowly exhale through your mouth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:38">[00:00:38]</a>.
3.  **Establishing Rhythm**: Allow your breath to become steady and calm <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:42">[00:00:42]</a>.
4.  **Sustained Breathing**: As you continue to breathe, bring to mind something you are grateful for, whether it's small or big, tangible or intangible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:47">[00:00:47]</a>. Fully feel the gratitude and appreciation, noticing sensations in your body <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:06">[00:01:06]</a>. Express gratitude silently or with a prayer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:21">[00:01:21]</a>. Continue breathing while thinking of more things to be grateful for, allowing yourself to feel the warmth and joy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:40">[00:01:40]</a>.
5.  **Concluding Breath**: Take one final deep breath in, and as you exhale, slowly open your eyes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:58">[00:01:58]</a>.

## Benefits

This [[gratitude_meditation | gratitude meditation]] practice, supported by focused breathing, is described as a powerful practice that can transform one's life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:21">[00:02:21]</a>. Cultivating a sense of gratitude through such practices can attract more abundance, joy, and positivity into one's life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:26">[00:02:26]</a>.

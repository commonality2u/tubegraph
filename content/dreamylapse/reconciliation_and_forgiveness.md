Ho'oponopono is a Hawaiian practice of reconciliation and forgiveness that has recently gained popularity as a self-help technique <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:04"></a>.

## The Four Phrases
The practice involves four simple phrases <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:15"></a>:
*   "I'm sorry" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:15"></a>
*   "Please forgive me" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:18"></a>
*   "Thank you" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:20"></a>
*   "I love you" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:22"></a>

## Purpose and Benefits
These phrases are utilized to clear negative energy, facilitate [[healing_relationships|healing relationships]], and foster [[inner_peace_techniques|inner peace]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:26"></a>. Ho'oponopono can be a powerful tool for [[Spiritual Recovery|healing]] and transformation, benefiting individuals and their relationships <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:29"></a>. By embracing responsibility for our thoughts and actions and expressing love and [[expressing_gratitude_and_appreciation|gratitude]], individuals can release negative energy and cultivate a more positive and peaceful existence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:36"></a>.

## How to Practice Ho'oponopono
To engage in the Ho'oponopono practice, follow these steps <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:36"></a>:
1.  **Find a Quiet Space**
    Locate a quiet and comfortable place where you will not be disturbed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:41"></a>.
2.  **Relax and Center Yourself**
    Close your eyes and take a few deep breaths, allowing yourself to relax <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:46"></a>.
3.  **Identify the Source of Negativity**
    Bring to mind a person or situation that is causing you stress, anxiety, or negativity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:52"></a>. This could be someone you are in conflict with, a memory that evokes negative emotions, or a current stressful situation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00"></a>.
4.  **Repeat the Phrases**
    Repeat the four Ho'oponopono phrases either silently or out loud <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:10"></a>:
    > "I'm sorry" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:15"></a>
    > "Please forgive me" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:18"></a>
    > "Thank you" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:20"></a>
    > "I love you" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:22"></a>

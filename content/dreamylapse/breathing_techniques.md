## Guided Breathing Practice

The following steps outline a [[deep_breathing_exercises_for_relaxation | deep breathing exercise for relaxation]] and focus used in a gratitude [[meditation | meditation]]:

1.  **Comfortable Posture**: Begin by finding a comfortable sitting position and closing your eyes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:22">[00:00:22]</a>.
2.  **Inhale Deeply**: Take a deep breath in through your nose, filling your lungs with air <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:30">[00:00:30]</a>.
3.  **Exhale Slowly**: Slowly exhale through your mouth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:38">[00:00:38]</a>.
4.  **Steady Breath**: Allow your breath to become steady and calm <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:42">[00:00:42]</a>.
5.  **Continued Breathing**: Continue to breathe throughout the practice, maintaining a calm and steady rhythm <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:40">[00:01:40]</a>.
6.  **Final Breath**: Conclude the practice with one final deep breath in, and as you exhale, slowly open your eyes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:59">[00:01:59]</a>.

> [!NOTE] Purpose
> While continuing to breathe, the focus shifts to bringing to mind things you are grateful for, whether small or big, tangible or intangible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:50">[00:00:50]</a>. The breath helps to anchor the mind in the present moment as you acknowledge and express gratitude <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:22">[00:01:22]</a>.

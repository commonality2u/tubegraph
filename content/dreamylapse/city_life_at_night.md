## Characteristics of the Night City

The city at night doesn't truly sleep; it merely pretends to <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:25">[00:01:25]</a>. This atmosphere contributes to a pervasive sense of secrecy and hidden motives:
*   **Deception and Distrust**
    *   "Everyone had something to hide" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:49">[00:02:49]</a>.
    *   "Trust is a currency in this town" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:58">[00:02:58]</a>, yet the narrator states they were "always broke" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:20">[00:03:20]</a>, implying a lack of trust.
    *   Truth is elusive, often slipping "through my fingers like smoke" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:11">[00:04:11]</a>.
    *   People, like Susan, might appear trustworthy with a smile that makes you "want to believe her" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:36">[00:04:36]</a>, but their eyes "already told the whole story" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:21">[00:04:21]</a>.

*   **Darkness and Foreboding**
    *   The act of driving deeper into the night is described as the night wrapping itself around the individual "like a velvet noose" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:14">[00:08:14]</a>.
    *   The city's unchanging nature is acknowledged, but the individual becomes "better at seeing what was always rotten" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:39">[00:09:39]</a>. This suggests a deeper, perhaps [[film_noir_ambiance | film noir ambiance]], understanding of the city's underbelly.
    *   Every street light casts a longer shadow than the person walking beneath it, indicating that even individuals are overshadowed by the pervasive gloom <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:26">[00:12:26]</a>.

## Initial Setup

The [[meditation | meditation]] guides individuals to imagine themselves standing at the edge of the sea <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:13">[00:00:13]</a>. Waves roll in slow and steady, mirroring the rhythm of one's breath <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:26">[00:00:26]</a>. A key element of this practice is the imagery of a lighthouse glowing in the distance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:40">[00:00:40]</a>. This lighthouse is not external but exists inside oneself <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:49">[00:00:49]</a>, representing an inherent light that has always been present <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:56">[00:00:56]</a>. Each breath taken fuels this internal light <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:05">[00:01:05]</a>, with inhalations making the light grow warmer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:10">[00:01:10]</a> and exhalations causing any perceived "storm" to fade slightly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:16">[00:01:16]</a>.

## Managing Thoughts

During the [[meditation | meditation]], thoughts may arise "like waves" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:27">[00:01:27]</a>. The guidance encourages allowing these thoughts to come and go <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:33">[00:01:33]</a>, emphasizing that they "cannot shake the lighthouse" within <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:44">[00:01:44]</a>.

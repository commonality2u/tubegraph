## Ho'oponopono: A Hawaiian Practice

Ho'oponopono is a Hawaiian practice of reconciliation and forgiveness that has recently gained popularity as a self-help [[techniques_for_relaxation_and_stress_relief | technique for relaxation and stress relief]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:04">[00:00:04]</a>. The practice utilizes four simple phrases: "I'm sorry," "Please forgive me," "Thank you," and "I love you" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:15">[00:00:15]</a>. These phrases are used to clear negative energy, heal relationships, and promote inner peace <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:26">[00:00:26]</a>.

### How to Use Ho'oponopono

To engage in Ho'oponopono, follow these steps:
1.  Find a quiet and comfortable place where you will not be disturbed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:41">[00:00:41]</a>.
2.  Close your eyes and take a few deep breaths, allowing yourself to relax <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:46">[00:00:46]</a>.
3.  Bring to mind a person or situation that is causing you stress, anxiety, or negativity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:52">[00:00:52]</a>. This could be someone you are in conflict with, a memory that brings up negative emotions, or a current stressful situation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>.
4.  Repeat the following phrases, either silently or out loud <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:10">[00:01:10]</a>:
    *   "I'm sorry" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:15">[00:01:15]</a>
    *   "Please forgive me" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:18">[00:01:18]</a>
    *   "Thank you" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:20">[00:01:20]</a>
    *   "I love you" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:22">[00:01:22]</a>

Ho'oponopono can serve as a powerful tool for healing and transformation, both individually and in relationships <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:27">[00:01:27]</a>. By taking responsibility for one's thoughts and actions and expressing love and gratitude, individuals can release negative energy and create a more [[promoting_positive_and_peaceful_life_through_love_and_gratitude | positive and peaceful life]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:36">[00:01:36]</a>.

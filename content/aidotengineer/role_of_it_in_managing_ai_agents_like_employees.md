## AI Agents as Digital Employees

Enterprise [[role_of_ai_agents_in_workflow_automation | AI agents]] should function akin to any other employee <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:00">[00:02:00]</a>. This means they must:
*   Follow established security policies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:03">[00:02:03]</a>
*   Use approved systems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:05">[00:02:05]</a>
*   Operate within existing data boundaries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:07">[00:02:07]</a>
*   Access only necessary information <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:08">[00:02:08]</a>
*   Be monitored and audited just like human employees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:10">[00:02:10]</a>

### Agent-to-Agent Communication via Email

Email presents a powerful pattern for agent-to-agent communications, mirroring how humans collaborate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:45">[00:04:45]</a>. This allows [[role_of_ai_agents_in_workflow_automation | AI agents]] to exchange data and coordinate work <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:52">[00:04:52]</a>. Every interaction is fully logged and auditable, permissions are automatically enforced, and data flows are transparent and controllable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:57">[00:04:57]</a>. This approach creates a framework for building observable, controllable [[role_of_ai_agents_in_workflow_automation | AI systems]] at enterprise scale <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:06">[00:05:06]</a>. While Microsoft's ecosystem is one example, these patterns apply to Google Workspace or other enterprise platforms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:13">[00:05:13]</a>.

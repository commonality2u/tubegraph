## Joel Greenblatt's Original Backtest

Joel Greenblatt backtested his [[Magic Formula Investing Strategy by Joel Greenblatt | Magic Formula]] between 1988 and 2004, spanning a 16-year period <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:57">[00:09:57]</a>.
His findings were notable:
*   The Magic Portfolio experienced only one down year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:06">[00:10:06]</a>.
*   It achieved a Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) of 30.8% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:09">[00:10:09]</a>.
*   During the same period, the S&P 500's annual return was 12.4% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:12">[00:10:12]</a>.
*   Between 1998 and 2009, the Magic Portfolio's analyzed return was 23.8%, significantly higher than the S&P 500's 9.6% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:32">[00:01:32]</a>.

## Performance in the Indian Stock Market

Research closer to home also indicates the effectiveness of the [[Magic Formula Investing Strategy by Joel Greenblatt | Magic Formula]].

### Sri Guru Gobind Singh College of Commerce Study

A 2021 paper by research students from Sri Guru Gobind Singh College of Commerce simulated the [[Magic Formula Investing Strategy by Joel Greenblatt | Magic Formula]] on Indian stocks over an eight-year period <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:28">[00:10:28]</a>.
The study revealed:
*   On a risk-unadjusted basis, the [[Magic Formula Investing Strategy by Joel Greenblatt | Magic Formula]] outperformed the market in 5 out of 8 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:43">[00:10:43]</a>.
*   It delivered a CAGR of 13.9% compared to the BSE Sensex's 9.3% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:47">[00:10:47]</a>.
*   Testing different portfolio sizes, a 30-stock portfolio offered better returns than 10-stock or 50-stock portfolios, highlighting the balance between focus and diversification <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:56">[00:10:56]</a>.

### Amit Madha's Backtest

Another article by Amit Madha backtested the [[Magic Formula Investing Strategy by Joel Greenblatt | Magic Formula]] framework from 2002 to 2020 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:11">[00:11:11]</a>. He simulated an equally weighted portfolio of 25 stocks (4% allocation per stock) over 18 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:21">[00:11:21]</a>.
His results were striking:
*   While the Nifty 50 generated a 12.7% return during this period <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:32">[00:11:32]</a>.
*   His [[Magic Formula Investing Strategy by Joel Greenblatt | Magic Formula]] portfolio would have delivered an "astounding" 35.4% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:37">[00:11:37]</a>.
*   An initial investment of 1 lakh rupees in 2002 would have grown to 2.33 crores by 2020 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:43">[00:11:43]</a>.

## Conclusion on Performance

The consistent performance across various backtests and geographies suggests that the [[Magic Formula Investing Strategy by Joel Greenblatt | Magic Formula]] presents a strong case for consideration in an investor's approach <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:52">[00:11:52]</a>. It is considered a powerful heuristic, much like the "rule of 72," providing a clear strategy for wealth creation in stock markets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:45">[00:15:45]</a>.

## The Nifty Smallcap 250 Index Explained

The Nifty Smallcap 250 Index is composed of 250 [[performance_and_execution_of_investments_in_small_and_mid_cap_stocks | small cap]] companies, which are defined as companies ranked from 251 to 500 based on their market capitalization among the top 500 companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:06:00">[03:06:00]</a>. These 250 stocks are spread across 21-22 sectors, providing broad diversification <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:26:00">[03:26:00]</a>.

### Valuation (PE Ratio)
Valuation helps investors determine if a stock is overpriced, underpriced, or fairly priced <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:36:00">[09:36:00]</a>. A common parameter for valuation is the Price-to-Earnings (PE) ratio, calculated by dividing the stock price by its earnings per share <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:52:00">[09:52:00]</a>. A lower PE ratio is generally preferred, as it indicates less price paid per unit of earnings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:29:00">[10:29:00]</a>.

The PE ratio of the [[historical_performance_and_valuation_of_small_cap_index | small cap]] index is approximately 25.62 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:58:00">[10:58:00]</a>. At this point, the index appears undervalued compared to active funds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:01:00">[11:01:00]</a>. Furthermore, the current PE ratio of the [[historical_performance_and_valuation_of_small_cap_index | small cap]] index is lower than its five-year average, suggesting it is favorably priced for investment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:13:00">[11:13:00]</a>.

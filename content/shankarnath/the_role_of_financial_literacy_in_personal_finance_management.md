## Defining Financial Literacy

[[financial_habits_for_young_adults | Financial literacy]] is the basic understanding of six core financial matters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:54">[00:12:54]</a>:

1.  **Budgeting Money** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:58">[00:12:58]</a>
2.  **Setting Financial Goals** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:01">[00:13:01]</a>
3.  **Saving Money** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:04">[00:13:04]</a>
4.  **Understanding Debt** – including loans, credit cards, and credit scores <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:07">[00:13:07]</a>
5.  **Understanding Taxes** – how they work and strategies for savings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:11">[00:13:11]</a>
6.  **Basics of Investing** – covering instruments like [[role_of_fixed_deposits_in_portfolio_management | fixed deposits]], NPS, mutual funds, and sovereign gold bonds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:16">[00:13:16]</a>

The importance of understanding these six areas cannot be understated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:26">[00:13:26]</a>.

## Practical Applications of Financial Literacy

A robust understanding of financial literacy directly impacts several annual financial tasks:

### 1. Understanding Tax Law Changes
The Union Budget often introduces changes to tax laws, such as adjustments in income tax slabs, tax rebates, surcharge reductions for HNIs, and increased limits on presumptive taxation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:43">[00:00:43]</a>. Comprehending and complying with these changes is crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:11">[00:01:11]</a>. For salaried individuals, choosing between old and new tax regimes significantly affects take-home salary and tax payments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:19">[00:01:19]</a>. Seeking advice from a tax advisor or chartered accountant is recommended if tax matters become overwhelming <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:56">[00:09:56]</a>.

### 2. Reviewing Financial Goals
The beginning of a financial year is an appropriate time to [[importance_of_reviewing_financial_goals_at_the_start_of_a_financial_year | review financial goals]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:56">[00:01:56]</a>. This involves understanding current goals, tracking progress, and adjusting for changes in internal factors (like income or new expenses) or external factors (like tax laws or inflation) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:35">[00:02:35]</a>. This helps ensure preparedness and alignment with current circumstances <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:10">[00:03:10]</a>.

### 3. Reviewing Liabilities
A detailed review of liabilities is recommended <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:21">[00:03:21]</a>. This includes assessing the ability to pay loan installments, exploring opportunities to foreclose or prepay loans (especially with an annual bonus), and negotiating better interest rates with banks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:39">[00:03:39]</a>. [[behavioral_finance_and_valuation | Financial literacy]] empowers individuals to seek better deals, including balance transfers if necessary <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:31">[00:04:31]</a>.

### 4. Reviewing Investment Portfolio
An annual review of one's investment portfolio is critical <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:40">[00:04:40]</a>. Key areas include:
*   **[[effective_asset_allocation_and_portfolio_management | Asset Allocation]]**: Smartly dividing the portfolio across different assets to improve returns and reduce volatility or risk <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:01">[00:05:01]</a>.
*   **Performance Review**: Identifying underperformers and instruments likely to perform well by understanding returns, consistency, fund manager style, [[analysis_of_financial_statements | valuation]], balance sheet strength, and growth estimates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:28">[00:05:28]</a>. This includes recognizing business cycles for thematic funds or cyclical stocks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:52">[00:05:52]</a>.
*   **Rebalancing**: Bringing the portfolio back to the original asset allocation by selling overperforming assets and buying underperforming ones <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:13">[00:06:13]</a>. This reduces variability and can improve returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:28">[00:06:28]</a>. Awareness of tax implications like capital gains tax and tax loss harvesting is also important <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:45">[00:06:45]</a>.

### 5. Smart Utilization of Annual Increments
Instead of simply increasing monthly SIPs proportionally to an increment, a financially literate approach focuses on allocating a larger portion of the *incremental* income to investments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:47">[00:07:47]</a>. For example, if a 20% increment results in an extra 20,000 rupees monthly, and existing expenses remain stable, the entire 20,000 rupees can be added to investments, significantly boosting wealth growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:17">[00:08:17]</a>. This aligns with the concept of [[financialization_of_household_savings | financialization of household savings]].

### 6. Tax Planning
[[using_customized_financial_planning_for_retirement | Effective tax planning]] ensures individuals do not pay more taxes than legally required <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:01">[00:09:01]</a>. This includes utilizing deductions under sections like 80C and 80D (for investments in NPS, PPF, and insurance policies), tax loss harvesting, deductions on home loan interest, and savings on education loan repayments (Section 80E) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:07">[00:09:07]</a>. Starting tax planning early in the financial year is crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:44">[00:09:44]</a>.

### 7. Reviewing Insurance Policies
An annual review of term insurance and health insurance policies is warranted <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:07">[00:10:07]</a>. Changes in dependents, relocation to a higher cost-of-living area, new liabilities like a home loan, and medical inflation (e.g., 14%) all necessitate re-evaluation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:12">[00:10:12]</a>. Staying informed about competitive insurance markets, new products, and innovations allows for finding better deals that align with current needs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:53">[00:10:53]</a>.

### 8. Checking Credit Score
Periodically checking one's credit score is beneficial even without an immediate need for a loan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:18">[00:11:18]</a>. It helps identify inaccuracies in the credit report that can be rectified <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:29">[00:11:29]</a>. An improved credit score can lead to better loan offers and interest rates from existing or new lenders <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:42">[00:11:42]</a>.

### 9. Scheduling Financial Events
Integrating all financial events into a calendar, such as income tax return filing dates, document submission deadlines, PF contribution dates, and insurance renewal dates, ensures discipline and organization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:04">[00:12:04]</a>. This simple practice helps avoid unnecessary complications <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:27">[00:12:27]</a>.

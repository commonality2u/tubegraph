## The Free Lunch Portfolio

The "Free Lunch Portfolio" is built on the premise that investors can replicate the success of great investors by observing their publicly disclosed holdings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:59">[00:00:59]</a>. The U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) mandates that individuals and entities with at least $100 million invested in U.S. equity markets disclose their holdings quarterly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>. This public information enables Pabrai's simple [[shameless_cloning_strategy | cloning strategy]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:13">[00:01:13]</a>.

A copycat portfolio based on this approach achieved an annualized return of 15.5% over a 17-year period, significantly outperforming the S&P 500's 4.8% during the same time, yielding an alpha of 10.7% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:21">[00:01:21]</a>. This performance motivated Pabrai to establish his own Free Lunch Portfolio <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:35">[00:01:35]</a>.

The Free Lunch Portfolio comprises 15 stocks, divided equally into three distinct categories <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:03">[00:02:03]</a>:
1.  Spawners <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:09">[00:02:09]</a>
2.  Shameless Cloners <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:12">[00:02:12]</a>
3.  Uber Cannibals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:14">[00:02:14]</a>

While this portfolio is not exactly Pabrai's personal one, it largely reflects his investment philosophy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:20">[00:02:20]</a>. It serves as an experimental framework to enhance long-term stock or fund selection <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:31">[00:02:31]</a>.

## Spawners

Spawners are companies with an inherent ability to incubate new businesses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:42">[00:02:42]</a>. They leverage existing capital, infrastructure, and human resources to launch new ventures that can eventually become significant, multi-billion dollar entities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:46">[00:02:46]</a>. These companies typically use a "cash cow" business to aggressively reinvest profits into other, potentially unrelated, businesses that can drive future growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:32">[00:03:32]</a>.

Its advertising revenue acts as the cash cow <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:34">[00:03:34]</a>.
*   **Disney**: Encompasses film studios, theme parks, toys, Pixar, and OTT channels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:14">[00:03:14]</a>.
*   **Amazon**: Includes amazon.com, Prime, games, Whole Foods, AWS, Kindle, and logistics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:21">[00:03:21]</a>.

## Uber Cannibals

Uber Cannibals are companies that consistently buy back their own shares <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:26">[00:06:26]</a>. Companies typically engage in buybacks when they possess significant cash reserves, anticipate minimal competition or disruption, and believe their stock is undervalued <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:32">[00:06:32]</a>.

**Benefit of Share Buybacks for Investors:**
When a company buys back shares, it reduces the total number of outstanding shares. This action increases an existing shareholder's proportional stake in the company without requiring additional capital investment from the shareholder. It also boosts earnings per share (EPS) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:15">[00:07:15]</a>.

*Example*: If a company with 1,000 shares and 30% investor stake buys back 200 shares, the outstanding shares reduce to 800. The investor's stake effectively increases to 37.5%, and earnings per share rise from ₹50 to ₹62.50, assuming ₹50,000 in annual profit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:44">[00:06:44]</a>. This represents a [[methods_promoters_use_to_increase_their_stake_in_a_company | method to increase stake]] and potential upside for investors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:35">[00:07:35]</a>.

## Portfolio Construction and Performance

The Free Lunch Portfolio is a dynamic list, with constituents announced annually, allowing for flexibility for replacements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:02">[00:09:02]</a>. Pabrai recommends equal weighting for stocks within such a portfolio; for example, investing $1,000 into each stock if the total capital is $15,000 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:07">[00:10:07]</a>.

In terms of [[performance_of_copycat_investing_strategy | performance]], the Free Lunch Portfolio initially struggled, particularly from 2018 to 2020 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:22">[00:09:22]</a>. However, after methodological changes in 2021, its performance has become more aligned with the S&P 500 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:30">[00:09:30]</a>. Pabrai views this portfolio as built around value principles like [[spin_offs_and_demergers | spin-offs]], cash flows, and moats, which often take time to materialize results <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:52">[00:09:52]</a>.

This stock-picking approach benefits from applying learnings from other investment strategies such as identifying "coffee can" stocks, monopolies, the magic formula, and cash-rich companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:48">[00:14:48]</a>. The understanding of Spawners, Shameless Cloners, and Uber Cannibals provides a robust framework for developing a successful long-term investment portfolio <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:16">[00:15:16]</a>.

## Taxation of Listed Bonds vs. Debt Mutual Funds

For most investors, listed bonds can offer a more favorable tax treatment compared to [[understanding_debt_mutual_funds_and_bond_investments | debt mutual funds]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:14:27">[14:14:27]</a>.

*   **Listed Bonds**: The long-term capital gain on listed [[understanding_debt_mutual_funds_and_bond_investments | bonds]] is taxed at just 10% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:14:27">[14:14:27]</a>.
*   **Debt Mutual Funds**: In contrast, [[understanding_debt_mutual_funds_and_bond_investments | debt mutual funds]] are taxed as per an investor's marginal tax rate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:24:27">[14:24:27]</a>.

Given the potential for a 0.5% to 0.75% reduction in the repo rate, the chances for investors to take advantage of this by allocating a portion of their fixed income portfolio to long-duration funds for the next 12 to 18 months are quite high <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:28:27">[14:28:27]</a>. While the [[Impact of government policy and taxation on Gold investments | taxation]] aspect is a significant consideration, investors should also note that listed [[understanding_debt_mutual_funds_and_bond_investments | bonds]] provide some tax advantages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:14:27">[14:14:27]</a>. Therefore, it is worth keeping an eye on this type of investment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:17:27">[14:17:27]</a>.

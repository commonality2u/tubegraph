## Valuation of Small Cap Index
Valuation helps investors understand if a stock is overpriced, underpriced, or fairly priced <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:36:00">[09:36:00]</a>. A common parameter for valuation is the PE (Price-to-Earnings) ratio, calculated by dividing the stock's price by its earnings per share <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:52:00">[09:52:00]</a>. A lower PE ratio is generally considered better, as it indicates an investor is paying less for each unit of earnings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:25:00">[10:25:00]</a>.

Currently, the PE ratio of the [[SME IPO Index performance | small cap index]] is around 25.62 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:50:00">[10:50:00]</a>. This suggests that the index is undervalued compared to many active funds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:01:00">[11:01:00]</a>. Furthermore, the current PE ratio of the [[SME IPO Index performance | small cap index]] is at least five years lower than its five-year average <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:13:00">[11:13:00]</a>. This indicates that the index is currently [[Tactical approaches to maximize small cap investment performance | favourably priced]] for investors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:23:00">[11:23:00]</a>.

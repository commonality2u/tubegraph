## What is a Bond?

At its core, a bond is a loan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:27">[00:01:27]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:31">[00:01:31]</a>. Loans are needed by various institutions, including banks, companies, Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs), home loan providers, and especially the state and central governments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:35">[00:01:35]</a>. Governments borrow to fund welfare schemes, salaries, defense, subsidies, and infrastructure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:41">[00:01:41]</a>. A significant portion of government borrowing occurs through the issuance of treasury bills and government securities (G-Secs) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:54">[00:01:54]</a>.

When an individual buys a bond, invests in a [[debt_mutual_funds_and_safety_considerations | debt mutual fund]], or puts money in a Fixed Deposit (FD), they are essentially lending money to an institution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:03">[00:02:03]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:06">[00:02:06]</a>. In return, the lender receives a "coupon" or interest on the principal, which currently ranges from 7% to 8% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:11">[00:02:11]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:13">[00:02:13]</a>.

## The Role of the RBI and Repo Rate

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) is responsible for regulating the money supply within the financial system to prevent issues like high inflation, asset bubbles, or currency weakening <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:30">[00:02:30]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:35">[00:02:35]</a>. It achieves this primarily by adjusting the benchmark policy rate, or repo rate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:38">[00:02:38]</a>. This rate is constantly changing; for instance, since 2000, the RBI has changed the repo rate 77 times, with the current rate at 6.5% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:48">[00:02:48]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:52">[00:02:52]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:55">[00:02:55]</a>.

### Impact of Repo Rate on Bond Prices

The price of a bond is the sum of its future cash flows discounted back to their present value <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:08">[00:03:08]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:10">[00:03:10]</a>. The discount rate used in this calculation is closely tied to the RBI's interest rate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:05">[00:04:05]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:08">[00:04:08]</a>.

**Example:**
Consider a three-year bond paying 7% interest annually, with a principal of 1,000 rupees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:47">[00:04:47]</a>.
*   If the discount rate (repo rate) is 7%, the bond's present value is 1,000 rupees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:10">[00:04:10]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:20">[00:04:20]</a>.
*   If the RBI suddenly reduces the repo rate to 5%, the discount rate decreases, and the present value of the bond's future cash flows increases to 1,054 rupees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:23">[00:04:23]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:26">[00:04:26]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:40">[00:04:40]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:43">[00:04:43]</a>.

This 2% drop in interest rates leads to a 5.4% increase in the bond's price, providing an additional capital gain to the investor on top of the interest received <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:47">[00:04:47]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:52">[00:04:52]</a>. This mechanism allows fixed-income investors to profit significantly by leveraging the interest rate cycle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:02">[00:05:02]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:04">[00:05:04]</a>.

## Factors Influencing Future Repo Rate Decisions (2024 Outlook)

The RBI's Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) continuously monitors several factors to determine the repo rate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:50">[00:06:50]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:00">[00:07:00]</a>:

1.  **Global Rate Cuts:** A growing number of central banks globally have paused or are cutting interest rates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:15">[00:07:15]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:20">[00:07:20]</a>. With inflation becoming more manageable (e.g., 3-4% in the US and Europe), many developed economies are expected to reduce rates from Q2 or Q3 2024 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:30">[00:07:30]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:33">[00:07:33]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:36">[00:07:36]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:39">[00:07:39]</a>. Indian [[debt_mutual_funds_and_safety_considerations | debt mutual funds]] managers anticipate domestic rate cuts to follow this global easing cycle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:41">[00:07:41]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:43">[00:07:43]</a>.
2.  **Inflation:** High inflation typically prompts central banks to increase interest rates to curb borrowing and spending <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:17">[00:08:17]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:24">[00:08:24]</a>. While India's retail inflation (CPI) has decreased from 7.4% in July last year to 5.1% in January this year, the RBI's target is 4% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:33">[00:08:33]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:39">[00:08:39]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:42">[00:08:42]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:44">[00:08:44]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:47">[00:08:47]</a>. Core inflation has also significantly reduced from 6.5% to 3.5% within a year, presenting promising signs for rate cuts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:51">[00:08:51]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:52">[00:08:52]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:56">[00:08:56]</a>. The difference between the repo rate (6.5%) and the estimated one-year forward CPI (4.5%) is 200 basis points, creating room for a 50 basis point interest rate cut as RBI targets a real rate of 150 basis points <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:02">[00:09:02]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:12">[00:09:12]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:15">[00:09:15]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:16">[00:09:16]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:20">[00:09:20]</a>.
3.  **Government Financial Prudence:** The Indian government has shown restraint in its interim budget, pegging lower gross market borrowings for FY25 (14.1 lakh crores) compared to FY24 (17.8 lakh crores) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:26">[00:09:26]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:34">[00:09:34]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:36">[00:09:36]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:41">[00:09:41]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:43">[00:09:43]</a>. This aims to reduce the fiscal deficit, providing the RBI more confidence to consider rate reductions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:53">[00:09:53]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:19">[00:10:19]</a>.
4.  **Inclusion in JP Morgan Emerging Market Debt Index:** Indian government bonds will be included in the JP Morgan Emerging Market debt index starting June 2024 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:25">[00:10:25]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:29">[00:10:29]</a>. This has already increased demand for Indian gilts, with foreign investors buying over 65,000 crores in the past five months <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:36">[00:10:36]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:39">[00:10:39]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:43">[00:10:43]</a>. This inclusion is expected to bring in over $25 billion (2 lakh crores INR) into government securities, further reducing bond yields <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:57">[00:10:57]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:00">[00:11:00]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:02">[00:11:02]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:06">[00:11:06]</a>.
5.  **Other Factors:** The RBI also considers increases in indirect tax collections, growth in corporate earnings and GDP (recently upgraded from 6.5% to 7%), benign commodity prices, high foreign currency reserves, and softening crude oil prices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:11">[00:11:11]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:13">[00:11:13]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:16">[00:11:16]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:18">[00:11:18]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:20">[00:11:20]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:24">[00:11:24]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:26">[00:11:26]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:28">[00:11:28]</a>.

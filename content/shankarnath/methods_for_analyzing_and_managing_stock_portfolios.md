## Investment Philosophy and Stock Selection

Dolly Khanna's core investment philosophy centers on [[constructing_a_trending_value_stock_portfolio | capturing growth opportunities]] primarily within the small and mid-cap sectors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:25">[00:02:25]</a>. This includes companies like Neil Kamill, Rain Industries, Aanti Feeds, Pondi Oxides and Chemicals, Monte Carlo Fashion, and Siman Farms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:34">[00:02:34]</a>.

### Sectoral Preferences
The portfolio exhibits a preference for the growth style of investing, avoiding traditional sectors like banking, technology, or government companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:49">[02:49]</a>. Instead, there's a strong focus on industries such as textiles, chemicals, rubber, automotive, and manufacturing, where potential undervaluation and mispricing are identified <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:59">[02:59]</a>.

### Everyday Product Focus
A distinct characteristic of this approach is prioritizing companies that deal in products and services commonly used in daily life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:10">[03:10]</a>. This includes investments in:
*   **Unitech Limited:** Identified when looking to buy a house <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:18">[03:18]</a>.
*   **Mjushi Technopac:** Discovered while seeking a bottle manufacturer for a food business <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:23">[03:23]</a>.
*   **Hawkins Cookers:** Based on the need for everyday cooking devices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:29">[03:29]</a>.
*   **Femare:** Resulted from observing cosmetic product use <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:36">[03:36]</a>.

This preference for small B2C (business-to-consumer) companies is attributed to Rajiv Khanna's background in running a business (Quality Milk Foods) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:45">[03:45]</a>.

## Portfolio Management and Exit Strategy

### Active Management and Timely Exits
Dolly Khanna is not a "buy and hold" investor and actively [[managing_a_portfolio_of_turnaround_stocks | manages her portfolio]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:10">[04:10]</a>. A crucial aspect of the strategy is knowing when to exit a stock. Examples include:
*   Selling IT holdings before the dot-com bubble <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:01">[04:01]</a>.
*   Exiting Unitech before the global financial crisis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:06">[04:06]</a>.
*   Exiting stocks with high allocations if company performance dips or falls short of expectations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:17">[04:17]</a>.

### Learning from Mistakes
Mistakes are acknowledged, such as the premature exit from many positions during the initial COVID-19 lockdowns, based on an incorrect assumption of severe damage to portfolio companies, and not re-entering the market decisively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:24">[04:24]</a>.

### Information Consumption and Decision Making
The investment decisions are primarily based on publicly available data, with limited direct engagement with company management or "scuttlebutt" investigations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:47">[04:47]</a>. The focus is on making sense of public information and recognizing patterns with experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:10">[05:10]</a>.

## Evolving Investment Approaches

The Khanna's [[improving_stock_and_mutual_fund_selection | investment approach]] continually evolves, incorporating new strategies and mental models <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:28">[05:28]</a>.

### Special Situation Investing
[[strategies_for_selecting_a_portfolio_of_monopoly_stocks | Special situation investing]] is an area of interest, where significant returns can be made in a short period <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:32">[05:32]</a>.

### Beyond Buy and Hold
While not entirely against [[reviewing_and_managing_investment_portfolios_for_optimal_returns | buy and hold investing]], it is viewed as just one of several available techniques <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:53">[05:53]</a>.

### Incorporating Technical Analysis
More recently, a notable shift includes the integration of technical analysis, specifically checking the 30-day moving average (DMA) for each stock in a portfolio that can exceed 500 stocks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:16">[06:16]</a>.
*   **Buying signal:** If a stock trades above its 30-day moving average, it suggests a positive breakout, prompting investment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:31">[06:31]</a>.
*   **Selling signal:** If the stock price falls below its 30-day moving average, it's considered for selling <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:40">[06:40]</a>.

This simple filter aims to facilitate timely market entry when a breakout begins and exit before potential calamities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:46">[06:46]</a>.

## Replicating a Portfolio

Attempting to replicate a seasoned investor's portfolio, such as Dolly Khanna's, involves using publicly available data <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:33">[07:33]</a>.

### Data Availability
Since 2016, SEBI (Securities and Exchange Board of India) has mandated that all companies disclose shareholders holding more than 1% stake <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:58">[07:58]</a>. Websites like Moneycontrol, Trendlyne, and Phycoology offer insights into these holdings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:47">[07:47]</a>. However, holdings below 1% are not disclosed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:10">[08:10]</a>.

### Case Study on Replication
A personal experiment to replicate Dolly Khanna's 1%+ holdings from June 2021 to June 2023 across 37 stocks yielded an XIRR of 6.4% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:00">[10:00]</a>.
*   **Methodology:** Involved manual data collection, calculating shares and prices quarterly, and mapping positive/negative increments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:38">[08:38]</a>.
*   **Assumptions:** The starting point (June 2021) was assumed as the initial acquisition date for shares, and investment costs were based on this <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:51">[08:51]</a>.
*   **Limitations:** The study ignored dividends, taxation, and relied heavily on assumptions for acquisition costs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:18">[10:18]</a>. Crucially, it only analyzed 1% holdings, while the Khannas have invested in over 500 companies in total <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:30">[10:30]</a>.

## Components of Defining Investment Goals

Defining investment goals can be broken down into three simple components:

1.  **Identify the Purpose of Wealth Building** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:59">[00:02:59]</a>: Determine what specific objectives the wealth is being accumulated for. Common examples include:
    *   Retirement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:02">[00:03:02]</a>
    *   Emergencies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:02">[00:03:02]</a>
    *   Education <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:03">[00:03:03]</a>
    *   Constructing a home <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:04">[00:03:04]</a>
    *   Starting a business <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:05">[00:03:05]</a>

2.  **Match Goals to Strategies** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:07">[00:03:07]</a>: Each goal should correspond to a specific [[investment_and_portfolio_growth_strategies | investment strategy]] that aligns with its nature and the investor's risk tolerance level <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:08">[00:03:08]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:30">[00:03:30]</a>.
    *   **Preservation**: For short-term needs like medical treatments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:11">[00:03:11]</a>.
    *   **Cash Flow**: For managing regular monthly household expenses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:15">[00:03:15]</a>.
    *   **Growth**: For long-term objectives such as retirement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:18">[00:03:18]</a>.
    *   Investments can be tagged based on risk tolerance: safe, moderate, or aggressive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:32">[00:03:32]</a>.

3.  **Establish an Investing Workflow** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:35">[00:03:35]</a>: This involves practical steps to monitor and manage progress:
    *   Write down both short-term and long-term goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:39">[00:03:39]</a>.
    *   Assign a timeline to each goal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:41">[00:03:41]</a>.
    *   Periodically check progress <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:44">[00:03:44]</a>.
    *   Determine the necessary annual savings and investments required to meet these goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:46">[00:03:46]</a>.

## Benefits and Practical Application

Clearly defining investment goals is not just theoretical; it serves multiple practical purposes:
*   **Tracking Progress**: It helps investors keep track of their financial journey <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:33">[00:03:33]</a>.
*   **Built-in Review System**: It acts as an inbuilt review mechanism, ensuring that the appropriate investment is aligned with the correct goal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:37">[00:03:37]</a>.

### Example: Retirement Planning

For instance, a retirement goal typically requires a growth strategy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:57">[00:03:57]</a>. An example from 2015 illustrates this: a relative shifted a significant amount from a PPF account (Public Provident Fund) to an equity instrument after analyzing their retirement needs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:00">[00:04:00]</a>. While regular PPF investments over 10 years would yield approximately 7.7% annual return, the same money invested monthly in a Nifty 50 ETF would have delivered a significantly higher 13.6% return <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:11">[00:04:11]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:20">[00:04:20]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:25">[00:04:25]</a>. This demonstrates how defining goals can prompt a strategic realignment of investments to achieve better outcomes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:05">[00:04:05]</a>.

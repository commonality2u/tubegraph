When examining potential turnaround stocks, performing basic [[Technical analysis and chart patterns|technical analysis]] is crucial to determine if the share price is forming a base or a bottom <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:54">[00:11:54]</a>. While some investors may consider it subjective or lacking predictive value, charting offers significant utility in identifying promising opportunities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:03">[00:12:03]</a>.

## Utility of Charting

[[Technical analysis and chart patterns|Charting]] can help investors in several ways:
*   It determines if a stock is being accumulated, especially by institutional investors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:18">[00:12:18]</a>.
*   It helps gauge the psychology and emotions influencing a stock's price action <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:26">[00:12:26]</a>.
*   The price and trading volumes of a turnaround stock provide important signals that can be very helpful for investors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:06">[00:13:06]</a>.

## Key Chart Patterns

Successful turnaround stocks often display repeatable and identical patterns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:33">[00:13:33]</a>. Three basic charts that show promise for identifying turnarounds include:
*   **Double Bottom** This pattern indicates the support levels of a stock <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:51">[00:12:51]</a>.
*   **Reverse Head and Shoulders** This pattern signals when a downward trend is nearing its end <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:55">[00:12:55]</a>.
*   **Breakout Scenario** This occurs when the price for a stock moves to the right of a trend line <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:01">[00:13:01]</a>.

## Strategic Entry and Exit

A cardinal rule of [[Turnaround investing strategies|turnaround investing]] is to avoid buying turnaround stocks in a downtrend, often referred to as "catching a falling knife" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:14">[00:13:14]</a>. It is advisable to enter only after a clear bottom has been formed and there is a significant break in the technical price support levels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:21">[00:13:21]</a>. This technical signal should ideally be backed by some good news about the company <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:28">[00:13:28]</a>. An understanding of these patterns can provide a serious advantage in [[Turnaround investing strategies|turnaround investing]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:38">[00:13:38]</a>.

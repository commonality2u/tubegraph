## What is a REIT?

A Real Estate Investment Trust (REIT) operates similarly to a mutual fund <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:44">[00:00:44]</a>. Retail investors provide money to a REIT manager, who then invests in income-producing properties <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:50">[00:00:50]</a>. These properties are typically commercial, but can also include hospitals, malls, and residential spaces <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>. The rental income generated by these properties is transferred to investors in the form of dividends <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:07">[00:01:07]</a>.

### Advantages of Investing in REITs
From an investor's perspective, REITs offer several advantages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:30">[00:01:30]</a>:
*   Makes investing in commercial real estate more affordable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:35">[00:01:35]</a>.
*   Simplifies real estate investing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:37">[00:01:37]</a>.
*   Eliminates the need for property maintenance or direct property tax payments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:39">[00:01:39]</a>.
*   REITs are professionally managed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:44">[00:01:44]</a>.
*   In India, the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) regulates and monitors REITs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:47">[00:01:47]</a>.

## [[introduction_to_real_estate_investment_trusts_reits_in_india | Introduction to Real Estate Investment Trusts REITs in India]]

The concept of REITs is relatively new for Indian investors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:59">[00:01:59]</a>. The listing of Embassy Office Parks in 2019 marked the beginning, with Mindspace and Brookfield joining in 2020 and 2021, respectively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:04">[00:02:04]</a>. SEBI has been taking progressive steps to enhance the product's attractiveness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:17">[00:02:17]</a>.

### [[sebi_regulations_and_rules_governing_reits_in_india | SEBI Regulations and Rules Governing REITs in India]]
Key SEBI rules regarding REITs include <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:24">[00:02:24]</a>:
1.  **Property Investment Proportion**: SEBI follows an 80:20 rule <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:36">[00:02:36]</a>.
    *   80% of a REIT's asset value must be in completed and income-generating real assets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:40">[00:02:40]</a>.
    *   The remaining 20% can be invested in riskier assets like under-construction buildings, equity shares, or mortgage-backed securities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:47">[00:02:47]</a>. This ensures that 80% of the portfolio is relatively safe and stable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:54">[00:02:54]</a>.
2.  **Mandatory Distribution**: REITs must distribute at least 90% of their net distributable cash earnings to investors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:04">[00:03:04]</a>. This is typically paid quarterly as dividends or interest <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:23">[00:03:23]</a>.
3.  **Public Listing**: All SEBI-registered REITs must be publicly listed, allowing investors to buy shares or units similar to listed companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:28">[00:03:28]</a>.

### How Investors Earn from REITs
Investors can earn from REITs in two primary ways <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:40">[00:03:40]</a>:
1.  **Dividends**: REITs typically offer a dividend yield of 5% to 7% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:42">[00:03:42]</a>.
2.  **Capital Gains**: This occurs when the REIT's per-unit price increases <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:50">[00:03:50]</a>.

## [[evaluation_metrics_for_real_estate_companies | Evaluation Metrics for Real Estate Companies]]

When [[evaluating_stock_market_metrics_for_investment_decisions | evaluating stock market metrics for investment decisions]] and [[financial_metrics_for_evaluating_stocks | financial metrics for evaluating stocks]], especially for REITs, several factors beyond growth, revenue, and profits are important <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:59">[00:03:59]</a>. These can be grouped into categories such as portfolio, operating financials, distribution returns, and leverage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:11">[00:04:11]</a>.

### 1. Net Asset Value (NAV) Per Unit
The REIT's NAV represents the market value of its properties minus any liabilities or debts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:47">[00:04:47]</a>. The NAV per unit is calculated by dividing the net asset value by the number of units issued <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:16">[00:05:16]</a>.

Unlike mutual funds, REIT NAV is not declared daily; it is calculated once every six months because it's impractical to value properties daily <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:36">[00:05:36]</a>.

Comparing the NAV per unit with the REIT's market value is a crucial [[valuation_metrics_in_stock_selection | valuation metric in stock selection]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:52">[00:05:52]</a>. Indian REITs typically trade at a discount of 14% to 16% to their NAV <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:14">[00:06:14]</a>. Fluctuations in this market price to NAV percentage can create opportunities for investors to buy or sell units for profit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:22">[00:06:22]</a>. Extreme market price to NAV periods can serve as excellent entry or exit points <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:09">[00:07:09]</a>.

### 2. Occupancy Rate
The occupancy rate is a straightforward metric: a higher occupancy rate is better <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:20">[00:07:20]</a>. While the current occupancy rates are generally in the 87% to 89% range, they were higher pre-COVID (high 80s and 90s) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:24">[00:07:24]</a>. An occupancy rate between 85% and 90% is considered good and acceptable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:49">[00:07:49]</a>.

### 3. Weighted Average Lease Expiry (WALLE)
WALLE (Weighted Average Lease Expiry) is the opposite of occupancy; it calculates the time remaining before an asset or property becomes vacant <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:06">[00:08:06]</a>. A higher WALLE is preferable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:19">[00:08:19]</a>. Globally, a WALLE of five years is considered good <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:21">[00:08:21]</a>.

### 4. Distribution Yield
The distribution yield represents the returns earned by REIT unit holders as a percentage of the current market price <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:35">[00:08:35]</a>. It is calculated by dividing the distributed dividends and interest (on a per-unit basis) by the current market price <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:49">[00:08:49]</a>. Investors should be careful when comparing distribution yields, as some companies might calculate it based on the issue price rather than the current market price <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:05">[00:09:05]</a>.

### 5. Portfolio Quality
Portfolio quality, one of the key [[factors_influencing_real_estate_valuation | factors influencing real estate valuation]], consists of two variables <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:10">[00:10:10]</a>:

*   **Tenants**: A strong REIT should not be overly dependent on a few clients <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:18">[00:10:18]</a>. Diversification of the tenant base reduces risk.
*   **Geographical Spread**: While concentrating properties in a few locations can improve economics, it introduces city-specific risks (e.g., lockdowns, floods, political issues, disruptions) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:45">[00:10:45]</a>. A more spread-out and diverse geographical presence is generally better <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:17">[00:11:17]</a>.

### 6. [[tax_treatment_and_returns_on_reit_investments | Tax Treatment and Returns on REIT Investments]]

The taxation of REITs can be complex, as different REITs may have varying tax structures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:27">[00:11:27]</a>. REITs in India receive some tax exemptions on rental income and leasing interest, which helps reduce tax liability and generate higher income for investors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:36">[00:11:36]</a>.

Investors make returns in four ways <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:46">[00:11:46]</a>:
*   **Interest Income**: Fully taxable based on the investor's income tax slab <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:01">[00:12:01]</a>.
*   **Amortization of SPV Debt (Return of Capital)**: Entirely non-taxable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:07">[00:12:07]</a>.
*   **Capital Gains**:
    *   Short-term capital gains (units held for less than one year) are taxed at 15% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:12">[00:12:12]</a>.
    *   Long-term capital gains are taxed at 10% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:21">[00:12:21]</a>.
*   **Dividends**: Taxability depends on whether the REIT's Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) opted for a lower tax regime <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:24">[00:12:24]</a>. If the SPV took tax concessions, the dividend is taxable for the investor; otherwise, it is tax-free <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:38">[00:12:38]</a>. This means dividend tax treatment can differ between REITs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:51">[00:12:51]</a>.

Investors should check the investor presentations of individual REITs for specific tax information, as it can significantly impact post-tax returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:58">[00:12:58]</a>.

## Investing in REITs

Units in REITs can be bought or sold in the secondary market through various trading platforms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:33">[00:13:33]</a>. The process is similar to buying shares of a listed company <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:42">[00:13:42]</a>. With the minimum buying quantity reduced to just one unit, investors can start investing in real estate via REITs from approximately 300 to 350 rupees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:46">[00:13:46]</a>.

### Recommended Exposure and Expectations
Given that REITs are a relatively new asset class in India and the laws are still developing, it is suggested to limit exposure to not more than 10% of a portfolio <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:15">[00:14:15]</a>. From an expectation perspective, REITs may deliver 5% to 6% in post-tax dividends and 4% to 5% in capital appreciation, assuming thorough due diligence on the major factors discussed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:24">[00:14:24]</a>.

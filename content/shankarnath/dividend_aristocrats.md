The [[dividend_investing_strategies | Dividend Aristocrats]] are a prominent and often-cited dividend growth strategy. This term refers to an index designed to track the performance of S&P 500 companies that have consistently increased their dividend payouts annually for a minimum of 25 consecutive years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:06">[00:10:06]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:10">[00:10:10]</a>.

## Characteristics and Performance
Unlike strategies focused purely on [[high_dividend_yield_stocks | high dividend yield]] or just capital appreciation, [[dividend_growth_vs_high_dividend_yield | Dividend Aristocrats]] exhibit both capital growth and dividend income characteristics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:23">[00:10:23]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:27">[00:10:27]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:29">[00:10:29]</a>.

Studies across various markets indicate that [[dividend_growth_vs_high_dividend_yield | dividend growth stocks]] generally outperform [[high_dividend_yield_stocks | high dividend yield stocks]] in bullish, bearish, and neutral market conditions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:51">[00:09:51]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:55">[00:09:55]</a>. They tend to offer higher and less volatile returns compared to most other investment strategies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:00">[00:10:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:03">[00:10:03]</a>.

The [[dividend_investing_strategies | Dividend Aristocrat strategy]] has historically delivered higher returns with lower volatility when compared to its benchmark, the S&P 500 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:38">[00:10:38]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:40">[00:10:40]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:42">[00:10:42]</a>. Their consistent dividend increases over 25 years have not compromised their dividend yield, as they have consistently maintained a yield higher than the S&P 500 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:46">[00:10:46]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:51">[00:10:51]</a>. As of 2023, [[dividend_investing_strategies | Aristocrats]] yielded 2.5%, while the S&P 500 yielded 1.8% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:03">[00:11:03]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:06">[00:11:06]</a>.

### Underlying Factors
The strong performance of [[dividend_investing_strategies | Dividend Aristocrats]] is often attributed to their accidental exposure to other beneficial factors like value, quality, price-to-earnings ratios, and free cash flow <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:12">[00:12:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:14">[00:12:14]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:16">[00:12:16]</a>. This means the strategy exhibits both growth characteristics (from increasing dividends and share price) and value characteristics (from a high dividend yield and lower price multiples) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:20">[00:11:20]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:24">[00:11:24]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:27">[00:11:27]</a>. Academic research supports that combining dividend yield with other factors like volatility, quality, or momentum can lead to returns exceeding a market-cap-weighted index <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:34">[00:12:34]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:47">[00:12:47]</a>.

## Importance of Dividend Quality
Not all dividends are created equal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:48">[00:14:48]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:51">[00:14:51]</a>. A healthy dividend payment is sourced from a company's cash flow from operations, indicating a self-sufficient setup <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:56">[00:14:56]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:57">[00:14:57]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:00">[00:15:00]</a>. If a company resorts to borrowing or raising capital to fund dividends, it suggests an unstable financial situation and an unreliable payout <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:05">[00:15:05]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:07">[00:15:07]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:09">[00:15:09]</a>. Investors should distinguish between high-quality and low-quality dividend payers by examining net profits, cash flow from operations, and free cash flow <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:15">[00:15:15]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:17">[00:15:17]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:21">[00:15:21]</a>.

### Avoiding the Dividend Value Trap
A "dividend value trap" occurs when an investor is swayed by a high dividend yield but overlooks a company's stagnating or declining business model <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:28">[00:15:28]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:30">[00:15:30]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:33">[00:15:33]</a>. To avoid this, it's crucial to examine the company's return on capital employed (ROCE). If ROCE consistently exceeds the cost of capital (e.g., above 14-15%), the risk of falling into a dividend value trap is significantly reduced <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:41">[00:15:41]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:44">[00:15:44]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:47">[00:15:47]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:48">[00:15:48]</a>.

Overall, a [[dividend_growth_vs_high_dividend_yield | dividend growth strategy]] is considered more superior to a [[high_dividend_yield_stocks | high dividend yield strategy]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:29">[00:14:29]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:32">[00:14:32]</a>. Combining it with other factors can lead to superior returns, reduced volatility, and better downside protection for an investment portfolio <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:37">[00:14:37]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:39">[00:14:39]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:41">[00:14:41]</a>. Dividends are not merely a source of passive income but can be a powerful stock selection criterion that enhances portfolio returns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:02">[00:16:02]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:05">[00:16:05]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:07">[00:16:07]</a>.

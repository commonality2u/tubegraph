For Yu-Gi-Oh! players interested in acquiring decks not yet released in their region, or those seeking unique card characteristics, importing Japanese structure decks can be an appealing option <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:10">[00:00:10]</a>.

## Availability

While certain structure decks might not be available in the US, such as the [[yugioh_synchron_extreme_japanese_structure_deck_overview | Synchron Extreme]] structure deck, they are released in other markets like Japan and Korea <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:10">[00:00:10]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:11">[00:01:11]</a>.

## Importing through Amazon

One common method for acquiring Japanese Yu-Gi-Oh! decks in regions like America is through online retailers such as Amazon <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:16">[00:07:16]</a>.

*   **Cost**
    *   A Japanese structure deck can cost approximately twenty dollars on Amazon <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:14">[00:07:14]</a>.
*   **Shipping**
    *   Standard shipping from Japan can take longer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:18">[00:07:18]</a>.
    *   If you have Amazon Prime, decks may ship in as little as two days from a US-based facility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:20">[00:07:20]</a>.

## Unique Characteristics of Japanese Decks

[[comparison_of_japanese_and_us_yugioh_decks | Japanese Yu-Gi-Oh! structure decks]] often feature distinct qualities compared to their Western counterparts:

*   **Packaging**
    *   Japanese structure decks typically come in plastic holders rather than the cardboard packaging common for entire decks in other regions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:01">[00:03:01]</a>.
*   **Card Feel**
    *   The physical feel of Japanese cards can be notably different, with some players preferring their texture <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:16">[00:04:16]</a>.
*   **Artwork and Rarity**
    *   The artwork on Japanese cards and packaging is often highly regarded <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:07">[00:01:07]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:34">[00:01:34]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:59">[00:02:59]</a>.
    *   The [[yugioh_synchron_extreme_japanese_structure_deck_overview | Synchron Extreme]] Japanese structure deck, for example, includes specific rarities:
        *   One Ultra Parallel Rare card (e.g., Stardust Warrior) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:58">[00:00:58]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>.
        *   Three Super Parallel Rares <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:02">[00:01:02]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:51">[00:06:51]</a>.
        *   Six Normal Parallel Rares <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:04">[00:01:04]</a>.
        *   Thirty-four Commons <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:06">[00:01:06]</a>.
    *   The "shininess" of the foil cards, particularly the new Synchron cards, is often highlighted <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:47">[00:06:47]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:50">[00:06:50]</a>.

## Contents of Japanese Structure Decks

In addition to the main deck, Japanese structure decks typically include supplementary materials:

*   **Play Mat:** Often features cover artwork of the deck <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:57">[00:01:57]</a>.
*   **Player Manual/Guide:** A manual for beginners <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:25">[00:02:25]</a>.
*   **Promotional Packet:** May contain information on card combos <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:42">[00:02:42]</a>.
*   **Extra Deck Cards:** The [[yugioh_synchron_extreme_japanese_structure_deck_overview | Synchron Extreme]] deck includes four cards for the extra deck <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:54">[00:00:54]</a>.

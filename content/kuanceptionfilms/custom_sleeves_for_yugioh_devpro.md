Custom sleeves are personalized card backs that can be applied to your deck in Yu-Gi-Oh! DevPro, allowing for a unique visual experience during gameplay <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:15">[00:00:15]</a>.

## How to Add Custom Sleeves

Adding custom sleeves to Yu-Gi-Oh! DevPro involves several steps within the game's customization settings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:17">[00:00:17]</a>. This process is part of [[how_to_add_game_customizations_in_yugioh_devpro | adding game customizations]] to DevPro.

1.  **Navigate to Customization Tab**: From the DevPro game list, select the "customize" tab <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:25">[00:00:25]</a>.
2.  **Access Content Settings**: Click on "content" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:30">[00:00:30]</a>.
3.  **Select Player One Covers**: To customize your sleeves, go to "player one covers" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:34">[00:00:34]</a>.
4.  **Prepare Image**: Ensure your desired image is correctly sized to avoid distortion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:48">[00:00:48]</a>.
5.  **Add New Sleeve**: Click the "ADD" button and locate the picture you wish to use for your sleeves <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:56">[00:00:56]</a>.
6.  **Open Image**: After selecting your image, press "open" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:13">[00:01:13]</a>.
7.  **Resize Prompt**: If prompted to resize the image to the correct dimensions, confirm by pressing "yes" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:15">[00:01:15]</a>.
8.  **Install Sleeve**: Once the image is added, click on your newly added sleeve, right-click, and select "install success" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:24">[00:01:24]</a>.
9.  **Preview**: You can preview your customized sleeves by pressing "preview" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:34">[00:01:34]</a>.
10. **Verify In-Duel**: To ensure the sleeves are correctly applied, start an actual duel <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:36">[00:03:36]</a>. If formatted correctly, your custom sleeves will appear in the duel <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:46">[00:03:46]</a>.

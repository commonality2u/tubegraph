## Impact on [[yugioh_card_market_value | Market Value]]
While [[yugioh_card_reprints | reprints]] are popular with players for making powerful or rare cards more accessible, they can negatively affect the [[yugioh_card_market_value | market value]] of existing copies. It has been noted that Konami's frequent [[yugioh_card_reprints | reprints]] sometimes "screw" the market by "killing the [[yugioh_card_market_value | market value]] of the currents" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:34">[00:01:34]</a>.

However, [[yugioh_card_reprints | reprints]] are sometimes seen as necessary, especially for highly sought-after cards like Number 11: Big Eye <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:31">[00:03:31]</a>. The aesthetic quality of the gold foil [[yugioh_card_reprints | reprints]] in [[yugioh_card_reprints_in_premium_gold | Premium Gold]] is also highly praised <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:12">[00:02:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:51">[00:03:51]</a>. Despite the potential drop in [[yugioh_card_market_value | market value]], players express excitement over pulling desired [[yugioh_card_reprints | reprints]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:09">[00:04:09]</a> and the overall quality of the product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:22">[00:05:22]</a>. Players often replace common versions of cards with the new, higher-quality [[yugioh_card_reprints | reprints]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:58">[00:05:58]</a>.

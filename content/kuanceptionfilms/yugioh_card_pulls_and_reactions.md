## Notable Card Pulls and Reactions

### Desired and Valuable Pulls

*   **Dark Magician Girl (OCG Foil)**: Pulled early in the opening, described as "really really nice" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:33">[00:00:33]</a>.
*   **Mass Hero Acid**: This was a highly anticipated card, with the opener stating it was "what I was hoping for" and the reason for purchasing the pack <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:59">[00:00:59]</a>. It was noted to be worth "six bucks" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:08">[00:01:08]</a>. This pull exemplifies [[reactions_to_unexpected_and_rare_card_pulls | Reactions to unexpected and rare card pulls]].
*   **Elder God Noden**: Described as making it "a good day" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:34">[00:02:34]</a>.
*   **Bottomless Trap Hole (Secret Rare)**: This pull elicited extreme excitement, with the opener exclaiming, "Do you know how excited I am right now?" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:11">[00:03:11]</a>. Another Secret Rare "Bottomless Trap Hole" was pulled later, leading to the opener declaring, "I'm done" out of disbelief and excitement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:34">[00:03:34]</a>. This is a clear example of [[reactions_to_unexpected_and_rare_card_pulls | Reactions to unexpected and rare card pulls]].
*   **Foolish Burial**: Another pull that caused immense excitement, described as "beyond words" and causing the opener to "hyperventilate" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:22">[00:03:22]</a>.
*   **Elemental Hero Blaze Man**: Pulled with a positive, relaxed reaction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:46">[00:03:46]</a>.
*   **Prismatic Rare Artifact Xe's Card**: Though unfamiliar with the specific card, the opener was excited by its rarity, hoping to "keep this up" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:56">[00:03:56]</a>.
*   **New Rack**: A new card that received a positive reaction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:12">[00:04:12]</a>.
*   **Cover Card**: The cover card for the pack was pulled, which despite not being as rare as other finds, was still a significant pull for the opener <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:12">[00:05:12]</a>.

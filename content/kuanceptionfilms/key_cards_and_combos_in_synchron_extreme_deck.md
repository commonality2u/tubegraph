The [[yugioh_synchron_extreme_japanese_structure_deck_overview | Synchron Extreme Structure Deck]] is a Yu-Gi-Oh! product that was released in Japanese and Korean territories before its US release <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:12">[00:00:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:14">[00:01:14]</a>. ## Deck Contents Overview
The [[yugioh_synchron_extreme_japanese_structure_deck_overview | Synchron Extreme Structure Deck]] contains 44 cards in total, including four cards for the Extra Deck <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:52">[00:00:52]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:54">[00:00:54]</a>. The rarity breakdown is as follows <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:56">[00:00:56]</a>:
*   **Ultra Parallel Rare**: 1 (the Stardust Warrior card) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:58">[00:00:58]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>
*   **Super Parallel Rares**: 3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:03">[00:01:03]</a>
*   **Normal Parallel Rares**: 6 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:05">[00:01:05]</a>
*   **Commons**: 34 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:07">[00:01:07]</a>

The deck also comes with a playmat featuring the Synchron Extreme cover artwork <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:57">[00:01:57]</a>, an ARC-V player manual guide for beginners <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:25">[00:02:25]</a>, and a promotional packet that provides information on good card combos <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:43">[00:02:43]</a>. The Japanese structure decks are notable for coming in plastic holders rather than typical packaging <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:03">[00:03:03]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:05">[00:03:05]</a>.

## Combos and Synergies
While specific combos aren't detailed in the narration, the deck includes cards designed to work together and support Synchro summons:
*   A promotional packet within the deck explicitly aims to teach players about "good combos with certain cards" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:43">[00:02:43]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:46">[00:02:46]</a>.
*   The new trap card, **Scrap-Iron Statue**, is designed to "complement the scrap iron scarecrow" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:49">[00:04:52]</a>.
*   **Quickdraw Scarecrow** is included to "complement the quick draw synchron," suggesting the possibility of building a "quick draw deck" with these two cards <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:16">[00:05:18]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:23">[00:05:24]</a>.
*   **Genex** cards are noted for their ability to "complement the effects" of other cards in the deck <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:44">[00:05:48]</a>.
*   The new field card is stated to "go great with Synchron decks" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:36">[00:04:40]</a>.

The inclusion of cards like **Plague Spreader Zombie** (great for milling) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:50">[00:05:52]</a>, **Tuning** (which hasn't been reprinted often) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:59">[00:06:01]</a>, and **Doppel Warrior** (also rarely printed) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:49">[00:07:52]</a> are highlighted as valuable inclusions for building and optimizing Synchron strategies.

## Loss of His Mother
[[Dame Dashs views on societal conditioning and education | Dame Dash's]] mother passed away when he was 16 years old <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:23:24">[04:23:24]</a>. This early loss, which he describes as his biggest fear as a young man <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:50:00">[11:50:00]</a>, had several lasting impacts:
*   **Development of Fearlessness and Resilience** The experience of facing his "nightmares early" made him a "beast" and eliminated his fear of death <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:56:00">[11:56:00]</a>. He learned to appreciate what a "real problem" is, defining true problems as those related to health, death, or freedom <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:39:27">[12:39:27]</a>. This perspective enables him to avoid overreacting to minor issues and appreciate good times <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:57:00">[12:57:00]</a>.
*   **Impact on Authority and Self-Expression** His mother taught him "to never ever let someone tell you something that you don't believe without saying how you feel about it" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:01:04">[05:01:04]</a>. *   **Grieving Through Hustle** His immediate response to his mother's death was to "go get money" through selling drugs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:57:00">[14:57:00]</a>. He viewed this as a distraction to cope, believing at the time that money meant happiness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:05:00">[15:05:00]</a>. He later acknowledged this might have been a distraction rather than genuine grieving, as he lacked the resources for therapy at that age <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:37:00">[15:37:00]</a>.

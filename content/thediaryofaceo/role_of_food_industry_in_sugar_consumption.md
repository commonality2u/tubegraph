Dr. Robert Lustig, an endocrinologist, has extensively researched the impact of sugar on global health conditions and emphasizes the food industry's significant role in fueling this crisis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:09">[00:00:09]</a>. He asserts that the issue isn't calories, but the specific type of sugar consumed and the industry's practices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:02">[00:00:02]</a>.

## Sugar's Addictive Nature and Industry Practices

Sugar is highly addictive, and the food industry intentionally adds it to 73% of all items in grocery stores, knowing that it drives increased consumption <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:37">[00:00:37]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:46">[00:00:46]</a>. This deliberate strategy often involves misleading consumers with "healthy" claims that are often the opposite of the truth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:50">[00:00:50]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:59">[00:47:59]</a>.

Dr. Lustig is involved in numerous lawsuits against the food industry for deceptive advertising, misbranding, and mislabeling, noting that 70% of items in American grocery stores are misbranded or mislabeled <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:06">[00:48:06]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:16">[00:48:16]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:22">[00:48:22]</a>. For example, products might claim "no added sugar" but contain apple puree, raspberry puree, or evaporated cane juice, which are all forms of sugar, or use one of the 262 other names for sugar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:39">[00:48:39]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:48">[00:48:48]</a>. An illustration of this deception is the case of Kellogg's Raisin Bran, where extra sugar was added to the raisins to make them sweeter, beyond the natural sugar content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:02">[00:49:02]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:46">[00:49:46]</a>.

## Historical Deception Regarding Sugar

The food industry has actively suppressed information about sugar's dangers for decades.
Documents from the UCSF Food Industry Documents Library reveal that:
*   In 1965, the sugar industry paid two Harvard School of Public Health scientists $50,000 (in today's money) to produce articles for the *New England Journal* that exonerated sugar and blamed saturated fat <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:26:01">[02:26:01]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:31:00">[02:31:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:39:31">[02:39:31]</a>.
*   The industry also infiltrated the National Institute of Dental Research (NIDR) to divert funds from nutrition research for dental health towards developing a "caries vaccine" instead <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:40:04">[02:40:04]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:41:07">[02:41:07]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:42:04">[02:42:04]</a>.

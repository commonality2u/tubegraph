### Your Home: Asset or Liability?
Many people mistakenly believe their home is an asset that builds "generational wealth" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:08">[00:18:08]</a>. However, a personal home is typically a [[building_wealth_from_scratch | money pit]] and a liability because it takes money out of your pocket through property taxes, insurance, maintenance, and mortgage payments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:19">[00:18:19]</a>. While it's not wrong to own a house, it should be treated as a liability, just like a suit or a watch <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:32">[00:18:32]</a>.

Mortgage payments primarily benefit the bank, especially in the first 15 years of a 30-year mortgage, where the majority of payments go towards interest <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:51">[00:22:51]</a>. This means money that could be saved or invested in income-generating assets is instead tied up in a depreciating liability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:55">[00:23:55]</a>.

#### Opportunity Cost
[[the_psychology_of_money_and_building_wealth | Opportunity cost]] is the value of the next best alternative that was not taken <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:10">[00:24:10]</a>. If you put a $20,000 down payment on a house, you lose the opportunity to invest that money in a rental property, the stock market, or a business, which could potentially offer greater growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:47">[00:24:47]</a>.

It's a myth that you can't [[financial_security_and_wealth_creation | build wealth if you rent]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:39">[00:27:39]</a>. Renting allows liquidity that can be used to invest in assets. Whether you're ready to buy a house depends on if you can genuinely afford it:
1.  **Down Payment:** Have at least a 20% down payment to build equity and afford the house <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:42">[00:28:42]</a>.
2.  **Monthly Payment:** Follow a personal finance system like the **75/15/10 plan** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:45">[00:29:45]</a>:
    *   **75%** of income is the maximum you can spend.
    *   **15%** of income is the minimum you invest.
    *   **10%** of income is the minimum you save.
3.  **Moving Costs:** Factor in expenses beyond closing costs, such as movers, new furniture, or house upgrades <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:13">[00:28:13]</a>.

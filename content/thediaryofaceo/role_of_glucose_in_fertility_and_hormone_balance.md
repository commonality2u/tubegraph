Glucose levels have a significant impact on hormone balance and fertility in both men and women. ## Glucose and Infertility

High [[effects of glucose spikes on health | glucose spikes]] and subsequent insulin responses are strongly linked to infertility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:36">[00:25:36]</a>. Insulin's role is to store excess glucose in the liver, muscles, and fat cells <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:29">[00:11:29]</a>. When there's a lot of insulin in a female body, it can trigger the ovaries to produce more testosterone, the male sex hormone <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:04">[00:26:04]</a>. Excess testosterone can lead to problems like acne, balding or hair growth on the face, and disruption of the menstrual cycle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:17">[00:26:17]</a>.

### Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS)
[[PCOS]] is one of the leading causes of infertility in women <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:35">[00:00:35]</a>. Approximately 60% of [[PCOS]] cases are associated with insulin resistance, a condition resulting from consistently high insulin levels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:51">[00:25:51]</a>. While some believe [[PCOS]] is genetic, others argue it is primarily a metabolic disease stemming from high insulin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:26">[00:27:26]</a>. Addressing [[glucose]] and insulin levels can often alleviate [[PCOS]] symptoms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:10">[00:27:10]</a>.

Many women with [[PCOS]] are prescribed the [[role of hormone replacement therapy | birth control pill]], which introduces female hormones to balance high testosterone <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:24">[00:26:24]</a>. However, this only manages symptoms without resolving the underlying insulin issue, meaning high testosterone often returns when the pill is stopped <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:50">[00:26:50]</a>.

## Glucose and Menopause
For women going through menopause, the body's glucose response to the same foods tends to be higher, leading to naturally increased [[glucose spikes and health implications | glucose spikes]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:23">[01:19:23]</a>. Reducing [[sugar]] intake and managing [[glucose spikes and health implications | glucose spikes]] during menopause can provide relief from symptoms such as insomnia and hot flashes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:44">[01:19:44]</a>.

## Strategies for Hormonal Balance and Fertility
Implementing specific [[hacks for managing glucose levels | hacks]] can help stabilize [[glucose]] levels, improve hormone balance, and support fertility:

*   **Savory Breakfast:** Starting the day with a savory breakfast, rich in protein and healthy fats, helps maintain steady [[glucose]] levels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:37">[00:29:37]</a>. This prevents a morning [[glucose]] roller coaster that can lead to brain fog, fatigue, increased cravings, and deregulation of [[glucose]] levels throughout the day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:50">[00:29:50]</a>. Even when [[fasting and its impact on hormones | fasting]], the first meal should be savory <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:31">[00:34:31]</a>.
*   **Vinegar Before Meals:** Consuming one tablespoon of vinegar diluted in a large glass of water before the biggest meal of the day can reduce [[glucose spikes and health implications | glucose spikes]] by up to 30% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:36">[00:47:36]</a>. Acetic acid in vinegar temporarily inactivates digestive enzymes that break down starches and sugars, slowing down glucose absorption <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:08">[01:05:08]</a>. Vinegar also impacts visceral fat, cholesterol, and insulin levels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:35">[01:03:35]</a>.
*   **Veggie Starters:** Eating vegetables first at a meal provides fiber, which creates a protective barrier in the intestine <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:11">[00:49:11]</a>. This barrier slows down the absorption of subsequent [[glucose]] from other foods, leading to a smaller [[glucose spikes and health implications | glucose spike]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:20">[00:49:20]</a>.
*   **Moving After Eating:** Engaging in physical activity for 10 minutes after a meal helps muscles absorb [[glucose]] from the bloodstream for energy, reducing the [[glucose spike and health implications | glucose spike]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:05">[00:50:05]</a>. This includes activities like walking, dancing, or even calf raises <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:09">[00:50:09]</a>.

A 2019 pilot experiment with 2,700 participants from 110 countries showed that applying these four [[hacks for managing glucose levels | hacks]] (savory breakfast, vinegar, veggie starters, and moving after eating) for four weeks significantly reduced cravings, increased energy, and had positive impacts on sleep, [[hormones]], skin, mood, and diabetes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:07">[00:52:07]</a>. Roughly 40% of participants who wanted to lose weight did so without calorie restriction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:02">[00:54:02]</a>.

Other useful [[dietary hacks to manage glucose levels | hacks]] include:
*   **Eating food in the right order:** Prioritize vegetables, then proteins and fats, followed by starches and [[sugar]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:06">[00:55:06]</a>. This can reduce the [[glucose spikes and health implications | glucose spike]] of a meal by up to 75% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:16">[00:55:16]</a>.
*   **"Clothes on carbs":** Never eat starches or [[sugar]] on their own. Pair them with fats, protein, or fiber to slow down [[glucose]] absorption <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:02">[00:56:02]</a>.
*   **Savory snacks:** Choose savory snacks over sweet ones between meals to avoid [[glucose spikes and health implications | glucose spikes]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:44">[00:56:44]</a>.
*   **Sweet foods as dessert:** If consuming something sweet, have it as a dessert after a full meal rather than on an empty stomach to minimize the [[glucose spikes and health implications | glucose spike]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:57">[00:57:02]</a>.
*   **Eat fruit whole:** Always consume fruit whole, never juiced, dried, or blended into smoothies, to retain its protective fiber <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:21">[00:57:21]</a>.

By understanding and managing [[glucose]] levels, individuals can significantly improve their hormonal health and fertility outcomes.

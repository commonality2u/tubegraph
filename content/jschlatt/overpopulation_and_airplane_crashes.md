## Humidity and Sensory Experience in Airplanes

An intriguing fact discussed is the extremely low humidity levels in aircraft cabins which can be drier than a desert. This arid environment impacts passengers' sense of taste, particularly affecting the perception of airplane food. The dry air desensitizes the nose, making meals taste bland or mediocre at best, largely because the food is often prepared days in advance and frozen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:12">[00:01:12]</a>.

The Akidna Strategy is a proposed [[australias_selfreliant_defense_strategies | self-reliant defense strategy]] for Australia, developed by Sam Roggeveen, Director of the Lowy Institute's International Security Program and author of "The Akidna Strategy" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:52:00">[01:52:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:03:00">[02:03:00]</a>. This strategy argues that Australia can defend itself without significantly increasing defense spending as a percentage of GDP <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:32:00">[01:32:00]</a>. The core idea is for Australia to become "spiky but not threatening" and to exploit its geographic distance from potential adversaries like China <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:40:00">[01:40:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:45:00">[01:45:00]</a>.

## Geopolitical Context and US Role

The Akidna Strategy is posited against a backdrop of shifting global power dynamics. Historically, the United States has not faced an adversary whose GDP exceeded 60% of its own <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:00">[00:13:00]</a>. China, however, surpassed this threshold in 2014 and, on a purchasing power parity basis, exceeded US GDP entirely by 2017, now being more than 20% larger <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:00">[00:32:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:00">[00:36:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:00">[00:43:00]</a>. This economic power is being converted into military might <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:00">[00:47:00]</a>.

The strategy assumes that the United States will not have the resolve to fight China for dominance in Asia due to a lack of vital interests in the region <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:00">[00:53:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:00">[01:00:00]</a>. This implies Australia will be without the protection of a great power for the first time in its history <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:00">[01:07:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:00">[01:12:00]</a>.

Roggeveen differentiates his view from Hugh White's, who suggests America will leave Asia entirely <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:00">[01:12:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:00">[01:14:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:00">[01:16:00]</a>. Instead, Roggeveen predicts a "long interregnum" where America enters a power-sharing relationship with China <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:00">[01:16:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:00">[01:18:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:46:00">[01:46:00]</a>. This means the US presence in Asia would stay roughly where it is, leading to a relative decline as China's military capabilities expand <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:54:00">[01:54:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:59:00">[01:59:00]</a>. This "middle scenario" is expected to last a long time before a full US withdrawal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:50:00">[01:50:00]</a>. Even in a power-sharing capacity, an American presence preoccupies China, forcing it to devote more resources to its relationship with the US, which could otherwise be directed towards Australia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:04:00">[02:04:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:21:00">[02:21:00]</a>.

The Akidna Strategy emphasizes [[australias_selfreliant_defense_strategies | self-reliance]], meaning its defensive policy remains consistent regardless of whether the US leaves Asia entirely or adopts a power-sharing role <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:26:00">[02:26:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:30:00">[02:30:00]</a>.

## Operational Aspects

The Akidna Strategy focuses on defending Australia's northern maritime approaches <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:24:00">[02:24:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:27:00">[02:27:00]</a>. Its operational effectiveness relies on the concept of "defensive dominance," where the costs of defensive military technologies are significantly lower than offensive ones <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:00:00">[03:00:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:03:00">[03:03:00]</a>.

The strategy proposes a force structure primarily aimed at denying an adversary access to Australia's territory by sinking ships and shooting down planes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:48:00">[03:48:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:50:00">[03:50:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:50:00">[03:50:00]</a>. This "not particularly complicated" and "not very expensive" mission is well within Australia's capability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:28:00">[03:28:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:29:00">[03:29:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:32:00">[03:32:00]</a>. Key components include:
*   **Submarines**: While the exact number isn't specified, at least six (the current Collins class fleet) and likely up to 8 or 12 submarines would be beneficial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:33:00">[03:33:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:41:00">[03:41:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:48:00">[03:48:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:50:00">[03:50:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:51:00">[03:51:00]</a>. Submarines are highly effective wartime weapons for sinking ships and other submarines <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:53:00">[03:53:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:56:00">[03:56:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:57:00">[03:57:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:00:00">[04:00:00]</a>.
*   **Maritime patrol aircraft**: A significant number would be needed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:51:00">[04:51:00]</a>.
*   **Fast jets**: These would complement other capabilities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:56:00">[04:56:00]</a>.
*   **Land-based anti-ship weapons**: Australia is beginning to acquire these <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:00:00">[04:00:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:05:00">[04:05:00]</a>.

The strategy leverages Australia's geographic distance, which is considered its "single biggest defense asset" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:57:00">[04:57:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:00:00">[05:00:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:02:00">[05:02:00]</a>. Projecting significant military power against Australia is either impossible or extremely expensive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:05:00">[03:05:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:09:00">[03:09:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:13:00">[03:13:00]</a>.

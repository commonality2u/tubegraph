## Fundamental Differences in Market Environment

In the United States, the private sector operates with the assumption that many [[public_goods_in_the_us_versus_india | public goods]] are already in place <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>. These include essentials like clean water, sidewalks, basic law enforcement, and efforts to mitigate air pollution, along with a general sense of safety <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:07">[00:00:07]</a>. With these foundational elements taken for granted, the private sector, particularly in the digital space, focuses on supplying other services and products <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:23">[00:00:23]</a>.

Conversely, in India, the situation is "flipped" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:32">[00:00:32]</a>. The private sector is still required to build and design numerous hardware solutions that are simply assumed to exist in the United States <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:36">[00:00:36]</a>.

## Venture Capital Investment Patterns

### US Venture Capital Focus

[[Venture Capital and risk management | Venture Capital firms]] (VCs) in the US, and those globally mimicking their approach, tend to focus heavily on software solutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:57">[00:00:57]</a>. This preference stems from the perception that software development carries virtually no product risk <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>. Software solutions can be created quickly by a few developers, shifting the primary concern for VCs to market risk <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:04">[00:01:04]</a>. This highlights the [[Venture Capital focus on software versus hardware | Venture Capital focus on software versus hardware]].

### Indian Venture Capital Landscape

There's a significant [[impact_of_misconceptions_on_investments_in_indian_tech_sector | misunderstanding]] among VCs, both Western and Indian, regarding the Indian market <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:47">[00:00:47]</a>. Many VCs are "not picking enough hardware solution space" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:54">[00:00:54]</a>.

When it comes to hardware, such as devices for air pollution mitigation, VCs incorrectly perceive both significant product risk and market risk <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:14">[00:01:14]</a>. In reality, there is "very little market risk" for such products, as evidenced by numerous ads for air pollution devices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:23">[00:01:23]</a>. The actual challenge lies in the "quite high" product risk, which VCs struggle to understand due to their focus on digital goods rather than physical manufacturing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:31">[00:01:31]</a>.

This misjudgment represents a significant missed opportunity, akin to "$20 bills lying on the table" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:49">[00:01:49]</a>. While American VCs are noted for this oversight, Indian VCs often mimic their American counterparts, also failing to adequately fund hardware solutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:51">[00:01:51]</a>. This contributes to the [[overestimation_of_market_size_in_indian_startups | overestimation of market size in Indian startups]] for software, while neglecting the fundamental needs in hardware.

### The Role of Angel Investors in India

The primary funders of these hardware solutions in India are angel investors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:57">[00:01:57]</a>. Angels often possess a deeper understanding, having built such products before <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:02">[00:02:02]</a>. They are adept at pooling money, de-risking product development, and strategically planning for hardware ventures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:05">[00:02:05]</a>. In contrast, VCs predominantly focus on product-to-market fit, a concept heavily influenced by the US VC model <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:15">[00:02:15]</a>. While successful in the US, this approach may not perfectly translate to India, where a sole focus on market risk and avoidance of product risk overlooks crucial opportunities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:25">[00:02:25]</a>. This impacts the [[analysis_of_the_true_addressable_market_for_western_vcs_in_india | true addressable market for Western VCs in India]].

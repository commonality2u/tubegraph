A 2022 poll by the Lowy Institute indicated that approximately 36% of Australians either support or strongly support the country [[australian_public_opinion_on_nuclear_weapons_acquisition | acquiring nuclear weapons]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>.

## Historical Context and Shifting Views

This finding was surprising to some, especially considering the prominent [[australias_historical_antinuclear_stance | anti-nuclear campaigns]] in [[Australias historical antinuclear stance | Australia]] during the 1980s <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:14">[00:00:14]</a>. Upon the announcement of AUKUS, an expected ramp-up of anti-nuclear sentiment did not materialize <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:17">[00:00:17]</a>. There has been no significant popular reaction to the idea of [[Australia and nuclear power | Australia birthing nuclear reactors]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:26">[00:00:26]</a>. This has led to the observation that Australians appear more relaxed about [[Australia and nuclear power | nuclear power]] than previously assumed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:32">[00:00:32]</a>.

## Interpretation of Poll Results

Poll questions, such as those related to [[nuclear weapons poll in Australia | nuclear weapons]] acquisition, are generally presented without counterfactuals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:41">[00:00:41]</a>. For instance, respondents are typically not asked if they desire [[nuclear weapons poll in Australia | nuclear weapons]] if it involves specific costs or trade-offs with other priorities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:46">[00:00:46]</a>.

This stems from a fundamental misunderstanding of [[indias_unique_digital_revolution_compared_to_the_developed_world | India's unique digital revolution]] and the actual purchasing power of its digitally connected population <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:03">[00:00:03]</a>.

## India's Unique Digital Revolution

Unlike the developed world, where digital revolutions occurred after high GDP per capita allowed widespread phone ownership and infrastructure development, India's digital transformation has been "upside down" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:06">[00:00:06]</a>. In parts of India, such as Varanasi and the poorest areas of Uttar Pradesh and Bihar, excellent internet access exists even where basic amenities like water might be lacking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:24">[00:00:24]</a>.

## Misleading Market Statistics

Commonly cited figures suggest India has 860 million people connected to the digital space via smartphones, with at least one per household <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:35">[00:00:35]</a>. This leads to projections, such as an edtech company potentially having a market size of 300 million users <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:50">[00:00:50]</a>. However, this is considered a significant [[overestimation_of_the_indian_market_size_by_western_vcs | over estimate]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:57">[00:00:57]</a>.

The reality is that only about 25 to 30 million people in India possess the same level of [[differences_in_disposable_income_and_digital_consumption_in_india | disposable income]] comparable to internet users in the US <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:02">[00:01:02]</a>. These are the individuals who can afford services like Netflix or Amazon subscriptions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:12">[00:01:12]</a>.

## The "Indus Valley Report" Perspective

Sajit Pai's "Indus Valley Report" (a play on Silicon Valley) offers a more realistic assessment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:17">[00:01:17]</a>. He estimates that the actual size of India's *spending* market is comparable to Taiwan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:28">[00:01:28]</a>. The group actively spending on the internet is approximately 200 million, potentially even less <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:33">[00:01:33]</a>. The remaining "next billion" people do not spend significant money on digital services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:42">[00:01:42]</a>.

## Western Venture Capitalist (VC) Misconceptions

[[angel_investors_versus_venture_capitalists_in_india | Venture capitalists]] often seek to replicate successful Western business models, such as [[challenges_in_replicating_western_business_models_in_india | DoorDash and...Uber for India]], assuming a direct translation of market potential <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:48">[00:01:48]</a>. They incorrectly equate California's market of millions with India's 860 million smartphone users, failing to account for vast differences in purchasing power <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:55">[00:01:55]</a>. Consequently, foreign investors are often investing in an "India Taiwan" which is significantly smaller and poorer than California <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:09">[00:02:09]</a>.

## Superhuman's AI Features
Superhuman's integration of [[applications_of_ai_in_email_management_and_debugging | AI]] has evolved through distinct phases:

### Phase One: On-Demand AI Features
The initial approach focused on "on-demand" [[applications_of_ai_in_email_management_and_debugging | AI]] features that users actively invoked <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:26">[00:06:26]</a>.
*   **Write with AI**: This feature allows users to jot down a few words, which are then transformed into a fully written email that matches the user's voice and tone from previously sent emails <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:21">[00:04:21]</a>. It can also shorten, lengthen, improve writing, fix mistakes, and change language <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:32">[00:04:32]</a>.
    *   This feature was launched in June or July of the previous year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:39">[00:04:39]</a> and led to significant virality for Superhuman <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:24">[00:15:24]</a>.
This phase served as a way to understand the technology, assess its quality, and gauge user adoption <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:43">[00:06:43]</a>.

### Phase Two: Always-On AI Features
Following the success of on-demand features, Superhuman transitioned to more ambitious, "always-on" [[applications_of_ai_in_email_management_and_debugging | AI]] capabilities that continuously work for the user <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:56">[00:06:56]</a>.
*   **Auto Summarize**: Launched in October <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:47">[00:04:47]</a>, this feature provides a one-line summary above email conversations, which updates instantly as new emails arrive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:00">[00:05:00]</a>. Users can also access a bullet-point summary <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:11">[00:05:11]</a>. This often eliminates the need to read the full email, enabling faster processing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:21">[00:05:21]</a>.
*   **Instant Reply**: Released recently, this feature drafts replies for every incoming email, allowing users to simply edit and send, or sometimes send without any edits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:30">[00:05:30]</a>. Users of Instant Reply are writing emails twice as fast, effectively cutting reply time in half <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:57">[00:05:57]</a>. The product design of Instant Reply was carefully considered, opting for always-visible short, snappy replies to reduce user activation energy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:12">[00:18:12]</a>. Key to its usability is the seamless "Tab and Enter" interaction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:17">[00:22:17]</a>.

These features are more complex and expensive to operate due to their continuous nature and the massive scale at which they process emails (e.g., 4 billion emails processed since launch for Auto Summarize and Instant Reply) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:05">[00:07:05]</a>.

*   These [[ai_agent_capabilities and_limitations | agents]] will be largely autonomous, capable of handling ambiguity by asking questions and interacting with other systems or even other [[future_of_ai_agents_in_software_development | agents]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:08">[00:08:08]</a>.
*   The goal is for [[ai_agent_capabilities_and_limitations | AI agents]] to handle routine tasks, freeing up humans for more creative, strategic, and impactful work <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:32">[00:08:32]</a>.

## Behind the Scenes: AI Development
Superhuman's rapid [[applications_of_ai_in_email_management_and_debugging | AI]] development was achieved through a specific operating model:
*   **Prioritization**: The company made an existential decision to prioritize [[the_future_and_current_state_of_ai_agents | AI]], operating under a "Theta mode" where an executive (the CEO) is embedded directly in the project team, ensuring rapid progress and existential commitment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:00">[00:14:00]</a>.
*   **Prompt Engineering**: Superhuman relies heavily on advanced prompt engineering and multi-shot learning (using past emails to match voice) rather than fine-tuning models, which avoids time-consuming data labeling and training <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:06">[00:25:06]</a>. Their prompt for Instant Reply is notably complex and large <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:22">[00:23:22]</a>.
*   **Testing and Iteration**: Due to the unpredictable nature of LLM updates, they built a robust regression testing and evaluation framework using Brain Trust to ensure features like Instant Reply function correctly across models and prevent issues like irrelevant replies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:41">[00:26:41]</a>.
*   **Model Selection**: Superhuman primarily uses OpenAI models (GPT-3.5 Turbo for Instant Reply) due to their perceived 6-9 month lead in model quality, commitment to aggressive cost reduction, and strong collaboration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:28">[00:29:28]</a>. Throughput and latency are more critical than raw model intelligence for bursty, real-time features like Instant Reply <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:44">[00:31:44]</a>.

## Teaching New Workflows
A core challenge for [[the_future_and_current_state_of_ai_agents | AI]] companies is teaching users new workflows <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:36">[00:34:36]</a>. Superhuman's strategy is to:
*   Start with "always-on" features (like Auto Summarize and Instant Reply) which have 100% reach <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:06">[00:35:06]</a>.
*   Connect these to "on-demand" features (like Write with AI) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:09">[00:36:09]</a>. For example, after an Instant Reply is inserted, buttons like "lengthen" or "custom" could lead to the full Write with AI prompt, subtly educating users about the on-demand feature <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:26">[00:36:26]</a>.
This approach leverages the inherent utility of always-on features to naturally introduce users to more powerful, but less immediately obvious, capabilities.

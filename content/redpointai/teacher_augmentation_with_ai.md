## Khan Academy's KIGO: An AI-Powered Assistant

Khan Academy, an influential education platform serving over 150 million learners in 190 countries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:00">[00:27:00]</a>, has developed KI Migo, an [[ai_in_education | AI-powered tutoring assistant]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:00">[00:36:00]</a>. KIGO functions as both a tutor and a teaching assistant, with safeguards like teacher oversight, anti-cheating measures, and privacy controls <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:30:00">[06:30:00]</a>. It is designed to be highly Socratic and pedagogically sound <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:44:00">[06:44:00]</a>.

KIGO has been deployed to over 1.4 million students and teachers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:00">[00:40:00]</a>, with a rapid adoption rate that exceeded initial projections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:55:00">[06:55:00]</a>. Districts pay approximately $15 per student per year for the service <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:17:00">[07:17:00]</a>.

### Proactive AI for Enhanced Engagement

Initially, KIGO functioned reactively, available for students to ask questions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:42:00">[07:42:00]</a>. However, only a small percentage of students (10-15%) proactively engage with such tools <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:47:00">[07:47:00]</a>. Khan Academy is moving towards a more proactive AI approach, called "Khan Academy Classroom" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:54:00">[07:54:00]</a>.

This proactive [[ai_in_education | AI]] will:
*   Welcome students back and highlight teacher assignments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:03:00">[08:03:00]</a>.
*   Act as a concierge for teachers, front and center in the platform <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:10:00">[08:10:00]</a>.
*   Suggest actions to users through dynamic bubbles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:47:00">[08:47:00]</a>.

The ultimate goal is for the AI to identify student struggles (e.g., with the distributive property) and then inform the teacher, who can then assign an AI-tutoring session for the student to complete <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:00:00">[08:00:00]</a>. This human-AI accountability loop is crucial for driving student engagement and learning <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:17:00">[10:17:00]</a>.

### Practical Applications for Teachers

Teachers are leveraging [[ai_in_education | AI]] in several ways:
*   **Lesson Planning:** AI assists in tweaking lesson plans, making them more engaging and appropriately sized for the classroom <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:18:00">[14:18:00]</a>.
*   **Content Generation:** KIGO can generate question sets for in-class gaming platforms like Blooket in minutes, a task that previously took teachers half an hour to an hour <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:34:00">[14:34:00]</a>.
*   **Insights and Data:** Teachers gain insights from AI-generated data, informing future planning <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:56:00">[14:56:00]</a>.
*   **Interactive Simulations:** Teachers use [[ai_in_education | AI simulations]] of historical or literary figures (e.g., Harriet Tubman, Jay Gatsby) to open classes and encourage students to ask challenging questions, fostering engagement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:41:00">[11:41:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:25:00">[15:25:00]</a>.
*   **Writing Coach:** A "writing coach" tool addresses cheating concerns by allowing teachers to create assignments with AI, have students work with the AI as an ethical coach, and then submit the process history (not just the final output) to the teacher. This discourages AI cheating and other forms of academic dishonesty <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:39:00">[15:39:00]</a>.

### Addressing Challenges in AI Deployment

Building effective [[ai_in_education | AI]] tools for classrooms requires significant development beyond just prompting large language models <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:32:00">[21:32:00]</a>. Key challenges include:
*   **Safety and Moderation:** Implementing robust safety and moderation protocols, including overcoming false positives in moderation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:48:00">[21:48:00]</a>.
*   **Accuracy:** Ensuring high accuracy, especially in math, where error rates need to be driven to near zero. Khan Academy's KIGO, anchored to its content, currently has an approximate 2% error rate, split between math errors and evaluation errors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:02:00">[13:02:00]</a>.
*   **User Interface:** Designing intuitive and natural user interfaces <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:35:00">[22:35:00]</a>.
*   **Integration:** Seamlessly integrating AI into existing educational workflows and platforms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:52:00">[22:52:00]</a>.

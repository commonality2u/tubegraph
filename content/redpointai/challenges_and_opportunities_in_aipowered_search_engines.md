### Behind the Scenes: Delivering Answers

Building Perplexity involves a complex process for each query:
*   **Query Understanding and Reformulation** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:20">[00:02:20]</a>
*   **Page Selection and Content Extraction** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:24">[00:02:24]</a>
*   **Answer Rendering**: Determining the best format (summary, bullets, visuals like images or videos) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:33">[00:02:33]</a>.
*   **Citation and Hallucination Minimization**: Ensuring each sentence has supporting citations and reducing factual errors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:41">[00:02:41]</a>.
*   **Shareability**: Innovating with permalinks so answers can be easily shared and benefit others <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:09">[00:03:09]</a>. This feature was later adopted by competitors like Bing and ChatGPT <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:25">[00:03:25]</a>.
*   **Follow-up Questions and Co-pilot**: Suggesting further questions helps users, as articulating precise questions is a common human limitation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:33">[00:03:33]</a>. The "Co-pilot" feature aims to improve user query formulation, reflecting the philosophy that "the user is never wrong" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:25">[00:04:25]</a>.

Resource allocation for these development areas is a continuous discussion among design, product, and AI leads, emphasizing vertical integration and shared values like quality, truth, and velocity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:01">[00:06:01]</a>.

## Model Strategy and Moats

Perplexity's journey in model development serves as a case study for other AI companies.
*   **Starting with Third-Party Models**: It is advisable for product-focused companies to begin with existing models (e.g., OpenAI models) to achieve product-market fit and attract users <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:55">[00:07:55]</a>.
*   **Building Internal Capabilities**: Over time, as usage sustains, companies can fine-tune smaller, faster models and eventually transition to open-source or proprietary models to control destiny and drive down costs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:08">[00:08:08]</a>. This transition should align with market shifts, like the arrival of open-source waves (e.g., Llama 2) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:49">[00:10:49]</a>.
*   **Addressing the "Rapper" Criticism**: Companies starting with external models are often labeled "wrappers." Perplexity embraces this initial approach, prioritizing user acquisition over immediate model building. "I would rather be a rapper with 100,000 users than having some model inside and like nobody even knows who I am" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:42">[00:12:42]</a>.
*   **The Concept of a Moat**: A moat only matters once a product has achieved significant user adoption <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:26">[00:12:26]</a>. User value is paramount; users don't care about a company's underlying models or profitability, only about getting the best answer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:06">[00:14:06]</a>.
*   **Model Agnosticism**: Perplexity aims to be model-agnostic, ready to integrate the best available model, whether proprietary or open-source, to provide optimal answers to users <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:55">[00:13:55]</a>.
*   **Talent Acquisition**: Recruiting top-tier engineers and AI researchers is often harder than securing funding in the current generative AI landscape <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:59">[00:08:59]</a>.

### [[Challenges and Strategies in Enterprise AI Deployment|Challenges in using AI for research advancements]] and Hallucinations

Perplexity has been effective in using Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) to reduce hallucinations in web search <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:45">[00:27:45]</a>. ### Origin Story and Viral Growth

Perplexity's initial ideas included a vision-based search (glasses, microphone, audio answers) and search over databases (text-to-SQL for enterprises) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:13">[00:39:13]</a>. The text-to-SQL idea was abandoned because existing visual tools (e.g., Tableau, Power BI) were more efficient for enterprise users <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:15">[00:41:15]</a>.

The pivotal moment came from an internal Slack/Discord bot that provided summarized answers with links <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:01">[00:45:01]</a>. This tool, originally for internal team use for quick answers on coding or health insurance, proved highly useful <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:12">[00:45:12]</a>. When launched to the public as a consumer-facing search, it gained viral traction, notably amplified by Jack Dorsey's tweet, which led to a surge in users searching their own Twitter handles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:16">[00:47:16]</a>. The product's ability to pull holistic summaries of individuals across platforms "spooked people out" and generated significant screenshot virality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:18">[00:48:18]</a>. This demonstrated real user excitement and confirmed the viability of a consumer-facing search product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:48">[00:48:48]</a>.

## AI Regulation and the Broader Ecosystem

AI regulation is currently "too premature," as the widespread economic benefits have not yet been fully realized <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:30">[00:53:30]</a>. Regulating too early risks stifling innovation and centralizing power in the hands of a few large organizations, which could be more dangerous than allowing widespread development and public scrutiny <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:30">[00:54:30]</a>.

Looking beyond search, areas like AI-generated movies, realistic voices, and tools that can preserve memories (e.g., recording parents' voices and personalities to interact with later) are exciting applications of generative AI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:49">[00:55:49]</a>. The reduction in marginal cost of creation through these tools will foster a future of amazing visualizations and explanations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:57">[00:56:57]</a>.

The recent events at OpenAI suggest internal disagreements between those advocating for faster development and those for more cautious approaches <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:33">[00:57:33]</a>. While OpenAI remains a cutting-edge organization, it may not be able to pursue every possible AI application simultaneously, potentially leading to a more focused strategy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:04">[00:58:04]</a>.

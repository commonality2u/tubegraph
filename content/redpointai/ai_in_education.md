## Challenges and Solutions in AI Development

Developing [[AI and education technology | AI for education]] involves significant challenges beyond just integrating models:
*   **Safety and Moderation** Essential for a student audience, requiring careful implementation and tuning to avoid false positives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:48">[00:21:48]</a>.
*   **Math Accuracy** Achieving low error rates in math is particularly challenging <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:07">[00:22:07]</a>. Khanmigo, when anchored on Khan Academy content, has an error rate of about 2%, split between straight math errors and evaluation errors (e.g., accepting 0.33 instead of 1/3) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:02">[00:13:02]</a>. This is already considered better than many human tutors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:45">[00:13:45]</a>.
*   **User Interface and Experience** Making the [[AI in education and human interaction | AI]] feel natural and integrating it seamlessly into the learning experience, rather than just being a chatbot, requires significant re-engineering <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:35">[00:22:35]</a>.
*   **Memory and Context** Future advancements in [[AI models and educational engagement | AI]] memory will allow models to retain context from previous interactions, providing more personalized and effective support <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:58">[00:23:58]</a>.
*   **Multimodal Capabilities** The ability for [[AI in education and human interaction | AI]] to "see" and understand student work (e.g., written notes on a tablet) and provide real-time feedback is a significant upcoming capability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:33">[00:25:33]</a>.
*   **Robust Evaluation** Khan Academy uses a framework including hundreds of "tough test cases" where models historically struggled, machine labeling, and human labeling of conversations to assess model performance and identify areas for improvement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:12">[00:29:12]</a>.

## The AI Landscape: Models, Infrastructure, and Applications

The [[AI applications in sports analytics | AI landscape]] can be conceptualized in three layers:

1.  **Model Layer**: This layer includes foundational models like Large Language Models (LLMs) that serve as the "brains" for [[AI applications in sports analytics | AI capabilities]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:10">[00:06:10]</a>. The value of these model companies lies in the products that can be built on top of them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:52">[00:06:52]</a>.
    *   **Developments**:
        *   Companies like OpenAI are moving up the stack by shipping products like deep research and enterprise [[salesforce_ai_and_its_applications | AI agents]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:58">[00:06:58]</a>.
        *   [[Developing and utilizing AI models in the tech industry | Building a cutting-edge foundation model]] allows for the creation of unique products requiring state-of-the-art models <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:03">[00:07:03]</a>.
        *   The cost of entering the state-of-the-art LLM game is prohibitively expensive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:22">[00:07:22]</a>.
        *   Adjacent model categories beyond LLMs, such as those for robotics, biology, and material sciences, are of significant interest <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:29">[00:07:29]</a>.
    *   **Commoditization of Models**: The DeepSeek announcement highlighted that models are becoming cheaper, with costs for inference and training dropping about 10x per year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:13">[00:08:13]</a>. This is beneficial for [[AI product market fit and emerging applications | application companies]] as it improves margin structures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:27">[00:08:27]</a>. Scale is not an enduring moat for model companies; they will need to build moats through distribution (like OpenAI moving into apps and agents) or specialization (like robotics) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:36">[00:08:36]</a>. Switching costs between models are very low <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:36">[00:09:36]</a>.

2.  **Infrastructure Layer**: This layer consists of the "picks and shovels" that bridge the gap between models and application vendors, providing tools for developers to build [[AI applications in sports analytics | AI applications]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:19">[00:06:19]</a>.
    *   **Challenges**: The rapid pace of change in the model layer means building patterns for developers also change quickly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:59">[00:10:59]</a>. Early [[AI applications in sports analytics | AI]] adoption focused on "use case discovery mode" using powerful, branded models like OpenAI and Anthropic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:11">[00:11:11]</a>.
    *   **Future**: This year (2024) is seen as promising for infrastructure, with the emergence of agents expected to create common patterns for web access and tool usage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:37">[00:11:37]</a>.

3.  **Application Tier**: This tier comprises companies building horizontal and vertical Software as a Service (SaaS) solutions that leverage [[AI capabilities]] to replace services with software <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:28">[00:06:28]</a>.
    *   **Opportunity**: The application space has seen an "explosion" of companies building [[AI applications in sports analytics | AI-powered applications]] to deliver differentiated experiences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:19">[00:12:19]</a>.
    *   **Disruption**: The advent of [[AI applications in sports analytics | AI]] brings a "business model change" where companies can charge for "work rather than for a seat" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:38">[00:13:38]</a>. ## Challenges and Considerations for [[AI Applications in Sports Analytics | AI]] Companies

### Product Depth and Moats
Successful [[AI applications in sports analytics | AI companies]] demonstrate significant product depth that is challenging to replicate. This often means their solution is more heavily weighted towards workflow (e.g., 80% workflow, 20% model) rather than being primarily model-dependent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:14">[00:23:14]</a>. The "moat" for [[AI applications in sports analytics | AI apps]] often comes from "a thousand little things" like user experience (UX), product breadth, and overall user experience, similar to traditional SaaS products <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:41">[00:24:41]</a>.

### Velocity and First-Mover Advantage
"First mover advantage matters" significantly in [[AI]], with companies quickly becoming synonymous with a category within six to nine months <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:44">[00:23:44]</a>. This rapid velocity allows them to become a default choice for customers, attracting partnerships and capital <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:06">[00:24:06]</a>. However, being first is not always the ultimate determinant of success, as seen with Google in search and Facebook in social networking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:10">[00:25:10]</a>.

### Expertise and Founding Teams
While [[AI expertise]] (e.g., former DeepMind or OpenAI researchers) was initially sought after, it's becoming less critical than understanding the pace of model development and being able to adapt quickly. The ability to integrate new reasoning models is more important than building them from scratch <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:00">[00:26:00]</a>. Domain expertise matters for understanding end-user problems, but the [[AI market]] is more "democratic," allowing new entrants to engage with industry leaders more easily <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:44">[00:26:44]</a>. The ideal team often combines both strong building capabilities and founder-market fit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:31">[00:22:31]</a>.

### Durable Revenue vs. Experimental Budget
Many [[AI companies]] have gained early success from "[[AI tourist]]" or "experimental budget," as businesses are eager to explore [[AI]] integration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:29">[00:27:29]</a>. However, the challenge lies in translating this into "durable revenue" by securing business line budgets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:20">[00:28:20]</a>. High engagement and usage metrics among end-users are key indicators of genuine product value <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:50">[00:28:50]</a>.

[[Strategic considerations for AI application developers | AI valuations]] are significantly higher, reflecting expectations of larger market outcomes and faster growth rates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:55">[00:35:55]</a>.

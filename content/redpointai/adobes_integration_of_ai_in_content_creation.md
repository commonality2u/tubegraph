## Adobe's AI Strategy and Product Development

Adobe's AI initiatives span across its three main business units: Creative Cloud, Document Cloud, and Experience Cloud <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:51">[00:02:51]</a>.

*   **Creative Cloud:** AI models are trained on Adobe's internal Sensei platform and integrated into products like Photoshop, Illustrator, and Adobe Express <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:32">[00:03:32]</a>. Examples of current [[aidriven_tools_in_adobes_creative_cloud | AI-driven tools in Adobe's Creative Cloud]] include auto-masking, content-aware fill, object detection, and neural filters in Photoshop <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:48">[00:03:48]</a>.
*   **Document Cloud:** Acrobat's "Liquid Mode" uses [[AI in creative workflows | AI]] to Reflow documents for easier mobile reading, particularly useful for academic papers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:06">[00:04:06]</a>.
*   **Experience Cloud:** AI capabilities include anomaly detection in analytics, and marketing content/brand generation within Adobe Experience Manager (CMS) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:29">[00:04:29]</a>.

Since 2016, Adobe has focused on integrating smaller [[AI in creative workflows | AI]] models into various workflows <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:42">[00:04:42]</a>. Recognizing the improved quality of content generated by larger models, Adobe fully embraced large language models (LLMs) and large diffusion models in the last year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:05">[00:05:05]</a>. This led to the creation of the [[generative_ai_and_adobes_firefly_suite | Firefly Suite of models]], launched in March <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:12">[00:05:12]</a>.

*   Firefly.com <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:31">[00:05:31]</a>
*   Photoshop (Generative Fill) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:34">[00:05:34]</a>

*   Illustrator (forthcoming) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:45">[00:05:45]</a>

Adobe's strategic perspective is that [[AI in creative workflows | AI]] will touch most content, but not fully automate it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:12">[00:09:12]</a>. Creative professionals will remain in control of both creation and curation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:21">[00:09:21]</a>. The demand for digital content is expected to increase exponentially, making [[AI in creative workflows | AI]] a tool to enhance productivity and creativity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:35">[00:08:35]</a>.

## AI in Creative Workflows and User Experience

Adobe focuses on making [[AI in creative workflows | AI]] tools intuitive and accessible across its diverse user base.
*   **Intent-Driven Editing:** Early innovations like Neural Filters in Photoshop, which used GANs (Generative Adversarial Networks) before diffusion models became prevalent, introduced intent-driven editing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:15">[00:11:15]</a>. Features like Smart Portrait allowed users to control attributes (e.g., smile, age) with simple sliders, simplifying complex manual processes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:01">[00:12:01]</a>.
*   **Prompt-Based Interfaces:** While prompt-based UIs lower the entry barrier, Adobe's professional users often desire more control than just a prompt <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:12">[00:13:12]</a>. The company aims for a mix of language, pointing, and sliders to optimize human-computer interaction for creativity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:19">[00:13:19]</a>.
*   **"Co-pilot" Experience:** Adobe envisions [[AI in creative workflows | AI]] as a "co-pilot" that helps users achieve the end result faster, rather than instructing them on complex tool usage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:17">[00:15:17]</a>. For example, in Photoshop's Generative Fill, users select an area and can either type a prompt or leave it blank, letting the surrounding image act as an implied prompt <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:51">[00:22:51]</a>. This approach aims to intercept frequent workflows and integrate generative [[AI in creative workflows | AI]] contextually <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:40">[00:16:40]</a>.

### User Education and Guidance

To address the "cold start problem" for new users and educate the existing user base, Adobe offers different approaches for various customer segments:
*   **Consumers (Firefly.com):** Features include an inspiration stream to remix existing creations, allowing new users to find prompts they like and "riff" from them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:56">[00:18:56]</a>. The Adobe Style Engine provides curated style options (e.g., light conditions, composition rules) as selectable controls, reducing the need for complex prompt engineering <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:30">[00:20:30]</a>. Prompt auto-completion models will also provide guidance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:26">[00:21:26]</a>.
*   **Communicators/Marketers (Adobe Express):** Templates serve as the entry gateway, with generative [[AI in creative workflows | AI]] capabilities baked directly into them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:50">[00:21:50]</a>.
*   **Creative Professionals (Photoshop):** The contextual bar near selections provides generative fill options without navigating the UI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:30">[00:22:30]</a>.
*   **Enterprises (Adobe Experience Manager):** Contextual recommendations for auto-generating text portions will respect brand voice, ensuring compliance with brand guidelines <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:41">[00:23:41]</a>.

## Challenges and Opportunities in Creative AI Tools

### Trust and Safety
A key challenge for Adobe is ensuring trust and safety, minimizing bias, and removing the chance of harmful content generation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:19">[00:26:19]</a>.

*   **Data Sourcing:** Adobe's differentiated strategy involves training Firefly models on licensed content from Adobe Stock, rather than scraping the internet <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:41">[00:26:41]</a>. This approach aims to reduce risks of IP infringement and harmful content, as Adobe Stock undergoes a rigorous manual and automated moderation process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:01">[00:27:01]</a>.
*   **Bias Reduction:** While Adobe Stock data has inherent biases, Adobe actively debiases generated content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:59">[00:27:59]</a>. A "person detector" model helps identify person references in prompts and introduces fair distributions of skin tones, genders, and age groups based on the user's country of origin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:21">[00:29:21]</a>.
*   **Harmful Content Filters:** Toxicity detector models, deny lists, and block lists are employed to prevent the generation of "not safe for work" (NSFW) content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:05">[00:31:05]</a>. Specific systems detect prompts referencing children to avoid generating inappropriate content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:33">[00:31:33]</a>.
*   **Customer Feedback Loop:** Explicit feedback mechanisms (like/dislike, report) and implicit signals (download, save, share) on Firefly.com provide crucial data for reinforcement learning from human feedback (RHF), enabling future models to generate content users prefer and avoid disliked content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:01">[00:34:01]</a>.

### Scalability and Production
Deploying [[advancements_in_ai_for_creative_tools | AI]] at scale requires robust infrastructure and organizational structure.

*   **Foundational Work:** The rapid deployment of Firefly was possible due to years of foundational work, beginning with research into GANs and projects like "GenShop" (generative Photoshop) in 2019 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:28">[00:36:28]</a>. The Neural Filters team was built to accelerate the productization of [[advancements_in_ai_for_creative_tools | AI]] research models <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:39">[00:37:39]</a>.
*   **Organizational Structure:** Adobe employs a hybrid organizational structure for its [[Salesforce AI and its applications | AI]] teams <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:28">[00:41:28]</a>:
    *   **Horizontal Teams:**
        *   **Adobe Research:** Focuses on fundamental research and publishing papers, with labs for different modalities (images, video, vector, 3D) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:54">[00:41:54]</a>.
        *   **Sensei Team:** Manages a distributed training platform for researchers and applied researchers, providing curated, high-quality datasets and fast training infrastructure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:19">[00:42:19]</a>.
        *   **Applied Research:** Refines models from research, often involving more training or architecture changes, to prepare them for productization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:50">[00:42:50]</a>.
        *   **Gen Services Team:** Creates efficient APIs that run these models in the cloud, leveraging open-source technologies (like PyTorch) and custom orchestration layers for optimized inference, aiming for fast response times (e.g., under 15 seconds for a 1Kx1K image) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:09">[00:43:09]</a>. This horizontal layer is referred to as an "AI Super Highway" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:00">[00:45:00]</a>.
    *   **Vertical Teams:** Each product line (Photoshop, Express, Illustrator) has "Tech Transfer" subgroups. These teams leverage the horizontal APIs to build amazing user experiences, as they are the product and segment experts who understand core workflows best <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:19">[00:44:19]</a>. They can also contribute to the managed model zoo <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:17">[00:45:17]</a>.

The pace of [[advancements in AI for creative tools | AI innovation]] is exponential, with model architectures constantly evolving <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:39">[00:49:39]</a>.

A key opportunity is **hyper-personalization**, addressing the "content velocity" or "content supply chain" problem for enterprises <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:31">[00:50:31]</a>. [[generative_ai_and_adobes_firefly_suite | Generative AI]] will enable infinite content generation, personalized to individual viewers or segments of one <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:53">[00:50:53]</a>.

Adobe's Creative Cloud will evolve to empower customers to shift from visual designers to "creative directors of meta experiences" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:25">[00:51:25]</a>. This future promises more fluid, less convoluted, and more accessible human-computer interaction, leading to content creation with significantly higher creativity and velocity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:51">[00:51:51]</a>.

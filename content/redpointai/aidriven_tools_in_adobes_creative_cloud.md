## AI Integration Across Adobe Products

[[adobes_ai_strategy_and_product_development | Adobe's AI strategy]] is to embed [[ai_in_creative_workflows | AI]] directly into existing workflows, making tools more intuitive and powerful <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:49:00">[04:49:00]</a>.

### Creative Cloud
The Creative Cloud, which includes flagship products like Photoshop, Illustrator, and Adobe Express, leverages [[advancements_in_ai_for_creative_tools | AI]] for various content creation and authoring tasks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:02:00">[03:02:00]</a>.
*   **Photoshop**: Features like auto-masking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:50:00">[03:50:00]</a>, content-aware fill <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:54:00">[03:54:00]</a>, object detection <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:57:00">[03:57:00]</a>, and neural filters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:59:00">[03:59:00]</a> are powered by [[adobes_integration_of_ai_in_content_creation | AI]]. The "generative fill" feature allows users to select an area and generate pixels, even without typing a prompt, by using the rest of the image as an implied prompt <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:56:00">[09:56:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:50:00">[22:50:00]</a>.
*   **Adobe Express**: This marketer/communicator design suite integrates [[generative_ai_and_adobes_firefly_suite | Firefly]] to facilitate the creation of social content through templates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:58:00">[17:58:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:50:00">[21:50:00]</a>.
*   **Illustrator**: [[generative_ai_and_adobes_firefly_suite | Firefly]] integration is planned for Illustrator <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:45:00">[05:45:00]</a>.

### Document Cloud

## [[generative_ai_and_adobes_firefly_suite | Adobe's Firefly Suite]]
In 2022, Adobe decided to fully embrace large models, including diffusion models and large language models (LLMs), leading to the creation of the [[generative_ai_and_adobes_firefly_suite | Firefly Suite]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:05:00">[05:05:00]</a>.
*   **Rapid Adoption**: Launched in March as a beta, [[generative_ai_and_adobes_firefly_suite | Firefly]] has already seen over a billion images generated across platforms like firefly.com, Photoshop's Generative Fill, and Adobe Express <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:17:00">[05:17:00]</a>.
*   **Accessibility**: [[generative_ai_and_adobes_firefly_suite | Firefly]] aims to lower the entry barrier for content creation, allowing users to express their intent in natural language <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:49:00">[12:49:00]</a>.
*   **User Control**: While prompt-based interfaces are powerful, Adobe recognizes the need for more control, especially for professional users who demand "pixel-perfect" results <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:09:00">[13:09:09]</a>. The user experience combines language inputs with pointing and sliders to provide nuanced control <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:19:00">[13:19:00]</a>.

## AI's Transformative Impact on Creative Workflows
Adobe views [[adobes_integration_of_ai_in_content_creation | AI]] as the latest wave of technological disruption, akin to digital publishing, digital photography, the internet, and mobile <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:23:00">[07:23:00]</a>.
*   **Increased Demand**: Historically, each technological shift has made content creation cheaper and more accessible, leading to an exponential increase in demand for content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:18:00">[08:18:00]</a>. AI is expected to continue this trend.
*   **Creative Augmentation, Not Automation**: Adobe believes [[ai_in_creative_workflows | AI]] will touch most content, but creative people will remain in control of both the creation and curation process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:12:00">[09:12:00]</a>. It functions as a "co-pilot," enabling users to reach their desired outcome sooner without needing to master complex tools <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:15:00">[15:15:00]</a>.

## Trust, Safety, and Responsible AI
Responsible [[adobes_ai_strategy_and_product_development | AI]] development, including trust, safety, and bias reduction, is a top priority for Adobe <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:19:00">[26:19:00]</a>.
*   **Training Data**: [[generative_ai_and_adobes_firefly_suite | Firefly]] models are trained exclusively on data from [[generative_ai_and_adobes_firefly_suite | Adobe Stock]] and licensed content like museums, rather than scraped internet data <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:41:00">[26:41:00]</a>. This approach minimizes risks of IP infringement and harmful content, as Adobe Stock assets undergo automated and manual curation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:08:00">[27:08:00]</a>. Adobe explicitly states it does not train models on customer data stored in Creative Cloud <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:30:00">[33:30:00]</a>.
*   **Bias Reduction**: Recognizing inherent biases in any dataset, Adobe invested significantly in debiasing techniques. For example, when a prompt refers to a person (e.g., "lawyer"), models detect jobs and debias the generated content to reflect a fair distribution of skin tones, genders, and age groups based on the country of origin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:17:00">[29:17:00]</a>.
*   **Harmful Content Prevention**: Toxicity detector models, deny lists, block lists, and NSFW (Not Safe For Work) filters are in place to prevent the generation of inappropriate content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:46:00">[30:46:00]</a>. Specific systems detect prompts referencing children and minimize the chance of generating inappropriate associations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:21:00">[31:21:00]</a>.
*   **Customer Feedback**: A robust feedback mechanism on firefly.com and within Photoshop allows beta customers to report issues and provide data points for continuous model refinement and rule adjustments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:02:00">[32:02:00]</a>. Generated images and prompts from firefly.com are stored and used for training, serving as a form of Reinforcement Human Feedback (RHF) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:01:00">[34:01:00]</a>. This feedback helps teach models what users like and dislike <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:40:00">[34:40:00]</a>.

## [[ai_infrastructure_and_developer_tools | Scaling AI Development]] and Organizational Structure
Adobe's rapid deployment of [[generative_ai_and_adobes_firefly_suite | Firefly]] was possible due to years of foundational work <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:17:00">[36:17:00]</a>.
*   **Early Investment**: The concept of "Generative Photoshop" (GenShop) using GANs was explored in Adobe Research as early as 2019 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:11:00">[37:11:00]</a>. This led to the development of the Neural Filters team, which shipped over 100 smaller [[ai_video_creation_tools | AI]] models (e.g., colorization, smart portrait, season changing), building expertise in distributed model training and optimized inference <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:39:00">[37:39:00]</a>.
*   **Organizational Structure**: Adobe employs a hybrid organizational structure for [[ai_infrastructure_and_developer_tools | AI]] development <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:33:00">[41:33:00]</a>.
    *   **Horizontal Layers**:
        *   **Adobe Research**: Over 200 researchers and 300 PhD interns focus on cutting-edge [[ai_infrastructure_and_developer_tools | AI]] models across modalities (images, video, vector, 3D) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:52:00">[41:52:00]</a>.
        *   **Sensei Team**: Provides the distributed training platform, enabling researchers to train models in a multi-tenant environment, and manages curated, high-quality datasets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="42:16:00">[42:16:00]</a>.
        *   **Applied Research**: Refines models from Adobe Research, often involving more training or architectural changes, to prepare them for productization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="42:50:00">[42:50:00]</a>.
        *   **Gen Services Team**: Creates efficient services and APIs to run these models in the cloud, focusing on optimized inference. They leverage open-source technologies (like PyTorch 2) and custom orchestration layers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="43:09:00">[43:09:00]</a>. The goal is an "AI Super Highway" that provides a managed model zoo for product teams <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="44:59:00">[44:54:00]</a>.
    *   **Vertical Teams**: Each product line (Photoshop, Adobe Express, Illustrator) has "Tech transfer subgroups" that leverage the APIs created by the horizontal teams to build specific user experiences and workflows tailored to their product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="44:12:00">[44:12:00]</a>. These teams are product and segment experts, understanding where [[ai_in_creative_workflows | AI]] can have the most impact.

*   **Fluid Human-Computer Interaction**: The interaction with creative tools will become more fluid, less convoluted, and accessible to broader audiences, with [[ai_in_creative_workflows | AI]] at its core, even if not explicitly mentioned <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="51:51:00">[51:51:00]</a>.

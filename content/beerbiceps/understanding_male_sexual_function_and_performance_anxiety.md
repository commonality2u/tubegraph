## Performance Anxiety

[[personal_challenges_and_mental_health | Performance anxiety]] is a very common issue among men <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:00">[01:16:00]</a>. When sex becomes a "performance" rather than a pleasurable act, the pleasure element is removed, leading to a vicious cycle of poor performance, guilt, depression, and subsequent [[causes_of_fear_and_anxiety_change_and_internal_conflicts | pre-performance anxiety]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:19:00">[04:19:00]</a>.

### Partner Support

A crucial part of addressing performance anxiety involves the partner's support <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:09:00">[04:09:00]</a>. Verbal encouragement and nurturing can help a man break out of this cycle, though it might take a few months <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:11:00">[04:11:00]</a>. Without the partner's willingness to work through the issue, treatment can be difficult <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:41:00">[04:41:00]</a>.

### Pharmacological Approaches

Certain substances can inhibit inhibitions, aiding performance. A drink or two of alcohol can improve performance by reducing inhibitions, though excessive consumption reduces performance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:59:00">[04:59:00]</a>. Dr. Tanja uses Trazodone, an old antidepressant whose side effect is causing erections, to help men overcome anxiety <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:53:00">[04:53:00]</a>.

### Societal Influence

Societal pressure, especially among young men, can lead to psychological damage related to performance anxiety <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:56:00">[05:56:00]</a>. Stories of unproven claims from "sadhu Babas" or peer comparisons in school can make men feel useless and lead to self-doubt <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:22:00">[05:22:00]</a>.

### Distinguishing Psychogenic from Organic Dysfunction

A simple question can help differentiate between psychogenic (psychological) and organic (physical) erectile dysfunction: "Do you have hard-ons early morning spontaneous erections?" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:58:00">[05:58:00]</a> If the answer is yes, the organ is physically fine, indicating a psychological disconnect between the organ and the brain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:00">[01:00:00]</a>. ## Testosterone Replacement Therapy (TRT)

[[mens_psychological_and_physiological_health_relating_to_testosterone | Testosterone Replacement Therapy]] (TRT) is a treatment for low [[mens_psychological_and_physiological_health_relating_to_testosterone | testosterone]] levels.

### Considerations and Risks

TRT is not a simple solution and carries significant implications:

*   **Sperm Count**: Administering exogenous [[mens_psychological_and_physiological_health_relating_to_testosterone | testosterone]] can suppress the body's natural production, leading to a drop in sperm counts, potentially to zero <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:02:00">[04:02:00]</a>. This is a crucial consideration for men who wish to have children <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:09:00">[04:09:00]</a>.
*   **Lifelong Commitment**: For men around 40 years old, starting TRT often means a lifelong commitment, as the body's natural [[mens_psychological_and_physiological_health_relating_to_testosterone | testosterone]] production may not resume if treatment stops <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:14:00">[04:14:00]</a>.
*   **Dosage and Method**: Injecting [[mens_psychological_and_physiological_health_relating_to_testosterone | testosterone]] to "supra-physiological" levels (above the normal range) can shock the hypothalamus, leading to testicular atrophy (shrinking of the testicles) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:52:00">[04:52:00]</a>. Safer options include gels, capsules, or slow-release Depo injections that keep [[mens_psychological_and_physiological_health_relating_to_testosterone | testosterone]] within the physiological range <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:54:00">[04:54:00]</a>.
*   **Effectiveness**: TRT acts as a "maintenance fluid" for erections; it doesn't provide immediate effects like Viagra <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:03:00">[04:03:00]</a>. It takes time for its benefits to manifest <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:05:00">[04:05:00]</a>.

### Costs

The cost of TRT varies by method. An intramuscular injection given once every three months might cost around 5,000 rupees (approximately $60 USD) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:51:00">[05:51:00]</a>. Daily capsules or gels can be more expensive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:54:00">[05:54:00]</a>.

### When TRT is Appropriate

TRT is typically considered when low [[mens_psychological_and_physiological_health_relating_to_testosterone | testosterone]] levels manifest in symptoms such as:
*   Irritability and grumpiness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:48:00">[05:48:00]</a>
*   Inability to handle [[causes_of_fear_and_anxiety_change_and_internal_conflicts | stress]] or crisis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:52:00">[05:52:00]</a>
*   Cognitive disorders like forgetfulness and indecisiveness, which can be linked to early-onset dementia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:59:00">[05:59:00]</a>.

It is crucial that TRT is prescribed and managed by experts who understand the nuances of its effects <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:05:00">[06:05:00]</a>. Lifestyle modifications like diet, exercise, and adherence to Circadian rhythm are considered 90% of the treatment, with TRT being only 10% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:14:00">[06:14:00]</a>.

## [[role_and_function_of_the_prostate_gland | Prostate Health]]

The [[role_and_function_of_the_prostate_gland | prostate gland]] is a walnut-sized organ located between the urinary bladder and the urethra <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:13:00">[05:13:00]</a>. ### Prostate Enlargement (BPH)

Not all enlarged prostates cause symptoms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:02:00">[06:02:00]</a>.

Common symptoms of BPH include:
*   Frequent urination <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:29:00">[05:29:00]</a>
*   Urgency to urinate, sometimes leading to leakage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:32:00">[05:32:00]</a>
*   Difficulty starting the urine stream <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:40:00">[05:40:00]</a>
*   Prolonged or dribbling stream <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:43:00">[05:43:00]</a>
*   A feeling of incomplete bladder emptying <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:47:00">[05:47:00]</a>

There can be a familial predisposition to BPH <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:24:00">[06:24:00]</a>.

### [[prostate_examinations_and_health | Prostate Examinations and Health]]

[[prostate_examinations_and_health | Prostate examinations]] are done to check for enlargement or cancer. A digital rectal examination (DRE) should not be painful if performed correctly by a skilled doctor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:55:00">[05:55:00]</a>.

Modern diagnostics include:
*   **Ultrasound**: Provides information on the [[role_and_function_of_the_prostate_gland | prostate's]] weight, volume, shape, and its effect on the bladder <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:01:00">[06:01:00]</a>.
*   **PSA (Prostate Specific Antigen) blood test**: If PSA levels are normal, a rectal examination for cancer may not be necessary <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:04:00">[06:04:00]</a>. A rising PSA prompts further investigation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:07:00">[06:07:00]</a>.
*   **MRI**: Can help detect cancer when PSA levels are high <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:17:00">[06:17:00]</a>.
*   **Biopsy**: Ordered if a hard nodule, indicative of cancer, is felt during a DRE or suggested by other tests <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:14:00">[06:14:00]</a>. A cancerous [[role_and_function_of_the_prostate_gland | prostate]] feels hard like a stone, unlike a normal, firm [[role_and_function_of_the_prostate_gland | prostate]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:19:00">[06:19:00]</a>.

*   **Social Connection and Recreation**: Spending time with family, friends, and engaging in hobbies or travel are vital de-stressors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:19:00">[03:19:00]</a>.

The autonomic nervous system, comprising sympathetic and parasympathetic branches, largely controls involuntary bodily functions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:22:00">[03:22:00]</a>. While typically autonomous, practices like yoga can give individuals some control over these systems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:29:00">[03:29:00]</a>. A higher parasympathetic activity leads to increased [[mens_psychological_and_physiological_health_relating_to_testosterone | testosterone]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:10:00">[04:10:00]</a>.

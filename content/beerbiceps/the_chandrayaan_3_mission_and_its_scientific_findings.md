## Scientific Discoveries
The Chandrayaan 3 mission provided many insights into the moon <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:54">[00:00:54]</a>. The mission carried five important instruments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:11">[00:17:11]</a>:
*   **Vikram Lander Instruments**
    *   **Seismic Behavior Measurement** Also known as lunar vibrations or "moon quakes" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:25">[00:18:25]</a>.
    *   **Thermophysical Characteristics of the Regolith** A probe was inserted into the surface material (regolith) to measure temperature gradients and deduce properties like conductivity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:36">[00:18:36]</a>.
    *   **Electron Measurement** A one-meter long probe measured electrons around the moon, observing how charged electrons rise during the day and go down at night due to the absence of atmosphere and sunlight <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:02">[00:19:02]</a>.
*   **Pragyan Rover Instruments**
    *   **Alpha Particle X-ray Spectrometer (APXS)** This instrument applied alpha particles to hit surface materials. The scattered particles were then measured by a spectrometer to identify elements like carbon, hydrogen, oxygen, nitrogen, phosphorus, and silicon <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:22">[00:17:22]</a>.
    *   **Laser-Induced Breakdown Spectrometer (LIBS)** This laser-based instrument sent a beam to the surface, ablating fumes which were then imaged by a spectrometer to determine elemental composition <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:45">[00:17:45]</a>.

The rover, Pragyan, moved approximately 100 meters, stopping at various locations to perform these measurements and collect data <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:01">[00:18:01]</a>. The mission also collected images using two stereo cameras on the rover <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:17">[00:18:17]</a>. Interesting observations from this data have been published <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:12">[00:18:12]</a>.

## Geographical Extent
Most of the [[lost_history_and_archeological_mysteries_of_ancient_india | Indus Valley Civilization]] is located in Pakistan, with a significant portion also in [[role_of_india_in_ancient_asian_cultural_exchange | India]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>. Unfortunately, much of the civilization within [[role_of_india_in_ancient_asian_cultural_exchange | India]] remains underground <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:05">[00:00:05]</a>. The civilization spread widely across [[role_of_india_in_ancient_asian_cultural_exchange | India]], with most sites either unexcavated or only mildly excavated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:11">[00:31:11]</a>.

### Key Sites
*   **Rakhigarhi**: An ongoing excavation is being conducted in Rakhigarhi, located in Hissar, Haryana <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:09">[00:00:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:04">[00:27:04]</a>. The entire site is situated on a mound, with a heavily populated village currently built on top of it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:12">[00:00:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:13">[00:27:13]</a>.
*   **Lothal**: This port city of the [[lost_history_and_archeological_mysteries_of_ancient_india | Indus Valley Civilization]] is found in Gujarat <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:18">[00:00:18]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:57">[00:27:57]</a>. Sites like Lothal served as reception points for people from Mesopotamia, Central Asia, and other regions, facilitating transcontinental human interactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:03">[00:28:03]</a>.
*   **Dholavira**: Located on the [[role_of_india_in_ancient_asian_cultural_exchange | India]]-Pakistan border in the Rann of Kutch (a salt desert), Dholavira was a thriving civilization around 2000-1500 BCE <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:20">[00:31:20]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:26">[00:31:26]</a>. In this extremely challenging environment, the inhabitants built a citadel, created trade objects, and developed techniques to filter salty water to grow food and sustain themselves <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:52">[00:31:52]</a>.

## Trade and Interaction
The [[lost_history_and_archeological_mysteries_of_ancient_india | Indus Valley Civilization]] was contemporary with Mesopotamia and the Harappan Civilization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:38">[00:08:38]</a>. Evidence of trade between these civilizations includes Mesopotamian seals found in [[role_of_india_in_ancient_asian_cultural_exchange | India]] and [[lost_history_and_archeological_mysteries_of_ancient_india | Indus Valley Civilization]] seals found in Mesopotamia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:43">[00:08:43]</a>.

## Undeciphered Script
A significant amount of information about the [[lost_history_and_archeological_mysteries_of_ancient_india | Indus Valley Civilization]] remains unknown because the Harappan script has not yet been deciphered <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:10">[00:28:10]</a>.

Sites related to the [[lost_history_and_archeological_mysteries_of_ancient_india | Indus Valley Civilization]] are considered areas of interest for global archaeology because they represent early connecting points with other civilizations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:19">[00:26:19]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:40">[00:26:40]</a>.

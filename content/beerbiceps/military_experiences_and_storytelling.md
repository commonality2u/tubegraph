## The Depth of Military Experiences

The host believes that these life experiences, rather than conventional societal achievements, are what truly define a person's life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:37">[00:03:37]</a>.

Colonel Bakshi, when asked about his profession, candidly stated that he "used to kill people for [his] leaving" and was currently on a break <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:57">[00:03:57]</a>. This directness underscores the harsh realities of combat. He finds immense satisfaction in combat, where one operates at their [[mental_and_spiritual_aspects_of_being_a_soldier | mental and physical threshold]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:00">[00:05:00]</a>, making life-or-death decisions in seconds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:06">[00:05:06]</a>. ## Beyond the Uniform: The True Soldier
Colonel Bakshi shared a crucial message for aspiring soldiers and the youth: it is not the uniform that makes a soldier, but the attitude and heart <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:47">[01:12:47]</a>. He notes that many individuals who have never worn a uniform possess the spirit of a soldier, while some in uniform may lack it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:50">[01:12:50]</a>. This philosophy emphasizes that true service and a soldier's mindset are about inner commitment and character.

For Mr. Narayan Murty, life is defined by passion in his chosen field, working extremely hard with a singular aim, giving 100% to it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:21">[00:05:21]</a>. ## Navigating the Early Years: Challenges and Sacrifices

The initial phase of [[challenges_in_infrastructure_development_in_india | Infosys]] was fraught with significant difficulties:
*   **Financial Scarcity** There was a constant lack of money while raising small children <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:40">[00:22:40]</a>.
*   **Frequent Travel** Mr. Murty traveled extensively, up to 200-250 days a year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:44">[00:22:44]</a>.
*   **Lack of Infrastructure** In 1985 Bangalore, obtaining a telephone line was nearly impossible, and they did not own a car <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:50">[00:22:50]</a>.
*   **Family Responsibilities** Sudha Murty had to manage the household and children, who were often unwell, on a small salary, as she was unable to work much due to their young age <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:58">[00:22:58]</a>.
*   **Uncertain Future** They lived "hand to mouth," with no certainty about the company's success or whether their financial struggles would ever end <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:07">[00:23:07]</a>.
*   **Business Hurdles** There were constant "hiccups" in the business, including difficulties in acquiring clients and the absence of a dedicated office space, necessitating renting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:44">[00:23:44]</a>.

## Sudha Murty's Unwavering Support

Throughout these [[overcoming_challenges_in_early_career | challenges]], Sudha Murty remained positive, providing crucial support and motivation. When morale was low, she would reassure her husband:
> "Why do you worry? I have a degree, I can always work" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:48">[00:24:48]</a>.
> "We are capable of working somewhere why do you worry our requirements are very very few why do you worry" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:10">[00:25:10]</a>.

She exemplified this commitment by selling her shares in other companies, including Tata, to purchase a small two-bedroom house, ensuring they had a "rent-free" home <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:59">[00:24:59]</a>. Her expectations were minimal, not dreaming of bungalows or Mercedes cars <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:13">[00:25:13]</a>. She firmly believed that happiness does not come from money, but from courage and inner contentment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:23">[00:25:23]</a>. This positive outlook and focus on the long-term helped them navigate the uncertainties <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:17">[00:32:17]</a>.

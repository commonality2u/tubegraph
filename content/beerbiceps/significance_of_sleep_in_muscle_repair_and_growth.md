## The Role of Hormones in Muscle Repair
For muscle repair, the body utilizes various hormones <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:11:00">[04:11:00]</a>. Among these, the [[the_role_of_human_growth_hormone_hgh_in_fasting | human growth hormone (HGH)]] is considered the most important <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:13:00">[04:13:00]</a>. HGH is predominantly released during the early hours of sleep <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:20:00">[04:20:00]</a>. While some bodybuilders use HGH injections for muscle growth, a natural and effective way to stimulate substantial HGH release is through sufficient sleep <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:23:00">[04:23:00]</a>.

## Sleep vs. Supplements
Sleep is often considered more vital for muscle development than many market supplements, such as BCAAs and glutamine <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:35:00">[04:35:00]</a>. If sleep could be bottled and taken as a supplement, it would be the most effective one available <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:41:00">[04:41:00]</a>. Therefore, for individuals aiming to build muscle mass and increase strength, prioritizing enough sleep is paramount <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:49:00">[04:49:00]</a>.

## Recommended Sleep Duration
It is generally recommended to get around 7 to 8.5 hours of sleep, with some individuals potentially needing up to 9 hours, depending on their body's specific recovery needs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:54:00">[04:54:00]</a>. Consistent and adequate [[importance_of_sleep_for_health | sleep]] significantly contributes to overall recovery, leading to better gains and muscle size <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:57:00">[03:57:00]</a>.

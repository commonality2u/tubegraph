## Nature of Engagement with Wildlife

The nature of military operations, especially for [[cultural_and_operational_dynamics_of_the_indian_army | Special Forces]], involves being constantly immersed in wild nature, often far from civilization [00:55:51]. This environment is often a combat zone that exists within high-altitude mountains and deep forests [00:56:09]. Soldiers tend to merge with nature not only physically but also at a deeper consciousness level, as they operate beyond judgment [01:02:49 - 01:03:00]. This lack of fear or judgment can be sensed by animals [01:02:49], and soldiers may even start smelling like the forest itself, making them a familiar scent to wildlife [01:06:23 - 01:06:35].

It is rare for soldiers to be bitten by snakes or scorpions despite operating in terrains where these creatures are prevalent [01:01:09 - 01:01:12].

### Leopard Encounters

Major Vivek Jacob recounted an instance where he was on sentry duty at night in a dangerous forested mountain area with his buddy asleep [00:58:08 - 00:59:16]. He heard a noise close by, and upon switching on his night vision goggles, he saw a leopard sitting less than a meter away, staring at the same spot he had been observing [00:59:21 - 01:00:03]. Major Jacob aimed his weapon at the leopard, but after about four to five minutes, the leopard calmly got up and walked away without incident [01:00:31 - 01:00:53]. This peaceful coexistence was attributed to both beings being "in alignment" and "in peace" [01:02:41 - 01:02:44].

### Bear Encounters

Bears in the Indian forests, while appearing "cute," can be extremely terrifying and dangerous in a direct encounter [01:07:12 - 01:07:22]. They possess immense strength and long, sharp claws capable of tearing a person apart or stripping skin from a face with a single swipe [01:07:32 - 01:07:45]. The most dangerous type of bear is a mother with cubs, as she is most likely to attack to protect her young [01:08:18 - 01:08:30].

Major Jacob shared two bear encounters:
*   In one instance, he and his team encountered a mother bear with two cubs, and despite expecting an attack, she calmly walked off with her cubs, showing no aggression [01:08:44 - 01:08:53]. This highlights an "alignment between wild animals and [[combat experience and mentality | special forces or soldiers]]" [01:08:58 - 01:09:02].
*   In a more intense encounter, while on an observation mission, Major Jacob and his team were positioned on a cliff near a bear's cave [01:14:58 - 01:15:05]. Two large bears approached their position, and one charged directly at Major Jacob, stopping just two meters away and roaring [01:15:31 - 01:16:01]. In this moment of extreme [[combat scenarios and survival instincts | combat scenarios and survival instincts]], Major Jacob had his weapon ready but hesitated to fire, as the bullets were small and the gunfire would compromise their mission [01:16:53 - 01:17:02]. A local Special Forces soldier behind him flashed a laser light into the bear's eyes, causing it to stumble backward and retreat [01:17:25 - 01:18:00]. During this five-second intense encounter, Major Jacob experienced a [[combat experience and mentality | flow state]], with no fear or emotions, solely focused on the bear's potential swipe [01:18:38 - 01:19:12].

## Current Applications of AI at ISRO

The [[ISROs plans for human space missions to the moon and Mars | Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO)]] actively uses AI across various domains, enhancing its operational capabilities and data analysis:

*   **Image Processing**
    *   Satellites continuously capture images, and AI is applied to analyze these, identifying objects like trees, roads, people, and cars <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:26:13">[01:26:13]</a>.
    *   For surveillance missions, AI engines on satellites can identify specific targets like troop movements, tanks, vessels, or aircraft, providing immediate warnings without needing to download full images, thereby improving throughput <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:26:51">[01:26:51]</a>. This application extends to monitoring animals, freight movement, agriculture, and healthcare <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:27:31">[01:27:31]</a>.
*   **Rocket Data Analysis**
    *   AI engines are used aboard rockets to analyze performance data and determine if the rocket is functioning correctly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:27:47">[01:27:47]</a>.
    *   In cases of fault, AI can suggest corrections, initiate switch-overs, or even recommend destroying the rocket to ensure crew safety during manned missions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:27:55">[01:27:55]</a>.
*   **Non-Destructive Evaluation**
    *   AI assists in analyzing X-rays of rocket hardware to detect defects in materials or manufacturing processes, a task traditionally performed by human skill <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:28:24">[01:28:24]</a>.

> [!info] ISRO's Own AI Development
> ISRO has many teams dedicated to developing its own AI capabilities across various applications <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:28:08">[01:28:08]</a>. Hardware capable of utilizing these AI advancements is expected to fly soon <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:28:15">[01:28:15]</a>.

## Future of Robotics in Exploration

Robots are poised to play a transformative role in future space endeavors, potentially altering the necessity for human physical presence in hazardous or distant environments:

*   **Experiential Exploration**: In the future, individuals might experience places like Mount Everest, the Moon, or [[isros_plans_for_human_space_missions_to_the_moon_and_Mars | Mars]] by sending robots equipped with advanced sensory perception (vision, smell, touch, temperature, wind flow). The robot's experiences could be integrated directly into a human mind, allowing for exploration from the comfort of Earth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:21:43">[01:21:43]</a>.
*   **Mining and Resource Extraction**: Robotics could solve problems associated with mining resources on celestial bodies like the Moon or asteroids by significantly reducing risks and costs associated with sending humans <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:54">[01:22:54]</a>.

## Human vs. Robot Capabilities

While humans currently offer unique advantages in space exploration, the capabilities of robots are rapidly advancing:

*   **Superior Functional Capability**: Robots are expected to surpass human functional capabilities, exhibiting greater dexterity and ability to perform tasks in challenging situations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:23:57">[01:23:57]</a>. For instance, robots are already more capable than humans in delicate operations like surgical dissections and threading <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:24:30">[01:24:30]</a>.
*   **Complementary Roles**: Robots may not entirely replace humans <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:24:42">[01:24:42]</a>. Instead, humans could control robots remotely from Earth, leveraging the speed of electromagnetic signals for communication across vast distances <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:24:46">[01:24:46]</a>.
*   **Intelligence and Perception**: By the 2040s, robots could easily be sent on missions, possessing intelligence, perceptions, and behaviors so advanced that they would be indistinguishable from humans <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:23:30">[01:23:30]</a>. However, their emotional capacity might remain distinct from human beings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:24:06">[01:24:06]</a>.

## Technological Advancement

The rapid advancement of technology in the last century, particularly in AI, points to an explosive future <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:24">[01:29:24]</a>. The concept of Artificial General Intelligence (AGI), where AI becomes even more capable, could significantly accelerate scientific progress by acting as an assistant to scientists <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:42">[01:20:42]</a>. The combination of AGI with advanced robotics is envisioned to solve many complex problems in space exploration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:21:00">[01:21:00]</a>.

This vision aligns with a "solar punk" future, emphasizing a bright and sustainable future achieved through technological progress, rather than the darker "cyberpunk" visions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:21:19">[01:21:19]</a>.

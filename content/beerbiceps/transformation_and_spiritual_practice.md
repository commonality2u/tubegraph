### Samsara and Nirvana

In Buddhism, Samsara is not a physical place but a deluded state of mind where default emotions are ego, ignorance, anger, attachment, jealousy, and greed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="45:50:00">[45:50:00]</a>. If these default emotions are purified and replaced with patience, compassion, love, and generosity, that is Nirvana <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="46:21:00">[46:21:00]</a>. The ultimate motivation in Buddhism, especially Mahayana and Vajrayana, is for all sentient beings to be free from suffering and find everlasting happiness, which is Enlightenment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="46:33:00">[46:33:00]</a>.

### Three Vehicles of Buddhism

There are three main paths in Buddhism <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="48:43:00">[48:43:00]</a>:
*   **Theravada:** Practiced in places like Thailand and Cambodia, this form is very traditional and discipline-based, focused on the individual and their small community <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="48:51:00">[48:51:00]</a>.
*   **Mahayana:** Expands the focus to include the welfare of all sentient beings, recognizing that all beings desire happiness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="49:17:00">[49:17:00]</a>.
*   **Vajrayana:** Described as the "turbo version" of Mahayana, it's a shortcut path to Enlightenment, incorporating Tantra, havans, and practices from other faiths like Bon and Hinduism <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="49:44:00">[49:44:00]</a>. Vajrayana emphasizes transformation rather than struggling bit by bit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:00">[01:01:00]</a>.

Chiyaan Vikram is widely recognized for his intense dedication and transformative approach to acting, often immersing himself completely into the characters he portrays <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:34">[00:00:34]</a>. His commitment to his craft is so profound that it extends beyond the screen, influencing his physical and mental state for months on end <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:49">[00:14:49]</a>.

## Dedication to the Craft
Vikram views cinema as his "blood" and becomes utterly "lost in it" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:58">[00:18:58]</a>. He aims for each film to feature a fundamentally different portrayal, from his appearance to his mannerisms, speech, and even how he fights or eats <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:03">[00:11:03]</a>. This drive stems from his childhood fascination with actors like Sivaji, who could convey an entire movie through a single picture <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:48">[00:10:48]</a>.

He considers himself a "100-percenter," giving his absolute best to every project, no matter how big or small <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:47">[00:06:47]</a>. This intensity means that what starts as a short interview can easily extend to hours because he feels compelled to do it "right" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:07">[00:07:07]</a>. His approach isn't perfectionism, but rather an obsession with the intricate details, believing there's always a "right way" to do things <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:34">[00:07:34]</a>.

> [!NOTE] Obsession with Transformation
> "Every film of mine I should be different but not just in the pictures I should walk different I should talk different I should laugh different I should eat different I should fight different." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:03">[00:11:03]</a>

## Role Preparation and Immersion
Vikram's process begins with a deep dive into the script and discussions with the director, allowing an image of the character to form in his mind <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:50">[00:24:50]</a>. He then spends months formulating the character mentally <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:02">[00:25:02]</a>. Once filming begins, he aims to "become that guy," acting instinctively rather than consciously planning every move <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:21">[00:25:21]</a>.

His goal is always to portray someone distinctly different from himself, with the most distant roles being the most exciting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:37">[00:18:37]</a>.

### Notable Transformations and Challenges

*   **Dhoool** (takeoff of *I Am Sam*): Playing a differentially abled character was profoundly challenging. He spent time at a facility with children with similar conditions to absorb their energy and mannerisms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:30">[00:13:30]</a>. He deliberately avoided dialogues, instead speaking instinctively on set <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:16">[00:14:16]</a>. This role was so consuming that he found it difficult to switch off, needing to actively remind himself he was "Vikram" after shooting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:49">[00:14:49]</a>.

*   **Anniyan/Aparichit**: Portraying three distinct personalities required immense mental shifting. To manage the transitions, Vikram would spend days in isolation on his terrace, observing pigeons, and avoiding conversation, unable to switch off from the characters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:07">[00:15:07]</a>.

*   **Kasi**: For his role as a blind man, he practiced rolling his eyes up to hide his pupils, enduring significant physical strain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:55">[00:19:55]</a>. This commitment led to vision problems for three months after the film <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:49">[00:20:49]</a>.

*   **Sethu** (original of *Tere Naam*): While playing a mentally challenged character, he restricted his diet to one dry chapati, one beetroot or carrot juice, and one egg white per day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:01">[00:21:01]</a>. He also walked 8.5 km to and from the location to lose weight <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:33">[00:21:33]</a>. To maintain the authenticity of his costume's blood stains, he wore the same unwashed clothes for two months <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:47">[00:21:47]</a>.

*   **I**: This film demanded extreme physical transformations, requiring him to shift from a bodybuilder physique to a model, and then to a character weighing only 52 kg (lighter than his co-star) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:54">[00:21:54]</a>. He achieved this by eating only 50 calories while burning 100, combined with daily 15 km cycling and intense workouts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:18">[00:22:18]</a>. Doctors warned him about potential organ failure, but he pushed through for a single scene, considering the effort "worth it" for its impact on his legacy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:44">[00:22:44]</a>.

*   **Thangalaan**: This was his most difficult film to date. He gave his "all," focusing on authenticity beyond just wearing a loincloth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:56">[00:15:56]</a>. The filming involved long, continuous single shots with hundreds of people, without creature comforts or breaks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:21">[00:16:21]</a>. He would endure five hours of makeup, then shoot in freezing conditions, covered in mud or dust, which would crack on his skin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:27">[00:17:27]</a>. He even maintained the character's detachment by avoiding conversation with his English co-star off-screen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:07">[00:26:07]</a>.

## The Role of Observation
Vikram is a "people watcher," constantly absorbing data from his surroundings, whether at an airport or station <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:12">[00:40:12]</a>. This observational habit acts like an "encyclopedia" in his mind, providing a vast library of human behaviors that he can draw upon instinctively for his roles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:27">[00:40:27]</a>.

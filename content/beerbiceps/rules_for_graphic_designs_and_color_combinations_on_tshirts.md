## Understanding the T-shirt Maturity Scale

The [[tshirt_maturity_scale_and_ageappropriate_styles | t-shirt maturity scale]] distinguishes between young and mature styles, guiding appropriate dressing based on age <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:29">[00:00:29]</a>. While college-aged individuals can lean towards a younger style, those above 22 should aim for a slightly more mature appearance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:39">[00:00:39]</a>.

Two primary factors determine whether a T-shirt makes you look younger or older:
*   **Pattern vs. Plain**: Whether the T-shirt has a [[pattern_shirt_rules | pattern]] or is plain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:54">[00:00:54]</a>.
*   **Color Brightness**: Whether the T-shirt is bright and colorful, or dark/white in color <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:58">[00:00:58]</a>.

Generally, dark or white T-shirts contribute to an older look, while bright and colorful T-shirts with many [[pattern_shirt_rules | patterns]] make you appear younger <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:04">[00:01:04]</a>.

Combining these factors:
*   A dark T-shirt with a [[pattern_shirt_rules | pattern]] appears average, neither overtly old nor young <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:17">[00:01:17]</a>.
*   A plain T-shirt that is bright in color also appears average <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:23">[00:01:23]</a>.
*   A bright-colored T-shirt with many [[pattern_shirt_rules | patterns]] will make you look very young <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:29">[00:01:29]</a>.
*   A dark, plain T-shirt will make you look significantly more mature <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:34">[00:01:34]</a>.

> If you are above 22, it is acceptable to choose styles that lean towards a more mature look <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:40">[00:01:40]</a>. If you are still in college, bright colors and more graphics/[[pattern_shirt_rules | patterns]] are suitable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:45">[00:01:45]</a>.

## Rules for Graphic T-shirts

When selecting T-shirts with graphics, adhere to specific color rules:
*   **Two-Color Maximum**: Ideally, opt for a maximum of two colors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:00">[00:02:00]</a>. This includes one base color for the T-shirt and one simple color for the graphic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:02">[00:02:02]</a>. Examples include black on red, blue on black, or white on green <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:07">[00:02:07]</a>.
*   **Minimal Third Color**: If a graphic contains two colors, any third color should be minimal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:13">[00:02:13]</a>. The third color should serve only to highlight the [[pattern_shirt_rules | pattern]] and the T-shirt's natural base color <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:28">[00:02:28]</a>. An example is a white print on a black T-shirt with a small pop of red <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:22">[00:02:22]</a>.

### Common Mistakes with Graphic T-shirts
A frequent mistake is choosing [[pattern_shirt_rules | patterns]] where a third color is dominating, which can ruin the overall look <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:36">[00:02:36]</a>. For instance, a red T-shirt with a white [[pattern_shirt_rules | pattern]] can be disrupted by a prominent blue [[pattern_shirt_rules | pattern]] if the colors don't harmonize <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:42">[00:02:42]</a>.

## Avoiding Logos on T-shirts

While a logo might seem insignificant, it can significantly impact how a T-shirt is perceived <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:12">[00:03:12]</a>.
*   **Societal Judgment**: People often judge based on clothing and brands <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:16">[00:03:16]</a>. Flaunting a budget-friendly brand can lead to negative assumptions, while flaunting a high-end brand can be seen as showy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:23">[00:03:23]</a>.
*   **Simplicity**: T-shirts generally look better without any logos, as simplicity is considered beautiful <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:40">[00:03:40]</a>.

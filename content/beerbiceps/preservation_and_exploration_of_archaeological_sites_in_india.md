## Current State of Archaeological Discovery

Despite its rich past, only an estimated 10% of human history has been discovered through archaeological efforts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:11">[00:05:11]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:23">[00:11:23]</a>. A significant portion of history remains lost, unfound, or destroyed due to factors like urbanization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:34">[01:17:34]</a>.

### The Archaeological Survey of India (ASI)

The primary institution responsible for archaeology in India is the [[Archaeological Survey of India]]. It was founded by the British, specifically by Alexander Cunningham, who began as a "treasure hunter" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:52">[00:03:52]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:58">[00:03:58]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:06">[00:04:06]</a>. The ASI's mandate revolves around three core activities: exploration, excavation, and conservation of historical sites <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:52">[00:03:52]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:58">[00:03:58]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:06">[00:04:06]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:29">[00:04:29]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:39">[00:04:39]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:11">[00:05:11]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:51:24">[02:51:24]</a>.

An ongoing project uses satellite remote sensing for archaeological purposes, allowing for the discovery of sites by looking at satellite imagery and beneath the earth's surface <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:47">[00:04:47]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:57">[00:04:57]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:02">[00:05:02]</a>.

## Significant Archaeological Sites and Discoveries

### [[indus_valley_civilization_and_its_archaeological_significance | Indus Valley Civilization]]
Much of the [[indus_valley_civilization_and_its_archaeological_significance | Indus Valley Civilization]] is located in Pakistan, with a significant portion in India still underground <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:05">[00:00:05]</a>. Key sites include:
*   **Rakhigarhi:** An ongoing excavation site in Hissar, Haryana, where a heavily populated village sits atop the ancient mound <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:09">[00:00:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:06">[00:07:06]</a>.
*   **Lothal:** A port found in Gujarat, indicating maritime trade connections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:18">[00:00:18]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:57:59">[02:57:59]</a>.
*   **Dholavira:** Located on the India-Pakistan border in Gujarat's Rann of Kutch, this site demonstrates a civilization's ability to thrive in a harsh salt desert, filter salty water, and establish trade <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:19:19">[03:19:19]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:31:39">[03:31:39]</a>.

The [[indus_valley_civilization_and_its_archaeological_significance | Indus Valley Civilization]] is considered the first civilization of India with direct contact with the rest of the world, evidenced by Mesopotamian seals found in India and [[indus_valley_civilization_and_its_archaeological_significance | Indus Valley Civilization]] seals in Mesopotamia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:53:14">[02:53:14]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:55:04">[02:55:04]</a>. The inability to read the Harappan script, however, limits current understanding of their society <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:58:10">[02:58:10]</a>.

### Dwarka (Indian Atlantis)
Dwarka, an ancient city off the coast of Gujarat, is considered the "Indian Atlantis" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:21:49">[02:21:49]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:52:19">[02:52:19]</a>. It was a well-planned city, potentially a large trading port connecting India with Africa, Europe, and Central Asia, before it submerged underwater <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:24:52">[02:24:52]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:25:59">[02:25:59]</a>. Underwater archaeology efforts are ongoing to explore its extent and significance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:25:13">[02:25:13]</a>. These submerged sites are areas of interest because they represent early connecting points with other civilizations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:28:42">[02:28:42]</a>.

### Delhi and its Layers of History
Delhi, a historical capital, offers layered evidence of human history <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:03">[01:03:03]</a>.
*   **Purana Qila (Old Fort):** This site stands on a mound, an important excavation location revealing layers of human history like "rings on a tree" when dug down <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:38">[01:08:38]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:01">[01:19:01]</a>.
*   **Red Fort:** The Queen's Hamam, a delicate marble bath, is closed to the public to prevent disintegration from traffic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:27:17">[04:27:17]</a>.
*   **Mughal Monuments:** Many contain tunnels leading to Delhi, often shut for security reasons <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:47:45">[04:47:45]</a>.

### [[indian_temples_and_their_philosophical_and_cultural_meanings | Indian Temples]]
[[indian_temples_and_their_philosophical_and_cultural_meanings | Indian temples]] are generally pyramidal, not square, with designs that hold vital philosophical meaning <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:06:06">[04:06:06]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:13:00">[04:13:00]</a>.
*   **Yogini Temple:** Circular in shape, the Parliament of India building is designed after it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:20:20">[04:20:20]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:29:20">[04:29:20]</a>.
*   **Khajuraho and Konark Temples:** These temples, particularly those in Odisha like Konark, are known for their explicit sexual imagery on the exterior. This imagery represents sex as a "fringe layer of life" leading to creation and being part of everyday life, with the austere inner sanctum representing the silence of the divine <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:37:37">[04:37:37]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:51:24">[04:51:24]</a>.

## Challenges in Preservation

### Funding and Public Awareness
Funding for archaeology in India is significantly backward <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:09">[01:12:09]</a>. There's a call for wealthy individuals and businesses to invest in Indian history and culture through philanthropic grants rather than seeking commercial returns on investment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:55">[01:12:55]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:55">[01:13:55]</a>. The Tata Trust is highlighted as a consistent, long-standing example of such support <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:12">[01:13:12]</a>.

There's a concern that Indians often take their history and culture for granted, despite growing up immersed in it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:31">[01:09:31]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:00">[01:10:00]</a>. Understanding cultural practices scientifically is crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:07">[01:10:07]</a>.

### Illegal Trafficking of Antiquities
Countries like India, Egypt, and Italy are major victims of illegal trafficking of antiquities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:57:57">[01:57:57]</a>. Unsecured sites lead to looting, with stolen sculptures often ending up in black markets, major museums, or auction houses, fetching millions of dollars <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:58:00">[01:58:00]</a>. This illegal trade is as significant as the trade in drugs and ammunition worldwide <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:58:22">[01:58:22]</a>. Satellite remote sensing is being deployed to help combat this issue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:57:53">[01:57:53]</a>.

### Geopolitical Factors
Geopolitics plays a role in the revelation and concealment of historical aspects <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:44:03">[01:44:03]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:47:00">[01:47:00]</a>. Historical research is still influenced by powerful individuals and their control over what information becomes publicly available <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:48:47">[01:48:47]</a>.

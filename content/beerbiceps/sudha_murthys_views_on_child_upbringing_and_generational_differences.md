## Generational Differences and Challenges
Sudha Murthy acknowledges that each generation is different and that younger generations have grown up with greater luxury and comfort <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:32:00">[27:32:00]</a>.

### Economic Conditions and Expectations
She contrasts her generation, where sharing was common among four children with one working parent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:00:00">[27:00:00]</a>, with the current generation, where families are smaller, and children have plenty of everything <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:19:00">[28:19:00]</a>. This abundance can lead to a lack of appreciation, as expectations constantly increase (e.g., from local to international holidays) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:24:00">[28:24:00]</a>.

### Mental Health and Resilience
The lack of early exposure to difficulties means that "nothing makes them happy" once they are brought up in prosperity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:28:00">[28:28:00]</a>. This can lead to depression over small things <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:03:00">[29:03:00]</a>. Unlike her generation, which was "used to rejection" and would share and enjoy even with limited means <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:12:00">[29:12:00]</a>, today's children are scared of rejection, leading to extreme reactions like suicide attempts over exam failures or relationship issues <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:25:00">[29:25:00]</a>.

Military-themed films such as *Border* and *LOC Kargil* have had a significant cultural impact on Indian society, particularly in inspiring younger generations and shaping public perception of the armed forces <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:22">[00:49:22]</a>.

## Cultural Influence and Inspiration
*Border*, released in 1997, is noted for drawing its energy from the 1971 war, which inspired a generation of Indians towards the [[cultural_and_operational_dynamics_of_the_indian_army | Indian military]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:36">[00:49:36]</a>. The filmmakers recognized early on the importance of the film <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:54">[00:49:54]</a>. The movie's impact was such that a four-year-old child could be inspired to "be a soldier in life" after seeing characters like Sunil Shetty running into a battlefield <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:20">[00:52:20]</a>. These films are considered very impactful for the [[cultural_and_operational_dynamics_of_the_indian_army | military's branding]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:35">[00:52:35]</a>.

### Filmmaking and Intent
Despite its eventual success, Sunil Shetty initially rejected the role in *Border*, citing concerns about the director J.P. Dutta's hotheadedness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:00">[00:50:00]</a>. It was his mother-in-law who convinced him to take the role <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:38">[00:50:38]</a>. Through working with J.P. Dutta, Shetty realized the director's deep love for the [[cultural_and_operational_dynamics_of_the_indian_army | Indian armed forces]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:17">[00:51:17]</a>. Shetty described *Border* as a "life lesson" because J.P. Dutta only pursued projects he genuinely believed in <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:28">[00:51:28]</a>. The energy of *Border* was attributed to the armed forces and uniform, representing the sacrifices of many individuals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:32">[00:51:32]</a>.

## Military Reception and Portrayal
Members of the [[cultural_and_operational_dynamics_of_the_indian_army | Indian military]] have varying views on these films:
*   Some are grateful for the inspiration *Border* and *LOC Kargil* provided to younger generations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:57">[00:51:57]</a>.
*   Others are critical of certain details, noting that the films may not always be an exact representation of the military <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:04">[00:52:04]</a>.

Despite occasional inaccuracies, these films are widely seen as beneficial for the military's image <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:11">[00:52:11]</a>. The portrayal of soldiers, even a four-year-old's impression of Sunil Shetty running into a battlefield, fosters a desire to serve <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:20">[00:52:20]</a>.

## Government and Army Support
The government and the [[cultural_and_operational_dynamics_of_the_indian_army | Indian Army]] are now actively encouraging more military-themed productions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:42">[00:52:42]</a>. They invite filmmakers to submit scripts and, if approved, provide infrastructure, technical expertise, and personnel to control the filming on set <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:47">[00:52:47]</a>. This collaborative approach aims to produce more authentic and impactful stories about the armed forces <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:02">[00:53:02]</a>.

## Personal Connection and Respect for the Uniform
Sunil Shetty emphasized his deep respect for the uniform and the sacrifices of the [[cultural_and_operational_dynamics_of_the_indian_army | Indian armed forces]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:39">[00:54:39]</a>. He recounts a poignant experience visiting a military hospital in Kargil during wartime, where a young Sikh soldier who had lost an arm in combat expressed admiration for him, deeply affecting Shetty <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:11">[00:56:11]</a>. Such experiences highlight the extreme conditions and pain soldiers endure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:24">[00:57:24]</a>.

The resilience and commitment of soldiers, even those with severe injuries, deeply impressed Shetty <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:38">[00:57:38]</a>. He noted the sheer physical and mental strength required to climb mountains like Kargil with heavy backpacks, reinforcing his belief that the [[cultural_and_operational_dynamics_of_the_indian_army | Indian Army]] is the most powerful in the world <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:51">[00:57:51]</a>. He also acknowledged the vital roles of other forces like the CISF and BSF in safeguarding the country <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:20">[00:58:20]</a>.

For Shetty, the uniform and the characters from films like *Border* symbolize unwavering respect and will be remembered for generations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:44">[00:58:44]</a>. The host concluded that such films help raise awareness and celebrate the military, which is crucial for citizens <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:27">[01:01:27]</a>.

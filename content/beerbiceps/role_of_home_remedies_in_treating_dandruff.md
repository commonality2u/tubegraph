## Understanding Scalp pH and Dandruff

The skin's natural pH is 5.5, indicating it is slightly acidic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:38:00">[03:38:00]</a>. This acidity acts as a protective layer, helping to kill harmful bacteria and infections on the skin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:51:00">[03:51:00]</a>. Individuals experiencing [[understanding_different_types_of_dandruff | dandruff]] often have a scalp pH that is "off" or not as acidic as it should be <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:01:00">[04:01:00]</a>. Both commercial anti-dandruff products and home remedies aim to restore this acidic pH balance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:09:00">[04:09:00]</a>.

It is important to distinguish between common dandruff and seborrheic dermatitis. If you are suffering from seborrheic dermatitis, characterized by severe itching, boils, rashes, and irritation in the scalp and hairline areas, it is advised to consult a dermatologist instead of relying on home remedies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:00">[00:29:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:00">[00:48:00]</a>.

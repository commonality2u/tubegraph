## Biological and Mental Benefits of Meditation

This shift activates the parasympathetic nervous system, which is responsible for "rest and digest" functions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:21">[00:13:21]</a>.

The objective of [[importance_of_spiritual_practices_and_meditation | meditation]] is not to force thoughts to stop, but to be aware of them and consistently return focus to the breath <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:30">[00:13:30]</a>. ## The Role of Breathing Exercises (Pranayama)

Breathing exercises like Pranayama are essential because oxygen is the life force required by trillions of cells in the body for absorption <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:53">[00:19:53]</a>. Increased oxygenation of cells boosts immunity and enhances brain function <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:17">[00:20:17]</a>. Just six deep breaths can initiate a process that shifts one from a state of stress to a state of calm <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:32">[00:20:32]</a>.

A simple Pranayama practice involves sitting with a straight spine, inhaling for a certain count, holding for the same count, and exhaling for the same count, repeated until a sense of calm is achieved <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:58">[00:20:58]</a>. Regular practice (e.g., 15 minutes daily) can improve skin and hair health, accelerate brain function, and provide an alternative perspective, which is particularly beneficial for those experiencing anxiety attacks or other [[mental_health_and_spirituality | mental health]] issues <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:25">[00:21:25]</a>. This simple breathing technique can lead to sustained feelings of well-being for 24 to 48 hours <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:52">[00:21:52]</a>.

## The Impact of Public Scrutiny and Social Media
KL Rahul describes how people often jump on negative comments to make someone who is already feeling down feel even worse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>. For athletes, online trolling and criticism can overshadow positive feedback, even if 90% of fans are supportive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:13:00">[14:13:00]</a>. This hate and negativity are ever-present <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:53:00">[15:53:00]</a>.

### Coping Mechanisms
Upon his debut in Australia, cricketer Ishant Sharma advised KL Rahul to stay away from social media, regardless of performance, to avoid being swayed by either positive or negative comments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:05:00">[16:05:00]</a>.

KL Rahul initially believed he could prove critics wrong, but eventually realized that the constant negativity did affect him <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:05:00">[21:05:00]</a>. Now, he prioritizes his mental peace by avoiding reading about himself online, allowing him to focus on what truly matters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:32:00">[21:32:00]</a>. He emphasizes that players are aware of fan support but must also contend with the "hate and negativity" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:40:00">[15:40:00]</a>.

### Misconceptions and Challenges
Athletes often feel that when they are struggling, some people unfairly assume they are not serious or working hard enough <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:24:00">[19:24:00]</a>. KL Rahul notes that sport does not always directly correlate hard work with immediate results <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:04:00">[20:04:00]</a>.

## [[Balancing career success and personal wellbeing | Challenges of a Cricketer's Life]]
KL Rahul spent his entire life, since age 11, playing cricket, with no "plan B" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:46:00">[07:46:00]</a>. This dedication meant he missed out on typical schooling experiences, friendships, and developing other hobbies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:51:00">[06:51:00]</a>. He realized there was "nothing else to fall back on" apart from cricket <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:18:00">[07:18:00]</a>. This prompted him to consider developing new hobbies to help him detach from the game <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:27:00">[07:27:00]</a>.

The fast-paced nature of a cricketer's career means they often grow up in the public eye <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:40:00">[03:40:00]</a>. Unlike others, cricketers may not have consistent mentors from a young age to prepare them for the pressures of international or IPL levels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:45:00">[04:45:00]</a>. Many learn to handle pressure and challenges on their own <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:27:00">[05:27:00]</a>. He personally received his first big contract at 25-26, after experiencing career ups and downs, which helped him remain more balanced <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:37:00">[29:37:00]</a>.

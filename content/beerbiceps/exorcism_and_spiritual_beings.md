## Understanding Spiritual Entities
In the context of Himalayan Buddhism, spiritual beings are understood in diverse ways. There are spirits that reside in natural environments like plains, mountains, and rivers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:35">[01:03:35]</a>. When these spirits are disturbed, for instance, by construction on a sacred or inhabited site without proper rituals, they can become angry and may [[dealing_with_entities_and_dark_forces | attack]] or possess individuals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:19">[01:04:19]</a>.

Deities such as Tara, Avalokiteshvara, Manjushri, and Vajrapani are recognized, existing in both peaceful and wrathful forms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:08">[00:53:08]</a>. These deities are not seen as external gods but rather as manifestations of one's own true nature, reflecting compassion, wisdom, and power <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:05">[00:54:05]</a>. By chanting their mantras and visualizing them, practitioners connect with their primordial, enlightened true nature <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:50">[00:54:50]</a>.

### Oracles and Spiritual Dimensions
Oracles in Ladakh are individuals who become possessed by "good deities" and can prophesize the future <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:16">[01:16:16]</a>. They can offer guidance, reveal personal information to gain trust, and advise on areas needing spiritual cleansing, such as specific villages or places <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:25">[01:16:25]</a>. These oracles perceive the spiritual dimension, while most humans only see the human dimension <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:01">[01:18:01]</a>. They also provide warnings about individuals or periods of caution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:09">[01:17:09]</a>.

## The Practice of Exorcism
[[exorcism_and_demonic_possession | Exorcism]] is a significant duty for a Rinpoche, particularly when individuals are possessed by spirits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:48">[01:02:48]</a>. The goal is to [[dealing_with_entities_and_dark_forces | exorcise]] the person and protect them from further harm <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:04">[01:03:04]</a>.

### Experiences with Possessed Individuals
The Rinpoche describes [[experiences_with_demonic_entities | experiences with demonic entities]] where innocent Ladakhi individuals, often women, become possessed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:12">[01:05:12]</a>. While possessed, they may speak in different languages, such as perfect Nepali or Tibetan, or with a man's voice <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:33">[01:05:33]</a>. The spirits might identify themselves as being from unknown places, which later prove to be real locations where someone died tragically, now wandering and seeking liberation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:37">[01:05:37]</a>.

The Rinpoche approaches these situations with compassion, understanding that the spirits are also suffering and expressing their anger, revulsion, or dissatisfaction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:35">[01:04:35]</a>. Direct conversations with these spirits have occurred <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:06">[01:05:06]</a>.

### Spiritual Prowess and Protection
Spirits often acknowledge the Rinpoche's spiritual prowess, showing fear and requesting not to be harmed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:21">[01:06:21]</a>. They may perceive the Rinpoche's accompanying "protection deities," like Chuckman (a powerful male deity) and Palden (a female deity like Kali Mata), on their shoulders or forehead <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:51">[01:06:51]</a>. These deities protect the Rinpoche's activities from obstacles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:23">[01:07:23]</a>.

When a spirit is particularly "naughty" and resists, the Rinpoche may exert a "fierce" or "wrathful" presence without physical harm <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:18">[01:08:18]</a>. This involves a heightened focus, a "fire burning" internally, and a dark reddish-black and white visual perception, which forces the spirit to release the person <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:28">[01:08:28]</a>. With age and practice, this wrathful side becomes more controlled <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:42">[01:13:42]</a>.

## Role of Rituals and Practices
The practice of [[the_relationship_between_spiritual_practices_and_energy_cleansing | Tumo]], part of the six yogas of Naropa, is a secret practice involving the invocation of inner fire (Kundalini Yoga) through breathing and visualization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:42">[00:57:42]</a>. This generates immense body heat, melting snow around meditators in sub-zero Himalayan conditions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:12">[00:59:12]</a>. The purpose of Tumo is to burn negative emotions and inflame compassion, leading to a state of "great joy" or "deino" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:08">[01:00:08]</a>.

Other [[role_of_rituals_and_objects_in_paranormal_activities | rituals and objects in paranormal activities]] include chanting powerful mantras and performing Havan (fire rituals) for cleansing and spiritual advancement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:39">[01:17:39]</a>. The core of Vajrayana Buddhist practice involves receiving empowerment from a deity, then visualizing oneself as that deity and chanting their mantra for three years and three months continuously <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:19">[01:12:19]</a>. This intensive practice allows practitioners to absorb the deity's personality and powers, leading to transformation and manifestation of those qualities in daily life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:24">[01:13:24]</a>.

## Skepticism and Belief
While not superstitious, the Rinpoche states that direct observation of possessed individuals speaking different languages and exhibiting altered states has led to a belief in such phenomena <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:05">[01:06:05]</a>. This firsthand experience overrides initial [[skepticism_and_belief_in_spiritual_phenomena | skepticism and belief in spiritual phenomena]].

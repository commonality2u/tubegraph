## Societal and Spiritual Significance of Pleasure

In ancient times, physical intimacy was believed to be the starting point for everything <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:48">[00:01:48]</a>. This belief extended to governance and societal stability. For instance, when a king ascended the throne, a version of the [[the_philosophy_and_purpose_of_the_kamasutra | Kamasutra]] would be commissioned for their kingdom <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:01">[00:02:01]</a>. The prevailing philosophy was that if a couple could share good, mutually pleasurable intimacy, their relationship would be stable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:17">[00:02:17]</a>. A stable relationship would lead to a stable society, and a stable society would ensure a stable kingdom <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:26">[00:02:26]</a>. Success in any aspect of life, whether as a warrior, economist, or trader, was believed to depend on success in one's physical intimate life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:39">[00:02:39]</a>.

Furthermore, the pursuit of love culminating in reaching God was believed to begin at the physical level before it could be elevated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:57">[00:02:57]</a>. The [[the_philosophy_and_purpose_of_the_kamasutra | Kamasutra]] emphasizes pleasure, arousal, and desire because ancient cultures considered sexual energy the highest form of energy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:16">[00:03:16]</a>. The state of arousal and elevating pleasure increases metabolism more than physical exercise, activating every part of the body, including heart rate, breathing, and hormone secretion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:36">[00:03:36]</a>. This energy is deemed powerful enough to create life, a feat only God can accomplish, thus reinforcing its status as the highest form of energy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:06">[00:04:06]</a>.

## The Kamasutra as a Scientific Treatise

Texts like the Arthashastra, Bhagavad Gita, and Kamashastra were written as treatises in a scientific manner and dedicated to God <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:14">[00:04:14]</a>. They were composed in the form of hymns and poetry, not intended for the average person, but rather written in metaphors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:26">[00:04:26]</a>. To truly understand them, one needs to read accompanying commentaries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:40">[00:04:40]</a>.

The common misconception that the [[the_philosophy_and_purpose_of_the_kamasutra | Kamasutra]] is solely about sexual positions stems from the fact that its chapter on positions is the only part easily understood by a normal reader without knowledge of the underlying metaphors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:47">[00:04:47]</a>. However, even these positions had a deeper reason and context within the text <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:10">[00:05:10]</a>.

## Key Learnings on Pleasure and Relationships

The [[teachings_on_relationships_and_intimacy_from_the_kamasutra | Kamasutra]] offers several key takeaways that apply universally, transcending gender or sexual orientation, focusing on the understanding of pleasure itself <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:03">[00:06:03]</a>:

### 1. The Power of Language
The text uses no bad or abusive language, even when referring to sexual organs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:29">[00:06:29]</a>. Historically, these terms were spoken of with beauty; for example, the vagina was called the "chandan Mahal," and the clitoris, the "Madan chhatri" (umbrella of the love God) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:53">[00:06:53]</a>. The words used define one's actions, and shifting away from abusive terms changes one's approach to intimacy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:09">[00:07:09]</a>.

### 2. No Violence and Emphasis on Consent
The [[gender_equality_and_consent_in_the_kamasutra | Kamasutra]] absolutely forbids any form of violence or aggression <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:29">[00:07:29]</a>. It explicitly states that a man should not lash out or hit hard in a moment of passion, citing examples of kings who harmed their mistresses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:36">[00:07:36]</a>. This text is notably progressive for its time, granting women a platform of equality and the explicit right to consent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:00">[00:08:00]</a>. It is up to the woman to decide if the passion used is too aggressive; if a lover bites too hard, she should tell him to stop, and if he doesn't, she should bite him back twice as hard <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:07">[00:08:07]</a>.

### 3. Understanding Different Forms of Pleasure
Men and women experience pleasure differently <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:44">[00:08:44]</a>. A man's pleasure is likened to fire: quick to ignite and quick to extinguish, starting from below and moving upward <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:50">[00:08:50]</a>. A woman's pleasure is compared to water: it starts from above and goes downward, taking a long time to come to a boil and to cool down <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:00">[00:09:00]</a>. The text emphasizes that if men and women are left to their own devices, their pleasure will never coincide due to these differences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:21">[00:09:21]</a>. The goal is to learn how to align arousal and share pleasure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:30">[00:09:30]</a>. This involves slowing everything down <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:40">[00:09:40]</a>. The [[teachings_on_relationships_and_intimacy_from_the_kamasutra | Kamasutra]] specifically advises men that if the first few times of lovemaking are rushed and not pleasurable for the woman, it could put her off intimacy for the rest of her life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:54">[00:09:54]</a>, thus teaching men how to approach a woman's pleasure.

[[teachings_on_relationships_and_intimacy_from_the_kamasutra | Conversation]] is highlighted as paramount for pleasure and foreplay <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:30">[00:10:30]</a>.
*   **Before lovemaking**: The man should tell stories to the woman to help her shed inhibitions and ignite her desire. These stories can be gossipy or naughty, eliciting a reaction from her <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:43">[00:10:43]</a>.
*   **After lovemaking**: The conversation is even more crucial as it defines how quickly she will return to the bed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:23">[00:11:23]</a>. The man should tell beautiful stories of lovers who come together and are happy, convincing her that the shared experience was positive for both <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:35">[00:11:35]</a>.

## The Origins of Archaeology

The Archaeological Survey of India (ASI), for example, was founded by the British, with Alexander Cunningham, a British officer and treasure hunter, being a key figure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:47:00">[03:47:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:58:00">[03:58:00]</a>. ## [[Theories on Human Origin and Migration | Theories on Human Origin and Migration]]

## Archaeological Evidence and Limitations
### Undiscovered History
The Earth's crust holds vast amounts of undiscovered history. For example, in Delhi, which has been a capital for various civilizations, including Indraprastha, Mughal, and British, layers of human history can be observed by digging through sites like Purana Qila (Old Fort) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:03:00">[18:03:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:13:00">[18:13:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:35:00">[18:35:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:01:00">[19:01:00]</a>. The crust also contains relics of extinct animals like dinosaurs and mammoths <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:22:00">[19:22:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:29:00">[19:29:00]</a>.

### Interpreting Limited Data
Archaeological interpretations of early human history are based on very small datasets. For instance, prehistoric cave paintings, while showing signs of sophisticated minds and imagination, don't always come with bone fragments or other direct evidence of the people who created them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:54:00">[15:54:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:36:00">[15:36:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:48:00">[15:48:00]</a>. This often leads to romanticizing small data sets, which then form the basis of consensus about origins <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:04:00">[16:04:00]</a>.

The speaker emphasizes the need to be open to new findings that might change current understandings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:30:00">[12:30:00]</a>.

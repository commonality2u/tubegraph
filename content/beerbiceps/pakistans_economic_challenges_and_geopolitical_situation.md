## Geopolitical Landscape

### Relationship with the United States
Historically, the US has been Pakistan's traditional support base <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:05">[00:33:05]</a>. For a long time, the Americans "kept India in play but also kept India in check" by promoting Pakistanis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:29">[00:33:29]</a>. This was evident in situations like the 1990s when the US imposed sanctions on India's space and defense organizations (ISRO, DRDO) for developing missiles, while turning a blind eye to Pakistan's activities with the A.Q. Khan network <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:41">[00:33:41]</a>. The turning point was considered to be the Kargil misadventure from Pakistan's side <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:33:59">[00:33:59]</a>.

The source of US financial support for Pakistan is now drying up <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:11">[00:34:11]</a>.

### Relationship with China
Pakistan is deepening its ties with China, entering what is referred to as the "China Club" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:33">[00:37:33]</a>. However, Chinese financial assistance comes with risks, as it can be a debt trap <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:21">[00:34:21]</a>.

### Relations with Afghanistan and the Taliban
The Pakistani state invested significantly in the Taliban <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:42">[00:34:42]</a>. However, the Taliban, now governing Afghanistan, are asserting their independence and posing a serious security challenge to Pakistan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:47">[00:34:47]</a>. There have been instances of gunfire at the border between Pakistan and Afghanistan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:03">[00:35:03]</a>, with the Taliban demanding payment to curb Pakistani Taliban terrorists on their soil <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:11">[00:35:11]</a>.

### Relations with West Asia
For a long time, Pakistan relied on countries in West Asia based on a "Muslim Ummah Brotherhood thing" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:32">[00:35:32]</a>. However, [[Indias foreign policy and its relationship with the US and China | India]] has significantly strengthened its relationships with Arab states like UAE, Oman, and Saudi Arabia in recent years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:37">[00:35:37]</a>, leading to reduced support for Pakistan from these nations, including on the [[Challenges and Realities of the Kashmir Conflict | Kashmir]] issue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:51">[00:35:51]</a>.

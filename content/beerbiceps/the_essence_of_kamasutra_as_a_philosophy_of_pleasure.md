## Misconceptions and True Intent

The Kamasutra, like other ancient texts such as the Arthashastra, Bhagavad Gita, and Natya Shastra, was written as a treatise in a scientific manner <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:14">[00:04:14]</a>. These sciences were dedicated to God and written in the form of hymns and poetry <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:25">[00:04:25]</a>. They were not intended for the average person but rather written in metaphors, requiring commentaries for full comprehension <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:38">[00:04:38]</a>.

A significant [[misconceptions_and_true_teachings_of_the_kamasutra | misconception about the Kamasutra]] stems from the fact that for the normal reader, the only chapter that makes immediate sense is the small section on sexual positions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:50">[00:04:50]</a>. This has led to the common misunderstanding that the Kamasutra is solely about positions, without understanding their underlying reasons or the broader context of the text <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:04">[00:05:04]</a>.

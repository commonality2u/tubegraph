## The Role of Hormones in Fat Burning

[[intermittent_fasting_overview_and_benefits | Intermittent fasting]] significantly impacts the body's hormonal system, particularly [[the_role_of_human_growth_hormone_hgh_in_fasting | Human Growth Hormone]] (HGH) and insulin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:11">[00:01:11]</a>.

*   **[[the_role_of_human_growth_hormone_hgh_in_fasting | Human Growth Hormone]] (HGH)**: Fasting increases [[the_role_of_human_growth_hormone_hgh_in_fasting | HGH]] levels in the body, which typically decline with age <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:07">[00:02:07]</a>. [[the_role_of_human_growth_hormone_hgh_in_fasting | HGH]] helps in muscle recovery, muscle and bone growth (in younger individuals), and significantly aids in fat mobilization, meaning the body uses fat for fuel <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:41">[00:03:41]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:08">[00:04:08]</a>. Higher [[the_role_of_human_growth_hormone_hgh_in_fasting | HGH]] levels make it easier to gain muscle and [[dietbased_weight_loss_strategies | burn off fat]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:15">[00:04:15]</a>. It also acts as a "protein sparer," signaling the body to preserve muscle and [[dietbased_weight_loss_strategies | burn off fat]] when food is scarce <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:28">[00:04:28]</a>. Studies show that longer fasts, such as a 36-hour fast, can lead to a 250% increase in [[the_role_of_human_growth_hormone_hgh_in_fasting | HGH]] levels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:04">[00:05:04]</a>.
*   **Insulin Sensitivity**: [[intermittent_fasting_overview_and_benefits | Intermittent fasting]] improves insulin sensitivity, making the body more efficient at using insulin to build muscle and [[dietbased_weight_loss_strategies | burn fat]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:25">[00:05:25]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:23">[00:06:23]</a>. Younger individuals generally have higher insulin sensitivity, which decreases with age <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:32">[00:06:32]</a>.
*   **Adrenaline and Noradrenaline**: IF also increases levels of adrenaline and noradrenaline, the "fight or flight" hormones, which boost metabolism and increase alertness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:07">[00:07:07]</a>.

Together, these hormonal changes enhance nutrient repartitioning, allowing the body to more efficiently use consumed food for muscle gain rather than fat deposition <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:47">[00:06:47]</a>.

## Fasting Period Guidelines (16 Hours)

The typical [[intermittent_fasting_overview_and_benefits | intermittent fasting]] protocol involves a 16-hour fasting period, where only certain liquids are consumed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:29">[00:01:29]</a>.

*   **Allowed Beverages**:
    *   Water <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:23">[00:01:23]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:15">[00:11:15]</a>.
    *   Black coffee without sugar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:26">[00:01:26]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:18">[00:11:18]</a>.
    *   Green tea without sugar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:27">[00:01:27]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:21">[00:11:21]</a>.
    *   Black tea without sugar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:20">[00:11:20]</a>.
*   **Avoidances**: Coffee and tea should ideally be avoided from a general health perspective if possible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:25">[00:11:25]</a>.

## Eating Period Guidelines (8 Hours)

During the 8-hour eating window, the focus is on consuming healthy, nutrient-dense foods <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:32">[00:01:32]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:37">[00:01:37]</a>.

### Caloric Deficit for Fat Loss
The most crucial rule for [[dietbased_weight_loss_strategies | fat loss]] is maintaining a caloric deficit, meaning consuming fewer calories than your body needs to maintain its current weight <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:25">[00:10:25]</a>. With [[intermittent_fasting_overview_and_benefits | intermittent fasting]], a slightly larger caloric deficit can be maintained without the fear of losing muscle mass due to the beneficial hormonal changes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:00">[00:11:00]</a>.

### Macronutrient Intake
The eating window typically involves three meals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:01">[00:12:01]</a>.

*   **Protein**:
    *   Meet your individual [[nutrition_tips_for_teenagers_including_protein_and_carbohydrate_intake | protein requirements]], which for most individuals range from 70 to 100 grams per day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:39">[00:11:39]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:55">[00:11:55]</a>.
    *   Distribute protein intake across each of your three meals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:06">[00:12:06]</a>.
*   **Fat**:
    *   Keep fat intake moderate, neither too high nor completely eliminated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:08">[00:12:08]</a>.
    *   Include healthy fats like egg yolks and nuts to help reach target calorie levels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:18">[00:12:18]</a>.
*   **Carbohydrates**:
    *   Choose healthier, more filling complex carbohydrates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:26">[00:12:26]</a>.
    *   Examples include oats, wheat, brown rice, and sweet potato <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:37">[00:12:37]</a>.
    *   Avoid simple carbs like white rice, white bread, and maida <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:30">[00:12:30]</a>.

### General Nutrition
*   **Vegetables**: Ensure a high intake of vegetables <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:47">[00:12:47]</a>.
*   **Breaking the Fast**: A good way to break your fast is with a little bit of fruit, as this is an excellent time for your body to utilize fruit sugar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:52">[00:12:52]</a>.

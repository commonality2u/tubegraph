### [[special_forces_mindset_and_training | Special Forces Mindset]] and Airborne Operations
Special Forces are recognized for their high efficiency within the military <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:25:00">[01:25:00]</a>. [[special_forces_mindset_and_training | Airborne forces]], as the name suggests, operate from the air, demonstrating unparalleled reach and penetration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:00">[00:46:00]</a>. Every individual in an Airborne unit is capable of exiting an aircraft at speeds of 600 km/h with full gear <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:01:00">[03:01:00]</a>.

Airborne operations are among the most difficult to undertake due to the high casualty rate associated with jumping, especially in challenging terrains like jungles, high-altitude areas, or snow-covered peaks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:19:00">[03:19:00]</a>. Despite these risks, the expectation is that every man will jump <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:57:00">[03:57:00]</a>. The mindset in Airborne units is that "we don't exist," emphasizing their discreet and effective penetration capabilities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:22:00">[03:22:00]</a>.

A critical aspect of [[special_forces_mindset_and_training | Special Forces mindset and training]] involves reinforcing readiness immediately after an incident. For example, if a mishap occurs during a jump, all boots on the ground are given new parachutes and told to board aircraft to jump again the same night, ensuring the "fear factor" does not set in <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:22:00">[04:22:00]</a>.

### Fighting in Built-Up Areas (FIBUA)
Fighting in Built-Up Areas (FIBUA) is considered one of the most challenging operations in military terminology <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:16">[00:06:16]</a>. This is because there's no set pattern; every house has a different layout and demographic, making operations unpredictable and complex <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:22">[00:06:22]</a>. Initial [[combat_experience_and_mentality | combat experiences]] can be harrowing, like confronting an armed individual immediately upon entering a room <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:42">[00:10:42]</a>. In such moments, training takes over, enabling soldiers to react decisively even when momentarily stunned <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:38">[00:11:38]</a>.

### Weaponry and Tactics
Different weapons are preferred for different [[combat_scenarios_and_survival_instincts | combat scenarios]].
*   **Pistols:** Preferred for close-quarters combat in small rooms or built-up areas due to their swiftness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:27:00">[02:27:00]</a>. However, they have less accuracy due to recoil <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:37:00">[02:37:00]</a>.
*   **AK-47/56:** Preferred for slightly more open areas due to their stability provided by a buttstock <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:46:00">[02:46:00]</a>.
*   **Grenades:** Considered largely ineffective by some, as they often fail to detonate as expected <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:48:00">[02:48:00]</a>. They have a limited killing zone (2-3 meters) and are typically used to flush out enemies from hiding spots or bunkers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:37:00">[02:37:00]</a>. Unexploded grenades must be safely disposed of <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:22:00">[02:22:00]</a>.
*   **Automatic Grenade Launcher (AGL):** A more sophisticated and precise alternative to grenades, capable of pinpoint shots from distances up to 800-900 meters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:57:00">[02:57:00]</a>.

### Unconventional Food and Water
Commando training includes teaching soldiers to consume "anything that walks, crawls, creeps, or flies" for survival <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:32:00">[05:32:00]</a>. This includes eating snakes (raw or cooked) and insects, which are considered tasteless or require seasoning <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:25:00">[05:25:00]</a>. Snails are considered particularly unappetizing but edible when necessary <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:02:00">[04:02:00]</a>. Crocodile meat, however, is surprisingly considered better than chicken <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:06:00">[05:06:00]</a>.

To obtain potable water in scarcity, soldiers are trained to create organic filters using mud, sand, stone, and charcoal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:50:00">[04:50:00]</a>.

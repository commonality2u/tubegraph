## Recommended Sleep Duration

Teenagers should aim to sleep at least 8 to 9 hours every single night <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:20">[00:03:20]</a>. This duration is crucial for the body's recovery and overall [[height_growth_during_teenage_years | growth]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:25">[00:03:25]</a>.

## The Role of Sleep in Recovery and Growth

During sleep, the body undergoes significant recovery processes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:25">[00:03:25]</a>. Daily activities, such as climbing stairs or walking, accumulate as damage in the body <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:30">[00:03:30]</a>. Sleep allows the body to repair itself <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:25">[00:03:25]</a>.

While sleeping, the body releases hormones like Human Growth Hormone (HGH), which are vital for overall body recovery and, importantly, for overall [[height_growth_during_teenage_years | growth]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:37">[00:03:37]</a>. To maximize [[height_growth_during_teenage_years | height]], it is important to maximize the amount of sleep <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:46">[00:03:46]</a>.

## Quality of Sleep

Beyond just the length of the sleep cycle, the quality of sleep is equally important <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:51">[00:03:51]</a>. Teenagers who consistently sleep late and only get around 6-7 hours of sleep per night are not getting enough, and this can negatively affect their [[height_growth_during_teenage_years | height]] in the long term <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:04">[00:04:04]</a>. Focusing on a proper sleep cycle is considered one of the best things a teenager can do for themselves <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:12">[00:04:12]</a>.

## Challenges

### Licensing Burden
One significant hurdle for [[challenges_faced_by_startups_in_india | startups in India]] is the extensive licensing requirement. To sell a sandwich, it might require up to 36 licenses and No-Objection Certificates (NOCs) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:05">[00:01:05]</a>. This is notably more complex than the 14 licenses needed to open an arms factory <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:08">[00:01:08]</a>.

### Stressful Profession
Being a chef in a busy kitchen is considered one of the three most stressful jobs in the world <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:15">[00:00:15]</a>.

### Historical Societal Perceptions
Historically, the idea of a girl becoming a chef was largely unheard of <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:09">[00:00:09]</a>.

## Key Strategies for India

### Focus on Micro Markets
For anyone building a business in India, a crucial strategy is to target a micro-market <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:27">[00:00:27]</a>. It is advisable not to build anything generic with the expectation that the entire country will like it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:30">[00:00:30]</a>. This approach is vital for navigating the diverse Indian market, a common theme among [[challenges_and_strategies_of_indian_startups | challenges and strategies of Indian startups]].

### Authenticity
Authenticity in one's story and a hands-on approach resonate more deeply with the audience than content created by someone else <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:44">[00:00:44]</a>.

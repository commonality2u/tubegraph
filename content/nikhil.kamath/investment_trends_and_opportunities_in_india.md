## Investment Landscape in India

The investment ecosystem in India comprises various entities, each with distinct characteristics and investment strategies.

### Types of Investors

*   **Angel Investors**: These individuals typically invest their own capital in very early-stage companies, usually pre-launch [00:38:48](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:48">[00:38:48]</a>. The investment quantum can range from zero up to one to two crores [00:39:51](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:51">[00:39:51]</a>. In 2008, the US had 300,000 registered angels, with about 5% becoming professional angels [00:39:01](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:01">[00:39:01]</a>. In India, there are estimated to be 6,000 to 25,000 active angel investors [00:44:51](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:51">[00:44:51]</a>.
    *   **Returns**: 90% of angel investors often perform poorly or lose money [00:40:40](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:40">[00:40:40]</a>. The best odds for an angel investor are to invest in something where their value addition is not just money, but expertise and network [00:41:32](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:32">[00:41:32]</a>, [00:42:24](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:24">[00:42:24]</a>.
    *   **Role in Ecosystem**: Angels are crucial for the ecosystem as they take risks where institutions cannot, supporting companies when friends and family money runs out [00:44:28](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:28">[00:44:28]</a>.

*   **Venture Capital (VC) Firms**: These are organized fund managers raising institutional capital from limited partners (LPs) like family offices and sovereign funds [00:37:39](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:39">[00:37:39]</a>, [00:45:35](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:35">[00:45:35]</a>. Fund sizes can range from micro VCs (as small as $5-10 million or 100 crores) to large funds ($2.85 billion) [00:46:04](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:04">[00:46:04]</a>, [00:47:51](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:51">[00:47:51]</a>.
    *   **Investment Stages**: VCs invest from seed stage (e.g., three to four million or less) up to growth stages, supporting companies towards profitability or exit [00:49:50](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:50">[00:49:50]</a>.
    *   **Popular Indian VCs**: Examples include Accel, Peak XV, Lightspeed, Nexus, Elevation, Blume, Chiratae, Kalaari, and Matrix [00:49:03](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:03">[00:49:03]</a>.
    *   **Fund Structure**: Most VC firms operate on a 10-year fund life with a two-year extension option [00:49:27](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:27">[00:49:27]</a>. They charge a 2% annual management fee on the funds raised and typically take a 20% "carry" (percentage of gains) on distribution, after returning all capital and fees [00:49:44](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:44">[00:49:44]</a>, [00:51:07](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:07">[00:51:07]</a>.
    *   **LP Expectations**: International investors expect VCs to deliver around 25% compounded returns, currency adjusted [00:09:04](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:04">[00:09:04]</a>. However, for LPs, India is still an "experimental" market, and they are seeking consistent returns from the region [00:31:16](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:16">[00:31:16]</a>.
    *   **Exits**: VCs exit companies through public offerings (IPOs), sales to strategic buyers (M&A), or secondary sales to later-stage funds [00:50:07](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:07">[00:50:07]</a>.
    *   **Power Law**: Venture capital is a power law business, meaning a small percentage of investments (typically 2-5%) generate 80-90% of the returns [00:26:47](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:47">[00:26:47]</a>, [00:27:01](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:01">[00:27:01]</a>.

*   **Private Equity (PE) Firms**: PE funds primarily invest in established, mature, and profitable companies [00:48:44](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:44">[00:48:44]</a>. Their investment horizons are shorter, typically aiming for an IPO or exit within two to three years [00:56:50](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:50">[00:56:50]</a>.
    *   **Returns**: PE has historically been more consistent in cash returns than VC in India, partly due to being in the ecosystem longer and investing in diverse non-tech sectors [00:57:52](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:52">[00:57:52]</a>, [00:58:00](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:00">[00:58:00]</a>.
    *   **AUM**: PE assets under management (AUM) in India are estimated at about $200 billion, with 85% being foreign capital [00:57:40](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:40">[00:57:40]</a>.
    *   **Popular PE Firms**: Include Blackstone, KKR, and Warburg Pincus [00:59:16](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:16">[00:59:16]</a>.

*   **Category II (Cat II)**: Funds that can invest in a broader range of securities, including debt [00:47:14](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:14">[00:47:14]</a>. Private equity funds often fall into this category [00:47:20](<a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:20">[00:47:20]</a>.

## The Desire for Online Validation

The current generation exhibits a strong desire for online validation, particularly evident in the prevalence of phenomena like Instagram's "blue ticks" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:18">[01:19:18]</a>. This desire for online recognition and perceived importance on social media platforms is significantly higher for those aged 20 to 30 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:57">[01:19:57]</a>. Some believe this trend to be "unhealthy" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:05">[01:20:05]</a>, as it can lead to the perception of one's social media persona becoming more important than their real identity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:39">[01:19:39]</a>.

Businesses are exploring ways to [[The psychology and impact of social media on mental health | serve this need for validation]] in Gen Z <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:32">[01:20:32]</a>. Strategies include building platforms that provide recognition and positive feedback, such as anonymous compliment apps <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:00">[01:22:00]</a>. Receiving a response or acknowledgement from a celebrity, or having one's content shared by a famous person, is highly valued and something people might even pay for <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:11">[01:22:11]</a>. Another tactic, observed in luxury goods and certain restaurants, is "false scarcity" or "arrogance as a sales strategy," where limiting availability or denying access increases desire <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:44">[01:22:44]</a>.

## Evolving Consumption and Marketing Strategies

Gen Z's consumption patterns highlight a demand for high-quality service at a reasonable cost, indicating that good service is now an expectation, not a premium feature <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:57:15">[01:57:15]</a>. They are not willing to endure poor service for cheap products <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:57:48">[01:57:48]</a>. This generation is also less inclined towards ownership and more towards convenience, often preferring to rent or use services for a short period rather than purchasing items <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:57:52">[01:57:52]</a>. This extends to fashion, where renting clothes is becoming more common <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:01">[01:08:01]</a>.

Marketing to Gen Z is evolving from traditional TV and newspaper ads to more integrated digital experiences. While direct Instagram ads may not always be effective for some <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:24:06">[01:24:06]</a>, many consumers, including Gen Z, shop directly through Instagram ads, especially for independent brands <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:24:14">[01:24:14]</a>. The shopping experience is seamless, often linking directly from a post to a website for purchase <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:24:39">[01:24:39]</a>. Platforms like TikTok in the US have a large e-commerce vertical based on this model, where users can buy products directly seen in videos <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:24:52">[01:24:52]</a>.

A more [[Role of Social Media and Influencers in Product Promotion | organic and genuine approach to product discovery]] is preferred. Consumers are more likely to buy something if they discover it naturally within content they are already consuming, such as seeing an item in a show or a friend's social media post, rather than through a direct advertisement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:25:21">[01:25:21]</a>. This kind of "passive" exposure feels less pushed and more authentic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:25:49">[01:25:49]</a>.

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) is the preferred payment method for Gen Z in India, chosen for its ease and widespread acceptance by vendors, from online orders to street food <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:28:08">[01:28:08]</a>. For businesses targeting Gen Z, UPI is the "go-to payment" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:03">[01:29:03]</a>, and content is a crucial part of the communication strategy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:04">[01:29:04]</a>.

## The Role of Influencers and Content Creation

Instagram is considered the most relevant social media app for younger demographics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:15">[01:29:15]</a>, attracting all age groups due to its convenience and diverse offeringsâ€”shopping, entertainment, short and long videos, talks, music, and branded content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:51">[01:29:51]</a>.

The line between traditional actors and influencers is blurring, but it's not seen as a threat to actors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:08:48">[02:08:48]</a>. Influencer marketing's long-term effectiveness is questioned as more people become influencers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:26:29">[02:26:29]</a>. While financially rewarding for those with large followings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:09:08">[02:09:08]</a>, being an influencer requires significant energy, time, and strategic thinking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:09:27">[02:09:27]</a>.

The "right reason" to be an influencer stems from a genuine passion, such as educating people about financial principles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:11:26">[02:11:26]</a>. However, the role has become "sexy," leading some young people to pursue it for superficial reasons <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:10:43">[02:10:43]</a>. Influencers, like all individuals who post content, have a responsibility for what they promote <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:09:47">[02:09:47]</a>. Ultimately, the audience decides what content to consume, and many differentiate between genuine and inauthentic content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:11:50">[02:11:50]</a>.

## Navigating the Challenges of Public Presence

Growing up in an "Instagram world" has led to everything being presented as a "presentation" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:22:01">[02:22:01]</a>. This creates a disconnect between one's social media depiction and their true self, leading to significant personal struggles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:22:20">[02:22:20]</a>. The younger generation often molds themselves into "acceptable versions" for social media, which can be catastrophic for their mental well-being <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:23:07">[02:23:07]</a>. The advice is to stay true to oneself, as online popularity and trends are transient, and only authentic, deep content will last <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:23:30">[02:23:30]</a>.

On the topic of "cancel culture" and trolling, individuals in the public eye often receive criticism due to their public choices and family background <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:28:26">[02:28:26]</a>. Acknowledging one's privilege and being open to criticism is important, especially when working in public-facing sectors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:29:08">[02:29:08]</a>. The best way to deal with trolling is not to care, as giving attention to negative comments only makes them more bothersome <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:30:02">[02:30:02]</a>. Humor and a strong support system also help in navigating such experiences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:26:33">[02:26:33]</a>.

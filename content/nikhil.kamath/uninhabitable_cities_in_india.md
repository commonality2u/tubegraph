A significant concern regarding the future of Indian cities is the declining [[air_quality_in_india | air quality]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:12">[00:00:12]</a>.

It has been noted that in India, real estate prices are influenced by air quality, with higher costs in areas where the Air Quality Index (AQI) is most severe <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:22">[00:00:22]</a>. This highlights a peculiar aspect of [[challenges_and_opportunities_in_indian_real_estate | real estate]] in the country <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:22">[00:00:22]</a>.

There is a projection that within the next 5 to 10 years, most cities in India could become "uninhabitable" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:28">[00:00:28]</a>. This statement underscores the severity of the environmental challenges faced by urban centers in the country <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:28">[00:00:28]</a>.

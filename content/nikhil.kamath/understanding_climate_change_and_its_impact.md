## Defining Climate Change

[[Environmental awareness and education | Environmental awareness and education]] is crucial for understanding [[climate_change_impacts | climate change impacts]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:11">[00:46:11]</a>.
*   One perspective defines climate change as a system where the rate of change in temperature, ocean acidification, and other impacting parameters is higher than the ability of people or organisms in the locality to adapt <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:57">[00:46:57]</a>.
*   The Earth is now warmer on average than at any point in the last 100,000 years, showing an incredible spike in the last 30-40 years that correlates with human involvement and increased carbon <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:30">[00:47:30]</a>.
*   It is a social and political reality that will require changing how human beings organize economies and societies to both reduce emissions and adjust to inevitable harms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:51">[00:54:51]</a>.
*   The planet is heating up, with 2023 being the hottest year in many thousands of years, and 2022 being 1.2 degrees higher than pre-industrial temperatures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:30">[00:55:30]</a>.
*   The Earth has a blanket on it due to gases like carbon dioxide and methane, trapping heat and increasing temperature <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:31">[00:58:31]</a>. While greenhouse gases are essential for life, the issue lies with the excess amount <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:58">[01:00:58]</a>.
*   The intensity and frequency of extreme weather events—such as floods, droughts, and landslides—are increasing like never before <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:12">[00:59:12]</a>. India experiences one extreme weather event per day, as defined by the Indian Meteorological Department (IMD) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:14">[01:00:14]</a>.

### Scientific Evidence

Scientists use ice core data, like the Vostok ice core data going back 800,000 years, to measure trapped air bubbles and determine past carbon dioxide percentages and temperatures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:35">[00:48:35]</a>. This data shows a one-to-one relationship between spikes in carbon dioxide and spikes in temperature <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:37">[00:49:37]</a>. Climate models, when applied with current scientific understanding, accurately predict temperature increases that match real-world data <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:00">[00:50:00]</a>.

## [[climate_change_impacts | Climate Change Impacts]]

The observed impacts of climate change are widespread:
*   **Ocean Warming and Acidification**: 90% of excess heat is in the oceans <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:47">[00:51:47]</a>, causing water to expand and contribute to sea-level rise <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:27">[00:53:27]</a>. The ocean's ability to absorb excess carbon dioxide is reaching a limit, meaning more will remain in the air <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:37">[00:53:37]</a>.
*   **Extreme Weather Events**: Examples from 2023 include floods in Libya (200 times expected rainfall, 50 times more likely in a warming world) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:52">[00:53:52]</a>, wildfires in Canada and Hawaii <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:17">[00:54:17]</a>, floods in Himachal, and the Yamuna River reaching a 45-year peak <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:22">[00:54:22]</a>.
*   **Ecological Disruptions**: Mass extinctions are projected for marine species <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:17">[00:56:17]</a>. Wildfires in Australia wiped out over a billion animals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:04">[00:56:04]</a>.
*   **Social and Economic Disruptions**: Climate change is causing significant displacement of people due to floods, droughts, and agrarian distress, leading to migration issues <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:53">[01:04:53]</a>.

## Temperature Projections and Carbon Budget

Currently, the world is on track for approximately a 2.6°C rise by 2100 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:32:27">[01:32:27]</a>. To limit warming to no more than 1.5°C above pre-industrial levels, future combustion of fossil fuels must be limited to 500 gigatons of carbon <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:55:31">[01:55:31]</a>. Given current annual emissions of about 55 gigatons, this budget would be depleted in under 10 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:55:55">[01:55:55]</a>. For 2 degrees, the budget is closer to 1,000 gigatons <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:56:00">[01:56:00]</a>.

## Perception and Awareness

Public perception of climate change is often influenced by media portrayals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:32:50">[01:32:50]</a>. Movies depicting apocalyptic scenarios can create a disconnect when reality doesn't match the extreme imagery <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:38:21">[01:38:21]</a>. There is a need for films and media to show the lived experiences of climate impacts, such as heatwaves and floods, rather than abstract "end of the world" scenarios <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:38:43">[01:38:43]</a>. A significant portion of the population, particularly the wealthy, may not feel directly affected by climate change, believing they are protected by their resources <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:34:24">[01:34:24]</a>. Conversely, farmers, fishermen, and nomads in India deeply understand the changes, even if they don't use the scientific term "climate change" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:32:28">[01:32:28]</a>.

## [[influence_of_economic_systems_and_policies_on_climate_change | Influence of economic systems and policies on climate change]]

Political willingness to bear the costs of adjusting to climate change has been low globally <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:47">[01:06:47]</a>. The shift to low-carbon economies presents an economic opportunity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:06">[01:07:06]</a>, with countries now seeing future economic gains in building these industries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:06">[01:07:06]</a>. China has assiduously built green industries, making the West play catch-up <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:15">[01:07:15]</a>.

However, the speed of transition is critical, as obligations can impose costs on the economy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:27">[01:07:27]</a>. Climate finance, intended for developing countries, is not adequately reaching Africa or even India, largely going to China and other nations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:48">[01:09:48]</a>. This is partly due to the high cost of capital in those regions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:04">[01:10:04]</a>. The quality of climate money is also an issue, with a significant portion given as loans, which further indebts countries like Pakistan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:52">[01:10:52]</a>.

## [[climate_change_solutions | Climate Change Solutions]]

### Energy Transition
Energy transition is the single biggest [[climate_change_solutions | climate change solution]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:17">[01:17:17]</a>.
*   **Renewable Energy**: Solar panel costs fell 80% and wind costs fell 50% in the last decade, making them cost-competitive with coal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:15">[01:13:15]</a>. Solar power can be as low as 2 rupees per unit, while solar with storage is around 4 rupees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:24:21">[01:24:21]</a>.
*   **Grid Storage**: Innovation in grid storage, particularly non-lithium-ion based solutions, is crucial for scaling up solar and wind power, as current lithium-ion batteries are prioritized for vehicles and face supply deficits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:41">[01:13:41]</a>. India's grid storage needs are estimated at 200 GW hours by 2030 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14:26">[01:14:26]</a>.
*   **Decentralized Solutions**: Shifting towards decentralized energy systems, similar to the move from landlines to cell phones, offers a huge opportunity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:42:04">[01:42:04]</a>. For example, using solar for agricultural pump sets can take demand off the grid and be combined with rainwater harvesting and appropriate cropping patterns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:26:04">[01:26:04]</a>.
*   **Discom Reform**: India's electricity distribution companies (discoms) are often unprofitable due to political decisions to provide free or discounted power to farmers and low-income groups, leading to theft and accounting holes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:06">[01:20:06]</a>. This means they view renewable energy by individuals as a threat <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:28:47">[01:28:47]</a>. Average technical and commercial losses can range from 15% to 30% across states <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:35">[01:22:35]</a>. Reimagining electricity as a service rather than just providing electrons can incentivize payment and enhance productivity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:25">[01:29:25]</a>.
*   **Waste Management**: Cities like Indore are converting household food waste into compressed natural gas (CBG) using biomethanation technology <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:01:00">[02:01:00]</a>. This solves waste problems and generates energy for public transport, like buses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:01:11">[02:01:11]</a>.

### [[debate_on_nuclear_fusion_in_climate_change | Debate on nuclear fusion in climate change]]

*   **Fission**: Nuclear fission is seen as an important part of the solution, particularly Generation 4 reactors (small modular reactors) using thorium <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:53:16">[01:53:16]</a>. While there's public concern about safety and disaster, the track record of nuclear fission is good <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:53:51">[01:53:51]</a>. Its supporters argue that not building nuclear plants means relying on fossil fuels, leading to deaths from air pollution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:54:20">[01:54:20]</a>.
*   **Fusion**: Nuclear fusion is often joked to be "30 years away" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:54:41">[01:54:41]</a>, and while recent breakthroughs show net energy gain, it is unlikely to have a scalable impact on the climate crisis before 2050 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:54:47">[01:54:47]</a>.

The challenge with nuclear fission in India is its slow gestation time (10-12 years) and historical under-delivery on plans, creeping from 6 GW to 7.5 GW when 50 GW was projected since the 1970s <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:56:15">[01:56:15]</a>. Furthermore, India's energy R&D budget heavily favors nuclear and fossil fuels (a third goes to fossil fuels, the rest is nuclear) over clean energy, which receives only 3% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:57:43">[01:57:43]</a>. This is significantly less than China and the US <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:58:09">[01:58:09]</a>.

### [[perspectives_on_geoengineering_and_environmental_governance | Perspectives on geoengineering and environmental governance]] and Carbon Capture

[[Perspectives on geoengineering and environmental governance | Geoengineering]] methods include adding sulfur to the stratosphere, marine cloud brightening, and ocean seeding/fertilization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:31:09">[02:31:09]</a>. While some advocate for research, there's concern about the "arrogance of man" in further disrupting nature to fix problems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:31:51">[02:31:51]</a>. The risk of cascading tipping points (e.g., permafrost thaw releasing vast amounts of carbon, or the collapse of the West Antarctic Ice Sheet) means research is needed, but deployment requires very strong governance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:32:20">[02:32:20]</a>.

Carbon capture, particularly at the source (e.g., in cement production), has a role, but direct air capture is currently too energy-intensive for scalable deployment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:22:17">[02:22:17]</a>. The demand for carbon dioxide removal and solar radiation management in IPCC models is often driven by the inability to close emission targets without such assumptions, and by the politics of oil-producing countries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:25:53">[02:25:53]</a>.

### Global Governance and Policy
The [[the_role_of_the_intergovernmental_panel_on_climate_change_ipcc | Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC)]] compiles authoritative existing information through working groups, with governments approving reports line by line <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:05">[07:05]</a>. This complex, painstaking process highlights the slow and cumbersome nature of global cooperation on an urgent issue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:48">[08:48]</a>.

*   **Ozone Layer Success and Lessons**: The Montreal Protocol, which addressed the ozone layer depletion, was a success in phasing out specific chemicals (CFCs) that were destroying the ozone layer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:14:26">[02:14:26]</a>. This was facilitated by an alternative technology owned by the same multinational corporations (DuPont, ICI) that made the original chemicals, which was then purchased using public money and provided to developing countries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:15:21">[02:15:21]</a>. However, this model does not directly apply to climate change, as fossil fuel burning is deeply embedded in the Industrial Revolution and human development <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:19:25">[02:19:25]</a>. The subsequent chemicals (HFCs) are now understood to have a major impact on climate change <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:17:21">[02:17:21]</a>.
*   **Carbon Pricing**: A carbon tax on fossil fuels at production or import points is generally considered a good economic mechanism <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:34:50">[02:34:50]</a>. However, measures like Europe's Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM) and the US's Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) are viewed by some as trade protectionism that hinders global cooperation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:35:17">[02:35:17]</a>.
*   **International Agreements**: The Kyoto Protocol established a rule-based system for developed countries to reduce emissions and transfer finance and technology to developing nations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:41:25">[02:41:25]</a>. The Paris Agreement, however, shifted to Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs), where each country voluntarily states what it will do <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:40:09">[02:40:09]</a>. This bottom-up approach, while allowing sovereign flexibility, has been criticized for not enforcing sufficient action globally and leading to a projected 3°C rise <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:42:51">[02:42:51]</a>.

## Lifestyle and Consumption
The primary climate issue related to food is not necessarily vegetarianism or veganism, but rather how food is grown, the quantities consumed, and the associated waste <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:43:56">[02:43:56]</a>. Industrial farming systems, which use chemicals, antibiotics, and create monocultures (like sugar cane requiring excessive water) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:51:14">[02:51:14]</a>, are major contributors to environmental problems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:45:09">[02:45:09]</a>. Conversely, traditional Indian farming, which integrates livestock (cows, buffaloes) for dung and risk mitigation, is crucial for farmers' survival and adaptability to climate change <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:46:17">[02:46:17]</a>.

## [[career_opportunities_in_energy_transition_and_climate_innovation | Career opportunities in energy transition and climate innovation]]

For young individuals looking for [[career_opportunities in energy transition and climate innovation | career opportunities]], several areas present significant potential:
*   **Bioenergy, Waste Management, and Circularity**: These interconnected fields promote circularity and offer solutions particularly relevant to India's context <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:05:52">[02:05:52]</a>. Examples include upcycling waste materials and creating products from temple flowers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:07:00">[02:07:00]</a>.
*   **Decentralized Energy Services**: Opportunities exist in providing energy services that enhance productivity in rural areas, such as solar solutions for farmers that increase purchasing power and livelihood opportunities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:08:02">[02:08:02]</a>.
*   **Green Businesses and Innovation**: Supporting or starting green businesses that offer disruptive innovations, like making ink from carbon in the air or recycling single-use plastic, are areas of interest <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:11:00">[02:11:00]</a>.
*   **Sector-Specific Sustainability**: All businesses, from iron and steel to cement or textiles, must incorporate sustainability in real terms, not just in words <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:54:30">[02:54:30]</a>.

For entrepreneurs, it is advised to ground business ideas in solving a real climate problem, rather than solely focusing on business opportunity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:52:51">[02:52:51]</a>. For the government, key actions include acting at scale on existing plans and creating a low-carbon development commission to systematically identify and support intersectional opportunities (e.g., food and climate, buildings and urban planning) within a comprehensive climate law <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:54:02">[02:54:02]</a>.

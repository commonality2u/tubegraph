The [[India Stack and digital ID creation | India Stack]] is a set of open APIs and digital public goods that form the backbone of India's digital infrastructure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:55:00">[01:55:00]</a>. It is particularly relevant for young entrepreneurs in India seeking to build new ventures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:00">[00:48:00]</a>. The philosophy behind its design is to create digital public infrastructure at population scale and open it up with Application Programming Interfaces (APIs) to unleash innovation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:40:00">[01:40:00]</a>.

## The Aadhaar Project: Digital ID at Population Scale

The idea for a digital ID program was discussed in the book "Imagining India" in 2008 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:33:00">[06:33:00]</a>. In July 2009, the government offered Nandan Nilekani the role to lead the [[Aadhaar and digital ID implementation | Aadhaar project]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:00:00">[07:00:00]</a>. While the initial request was to build a unique ID, it was made digital from the outset, designed to be online and verifiable in real-time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:51:00">[09:51:00]</a>. Unlike the paper-based US Social Security Number from 1936, Aadhaar was a pioneering effort as nobody had done a digital unique ID at this scale before <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:27:00">[10:27:00]</a>.

The main drivers for Aadhaar were:
*   **Efficient [[Business strategies in India | benefit transfer]]**: To provide a solid and robust way to send money to people through India's growing welfare programs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:36:00">[11:36:00]</a>.
*   **Inclusion**: To provide identification to the large portion of the population, especially those in villages, who lacked formal IDs like birth certificates, thus impeding their progress and formal system inclusion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:10:00">[12:10:00]</a>.

Nandan Nilekani committed to delivering 600 million Aadhaar IDs within 4.5 years, a target he met before stepping down in 2013 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:40:00">[14:40:00]</a>. By 2016, India had reached 1 billion Aadhaar IDs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:55:00">[20:55:00]</a>, and currently, 1.3 billion people have the ID <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:01:00">[15:01:00]</a>.

## Expanding the Stack: eKYC, eSign, and DigiLocker

After Aadhaar established unique digital identities, additional capabilities were built on top of it:
*   **eKYC (Electronic Know Your Customer)**: This feature allowed for online authentication and verification of identity, enabling real-time KYC using Aadhaar data (name, address, date of birth, mobile number, sex, email ID) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:35:00">[15:35:00]</a>. It was approved by various regulators (RBI for banks, DoT/TRAI for mobile, IRDA for insurance, SEBI for capital markets) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:35:00">[16:35:00]</a>.
    *   **Impact**:
        *   **Banking**: Accelerated the Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana program from 2015, which aimed to provide bank accounts to everyone. eKYC reduced the time to achieve widespread banking penetration from an estimated 46 years to 9 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:12:00">[17:12:00]</a>.
        *   **Mobile**: Reliance Jio utilized Aadhaar eKYC to onboard a million customers a day after its launch in 2016, quickly reaching 100 million customers in six months and transforming the mobile industry <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:20:00">[18:20:00]</a>.
*   **eSign (Electronic Signature)**: Launched in 2015, eSign enabled digital signatures for legal documents like property agreements and contracts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:00:00">[19:00:00]</a>.
*   **DigiLocker**: Also launched around 2015-2016, DigiLocker is a general-purpose, secure digital document wallet that allows individuals to store and access various documents (driver's license, PAN card, educational certificates) on their phone or in the cloud <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:25:00">[19:25:00]</a>. It now holds billions of documents <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:03:00">[20:03:00]</a>.

BHIM, an app launched by NPCI (National Payments Corporation of India), was the first mobile payment app that exposed people to easy digital transactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:38:00">[22:38:00]</a>.

## UPI: The Payment Revolution

[[UPI and digital payment systems | UPI]] is a Unified Payments Interface, an underlying protocol that facilitates real-time payments between different apps and bank accounts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:36:00">[23:36:00]</a>. It simplifies transactions, making sending money as easy as sending an email or a WhatsApp message <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:13:00">[28:13:00]</a>. Key features include the use of UPI IDs, account numbers, mobile numbers, or virtual names as addresses for transactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:21:00">[28:21:00]</a>.

*   **Scale**: UPI now handles 14.4 billion transactions a month <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:12:00">[23:12:00]</a>.
*   **Drivers of adoption**: Demonetization provided an initial push by limiting cash, and the pandemic further accelerated its adoption due to people's reluctance to handle physical money <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:53:00">[28:53:00]</a>.
*   **Global potential**: UPI has built connections with other countries, such as Singapore's Fast Pay, enabling cross-border remittances by linking UPI to foreign bank accounts and converting currencies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:41:00">[25:41:00]</a>. However, this requires building "bridges" and aligning regulatory rules, including those for fraud prevention, money laundering, and capital account convertibility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:11:00">[26:11:00]</a>.

The consistent focus on "population scale" means ensuring digital technology reaches everyone for societal transformation and inclusion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:00:00">[30:00:00]</a>.

## Data Exchange and Financial Inclusion: Account Aggregation

Conceived in 2016, Account Aggregation is an architecture designed to aggregate and exchange financial data (and potentially other data) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:02:00">[33:02:00]</a>. It allows individuals to construct their personal balance sheets by gathering data from various financial institutions like banks, mutual funds, insurance providers, and even tax systems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:40:00">[33:40:00]</a>.

*   **Functionality**: It acts as a real-time data exchange, not a repository. The "Account Aggregator" acts as a conduit, passing encrypted and signed data directly from a "financial information provider" (e.g., a bank) to a "financial information user" (e.g., a lender) with the user's consent, without peeking into the data itself <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:45:00">[34:45:00]</a>.
*   **Impact**: It significantly reduces friction for financial activities, particularly in lending, by enabling quick, digital verification of financial eligibility for individuals and businesses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:29:00">[36:29:00]</a>. It also enables the creation of Personal Finance Management apps and other Capital Market applications <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:18:00">[37:18:00]</a>.

## Beyond Finance: Fastag and ONDC/Beckn

The "platform thinking" approach of the India Stack extends beyond individual identity and financial transactions:
*   **Fastag**: Designed in 2010, Fastag gives every vehicle a unique ID linked to a digital wallet or bank account <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:45:00">[37:45:00]</a>. It streamlined toll collection on highways, reducing waiting times from hours to seconds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="38:06:00">[38:06:00]</a>. Fastag also serves as a platform for vehicles to make digital payments on the fly, with potential uses for congestion charges or parking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="38:41:00">[38:41:00]</a>.
*   **Beckn Protocol and ONDC (Open Network for Digital Commerce)**:
    *   **Beckn** is a general-purpose, open-source transaction protocol – a set of rules and language for how transactions occur between parties <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="44:28:00">[44:28:00]</a>.
    *   **ONDC** uses Beckn as its underlying protocol <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="45:31:00">[45:31:00]</a>. This approach aims to "unbundle" integrated e-commerce transactions, allowing a consumer on one app to order from any supplier listed on the ONDC network and use any delivery agent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="43:35:00">[43:35:00]</a>.
    *   **Impact**:
        *   **Commerce**: ONDC currently handles 12 billion transactions a month <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="45:39:00">[45:39:00]</a>.
        *   **Mobility**: Namayatri, built on Beckn, handles 50,000 transactions a day, allowing users to find autos and pay drivers directly, with the platform charging a daily SaaS fee to the driver rather than taking a commission <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="45:42:00">[45:42:00]</a>.
        *   **Energy**: A "Unified Energy Interface" is being built for buying and selling electricity, potentially for charging stations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="45:58:00">[45:58:00]</a>.
    *   This approach is expected to disrupt many industries, reduce monopolies and duopolies, and create more opportunities by opening up markets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="47:55:00">[47:55:00]</a>.

## The Role of AI in India's Digital Future

The focus for India in AI is not on building large language models (LLMs), which are rapidly becoming commoditized by massive investments from Western companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="49:11:00">[49:11:00]</a>. Instead, India's strategy is to leverage this technology at a low cost to deliver value to its people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="50:40:00">[50:40:00]</a>.

*   **Indian Languages**: A top priority is to use AI to bridge the communication gap in India, with its 22 official languages and thousands of dialects <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="50:51:00">[50:51:00]</a>. By enabling people to speak to computers in their mother tongue, AI can dramatically increase access to information, for example, allowing a farmer to access planting techniques in Hindi <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="51:27:00">[51:27:00]</a>.
*   **Voice Automation**: Voice-controlled automation is seen as crucial, especially for those who cannot read or write, making digital access universal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="52:37:00">[52:37:00]</a>.

AI is viewed as pervasive, like electricity, integrating into every aspect of life to improve people's lives in education, health, and farming <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="52:10:00">[52:10:00]</a>.

## Finnet: Marrying Traditional Finance with Cryptographic Tech

Finnet is an architecture that brings together the traditional financial system with the possibilities offered by cryptographic techniques, like blockchains <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="55:59:00">[55:59:00]</a>. It leverages powerful ideas such as immutability and public reconciliation of transactions, but separates them from the "ideology" and speculative currency aspects of cryptocurrencies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="57:15:00">[57:15:00]</a>.

Finnet allows for the tokenization of different assets (e.g., bank deposits, bonds, stocks) and their high-speed trading, with the ability to convert them back to their original form <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="59:48:00">[59:48:00]</a>.

*   **Potential Use Cases**:
    *   **Capital Markets**: New types of ETFs and consumer products that can be traded very fast <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:32:00">[01:00:32:00]</a>.
    *   **Banking**: Tokenized deposits and collateralization of private assets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:46:00">[01:00:46:00]</a>.

This framework aims to turbocharge the mainstream economy by introducing high-volume, low-cost transaction capabilities into existing systems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="59:08:00">[59:08:00]</a>.

## Future Outlook

*   **CBDCs (Central Bank Digital Currencies)**: CBDCs are seen as another category within the Finnet architecture, with significant use cases in wholesale transactions, particularly for faster cross-country settlement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:24:00">[01:01:24:00]</a>.
*   **Capital Account Convertibility**: India's capital account is expected to liberalize progressively over the next 20 years, driven by increasing inflows from remittances (currently $108 billion, projected to reach $200 billion), FDI, and FII <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:51:00">[01:01:51:00]</a>.
*   **Crypto World**: While acknowledging technological advantages in crypto, the speaker points to its inherent issues arising from building financial systems outside regulatory authority, leading to concerns like money laundering and terrorist financing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:56:00">[01:02:56:00]</a>. Finnet aims to integrate the technology advantages into the mainstream economy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:06:00">[01:03:06:00]</a>.

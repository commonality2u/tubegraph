## Gazal Alagh: Lessons from a Humble Upbringing
Gazal Alagh, co-founder of Mama Earth, shares how her early life influenced her [[Entrepreneurial Journey and Challenges | entrepreneurial journey]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:06">[00:06:06]</a>.

### Family Dynamics & Financial Hardship
Born and raised in Chandigarh in a humble middle-class family <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:25">[00:02:25]</a>, Gazal was likely the first woman in her family to work outside the home, as her parents were not "pro sending women out for jobs or working" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:42">[00:02:42]</a>. However, her parents shared a strong conviction for their children's independence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:02">[00:03:02]</a>.

A pivotal moment occurred when she was around 12-13 years old: her father's business, run with his brothers, experienced friction and an unfair distribution of wealth after her grandparents passed away <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:09">[00:04:09]</a>. This broken trust was an eye-opener <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:54">[00:04:54]</a>. Gazal felt helpless regarding money and comforting her father, who broke down and showed signs of depression <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:10">[00:07:10]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:41">[00:07:41]</a>. The family incurred a significant debt of 40-50 lakhs, which was a huge amount at the time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:03">[00:09:03]</a>.

### Mother's Influence & Financial Independence
Her mother, a lifelong housewife, demonstrated incredible resilience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:01">[00:08:01]</a>. She sold her gold savings to help her father restock the shop and even started taking tuitions for children to contribute financially <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:37">[00:10:37]</a>. This instilled in Gazal the belief that women have the ability to change situations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:46">[00:09:46]</a> and inspired her to embody her mother's courage and determination <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:50">[01:10:50]</a>.

These early financial struggles left a profound mark. Gazal's father taught her the crucial lesson that "Money only comes home when the business makes profit" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:15">[00:10:15]</a>, and the family operated on a fixed budget <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:41">[00:11:41]</a>. This experience instilled in her a deep understanding of the value of money and a subconscious drive for financial security <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:42">[00:25:42]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:12">[00:26:12]</a>.

### Pursuing Passion & Building Confidence
Her mother's advice that "Till the time you're taking money from anybody else... they will make decisions for you. The day you start earning your own money is the day you get to choose where to spend it on" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:11:20">[02:11:20]</a> drove Gazal to seek financial independence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:11:20">[02:11:20]</a>. In college, she started giving tuitions to earn her own money <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:22:25">[02:22:25]</a>.

Gazal also struggled with self-image as a child due to her darker complexion and height <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:44">[00:13:44]</a>. Her mother's efforts to compliment her and introduce her to magazines helped her overcome this insecurity and build confidence in her appearance after grade 12 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:58">[00:15:58]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:39">[00:16:39]</a>. This newfound self-confidence became a significant part of her overall confidence as an entrepreneur <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:51">[01:10:51]</a>.

The experience of her father's trust being broken also fostered a strong emphasis on loyalty in all her relationships, professional and personal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:00">[01:09:00]</a>. She finds it hard to forget when loyalty is broken, although she strives to forgive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:30">[01:19:30]</a>.

## Manish Kapoor: The Power of Hustle and Creative Vision
Manish Kapoor, founder of Rare Rabbit, grew up in a very different environment, but his childhood also forged his [[character_traits_of_successful_entrepreneurs | character traits of successful entrepreneurs]].

### Early Life & Family Separation
Manish was born and raised in Mumbai, in a bustling textile market area <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:13">[01:15:13]</a>. His father, a "great businessman and a great hustler," also separated from his brother due to business disagreements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:35">[01:15:35]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:01">[01:22:01]</a>. Though Manish was too young to remember the specifics, he later understood the nuances of the business split through his father's stories <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:43">[01:16:43]</a>. His father's relentless focus on profit and quick rotation of stock made a strong impression <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:57">[01:22:57]</a>.

Manish experienced a childhood with limited access to luxuries, relying on neighbors to watch movies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:16:12">[01:16:12]</a>. His mother was very hard on him, as he was a rebellious child who engaged in activities like stealing money and cars <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:34">[01:19:34]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:02">[01:20:02]</a>. He learned the importance of "juice in the game" (profit) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:13:10">[02:13:10]</a>.

### Entrepreneurial Drive & Aesthetics
From a young age, Manish showed a strong affinity for business, even carrying his father's VIP suitcase to school to feel like a "businessman" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:11:14">[02:11:14]</a>. He also possessed an innate "eye for design and color" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:52">[01:17:52]</a> and a love for fashion, being inspired by movie stars and even stitching his own clothes from stolen fabric <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:00">[01:20:00]</a>. This early immersion in textiles and his personal passion for style laid the groundwork for his future in the fashion industry.

Manish's father's willingness to take risks and hustle to establish his own business after the family split heavily influenced Manish <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:24:26">[01:24:26]</a>. His father's "no ego" approach, even approaching a chairman directly without proper English, instilled in Manish the importance of being "shameless and relentless" in pursuing business opportunities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:47:56">[01:47:56]</a>. Manish believes this ability to bend one's back and have no ego is a superpower in a world full of ego <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:18:11">[02:18:11]</a>.

Manish's entrepreneurial drive led him to start working with his father in the textile mills at 16-17, designing textiles and creating mood boards <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:28:56">[01:28:56]</a>. He didn't receive a salary, as in his family's Marwari tradition, children are expected to add value rather than be paid back for their upbringing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:29:40">[01:29:40]</a>.

### Risk-Taking and Resilience
Manish's career is marked by significant risk-taking, such as taking a 37 crore loan (a huge amount with only 10 crore to his name) to build his own factory <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:03:01">[02:03:01]</a>. This period of stress and depression was overcome by his relentless effort and willingness to do everything himself to save costs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:08:38">[02:08:38]</a>. He believes that most businesses are created from personal realization of a missing need <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:22:02">[02:22:02]</a>.

## Ritesh Agarwal: Serendipity and the Rebellious Optimist
Ritesh Agarwal, founder of OYO, attributes his success to a combination of upbringing, a rebellious nature, and a strong belief in Serendipity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:34:51">[03:34:51]</a>.

### Parental Encouragement & Rebellious Nature
Ritesh was the youngest of four siblings, growing up in a small town in Odisha with a predominant tribal population <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:45:30">[02:45:30]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:48:01">[02:48:01]</a>. His parents, particularly his mother, strongly encouraged education as a way to "graduate out of the town" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:48:33">[02:48:33]</a>. His eldest sister, an overachiever who went to engineering school and secured a good job, served as a role model and source of peer pressure for the younger siblings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:54:06">[02:54:06]</a>.

Ritesh describes himself as the "rebel" among his siblings, often "naughty" and engaging in activities his parents wouldn't have been comfortable with for his older siblings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:50:22">[02:50:22]</a>. This rebellious nature, coupled with being the youngest and receiving more leniency from his parents, fostered his contrarian mindset <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:01:31">[03:01:31]</a>.

### The Spark of Entrepreneurship
His eldest sister's mention of an "entrepreneurship Fest" while he was in middle school first introduced him to the concept of entrepreneurship <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:55:37">[02:55:37]</a>. His rebellious nature led him to define himself by choosing to be an entrepreneur, rather than a pilot like his sister initially aspired to be <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:00:54">[03:00:54]</a>.

Ritesh's passion for sports, learned from his elder brother, also shaped his character. He believes that people good at team sports excel in business due to their ability to collaborate, their pursuit of excellence, and their competitive spirit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:56:47">[02:56:47]</a>.

### Serendipity and Humility
A significant turning point for Ritesh was the Peter Thiel Fellowship, which he learned about through his social network <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:23:45">[03:23:45]</a>. Despite giving himself a "0% chance" of selection, he applied and eventually received the $100,000 grant, believing it was purely due to Serendipity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:29:19">[03:29:19]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:30:11">[03:30:11]</a>. This fellowship, with its condition to drop out of college, aligned with his contrarian view that "education is everywhere... you don't have to go to university for it" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:24:33">[03:24:33]</a>.

Ritesh highlights the importance of humility, especially for young entrepreneurs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:19:05">[03:19:05]</a>. He believes that one can be self-confident (e.g., believing in one's ability to build a successful company) while remaining humble (e.g., acknowledging that one is still learning and willing to ask for help) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:19:32">[03:19:32]</a>. He also stresses the value of admitting mistakes early, as it fosters authentic conversations and prevents deeper problems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:50:51">[03:50:51]</a>.

### Optimism and Perseverance
Ritesh faced numerous challenges, including social pressure (being called a "dog" for focusing on business at 19 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:16:38">[03:16:38]</a>), and the stress of managing a rapidly growing business with significant external investment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:49:26">[03:49:26]</a>. His key trait in dealing with lows is "unreasonable optimism" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:43:10">[03:43:10]</a> and an awareness of the cyclical nature of life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:41:22">[03:41:22]</a>. He believes that if one perseveres, opportunities will arise, and overcoming setbacks is crucial for success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:33:51">[03:33:51]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:56:19">[03:56:19]</a>. This includes being indifferent to noise and non-constructive criticism <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:56:50">[03:56:50]</a>.

## The Pursuit of Fulfillment and Validation
For some, work is a core source of validation and a feeling of being alive. Kriti Sanon, for example, states there is no "end goal" in her work; she loves being on set and finds it makes her "feel alive" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:07">[01:18:07]</a>. This drive is partly linked to a past need for external validation, stemming from childhood pressure to excel academically and in dance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:04">[00:09:04]</a>. Over time, she has gained a "sense of validation" through her professional achievements, which has lessened the need to "prove myself" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:49">[00:19:49]</a>.

### Finding Peace in Restlessness
Kriti describes her current state as a "happy Restless" phase, characterized by a sense of "peace and calm" alongside a "hunger" and "ambition" to push to the next level <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:28">[00:21:28]</a>. This internal peace allows her to [[choosing_work_for_personal_fulfillment | choose work for personal fulfillment]] rather than succumbing to external pressures or market demands <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:58">[00:24:58]</a>. This perspective suggests that true balance is not a static state but a dynamic equilibrium between contentment and aspiration.

### The Role of Passion and Talent
The panelists discuss the interplay of passion, talent, and hard work in achieving success. However, he also acknowledges the necessity of hard work and persistence. Kriti, similarly, believes that while talent is important, one must discover their passion. ## The Importance of Support Systems
The discussion also touches upon the role of family and relationships in providing a sense of stability and support.

Rahul finds immense stability in his relationship with his partner, describing her as his "safe space" who keeps him "grounded" regardless of his professional performance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:31:42">[02:31:42]</a>. He believes a strong relationship helps him "stay a lot more focused" at work <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:32:06">[02:32:06]</a>. Kriti, while more detached, relies on her family for a similar sense of stability, frequently connecting with them despite her busy schedule <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:32:50">[02:32:50]</a>.

The host expresses a nuanced view on relationships, appreciating their wonder but rejecting the "societal construct with a bunch of rules" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:34:04">[02:34:04]</a>, stating he can be "madly in love with someone but not want to subject our relationship to the rules derived from society" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:34:14">[02:34:14]</a>. This highlights different philosophies on finding [[balance_in_entrepreneurship | balance in entrepreneurship]] and life while maintaining personal freedom.

## Advice for Aspirants
The panelists offer unconventional advice for young people aspiring to careers in creative fields or entrepreneurship:

*   **Patience and Persistence:** Jay-Z's quote, "the only genius thing that I did was not giving up," resonates with Badshah, who emphasizes that true passion, combined with belief, will eventually lead to success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:13:37">[02:13:37]</a>.
*   **Originality over Influence:** Badshah advises aspiring musicians to "Be Inspired don't be influenced" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:35:51">[02:35:51]</a> and to embrace their unique "story" with conviction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:36:07">[02:36:07]</a>.
*   **Strategic Entrepreneurship:** For aspiring entrepreneurs, the advice is to avoid "cool" or "flavor of the season" industries, as they tend to be "hard [and] expensive spaces to enter" due to intense competition among passionate individuals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:37:51">[02:37:51]</a>. Instead, focus on identifying growth sectors, like "energy transition" or "health wellness" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:38:34">[02:38:34]</a>, where systemic changes create opportunities.
*   **Fail Fast:** In business, it's crucial to "ditch it fast" if something isn't working within a year or two, as "time is the only finite element" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:39:52">[02:39:52]</a>.

The collective wisdom emphasizes the deep interconnections between one's upbringing, personal values, career choices, and the ongoing journey of self-discovery and adaptation in the face of both success and adversity.

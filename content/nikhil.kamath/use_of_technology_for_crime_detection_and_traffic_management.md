## Command Center Operations

The police operate a 24/7 command center with staff working in three shifts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:44:00">[02:44:00]</a>. This center handles approximately 8,000 to 9,000 calls daily, with about 1,400 requiring active dispatch of patrol vehicles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:21:00">[03:21:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:25:00">[03:25:00]</a>. The average response time for a patrol vehicle to reach a spot after information is sent is around 8 minutes, and the overall response time from the moment a caller makes the call to police arrival is about 10 minutes, which is considered "very good" even by international standards <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:52:00">[02:52:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:06:00">[03:06:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:15:00">[03:15:00]</a>.

To ensure service quality, a follow-up call is made to the complainant after the officer files an action taken report, and an SMS with a feedback link is sent for caller satisfaction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:36:00">[03:36:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:42:00">[03:42:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:48:00">[03:48:00]</a>. Police patrol vehicles are equipped with MDT (Mobile Data Terminal) devices that receive real-time information including caller's name, address, and complaint type, aiding faster navigation to the scene <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:03:00">[04:03:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:07:00">[04:07:00]</a>. Similar to ride-sharing apps, the caller can also track the responding vehicle on a map <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:34:00">[04:34:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:36:00">[04:36:00]</a>.

## Crime Detection Technologies

The CCTV camera command center plays a significant role in crime detection:
*   **Facial Recognition:** The system stores photos of "bad characters" and can recognize them when they appear on camera <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:56:00">[04:56:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:00:00">[05:00:00]</a>.
*   **Number Plate Recognition:** This technology has been particularly effective in solving hit-and-run cases by identifying vehicle number plates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:13:00">[05:13:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:19:00">[05:19:00]</a>.

To counter issues like corruption and harassment, the police utilize:
*   **Body-Worn Cameras:** These cameras are live-streamed to the control room and cannot be switched off by officers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:54:00">[02:54:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:57:00">[02:57:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:00:00">[03:00:00]</a>. Their mandatory use during enforcement, especially for cases like drunk driving, has helped absolve policemen from false complaints, as recorded footage serves as evidence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:52:00">[02:52:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:09:00">[03:09:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:46:00">[03:46:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:52:00">[03:52:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:55:00">[03:55:00]</a>.
*   **IoT Devices and Geofencing:** All police vehicles are fitted with IoT devices that monitor their GPS location, ensuring officers are at their assigned posts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:24:00">[02:24:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:26:00">[02:26:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:28:00">[02:28:00]</a>. Policemen are also geofenced to specific junctions, triggering an alert if they leave the designated area without explanation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:38:00">[02:38:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:40:00">[02:40:00]</a>.

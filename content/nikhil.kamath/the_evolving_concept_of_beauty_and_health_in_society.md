## Core Pillars of Health

Despite varying individual approaches, a consensus emerges on fundamental [[health_and_fitness_theories | health and fitness theories]]:

*   **Sleep**: Prioritizing 7-8 hours of quality sleep is crucial for overall health and recovery <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:08:08">[02:08:08]</a>.
*   **Consistency in Movement**: Regular physical activity, whether structured workouts or general movement throughout the day, is vital <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:48">[00:28:48]</a>. This includes stretching, mobility work (like hot yoga or animal flow), and even just standing during meetings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:38:01">[01:38:01]</a>.
*   **Mindful Eating**: Being particular about food choices, avoiding excessive outside food, and managing portion sizes are key. The importance of starting meals with salads as a "hack" for portion control is mentioned <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:57:07">[01:57:07]</a>. Mukesh also notes that food is a powerful drug, both abused for anxiety and underutilized as an antidepressant (exercise) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:59:53">[01:59:53]</a>.
*   **Mental Well-being**: Engaging in meditation, hobbies, or simply processing thoughts and emotions to avoid keeping things bottled up is seen as essential for overall well-being <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:20">[01:09:20]</a>.
*   **Recovery**: Incorporating practices like massages, heat therapy (sauna), and cold therapy (cold showers/plunges) can significantly aid recovery and boost immunity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:04">[01:04:04]</a>.

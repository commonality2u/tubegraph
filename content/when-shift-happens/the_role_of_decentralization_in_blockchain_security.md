## Nars Protocol: A Universal Trust System

The protocol ensures that information previously considered secret, like transactions or keys, is no longer so, as it aims to [[decentralization_vs_centralization_in_crypto_platforms | decentralize trust itself]] from an infrastructure perspective <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:00">[00:18:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:55">[00:44:55]</a>.

Centralized principles often force reliance on systems that cannot be truly trusted, leading to inherent risks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:21">[00:46:21]</a>.

### Decentralization and Trust in the Digital World
Nars Protocol aims to apply the concept of trust, similar to how it naturally exists in a village where everyone knows who to trust <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:55">[00:43:55]</a>. They use mathematical principles, including post-quantum level resilience, to allow machines to validate and verify each other, establishing a digital "consensus" of trust <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:30">[00:44:30]</a>.

## Quantum Resistance and Blockchain Security
Nars Protocol claims to be the first post-quantum blockchain in production <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:26">[00:46:26]</a>.
### Why Quantum Resistance Matters
Current cryptography, known as elliptic curve cryptography, is vulnerable to quantum computers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:06">[00:47:06]</a>. While classical computers would take billions of years to break these keys, quantum computers are exceptionally good at mathematical computations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:51">[00:47:51]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:40">[00:49:40]</a>. Specialists predict quantum computing will become a significant threat in the next 5 to 7 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:10">[00:49:10]</a>.

The risk is that data can be harvested now, stored, and decrypted in the future once quantum computing power is sufficient <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:01">[00:50:01]</a>. This includes sensitive information, nuclear codes, and even cryptocurrencies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:18">[00:50:18]</a>. The inability to protect value due to quantum-vulnerable cryptography could lead to the "biggest geopolitical imbalance in our lifetimes" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:50">[00:50:50]</a>.

### Vulnerabilities in Current Blockchains
Blockchains, like other digital systems, are not immune to quantum threats <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:57">[00:50:57]</a>. Nars Protocol addresses this by:
1.  **Decentralizing the infrastructure (Sub-Zero Layer)**: Most blockchain validators operate from centralized points of failure (e.g., cloud servers, individual houses) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:59">[00:53:59]</a>. Nars Protocol aims to ensure these validators can prove their trustworthiness to each other, preventing participation if compromised <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:12">[00:54:12]</a>. This means a new layer of [[advantages_and_challenges_of_deploying_decentralized_infrastructure | decentralized infrastructure]] where trust is inherent.
2.  **Post-quantum transactions**: Even if the infrastructure is secure, transactions themselves (hashes, public keys) are vulnerable to quantum attacks that could enable double spending or manipulation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:51">[00:54:51]</a>. Nars Protocol also applies quantum-safe measures to transactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:08">[00:55:08]</a>.

## Trust by Design
Nars Protocol distinguishes between security and trust <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:59">[00:58:59]</a>. While security is about protection, trust is about the verifiable integrity of a system <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:02">[00:59:02]</a>. For example, in the context of AI, there's no way to prove the integrity of the data source, the model, or the transmission of questions and answers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:54">[00:59:54]</a>.

> "Security is very different than trust. What you want to have is trust about the truth you're seeing." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:00">[00:42:00]</a>

By focusing on "trust by design," Nars Protocol creates a new paradigm that is transversal to any digital system or operation, from smart cars to telecommunications <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:44">[01:00:44]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:04">[00:58:04]</a>. This approach allows for highly resilient environments where the integrity of digital processes can be mathematically proven <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:40">[00:58:40]</a>.

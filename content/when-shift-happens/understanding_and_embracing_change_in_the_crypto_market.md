Anthony Scaramucci, an American financier, broadcaster, and bestselling author, and the founder and managing partner at Skybridge, a multi-billion dollar global investment firm, shares his journey into the crypto market, highlighting how personal philosophy and adaptability are crucial for navigating volatile industries. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:27">[00:00:27]</a>

## A Personal Odyssey into Bitcoin

Scaramucci recounts his initial skepticism towards Bitcoin. This changed when he was in the White House in 2017, where two Federal Reserve officials published a white paper on the digitization of the US dollar over the blockchain, similar to Bitcoin. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:22">[00:40:22]</a> Realizing he was "out of it" as a 53-year-old man, he returned to Skybridge, purchased the URL bitcoin.com, and began his due diligence. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:18">[00:00:18]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:11">[00:41:11]</a>

Despite his institutional investor background, Scaramucci was nervous about buying Bitcoin due to its speculative and volatile nature in 2017. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:19">[00:41:19]</a> He identified three key criteria before making a significant investment:
1.  **Scalability**: Would Bitcoin reach 100 million wallets? <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:05">[00:42:05]</a> By late 2020, it was nearing 80-85 million. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:42">[00:42:42]</a>
2.  **Regulatory Comfort**: Could he get comfortable with US regulation of Bitcoin? <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:11">[00:42:11]</a> The IRS deeming Bitcoin as tangible property was a crucial precursor for a favorable long-term regulatory environment, making it hard to make it illegal in the United States. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:49">[00:42:49]</a>
3.  **Secure Storage**: Where could institutional investors store significant amounts of Bitcoin? <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:22">[00:42:22]</a> Fidelity, Naidig, and Coinbase Pro began offering storage solutions, providing the necessary infrastructure. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:43">[00:43:43]</a>

Once these criteria were met, Skybridge made a $100 million purchase of Bitcoin between October and December 2020, in the $16,000-$20,000 range. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:58">[00:43:58]</a>

## Navigating Crypto Volatility

The [[navigating_opportunities_and_risks_in_the_crypto_market | crypto market]] is known for its volatility, which Scaramucci experienced firsthand. Clients initially called him an "idiot" for investing in Bitcoin, then a "genius" by the end of 2021 when prices soared, and an "idiot" again by the end of 2022 when Bitcoin crashed. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:20">[00:44:20]</a>

> [!INFO] Investment Philosophy for Volatility
> Scaramucci emphasizes the importance of holding onto the asset rather than short-term trading. His personal net worth is now over 70% in Bitcoin, a "byproduct of buying it and sitting in it." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:09">[00:45:09]</a> He considers Bitcoin "digital property" to be held long-term, similar to owning a building. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:17">[00:47:17]</a>

A critical lesson learned from the 2022 market crash, which was exacerbated by fraud and excess leverage, is to **avoid leverage** when holding volatile assets like Bitcoin. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:25">[00:50:25]</a>

> [!WARNING] The Danger of Leverage
> "The leverage, to quote Warren Buffett, is like a spear coming out of the steering wheel. You're in a very fast sports car and you're driving on an icy road... When you need to break, this is when the leverage is going to hurt you the most." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:19">[00:51:19]</a>

He also advises against letting the press manage investments, as news media often seeks eyeballs and controversy rather than intrinsic value, particularly in times of market distress. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:51">[00:49:51]</a>

## Belief and Technology: The Core of Crypto

Scaramucci highlights the convergence of belief and technology as the driving force behind crypto. He believes the anonymous creators of Bitcoin (Satoshi team) aimed to create a hard, unhackable, immutable digital currency that removes trust from central authorities. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:00">[00:53:00]</a> This aligns with the concepts explored in books like "The Bitcoin Standard," which discusses the evolution of money and the power of decentralization. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:07">[00:55:07]</a>

While acknowledging Bitcoin as the ultimate store of value, Scaramucci, as a "Wall Streeter," also sees the utility in other layer-one protocols like Avalanche, Ethereum (though expensive), and Solana. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:39">[00:57:39]</a> He supports the tokenization of traditional assets like stocks and bonds over the blockchain, which can lead to significant cost savings and economic efficiency by bypassing intermediaries. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:42">[00:56:42]</a>

### The Cloud Analogy
The adoption of blockchain technology for tokenization is compared to the initial resistance and eventual embrace of cloud computing. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:16">[00:58:16]</a> Despite initial fears about data security, the cloud's cost savings and efficiency ultimately led to widespread adoption, similar to how centralized power plants replaced individual home generators. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:03">[00:59:03]</a> This demonstrates a broader pattern of technological shifts facing initial skepticism but ultimately leading to greater efficiency.

## Strategic Importance: US Bitcoin Reserve

Scaramucci advocates for a United States strategic Bitcoin reserve, not primarily to help Bitcoin, but to benefit the United States. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:47">[01:01:47]</a> If Bitcoin is indeed the future of value storage and a digital property akin to gold, the U.S. should hold it in reserve, similar to how it reserves gold, oil, and critical minerals. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:08">[01:03:08]</a> This move would strengthen the nation's balance sheet, especially if Bitcoin's value appreciates as predicted. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:32">[01:03:32]</a>

## Life Lessons for Adapting to Change

Scaramucci weaves several profound life lessons into his discussion, offering a holistic perspective on [[resilience_in_business_and_its_importance_in_the_crypto_market | resilience in business and its importance in the crypto market]]:

### The Paradox of Wealth
Having grown up with financial anxiety, Scaramucci's initial mission was to be financially independent. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:09">[00:01:09]</a> However, he realized he placed too much emphasis on it, suggesting he might have been richer with less focus on money. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:17">[00:01:17]</a>

> [!NOTE] The Paradox of Wealth
> "There's a paradox to wealth... I have friends of mine that are billionaires. The interest on the interest is more than they could spend, but they're perpetually anxious to acquire more money." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:23">[00:01:23]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:14">[00:37:14]</a>
Wealth can lead to isolation and a constant state of guard, eroding trust in others. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:20">[00:37:20]</a>

### Overcoming Personal Obstacles
His personal drive for success stemmed from a childhood marked by financial struggle and witnessing his father's anxiety about money. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:17">[00:18:17]</a> Growing up in a poor, industrial area with a family of day workers, his motivation was to break the cycle by getting an education and securing a white-collar job. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:49">[00:21:49]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:01">[00:22:01]</a>

A pivotal moment was his father cashing in his union life insurance policy for $10,000 to help pay for Scaramucci's expensive university education. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:51">[00:24:51]</a> This act instilled a profound sense of responsibility and motivation, changing his life's trajectory. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:31">[00:25:31]</a>

### The Importance of Sharing and Empathy
A key philosophy for business and life, learned from Hong Kong property tycoon Lee Kashing, is to "leave money on the table for your partners." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:12">[00:09:12]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:38">[00:10:38]</a> This approach fosters loyalty and continued business, as people will want to partner with someone who shares. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:48">[00:10:48]</a>

He also emphasizes the importance of overcoming the "dark side" of human nature, particularly envy and comparative analysis. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:02">[00:28:02]</a> Recognizing the primal origins of envy, he advocates for a "transcendental" shift:
> "You will be better off in your life if you can celebrate the successes of your friends." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:32">[00:29:32]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:42">[00:39:42]</a>

This concept aligns with the psychological term "sonder," which describes the realization that every passerby has a life as complex and vivid as one's own. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:41">[00:16:41]</a> Cultivating this level of "exponential empathy" leads to healthier relationships. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:10">[00:18:10]</a>

### Self-Awareness and Acceptance
Scaramucci advises his younger self to "Calm down. It'll all come, but calm down. You don't have to be tight. You could be loose." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:46">[00:08:46]</a> He believes that balance and reduced stress lead to better decision-making. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:11">[00:08:11]</a> This involves self-awareness, confidence, and addressing imbalances in relationships directly rather than through passive aggression. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:46">[00:13:46]</a>

He advocates for therapy that focuses on future progress rather than dwelling solely on past traumas. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:10">[01:10:10]</a>

### Embracing Mortality
A central theme in Scaramucci's philosophy, particularly from his book "From Wall Street to the White House and Back," is the acceptance of death. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:37">[01:09:37]</a> He believes that understanding our own mortality is liberating, enabling us to live fully and without fear, pursuing what we love. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:12">[01:12:12]</a>

> "You have to live like you really mean it. You can't be living in fear. What are you fearing? Your life's going to end in death anyway. Go go go go for the thing that you really want to do." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:58">[00:35:58]</a>

The worst advice, he contends, is person-centered advice that does not consider the individual's unique life arc and goals. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:06">[01:08:06]</a> Instead, advice should empower individuals to pursue their passions, even if they involve risk. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:57">[01:08:57]</a>

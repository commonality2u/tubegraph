He attributes much of his success to being based in the Bay Area, where the "freedom to fail" is unique and enables continuous reinvention <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:39">[00:34:39]</a>.

## Genesis of Solana

Yakovenko stumbled into crypto in 2017 while working on a side project building deep learning hardware, initially mining crypto to pay for GPUs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:19">[00:19:19]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:34">[00:27:34]</a>. His interest in crypto intensified following the 2008 financial crisis and the seizing of customer funds in Greece, which he found "very jarring" and reminiscent of life in the USSR where property rights vanished <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:19">[00:23:19]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:04">[00:24:04]</a>.

He had a "4 AM Eureka moment" where he realized a data structure could track time instead of energy or entropy, leading to the core innovation of Solana: Proof of History (PoH) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:30">[00:28:30]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:06">[00:30:06]</a>. Proof of History is a verifiable delay function that allows estimation of how long it took to generate a piece of data, creating a universally agreed-upon clock <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:17">[00:37:17]</a>. This innovation allows for time-division multiple access in blockchain, enabling 100% channel utilization by ensuring nodes transmit signals at alternating times, preventing interference <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:23">[00:38:23]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:40">[00:40:40]</a>. This concept was crucial for Solana's ability to scale with hardware <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:51">[00:40:51]</a>.

At the time, verifiable delay functions and RSA time locks were primarily academic papers, not yet integrated into the industry <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:37">[00:39:37]</a>. When scaling became a critical problem in 2017, Yakovenko saw an opportunity with his unique approach <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:44">[00:29:44]</a>. His wife strongly encouraged him to take the leap and pursue this idea, highlighting the critical 6-month window in Silicon Valley to raise capital and build a team around new meta trends <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:12">[00:31:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:43">[00:31:43]</a>. He decided to quit his job at Dropbox, supported by his wife, and move quickly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:07">[00:32:07]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:50">[01:05:50]</a>.

## Solana's Technical Innovations

Solana's approach was often discounted by others, who didn't see the need for its unique scaling capabilities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:52">[00:39:52]</a>. Yakovenko compares Solana to "black licorice" â€“ a product not for everyone, but deeply loved by a niche group, which is essential for changing a market <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:02">[00:42:02]</a>.

Key aspects of Solana's technical innovation include:
*   **Proof of History:** A core innovation that allows for high transaction throughput by creating a verifiable, global clock <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:30">[00:28:30]</a>.
*   **Hardware Utilization:** Designed to fully utilize hardware bandwidth, allowing the chain to scale naturally with advancements in chips and networks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:16">[00:41:16]</a>.
*   **Custom Virtual Machine:** Instead of building for EVM support, Solana developed its own fast virtual machine, initially supporting low-level languages like C and Rust <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:56">[00:58:56]</a>. This decision, partly due to limited funding, led to a truly differentiated product that was cheap, fast, and could handle large concurrent loads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:26">[00:59:26]</a>.

> [!info] Current Status
> Solana processes more transactions per day than all other major chains and Layer 2s combined <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:27:29">[01:27:29]</a>. Despite being open-source, no one has been able to easily fork Solana and compete <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:27:44">[01:27:44]</a>.

## Solana Mobile and Decentralized Hardware

One of Solana Labs' key initiatives is the Solana Seeker smartphone, built for the crypto era <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:39">[00:00:39]</a>. This mobile venture, championed by Yakovenko due to his background at Qualcomm, aims to leverage secure element and trusted display technologies embedded in modern phone chips for secure cryptographic signatures, similar to a hardware wallet <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:30">[01:12:30]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:12:50">[01:12:50]</a>.

The goal of Solana Mobile is to offer:
*   **Enhanced User Experience:** A more secure and slicker signing experience for users <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:12">[01:13:12]</a>.
*   **Developer Opportunities:** A crypto-friendly app store that avoids the 30% fees charged by Apple and Google <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:13:20">[01:13:20]</a>.
*   **Disruption of Duopoly:** To challenge the current market equilibrium dominated by Apple and Google, who currently do not compete aggressively to reduce costs for developers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:12">[01:19:12]</a>.

The Solana Seeker features a "Seed Vault" powered by Soulflare, which securely manages seed phrases without requiring users to re-enter them across applications, significantly reducing security risks common with hot wallets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:04">[01:18:04]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:59">[01:17:59]</a>. As of the interview, the Seeker had about 150,000 pre-orders, with a long-term goal of reaching 10 million units <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:15:44">[01:15:44]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:56">[01:19:56]</a>.

## Community and Ecosystem Growth

Beyond the mobile phone, Solana supports various [[innovations_in_digital_payments_and_cryptocurrency | innovations]] and communities:

*   **DePIN (Decentralized Physical Infrastructure Networks):** Yakovenko has a unique focus on DePIN projects, which leverage crowdfunding in the hardware space to build decentralized networks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:21:52">[01:21:52]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:23:06">[01:23:06]</a>.
    *   **Helium:** A prime example, building a decentralized 5G carrier that now has around 300,000 users competing with traditional providers like Verizon <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:15">[01:22:15]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:24:16">[01:24:16]</a>.
    *   **Hivemapper:** Created a decentralized version of Google Street View, with 20-25% of the world's roads covered and data fidelity up to 200 times faster than Google <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:24:32">[01:24:32]</a>.
*   **NFTs:** Solana rapidly built NFT standards in 48 hours to meet user demand, fostering a culture of experimentation and self-launching for creators <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:12">[01:08:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:34">[01:08:34]</a>. The launch of Mad Lads NFTs was a turning point, blowing records for NFT volumes and signaling a resilient community <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:43:00">[01:43:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:43:00">[01:00:44]</a>.
*   **Memecoins:** While some critics dismiss Solana as a "casino chain" due to memecoins, Yakovenko sees them as "silly dumb casual stuff that pays for the infrastructure" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:17">[01:17:17]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:21:17">[01:21:17]</a>. He compares this to the early iOS App Store, where "fart apps" and casual games generated the vast majority of revenue, funding the ecosystem <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:26:42">[01:26:42]</a>. Bonk was the first major memecoin on Solana, emerging from the NFT community and gaining virality due to its fun and innocent nature <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:38">[01:07:38]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:13">[01:10:13]</a>. The success of Bonk even led to airdrops that covered the cost of the Solana mobile phone at one point <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:11:17">[01:11:17]</a>.

## Challenges and Resilience

Solana faced significant challenges, particularly during the FTX collapse in late 2022, which had a strong affiliation with Solana <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:19">[00:50:19]</a>. Yakovenko was concerned that founders building on the network had their funds on FTX, which could have decimated the ecosystem <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:52">[00:50:52]</a>. While some great founders like Armani Backpack were severely affected, the community demonstrated resilience, with many working harder to overcome the crisis, leading to projects like Mad Lads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:36">[00:51:36]</a>.

A public tweet from Vitalik Buterin, Ethereum's co-founder, in December 2022, acknowledging a "smart developer community in Solana" and hoping for its future, provided significant moral support <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:47">[00:53:47]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:54:04">[00:54:04]</a>.

Yakovenko takes FUD (fear, uncertainty, and doubt) in stride, having been "born in the FUD" from his early online experiences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:56">[00:52:56]</a>. He views online sparring as "part of the fun" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:53:07">[00:53:07]</a>.

## Philosophy on Innovation and Development

Yakovenko emphasizes several principles in his approach to innovation:

*   **Focus on Quality and Long-Term Vision:** He believes that success in an open market comes from focusing on quality over a very long period, enduring years of grinding to find product-market fit and power users <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:35">[00:49:35]</a>.
*   **"Black Licorice" Principle:** Build something unique that a niche group loves, rather than something generic for mass appeal, to gain a foothold and potentially shift the market <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:02">[00:42:02]</a>.
*   **Acceleration and Action:** The "acceleration" mindset prevalent in Solana's community refers to making decisions quickly and taking action, reducing delays in building and launching products <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:20:28">[01:20:28]</a>.
*   **Founder as Customer Support:** Founders should consistently talk to users to understand their pain points and integrate feedback rapidly. He encourages engineers to work openly with validators to shorten iteration cycles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:40">[01:02:40]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:26">[01:03:26]</a>.
*   **Technical Proficiency for Founders:** He believes a founder or co-founder should be capable of building the entire product themselves, as this allows them to effectively hire and direct teams <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:47">[01:04:47]</a>.

> [!tip] Advice for Founders
> The only real runway for a startup is treasuries, not cash in a bank, due to potential banking failures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:47">[00:47:47]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:05">[00:48:05]</a>. Founders should not pay attention to token price fluctuations but rather focus on securing their runway <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:27">[00:48:27]</a>.

Yakovenko's personal definition of success is "when all the code that I wrote has been replaced" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:31:04">[01:31:04]</a>, indicating a desire for constant improvement and evolution beyond his initial contributions. He is happy being the "second to worst guy on a team" because it means he can push himself, learn, and grow <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:31:18">[01:31:18]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:22:43">[01:22:43]</a>.

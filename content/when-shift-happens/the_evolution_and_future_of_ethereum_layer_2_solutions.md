## Current Landscape and Challenges
Currently, there's a significant "hole" in the EVM (Ethereum Virtual Machine) space regarding consumer experience optimization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:26:00">[28:26:00]</a>. Many chains, including various Layer 2s, are general purpose, attempting to do "a bunch of different things" like DeFi and payments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:08:00">[29:08:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:46:00">[29:46:00]</a>. This multi-purpose approach often compromises the ability to provide an optimal consumer journey <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:40:00">[29:40:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:48:00">[29:48:00]</a>.

### Key Players and Their Approaches
*   **Mentor**: An [[the_role_of_layer_2_solutions_in_scaling_ethereum | Ethereum Layer 2]] solution that has developed products like fBTC (for borrowing and lending Bitcoin in DeFi) and meETH (a large ETH liquid staking protocol) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:07:00">[02:07:00]</a>.
*   **Pudgy Penguins**: Utilizes Layer 2s for its products, with Pudgy World powered by ZkSync and Overpass by Polygon <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:31:00">[28:31:00]</a>.
*   **Base**: Cited as the highest-executing chain, including all Layer 2s and Layer 1s, consistently shipping new features, content, and campaigns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:26:00">[33:26:00]</a>. Jesse Pollak is credited with this operational excellence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:18:00">[34:18:00]</a>. However, Base faces handicaps related to its Silicon Valley culture and challenges with international adoption due to Coinbase being a primarily American exchange <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:48:00">[34:48:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:01:00">[35:01:00]</a>.
*   **Other Tech Stacks (Arbitrum, Optimism, ZkSync)**: While considered "tech stacks" rather than direct L2 competitors in the context of consumer focus, they are pushing concepts like the "super chain" and "elastic chain" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:05:00">[35:05:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:38:00">[35:38:00]</a>.

## The Future: Consumer-Centric Layer 2s
A significant future direction for Layer 2s is to hyper-focus on the consumer experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:52:00">[29:52:00]</a>. This means prioritizing the user above all else, which is an "unorthodox approach for chains" that typically prioritize decentralization or block integrity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:57:00">[30:57:00]</a>.

### Focus on Fun and Virality
The aim is to create "crypto's digital Disneyland" and the "internet's next amusement park," emphasizing "fun and virality" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:28:00">[30:28:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:43:00">[30:43:00]</a>. While builders can still deploy DeFi applications, the core focus would be on entertainment and user engagement. This strategy acknowledges that most people initially enter crypto to make money, but they stay because they find it fun <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:08:00">[30:08:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:11:00">[30:11:00]</a>.

### The Role of Intellectual Property (IP)
IP is seen as the "Trojan horse" that [[the_evolving_narrative_and_importance_of_ethereum | crypto needs]] to bring in a broader audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:28:00">[24:28:00]</a>. It provides an angle for adoption not solely predicated on price speculation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:51:00">[24:51:00]</a>. IP allows for digital ownership, participation, and brand building <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:02:00">[25:02:00]</a>. Tokenization, in this context, facilitates alignment and participation by creating aligned incentives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:35:00">[25:35:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:38:00">[25:38:00]</a>.

## Value of L2s to L1s
The relationship between Layer 2s and Layer 1s, especially Ethereum, is crucial. While Layer 2s attract projects with significant funding, they also bring substantial value to the Layer 1 ecosystem <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:53:00">[28:53:00]</a>. This dynamic influences the overall value of Layer 1 assets like Ethereum, as the success of Layer 2 applications directly impacts the underlying chain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:19:00">[27:19:00]</a>. The belief is that a chain is only as good as the applications and tokens built on top of it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:00">[01:02:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:03:00">[33:03:00]</a>.

## The Challenge of Launching a Token
While tokens can align community members in an unparalleled way <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="43:38:00">[43:38:00]</a>, launching one can be "extremely distracting for product" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="45:05:00">[45:05:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="46:58:00">[46:58:00]</a>. Projects like Jupiter prioritize building trust and a strong product for years without a token, ensuring the community and product are synergistic before a launch <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="45:49:00">[45:49:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="46:00:00">[46:00:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="46:27:00">[46:27:00]</a>. This approach minimizes liabilities and allows for a more focused development period <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:01:00">[41:01:01]</a>.

## Defining First-Principle Thinking

First-principle thinking involves breaking down complex problems to their most basic elements and reasoning up from there, rather than reasoning by analogy or relying on conventional wisdom. In the context of investing, it means asking fundamental questions about an asset or industry <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:21">[01:02:21]</a>.

## Applying First Principles in Crypto

For Chiang, applying first-principle thinking was vital during the ICO (Initial Coin Offering) bubble, when there was a significant question about what utility crypto offered beyond speculation and fundraising <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:06">[01:03:06]</a>. Through research and discussion, he narrowed down the use cases for crypto and blockchain technology that truly made sense at that early stage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:25">[01:03:25]</a>:

*   **Decentralized Finance (DeFi)**: Chiang became a strong believer in DeFi, seeing it as a viable and superior form of fintech because it fundamentally redefines financial organization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:44">[01:03:44]</a>. Unlike traditional fintech, which often only brings incremental technological improvements that banks can eventually replicate, DeFi introduces a new paradigm of financial services <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:31">[01:04:31]</a>.
*   **Gaming**: Another sector that resonated with Chiang's first-principle analysis was gaming, due to its natural fit with crypto's ability to facilitate permissionless exchange of digital assets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:15">[01:05:15]</a>.

This [[importance_of_integrity_and_contrarian_thinking_for_founders_and_investors | contrarian thinking]], grounded in first principles, guided his early investments, such as in Synthetix and Axie Infinity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:09">[01:05:09]</a>.

## Navigating Volatility and Filtering Noise

First-principle thinking is essential for investors, especially in markets with high [[the_psychology_and_strategies_of_handling_investment_volatility | volatility]] like crypto <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:24">[01:09:24]</a>. It enables investors to:
*   **Filter Noise**: The crypto market is rife with noise, making it difficult to discern legitimate opportunities from scams or misleading information <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:02">[01:02:02]</a>. A first-principle approach helps filter out irrelevant information and focus on what truly matters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:15">[01:02:15]</a>.
*   **Maintain Conviction**: By deeply understanding the underlying value and thesis of an investment, an investor can better withstand extreme [[the_psychology_and_strategies_of_handling_investment_volatility | volatility]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:25">[01:09:25]</a>. If an investment drops 20%, an investor grounded in first principles can determine if the fundamental thesis remains intact or if it warrants selling <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:09:35">[01:09:35]</a>.
*   **Become a Specialist**: For those with limited capital, first-principle thinking encourages focusing on a few projects or sectors that genuinely excite them. This allows individuals to become subject matter experts, gaining an "alpha" (edge) over other market participants, including full-time industry professionals who must cover a broader range of topics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:50">[01:10:50]</a>.

This deep understanding, cultivated through first-principle research, is what enables investors to make [[managing_risk_and_making_informed_investment_decisions | concentrated bets]] and potentially achieve significant returns, rather than blindly diversifying in a correlated market <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:08:29">[01:08:29]</a>.

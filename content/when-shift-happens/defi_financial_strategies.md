## Terra Station Wallet Overview
The Terra Station wallet serves as a central hub for interacting with the Terra ecosystem <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:50">[00:00:50]</a>. Its dashboard provides a snapshot of current metrics such as LUNA price, tax rate, LUNA issuance, community pool size, and staking ratio <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:09">[00:01:09]</a>. Transaction volume on Terra has been substantial, with over $244 million processed in one week <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:50">[00:01:50]</a>. The total number of accounts on Terra has shown a steady upward trend, nearing three million wallets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:06">[00:02:06]</a>.

Key features of the Terra Station wallet include:
*   **Wallet Balances** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:16">[00:02:16]</a>: Displays current holdings, such as UST (TerraUSD) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:19">[00:02:19]</a>.
*   **History** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:25">[00:02:25]</a>: Provides a brief transaction history, allowing users to follow transactions across the blockchain similar to Etherscan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:47">[00:02:47]</a>.
*   **Swap (Terra Swap)** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:15">[00:03:15]</a>: Allows users to exchange one cryptocurrency for another within the wallet, such as swapping UST for LUNA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:35">[00:03:35]</a>. Terra Swap generally offers a slightly better price than market settings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:05">[00:04:05]</a>. Transactions are confirmed with a password and incur minimal fees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:23">[00:04:23]</a>.
*   **Staking** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:55">[00:04:55]</a>: Users can stake LUNA by delegating it to a validator <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:58">[00:04:58]</a>. It's recommended to research validators based on voting power, self-delegation percentage, commission, and uptime <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:18">[00:05:18]</a>. Some validators may offer more airdrops or lower fees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:42">[00:05:42]</a>, but some staking options might include a lock-up period <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:50">[00:05:50]</a>.

## Anchor Protocol: Earn and Borrow
Anchor Protocol is described as a platform for "better savings" due to its stable and attractive yield powered by staking returns from multiple proof-of-stake blockchains <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:16">[00:06:16]</a>.

### Earn (Depositing UST)
Anchor Earn provides a high Annual Percentage Yield (APY) on stablecoin deposits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:24">[00:07:24]</a>. The APY usually hovers around 20%, offering a significant return compared to traditional finance (CeFi) which typically yields 8-16% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:31">[00:07:31]</a>.
*   **Deposit Process**: Users connect their Terra Station wallet, enter the desired UST amount (ensuring enough UST for fees), confirm the transaction with their password, and the deposit starts earning interest immediately <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:56">[00:06:56]</a>.
*   **Interest Calculation**: The platform includes a calculator to estimate daily, weekly, monthly, and yearly returns based on the deposited amount <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:02">[00:10:02]</a>.
*   **Withdrawal**: Funds can be withdrawn from Anchor Earn following a similar process to depositing, incurring a small transaction fee <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:34">[00:11:34]</a>.

### Borrow (Using LUNA as Collateral)
Anchor Protocol allows users to provide bonded LUNA (bLUNA) as collateral to take out a loan, effectively allowing borrowing against assets without selling them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:01">[00:06:01]</a>.
*   **Net APR**: The protocol often pays users to borrow, indicated by a positive net APR (the difference between borrow APR and distribution API of ANC tokens) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:40">[00:12:40]</a>.
*   **Bonding LUNA**: To use LUNA as collateral, it must first be "bonded" into bLUNA (bonded LUNA) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:08">[00:13:08]</a>. This process involves selecting a validator and requires UST for transaction fees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:27">[00:13:27]</a>.
*   **Providing Collateral**: Once bLUNA is minted, it can be deposited as collateral, which then establishes a borrow limit (e.g., 60% of the collateral value) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:40">[00:14:40]</a>.
*   **Borrowing UST**: Users can then borrow UST up to their limit, monitoring their Loan-to-Value (LTV) ratio in real-time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:47">[00:15:47]</a>.
*   **Liquidation Risk**:
    > [00:17:10] It is important to monitor your collateral level as this is a [[decentralized_finance_defi_and_its_potential | DeFi]] space and you can get liquidated if your loan to value goes too high. It's not like Celsius or Nexo or any of these other lending platforms where you will get a person at the end of an email asking you to post more collateral if required, you will just get liquidated. <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:10">[00:17:10]</a>
*   **Earning Governance Tokens**: Borrowing and bonding actions are rewarded with Anchor Protocol (ANC) governance tokens <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:53">[00:17:53]</a>. These can be claimed and restaked for additional yield (e.g., ~9.73% APY for staking ANC) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:42">[00:18:42]</a>. Holding ANC tokens also grants the ability to vote on proposals for the Anchor protocol, such as onboarding new collateral types like bonded ETH <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:26">[00:19:26]</a>.
*   **Repaying Loans**: To repay a loan, users return to the borrow page, specify the amount, and confirm the transaction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:24">[00:20:24]</a>.
*   **Withdrawing Bonded LUNA**: After repaying the loan, bLUNA collateral must be withdrawn <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:10">[00:21:10]</a>.
*   **Burning Bonded LUNA**: To convert bLUNA back to LUNA, it must be "burned" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:13">[00:22:13]</a>. This process typically takes at least 21 days (up to 24 days due to three-day processing batches), and slashing events during this period could affect the final amount <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:47">[00:22:47]</a>. There may also be a small "slippage" or fee when converting bLUNA back to LUNA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:57">[00:23:57]</a>.

## General Considerations for [[Decentralized_finance_DeFi_and_its_potential | DeFi]] Strategies
*   **Transaction Costs**: Transactions in the Terra ecosystem are generally low-cost and fast, typically ranging from 25 to 50 cents <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:11">[00:14:11]</a>.
*   **Impermanent Loss**: While not always applicable, users should be aware of impermanent loss in liquidity pools (e.g., ANC-UST LP) where higher APRs come with the risk of loss if token values fluctuate significantly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:55">[00:18:55]</a>.
*   **Research**: It is crucial to conduct personal research into products and platforms before engaging <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:33">[00:12:33]</a>.
*   **Starting Small**: It's advisable to begin with a small amount (e.g., $100) to understand how the [[decentralized_finance_defi_and_its_potential | DeFi]] earning, borrowing, bonding, and governance mechanisms work <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:57">[00:24:57]</a>.

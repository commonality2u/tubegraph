## Personal Health Journey
Joe experienced chronic insomnia, manifesting as difficulty falling asleep, a condition he attributes to the intense "Silicon Valley startup culture" and working from bed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:24">[00:14:24]</a>. His body developed a "psychosomatic" response, perceiving the bed as unsafe for rest due to work activities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:39">[00:15:39]</a>. He learned that the bed should ideally only be used for sleep and sex, not for activities like watching TV or reading <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:09">[00:15:09]</a>.

To overcome this, Joe underwent a year of therapy, which taught him to retrain his body <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:04">[00:16:04]</a>. If he felt himself dozing on the couch, he would stand up and watch TV to avoid associating the couch with sleep. Conversely, if he felt tired, he would immediately go to bed to train his body to fall asleep there <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:04">[00:16:04]</a>. This [[personal_growth_and_recovery_experiences | process of retraining]] took months <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:17">[00:16:17]</a>.

## Skepticism and the Healthcare System
Joe's skepticism about conventional systems extends to the medical industry, particularly in the United States <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:31:13">[01:31:13]</a>. His questioning stems from a personal experience at age 20 or 21 when he blew out his ACL and needed reconstructive surgery <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:31:30">[01:31:30]</a>. Lacking health insurance, he negotiated the $10,000 surgery down to $900, receiving a "91% discount" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:32:06">[01:32:06]</a>. This eye-opening experience led him to believe the system is "captured by insurance, big Pharma, the medical providers" who inflate costs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:32:34">[01:32:34]</a>.

He observed that many effective treatments are not covered by insurance because "they fix you," thereby eliminating the need for recurring treatments that generate continuous revenue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:34:25">[01:34:25]</a>.

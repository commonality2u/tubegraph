SAT Layer is a project focused on building the economic layer for Bitcoin, aiming to make it a "smart asset" or "programmable collateral" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:00:00">[03:00:00]</a>. The goal is to rebuild the financial system to be more accessible, fair, and based on Bitcoin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:00">[00:57:00]</a>.

## Why Bitcoin Needs More Utility
Bitcoin is widely recognized as "digital gold" and the best store of value, easier to custody and transfer than physical gold <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:44:00">[03:44:00]</a>. However, some, including "old OG Bitcoiners," argue that Bitcoin purely as digital gold is not enough for its long-term survival <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:00:00">[13:00:00]</a>. The current security model relies on miner fees, which could decrease significantly after future halving events, potentially breaking the model unless miners earn enough fees to sustain their operations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:10:00">[13:10:00]</a>. If miners aren't sufficiently compensated, Bitcoin's security could be compromised, making it more susceptible to attack <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:16:00">[14:16:00]</a>. Therefore, Bitcoin needs more utility to generate transaction fees and sustain its security <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:30:00">[14:30:00]</a>.

## Bitcoin as Programmable Collateral
The vision is for Bitcoin to become the foundation of an entirely new financial system, serving as the "best smart asset" or "programmable collateral" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:24:00">[03:24:00]</a>. This means enabling Bitcoin to be the largest and best collateral in DeFi, allowing people to borrow against it and build financial primitives like decentralized exchanges (DEXs) on top <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:20:00">[20:20:00]</a>.

### Global Financial System Shift
Historically, reserve currencies have not lasted more than a century, and a similar shift is predicted for the US Dollar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:56:00">[16:56:00]</a>. Nation-states are increasingly accumulating Bitcoin and using it for settlement, as seen with China and Russia settling oil trades with Bitcoin to avoid the US Dollar and its geopolitical influence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:31:00">[17:31:00]</a>. Bitcoin is considered an incredibly neutral asset, uncontrolled by any single country, making it an attractive option for international settlement alongside stablecoins <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:36:00">[18:36:00]</a>.

Pioneers in DeFi created essential primitives that were more efficient, fair, and transparent versions of traditional finance offerings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:17:00">[21:17:00]</a>. ## The Right Moment for Bitcoin DeFi
Several developments have made now the opportune time for Bitcoin DeFi:
*   **Taproot**: This Bitcoin protocol improvement enabled pseudo-smart contract capabilities, allowing for native time-locking of Bitcoin on the Bitcoin blockchain itself to facilitate staking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:45:00">[22:45:00]</a>. Before Taproot, Bitcoin was largely non-programmable for complex functionalities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:25:00">[23:25:00]</a>.
*   **Ordinals and Inscriptions**: These post-Taproot innovations showcased increased programmability, allowing for Bitcoin NFTs (Ordinals) and inscribing art or messages directly onto Bitcoin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:58:00">[23:58:00]</a>. This development helped make the Bitcoin space more open to innovation, softening the "Bitcoin maximalist" stance that Bitcoin shouldn't change <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:20:00">[25:20:00]</a>.

## What is [[Future of Bitcoin DeFi | Bitcoin DeFi]]?
Bitcoin DeFi encompasses two main approaches:
1.  **Natively Building on Bitcoin**: Creating DeFi protocols that settle directly on Bitcoin or its L2s <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:56:00">[26:56:00]</a>. The rationale is that if Bitcoin accrues more value and other protocols go to zero, settling transactions directly on Bitcoin ensures maximum security and immutability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:57:00">[27:57:00]</a>.
2.  **Plugging Bitcoin into Existing Crypto DeFi**: Integrating Bitcoin into established blue-chip DeFi protocols like Compound or Pendle, making Bitcoin the premier asset across the broader crypto space <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:28:00">[28:28:00]</a>. SAT Layer focuses on this approach and aims to connect Bitcoin to institutions and nation-states <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:53:00">[28:53:00]</a>.

For founders considering where to build, Bitcoin DeFi presents compelling advantages:
*   **Market Opportunity (Total Addressable Market - TAM)**: Less than 1% (closer to 0.5%) of all Bitcoin is currently utilized in DeFi, compared to 20-30% for Ethereum and Solana <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:39:00">[30:39:00]</a>. Even a conservative estimate of 10% Bitcoin utilization implies a 10x to 20x growth opportunity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:01:00">[31:01:00]</a>. With increasing institutional and nation-state adoption of Bitcoin, there's growing demand for [[bitcoin_yield_earning_strategies | Bitcoin yield]], making DeFi an interesting avenue to generate it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:42:00">[31:42:00]</a>.
*   **Competitive Dynamics**: Unlike Ethereum's EVM chains where competition is high, Bitcoin's newer DeFi space has significantly less competition <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:02:00">[32:02:00]</a>. This allows builders to apply bleeding-edge innovations and research from EVM to Bitcoin, creating a "founder arbitrage" opportunity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:15:00">[32:15:00]</a>.

## How to Earn Yield on Bitcoin
While traditional delta-neutral trading strategies exist, they carry risks due to Bitcoin's volatility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:47:00">[32:47:00]</a>. A more "crypto-native" approach allows users to earn four sets of yield simultaneously on their Bitcoin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:28:00">[33:28:00]</a>:

1.  **Liquid Staking Tokens (LSTs)**: Projects like Lombard or Solve stake Bitcoin into Babylon (the Bitcoin staking protocol) and mint an LST token in return, allowing users to do more with their staked Bitcoin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:21:00">[34:21:00]</a>. Yield is typically paid in the LST project's tokens or points, estimated around 1-2% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:04:00">[35:04:00]</a>. The risk here is that these protocols take custody of your Bitcoin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:49:00">[35:49:00]</a>.
2.  **Babylon Staking**: Babylon is the leading Bitcoin staking protocol, which natively time-locks Bitcoin on the Bitcoin L1 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:58:00">[35:58:00]</a>. The yield from Babylon comes from providing Bitcoin's security to other Proof-of-Stake blockchains like Sui <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:37:00">[36:37:00]</a>.
3.  **L1/L2 Incentives**: Users can mint their LST tokens on different Layer 1s or Layer 2s that incentivize having more Bitcoin on their chain for use in their DeFi ecosystems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="43:48:00">[43:48:00]</a>.
4.  **SAT Layer Deposit**: Depositing the LST into SAT Layer provides a fourth source of yield <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="44:09:00">[44:09:00]</a>.

### Why Risk Bitcoin for Yield?
While Bitcoin's capital gains have been substantial, there are two main reasons to seek yield:
*   **Institutional Demand**: Institutional investors and liquid funds are increasingly benchmarked against Bitcoin. Simply holding Bitcoin is no longer enough; they need to outperform it, which is very difficult <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="38:11:00">[38:11:00]</a>. Earning yield offers a way to potentially outperform <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="39:05:00">[39:05:00]</a>.
*   **Portfolio Allocation**: Individuals can allocate a portion of their Bitcoin (distinct from long-term cold storage) to DeFi farming to earn additional yield, potentially 5-10% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="39:08:00">[39:08:00]</a>. This comes with additional risk but also additional potential reward <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="39:45:00">[39:45:00]</a>.

### Bitcoin Staking vs. Restaking
*   **Bitcoin Staking**: Involves locking up Bitcoin to provide security to other blockchains, earning yield in return <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="40:19:00">[40:19:00]</a>.
*   **Bitcoin Restaking**: Uses already staked Bitcoin to secure additional applications or infrastructure, or to provide collateral in DeFi <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:20:00">[41:20:00]</a>.

### Risks: Slashing and Rehypothecation
Earning additional yield through staking and restaking comes with risk, including slashing and rehypothecation (similar to leverage) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="42:20:00">[42:20:00]</a>. Slashing is a mechanism in staking/restaking protocols to enforce good behavior and punish bad behavior by economically penalizing attackers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="42:29:00">[42:29:00]</a>. This risk is inherent, as additional yield typically requires some risk <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="43:03:00">[43:03:00]</a>.

## SAT Layer Explained
SAT Layer enables users to earn high yield on their Bitcoin by using the deposited Bitcoin (via LSTs) as premium collateral to secure applications and infrastructure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="45:15:00">[45:15:00]</a>.

### Use Cases for Restaked Bitcoin
One key use case for the restaked Bitcoin is **insurance** for lending protocols in crypto, which are typically overcollateralized <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="46:00:00">[46:00:00]</a>. SAT Layer offers insurance to these protocols or their users, providing coverage in case of adverse events like exploits or economic attacks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="46:46:00">[46:46:00]</a>. The yield for this insurance comes from the premiums paid by lending protocols or users <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="47:21:00">[47:21:00]</a>. This insurance capability is a critical step towards unlocking **undercollateralized loans** in crypto, a "holy grail" for building a more full-fledged financial system <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="47:46:00">[47:46:00]</a>. The insurance model can expand to cover perpetual exchanges (perps) against bad liquidations and even offer slashing insurance for staking/restaking, providing principal and downside protection for yield farmers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="48:27:00">[48:27:00]</a>.

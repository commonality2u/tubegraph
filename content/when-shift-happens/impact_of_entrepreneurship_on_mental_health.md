## The Taxing Nature of Entrepreneurship

Starting a business inherently means having "pretty much all odds stacked against you" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:19">[00:24:19]</a>. Entrepreneurship is "particularly taxing" for individuals prone to extraordinary anxiety or stress, as many unforeseen challenges will arise <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:00">[00:45:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:43">[00:52:43]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:01:46">[09:01:46]</a>.

## Personal Experiences and Consequences

Carey shares his [[personal_reflections_on_entrepreneurship_and_mindset | personal reflection]] on the all-consuming nature of his work, which led to an unhealthy relationship with it for a long time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:21">[01:01:21]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:49:50">[02:49:50]</a>. This had direct consequences on his personal life, notably affecting his sleep due to a specific sleeping condition <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:07:05">[01:07:05]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:51:30">[02:51:30]</a>.

He recounts feeling "tired all the time" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:39:41">[02:39:41]</a>, a common sentiment among entrepreneurs, until a doctor friend suggested a sleep study that helped resolve the issue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:48:45">[02:48:45]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:53:30">[02:53:30]</a>. This highlights the critical importance of [[role_of_therapy_and_mental_health_in_entrepreneurship | addressing physical and mental health issues professionally]].

## Strategies for Well-being

To manage the [[impact_of_mental_health_on_creativity_and_business | impact of mental health]], Carey emphasizes the need for an individualized program <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:36:03">[02:36:03]</a>. His approach includes:
*   **Routine**: Establishing a helpful morning routine <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:53:13">[02:53:13]</a>.
*   **Physical Activity**: Incorporating exercise, like running (a personal passion for 15 years), to physically stress the body in a positive way <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:54:19">[02:54:19]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:55:01">[02:55:01]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:31:07">[03:31:07]</a>.
*   **Nutrition**: Being thoughtful about the fuel put into the body, recognizing its impact on biochemistry <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:47:04">[02:47:04]</a>.
*   **Gratitude**: Taking a moment to express gratitude upon waking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:59:44">[02:59:44]</a>.

Carey stresses that "you only get one body" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:59:44">[02:59:44]</a>, and neglecting it through sleep deprivation, poor eating, or other unhealthy habits will eventually catch up <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:08:14">[07:08:14]</a>. Your body will signal when things are not going well, and it's crucial to listen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:47:04">[02:47:04]</a>.

## Identifying and Addressing Warning Signs

Entrepreneurs often prioritize their business, believing it won't succeed without them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:27:07">[03:27:07]</a>. However, this "bull-headedness" can also be a "greatest demon" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:55:06">[03:55:06]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:57:33">[03:57:33]</a>.

Warning signs that indicate a need for mental health attention include:
*   Consistent tiredness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:31:54">[03:31:54]</a>.
*   Snapping at people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:33:04">[03:33:04]</a>.
*   A physically messy personal environment, which can reflect the state of mind <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:36:19">[03:36:19]</a>.

### Rejecting "Work to Death" Culture
Carey explicitly states that the "work to yourself to death culture is stupid" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:02">[00:00:02]</a>. If you are dead, you cannot work on your project, and "no one's going to care" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:42">[00:02:42]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:58:39">[03:58:39]</a>. This highlights the ultimate futility of sacrificing health for work.

## Building Resilience and Support Systems

[[Mindset and personal growth in entrepreneurship | Cultivating a learning mindset]] is key; problems should be seen as opportunities to learn and overcome, rather than personal attacks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:32:00">[09:32:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:40:53">[09:40:53]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:47:56">[09:47:56]</a>. This perspective allows one to move from one problem to the next, becoming better at solving increasingly larger challenges <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:59:59">[02:59:59]</a>.

### Compounding Discipline
Resilience is built through consistent practice and discipline, similar to how one trains for a marathon <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:07:45">[03:07:45]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:52:43">[03:52:43]</a>. It's not just about running one marathon, but consistently running one every year, building compounded gains in physical and mental fortitude <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:56:04">[03:56:04]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:59:12">[03:59:12]</a>.

### The Importance of Support
[[The importance of support and relationships in entrepreneurship | A strong support network]] of long-term friends, family, or community members is vital <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:02:00">[03:02:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:41:02">[03:41:02]</a>. These individuals can identify patterns not obvious to new relationships and provide a safe space to discuss challenges <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:29:20">[03:29:20]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:58:39">[03:58:39]</a>. There are also professional resources for mental and physical health <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:07:07">[03:07:07]</a>, and other entrepreneurs who understand the "lonely crucible" of the journey <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:41:02">[03:41:02]</a>.

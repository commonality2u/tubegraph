### Leverage Trading: A Risky Endeavor
Leverage trading allows individuals to trade with more money than they possess, amplifying potential gains but also losses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:02">[00:05:02]</a>. One individual's two-month journey involved experiencing significant profits, returning to zero, and being liquidated due to over-leveraging <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:23">[00:05:23]</a>. Despite these setbacks, the individual continued trading, viewing it as a habit rather than an addiction, aiming to avoid further losses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:56">[00:05:56]</a>. This pursuit led to studying trading strategies, including the use of stop-loss orders <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:28">[00:06:28]</a>.

Another experienced trader shared a similar, albeit more extreme, journey with leverage trading, which started in late 2019 with a strategy to increase [[Bitcoin valuation and future predictions | Bitcoin]] holdings using 2-3x leverage on Bitmex <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:07">[00:07:07]</a>. The initial strategy involved compounding gains by closing positions after small increases and re-entering <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:09">[00:09:09]</a>. This led to significant unrealized profits, prompting a shift to more volatile assets like Ethereum (ETH) for higher gains <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:09">[00:10:09]</a>. However, the COVID-19 market crash in March 2020 resulted in the loss of 80% of net worth due to liquidation, particularly after ETH dropped 70% in a single day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:53">[00:10:53]</a>. This experience taught that leverage trading was not suitable, leading to significant stress and sleep deprivation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:36">[00:11:36]</a>.

> "Trading on leverage is not for me, and I was never sleeping, it was f***** horrible." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:39">[00:11:39]</a>

### Market Dynamics and Liquidation Events
The [[Crypto Market Cycles and Predictions | crypto market]] frequently experiences "liquidation events," where sudden, drastic price movements are designed to liquidate both long and short positions, primarily benefiting exchanges <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:35">[00:12:35]</a>. While stop-losses are strategies to mitigate risk, they can be dangerous as sudden drops might trigger them, only for the price to recover shortly after <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:13">[00:13:13]</a>. Ultimately, many experienced traders conclude that simply holding spot assets (buying and holding) can outperform complex trading strategies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:34">[00:13:34]</a>.

> "People always share about how much money they made... they never talk about how much money they lose." <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>

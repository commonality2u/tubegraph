[[Terry Davis legacy and impact | Terry Davis]], an exceptionally talented programmer, was diagnosed with schizophrenia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:06">[00:00:06]</a>. He created the open-source 64-bit operating system TempleOS in 2005 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>, single-handedly writing the entire system from his parents' house in Las Vegas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:12">[00:00:12]</a>.

## Influence on TempleOS

[[Terry Davis legacy and impact | Davis]] believed he was "God's chosen programmer" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:30">[00:00:30]</a>. This belief influenced the design of TempleOS, which was intended to be the "third temple prophesized by the bible" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:04">[00:00:04]</a>. He implemented features described as "a modern day way of speaking in tongues" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:33">[00:00:33]</a>. Within the OS, users can press F7 to "start speaking in tongues" by generating a pseudo-random word from "god" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:32">[00:01:32]</a>, or use Shift+F6 to have "god draw you a picture" with a "god doodle" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:37">[00:01:37]</a>.

## Legacy

[[Terry Davis legacy and impact | Terry Davis]] passed away in 2018 at the age of 48 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:16">[00:02:16]</a>. He was recognized as both a highly controversial internet personality and a genius programmer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:20">[00:02:20]</a>. TempleOS stands as one of the most complex pieces of software ever written by a solo developer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:11">[00:02:11]</a>.

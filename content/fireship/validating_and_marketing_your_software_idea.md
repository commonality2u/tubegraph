## The Side Hustler Mindset: Fail Forward

Building successful side hustles involves a lot of hard work and some luck, with many attempts failing miserably <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:30">[00:00:30]</a>. A key mindset is to "fail early, fail often, but always fail forward" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:31">[00:01:31]</a>.

### Prioritizing Customer Feedback
The only reliable indicator of success or failure is the customer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:35">[00:01:35]</a>. It's crucial to get real users to provide feedback as soon as possible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:40">[00:01:40]</a>. A major pitfall is spending years [[common_frustrations_in_software_engineering | building a project in secret only to release it and find out nobody actually wants it]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:42">[00:01:42]</a>.

### Idea Validation
Before investing significant time into an idea, it's wise to validate it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:08">[00:03:08]</a>. This helps to prevent the delusion that your idea is valuable when it might not be <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:12">[00:03:12]</a>.

*   **Example: Vocalize.cloud**
    The idea for Vocalize.cloud, a platform for legal celebrity voice cloning, emerged from encountering an illegal use of the speaker's cloned voice in a YouTube channel <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:04">[00:02:04]</a>. Despite the concept, the biggest challenge was determining if anyone actually wanted to use it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:03">[00:03:03]</a>. The speaker planned to use his YouTube channel to promote the idea and gauge interest, expecting to know within 24 hours if it was a viable concept <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:15">[00:03:15]</a>.

## Execution is Everything
While ideas are abundant, execution is paramount <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:23">[00:03:23]</a>. This isn't just about the technology, but primarily the business model <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:27">[00:03:27]</a>. Even the best app will go unused without a sound marketing plan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:30">[00:03:30]</a>. It is recommended to avoid writing any code until a marketing plan is in place <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:01">[00:04:01]</a>.

## Marketing Strategies

### Organic Marketing
For those without significant capital for advertisements or influencer marketing, organic marketing, typically through social media platforms like Twitter, YouTube, or TikTok, is a viable approach <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:40">[00:03:40]</a>.

The process of marketing is likened to fishing:
> "You want to First figure out where the fish are then put out some bait like an advertisement or a call to action then once a user signs up you'll want to Club them to death and then Grill them for dinner and by that I mean provide them with an awesome service that they'll be happy to pay for" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:47">[00:03:47]</a>

### Monetization and Pricing
When it comes to earning money, utilizing a [[monetization_strategies_and_using_payment_apis_for_software_applications | payments API]] like [[monetization_strategies_and_using_payment_apis_for_software_applications | Stripe]] is essential <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:28">[00:07:28]</a>.

A good strategy is to offer some form of free trial to allow users to experience the app <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:38">[00:07:38]</a>. For example, Vocalize.cloud provides 100 free tokens, requiring users to purchase more once they run out <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:36">[00:07:36]</a>.

For pricing, the "Vercel strategy" is suggested <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:18">[00:08:18]</a>. This involves significantly marking up the cost of underlying services. For instance, if an API like [[software_engineering_and_development_tools | 11Labs API]] charges 18 cents per 1,000 characters for voice cloning, you might charge 69 cents <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:21">[00:08:21]</a>. This is how you build a "money printing machine" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:31">[00:08:31]</a>.

## Tech Stack Considerations (After Marketing Plan)
While technology choice is important, it's secondary to the marketing plan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:01">[00:04:01]</a>. The most important aspect of a tech stack is that "it works" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:12">[00:04:12]</a>. Focusing on technologies that optimize productivity and sticking to them is critical, rather than chasing every new trend <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:23">[00:04:23]</a>. For example, Peter Levels, a successful indie maker, uses "boring old PHP" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:13">[00:04:13]</a>.

The speaker's personal tech stack includes SvelteKit and Firebase (the "Sfelt Kit" stack), along with IBM's Carbon Design System <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:31">[00:04:31]</a>. This choice is based on personal preference and productivity, as working with tools you enjoy is less demoralizing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:08">[00:05:08]</a>. The use of Firebase allows for simplified backend logic and real-time data synchronization with secure access rules <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:12">[00:06:12]</a>.

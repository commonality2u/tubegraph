OpenAI's GPT-4o includes a new image generator that has significantly impacted the internet, transforming it into a "Ghibli anime cartoon nightmare" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:18">[00:00:18]</a>. This release has been met with both criticism, echoing sentiments from Hayao Miyazaki about AI art <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:22">[00:00:22]</a>, and praise for its advanced capabilities.

## Initial Reception and Impact
Initially, expectations for the GPT-4o image generator were low, especially following the disappointments of previous releases like Sora and the [[underwhelming_release_of_gpt_45 | GPT-4.5]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:06">[00:01:06]</a>. However, OpenAI "redeemed Itself" with this tool, which is considered a "fire" release <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:14">[00:01:14]</a>.

The generator has reportedly "ruined" popular memes by applying its distinctive style <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:45">[00:00:45]</a>.

## Key Capabilities
The GPT-4o image generator offers a range of impressive features:
*   **Text Rendering** It can create infographics and marketing material with "near perfect text rendering" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:24">[00:01:24]</a>.
*   **Comic Strips** The tool is capable of producing "almost good comic strip[s]" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:27">[00:01:27]</a>.
*   **Transparency Handling** Unlike many other [[opensource_ai_image_generators | image generators]], GPT-4o can handle transparency effectively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:29">[00:01:29]</a>.
*   **Art Style Transformation** It can transform images into specific art styles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:35">[00:01:35]</a>.
*   **Character Continuity** A significant feature is its ability to maintain character continuity across different images, allowing for "AI girlfriends" to be rendered with new poses, outfits, or integrated into existing photos while maintaining a consistent appearance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:44">[00:01:44]</a>. This capability enables [[techniques_for_enhancing_aigenerated_images | enhancing AI-generated images]] with consistent elements.

## Underlying Technology
Unlike many other [[opensource_tools_for_generating_realistic_images | image generation models]] like Stable Diffusion and Midjourney that use a diffusion algorithm, GPT-4o employs an [[autoregressive_approach_in_AI_image_generation | autoregressive approach]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:57">[00:01:57]</a>. Instead of generating the entire image at once, it constructs the image "pixel by pixel from left to right, top to bottom" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:09">[00:02:09]</a>. While the underlying code is not publicly available due to its closed-source nature <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:54">[00:01:54]</a>, this approach results in images that "almost doesn't even look artificial" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:10">[00:02:10]</a>.

## Watermarking and Disclosure
The images generated by GPT-4o include a controversial watermark provided by the Coalition for Content Providence and Authenticity (C2PA) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:16">[00:02:16]</a>. Uploading a GPT-4o generated image to the C2PA tool reveals its origin from OpenAI and any modification history <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:21">[00:02:21]</a>. This technology, also being implemented by camera manufacturers and software developers like Adobe, aims to track changes to digital assets to combat misinformation, albeit at the expense of privacy and freedom <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:37">[00:02:37]</a>.

Platforms like YouTube and Steam are beginning to require disclosure when AI assets are used in creative works <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:43">[00:02:43]</a>. This raises a philosophical debate, often summarized by "Slop's Razor": if an AI-generated image is indistinguishable from human work, then no disclosure is needed. If it is visibly "slop," then again, no disclosure is needed because its artificial origin is evident <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:59">[00:02:59]</a>.

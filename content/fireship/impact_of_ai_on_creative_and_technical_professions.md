## OpenAI Sora: A Leap in Video Generation

Sora, deriving its name from the Japanese word for "Sky" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:45">[00:00:45]</a>, surpasses previous AI video models like Stable Video Diffusion and Pika <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:55">[00:00:55]</a>. Its key advancements include:
*   **Realism and Cohesion** The generated images are more realistic, and videos maintain cohesion between frames for up to a minute <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>.
*   **Flexibility** Videos can be created from text prompts or by bringing a starting image to life <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:09">[00:01:09]</a>. They can also be rendered in various aspect ratios <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:06">[00:01:06]</a>.
*   **On-demand Generation** OpenAI CEO Sam Altman demonstrated Sora's capability by taking real-time requests on Twitter and generating examples within minutes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:19">[00:01:19]</a>.

### Technical Underpinnings
Sora functions as a diffusion model, akin to Dolly and Stable Diffusion, which starts with random noise and gradually refines it into a coherent image <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:21">[00:02:21]</a>. Processing video data is a massive undertaking, as a one-minute video at 60 frames per second contains over 10 billion data points, a significantly larger scale than a still image <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:38">[00:02:38]</a>. To manage this, Sora employs an approach similar to large language models by "tokenizing" visual patches â€“ small, compressed chunks of images that capture both visual information and movement through time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:00">[00:03:00]</a>. Unlike typical video models that crop training data, Sora can train on native resolutions and output variable resolutions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:14">[00:03:14]</a>.

However, it is anticipated that these limitations will be addressed over time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:07">[00:04:07]</a>.

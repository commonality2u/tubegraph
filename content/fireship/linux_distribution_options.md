[[introduction_to_linux | Linux]] is an open-source operating system that enables human interaction with computers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>. It was originally created in 1991 by Linus Torvalds, a student at the University of Helsinki, with the aim of developing a free version of the Minix operating system, which itself was based on Unix <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:06">[00:00:06]</a>.

## Widespread Use and Personal Computers

Today, [[introduction_to_linux | Linux]] is ubiquitous, powering the vast majority of web servers, embedded applications like smart TVs, and mobile devices running Android <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:15">[00:00:15]</a>. It is considered the best choice for personal computers, offering many different distributions, or "distros," to choose from <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:21">[00:00:21]</a>.

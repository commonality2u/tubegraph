## Redefining "Work"
The conventional definitions of "work" — force times distance, time input, or sheer effort — are largely irrelevant for knowledge workers and entrepreneurs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:08">[00:03:08]</a>. Many claim they "outwork" others by putting in more hours, but this is capped by time and physical ability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:11">[00:03:11]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:37">[00:06:37]</a>.

The speaker's definition of work is:
> "Work equals outputs" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:07">[00:04:07]</a>
> "Outputs equal volume times Leverage" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:12">[00:04:12]</a>

In simpler terms, this means the number of times you do something multiplied by how much you get out of each time you do it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:17">[00:04:17]</a>. Working faster means achieving more output per minute <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:25">[00:04:25]</a>. While volume (doing more) is capped, leverage (getting more from each action) is "uncapped" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:45">[00:06:45]</a>.

To truly "outwork" the competition, one must "out-leverage" them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:14">[00:11:14]</a>. Leverage is the difference between what you put in and what you get out <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:51">[00:10:51]</a>.

## Why New Ventures Make You Poor (How Not to Get Leverage)

[[Avoiding distractions and shiny object syndrome | Avoiding distractions and shiny object syndrome]] is critical because pursuing "new stuff" or multiple ventures simultaneously can actively prevent you from gaining leverage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:13">[00:11:13]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:24">[00:11:24]</a>. This often manifests in five ways:

### 1. Uninformed Optimism
This is the first stage in the "stages of change" or "stages of transition" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:56">[00:11:56]</a>. When a friend talks about making money in a new venture, people often feel inadequate about their own path and immediately consider switching <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:12">[00:12:12]</a>. This feeling arises because they are only hearing the "Instagram reel" — the highlights — not the full reality of the challenges involved <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:34">[00:12:34]</a>.

Transitioning to a new venture leads to "informed pessimism" as the true difficulties become apparent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:03">[00:13:03]</a>. When time elapses, and no money is made, it can lead to a "crisis of meaning" or "valley of despair" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:20">[00:13:20]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:01">[00:15:01]</a>. Many entrepreneurs then jump to another "uninformed optimism" phase with a new idea, repeating the cycle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:04">[00:14:04]</a>. In entrepreneurship, restarting due to a lack of [[Longterm focus and commitment in entrepreneurship | focus]] can cost years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:38">[00:14:38]</a>. The "boss" to be beaten is the ability to stick with one thing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:55">[00:14:55]</a>.

### 2. The "Construction Buddy" Story
Many entrepreneurs, especially those making less than $3 million, fall into the trap of diversification to "not leave money on the table" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:26">[00:17:26]</a>. A prime example is a roofing business owner who also did general contracting and flipped houses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:18">[00:17:18]</a>. This diversification, while appearing to capture all opportunities, leads to distraction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:53">[00:17:53]</a>. The true "money left on the table" is the [[Importance of focus in business | focus]] not given to the single, most important venture <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:18">[00:18:18]</a>.

Successful entrepreneurs like Diane Hendricks, the wealthiest self-made woman, focused solely on roofing supplies (ABC Supply), not house flipping or general contracting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:28">[00:18:28]</a>. Building something "epic" requires doing the same thing for an extended period without believing you're "smarter than you are" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:26">[00:19:26]</a>.

To avoid making money:
*   Do many different things <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:40">[00:19:40]</a>.
*   Do them for short periods <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:43">[00:19:43]</a>.
*   Don't get help <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:50">[00:19:50]</a>.
*   Make all mistakes yourself <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:53">[00:19:53]</a>.
*   Repeat mistakes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:00">[00:20:00]</a>.
    *   Learning is defined as "same condition, different behavior" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:12">[00:20:12]</a>. If behavior doesn't change, no learning occurred <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:35">[00:20:35]</a>.

To make money, do the opposite:
*   Do one thing for a very long time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:06">[00:21:06]</a>.
*   Get help from others <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:08">[00:21:08]</a>.
*   Observe what others are doing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:10">[00:21:10]</a>.
*   Make mistakes only once <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:13">[00:21:13]</a>.
*   Continuously challenge your beliefs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:16">[00:21:16]</a>.

Ordinary business done for extraordinary time creates extraordinary results through [[Commitment and focus in entrepreneurship | compounding]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:24">[00:21:24]</a>. If your current "vehicle" doesn't compound over time, change it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:33">[00:21:33]</a>.

### 3. The "Magic Wand" Story
This addresses the belief that one must wait for a business idea to "take off" before fully committing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:06">[00:22:06]</a>. Any single business — roofing, general contracting, house flipping — *could* become a billion-dollar venture <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:25">[00:22:25]</a>. Trying to do multiple things simultaneously is arrogant, as it's impossible to compete with someone who dedicates all their time to one thing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:58">[00:22:58]</a>. [[Eliminating distractions for selfimprovement | Focus]] means "one thing, all in" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:37">[00:23:37]</a>. The riches lie on the other side of a few hard conversations (e.g., firing people, shutting down projects) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:41">[00:24:41]</a>.

### 4. The "Woman in the Red Dress" (Shiny Object Syndrome)

As you grow, the "woman in the red dress" (new opportunities) becomes more seductive and attractive, requiring stronger discipline <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:39">[00:26:39]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:40">[00:26:40]</a>.

## The Solution: Focus and "Better is Leverage"

[[The importance of focus and avoiding distractions | New distractions kill leverage and keep you poor]], while [[Importance of focus in business | focus multiplies it]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:44">[00:29:44]</a>. The third principle is that "better is leverage" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:00">[00:30:00]</a>. However, this same impulse can cause them to abandon a potentially billion-dollar opportunity when it becomes "boring" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:08">[00:31:08]</a>.

Boring but better activities include:
*   Weekly split-testing landing pages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:43">[00:40:43]</a>.
*   Weekly split-testing emails <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:49">[00:40:49]</a>.
*   Creating new ads *before* they are needed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:53">[00:40:53]</a>.
*   Daily role-playing with sales and customer success teams <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:59">[00:40:59]</a>.
*   Taking more interviews to find the right person <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:23">[00:41:23]</a>.

These tasks, though unsexy, are the secret to success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:53">[00:41:53]</a>. Most people know what they should be doing but don't do it because it's not stimulating or exciting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:25">[00:32:25]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:32:27">[00:32:27]</a>.

## Traditional Automation

Traditional automation is characterized by predefined workflows and scripts that consistently execute specific, often repetitive, and routine tasks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:30">[00:00:30]</a>. These workflows are clear, step-by-step processes where data and the process itself do not significantly change over time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:40">[00:00:40]</a>.

### Characteristics and Limitations
*   **Predefined Rules:** Operates strictly based on fixed rules and inputs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:48">[00:06:48]</a>.
*   **Repetitive Tasks:** Excels at accomplishing very specific, repetitive tasks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:07">[00:02:07]</a>.
*   **Limited Flexibility:** Requires manual updates for scaling; even minor changes to workflows or data inputs (e.g., case sensitivity) can break the automation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:45">[00:02:45]</a>.
*   **Lack of Context:** Does not understand the broader context of its operations; it simply follows its rule set <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:41">[00:06:41]</a>.
*   **Inflexible Decision-Making:** Follows predefined rules without the ability to make complex decisions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:58">[00:10:58]</a>.

### Examples of Traditional Automation Use Cases
*   Simple data entry <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:16">[00:11:16]</a>.
*   Generating and sending onboarding documents with variables <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:18">[00:11:18]</a>.
*   Rule-based chatbots that provide predefined answers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:21">[00:11:21]</a>.
*   Automatically sorting incoming emails based on rigid, predefined rules <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:02">[00:12:02]</a>.
*   Extracting data from invoices and entering it into an accounting system <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:12">[00:13:12]</a>.

### Pros of Traditional Automation
*   **Stability, Reliability, Predictability:** Performs the same action consistently every time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:57">[00:16:57]</a>.
*   **Ease of Implementation:** Relatively easy to implement and manage for simple tasks; many can learn to set them up quickly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:40">[00:16:40]</a>.

### Cons of Traditional Automation
*   **Lack of Adaptability:** Requires constant updates for new tasks or minor changes in workflows, leading to frequent breakage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:04">[00:17:04]</a>.
*   **Scalability Challenges:** Makes it hard to scale, as every change necessitates manual adjustment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:24">[00:18:24]</a>.
*   **Working Around the System:** Teams might end up working around the automation rather than the automation working for them, similar to Amazon warehouse workers needing to keep pace with robots <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:32">[00:17:32]</a>.

## AI Agents

[[Impact of Agentic AI on business automation | AI agents]] are autonomous, adaptive, and context-aware <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:59">[00:00:59]</a>. They can learn from data and past experiences to make informed decisions, adapting their behavior based on new information and changing environments <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:02">[00:01:02]</a>. They extend traditional automation by incorporating reasoning and decision-making abilities, understanding natural language, and manipulating data accordingly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:12">[00:01:12]</a>.

### Characteristics and Advantages
*   **Autonomous and Adaptive:** Can adjust steps to complete tasks based on incoming data, understanding, and knowledge base <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:29">[00:01:29]</a>.
*   **Flexible:** Adapts to new tasks and improves with experience, for example, by getting better at handling inbound emails or customer success questions over time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:10">[00:02:10]</a>.
*   **Scalable:** [[Automating business processes with AI agents | Automating business processes with AI agents]] with AI agents means they automatically scale as they learn and grow, handling workflow changes easily through their reasoning ability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:33">[00:03:33]</a>. They only need tools and correct prompts to handle any changes to their designed workflows <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:41">[00:04:41]</a>.
*   **Contextual Awareness:** Possess a contextual database and are prompted to understand their role within a job function, workflow, or business, enabling better decision-making <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:01">[00:07:01]</a>. This allows for genuinely personalized outputs, such as emails that resonate with recipients based on their full context (conversations, job role, recent activities) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:44">[00:08:44]</a>.
*   **Complex Decision-Making:** Can make complex decisions based on data, unlike traditional automation which follows predefined rules <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:01">[00:11:01]</a>.

### Examples of AI Agent Use Cases
*   Analyze customer behavior to send personalized product recommendations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:34">[00:13:34]</a>.
*   Provide context-aware and quick responses to clients <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:38">[00:13:38]</a>.
*   Update project statuses, assign tasks, and predict project timelines <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:42">[00:13:42]</a>.
*   Generate content ideas, draft posts, and schedule posts dynamically <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:46">[00:13:46]</a>.
*   Dynamically manage inboxes, CRMs, project management software, and other platforms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:48">[00:13:48]</a>.
*   Summarize notes from calls and accurately input them into project management systems or CRMs, eliminating mundane data entry tasks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:50">[00:14:50]</a>.

### Pros of AI Agents
*   **Adaptable, Intelligent, Contextually Aware:** Can handle dynamic environments and learn from experiences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:28">[00:18:28]</a>.
*   **Complex Task Handling:** Ability to handle complex tasks and make sophisticated decisions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:30">[00:18:30]</a>.
*   **Human-like Reasoning:** It's akin to having a human in the automation loop, or a human deciding when to trigger specific automations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:22">[00:19:22]</a>.

### Cons of AI Agents
*   [[Challenges of implementing AI agents | Challenges of implementing AI agents]] include complexity in implementation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:37">[00:18:37]</a>.
*   Require quality data: Agents are only as good as the incoming data, knowledge base data, and prompts they receive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:40">[00:18:40]</a>. Proper setup is crucial for performance and scalability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:09">[00:19:09]</a>.

## When to Choose Each

### Traditional Automation
Choose traditional automation for straightforward, repetitive tasks that are unlikely to change significantly, such as simple data entry where the source, destination, and data type remain consistent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:09">[00:20:09]</a>. It's often a waste of an [[Automating business processes with AI agents | AI agent's]] potential to use it for such simple tasks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:28">[00:20:28]</a>.

### AI Agents
[[Workflow and task automation using AI agents | AI agents]] are ideal for dynamic environments that require complex decision-making <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:36">[00:20:36]</a>. Consider using an agent if a workflow typically requires a human in the loop, such as a virtual assistant or entry-level employee, as an agent can often perform these tasks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:48">[00:20:48]</a>.

## Combination Approach

It is often beneficial to use both AI agents and traditional automation together <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:27">[00:21:27]</a>. Traditional automation workflows can be built and then used as tools for AI agents <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:45">[00:21:45]</a>.

For example, an agent can coordinate a multi-step workflow like reaching out to 100 potential clients:
1.  An automation finds accounts based on parameters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:02">[00:24:02]</a>.
2.  Another automation scrapes information for those accounts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:04">[00:24:04]</a>.
3.  An automation identifies email addresses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:15">[00:24:15]</a>.
4.  The agent then decides which qualified leads to pursue and crafts personalized emails based on research <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:07">[00:24:07]</a>.

This [[designing_and_integrating_ai_agent_teams_to_automate_business_functions | designing and integrating AI agent teams to automate business functions]] allows for the automation of complex, multi-step processes that previously required human delegation due to their evolving nature and decision-making requirements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:32">[00:24:32]</a>. Many workflows, like managing a newsletter or prospecting, fall into a "limbo area" where they are too complex for traditional automation but not important enough to hire a dedicated person <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:56">[00:25:56]</a>; this is where [[automating_business_processes_with_ai_agents | AI agents]] excel <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:59">[00:26:59]</a>.

The evolution of business automation has seen significant advancements, from Robotic Process Automation (RPA) in the 2010s to the emerging field of [[Impact of Agentic AI on business automation | agentic AI]] and large language models (LLMs) today <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:00">[01:19:00]</a>. While distinct in their technological underpinnings, both represent efforts to optimize business operations and face similar challenges in adoption and implementation.

## Robotic Process Automation (RPA)

Ian, a leader in digital transformation, played a significant role in the RPA era, which gained prominence in the 2010s <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:32:00">[01:32:00]</a>. RPA emerged as a solution for automating tasks by emulating human actions within existing systems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:58:00">[02:58:00]</a>.

### Characteristics and Applications of RPA

*   **Focus on Routine Tasks**: RPA was primarily applied to routine, mundane, structured, and repeatable processes, often found in areas that enterprises would traditionally outsource <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:08:00">[03:08:00]</a>.
*   **Emulating Human Work**: The core idea was that automation could mimic the work people do, leading to narratives like "robots don't sleep, don't take vacations, don't need to go to the bathroom" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:03:00">[05:03:00]</a>.
*   **Early Adoption**: Pioneering efforts included applying RPA in Business Process Outsourcing (BPO) for order management in supply chains, such as with Dell <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:21:00">[04:21:21]</a>.
*   **Organizational Structure**: The rise of RPA led to the establishment of dedicated RPA Centers of Excellence (CoEs) within companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:32:00">[05:32:00]</a>.
*   **Process Complexity**: While initially advertised as simple enough for business analysts, building robust, enterprise-grade RPA systems proved complex <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="42:51:00">[42:51:00]</a>. Projects often involved intricate processes with thousands of rules and permutations, making reusability difficult <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:38:00">[31:38:00]</a>.

## Agentic AI

[[Impact of Agentic AI on business automation | Agentic AI]] is considered the "new version" of automation, building on the concepts of AI and automation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:19:00">[01:19:00]</a>. It leverages advancements in large language models (LLMs) to perform more autonomous and adaptive tasks.

### Characteristics and Capabilities of Agentic AI

*   **Advanced Automation**: [[Impact of Agentic AI on business automation | Agentic AI]] encompasses a broader range of capabilities, from basic workflow automation to more esoteric data analysis and content creation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:30:00">[21:30:00]</a>.
*   **Autonomy and Reasoning**: Unlike RPA's rigid rule-following, [[Impact of Agentic AI on business automation | agentic workflows]] have the potential to adapt, reason, and make decisions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:39:00">[34:39:00]</a>.
*   **Hybrid Solutions**: The most powerful solutions combine AI with "world-class talent" in a "human-in-the-loop" model, leveraging human expertise for design, context, support, and iteration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:10:00">[21:10:00]</a>.
*   **Overcoming Silos**: [[Impact of Agentic AI on business automation | Agentic AI]] has the potential to work across various organizational realities (data, unstructured information, workflow execution, continuous improvement, analytics) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="40:24:00">[40:24:00]</a>.

## Key Comparisons and Lessons Learned

### Similarities

*   **Focus on Business Outcomes**: Both RPA and [[Impact of Agentic AI on business automation | agentic AI]] must ultimately address clients' core business problems, such as increasing efficiency, reducing churn, or boosting top-line revenue, rather than merely showcasing the technology itself <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:42:00">[09:42:00]</a>.
*   **Communication Challenges**: Both technologies faced initial skepticism and a knowledge gap between experts and laypeople. Explaining "RPA" or "[[Impact of Agentic AI on business automation | AI agent]]/[[Impact of Agentic AI on business automation | agentic]]" to most people can be challenging <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:01:00">[09:01:00]</a>. ChatGPT helped to popularize AI concepts by making them easy to grasp <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:16:00">[09:16:00]</a>.
*   **Pricing Models**: Early adoption for both often involved time and material consulting models, evolving to "t-shirt models" (small, medium, large projects) as experience grew <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:42:00">[11:42:00]</a>. Outcome-based pricing remains challenging due to difficulties in attributing impact and client transparency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:47:00">[12:47:00]</a>.
*   **Horizontal Application**: Both are primarily horizontal capabilities, serving as a "lens" to look at operations and assess tasks for automation, rather than being confined to specific industry niches <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:00:00">[16:00:00]</a>. Success lies in being "masters of process," able to identify opportunities and redesign processes for digitization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:00:00">[17:00:00]</a>.
*   **Process Engineering**: [[Impact of Agentic AI on business automation | Agentic automation]] is seen as a further expansion in the process engineering world, building upon the principles of simplification, automation, virtualization, and orchestration (SAVO) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="51:30:00">[51:30:00]</a>.

### Differences and Evolution

*   **Flexibility and Adaptability**:
    *   RPA struggled with fragmented, non-linear workflows and seamlessly integrating human intervention, often leading to "rejected donor organ" scenarios <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:19:00">[24:19:00]</a>.
    *   [[Impact of Agentic AI on business automation | Agentic AI]] promises greater flexibility to adapt and reason within workflows, potentially simplifying complex, variable processes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:39:00">[34:39:00]</a>.
*   **Reusability**:
    *   RPA solutions had limited reusability, with each client's unique system configurations and historical evolution making it "harder to edit an existing one than to build a new one from scratch" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:42:00">[29:42:00]</a>.
    *   [[Impact of Agentic AI on business automation | Agentic AI]] offers the potential for greater reusability of underlying "steps and logic and reasoning" if tasks can be isolated from client-specific data <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:15:00">[35:15:00]</a>.
*   **Client Expectations and Democratization**:
    *   RPA vendors often oversimplified the ease of use, leading to client annoyance when specialists were still needed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="42:49:00">[42:49:00]</a>.
    *   The "citizen development" movement and low/no-code tools in the AI space contribute to a narrative that AI is "so easy," leading to unrealistic client expectations about building robust, enterprise-grade systems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="43:20:00">[43:20:00]</a>.
*   **Organizational Integration**:
    *   RPA led to the creation of specialized, often isolated, "Centers of Excellence" (e.g., RPA CoE, ML team, process mining) that struggled to integrate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="39:05:00">[39:05:00]</a>.
    *   For [[Impact of Agentic AI on business automation | agentic AI]] to scale across an organization, it requires a "hybrid team" approach, integrating various capabilities rather than creating new functional silos <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="40:48:00">[40:48:00]</a>.
*   **Subject Matter Expertise (SME) Extraction**:
    *   Extracting accurate process information from human SMEs for RPA implementation was notoriously difficult due to communication gaps and the nuances of real-world work <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:47:00">[35:47:00]</a>.
    *   LLMs have the potential to assist in this "interrogation stage" by asking questions, seeking additional answers, and filling gaps using digital common sense <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:40:00">[37:40:00]</a>.

## The Future of Automation

The future likely involves a "hybrid" model where [[Impact of Agentic AI on business automation | agentic AI]] complements human talent, solving problems by combining "world-class talent" with advanced AI capabilities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:10:00">[21:10:00]</a>. This approach respects both the heritage of BPO and the power of new technology, aiming to create solutions that make an impact for end customers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:50:00">[21:50:00]</a>. The focus remains on delivering a service at agreed-upon levels, regardless of the percentage of AI versus human intelligence involved <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:09:00">[26:09:00]</a>. However, the proliferation of thousands of "little geniuses" (agents) in an organization could lead to a "dumpster fire of complexity" without proper orchestration and a guiding framework beyond traditional Software as a Service (SaaS) models <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="50:58:00">[50:58:00]</a>.

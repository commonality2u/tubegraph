## Defining Agentic OS
The Agentic OS is a culmination of the AI agent trend, specifically focusing on building workflows and agents on top of a foundational data layer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:01">[00:10:01]</a>. Most builds start with an ETL pipeline, syncing data into vector or relational databases, or a combination, before building the workflow <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:13">[00:10:13]</a>. Once data is processed, contextualized, and stored, it becomes easily retrievable for agents, simplifying the creation of actions, prompts, and workflows <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:37">[00:10:37]</a>.

AI, Generative AI (GenAI), and Large Language Models (LLMs) are seen as the next evolution of software, bringing about a horizontal disruption across industries, not just vertical ones <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:14">[00:12:14]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:52">[00:12:52]</a>. For LLMs and systems to function effectively, they require access to relevant, real-time, and up-to-date contextual data <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:00">[00:13:00]</a>. The Agentic Operating System approach involves building an underlying software system that supports the [[Human and AI collaboration | human team]] by removing repetitive tasks and creating a "hive mind" for the business <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:12">[00:13:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:29">[00:13:29]</a>. This system is predicted to become a normal part of business operations, much like having a website today <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:50">[00:13:50]</a>. Intelligence, as a commodity, can be infused with proprietary data, taste, style, and approach to yield differentiated outputs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:04">[00:14:04]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:19">[00:20:19]</a>.

### The Optimization Engine and Flywheel Effect
For tasks like appointment setting, an optimization engine can review performance metrics (e.g., conversion rates) and, based on defined logic, automatically improve and A/B test prompts for agents <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:21">[00:18:21]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:40">[00:18:40]</a>. This feedback loop can potentially eliminate entire teams, including individual contributors and managers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:02">[00:19:02]</a>, leading to a [[Future of AI and human integration in business | commoditized intelligence]] that transforms business operations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:16">[00:19:16]</a>.

The **flywheel effect** is a positive feedback loop where increased output per unit of input allows for more input, leading to even more output, creating continuous positive growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:39">[00:36:39]</a>. In the context of AI, it means that businesses that adopt AI early will gain an arbitrage advantage, outcompeting those who are not early adopters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:31">[00:20:31]</a>. Eventually, AI will be universally adopted, and the differentiator will shift to a business's proprietary data and the logic of its processes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:45">[00:20:45]</a>.

## Real-World Examples
### Law Firm Assistant Automation
A Florida law firm specializing in personal injury defense faced issues with its six legal assistants, who cost approximately $60,000-$65,000 annually each <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:36">[00:24:36]</a>. Their key pain points included:
- Missing court deadlines (14% of the time) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:00">[00:25:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:19">[00:25:19]</a>
- Mislabeling documents, leading to lost revenue from insurance companies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:27">[00:25:27]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:40">[00:25:40]</a>
- Paralegals and attorneys spending time fixing these mistakes, reducing their availability for case work <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:53">[00:25:53]</a>

**Solution and Impact:**
Custom AI Studio first suggested migrating from their outdated "Aderant Total Office" system to a modern legal platform with robust API documentation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:12">[00:26:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:30">[00:26:30]</a>. Leveraging this new platform, a backend system (using tools like N8N and Superbase) was built to automate the legal assistant's processes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:54">[00:26:54]</a>. The goal is to replace five out of six roles, with one person managing the AI system, significantly reducing mistakes, especially scheduling errors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:11">[00:27:11]</a>.

This [[workflow_and_task_automation_using_ai_agents | automation]] creates a flywheel effect:
- **Cost Savings:** Approximately $300,000 less in salaries per year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:39">[00:27:39]</a>.
- **Reduced Mistakes:** Projected reduction from 14% to 5% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:47">[00:27:47]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:01">[00:28:01]</a>.
- **Increased Capacity:** More time for paralegals and attorneys allows them to take on more cases <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:07">[00:28:07]</a>.
- **Higher Profitability:** Increased likelihood of winning cases combined with lower operational costs leads to more cash flow <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:24">[00:28:24]</a>.
- **Reinvestment and Growth:** Increased cash flow allows for reinvestment in the business, potentially hiring more attorneys, and scaling headcount <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:49">[00:28:49]</a>.

The overall outcome is a raised bar for productivity, where one person effectively has the "energy input of 10" compared to one <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:29:19">[00:29:19]</a>. This arbitrage effect favors early adopters of AI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:17">[00:30:17]</a>.

## Human-in-the-Loop vs. Automation
The process of [[combining_ai_agents_with_traditional_automation_workflows | integrating AI agents]] into workflows often begins incrementally. For example, an agent might first be trained to respond to technical troubleshooting questions, accounting for approximately 20% of interactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:51">[00:38:51]</a>. After validation and hallucination checks, the agent's capabilities are expanded to include taking actions like executing refunds, tracking orders, or managing subscriptions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:48">[00:39:48]</a>.

Currently, AI can typically take over 60-70% of a role <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:04">[00:40:04]</a>. The remaining 20-30% involves complex "edge cases" that still require human intuition <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:17">[00:40:17]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:00">[00:42:00]</a>. AI systems still face challenges with nuanced situations that human reasoning can easily solve but are difficult for AI to grasp <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:22">[00:41:22]</a>.

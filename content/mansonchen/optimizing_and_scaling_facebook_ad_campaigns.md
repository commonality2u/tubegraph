[[creative_testing_for_facebook_ads | Creative testing]] is a vital process for growth in both web funnels and apps, as creatives are considered "the king" in advertising performance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:43:00">[02:43:00]</a>. ## The Current Landscape of Creative Testing

Finding high-performing "unicorn ads" is challenging <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:37:00">[03:37:00]</a>.
*   Only 2% of ad variations capture 68% of ad spend <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:43:00">[04:43:00]</a>.
*   90% of spend is directed towards just 10% of ads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:50:00">[04:50:00]</a>.
*   A significant number of ads (less than 1 in 50) don't even reach a $50 spend threshold <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:00:00">[05:00:00]</a>.

Historically, faster-growing advertisers tested 11 times more ads per month (45 vs. 4) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:42:00">[05:42:00]</a>. The average return on ad spend (ROAS) variance between top and bottom-performing creatives was 11x, a principle that likely holds even more true today <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:48:00">[05:48:00]</a>.

Advertisers with higher monthly ad spend tend to have more active ads:
*   $100K-$500K/month spenders average 335 ads with over 20 impressions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:51:00">[06:51:00]</a>.
*   $500K-$1M/month spenders average 422 active ads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:00:00">[07:00:00]</a>.
*   $10K-$100K/month spenders average 103 active ads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:09:00">[07:09:00]</a>.
This indicates a "chicken and egg" scenario where extensive testing is needed for growth, requiring sufficient budget <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:15:00">[07:15:00]</a>.

## Creative Trends

Several creative trends are emerging for effective ad campaigns:

### Low-Fidelity (Lo-Fi) Ads
These ads are unpolished, don't immediately appear as advertisements, and often perform well due to their authentic feel <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:18:00">[08:18:00]</a>. Examples include:
*   Post-its <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:32:00">[08:32:00]</a>
*   Writing on a whiteboard <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:34:00">[08:34:00]</a>
*   iMessage or Slack conversations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:34:00">[08:34:00]</a>
*   Google Hangouts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:38:00">[08:38:00]</a>
*   Stickers on videos <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:51:00">[08:51:00]</a>

### AI-Generated Content
AI tools are significantly reducing the cost of ad production, enabling greater [[high_volume_of_creative_for_scaling_facebook_ads | creative diversity]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:53:00">[09:53:00]</a>.
*   **AI UGC (User-Generated Content):** Software like Arc ads, Pulday, Synthesify, Captions AI, TikTok Creative Symphony, HeyGen, and CapCut help create natural-looking UGC ads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:43:00">[09:43:00]</a>. These can appear natural and not obviously AI-generated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:32:00">[10:32:00]</a>.
*   **AI Statics:** Tools like Midjourney and Flux (open-source text-to-image model) allow for rapid testing of static ad concepts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:44:00">[11:44:00]</a>. Utilizing platforms like Replicate.com or .ai allows for generating hundreds of images via API calls, then adding text overlays with tools like Canva <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:59:00">[11:59:00]</a>.
*   **AI Video:** Runway's Gen 3 is a leading AI video tool <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:41:00">[12:41:00]</a>. Advertisers can test AI visual hooks that lead to a consistent body video, creating a modular testing approach <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:51:00">[12:51:00]</a>.

### Podcast-style Ads
These ads often leverage AI voices and mimic the feel of a podcast, making them appear more trusted and authentic than traditional media <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:48:00">[14:48:00]</a>. They attract attention because they don't immediately seem like an ad, making the audience listen to what they perceive as normal conversation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:41:00">[15:41:00]</a>.

## Foundations for Successful Facebook Ad Performance

To [[optimizing_facebook_ads_for_better_cost_per_acquisition | optimize Facebook ads for better cost per acquisition]] and [[scaling_facebook_ads | scale]], several foundational elements are crucial:

*   **Conversion API:** Using the Conversion API in addition to the Facebook Pixel provides more signal to Facebook's algorithm, leading to better delivery decisions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:34:00">[16:34:00]</a>. Without it, approximately 15% of conversion data might be lost <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:37:00">[16:37:00]</a>.
*   **[[high_volume_of_creative_for_scaling_facebook_ads | High Volume of Ad Variations]]:** Advertisers should create many ad variations and let the audience's engagement dictate which perform best <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:30:00">[17:30:00]</a>. Winning ads will naturally receive more spend <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:40:00">[17:40:00]</a>.
*   **Low Cost Per Creative:** Given that the vast majority of ads won't meet performance benchmarks, keeping the cost of creative production low allows for more ideas to be tested <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:46:00">[17:46:00]</a>.
*   **Never Stop Testing:** Brands should avoid complacency after finding a winning ad. Continuous testing is essential for [[scaling_ad_spend_on_facebook | scaling ad spend on Facebook]] because ads fatigue as they receive more impressions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:12:00">[18:12:00]</a>. An "army of new ads" is needed to maintain spend and scale the ad account <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:30:00">[18:30:00]</a>. It's recommended to dedicate 10-20% of the Facebook budget to always-on creative testing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:05:00">[19:05:00]</a>.

## Audience Research for Creative Development

Effective creatives stem from deep audience understanding:
*   **Upload Reviews into ChatGPT:** Use ChatGPT to analyze customer reviews and identify concise, five-star testimonials for use in Lo-Fi static ads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:40:00">[19:40:00]</a>. Testing 50 different testimonials can surface the top 3-5 that resonate most, which can then be integrated into other video ads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:58:00">[19:58:00]</a>.
*   **"Gigabrain" Tool / Niche Forums:** Search tools like "Gigabrain" or explore Reddit threads and other online communities to understand the language and concerns people use when discussing your brand, category, or niche <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:43:00">[20:43:00]</a>. Incorporating this language into ads can improve resonance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:04:00">[21:04:00]</a>.
*   **Post and Ad Comments:** Analyze comments on posts and ads to identify common pain points, questions, or resonant language that can be repurposed for ad copy or concepts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:33:00">[21:33:00]</a>.
*   **Ad Libraries:** Utilize ad library tracking tools (e.g., Motion Analytics' free tool) to study the creative strategies of successful brands (e.g., Headway, Loop Ear Plugs, Rise Superfoods, BetterMe, Rise Science, Calm) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:00:00">[22:00:00]</a>. These brands often employ diverse and high-volume testing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:19:00">[22:19:00]</a>.

## Creative Diversity Framework

[[creative_team_strategies_for_facebook_ads | Creative Team Strategies for Facebook Ads]] should focus on a structured approach to diversity:
*   **Universal Human Motivators/Angles:** Start with core motivators (found in copywriting books) as the foundation for ad angles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:59:00">[23:59:00]</a>.
*   **Stage of Awareness:** Tailor creatives to different stages of audience awareness (unaware, problem-aware, solution-aware, product-aware, most aware) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:21:00">[24:21:00]</a>. For example, static ads with testimonials work well for bottom-of-funnel audiences, while video ads with problem-solution scripts suit top-of-funnel users <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:30:00">[24:30:00]</a>.
*   **Messaging Frameworks:** Experiment with various messaging styles, such as listicles ("3 Reasons Why"), common myths, or problem-agitate-solution (PAS) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:48:00">[24:48:00]</a>.
*   **Ad Formats:** Once the message and angle are determined, apply them across diverse formats:
    *   **Static Ad Formats:** Post-it notes, Reddit post screenshots, iMessage conversations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:10:00">[25:10:00]</a>.
    *   **Video Formats:** AI UGC, talking head UGC <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:22:00">[25:22:00]</a>.

The goal is to test combinations of concepts and messages, doubling down on what performs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:11:00">[26:11:00]</a>.

## Setting Up a Creative Testing System

A structured [[creative_testing_for_facebook_ads | creative testing]] setup is key for objective and cost-effective testing:

### Campaign Structure
*   **Separate Testing Campaign:** Maintain a distinct campaign for creative testing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:33:00">[26:33:00]</a>.
*   **Adset Concepts/Tests:** Within the testing campaign, create ad sets for different concepts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:37:00">[26:37:00]</a>.
*   **Ad Variations:** Each ad set should contain around four to six ad variations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:41:00">[26:41:00]</a>. When testing, keep all variables constant except for the specific element being tested (e.g., different text hooks or visual hooks) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:52:00">[26:52:00]</a>.

### Targeting and Budget
*   **Broad Targeting:** Use broad country-level targeting for delivery <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:28:00">[27:28:00]</a>.
*   **Budget:** Start with $100-$200 per day on testing ad sets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:30:00">[27:30:00]</a>. As mentioned previously, allocate 10-20% of your total Facebook budget to continuous creative testing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:38:00">[27:38:00]</a>. For an app developer, a minimum monthly budget of $20-$30K is recommended to realistically see results, understanding that initial weeks may not yield optimal performance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="39:59:00">[39:59:00]</a>.
*   **CBO vs. Adset Budgets:** The choice between Campaign Budget Optimization (CBO) or ad set level budgets is less critical than setting appropriate bid caps or cost caps <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:03:00">[35:03:00]</a>. If using CBO, set a high campaign budget; if using ad set budgets, set high individual ad set budgets and let bid/cost caps regulate delivery <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:29:00">[35:29:00]</a>.

### Stop-Loss Triggers
To ensure objective and cost-effective testing, implement automated stop-loss triggers for ads in the testing campaign <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:49:00">[27:49:00]</a>. Examples include pausing an ad if:
*   It spends 3x the target Cost Per Acquisition (CPA) without a conversion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:01:00">[28:01:00]</a>.
*   It spends $300 and has a CPA over $75 (for a $50 target CPA) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="46:36:00">[46:36:00]</a>.
*   It spends $500 and its CPA is still $65 (for a $50 target CPA) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="46:52:00">[46:52:00]</a>.

These gates ensure each ad receives a minimum impression or reach level before a decision is made <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="47:09:00">[47:09:00]</a>. This balances testing volume with confidence in the data <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="47:20:00">[47:20:00]</a>.

### Leveraging Post IDs for Winning Ads
When moving winning ads from the testing campaign to the Evergreen campaign, use the post ID rather than duplicating the ad <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:35:00">[28:35:00]</a>. Duplicating creates a new post, fragmenting social proof (likes, comments, shares) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:40:00">[28:40:00]</a>.
*   Find the post ID in Ads Manager under "See Facebook posts with comments" in the URL <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:02:00">[29:02:00]</a>.
*   In the Evergreen campaign, create a new ad and select "Use Existing Post" to paste the ID <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:16:00">[29:16:00]</a>.

## [[strategies_for_scaling_successful_ads | Strategies for Scaling Successful Ads]] in Evergreen Campaigns

For [[scaling_facebook_ads | scaling Facebook Ads]] effectively:
*   **Bid Caps or Cost Caps with High Daily Budgets:** Set bid caps or cost caps on Evergreen campaign ad sets and assign very high daily budgets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:38:00">[29:38:00]</a>. This ensures delivery is capped by your cost goal, preventing cost increases that occur when [[scaling_ad_spend_on_facebook | scaling ad spend on Facebook]] with lowest cost or auto-bid settings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:47:00">[29:47:00]</a>.
    *   For cost caps, start at 25-30% above your CPA goal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="43:31:00">[43:31:00]</a>.
    *   For bid caps, start high and decrease if delivery maxes out <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="43:45:00">[43:45:00]</a>.
    *   Adjust bid caps by about 20% per day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="44:11:00">[44:11:00]</a>.
*   **Broad Targeting and Exclusions:** Continue with broad targeting, but exclude current subscribers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:05:00">[30:05:00]</a>.
*   **Continuous Creative Refresh:** Always feed the Evergreen campaign with fresh creative <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:10:00">[30:10:00]</a>. When winning ads show signs of fatigue (e.g., dropping CTR, increasing CPA), pause them and replace with pre-tested ads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:11:00">[34:11:00]</a>. Monitor performance over a 7- or 14-day rolling average before making decisions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:24:00">[34:24:00]</a>.
*   **Separate Ad Sets for New Winners:** If new ads aren't taking off in ad sets with existing winners, create a separate ad set specifically for new winning creatives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:14:00">[30:14:00]</a>.
*   **Optimum Ads per Ad Set:** Aim for around six ads per ad set, as data from Smartly and Facebook reps suggest this is the sweet spot <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:32:00">[30:32:00]</a>.
*   **Bid Multipliers API:** If available, get the account whitelisted for Facebook's bid multipliers API <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:56:00">[30:56:00]</a>. This allows bidding higher on high Lifetime Value (LTV) demographics (e.g., females over 40) and lower on low LTV demos (e.g., 18-24), skewing delivery within broad targeting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:02:00">[31:02:00]</a>.

### Creative Strategy for App vs. Web Campaigns
The overall creative approach is similar for app and web campaigns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:37:00">[37:37:00]</a>. The main difference lies in optimization events and data tracking:
*   **Web Advantage:** Web campaigns often retain the advantage of user-level data tracking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:52:00">[13:52:00]</a>.
*   **App Limitations (iOS):** iOS campaigns face challenges with Scan attribution, which requires meeting privacy thresholds for user-level data <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:59:00">[13:59:00]</a>. Using Facebook's Aggregated Event Management (AEM) offers a faster feedback loop as data is modeled <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:15:00">[14:15:00]</a>. It's suggested that testing and [[scaling_facebook_ads | scaling]] on web might be a good idea due to more immediate feedback <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="37:03:00">[37:03:00]</a>.

## Measurement and Benchmarks

*   **Facebook's Ad Reporting Tool:** Utilize Facebook's free ad reporting tool for easy setup and monitoring <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:38:00">[31:38:00]</a>.
*   **Color Codes:** Implement color codes (e.g., based on Cost Per Result/CPA) for quick visual assessment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:48:00">[31:48:00]</a>.
*   **Creative Hit Rate:** Monitor the percentage of creatives that hit your KPI/CPA goal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:02:00">[32:02:00]</a>. A 10% creative hit rate (1 out of 10 ads meeting CPA goal) is considered very good <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:13:00">[32:13:00]</a>. Some brands report a 2% creative win rate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="42:37:00">[42:37:00]</a>.
*   **Key Video Metrics:**
    *   **Hook Rate:** (3-second video view / Impressions); aim for above 30% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:25:00">[32:25:00]</a>.
    *   **Hold Rate:** (Through-play or 15-second video view / Impressions); aim for around 10% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:37:00">[32:37:00]</a>.
    *   **Link Clickthrough Rate (CTR):** Aim for above 1% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:43:00">[32:43:00]</a>.
*   **Standardized Naming Convention:** Crucial for slicing and dicing data in external tools (e.g., Excel pivot tables) to identify working concepts, text hooks, and visual hooks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:48:00">[32:48:00]</a>.
*   **Open Mindset:** Always be open to unexpected results, as it's impossible to predict which creative will succeed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:14:00">[33:14:00]</a>. Continue testing and experimenting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:21:00">[33:21:00]</a>.

The message is often the most impactful creative element, contributing about 60% of importance, while the visual contributes 40% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="45:04:00">[45:04:00]</a>. Testing both visual and text hooks is beneficial, but always start with the message <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="45:28:00">[45:28:00]</a>.

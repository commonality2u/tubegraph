This approach is applicable to app founders, e-commerce founders, and new marketers alike <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:41">[00:00:41]</a>.

## Key Principles for Scaling Facebook Ads

### 1. Embrace a "Testing First" Culture

Successful companies like BetterMe, Loop, and Headway aren't primarily successful due to having 99 marketers, pixel-perfect designs, or insane branding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:01">[00:01:01]</a>. The foundation of their success is their culture <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:21">[00:01:21]</a>.

-   **Customer-Centricity**: Acknowledge that customers care more about themselves than your brand colors, fonts, or mission statements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:30">[00:01:30]</a>.
-   **Encourage Wild Ideas**: Avoid putting too many "guard rails" on creativity, as this can stifle the discovery of ideas that truly resonate with your audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:48">[00:01:48]</a>. Average content, easily generated by AI, will be ignored <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:04">[00:02:04]</a>. Humans are still best at generating wild, innovative ideas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:22">[00:02:22]</a>.
-   **Let Data Decide**: For performance creative, do not have gatekeepers who subjectively decide if an ad is good <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:32">[00:02:32]</a>. Instead, let the data determine whether an ad works <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:02">[00:03:02]</a>. It's common for even experienced marketers to be wrong about which ads will perform well <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:08">[00:03:08]</a>.

### 2. Start Small and Nail the Core Message

When adopting a testing-first culture, begin by starting small and focusing on your core message <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:51">[00:03:51]</a>.

-   **Clarity Over Cleverness**: Avoid being overly clever, as confusion will cause people to scroll past your ad <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:14">[00:04:14]</a>. Be clear and concise in your message <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:16">[00:04:16]</a>.
-   **Message Impact**: The message accounts for approximately two-thirds to three-quarters of your ad's performance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:27">[00:04:27]</a>. Focus on crafting compelling copy that grabs attention and resonates with the audience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:34">[00:04:34]</a>.

### 3. Master Statics Before Moving to Video

It's recommended to become proficient with static ads before transitioning to video <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:44">[00:04:44]</a>.

-   **Ease and Cost-Effectiveness**: Static ads are cheap, fast, and easy to create and test <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:50">[00:04:50]</a>. Examples include post-it note ads, screenshots from Apple Notes, or printed paper pasted on someone's back <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:57">[00:04:57]</a>.
-   **Complexity of Video**: Video ads are significantly more complex, involving low-fi and high-fi options, creative briefs, video editors, and multiple dimensions like duration, pacing, script, problem statements, solution statements, hooks, and audio <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:33">[00:05:33]</a>. Nailing your message with statics first simplifies the process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:02">[00:06:02]</a>.

### 4. Double Down on What's Working (Red, Yellow, Green Framework)

Ignoring signals from your ad performance is a significant mistake <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:13">[00:06:13]</a>. Categorize ads into a "Red, Yellow, Green" system to guide iteration:

-   **Red Ads**: These have a CPA (Cost Per Acquisition) that is too high and are unlikely to reach your CPA goal even with iterations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:42">[00:06:42]</a>.
-   **Yellow Ads**: These are close to your CPA goal, ideally around 25% of the target CPA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:54">[00:06:54]</a>. It can take up to three rounds of iteration on a concept before a winning ad emerges from yellow ads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:07">[00:07:07]</a>.
-   **Green Ads**: These are winning ads that are currently [[creative_testing_for_facebook_ads | scaling]] in evergreen campaigns or have passed creative testing campaigns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:16">[00:07:16]</a>. They possess winning elements and should be continually iterated upon <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:25">[00:07:25]</a>. At least half of your ads should focus on iterations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:31">[00:07:31]</a>.

### 5. Effective Iteration Strategies

-   **Remix Video Blocks**: For video ads, remix different hooks, body content, and calls to action <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:37">[00:07:37]</a>. If a winning hook (e.g., >30% hook rate) isn't converting, test it with new body videos <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:51">[00:07:51]</a>. If a body video has a high hold rate but doesn't convert, try different hooks to find a new audience segment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:03">[00:08:03]</a>.
-   **Test Winning Scripts with New Creators**: If a winning script isn't scaling further with the same creator, try using different creators to perform the same script <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:24">[00:08:24]</a>. This approach has consistently led to new winning ads that scale as much as or more than the original <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:08">[00:09:08]</a>.

This can be done using Facebook's ad reporting dashboard for free <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:13">[00:10:13]</a>.

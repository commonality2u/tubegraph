From: [[mansonchen]] <br/> 
This article outlines 13 brutal lessons learned from solo bootstrapping a B2B SaaS product, shared by a marketer with experience scaling Facebook ads for B2C apps <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>. The aim is to help other founders avoid common [[learning_from_entrepreneurial_mistakes | mistakes]] and reach their goals faster <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:20">[00:00:20]</a>.

### Challenges of Solo Bootstrapping
A key question for a solo bootstrapped B2B SaaS is whether it's possible to compete against VC-funded companies and sustain without a co-founder <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:28">[00:00:28]</a>.

### 1. Validate with Money
It is crucial to validate your product with actual money <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:41">[00:00:41]</a>.
*   **Process**: Get your ideal customer profile (ICP) on a call, demo the platform, and directly ask them to pay (e.g., $50) at the end <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:44">[00:00:44]</a>. If they say no, ask why <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:55">[00:00:55]</a>.
*   **"The Mom Test"**: Friends and family don't count unless they are in your ICP <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:02">[00:01:02]</a>. The book "The Mom Test" emphasizes learning how to avoid asking leading questions, as people tend to be nice and want to support you <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:07">[00:01:07]</a>. Instead of "Do you like this?", ask "How valuable do you think this feature is?" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:33">[00:01:33]</a>.
*   **User Observation**: When [[Building and Growing a Startup | building your MVP]], watch users screen-share and observe where they get confused to improve the product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:51">[00:01:51]</a>.
*   **First Sale**: It took 3 months from building the MVP to getting the first validated sale, which happened organically <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:37">[00:02:37]</a>.

### 2. Learn to Say No More Often
Shiny object syndrome can derail your progress <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:37">[00:03:37]</a>. Taking on large client work, even if successful (e.g., scaling Facebook ad spend profitably <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:45">[00:03:45]</a>), can consume too much time and resources, pulling focus away from product development <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:11">[00:04:11]</a>. Saying no allows you to say yes to your most important priorities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:50">[00:04:50]</a>.

### 3. Consider Your Mental, Emotional, and Spiritual Runway
Beyond financial runway ([[Financial management in startups | which is important]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:00">[00:05:00]</a>), it's crucial to consider your mental, emotional, and spiritual runway <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:08">[00:05:08]</a>. If you want to quit every day and lack a strong "why," you won't get far <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:16">[00:05:16]</a>.

### 4. Take Care of Your Physical Health
Physical health (sleep, diet, exercise) is fundamental for longevity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:32">[00:05:32]</a>. Establishing a consistent routine, like sleeping at the same time and waking up early <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:41">[00:05:41]</a>, and practicing "morning pages" (writing three pages of notes daily) can help clear thoughts and improve focus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:49">[00:05:49]</a>.

### 5. Embrace the Suck
The entrepreneurial journey is long and its duration unknown <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:10">[00:07:10]</a>.
*   **Perspective**: Focus on how far you've come rather than how far you still have to go <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:22">[00:07:22]</a>. Learn to appreciate accomplishments and be grateful for your current circumstances <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:28">[00:07:28]</a>.
*   **Self-Improvement**: Strive for self-improvement, as your current self may not be ready for the reward; you must earn it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:06">[00:08:06]</a>.
*   **Mindset**: Adopt a positive mindset, reframing challenges. As Jocko Willink suggests, if something bad happens, say "good" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:18">[00:08:18]</a>.

### 6. Team Building
While you can go fast alone, you go far together <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:48">[00:08:48]</a>.
*   **Delegation**: Learn to delegate tasks to experts, allowing you to focus on your "superpowers" and buy back time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:11">[00:09:11]</a>.
*   **Asynchronous Teams**: With global, asynchronous teams, regular syncs are crucial for building trust and verifying work <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:33">[00:09:33]</a>. This is a challenging lesson to master <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:59">[00:09:59]</a>.

### 7. Product-led or Sales-led
Choosing a go-to-market approach is critical <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:06">[00:10:06]</a>.
*   A sales-led approach is not viable for a low-priced product, and a low price makes profitable advertising difficult <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:15">[00:10:15]</a>.
*   **Sales-led benefits**: Higher price points, more support, better client results, less churn, higher LTV <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:24">[00:10:24]</a>.
*   **Sales-led drawback**: Less scalable than pure software due to requiring more people <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:41">[00:10:41]</a>.
*   The founder is still learning and has teetered between a product-led approach with a free trial and a sales-led approach requiring a booking call <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:48">[00:10:48]</a>.

### 8. Talk to Your Customers
Beyond receiving product feedback and feature requests, talking to customers provides "fuel for the road" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:05">[00:11:05]</a>. Knowing that your product adds value and saves customers time or headaches provides mental, emotional, and spiritual runway <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:21">[00:11:21]</a>.

### 9. Prioritize User Experience (UX/UI)
Underestimating the importance of UI/UX can cost customers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:50">[00:11:50]</a>.
*   **Refinement**: Eliminate unnecessary clicks and elements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:11">[00:12:11]</a>.
*   **Handholding**: Handhold the user and add a perception of speed, for instance, by using skeleton loaders during loading times (e.g., LinkedIn feed) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:17">[00:12:17]</a>. This ensures users know what's happening at all times <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:44">[00:12:44]</a>.
*   **Resources**: `vzero.dev` is a helpful resource for UI/UX examples <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:48">[00:12:48]</a>.

### 10. Start with a Design System
A design system, which is more UI-focused, makes the product more scalable, especially with multiple developers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:04">[00:13:04]</a>. Without it, colors, shadows, and font sizes can become inconsistent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:24">[00:13:24]</a>. "Untitled UI" (`untitledui.com`) is a recommended UI kit with a useful Figma file <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:42">[00:13:42]</a>.

### 11. Start Marketing Now
The notion of "build it and they will come" is incorrect; getting people to care is as hard as building the product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:05">[00:14:05]</a>.
*   **Channels**: Start marketing on platforms like YouTube, LinkedIn, X, and email newsletters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:23">[00:14:23]</a>. YouTube content has a longer shelf life than LinkedIn posts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:43">[00:14:43]</a>.
*   **Authenticity**: Be yourself online, as nobody can be you better than you, especially with the rise of AI content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:57">[00:14:57]</a>.
*   **Early Marketing**: Cold DMs and cold emails can be effective to get the first 30-50 users for MVP feedback <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:16">[00:15:16]</a>.

### 12. Speed is King (Feature Speed & Iteration)
Startups disrupt industries through speed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:41">[00:15:41]</a>. Continuously iterate and improve your product rapidly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:46">[00:15:46]</a>. VC-funded companies excel at this due to larger engineering and product teams <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:54">[00:15:54]</a>.

### 13. Keep Showing Up
Persistence is essential <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:15">[00:16:15]</a>. As Woody Allen said, "80% of success is just showing up" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:17">[00:16:17]</a>. Focus on controllable inputs, like making one YouTube video a week <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:25">[00:16:25]</a>. Given that 95% of buyers are out of market, consistent marketing efforts mean that conversations started now may convert into customers a year later <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:38">[00:16:38]</a>. The founder emphasizes that he is a "mistake-making machine" but strives to learn from them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:11">[00:17:11]</a>.

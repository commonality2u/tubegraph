## The Current Landscape of Creative Testing

Finding high-performing ads, often referred to as "unicorn ads," is challenging <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:37:37">[03:37:37]</a>.
*   A study by AppsFlyer revealed that only 2% of ad variations capture 68% of ad spend <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:43:24">[04:43:24]</a>.
*   Furthermore, 90% of ad spend is directed towards just 10% of ads, excluding those with less than $50 in spend, meaning even fewer ads actually reach this point <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:50:06">[04:50:06]</a>. This implies that far less than one out of 50 ads even make it to a meaningful spend level <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:07:07">[05:07:07]</a>.
*   Facebook data from eight years ago showed that the fastest-growing advertisers tested 11 times more ads per month (45 versus 4) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:39:10">[05:39:10]</a>. This data also found an 11x average variance in ROAS (Return on Ad Spend) between top and bottom performing creatives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:48:07">[05:48:07]</a>. These trends likely hold true, or are even more pronounced today <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:00:06">[06:00:06]</a>.
*   More recent data from Veros, analyzing 5,000 Meta accounts, showed that advertisers spending $100k-$500k per month had an average of 335 ads getting over 20 impressions, while those spending $500k-$1M had 422 active ads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:49:03">[06:49:03]</a>. Smaller advertisers ($10k-$100k/month) had 103 active ads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:09:00">[07:09:00]</a>. This suggests a "chicken and egg" situation where extensive testing is needed for growth, but also requires budget <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:17:15">[07:17:15]</a>.

## Creative Trends for 2025

Several exciting [[Facebook advertisement strategies | creative trends]] are emerging:

### Loi Ads (Low-Interest)
These ads are unpolished and intentionally don't look like traditional advertisements <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:18:21">[08:18:21]</a>. Examples include:
*   Post-it notes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:32:00">[08:32:00]</a>
*   Writing on a whiteboard <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:32:00">[08:32:00]</a>
*   iMessage conversations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:34:00">[08:34:00]</a>
*   Slack messages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:34:00">[08:34:00]</a>
*   Google Hangouts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:38:00">[08:38:00]</a>
*   Using stickers that pose questions like "We are looking for women who are trying to lose weight" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:51:00">[08:51:00]</a>

### AI UGC (User-Generated Content)
Software like Arc Ads, Puldai, Captions AI, TikTok Creative Symphony, HeyGen, and CapCut assist in generating AI-driven UGC, significantly reducing production costs and aiding [[Creative Team Strategies for Facebook Ads | creative diversity]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:43:00">[09:43:00]</a>. These look natural and are difficult to distinguish from human-generated content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:32:00">[10:32:00]</a>. It's estimated that 10-20% of creatives might currently be produced with AI <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:00:00">[11:00:00]</a>.

### AI Statics
Tools like Midjourney and Flux (an open-source text-to-image model) enable rapid testing and iteration of static ad concepts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:44:00">[11:44:00]</a>. Replicate.ai offers open-source text-to-image models that can generate hundreds of images at once via API calls, allowing for quick overlay of text in tools like Canva <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:59:00">[11:59:00]</a>.

### AI Video
Runway's Gen 3 is a leader in AI video <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:41:00">[12:41:00]</a>. Advertisers often test AI visual hooks that lead into a consistent main video body, like BetterMe does <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:52:00">[12:52:00]</a>. This modular approach allows for efficient testing of different hooks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:13:00">[13:13:00]</a>.
*   **Creative Strategy for Apps vs. Web**: The overall approach to creative strategy is similar for app and web campaigns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:40:00">[13:40:00]</a>. The main difference lies in optimization events. Web campaigns still benefit from user-level data, while iOS campaigns often rely on SKAdNetwork (Scan) or Aggregated Event Measurement (AEM) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:45:00">[13:45:00]</a>. AEM provides a faster feedback loop than Scan <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:15:00">[14:15:00]</a>.

### Podcast-Style Ads
These ads mimic podcast conversations, leveraging authenticity and the trust audiences place in podcasts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:48:00">[14:48:00]</a>. They don't immediately appear as ads, drawing attention by sounding like genuine discussions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:17:00">[15:17:00]</a>. Some even use AI voices <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:59:00">[14:59:00]</a>.

## Foundations of a Successful Creative Testing Program

To build a successful [[Principles of creative testing campaigns | Facebook creative testing program]], several foundations are essential:

*   **Conversion API (CAPI)**: Implementing the Conversion API in addition to the Facebook Pixel can recover approximately 15% of lost conversion data <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:34:00">[16:34:00]</a>. This provides Facebook's algorithm with more robust signals, leading to better ad delivery decisions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:48:00">[16:48:00]</a>.
*   **High Volume of Ad Variations**: Allow your audience to "vote" on the best ads; bottom performers will naturally get less spend, while top performers will receive more <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:32:00">[17:32:00]</a>.
*   **Low Cost Per Creative**: Given that most ads won't meet benchmarks, keeping creative production costs low allows for testing a greater number of ideas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:49:00">[17:49:00]</a>.
*   **Never Stop Testing**: Brands often make the mistake of stopping testing after finding a winning ad <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:12:00">[18:12:00]</a>. However, as ad spend increases, ads fatigue <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:24:00">[18:24:00]</a>. An "army of new ads" is continuously needed to sustain and scale ad accounts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:35:00">[18:35:00]</a>.

### Budget Allocation for Testing
Allocate 10-20% of your Facebook budget to always-on creative testing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:05:00">[19:05:00]</a>. Automated triggers can ensure each ad receives minimum spend, preventing under-spending on ad tests <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:16:00">[19:16:00]</a>. A minimum budget of $20K-$30K per month is recommended for app developers to realistically see results from testing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="39:59:00">[39:59:00]</a>.

## Audience & Creative Research Phase

*   **Utilize Chat GPT for Reviews**: Upload customer reviews into Chat GPT and ask it to find concise, five-star reviews suitable for Facebook ads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:40:00">[19:40:00]</a>. These can be used as [[Principles of creative testing campaigns | Loi static ads]] (testimonials) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:51:00">[19:51:00]</a>. A test of 50 testimonials can surface the top 3-5 best-performing ones for use in video ads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:58:00">[19:58:00]</a>.
*   **"Gigabrain" for Language Insight**: Use tools like "Gigabrain" to search online discussions (e.g., Reddit threads) about your brand, category, or niche <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:46:00">[20:46:00]</a>. This helps identify the specific language people use, which can then be incorporated into ad messaging and copy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:04:00">[21:04:00]</a>.
*   **Analyze Post & Ad Comments**: Similarly, examine comments on posts and ads to understand customer language and common pain points <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:33:00">[21:33:00]</a>.
*   **Review Ad Libraries**: Use ad library tracking tools (e.g., Motion Creative Analytics' free tool) to monitor competitors and inspirational brands <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:00:00">[22:00:00]</a>. Brands like Headway, Loop Earplugs, Rise Superfoods, BetterMe, Rise Science, and Calm are good examples of high-volume, diverse creative testers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:10:00">[22:10:00]</a>.

## Understanding Creative Diversity

Creative diversity involves systematically exploring various ad concepts and messages. A mind map approach can be used:
1.  **Universal Human Motivators**: Start with core motivators (e.g., gain, fear, belonging) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:59:00">[23:59:00]</a>.
2.  **Stage of Awareness**: Tailor messaging based on the audience's awareness level (unaware, problem-aware, solution-aware, product-aware, most aware) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:21:00">[24:21:00]</a>. For example, static ads with testimonials work well for bottom-of-funnel (most aware) audiences, while video ads with problem/solution scripts are effective for top-of-funnel (unaware) audiences <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:30:00">[24:30:00]</a>.
3.  **Messaging Frameworks**: Experiment with styles like listicles ("3 reasons why"), common myths, or problem-agitate-solution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:48:00">[24:48:00]</a>.
4.  **Formats**: Once an angle and message are defined, apply them to various formats:
    *   **Static Ad Formats**: Post-it notes, Reddit post screenshots, iMessage conversations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:10:00">[25:10:00]</a>.
    *   **Video Formats**: AI UGC, talking head UGC <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:22:00">[25:22:00]</a>.

The goal is to test combinations of concepts and messages until winning variations are found, then double down on what works <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:11:00">[26:11:14]</a>.

## Setting Up a Facebook Creative Testing Campaign

*   **Campaign Structure**:
    *   Use a separate testing campaign <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:33:00">[26:33:00]</a>.
    *   Within the testing campaign, create adset concepts or adset tests <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:37:00">[26:37:00]</a>.
    *   Each adset should contain 4-6 ad variations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:41:00">[26:41:00]</a>.
    *   When testing, isolate variables (e.g., test four different text hooks or visual hooks while keeping everything else constant) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:48:00">[26:48:00]</a>.
*   **Targeting & Budget**:
    *   Use broad, country-level targeting for maximum delivery volume <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:28:00">[27:28:00]</a>.
    *   Start with a daily budget of $100-$200 per adset <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:30:00">[27:30:00]</a>.
    *   Allocate 10-20% of the Facebook budget to "always-on" creative testing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:41:00">[27:41:00]</a>.
*   **Stop-Loss Triggers**: Implement objective and cost-effective stop-loss triggers for ads in the testing campaign <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:51:00">[27:51:00]</a>.
    *   **Example Gates**:
        *   Pause if an ad spends 3x CPA (Cost Per Acquisition) goal with zero conversions (e.g., $150 spend for a $50 CPA goal) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:01:00">[28:01:00]</a>.
        *   Pause if an ad spends $300 and CPA is over $75 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:36:00">[28:36:00]</a>.
        *   Pause if an ad spends $500 and CPA is still $65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:52:00">[28:52:00]</a>.
    *   These gates ensure each ad receives a minimum spend or reach level before a decision is made <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="47:09:00">[47:09:00]</a>.

### Testing in Automated Campaigns (AA+/ASC)
Some brands find success uploading up to 50 ads into a single automated shopping campaign (ASC), also known as the "Battle Royale" style <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="38:19:00">[38:19:00]</a>. This approach can be successful, but requires careful monitoring of trial-to-purchase rates, as different ad concepts can have varied rates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="38:52:00">[38:52:00]</a>. A drawback is less clarity on *why* specific ads are working <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="39:31:00">[39:31:00]</a>.

### Flexible/Dynamic Creative Ads
These ads allow uploading multiple videos/images, text variations, and headlines into one ad unit for testing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="40:49:00">[40:49:00]</a>. A drawback is the inability to get a post ID from the ad variation for re-use <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:17:00">[41:17:00]</a>, but they can scale effectively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:39:00">[41:39:00]</a>.

## Evergreen Scaling Setup

Once winning ads are identified, move them to evergreen campaigns:

*   **Use Post IDs**: Instead of duplicating ads, use the post ID of the winning ad <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:35:00">[28:35:00]</a>. Duplicating fragments social proof <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:40:00">[28:40:00]</a>.
    *   Find the post ID in Ads Manager under "See Facebook posts with comments" in the URL after "post/" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:02:00">[29:02:00]</a>.
    *   In the evergreen campaign, create a new ad and select "Use existing post" then paste the post ID <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:16:00">[29:16:00]</a>.
*   **Evergreen Campaign Structure**:
    *   Set bid caps or cost caps on adset levels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:08:00">[30:08:00]</a>.
    *   Use a very high daily budget to be capped by your cost goal, not the budget <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:44:00">[29:44:00]</a>.
    *   Use broad targeting, excluding current subscribers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:08:00">[30:08:00]</a>.
    *   Continuously pump fresh creative into these adsets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:10:00">[30:10:00]</a>.
    *   If new ads don't take off alongside existing winners, set up a separate adset specifically for new winners <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:26:00">[30:26:00]</a>.
    *   Aim for about six ads per adset in evergreen campaigns; this was found to be the sweet spot by Smartly's data science team and Facebook <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:37:00">[30:37:00]</a>.
*   **Bid Multipliers API**: If available through a Facebook rep, get your account whitelisted for the bid multipliers API <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:56:00">[30:56:00]</a>. This allows bidding higher on high LTV (Lifetime Value) demographics (e.g., females over 40) and lower on low LTV demos (e.g., 18-24), skewing delivery towards more valuable users while maintaining broad targeting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:02:00">[31:02:00]</a>.

## Measurement & Benchmarking

*   **Facebook's Free Ad Reporting Tool**: Utilize this tool for easy setup and analysis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:38:00">[31:38:00]</a>.
    *   Color-code results based on Cost Per Result (CPA) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:48:00">[31:48:00]</a>.
    *   Monitor video metrics like hook rate and hold rate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:55:00">[31:55:00]</a>.
*   **Key Metrics & Benchmarks**:
    *   **Creative Hit Rate**: Number of creatives that hit your KPI goal (CPA goal) divided by total creatives tested <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:02:00">[32:02:00]</a>. A 10% creative hit rate is considered very good <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:13:00">[32:13:00]</a>. Some brands achieve a 2% creative win rate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="42:40:00">[42:40:00]</a>.
    *   **Hook Rate**: (3-second video views / Impressions). Aim for above 30% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:25:00">[32:25:00]</a>.
    *   **Hold Rate**: (Throughplay or 15-second video views / Impressions). Aim for around 10% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:37:00">[32:37:00]</a>.
    *   **Link Click-Through Rate (CTR)**: Aim for above 1% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:43:00">[32:43:00]</a>.
*   **Standardized Naming Convention**: Crucial at the ad level to allow for easy data analysis (slicing, dicing, pivot tables) in Excel to identify working concepts (text hooks, visual hooks, etc.) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="32:48:00">[32:48:00]</a>.
*   **Open Mind**: Always be open to unexpected results, as you "never never never know which creative will work" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:14:00">[33:14:00]</a>. Keep testing and experimenting <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="33:21:00">[33:21:00]</a>.

### Insights from Q&A

*   **Creative Refresh Rate**: Maintain an "always-on" [[Effective creative testing strategies on Facebook | creative testing campaign]] to continuously feed fresh creatives into evergreen adsets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:01:00">[34:01:00]</a>. Pause winning ads when fatigue sets in (e.g., CTR drops, CPA increases) based on a 7- or 14-day rolling average <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:14:00">[34:14:00]</a>.
*   **Campaign Budget Optimization (CBO) vs. Adset Budget**: The choice is less critical; focus on setting high daily budgets and capping delivery with bid caps or cost caps <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:03:00">[35:03:00]</a>.
*   **New Concepts to Variations Ratio**: Start with a 50/50 mix. If ad fatigue is high or new winners are scarce, increase the ratio to 70/30 (new concepts to iterations) to find new "local maximums" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:51:00">[35:51:00]</a>.
*   **Bid Cap vs. Cost Cap**: Both can work <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="43:27:00">[43:27:00]</a>.
    *   **Cost Cap**: Start 25-30% above your CPA goal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="43:34:00">[43:34:00]</a>.
    *   **Bid Cap**: Start high and decrease if the adset maxes out its daily budget, indicating strong delivery <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="43:48:00">[43:48:00]</a>. When adjusting, change by about 20% per day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="44:11:00">[44:11:00]</a>. Monitor hourly delivery in Facebook's ad reporting to avoid underbidding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="44:29:00">[44:29:00]</a>.
*   **Most Impactful Creative Elements**: Message is the most important piece (60%), followed by the visual (40%) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="45:04:00">[45:04:00]</a>. Agencies often AB test visual hooks with text hooks to understand what works <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="45:26:00">[45:26:00]</a>. Always start with the message <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="45:41:00">[45:41:00]</a>.
*   **Conversions for Testing**: The stop-loss triggers mentioned above serve as conversion gates, ensuring enough spend and impressions to make a decision without needing a large number of conversions for every test <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="46:06:00">[46:06:00]</a>. This balances testing volume with statistical confidence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="47:20:00">[47:20:00]</a>.

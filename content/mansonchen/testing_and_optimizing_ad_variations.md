## Creative Testing Setup

The recommended framework involves separating a creative testing campaign from the Evergreen campaign <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:51">[00:01:51]</a>.

### Core Principles
*   **Ad Set Structure**: Within the testing campaign, use ad set tests where each ad set tests four different ad variations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:01">[00:02:01]</a>.
*   **Single Variable Testing**: Each variation should test *one single thing*, with all other elements remaining constant <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:08">[00:02:08]</a>. For example, testing four different AI influencers while keeping the script and other elements the same helps identify which influencer is winning <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:16">[00:02:16]</a>.
*   **Ad Lifecycle**:
    *   Ads that meet Key Performance Indicator (KPI) goals (e.g., cost per trial start, purchase) are moved into the Evergreen campaign <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:41">[00:02:41]</a>.
    *   Ads that are far from the CPA goal are cut <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:54">[00:02:54]</a>.
    *   Ads that are close to the goal can be [[scaling_and_iterating_on_successful_ad_variations | iterated]] on with new hooks, scripts, or visual concepts, a process that can be continued indefinitely <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:00">[00:03:00]</a>.

### Campaign Configuration
*   **Audience**: Start with a broad audience in your target country (e.g., the US) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:25">[00:03:25]</a>.
*   **Optimization**: Optimize for your specific bottom-funnel KPI, such as a purchase, install, lead, or trial start <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:32">[00:03:32]</a>.
*   **Conversion Volume**: Allow each ad to run until it achieves at least 20 conversions to get a reliable CAC <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:38">[00:03:38]</a>.
*   **Spend Distribution**: Typically, one ad will dominate spending, indicating better engagement and scalability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:49">[00:03:49]</a>.
*   **Pausing Underperforming Ads**: Set triggers to automatically pause ads that spend a certain amount without conversions (e.g., $300 without a conversion), saving money and allowing for high-volume [[testing_and_iterating_ad_concepts | testing]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:10">[00:04:10]</a>.
*   **Managing Winning Ads**:
    *   Copy winning ads that meet performance goals into your Business As Usual (BAU) campaigns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:29">[00:04:29]</a>.
    *   Continue running the winning ad in the creative testing campaign to avoid performance fluctuations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:38">[00:04:38]</a> (though pausing it for a cleaner account is also an option) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:50">[00:04:50]</a>.
*   **Budget Allocation**: A good rule of thumb is to allocate 10% to 20% of your overall paid ads budget to [[creative_testing_for_facebook_ads | creative testing]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:03">[00:05:03]</a>.

### Alternative Frameworks
While the above is recommended, other [[creative_testing_for_facebook_ads | creative testing frameworks]] exist with their own pros and cons:
*   **Cheaper Countries**: Test in a separate, cheaper country like India or the Philippines <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:19">[00:05:19]</a>.
*   **Upper-Funnel Objectives**: Test with an upper-funnel objective first, such as view content or clicks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:27">[00:05:27]</a>.
*   **Different Channels**: Test on other channels like TikTok <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:36">[00:05:36]</a>.
*   **Direct Launch**: Launch new creatives straight into BAU campaigns <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:42">[00:05:42]</a>.

The main takeaway is to follow a framework and always [[testing_and_iteration_for_successful_ads | test]] your framework <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:50">[00:05:50]</a>. Creatives should be constructed to allow measurement of individual element performance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:00">[00:06:00]</a>.

## The 4-Week Roadmap for Testing Ad Variations

This roadmap provides a structured approach to [[testing_strategies_for_successful_ads | testing and isolating variables in ads]].

### Week 1: Message Testing
This week focuses on the lowest-hanging fruit for ad testing: messaging <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:26">[00:06:26]</a>.
*   **Objective**: Identify the most impactful pain point your product alleviates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:35">[00:06:35]</a>.
*   **Methodology**:
    *   **Control**: Use a simple static image concept (e.g., a black and white fake tweet style or Post-it note/iPhone note app format) as a control <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:51">[00:06:51]</a>.
    *   **Variations**: Test four different pain points in your messaging within each ad set <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:28">[00:07:28]</a>. You can scale up to 8, 12, or 16 pain points across multiple ad sets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:30">[00:07:30]</a>.
*   **Goal**: Run ads until you get up to 20 conversions per ad to determine the best performing message <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:02">[00:08:02]</a>.

### Week 2: Concept Testing
This week explores different visual distinctions of ads. A "concept" refers to a high-level visual distinction, not minor iterations like background colors or headlines <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:23">[00:08:23]</a>.
*   **Objective**: Test the winning pain point from Week 1 across different image, video, and carousel concepts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:55">[00:08:55]</a>.
*   **Methodology**: Create four different concepts, keeping the message consistent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:36">[00:09:36]</a>. A suggestion is to test two image concepts and two video concepts to see which format performs best <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:43">[00:09:43]</a>.
*   **Goal**: Run ads in separate ad set tests to achieve around 20 conversions per ad <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:16">[00:10:16]</a>. This identifies a winning video or image ad for further iteration.

### Week 3: Script Testing
If a winning video ad (or one showing promise) emerges from Week 2, this week focuses on iterating on its script <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:30">[00:10:30]</a>. A "script" is the sales copy or voice-over that converts prospects into customers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:46">[00:10:46]</a>.
*   **Objective**: Test different copywriting frameworks for the script, based on the winning pain point from Week 1 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:04">[00:11:04]</a>.
*   **Methodology**: Utilize proven copywriting frameworks, such as:
    *   **Problem-Solution**: Text hook, present problem, present solution, onboarding (if app), benefits, Call To Action (CTA) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:10">[00:11:10]</a>.
    *   **Differentiator**: Text hook, polarize (what others do), unique mechanism, product introduction, benefits, social proof, CTA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:27">[00:11:27]</a>.
    *   **Pain Point Script**: Text hook, present problem, agitate the solution ("twist the knife"), product introduction, differentiate product, benefit, social proof, CTA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:02">[00:12:02]</a>.
*   **Task**: Write four different scripts to test with the winning video ad from the previous week <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:48">[00:12:48]</a>. Elements like benefits and CTAs can be reused across scripts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:47">[00:11:47]</a>.

### Week 4: Visual Hook Testing
Visual hooks occur within the first 3 seconds of an ad and are crucial for grabbing attention <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:01">[00:13:01]</a>.
*   **Objective**: [[testing_and_iteration_for_successful_ads | Test]] different visual hooks for the winning video ad from Week 3 to determine which one yields the best CAC <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:48">[00:14:48]</a>.
*   **Methodology**:
    *   **Measurement**: Create a custom metric in Facebook Ads Manager called "hook rate" or "thumb stop rate" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:09">[00:14:09]</a>. The formula is (3-second video views / impressions) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:19">[00:14:19]</a>.
    *   **Benchmarking**: A good benchmark hook rate is 30% for B2C, or anything over 20% (aim for 25%) for B2B <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:37">[00:14:37]</a>. A higher hook rate means people stop scrolling and are more likely to watch and be sold by the script <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:24">[00:14:24]</a>.
*   **Task**: Test four different visual hooks with your winning video ad <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:48">[00:14:48]</a>.

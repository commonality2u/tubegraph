## User-Generated Content (UGC) Hooks

User-Generated Content (UGC) is a powerful element in [[visual_hooks_and_modular_creative_strategy | visual hooks]]. It can be used directly or combined with other techniques like AI.

### UGC Integration with AI
The Simple Dieting app frequently utilizes [[aigenerated_visual_hooks | AI-generated visuals]] that lead into a UGC testimonial mashup video <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:16">[00:03:16]</a>. Another method involves taking the last frame from a UGC creator's video and inputting it into an AI tool like Pika art to achieve a specific effect <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:44">[00:02:44]</a>.

### Classic UGC Formats
*   **Face Forward UGC**: A "jack dude" UGC face forward video is a classic and easily testable format <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:51">[00:10:51]</a>.
*   **In-Car UGC**: A common visual hook involves UGC filmed inside a car <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:04">[00:16:04]</a>.
*   **Reaction Videos**: Filming a customer's reaction to a product can be an interesting ad concept to test <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:56">[00:13:56]</a>.
*   **Unboxing**: The classic unboxing video is a straightforward [[visual_hooks_in_video_ads | visual hook]] to test <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:23">[00:04:23]</a>.
*   **Aspirational**: For products like True Classic t-shirts, ads feature aspirational UGC with jacked individuals getting positive reactions from others <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:03">[00:15:03]</a>.

## Social Media Format Hooks

Playing on the familiarity of social media formats can create highly engaging [[visual_hooks_in_video_ads | visual hooks]].

### Replicating Social Media Experiences
*   **Fake FaceTime**: A creative [[visual_hooks_in_video_ads | hook]] involves creating a fake FaceTime call with an overlay <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:38">[00:07:38]</a>.
*   **Fake News**: [[visual_hooks_in_video_ads | Viral hooks]] designed to appear like news segments can be very creative and effective <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:55">[00:07:55]</a>.
*   **Fake Podcast**: The Simple Dieting app uses a fake podcast visual hook, often featuring a mouth hidden behind a microphone and an AI voice <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:27">[00:08:27]</a>. These hooks leverage social media familiarity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:41">[00:08:41]</a>. Another variation shows someone in a studio setting with a microphone <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:52">[00:08:52]</a>.
*   **Fake Standup Shoot**: Rocket Money has utilized a fake standup comedy shoot as a unique [[visual_hooks_in_video_ads | visual hook]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:48">[00:09:48]</a>.
*   **Twitter Feed Look-alike**: A [[visual_hooks_in_video_ads | hook]] that resembles a Twitter feed can be used for social media familiarity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:26">[00:14:26]</a>.
*   **Street Interview/Reaction**: Classic street interview or street reaction [[visual_hooks_in_video_ads | visual hooks]] are used by companies like Rocket Money and True Classic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:50">[00:14:50]</a>.

### Visual Layouts and Concepts
*   **Split Frame**: A split frame concept or layout is highly effective and seen in ads from companies like Headway <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:07">[00:09:07]</a>.
*   **Triple Frame**: A variation of the vertical split frame, a triple frame layout (or even four frames) can also be tested <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:36">[00:15:36]</a>.
*   **Green Screen UGC**: Using a green screen with UGC allows for versatile backgrounds and effects <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:55">[00:15:55]</a>.

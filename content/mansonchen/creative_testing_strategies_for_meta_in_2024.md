## Key Insights Supporting Volume and Diversity

Recent studies and expert opinions emphasize the importance of extensive [[Creative testing for Facebook ads | creative testing]]:
*   **AppsFlyer Study**: A study by AppsFlyer revealed that only one out of 50 ad variants captures 68% of ad spend <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:07">[00:00:07]</a>. This figure excludes video ads or ads with less than $50 in spend, suggesting that in reality, even fewer variants achieve significant spend <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:14">[00:00:14]</a>. This data supports the need to test more ads than one might initially consider <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:49">[00:01:49]</a>.
*   **Old Meta Study**: An earlier Meta study indicated that the fastest-growing advertisers tested 11 times more creatives per month <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:35">[00:01:35]</a>.
*   **Alex Hormozi's Principle**: Alex Hormozi's insight that "volume negates luck" is a valuable approach for [[Creative testing for Facebook ads | creative testing]] on [[creative_testing_road_map_on_meta | Meta]] and TikTok <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:04">[00:02:04]</a>.

## Recommended Approach for [[Creative testing structure for Meta ads | Creative Testing for Meta Ads]]

To implement an [[Effective creative testing strategies on Facebook | effective creative testing strategy]], consider the following [[principles_of_creative_testing_campaigns | principles]]:

### Maximize Volume While Maintaining Quality
The strategy in 2024 is to produce and test as many ads as possible, while ensuring that the quality remains high and avoiding "shitty ads" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:24">[00:00:24]</a>.

### Implement a Robust Tracking System
It is crucial to keep meticulous track of every ad being tested <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:36">[00:00:36]</a>. For long-term analysis (one or two months out), an effective ad naming convention is essential to analyze what is working <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:43">[00:00:43]</a>.

### Embrace Creative Diversity
A diverse set of creatives is necessary; ads should not all look, sound, or convey the same message <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>. This [[creative_strategy_and_testing_for_meta_ads | creative strategy]] should include a wide range of ad types:
*   **Static Ads**: Consider "ugly ads," Post-it ads, and product-focused ads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:15">[00:01:15]</a>.
*   **Video Ads**: This category can include User-Generated Content (UGC) ads, 3D motion, and AI-generated content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:08">[00:01:08]</a>.

This [[Creative Testing Framework for Meta Ads | creative diversity]] is vital for discovering top-performing ads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:26">[00:01:26]</a>. This approach for [[Creative testing in Facebook ads | creative testing in Facebook ads]] is recommended for both [[creative_testing_road_map_on_meta | Meta]] and TikTok platforms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:10">[00:02:10]</a>.

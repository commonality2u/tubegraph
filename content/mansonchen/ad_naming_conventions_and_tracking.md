## The Case for High-Volume Testing and Tracking

Recent studies underscore the importance of testing a large volume of ad creatives:
*   An Appsflyer study found that only one out of 50 ad variants receives 68% of ad spend. This figure is even lower when excluding video ads or ads with less than $50 in spend <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:04">[00:00:04]</a>. This highlights the rarity of top-performing ads.
*   An older Meta study revealed that the fastest-growing advertisers tested 11 times more creative per month <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:34">[00:01:34]</a>. This finding was further supported by the Appsflyer study <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:49">[00:01:49]</a>.

The need to "find that needle in the haystack" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:56">[00:01:56]</a> necessitates extensive testing. As Alex Hormozi suggests, "volume negates luck" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:04">[00:02:04]</a>, a principle applicable to creative testing on platforms like Meta and TikTok <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:10">[00:02:10]</a>.

Given the high volume of ads to be tested and tracked, establishing a clear [[importance_of_naming_conventions_in_performance_marketing | ad naming convention]] is paramount <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:53">[00:00:53]</a>. Without a structured naming system, analyzing past performance of numerous ads becomes challenging <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:46">[00:00:46]</a>. ### Creative Diversity

For effective creative testing, a diverse set of ad creatives is crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>. This diversity ensures that not all ads look, sound, or convey the same message <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:03">[00:01:03]</a>.

Types of ad formats and styles to consider for testing include:
*   **Static ads** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:08">[00:01:08]</a>
    *   "Ugly ads" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:17">[00:01:17]</a>
    *   Post-it ads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:20">[00:01:20]</a>
    *   Product-focused ads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:21">[00:01:21]</a>
*   **Video ads** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:08">[00:01:08]</a>
    *   User-Generated Content (UGC) ads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:11">[00:01:11]</a>
    *   3D motion ads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:13">[00:01:13]</a>
    *   AI-generated content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:14">[00:01:14]</a>
*   **A/B testing** ("as versus them") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:22">[00:01:22]</a>

Embracing this "creative diversity" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:26">[00:01:26]</a> is a key component of a robust creative testing strategy in 2024 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:31">[00:01:31]</a>.

## Ad Score Metric

When [[evaluating_ad_performance | evaluating ad performance]], it can be misleading to sort ads solely by Cost Per Acquisition (CPA) or Impressions, especially when aiming for scale <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:01"></a>. An ad with the lowest CPA might have only spent a small amount or nothing at all, making it difficult to assess true performance for scaling <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:11"></a>.

### What is Ad Score?
The "Ad Score" is a [[custom_metrics_in_facebook_ads | custom metric]] designed to provide a more effective way to rank ad performance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:22"></a>. While it can be named anything, such as "Purchase Score," the simple formula provides approximately 90% accuracy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:27"></a>.

### Formula and Creation
The [[formula_for_ad_score | formula]] for Ad Score is created in Facebook Ads Manager <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:38"></a>.

> Purchases over Cost Per Purchase (CPA) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:46"></a>

### Benefits of Ad Score
This metric helps in analyzing ads by providing a comprehensive ranking that considers both volume and efficiency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:49"></a>:
*   Ads with a higher number of purchases will achieve a higher score <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:54"></a>.
*   Ads with a lower Cost Per Acquisition (CPA) will also result in a higher score <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:56"></a>.
*   If two ads have the same CPA, the ad that generated more purchases will be assigned a higher score <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:02"></a>.

This approach allows for easier ranking of ads against each other, guiding decisions on where to [[improving_ad_performance_with_datadriven_strategies | invest your limited resources]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:06"></a>.

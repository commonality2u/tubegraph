## Categorizing Creative Performance

Creative elements are categorized based on their performance, which aids in [[testing_strategies_for_successful_ads | strategic testing]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:40">[00:01:40]</a>:

*   **Scalers** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:50">[00:01:50]</a>: Elements that reliably scale a [[creative_testing_in_marketing_campaigns | creative concept]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:56">[00:01:56]</a>.
*   **Works** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:00">[00:02:00]</a>: Elements that have shown positive results <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:00">[00:02:00]</a>.
*   **Middle of the Road** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:04">[00:02:04]</a>: Some variations succeed, while others don't meet CPA goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:07">[00:02:07]</a>. These are areas for potential iteration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:57">[00:00:57]</a>.
*   **Don't Work** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:21">[00:02:21]</a>: Formats or treatments that consistently underperform despite testing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:24">[00:02:24]</a>. It's important to label these for future reference <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:30">[00:02:30]</a>.
*   **Currently Testing** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:37">[00:02:37]</a>: Elements that are actively being evaluated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:37">[00:02:37]</a>.

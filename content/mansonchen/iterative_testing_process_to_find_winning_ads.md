### Methodology
In this round, the strategy is to [[Testing and iterating ad concepts | test]] different visual and text hooks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:13">[00:01:13]</a>.

*   **Example (Mudwater)**:

To [[Testing and iterating ad concepts | test]] these variations scientifically, they are separated into different ad set tests <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:07">[00:02:07]</a>. ### Identifying Winners
After running these tests, the goal is to identify combinations with the lowest Cost Per Acquisition (CPA) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:43">[00:02:43]</a>. For instance, if Visual Hook 2 with Text Hook B has the lowest CPA, and Visual Hook 4 with Text Hook B also performs well, it signals that Text Hook B is a strong performer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:47">[00:02:47]</a>. These [[Testing and Iteration for Successful Ads | winning hooks]] are then stored in a "winning hooks database," emphasizing the importance of clear naming conventions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:05">[00:03:05]</a>.

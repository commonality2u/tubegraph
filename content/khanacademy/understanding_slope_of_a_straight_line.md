## Defining the Slope of a Straight Line

For any straight line, the [[Slope of a line in algebra | slope]] is defined as the change in the y-coordinate divided by the change in the x-coordinate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:37">[00:00:37]</a>. This can be expressed using the Greek letter delta (Δ) to denote change: Δy / Δx <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:37">[00:01:37]</a>.

A standard straight line adheres to the equation `y = mx + b` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:02">[00:02:02]</a>.

## Calculating Slope Using Two Points

To calculate the [[finding the slope of a line | slope]] of a straight line, you can pick any two distinct points on that line <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>. If you have two points, `(x1, y1)` and `(x2, y2)`, the formula for the [[finding the slope of a line using two points | slope]] (m) is:

m = (y2 - y1) / (x2 - x1)

This represents the [[finding the slope of a line using two points | "change in y"]] (Δy) over the [[finding the slope of a line using two points | "change in x"]] (Δx) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:05">[00:01:05]</a>.

## Consistent Nature of Slope in Straight Lines

A key [[properties of slope in linear equations | property of slope in linear equations]] is that the [[Slope of a line in algebra | slope]] remains constant throughout the entire line <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:43">[00:00:43]</a>. Whether you calculate it between two points near the beginning, middle, or end of the line, the result will be the same <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:46">[00:00:46]</a>. This consistency is why a single slope value can describe an entire straight line <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:48">[00:02:48]</a>.

Contrast this with curves (like `y = x^2`), where the [[graph interpretation for slope calculation | slope]] is constantly changing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:30">[00:02:30]</a>. For a curve, there isn't one universal [[introduction to slope of a curve | slope]] for the entire function <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:45">[00:02:45]</a>. Instead, you'd look for the [[slope of a tangent line on a curve | slope of a tangent line]] at a specific point, which is where the concept of derivatives becomes crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:56">[00:02:56]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:08">[00:03:08]</a>. The [[slope of a curve and tangent lines | derivative]] is defined as the [[slope of a tangent line on a curve | slope of a curve]] at an exact point <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:46">[00:08:46]</a>.

## What is a Radical?
A "radical" refers to the square root symbol (âˆš) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:13">[00:00:13]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:15">[00:00:15]</a>. You may be more familiar with calling it the square root symbol <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:17">[00:00:17]</a>.

## What Does it Mean to Simplify a Radical?
Simplifying a radical involves rewriting it in a form where the number under the radical sign has no perfect square factors other than 1 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:39">[00:03:39]</a>. While some might argue it makes an expression more complicated, it is a standard mathematical process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:23">[00:00:23]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:25">[00:00:25]</a>.

For example, the square root of 36 is simply 6 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:32">[00:00:32]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:37">[00:00:37]</a>. However, for a number like the square root of 72, simplification is required <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:43">[00:00:43]</a>.

### Connection to [[exponents_and_radical_notation | Exponent Rules]]
Simplifying radicals is an extension of [[exponents_and_radical_notation | exponent rules]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:13">[00:01:13]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:15">[00:01:15]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:20">[00:01:20]</a>. The square root of a number is equivalent to raising that number to the 1/2 power <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:10">[00:01:10]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:13">[00:01:13]</a>.

A fundamental rule for exponents states that when you multiply two numbers and raise the product to a power, it's the same as raising each number to that power individually and then multiplying the results <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:33">[00:01:33]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:35">[00:01:35]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:38">[00:01:38]</a>:

$(a \cdot b)^{1/2} = a^{1/2} \cdot b^{1/2}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:43">[00:01:43]</a>

Translating this back to radical notation:

$\sqrt{a \cdot b} = \sqrt{a} \cdot \sqrt{b}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:50">[00:01:50]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:53">[00:01:53]</a>

This property is key to simplifying radicals.

## Strategy for Simplifying Radicals
The core strategy is to find the largest factor of the number under the radical that is a perfect square <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:35">[00:03:35]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:39">[00:03:39]</a>.

### Steps:
1.  **Identify Perfect Square Factors:** Consider the factors of the number inside the radical <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:52">[00:03:52]</a>.
2.  **Look for Largest Perfect Square:** Find the largest perfect square (e.g., 1, 4, 9, 16, 25, 36, 49, 64) that divides evenly into the number <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:52">[00:03:52]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:03">[00:04:03]</a>.
3.  **Rewrite the Radical:** Rewrite the number as a product of the perfect square and another factor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>.
4.  **Separate the Radicals:** Use the property $\sqrt{a \cdot b} = \sqrt{a} \cdot \sqrt{b}$ to separate the radical into two <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:53">[00:01:53]</a>.
5.  **Simplify the Perfect Square:** Take the square root of the perfect square factor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:58">[00:01:58]</a>.
6.  **Multiply (if applicable):** Multiply any whole numbers outside the radical <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:01">[00:02:01]</a>.
7.  **Final Check:** Ensure the remaining number under the radical has no perfect square factors other than 1 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:20">[00:09:20]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:25">[00:09:25]</a>.

## Examples of Simplifying Radicals

### Example 1: Simplifying $\sqrt{72}$
1.  **Find perfect square factors:** 72 can be written as 36 multiplied by 2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:50">[00:00:50]</a>. 36 is a perfect square.
2.  **Rewrite:** $\sqrt{72} = \sqrt{36 \times 2}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:55">[00:00:55]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>
3.  **Separate:** $\sqrt{36} \times \sqrt{2}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:50">[00:01:50]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:53">[00:01:53]</a>
4.  **Simplify:** Since $\sqrt{36} = 6$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:58">[00:01:58]</a>, the expression becomes $6 \times \sqrt{2}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:01">[00:02:01]</a>.

### Example 2: Simplifying $\sqrt{50}$
1.  **Find perfect square factors:** 50 can be written as 25 multiplied by 2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:37">[00:02:37]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:40">[00:02:40]</a>. 25 is the largest perfect square factor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:39">[00:03:39]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:40">[00:03:40]</a>.
2.  **Rewrite:** $\sqrt{50} = \sqrt{25 \times 2}$
3.  **Separate:** $\sqrt{25} \times \sqrt{2}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:49">[00:02:49]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:53">[00:02:53]</a>
4.  **Simplify:** Since $\sqrt{25} = 5$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:01">[00:03:01]</a>, the result is $5 \times \sqrt{2}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:03">[00:03:03]</a>.
    *   Note: If you had chosen 5 and 10 as factors, neither are perfect squares, so no simplification would occur <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:19">[00:03:19]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:42">[00:03:42]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:44">[00:03:44]</a>.

### Example 3: Simplifying $7\sqrt{27}$
1.  **Find perfect square factors of 27:** 9 is a factor of 27, and 9 is a perfect square <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:50">[00:04:50]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:52">[00:04:52]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:58">[00:04:58]</a>.
2.  **Rewrite:** $7\sqrt{27} = 7\sqrt{9 \times 3}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:02">[00:05:02]</a>
3.  **Separate:** $7 \times \sqrt{9} \times \sqrt{3}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:11">[00:05:11]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:18">[00:05:18]</a>
4.  **Simplify:** Since $\sqrt{9} = 3$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:21">[00:05:21]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:25">[00:05:25]</a>, the expression becomes $7 \times 3 \times \sqrt{3}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:21">[00:05:21]</a>.
5.  **Multiply:** $21\sqrt{3}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:29">[00:05:29]</a>.

### Example 4: Simplifying $9\sqrt{18}$
1.  **Find perfect square factors of 18:** 9 is a factor of 18, and 9 is a perfect square <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:54">[00:05:54]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:56">[00:05:56]</a>.
2.  **Rewrite:** $9\sqrt{18} = 9\sqrt{2 \times 9}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:59">[00:05:59]</a>
3.  **Separate:** $9 \times \sqrt{2} \times \sqrt{9}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:07">[00:06:07]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:11">[00:06:11]</a>
4.  **Simplify:** Since $\sqrt{9} = 3$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:15">[00:06:15]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:20">[00:06:20]</a>, the expression becomes $9 \times \sqrt{2} \times 3$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:15">[00:06:15]</a>.
5.  **Multiply:** $27\sqrt{2}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:24">[00:06:24]</a>.

### Example 5: Simplifying $4\sqrt{25}$
This is a straightforward case as 25 itself is a perfect square <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:39">[00:06:39]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:43">[00:06:43]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:45">[00:06:45]</a>.
1.  **Simplify:** $\sqrt{25} = 5$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:47">[00:06:47]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:52">[00:06:52]</a>.
2.  **Multiply:** $4 \times 5 = 20$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:50">[00:06:50]</a>.

### Example 6: Simplifying $3\sqrt{29}$
1.  **Find factors of 29:** 29 is a prime number, so its only factors are 1 and 29 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:04">[00:07:04]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:06">[00:07:06]</a>.
2.  **Check for perfect squares:** Neither 1 nor 29 are perfect squares that allow for further simplification <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:09">[00:07:09]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:11">[00:07:11]</a>.
3.  **Conclusion:** This expression is already in its simplest form <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:14">[00:07:14]</a>.

### Example 7: Simplifying $7\sqrt{320}$ (Multi-Step Simplification)
For larger numbers, you can simplify in steps if you don't immediately see the largest perfect square factor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:35">[00:07:35]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:38">[00:07:38]</a>.

1.  **Find a perfect square factor of 320:** 16 goes into 320 (since 16 goes into 32) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:43">[00:07:43]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:47">[00:07:47]</a>.
2.  **Rewrite:** $7\sqrt{320} = 7\sqrt{16 \times 20}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:48">[00:07:48]</a>
3.  **Separate:** $7 \times \sqrt{16} \times \sqrt{20}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:58">[00:07:58]</a>
4.  **Simplify the first part:** $\sqrt{16} = 4$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:06">[00:08:06]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:08">[00:08:08]</a>. So, $7 \times 4 \times \sqrt{20} = 28\sqrt{20}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:10">[00:08:10]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:11">[00:08:11]</a>.
5.  **Check the remaining radical ($\sqrt{20}$):** 20 can still be factored by a perfect square; $20 = 4 \times 5$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:19">[00:08:19]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:21">[00:08:21]</a>.
6.  **Rewrite again:** $28\sqrt{4 \times 5}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:24">[00:08:24]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:28">[00:08:28]</a>.
7.  **Separate and Simplify:** $28 \times \sqrt{4} \times \sqrt{5} = 28 \times 2 \times \sqrt{5}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:33">[00:08:33]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:38">[00:08:38]</a>.
8.  **Multiply:** $56\sqrt{5}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:38">[00:08:38]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:43">[00:08:43]</a>.
    *   The largest perfect square factor of 320 is actually 64, but simplifying in steps by taking out smaller perfect squares (like 16 then 4) also works <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:52">[00:08:52]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:54">[00:08:54]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:03">[00:09:03]</a>. Continue until the number under the radical cannot be reduced further <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:17">[00:09:17]</a> <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:20">[00:09:20]</a>.

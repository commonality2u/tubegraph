[[Introduction to Trigonometry | Trigonometric functions]] (also referred to as [[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | trig functions]]) can be calculated for angles within [[trigonometric_functions_and_right_triangles | right triangles]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:15">[00:00:15]</a>. If an angle is not part of a right triangle, a right triangle may need to be constructed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:23">[00:00:23]</a>.

## Key Concept: SOH CAH TOA

To calculate sine, cosine, and tangent, the mnemonic "[[mnemonic_for_trigonometric_identities__soh_cah_toa | SOH CAH TOA]]" is very useful <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:23">[00:02:23]</a>:

> **SOH**: **S**ine is **O**pposite over **H**ypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:19">[00:04:19]</a>.
> **CAH**: **C**osine is **A**djacent over **H**ypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:43">[00:02:43]</a>.
> **TOA**: **T**angent is **O**pposite over **A**djacent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:27">[00:05:27]</a>.

The **hypotenuse** is the side opposite the right angle. The **opposite** side to an angle is the side directly across from it. The **adjacent** side to an angle is the non-hypotenuse side next to it that helps form the angle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:10">[00:03:10]</a>.

## Example 1: General Right Triangle

Consider a right triangle with two known sides: one side is 7 and the other is 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:31">[00:00:31]</a>.

### Step 1: Find the Hypotenuse

Using the Pythagorean theorem (a² + b² = c²), where 'h' is the hypotenuse:
h² = 7² + 4² <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:45">[00:00:45]</a>
h² = 49 + 16 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:04">[00:01:04]</a>
h² = 65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:18">[00:01:18]</a>
h = √65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:33">[00:01:33]</a>
The square root of 65 cannot be simplified further (13 x 5) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:42">[00:01:42]</a>.

### Step 2: Calculate [[finding_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Trig Ratios]] for Angle Theta (θ)

Let's find the [[trigonometric_functions_definition_for_right_triangles | trig functions]] for the angle theta (θ) located at the top of the triangle, opposite the side of length 7 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:02">[00:02:02]</a>.

*   **Cosine (CAH)**: Adjacent / Hypotenuse
    *   For angle θ, the adjacent side is 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:07">[00:03:07]</a>.
    *   Cosine (θ) = 4 / √65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:17">[00:03:17]</a>
    *   To rationalize the denominator, multiply by √65/√65:
        Cosine (θ) = (4 * √65) / (√65 * √65) = 4√65 / 65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:52">[00:03:52]</a>

*   **Sine (SOH)**: Opposite / Hypotenuse
    *   For angle θ, the opposite side is 7 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:34">[00:04:34]</a>.
    *   Sine (θ) = 7 / √65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:47">[00:04:47]</a>
    *   To rationalize:
        Sine (θ) = (7 * √65) / (√65 * √65) = 7√65 / 65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:59">[00:04:59]</a>

*   **Tangent (TOA)**: Opposite / Adjacent
    *   For angle θ, the opposite side is 7 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:38">[00:05:38]</a>.
    *   For angle θ, the adjacent side is 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:46">[00:05:46]</a>.
    *   Tangent (θ) = 7 / 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:51">[00:05:51]</a>

## Example 2: 30-60-90 Triangle

Consider a right triangle with a hypotenuse of length 4, and sides of length 2 and 2√3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:17">[00:06:17]</a>.

### Step 1: Verify with Pythagorean Theorem

(2√3)² + 2² = 4² <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:36">[00:06:36]</a>
(4 * 3) + 4 = 16 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:42">[00:06:42]</a>
12 + 4 = 16 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:49">[00:06:49]</a>
16 = 16. The sides satisfy the Pythagorean theorem <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:53">[00:06:53]</a>.

This is a special [[understanding_and_applying_trigonometric_ratios_in_306090_triangles | 30-60-90 triangle]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:07">[00:07:07]</a>. The angle opposite the side of length 2 is 30 degrees, and the angle opposite the side of length 2√3 is 60 degrees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:15">[00:07:15]</a>.

### Step 2: Calculate [[using_soh_cah_toa_to_determine_trigonometric_ratios | Trig Ratios]] for 30 Degrees

*   **Sine (SOH)**: Opposite / Hypotenuse
    *   For 30 degrees, the opposite side is 2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:26">[00:08:26]</a>.
    *   Sine (30°) = 2 / 4 = 1/2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:32">[00:08:32]</a>

*   **Cosine (CAH)**: Adjacent / Hypotenuse
    *   For 30 degrees, the adjacent side is 2√3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:59">[00:08:59]</a>.
    *   Cosine (30°) = 2√3 / 4 = √3 / 2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:05">[00:09:05]</a>

*   **Tangent (TOA)**: Opposite / Adjacent
    *   For 30 degrees, the opposite side is 2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:38">[00:09:38]</a>.
    *   For 30 degrees, the adjacent side is 2√3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:46">[00:09:46]</a>.
    *   Tangent (30°) = 2 / (2√3) = 1 / √3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:52">[00:09:52]</a>
    *   To rationalize:
        Tangent (30°) = (1 * √3) / (√3 * √3) = √3 / 3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:56">[00:09:56]</a>

### Step 3: Calculate [[using_soh_cah_toa_to_determine_trigonometric_ratios | Trig Ratios]] for 60 Degrees

*   **Sine (SOH)**: Opposite / Hypotenuse
    *   For 60 degrees, the opposite side is 2√3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:36">[00:10:36]</a>.
    *   Sine (60°) = 2√3 / 4 = √3 / 2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:50">[00:10:50]</a>

*   **Cosine (CAH)**: Adjacent / Hypotenuse
    *   For 60 degrees, the adjacent side is 2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:10">[00:11:10]</a>.
    *   Cosine (60°) = 2 / 4 = 1/2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:13">[00:11:13]</a>

*   **Tangent (TOA)**: Opposite / Adjacent
    *   For 60 degrees, the opposite side is 2√3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:34">[00:11:34]</a>.
    *   For 60 degrees, the adjacent side is 2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:39">[00:11:39]</a>.
    *   Tangent (60°) = 2√3 / 2 = √3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:44">[00:11:44]</a>

Notice that Sine (30°) = Cosine (60°) and Cosine (30°) = Sine (60°) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:54">[00:11:54]</a>. This relationship holds because the angles are complementary (add up to 90 degrees).

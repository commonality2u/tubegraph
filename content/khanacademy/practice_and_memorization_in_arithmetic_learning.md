## Strategies for Practice

When starting out, especially before concepts are "memorized" or "mastered," various strategies can be employed to understand and solve addition problems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:29">[00:00:29]</a>:

*   **Using Concrete Objects** One effective method is to visualize the numbers using objects. For example, to solve "1 plus 1," one can imagine having "1 avocado" and then receiving "another avocado," resulting in "2 avocados" total <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:35">[00:00:35]</a>. Similarly, for "3 plus 4," one can visualize three avocados and then add four more to count a total of seven <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:11">[00:01:11]</a>. Drawing circles to represent these objects is a practical way to apply this method <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:32">[00:03:32]</a>.
*   **[[Using a number line for arithmetic | Using a Number Line]]** Another useful tool is the [[Using a number line for arithmetic | number line]], which visually represents numbers in order <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:01">[00:02:01]</a>. To add using a [[Using a number line for arithmetic | number line]], start at the first number and move to the right (or "go up") by the amount of the second number <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:41">[00:02:41]</a>. For instance, to calculate "3 plus 4," begin at 3 and move four steps to the right, landing on 7 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:41">[00:02:41]</a>. This method can also be used for problems like "8 plus 1," where starting at 8 and moving one step to the right yields 9 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:16">[00:03:16]</a>. Even more complex problems like "5 plus 6" can be solved by starting at 5 and moving six steps, resulting in 11 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:58">[00:04:58]</a>. The [[Using a number line for arithmetic | number line]] also demonstrates the commutative property of addition, showing that 5 plus 6 and 6 plus 5 both lead to the same answer, 11 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:28">[00:05:28]</a>.

## The Role of Memorization

While visual aids and methods like the [[Using a number line for arithmetic | number line]] are crucial for initial understanding, the ultimate goal in basic [[Arithmetic operations | arithmetic]] is memorization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:50">[00:06:50]</a>. Through consistent practice and repeated exposure, learners will naturally begin to commit these sums to memory <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:36">[00:03:36]</a>. This memorization allows for much faster problem-solving, eventually enabling answers to be recalled in "half a second" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:41">[00:03:41]</a>.

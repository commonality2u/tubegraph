## [[trigonometric_functions_and_their_definitions | Trigonometric Functions]]

[[trigonometric_functions_and_their_definitions | Trig functions]] define the ratios of the sides of a triangle relative to a specific angle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:12">[00:01:12]</a>. Common functions include [[basic_trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | sine]], [[basic_trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | cosine]], and [[basic_trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | tangent]].

### The SOH CAH TOA Mnemonic

To remember the definitions of the basic [[basic_trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | trigonometric functions]], the mnemonic [[sohcahtoa_mnemonic_for_trigonometric_functions | SOHCAHTOA]] is commonly used <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:00">[00:02:00]</a>.

> [!info] SOH CAH TOA
> *   **SOH**: **S**ine = **O**pposite / **H**ypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:21">[00:02:21]</a>
> *   **CAH**: **C**osine = **A**djacent / **H**ypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:26">[00:02:26]</a>
> *   **TOA**: **T**angent = **O**pposite / **A**djacent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:31">[00:02:31]</a>

In a [[application_of_trigonometry_in_right_triangles | right triangle]]:
*   The **hypotenuse** is the longest side, opposite the right angle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:35">[00:03:35]</a>.
*   The **opposite** side is the side directly across from the angle in question <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:14">[00:03:14]</a>.
*   The **adjacent** side is the side next to the angle that is not the hypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:27">[00:05:27]</a>.

## [[calculating_trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_tangent | Calculating Trigonometric Functions]]

Consider a [[application_of_trigonometry_in_right_triangles | right triangle]] with sides 3, 4, and a hypotenuse of 5 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:05">[00:01:05]</a>. This concept builds on previous modules covering the [[introduction_to_the_pythagorean_theorem | Pythagorean theorem]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:32">[00:03:32]</a>.

### Example 1: Angle Theta (θ)

Let's find the [[calculating_trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_tangent | trigonometric functions]] for an angle denoted as theta (θ) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:22">[00:01:22]</a>. In this example, the side opposite θ is 4, and the side adjacent to θ is 3. The hypotenuse is 5.

*   **[[basic_trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Sine]] of Theta (sin θ)**:
    *   Using SOH (Opposite/Hypotenuse) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:49">[00:02:49]</a>, the opposite side is 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:17">[00:03:17]</a> and the hypotenuse is 5 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:40">[00:03:40]</a>.
    *   Therefore, sin θ = 4/5 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:50">[00:03:50]</a>.

*   **[[basic_trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Cosine]] of Theta (cos θ)**:
    *   Using CAH (Adjacent/Hypotenuse) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:01">[00:05:01]</a>, the adjacent side is 3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:29">[00:05:29]</a> and the hypotenuse is 5 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:40">[00:05:40]</a>.
    *   Therefore, cos θ = 3/5 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:46">[00:05:46]</a>.

*   **[[basic_trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Tangent]] of Theta (tan θ)**:
    *   Using TOA (Opposite/Adjacent) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:43">[00:06:43]</a>, the opposite side is 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:56">[00:06:56]</a> and the adjacent side is 3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:01">[00:07:01]</a>.
    *   Therefore, tan θ = 4/3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:03">[00:07:03]</a>.

### Example 2: Angle X (x)

The specific angle chosen within the [[application_of_trigonometry_in_right_triangles | right triangle]] for calculations matters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:56">[00:07:56]</a>. Let's consider another angle, x, in the same triangle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:10">[00:07:10]</a>. For angle x, the opposite side is 3, and the adjacent side is 4.

*   **[[basic_trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Tangent]] of X (tan x)**:
    *   Using TOA (Opposite/Adjacent), the opposite side for angle x is 3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:36">[00:07:36]</a> and the adjacent side is 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:43">[00:07:43]</a>.
    *   Therefore, tan x = 3/4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:43">[00:07:43]</a>.
    *   Notice that tan x is the inverse of tan θ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:49">[00:07:49]</a>.

## Applications of [[introduction_to_trigonometry | Trigonometry]]

[[applying_trigonometry_to_different_angles_in_a_triangle | Trigonometry]] allows for calculating unknown sides or angles of a [[application_of_trigonometry_in_right_triangles | right triangle]] if sufficient information (e.g., an angle and a side, or two sides) is known <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:06">[00:08:06]</a>. This can be done using a slide rule, [[trigonometric_functions_and_their_definitions | trig table]], or a calculator <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:15">[00:08:15]</a>.

[[introduction_to_trigonometry | Trigonometry]] is fundamentally the study of the ratios of the sides of triangles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:10">[00:00:10]</a>. The term "trig" in trigonometry means triangle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:14">[00:00:14]</a>, and "metry" means measure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:18">[00:00:18]</a>.

## Right Triangles

Trigonometry often begins with [[Trigonometric Functions and Right Triangles | right triangles]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:26">[00:00:26]</a>. A [[Trigonometric Functions and Right Triangles | right triangle]] is defined by having one angle that measures exactly 90 degrees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:33">[00:00:33]</a>, also known as a right angle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:37">[00:00:37]</a>.

### Hypotenuse
In a [[Trigonometric Functions and Right Triangles | right triangle]], the **hypotenuse** is:
*   Present only in [[Trigonometric Functions and Right Triangles | right triangles]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:13">[00:01:13]</a>.
*   The side opposite the 90-degree angle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:16">[00:01:16]</a>.
*   The longest side of a [[Trigonometric Functions and Right Triangles | right triangle]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:20">[00:01:20]</a>.

For example, in a [[Trigonometric Functions and Right Triangles | right triangle]] with sides of length 3 and 4, the hypotenuse would be 5 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:07">[00:01:07]</a>. This relationship can be verified using the Pythagorean theorem, which states that 3² + 4² = 5² <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:34">[00:01:34]</a>.

## Core Trigonometric Functions

The primary [[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | trigonometric functions]] are:
*   [[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Sine]] (sin) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:01">[00:02:01]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:10">[00:02:10]</a>
*   [[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Cosine]] (cos) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:04">[00:02:04]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:10">[00:02:10]</a>
*   [[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Tangent]] (tan) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:07">[00:02:07]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:10">[00:02:10]</a>

These functions define the ratios of specific sides for any given angle within a [[Trigonometric Functions and Right Triangles | right triangle]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:19">[00:02:19]</a>.

### [[using_soh_cah_toa_to_determine_trigonometric_ratios | SOH CAH TOA]]

A useful mnemonic to remember the definitions of these functions is **[[using_soh_cah_toa_to_determine_trigonometric_ratios | SOH CAH TOA]]** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:31">[00:02:31]</a>:

*   **SOH**: **S**ine = **O**pposite / **H**ypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:47">[00:02:47]</a>
*   **CAH**: **C**osine = **A**djacent / **H**ypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:03">[00:03:03]</a>
*   **TOA**: **T**angent = **O**pposite / **A**djacent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:17">[00:03:17]</a>

### [[Finding Sine Cosine and Tangent | Applying Trigonometric Ratios]]

To apply these ratios, you must identify the sides relative to a *specific angle* within the [[Trigonometric Functions and Right Triangles | right triangle]]:

*   **Opposite**: The side directly across from the angle.
*   **Adjacent**: The non-hypotenuse side next to the angle that forms it.
*   **Hypotenuse**: Always the longest side, opposite the 90-degree angle.

#### Example: Angle Theta (θ)
Consider a [[Trigonometric Functions and Right Triangles | right triangle]] with sides 3, 4, and hypotenuse 5. Let's find the [[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | trigonometric functions]] for angle theta (θ) located between the side of length 4 and the hypotenuse of length 5 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:30">[00:03:30]</a>:

*   **Opposite** side to θ: Length 3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:12">[00:04:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:03">[00:05:03]</a>
*   **Adjacent** side to θ: Length 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:09">[00:05:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:18">[00:05:18]</a>
*   **Hypotenuse**: Length 5 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:22">[00:04:22]</a>

Therefore, for angle θ:
*   [[Finding Sine Cosine and Tangent | Sine (θ)]] = Opposite / Hypotenuse = 3 / 5 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:24">[00:04:24]</a>
*   [[Finding Sine Cosine and Tangent | Cosine (θ)]] = Adjacent / Hypotenuse = 4 / 5 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:42">[00:05:42]</a>
*   [[Finding Sine Cosine and Tangent | Tangent (θ)]] = Opposite / Adjacent = 3 / 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:09">[00:06:09]</a>

#### Example: Angle X
Now, consider the other acute angle in the same triangle, let's call it x <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:06">[00:07:06]</a>. The relationships between sides and angles change:

*   **Opposite** side to x: Length 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:27">[00:07:27]</a>
*   **Adjacent** side to x: Length 3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:09">[00:08:09]</a>
*   **Hypotenuse**: Length 5 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:44">[00:07:44]</a> (hypotenuse remains the same regardless of the angle chosen)

Therefore, for angle x:
*   [[Finding Sine Cosine and Tangent | Sine (x)]] = Opposite / Hypotenuse = 4 / 5 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:46">[00:07:46]</a>
*   [[Finding Sine Cosine and Tangent | Cosine (x)]] = Adjacent / Hypotenuse = 3 / 5 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:14">[00:08:14]</a>
*   [[Finding Sine Cosine and Tangent | Tangent (x)]] = Opposite / Adjacent = 4 / 3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:42">[00:08:42]</a>

This [[trigonometric_functions_definition_for_right_triangles | definition for right triangles]] (SOH CAH TOA) is primarily applicable for angles between 0 and 90 degrees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:59">[00:08:59]</a>. More advanced definitions are used for angles beyond this range <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:06">[00:09:06]</a>.

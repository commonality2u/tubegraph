## Basic Application of the Distributive Property

Consider the [[understanding_algebraic_expressions | expression]] `2(3x + 5)` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:15">[00:00:15]</a>.
Literally, this means "two `(3x + 5)`" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:21">[00:00:21]</a>. This can be understood as adding `(3x + 5)` to itself <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:28">[00:00:28]</a>.

Expanded, `2(3x + 5)` is the [[understanding_equivalent_expressions | exact same thing]] as `(3x + 5) + (3x + 5)` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:30">[00:00:30]</a>.
When grouped, this becomes `(3x + 3x) + (5 + 5)` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:37">[00:00:37]</a>, which simplifies to `2(3x) + 2(5)` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:40">[00:00:40]</a>.

This process is known as the distributive property, a concept often familiar from arithmetic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:53">[00:00:53]</a>. It shows that multiplying a sum by a number is the same as multiplying each addend by the number and then adding the products <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:09">[00:01:09]</a>.

To further [[stepbystep_algebraic_simplification | simplify]] `2(3x) + 2(5)`:
*   `2 * 3x` equals `6x` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:15">[00:01:15]</a>.
*   `2 * 5` equals `10` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:21">[00:01:21]</a>.

Thus, `2(3x + 5)` simplifies to `6x + 10` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:24">[00:01:24]</a>.

## More Complex Expression Simplification

Let's consider a more involved [[understanding_algebraic_expressions | expression]]: `7(3y - 5) - 2(10 + 4y)` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:34">[00:01:34]</a>.

### Step 1: Distribute on the left-hand side

For `7(3y - 5)`, distribute the `7`:
*   `7 * 3y` gives `21y` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:03">[00:02:03]</a>.
*   `7 * -5` gives `-35` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:16">[00:02:16]</a>.
So, `7(3y - 5)` simplifies to `21y - 35` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:22">[00:02:22]</a>.

### Step 2: Distribute on the right-hand side

For `-2(10 + 4y)`, it's helpful to distribute the entire `-2` (including the negative sign) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:35">[00:02:35]</a>:
*   `-2 * 10` gives `-20` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:39">[00:02:39]</a>.
*   `-2 * 4y` gives `-8y` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:56">[00:02:56]</a>.
So, `-2(10 + 4y)` simplifies to `-20 - 8y` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:03">[00:03:03]</a>.

### Step 3: Combine the simplified parts and [[combining_like_terms_in_mathematical_expressions | combine like terms]]

Now the original [[understanding_algebraic_expressions | expression]] is `21y - 35 - 20 - 8y`.
[[combining_like_terms_in_mathematical_expressions | Combine the terms]] with `y`:
*   `21y - 8y` equals `13y` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:33">[00:03:33]</a>.

[[combining_like_terms_in_mathematical_expressions | Combine the constant terms]]:
*   `-35 - 20` equals `-55` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:42">[00:03:42]</a>.

Therefore, the fully [[stepbystep_algebraic_simplification | simplified expression]] is `13y - 55` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:56">[00:03:56]</a>.

> [!Summary]
> Simplifying [[understanding_algebraic_expressions | expressions]] often involves two main steps:
> 1.  **Distributing**: Apply the distributive property to remove parentheses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:59">[00:01:59]</a>. Remember to distribute any negative signs along with the number <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:35">[00:02:35]</a>.
> 2.  **[[combining_like_terms_in_mathematical_expressions | Combining like terms]]**: Group and sum terms that have the same variable and exponent (or are just constants) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:17">[00:03:17]</a>.

These fundamental concepts are pure and more foundational than their specific [[applications_of_algebra_in_various_domains | applications]] in fields like economics, finance, physics, or chemistry <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:51">[00:00:51]</a>.

## From Concrete to Abstract: A Discount Example

To illustrate this process of abstraction, consider a simple scenario of calculating a discount at a store <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:15">[00:01:15]</a>.

### Specific Calculation (Arithmetic)

If a pair of pants costs $20 and is on a 30% off sale <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:39">[00:01:39]</a>, the discount is calculated by multiplying the percentage by the price <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:01">[00:02:01]</a>.
*   Discount = 30% × $20 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:09">[00:02:09]</a>
*   Or, as a decimal: 0.30 × $20 = $6 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:18">[00:02:18]</a>

This is an [[applying_arithmetic_to_algebra | arithmetic]] calculation, not yet algebraic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:57">[00:01:57]</a>.

### Generalizing the Product Price

To generalize this for any product in the store, a variable is introduced <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:29">[00:02:29]</a>. Let `x` represent the non-discount price of any product <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:45">[00:02:45]</a>.

*   Discount = 30% × `x` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:02">[00:03:02]</a>
*   Or: 0.30 × `x` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:18">[00:03:18]</a>

This allows for calculating the discount for any product price by substituting its value for `x` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:26">[00:03:26]</a>. This represents an early step into [[understanding_basic_algebraic_notation | algebraic abstraction]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:38">[00:03:38]</a>.

### Generalizing the Percentage Off

Further generalization involves making the discount percentage itself a variable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:13">[00:04:13]</a>. Let `p` represent the percentage off <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:25">[00:04:25]</a>.

*   Discount = `p` × `x` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:05">[00:05:05]</a>

If `y` is defined as the discount <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:21">[00:05:21]</a>, the equation becomes:

*   `y` = `p` × `x` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:33">[00:05:33]</a>

This equation, where `y` is equated to `p` times `x`, is a foundational concept in algebra <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:02">[00:06:02]</a>.

## Universal Application of Algebraic Relationships

The beauty of such an algebraic equation is its applicability across seemingly unrelated domains <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:07">[00:06:07]</a>. For example, in physics, the famous equation Force = Mass × Acceleration is fundamentally the same idea <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:15">[00:06:15]</a>.

If:
*   `y` = Force <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:25">[00:06:25]</a>
*   `p` = Mass <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:36">[00:06:36]</a>
*   `x` = Acceleration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:48">[00:06:48]</a>

Then, Force = Mass × Acceleration can be written as `y` = `p` × `x` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:02">[00:07:02]</a>. This demonstrates that the same abstract relationship applies to vastly different concepts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:42">[00:06:42]</a>.

This singular equation has an "infinite number of [[applications_of_algebra_in_various_domains | applications]]" in fields like economics, finance, computer science, logic, and electrical engineering <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:23">[00:07:23]</a>.

## The Power of Abstract Manipulation

One of the significant benefits of algebra is the ability to focus on and manipulate these abstract relationships <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:42">[00:07:42]</a>. Discoveries made through these manipulations can then be reapplied to all specific contexts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:51">[00:07:51]</a>. Moreover, these abstract manipulations reveal the true structure of the universe, stripped of human-defined applications <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:03">[00:08:03]</a>.

Consider the equation `y` = `p` × `x` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:10">[00:08:10]</a>. If both sides are divided by `x` (an [[algebraic_operations_and_their_legitimacy | algebraic operation]]):

*   `y` / `x` = (`p` × `x`) / `x` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:38">[00:08:38]</a>
*   `y` / `x` = `p` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:04">[00:09:04]</a>

This manipulation, performed on the abstract form, has implications for every specific application <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:16">[00:09:16]</a>. It reveals a fundamental truth about relationships that are independent of any specific domain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:20">[00:09:20]</a>. The existence of an infinite number of applications, many yet undiscovered, underscores the timeless utility of algebraic abstraction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:30">[00:09:30]</a>. This highlights why mathematics is considered a universal language, even as a potential common ground for communication with alien life forms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:51">[00:09:51]</a>.

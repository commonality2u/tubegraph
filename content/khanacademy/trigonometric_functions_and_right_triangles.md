## Understanding Right Triangles

A [[properties_of_right_triangles | right triangle]] is a triangle that contains a 90-degree angle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:01">[00:01:01]</a>. In a [[properties_of_right_triangles | right triangle]], the longest side, which is opposite the right angle, is called the hypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:35">[00:03:35]</a>.

### Example Right Triangle
For illustrative purposes, consider a [[properties_of_right_triangles | right triangle]] with sides of length 3 and 4, and a hypotenuse of length 5 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:05">[00:01:05]</a>. This adheres to the [[pythagorean_theorem_in_right_triangles | Pythagorean theorem]], where 3² + 4² = 5² (9 + 16 = 25).

## [[trigonometric_functions_definition_for_right_triangles | Trigonometric Functions]]

[[trigonometric_functions_definition_for_right_triangles | Trig functions]] reveal the ratios of the sides of a triangle relative to a specific angle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:12">[00:01:12]</a>.

### The SOH CAH TOA Mnemonic

A common mnemonic used to remember the definitions of the basic [[trigonometric_functions_definition_for_right_triangles | trig functions]] is SOH CAH TOA <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:00">[00:02:00]</a>:

> [!info] SOH CAH TOA
> *   **SOH**: **S**ine = **O**pposite / **H**ypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:21">[00:02:21]</a>
> *   **CAH**: **C**osine = **A**djacent / **H**ypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:26">[00:02:26]</a>
> *   **TOA**: **T**angent = **O**pposite / **A**djacent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:31">[00:02:31]</a>

Let's use the example triangle (sides 3, 4, hypotenuse 5) and an angle denoted as theta (θ) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:22">[00:01:22]</a>.

### [[finding_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Sine (sin)]]

To find the [[finding_sine_cosine_and_tangent | sine]] of theta (sin θ) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:44">[00:01:44]</a>:
*   According to SOH, [[finding_sine_cosine_and_tangent | sine]] = Opposite / Hypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:49">[00:02:49]</a>.
*   The side opposite angle theta is 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:14">[00:03:14]</a>.
*   The hypotenuse is 5 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:38">[00:03:38]</a>.
*   Therefore, sin θ = 4/5 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:50">[00:03:50]</a>.

### [[finding_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Cosine (cos)]]

To find the [[finding_sine_cosine_and_tangent | cosine]] of theta (cos θ) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:50">[00:04:50]</a>:
*   According to CAH, [[finding_sine_cosine_and_tangent | cosine]] = Adjacent / Hypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:01">[00:05:01]</a>.
*   The side adjacent to angle theta is 3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:27">[00:05:27]</a>.
*   The hypotenuse is 5 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:36">[00:05:36]</a>.
*   Therefore, cos θ = 3/5 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:46">[00:05:46]</a>.

### [[finding_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Tangent (tan)]]

To find the [[finding_sine_cosine_and_tangent | tangent]] of theta (tan θ) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:31">[00:06:31]</a>:
*   According to TOA, [[finding_sine_cosine_and_tangent | tangent]] = Opposite / Adjacent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:43">[00:06:43]</a>.
*   The side opposite angle theta is 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:54">[00:06:54]</a>.
*   The side adjacent to angle theta is 3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:00">[00:07:00]</a>.
*   Therefore, tan θ = 4/3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:03">[00:07:03]</a>.

### The Importance of the Reference Angle

The choice of angle in a [[properties_of_right_triangles | right triangle]] significantly impacts the ratios for [[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | trigonometric functions]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:56">[00:07:56]</a>.

Consider another angle in the same triangle, labeled 'x' <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:17">[00:07:17]</a>.
*   To find the [[finding_sine_cosine_and_tangent | tangent]] of x (tan x) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:23">[00:07:23]</a>:
    *   The side opposite angle x is 3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:33">[00:07:33]</a>.
    *   The side adjacent to angle x is 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:43">[00:07:43]</a>.
    *   Therefore, tan x = 3/4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:43">[00:07:43]</a>.

Notice that tan x (3/4) is the inverse of tan θ (4/3) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:49">[00:07:49]</a>. This highlights that the "opposite" and "adjacent" sides depend entirely on which acute angle of the [[properties_of_right_triangles | right triangle]] is being referenced.

## [[realworld_applications_of_trigonometry | Applications]]

These calculations can be performed using calculators, slide rules, or [[trigonometric_functions_definition_for_right_triangles | trig]] tables <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:15">[00:08:15]</a>.

Trigonometry is fundamentally the study of the ratios of sides of triangles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:10">[00:00:10]</a>. The term "trig" means triangle, and "metry" means measure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:18">[00:00:18]</a>.

## Core Trigonometric Functions

The primary [[function_notation_and_examples | functions]] in trigonometry are:
*   [[function_notation_and_examples | Sine]] (sin) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:01">[00:02:01]</a>
*   [[function_notation_and_examples | Cosine]] (cos) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:04">[00:02:04]</a>
*   [[function_notation_and_examples | Tangent]] (tan) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:07">[00:02:07]</a>

These [[function_notation_and_examples | functions]] define specific ratios of sides for any given angle within a right triangle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:19">[00:02:19]</a>.

### The [[sohcahtoa_mnemonic_for_trigonometric_functions | SOH CAH TOA]] [[mnemonic_soh_cah_toa_for_trigonometry | Mnemonic]]

A useful [[mnemonic_soh_cah_toa_for_trigonometry | mnemonic]] to remember the definitions of these [[function_notation_and_examples | functions]] is [[sohcahtoa_mnemonic_for_trigonometric_functions | SOH CAH TOA]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:31">[00:02:31]</a>:
*   **SOH**: [[function_notation_and_examples | Sine]] = Opposite / Hypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:47">[00:02:47]</a>
*   **CAH**: [[function_notation_and_examples | Cosine]] = Adjacent / Hypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:03">[00:03:03]</a>
*   **TOA**: [[function_notation_and_examples | Tangent]] = Opposite / Adjacent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:17">[00:03:17]</a>

## Right Triangles

Trigonometry, using the [[sohcahtoa_mnemonic_for_trigonometric_functions | SOH CAH TOA]] definitions, applies to right triangles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:26">[00:00:26]</a>. A right triangle is characterized by having one angle that measures 90 degrees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:33">[00:00:33]</a>.

In a right triangle:
*   **Hypotenuse**: The side opposite the 90-degree angle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:16">[00:01:16]</a>. It is always the longest side of a right triangle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:20">[00:01:20]</a>.
*   **Opposite**: The side directly across from the angle being considered.
*   **Adjacent**: The side next to the angle being considered, which is not the hypotenuse.

The Pythagorean theorem (a² + b² = c²) applies to right triangles, for instance, 3² + 4² = 5² <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:32">[00:01:32]</a>.

## Applying [[sohcahtoa_mnemonic_for_trigonometric_functions | SOH CAH TOA]] with Examples

Consider a right triangle with side lengths 3, 4, and 5 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:57">[00:00:57]</a>.

### Example 1: Angle Theta (θ)

Let's identify the ratios for angle theta (θ), located between the sides of length 4 and 5 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:30">[00:03:30]</a>.

*   The **opposite** side to theta is 3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:12">[00:04:12]</a>.
*   The **adjacent** side to theta is 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:09">[00:05:09]</a>.
*   The **hypotenuse** is 5 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:22">[00:04:22]</a>.

Using [[sohcahtoa_mnemonic_for_trigonometric_functions | SOH CAH TOA]]:
*   [[examples_of_calculating_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Sine (sin) of θ]] = Opposite / Hypotenuse = 3 / 5 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:27">[00:04:27]</a>
*   [[examples_of_calculating_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Cosine (cos) of θ]] = Adjacent / Hypotenuse = 4 / 5 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:42">[00:05:42]</a>
*   [[examples_of_calculating_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Tangent (tan) of θ]] = Opposite / Adjacent = 3 / 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:59">[00:05:59]</a>

### Example 2: Angle X

Now, let's consider the other acute angle, X, in the same triangle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:46">[00:06:46]</a>. The definitions of "opposite" and "adjacent" change relative to the new angle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:27">[00:07:27]</a>.

*   The **opposite** side to X is 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:23">[00:07:23]</a>.
*   The **adjacent** side to X is 3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:07">[00:08:07]</a>.
*   The **hypotenuse** remains 5 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:40">[00:07:40]</a>.

Using [[sohcahtoa_mnemonic_for_trigonometric_functions | SOH CAH TOA]]:
*   [[examples_of_calculating_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Sine (sin) of X]] = Opposite / Hypotenuse = 4 / 5 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:46">[00:07:46]</a>
*   [[examples_of_calculating_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Cosine (cos) of X]] = Adjacent / Hypotenuse = 3 / 5 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:10">[00:08:10]</a>
*   [[examples_of_calculating_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Tangent (tan) of X]] = Opposite / Adjacent = 4 / 3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:34">[00:08:34]</a>

This [[sohcahtoa_mnemonic_for_trigonometry | mnemonic]] is highly effective for angles between 0 and 90 degrees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:57">[00:08:57]</a>. For angles outside this range or at the boundaries (0 or 90 degrees), more general definitions of sine, cosine, and tangent are introduced <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:06">[00:09:06]</a>.

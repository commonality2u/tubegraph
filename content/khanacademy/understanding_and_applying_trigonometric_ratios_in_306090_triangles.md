## Calculating Trigonometric Ratios for a General Right Triangle

Consider a [[trigonometric_functions_and_right_triangles | right triangle]] with two sides given as 7 and 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:31">[00:00:31]</a>.

### Determining the Hypotenuse

To find the hypotenuse (h), the [[pythagorean_theorem_in_right_triangles | Pythagorean theorem]] states that the square of the hypotenuse is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:52">[00:00:52]</a>:
$h^2 = 7^2 + 4^2$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:45">[00:00:45]</a>
$h^2 = 49 + 16$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:00">[01:04:00]</a>
$h^2 = 65$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18:00">[01:18:00]</a>
Therefore, $h = \sqrt{65}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:33:00">[01:33:00]</a>. This value cannot be simplified further <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:47">[00:01:47]</a>.

### Applying "SOH CAH TOA"

To determine the [[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | trig functions]] for an angle, let's call it theta (θ), it's useful to recall the mnemonic "[[using_soh_cah_toa_to_determine_trigonometric_ratios | SOH CAH TOA]]" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:09">[00:02:09]</a>.

*   **SOH**: [[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Sine]] = Opposite / Hypotenuse
*   **CAH**: [[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Cosine]] = Adjacent / Hypotenuse
*   **TOA**: [[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Tangent]] = Opposite / Adjacent

#### [[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Cosine]] of Theta (θ)

Using "CAH", [[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | cosine]] is adjacent over hypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:43:00">[02:43:00]</a>.
For angle θ, the adjacent side is 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:04:00">[03:04:00]</a>, and the hypotenuse is $\sqrt{65}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:17:00">[03:17:00]</a>.
$\cos(\theta) = \frac{4}{\sqrt{65}}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:21:00">[03:21:00]</a>
To rationalize the denominator, multiply the numerator and denominator by $\sqrt{65}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:39:00">[03:39:00]</a>:
$\cos(\theta) = \frac{4}{\sqrt{65}} \times \frac{\sqrt{65}}{\sqrt{65}} = \frac{4\sqrt{65}}{65}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:54:00">[03:54:00]</a>

#### [[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Sine]] of Theta (θ)

Using "SOH", [[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | sine]] is opposite over hypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:19:00">[04:19:00]</a>.
For angle θ, the opposite side is 7 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:34:00">[04:34:00]</a>, and the hypotenuse is $\sqrt{65}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:47:00">[04:47:00]</a>.
$\sin(\theta) = \frac{7}{\sqrt{65}}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:51:00">[04:51:00]</a>
Rationalizing the denominator:
$\sin(\theta) = \frac{7}{\sqrt{65}} \times \frac{\sqrt{65}}{\sqrt{65}} = \frac{7\sqrt{65}}{65}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:55:00">[04:55:00]</a>

#### [[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Tangent]] of Theta (θ)

Using "TOA", [[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | tangent]] is opposite over adjacent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:27:00">[05:27:00]</a>.
For angle θ, the opposite side is 7 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:38:00">[05:38:00]</a>, and the adjacent side is 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:46:00">[05:46:00]</a>.
$\tan(\theta) = \frac{7}{4}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:51:00">[05:51:00]</a>

## Applying Trigonometric Ratios in a 30-60-90 Triangle

Consider a [[trigonometric_functions_and_right_triangles | right triangle]] with a hypotenuse of length 4, one side of length 2, and another side of length $2\sqrt{3}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:17:00">[06:17:00]</a>.

### Verification using [[pythagorean_theorem_in_right_triangles | Pythagorean Theorem]]

To verify it's a [[trigonometric_functions_and_right_triangles | right triangle]], check if the [[pythagorean_theorem_in_right_triangles | Pythagorean theorem]] holds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:31:00">[06:31:00]</a>:
$(2\sqrt{3})^2 + 2^2 = (4 \times 3) + 4 = 12 + 4 = 16$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:42:00">[06:42:00]</a>
Since $16 = 4^2$, the theorem is satisfied, confirming it's a [[trigonometric_functions_and_right_triangles | right triangle]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:53:00">[06:53:00]</a>.

This specific triangle is a 30-60-90 triangle because the side opposite the 30-degree angle (length 2) is half the hypotenuse (length 4) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:27:00">[07:27:00]</a>, and the side opposite the 60-degree angle (length $2\sqrt{3}$) is $\sqrt{3}$ times the side opposite the 30-degree angle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:31:00">[07:31:00]</a>.

### [[calculation_of_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Trig Ratios]] for the 30-degree Angle

#### [[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Sine]] of 30 degrees

Using "SOH" ([[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Sine]] = Opposite / Hypotenuse) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:17:00">[08:17:00]</a>:
For the 30-degree angle, the opposite side is 2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:26:00">[08:26:00]</a>, and the hypotenuse is 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:30:00">[08:30:00]</a>.
$\sin(30^\circ) = \frac{2}{4} = \frac{1}{2}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:32:00">[08:32:00]</a>

#### [[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Cosine]] of 30 degrees

Using "CAH" ([[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Cosine]] = Adjacent / Hypotenuse) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:52:00">[08:52:00]</a>:
For the 30-degree angle, the adjacent side is $2\sqrt{3}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:05:00">[09:05:00]</a>, and the hypotenuse is 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:12:00">[09:12:00]</a>.
$\cos(30^\circ) = \frac{2\sqrt{3}}{4} = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:16:00">[09:16:00]</a>

#### [[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Tangent]] of 30 degrees

Using "TOA" ([[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Tangent]] = Opposite / Adjacent) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:31:00">[09:31:00]</a>:
For the 30-degree angle, the opposite side is 2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:38:00">[09:38:00]</a>, and the adjacent side is $2\sqrt{3}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:42:00">[09:42:00]</a>.
$\tan(30^\circ) = \frac{2}{2\sqrt{3}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:54:00">[09:54:00]</a>
Rationalizing the denominator:
$\tan(30^\circ) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{3}} = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:56:00">[09:56:00]</a>

### [[calculation_of_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Trig Ratios]] for the 60-degree Angle

#### [[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Sine]] of 60 degrees

Using "SOH" ([[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Sine]] = Opposite / Hypotenuse) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:30:00">[10:30:00]</a>:
For the 60-degree angle, the opposite side is $2\sqrt{3}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:36:00">[10:36:00]</a>, and the hypotenuse is 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:50:00">[10:50:00]</a>.
$\sin(60^\circ) = \frac{2\sqrt{3}}{4} = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:54:00">[10:54:00]</a>

#### [[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Cosine]] of 60 degrees

Using "CAH" ([[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Cosine]] = Adjacent / Hypotenuse) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:05:00">[11:05:00]</a>:
For the 60-degree angle, the adjacent side is 2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:10:00">[11:10:00]</a>, and the hypotenuse is 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:13:00">[11:13:00]</a>.
$\cos(60^\circ) = \frac{2}{4} = \frac{1}{2}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:17:00">[11:17:00]</a>

#### [[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Tangent]] of 60 degrees

Using "TOA" ([[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Tangent]] = Opposite / Adjacent) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:27:00">[11:27:00]</a>:
For the 60-degree angle, the opposite side is $2\sqrt{3}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:34:00">[11:34:00]</a>, and the adjacent side is 2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:39:00">[11:39:00]</a>.
$\tan(60^\circ) = \frac{2\sqrt{3}}{2} = \sqrt{3}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:44:00">[11:44:00]</a>

Notice the relationship between the [[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | sine]] and [[trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | cosine]] of 30 and 60 degrees:
*   $\sin(30^\circ) = \cos(60^\circ) = \frac{1}{2}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:54:00">[11:54:00]</a>
*   $\cos(30^\circ) = \sin(60^\circ) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:57:00">[11:57:00]</a>

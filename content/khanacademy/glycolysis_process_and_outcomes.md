The [[glycolysis_process | glycolysis process]] involves the splitting of a 6-carbon glucose molecule into two 3-carbon pyruvic acid (or pyruvate) molecules <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">[00:00:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:03">[00:00:03]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:12">[00:00:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:15">[00:00:15]</a>. The term [[glycolysis|glycolysis]] literally means "splitting glucose" or "lysing glucose" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:19">[00:00:19]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:23">[00:00:23]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:44">[00:00:44]</a>.

## Location

[[glycolysis|Glycolysis]] occurs in the cytoplasm of the cell <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:37">[00:02:37]</a>, which is the fluidic space between the organelles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:12">[00:02:12]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:27">[00:02:27]</a>.

## Oxygen Requirement

This process does not require oxygen and can occur in either the presence or absence of oxygen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:54">[00:00:54]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:57">[00:00:57]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>.

## [[atp_production_and_consumption_in_glycolysis | ATP Production and Consumption]]

During [[glycolysis|glycolysis]], two ATP molecules are consumed in the investment stage, and four ATP molecules are generated, resulting in a net payoff of two ATPs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:02">[00:01:02]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:08">[00:01:08]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:11">[00:01:11]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:16">[00:01:16]</a>.

## [[reduction_of_nad_to_nadh_in_glycolysis | NADH Production]]

In addition to ATP, [[glycolysis|glycolysis]] also produces two NADH molecules <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:20">[00:01:20]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:28">[00:01:28]</a>.

## Outcomes Summary

For every glucose molecule, [[glycolysis|glycolysis]] yields:
*   Two 3-carbon pyruvate molecules <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:15">[00:00:15]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:25">[00:00:25]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:29">[00:00:29]</a>
*   A net of two ATPs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:02">[00:01:02]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:06">[00:10:06]</a>
*   Two NADHs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:20">[00:01:20]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:06">[00:10:06]</a>

These outputs then proceed to subsequent stages of cellular respiration, such as [[krebs_cycle_and_pyruvate_oxidation | pyruvate oxidation]] and the [[krebs_cycle_and_pyruvate_oxidation | Krebs cycle]], to generate more energy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:09">[00:04:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:12">[00:05:12]</a>.

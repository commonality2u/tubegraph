The [[sohcahtoa_mnemonic_for_trigonometric_functions | SOH CAH TOA mnemonic]] is a useful tool for remembering the definitions of the primary [[basic_trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | trigonometric functions]]: sine, cosine, and tangent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:03">[00:00:03]</a>. This mnemonic is particularly applicable when working with [[application_of_trigonometry_in_right_triangles | right triangles]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:15">[00:00:15]</a>. When calculating [[calculating_trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_tangent | trigonometric functions]] for angles that are not part of [[application_of_trigonometry_in_right_triangles | right triangles]], it is often necessary to construct them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:23">[00:00:23]</a>.

## [[using_the_mnemonic_soh_cah_toa | Using the Mnemonic SOH CAH TOA]]

The mnemonic breaks down as follows <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:09">[00:02:09]</a>:

*   **SOH** (Sine = Opposite / Hypotenuse) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:19">[00:04:19]</a>
*   **CAH** (Cosine = Adjacent / Hypotenuse) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:43">[00:02:43]</a>
*   **TOA** (Tangent = Opposite / Adjacent) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:27">[00:05:27]</a>

### [[examples_of_calculating_sine_cosine_and_tangent | Examples of Calculating Sine, Cosine, and Tangent]]

#### Example 1: Triangle with sides 4, 7

Consider a [[application_of_trigonometry_in_right_triangles | right triangle]] with two sides of length 7 and 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:31">[00:00:31]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:37">[00:00:37]</a>.

1.  **Calculate the Hypotenuse:**
    Using the Pythagorean theorem (hypotenuse² = side1² + side2²) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:55">[00:00:55]</a>:
    h² = 7² + 4² <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:45">[00:00:45]</a>
    h² = 49 + 16 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:04">[00:01:04]</a>
    h² = 65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:18">[00:01:18]</a>
    h = √65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:33">[00:01:33]</a>
    The square root of 65 cannot be simplified further <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:42">[00:01:42]</a>.

2.  **Calculate Trigonometric Functions for angle Theta (θ):**
    Let θ be the angle opposite the side of length 7 and adjacent to the side of length 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:02">[00:02:02]</a>.
    *   **Cosine (CAH):** Adjacent / Hypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:43">[00:02:43]</a>
        For θ, the adjacent side is 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:04">[00:03:04]</a>, and the hypotenuse is √65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:17">[00:03:17]</a>.
        cos(θ) = 4 / √65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:21">[00:03:21]</a>
        To rationalize the denominator, multiply by √65 / √65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:39">[00:03:39]</a>:
        cos(θ) = (4√65) / 65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:54">[00:03:54]</a>

    *   **Sine (SOH):** Opposite / Hypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:25">[00:04:25]</a>
        For θ, the opposite side is 7 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:34">[00:04:34]</a>, and the hypotenuse is √65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:47">[00:04:47]</a>.
        sin(θ) = 7 / √65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:51">[00:04:51]</a>
        To rationalize, multiply by √65 / √65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:55">[00:04:55]</a>:
        sin(θ) = (7√65) / 65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:59">[00:04:59]</a>

    *   **Tangent (TOA):** Opposite / Adjacent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:27">[00:05:27]</a>
        For θ, the opposite side is 7 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:38">[00:05:38]</a>, and the adjacent side is 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:46">[00:05:46]</a>.
        tan(θ) = 7 / 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:51">[00:05:51]</a>

#### Example 2: 30-60-90 Right Triangle

Consider a [[application_of_trigonometry_in_right_triangles | right triangle]] with sides of length 2, 2√3, and a hypotenuse of 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:17">[00:06:17]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:21">[00:06:21]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:26">[00:06:26]</a>. This is a special 30-60-90 triangle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:07">[00:07:07]</a>, where the angle opposite side 2 is 30 degrees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:15">[00:07:15]</a>, and the angle opposite side 2√3 is 60 degrees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:23">[00:07:23]</a>.

1.  **Calculate Trigonometric Functions for 30 Degrees:**
    *   **Sine (SOH):** Opposite / Hypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:21">[00:08:21]</a>
        For 30 degrees, the opposite side is 2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:26">[00:08:26]</a>, and the hypotenuse is 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:30">[00:08:30]</a>.
        sin(30°) = 2 / 4 = 1/2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:32">[00:08:32]</a>

    *   **Cosine (CAH):** Adjacent / Hypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:52">[00:08:52]</a>
        For 30 degrees, the adjacent side is 2√3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:05">[00:09:05]</a>, and the hypotenuse is 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:09">[00:09:09]</a>.
        cos(30°) = (2√3) / 4 = √3 / 2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:13">[00:09:13]</a>

    *   **Tangent (TOA):** Opposite / Adjacent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:31">[00:09:31]</a>
        For 30 degrees, the opposite side is 2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:38">[00:09:38]</a>, and the adjacent side is 2√3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:42">[00:09:42]</a>.
        tan(30°) = 2 / (2√3) = 1 / √3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:52">[00:09:52]</a>
        To rationalize, multiply by √3 / √3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:56">[00:09:56]</a>:
        tan(30°) = √3 / 3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:05">[00:10:05]</a>

2.  **Calculate Trigonometric Functions for 60 Degrees:**
    *   **Sine (SOH):** Opposite / Hypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:30">[00:10:30]</a>
        For 60 degrees, the opposite side is 2√3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:36">[00:10:36]</a>, and the hypotenuse is 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:50">[00:10:50]</a>.
        sin(60°) = (2√3) / 4 = √3 / 2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:54">[00:10:54]</a>

    *   **Cosine (CAH):** Adjacent / Hypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:05">[00:11:05]</a>
        For 60 degrees, the adjacent side is 2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:10">[00:11:10]</a>, and the hypotenuse is 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:13">[00:11:13]</a>.
        cos(60°) = 2 / 4 = 1/2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:17">[00:11:17]</a>

    *   **Tangent (TOA):** Opposite / Adjacent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:27">[00:11:27]</a>
        For 60 degrees, the opposite side is 2√3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:32">[00:11:32]</a>, and the adjacent side is 2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:38">[00:11:38]</a>.
        tan(60°) = (2√3) / 2 = √3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:44">[00:11:44]</a>

Notice the relationship between the functions: sin(30°) = cos(60°) and cos(30°) = sin(60°) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:54">[00:11:54]</a>.

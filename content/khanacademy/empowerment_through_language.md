## What is Grammar?
[[introduction_to_grammar | Grammar]] is defined as a set of conventions and rules that govern language <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:18">[00:00:18]</a>.
*   **Rules** are the fundamental requirements for language to be understandable by others <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:26">[00:00:26]</a>. For example, for a car to move forward as intended, its wheels must be on the bottom, not the roof; this is a rule <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:34">[00:00:34]</a>.
*   **Conventions** are practices that are not strictly necessary for understanding but are widely adopted <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:24">[00:00:24]</a>. An example of a convention would be if all cars were painted tealâ€”it's not essential for function <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:46">[00:00:46]</a>.

## Grammar in Context
Grammar is highly context-dependent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:54">[00:00:54]</a>. The type of grammar used changes based on who is being addressed, what is being communicated, and how it is being expressed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:01">[00:01:01]</a>. People naturally use multiple forms of grammar throughout their daily lives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:07">[00:01:07]</a>.

## Unlocking Existing Knowledge
A key aspect of this educational approach is recognizing that learners already possess a vast amount of grammatical knowledge simply from living and interacting in the world <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:11">[00:01:11]</a>. If one can understand spoken language, they already know a significant amount about grammar <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:22">[00:01:22]</a>. The purpose of these videos is to provide names to this existing knowledge, thereby giving learners a greater command over it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:31">[00:01:31]</a>.

## Beyond "Right" and "Wrong"
While the videos specifically focus on Standard American English <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:38">[00:01:38]</a>, it is emphasized that "there are many Englishes" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:45">[00:01:45]</a>. All variations are considered "great," "wonderful," "vibrant," "important," and "special" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:48">[00:01:48]</a>. The goal is not to dictate what is "right" or "wrong" in language, but to provide tools for linguistic dexterity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:53">[00:01:53]</a>. The analogy of the car is revisited: the color of a car (a convention) doesn't matter, but ensuring the wheels are on straight (a rule for function) does <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:20">[00:02:20]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:27">[00:02:27]</a>.

## Identifying Sides in a Right Triangle

In a [[right_triangles_and_their_properties | right triangle]], there are three key sides relative to an angle:

*   **[[identifying_the_hypotenuse_in_right_triangles | Hypotenuse]]**: The longest side, opposite the right angle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:55">[00:02:55]</a>.
*   **Adjacent Side**: The side next to the angle of interest that is not the hypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:00">[00:03:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:10">[00:03:10]</a>.
*   **Opposite Side**: The side directly across from the angle of interest <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:31">[00:04:31]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:34">[00:04:34]</a>.

## [[using_the_pythagorean_theorem | Pythagorean Theorem]]

The [[using_the_pythagorean_theorem | Pythagorean Theorem]] states that in a [[right_triangles_and_their_properties | right triangle]], the square of the [[identifying_the_hypotenuse_in_right_triangles | hypotenuse]] (h) is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:52">[00:00:52]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:55">[00:00:55]</a>.
Expressed as a formula: `h² = a² + b²` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:01">[00:01:01]</a>.

**Example**:
If a [[right_triangles_and_their_properties | right triangle]] has sides of length 7 and 4, the [[identifying_the_hypotenuse_in_right_triangles | hypotenuse]] (h) can be found using the [[using_the_pythagorean_theorem | Pythagorean theorem]]:
h² = 7² + 4² <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:45">[00:00:45]</a>
h² = 49 + 16 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:04">[00:01:04]</a>
h² = 65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:18">[00:01:18]</a>
h = √65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:33">[00:01:33]</a>

## [[introduction_to_basic_trigonometry | Basic Trigonometric Functions]] (SOH CAH TOA)

The core trigonometric functions (sine, cosine, and tangent) can be remembered using the mnemonic "SOH CAH TOA" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:09">[00:02:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:23">[00:02:23]</a>:

*   **SOH**: **S**ine = **O**pposite / **H**ypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:19">[00:04:19]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:25">[00:04:25]</a>
*   **CAH**: **C**osine = **A**djacent / **H**ypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:37">[00:02:37]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:43">[00:02:43]</a>
*   **TOA**: **T**angent = **O**pposite / **A**djacent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:20">[00:05:20]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:27">[00:05:27]</a>

### [[application_of_trigonometry_in_right_triangles | Application in a Right Triangle]] (Example 1)

Consider a [[right_triangles_and_their_properties | right triangle]] with sides 7, 4, and [[identifying_the_hypotenuse_in_right_triangles | hypotenuse]] √65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:31">[00:00:31]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:37">[00:00:37]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:51">[00:01:51]</a>. Let's find the trigonometric functions for angle theta (θ) positioned such that the side with length 4 is adjacent and the side with length 7 is opposite <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:02">[00:02:02]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:07">[00:03:07]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:38">[00:04:38]</a>.

*   **Cosine (cos θ)**:
    *   Adjacent side = 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:07">[00:03:07]</a>
    *   [[identifying_the_hypotenuse_in_right_triangles | Hypotenuse]] = √65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:17">[00:03:17]</a>
    *   cos θ = 4 / √65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:21">[00:03:21]</a>
    *   To rationalize the denominator, multiply by √65/√65: (4 * √65) / 65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:39">[00:03:39]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:54">[00:03:54]</a>.

*   **Sine (sin θ)**:
    *   Opposite side = 7 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:38">[00:04:38]</a>
    *   [[identifying_the_hypotenuse_in_right_triangles | Hypotenuse]] = √65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:47">[00:04:47]</a>
    *   sin θ = 7 / √65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:51">[00:04:51]</a>
    *   To rationalize, multiply by √65/√65: (7 * √65) / 65 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:55">[00:04:55]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:59">[00:04:59]</a>.

*   **Tangent (tan θ)**:
    *   Opposite side = 7 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:38">[00:05:38]</a>
    *   Adjacent side = 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:46">[00:05:46]</a>
    *   tan θ = 7 / 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:51">[00:05:51]</a>

### [[special_right_triangles_306090 | Special Right Triangles]] (Example 2: 30-60-90 Triangle)

A [[special_right_triangles_306090 | 30-60-90 triangle]] is a [[right_triangles_and_their_properties | right triangle]] with angles measuring 30°, 60°, and 90° <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:07">[00:07:07]</a>. The sides have specific ratios:
*   The side opposite the 30° angle is half the [[identifying_the_hypotenuse_in_right_triangles | hypotenuse]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:27">[00:07:27]</a>.
*   The side opposite the 60° angle is √3 times the side opposite the 30° angle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:31">[00:07:31]</a>.

Consider a [[special_right_triangles_306090 | 30-60-90 triangle]] with a [[identifying_the_hypotenuse_in_right_triangles | hypotenuse]] of 4, a side of 2 (opposite 30°), and a side of 2√3 (opposite 60°) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:17">[00:06:17]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:21">[00:06:21]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:26">[00:06:26]</a>. This configuration satisfies the [[using_the_pythagorean_theorem | Pythagorean theorem]]: (2√3)² + 2² = 4² <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:36">[00:06:36]</a>, which simplifies to 12 + 4 = 16 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:49">[00:06:49]</a>, confirming it's a [[right_triangles_and_their_properties | right triangle]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:53">[00:06:53]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:57">[00:06:57]</a>.

#### [[applying_trigonometry_to_different_angles_in_a_triangle | Trig Ratios for 30 Degrees]]

*   **Sine (sin 30°)**:
    *   Opposite side = 2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:26">[00:08:26]</a>
    *   [[identifying_the_hypotenuse_in_right_triangles | Hypotenuse]] = 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:30">[00:08:30]</a>
    *   sin 30° = 2 / 4 = 1/2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:32">[00:08:32]</a>.

*   **Cosine (cos 30°)**:
    *   Adjacent side = 2√3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:05">[00:09:05]</a>
    *   [[identifying_the_hypotenuse_in_right_triangles | Hypotenuse]] = 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:12">[00:09:12]</a>
    *   cos 30° = (2√3) / 4 = √3 / 2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:09">[00:09:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:16">[00:09:16]</a>.

*   **Tangent (tan 30°)**:
    *   Opposite side = 2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:38">[00:09:38]</a>
    *   Adjacent side = 2√3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:42">[00:09:42]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:46">[00:09:46]</a>
    *   tan 30° = 2 / (2√3) = 1/√3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:54">[00:09:54]</a>
    *   To rationalize, multiply by √3/√3: √3 / 3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:56">[00:09:56]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:00">[00:10:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:05">[00:10:05]</a>.

#### [[applying_trigonometry_to_different_angles_in_a_triangle | Trig Ratios for 60 Degrees]]

*   **Sine (sin 60°)**:
    *   Opposite side = 2√3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:36">[00:10:36]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:39">[00:10:39]</a>
    *   [[identifying_the_hypotenuse_in_right_triangles | Hypotenuse]] = 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:50">[00:10:50]</a>
    *   sin 60° = (2√3) / 4 = √3 / 2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:54">[00:10:54]</a>.

*   **Cosine (cos 60°)**:
    *   Adjacent side = 2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:10">[00:11:10]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:13">[00:11:13]</a>
    *   [[identifying_the_hypotenuse_in_right_triangles | Hypotenuse]] = 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:13">[00:11:13]</a>
    *   cos 60° = 2 / 4 = 1/2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:17">[00:11:17]</a>.

*   **Tangent (tan 60°)**:
    *   Opposite side = 2√3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:34">[00:11:34]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:38">[00:11:38]</a>
    *   Adjacent side = 2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:39">[00:11:39]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:42">[00:11:42]</a>
    *   tan 60° = (2√3) / 2 = √3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:48">[00:11:48]</a>.

Notice that the sine of 30 degrees is the same as the cosine of 60 degrees (1/2), and the cosine of 30 degrees is the same as the sine of 60 degrees (√3/2) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:54">[00:11:54]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:57">[00:11:57]</a>.

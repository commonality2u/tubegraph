When working with [[how_to_add_and_subtract_negative_numbers | adding and subtracting negative numbers]], a key concept to understand is how to handle subtracting a [[introduction_to_negative_numbers | negative number]].

## Rule: Subtracting a Negative is Adding a Positive

A fundamental rule in mathematics states that when you [[subtracting_negative_numbers | subtract a negative]] quantity, it is equivalent to [[adding_negative_numbers | adding a positive]] quantity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:59">[02:59]</a>. This means that two consecutive negative signs (minus a negative) transform into a positive sign (plus a positive) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:01">[03:01]</a>.

> In other videos, it is explained why this concept of subtracting a negative making sense <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:54">[02:54]</a>.

## Examples

### Example 1: Positive Number Minus a Negative

Consider the expression `2 - (-3)` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:47">[02:47]</a>.
According to the rule, [[subtracting_negative_numbers | subtracting a negative 3]] is the same as [[adding_negative_numbers | adding positive 3]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:04">[03:04]</a>.
Therefore:
`2 - (-3)` is equivalent to `2 + 3` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:04">[03:04]</a>.
This simplifies to `5` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:18">[03:18]</a>.

### Example 2: Negative Number Minus a Negative

Consider the expression `-2 - (-3)` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:23">[03:23]</a>.
Although this expression might appear intimidating with multiple negative signs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:34">[03:34]</a>, the same rule applies: [[subtracting_negative_numbers | subtracting a negative]] results in a positive <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:38">[03:38]</a>.
Therefore:
`-2 - (-3)` is the exact same thing as `-2 + 3` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:45">[03:45]</a>.

To solve `-2 + 3`, one can visualize it on a [[visualizing_negative_numbers_on_a_number_line | number line]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:55">[03:55]</a>:
*   Start at negative 2 (two units to the left of zero) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:55">[03:55]</a>.
*   [[adding_negative_numbers | Adding 3]] means moving 3 units to the right <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:59">[03:59]</a>.
*   Moving 1, 2, 3 units to the right from -2 lands at positive 1 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:01">[04:01]</a>.

Thus, `-2 - (-3)` equals `1`.

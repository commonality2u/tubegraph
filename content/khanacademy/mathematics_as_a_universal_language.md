## Abstraction through Algebra

[[Introduction to Algebra | Algebra]] allows for the abstraction of concepts, moving from concrete scenarios to generalized ideas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:43">[00:00:43]</a>. This process reveals how the same underlying ideas connect across various domains <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:17">[00:01:17]</a>.

### Example: Discount Calculation

1.  **Concrete Example**: Calculating a 30% discount on a $20 pair of pants involves multiplying 30% (or 0.30) by $20, resulting in a $6 discount <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:39">[00:01:39]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:01">[00:02:01]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:23">[00:02:23]</a>.

2.  **Generalizing the Product**: By introducing a symbol 'x' to represent the price of *any* product, the discount can be expressed as 30% * x or 0.30 * x <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:38">[00:02:38]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:02">[00:03:02]</a>. This allows for calculating the discount for any product price by substituting its value for 'x' <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:26">[00:03:26]</a>. This marks the beginning of the abstraction inherent in [[Introduction to Algebra | algebra]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:37">[00:03:37]</a>.

3.  **Generalizing the Percentage**: Further abstraction involves using a symbol 'p' (or 'm') to represent the percentage off for *any* sale <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:13">[00:04:13]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:25">[00:04:25]</a>. This leads to the general [[Mathematical expressions | expression]] for discount: Discount = p * x <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:05">[00:05:05]</a>. This relationship can also be written using other variables, such as y = p * x, where 'y' represents the discount <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:21">[00:05:21]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:33">[00:05:33]</a>. The specific letters or symbols used are interchangeable, as long as their meaning is understood <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:44">[00:05:44]</a>.

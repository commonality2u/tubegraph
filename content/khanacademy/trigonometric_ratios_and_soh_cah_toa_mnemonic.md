[[Introduction to basic trigonometric functions | Trigonometric functions]], as defined in this context, are applicable only to [[Right Triangles and Trigonometry | right triangles]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:15">[00:00:15]</a>. If one is attempting to find the [[Trigonometric ratios and angle identification | trigonometric functions]] of angles that are not part of [[Right Triangles and Trigonometry | right triangles]], it becomes necessary to construct [[Right Triangles and Trigonometry | right triangles]] first <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:23">[00:00:23]</a>.

## Calculating Trigonometric Ratios in a Right Triangle Example

Consider a [[Right Triangles and Trigonometry | right triangle]] with two sides measuring 7 and 4 units <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:31">[00:00:31]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:37">[00:00:37]</a>.

### Determining the Hypotenuse

Using the Pythagorean theorem, the square of the hypotenuse (`h`) is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:55">[00:00:55]</a>.
*   `h^2 = 7^2 + 4^2` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:45">[00:00:45]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:01">[00:01:01]</a>
*   `h^2 = 49 + 16` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:04">[00:01:04]</a>
*   `h^2 = 65` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:11">[00:01:11]</a>
*   `h = sqrt(65)` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:33">[00:01:33]</a>
The square root of 65 cannot be simplified further as 65 is the product of 13 and 5, both prime numbers that are not perfect squares <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:44">[00:01:44]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:50">[00:01:50]</a>.

## SOHCAHTOA Mnemonic

To remember the definitions of the [[core_trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | sine]], [[core_trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | cosine]], and [[core_trigonometric_functions_sine_cosine_and_tangent | tangent]] functions, the [[SOHCAHTOA Mnemonic | SOHCAHTOA mnemonic]] is very useful <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:09">[00:02:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:23">[00:02:23]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:26">[00:02:26]</a>:
*   **SOH**: **S**ine is **O**pposite over **H**ypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:19">[00:04:19]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:25">[00:04:25]</a>.
*   **CAH**: **C**osine is **A**djacent over **H**ypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:40">[00:02:40]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:43">[00:02:43]</a>.
*   **TOA**: **T**angent is **O**pposite over **A**djacent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:20">[00:05:20]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:27">[00:05:27]</a>.

### Calculating Core Trigonometric Functions for Angle Theta

Let's find the [[Trigonometric ratios and angle identification | trigonometric ratios]] for an angle, theta (θ), in the constructed triangle, where the side of length 4 is adjacent to theta and the side of length 7 is opposite to theta <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:02">[00:02:02]</a>.

#### Cosine of Theta (cos θ)
*   **Definition**: Adjacent over Hypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:43">[00:02:43]</a>.
*   **Adjacent Side**: 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:04">[00:03:04]</a>.
*   **Hypotenuse**: `sqrt(65)` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:17">[00:03:17]</a>.
*   `cos(theta) = 4 / sqrt(65)` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:21">[00:03:21]</a>.
To rationalize the denominator, multiply both numerator and denominator by `sqrt(65)` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:39">[00:03:39]</a>:
*   `cos(theta) = (4 * sqrt(65)) / (sqrt(65) * sqrt(65)) = (4 * sqrt(65)) / 65` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:54">[00:03:54]</a>.

#### Sine of Theta (sin θ)
*   **Definition**: Opposite over Hypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:19">[00:04:19]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:25">[00:04:25]</a>.
*   **Opposite Side**: 7 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:34">[00:04:34]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:38">[00:04:38]</a>.
*   **Hypotenuse**: `sqrt(65)` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:47">[00:04:47]</a>.
*   `sin(theta) = 7 / sqrt(65)` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:51">[00:04:51]</a>.
To rationalize the denominator:
*   `sin(theta) = (7 * sqrt(65)) / (sqrt(65) * sqrt(65)) = (7 * sqrt(65)) / 65` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:55">[00:04:55]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:59">[00:04:59]</a>.

#### Tangent of Theta (tan θ)
*   **Definition**: Opposite over Adjacent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:27">[00:05:27]</a>.
*   **Opposite Side**: 7 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:35">[00:05:35]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:41">[00:05:41]</a>.
*   **Adjacent Side**: 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:46">[00:05:46]</a>.
*   `tan(theta) = 7 / 4` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:51">[00:05:51]</a>.

## Trigonometric Ratios in a 30-60-90 Triangle Example

Let's consider another [[Right Triangles and Trigonometry | right triangle]] with specific side lengths: hypotenuse 4, one side 2, and the other side `2 * sqrt(3)` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:17">[00:06:17]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:21">[00:06:21]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:26">[00:06:26]</a>.
Verifying the Pythagorean theorem: `(2 * sqrt(3))^2 + 2^2 = (4 * 3) + 4 = 12 + 4 = 16`. Since `4^2 = 16`, the sides satisfy the theorem <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:36">[00:06:36]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:49">[00:06:49]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:53">[00:06:53]</a>. This triangle is a [[Trigonometric functions in 30 60 90 triangles | 30-60-90 triangle]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:07">[00:07:07]</a>.

### Ratios for the 30-Degree Angle
The angle opposite the side of length 2 is the 30-degree angle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:15">[00:07:15]</a>.

#### Sine of 30 Degrees (sin 30°)
*   **Definition**: Opposite over Hypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:17">[00:08:17]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:21">[00:08:21]</a>.
*   **Opposite Side**: 2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:26">[00:08:26]</a>.
*   **Hypotenuse**: 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:30">[00:08:30]</a>.
*   `sin(30°) = 2 / 4 = 1/2` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:32">[00:08:32]</a>.

#### Cosine of 30 Degrees (cos 30°)
*   **Definition**: Adjacent over Hypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:50">[00:08:50]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:52">[00:08:52]</a>.
*   **Adjacent Side**: `2 * sqrt(3)` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:56">[00:08:56]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:01">[00:09:01]</a>.
*   **Hypotenuse**: 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:09">[00:09:09]</a>.
*   `cos(30°) = (2 * sqrt(3)) / 4 = sqrt(3) / 2` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:13">[00:09:13]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:16">[00:09:16]</a>.

#### Tangent of 30 Degrees (tan 30°)
*   **Definition**: Opposite over Adjacent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:31">[00:09:31]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:33">[00:09:33]</a>.
*   **Opposite Side**: 2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:38">[00:09:38]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:42">[00:09:42]</a>.
*   **Adjacent Side**: `2 * sqrt(3)` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:42">[00:09:42]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:46">[00:09:46]</a>.
*   `tan(30°) = 2 / (2 * sqrt(3)) = 1 / sqrt(3)` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:52">[00:09:52]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:54">[00:09:54]</a>.
To rationalize the denominator:
*   `tan(30°) = (1 * sqrt(3)) / (sqrt(3) * sqrt(3)) = sqrt(3) / 3` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:56">[00:09:56]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:05">[00:10:05]</a>.

### Ratios for the 60-Degree Angle
The angle opposite the side of length `2 * sqrt(3)` is the 60-degree angle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:23">[00:07:23]</a>.

#### Sine of 60 Degrees (sin 60°)
*   **Definition**: Opposite over Hypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:30">[00:10:30]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:45">[00:10:45]</a>.
*   **Opposite Side**: `2 * sqrt(3)` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:36">[00:10:36]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:39">[00:10:39]</a>.
*   **Hypotenuse**: 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:50">[00:10:50]</a>.
*   `sin(60°) = (2 * sqrt(3)) / 4 = sqrt(3) / 2` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:54">[00:10:54]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:59">[00:10:59]</a>.

#### Cosine of 60 Degrees (cos 60°)
*   **Definition**: Adjacent over Hypotenuse <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:05">[00:11:05]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:09">[00:11:09]</a>.
*   **Adjacent Side**: 2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:10">[00:11:10]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:13">[00:11:13]</a>.
*   **Hypotenuse**: 4 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:13">[00:11:13]</a>.
*   `cos(60°) = 2 / 4 = 1/2` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:17">[00:11:17]</a>.

#### Tangent of 60 Degrees (tan 60°)
*   **Definition**: Opposite over Adjacent <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:27">[00:11:27]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:32">[00:11:32]</a>.
*   **Opposite Side**: `2 * sqrt(3)` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:34">[00:11:34]</a>.
*   **Adjacent Side**: 2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:38">[00:11:38]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:42">[00:11:42]</a>.
*   `tan(60°) = (2 * sqrt(3)) / 2 = sqrt(3)` <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:44">[00:11:44]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:48">[00:11:48]</a>.

Notice that the sine of 30 degrees is the same as the cosine of 60 degrees, and the cosine of 30 degrees is the same as the sine of 60 degrees <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:54">[00:11:54]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:57">[00:11:57]</a>.

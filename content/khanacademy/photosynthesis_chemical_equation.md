[[photosynthesis_process_overview | Photosynthesis]] is a fundamental biological process vital for life on Earth, as it produces the food and oxygen necessary for most living species <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:04">[00:04:04]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:04">[01:04:04]</a>. Primarily associated with plants, this process is also carried out by bacteria and algae <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:00">[00:23:00]</a>.

## Overview of the Process

In very simple terms, [[photosynthesis_process_overview | photosynthesis]] is the process by which plants take carbon dioxide, water, and sunlight, and convert them into sugars (carbohydrates) and oxygen <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:00">[00:39:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:00">[00:43:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:00">[01:00:00]</a>.

The two profound outputs of [[photosynthesis_process_overview | photosynthesis]] are:
*   **Carbohydrates/Sugars** These fuel living organisms; for instance, glucose is used in cellular respiration to generate ATP <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:10:00">[01:10:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:17:00">[01:17:00]</a>.
*   **Oxygen** Essential for organisms that breathe oxygen to break down glucose and perform cellular respiration <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:31:00">[01:31:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:34:00">[01:34:00]</a>.

## The General Chemical Equation for Photosynthesis

The general [[understanding_the_concept_of_a_chemical_equation | chemical equation]] for [[photosynthesis_process_overview | photosynthesis]] represents the overall inputs and outputs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:16:00">[03:16:00]</a>.

**Reactants (Inputs):**
*   Carbon dioxide ($CO_2$) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:27:00">[03:27:00]</a>
*   Water ($H_2O$) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:30:00">[03:30:00]</a>
*   Photons (from sunlight), which excite electrons in chlorophyll <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:34:00">[03:34:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:38:00">[03:38:00]</a>

**Products (Outputs):**
*   Carbohydrate (a general term, e.g., glucose) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:03:00">[04:03:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:11:00">[04:11:00]</a>
*   Oxygen ($O_2$) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:42:00">[04:42:00]</a>

Carbohydrates can be generally represented as $(CH_2O)_n$, where 'n' indicates multiples of the basic unit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:14:00">[04:14:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:20:00">[04:20:00]</a>. For glucose, 'n' is 6, resulting in the formula $C_6H_{12}O_6$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:27:00">[04:27:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:29:00">[04:29:00]</a>.

The [[balancing_chemical_equations | balanced chemical equation]] for [[photosynthesis_process_overview | photosynthesis]] is:

$nCO_2 + nH_2O + \text{photons} \rightarrow (CH_2O)_n + nO_2$ <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:53:00">[04:53:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:06:00">[05:06:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:10:00">[05:10:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:13:00">[05:13:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:18:00">[05:18:00]</a>

## Breakdown of the Process

While the equation provides an [[understanding_the_concept_of_a_chemical_equation | overview of photosynthesis]], the actual process is not direct but occurs through multiple steps <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:27:00">[05:27:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:29:00">[05:29:00]</a>. [[photosynthesis_process_overview | Photosynthesis]] can be broken down into two main stages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:37:00">[05:37:00]</a>:

1.  **Light-Dependent Reactions** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:54:00">[05:54:00]</a>
    *   These reactions *require* sunlight (photons) to occur <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:05:00">[06:05:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:58:00">[06:58:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:24:00">[07:24:00]</a>.
    *   Water ($H_2O$) is consumed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:42:00">[07:42:00]</a>.
    *   Oxygen ($O_2$) is produced and released <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:49:00">[07:49:00]</a>.
    *   They generate ATP (adenosine triphosphate) and NADPH (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide phosphate) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:57:00">[07:57:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:07:00">[08:07:00]</a>. These molecules serve as energy carriers and reducing agents, respectively, for the next stage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:52:00">[03:52:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:10:00">[09:10:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:56:00">[09:56:00]</a>.

2.  **Light-Independent Reactions (Dark Reactions)** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:12:00">[06:12:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:41:00">[06:41:00]</a>
    *   Also known as the Calvin Cycle in most plants <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:32:00">[10:32:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:35:00">[10:35:00]</a>.
    *   These reactions *do not directly require photons* but can still occur in the light <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:23:00">[06:23:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:03:00">[07:03:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:07:00">[07:07:00]</a>.
    *   They utilize the ATP and NADPH produced by the light-dependent reactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:43:00">[10:43:00]</a>.
    *   Carbon dioxide ($CO_2$) is consumed to provide the carbon atoms <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:45:00">[12:45:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:47:00">[12:47:00]</a>.
    *   The primary product is phosphoglyceraldehyde (PGAL) or glyceraldehyde 3-phosphate (G3P) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:00:00">[11:00:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:02:00">[11:02:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:23:00">[11:23:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:35:00">[11:35:00]</a>. This molecule is a simple three-carbon building block that can then be used to produce other carbohydrates, including glucose <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:41:00">[11:41:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:48:00">[11:48:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:52:00">[11:52:00]</a>.

Essentially, the [[lightdependent_and_lightindependent_reactions | light-dependent reactions]] capture light energy to create energy currency (ATP) and reducing power (NADPH), which are then used by the [[lightdependent_and_lightindependent_reactions | light-independent reactions]] (Calvin Cycle) to fix carbon dioxide into sugars <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:21:00">[05:21:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:27:00">[05:27:00]</a>. This detailed process is the mechanism behind the overall [[understanding_the_concept_of_a_chemical_equation | chemical equation]] of [[photosynthesis_process_overview | photosynthesis]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:00:00">[03:00:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:03:00">[03:03:00]</a>.

Instrumental delivery involves the use of devices such as forceps or vacuum extractors to assist in vaginal childbirth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:59:01">[09:59:01]</a>.

## Indications for Instrumental Delivery

Instrumental delivery is typically indicated in cases of:
*   Fetal distress during the second stage of labor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:30:38">[10:30:38]</a>.
*   Maternal exhaustion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:35:10">[10:35:10]</a>.
*   Prolonged second stage of labor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:37:05">[10:37:05]</a>.

It is done in conditions where a vaginal delivery is possible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:11:00">[11:11:00]</a>.

## Conditions for Application

For both forceps and vacuum application, the following conditions must be met:
*   The cervix must be fully dilated (10 cm) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:19:19">[11:19:19]</a>.
*   The fetal head station must be at or beyond +2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:22:24">[11:22:24]</a>.

## Types of Forceps Application

*   **Low Forceps Application**: Used when the fetal head station is at +2 or +3 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:34:49">[11:34:49]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:42:30">[11:42:30]</a>.
*   **Outlet Forceps Application**: Used when the fetal head station is at +3, +4, or +5 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:57:35">[11:57:35]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:00:26">[12:00:26]</a>.
*   **At +3 Station**:
    *   If the sagittal suture of the fetal head is in the anteroposterior (AP) diameter of the pelvis, outlet forceps are applied <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:16:35">[12:16:35]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:26:01">[12:26:01]</a>.
    *   If the sagittal suture is not in the AP diameter, low forceps are applied <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:28:43">[12:28:43]</a>.

## Absolute Contraindications for Instrumental Delivery

Instrumental delivery is absolutely contraindicated in any condition that necessitates a cesarean section <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:40:42">[10:40:42]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:38:43">[12:38:43]</a>. These include:
*   Cephalopelvic disproportion (CPD) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:45:06">[12:45:06]</a>.
*   Adhered pelvis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:46:17">[12:46:17]</a>.
*   Fetal bony deformities such as osteogenesis imperfecta <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:49:15">[12:49:15]</a>.
*   Fetal bleeding disorders (e.g., non-coagulopathy) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:54:19">[12:54:19]</a>.
*   Maternal HIV positive status <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:59:40">[12:59:40]</a>.
*   Malpresentations requiring C-section, such as transverse lie, brow presentation, or mentoposterior face presentation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:05:40">[13:05:40]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:08:18">[13:08:18]</a>.

## Comparison of Vacuum and Forceps

| Feature | Vacuum | Forceps |
| :------------------ | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Rotation of Head** | Can rotate the baby's head <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:29:43">[13:29:43]</a>. | Cannot rotate the baby's head <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:31:39">[13:31:39]</a>. |
| **Cervical Dilation** | Can be applied when cervix is >/= 6 cm dilated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:37:37">[13:37:37]</a>. | Applied only when cervix is 10 cm dilated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:19:19">[11:19:19]</a>. |
| **Preterm Delivery** | Cannot be applied in preterm delivery <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:44:00">[13:44:00]</a>. | Can be applied in preterm delivery <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:46:44">[13:46:44]</a>. |
| **After-coming Head of Breech** | Cannot be applied for after-coming head of breech <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:50:33">[13:50:33]</a>. | Can be applied for after-coming head of breech <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:50:33">[13:50:33]</a>. |
| **Maternal Injury** | Less maternal injury <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:48:02">[14:48:02]</a>. | More maternal injury <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:48:02">[14:48:02]</a>. |
| **Fetal Injury** | More fetal injury (e.g., caput succedaneum) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:51:30">[14:51:30]</a>. | Less common fetal injuries like facial nerve palsy and brachial plexus injury <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:53:11">[14:53:11]</a>. |

### Specific Points for Vacuum Delivery
*   **Flexion Point**: The center of the vacuum cup should be applied at the flexion point <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:27:54">[14:27:54]</a>. This point is located 3 cm anterior to the posterior fontanelle and 6 cm posterior to the anterior fontanelle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:35:05">[14:35:05]</a>.
*   **Caput Succedaneum**: This fetal head swelling is more common with vacuum use <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:51:30">[14:51:30]</a>.

## Prophylactic Use of Forceps or Vacuum
Prophylactic use of forceps or vacuum is done in patients with [[physiological_changes_in_pregnancy|heart disease]] or PI (Pregnancy-Induced Hypertension) to shorten the second stage of labor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:02:08">[14:02:08]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:09:07">[14:09:07]</a>.

## Causes of Hemorrhage

The most common source of hemorrhage following [[techniques_and_complications_of_tonsilectomy | tonsillectomy]] is the paratonsillar vein, also known as the external palatine vein <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:14">[01:14]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:18">[01:18]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:42">[09:42]</a>. Additionally, most arteries supplying the tonsil enter through the lower pole, making proper ligation of the lower pole crucial during surgery <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:36">[02:36]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:41">[02:41]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:44">[02:44]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:04">[12:04]</a>.

## Types of Hemorrhage

Hemorrhage after [[techniques_and_complications_of_tonsilectomy | tonsillectomy]] can be categorized by its timing:

*   **Primary Hemorrhage**
    This occurs while the patient is still undergoing the surgery <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:46">[09:46]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:46">[10:46]</a>.
*   **Reactionary Hemorrhage**
    This type usually occurs in the recovery room, within 24 hours after surgery <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:52">[09:52]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:57">[09:57]</a>. It can be due to the slippage of a ligature or the dislodgment of a clot <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:00">[10:00]</a>.
*   **Secondary Hemorrhage**
    Secondary hemorrhage occurs any time after 24 hours, up to 10 days post-surgery <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:04">[10:04]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:08">[10:08]</a>. It is typically secondary to infection, most commonly occurring around 5 to 6 days after the procedure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:07">[10:07]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:14">[10:14]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:16">[10:16]</a>.

## Management Steps

The management of hemorrhage following [[techniques_and_complications_of_tonsilectomy | tonsillectomy]] varies slightly depending on the type:

*   **For Secondary Hemorrhage:**
    Since secondary hemorrhage is caused by infection, the initial step in management is to start the patient on intravenous (IV) antibiotics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:30">[10:30]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:32">[10:32]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:33">[10:33]</a>.

*   **For Primary or Reactionary Hemorrhage:**
    The management steps for primary and reactionary hemorrhage are the same <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:38">[10:38]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:40">[10:40]</a>. If it's reactionary hemorrhage, the patient should be immediately taken back to the operating theatre (OT) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:41">[10:41]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:43">[10:43]</a>.

    1.  **Removal of Clots:** The first step is to remove any clots <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:50">[10:50]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:53">[10:53]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:56">[10:56]</a>. This allows for proper clipping action of the superior constrictor muscle, which helps to stop the bleeding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:59">[10:59]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:01">[11:01]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:06">[11:06]</a>.
    2.  **Pressure with Vasoconstrictor:** If bleeding persists after clot removal, apply pressure using a vasoconstrictor gauze, pack, or cotton <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:11">[11:11]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:13">[11:13]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:16">[11:16]</a>.
    3.  **Cauterization (Quize):** If bleeding still does not stop, the next step is to cauterize (quize) the visible bleeding blood vessel <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:23">[11:23]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:25">[11:25]</a>.
    4.  **Ligation of the Vessel:** As a last resort, if the bleeding continues, the vessel should be ligated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:31">[11:31]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:35">[11:35]</a>.

    An anterior pillar retractor can be used to see if bleeding is occurring and from where it is originating <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:13">[12:13]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:15">[12:15]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:18">[12:18]</a><a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:20">[12:20]</a>.

### ORIF (Open Reduction Internal Fixation)

The acronym ORIF stands for "Open Reduction Internal Fixation" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:15">[00:01:15]</a>. This term combines two key aspects of fracture treatment: "Open Reduction" (surgical realignment of the bone) and "Internal Fixation" (using implants like plates, screws, or rods to hold the bone in place) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:15">[00:01:15]</a>. Initially, one might only know "ORIF" without understanding its component parts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:44">[00:00:44]</a>, highlighting the importance of breaking down complex terms.

Understanding these fundamental terminologies significantly simplifies the process of learning and keeping up with more advanced [[fracture_management_strategies | fracture management strategies]] and [[types_of_fracture_healing_and_fixation_methods | fixation methods]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:27">[00:01:27]</a>.

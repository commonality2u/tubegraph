A 45-year-old male presents with a [[chronic_productive_cough | chronic productive cough]] that has lasted several months, producing over a tablespoon of loose sputum daily, sometimes streaked with blood <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00"></a>. He also reports intermittent shortness of breath and wheezing, particularly during exertion and following respiratory infections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:14"></a>. Over the past year, he has experienced multiple respiratory infections, necessitating three courses of antibiotics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:20"></a>.

## Medical History

The patient's significant medical history includes pulmonary tuberculosis, which was diagnosed and successfully treated five years prior to his current presentation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:27"></a>.

## Clinical Findings

Upon examination, the patient is afebrile with normal vital signs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:36"></a>. Auscultation of the chest reveals crackles predominantly in the lower lung zones <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:41"></a>. [[Spirometry and lung function | Spirometry]] results indicate a reduced AVC ratio <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:47"></a>.

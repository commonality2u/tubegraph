Poisons can be categorized based on their chemical properties, effects on the body, or origin. A common classification system uses the mnemonic "SYNC CAM" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:00:59">[09:00:59]</a>.

## Classification of Poisons

### Corrosives (C)
Corrosives are substances that cause tissue damage, typically through chemical reactions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:12:00">[09:12:00]</a>.
*   **Acids**
    *   Induce coagulative necrosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:19:00">[09:19:00]</a>.
    *   Examples: Sulfuric acid, nitric acid, oxalic acid, acetic acid, boric acid, formic acid <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:34:00">[09:34:00]</a>.
    *   Exception: Hydrofluoric acid does not cause coagulative necrosis like other acids <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:01:00">[10:01:00]</a>.
*   **Alkalies**
    *   Cause liquefactive necrosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:23:00">[09:23:00]</a>.
    *   Spread more extensively than acids, making them generally more dangerous <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:27:00">[09:27:00]</a>.
    *   Examples: Sodium hydroxide, potassium hydroxide, potassium carbonate, sodium carbonate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:41:00">[09:41:00]</a>.

### Irritants (I)
Irritants are substances that induce inflammation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:12:00">[10:12:00]</a>. They are subdivided into non-metallic, metallic, plant, and animal irritants <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:18:00">[10:18:00]</a>.
*   **Non-metallic irritants**: Phosphorus, chloride, iodide, bromine <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:29:00">[10:29:00]</a>.
*   **Metallic irritants**: Mercury, [[lead_poisoning_and_its_effects_on_heme_synthesis | lead]], iron, copper, thallium, barium, zinc, antimony, cadmium <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:39:00">[10:39:00]</a>. Important examples include mercury, lead, and arsenic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:47:00">[10:47:00]</a>.
*   **Plant irritants**: Ricinus communis (castor), Abrus precatorius (rosary pea), Semecarpus anacardium (marking nut), Calotropis, Croton tiglium, Capsicum <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:52:00">[10:52:00]</a>.
*   **Animal irritants**: Snake venoms, scorpion stings, honeybee stings, centipede venom <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:18:00">[11:18:00]</a>.

### Neurotoxics (N)
Neurotoxics affect the nervous system <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:29:00">[11:29:00]</a>.
*   **Peripheral Nerve Poisons**: Curare, Conium maculatum <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:41:00">[11:41:00]</a>.
*   **Central Nervous System (CNS) Poisons**:
    *   **Spinal Cord Poisons**: Strychnos nux-vomica (strychnine), Gelsemium <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:59:00">[11:59:00]</a>. In cases of poisoning by these, the spinal cord should be preserved during autopsy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:16:00">[12:16:00]</a>.
    *   **Brain Poisons**:
        *   **Somniferous (Depressants)**: Induce sleep, depressing brain activity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:23:00">[12:23:00]</a>. Example: [[Opioid poisoning and its treatment | Opioid]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:33:00">[12:33:00]</a>.
        *   **Delirants (Excitants)**: Induce delirium and CNS excitation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:37:00">[12:37:00]</a>. Examples: Datura, [[Drug interactions and toxicities | Cocaine]], Cannabis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:44:00">[12:44:00]</a>.
        *   **Inwebriants (Intoxication)**: Cause intoxication. Example: Alcohols <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:58:00">[12:58:00]</a>.

### Cardiotoxics (C)
Cardiotoxics are poisons that specifically affect the heart <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:20:00">[13:20:00]</a>.
*   **Plant Cardiotoxics** (mnemonic "Card Dona"): Digitalis (foxglove), Oleander, Nicotiana, Aconites <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:27:00">[13:27:00]</a>.

### Asphyxiants (A)
Asphyxiants are gases that interfere with oxygen delivery or utilization <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:49:00">[13:49:00]</a>.
*   Examples: Carbon monoxide, carbon dioxide, hydrogen sulfide, hydrogen cyanide <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:53:00">[13:53:00]</a>. Hydrogen sulfide is a commonly known sewer gas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:01:00">[14:01:00]</a>.
*   **Cyanide**: Binds with cytochrome oxidase, inhibiting the electron transport chain and causing histotoxic anoxia (cells cannot utilize oxygen) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="51:46">[51:46]</a>.
*   **Carbon Monoxide**: Primarily binds with hemoglobin, causing anemic anoxia (oxygen cannot bind) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="51:01">[51:01]</a>. At higher concentrations, it also binds with cytochrome oxidase <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="51:26">[51:26]</a>.
*   Both cyanide and carbon monoxide can lead to metabolic acidosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="52:04">[52:04]</a>.

### Miscellaneous (M)
This category includes other poisons that do not fit into the main groups, such as agricultural poisons <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:06:00">[14:06:00]</a>.

## Specific Poisonings and Their Effects

### [[Aluminium Phosphate Poisoning Diagnosis and Treatment | Aluminium Phosphide Poisoning]]
*   **Characteristics**: Commonly known as a rat killer, marketed as "Celphos" tablet <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:08:00">[15:08:00]</a>. It has a garlic odor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:15:00">[15:15:00]</a>.
*   **Mechanism**: When ingested, the tablet releases phosphine gas upon contact with moisture in the gastric cavity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:30:00">[19:30:00]</a>. Phosphine gas is a protoplasmic poison that affects cellular respiration enzymes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:35:00">[15:35:00]</a>.
*   **Systemic Effects**:
    *   **Cardiovascular**: Shock, hypotension, arrhythmias <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:52:00">[15:52:00]</a>.
    *   **Respiratory**: Features similar to ARDS (Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:06:00">[16:06:00]</a>.
    *   **Metabolic**: Metabolic acidosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:18:00">[16:18:00]</a>.
    *   **Electrolyte Imbalance**: Hypomagnesemia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:32:00">[16:32:00]</a>.
*   **Diagnosis**:
    *   **Silver Nitrate Breath Test**: Patient breathes onto a silver nitrate-impregnated filter paper, which turns black <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:10:00">[18:10:00]</a>.
    *   **Silver Nitrate Gastric Lavage Test**: Gastric lavage fluid is heated in a flask covered with silver nitrate paper; the paper turns black <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:38:00">[18:38:00]</a>.
*   **Management**: No specific antidote <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:43:00">[16:43:00]</a>. Treatment is supportive and focuses on managing complications <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:52:00">[16:52:00]</a>:
    *   Anti-shock measures for hypotension <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:55:00">[16:55:00]</a>.
    *   Anti-arrhythmics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:59:00">[16:59:00]</a>.
    *   Mechanical ventilation for ARDS <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:06:00">[17:06:00]</a>.
    *   IV sodium bicarbonate for metabolic acidosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:15:00">[17:15:00]</a>.
    *   Correction of hypomagnesemia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:22:00">[17:22:00]</a>.
*   **Other Poisons with Garlic Smell**: Arsenic, phosphorus, and organophosphorus compounds (OPC) may also have a garlic smell <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:46:00">[17:46:00]</a>.

### Chronic Mercury Exposure (Hydroggerism)
*   **Occupations**: Commonly seen in glass blowers and hatters <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="39:40">[39:40]</a>.
*   **Symptoms (mnemonic "METAL")**:
    *   **Minamata Disease (M)**: Caused by organic mercury (e.g., methyl mercury) from fish consumption, not direct exposure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="40:00">[40:00]</a>.
    *   **Emotional Instability (E)**: Known as "mad hatter's disease" or "mad hatter syndrome," a neuropsychiatric manifestation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="40:20">[40:20]</a>.
    *   **Tremors (T)**: Coarse intentional tremors, referred to as "hatter shakes," "glass blower shakes," or "Danberry tremors." They typically start in the fingers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="40:34">[40:34]</a>.
    *   **Acrodynia (A)**: Also known as "pink disease," "calomel disease," or "Swift's pea disease." Characterized by pain in the peripheries <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="40:53">[40:53]</a>.
    *   **Lentis (L)**: Mercury deposition in the anterior lens capsule <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:11">[41:11]</a>.

### [[Common Poisons and Their Antidotes | Asphyxiant Poisoning Treatment]]
*   **Carbon Monoxide**: Treated by administering hyperbaric oxygen or high-flow oxygen. High-flow oxygen is generally preferred to avoid barotrauma, with hyperbaric oxygen reserved for severe cases <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="52:22">[52:22]</a>.
*   **Cyanide**: Antidotes include the "Lyles antidote" kit containing sodium nitrate, amyl nitrate, and sodium thiosulfate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="52:44">[52:44]</a>. Digital EDTA is another option <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="52:58">[52:58]</a>. The most effective and safest option, often considered the drug of choice, is hydroxocobalamin <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="53:04">[53:04]</a>.

A 45-year-old male presents with a constellation of symptoms and signs suggestive of a chronic respiratory condition.

## Presenting Symptoms

The patient reports a chronic productive cough lasting several months <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:02"></a>. Key characteristics of the cough include:
*   Daily production of loose sputum, exceeding a tablespoon in quantity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:07"></a>.
*   Sputum sometimes streaked with blood <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:10"></a>.
*   Intermittent shortness of breath <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:12"></a>.
*   Wheezing, particularly during exertion and after respiratory infections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:15"></a>.

Over the past year, he has experienced multiple episodes of respiratory infections, necessitating three courses of antibiotics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:20"></a>.

## Past Medical History

A significant part of the patient's medical history is a diagnosis of pulmonary tuberculosis, which was successfully treated five years prior to the current presentation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:30"></a>.

## Physical Examination

Upon examination, the patient's vital signs are within normal limits, and he is afebrile <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:36"></a>.
Auscultation of the chest reveals:
*   Wheezing sounds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:42"></a>.
*   Crackles predominantly in the lower lung zones <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:44"></a>.
*   No weeping (wheezing was a reported symptom, but "no weeping" was noted on examination) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:47"></a>.

## Diagnostic Test Results

Spirometry performed on the patient indicates a reduced AVC ratio at WASN <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:47"></a>.

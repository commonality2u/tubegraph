## Formula for Tetising Frequency

The formula for calculating tetising frequency is straightforward:
$$
\text{Tetising Frequency (Hertz)} = \frac{1}{\text{Contraction Period (Seconds)}}
$$

## Example Calculation

Consider a simple muscle twitch experiment with the following parameters:
*   Latent period: 10 milliseconds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:29">[00:01:29]</a>
*   Contraction period: 40 milliseconds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:30">[00:01:30]</a>
*   Relaxation period: 20 milliseconds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:32">[00:01:32]</a>

To find the fusion frequency required to tetanize this muscle in Hertz:

1.  **Identify the contraction period**: The contraction period is 40 milliseconds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:51">[00:01:51]</a>.
2.  **Convert milliseconds to seconds**: Since 1 second = 1000 milliseconds, 40 milliseconds = 0.040 seconds <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:56">[00:01:56]</a>.
3.  **Apply the formula**:
    $$
    \text{Tetising Frequency} = \frac{1}{0.040 \text{ seconds}} = 25 \text{ Hertz}
    $$
    <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:01">[00:02:01]</a>

Therefore, the tetising frequency required for this muscle is 25 Hertz <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:01">[00:02:01]</a>.

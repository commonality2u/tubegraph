## Early Adoption and Trust
The successful candidate began [[Marrow as a primary study resource | utilizing Marrow]] with Edition 5, aligning with its theme "put your heart into it" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:18">[00:02:18]</a>. This approach encouraged deep conceptual learning and made the study process enjoyable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:32">[00:02:32]</a>. Video watching started in the third year of MBBS, covering half of the final year subjects and all third-year subjects, with the rest completed in the final year <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:04">[00:03:04]</a>.

The effectiveness of [[use of Marrow videos for medical education | Marrow videos]] is attributed to the engaging teaching styles and comprehensive content:
*   **Dr. Chetanya's Anesthesia Videos**: These videos covered the entire subject with fun facts, making the learning process enjoyable and leading to the subject being binged in a week <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:04">[00:00:04]</a>.
*   **Dr. Sakshi Ma'am (Obstetrics & Gynecology)**: Her videos include significant effort in creating models and dummy representations, explaining mechanisms like labor beautifully <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:04">[00:04:04]</a>. Concepts learned from her videos were conceptually understood and retained for a very long time <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:17">[00:04:17]</a>.
*   **Dr. Ranser Sir (Surgery)**: His videos are praised for including beautiful images he clicked himself, which built trust in the subject and in learning from him <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:46">[00:03:46]</a>. His teaching style clarifies even the minutest details <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:58">[00:03:58]</a>.
*   **Dr. Manisha Ma'am (ENT)**: Her teaching style is described as like attending a live class, with her asking questions repeatedly, making the learning process engaging <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:24">[00:04:24]</a>.

## Integration with Study Strategy
The videos were an integral part of a structured study plan:
*   **Sequential Learning**: The strategy involved first watching video lectures, then referring to standard textbooks for related text, and finally solving question banks to reinforce concepts and facts <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:45">[00:04:45]</a>.
*   **Note-Taking**: A mixed approach was used for note-making, with handwritten notes for subjects like pharmacology, pathology, surgery, and obstetrics/gynecology <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:01">[00:13:01]</a>. For other subjects, printed notes were used based on time availability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:14">[00:05:14]</a>. Information from the [[Utilization of educational resources like Marrow videos and Q Bank | question bank]] was selectively added to notes, focusing on highly catchy or conceptual facts not previously encountered, to avoid making notes too bulky <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:41">[00:13:41]</a>.
*   **Revision Videos and Notes**: [[Utilizing online resources like Marrow effectively | Marrow's revision videos]] and notes were used for major subjects like surgery, obstetrics, gynecology, ENT, and ophthalmology, especially towards the end of preparation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:56">[00:16:56]</a>. These "crisp" and "precise" revision videos, crafted by teachers and covering previous year questions and topics, were found to be time-saving <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:41">[00:16:41]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:26">[00:17:26]</a>.

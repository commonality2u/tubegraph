A 45-year-old male presented with a range of symptoms indicative of a respiratory infection, alongside relevant clinical findings.

## Presenting Symptoms

The patient's chief complaint was a [[chronic_productive_cough | chronic productive cough]] that had persisted for several months <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:00">00:00:00</a>. Specific details of his cough included:
*   Coughing up loose sputum daily, with the quantity often exceeding a tablespoon <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:07">00:00:07</a>.
*   Sputum sometimes appeared streaked with blood <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:10">00:00:10</a>.

In addition to the cough, he reported:
*   Intermittent shortness of breath <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:12">00:00:12</a>.
*   Weeping, especially noted during exercise and following respiratory infections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:15">00:00:15</a>.

He also had a history of multiple episodes of respiratory infections over the past year, which necessitated three courses of antibiotics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:20">00:00:20</a>. A significant past medical history included pulmonary tuberculosis, which was successfully treated five years prior <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:30">00:00:30</a>.

## Clinical Examination Findings

Upon examination, the patient was afebrile with normal vital signs <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:36">00:00:36</a>. Auscultation of the chest revealed:
*   A "biting sound" crackles, predominantly in the lower lung zones <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:42">00:00:42</a>.
*   No weeping was noted during the examination <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:47">00:00:47</a>.

Spirometry results showed a reduced FVC ratio <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:47">00:00:47</a>.

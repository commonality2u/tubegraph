Solving Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) is crucial for preparation for medical competitive exams like FMG [05:28:09]. Many students, particularly those preparing for FMG exams, often focus heavily on theoretical knowledge but neglect sufficient MCQ practice, which can hinder their performance <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:30:09">[05:30:09]</a>.

## Benefits of Solving MCQs
*   **Understanding and Identifying Weaknesses** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:51:39">[02:51:39]</a>: Engaging with [[Utilizing Question Banks for Competitive Medical Exams | Q banks]] helps students understand concepts better and pinpoint areas where their knowledge is lacking <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:48:09">[02:48:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:55:09">[02:55:09]</a>.
*   **Active Recall and Retention** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:24:09">[05:24:09]</a>: [[Study techniques for medical exams | Revision is more effective through solving questions]] compared to passively re-reading notes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:17:09">[05:17:09]</a>. It promotes active recall, which solidifies learning <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:21:09">[05:21:09]</a>.
*   **Familiarity with Exam Format** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:51:09">[05:51:09]</a>: Consistent MCQ practice prevents undue pressure during the actual exam by familiarizing candidates with the question styles and the process of ruling out options <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:53:09">[05:53:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:00:09">[06:00:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:47:09">[09:47:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:49:09">[09:49:09]</a>.

## Strategy for MCQ Practice
*   **Gradual Increase in Quantity** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:00:09">[05:00:09]</a>: Start with a manageable number of questions, such as 50 to 100 daily, and gradually increase the volume without over-exerting yourself early on <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:02:09">[05:02:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:06:09">[05:06:09]</a>.
*   **Targeting Weak Subjects** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:56:09">[03:56:09]</a>: Prioritize solving Q banks for subjects where you feel less confident, such as preclinical subjects <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:59:09">[03:59:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:01:09">[04:01:09]</a>.
*   **Utilizing Varied Question Banks** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:02:09">[03:02:09]</a>: Don't limit practice to only one type of question bank (e.g., FMG-specific Q banks). Pushing oneself with NEET PG modules can lead to better overall preparation and higher scores <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:15:09">[03:15:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:19:09">[03:19:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:22:09">[03:22:09]</a>. It is recommended to solve a wide variety of [[Utilizing Question Banks for Competitive Medical Exams | Q banks]], including NEET PG and INI-CET hashtags, to build a strong foundation, as the same board conducts these exams <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:49:09">[10:49:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:52:09">[10:52:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:57:09">[10:57:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:00:09">[11:00:09]</a>.
*   **Revising Incorrect Questions** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:10:09">[06:10:09]</a>: Bookmark questions answered incorrectly and integrate related images or flowcharts into your notes. This ensures that you revisit and understand the specific topics associated with those questions during subsequent revisions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:21:09">[06:21:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:23:09">[06:23:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:24:09">[06:24:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:27:09">[06:27:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:30:09">[06:30:09]</a>.
*   **Focusing on Topics, Not Just PYQs** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:17:09">[10:17:09]</a>: Given the reduction in direct repeats in recent FMG exams, it's more beneficial to understand the underlying topics around previous year questions (PYQs) rather than just memorizing the PYQs themselves <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:07:09">[10:07:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:09:09">[10:09:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:12:09">[10:12:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:25:09">[10:25:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:27:09">[10:27:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:29:09">[10:29:09]</a>. This approach also helps build a strong foundation for future exams like NEET PG <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:33:09">[10:33:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:35:09">[10:35:09]</a>.
*   **Last-Month Strategy** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:15:09">[11:15:09]</a>: In the final month leading up to the exam, continue solving question banks, especially for subjects not thoroughly completed. Use the explanations provided for questions to review and supplement notes on those topics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:47:09">[11:47:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:49:09">[11:49:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:51:09">[11:51:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:54:09">[11:54:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:56:09">[11:56:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:59:09">[11:59:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:01:09">[12:01:09]</a>.
*   **Using Custom Modules** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:45:09">[12:45:09]</a>: Utilize features like custom modules in study applications to organize and revisit questions effectively <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:49:09">[12:49:09]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:52:09">[12:52:09]</a>.

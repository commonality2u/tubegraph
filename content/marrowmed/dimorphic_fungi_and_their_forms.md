From: [[marrowmed]] <br/> 

Dimorphic fungi are a group of fungi that can exist in two distinct forms: a yeast form and a mold form <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:14">[00:01:14]</a>. This ability to switch between forms is often dependent on environmental conditions, such as temperature.

Five notable dimorphic fungi are responsible for causing [[endemic_fungal_atypical_pneumonia | endemic fungal atypical pneumonia]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:06">[00:00:06]</a>, a type of [[clinical_manifestations_of_atypical_pneumonia_caused_by_fungi | atypical pneumonia]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:24">[00:01:24]</a>. These fungi include:
*   *Histoplasma* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:13">[00:00:13]</a>
*   *Blastomyces* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:13">[00:00:13]</a>
*   *Coccidioides* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:23">[00:00:23]</a>
*   *Paracoccidioides* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:23">[00:00:23]</a>
*   *Talaromyces marneffei* <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:31">[00:00:31]</a>

## Clinical Manifestations and Treatment

The [[clinical_manifestations_of_atypical_pneumonia_caused_by_fungi | clinical presentation]] for infections caused by all five of these fungi is generally similar. They manifest as pneumonia with symptoms such as cough, chest pain, and dyspnea (difficulty breathing), typically without consolidation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:27">[00:01:27]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:52">[00:01:52]</a>.

The treatment protocol for all five fungi is also the same <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:36">[00:01:36]</a>:
*   **Mild to Moderate Cases**: Itraconazole is the drug of choice <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:41">[00:01:41]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:46">[00:01:46]</a>.
*   **Severe Cases**: Liposomal Amphotericin B is used <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:43">[00:01:43]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:47">[00:01:47]</a>.

## Diagnostic Methods for Dimorphic Fungi

While their clinical presentations and treatments are similar, these fungi primarily differ in their [[diagnostic_methods_for_dimorphic_fungi | laboratory diagnosis]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:04">[00:02:04]</a>. Identifying their specific yeast and mold forms under a microscope is crucial for accurate diagnosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:07">[00:02:07]</a>.

### Histoplasma

*   **Associated Diseases**: Histoplasmosis, Darling's disease, Ohio Valley Fever <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:26">[00:02:26]</a>.
*   **Prevalence**: Most commonly seen along the Ohio Valley <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:47">[00:02:47]</a>.
*   **Yeast Form**: Characterized by intracellular yeast cells, often found within macrophages <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:00">[00:03:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:20">[00:03:20]</a>. *Histoplasma* is notable as the only intracellular fungus among these five <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:08">[00:03:08]</a>.
*   **Mold Form**: Identified by tuberculate conidia, which are conidia surrounded by tubercles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:49">[00:03:49]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:55">[00:03:55]</a>.

### Blastomyces

*   **Associated Diseases**: Blastomycosis, Gilchrist's disease, North American Blastomycosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:13">[00:04:13]</a>.
*   **Prevalence**: Most commonly seen in North America <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:24">[00:04:24]</a>.
*   **Yeast Form**: Exhibits broad-based budding yeast cells <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:31">[00:04:31]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:37">[00:04:37]</a>. This appearance is sometimes referred to as a "figure of eight" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:47">[00:04:47]</a>.
*   **Mold Form**: Characterized by pear-shaped conidia found along the hyphae <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:56">[00:04:56]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:01">[00:05:01]</a>.

### Coccidioides

*   **Associated Diseases**: Coccidioidomycosis, Valley Fever, California Valley Fever, Desert Rheumatism <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:47">[00:05:47]</a>.
*   **Prevalence**: Found along the valleys of California and in desert areas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:55">[00:05:55]</a>.
*   **Yeast Form**: Appears as spherules containing endospores <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:05">[00:06:05]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:11">[00:06:11]</a>.
*   **Mold Form**: Features arthroconidia (rectangular-shaped conidia) that appear to fragment the hyphae <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:22">[00:06:22]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:31">[00:06:31]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:46">[00:06:46]</a>.

### Paracoccidioides

*   **Associated Diseases**: Paracoccidioidomycosis, South American Blastomycosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:08">[00:07:08]</a>.
*   **Prevalence**: Most commonly found in South America <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:16">[00:07:16]</a>.
*   **Yeast Form**: Yeast cells show projections that resemble "Mickey Mouse ears" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:18">[00:08:18]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:23">[00:08:23]</a>.
*   **Mold Form**: Similar to *Blastomyces*, it presents with pear-shaped conidia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:46">[00:07:46]</a>, which is why it's sometimes called South American Blastomycosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:59">[00:07:59]</a>.

### Talaromyces marneffei

*   **Associated Diseases**: Talaromycosis, Penicilliosis (due to its former name *Penicillium marneffei*) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:56">[00:08:56]</a>.
*   **Prevalence**: Most commonly seen in Southeast Asia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:11">[00:09:11]</a>.
*   **Distinctive Feature**: Produces a red pigment when grown on SDA (Sabouraud Dextrose Agar) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:46">[00:00:46]</a>. This is a very important [[microbial_phenomena_and_tests | diagnostic point]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:52">[00:00:52]</a>.
*   **Yeast Form**: Uniquely presents as oval-shaped yeast cells with an internal septum <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:17">[00:09:17]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:27">[00:09:27]</a>.
*   **Mold Form**: Displays a "bottle brush appearance" formed by conidiophores ending with phialides that produce chains of conidia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:43">[00:09:43]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:05">[00:10:05]</a>.

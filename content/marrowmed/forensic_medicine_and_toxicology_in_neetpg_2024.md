### Rigor Mortis

The question asked about the order of appearance of rigor mortis, well-established 12 to 24 hours after death <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:01">[00:05:01]</a>.

*   **Stages of Muscle Status After Death:**
    1.  Primary flaccidity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:31">[00:05:31]</a>
    2.  Rigor mortis (stiffening due to ATP depletion) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:36">[00:05:36]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:47">[00:05:47]</a>
    3.  Secondary flaccidity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:40">[00:05:40]</a>

*   **Order of Appearance:**
    *   Starts 60 minutes (1 hour) after death <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:55">[00:05:55]</a>.
    *   First in involuntary muscles, then voluntary muscles <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:15">[00:06:15]</a>.
    *   First organ: myocardium <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:26">[00:06:26]</a>.
    *   First external site: eyelids <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:06:43">[00:06:43]</a>.
    *   Follows Nysten's Rule: eyelids, neck, jaw, facial muscles, thorax, upper limb, abdomen, lower limb, fingers, toes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:04">[00:07:04]</a>.
    *   Progression is proximo-distal, from top to bottom (descending order) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:13">[00:07:13]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:05">[00:08:05]</a>.

*   **Rule of 12:**
    *   Starts and involves the entire body in 12 hours <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:34">[00:07:34]</a>.
    *   Stays in the body for another 12 hours <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:46">[00:07:46]</a>.
    *   Gradually disappears in another 12 hours <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:50">[00:07:50]</a>.
    *   Disappearance also occurs in descending order <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:12">[00:08:12]</a>.

*   **Mummification:** Occurs in hot and dry climates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:16">[00:41:16]</a>, involves drying and dehydration of tissues <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:43">[00:40:43]</a>, and is typically odorless <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:08">[00:41:08]</a>. ### Medico-Legal Autopsy

A 36-year-old man died in an RTA (Road Traffic Accident), and the body was shifted for autopsy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:21">[00:41:21]</a>.

*   **Type of Autopsy:** For unnatural deaths like RTA, a Medico-legal autopsy is conducted <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:37">[00:41:37]</a>. This is the most common type of autopsy in India <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:17">[00:41:17]</a>.
*   **Authorization:** Authorization must be given by the Investigating Officer (IO), who can be a police officer or a magistrate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:48">[00:41:48]</a>.
*   **Consent:** Consent from relatives is **not** needed for medico-legal autopsy as it is mandated by law <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:32">[00:42:32]</a>.
*   **Body Handover:** After autopsy, the body is handed back to the IO, not directly to relatives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:01">[00:43:01]</a>.
*   **Pathological Autopsy:** Done in natural deaths, where relatives' consent is mandatory, and IO authorization is not needed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:43">[00:42:43]</a>.

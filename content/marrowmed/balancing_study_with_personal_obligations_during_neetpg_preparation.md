Successfully [[strategies_for_neet_pg_preparation | preparing for NEET PG]] while managing personal obligations requires a tailored and consistent approach, especially during the [[strategy_for_last_80_days_of_neetpg_preparation | last 80 days of NEETPG preparation]] before the exam <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:43">[00:00:43]</a>. The key is to optimize study time and maintain personal well-being <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:58">[00:46:58]</a>.

## Tailoring Your Strategy

For students with limited time, such as those working or with family responsibilities, the general consensus is to prioritize efficiency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:15">[00:37:15]</a>.

*   **Revision Videos:** Stick to revision videos for core content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:32">[00:37:32]</a>. They offer focused content in less time, providing almost similar relevant information as regular videos, especially for short subjects <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:09">[00:12:09]</a>. Teachers often highlight new or modified information in introductory videos <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:34">[00:11:34]</a>.
*   **Question Bank (QBank):** Integrate QBank practice with your video revisions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:37">[00:37:37]</a>. Focus on previous year questions (PYQs) and frequently tested topics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:40">[00:37:40]</a>.
*   **MCQ Discussion Videos:** These videos are crucial as they teach application of theory to questions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:48">[00:38:48]</a>. They cover relevant, important, and potentially new areas that could be tested <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:54">[00:34:54]</a>.

It's better to revise a smaller number of concepts multiple times to ensure they are cemented in your memory, rather than trying to cover too much material once, which may not be retained <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:14">[00:39:14]</a>.

## The Role of INI-CET

Appearing for the INI-CET exam, even if NEET PG is your primary target, is highly recommended <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:08">[00:43:08]</a>:

*   **Motivation:** It pushes you to study more intensely before the NEET PG exam <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:21">[00:43:21]</a>.
*   **Subject Focus:** INI-CET often emphasizes first and second-year subjects, encouraging you to dedicate effort to these areas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:34">[00:43:34]</a>.
*   **Confidence Boost:** A good score or rank in INI-CET can provide significant confidence for the NEET PG exam <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:43:56">[00:43:56]</a>.
*   **Early Relief:** INI-CET results are declared before NEET PG, potentially freeing you from the need to appear for NEET PG if you perform well <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:03">[00:44:03]</a>.

## Managing Study Resources and Habits

### Daily Practice
Solving at least 100 MCQs daily is recommended, allocating about 3.5 to 4 hours for questions and explanation review <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:11">[00:08:11]</a>. The time distribution between notes and MCQs should broadly be 60:40 or 50:50 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:43">[00:08:43]</a>.

### Grand Tests (GTs)
GTs are essential for developing exam skills <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:09">[00:26:09]</a>.
*   **Frequency:** Aim for once a week, or at most twice in 10 days <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:34">[00:25:34]</a>. Daily GTs are not advisable as they don't allow enough time for revision <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:28">[00:25:28]</a>.
*   **Review:** Focus on questions you got wrong or guessed correctly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:29">[00:28:29]</a>. Prioritize conceptual questions and those you thought you knew but answered incorrectly <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:35">[00:30:35]</a>.
*   **Strategy:** Use GTs to experiment with different exam strategies, such as attempting 180-190 questions or being more conservative, to find what works best for you <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:19">[00:31:19]</a>.

### Dynamic Notes (WhatsApp/Telegram Groups)
Instead of making a 20th notebook, which is time-consuming, create personal WhatsApp or Telegram groups for each subject <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:53">[00:41:53]</a>.
*   **Content:** Take screenshots or download images of challenging concepts, facts, or staging (e.g., breast cancer staging, ERCP images) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:44">[01:00:44]</a>. Add brief notes to them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:57">[01:00:57]</a>.
*   **Revision:** Quickly scroll through these groups whenever you have a few minutes (e.g., traveling, in the ward) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:00:59">[01:00:59]</a>.
*   **Optimization:** Once you've thoroughly learned a concept, delete the image from the group <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:23">[01:01:23]</a>. This creates a highly filtered, dynamic set of information focused on your weak areas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:01:34">[01:01:34]</a>.

### Overcoming Procrastination
The Pomodoro Technique is effective for long study sessions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:14">[00:45:14]</a>:
*   **Blocks:** Work in focused blocks of 30-45 minutes without distractions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:20">[00:45:20]</a>.
*   **Breaks:** Take short 5-10 minute breaks away from your study material <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:45">[00:45:45]</a>. Avoid endless scrolling on social media <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:45:57">[00:45:57]</a>.
*   **Benefits:** Breaking down study into smaller, less daunting chunks makes it easier to start and maintain focus, accumulating significant study time throughout the day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:07">[00:46:07]</a>.

## [[balancing_study_and_personal_wellbeing_during_exam_preparation | Balancing Study and Personal Well-being]]

### Addressing Fatigue and Mental Health
Mental and physical fatigue can set in during intense study periods <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:04">[00:47:04]</a>.
*   **Sleep:** Prioritize adequate sleep and try to maintain a fixed sleep schedule <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:20">[00:47:20]</a>.
*   **Nutrition & Hydration:** Eat healthy and stay well-hydrated <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:43">[00:47:43]</a>. Consider multivitamins as deficiencies (e.g., D3, B12) are common <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:57">[00:47:57]</a>.
*   **Breaks & Social Support:** Incorporate regular breaks into your routine <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:26">[00:48:26]</a>. Surround yourself with positive people and take time out with friends and family to reset and recharge <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:08">[00:48:08]</a>.

### Mindset and Expectations
*   **No "Enough":** There's no definitive answer to whether a specific resource is "enough" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:00">[00:41:00]</a>. Focus on doing revision videos, QBank, and MCQ discussions sincerely, as they are designed to cover high-yield areas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:19">[00:41:19]</a>.
*   **Focus on Effort, Not "Drop":** Instead of questioning whether to take a drop year, focus on maximizing effort now <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:03">[00:40:03]</a>. Many have achieved good ranks with focused preparation in the last few months <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:26">[00:40:26]</a>.
*   **Avoid Comparison:** Do not compare your strategy or progress with others <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:40">[00:39:40]</a>. Your preparation is unique to your needs and goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:12">[01:05:12]</a>.
*   **Realistic Expectations:** Be clear about your expectations from the exam <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:42">[01:04:42]</a>. Different rank goals require different levels of effort and strategy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:03">[01:05:03]</a>.
*   **Avoid New Sources:** Stay away from new short courses or materials that appear late in the preparation cycle <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:14">[01:06:14]</a>. Stick to one process and trust it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:06:30">[01:06:30]</a>.

## Conclusion

The last 80 days of [[strategies_for_neet_pg_exam | NEET PG exam]] preparation demand **consistency** and **revision** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:02">[01:03:02]</a>. Consistency means continuing to study even when unmotivated or facing difficulties <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:03:26">[01:03:26]</a>. By adopting a tailored strategy, prioritizing core resources, and addressing personal well-being, students can maximize their chances of success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:04:17">[01:04:17]</a>.

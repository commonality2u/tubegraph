The Medical Termination of Pregnancy (MTP) Act, specifically the new act that came into force on September 24, 2021, introduced significant changes to the provisions for medical termination of pregnancy in India <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:08">[00:08:08]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:20">[00:11:20]</a>. These changes are important for both INIC and NEET PG exams <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:45">[00:00:45]</a>.

## Key Provisions of the [[mtp_act_2021_guidelines | MTP Act 2021]]

### Maximum Gestational Age
Under the new [[mtp_act_2021_guidelines | MTP Act 2021]], MTP can be performed up to 24 weeks of gestation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:08">[00:08:08]</a>.

### Required Doctor Opinions
*   **Less than 20 weeks:** Opinion of one registered medical practitioner (RMP) is required <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:52">[00:08:52]</a>.
*   **20 to 24 weeks:** Opinions of two RMPs are required <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:57">[00:07:57]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:55">[00:08:55]</a>.

### Special Circumstances
*   **Contraceptive Failure:** In cases of contraceptive failure, MTP can be performed only up to 20 weeks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:17">[00:08:17]</a>.
*   **Severe Congenital Anomaly:** There is no upper gestational limit for MTP in cases of severe congenital anomaly, provided it is approved by a medical board <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:21">[00:08:21]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:47">[00:08:47]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:49">[00:08:49]</a>.
    *   **Medical Board Composition:** The medical board comprises a radiologist, a gynecologist, and a pediatrician <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:08:33">[00:08:33]</a>.

### Record Keeping
All MTP records must be maintained for up to 5 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:00">[00:09:00]</a>.

### Consent Requirements
*   **Adult Females (â‰¥ 18 years):** Consent must be given by the female herself <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:03">[00:09:03]</a>.
*   **Minor Females or Mentally Ill Individuals:** Consent must be given by the legal guardian <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:10">[00:09:10]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:11">[00:11:11]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:31">[00:11:31]</a>.

### Marital Status and FIR
*   A marriage certificate is **not** required for MTP, meaning MTP can be performed in unmarried females <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:23">[00:09:23]</a>.
*   An FIR report of rape is **not** required, even if the female states her pregnancy resulted from rape <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:31">[00:09:31]</a>. The healthcare provider proceeds based on her statement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:35">[00:09:35]</a>.

### Qualifications for Performing MTP
An MTP can be performed by:
*   Any person with an MD, MS, or DNB in Obstetrics and Gynecology <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:48">[00:09:48]</a>.
*   Any registered medical practitioner who has assisted in 25 MTPs, with at least 5 as the primary surgeon <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:09:57">[00:09:57]</a>.
*   Any registered medical practitioner who has completed 6 months of house job in Obstetrics and Gynecology <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:14">[00:10:14]</a>.

### Minimum Age to Perform MTP
There is no specified minimum age for a woman to undergo MTP under the Act <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:58">[00:10:58]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:26">[00:11:26]</a>. As MTP can be performed in minor females, and their consent is provided by a legal guardian, it implies that even a 16-year-old or 14-year-old can undergo MTP if required <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:10:42">[00:10:42]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:04">[00:11:04]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:24">[00:12:24]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:12:27">[00:12:27]</a>.

*   **POXO Act Consideration:** The Protection of Children from Sexual Offenses (POXO) Act mandates that any sexual activity involving individuals less than 18 years old is considered statutory rape, requiring mandatory reporting and care by healthcare providers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:38">[00:11:38]</a>.

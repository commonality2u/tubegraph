## Core Pillars of Revision and Practice

### 1. The QBank and Practice Tests

*   **Learning Tool:** The QBank should be treated as a learning tool, not just for assessment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:56:00">[03:56:00]</a>.
*   **Prioritization:** When using the QBank, prioritize solving previously asked questions (PYQs) and high-yield topics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:15:00">[03:15:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:21:00">[03:21:00]</a>. Modules with frequently asked questions, such as "P" in OBGY, should be done first <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:40:00">[03:40:00]</a>.
*   **Custom Modules:** Create custom modules based on specific needs, such as drug of choice, investigation of choice, image-based questions, or clinical problems <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:08:00">[04:08:00]</a>.
*   **Difficulty Levels:** If finding questions too difficult, focus on easy and moderate difficulty levels <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:11:00">[04:11:00]</a>.
*   **Consistency:** Consistent [[importance_of_practice_questions and_revision | practice questions]] are key; forming a habit of daily question solving takes about 36-38 days <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:55:00">[04:55:00]</a>.

### 2. Grand Tests (GTs)

*   **Regularity:** Take [[importance_of_revision_and_grand_tests | Grand Tests]] regularly, whether weekly or fortnightly, without waiting to complete all subjects <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:10:00">[04:10:00]</a>.
*   **Immediate Review:** Review GTs on the same day <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:37:00">[04:37:00]</a>. Focus primarily on incorrect answers and guessed questions that were correct using the guess tracker <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:46:00">[04:46:00]</a>.
*   **Error Analysis:** Categorize incorrect answers into three types <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:19:00">[04:19:00]</a>:
    *   **Silly Mistakes:** Errors due to misreading or oversight; these will decrease with more practice <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:24:00">[04:24:00]</a>.
    *   **Concept-Based Mistakes:** Indicate a lack of understanding. Go back to main videos to revise the concept <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:42:00">[04:42:00]</a>.
    *   **Factual Mistakes:** Indicate a forgotten fact. These should be noted in your [[effective_use_of_grand_tests_and_revision_notes | 20th notebook]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:59:00">[04:59:00]</a>.
*   **Time Management:** GTs also help in improving time management during the exam, as answering quickly is crucial <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:51:00">[05:51:00]</a>.

### 3. Revision Techniques and Tools

*   **Funnel Approach:** Aim to cut down [[importance_of_consistent_revision_for_medical_exams | revision]] time by half with each successive round <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:34:00">[03:34:00]</a>.
*   **Underlining Notes:** Develop a uniform pattern for underlining notes with different colors for keywords, concepts, and investigations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:31:00">[04:31:00]</a>. This creates a visual impression that aids recall during the exam <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:36:00">[04:36:00]</a>.
*   **20th Notebook/Digital Group:** Create a personalized "20th notebook" (or a digital equivalent like a WhatsApp group with only yourself) for facts, TNM classifications, or PYQs that are difficult to remember <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:33:00">[04:33:00]</a>. ## Overcoming Challenges

*   **Procrastination:** Start with small, achievable goals, such as two hours of study or solving a few questions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:59:00">[07:59:00]</a>. Begin with subjects you enjoy to build momentum <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:27:00">[08:27:00]</a>.
*   **Focus on Control:** Dedicate more time to the "circle of control" (how many questions to solve, daily planning) rather than the "circle of concern" (exam date, difficulty), which is outside one's influence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:16:00">[09:16:00]</a>.
*   **Positive Environment:** Surround yourself with positive influences (friends, family) who support your goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:12:00">[10:12:00]</a>.

Ultimately, cracking the exam is about effective [[the_role_of_revision_and_practice_tests | revision]] and application of knowledge, not just the quantity of study <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:40:00">[03:40:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:40:00">[04:40:00]</a>. It's a competitive exam requiring a "topper mindset" focused on continuous improvement and strategic edge over competitors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:19:00">[12:19:00]</a>.

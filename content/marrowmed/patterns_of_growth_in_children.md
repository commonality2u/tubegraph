The assessment of [[developmental_milestones_in_pediatrics | developmental milestones in pediatrics]] and growth patterns is a crucial aspect of child health, often evaluated using standardized tools like growth charts. Understanding these patterns helps in identifying healthy development versus potential issues such as growth failure.

## WHO Growth Curves and Monitoring
[[growth_charts_and_growth_monitoring | Growth charts and growth monitoring]] utilize WHO growth curves to assess a child's growth trajectory <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:02:00">[01:02:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:55:00">[02:55:00]</a>.
*   **Normal Growth**: A growth trajectory between 0 and +2 standard deviations is considered absolutely normal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:21:00">[01:21:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:30:00">[01:30:00]</a>. Growth between 0 and -2 standard deviations is also considered normal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:42:00">[01:42:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:48:00">[01:48:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:03:00">[02:03:00]</a>.
*   **Growth Faltering/Failure**: This refers to a value below -2 standard deviations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:54:00">[01:54:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:59:00">[02:59:00]</a>. A child's weight decreasing or not keeping up with their percentile indicates growth faltering <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:25:00">[02:25:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:28:00">[02:28:00]</a>.

## Schmon's Growth Curves
Schmon's growth curves illustrate the differing patterns of growth for various tissues and organs in the body based on age and percentage of adult size <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:51:00">[05:51:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:54:00">[05:54:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:59:00">[05:59:00]</a>. There are four distinct patterns:
*   **Curve A (Brain Growth)**: This curve is parabolic, showing a steep initial increase, followed by a slower increase <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:10:00">[06:10:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:14:00">[06:14:00]</a>. Approximately 90% of adult brain size is attained by two years of age <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:21:00">[06:21:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:28:00">[06:28:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:30:00">[06:30:00]</a>.
*   **Curve B (Lymphoid Tissues)**: This curve indicates that some tissues grow to more than 100% of their adult size, then regress back to 100% <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:40:00">[06:40:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:46:00">[06:46:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:58:00">[06:58:00]</a>. This pattern is typical for lymphoid tissues like tonsils, adenoids, and lymph nodes, particularly between ages 4 and 8 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:01:00">[07:01:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:04:00">[07:04:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:06:00">[07:06:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:13:00">[07:13:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:18:00">[07:18:00]</a>.
*   **Curve C (Somatic Growth/Height Increase)**: This is a sigmoid curve, showing a rapid increase during the initial two years, followed by a slower increase, and then another rapid increase after approximately 12 years <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:21:00">[07:21:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:25:00">[07:25:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:29:00">[07:29:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:35:00">[07:35:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:38:00">[07:38:00]</a>. This pattern represents general somatic growth, such as height <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:43:00">[07:43:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:46:00">[07:46:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:51:00">[07:51:00]</a>.
*   **Curve D (Gonadal Growth)**: This curve shows a largely silent period until 10 to 12 years of age, followed by a rapid increase in size <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:54:00">[07:54:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:58:00">[07:58:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:00:00">[08:00:00]</a>. This pattern is characteristic of gonadal growth, including testicular growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:02:00">[08:02:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:05:00">[08:05:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:33:00">[08:33:00]</a>.

## Conditions Affecting Growth
Several conditions can impact a child's growth, often presenting with symptoms like poor weight gain or growth retardation.

### Celiac Disease
Celiac disease, or gluten hypersensitivity, is a condition where growth failure can be a key symptom <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:15:00">[03:15:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:51:00">[03:51:00]</a>. It is characterized by symptoms such as lack of weight gain, abdominal pain on eating, poor eating habits, and chronic diarrhea <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:15:00">[03:15:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:06:00">[04:06:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:11:00">[04:11:00]</a>. The definitive diagnostic clue and treatment is a positive response to a gluten-free diet <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:20:00">[03:20:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:31:00">[03:31:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:53:00">[03:53:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:00:00">[05:00:00]</a>.
*   **Diagnosis**:
    *   **Initial Testing**: Antibody testing, specifically IgA levels along with anti-tissue transglutaminase antibody (TTG) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:16:00">[04:16:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:18:00">[04:18:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:22:00">[04:22:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:28:00">[04:28:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:20:00">[05:20:00]</a>.
    *   **Confirmation**: If antibody levels are elevated, diagnosis is confirmed with anti-endomysial antibody (EMA) testing or a biopsy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:37:00">[04:37:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:41:00">[04:41:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:46:00">[04:46:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:50:00">[04:50:00]</a>.

### Rickets
Rickets, a condition affecting bone growth, can lead to deformities like "wind-swept deformity" (a combination of varus and valgus deformities of the limbs) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:07:00">[09:07:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:40:00">[18:40:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:45:00">[18:45:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:50:00">[18:50:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:53:00">[18:53:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:57:00">[18:57:00]</a>.
*   **Nutritional Rickets (Vitamin D Deficiency)**: Unlikely if parathyroid hormone (PTH) levels are normal <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:50:00">[19:50:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:53:00">[19:53:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:02:00">[20:02:00]</a>. In nutritional rickets, PTH levels are typically increased <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:56:00">[19:56:00]</a>.
*   **Refractory Rickets**: This category includes conditions like Vitamin D Dependent Rickets (VDDR) types 1 and 2, renal tubular acidosis (RTA), chronic kidney disease (CKD)-associated rickets, and familial hypophosphatemic rickets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:21:00">[20:21:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:24:00">[20:24:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:27:00">[20:27:00]</a>.
    *   **Diagnosis of Refractory Rickets based on Biochemicals**:
        *   **Serum Phosphate**: High serum phosphate levels indicate CKD-associated rickets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:50:00">[20:50:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:53:00">[20:53:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:56:00">[20:56:00]</a>. Other conditions typically show low serum phosphate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:08:00">[21:08:00]</a>.
        *   **Acidosis (ABG)**: If acidosis is present, renal tubular acidosis (RTA) is the likely possibility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:12:00">[21:12:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:15:00">[21:15:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:19:00">[21:19:00]</a>.
        *   **PTH Levels**:
            *   **High PTH**: Encountered in Vitamin D dependent rickets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:29:00">[21:29:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:32:00">[21:32:00]</a>.
            *   **Normal PTH**: Encountered in familial hypophosphatemic rickets <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:35:00">[21:35:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:37:00">[21:37:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:43:00">[21:43:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:47:00">[21:47:00]</a>.

### Glycogen Storage Disorders
These disorders can present with various symptoms, including those impacting growth or general well-being.
*   **Pompe's Disease (Type II Glycogen Storage Disease)**: Characterized by a deficiency of alpha-glucosidase (acid maltase) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:02:00">[30:02:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:06:00">[30:06:00]</a>. It leads to involvement of both skeletal and cardiac muscles, causing cardiomyopathy and extreme hypotonia (floppy infant) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:44:00">[29:44:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:47:00">[29:47:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:50:00">[29:50:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:52:00">[29:52:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:24:00">[30:24:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:41:00">[30:41:00]</a>. Other features include macroglossia and feeding difficulties <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:35:00">[30:35:00]</a>. Enzyme replacement therapy (ERT) is available <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:55:00">[30:55:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:58:00">[30:58:00]</a>.
*   **Von Gierke's Disease (Type I Glycogen Storage Disease)**: A liver glycogen storage disorder that typically presents with repeated episodes of hypoglycemia, but without cardiomyopathy or floppiness <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:23:00">[29:23:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:26:00">[29:26:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:31:00">[29:31:00]</a>.
*   **McArdle's Disease (Type V Glycogen Storage Disease)**: A muscle glycogen storage disorder primarily affecting skeletal muscles, with no cardiac involvement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:01:00">[29:01:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:04:00">[29:04:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="29:10:00">[29:10:00]</a>.

### Gaucher's Disease
This is a lysosomal storage disorder caused by glucocerebrosidase enzyme deficiency <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:18:00">[28:18:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:20:00">[28:20:00]</a>. Key features include growth retardation, hepatosplenomegaly, anemia, and bone pain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:22:00">[27:22:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:25:00">[27:25:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:27:00">[27:27:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:29:00">[27:29:00]</a>. A characteristic diagnostic finding on bone marrow examination is the presence of Gaucher cells, which have a "crumpled tissue paper appearance" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:30:00">[27:30:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:45:00">[27:45:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="27:50:00">[27:50:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="28:27:00">[28:27:00]</a>.

## Other Relevant Conditions
*   **Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia (ALL)**: The overall most common leukemia in children <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:01:00">[13:01:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:07:00">[13:07:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:28:00">[13:28:00]</a>. Initial treatment involves an induction phase with drugs like prednisone, vincristine, L-asparaginase, and anthracyclines <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:57:00">[13:57:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:01:00">[14:01:00]</a>. Prophylactic CNS therapy with intrathecal methotrexate may also be given <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:21:00">[14:21:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:31:00">[14:31:00]</a>.
*   **Acute Rheumatic Fever**: Characterized by major Jones criteria including arthritis and carditis (cardiac involvement, such as valvulitis leading to a diastolic murmur like Carry-Coombs murmur) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:10:00">[09:10:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:14:00">[09:14:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:17:00">[09:17:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:20:00">[09:20:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:43:00">[09:43:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:46:00">[09:46:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="09:56:00">[09:56:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:01:00">[10:01:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:07:00">[10:07:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:35:00">[10:35:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:49:00">[10:49:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:54:00">[10:54:00]</a>. Fever is a minor criterion <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:22:00">[10:22:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:24:00">[10:24:00]</a>.
*   **Congenital Toxoplasmosis**: A torch infection with a classic triad: chorioretinitis, diffuse intra-parenchymal calcifications in the brain, and hydrocephalus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:11:00">[11:11:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:14:00">[11:14:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:16:00">[11:16:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:19:00">[11:19:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:22:00">[11:22:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:25:00">[11:25:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:27:00">[11:27:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:30:00">[11:30:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:51:00">[11:51:00]</a>. ELISA testing for IgA is considered more sensitive than IgM for diagnosis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:02:00">[12:02:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:05:00">[12:05:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:11:00">[12:11:00]</a>.
*   **Congenital Diaphragmatic Hernia (CDH)**: Often presents with difficulty breathing in a newborn, shifted heart sounds (e.g., to the right hemithorax if hernia is on the left), and gurgling sounds on the affected side due to intestinal shadows in the lung <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:26:00">[22:26:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:28:00">[22:28:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:30:00">[22:30:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:34:00">[22:34:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:37:00">[22:37:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:41:00">[22:41:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:53:00">[22:53:00]</a>. The most common type is a left posterolateral defect (Bochdalek hernia) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="22:58:00">[22:58:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:00:00">[23:00:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:11:00">[23:11:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:13:00">[23:13:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:17:00">[23:17:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="23:23:00">[23:23:00]</a>. Unlike congenital pulmonary adenomatoid malformation, the diaphragm border is typically not well-defined in CDH <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:12:00">[16:12:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:14:00">[16:14:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:16:00">[16:16:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:21:00">[16:21:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:23:00">[16:23:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:26:00">[16:26:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:25:00">[18:25:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:27:00">[18:27:00]</a>.
*   **Congenital Pulmonary Adenomatoid Malformation (CPAM)**: Formerly known as CCAM (congenital cystic adenomatoid malformation) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:47:00">[15:47:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:53:00">[15:53:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:13:00">[17:13:00]</a>. It is the most common cause of cystic lung disease in newborns, characterized by multiple rounded cysts within the lung parenchyma <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:08:00">[15:08:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:11:00">[15:11:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:17:00">[15:17:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:58:00">[15:58:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:00:00">[16:00:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:07:00">[16:07:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:57:00">[16:57:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:00:00">[17:00:00]</a>. It can present with respiratory distress and recurrent respiratory infections <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:48:00">[14:48:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:50:00">[14:50:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:53:00">[14:53:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:37:00">[17:37:00]</a>. CPAM is typically unilateral and most commonly involves the left lung <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:22:00">[17:22:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:25:00">[17:25:00]</a>. A critical point is the risk of malignancy (sarcoma or carcinoma) in the cystic areas, necessitating resection of the malformation before one year of age <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:45:00">[17:45:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:47:00">[17:47:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:51:00">[17:51:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="17:54:00">[17:54:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:00:00">[18:00:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:07:00">[18:07:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:09:00">[18:09:00]</a>.
*   **Infantile Hypertrophic Pyloric Stenosis (IHPS)**: Presents with repeated episodes of non-bilious vomiting in infants <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:26:00">[25:26:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="25:29:00">[25:29:00]</a>. It is caused by hypertrophy of the circular muscle fibers of the pylorus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:01:00">[26:01:00]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="26:04:00">[26:04:00]</a>. Understanding these patterns and their associated conditions is fundamental for pediatric diagnosis and management.

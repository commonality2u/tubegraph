## Pharmacological Treatment Approaches

### Drug of Choice: Mirabegron
For urge urinary incontinence, the drug of choice is the beta-3 agonist, Mirabegron <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:08">[00:01:08]</a>.

### Anticholinergics and Dementia Risk
Anticholinergic drugs can also be used, but a significant concern with their use is their potential to cause dementia by blocking M1 receptors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:20">[00:01:20]</a>. This is especially relevant for elderly patients who may already have senile dementia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:15">[00:02:15]</a>.

### Preferred Anticholinergics
If Mirabegron cannot be used due to reasons like price or availability, certain anticholinergics are preferred because they cause lesser dementia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:28">[00:01:28]</a>:
*   **Darifenacin** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:38">[00:01:38]</a>
*   **Solifenacin** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:48">[00:01:48]</a>
*   **Trospium** <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:50">[00:01:50]</a>

The preference for Darifenacin and Solifenacin is due to their selective M3 receptor blockade <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:58">[00:01:58]</a>. Trospium is preferred because it does not cross the blood-brain barrier, thereby minimizing central nervous system side effects like dementia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:02">[00:02:02]</a>. These options are considered the "Holy Grail" for urge incontinence treatment, especially when cognitive decline is a factor <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:04">[00:02:04]</a>.

### Other Anticholinergics
Tolterodine is another anticholinergic that can be used for urinary incontinence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:56">[00:00:56]</a>. While not absolutely selective for M3 receptors, it shows more selectivity for M3 compared to M1 or M2 <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:30">[00:02:30]</a>. However, it can cause dementia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:42">[00:02:42]</a>.

### Drugs Not Used for Incontinence
Other drugs such as Tiotropium, Ipratropium, and Telenzepine are not used for treating incontinence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:52">[00:02:52]</a>. Tiotropium and Ipratropium are typically used for Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD), and Telenzepine can be used in peptic disease <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:59">[00:02:59]</a>.

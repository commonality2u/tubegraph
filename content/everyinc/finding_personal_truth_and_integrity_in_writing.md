Finding one's personal truth and living with integrity are deeply intertwined with the act of creation, particularly writing. ## The Quest for Self-Understanding

The journey begins with a mission to understand "who you are" and reflect that understanding back into your work <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:40:00">[01:40:00]</a>. This self-discovery can be surprisingly difficult to achieve and accept <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:49:00">[01:49:00]</a>. A foundational belief is that each individual is endowed with unique gifts, a "unique fingerprint," and a specific "grain" or "shape" of personality that makes certain tasks easier and others more challenging <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:20:00">[02:20:00]</a>. Examples like Michael Phelps being "made to be a swimmer" or LeBron James "made to be a basketball player" illustrate this concept for physical talents, yet it's often overlooked for intellectual aptitudes <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:36:00">[02:36:00]</a>.

Ultimately, this understanding necessitates a "surrender" to one's inherent nature, asking what one is truly "made to be doing" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:59:00">[02:59:00]</a>.

## Integrity and Values

The popular exhortation to "be yourself" is only "halfway right" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:13:00">[02:13:00]</a>. In practice, it can sometimes be misinterpreted as a "moral hall pass" leading to degeneracy <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:22:00">[03:22:00]</a>. True integrity, in contrast, is rooted in clear values and principles, coupled with consistency in living by them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:38:00">[03:38:00]</a>.

A lack of integrity arises when individuals, often influenced by societal or familial expectations, force themselves into roles that disregard their unique individuality, such as becoming lawyers or doctors without true personal alignment <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:55:00">[03:55:00]</a>. This concept is likened to the Greek myth of Procrustes, a tyrannical hotel owner who forced guests to fit his single-sized bed by stretching or chopping them <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:06:00">[10:06:00]</a>. Many people adopt a "Procrustean approach to their career," conforming themselves to an external mold rather than shaping their path to fit who they are, which leads to misery <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:37:00">[10:37:00]</a>. This often stems from a "deep shame" or guilt about perceived weaknesses or not being able to do something one believes they "should" do <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:52:00">[10:52:00]</a>.

## Pursuit of Excellence and Compulsion

A core value should be the pursuit of excellence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:27:00">[02:27:00]</a>. Observing world-class individuals reveals a "graciousness to their effort" and a sense that they are "compelled" to do what they do <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="04:43:00">[04:43:00]</a>. For example, a drummer might constantly tap fingers, or a golfer will naturally spend all day at the course <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:25:00">[05:25:00]</a>.

This alignment between one's natural inclinations and work leads to "joyousness" and a powerful "charisma" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:07:00">[06:07:00]</a>. Tyler Cowan is cited as a model for this, demonstrating a profound alignment between his career and his natural inclinations, which manifests as joy in every interaction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:50:00">[05:50:00]</a>. Achieving the pinnacle of success, being "the best in the world," is only possible when aligned with the "shape of your soul" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:01:00">[08:01:00]</a>. Fighting one's nature puts one at a disadvantage against those who are aligned <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:10:00">[08:10:00]</a>.

[[Balancing personalization and professional identity at work|Balancing personalization and professional identity at work]] is critical, as emulating heroes wholesale can lead to following "their path and not yours" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:32:00">[08:32:00]</a>. It's crucial to identify the aspects of heroes that truly resonate and separate them from elements that don't fit one's unique self <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:45:00">[08:45:00]</a>.

## Leveraging AI for Self-Discovery in Writing

Artificial intelligence, specifically large language models like ChatGPT, can be a powerful tool in this self-discovery process, especially for writers.

### Understanding Oneself
ChatGPT can help in identifying and articulating personal inspirations, strengths, and weaknesses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:00:00">[07:00:00]</a>. By providing a list of admired figures and reasons for their admiration, ChatGPT can synthesize underlying commonalities, providing "more depth and clarity and insight" into one's own sensibility <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:08:00">[07:08:00]</a>. The process is iterative, likened to a "dance," where initial answers might be broad, but refinement through specific prompts leads to higher quality insights <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="12:45:00">[12:45:00]</a>.

For instance, providing a list of heroes and their admirable traits allowed ChatGPT to identify core values such as "passion for excellence and innovation," "intellectual curiosity and insightfulness," "heart-led and joyous approach," and "commitment to personal and spiritual values" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:58:00">[14:58:00]</a>. This process can quickly articulate intuitions that might otherwise take significant time to recognize and put into words <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="14:40:00">[14:40:00]</a>. One profound discovery was the "balance of joyfulness and seriousness" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:24:00">[16:24:00]</a>, providing a new vocabulary for self-understanding. This highlights the value of [[Analyzing personal sensibility through saved quotes]].

### Unpacking Weaknesses and Building Teams
ChatGPT can be used for "motivational interviewing," a psychological technique to unpack conflicting internal forces <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="19:31:00">[19:31:00]</a>. By articulating specific fears or concerns, like being seen as "vengeful or uncaring" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:44:00">[20:44:00]</a>, ChatGPT can help identify patterns and underlying reasons for certain behaviors <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:14:00">[21:14:00]</a>.

This self-awareness then extends to understanding optimal working relationships. By inputting personal psychology (e.g., being a "three-wing four" on the Enneagram, prone to "sensitivity to perception" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="24:10:00">[24:10:00]</a>), ChatGPT can suggest ideal collaborators. It can even accurately predict compatible Enneagram types, such as Fives (analytical, perceptive, innovative) and Eights (assertive, confident, decisive), who complement one's own traits <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="31:16:00">[31:16:00]</a>. This demonstrates how AI can help identify who can "cover" one's weaknesses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="34:41:00">[34:41:00]</a>, enabling a focus on strengths. The key is to be honest about what one is and isn't good at, aligning life with one's "circle of competence" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="35:11:00">[35:11:00]</a>.

## The Evolution of AI Interfaces
The development of AI has seen rapid acceleration, particularly with the widespread adoption of tools like ChatGPT <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:38">[00:05:38]</a>. Early AI models like BERT and GPT-2 made it challenging to articulate the full potential of AI, but the landscape shifted significantly after ChatGPT's launch <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:39">[00:05:39]</a>.

A key debate in [[the_future_of_ai_agents_and_user_interaction | AI interaction]] revolves around the efficacy of chat interfaces. While language is a powerful tool for communication, it may not be the most natural way to access AI capabilities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:21">[00:13:21]</a>. The belief is that AI should function more like background agents that intuit and accomplish tasks without requiring explicit, conversational commands <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:42">[00:13:42]</a>. Personal use of chat interfaces has shown some utility, particularly for exploring new topics or seeking pros and cons of ideas when human colleagues are unavailable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:11">[00:14:11]</a>.

### Beyond Chat: Visual and Artifact-Based Interactions
An alternative to purely textual chat interfaces involves AI generating artifacts, such as visual diagrams (e.g., Mermaid diagrams) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:16">[00:15:16]</a>. This allows for [[combining_ai_interfaces_with_human_agency | non-textual collaboration]] and better suits visual thinkers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:43">[00:15:43]</a>. The ideal interface allows for pushing forward along multiple dimensions simultaneously, which is beneficial for creative processes where dimensions are not predefined <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:43">[00:16:43]</a>. This approach fosters an "interesting teacher-student relationship" where humans provide judgment and feedback on AI-generated outputs, allowing for iterative refinement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:39">[00:20:39]</a>.

Key aspects of this human-AI dynamic include:
*   **Human in the Loop:** Essential for creative judgment and refinement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:55">[00:20:55]</a>.
*   **Knowledge Sharing:** AI outputs can offer insight into its "thinking," enabling users to provide better feedback <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:26">[00:21:26]</a>.
*   **Shared Understanding:** The more the human and AI can achieve a shared understanding of each other's knowledge and preferences, the more effectively they can fill in gaps and collaborate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:11">[00:22:11]</a>.

## AI's Impact on Science and Research
AI is fundamentally changing how science is conducted, particularly from a knowledge standpoint. It can comb through vast amounts of information, saving researchers countless hours <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:38:40">[00:38:40]</a>.

### Data-Centric Science and Predictions
The focus in science is shifting towards collecting and aggregating high-quality data sets. Instead of primarily publishing scientific papers, which often only report successful hypotheses and can be narrow in scope, there should be a greater emphasis on releasing data <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:40:24">[00:40:24]</a>. This would allow any scientist to build models and test hypotheses on shared data, fostering combinatorial power <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:01">[00:41:01]</a>.

> "The underlying data is actually probably just as important as the story" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:40">[00:00:40]</a>

This change means scientists are becoming more computational by nature, even if they weren't before, due to increased access to data and computing power <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:27">[00:44:27]</a>. While papers are still useful for sharing information, the current system often fails to publish results of failed experiments or attempts, leading to a skewed view of scientific progress <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:48">[00:44:48]</a>.

There is a growing argument for prioritizing predictions over causal explanations, especially in complex fields like psychology or psychiatry <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:35">[00:41:35]</a>. If AI can accurately predict phenomena like OCD symptoms or effective interventions, it can be life-changing, even without a full causal understanding <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:50">[00:41:50]</a>. This aligns with the shift from symbolic AI (rule-based) to sub-symbolic AI (data-driven models) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:42:23">[00:42:23]</a>.

AI can also assist in:
*   **Simulating Data:** Training models to simulate more data based on existing datasets helps scientists explore possibilities and narrow down research questions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:05">[00:39:05]</a>.
*   **Hypothesis Generation:** Humans still excel at critical thinking and hypothesis generation, but AI can support the data collection and model building aspects <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:39:56">[00:39:56]</a>.
*   **Personalized Prediction (N=1 studies):** AI enables personalized predictions for individuals based on their unique data (e.g., biometric data, video journals) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:50">[00:26:50]</a>. This contrasts with traditional scientific methods focused on generalization.

## AI's Influence on Media and Storytelling
Just as in science, there's a trend in journalism and media to unbundle the story from the underlying data. Historically, the story and its data were always bundled, which created problems. Now, the underlying data is as crucial as the story itself because AI makes it more discoverable and legible without relying solely on authorial perspective <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:53">[00:50:53]</a>.

This shift allows for:
*   **Extendable Articles:** Providing access to all source materials (interviews, articles) via a chat bot alongside a published article allows readers to form their own opinions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:19">[00:49:19]</a>.
*   **Cheaper Storytelling:** AI can tell stories in places where it would have been too expensive otherwise, for example, creating podcasts about internal company meetings or daily strategy updates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:55:41">[00:55:41]</a>.
*   **Increased Accessibility:** AI can translate and adapt stories into different languages, audio, or video formats, making culture and information accessible to a wider audience, regardless of their preferred consumption method <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:56:37">[00:56:37]</a>. This includes making classic books more readable, visual, or audio <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:57:13">[00:57:13]</a>.
*   **Reviving Local News:** AI can help generate local news stories by processing available data, filling a gap where traditional journalism might be struggling <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:58:01">[00:58:01]</a>.

Ultimately, [[the_future_of_ai_and_human_interaction | AI's impact]] is about making information accessible and useful for humans <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:40">[00:03:40]</a>. It empowers individuals and organizations to extract richer understanding and create new narratives from existing data <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:09">[00:50:09]</a>.

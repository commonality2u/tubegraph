Running a [[Founding and running a startup | startup]] involves significant personal challenges and opportunities for learning, particularly when navigating different [[Entrepreneurial journey and challenges | entrepreneurial journeys]]. The model of operating a media business as a foundation for building software offers a unique perspective on managing stress, fostering creativity, and making strategic choices without the constant pressure of traditional venture capital.

## The Pressure of Traditional Venture Capital

In past experiences, particularly when a company raised a substantial amount of money, such as $50 million, the founder felt a constant need to sprint to prevent the business from collapsing. This created an environment where the focus was always on "running to keep my feet under me," rather than sustained growth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:17">[00:24:17]</a>. This tension often existed between what felt right for customers and the immediate business needs, versus a more grandiose long-term vision driven by investor expectations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:50">[00:04:50]</a>.

> [!quote] "When you raise $50 million I kind of felt like I personally and the business was always sprinting to make sure that we didn't topple over ourselves like we didn't fall forward I was always running to keep my feet under me" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:17">[00:24:17]</a>

The constant need to secure subsequent funding rounds within an 18-month window added immense pressure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:41">[00:07:41]</a>. This external stress differs significantly from self-imposed stress <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:25:29">[00:25:29]</a>. While external pressure can lead to intense periods of productivity, it can also "fizzle out" quickly, requiring significant effort to re-engage <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:03">[00:28:03]</a>.

## Embracing a Different Model

The current approach, which avoids raising significant venture capital, fosters a different environment. This model allows for more freedom and control over the business's direction, reducing the feeling of being perpetually "on the run" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:42">[00:24:42]</a>.

> [!info] A "Creative Playground"
> Internally, the team aspires to build a "creative playground," where experimentation and genuine interest in the work are prioritized <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:33">[00:00:33]</a>. This involves:
> *   Building software out of experiences and ideas from running a core media business <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:53">[00:01:53]</a>.
> *   The media business (newsletter, YouTube channel, podcasts) supports the software development, reducing external financial pressure <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:07:38">[00:07:38]</a>.
> *   The flexibility to "choose when to be stressed" rather than being driven by external expectations <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:08">[00:26:08]</a>.

This model, while potentially having a "ceiling" on hyper-growth, allows for more creativity due to the "lack of just like we can burn this cuz we're going to go race some more" mentality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:49">[00:23:49]</a>. It forces greater resourcefulness in decision-making and spending <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:26:36">[00:26:36]</a>.

## The Role of Personal Expectations and Ownership

The current motivation largely stems from personal expectations rather than external pressures, which is described as a "longer lasting" fuel <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:27:19">[00:27:19]</a>. As an owner of the new product, Spiral, the individual's motivation is significantly different from previous experiences where equity as an employee was often insufficient to drive through difficult times <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:28:22">[00:28:22]</a>.

The journey is also seen as an experiment to test personal opinions and ideas, embodying a desire to "throw myself kind of in the deep end" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:11">[00:11:11]</a>. This continuous learning and adaptation are crucial, especially when initial assumptions are challenged by reality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:30:04">[00:30:04]</a>.

## Lessons from the Trenches

A key principle applied to life is to "fuck around and find out" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:41">[00:00:41]</a>. This contrasts with the "measure twice, cut once" approach, suggesting a readiness to experiment and learn through direct experience <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:55">[00:52:55]</a>. This mindset is reflected in personal anecdotes:
*   Attempting to service his mother's car as a teenager, resulting in a broken spark plug and weeks of self-taught repair <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:41:05">[00:41:05]</a>. This experience of "getting punched in the face" and learning to "do it again" is a common theme for founders <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:31:17">[00:31:17]</a>.
*   Not attending college, instead choosing to delve into technology and [[developing_and_selling_tech_startups | startups]] after seeing others achieve success <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:04">[00:36:04]</a>. This was driven by a desire to explore interesting applications of technology beyond traditional academic paths <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:37:45">[00:37:45]</a>.

Traits like impulsiveness, noted by a third-grade teacher, are seen as beneficial in the startup context, allowing for quick action and exploration of ideas <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:11">[00:44:11]</a>.

## The Tech Industry's Unique Environment

The technology industry stands out for its meritocratic nature, where individuals are taken seriously based on their capabilities rather than credentials <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:46:58">[00:46:58]</a>. This environment allows young people to be heard and contribute meaningfully from an early age <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:06">[00:47:06]</a>. This valuing of "potential" as much as "experience" is a defining characteristic <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:47:44">[00:47:44]</a>.

However, this unique environment can also present [[challenges_and_strategies_for_ai_startups | challenges and strategies for AI startups]]. For instance, young founders, like a 22-year-old receiving $50 million, may face a "scarring experience" if they are unprepared for the significant weight and responsibilities of running a business, including taxes, hiring, and firing <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:24">[00:48:24]</a>. The industry is currently undergoing a "correction" in this regard <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:48:02">[00:48:02]</a>.

## Choosing the Right People

A critical aspect of [[Founding and running a startup | founding and running a startup]] successfully is choosing the right people to work with <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:17:12">[00:17:12]</a>. This involves seeking individuals with:
*   High IQ and technical ability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:27">[00:18:27]</a>.
*   Good taste and attention to detail <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:04">[00:18:04]</a>.
*   Most importantly, high EQ (emotional intelligence) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:18:29">[00:18:29]</a>.

These qualities enable deep discussions and collaboration, allowing individuals to "riffing on an idea" and take it further <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:00">[00:19:00]</a>. The shared mission of charting one's own destiny in a way that feels authentic to individuals is a powerful aligning force <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:47">[00:05:47]</a>.

This journey of [[entrepreneurial_journey_and_challenges | entrepreneurial journey and challenges]] and personal growth, likened to "growing like a garden," emphasizes creating the right conditions for ideas and individuals to flourish, rather than a "war" mentality <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:59:33">[00:59:33]</a>.

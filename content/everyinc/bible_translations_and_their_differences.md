## The Nature of Biblical Languages
Hebrew is described as a "right-brained," intuitive language <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:22">[00:00:22]</a>. In contrast, Greek is a "left-brained," analytical, and logical language <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:25">[00:00:25]</a>. These two languages, through their structure, are thought to reflect the two hemispheres of the cognitive brain <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:30">[00:00:30]</a>.

### Key Bible Translations
Various English Bible translations exist, each with its own approach:
*   **ESV (English Standard Version) & NIV (New International Version)**: These are more modern and generally accessible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:38">[00:03:38]</a>.
*   **KJV (King James Version)**: An older, influential translation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:42">[00:03:42]</a>.
*   **NASB (New American Standard Bible)**: Praised for being highly faithful to the original text <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:56">[00:02:56]</a>.
*   **The Message**: A more accessible version that "twists the word so that it can be easily understood," which can be acceptable if one understands its interpretative nature <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:42">[00:02:42]</a>.

### Discrepancies in Translation: The Word "Archegos" (ἀρχηγός)
When studying, it's beneficial to look for discrepancies between translations to reveal deeper meanings <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:50">[00:03:50]</a>.

For instance, in Acts 3:15, the Greek word "archegos" (ἀρχηγός) is translated differently:
*   **ESV & NIV**: Translate "archegos" as "author" (e.g., "You killed the author of Life") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:58">[00:03:58]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:00">[00:04:00]</a>.
*   **KJV**: Translates "archegos" as "Prince" (e.g., "You killed the Prince of Life") <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:04">[00:04:04]</a>.

The words "author" and "Prince" appear to be distinct in English: an author writes, while a prince leads <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:13">[00:04:13]</a>. However, the original Greek word "archegos" encompasses both concepts, meaning "author, leader, Prince, or Pioneer" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:40">[00:04:40]</a>.

This revelation through [[Etymology and Greek Words in the Bible | Greek etymology]] highlights a profound truth about [[Role of Jesus in the New Testament | Jesus]]:
*   [[Role of Jesus in the New Testament | Jesus]] is the **Author of Life**, echoing John 1 where he is identified as "the Word" through whom all things came into being <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:02">[00:05:02]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:16">[00:05:16]</a>.
*   From being the Author, [[Role of Jesus in the New Testament | Jesus]] becomes a **Leader** of a new movement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:33">[00:05:33]</a>.
*   He is also a **Prince**, reigning as King <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:35">[00:05:35]</a>.
*   Finally, [[Role of Jesus in the New Testament | Jesus]] is a **Pioneer** of a new way of living <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:42">[00:05:42]</a>.

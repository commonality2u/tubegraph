## The Power of Niche Interests

A key insight is that the most niche interests on the internet are often "no longer Niche" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:41">[00:00:41]</a>. Due to the internet's vast scale, enough people can now care about even highly specific topics <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:01">[00:01:01]</a>. This scale enables the creation of things that would never have existed before, as individuals are motivated to produce content or projects knowing there's an audience for it <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:09">[00:01:09]</a>. Examples include someone sending garlic bread to space or creating a maze for a squirrel <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:16">[00:01:16]</a>. This shift in paradigm allows creators to simply "do cool stuff for the hell of it" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:52">[00:01:52]</a>, which the internet facilitates <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:54">[00:01:54]</a>.

## Case Studies in Unexpected Popularity

### Cats on Reddit
One example of latent interest finding a platform is the popularity of cats on Reddit <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:58">[00:01:58]</a>. Before Reddit, there wasn't a dedicated place for people to share content about their cats <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:02">[00:02:02]</a>.

### The "TV Too High" Phenomenon
Another example is the subreddit "TV Too High" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:22">[00:04:22]</a>, which has 180,000 subscribers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:52">[00:04:52]</a>.

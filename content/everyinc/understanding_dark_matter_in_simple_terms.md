## Explaining Dark Matter with AI

To make [[explaining_dark_matter_with_chatgpt | dark matter more accessible]], a user leveraged ChatGPT with a unique prompt <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:38">[00:00:38]</a>:

> Hey ChatGPT, could you explain what dark matter is in the voice of SpongeBob? <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:00:40">[00:00:40]</a>

The choice of SpongeBob was made because it is a simplistic and fun cartoon character <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:00">[00:01:00]</a>, aiming to introduce conceptual elements in an engaging way <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:06">[00:01:06]</a>. This approach contrasts with a more "boring" prompt like "explain this to me like I'm five," which can feel condescending and less memorable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:14">[00:01:14]</a>.

The AI's response began by imitating SpongeBob's enthusiastic and playful voice, opening with "AHOY MATEY!" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:36">[00:01:36]</a>. It then introduced dark matter as a "wondrous mystery of the deep Universe" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:01:46">[00:01:46]</a>. The explanation didn't just dive into dark matter; it first covered concepts like stars and galaxies to establish a baseline of what is known <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:06">[00:02:06]</a>, before moving on to the "mysterious friend," dark matter <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:16">[00:02:16]</a>.

Later, the user experimented with another iconic character, asking ChatGPT to pick its favorite, which resulted in an explanation of dark matter in the "wise and somewhat grave tone" of Gandalf <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:22">[00:03:22]</a>.

## Scientific Understanding of Dark Matter

While the explanation aimed for simplicity, it aligns with the general scientific consensus that dark matter exists <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:37">[00:03:37]</a>. Scientists can measure its effects, even though it doesn't follow the laws of normal matter and cannot be seen, heard, or felt <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:02:40">[00:02:40]</a>. Our brains are not naturally structured to understand dark matter <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:03:01">[00:03:01]</a>.

## AI's Role in Accessible Explanations

This method of using AI for explanations is seen as a significant improvement over traditional resources like Wikipedia <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:02">[00:04:02]</a>. Wikipedia articles can be dense <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:13">[00:04:13]</a>, but AI allows for an explanation tailored to the user's desired [[the_complexity_of_scientific_explanations | level of complexity]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:20">[00:04:20]</a>. If an explanation is too complex, the user can simply ask for it to be brought down further <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:31">[00:04:31]</a>.

The ability of AI to adapt explanations addresses a common challenge known as the "curse of knowledge," where experts struggle to articulate complex concepts to non-experts due to the significant knowledge gap <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:04:52">[00:04:52]</a>. This program can take something very complex and simplify it, or even make it more complex, as needed <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:05:09">[00:05:09]</a>.

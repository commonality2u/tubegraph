## Co-pilot vs. Delegation Modes

AI assists humans in two primary modes:
*   **Co-pilot Mode** This mode involves a conscious decision in real-time to interact with AI for immediate assistance, with the human remaining the primary agent in pursuing their goals <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:57:00">[01:57:00]</a>. [[the_role_of_chatgpt_in learning_and_research | ChatGPT]] is primarily used in this mode <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:01:00">[02:01:00]</a>.
*   **Delegation Mode** This mode involves truly offloading a task to AI, aiming for consistent output that requires minimal human review <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:36:00">[02:36:00]</a>. This approach saves time and money on tedious tasks or scales capabilities not currently scalable <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:10:00">[03:10:00]</a>.

While co-pilot mode enhances human capability (intelligence augmentation), delegation mode focuses on efficiency and scalability <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:03:00">[03:03:00]</a>. The gap between these two modes, known as ad hoc delegation (delegating larger subtasks on the fly), is expected to close as AI agents improve <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:53:00">[03:53:00]</a>.

## AI, Truth, and Language

[[understanding_consciousness_and_its_implications_for_ai | AI]], particularly large language models (LLMs), introduces new lenses through which to consider perennial philosophical questions such as "what is truth?" and "what is reality?" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:11:10">[00:11:10]</a>.

The development of LLMs has brought the philosophical debate between essentialism and nominalism into sharper focus <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:05">[00:15:05]</a>.
*   **Essentialism**: Believes in a fundamental, objective reality that is knowable, allowing nature to be "carved at its joints" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:40">[00:14:40]</a>. Early Wittgenstein's view of truth, where it cashes out into states of possibilities and actualities in a logical space, aligns with this <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:22:21">[00:22:21]</a>.
*   **Nominalism**: Suggests truth is relative, about social convention, or about what works <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:57">[00:14:57]</a>. Later Wittgenstein, who focused on "language games" and how meaning is derived from use in practice, falls into this category <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:14:50">[00:14:50]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:19:55">[00:19:55]</a>.

LLMs, which predict the next token based on language patterns, initially function more like later Wittgenstein or nominalist perspectives <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:21">[00:15:21]</a>. However, the goal in developing LLMs is to embody more essentialist characteristics, grounding them in truth and reducing "hallucinations" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:15:43">[00:15:43]</a>. This dynamic of moving from a pragmatic base (like token prediction) towards grounding in reality (like reducing hallucinations) resembles Hegel's thesis-antithesis-synthesis <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:16:11">[00:16:11]</a>, <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:20:08">[00:20:08]</a>.

While LLMs are trained on the vast corpus of human knowledge and language, their learning mechanism is different from biological entities <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:23:41">[00:23:41]</a>. This raises the question of whether their "language game" (how they determine truth functions) is fundamentally different from a human one <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:05">[00:24:05]</a>. The objective of current LLM development is to make them more reliable on a truth basis, prioritizing truthfulness alongside creativity and generativity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:24:19">[00:24:19]</a>.

One of the most promising areas in [[practical_approaches_to_ai | AI development]] is incorporating reasoning into language models <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:34:27">[00:34:27]</a>. This is achieved by training models on data sources that contain crisp reasoning:
*   **Computer Code**: Training on code helps models learn patterns of reasoning beyond just programming, as code embodies a precise, mathematical-like form of reasoning <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:02">[00:35:02]</a>. This is part of [[the_evolution_of_ai_in_software_engineering | the evolution of AI in software engineering]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:02">[00:35:02]</a>.
*   **Textbooks**: Using textbooks, which encapsulate human training disciplines, can create smaller yet highly effective models <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:35:47">[00:35:47]</a>.

Philosophical concepts, such as theories of science (e.g., Lakatos building on Popper) and Bayesian logic, may offer further avenues for computational philosophy to enhance AI reasoning <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:36:06">[00:36:06]</a>. This relates to the broader concept of [[chain_of_thought_reasoning_in_ai_models | chain of thought reasoning in AI models]].

## [[impact_of_ai_on_human_intellect | Impact of AI on Human Intellect]] and Evolution

Technology fundamentally changes the definition of what it means to be human <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:13:41">[00:13:41]</a>. Humans are not static; they are constituted by the technology they engage with, which impacts perception and, consequently, theories of truth <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:44:56">[00:44:56]</a>. This ongoing process of discovery through language and interaction with the world highlights that truth is a dynamic process <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:21:54">[00:21:54]</a>.

The progress of cultural knowledge is a key driver of human evolution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:49:19">[00:49:19]</a>. While biological evolution is slow, cultural evolution (driven by knowledge and digital advancements) is much faster <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:24">[00:50:24]</a>. [[the_role_of_ai_in_personal_growth_and_integration | AI and LLMs]] act as tools to accelerate this cultural-digital evolution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:50:53">[00:50:53]</a>. For example, LLMs function as immediate, on-demand personal research assistants, making humanity's collective knowledge accessible and thus accelerating cultural evolution <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:51:05">[00:51:05]</a>. This is a profound next step in cultural transmission technology, following inventions like language, reading, and the printing press <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="00:52:00">[00:52:00]</a>.

### Clayton Christensen's Disruptive Innovation
This theory suggests that smaller, nimbler competitors offer cheaper, more accessible, or innovative solutions that gradually move upmarket <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:28:00">[05:28:00]</a>. This is observed with independent creators and small [[creating_content_and_managing_growth_in_a_media_business | media companies]] like Chris Williamson's podcast, which is a one-person operation increasing its production quality and moving upmarket <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:37:00">[05:37:00]</a>.

### Michael Porter's Five Forces
Porter's framework analyzes the competitive forces within an industry:

1.  **Threat of New Entrants**: New competitors can enter the market with lower barriers, especially in the digital space, as the ability to produce and distribute [[creating_content_and_managing_growth_in_a_media_business | content]] has been democratized <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:01:00">[06:01:00]</a>.
2.  **Bargaining Power of Customers**: With more options for news and [[creating_content_and_managing_growth_in_a_media_business | content]], customers' willingness to pay might decrease unless The New York Times offers "distinct value" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:14:00">[06:14:00]</a>.
3.  **Threat of Substitutes**: Alternative sources of news and information, such as [[challenges_and_opportunities_in_journalism | citizen journalism]] on platforms like Twitter, are rising <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:32:00">[06:32:00]</a>. Traditional [[shifts_in_digital_media_landscape_and_its_impact_on_traditional_media_companies | media companies]] can no longer compete on certain vectors, as individuals with "boots on the ground" can distribute information faster <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:43:00">[06:43:00]</a>. A notable example is the US Airways plane crash on the Hudson, where people on the scene reported better than many [[shifts_in_digital_media_landscape_and_its_impact_on_traditional_media_companies | media companies]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:06:00">[07:06:00]</a>.
4.  **Bargaining Power of Suppliers (Talent)**: The cost of retaining top talent is increasing as journalists leave traditional [[shifts_in_digital_media_landscape_and_its_impact_on_traditional_media_companies | media companies]] to launch their own ventures <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:19:00">[07:19:00]</a>.

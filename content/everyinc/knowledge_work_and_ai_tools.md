## Recollect: AI for Knowledge Workers

Recollect, a company founded by Alice and later acquired by SmartNews, aimed to empower knowledge workers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="01:05:05">[01:05:05]</a>. Its core mission was to connect information consumed by users in a personal way, similar to how the human mind would, and then leverage that for productivity <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="02:58:04">[02:58:04]</a>.

Early versions of Recollect helped users recall thoughts for writing or research, predating the widespread use of ChatGPT <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:13:00">[03:13:00]</a>. Over time, the tool evolved to help users define their goals and then [[enhancing_productivity_with_ai_tools | bring all necessary materials]], synthesize them, or alter them to be useful for the knowledge worker <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:26:00">[03:26:00]</a>. The overarching goal was to enable human intelligence, not just machine intelligence <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="03:46:00">[03:46:00]</a>.

### Challenges in Building AI Products for Knowledge Work

Building an AI-driven business for knowledge workers presented unique challenges:
*   **Early Model Limitations (Pre-ChatGPT)**: In 2019, when Recollect started, models like BERT and GPT-2 were nascent, making it difficult to demonstrate their full potential to others <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="05:01:00">[05:01:00]</a>.
*   **Balancing Trust and Innovation**: Post-ChatGPT, while public interest surged, there was skepticism about allowing models to directly generate content safely and reliably <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:01:00">[06:01:01]</a>.
*   **Shipping Early**: A key lesson learned was the importance of releasing products earlier, even if messy, to gather user feedback and iterate <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="06:41:00">[06:41:00]</a>. This often means embracing a less polished initial product to find what resonates with users <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="08:05:00">[08:05:00]</a>.
*   **Beyond Retrieval**: Once information retrieval became easier, the challenge shifted to determining the most useful applications of that retrieved information <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="07:28:00">[07:28:00]</a>.

## Human-AI Collaboration: The Cyborg Hypothesis

A central thesis in [[exploring_human_and_ai_collaboration | human and AI collaboration]] is the "cyborg hypothesis," which posits that technology should augment humans rather than aim for an independent superintelligence (AGI) <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:10:00">[10:10:00]</a>. This perspective suggests connecting AI technology to humans to make processes easier, allowing humans to focus on the [[role_of_ai_in_creative_work | creative piece]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:05:00">[11:05:00]</a>.

For knowledge work, AI can help with:
*   Consuming and distilling information <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:43:00">[10:43:00]</a>.
*   Understanding and propagating knowledge <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="10:46:00">[10:46:00]</a>.

*   Addressing human "evolutionary constraints" or struggles where technology can assist, without necessarily creating artificial senses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="11:53:00">[11:53:00]</a>.

## AI Interfaces for Knowledge Work

The optimal interface for AI interaction in knowledge work is a subject of debate:
*   **Chat Interfaces**: While powerful for language, chat interfaces may not be the most natural way to access or interact with AI for certain tasks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:30:00">[13:30:00]</a>. They excel in pushing forward along many dimensions simultaneously, particularly in [[using_ai_tools_for_creative_projects | creative processes]] where the dimensions are not predefined, allowing for exploration and refinement <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="16:43:00">[16:43:00]</a>.
*   **Agents and Background Work**: The trend towards AI agents that intuit tasks and work in the background without explicit "hello robot" commands is gaining traction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="13:42:00">[13:42:00]</a>.
*   **Visual and Artifact-Based Output**: For visual thinkers, AI generating artifacts like diagrams or maps can be highly beneficial for collaboration and understanding, moving beyond purely conversational interfaces <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="15:18:00">[15:18:00]</a>.
*   **Specific vs. General Interfaces**: [[vertical_ai_tools_for_specific_use_cases | Vertical AI tools for specific use cases]] that are less general, like Spiral (automating repetitive creative tasks, e.g., turning podcast transcripts into tweets), can be very effective, though user feedback often highlights the desire for a chat-like refinement layer <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="18:08:00">[18:08:00]</a>. This resembles a "manager-employee" relationship where the human provides feedback on an AI's output <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:06:00">[20:06:00]</a>.

The relationship between human and AI often takes on a "teacher-student" dynamic, where the human provides judgment and feedback <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="20:41:00">[20:41:00]</a>. AI's ability to create artifacts also provides insight into its "thinking," fostering a shared understanding between human and model <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="21:34:00">[21:34:00]</a>.

## AI's Role in Science and Research

AI is fundamentally changing how science is conducted:
*   **Knowledge Retrieval**: AI can comb through vast amounts of information, saving researchers countless hours of literature review <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="38:45:00">[38:45:00]</a>.
*   **Data Simulation**: AI models are becoming adept at simulating data based on existing datasets, allowing scientists to explore vast possibility spaces and narrow down hypotheses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="39:05:00">[39:05:05]</a>. This aids in hypothesis generation <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="39:56:00">[39:56:00]</a>.
*   **Shift from Papers to Data**: The traditional scientific paper format is questioned, with a strong argument for prioritizing the publication and aggregation of high-quality, usable data sets over just papers <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="40:31:00">[40:31:00]</a>. This approach encourages more [[experiments_and_tools_in_ai_research | experiments and tools in AI research]] and opens up data for diverse uses <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="45:14:00">[45:14:00]</a>.
    *   Publishing only successful experiments leads to a skewed view of science; making all data available, including failed attempts, provides a more complete picture <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="44:50:00">[00:44:50]</a>.
*   **Prediction Over Causal Explanation**: Especially in complex fields like psychology or psychiatry where causal explanations are highly complicated, AI can enable a focus on prediction <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:37:00">[41:37:00]</a>. For example, using personal biometric and behavioral data (N=1 experiment) to predict conditions like OCD could be life-changing, even if it deviates from traditional generalized scientific methods <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="41:50:00">[41:50:00]</a>. This mirrors the shift from symbolic to subsymbolic AI, where data-driven models learn patterns rather than relying on explicit rules <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="42:42:00">[42:42:00]</a>.
*   **Role of LLMs in Prediction**: While large language models (LLMs) can classify, they are not primarily designed for predictive modeling of time-series data or complex signals like physiological responses. Specialized models are often better for such tasks <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="30:01:00">[30:01:00]</a>, though LLMs can assist in building these models by generating code or processing data into features <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="36:31:00">[36:31:00]</a>.
*   **Data Access and Trusts**: Large tech companies holding vast amounts of data could establish "data trusts" to provide qualified researchers with access, enabling broader scientific inquiry that is currently limited by small grant-funded studies <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="47:36:00">[47:36:00]</a>.

## AI's Role in Media and Journalism

Just as in science, AI is set to transform media by unbundling the "story" from the "underlying data" <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="50:51:00">[50:51:00]</a>:
*   **Extendable Articles**: Tools like "extendable articles" allow readers to access all the source material (interviews, articles) used to create a piece of journalism via a chatbot, enabling them to form their own opinions <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="49:21:00">[49:21:00]</a>. This provides a "deeper understanding" by interconnecting information <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="50:14:00">[50:14:00]</a>.
*   **Cheaper Storytelling**: AI makes storytelling significantly cheaper, allowing for the creation of content in areas previously too expensive or niche for traditional media <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="55:41:00">[55:41:00]</a>.
    *   **TLDR**: An example is "TLDR," an AI-generated podcast summarizing company meetings, providing internal news that no traditional producer would create <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="54:43:00">[54:43:00]</a>.
    *   **Local News**: AI can revitalize local news by making it feasible to cover very localized events, filling a gap left by declining traditional journalism <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="58:01:00">[58:01:00]</a>.
*   **Accessibility of Stories**: AI can craft stories that are distilled, shorter, or translated into different formats (audio, video) to reach wider audiences who may not consume traditional written content <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="56:42:00">[56:42:00]</a>. This makes culture and information more accessible <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="57:37:00">[57:37:00]</a>.

The overarching impact of AI in knowledge work is to provide people with access to information they didn't have before, significantly [[enhancing_productivity_with_ai_tools | lowering the bar for storytelling and story consumption]] while [[enhancing_productivity_with_ai_tools | raising the ceiling on what's possible]] <a class="yt-timestamp" data-t="58:35:00">[58:35:00]</a>.
